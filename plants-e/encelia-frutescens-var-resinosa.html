Encelia frutescens var. resinosa

Encelia frutescens var. resinosa, commonly known as the resinous brittlebush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its glandular, resinous leaves that emit a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert and semi-arid environments.

Its unique botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
