Eugenia miegeana

Eugenia miegeana, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically bright red when ripe, encased in a thick, bumpy rind. The fruit is known for its high vitamin C content and is widely used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it a valuable species in both horticulture and agriculture.
