Eugenia bungadinnia

Eugenia bungadinnia, commonly known as the Philippine myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of the forest understory.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a desirable plant for horticultural purposes.
