Eschscholzia caespitosa var. rhombipetala

Eschscholzia caespitosa var. rhombipetala, commonly known as the rhombic bush poppy, is a perennial flowering plant native to California.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with a dense, mat-forming structure. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often hairy, while the flowers are showy, with bright orange to red petals. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of California.
