Eupatorium buddlejifolium

Eupatorium buddlejifolium, commonly known as the golden anise, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands and is known for its strong, licorice-like scent. It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

The botanical features of Eupatorium buddlejifolium make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
