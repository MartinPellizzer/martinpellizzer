Eupatorium serotinum var. polyneuron

Eupatorium serotinum var. polyneuron, commonly known as swamp boneset, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often growing in moist or swampy areas. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clustered heads, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal historical use.
