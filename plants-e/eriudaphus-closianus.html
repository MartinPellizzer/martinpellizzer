Eriudaphus closianus

Eriudaphus closianus, commonly known as the closeleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with white ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
