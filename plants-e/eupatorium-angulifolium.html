Eupatorium angulifolium

Eupatorium angulifolium, commonly known as boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its angular, ribbed stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite and often have a distinctive, toothed edge.

Boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
