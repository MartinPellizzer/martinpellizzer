Erythronium grandiflorum subsp. candidum

Erythronium grandiflorum subsp.

candidum, commonly known as the white dog orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features a rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that form a dense basal cluster.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive yellowish-green throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.
