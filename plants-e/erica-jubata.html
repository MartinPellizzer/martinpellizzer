Erica jubata

Erica jubata, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica jubata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a ground cover.

It is also noted for its ability to withstand drought conditions once established.
