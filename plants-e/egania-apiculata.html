Egania apiculata

Egania apiculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The leaves are typically leathery and have a distinctive velvety texture, contributing to its common name.
