Eryngium ebracteatum var. poterioides

Eryngium ebracteatum var. poterioides, commonly known as the purple thistle or sea holly, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It features striking, spiky purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silvery-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal gardens.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
