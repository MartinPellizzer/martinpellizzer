Eriobotrya capitata

Eriobotrya capitata, commonly known as the Chinese white flowering pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. Its fruit is a small, round pome that ripens in late summer, turning from green to yellow or red.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.
