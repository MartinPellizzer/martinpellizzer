Elaeocarpus integripetalus

Elaeocarpus integripetalus, commonly known as the smooth-petaled elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers with five petals. Its fruits are woody, spherical berries that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological importance.
