Exocarpos psilotiformis

Exocarpos psilotiformis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Rhizophoraceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in humid subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional crafts, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
