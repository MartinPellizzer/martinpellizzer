Encyclia sima

Encyclia sima, commonly known as the Simala orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, with flowers emerging from the base of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Encyclia sima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
