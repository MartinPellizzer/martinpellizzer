Eulophia albiflora

Eulophia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Eulophia albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its striking floral display.
