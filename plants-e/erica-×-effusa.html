Erica × effusa

Erica × effusa, commonly known as the cross-leaved heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of needle-like leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Erica × effusa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-maintenance ground cover.
