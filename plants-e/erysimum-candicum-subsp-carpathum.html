Erysimum candicum subsp. carpathum

Erysimum candicum subsp.

carpathum, commonly known as Carpathian wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its cruciform flowers and segmented fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, often arranged in racemes, and has linear, hairy leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
