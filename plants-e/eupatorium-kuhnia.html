Eupatorium kuhnia

Eupatorium kuhnia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Eupatorium kuhnia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
