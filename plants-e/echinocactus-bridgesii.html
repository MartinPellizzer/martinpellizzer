Echinocactus bridgesii

Echinocactus bridgesii, commonly known as the Golden Barrel Cactus, is a distinctive cactus native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of bright yellow spines, which give it its common name. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are typically yellow or orange and bloom at the top of the plant.

Echinocactus bridgesii is popular as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
