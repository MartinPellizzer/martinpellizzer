Eucalyptus albopurpurea

Eucalyptus albopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-honey gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or white. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in umbels. It is a hardy tree that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
