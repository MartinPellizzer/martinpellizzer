Evolvulus jacobinus var. ramosus

Evolvulus jacobinus var. ramosus, commonly known as the creeping blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as gardens and disturbed soils. The plant features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. Its small, showy blue to violet flowers are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
