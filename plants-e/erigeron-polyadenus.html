Erigeron polyadenus

Erigeron polyadenus, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps, and it is often found in the eastern United States. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while its flowers are blue to purple in color, attracting various pollinators.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
