Epidendrum calamarium

Epidendrum calamarium, commonly known as the sea horse orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescence that resembles a sea horse, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with red or orange markings, and it blooms throughout the year in its native habitat.

Epidendrum calamarium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable growing environment.
