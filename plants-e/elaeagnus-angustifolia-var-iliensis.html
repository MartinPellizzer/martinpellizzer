Elaeagnus angustifolia var. iliensis

Elaeagnus angustifolia var. iliensis, commonly known as the Ili silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with limited water resources.
