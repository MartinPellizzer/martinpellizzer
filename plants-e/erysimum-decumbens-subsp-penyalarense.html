Erysimum decumbens subsp. penyalarense

Erysimum decumbens subsp.

penyalarense, commonly known as the Penyalarense wallflower, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has prostrate or decumbent stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are linear to oblanceolate, with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
