Erucastrum obtusangulum subsp. intermedium

Erucastrum obtusangulum subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate bladderpod, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and pinnately compound leaves with rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by inflated, bladder-like pods. It is native to regions of North America and thrives in dry, disturbed soils.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
