Eragrostis trichocolea var. floridana

Eragrostis trichocolea var. floridana, commonly known as Florida lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, tufted blades with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its fine texture and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental and erosion control purposes.

It is also valued for its ability to provide forage for livestock.
