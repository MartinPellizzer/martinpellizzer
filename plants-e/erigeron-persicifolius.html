Erigeron persicifolius

Erigeron persicifolius, commonly known as the peachleaf coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy blue to purple daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has basal and upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that resemble peach leaves, giving it its common name. Its flowering heads consist of a central yellow disk surrounded by ray florets.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
