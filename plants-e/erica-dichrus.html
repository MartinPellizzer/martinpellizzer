Erica dichrus

Erica dichrus, commonly known as the two-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Erica dichrus typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
