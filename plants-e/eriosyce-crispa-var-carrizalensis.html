Eriosyce crispa var. carrizalensis

Eriosyce crispa var. carrizalensis, commonly known as the Carrizalensis Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that form a spherical or hemispherical shape. The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
