Erythroxylum monogynum

Erythroxylum monogynum, commonly known as the pink stimulant plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain seeds.

The plant is part of the Erythroxylaceae family and is known for its historical and cultural significance in certain regions.
