Eurycaulis crassimarginatus

Eurycaulis crassimarginatus, commonly known as the broad-margin eurycaulis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves with prominent margins, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
