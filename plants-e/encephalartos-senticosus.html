Encephalartos senticosus

Encephalartos senticosus, commonly known as the Natal cabbage cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, stout trunk and a crown of large, pinnate leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, dry climates.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
