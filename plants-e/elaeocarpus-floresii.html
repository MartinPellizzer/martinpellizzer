Elaeocarpus floresii

Elaeocarpus floresii, commonly known as the Flores satinwood, is a tropical tree native to the island of Flores in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds. This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
