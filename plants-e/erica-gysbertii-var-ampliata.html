Erica gysbertii var. ampliata

Erica gysbertii var. ampliata, commonly known as the Cape rosemary, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.

The variety ampliata is distinguished by its broader leaves and more prolific flowering compared to the typical form.
