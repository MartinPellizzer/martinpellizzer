Eremophila decipiens

Eremophila decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its succulent leaves.

Eremophila decipiens is often found in sandy soils and is a common sight in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
