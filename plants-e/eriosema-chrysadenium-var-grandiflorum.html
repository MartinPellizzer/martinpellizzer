Eriosema chrysadenium var. grandiflorum

Eriosema chrysadenium var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the golden-flowered eriosema, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features delicate, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
