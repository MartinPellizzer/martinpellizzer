Erythraea linariifolia

Erythraea linariifolia, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, spiny fruits that resemble small apples, which are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Erythraea linariifolia is often grown in gardens for its unique appearance and is also used in traditional medicine.
