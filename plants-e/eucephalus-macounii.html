Eucephalus macounii

Eucephalus macounii, commonly known as Macoun's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a silvery underside, while its flowers are small and white or pale pink.

Eucephalus macounii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and contributing to forest understory diversity.
