Elatostema dissectum var. dissectum

Elatostema dissectum var. dissectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false shamrock, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veined pattern and a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
