Erica sanguinolenta

Erica sanguinolenta, commonly known as the blood heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers during the summer months, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Erica sanguinolenta thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
