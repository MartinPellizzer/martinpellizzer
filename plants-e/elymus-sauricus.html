Elymus sauricus

Elymus sauricus, commonly known as the desert barley, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine layer of hairs, aiding in moisture retention.

Elymus sauricus is adapted to harsh environments, making it a valuable plant for erosion control and livestock forage in dryland ecosystems.
