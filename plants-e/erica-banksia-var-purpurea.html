Erica banksia var. purpurea

Erica banksia var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple banksia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows in sandy or acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. It produces woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
