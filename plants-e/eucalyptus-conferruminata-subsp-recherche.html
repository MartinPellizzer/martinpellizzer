Eucalyptus conferruminata subsp. recherche

Eucalyptus conferruminata subsp.

recherche, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and lanceolate leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. Its bark is fibrous and peels in thin strips, a common feature among many eucalyptus species.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland communities.
