Erucastrum rifanum

Erucastrum rifanum, commonly known as the Rif mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Erucastrum rifanum is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
