Erycibe pararan

Erycibe pararan, commonly known as the purple-leafed erycibe, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-purple in cooler temperatures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible but have a mildly toxic reputation. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
