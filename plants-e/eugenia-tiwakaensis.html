Eugenia tiwakaensis

Eugenia tiwakaensis, commonly known as the Kauri plum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, plum-like fruits.

Eugenia tiwakaensis is often found in coastal and lowland forests, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
