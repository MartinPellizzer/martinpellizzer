Euphorbia philochalix

Euphorbia philochalix, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stem and distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries that resemble small tomatoes. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and diverse morphological features. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
