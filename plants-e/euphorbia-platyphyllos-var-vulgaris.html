Euphorbia platyphyllos var. vulgaris

Euphorbia platyphyllos var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common spurge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a milky, toxic sap that can cause skin irritation, so caution is advised when handling it. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its foliage is typically broad and fleshy, with a distinctive appearance that makes it stand out in the landscape.
