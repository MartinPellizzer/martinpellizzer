Epipactis helleborine var. lancifolia

Epipactis helleborine var. lancifolia, commonly known as the lancolate helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

The orchid is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, often forming symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.
