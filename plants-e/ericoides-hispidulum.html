Ericoides hispidulum

Ericoides hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy ericoid, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in acidic conditions. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The hairy ericoid is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it contributes to the unique flora of these high-altitude environments.
