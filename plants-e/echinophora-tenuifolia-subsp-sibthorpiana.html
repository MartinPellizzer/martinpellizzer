Echinophora tenuifolia subsp. sibthorpiana

Echinophora tenuifolia subsp.

sibthorpiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved echinophora, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
