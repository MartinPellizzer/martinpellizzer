Erica propendens

Erica propendens, commonly known as the leaning heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in the summer months. Erica propendens thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
