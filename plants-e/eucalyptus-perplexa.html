Eucalyptus perplexa

Eucalyptus perplexa, commonly known as the slender gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that ranges from white to greyish in color. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue and a distinctive aromatic scent. It produces small, globular flower buds that open to reveal white or cream flowers, which are attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands.
