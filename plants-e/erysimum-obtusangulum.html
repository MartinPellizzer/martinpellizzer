Erysimum obtusangulum

Erysimum obtusangulum, commonly known as the broad-leafed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is well adapted to dry conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
