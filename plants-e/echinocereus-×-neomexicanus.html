Echinocereus × neomexicanus

Echinocereus × neomexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican hedgehog cactus, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless or sparsely spined columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and a tendency to form clumps. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
