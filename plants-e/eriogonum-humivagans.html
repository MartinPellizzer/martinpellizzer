Eriogonum humivagans

Eriogonum humivagans, commonly known as the desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss.
