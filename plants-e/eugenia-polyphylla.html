Eugenia polyphylla

Eugenia polyphylla, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Eugenia polyphylla thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
