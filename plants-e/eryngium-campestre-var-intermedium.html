Eryngium campestre var. intermedium

Eryngium campestre var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate field thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features spiky, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by stiff, silver-green bracts. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is often considered a subspecies of Eryngium campestre, showing intermediate characteristics between the typical and other forms.
