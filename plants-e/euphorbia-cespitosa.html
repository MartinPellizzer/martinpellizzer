Euphorbia cespitosa

Euphorbia cespitosa, commonly known as the upright spurge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Euphorbia cespitosa is also known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
