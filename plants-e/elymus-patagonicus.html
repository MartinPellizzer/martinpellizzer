Elymus patagonicus

Elymus patagonicus, commonly known as Patagonian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often arranged in panicles, which are the primary floral structures. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is adapted to temperate climates.

Elymus patagonicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
