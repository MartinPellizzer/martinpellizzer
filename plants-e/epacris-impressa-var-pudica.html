Epacris impressa var. pudica

Epacris impressa var. pudica, commonly known as the velvetleaf epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety down. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
