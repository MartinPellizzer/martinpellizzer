Euphorbia mandshurica

Euphorbia mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian spurge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Euphorbia mandshurica is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
