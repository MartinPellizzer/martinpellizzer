Echium aragonense

Echium aragonense, commonly known as the Aragon echium, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking, spiny, purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Echium aragonense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
