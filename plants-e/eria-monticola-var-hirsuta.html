Eria monticola var. hirsuta

Eria monticola var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mountain eria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
