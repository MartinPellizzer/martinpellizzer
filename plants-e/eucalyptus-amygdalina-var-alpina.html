Eucalyptus amygdalina var. alpina

Eucalyptus amygdalina var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine almond gum, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grey or white bark that peels in thin strips. The leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and usually have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
