Elymus excelsus

Elymus excelsus, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces spikelets that are arranged in panicles, with each spikelet containing two to three small, awned florets. Elymus excelsus thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open fields, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

It is valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage plant for livestock.
