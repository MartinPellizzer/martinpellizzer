Escobaria villardii

Escobaria villardii, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, spiny leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in a terminal inflorescence. Escobaria villardii is adapted to full sun and well-draining soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
