Eremophila petrophila

Eremophila petrophila, commonly known as the rock eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth form. The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red. It is adapted to grow in arid and rocky environments, often found in coastal and inland regions.

Eremophila petrophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
