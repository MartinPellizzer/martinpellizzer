Eria tomentella

Eria tomentella, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale green, often clustered in inflorescences.

Eria tomentella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
