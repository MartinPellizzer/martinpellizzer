Eremophila alatisepala

Eremophila alatisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, pink to purple flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila alatisepala is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
