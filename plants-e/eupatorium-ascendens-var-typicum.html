Eupatorium ascendens var. typicum

Eupatorium ascendens var. typicum, commonly known as the creeping blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or sprawling.

It plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
