Enteropogonopsis chloridea

Enteropogonopsis chloridea, commonly known as the green false bulbous grass, is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the grass family with ecological significance in its native range.
