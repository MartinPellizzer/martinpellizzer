Echinocactus polycephalus var. flavispinus

Echinocactus polycephalus var. flavispinus, commonly known as the golden spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense covering of spines, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its striking appearance and unique features, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
