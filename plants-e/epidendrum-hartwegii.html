Epidendrum hartwegii

Epidendrum hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The flowers often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
