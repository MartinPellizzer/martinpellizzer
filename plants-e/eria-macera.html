Eria macera

Eria macera, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green leaves and its unique, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the canopy of dense forests. Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Eria macera is considered an endangered species.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its value in botanical research and conservation efforts.
