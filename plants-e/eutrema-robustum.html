Eutrema robustum

Eutrema robustum, commonly known as the coastal tansy, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines, demonstrating strong adaptability to saline and wind-swept environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Eutrema robustum plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
