Epipactis helleborine var. tangutica

Epipactis helleborine var. tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers during the summer months.

It is considered a rare and protected species in many regions due to habitat loss and over-collection.
