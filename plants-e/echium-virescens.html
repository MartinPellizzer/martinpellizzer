Echium virescens

Echium virescens, commonly known as the greenweed or green echium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces striking spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Echium virescens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
