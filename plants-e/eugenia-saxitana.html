Eugenia saxitana

Eugenia saxitana, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its fruit, which is small and berry-like, contains seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Eugenia saxitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
