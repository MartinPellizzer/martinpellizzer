Escallonia farinacea var. jordanensis

Escallonia farinacea var. jordanensis, commonly known as Jordan's Escallonia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid climates.
