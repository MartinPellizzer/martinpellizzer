Erica bolusiae

Erica bolusiae, commonly known as the Bolus heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in winter and early spring. Erica bolusiae thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it forms dense thickets.
