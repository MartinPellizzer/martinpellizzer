Exacum quadrangulare

Exacum quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angled African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its square, woody stems and fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to both gardens and containers.
