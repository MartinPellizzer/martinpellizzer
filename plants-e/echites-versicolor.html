Echites versicolor

Echites versicolor, commonly known as the variable echites, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red, often with a contrasting central stripe. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
