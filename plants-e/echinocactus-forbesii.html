Echinocactus forbesii

Echinocactus forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white or yellow flowers. The plant has a slow growth rate and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal rainfall. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

Echinocactus forbesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
