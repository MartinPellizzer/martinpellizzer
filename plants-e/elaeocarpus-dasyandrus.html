Elaeocarpus dasyandrus

Elaeocarpus dasyandrus, commonly known as the woolly elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
