Eriocaulon magnificum

Eriocaulon magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent bulbous rush, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with a distinctive rosette structure that helps it thrive in wetland environments. The plant produces tall, spike-like inflorescences adorned with small, white to pale blue flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in shallow water.

Eriocaulon magnificum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing aquatic habitats.
