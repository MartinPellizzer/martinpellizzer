Elaphoglossum succubus

Elaphoglossum succubus, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Elaphoglossum succubus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
