Eurycoma longifolia var. cambodiana

Eurycoma longifolia var. cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its roots and bark are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often found in humid, lowland areas.
