Erinia campanula

Erinia campanula, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Erinia campanula has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
