Eucalyptus camphora subsp. camphora

Eucalyptus camphora subsp.

camphora, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species native to Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that release a strong camphor scent when crushed.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, peeling bark that reveals a fibrous inner layer. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
