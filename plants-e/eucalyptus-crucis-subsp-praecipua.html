Eucalyptus crucis subsp. praecipua

Eucalyptus crucis subsp.

praecipua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is part of the Eucalyptus genus, known for its aromatic oils and ecological importance in Australian ecosystems.
