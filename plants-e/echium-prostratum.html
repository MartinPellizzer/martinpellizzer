Echium prostratum

Echium prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to harsh conditions.
