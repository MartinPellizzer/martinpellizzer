Echites candelarianus

Echites candelarianus, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, candle-like appearance due to its elongated, cylindrical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive species for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
