Eriocaulon brunonis

Eriocaulon brunonis, commonly known as the black rush, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters in wetland environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically inconspicuous and wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
