Epacris navicularis

Epacris navicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf epacris, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Epacris navicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
