Escallonia rubra var. punctata

Escallonia rubra var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted escallonia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive red or purple spots, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to coastal and temperate landscapes.
