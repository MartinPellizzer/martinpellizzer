Erysimum halleri

Erysimum halleri, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and oval-shaped, while its flowers are often yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum halleri is often used in rock gardens and alpine plant collections due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
