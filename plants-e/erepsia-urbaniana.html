Erepsia urbaniana

Erepsia urbaniana, commonly known as the urbanian ericaceous plant, is a species native to specific regions in southern Europe.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant thrives in acidic soil conditions and requires well-drained, moist environments. It is often found in woodland areas and along rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
