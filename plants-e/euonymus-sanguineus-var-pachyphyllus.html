Euonymus sanguineus var. pachyphyllus

Euonymus sanguineus var. pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaved woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. Its foliage provides seasonal color changes, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
