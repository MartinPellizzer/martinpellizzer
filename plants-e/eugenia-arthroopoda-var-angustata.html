Eugenia arthroopoda var. angustata

Eugenia arthroopoda var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wax apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which is typically small and yellowish, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its potential medicinal properties.
