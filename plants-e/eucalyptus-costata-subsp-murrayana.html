Eucalyptus costata subsp. murrayana

Eucalyptus costata subsp.

murrayana, commonly known as the Murray's mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or loamy soils.

This subspecies is part of the Eucalyptus genus, which is well-known for its aromatic oils and ecological significance in Australian ecosystems.
