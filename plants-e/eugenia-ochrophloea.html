Eugenia ochrophloea

Eugenia ochrophloea, commonly known as the red-barked eugenia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and oval, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
