Echinopsis spinibarbis

Echinopsis spinibarbis, commonly known as the spiny-tailed cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and a distinctive, spiky, tail-like structure at the base. The plant produces large, showy white or yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in the summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
