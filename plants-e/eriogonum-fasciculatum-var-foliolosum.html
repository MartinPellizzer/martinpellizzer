Eriogonum fasciculatum var. foliolosum

Eriogonum fasciculatum var. foliolosum, commonly known as the leafy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leafy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
