Effusiella dilatata

Effusiella dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It has a flat, leaf-like structure with a greenish-yellow upper surface and a pale, leathery underside. The thallus is composed of a symbiotic association between a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forests.

Its growth is slow, and it plays a role in nutrient cycling within its ecosystem.
