Eupatorium frasieri

Eupatorium frasieri, commonly known as the frasier's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Eupatorium frasieri is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
