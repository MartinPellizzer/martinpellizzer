Euphorbia serpillifolia var. genuina

Euphorbia serpillifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the Mediterranean spurge, is a succulent shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, helping it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
