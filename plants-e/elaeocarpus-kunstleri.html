Elaeocarpus kunstleri

Elaeocarpus kunstleri, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
