Epiphanes

Epiphanes, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is characterized by its drooping panicles of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces leguminous pods that contain seeds. Epiphanes thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
