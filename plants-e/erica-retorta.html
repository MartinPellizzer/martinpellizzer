Erica retorta

Erica retorta, commonly known as the twisted heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica retorta thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
