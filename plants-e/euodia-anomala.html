Euodia anomala

Euodia anomala, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind or wild tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Euodia anomala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
