Epidendrum atwoodchlamys

Epidendrum atwoodchlamys, commonly known as the Atwood's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant prefers warm, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and covered in a fine, velvety texture, which helps in moisture retention.

Epidendrum atwoodchlamys is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
