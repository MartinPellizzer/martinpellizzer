Erica penicillata

Erica penicillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica penicillata produces pink to purple flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
