Eriogonum tenuissimum

Eriogonum tenuissimum, commonly known as the slender buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often found in desert and foothill regions. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Eriogonum tenuissimum is an important component of its native ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
