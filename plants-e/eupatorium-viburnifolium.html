Eupatorium viburnifolium

Eupatorium viburnifolium, commonly known as the swamp dewberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and moist woodlands, thriving in humid environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in swampy areas and is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
