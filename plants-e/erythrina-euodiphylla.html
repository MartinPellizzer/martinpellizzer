Erythrina euodiphylla

Erythrina euodiphylla, commonly known as the red torchwood tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are clustered in dense panicles and attract various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its bark is often covered in a thick, smooth layer. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Erythrina euodiphylla thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
