Eryngium aristulatum var. parishii

Eryngium aristulatum var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
