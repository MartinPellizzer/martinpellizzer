Euphorbia pilosa var. trichocarpa

Euphorbia pilosa var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves covered in fine, bristly hairs.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower structures typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its milky sap, it can be toxic if ingested and should be handled with care.
