Englerodendron

Englerodendron, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Englerodendron* tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, often clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The genus includes several species, such as *Englerodendron microphyllum*, which is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance. The plant's botanical features include pod-like fruits that split open to release seeds, and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Englerodendron species are often used in horticulture for their aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
