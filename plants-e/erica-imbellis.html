Erica imbellis

Erica imbellis, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are leathery and covered in a fine, white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica imbellis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
