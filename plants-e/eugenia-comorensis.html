Eugenia comorensis

Eugenia comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Comoros Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Eugenia comorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a significant plant in local horticulture.
