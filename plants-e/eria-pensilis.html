Eria pensilis

Eria pensilis, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous growth habit, with slender stems that hang downward from its base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often clustered in racemes. Eria pensilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor cultivation.
