Eupatorium nudum

Eupatorium nudum, commonly known as the naked stem eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Eupatorium nudum produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
