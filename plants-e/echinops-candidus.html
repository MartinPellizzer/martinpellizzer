Echinops candidus

Echinops candidus, commonly known as the white thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are covered in white, silky florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are pale yellow to white, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Echinops candidus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
