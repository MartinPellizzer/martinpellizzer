Euryops intermedius

Euryops intermedius, commonly known as the yellow oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a central brown disc, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and grows in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and open woodlands. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Euryops intermedius is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
