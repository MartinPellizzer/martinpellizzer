Ennealophus foliosus subsp. amazonicus

Ennealophus foliosus subsp.

amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian leafy fern, is a species native to the dense rainforests of South America. It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are often divided into small, pointed segments.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its reproductive structures are concealed within spore-bearing clusters, which are released into the air during specific seasons.

This subspecies plays a minor but ecologically significant role in the nutrient cycling of its habitat.
