Eucalyptus pachyphylla var. sessilis

Eucalyptus pachyphylla var. sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, often clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a significant role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in revegetation projects in degraded landscapes.
