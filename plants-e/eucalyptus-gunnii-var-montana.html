Eucalyptus gunnii var. montana

Eucalyptus gunnii var. montana, commonly known as the mountain grey box, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped, grey-green leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically pink or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
