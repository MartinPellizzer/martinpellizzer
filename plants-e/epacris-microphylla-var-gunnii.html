Epacris microphylla var. gunnii

Epacris microphylla var. gunnii, commonly known as the Gunns' epacris, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It is a member of the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a popular species among gardeners and conservationists.
