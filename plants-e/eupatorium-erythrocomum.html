Eupatorium erythrocomum

Eupatorium erythrocomum, commonly known as redstem boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, hairy stems that are reddish-brown in color, and it produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have serrated edges.

It is often found in wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
