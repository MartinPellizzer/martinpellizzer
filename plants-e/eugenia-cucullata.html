Eugenia cucullata

Eugenia cucullata, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The leaves and flowers are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
