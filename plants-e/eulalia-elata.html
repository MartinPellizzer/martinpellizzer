Eulalia elata

Eulalia elata, commonly known as the tall grass bluemink, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Eulalia elata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
