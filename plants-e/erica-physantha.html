Erica physantha

Erica physantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family, Ericaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. Erica physantha is well adapted to nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various insect and bird species.
