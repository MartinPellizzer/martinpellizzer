Eugenia cerasina

Eugenia cerasina, commonly known as the cherry guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red or orange berries. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, making it popular in local cuisines and as a ornamental plant.
