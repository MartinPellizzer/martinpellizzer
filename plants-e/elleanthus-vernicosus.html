Elleanthus vernicosus

Elleanthus vernicosus, commonly known as the roughleaf elleanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and serrated, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrubland or rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
