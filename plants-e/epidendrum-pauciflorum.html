Epidendrum pauciflorum

Epidendrum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown.

Epidendrum pauciflorum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
