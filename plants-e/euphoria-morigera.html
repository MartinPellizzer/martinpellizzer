Euphoria morigera

Euphoria morigera, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on water surfaces, with bright pink or white petals. The plant has a thick, waxy stem that allows it to float, and its leaves are broad and rounded, often with a distinctive veining pattern. Euphoria morigera is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures and is also valued for its medicinal properties.

Its growth cycle is closely tied to seasonal changes, with flowers opening at dawn and closing at sunset.
