Echinocactus sulcatus

Echinocactus sulcatus, commonly known as the Sulcate Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, spiny cactus characterized by its ribbed, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
