Euphorbia ballyi

Euphorbia ballyi, commonly known as the Bally's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
