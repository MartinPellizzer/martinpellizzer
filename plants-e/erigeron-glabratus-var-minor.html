Erigeron glabratus var. minor

Erigeron glabratus var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are white to pale blue in color.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
