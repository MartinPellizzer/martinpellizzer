Eriophorum virginicum var. gracile

Eriophorum virginicum var. gracile, commonly known as slender tussock sedge, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in a tight, tussock-like formation, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
