Euchilus crinipodus

Euchilus crinipodus, commonly known as the crownfoot orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in shaded forest areas. Its unique morphology, including the elongated, curling petals, makes it a distinctive member of the Orchidaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Euchilus crinipodus is considered a vulnerable species.
