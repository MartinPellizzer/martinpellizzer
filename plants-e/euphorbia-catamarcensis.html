Euphorbia catamarcensis

Euphorbia catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarcensis spurge, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and distinctive cyathia, which are modified inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, while the stems store water, aiding in survival during dry periods.

Euphorbia catamarcensis is also noted for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
