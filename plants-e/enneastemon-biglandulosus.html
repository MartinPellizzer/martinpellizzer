Enneastemon biglandulosus

Enneastemon biglandulosus, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
