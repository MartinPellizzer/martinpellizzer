Echinocactus ingens var. saltillensis

Echinocactus ingens var. saltillensis, commonly known as the Mexican giant cactus, is a striking species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its massive, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, covered in thick, ribbed structures. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and botanical gardens.
