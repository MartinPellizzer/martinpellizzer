Erechtites quadridentatus var. gunnii

Erechtites quadridentatus var. gunnii, commonly known as the Gunn's tickseed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small wildlife.
