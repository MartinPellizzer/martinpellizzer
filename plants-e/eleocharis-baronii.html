Eleocharis baronii

Eleocharis baronii, commonly known as the Baron's spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

Eleocharis baronii plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
