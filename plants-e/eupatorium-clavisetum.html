Eupatorium clavisetum

Eupatorium clavisetum, commonly known as the clustered boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are small, white to pale blue, and appear in clustered inflorescences, blooming from late summer to early autumn.
