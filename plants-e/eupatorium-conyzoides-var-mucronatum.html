Eupatorium conyzoides var. mucronatum

Eupatorium conyzoides var. mucronatum, commonly known as blackroot or mucronate eupatorium, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves that are usually opposite and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for treating ailments such as inflammation and digestive issues.
