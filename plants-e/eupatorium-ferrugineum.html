Eupatorium ferrugineum

Eupatorium ferrugineum, commonly known as rusty eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypselae, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
