Echinochloa spiralis

Echinochloa spiralis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
