Eugenia lagoensis

Eugenia lagoensis, commonly known as the Lagoense Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Eugenia lagoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
