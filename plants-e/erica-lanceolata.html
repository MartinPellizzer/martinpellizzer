Erica lanceolata

Erica lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or acidic soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica lanceolata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs.
