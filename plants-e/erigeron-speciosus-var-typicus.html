Erigeron speciosus var. typicus

Erigeron speciosus var. typicus, commonly known as the showy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which consist of white ray florets surrounding a yellow disk floret. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowering period occurs in the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
