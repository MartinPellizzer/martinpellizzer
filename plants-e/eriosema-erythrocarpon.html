Eriosema erythrocarpon

Eriosema erythrocarpon, commonly known as the red-fruited eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

The red, hairy pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
