Encelia alternifolia

Encelia alternifolia, commonly known as the desert cholla, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Encelia alternifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
