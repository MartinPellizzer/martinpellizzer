Erica × archeria

Erica × archeria, commonly known as the African heath or the common heath, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica × archeria thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing hedges or ground covers.
