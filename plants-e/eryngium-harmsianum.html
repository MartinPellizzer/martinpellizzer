Eryngium harmsianum

Eryngium harmsianum, commonly known as the African thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features striking blue to purple spiky flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its unique appearance and ecological benefits make it a notable species in its native habitat.
