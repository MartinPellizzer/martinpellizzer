Eulalia aurea

Eulalia aurea, commonly known as golden grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and bright yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of golden spikelets.

Eulalia aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
