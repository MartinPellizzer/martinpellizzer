Eryngium carlinae

Eryngium carlinae, commonly known as the California sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has silvery-green, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

Eryngium carlinae is a perennial plant that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
