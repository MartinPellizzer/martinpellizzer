Epilobium elegans

Epilobium elegans, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Epilobium elegans typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
