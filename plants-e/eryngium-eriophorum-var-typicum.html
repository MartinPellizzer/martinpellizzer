Eryngium eriophorum var. typicum

Eryngium eriophorum var. typicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant's structure and appearance make it well adapted to its wetland habitats.
