Euphorbia neobosseri var. itampolensis

Euphorbia neobosseri var. itampolensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small scales, and it thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
