Euphorbia esula var. cyparissioides

Euphorbia esula var. cyparissioides, commonly known as the wooly cupplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features upright, woody stems that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is commonly found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
