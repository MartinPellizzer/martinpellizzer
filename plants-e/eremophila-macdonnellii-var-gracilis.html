Eremophila macdonnellii var. gracilis

Eremophila macdonnellii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of tubular, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to drought and poor soils, making it a hardy species suitable for dry gardens. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
