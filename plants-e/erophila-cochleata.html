Erophila cochleata

Erophila cochleata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in open grasslands and along riverbanks. It is an annual species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
