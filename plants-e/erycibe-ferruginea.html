Erycibe ferruginea

Erycibe ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty erycibe, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red and orange. The plant is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often visited by hummingbirds and other pollinators.

The fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.
