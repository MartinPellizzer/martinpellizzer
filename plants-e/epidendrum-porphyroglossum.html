Epidendrum porphyroglossum

Epidendrum porphyroglossum, commonly known as the purple-lipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and green foliage and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive purple lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Epidendrum porphyroglossum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
