Eremostachys nuda

Eremostachys nuda, commonly known as the naked eremostachys, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers on slender stems during the summer months. It is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
