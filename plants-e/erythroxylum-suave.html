Erythroxylum suave

Erythroxylum suave, commonly known as the sweet erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves with a smooth texture. It produces a reddish or pinkish fruit that contains seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
