Elaeocarpus gigantifolius

Elaeocarpus gigantifolius, commonly known as the giant-leaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 50 cm in length. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
