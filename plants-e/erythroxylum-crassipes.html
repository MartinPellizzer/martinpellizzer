Erythroxylum crassipes

Erythroxylum crassipes, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a small, orange-red capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Erythroxylum crassipes is part of the Erythroxylaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
