Eria lindleyana

Eria lindleyana, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing plant with thin, wiry stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
