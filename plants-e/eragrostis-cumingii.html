Eragrostis cumingii

Eragrostis cumingii, commonly known as Cuming's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis cumingii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
