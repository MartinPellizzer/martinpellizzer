Erigeron schmalhausenii

Erigeron schmalhausenii, commonly known as the Schmalhausen's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-gray hue that helps it reflect sunlight in arid environments.
