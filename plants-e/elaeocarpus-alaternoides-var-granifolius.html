Elaeocarpus alaternoides var. granifolius

Elaeocarpus alaternoides var. granifolius, commonly known as the granifolius elaeocarpus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
