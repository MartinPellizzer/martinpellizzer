Echinocactus viznaga

Echinocactus viznaga, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking contrast to its green exterior. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.

Echinocactus viznaga is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.
