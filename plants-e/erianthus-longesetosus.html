Erianthus longesetosus

Erianthus longesetosus, commonly known as the long-hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, silky hairs covering its leaves and stems. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or brown in color. Erianthus longesetosus is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.

Its fibrous roots make it a useful plant for erosion control and soil stabilization.
