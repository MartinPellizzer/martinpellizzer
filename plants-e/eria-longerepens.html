Eria longerepens

Eria longerepens, commonly known as the long-leaf eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in green or reddish hues, and are borne on short pedicels.

Eria longerepens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
