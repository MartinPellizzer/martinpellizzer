Eupatorium pampalcense

Eupatorium pampalcense, commonly known as the Pampas feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Eupatorium pampalcense is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
