Ericoides mertensianum

Ericoides mertensianum, commonly known as the Mertens' ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Ericoides mertensianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to specific ecological niches in its native habitat.
