Eriocaulon aquatile f. viviparum

Eriocaulon aquatile f. viviparum, commonly known as the viviparous water starwort, is a variant of the water starwort species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and small, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow, still water or moist soils, often in marshy areas. One of its notable features is the production of viviparous structures, which allow for vegetative propagation through the development of shoots on the stems.

This adaptation enhances its ability to colonize and thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
