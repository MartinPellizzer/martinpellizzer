Entada patens

Entada patens, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain hard, woody seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for shade gardens and as a specimen tree in tropical landscapes.
