Eutrema humboldtii

Eutrema humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. It has a short growing season and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
