Elaeocarpus prunifolius

Elaeocarpus prunifolius, commonly known as the red-fruited elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is an important food source for various bird species.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
