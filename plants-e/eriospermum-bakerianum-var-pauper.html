Eriospermum bakerianum var. pauper

Eriospermum bakerianum var. pauper, commonly known as the poor eriosperm, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is a valued plant in botanical collections and native gardens.
