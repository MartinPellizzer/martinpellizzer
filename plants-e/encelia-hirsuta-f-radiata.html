Encelia hirsuta f. radiata

Encelia hirsuta f. radiata, commonly known as the radiate encelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features glandular-hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and aid in water retention. Its yellow-orange flower heads are composed of numerous small florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
