Erythroxylum acuminatum

Erythroxylum acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, oval-shaped berries that contain seeds.

This species is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes but is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
