Eugenia reticularis

Eugenia reticularis, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a bright red outer skin and a white, segmented flesh. Its botanical features include a dense root system and a tendency to form shrub-like growth in certain conditions.

The fruit is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
