Eupatorium simillimum

Eupatorium simillimum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, blue to purple flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium simillimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
