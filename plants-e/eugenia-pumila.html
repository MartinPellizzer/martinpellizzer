Eugenia pumila

Eugenia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, spherical berries that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia pumila is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
