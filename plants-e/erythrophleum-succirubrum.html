Erythrophleum succirubrum

Erythrophleum succirubrum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Due to overharvesting, it is now listed as an endangered species in many regions.
