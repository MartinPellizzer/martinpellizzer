Eryngium depressum var. flaccidum

Eryngium depressum var. flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid thistle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features spiny, blue-green foliage and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
