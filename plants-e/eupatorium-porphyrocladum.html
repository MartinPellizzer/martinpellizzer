Eupatorium porphyrocladum

Eupatorium porphyrocladum, commonly known as the purple-leaf eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking purple and green variegated foliage that adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and mixed borders.
