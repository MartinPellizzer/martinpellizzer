Erica unicolor subsp. mutica

Erica unicolor subsp.

mutica, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of South Africa. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers.

The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure. Erica unicolor subsp.

mutica is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
