Eugenia morii

Eugenia morii, commonly known as the Morii's cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries.

Eugenia morii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
