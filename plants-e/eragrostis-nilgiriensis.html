Eragrostis nilgiriensis

Eragrostis nilgiriensis, commonly known as Nilgiri lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to thrive in temperate to subtropical climates.

Eragrostis nilgiriensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
