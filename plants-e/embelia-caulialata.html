Embelia caulialata

Embelia caulialata, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. Its botanical features include aromatic leaves and a woody stem, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Embelia caulialata is often cultivated for its health benefits and is used in traditional medicine systems like Ayurveda.
