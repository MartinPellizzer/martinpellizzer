Erucastrum varium var. campestre

Erucastrum varium var. campestre, commonly known as the field bittercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its seeds are contained in slender, cylindrical pods that split open when mature.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be a common weed in agricultural fields.
