Eriosema rufescens

Eriosema rufescens, commonly known as the rough eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
