Eupatorium purpureum var. amoenum

Eupatorium purpureum var. amoenum, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, spire-like flower heads that range in color from white to deep purple, attracting various pollinators. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a composite flower structure composed of both ray and disk flowers.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
