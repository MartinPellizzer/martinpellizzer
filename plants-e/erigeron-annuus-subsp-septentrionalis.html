Erigeron annuus subsp. septentrionalis

Erigeron annuus subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern common fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a herbaceous annual with finely divided, silver-gray leaves and fluffy white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its widespread distribution.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
