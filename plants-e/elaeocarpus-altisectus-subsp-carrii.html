Elaeocarpus altisectus subsp. carrii

Elaeocarpus altisectus subsp.

carrii, commonly known as the Carrii Elaeocarpus, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Australia. It is a medium to large evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 30 meters.

The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a hard, woody drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays an important role in the ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
