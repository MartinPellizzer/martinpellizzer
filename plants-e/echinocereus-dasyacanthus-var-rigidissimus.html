Echinocereus dasyacanthus var. rigidissimus

Echinocereus dasyacanthus var. rigidissimus, commonly known as the rigid prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its structure and adaptations reflect its resilience to drought and high temperatures.
