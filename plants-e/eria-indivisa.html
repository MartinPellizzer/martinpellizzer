Eria indivisa

Eria indivisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, upright stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
