Euphorbia thymifolia

Euphorbia thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care to avoid skin irritation.
