Eupatorium schomburgkii

Eupatorium schomburgkii, commonly known as Schomburgk's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features tall, slender stems with serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing small, clustered white to pale purple flowers.

Schomburgk's boneset is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
