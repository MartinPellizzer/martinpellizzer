Eriogonum brevicaule var. nanum

Eriogonum brevicaule var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf bush poppy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming structure and produces showy, papery flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are typically small, hairy, and grayish-green, adapting to arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
