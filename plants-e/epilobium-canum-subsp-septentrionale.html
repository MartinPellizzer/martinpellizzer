Epilobium canum subsp. septentrionale

Epilobium canum subsp.

septentrionale, commonly known as northern willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas.

The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
