Ebenus longipes subsp. argentea

Ebenus longipes subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silverstem sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a resilient and hardy species.
