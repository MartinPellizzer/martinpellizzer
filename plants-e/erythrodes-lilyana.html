Erythrodes lilyana

Erythrodes lilyana, commonly known as the red-flowered lily, is a striking plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant belongs to the Liliaceae family and is known for its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Erythrodes lilyana thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject for both horticultural study and ornamental gardening.
