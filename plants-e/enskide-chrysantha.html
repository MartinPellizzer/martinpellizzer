Enskide chrysantha

Enskide chrysantha, commonly known as the golden sunblossom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, aiding in water retention. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
