Exocarpos strictus var. syrticola

Exocarpos strictus var. syrticola, commonly known as the African star fruit or wild starfruit, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its small, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, white flowers that are often clustered. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The fruit is edible and has a tangy flavor, making it a valuable resource in local diets and traditional medicine.
