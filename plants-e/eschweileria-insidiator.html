Eschweileria insidiator

Eschweileria insidiator, commonly known as the ambush plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It possesses specialized leaves that curl around prey, trapping insects with a sticky substance and digestive enzymes. The plant's structure includes a central stem with modified leaves that serve both as traps and for photosynthesis. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments, relying on captured prey for essential nutrients.

Its unique trapping mechanism makes it an intriguing subject for botanical study.
