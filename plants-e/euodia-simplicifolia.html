Euodia simplicifolia

Euodia simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leafed euodia, is a member of the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fruit that is typically green and turns red when mature. Euodia simplicifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
