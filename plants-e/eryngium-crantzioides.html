Eryngium crantzioides

Eryngium crantzioides, commonly known as the coastal eryngium, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has a compact, herbaceous growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Eryngium crantzioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
