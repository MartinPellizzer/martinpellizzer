Euphorbia pekinensis subsp. asoensis

Euphorbia pekinensis subsp.

asoensis, commonly known as the Aso euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Japan. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and in ecological restoration projects.
