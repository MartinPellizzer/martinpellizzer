Edraianthus

Edraianthus, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plants typically grow from bulbs and have narrow, grass-like leaves. Edraianthus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
