Eschscholzia urceolata

Eschscholzia urceolata, commonly known as the cupflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a basal rosette of bluish-gray, hairy leaves and produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a cup-shaped structure. The plant thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
