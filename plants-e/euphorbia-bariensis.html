Euphorbia bariensis

Euphorbia bariensis, commonly known as the Bari euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive in dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
