Eragrostis cuspidata

Eragrostis cuspidata, commonly known as speargrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
