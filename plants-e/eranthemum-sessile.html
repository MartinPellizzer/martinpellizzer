Eranthemum sessile

Eranthemum sessile, commonly known as the narrowleaf eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and stamens that are prominently displayed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
