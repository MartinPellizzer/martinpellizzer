Elytranthe carinatula

Elytranthe carinatula, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Elytranthe carinatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
