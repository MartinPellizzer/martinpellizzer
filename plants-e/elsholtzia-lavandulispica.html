Elsholtzia lavandulispica

Elsholtzia lavandulispica, commonly known as the lavender sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and emit a strong, minty fragrance.

Elsholtzia lavandulispica is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
