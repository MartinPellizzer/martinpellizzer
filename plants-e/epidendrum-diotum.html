Epidendrum diotum

Epidendrum diotum, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environment.

Epidendrum diotum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
