Erythroxylum novogranatense var. truxillense

Erythroxylum novogranatense var. truxillense, commonly known as the Truxillense coca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. It is closely related to the coca plant and shares similar botanical features.

This variety is notable for its potential use in traditional medicine and its ecological significance in its native habitat.
