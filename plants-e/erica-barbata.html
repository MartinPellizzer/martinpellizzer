Erica barbata

Erica barbata, commonly known as the barbed heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica barbata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
