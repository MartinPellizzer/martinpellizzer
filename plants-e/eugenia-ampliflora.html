Eugenia ampliflora

Eugenia ampliflora, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

It produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C. Eugenia ampliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
