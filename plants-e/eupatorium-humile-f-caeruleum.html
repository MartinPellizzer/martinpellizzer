Eupatorium humile f. caeruleum

Eupatorium humile f. caeruleum, commonly known as blue leaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late summer to early autumn. The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for various pollinators.
