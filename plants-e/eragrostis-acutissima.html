Eragrostis acutissima

Eragrostis acutissima, commonly known as sharpleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that have a distinctive sharp point. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for dryland environments.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
