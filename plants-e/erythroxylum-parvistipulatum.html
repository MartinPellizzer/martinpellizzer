Erythroxylum parvistipulatum

Erythroxylum parvistipulatum, commonly known as the small-bract erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its small, simple leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces elliptical, reddish-brown capsules that contain the seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
