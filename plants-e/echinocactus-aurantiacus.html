Echinocactus aurantiacus

Echinocactus aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and vibrant yellow to orange spines. The plant features a dense covering of cylindrical, bright orange spines that provide protection from herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the spring.

This cactus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
