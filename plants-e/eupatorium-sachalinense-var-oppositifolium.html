Eupatorium sachalinense var. oppositifolium

Eupatorium sachalinense var. oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
