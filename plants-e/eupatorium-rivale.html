Eupatorium rivale

Eupatorium rivale, commonly known as water boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Eupatorium rivale is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
