Eupatorium rotundifolium var. ovatum

Eupatorium rotundifolium var. ovatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
