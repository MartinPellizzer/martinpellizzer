Encephalartos brachyphyllus

Encephalartos brachyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cycad, is a species of cycad native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its distinctive, narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically appearing on separate plants. It is a slow-growing, long-lived species that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable plant for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
