Euonymus bicolor

Euonymus bicolor, commonly known as the two-color elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red and green foliage, which changes with the seasons, making it a visually appealing plant. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and can reach up to 5 inches in length. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
