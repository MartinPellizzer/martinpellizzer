Erigeron acris subsp. manshuricus

Erigeron acris subsp.

manshuricus, commonly known as the Siberian blueweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia. It typically grows in dry meadows and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
