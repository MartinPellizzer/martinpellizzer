Epipactis sancti-bruni

Epipactis sancti-bruni, commonly known as the Saint Bruno's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip. This orchid is native to Europe and prefers moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. It is a perennial plant that produces erect, unbranched stems.

The species is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific flies for reproduction.
