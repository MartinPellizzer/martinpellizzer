Elephantopus micropappus

Elephantopus micropappus, commonly known as the small pappus elephantopus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
