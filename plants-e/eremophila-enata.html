Eremophila enata

Eremophila enata, commonly known as the slender eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for low-water gardens.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a popular choice for native landscaping.
