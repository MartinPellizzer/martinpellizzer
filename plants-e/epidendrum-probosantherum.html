Epidendrum probosantherum

Epidendrum probosantherum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
