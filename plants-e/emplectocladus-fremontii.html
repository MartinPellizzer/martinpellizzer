Emplectocladus fremontii

Emplectocladus fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
