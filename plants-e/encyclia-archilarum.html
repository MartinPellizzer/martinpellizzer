Encyclia archilarum

Encyclia archilarum, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Encyclia archilarum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
