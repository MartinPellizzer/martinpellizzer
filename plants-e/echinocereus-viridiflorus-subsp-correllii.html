Echinocereus viridiflorus subsp. correllii

Echinocereus viridiflorus subsp.

correllii, commonly known as the green-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and bear clusters of bright green flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in desert gardens.
