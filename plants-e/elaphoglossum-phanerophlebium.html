Elaphoglossum phanerophlebium

Elaphoglossum phanerophlebium, commonly known as the bright vein holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a contrasting lighter midrib. The plant is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum phanerophlebium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
