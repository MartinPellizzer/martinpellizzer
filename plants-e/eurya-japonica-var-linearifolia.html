Eurya japonica var. linearifolia

Eurya japonica var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Japanese holly, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a distinctive linear shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

This variety is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
