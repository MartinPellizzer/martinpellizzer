Elymus marginatus subsp. kabylicus

Elymus marginatus subsp.

kabylicus, commonly known as the Kabylicus grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often hairy along the edges.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and is used in ecological restoration and livestock grazing.
