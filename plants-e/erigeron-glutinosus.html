Erigeron glutinosus

Erigeron glutinosus, commonly known as sticky fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, prairies, and disturbed soils.

Erigeron glutinosus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
