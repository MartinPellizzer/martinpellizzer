Elymus longearistatus subsp. badachschanicus

Elymus longearistatus subsp.

badachschanicus, commonly known as the Badakhshan needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces panicles of spikelets, which are the reproductive structures of the grass. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
