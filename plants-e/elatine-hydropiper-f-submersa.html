Elatine hydropiper f. submersa

Elatine hydropiper f. submersa, commonly known as water pepper, is a submerged aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are often difficult to see due to their minute size. It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize aquatic habitats make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
