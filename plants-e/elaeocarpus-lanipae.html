Elaeocarpus lanipae

Elaeocarpus lanipae, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarp, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is sometimes used in local crafts and construction.
