Eryngium × olivierianum

Eryngium × olivierianum, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spiky flower heads, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Eryngium × olivierianum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
