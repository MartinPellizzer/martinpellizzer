Epilobium × uechtritzianum

Epilobium × uechtritzianum, commonly known as the hybrid fireweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Epilobium × uechtritzianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural use.
