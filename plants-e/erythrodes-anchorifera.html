Erythrodes anchorifera

Erythrodes anchorifera, commonly known as the red-veined anchorweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red veins on its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
