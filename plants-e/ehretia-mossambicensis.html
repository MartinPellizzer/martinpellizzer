Ehretia mossambicensis

Ehretia mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique ehretia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters and has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
