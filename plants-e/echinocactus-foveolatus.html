Echinocactus foveolatus

Echinocactus foveolatus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-gray in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, apple-like fruits. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Echinocactus foveolatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
