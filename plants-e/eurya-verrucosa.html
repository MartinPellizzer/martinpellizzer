Eurya verrucosa

Eurya verrucosa, commonly known as the roughleaf eurya, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes many shrubs and small trees. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. It produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall.

Eurya verrucosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
