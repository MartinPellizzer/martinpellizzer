Embelia tiwiensis

Embelia tiwiensis, commonly known as the Tiwi embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
