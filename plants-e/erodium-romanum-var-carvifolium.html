Erodium romanum var. carvifolium

Erodium romanum var. carvifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its spread.
