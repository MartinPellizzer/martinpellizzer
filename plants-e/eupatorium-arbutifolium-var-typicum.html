Eupatorium arbutifolium var. typicum

Eupatorium arbutifolium var. typicum, commonly known as the roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, rough-textured leaves with serrated edges, and its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn. It produces small, clustered white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
