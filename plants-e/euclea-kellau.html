Euclea kellau

Euclea kellau, commonly known as the Kellau tree, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing small, dark seeds.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
