Eriochloa japonica

Eriochloa japonica, commonly known as Japanese false brome, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and is often found in moist, open habitats. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Eriochloa japonica is also known for its ability to form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion.
