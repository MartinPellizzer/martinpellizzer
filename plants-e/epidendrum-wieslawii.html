Epidendrum wieslawii

Epidendrum wieslawii, commonly known as the Wieslaw's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often colorful inflorescences. The plant typically produces pendulous or erect spikes of flowers, with showy, long-lasting blooms that can range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a waxy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Epidendrum wieslawii is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
