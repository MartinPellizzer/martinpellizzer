Echinocephalum discoideum

Echinocephalum discoideum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false dandelion, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and has hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as cypsela.

The species is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its short life cycle and efficient water use.
