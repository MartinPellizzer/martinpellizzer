Exomicrum pellucidum

Exomicrum pellucidum, commonly known as the glassy moss, is a unique species of moss characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along rocky crevices. The plant's leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth, glass-like surface that reflects light. It reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporophytes.

This moss plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
