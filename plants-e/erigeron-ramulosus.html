Erigeron ramulosus

Erigeron ramulosus, commonly known as the slender fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Erigeron ramulosus is often found in arid regions and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
