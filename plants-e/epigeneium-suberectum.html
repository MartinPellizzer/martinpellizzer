Epigeneium suberectum

Epigeneium suberectum, commonly known as the upright epigee, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vining habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and aerial roots that aid in climbing.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
