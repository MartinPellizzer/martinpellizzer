Elaeocarpus mollis

Elaeocarpus mollis, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Elaeocarpus mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
