Eucalyptus eremophila subsp. pterocarpa

Eucalyptus eremophila subsp.

pterocarpa, commonly known as the red box, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a red or pinkish inner layer, and its lance-shaped, glossy green leaves.

The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are usually pale blue to white in color. The plant produces woody, cylindrical capsules as fruit, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and timber qualities.
