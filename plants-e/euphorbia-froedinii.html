Euphorbia froedinii

Euphorbia froedinii, commonly known as the froedinii spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and can survive in poor soil conditions.

Euphorbia froedinii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
