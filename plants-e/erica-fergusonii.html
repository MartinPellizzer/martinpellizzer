Erica fergusonii

Erica fergusonii, commonly known as the Cape fynbos heath, is a flowering plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica fergusonii is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, with a dormant period during the summer months.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
