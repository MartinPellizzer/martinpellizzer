Eucalyptus obtusiflora subsp. obtusiflora

Eucalyptus obtusiflora subsp.

obtusiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved mallees, is a species native to Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped, aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, globular flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It is a hardy shrub or small tree that thrives in dry, sandy soils.

This subspecies is often found in heathlands and woodland areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
