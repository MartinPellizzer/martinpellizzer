Epacris apiculata

Epacris apiculata, commonly known as the spike epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open woodland or heathland environments.

Epacris apiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal and sunny gardens.
