Eucalyptus pachycalyx

Eucalyptus pachycalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaved malleeflower, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as mallee woodlands and sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
