Eugenia sripadaense

Eugenia sripadaense, commonly known as the Sripada's Eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, containing a single seed. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent when crushed.

Eugenia sripadaense is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
