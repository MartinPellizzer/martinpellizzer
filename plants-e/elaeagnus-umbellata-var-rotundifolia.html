Elaeagnus umbellata var. rotundifolia

Elaeagnus umbellata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, scale-like hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
