Eryngium pandanifolium var. chamissonis

Eryngium pandanifolium var. chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the family Asteraceae and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately lobed, resembling the leaves of the pandanus tree, which gives the species its common name.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.
