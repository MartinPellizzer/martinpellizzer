Eucalyptus bleeseri

Eucalyptus bleeseri, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and distinctive, rounded flower buds that open to reveal white or pale pink flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can withstand prolonged periods of drought. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Eucalyptus bleeseri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
