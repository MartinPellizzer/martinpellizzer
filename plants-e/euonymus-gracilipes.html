Euonymus gracilipes

Euonymus gracilipes, commonly known as the slender euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Euonymus gracilipes is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
