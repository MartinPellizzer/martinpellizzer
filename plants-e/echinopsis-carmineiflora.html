Echinopsis carmineiflora

Echinopsis carmineiflora, commonly known as the Crimson Flower Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stems and bright, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall. Its flowers are typically nocturnal, opening at night to attract specific pollinators.

Echinopsis carmineiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
