Escallonia pulverulenta var. resiniflua

Escallonia pulverulenta var. resiniflua, commonly known as the resinous escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a sticky resin, which gives it its common name and contributes to its unique fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract birds and bees.
