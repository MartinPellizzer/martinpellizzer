Erodium absinthoides cinereum

Erodium absinthoides cinereum, commonly known as silverleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pale green to silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its seed pods are slender and contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.
