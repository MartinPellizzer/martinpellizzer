Epicranthes octorhopalon

Epicranthes octorhopalon, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a unique species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, almost velvety texture. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in compact groups along the underside of the leaves. Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without being parasitic.

The golden crown fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
