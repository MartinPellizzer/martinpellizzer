Erythrina humilis

Erythrina humilis, commonly known as the dwarf coral tree, is a small, ornamental legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features vibrant red, fluffy flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like birds and bees. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its woody stems are often covered in a distinctive red or orange exodermis.

Erythrina humilis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
