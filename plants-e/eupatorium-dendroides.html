Eupatorium dendroides

Eupatorium dendroides, commonly known as the tree-like boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems that resemble small trees, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium dendroides is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is part of the Asteraceae family.
