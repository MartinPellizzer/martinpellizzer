Euonymus atropurpureus var. grandifolius

Euonymus atropurpureus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the Red-veined Mapleleaf Euonymus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins that become more vivid in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. Its foliage provides seasonal color changes, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in partial to full shade and is relatively low maintenance once established.
