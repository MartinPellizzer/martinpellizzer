Eucomis pole-evansii

Eucomis pole-evansii, commonly known as the purple owl's eye, is a striking member of the Hemerocallidaceae family native to South Africa.

This plant is characterized by its unique, spiky, purple-tipped inflorescence that resembles an owl's eye, giving it its common name. It produces showy, short-lived flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, seed-producing bracts. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a favored plant among gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
