Euphorbia spinosa subsp. glabriflora

Euphorbia spinosa subsp.

glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered spiny euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny stems and compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its fleshy stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
