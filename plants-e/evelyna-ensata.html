Evelyna ensata

Evelyna ensata, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals that resemble bells, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Evelyna ensata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
