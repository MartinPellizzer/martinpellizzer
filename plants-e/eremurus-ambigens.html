Eremurus ambigens

Eremurus ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous eremurus, is a striking plant native to central Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Mongolia, and Russia.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the genus Eremurus, which is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae. The plant is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that bears numerous yellowish-green to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremurus ambigens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic floral display.
