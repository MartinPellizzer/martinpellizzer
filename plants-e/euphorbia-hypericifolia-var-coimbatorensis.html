Euphorbia hypericifolia var. coimbatorensis

Euphorbia hypericifolia var. coimbatorensis, commonly known as the Coimbatore euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern India.

It features a thick, branching stem covered in small, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
