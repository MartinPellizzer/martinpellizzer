Echites macrocalyx

Echites macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that resemble dandelion fluff. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Echites macrocalyx is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
