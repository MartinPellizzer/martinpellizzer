Elephantopus nudicaulis var. major

Elephantopus nudicaulis var. major, commonly known as the large-leaved elephantopus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
