Elymus ciliaris var. japonensis

Elymus ciliaris var. japonensis, commonly known as Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient species in temperate climates.
