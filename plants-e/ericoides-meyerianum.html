Ericoides meyerianum

Ericoides meyerianum, commonly known as the Meyer's heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes evergreen shrubs and trees commonly found in acidic soils. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Ericoides meyerianum is well adapted to cold, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests and alpine regions.
