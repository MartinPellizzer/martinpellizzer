Epicampes coerulea var. submutica

Epicampes coerulea var. submutica, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a sprawling, mat-forming growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
