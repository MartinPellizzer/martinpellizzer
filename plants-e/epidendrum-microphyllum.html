Epidendrum microphyllum

Epidendrum microphyllum, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, strap-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
