Eryngium monocephalum

Eryngium monocephalum, commonly known as the single-headed thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, spiny stem with narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, large, spherical flower head composed of blue to purple bracts, which resemble a crown. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its striking appearance, it is cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
