Eupatorium gibbosum

Eupatorium gibbosum, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Eupatorium gibbosum is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of these environments.
