Erepsia tuberculata

Erepsia tuberculata, commonly known as the roughleaf erica, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family (Ericaceae).

It is native to South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

Erepsia tuberculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
