Ericoides thunbergii

Ericoides thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often appearing in clusters.

Ericoides thunbergii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.
