Ericoides trichophyllum

Ericoides trichophyllum, commonly known as the hairy heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the cool, moist environments of the Pacific Northwest.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
