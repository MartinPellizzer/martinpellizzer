Echinocactus pycnacanthus

Echinocactus pycnacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, apple-like fruits. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Echinocactus pycnacanthus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
