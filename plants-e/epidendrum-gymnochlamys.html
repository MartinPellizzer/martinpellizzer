Epidendrum gymnochlamys

Epidendrum gymnochlamys, commonly known as the velvet leaf epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum gymnochlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
