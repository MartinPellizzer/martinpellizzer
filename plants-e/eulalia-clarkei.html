Eulalia clarkei

Eulalia clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's grass, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it blend into its natural habitat.

Eulalia clarkei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect and bird species.
