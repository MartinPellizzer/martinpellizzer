Echeveria × ovata

Echeveria × ovata, commonly known as the hybrid echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy rosettes of thick, waxy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces vibrant pink or purple flowers in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
