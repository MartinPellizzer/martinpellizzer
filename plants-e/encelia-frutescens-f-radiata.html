Encelia frutescens f. radiata

Encelia frutescens f. radiata, commonly known as the radiate brittlebush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular, silver-green foliage and yellow to golden yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and is well adapted to drought conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and desert scrub habitats.

The radiate brittlebush is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
