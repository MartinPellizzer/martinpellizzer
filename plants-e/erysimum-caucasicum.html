Erysimum caucasicum

Erysimum caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves and often grows in rocky or gravelly soils. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Erysimum caucasicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
