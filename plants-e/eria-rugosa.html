Eria rugosa

Eria rugosa, commonly known as the rough eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant orchid-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Eria rugosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
