Erigeron echioides

Erigeron echioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring a sturdy, branching stem. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from late summer into early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

Erigeron echioides is often found in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.
