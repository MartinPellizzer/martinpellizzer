Eryngium chamissonis

Eryngium chamissonis, commonly known as the coastal thistle, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it thrive in salty, windy environments. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family, and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in a composite head. Eryngium chamissonis typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and is often found in maritime habitats.

Its hardy nature and unique appearance make it a notable plant in coastal botany.
