Euphorbia mendezii

Euphorbia mendezii, commonly known as the Mendez's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
