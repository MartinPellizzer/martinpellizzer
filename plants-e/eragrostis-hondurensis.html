Eragrostis hondurensis

Eragrostis hondurensis, commonly known as Honduras grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central America.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to warm climates and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in ecological studies and agricultural settings.
