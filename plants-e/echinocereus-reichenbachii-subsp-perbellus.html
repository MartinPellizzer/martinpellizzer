Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp. perbellus

Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp.

perbellus, commonly known as the Perbellus Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, cylindrical stem covered in spines and areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
