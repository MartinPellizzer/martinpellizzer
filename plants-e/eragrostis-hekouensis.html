Eragrostis hekouensis

Eragrostis hekouensis, commonly known as the Hekou grass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Eragrostis hekouensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
