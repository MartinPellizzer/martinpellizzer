Euclea keniensis

Euclea keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan euclea, is a shrub native to East Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species. Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
