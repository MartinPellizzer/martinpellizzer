Elatine paludosa subsp. macropoda

Elatine paludosa subsp.

macropoda, commonly known as the large-leaved waterwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as wetlands, marshes, and the edges of ponds.

The plant has slender, branched stems with small, opposite leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, with four petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
