Epidendrum deorsus

Epidendrum deorsus, commonly known as the Upside-down Orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique growth habit, with roots growing downward and aerial roots facing upward. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or green with red or purple markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum deorsus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
