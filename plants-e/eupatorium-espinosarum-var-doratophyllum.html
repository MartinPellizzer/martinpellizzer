Eupatorium espinosarum var. doratophyllum

Eupatorium espinosarum var. doratophyllum, commonly known as the golden eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as swampy areas and along stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
