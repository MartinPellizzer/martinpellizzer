Eria wariana

Eria wariana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive orange throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Eria wariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
