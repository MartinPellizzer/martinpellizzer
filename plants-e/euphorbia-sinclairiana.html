Euphorbia sinclairiana

Euphorbia sinclairiana, commonly known as the Sinclair's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
