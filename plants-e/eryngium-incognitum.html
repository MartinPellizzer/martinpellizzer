Eryngium incognitum

Eryngium incognitum, commonly known as the unknown thistle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces a tall, branching stem with numerous flower heads.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
