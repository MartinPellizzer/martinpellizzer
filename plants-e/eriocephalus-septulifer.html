Eriocephalus septulifer

Eriocephalus septulifer, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a hairy stem and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments. It is often found in dunes and along the edges of the ocean, where it tolerates salt spray and strong winds.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
