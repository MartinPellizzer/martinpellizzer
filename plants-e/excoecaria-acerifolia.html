Excoecaria acerifolia

Excoecaria acerifolia, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red bracts that surround its small yellow flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has palmate leaves that are often lobed and can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that contains toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care.
