Erythrina reniformis

Erythrina reniformis, commonly known as the Cow's Horn Acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped leaves and bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Erythrina reniformis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.

Its unique appearance and ecological benefits make it a popular choice in both ornamental and ecological gardening.
