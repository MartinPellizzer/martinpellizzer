Erica chamissonis var. hirtifolia

Erica chamissonis var. hirtifolia, commonly known as the hairy heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Erica chamissonis var. hirtifolia thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
