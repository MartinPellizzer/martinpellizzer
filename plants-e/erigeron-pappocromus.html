Erigeron pappocromus

Erigeron pappocromus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features a bushy growth habit with silver-gray foliage that contrasts beautifully with its blue to purple flower heads. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron pappocromus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
