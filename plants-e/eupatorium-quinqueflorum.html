Eupatorium quinqueflorum

Eupatorium quinqueflorum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal uses by indigenous peoples.
