Erica salicina

Erica salicina, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a hardy evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica salicina is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
