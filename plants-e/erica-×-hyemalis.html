Erica × hyemalis

Erica × hyemalis, commonly known as the winter heath, is a hybrid shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact form and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erica × hyemalis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
