Erythroxylum spectabile

Erythroxylum spectabile, commonly known as the showy coca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red bracts that surround small white flowers, making it a visually distinctive species. The plant belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is known for its medicinal and traditional uses by indigenous communities. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in shrubland or forested areas.

Its leaves contain alkaloids, which have been historically used for various purposes, including as a stimulant.
