Eugenia mexiae

Eugenia mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. Eugenia mexiae is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
