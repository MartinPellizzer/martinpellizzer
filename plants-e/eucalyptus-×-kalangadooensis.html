Eucalyptus × kalangadooensis

Eucalyptus × kalangadooensis, commonly known as the Kalangadoo gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and typically have a silver-blue hue on the upper surface. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring.

This species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrub habitats.
