Elaeagia barbata

Elaeagia barbata, commonly known as the barbed elaeagnus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Elaeagia barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
