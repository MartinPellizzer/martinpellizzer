Epilobium junceum f. cinereum

Epilobium junceum f. cinereum, commonly known as the gray willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with four petals, typically in shades of pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
