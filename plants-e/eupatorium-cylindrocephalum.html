Eupatorium cylindrocephalum

Eupatorium cylindrocephalum, commonly known as the narrow-headed thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and floodplains. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive, rough texture.

Eupatorium cylindrocephalum is often found in association with other wetland plants and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
