Elaeagnus bockii

Elaeagnus bockii, commonly known as the silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically reddish-brown in color. Elaeagnus bockii is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

It has historical uses in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
