Eremophila viridissima

Eremophila viridissima, commonly known as the green eremophila, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in sandy soils.

Eremophila viridissima is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value in dry climates.
