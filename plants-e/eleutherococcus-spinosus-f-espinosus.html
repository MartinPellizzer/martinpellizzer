Eleutherococcus spinosus f. espinosus

Eleutherococcus spinosus f. espinosus, commonly known as the spiny eleutherococcus, is a variant of the eleutherococcus plant native to the Far East.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces yellowish flowers in late spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
