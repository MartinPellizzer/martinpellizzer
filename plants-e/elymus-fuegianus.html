Elymus fuegianus

Elymus fuegianus, commonly known as the Falkland grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly the Falkland Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in coastal and grassland habitats, often growing in sandy or loamy soils.

Elymus fuegianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
