Erica savilea var. grandiflora

Erica savilea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Greatflower Heath, is a species of flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces white or pale pink blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
