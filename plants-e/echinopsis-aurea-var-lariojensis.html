Echinopsis aurea var. lariojensis

Echinopsis aurea var. lariojensis, commonly known as the Lariojense Candelabra Cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of northern Mexico, specifically the state of Chihuahua. This plant is characterized by its tall, branching structure and its ability to produce vibrant yellow flowers during the spring. The stems are ribbed and covered in white spines, which help to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water, making it a popular choice for experienced cactus enthusiasts.
