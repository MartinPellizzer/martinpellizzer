Eriogonum vimineum subsp. polygonoides

Eriogonum vimineum subsp.

polygonoides, commonly known as the Desert Buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
