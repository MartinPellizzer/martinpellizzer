Echeveria scopulorum

Echeveria scopulorum, commonly known as the Desert Crassula, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
