Euphorbia moenchiana

Euphorbia moenchiana, commonly known as the Cape Harebell, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often spiny members. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
