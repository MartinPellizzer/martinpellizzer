Elatine inaperta

Elatine inaperta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterwort, is a small aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and stream margins. The plant has slender, branched stems that are often covered in small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in whorls, producing small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers.

Elatine inaperta is often found in still or slow-moving water, where it plays a minor role in aquatic ecosystems.
