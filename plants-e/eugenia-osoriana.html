Eugenia osoriana

Eugenia osoriana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or osorina, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Eugenia osoriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.

Its growth habit includes a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
