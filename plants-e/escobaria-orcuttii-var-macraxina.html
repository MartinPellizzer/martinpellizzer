Escobaria orcuttii var. macraxina

Escobaria orcuttii var. macraxina, commonly known as the Macraux's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive drought conditions.

Escobaria orcuttii var. macraxina is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
