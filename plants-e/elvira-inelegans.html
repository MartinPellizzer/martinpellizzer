Elvira inelegans

Elvira inelegans, commonly known as the purple false heather, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, adapting to the dry, rocky environments it inhabits.

Elvira inelegans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
