Elatostema sesquifolium var. integrifolium

Elatostema sesquifolium var. integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaf false shamrock, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with smooth margins, distinguishing it from other varieties in the genus. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ornamental species in horticultural collections.
