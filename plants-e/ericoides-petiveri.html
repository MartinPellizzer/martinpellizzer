Ericoides petiveri

Ericoides petiveri, commonly known as the Petiver's ericoid, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Ericoides petiveri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
