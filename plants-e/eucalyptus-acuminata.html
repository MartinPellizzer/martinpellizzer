Eucalyptus acuminata

Eucalyptus acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pink to pale yellow in hue. Its bark is usually smooth and peels in thin, fibrous strips, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Eucalyptus acuminata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and nectar for various wildlife species.
