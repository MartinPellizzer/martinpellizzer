Eryngium pristis var. eupristis

Eryngium pristis var. eupristis, commonly known as the coastal thistle, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flowers and grey-green, lobed leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dunes or along shores. It has a compact growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

Its structure and appearance make it a distinctive feature of coastal flora.
