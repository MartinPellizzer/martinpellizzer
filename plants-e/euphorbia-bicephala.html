Euphorbia bicephala

Euphorbia bicephala, commonly known as the two-headed spurge, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with two prominent, spiky heads that resemble a split plant, giving it its common name. The plant has fleshy, ribbed stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling to avoid skin irritation.
