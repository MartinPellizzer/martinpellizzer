Eupatorium purpureum subsp. maculatum

Eupatorium purpureum subsp.

maculatum, commonly known as the spotted Joe-Pye weed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. This plant thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
