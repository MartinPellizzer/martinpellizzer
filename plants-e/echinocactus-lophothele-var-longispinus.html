Echinocactus lophothele var. longispinus

Echinocactus lophothele var. longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense clusters of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It requires well-draining soil and minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
