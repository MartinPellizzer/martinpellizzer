Echium pycnanthum subsp. trygorrhizum

Echium pycnanthum subsp.

trygorrhizum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific leaf morphology.
