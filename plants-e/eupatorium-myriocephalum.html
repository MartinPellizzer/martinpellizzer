Eupatorium myriocephalum

Eupatorium myriocephalum, commonly known as bone set or blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
