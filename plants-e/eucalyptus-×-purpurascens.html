Eucalyptus × purpurascens

Eucalyptus × purpurascens, commonly known as the purple mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that often displays a purple or reddish hue. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color. It produces small, globular flower spikes that range from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.

This species is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
