Erythrina suberosa var. glabrescens

Erythrina suberosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth erythrina, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
