Elaeagnus taliensis

Elaeagnus taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis silverleaf, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Elaeagnus taliensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
