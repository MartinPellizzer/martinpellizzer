Eulophia epiphanoides

Eulophia epiphanoides, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers that are often white or pale pink, with a distinctive, elongated shape. It typically blooms during the winter months, hence its common name.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
