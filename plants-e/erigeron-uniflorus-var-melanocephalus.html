Erigeron uniflorus var. melanocephalus

Erigeron uniflorus var. melanocephalus, commonly known as the black-headed fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a dark purple to black central disk. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has fine, hairy leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
