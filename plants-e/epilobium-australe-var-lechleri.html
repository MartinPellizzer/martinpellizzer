Epilobium australe var. lechleri

Epilobium australe var. lechleri, commonly known as the Chilean willow or *Lapageria rosea*, is a flowering plant native to central Chile.

It is characterized by its striking red and white flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in moist, shaded areas along riverbanks. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have a distinctive spiral arrangement.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
