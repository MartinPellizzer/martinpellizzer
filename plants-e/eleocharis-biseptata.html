Eleocharis biseptata

Eleocharis biseptata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly folded. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically borne on tall, upright inflorescences. Eleocharis biseptata thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its structure and growth habits make it well adapted to fluctuating water levels and nutrient-poor conditions.
