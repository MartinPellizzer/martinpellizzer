Eugenia livida

Eugenia livida, commonly known as the purple-leafed eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green to purple foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Eugenia livida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
