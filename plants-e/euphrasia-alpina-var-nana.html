Euphrasia alpina var. nana

Euphrasia alpina var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf eyebright, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, delicate yellow flowers with distinctive dark markings, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
