Eriocaulon transvaalicum subsp. hanningtonii

Eriocaulon transvaalicum subsp.

hanningtonii, commonly known as the Hannington's flat sedge, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to dark brown flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic vegetation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and localized species.
