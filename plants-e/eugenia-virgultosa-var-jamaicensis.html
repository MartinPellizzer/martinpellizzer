Eugenia virgultosa var. jamaicensis

Eugenia virgultosa var. jamaicensis, commonly known as Jamaican guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, yellow or orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in local cuisines.
