Eucalyptus × macmahonii

Eucalyptus × macmahonii, commonly known as the MacMahon's gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from pale yellow to red, depending on the variety. It is commonly found in coastal and dry sclerophyllous forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic oils, it is also cultivated for horticultural purposes.
