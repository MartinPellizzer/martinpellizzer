Eugenia pseudopsidium var. genuina

Eugenia pseudopsidium var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia* var. *genuina*, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, bright red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
