Eriocaulon perplexum

Eriocaulon perplexum, commonly known as the perplexing spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which is characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized adaptations for water environments. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, hairy layer, which helps reduce water loss and provides structural support.

Eriocaulon perplexum is also notable for its small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
