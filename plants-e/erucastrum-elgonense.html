Erucastrum elgonense

Erucastrum elgonense, commonly known as Elgon mustard, is a flowering plant native to East Africa, particularly found in the Mount Elgon region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important vegetables and oilseed crops. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and pinnately divided leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom during the dry season.

The seeds are encased in a hard pod and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
