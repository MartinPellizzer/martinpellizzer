Eugenia abbreviata

Eugenia abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia abbreviata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
