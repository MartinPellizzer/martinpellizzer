Erinus patens

Erinus patens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Erinus patens is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
