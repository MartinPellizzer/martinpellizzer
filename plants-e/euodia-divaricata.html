Euodia divaricata

Euodia divaricata, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
