Echinocereus waldeisii

Echinocereus waldeisii, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and are capable of storing water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent gardens.
