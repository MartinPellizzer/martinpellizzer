Elaeocarpus clethroides

Elaeocarpus clethroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
