Eumecanthus boerhaviifolius

Eumecanthus boerhaviifolius, commonly known as the Boerhavia-like euphorbia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to conserve water.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
