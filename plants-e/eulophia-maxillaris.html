Eulophia maxillaris

Eulophia maxillaris, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Eulophia maxillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
