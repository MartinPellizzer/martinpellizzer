Epidendrum echinatum

Epidendrum echinatum, commonly known as the spiny epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence bears numerous small, showy blooms that are often fragrant.

Epidendrum echinatum thrives in warm, moist conditions with good air circulation.
