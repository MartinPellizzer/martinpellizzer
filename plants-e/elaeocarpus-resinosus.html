Elaeocarpus resinosus

Elaeocarpus resinosus, commonly known as the resinous elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant's fruit is a woody drupe, often containing a single seed, and is known for its aromatic, resinous scent. Elaeocarpus resinosus thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

It has limited commercial use but is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
