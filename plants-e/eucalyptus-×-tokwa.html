Eucalyptus × tokwa

Eucalyptus × tokwa, commonly known as the Sydney peppermint, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and smooth, often peeling bark. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong mint-like scent when crushed.

Eucalyptus × tokwa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in essential oil production.
