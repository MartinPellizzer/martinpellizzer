Eupatorium trifoliatum var. maculatum

Eupatorium trifoliatum var. maculatum, commonly known as spotted Joe-pye weed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees. The plant has opposite, trifoliate leaves with white spots, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
