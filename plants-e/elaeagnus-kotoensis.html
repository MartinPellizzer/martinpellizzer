Elaeagnus kotoensis

Elaeagnus kotoensis, commonly known as the Koto buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Elaeagnaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

Elaeagnus kotoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
