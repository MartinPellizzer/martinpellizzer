Eucalyptus naudiniana

Eucalyptus naudiniana, commonly known as the Naudin's eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Queensland, Australia.

It typically grows in rainforest or coastal areas, characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its timber is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Eucalyptus naudiniana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
