Eremogone dahurica

Eremogone dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It features a robust root system and upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

Eremogone dahurica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to arid or semi-arid regions.
