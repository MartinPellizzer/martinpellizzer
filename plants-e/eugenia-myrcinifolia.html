Eugenia myrcinifolia

Eugenia myrcinifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features dense, glossy dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions and lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain areas.
