Eugenia verticilligera

Eugenia verticilligera, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or the red eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and showy red flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, attracting various wildlife species.
