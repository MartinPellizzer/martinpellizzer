Eria tridens

Eria tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are linear and arranged in two rows along the stem.

The species is noted for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
