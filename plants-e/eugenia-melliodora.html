Eugenia melliodora

Eugenia melliodora, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red or purple as they mature.

Eugenia melliodora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for various purposes.
