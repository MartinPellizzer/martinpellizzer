Eupatorium volckmannii

Eupatorium volckmannii, commonly known as the greenleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium volckmannii is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
