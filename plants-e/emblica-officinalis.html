Emblica officinalis

Emblica officinalis, commonly known as Indian gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that turn red when ripe. The plant is valued for its high vitamin C content and is widely used in traditional medicine systems like Ayurveda. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while its flowers are unisexual and greenish-yellow.

The fruit is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its nutritional and therapeutic properties.
