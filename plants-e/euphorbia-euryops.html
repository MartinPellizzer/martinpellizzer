Euphorbia euryops

Euphorbia euryops, commonly known as the yellow fever tree, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive, palmate leaves that are typically yellow or green, depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique flower-like structures. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia euryops is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
