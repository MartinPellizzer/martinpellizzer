Eriogonum giganteum var. giganteum

Eriogonum giganteum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant buckwheat, is a striking flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
