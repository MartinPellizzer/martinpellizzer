Eleutherococcus henryi var. henryi

Eleutherococcus henryi var. henryi, commonly known as Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
