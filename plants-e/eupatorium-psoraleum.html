Eupatorium psoraleum

Eupatorium psoraleum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
