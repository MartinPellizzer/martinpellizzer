Epacris crassifolia subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open forests or heathlands. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are pollinated by birds and insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds in capsules.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
