Eria quadricolor

Eria quadricolor, commonly known as the four-colored orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant has strikingly colorful flowers with four distinct color zones, typically in shades of purple, yellow, white, and green. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eria quadricolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
