Eugenia bankensis

Eugenia bankensis, commonly known as the Bank's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia bankensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
