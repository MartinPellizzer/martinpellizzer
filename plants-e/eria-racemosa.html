Eria racemosa

Eria racemosa, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Thailand, and the Philippines. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months.

Eria racemosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or near water sources.
