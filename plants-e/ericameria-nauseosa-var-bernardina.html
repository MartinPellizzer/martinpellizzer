Ericameria nauseosa var. bernardina

Ericameria nauseosa var. bernardina, commonly known as Bernard's ericameria, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is closely related to other members of the Ericameria genus.

This variety is notable for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
