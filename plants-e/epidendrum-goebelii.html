Epidendrum goebelii

Epidendrum goebelii, commonly known as the Goebel's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically features strap-like leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum goebelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
