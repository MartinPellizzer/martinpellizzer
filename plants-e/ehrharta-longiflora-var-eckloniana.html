Ehrharta longiflora var. eckloniana

Ehrharta longiflora var. eckloniana, commonly known as the Ecklon's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate poor soil, making it a resilient species.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife are notable features.
