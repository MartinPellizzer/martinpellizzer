Escallonia glandulosa

Escallonia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically red when mature. Escallonia glandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for gardens.
