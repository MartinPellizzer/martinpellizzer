Emilia kasaiensis

Emilia kasaiensis, commonly known as the Kasai emilia, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow with a central disc, and it produces small, oblong fruits that contain several seeds.

Emilia kasaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
