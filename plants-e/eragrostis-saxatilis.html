Eragrostis saxatilis

Eragrostis saxatilis, commonly known as the rockgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and rocky environments in Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

Eragrostis saxatilis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to stabilize soils in erosion-prone areas.
