Excoecaria parvifolia

Excoecaria parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with prominent veins, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Excoecaria parvifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
