Eupatorium haageanum

Eupatorium haageanum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties historically used by Native American tribes.
