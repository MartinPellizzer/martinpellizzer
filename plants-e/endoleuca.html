Endoleuca

Endoleuca, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to various parts of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. Endoleuca species are often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

They are valued for their hard, durable wood, which has been used historically in craftsmanship and traditional medicine.
