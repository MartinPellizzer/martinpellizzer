Equisetum arvense subsp. diffusum

Equisetum arvense subsp.

diffusum, commonly known as field horsetail, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family. It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a hollow, jointed stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It reproduces via spores and is known for its ability to thrive in environments with high humidity.

The species is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of silica.
