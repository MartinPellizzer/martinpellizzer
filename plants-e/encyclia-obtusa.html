Encyclia obtusa

Encyclia obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals and sepals.

Encyclia obtusa is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
