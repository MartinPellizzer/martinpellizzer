Echinocactus corynodes var. erinaceus

Echinocactus corynodes var. erinaceus, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
