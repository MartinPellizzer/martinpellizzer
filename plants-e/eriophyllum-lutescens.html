Eriophyllum lutescens

Eriophyllum lutescens, commonly known as the golden poppy, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Oregon.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Eriophyllum lutescens is often found in coastal and inland habitats, including grasslands and open woodlands.
