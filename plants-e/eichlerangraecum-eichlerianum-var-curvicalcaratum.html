Eichlerangraecum eichlerianum var. curvicalcaratum

Eichlerangraecum eichlerianum var. curvicalcaratum, commonly known as the curly calyx euphorbia, is a unique succulent plant characterized by its striking, spirally twisted stems and vibrant, orb-shaped flowers.

Native to the arid regions of South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting a compact growth habit. The plant's foliage consists of thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are typically yellow or orange and are borne on short, branched inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in cultivation and is often sought after by succulent enthusiasts.
