Ericoides grandiflorum

Ericoides grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered heath, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ericoides grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
