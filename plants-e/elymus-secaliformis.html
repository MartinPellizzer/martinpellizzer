Elymus secaliformis

Elymus secaliformis, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Elymus secaliformis is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields, where it thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
