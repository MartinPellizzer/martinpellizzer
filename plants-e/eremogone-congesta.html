Eremogone congesta

Eremogone congesta, commonly known as the clustered eremogone, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense heads, often resembling a cluster of tiny sunflowers. Eremogone congesta is well adapted to dry conditions, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It is often found in rocky soils and is a common sight in the succulent karoo biome.
