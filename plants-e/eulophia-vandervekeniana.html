Eulophia vandervekeniana

Eulophia vandervekeniana, commonly known as the Van der Veken's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in forest understories where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

The orchid's botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
