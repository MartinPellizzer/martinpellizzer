Euchroma purpurea

Euchroma purpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet and purple blooms. The plant features long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and emit a subtle fragrance.

Euchroma purpurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
