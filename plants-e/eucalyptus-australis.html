Eucalyptus australis

Eucalyptus australis, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Manna Gum, is a tall, slender tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white and grey. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a grey-green upper surface and a pale underside. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The tree is known for its aromatic oils, which have various medicinal and industrial uses.
