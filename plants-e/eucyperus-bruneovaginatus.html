Eucyperus bruneovaginatus

Eucyperus bruneovaginatus, commonly known as the Burmese flat sedge, is a grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Eucyperus bruneovaginatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
