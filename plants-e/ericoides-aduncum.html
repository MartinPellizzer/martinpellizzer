Ericoides aduncum

Ericoides aduncum, commonly known as the slender ericoid, is a shrub native to the wetlands and bogs of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in swampy or marshy environments.

Ericoides aduncum plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various insect and bird species.
