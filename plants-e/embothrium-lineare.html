Embothrium lineare

Embothrium lineare, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a flowering plant native to Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Embothrium lineare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
