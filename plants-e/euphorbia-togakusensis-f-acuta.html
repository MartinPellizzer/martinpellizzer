Euphorbia togakusensis f. acuta

Euphorbia togakusensis f. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-tipped euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, which is typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
