Erythroxylum longisetulosum

Erythroxylum longisetulosum, commonly known as the long-hair erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
