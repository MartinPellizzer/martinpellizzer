Euphorbia namibensis

Euphorbia namibensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. Its leaves are typically reduced to small spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Euphorbia namibensis is well adapted to the extreme conditions of the Namib Desert, showcasing remarkable resilience and unique botanical features.
