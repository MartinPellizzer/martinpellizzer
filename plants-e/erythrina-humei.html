Erythrina humei

Erythrina humei, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in dense, raceme-like clusters. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five leaflets, which are often trifoliate in appearance. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces flat, woody pods that contain seeds.

Erythrina humei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
