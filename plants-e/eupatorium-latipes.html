Eupatorium latipes

Eupatorium latipes, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, slightly fuzzy foliage.
