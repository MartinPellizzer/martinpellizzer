Espeletia nivea

Espeletia nivea, commonly known as the snow plant, is a striking alpine species native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that resembles a white cloud, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often considered a flagship species for alpine biodiversity.
