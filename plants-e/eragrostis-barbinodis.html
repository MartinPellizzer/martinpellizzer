Eragrostis barbinodis

Eragrostis barbinodis, commonly known as the hairy beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture along the edges. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or pale brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and disturbed sites.

Eragrostis barbinodis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
