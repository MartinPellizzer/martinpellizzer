Eugenia amatenangensis

Eugenia amatenangensis, commonly known as the Amatenango guava, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry with a sweet and acidic flavor.

It is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in humid, well-drained soils.
