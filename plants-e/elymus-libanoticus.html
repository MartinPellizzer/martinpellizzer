Elymus libanoticus

Elymus libanoticus, commonly known as the Levantine needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Elymus libanoticus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native habitat.
