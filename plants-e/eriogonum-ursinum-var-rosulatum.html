Eriogonum ursinum var. rosulatum

Eriogonum ursinum var. rosulatum, commonly known as the bear's paw buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive, bushy appearance.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
