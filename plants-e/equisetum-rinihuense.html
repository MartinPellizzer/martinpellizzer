Equisetum rinihuense

Equisetum rinihuense, commonly known as the Rinihuene horsetail, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, jointed stems and whorled arrangement of leaves, typical of horsetails. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stem, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. Equisetum rinihuense is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological role, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
