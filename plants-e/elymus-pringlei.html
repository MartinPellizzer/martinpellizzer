Elymus pringlei

Elymus pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Elymus pringlei is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with low precipitation.
