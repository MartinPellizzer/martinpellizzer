Erica triflora

Erica triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica triflora thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
