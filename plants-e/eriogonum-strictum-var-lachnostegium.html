Eriogonum strictum var. lachnostegium

Eriogonum strictum var. lachnostegium, commonly known as the stiffleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, stiff leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
