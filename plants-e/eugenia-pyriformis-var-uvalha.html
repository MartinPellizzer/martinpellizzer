Eugenia pyriformis var. uvalha

Eugenia pyriformis var. uvalha, commonly known as the Uvalha persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, orange-red berries in the fall. These berries are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought resistance make it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
