Eleocharis dregeana

Eleocharis dregeana, commonly known as the dwarf spikerush, is a species of sedge native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often covered in small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, dry fruits.

Eleocharis dregeana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
