Emilia limosa

Emilia limosa, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Emilia limosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
