Eucalyptus stuartiana var. longifolia

Eucalyptus stuartiana var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved stuart eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, long, and leathery leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are arranged in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It produces woody capsules as fruit, containing viable seeds.

This species is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.
