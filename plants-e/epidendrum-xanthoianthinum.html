Epidendrum xanthoianthinum

Epidendrum xanthoianthinum, commonly known as the golden-veined epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and a distinctive orange-red lip.

Epidendrum xanthoianthinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
