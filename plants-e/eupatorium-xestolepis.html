Eupatorium xestolepis

Eupatorium xestolepis, commonly known as the white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium xestolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
