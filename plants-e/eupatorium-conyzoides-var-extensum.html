Eupatorium conyzoides var. extensum

Eupatorium conyzoides var. extensum, commonly known as wild blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and roadside areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

Its ability to thrive in various soil types makes it a common sight in many regions of the southeastern United States.
