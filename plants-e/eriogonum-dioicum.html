Eriogonum dioicum

Eriogonum dioicum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and it has hairy, lobed leaves that help reduce water loss. Eriogonum dioicum is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
