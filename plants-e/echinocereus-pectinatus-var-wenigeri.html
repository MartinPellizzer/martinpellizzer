Echinocereus pectinatus var. wenigeri

Echinocereus pectinatus var. wenigeri, commonly known as the lesser pectinatus cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and typically features a pale green or bluish-gray stem. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements in desert gardens.
