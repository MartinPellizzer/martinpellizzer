Eugenia humblotii

Eugenia humblotii, commonly known as the Malayan cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

Eugenia humblotii is also appreciated for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile plant for horticultural use.
