Eucalyptus piperita

Eucalyptus piperita, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding insects and birds. Eucalyptus piperita is a hardy shrub or small tree, often found in dry, sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
