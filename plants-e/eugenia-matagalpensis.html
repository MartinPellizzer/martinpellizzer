Eugenia matagalpensis

Eugenia matagalpensis, commonly known as the Matagalpa myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm regions.
