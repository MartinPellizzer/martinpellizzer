Eriocaulon jaliscanum

Eriocaulon jaliscanum, commonly known as the Jalisco spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Eriocaulon jaliscanum is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
