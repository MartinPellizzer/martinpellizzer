Echinops lasiolepis

Echinops lasiolepis, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly bristles. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Echinops lasiolepis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
