Eucalyptus huberiana

Eucalyptus huberiana, commonly known as the mountain eucalyptus, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, felt-like hair. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or cream in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Eucalyptus huberiana is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
