Erythroxylum alaternifolium var. parvifolium

Erythroxylum alaternifolium var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved redweed, is a species of flowering plant in the Erythroxylaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.
