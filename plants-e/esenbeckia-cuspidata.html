Esenbeckia cuspidata

Esenbeckia cuspidata, commonly known as the Brazilian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded areas. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Esenbeckia cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
