Eupatorium chrysostyloides

Eupatorium chrysostyloides, commonly known as golden-stemmed boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium chrysostyloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
