Encyclia domingensis

Encyclia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive red lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Encyclia domingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
