Erysimum incanum

Erysimum incanum, commonly known as the hoary mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Erysimum incanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
