Eugenia limbata

Eugenia limbata, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and fragrant, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in perfumery due to their aromatic properties.

Eugenia limbata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
