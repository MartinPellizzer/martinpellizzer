Eritrichium elongatum

Eritrichium elongatum, commonly known as the longstalked pavement flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, elongated leaves and small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eritrichium elongatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native plant communities.
