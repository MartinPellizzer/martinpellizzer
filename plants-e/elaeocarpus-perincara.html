Elaeocarpus perincara

Elaeocarpus perincara, commonly known as the purple-berried elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that are typically purple or black when mature, containing one or two seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological importance.

The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is relatively uncommon in cultivation.
