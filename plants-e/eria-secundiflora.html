Eria secundiflora

Eria secundiflora, commonly known as the secondary-flowered eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, and it produces clusters of small, colorful blooms.

Eria secundiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
