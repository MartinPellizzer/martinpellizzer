Epidendrum trachysepalum

Epidendrum trachysepalum, commonly known as the rough-sepal epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are usually covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with dark markings, and they bloom during the summer months.

Epidendrum trachysepalum is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
