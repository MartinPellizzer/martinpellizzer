Euphorbia riebeckii

Euphorbia riebeckii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by a crown of bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Euphorbia riebeckii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
