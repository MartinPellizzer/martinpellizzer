Eleocharis yecorensis

Eleocharis yecorensis, commonly known as the Yecora sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Eleocharis yecorensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
