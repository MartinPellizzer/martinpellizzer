Ekebergia indeniensis

Ekebergia indeniensis, commonly known as the East African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for construction and fuel. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods. It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in savanna woodlands.

Due to its ecological importance and economic value, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
