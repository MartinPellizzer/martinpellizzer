Eugenia albimarginata

Eugenia albimarginata, commonly known as the white-margin guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy white flowers with pink margins. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, though they are not widely cultivated for consumption. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
