Eugenia dipoda var. brachypoda

Eugenia dipoda var. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
