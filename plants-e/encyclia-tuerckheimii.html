Encyclia tuerckheimii

Encyclia tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Encyclia tuerckheimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
