Echinochloa coarctata var. elatior

Echinochloa coarctata var. elatior, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stem, and its flowers are small and inconspicuous. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

This species is an important component of wetland ecosystems and can be invasive in some areas.
