Erodium lucidum

Erodium lucidum, commonly known as glossy bedstraw, is a small annual herb native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, branched stems covered in fine, sticky hairs and produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a glossy appearance. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
