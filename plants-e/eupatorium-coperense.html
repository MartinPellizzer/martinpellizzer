Eupatorium coperense

Eupatorium coperense, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green foliage that adds ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium coperense is a valuable plant for wildlife, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
