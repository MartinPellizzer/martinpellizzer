Eugenia ripidocarpa

Eugenia ripidocarpa, commonly known as the rough-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its thick, rough-textured leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The fruit is a small, smooth, reddish-brown drupe that is often used in local traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
