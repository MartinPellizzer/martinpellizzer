Eugenia miersiana var. venosa

Eugenia miersiana var. venosa, commonly known as the variegated miersia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The variegation in its foliage makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique and visually interesting plants.
