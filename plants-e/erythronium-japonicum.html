Erythronium japonicum

Erythronium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese dog's mercury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
