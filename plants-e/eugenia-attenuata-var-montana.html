Eugenia attenuata var. montana

Eugenia attenuata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests or paramo ecosystems. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers. Its fruits are edible, though they are often used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
