Eupatorium patens var. tomentosum

Eupatorium patens var. tomentosum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
