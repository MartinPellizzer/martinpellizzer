Ericoides flavicomum

Ericoides flavicomum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered heath, is a member of the Ericaceae family, characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers.

It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing dense foliage that helps retain moisture in its preferred environment.

Ericoides flavicomum is a slow-growing shrub that plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems.
