Euonymus acuminatus

Euonymus acuminatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape. During the fall, the plant produces striking red or orange berries that are attractive to birds. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It belongs to the family Cistaceae and is known for its adaptability to different light and moisture levels.
