Eupatorium coelestinum f. illinoense

Eupatorium coelestinum f. illinoense, commonly known as Illinois blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist to wet soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
