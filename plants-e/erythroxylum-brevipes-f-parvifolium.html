Erythroxylum brevipes f. parvifolium

Erythroxylum brevipes f. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved copal tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are typically small, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native habitat.
