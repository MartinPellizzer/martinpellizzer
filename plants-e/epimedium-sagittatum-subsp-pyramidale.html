Epimedium sagittatum subsp. pyramidale

Epimedium sagittatum subsp.

pyramidale, commonly known as the golden monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues.

The plant produces striking clusters of greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting foliage and unique floral display.
