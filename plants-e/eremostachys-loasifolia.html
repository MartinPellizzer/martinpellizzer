Eremostachys loasifolia

Eremostachys loasifolia, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, adapted to reflect sunlight and minimize heat stress.

Eremostachys loasifolia is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry mountainous areas.
