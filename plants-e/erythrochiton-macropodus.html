Erythrochiton macropodus

Erythrochiton macropodus, commonly known as the red-veined hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with prominent yellow stamens. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Erythrochiton macropodus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
