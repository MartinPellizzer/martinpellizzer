Erucastrum laevigatum var. pseudosinapis

Erucastrum laevigatum var. pseudosinapis, commonly known as Chinese mustard, is a biennial or annual herbaceous plant native to China.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a pungent, mustard-like odor when crushed.

The plant is known for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
