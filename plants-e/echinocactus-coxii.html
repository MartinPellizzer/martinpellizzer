Echinocactus coxii

Echinocactus coxii, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its striking appearance and drought tolerance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Echinocactus coxii is well-suited for xeriscaping and requires minimal care once established.
