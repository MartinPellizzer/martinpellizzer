Echinocactus fossulatus var. quadrispinus

Echinocactus fossulatus var. quadrispinus, commonly known as the Mexican hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white or yellow flowers. The plant has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its spines are often modified into short, hooked bristles, providing both protection and a unique aesthetic.

The plant's overall appearance is striking, with a rugged, spiky texture that resembles a hedgehog.
