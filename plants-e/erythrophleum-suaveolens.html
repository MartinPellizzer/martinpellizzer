Erythrophleum suaveolens

Erythrophleum suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and fragrant flowers that emit a sweet aroma. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
