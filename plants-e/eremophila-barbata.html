Eremophila barbata

Eremophila barbata, commonly known as the woolly emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its erect, woody stems and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry climates.

Eremophila barbata is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low water requirements.
