Epidendrum dariense

Epidendrum dariense, commonly known as the Darien Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Epidendrum dariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
