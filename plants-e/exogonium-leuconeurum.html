Exogonium leuconeurum

Exogonium leuconeurum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Exogonium leuconeurum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
