Erica nutans

Erica nutans, commonly known as the leaning heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Erica nutans thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
