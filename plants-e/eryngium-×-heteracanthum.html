Eryngium × heteracanthum

Eryngium × heteracanthum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a hybrid plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowering heads and fern-like, deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and long-lasting flowers.

The plant can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters and is known for its hardy nature.
