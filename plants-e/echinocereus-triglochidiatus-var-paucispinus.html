Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. paucispinus

Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. paucispinus, commonly known as the silver needle cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, rounded cactus with a compact growth habit and a grayish-green coloration. The plant features short, spiny stems and produces yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
