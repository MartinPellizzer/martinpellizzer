Evolvulus ericoides

Evolvulus ericoides, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, opposite leaf arrangement, and capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.
