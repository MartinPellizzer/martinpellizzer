Ericameria cooperi subsp. bajacalifornica

Ericameria cooperi subsp.

bajacalifornica, commonly known as the California goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, such as chaparral and coastal sage scrub. Its small, aromatic leaves and clustered inflorescences are adapted to the arid environments it inhabits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
