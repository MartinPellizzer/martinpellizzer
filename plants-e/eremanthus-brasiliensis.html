Eremanthus brasiliensis

Eremanthus brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian eremanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Eremanthus brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
