Erica tumida var. minor

Erica tumida var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to form compact, attractive mounds in garden settings.
