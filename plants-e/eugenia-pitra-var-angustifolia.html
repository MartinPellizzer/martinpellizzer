Eugenia pitra var. angustifolia

Eugenia pitra var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eugenia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to their aesthetic appeal.
