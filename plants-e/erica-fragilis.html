Erica fragilis

Erica fragilis, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. It is well adapted to harsh environments, including high elevations and coastal bluffs.

Erica fragilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
