Eleusine

Eleusine, commonly known as lovegrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It consists of several species, many of which are native to Africa, Asia, and Australia. These plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, spike-like inflorescences. Some species are cultivated for forage or ornamental purposes due to their hardy nature and attractive foliage.

Eleusine species are often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
