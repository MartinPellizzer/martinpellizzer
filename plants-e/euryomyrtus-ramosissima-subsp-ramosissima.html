Euryomyrtus ramosissima subsp. ramosissima

Euryomyrtus ramosissima subsp.

ramosissima, commonly known as the Tasmanian tea tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to Tasmania. It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage composed of narrow, leathery leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a significant species in Tasmanian flora.
