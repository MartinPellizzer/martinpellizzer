Euonymus latifolius subsp. cauconis

Euonymus latifolius subsp.

cauconis, commonly known as the broadleaf woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, lantern-like fruits that split open to reveal bright orange seeds. Its foliage and fruiting structures make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
