Enarthrocarpus clavatus

Enarthrocarpus clavatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its durability, the wood is valued for construction and furniture making.
