Eleocharis fuscopurpurea

Eleocharis fuscopurpurea, commonly known as purple-tinged spike rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, triangular leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
