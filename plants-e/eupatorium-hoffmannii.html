Eupatorium hoffmannii

Eupatorium hoffmannii, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
