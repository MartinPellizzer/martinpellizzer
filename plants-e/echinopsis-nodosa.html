Echinopsis nodosa

Echinopsis nodosa, commonly known as the night bloomer cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stem and clusters of spines. The plant produces large, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for succulent gardens.
