Erythrina buchii

Erythrina buchii, commonly known as the flame bean or red torchwood, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and its pods contain flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Erythrina buchii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
