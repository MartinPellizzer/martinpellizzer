Ethulia sinapifolia

Ethulia sinapifolia, commonly known as the false sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional crafts and incense.

Ethulia sinapifolia is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
