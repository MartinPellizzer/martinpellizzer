Elaeocarpus rumphii subsp. rumphii

Elaeocarpus rumphii subsp.

rumphii, commonly known as the Java elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, lanceolate leaves and showy white to pink flowers.

The plant produces woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible aril. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
