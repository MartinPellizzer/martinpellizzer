Eugenia chirindensis

Eugenia chirindensis, commonly known as the African myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature.

The plant is often valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
