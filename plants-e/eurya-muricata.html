Eurya muricata

Eurya muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf eurya, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, raised bumps. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. Eurya muricata typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded garden settings.
