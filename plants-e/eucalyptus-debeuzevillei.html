Eucalyptus debeuzevillei

Eucalyptus debeuzevillei, commonly known as the Beaufortia mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green to yellow in color, which bloom during the winter months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Eucalyptus debeuzevillei is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential for use in horticulture.
