Eugenia yrirensis

Eugenia yrirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yrirensis guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Eugenia yrirensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value is also appreciated due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
