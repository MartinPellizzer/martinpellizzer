Erigeron carpaticus

Erigeron carpaticus, commonly known as field blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to pale blue.

It is often considered an invasive species in North America due to its rapid spread and ability to thrive in various conditions.
