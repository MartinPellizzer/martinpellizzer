Erodium moschatum var. maculatum

Erodium moschatum var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive spotted appearance. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this annual herb thrives in disturbed soils and sunny environments. The plant produces small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
