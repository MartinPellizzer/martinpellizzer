Erigeron bonariensis var. leiothecus

Erigeron bonariensis var. leiothecus, commonly known as the silverleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue and white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its frost resistance and ability to attract pollinators.
