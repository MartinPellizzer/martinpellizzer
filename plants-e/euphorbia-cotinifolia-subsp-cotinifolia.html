Euphorbia cotinifolia subsp. cotinifolia

Euphorbia cotinifolia subsp.

cotinifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red bracts that surround small yellow flowers, giving it a dramatic appearance.

The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, greyish bark and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental landscaping for its vibrant seasonal display.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
