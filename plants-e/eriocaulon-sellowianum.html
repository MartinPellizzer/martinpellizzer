Eriocaulon sellowianum

Eriocaulon sellowianum, commonly known as the grass of paraguay, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and marshy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
