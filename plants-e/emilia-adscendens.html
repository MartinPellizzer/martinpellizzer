Emilia adscendens

Emilia adscendens, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. Emilia adscendens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
