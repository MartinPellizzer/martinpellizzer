Eragrostis velutina

Eragrostis velutina, commonly known as velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, including South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky leaves that give it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Velvetgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.
