Eleocharis fuscosanguinea

Eleocharis fuscosanguinea, commonly known as the purple false grass, is a species of sedge native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that often display a purplish hue. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of marsh and swamp ecosystems. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a fibrous texture.

Eleocharis fuscosanguinea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
