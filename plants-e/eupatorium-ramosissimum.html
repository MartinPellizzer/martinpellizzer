Eupatorium ramosissimum

Eupatorium ramosissimum, commonly known as the Mexican thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium ramosissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
