Eragrostis sporostachya

Eragrostis sporostachya, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis sporostachya is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions.
