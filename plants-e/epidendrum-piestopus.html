Epidendrum piestopus

Epidendrum piestopus, commonly known as the Spotted Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Epidendrum piestopus is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
