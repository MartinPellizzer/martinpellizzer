Eutrema wasabi

Eutrema wasabi, commonly known as Japanese wasabi, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root, known as wasabi, is the primary part used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often grown in shaded, well-drained soil.
