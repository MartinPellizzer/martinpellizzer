Eugenia corticopapyracea

Eugenia corticopapyracea, commonly known as the papaya tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a dense canopy and smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-colored fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
