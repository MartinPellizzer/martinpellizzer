Eucrypta chrysanthemifolia var. chrysanthemifolia

Eucrypta chrysanthemifolia var. chrysanthemifolia, commonly known as the golden chrysanthemum tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
