Eremothera chamaenerioides

Eremothera chamaenerioides, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eremothera chamaenerioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
