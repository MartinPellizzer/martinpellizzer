Eremurus korowinii

Eremurus korowinii, commonly known as the desert candle, is a striking plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, candle-like flower stalks that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces a single, large, yellowish-white flower at the top of the stalk, which blooms in the spring. Eremurus korowinii is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
