Eriogonum kennedyi var. austromontanum

Eriogonum kennedyi var. austromontanum, commonly known as the Great Basin buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
