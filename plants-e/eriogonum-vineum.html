Eriogonum vineum

Eriogonum vineum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum vineum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
