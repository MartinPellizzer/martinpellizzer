Erythroxylum mapuerae

Erythroxylum mapuerae, commonly known as the Mapuerae coca, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is closely related to the genus Erythroxylum, which includes species like coca and cocaine-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

It produces small, elliptical berries that contain the seeds, and it is adapted to grow in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures.
