Eugenia nodiflora

Eugenia nodiflora, commonly known as the fragrant guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thin, smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Eugenia nodiflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
