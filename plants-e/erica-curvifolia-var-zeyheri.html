Erica curvifolia var. zeyheri

Erica curvifolia var. zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
