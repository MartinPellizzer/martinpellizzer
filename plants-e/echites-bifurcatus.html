Echites bifurcatus

Echites bifurcatus, commonly known as the two-branched echites, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or green. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
