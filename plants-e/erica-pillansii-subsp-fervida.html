Erica pillansii subsp. fervida

Erica pillansii subsp.

fervida, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade. Erica pillansii subsp.

fervida is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
