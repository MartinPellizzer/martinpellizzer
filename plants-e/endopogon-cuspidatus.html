Endopogon cuspidatus

Endopogon cuspidatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped grass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in restoration projects and as a ornamental plant in dry gardens.
