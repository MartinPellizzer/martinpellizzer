Erlangea alternifolia

Erlangea alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaf elms, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elongated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant has a slender, often multi-branched trunk with smooth, gray bark. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
