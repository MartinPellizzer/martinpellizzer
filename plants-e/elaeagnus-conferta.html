Elaeagnus conferta

Elaeagnus conferta, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Elaeagnus conferta is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its ornamental appeal.
