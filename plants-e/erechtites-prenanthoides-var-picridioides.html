Erechtites prenanthoides var. picridioides

Erechtites prenanthoides var. picridioides, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange composite flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its floral structure consists of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
