Erica austiniana

Erica austiniana, commonly known as Austin's heath, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States, particularly Texas.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica austiniana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
