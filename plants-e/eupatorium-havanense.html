Eupatorium havanense

Eupatorium havanense, commonly known as the Havana feverfew, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Eupatorium havanense is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
