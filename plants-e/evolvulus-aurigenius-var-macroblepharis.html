Evolvulus aurigenius var. macroblepharis

Evolvulus aurigenius var. macroblepharis, commonly known as the large-eyed blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
