Erigeron philadelphicus var. ricardii

Erigeron philadelphicus var. ricardii, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
