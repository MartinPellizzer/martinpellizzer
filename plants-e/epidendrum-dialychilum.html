Epidendrum dialychilum

Epidendrum dialychilum, commonly known as the showy epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features long, arching stems with numerous, showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Epidendrum dialychilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
