Eupatorium crenaeum

Eupatorium crenaeum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Eupatorium crenaeum is often found in wetland environments and is known for its medicinal properties historically used by Native American tribes.
