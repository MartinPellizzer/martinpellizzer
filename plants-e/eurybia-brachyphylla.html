Eurybia brachyphylla

Eurybia brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and basal, while its flowering stems are often branched.

Eurybia brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
