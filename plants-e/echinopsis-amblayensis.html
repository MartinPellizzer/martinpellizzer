Echinopsis amblayensis

Echinopsis amblayensis, commonly known as the Amblay's cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly found in Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months. The plant has a spiny exterior that provides protection against herbivores and excessive moisture loss. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Echinopsis amblayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in desert gardens.
