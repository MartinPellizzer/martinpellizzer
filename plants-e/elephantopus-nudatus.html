Elephantopus nudatus

Elephantopus nudatus, commonly known as the naked elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, fleshy roots that resemble the legs of an elephant, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
