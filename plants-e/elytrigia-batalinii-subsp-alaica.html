Elytrigia batalinii subsp. alaica

Elytrigia batalinii subsp.

alaica, commonly known as the Crimean wild oat, is a grass species native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic distinctiveness within the Elytrigia genus.
