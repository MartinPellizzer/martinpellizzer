Eulophia elata

Eulophia elata, commonly known as the tall eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and thrives in humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and multiple pseudobulbs. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Eulophia elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
