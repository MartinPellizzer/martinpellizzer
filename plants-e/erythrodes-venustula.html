Erythrodes venustula

Erythrodes venustula, commonly known as the red velvet orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and velvety texture, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, typical of epiphytic species.

Erythrodes venustula is often found growing on trees in humid, shaded environments, thriving in the understory of rainforests.
