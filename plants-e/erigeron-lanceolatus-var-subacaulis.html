Erigeron lanceolatus var. subacaulis

Erigeron lanceolatus var. subacaulis, commonly known as the slender fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a woolly texture, while its stems are slender and erect.
