Euphorbia biramensis

Euphorbia biramensis, commonly known as the two-branched spurges, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and yellowish-green sap. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, which are modified leaf-like structures.

Euphorbia biramensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
