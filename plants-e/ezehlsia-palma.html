Ezehlsia palma

Ezehlsia palma, commonly known as the golden palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, slender trunk with a smooth, pale green bark that shimmers in the sunlight. Its most distinctive characteristic is its large, fan-shaped leaves, which are a vibrant yellow-green and can grow up to two meters in diameter. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the trunk, followed by orange-red fruits containing edible seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
