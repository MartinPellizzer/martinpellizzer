Epilobium montanum proles carpetanum

Epilobium montanum proles carpetanum, commonly known as the Carpathian willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
