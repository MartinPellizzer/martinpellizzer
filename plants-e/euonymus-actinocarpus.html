Euonymus actinocarpus

Euonymus actinocarpus, commonly known as the spreading euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is valued for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
