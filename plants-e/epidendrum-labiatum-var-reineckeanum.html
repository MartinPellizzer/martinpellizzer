Epidendrum labiatum var. reineckeanum

Epidendrum labiatum var. reineckeanum, commonly known as the Reinecke's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, racemose inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture, and the flowers come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
