Eryngium yuccifolium

Eryngium yuccifolium, commonly known as the yucca-leaved thistle, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its sharp, spiny leaves that resemble those of yucca plants, and its blue to purple flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
