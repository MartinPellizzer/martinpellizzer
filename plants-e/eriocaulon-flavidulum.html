Eriocaulon flavidulum

Eriocaulon flavidulum, commonly known as the yellow threadgrass, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with pale yellow to brown hues.

Eriocaulon flavidulum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
