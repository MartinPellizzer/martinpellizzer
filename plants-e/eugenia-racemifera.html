Eugenia racemifera

Eugenia racemifera, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it the species name *racemifera*, which means "bearing racemes.". The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are aromatic and have a slightly citrusy scent, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Eugenia racemifera thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.
