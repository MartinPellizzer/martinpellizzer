Eugenia crossopterygoides

Eugenia crossopterygoides, commonly known as the red-fruit eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Eugenia crossopterygoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
