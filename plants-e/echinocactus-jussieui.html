Echinocactus jussieui

Echinocactus jussieui, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny ribs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green exterior. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
