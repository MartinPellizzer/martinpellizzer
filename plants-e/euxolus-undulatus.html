Euxolus undulatus

Euxolus undulatus, commonly known as the undulating euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with unique morphological features. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive undulating edge, giving it its scientific name.

Euxolus undulatus is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent-like structure.
