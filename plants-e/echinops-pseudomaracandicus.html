Echinops pseudomaracandicus

Echinops pseudomaracandicus, commonly known as the Persian thistle or desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a globe, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Echinops pseudomaracandicus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
