Eupatorium xylophylloides

Eupatorium xylophylloides, commonly known as the Florida aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing purple to pinkish disc flowers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets.
