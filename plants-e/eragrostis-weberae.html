Eragrostis weberae

Eragrostis weberae, commonly known as Weber's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a slight bluish or grayish tint.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its drought tolerance.
