Euphorbia gracilis

Euphorbia gracilis, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Euphorbia gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
