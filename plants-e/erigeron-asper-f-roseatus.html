Erigeron asper f. roseatus

Erigeron asper f. roseatus, commonly known as the roseate rough fleabane, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a pinkish to rose-colored central disk. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
