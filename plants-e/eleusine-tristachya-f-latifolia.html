Eleusine tristachya f. latifolia

Eleusine tristachya f. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf eleusine, is a variant of the annual grass species Eleusine tristachya.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and panicle-like inflorescence that produces small, spikelet-based flowers. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its botanical features include slender stems, hairy leaves, and a fibrous root system.

It is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance.
