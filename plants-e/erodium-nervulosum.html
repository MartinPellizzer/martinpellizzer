Erodium nervulosum

Erodium nervulosum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and also hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its delicate structure and short life cycle.
