Ecteinanthus divaricatus

Ecteinanthus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ecteinanthus divaricatus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
