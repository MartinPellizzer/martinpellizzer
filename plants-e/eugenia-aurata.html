Eugenia aurata

Eugenia aurata, commonly known as the golden eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with smooth, aromatic bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
