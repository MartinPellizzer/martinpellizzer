Elaeodendron laurifolium

Elaeodendron laurifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf elaeodendron, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Elaeodendron laurifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along riverbanks.
