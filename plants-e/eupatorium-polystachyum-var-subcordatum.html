Eupatorium polystachyum var. subcordatum

Eupatorium polystachyum var. subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, with a distinctive basal rosette arrangement. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
