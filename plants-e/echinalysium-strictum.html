Echinalysium strictum

Echinalysium strictum, commonly known as the rigid echinus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds with a unique, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
