Epidendrum gallopavinum

Epidendrum gallopavinum, commonly known as the Gallopavine Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Its inflorescence is a raceme of numerous small, lance-shaped flowers.

Epidendrum gallopavinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, moist conditions.
