Epilobium tetragonum subsp. lamyi

Epilobium tetragonum subsp.

lamyi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks, wet meadows, and woodland edges.

The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clustered inflorescences. It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and is often found in wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
