Eremurus angustifolius

Eremurus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eremurus, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous yellow-green or pale yellow flowers. The plant has linear, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Eremurus angustifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
