Erica linnaeoides

Erica linnaeoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica linnaeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
