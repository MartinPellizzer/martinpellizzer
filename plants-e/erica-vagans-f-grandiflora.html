Erica vagans f. grandiflora

Erica vagans f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Native to the western Mediterranean, this plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, such as coastal cliffs and woodland edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
