Eulophia chlorantha

Eulophia chlorantha, commonly known as the green orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eulophia chlorantha is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
