Eucalyptus subangusta subsp. subangusta

Eucalyptus subangusta subsp.

subangusta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically pale blue to white in hue. It is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

The species is noted for its aromatic oils, which are used in various traditional and commercial applications.
