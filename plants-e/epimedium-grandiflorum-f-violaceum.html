Epimedium grandiflorum f. violaceum

Epimedium grandiflorum f. violaceum, commonly known as the purple-flowered barberry, is a hybrid plant with distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial herb native to China, characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in early spring, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are typically leathery, elliptical, and have a deep green color with a slightly serrated margin.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shaded garden settings.
