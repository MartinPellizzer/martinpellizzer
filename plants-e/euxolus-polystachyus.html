Euxolus polystachyus

Euxolus polystachyus, commonly known as the mountain thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant features slender, upright stems covered with small, opposite leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are typically small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from white to pale purple.

Euxolus polystachyus is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
