Epilobium smithii

Epilobium smithii, commonly known as Smith's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant produces showy pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Epilobium smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
