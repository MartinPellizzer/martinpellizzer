Eupatorium amarum

Eupatorium amarum, commonly known as boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Boneset is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat fevers and digestive issues.

It thrives in moist, woodland environments and blooms from late summer into early fall.
