Eugenia cintalapana

Eugenia cintalapana, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or wild cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red, edible berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
