Enchylaena borysthenis

Enchylaena borysthenis, commonly known as the Crimean sand thorn, is a shrub native to the steppes of southern Ukraine and the Crimea.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and spiny, woody stems that are adapted to arid conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss, and it produces small, red fruits that are dispersed by animals. Enchylaena borysthenis is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is resilient to drought.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
