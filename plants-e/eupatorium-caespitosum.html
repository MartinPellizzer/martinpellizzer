Eupatorium caespitosum

Eupatorium caespitosum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium caespitosum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
