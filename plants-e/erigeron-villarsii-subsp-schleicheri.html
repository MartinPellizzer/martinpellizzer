Erigeron villarsii subsp. schleicheri

Erigeron villarsii subsp.

schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's blueweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet ray florets and yellow disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
