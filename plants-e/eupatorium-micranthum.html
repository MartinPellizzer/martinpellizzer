Eupatorium micranthum

Eupatorium micranthum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium micranthum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
