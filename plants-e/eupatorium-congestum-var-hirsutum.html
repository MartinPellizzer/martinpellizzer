Eupatorium congestum var. hirsutum

Eupatorium congestum var. hirsutum, commonly known as rough boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features tall, erect stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

It is often found in moist meadows and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
