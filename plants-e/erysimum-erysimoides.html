Erysimum erysimoides

Erysimum erysimoides, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has linear, hairy leaves and a branched stem that often grows along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to gardens in arid climates.
