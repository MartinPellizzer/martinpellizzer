Elymus hyperarcticus

Elymus hyperarcticus, commonly known as Arctic needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Elymus hyperarcticus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Its growth is closely tied to the seasonal patterns of the polar regions, making it an important component of Arctic ecosystems.
