Epilobium × ambigens

Epilobium × ambigens, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed sites.

Epilobium × ambigens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
