Echinops integrifolius

Echinops integrifolius, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble small globes, which give it its common name. The plant has blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the distinctive seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Echinops integrifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
