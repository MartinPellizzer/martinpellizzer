Evolvulus arizonicus var. laetus

Evolvulus arizonicus var. laetus, commonly known as the Arizona morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a trailing or climbing annual vine with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, round, greenish-white fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.
