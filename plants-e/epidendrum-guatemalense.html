Epidendrum guatemalense

Epidendrum guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
