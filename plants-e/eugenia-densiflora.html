Eugenia densiflora

Eugenia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological importance.

The fruit, which is small and fleshy, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
