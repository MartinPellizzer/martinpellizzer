Echinochloa phylloryzoides

Echinochloa phylloryzoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and reproductive structures that aid in its ecological success.
