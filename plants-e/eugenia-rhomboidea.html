Eugenia rhomboidea

Eugenia rhomboidea, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
