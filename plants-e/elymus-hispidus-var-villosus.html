Elymus hispidus var. villosus

Elymus hispidus var. villosus, commonly known as hairy wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive hairy leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, facilitating wind pollination.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
