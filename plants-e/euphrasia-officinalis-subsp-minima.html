Euphrasia officinalis subsp. minima

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

minima, commonly known as the lesser eyebright, is a small herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose racemes, with distinctive hooded petals.

It is known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat eye ailments and other inflammatory conditions.
