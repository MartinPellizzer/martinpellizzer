Eragrostis neesii

Eragrostis neesii, commonly known as the slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed sites, exhibiting a tufted growth habit with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, appearing in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

Eragrostis neesii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
