Erysimum arcuatum

Erysimum arcuatum, commonly known as the bent-leaf wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Erysimum arcuatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow or orange flowers.
