Eriogonum umbellatum var. californicum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. californicum, commonly known as California buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, coastal dunes, and disturbed sites.

California buckwheat plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
