Evolvulus glomeratus var. genuinus

Evolvulus glomeratus var. genuinus, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, sky-blue or white flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and is often found in dry, rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
