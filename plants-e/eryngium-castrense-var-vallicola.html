Eryngium castrense var. vallicola

Eryngium castrense var. vallicola, commonly known as the Alpine thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that form a distinctive crown-like structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
