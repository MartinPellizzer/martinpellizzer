Eriocephalus ambiguus

Eriocephalus ambiguus, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eriocephalus ambiguus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
