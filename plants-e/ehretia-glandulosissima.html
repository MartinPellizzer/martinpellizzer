Ehretia glandulosissima

Ehretia glandulosissima, commonly known as the woolly sycamore, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
