Epipactis foliosa

Epipactis foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers that often have a distinctive lip. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces erect, branched stems.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
