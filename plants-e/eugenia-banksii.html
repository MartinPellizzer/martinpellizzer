Eugenia banksii

Eugenia banksii, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Eugenia banksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
