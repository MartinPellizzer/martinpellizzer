Eragrostis balgooyi

Eragrostis balgooyi, commonly known as the Balgooy's lovegrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis balgooyi is often found in open grasslands and is sometimes used for forage in livestock grazing.
