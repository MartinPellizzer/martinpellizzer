Evolvulus diosmoides var. sericeus

Evolvulus diosmoides var. sericeus, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and bloom in the early morning. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
