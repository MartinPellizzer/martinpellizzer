Eragrostis burmanica

Eragrostis burmanica, commonly known as Burman's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.

Eragrostis burmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
