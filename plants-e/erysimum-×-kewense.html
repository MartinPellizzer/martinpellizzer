Erysimum × kewense

Erysimum × kewense, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a cross between Erysimum cheiri and Erysimum alpinum.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the British Isles. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in dense, fuzzy foliage. Its flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.
