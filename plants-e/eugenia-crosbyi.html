Eugenia crosbyi

Eugenia crosbyi, commonly known as the Texas holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dark red berry that develops in the fall.

Eugenia crosbyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
