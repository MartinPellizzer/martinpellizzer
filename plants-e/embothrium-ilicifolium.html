Embothrium ilicifolium

Embothrium ilicifolium, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that resemble those of holly, hence its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas with well-drained soil.

Embothrium ilicifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
