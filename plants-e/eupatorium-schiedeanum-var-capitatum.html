Eupatorium schiedeanum var. capitatum

Eupatorium schiedeanum var. capitatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
