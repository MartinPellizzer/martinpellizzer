Eria crassicaulis

Eria crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as an epiphytic or lithophytic orchid, attaching itself to trees or rocks. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The flowers are small and clustered, often in a spike, with a distinctive coloration that varies by species.
