Echinopsis candicans

Echinopsis candicans, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pink spines and a short, thick stem that often displays vibrant colors. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its succulent stems. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Echinopsis candicans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
