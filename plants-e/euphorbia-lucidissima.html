Euphorbia lucidissima

Euphorbia lucidissima, commonly known as the shining spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a milky sap that can be toxic and is often used as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Euphorbia lucidissima thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both ornamental and ecological contexts.
