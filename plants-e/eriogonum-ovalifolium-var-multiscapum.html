Eriogonum ovalifolium var. multiscapum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. multiscapum, commonly known as the multiscapous globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
