Evelyna graminifolia

Evelyna graminifolia, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine silvery or grayish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, appearing in late spring to early summer. Evelyna graminifolia thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its low maintenance requirements and ability to form dense clumps make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial.
