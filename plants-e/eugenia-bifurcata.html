Eugenia bifurcata

Eugenia bifurcata, commonly known as the two-branched eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia bifurcata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
