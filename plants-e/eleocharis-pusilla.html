Eleocharis pusilla

Eleocharis pusilla, commonly known as pigweed or slender spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems and tiny, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Eleocharis pusilla is often found in temperate regions and is a common component of wetland ecosystems.
