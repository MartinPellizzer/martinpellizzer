Erodium cicutarium var. glanduliferum

Erodium cicutarium var. glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it its specific epithet. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, bead-like fruits.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
