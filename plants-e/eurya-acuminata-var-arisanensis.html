Eurya acuminata var. arisanensis

Eurya acuminata var. arisanensis, commonly known as the Arisan evergreen holly, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly found in Japan and Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
