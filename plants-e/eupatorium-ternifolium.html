Eupatorium ternifolium

Eupatorium ternifolium, commonly known as boneset, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features opposite, serrated leaves that are often described as resembling bones, hence its common name. It produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Boneset is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
