Eugenia castanea

Eugenia castanea, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *pata de vaca*, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark red, cherry-like fruits that are edible. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The wood of Eugenia castanea is also valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts.
