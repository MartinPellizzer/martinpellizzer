Euphorbia tananarivae

Euphorbia tananarivae, commonly known as the Tananarive spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in its arid native environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cyathium, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant.
