Erica devoniana

Erica devoniana, commonly known as the Devon heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the United Kingdom.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica devoniana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
