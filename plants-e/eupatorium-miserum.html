Eupatorium miserum

Eupatorium miserum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery-gray underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eupatorium miserum is valued in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
