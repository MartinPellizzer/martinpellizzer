Erigeron × batumensis

Erigeron × batumensis, commonly known as the Caucasian fleabane, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grassy slopes, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are generally hairy and grayish-green, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
