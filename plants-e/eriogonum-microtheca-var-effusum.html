Eriogonum microtheca var. effusum

Eriogonum microtheca var. effusum, commonly known as the softleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, leathery seeds that are dispersed by wind.
