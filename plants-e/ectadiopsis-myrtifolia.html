Ectadiopsis myrtifolia

Ectadiopsis myrtifolia, commonly known as the Myrtleleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact form and drought tolerance.
