Echinopsis salmiana var. bridgesii

Echinopsis salmiana var. bridgesii, commonly known as the Salmiana cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stem and spiny areoles. The plant produces striking white or pale purple flowers during the night, a trait typical of many nocturnal blooming cacti. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
