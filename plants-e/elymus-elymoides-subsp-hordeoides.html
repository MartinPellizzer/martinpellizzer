Elymus elymoides subsp. hordeoides

Elymus elymoides subsp.

hordeoides, commonly known as the barleygrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem, with each spikelet containing several small, awned lemmas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks, contributing to ecological stability in riparian zones.
