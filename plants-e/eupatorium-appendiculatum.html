Eupatorium appendiculatum

Eupatorium appendiculatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small, white to pale blue flowers that attract pollinators.

Eupatorium appendiculatum is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade.
