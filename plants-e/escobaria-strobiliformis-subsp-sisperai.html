Escobaria strobiliformis subsp. sisperai

Escobaria strobiliformis subsp.

sisperai, commonly known as the Sisperai liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and fleshy, blue-green foliage.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
