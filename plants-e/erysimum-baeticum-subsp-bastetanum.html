Erysimum baeticum subsp. bastetanum

Erysimum baeticum subsp.

bastetanum, commonly known as the Baetican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing habit and bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, and its flowering period occurs in spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
