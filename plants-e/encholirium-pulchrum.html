Encholirium pulchrum

Encholirium pulchrum, commonly known as the Mexican snowbell, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers, which are highly ornamental. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that can reach up to 2 meters in height, making it a dramatic addition to gardens. Its leaves are strap-like and often have a bluish-green hue, adding to its visual appeal.

Encholirium pulchrum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.
