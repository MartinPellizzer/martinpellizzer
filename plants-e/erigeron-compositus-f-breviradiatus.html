Erigeron compositus f. breviradiatus

Erigeron compositus f. breviradiatus, commonly known as the short-rayed fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
