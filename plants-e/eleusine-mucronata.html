Eleusine mucronata

Eleusine mucronata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Eleusine mucronata is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.
