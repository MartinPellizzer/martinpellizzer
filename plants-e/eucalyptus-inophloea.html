Eucalyptus inophloea

Eucalyptus inophloea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a hardy shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and smooth, often peeling bark. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus inophloea is valued for its resilience and is often used in reforestation and land rehabilitation projects.
