Elaeocarpus tahanensis

Elaeocarpus tahanensis, commonly known as Tahan's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically encased in a fleshy, red or orange aril. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
