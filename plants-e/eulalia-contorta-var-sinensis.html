Eulalia contorta var. sinensis

Eulalia contorta var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing clumps.

Eulalia contorta var. sinensis is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
