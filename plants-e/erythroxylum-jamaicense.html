Erythroxylum jamaicense

Erythroxylum jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican chili or red pepper plant, is a member of the Erythroxylaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, particularly Jamaica. The plant is characterized by its small, green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red, berry-like fruits. The fruits contain the alkaloid compounds responsible for its spicy flavor, making it a valued plant in traditional medicine and culinary uses.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
