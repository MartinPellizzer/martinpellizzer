Euphorbia elegans var. laxa

Euphorbia elegans var. laxa, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often tinged with red, and it produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its characteristic flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
