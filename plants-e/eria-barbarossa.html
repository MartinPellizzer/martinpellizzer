Eria barbarossa

Eria barbarossa, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
