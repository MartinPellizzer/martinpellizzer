Eucharis boliviensis

Eucharis boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces a rosette of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Eucharis boliviensis is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
