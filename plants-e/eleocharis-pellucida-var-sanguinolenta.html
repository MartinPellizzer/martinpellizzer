Eleocharis pellucida var. sanguinolenta

Eleocharis pellucida var. sanguinolenta, commonly known as the bloodroot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it a common sight in swampy environments.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it well-suited for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
