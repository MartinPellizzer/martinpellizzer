Evolvulus frankenioides

Evolvulus frankenioides, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers and trailing growth habit. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, adapted to conserve water in hot, dry environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Evolvulus frankenioides is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in poor, well-drained conditions.
