Euphorbia quitensis

Euphorbia quitensis, commonly known as the Peruvian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features a distinctive appearance with its spiny stems and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a structure called a cyathium. Euphorbia quitensis is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
