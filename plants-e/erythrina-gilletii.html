Erythrina gilletii

Erythrina gilletii, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange floral clusters, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are followed by long, slender pods that contain several flat, winged seeds.

Erythrina gilletii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
