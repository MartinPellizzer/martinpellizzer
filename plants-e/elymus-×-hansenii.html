Elymus × hansenii

Elymus × hansenii, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is a hybrid between Elymus trachycaulus and Elymus rigens, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has tufted growth habit with dense, fibrous roots that help prevent soil erosion. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a hairy texture, and it produces spike-like inflorescences.

Elymus × hansenii is often used in restoration projects due to its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance.
