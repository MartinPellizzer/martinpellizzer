Eupatorium retifolium

Eupatorium retifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Eupatorium retifolium is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
