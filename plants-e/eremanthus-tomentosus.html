Eremanthus tomentosus

Eremanthus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly eremanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate in shape and have a soft, velvety texture.

Eremanthus tomentosus is often found in the Andes region and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
