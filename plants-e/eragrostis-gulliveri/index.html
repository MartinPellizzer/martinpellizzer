Eragrostis Gulliveri

Eragrostis gulliveri is a rare and fascinating grass species discovered in the remote highlands of Ethiopia. This plant is named after the renowned botanist James Gulliver, who played a key role in its identification. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of scientific interest. Studying Eragrostis gulliveri offers insights into plant resilience and biodiversity in extreme habitats.
