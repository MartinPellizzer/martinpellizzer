Eugenia yautepecana

Eugenia yautepecana, commonly known as the Yautepec holly, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to southern Mexico, particularly in the states of Oaxaca and Chiapas. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and oval in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
