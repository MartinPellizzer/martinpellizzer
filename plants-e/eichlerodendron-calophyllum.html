Eichlerodendron calophyllum

Eichlerodendron calophyllum, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a tropical tree native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed. It has adapted to saline environments through specialized root systems that filter out salt, making it an important species in mangrove ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for stabilizing coastal soils and supporting diverse marine and terrestrial life.
