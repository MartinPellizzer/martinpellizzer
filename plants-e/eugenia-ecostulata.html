Eugenia ecostulata

Eugenia ecostulata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters, followed by small, red, fleshy berries.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
