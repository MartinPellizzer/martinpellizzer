Eschscholzia macrantha

Eschscholzia macrantha, commonly known as the giant California poppy, is a striking annual plant native to the western United States.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from orange to red, with petals that can reach up to 5 centimeters in length. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Its distinctive appearance and vibrant color make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
