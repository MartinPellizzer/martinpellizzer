Echinocactus santa-maria

Echinocactus santa-maria, commonly known as the Santa Maria Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing cactus with a globular shape and prominent spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant features a ribbed stem covered in sharp, yellowish spines and produces yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry conditions.
