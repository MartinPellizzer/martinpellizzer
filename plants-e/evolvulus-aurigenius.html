Evolvulus aurigenius

Evolvulus aurigenius, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, opening in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silver-white hairs, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and brief floral displays.
