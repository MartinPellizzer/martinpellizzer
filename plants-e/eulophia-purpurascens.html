Eulophia purpurascens

Eulophia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients.

Eulophia purpurascens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
