Erica refulgens

Erica refulgens, commonly known as the shining heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica refulgens thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
