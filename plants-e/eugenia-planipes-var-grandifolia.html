Eugenia planipes var. grandifolia

Eugenia planipes var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved evergreen or grandifolia eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
