Eriophorum virginicum f. album

Eriophorum virginicum f. album, commonly known as the white tussock sedge, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, white-flowered inflorescences that give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as bogs, marshes, and meadows. Its leaves are grass-like and often have a bluish-gray hue, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
