Eryngium petiolatum var. minimum

Eryngium petiolatum var. minimum, commonly known as the dwarf thistle or small-leaf eryngium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces striking blue to purple cylindrical flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 12 inches, making it ideal for rock gardens or small spaces. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
