Eriophorum viridicarinatum f. fellowsii

Eriophorum viridicarinatum f. fellowsii, commonly known as the Fell's sedge, is a variant of the green-winged sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and bogs. Its inflorescence consists of small, greenish flowers that are tightly packed.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
