Eriogonum strictum var. greenei

Eriogonum strictum var. greenei, commonly known as the Greene's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink to reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
