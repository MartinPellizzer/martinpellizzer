Ericoides transparens

Ericoides transparens, commonly known as the translucent heath, is a rare and delicate plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that exhibit a unique translucent quality, allowing light to pass through them. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as bogs or heathlands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Ericoides transparens is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
