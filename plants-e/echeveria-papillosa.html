Echeveria papillosa

Echeveria papillosa, commonly known as the woolly echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves covered in fine, white hairs that give it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces showy clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its compact shape.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
