Eriogonum panamintense var. mensicola

Eriogonum panamintense var. mensicola, commonly known as the Panamint mountain buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the Mojave Desert in California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces showy clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid conditions with minimal water requirements. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing food for pollinators and wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
