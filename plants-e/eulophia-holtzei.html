Eulophia holtzei

Eulophia holtzei, commonly known as the Holtze's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in seasonal habitats.

Eulophia holtzei exhibits a unique floral structure with a spurred lip, which is a common feature among orchids.
