Erica arachnocalyx

Erica arachnocalyx, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance.

Erica arachnocalyx is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
