Elaeocarpus habbemensis subsp. habbemensis

Elaeocarpus habbemensis subsp.

habbemensis, commonly known as the Habbemans elaeocarp, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological features and ecological significance in its native habitat.
