Erigeron nemorale

Erigeron nemorale, commonly known as woodland fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron nemorale is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
