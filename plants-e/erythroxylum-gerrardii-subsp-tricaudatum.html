Erythroxylum gerrardii subsp. tricaudatum

Erythroxylum gerrardii subsp.

tricaudatum, commonly known as the three-tailed erythroxylum, is a shrub native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its branched, reddish stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elliptical, hairy fruits. It is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less commonly encountered species.
