Evolvulus simplex

Evolvulus simplex, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are trailing or creeping.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
