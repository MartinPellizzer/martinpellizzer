Erythrodes aratanhensis

Erythrodes aratanhensis, commonly known as the Red-veined Bunchgrass, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive red veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Erythrodes aratanhensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
