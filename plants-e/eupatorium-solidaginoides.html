Eupatorium solidaginoides

Eupatorium solidaginoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Blue mistflower is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
