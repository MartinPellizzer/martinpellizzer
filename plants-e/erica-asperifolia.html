Erica asperifolia

Erica asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers. The leaves are leathery and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving the plant its common name. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica asperifolia is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
