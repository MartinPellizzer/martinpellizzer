Enteropogon ramosus

Enteropogon ramosus, commonly known as the red grass or blood grass, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its distinctive red or purple coloration in its inflorescence. The plant produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers that are often brightly colored. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas, and is adapted to dry conditions.

Enteropogon ramosus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
