Elaeagnus glabra var. oxyphylla

Elaeagnus glabra var. oxyphylla, commonly known as silver leaf elaeagnus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early spring. Its fruits are small, reddish berries that mature in late summer.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
