Eragrostis guangxiensis

Eragrostis guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for erosion control, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
