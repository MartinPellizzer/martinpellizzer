Echinodorus paniculatus

Echinodorus paniculatus, commonly known as the broadleaf waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in a panicle, which is a type of branched inflorescence. It thrives in slow to moderate water currents and is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.

Echinodorus paniculatus is popular in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide oxygen and shelter for aquatic life.
