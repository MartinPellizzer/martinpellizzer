Erigeron acris var. elatus

Erigeron acris var. elatus, commonly known as the stiffleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, daisy-like blooms. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow, stiff, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing white to pale blue ray florets and yellow disk florets.
