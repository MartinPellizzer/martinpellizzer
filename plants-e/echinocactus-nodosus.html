Echinocactus nodosus

Echinocactus nodosus, commonly known as the Mexican horse chestnut, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stem and spiny, segmented ribs. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fleshy, red fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Echinocactus nodosus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
