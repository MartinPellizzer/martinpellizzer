Eupatorium eucosmoides

Eupatorium eucosmoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium eucosmoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
