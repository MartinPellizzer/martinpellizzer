Eupatorium campechense

Eupatorium campechense, commonly known as Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in partial to full shade.

Eupatorium campechense is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
