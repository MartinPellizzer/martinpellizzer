Epiphyllum crenulatum

Epiphyllum crenulatum, commonly known as the night bloomer or star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is a climbing or trailing plant with stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, often in shades of white, pink, or red. Its leaves are usually succulent and can be either opposite or alternate, depending on the growth conditions.

Epiphyllum crenulatum is often grown as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in various light conditions.
