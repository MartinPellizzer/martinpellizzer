Eugenia banderensis

Eugenia banderensis, commonly known as the Bander's evergreen, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, a small, round berry, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

Eugenia banderensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
