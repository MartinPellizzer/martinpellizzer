Eucalyptus piperata

Eucalyptus piperata, commonly known as the rough-barked apple, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are typically clustered in groups. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent.

Eucalyptus piperata is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.
