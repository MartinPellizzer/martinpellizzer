Eugenia casiguranensis

Eugenia casiguranensis, commonly known as the Casiguran eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
