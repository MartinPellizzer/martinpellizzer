Eugenia rariflora var. parvifolia

Eugenia rariflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved guava, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a fragrant aroma. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
