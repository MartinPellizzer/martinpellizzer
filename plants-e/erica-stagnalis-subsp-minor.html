Erica stagnalis subsp. minor

Erica stagnalis subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the British Isles. It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and closely packed. Erica stagnalis subsp.

minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
