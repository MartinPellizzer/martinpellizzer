Euphrasia purpurea f. iodantha

Euphrasia purpurea f. iodantha, commonly known as the purple-eyed gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its vivid purple flowers with a distinctive dark eye at the center, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
