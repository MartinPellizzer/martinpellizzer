Eulophidium silvaticum

Eulophidium silvaticum, commonly known as the forest erinose mite, is a minute arachnid belonging to the family Eulophidae.

It is primarily found in forested areas, where it inhabits the leaf litter and undergrowth. This species plays a role in the decomposition process by feeding on decaying plant material. Its life cycle includes several stages, with larvae and adults contributing to nutrient cycling in soil ecosystems.

Due to its small size and cryptic behavior, it is rarely observed without specialized equipment.
