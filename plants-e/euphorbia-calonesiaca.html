Euphorbia calonesiaca

Euphorbia calonesiaca, commonly known as the White Spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with thick, fleshy stems and small, white, spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.

Euphorbia calonesiaca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
