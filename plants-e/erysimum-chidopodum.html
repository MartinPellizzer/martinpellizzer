Erysimum chidopodum

Erysimum chidopodum, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. It produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds, typical of the family.

Erysimum chidopodum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
