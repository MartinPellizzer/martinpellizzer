Euphorbia transoxana

Euphorbia transoxana, commonly known as the Transoxiana spurge, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It features a short, thick stem with spiny leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Euphorbia transoxana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
