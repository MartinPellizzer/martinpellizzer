Entada adenanthera

Entada adenanthera, commonly known as the African coral tree, is a large deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, coral-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The tree has a thick, rugged bark that is often fissured and can be used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils.

The species is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in several African communities.
