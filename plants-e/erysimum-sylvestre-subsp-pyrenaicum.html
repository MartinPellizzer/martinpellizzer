Erysimum sylvestre subsp. pyrenaicum

Erysimum sylvestre subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often on slopes and cliffs. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
