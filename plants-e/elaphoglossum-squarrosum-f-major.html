Elaphoglossum squarrosum f. major

Elaphoglossum squarrosum f. major, commonly known as the broad-leaved holly fern, is a fern species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds with a distinctive, broad outline. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with closely spaced pinnae that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
