Epilobium glandulosum var. occidentale

Epilobium glandulosum var. occidentale, commonly known as western willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as streambanks and wet meadows, where it thrives in full to partial sunlight. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in glandular hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
