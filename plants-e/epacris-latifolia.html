Epacris latifolia

Epacris latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Epacris latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation areas.
