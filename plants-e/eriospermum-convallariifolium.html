Eriospermum convallariifolium

Eriospermum convallariifolium, commonly known as the creeping eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are usually oval to heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Eriospermum convallariifolium is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy tissues to survive dry periods.
