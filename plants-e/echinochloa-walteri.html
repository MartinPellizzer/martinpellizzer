Echinochloa walteri

Echinochloa walteri, commonly known as rough false brome, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, hairy texture.

Echinochloa walteri is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in open, sunny habitats.
