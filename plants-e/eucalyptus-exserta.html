Eucalyptus exserta

Eucalyptus exserta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are usually white or pale pink in color. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
