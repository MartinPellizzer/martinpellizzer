Euphorbia barrelieri

Euphorbia barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant has a unique ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It produces a milky sap that can be toxic and is often used in traditional medicine.

Euphorbia barrelieri is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appearance.
