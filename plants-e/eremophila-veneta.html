Eremophila veneta

Eremophila veneta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in clusters. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for dryland gardens.

Eremophila veneta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
