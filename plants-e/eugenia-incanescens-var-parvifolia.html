Eugenia incanescens var. parvifolia

Eugenia incanescens var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glabrous and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is closely related to other members of the Myrtaceae family, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
