Erigeron pumilus var. condensatus

Erigeron pumilus var. condensatus, commonly known as the denseleaf fleabane, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed, giving the plant its characteristic appearance. The species produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
