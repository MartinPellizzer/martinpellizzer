Embelia microphylla

Embelia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved embelia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
