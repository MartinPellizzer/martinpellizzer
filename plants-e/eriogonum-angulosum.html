Eriogonum angulosum

Eriogonum angulosum, commonly known as the angular buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its angular, often spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments.

Eriogonum angulosum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
