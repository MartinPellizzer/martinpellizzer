Eucalyptus greeniana

Eucalyptus greeniana, commonly known as the green mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a dull green. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have various industrial and medicinal applications.
