Eugeniopsis grandifolia

Eugeniopsis grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
