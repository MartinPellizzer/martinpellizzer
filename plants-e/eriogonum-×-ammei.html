Eriogonum × ammei

Eriogonum × ammei, commonly known as the desert flax, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Eriogonum × ammei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
