Eriosema psoraleoides var. grandiflorum

Eriosema psoraleoides var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a slender, upright stem with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
