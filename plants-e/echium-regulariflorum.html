Echium regulariflorum

Echium regulariflorum, commonly known as the common viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Echium regulariflorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
