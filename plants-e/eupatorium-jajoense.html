Eupatorium jajoense

Eupatorium jajoense, commonly known as the Jamaican boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium jajoense is valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat fevers and respiratory ailments.
