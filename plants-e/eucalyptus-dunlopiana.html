Eucalyptus dunlopiana

Eucalyptus dunlopiana, commonly known as the Dunlop's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee and woodland habitats.

Eucalyptus dunlopiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
