Euphorbia dumetorum

Euphorbia dumetorum, commonly known as the European spurge, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in cyathia. The plant has a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Euphorbiaceae family.
