Epilobium glandulosum var. perplexans

Epilobium glandulosum var. perplexans, commonly known as the woolly willow or sticky willow, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its glandular-studded stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, often wet habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
