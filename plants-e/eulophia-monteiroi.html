Eulophia monteiroi

Eulophia monteiroi, commonly known as the Monteiro's eulophia, is a rare orchid species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss, Eulophia monteiroi is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
