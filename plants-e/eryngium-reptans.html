Eryngium reptans

Eryngium reptans, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray, spiny leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and often forms dense mats, making it a common sight in rocky or dry soils. It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower heads and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Eryngium reptans is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
