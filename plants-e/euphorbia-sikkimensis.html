Euphorbia sikkimensis

Euphorbia sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by colorful bracts, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Euphorbia sikkimensis is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
