Ehretia reticulata

Ehretia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined ehretia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
