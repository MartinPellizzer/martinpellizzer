Eleocharis naumanniana var. naumanniana

Eleocharis naumanniana var. naumanniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and spike-like inflorescences composed of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
