Eurylepis azaleiflora

Eurylepis azaleiflora, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreens that thrive in acidic soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes where it adds both color and texture.
