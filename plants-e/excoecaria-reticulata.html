Excoecaria reticulata

Excoecaria reticulata, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which is the most notable feature, has a thick, purple rind and a segmented, white, juicy flesh. The plant thrives in humid climates and well-drained soils, often found in lowland forests.

It is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its medicinal properties.
