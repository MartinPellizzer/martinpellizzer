Enneapogon flavescens

Enneapogon flavescens, commonly known as yellow false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with yellow or reddish hues. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale yellow to reddish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
