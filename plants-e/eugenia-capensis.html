Eugenia capensis

Eugenia capensis, commonly known as the Cape cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Eugenia capensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and insects.
