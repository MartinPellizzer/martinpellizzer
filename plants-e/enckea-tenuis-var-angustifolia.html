Enckea tenuis var. angustifolia

Enckea tenuis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved enckea, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal attributes.
