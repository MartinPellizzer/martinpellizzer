Eulophia corallorhiziformis

Eulophia corallorhiziformis, commonly known as the coral root orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it dependent on its host for nutrients. It possesses a unique root system that resembles coral, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments.

Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
