Erica tetralix var. lawsoniana

Erica tetralix var. lawsoniana, commonly known as the Lawson's heath, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
