Eugenia pluriflora var. perforata

Eugenia pluriflora var. perforata, commonly known as the perforate guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptic leaves with a distinctive perforation along the midrib, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, yellowish or red fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
