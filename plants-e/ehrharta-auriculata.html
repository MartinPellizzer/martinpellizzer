Ehrharta auriculata

Ehrharta auriculata, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that resemble small ears, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Ehrharta auriculata also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small wildlife species.
