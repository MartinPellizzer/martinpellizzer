Eugenia ponapensis

Eugenia ponapensis, commonly known as the Caroline Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caroline Islands in the Pacific Ocean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly aromatic scent.
