Eupatorium pycnocephaloides var. glandulipes

Eupatorium pycnocephaloides var. glandulipes, commonly known as the glandular-headed eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, glandular-structured inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a herbaceous habit with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, purple to pink flower heads attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
