Ehrharta rehmannii var. filiformis

Ehrharta rehmannii var. filiformis, commonly known as silky lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves that give it a soft, silky appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
