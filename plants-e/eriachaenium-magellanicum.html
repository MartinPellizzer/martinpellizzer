Eriachaenium magellanicum

Eriachaenium magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic sand couch, is a perennial grass native to the southern regions of South America.

It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in the Patagonian region. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
