Echinocereus phoeniceus var. albispinus

Echinocereus phoeniceus var. albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements in xeriscaping.
