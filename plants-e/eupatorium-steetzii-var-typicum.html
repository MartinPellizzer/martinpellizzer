Eupatorium steetzii var. typicum

Eupatorium steetzii var. typicum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
