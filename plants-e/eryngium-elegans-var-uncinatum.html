Eryngium elegans var. uncinatum

Eryngium elegans var. uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked thistle, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has stiff, gray-green foliage with hairy textures, and its stems are often branched and covered in fine spines. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
