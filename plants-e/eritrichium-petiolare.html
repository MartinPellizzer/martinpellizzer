Eritrichium petiolare

Eritrichium petiolare, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with five petals and a prominent central spur. The plant has slender, upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in rock gardens and alpine collections.
