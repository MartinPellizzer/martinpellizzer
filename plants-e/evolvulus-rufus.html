Evolvulus rufus

Evolvulus rufus, commonly known as the redstem morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color.

Evolvulus rufus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
