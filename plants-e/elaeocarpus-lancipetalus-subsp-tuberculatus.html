Elaeocarpus lancipetalus subsp. tuberculatus

Elaeocarpus lancipetalus subsp.

tuberculatus, commonly known as the knob-leaf elaeocarpus, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with prominent tuberculate (knob-like) structures on the underside.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. This plant is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
