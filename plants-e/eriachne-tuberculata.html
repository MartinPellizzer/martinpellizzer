Eriachne tuberculata

Eriachne tuberculata, commonly known as the rough flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy areas, often forming dense tussocks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in panicles. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Eriachne tuberculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
