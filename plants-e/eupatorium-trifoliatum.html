Eupatorium trifoliatum

Eupatorium trifoliatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, three-lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies.

Eupatorium trifoliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
