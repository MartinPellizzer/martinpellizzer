Enneapogon polyphyllus

Enneapogon polyphyllus, commonly known as the many-leafed lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Enneapogon polyphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
