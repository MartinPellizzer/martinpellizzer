Eremophila magnifica subsp. velutina

Eremophila magnifica subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvet bushfire plant, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking red-orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native gardens.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
