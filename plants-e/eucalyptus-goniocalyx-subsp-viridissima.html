Eucalyptus goniocalyx subsp. viridissima

Eucalyptus goniocalyx subsp.

viridissima, commonly known as the green-leafed ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green.

The plant produces small, white or cream-colored flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its fruits are woody capsules that release seeds when mature.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
