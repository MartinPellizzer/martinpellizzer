Espinosa verticillata

Espinosa verticillata, commonly known as the spiral cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of branches, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its structure and growth pattern make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
