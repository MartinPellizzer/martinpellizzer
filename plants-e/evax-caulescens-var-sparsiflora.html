Evax caulescens var. sparsiflora

Evax caulescens var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered evax, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, branching stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in sparse, terminal umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
