Elaeocarpus elatus

Elaeocarpus elatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
