Enneapogon caerulescens var. occidentalis

Enneapogon caerulescens var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western blue grass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which are typical of the genus Enneapogon. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping in its natural range.
