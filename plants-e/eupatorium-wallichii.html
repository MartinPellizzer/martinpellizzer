Eupatorium wallichii

Eupatorium wallichii, commonly known as Himalayan boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Eupatorium wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
