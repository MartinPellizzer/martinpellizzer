Eugenia zuluensis

Eugenia zuluensis, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellowish-orange fruits that are rich in vitamins and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The Natal plum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
