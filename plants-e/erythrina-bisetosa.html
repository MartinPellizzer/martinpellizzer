Erythrina bisetosa

Erythrina bisetosa, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Erythrina bisetosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
