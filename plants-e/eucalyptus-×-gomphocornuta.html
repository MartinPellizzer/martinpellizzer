Eucalyptus × gomphocornuta

Eucalyptus × gomphocornuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub or small tree form, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the spring and summer seasons. Its bark is usually smooth and peels in strips, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique appearance and hardiness.
