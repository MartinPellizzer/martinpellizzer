Eupatorium laevigatum var. tomentosum

Eupatorium laevigatum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, pale blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant's name reflects its rough, woolly texture and its historical use in traditional medicine.
