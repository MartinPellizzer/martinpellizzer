Elleanthus magnicallosus

Elleanthus magnicallosus, commonly known as the grand display lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a tall, slender stem that supports its impressive floral display, often blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Elleanthus magnicallosus is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
