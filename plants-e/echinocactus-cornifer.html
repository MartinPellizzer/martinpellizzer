Echinocactus cornifer

Echinocactus cornifer, commonly known as the cornflower cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stem. Echinocactus cornifer is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
