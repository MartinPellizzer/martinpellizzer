Eugenia glomerata

Eugenia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Eugenia glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
