Eremitis monothalamia

Eremitis monothalamia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has a succulent, tubular stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
