Euterpe cuatrecasasiana

Euterpe cuatrecasasiana, commonly known as the Guianese palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon Basin.

It is a tall, slender palm that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, fibrous fruits containing a single seed.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within the ecosystem and its potential for sustainable use in local communities.
