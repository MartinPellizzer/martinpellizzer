Eupatorium guerreroanum

Eupatorium guerreroanum, commonly known as Guerrero's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and serrated leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are small and clustered, often in white or pale purple hues.

Eupatorium guerreroanum is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments.
