Echinodorus ranunculoides f. zosterifolius

Echinodorus ranunculoides f. zosterifolius, commonly known as the Zosterifolius waterweed, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that give it a unique, grass-like appearance. The plant is often cultivated in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide oxygen and shelter for aquatic life. It thrives in moderate to high light conditions and requires regular fertilization to maintain its vibrant green color.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, making it both a decorative and potentially invasive species in certain environments.
