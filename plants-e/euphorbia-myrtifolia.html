Euphorbia myrtifolia

Euphorbia myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant contains a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin, so caution is advised when handling it. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Euphorbia myrtifolia is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
