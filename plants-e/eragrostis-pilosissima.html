Eragrostis pilosissima

Eragrostis pilosissima, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
