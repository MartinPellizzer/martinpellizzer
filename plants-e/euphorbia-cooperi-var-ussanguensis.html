Euphorbia cooperi var. ussanguensis

Euphorbia cooperi var. ussanguensis, commonly known as the Congo euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to store water in its stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
