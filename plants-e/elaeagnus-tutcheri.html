Elaeagnus tutcheri

Elaeagnus tutcheri, commonly known as the Tutcher's silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Elaeagnus tutcheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
