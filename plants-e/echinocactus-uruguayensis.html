Echinocactus uruguayensis

Echinocactus uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in spines, which provide protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Echinocactus uruguayensis is also known for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
