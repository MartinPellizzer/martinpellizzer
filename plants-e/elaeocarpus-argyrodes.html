Elaeocarpus argyrodes

Elaeocarpus argyrodes, commonly known as the silver-leaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
