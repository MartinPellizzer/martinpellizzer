Eritrichium setosissimum

Eritrichium setosissimum, commonly known as the woolly eritrichium, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features dense, hairy foliage and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and purple. The plant is characterized by its stiff, branched stems and tightly packed leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
