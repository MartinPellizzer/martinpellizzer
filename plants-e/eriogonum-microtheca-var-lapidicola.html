Eriogonum microtheca var. lapidicola

Eriogonum microtheca var. lapidicola, commonly known as the gravel-leafed buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
