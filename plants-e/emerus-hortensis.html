Emerus hortensis

Emerus hortensis, commonly known as the garden emerus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters, typical of the family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
