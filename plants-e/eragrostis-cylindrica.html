Eragrostis cylindrica

Eragrostis cylindrica, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical inflorescence, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eragrostis cylindrica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
