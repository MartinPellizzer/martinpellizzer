Eryngium platyphyllum

Eryngium platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiky, blue-violet flower heads. The plant has basal leaves that are broad and flat, distinguishing it from other eryngium species. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
