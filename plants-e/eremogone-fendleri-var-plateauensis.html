Eremogone fendleri var. plateauensis

Eremogone fendleri var. plateauensis, commonly known as the Sierra lily, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada and other mountain ranges in western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
