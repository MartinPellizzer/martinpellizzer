Elytrigia repens var. caesia

Elytrigia repens var. caesia, commonly known as purple meadowgrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
