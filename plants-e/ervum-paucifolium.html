Ervum paucifolium

Ervum paucifolium, commonly known as the few-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a trailing growth habit and produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
