Elionurus platypus

Elionurus platypus, commonly known as the broad-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically broad and flat, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Elionurus platypus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
