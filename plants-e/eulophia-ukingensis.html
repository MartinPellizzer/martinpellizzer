Eulophia ukingensis

Eulophia ukingensis, commonly known as the Ukinge orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow and green coloration. Eulophia ukingensis thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
