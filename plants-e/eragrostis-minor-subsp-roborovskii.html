Eragrostis minor subsp. roborovskii

Eragrostis minor subsp.

roborovskii, commonly known as Roborovskii grass, is a perennial grass species native to central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
