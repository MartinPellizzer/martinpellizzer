Elaeocarpus davisii

Elaeocarpus davisii, commonly known as the Davis's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
