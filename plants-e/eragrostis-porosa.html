Eragrostis porosa

Eragrostis porosa, commonly known as slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery inflorescences and fine, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
