Elaeis odora

Elaeis odora, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that contains a high amount of oil. The plant's fruit develops in a large, spiky bunch and matures over several months. Elaeis odora is cultivated for its oil, which is used in food, cosmetics, and biofuel production.

Its growth is typically found in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
