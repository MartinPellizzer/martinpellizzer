Erigeron asperulus

Erigeron asperulus, commonly known as rough blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are small, daisy-like, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer.

Erigeron asperulus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of wetland ecosystems.
