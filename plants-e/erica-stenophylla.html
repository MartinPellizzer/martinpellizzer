Erica stenophylla

Erica stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. Erica stenophylla thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
