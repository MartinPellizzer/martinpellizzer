Ericoides irregulare

Ericoides irregulare, commonly known as the irregular ericoid, is a unique plant species belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense shrubs. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species in its native range.
