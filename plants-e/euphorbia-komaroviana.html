Euphorbia komaroviana

Euphorbia komaroviana, commonly known as the Komarov's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Central and South America.

It features a thick, fleshy stem with spines and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
