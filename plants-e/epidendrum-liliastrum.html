Epidendrum liliastrum

Epidendrum liliastrum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble a pink slipper, with a pouch-like lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The orchid produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
