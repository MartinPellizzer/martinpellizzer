Elatine paludosa var. octandra

Elatine paludosa var. octandra, commonly known as the eight-angled waterwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant found in moist habitats such as marshes and wet meadows.

It belongs to the family Elatinaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at water level. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, and it is often found in temperate regions.

This variant is distinguished by its eight-angled stem, which gives it its specific epithet "octandra.".
