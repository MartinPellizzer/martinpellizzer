Eulophia ochyrae

Eulophia ochyrae, commonly known as the orange-tinged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive orange and yellow coloration. The plant typically blooms in summer, with its inflorescence bearing several clustered flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eulophia ochyrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
