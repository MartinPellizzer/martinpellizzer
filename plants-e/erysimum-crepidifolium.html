Erysimum crepidifolium

Erysimum crepidifolium, commonly known as the rockcress or yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single cluster at the end of each stem, and the species is known for its resilience to cold and arid conditions.
