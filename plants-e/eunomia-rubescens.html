Eunomia rubescens

Eunomia rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson, depending on the variety. The plant has a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Eunomia rubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
