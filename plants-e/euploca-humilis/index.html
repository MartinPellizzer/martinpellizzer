Euploca Humilis

Euploca humilis is a small, flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes. This species is known for its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts. Its unique adaptations make it an important part of its local ecosystem.
