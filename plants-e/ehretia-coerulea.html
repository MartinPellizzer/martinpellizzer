Ehretia coerulea

Ehretia coerulea, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
