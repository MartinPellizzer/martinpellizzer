Eritrichium latifolium f. umbrosum

Eritrichium latifolium f. umbrosum, commonly known as the shade-loving eritrichium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

This fern-like perennial is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed and often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle floral display to shaded woodland environments. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a unique addition to shady garden settings.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in some regions.
