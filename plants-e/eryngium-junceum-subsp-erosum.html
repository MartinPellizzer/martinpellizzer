Eryngium junceum subsp. erosum

Eryngium junceum subsp.

erosum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
