Equilabium mafiense

Equilabium mafiense, commonly known as the Mafiense bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
