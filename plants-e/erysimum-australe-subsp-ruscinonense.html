Erysimum australe subsp. ruscinonense

Erysimum australe subsp.

ruscinonense, commonly known as the coastal wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils, showing adaptability to saline and well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
