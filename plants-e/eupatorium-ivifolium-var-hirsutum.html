Eupatorium ivifolium var. hirsutum

Eupatorium ivifolium var. hirsutum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the eastern United States. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal uses by indigenous peoples.
