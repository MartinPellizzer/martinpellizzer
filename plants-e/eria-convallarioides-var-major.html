Eria convallarioides var. major

Eria convallarioides var. major, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red when mature, containing seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
