Eulophia carinata

Eulophia carinata, commonly known as the ridge-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and fleshy roots. Its leaves are elongated and often have a distinctive ridge or carina, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a short spur and are arranged in a loose raceme.
