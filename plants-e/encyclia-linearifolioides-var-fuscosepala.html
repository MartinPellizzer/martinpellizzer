Encyclia linearifolioides var. fuscosepala

Encyclia linearifolioides var. fuscosepala, commonly known as the dark-sepaled orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and pendant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with brownish sepals, and it produces a single, elongated spike of blooms.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
