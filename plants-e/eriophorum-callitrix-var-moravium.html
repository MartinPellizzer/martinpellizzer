Eriophorum callitrix var. moravium

Eriophorum callitrix var. moravium, commonly known as the Moravian tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and other moist habitats, often forming dense tussocks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a distinctive, clustered growth habit.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
