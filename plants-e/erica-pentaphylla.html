Erica pentaphylla

Erica pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of pale pink or white flowers, typically blooming in spring.

Erica pentaphylla thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
