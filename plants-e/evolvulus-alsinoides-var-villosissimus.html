Evolvulus alsinoides var. villosissimus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. villosissimus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
