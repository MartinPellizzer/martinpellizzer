Elcismia parva

Elcismia parva, commonly known as the dwarf elcismia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its flowering period usually occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Elcismia parva is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
