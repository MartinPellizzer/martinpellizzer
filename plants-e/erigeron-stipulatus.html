Erigeron stipulatus

Erigeron stipulatus, commonly known as the stippled blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a stipulate base, giving it its scientific name.

The flowers are blue to purple in color and bloom in the summer months.
