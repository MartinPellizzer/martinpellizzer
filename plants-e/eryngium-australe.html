Eryngium australe

Eryngium australe, commonly known as the Australian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by a series of bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eryngium australe is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
