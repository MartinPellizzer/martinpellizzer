Eremophila spectabilis subsp. spectabilis

Eremophila spectabilis subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the showy emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces striking spikes of tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient addition to native gardens.
