Eritrichium glareosum

Eritrichium glareosum, commonly known as the alpine erinoid, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant thrives in cold, dry environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are adapted to pollination by wind or insects.

Eritrichium glareosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
