Eugenia saviifolia

Eugenia saviifolia, commonly known as the Savi's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. After flowering, it produces small, round berries that are typically reddish in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
