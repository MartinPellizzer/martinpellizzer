Eritrichium rigidum

Eritrichium rigidum, commonly known as rigid hoary vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, hairy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Eritrichium rigidum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
