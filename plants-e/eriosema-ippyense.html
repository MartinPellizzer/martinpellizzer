Eriosema ippyense

Eriosema ippyense, commonly known as the Natal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Eriosema ippyense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
