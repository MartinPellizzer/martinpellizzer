Eugenia kochiana

Eugenia kochiana, commonly known as the Java apple or purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple, segmented fruit that resembles a peach. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is valued for its aromatic, edible pulp and has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant's ornamental value and unique fruit make it a popular choice for cultivation in warm climates.
