Eupatorium sagittiferum

Eupatorium sagittiferum, commonly known as arrowleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, adding visual interest to gardens and natural landscapes.
