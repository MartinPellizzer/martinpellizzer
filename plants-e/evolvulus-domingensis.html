Evolvulus domingensis

Evolvulus domingensis, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing habits.

Evolvulus domingensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
