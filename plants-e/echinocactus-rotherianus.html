Echinocactus rotherianus

Echinocactus rotherianus, commonly known as the Rotherian hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, sharp spines that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
