Embelia rudis

Embelia rudis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is valued for its medicinal and nutritional properties.
