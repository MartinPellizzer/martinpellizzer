Eugenia malagsam

Eugenia malagsam, commonly known as the white guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp, often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant scent when crushed.

Eugenia malagsam is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
