Equisetum curvifolium

Equisetum curvifolium, commonly known as the curly-leaf scouring rush, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the order Equisetales.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and a distinctive, hollow stem that lacks true leaves. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and along stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called strobila, are located at the apex of the stem.

Equisetum curvifolium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
