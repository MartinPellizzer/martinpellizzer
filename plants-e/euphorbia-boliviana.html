Euphorbia boliviana

Euphorbia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian spurge, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

Euphorbia boliviana is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and cause skin irritation.
