Eria javanica

Eria javanica, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces erect, hairy stems with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually small, clustered in inflorescences, and come in shades of white, yellow, and green.

Eria javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
