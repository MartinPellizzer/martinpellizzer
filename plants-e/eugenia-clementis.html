Eugenia clementis

Eugenia clementis, commonly known as the Java apple or clementine guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. The fruit has a thin, smooth skin and a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.
