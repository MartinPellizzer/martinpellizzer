Eriocaulon vanheurckii f. minimum

Eriocaulon vanheurckii f. minimum, commonly known as the dwarf marsh plant, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
