Echthronema tenuifolia

Echthronema tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length, and it produces small, white or pale green flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while photosynthesis occurs through its own leaves. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in areas with high humidity and warm climates.

Its unique parasitic nature and delicate appearance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
