Elaeocarpus lepidus

Elaeocarpus lepidus, commonly known as the glossy elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a smooth texture.

The glossy elaeocarp is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
