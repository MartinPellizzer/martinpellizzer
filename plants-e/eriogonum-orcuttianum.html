Eriogonum orcuttianum

Eriogonum orcuttianum, commonly known as the Orcutt's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Eriogonum orcuttianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
