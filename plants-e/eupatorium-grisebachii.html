Eupatorium grisebachii

Eupatorium grisebachii, commonly known as Gray's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium grisebachii is known for its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported fever-reducing properties.
