Eupatorium pseudodalea var. typicum

Eupatorium pseudodalea var. typicum, commonly known as swamp boneset, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
