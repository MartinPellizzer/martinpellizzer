Euphorbia pyrifolia var. pyrifolia

Euphorbia pyrifolia var. pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers surrounded by red bracts, creating a dramatic visual display. It is adapted to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
