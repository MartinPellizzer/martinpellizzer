Eriochloa gracilis

Eriochloa gracilis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, white to light green flowers.

Eriochloa gracilis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
