Eriogonum hieraciifolium var. hemipterum

Eriogonum hieraciifolium var. hemipterum, commonly known as the Townsend's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
