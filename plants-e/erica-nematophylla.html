Erica nematophylla

Erica nematophylla, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is adapted to the humid, misty conditions of its coastal habitat. Erica nematophylla is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and trees adapted to rocky or sandy environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
