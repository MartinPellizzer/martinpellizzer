Eragrostis megastachya subsp. pooides

Eragrostis megastachya subsp.

pooides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and panicle-shaped inflorescence that produces small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This subspecies thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense, low-growing clumps.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
