Echites venenosus

Echites venenosus, commonly known as the death adder, is a venomous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded head and short, stocky body. The plant contains a potent neurotoxic venom that is used for hunting prey. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often with a yellow or greenish hue.

Despite its dangerous reputation, it plays an important role in the ecosystem as both predator and prey.
