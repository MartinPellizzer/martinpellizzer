Eremolimon botschantzevii

Eremolimon botschantzevii, commonly known as the Karakum sand thorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access underground moisture.

Eremolimon botschantzevii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
