Eulophia streptopetala

Eulophia streptopetala, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and pseudobulbs that support its leafy structure. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves and has fragrant, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a raceme. Its floral structure is characteristic of the Orchidaceae family, with three sepals and three petals, often with a showy lip.

Eulophia streptopetala thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
