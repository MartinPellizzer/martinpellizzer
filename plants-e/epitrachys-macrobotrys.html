Epitrachys macrobotrys

Epitrachys macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, making it one of the largest orchids in the world. The flowers are typically white or pale pink with intricate patterns, and they bloom in clusters.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
