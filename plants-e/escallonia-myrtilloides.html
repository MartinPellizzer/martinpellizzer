Escallonia myrtilloides

Escallonia myrtilloides, commonly known as the purple-flowered escallonia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Escallonia myrtilloides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
