Echinocactus acuatus var. arechavaletae

Echinocactus acuatus var. arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta's hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
