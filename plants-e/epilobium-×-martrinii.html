Epilobium × martrinii

Epilobium × martrinii, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Epilobium × martrinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
