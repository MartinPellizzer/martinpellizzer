Eupatorium tarapotense

Eupatorium tarapotense, commonly known as the Tarapotense boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads.

Eupatorium tarapotense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
