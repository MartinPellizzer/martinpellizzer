Escallonia punctata

Escallonia punctata, commonly known as the spotted escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance on the leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Escallonia punctata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to coastal and Mediterranean climates.
