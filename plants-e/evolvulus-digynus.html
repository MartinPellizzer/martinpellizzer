Evolvulus digynus

Evolvulus digynus, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, opening in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and somewhat hairy.

Evolvulus digynus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth patterns.
