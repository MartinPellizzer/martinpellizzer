Eupatorium phlebodes

Eupatorium phlebodes, commonly known as blue boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its name "blue boneset" refers to the blue color of its flowers and its use in traditional medicine for treating ailments.

Eupatorium phlebodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
