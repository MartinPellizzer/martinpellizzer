Eragrostis latifolia

Eragrostis latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

Eragrostis latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
