Eremostachys transoxana

Eremostachys transoxana, commonly known as the Transoxiana eremostachys, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with gray-green foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
