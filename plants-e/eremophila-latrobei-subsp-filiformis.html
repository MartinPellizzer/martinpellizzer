Eremophila latrobei subsp. filiformis

Eremophila latrobei subsp.

filiformis, commonly known as the slender eremophila, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
