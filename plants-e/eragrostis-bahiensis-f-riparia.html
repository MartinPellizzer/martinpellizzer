Eragrostis bahiensis f. riparia

Eragrostis bahiensis f. riparia, commonly known as riparian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the seeds are small and dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
