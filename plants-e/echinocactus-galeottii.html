Echinocactus galeottii

Echinocactus galeottii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of bright yellow spines. The plant has a thick, water-storing stem that allows it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are typically yellow or orange and bloom at the top of the plant.

Echinocactus galeottii is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
