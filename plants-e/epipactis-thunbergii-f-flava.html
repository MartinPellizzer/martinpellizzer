Epipactis thunbergii f. flava

Epipactis thunbergii f. flava, commonly known as the yellow-flowered helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with its dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It produces a single, erect flowering stem that bears several flowers arranged in a spiral.

This orchid is known for its complex pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
