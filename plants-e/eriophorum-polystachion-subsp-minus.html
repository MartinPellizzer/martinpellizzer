Eriophorum polystachion subsp. minus

Eriophorum polystachion subsp.

minus, commonly known as the lesser tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as bogs, fens, and marshes.

The plant is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous spikelets. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with short, stiff hairs.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
