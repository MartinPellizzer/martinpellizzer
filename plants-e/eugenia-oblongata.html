Eugenia oblongata

Eugenia oblongata, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Eugenia oblongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
