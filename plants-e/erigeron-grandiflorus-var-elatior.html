Erigeron grandiflorus var. elatior

Erigeron grandiflorus var. elatior, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray florets and disc florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
