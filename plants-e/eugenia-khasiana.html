Eugenia khasiana

Eugenia khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Eugenia khasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
