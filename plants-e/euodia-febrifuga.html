Euodia febrifuga

Euodia febrifuga, commonly known as Chinese prickly ash, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported antipyretic and analgesic properties.

The bark and fruits are often used in herbal remedies, though its botanical uses are less widely studied compared to other members of its family.
