Eupatorium conyzoides var. affine

Eupatorium conyzoides var. affine, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
