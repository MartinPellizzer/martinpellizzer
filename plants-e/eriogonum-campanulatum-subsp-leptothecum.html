Eriogonum campanulatum subsp. leptothecum

Eriogonum campanulatum subsp.

leptothecum, commonly known as the slender-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
