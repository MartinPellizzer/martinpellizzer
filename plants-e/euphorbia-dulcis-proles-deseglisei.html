Euphorbia dulcis proles deseglisei

Euphorbia dulcis proles deseglisei, commonly known as the sweet euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small scales, and it is known for its ability to form dense clumps over time.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
