Etlingera velutina subsp. longipedunculata

Etlingera velutina subsp.

longipedunculata, commonly known as the hairy torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that resemble torches, which bloom in dense inflorescences.

The plant has velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in humid, shady environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated peduncles, which support the clustered floral display.
