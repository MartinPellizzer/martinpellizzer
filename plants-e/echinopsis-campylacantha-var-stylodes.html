Echinopsis campylacantha var. stylodes

Echinopsis campylacantha var. stylodes, commonly known as the "Lace Flower Cactus," is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces spines that are typically short and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
