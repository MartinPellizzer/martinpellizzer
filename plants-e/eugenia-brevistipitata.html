Eugenia brevistipitata

Eugenia brevistipitata, commonly known as the short-stipitate eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, dark purple berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
