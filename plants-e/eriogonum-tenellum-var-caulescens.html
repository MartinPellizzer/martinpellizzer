Eriogonum tenellum var. caulescens

Eriogonum tenellum var. caulescens, commonly known as the slenderleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous form with slender, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
