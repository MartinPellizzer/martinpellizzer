Eupatorium paucicapitulatum

Eupatorium paucicapitulatum, commonly known as the few-headed thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small, clustered white to pale purple flowers.

Eupatorium paucicapitulatum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
