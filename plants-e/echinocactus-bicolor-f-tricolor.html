Echinocactus bicolor f. tricolor

Echinocactus bicolor f. tricolor, commonly known as the tri-colored hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking variant of the Echinocactus genus.

It is native to the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in well-draining soils and full sun exposure. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored spines, which can display shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its stems are typically cylindrical, with a ribbed structure that helps it store water.

This unique cactus is prized by collectors for its ornamental value and distinctive appearance.
