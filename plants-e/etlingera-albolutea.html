Etlingera albolutea

Etlingera albolutea, commonly known as the white-flowered torch ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking white flowers that resemble torches. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are also utilized in culinary applications, particularly in Southeast Asian cuisines.

Etlingera albolutea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
