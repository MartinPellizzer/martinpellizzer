Erophila elata

Erophila elata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water millet, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or damp soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. Its seeds are dispersed by water, contributing to its ability to colonize wetland areas.

Erophila elata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
