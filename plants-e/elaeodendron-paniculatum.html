Elaeodendron paniculatum

Elaeodendron paniculatum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble plumes, composed of numerous small, bright red or orange flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its trunk is often covered in a thick, rough bark. It thrives in full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.

Elaeodendron paniculatum is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
