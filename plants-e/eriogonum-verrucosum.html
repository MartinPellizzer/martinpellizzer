Eriogonum verrucosum

Eriogonum verrucosum, commonly known as the woolly buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It has adapted to arid environments with a deep root system and drought-resistant features.

Eriogonum verrucosum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
