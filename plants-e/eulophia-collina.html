Eulophia collina

Eulophia collina, commonly known as the mountain eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant has a pseudobulbous stem that supports its evergreen leaves, which are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Eulophia collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
