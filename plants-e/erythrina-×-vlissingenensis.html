Erythrina × vlissingenensis

Erythrina × vlissingenensis, commonly known as the hybrid flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often thorned. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable environments.
