Eulophia stuhlmannii

Eulophia stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Eulophia stuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
