Eucalyptus brachypoda

Eucalyptus brachypoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and small, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or yellow-green in hue. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus brachypoda plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
