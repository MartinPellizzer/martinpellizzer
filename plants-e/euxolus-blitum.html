Euxolus blitum

Euxolus blitum, commonly known as the common bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic prey. The plant has finely divided leaves and is typically found in slow-moving or still water. It thrives in nutrient-poor conditions, relying on capturing prey to supplement its nutrient intake.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studying plant adaptations to aquatic habitats.
