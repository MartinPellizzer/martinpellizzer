Euphorbia esula subsp. pseudovirgata

Euphorbia esula subsp.

pseudovirgata, commonly known as the Canadian tansy, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters.

The plant has a milky latex sap that can cause skin irritation. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
