Erigeron flagellaris f. breviligulatus

Erigeron flagellaris f. breviligulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fleabane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically white to pale blue, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often found in open habitats like meadows and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun.
