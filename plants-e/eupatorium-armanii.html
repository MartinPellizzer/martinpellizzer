Eupatorium armanii

Eupatorium armanii, commonly known as Armenian boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and is known for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the flora of its native region.
