Eleocharis savatieri

Eleocharis savatieri, commonly known as the swamp arrowgrass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, a common reproductive strategy in wetland plants. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Eleocharis savatieri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to improve water quality and support biodiversity.
