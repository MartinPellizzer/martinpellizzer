Euphorbia glyptosperma var. pubescens

Euphorbia glyptosperma var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and green, typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
