Emilia gracilis

Emilia gracilis, commonly known as the slender emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the upper surface. Emilia gracilis is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.

It is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
