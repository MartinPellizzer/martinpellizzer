Eriocephalus spinescens

Eriocephalus spinescens, commonly known as the Spiny Bushveld Thistle, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native environment.
