Erigeron aliceae

Erigeron aliceae, commonly known as Alice's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

Erigeron aliceae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
