Eugenia fragrans var. fajardensis

Eugenia fragrans var. fajardensis, commonly known as the Fajardensis clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the colorful flowers and fruits.
