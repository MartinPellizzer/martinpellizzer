Eupatorium humile f. erubescens

Eupatorium humile f. erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that turn reddish-purple with age, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in white to pale blue hues, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
