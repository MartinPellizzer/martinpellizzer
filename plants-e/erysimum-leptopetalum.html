Erysimum leptopetalum

Erysimum leptopetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in coastal areas and alpine regions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, round pods containing seeds.

Erysimum leptopetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
