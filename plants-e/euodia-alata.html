Euodia alata

Euodia alata, commonly known as the winged euodia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small white flowers. The plant's leaves are elliptical with distinctive wing-like structures along the edges, giving it its common name. Euodia alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its fruit, though not widely consumed, contains essential oils that have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
