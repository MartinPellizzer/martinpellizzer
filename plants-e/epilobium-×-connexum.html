Epilobium × connexum

Epilobium × connexum, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a hybrid species within the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and opposite leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes.

Epilobium × connexum is an annual or biennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
