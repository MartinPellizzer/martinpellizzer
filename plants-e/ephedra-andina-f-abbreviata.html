Ephedra andina f. abbreviata

Ephedra andina f. abbreviata, commonly known as the Andean ephedra, is a shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and compact, branched stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used by indigenous communities for various therapeutic purposes.
