Eucomis sonnetteana

Eucomis sonnetteana, commonly known as the Whitehead's pineapple flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiky, pineapple-like inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Eucomis sonnetteana is relatively low-maintenance and blooms in late summer to early autumn.
