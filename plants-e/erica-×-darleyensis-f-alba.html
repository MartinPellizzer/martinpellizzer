Erica × darleyensis f. alba

Erica × darleyensis f. alba, commonly known as the White Heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blooms that appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide year-round interest.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
