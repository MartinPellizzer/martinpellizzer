Elaeocarpus largiflorens

Elaeocarpus largiflorens, commonly known as the large-flowered elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red or orange fruits that are often edible and attract various birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
