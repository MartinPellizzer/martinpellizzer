Epilobium roseum var. indicum

Epilobium roseum var. indicum, commonly known as the Indian willow or Indian rosebay, is a herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
