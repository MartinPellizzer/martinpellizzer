Eroteum suberosum

Eroteum suberosum, commonly known as the rough-leaved eroteum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowering period usually occurs during the cooler months, and it is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Eroteum suberosum is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental foliage.
