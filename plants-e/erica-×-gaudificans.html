Erica × gaudificans

Erica × gaudificans, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and often used in rock gardens or as a landscape plant due to its ornamental value.

Erica × gaudificans is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
