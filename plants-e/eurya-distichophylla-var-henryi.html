Eurya distichophylla var. henryi

Eurya distichophylla var. henryi, commonly known as Henry's two-ranked eurya, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
