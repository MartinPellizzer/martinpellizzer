Echinochloa crus-galli var. praticola

Echinochloa crus-galli var. praticola, commonly known as common barnyardgrass, is an annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, narrow leaves, and spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple at maturity. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is well-adapted to a wide range of soil types and environmental conditions. It is considered a major weed in agricultural fields, particularly in rice and corn crops.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a challenging weed to manage in cultivated ecosystems.
