Eriophorum gracile var. caurianum

Eriophorum gracile var. caurianum, commonly known as the slender cotton grass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to northern regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silky white flowering heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
