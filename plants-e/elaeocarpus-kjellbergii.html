Elaeocarpus kjellbergii

Elaeocarpus kjellbergii, commonly known as the Kjellberg's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically bird-dispersed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
