Eupritchardia martii

Eupritchardia martii, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the high elevations of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth, long lifespan, and distinctive needle-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
