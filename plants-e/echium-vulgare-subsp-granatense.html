Echium vulgare subsp. granatense

Echium vulgare subsp.

granatense, commonly known as the Granate Thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer, and its spiny, lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland or wastelands. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, known for its hairy stems and showy flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
