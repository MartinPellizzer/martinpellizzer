Erigeron andryaloides

Erigeron andryaloides, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
