Elymus chubutensis

Elymus chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated. Elymus chubutensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
