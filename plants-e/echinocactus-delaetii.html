Echinocactus delaetii

Echinocactus delaetii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of bright yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the plant. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
