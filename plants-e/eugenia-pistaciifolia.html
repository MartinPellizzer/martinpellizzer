Eugenia pistaciifolia

Eugenia pistaciifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Eugenia pistaciifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
