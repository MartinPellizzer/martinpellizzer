Eugenia rufofulva

Eugenia rufofulva, commonly known as the red-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, reddish-brown leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible, dark purple berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia rufofulva is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
