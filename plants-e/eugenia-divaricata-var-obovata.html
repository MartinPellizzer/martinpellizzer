Eugenia divaricata var. obovata

Eugenia divaricata var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed satinwood, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, characterized by its glossy, obovate leaves that are often dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, aromatic berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

The wood of this species is also noted for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in carpentry and furniture-making.
