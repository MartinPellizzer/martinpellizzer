Evolvulus sericeus f. erecta

Evolvulus sericeus f. erecta, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to white in color. The plant has hairy, silver-tomentose leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
