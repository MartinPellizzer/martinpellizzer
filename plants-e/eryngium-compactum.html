Eryngium compactum

Eryngium compactum, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by silver-gray bracts. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes.
