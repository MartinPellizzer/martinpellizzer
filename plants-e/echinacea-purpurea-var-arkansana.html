Echinacea purpurea var. arkansana

Echinacea purpurea var. arkansana, commonly known as the Arkansas coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 to 3 feet tall, supporting its showy, purple-pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces distinctive, spiky yellow-orange central cones. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
