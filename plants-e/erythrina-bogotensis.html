Erythrina bogotensis

Erythrina bogotensis, commonly known as the Bogotá erythrina, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Erythrina bogotensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
