Erythroxylum zambesiacum

Erythroxylum zambesiacum, commonly known as the Zambesia copal tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for various purposes, including as a chewing gum. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The species is valued for its ecological role and potential economic applications.
