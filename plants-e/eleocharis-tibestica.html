Eleocharis tibestica

Eleocharis tibestica, commonly known as the Tibesti sedge, is a flowering plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibesti Mountains in Chad.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Eleocharis tibestica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its restricted range.
