Euonymus armasicus

Euonymus armasicus, commonly known as the Armenian spindle-tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Euonymus armasicus is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
