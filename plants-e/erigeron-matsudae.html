Erigeron matsudae

Erigeron matsudae, commonly known as the Matsuda's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Erigeron matsudae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
