Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. acroacanthus

Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. acroacanthus, commonly known as the spiny-crested hedgehog cactus, is a unique cactus species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged foliage. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
