Eucalyptus × rigida var. luehmanniana

Eucalyptus × rigida var. luehmanniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers, which appear during the winter months. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
