Ellisia racemosa

Ellisia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
