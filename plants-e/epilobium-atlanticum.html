Epilobium atlanticum

Epilobium atlanticum, commonly known as Atlantic willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along shorelines and dunes. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. It produces clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
