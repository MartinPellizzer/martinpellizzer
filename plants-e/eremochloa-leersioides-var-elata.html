Eremochloa leersioides var. elata

Eremochloa leersioides var. elata, commonly known as the tall eremochloa, is a grass species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
