Erythroxylum latifolium var. longipetiolatum

Erythroxylum latifolium var. longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled broadleaf erythroxylum, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by elliptical berries containing toxic seeds. It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family, which includes other plants with similar morphological features.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical regions.
