Euphorbia splendens var. betsileana

Euphorbia splendens var. betsileana, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often segmented and can grow in a branching or shrub-like form. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and may be arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in bright, indirect light and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
