Echites ciliatus

Echites ciliatus, commonly known as the silky spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
