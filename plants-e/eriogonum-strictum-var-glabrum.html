Eriogonum strictum var. glabrum

Eriogonum strictum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smoothleaf globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a slender, upright stem covered with small, glandular hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The showy yellow flowers are clustered in globular inflorescences, blooming in early summer.
