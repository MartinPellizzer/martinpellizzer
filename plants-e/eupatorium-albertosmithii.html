Eupatorium albertosmithii

Eupatorium albertosmithii, commonly known as Albert Smith's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is known for its aromatic foliage. It produces fluffy, spherical seed heads that are distinctive and often mistaken for small mushrooms.

Eupatorium albertosmithii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
