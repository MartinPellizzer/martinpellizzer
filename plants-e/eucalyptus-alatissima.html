Eucalyptus alatissima

Eucalyptus alatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue, which attract various pollinators. Eucalyptus alatissima is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its wood is hard and heavy, making it valuable for construction and fuel.
