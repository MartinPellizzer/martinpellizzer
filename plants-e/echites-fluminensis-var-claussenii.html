Echites fluminensis var. claussenii

Echites fluminensis var. claussenii, commonly known as the Brazilian spider plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features long, arching stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
