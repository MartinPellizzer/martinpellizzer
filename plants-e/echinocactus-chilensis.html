Echinocactus chilensis

Echinocactus chilensis, commonly known as the fish spine cactus, is a species native to central and southern Mexico.

It is a slow-growing cactus that typically reaches a height of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features thick, ribbed stems covered in prominent, spiny tubercles. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring.

It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a shallow root system.
