Echinocactus kunzei var. brevispinosus

Echinocactus kunzei var. brevispinosus, commonly known as the short-spined hedgehog cactus, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white to pale blue spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
