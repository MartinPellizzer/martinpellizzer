Espeletia × cuniculorum

Espeletia × cuniculorum, commonly known as the "Potosi flower," is a hybrid plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, daisy-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to cold, dry environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
