Echinochloa colonum

Echinochloa colonum, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is an annual weed characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as fields, marshes, and along waterways. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Echinochloa colonum is often considered a problematic weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
