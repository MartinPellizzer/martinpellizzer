Eupatorium galeottii

Eupatorium galeottii, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and features showy, clustered white to pinkish flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators.

Eupatorium galeottii plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems and is a valuable plant for wetland restoration projects.
