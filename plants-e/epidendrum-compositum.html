Epidendrum compositum

Epidendrum compositum, commonly known as the composite epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hair. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain healthy growth.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
