Erythrina burana

Erythrina burana, commonly known as the Burana torchwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its woody structure and showy inflorescences make it a popular ornamental plant.

Erythrina burana also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
