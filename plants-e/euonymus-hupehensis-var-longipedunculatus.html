Euonymus hupehensis var. longipedunculatus

Euonymus hupehensis var. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often display a distinctive red or purple vein pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

This ornamental plant is popular in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
