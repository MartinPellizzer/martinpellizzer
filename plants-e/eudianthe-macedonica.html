Eudianthe macedonica

Eudianthe macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Eudianthe macedonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
