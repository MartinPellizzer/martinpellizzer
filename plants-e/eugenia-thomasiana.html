Eugenia thomasiana

Eugenia thomasiana, commonly known as the Thomasiana myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Eugenia thomasiana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
