Euphorbia laciniata

Euphorbia laciniata, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are typically narrow and lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Euphorbia laciniata is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
