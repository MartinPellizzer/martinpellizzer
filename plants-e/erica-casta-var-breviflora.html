Erica casta var. breviflora

Erica casta var. breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf heather, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the western coast of South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in tight, compact sprays. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners in mild maritime climates.
