Eugenia hermannii

Eugenia hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann's eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia hermannii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
