Emerus sesban var. aculeata

Emerus sesban var. aculeata, commonly known as the spiny sesban, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
