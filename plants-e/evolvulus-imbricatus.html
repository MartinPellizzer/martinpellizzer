Evolvulus imbricatus

Evolvulus imbricatus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant is native to the Americas and thrives in warm, sunny environments. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
