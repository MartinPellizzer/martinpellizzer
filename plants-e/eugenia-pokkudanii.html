Eugenia pokkudanii

Eugenia pokkudanii, commonly known as the Kerala myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
