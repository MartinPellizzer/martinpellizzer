Erysimum pulchellum var. microphyllum

Erysimum pulchellum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features compact, hairy stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
