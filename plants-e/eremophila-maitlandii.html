Eremophila maitlandii

Eremophila maitlandii, commonly known as the Maitland's emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance and efficient water use.

Eremophila maitlandii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
