Ephedra chengiae var. chengiae

Ephedra chengiae var. chengiae, commonly known as the Cheng's ephedra, is a member of the Ephedraceae family and is native to China.

It is a small, branched shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, green to yellowish flowers and has distinctive, scale-like leaves. Its stems are woody and contain alkaloids, such as ephedrine, which have medicinal uses.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
