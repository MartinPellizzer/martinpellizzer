Eugenia dawei

Eugenia dawei, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or reddish when mature. The fruit pulp is sour and contains astringent properties, making it useful in traditional medicine.

It is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
