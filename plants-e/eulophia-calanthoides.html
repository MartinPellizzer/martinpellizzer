Eulophia calanthoides

Eulophia calanthoides, commonly known as the slender eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on the forest floor or along stream banks. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and several leafy shoots emerging from it. Its flowers are usually small and pale green to yellowish, arranged in a raceme, and are often fragrant.

Eulophia calanthoides is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
