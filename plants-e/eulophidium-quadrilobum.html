Eulophidium quadrilobum

Eulophidium quadrilobum, commonly known as the four-lobed grasshopper bean, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with four lobes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
