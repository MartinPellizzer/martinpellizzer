Eugenia cisplatensis

Eugenia cisplatensis, commonly known as the Southern Wax Tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina, Brazil, and Uruguay. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
