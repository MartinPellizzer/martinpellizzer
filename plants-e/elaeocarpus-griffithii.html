Elaeocarpus griffithii

Elaeocarpus griffithii, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and carving.

Elaeocarpus griffithii typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in lowland forests and is often found in shaded environments.
