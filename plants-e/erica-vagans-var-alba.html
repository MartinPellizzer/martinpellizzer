Erica vagans var. alba

Erica vagans var. alba, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Erica vagans var. alba is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
