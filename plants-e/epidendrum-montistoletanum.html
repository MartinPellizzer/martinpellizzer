Epidendrum montistoletanum

Epidendrum montistoletanum, commonly known as the Montistoletan Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum montistoletanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
