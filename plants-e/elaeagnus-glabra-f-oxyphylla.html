Elaeagnus glabra f. oxyphylla

Elaeagnus glabra f. oxyphylla, commonly known as silverleaf elaeagnus, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive lanceolate shape.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
