Erica cryptoclada

Erica cryptoclada, commonly known as the cryptic heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer. Erica cryptoclada is well adapted to harsh, rocky environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are densely packed, providing a mat-like appearance.
