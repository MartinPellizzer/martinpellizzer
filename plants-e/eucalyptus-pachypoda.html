Eucalyptus pachypoda

Eucalyptus pachypoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus pachypoda is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
