Eupatorium wageneri

Eupatorium wageneri, commonly known as the Florida bluetail, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and pine flatwoods. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium wageneri is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
