Erica umbellifera

Erica umbellifera, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Erica umbellifera thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
