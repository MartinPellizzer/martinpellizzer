Eriodictyon glutinosum

Eriodictyon glutinosum, commonly known as California wild tea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and inland regions, characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and flowers contain compounds that have been historically used by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.

The plant's unique botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
