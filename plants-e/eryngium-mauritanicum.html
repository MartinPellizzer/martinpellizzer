Eryngium mauritanicum

Eryngium mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritania thistle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads that resemble a thistle, surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Eryngium mauritanicum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and hardy nature.
