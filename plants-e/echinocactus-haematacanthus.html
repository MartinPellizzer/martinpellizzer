Echinocactus haematacanthus

Echinocactus haematacanthus, commonly known as the red-hair cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its spines are typically reddish-brown, giving it its common name, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

Echinocactus haematacanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
