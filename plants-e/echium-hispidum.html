Echium hispidum

Echium hispidum, commonly known as the hispid fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Echium hispidum is often found in rocky soils and sunny habitats, making it a hardy and resilient species.
