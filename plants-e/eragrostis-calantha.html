Eragrostis calantha

Eragrostis calantha, commonly known as the silverleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and compact growth habit, making it a distinctive species within the grass family. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Eragrostis calantha is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
