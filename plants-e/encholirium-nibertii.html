Encholirium nibertii

Encholirium nibertii, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the highlands of central Mexico, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that resemble firecrackers, hence its common name. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly coating.

Encholirium nibertii is a perennial herb that is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, humid climates.
