Eupatorium calophyllum

Eupatorium calophyllum, commonly known as the redstem goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and has a sprawling, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tint on the underside.

Eupatorium calophyllum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
