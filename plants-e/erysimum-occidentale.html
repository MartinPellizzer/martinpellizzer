Erysimum occidentale

Erysimum occidentale, commonly known as the western wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Erysimum occidentale is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, particularly in mountainous regions.
