Echium creticum var. grandiflorum

Echium creticum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Cretan Lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
