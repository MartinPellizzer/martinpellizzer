Eugenia ampullaria

Eugenia ampullaria, commonly known as the red bell pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
