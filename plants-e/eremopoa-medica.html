Eremopoa medica

Eremopoa medica, commonly known as the medicinal grass, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from lower soil layers. Eremopoa medica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties, though scientific research on its efficacy is limited.

Its resilience makes it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential resource for sustainable land management.
