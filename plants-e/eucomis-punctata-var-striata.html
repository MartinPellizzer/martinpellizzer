Eucomis punctata var. striata

Eucomis punctata var. striata, commonly known as the stripe-headed pineapple lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, and it produces tall, spiky flower stalks adorned with white to pale pink bracts. The plant is known for its unique, pineapple-like appearance, with the bracts resembling the scales of a pineapple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and long blooming period make it a versatile and attractive addition to the landscape.
