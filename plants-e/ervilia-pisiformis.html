Ervilia pisiformis

Ervilia pisiformis, commonly known as the kidney bean vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, bean-like pods. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds similar to common beans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The botanical features of Ervilia pisiformis make it an important species in both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
