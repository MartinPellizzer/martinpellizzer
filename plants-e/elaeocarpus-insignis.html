Elaeocarpus insignis

Elaeocarpus insignis, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in warm climates.
