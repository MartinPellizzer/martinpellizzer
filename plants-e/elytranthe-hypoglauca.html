Elytranthe hypoglauca

Elytranthe hypoglauca, commonly known as the bluish elytranthe, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, which helps it reflect excess light and reduce water loss. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in clusters, usually during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
