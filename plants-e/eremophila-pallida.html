Eremophila pallida

Eremophila pallida, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a compact growth habit. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

Eremophila pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
