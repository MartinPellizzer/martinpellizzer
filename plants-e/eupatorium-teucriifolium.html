Eupatorium teucriifolium

Eupatorium teucriifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and rain gardens.
