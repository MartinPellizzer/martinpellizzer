Erigeron lanatus

Erigeron lanatus, commonly known as the woolly fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale blue flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. It typically grows in open areas such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils.
