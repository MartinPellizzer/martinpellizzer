Eriogonum multiceps subsp. coloradense

Eriogonum multiceps subsp.

coloradense, commonly known as the Colorado flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
