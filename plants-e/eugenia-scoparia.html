Eugenia scoparia

Eugenia scoparia, commonly known as the bushy eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and are also appreciated for their ornamental value.
