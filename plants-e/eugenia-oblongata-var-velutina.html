Eugenia oblongata var. velutina

Eugenia oblongata var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet evergreen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple to black berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
