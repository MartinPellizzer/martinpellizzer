Eriogonum longifolium subsp. typicum

Eriogonum longifolium subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the longleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
