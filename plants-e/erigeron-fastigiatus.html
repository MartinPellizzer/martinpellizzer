Erigeron fastigiatus

Erigeron fastigiatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its blue-gray foliage and fluffy white to pale blue flowers make it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
