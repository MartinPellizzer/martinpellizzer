Erodendrum limoniifolium

Erodendrum limoniifolium, commonly known as the lemonleaf erodendrum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
