Eryngium nasturtiifolium

Eryngium nasturtiifolium, commonly known as the spiked eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features spiny, grass-like leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Eryngium nasturtiifolium is known for its ornamental value and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
