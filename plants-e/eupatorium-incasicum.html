Eupatorium incasicum

Eupatorium incasicum, commonly known as rough false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale blue, blooming in the summer months.

Eupatorium incasicum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
