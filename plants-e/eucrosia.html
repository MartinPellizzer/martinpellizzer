Eucrosia

Eucrosia, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Colchicaceae.

These plants are native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. They are characterized by their striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Eucrosia species typically grow from corms and have narrow, grass-like leaves.
