Eryngium duriaeanum

Eryngium duriaeanum, commonly known as the Duriaean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture.

Eryngium duriaeanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive and striking appearance.
