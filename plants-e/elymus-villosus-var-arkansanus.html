Elymus villosus var. arkansanus

Elymus villosus var. arkansanus, commonly known as the Arkansas hairy wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
