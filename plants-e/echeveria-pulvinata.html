Echeveria pulvinata

Echeveria pulvinata, commonly known as the Wooly Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, woolly leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Due to its compact size and ornamental leaves, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
