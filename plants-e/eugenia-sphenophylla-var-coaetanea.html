Eugenia sphenophylla var. coaetanea

Eugenia sphenophylla var. coaetanea, commonly known as the red-veined guava, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by edible, red or pink fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique fruit, which has a tart flavor.
