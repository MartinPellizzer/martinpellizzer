Equisetum telmateia subsp. braunii

Equisetum telmateia subsp.

braunii, commonly known as the dwarf horsehair fern, is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its slender, sparsely branched stems. It is a primitive vascular plant that retains primitive features such as microphylls and a homosporous reproductive system.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its appearance is marked by the presence of whorls of small, needle-like leaves and the production of spore-bearing strobila.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability.
