Eriogonum heracleoides var. leucophaeum

Eriogonum heracleoides var. leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-leaved buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale green foliage and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system and exhibits a low, spreading growth habit.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in native ecosystems.
