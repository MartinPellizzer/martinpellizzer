Echeveria venezuelensis

Echeveria venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Venezuela.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure composed of thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-gray or silver-green, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the growing season.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and arid environments due to its drought tolerance.
