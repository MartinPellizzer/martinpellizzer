Excoecaria rectinervis

Excoecaria rectinervis, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking red flowers and distinctive leaf venation.

The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves with prominent, parallel veins, giving it the species name "rectinervis.". It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The flowers are typically orange-red to deep red, with five petals and a yellow center, and bloom throughout the growing season.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
