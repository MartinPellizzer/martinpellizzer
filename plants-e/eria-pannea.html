Eria pannea

Eria pannea, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Eria pannea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
