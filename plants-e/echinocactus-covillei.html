Echinocactus covillei

Echinocactus covillei, commonly known as the Coville's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender stems. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
