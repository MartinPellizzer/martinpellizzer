Emilia fosbergii

Emilia fosbergii, commonly known as the Fosberg's emilia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers are usually yellow with white ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.
