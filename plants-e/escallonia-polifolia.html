Escallonia polifolia

Escallonia polifolia, commonly known as the coastal escallonia, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows along coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Escallonia polifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
