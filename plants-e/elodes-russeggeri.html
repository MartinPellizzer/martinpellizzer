Elodes russeggeri

Elodes russeggeri, commonly known as the waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that often branch out and bear small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged or just below the water surface. Elodes russeggeri plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often used in aquariums due to its ability to oxygenate water and its aesthetic appeal.
