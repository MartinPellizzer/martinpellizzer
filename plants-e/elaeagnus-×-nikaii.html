Elaeagnus × nikaii

Elaeagnus × nikaii, commonly known as the silver berry or silver thorn, is a hybrid plant in the Elaeagnaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring and has a distinctive, thorny appearance.

Elaeagnus × nikaii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in soil remediation.
