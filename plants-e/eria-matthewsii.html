Eria matthewsii

Eria matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with moderate to high rainfall. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a unique, spiral-shaped lip.

Eria matthewsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts.
