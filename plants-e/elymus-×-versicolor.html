Elymus × versicolor

Elymus × versicolor, commonly known as the red wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and vibrant red plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a perennial species that forms dense clumps with a fibrous root system.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
