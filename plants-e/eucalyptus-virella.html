Eucalyptus virella

Eucalyptus virella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white coating. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Eucalyptus virella is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It is also known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
