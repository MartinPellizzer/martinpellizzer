Erophila chlorina

Erophila chlorina, commonly known as the green everlasting or green everlasting grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Erophila chlorina is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in disturbed soils.
