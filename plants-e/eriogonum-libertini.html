Eriogonum libertini

Eriogonum libertini, commonly known as the Liberty's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum libertini is an important nectar source for native pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
