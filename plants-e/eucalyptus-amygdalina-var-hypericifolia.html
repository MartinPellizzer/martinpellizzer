Eucalyptus amygdalina var. hypericifolia

Eucalyptus amygdalina var. hypericifolia, commonly known as the honey gum, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a distinctive white underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting various pollinators.

It is a hardy tree that thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
