Emilia exigua

Emilia exigua, commonly known as the common emilia or small emilia, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers with dark centers, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Emilia exigua thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its delicate appearance and easy cultivation make it a popular choice for wildflower gardens and seed-saving projects.
