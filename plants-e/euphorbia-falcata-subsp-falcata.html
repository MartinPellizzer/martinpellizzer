Euphorbia falcata subsp. falcata

Euphorbia falcata subsp.

falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge.

The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
