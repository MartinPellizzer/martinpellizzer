Evolvulus lanatus

Evolvulus lanatus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Evolvulus lanatus is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
