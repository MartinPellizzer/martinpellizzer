Ericameria nauseosa var. salicifolia

Ericameria nauseosa var. salicifolia, commonly known as silverleaf ericameria, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and woodland habitats. It has aromatic leaves that release a pungent odor when crushed, and its root system is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
