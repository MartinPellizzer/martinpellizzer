Erica galpinii

Erica galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. Erica galpinii produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to reddish flowers during the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
