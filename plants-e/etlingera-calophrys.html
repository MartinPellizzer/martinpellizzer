Etlingera calophrys

Etlingera calophrys, commonly known as the golden torch ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red bracts that enclose small, pale yellow flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in humid environments.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
