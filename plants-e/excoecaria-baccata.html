Excoecaria baccata

Excoecaria baccata, commonly known as the sea mango, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals.

Its botanical features include a milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it an important plant to handle with care.
