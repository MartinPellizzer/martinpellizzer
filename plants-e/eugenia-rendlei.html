Eugenia rendlei

Eugenia rendlei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Eugenia rendlei thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for reforestation and landscaping projects.
