Ehretia esquirolii

Ehretia esquirolii, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeard, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
