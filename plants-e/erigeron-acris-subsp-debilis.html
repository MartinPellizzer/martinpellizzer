Erigeron acris subsp. debilis

Erigeron acris subsp.

debilis, commonly known as the slender fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is distinguished by its delicate, pale yellow to white ray florets and narrow, hairy leaves. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies.

The plant is often found in the central and eastern United States, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
