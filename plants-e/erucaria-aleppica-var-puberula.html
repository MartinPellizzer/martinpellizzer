Erucaria aleppica var. puberula

Erucaria aleppica var. puberula, commonly known as the Aleppo erucaria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
