Eurya brassii subsp. subrotunda

Eurya brassii subsp.

subrotunda, commonly known as the round-leaf waxleaf, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture and a glossy appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often followed by bright red berries.

This plant is well-suited for shaded garden environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
