Exocarpos

Exocarpos, commonly known as the "star fruit" or "carambola," is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed, star-shaped fruit. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and small, yellowish flowers that develop into the characteristic fruit. The exocarp, or outermost layer, is thin and edible, while the mesocarp is fleshy and juicy.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is widely cultivated for its unique flavor and nutritional value.
