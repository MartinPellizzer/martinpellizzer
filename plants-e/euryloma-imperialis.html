Euryloma imperialis

Euryloma imperialis, commonly known as the imperial euryloma, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family, native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often a deep emerald green with intricate veining. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet and white, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its unique morphology includes a branched stem with adventitious roots, aiding in its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Euryloma imperialis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
