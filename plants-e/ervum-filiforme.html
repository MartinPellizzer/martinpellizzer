Ervum filiforme

Ervum filiforme, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It has small, compound leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ervum filiforme is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
