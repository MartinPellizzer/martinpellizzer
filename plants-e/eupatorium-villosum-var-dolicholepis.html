Eupatorium villosum var. dolicholepis

Eupatorium villosum var. dolicholepis, commonly known as the longleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.
