Erigeron scaber

Erigeron scaber, commonly known as stiffleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
