Eupatorium multiserratum

Eupatorium multiserratum, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

Eupatorium multiserratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
