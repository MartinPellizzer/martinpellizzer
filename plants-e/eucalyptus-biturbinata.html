Eucalyptus biturbinata

Eucalyptus biturbinata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallet tree, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically white or pale yellow. It is a medium-sized tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Eucalyptus biturbinata is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
