Eremophila behriana

Eremophila behriana, commonly known as the Kangaroo Pouch Plant, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of bright red or pink flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its succulent stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice among native plant enthusiasts.
