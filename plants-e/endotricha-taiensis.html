Endotricha taiensis

Endotricha taiensis, commonly known as the Tai Mountains grasshopper plant, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale green flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the cool, humid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
