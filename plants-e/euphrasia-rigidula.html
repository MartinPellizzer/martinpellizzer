Euphrasia rigidula

Euphrasia rigidula, commonly known as rigid bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Euphrasia rigidula is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open woodland areas.
