Echium spinescens subsp. coincyanum

Echium spinescens subsp.

coincyanum, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
