Eremophila grandiflora

Eremophila grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is part of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Eremophila grandiflora is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
