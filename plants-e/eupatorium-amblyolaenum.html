Eupatorium amblyolaenum

Eupatorium amblyolaenum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
