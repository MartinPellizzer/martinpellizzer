Eritrichium pulviniforme

Eritrichium pulviniforme, commonly known as the purple poppy or the alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery petals that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Eritrichium pulviniforme is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
