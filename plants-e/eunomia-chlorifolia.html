Eunomia chlorifolia

Eunomia chlorifolia, commonly known as the greenleaf eunomia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are predominantly green, with some varieties displaying subtle variegation. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Eunomia chlorifolia thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
