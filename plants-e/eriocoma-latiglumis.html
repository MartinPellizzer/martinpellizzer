Eriocoma latiglumis

Eriocoma latiglumis, commonly known as the woolly goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of fine hairs on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Eriocoma latiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
