Erica × aristella

Erica × aristella, commonly known as the common heath, is a hybrid shrub native to the western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica × aristella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.

It is also an important component of fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
