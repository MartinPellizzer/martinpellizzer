Echinops spinosissimus

Echinops spinosissimus, commonly known as the spiny globe thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are covered in bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of its characteristic spiny fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, particularly in regions with dry climates.
