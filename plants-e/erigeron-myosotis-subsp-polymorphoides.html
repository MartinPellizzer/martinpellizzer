Erigeron myosotis subsp. polymorphoides

Erigeron myosotis subsp.

polymorphoides, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
