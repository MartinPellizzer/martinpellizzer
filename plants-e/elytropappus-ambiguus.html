Elytropappus ambiguus

Elytropappus ambiguus, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Elytropappus ambiguus is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
