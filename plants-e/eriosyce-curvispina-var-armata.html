Eriosyce curvispina var. armata

Eriosyce curvispina var. armata, commonly known as the hairy areca cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores.

This variety is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
