Erigeron inornatus var. keilii

Erigeron inornatus var. keilii, commonly known as the Keil's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms in shades of white and yellow.
