Eria gobiensis var. ternatensis

Eria gobiensis var. ternatensis, commonly known as the Gobi eria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and specialized adaptations to extreme desert conditions. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which helps it blend with its environment. It has fibrous roots that enable it to absorb and store water efficiently.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in its native habitat.
