Erodium chium var. renifolium

Erodium chium var. renifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically broad and heart-shaped, giving it its varietal name "renifolium.". The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by slender, hairy pods containing seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
