Eugenia smithii var. minor

Eugenia smithii var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a slight aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to reddish-purple as it matures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
