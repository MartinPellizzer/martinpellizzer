Ectadiopsis scandens

Ectadiopsis scandens, commonly known as the climbing dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial species characterized by its trailing growth habit and ability to climb using its stems. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into distinctive seed heads. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for certain insects and its use in traditional medicine.

Ectadiopsis scandens is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its resilience in disturbed environments.
