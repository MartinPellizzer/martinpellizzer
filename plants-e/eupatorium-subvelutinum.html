Eupatorium subvelutinum

Eupatorium subvelutinum, commonly known as velvetleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small, clustered white or pale purple flowers.

The plant is often found in wetlands and is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
