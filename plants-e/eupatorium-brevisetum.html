Eupatorium brevisetum

Eupatorium brevisetum, commonly known as the short-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Eupatorium brevisetum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
