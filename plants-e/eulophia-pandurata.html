Eulophia pandurata

Eulophia pandurata, commonly known as the pandurata orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia pandurata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
