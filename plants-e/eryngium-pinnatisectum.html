Eryngium pinnatisectum

Eryngium pinnatisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple spires of flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating called bloom. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Eryngium pinnatisectum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
