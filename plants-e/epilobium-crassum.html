Epilobium crassum

Epilobium crassum, commonly known as broadleaf willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of pink or reddish-purple flowers. Its flowers are actinomorphic and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Epilobium crassum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
