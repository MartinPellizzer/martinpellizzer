Eria albiflora

Eria albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered eria, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forests or understorey habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Eria albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
