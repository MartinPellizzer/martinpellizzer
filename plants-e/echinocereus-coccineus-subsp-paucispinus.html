Echinocereus coccineus subsp. paucispinus

Echinocereus coccineus subsp.

paucispinus, commonly known as the pink hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in bright red to pink spines.

The plant produces clusters of vibrant red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
