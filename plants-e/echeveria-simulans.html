Echeveria simulans

Echeveria simulans, commonly known as the "Coral Echeveria," is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of bright pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
