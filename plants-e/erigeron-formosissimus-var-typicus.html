Erigeron formosissimus var. typicus

Erigeron formosissimus var. typicus, commonly known as the showy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, and it thrives in full sun. Its showy white to pale blue flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
