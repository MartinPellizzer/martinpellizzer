Erica cymosa subsp. grandiflora

Erica cymosa subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Erica cymosa subsp.

grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
