Eulophia fusca

Eulophia fusca, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black-colored flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Eulophia fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
