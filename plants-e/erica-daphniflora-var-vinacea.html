Erica daphniflora var. vinacea

Erica daphniflora var. vinacea, commonly known as the red heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a rock garden plant.
