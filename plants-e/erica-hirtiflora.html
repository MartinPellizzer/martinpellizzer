Erica hirtiflora

Erica hirtiflora, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth and small, needle-like leaves covered in fine, white hairs. Erica hirtiflora produces clusters of pink or white bell-shaped flowers, which bloom in spring and early summer.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
