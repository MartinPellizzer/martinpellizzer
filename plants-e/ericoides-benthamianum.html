Ericoides benthamianum

Ericoides benthamianum, commonly known as the Bentham's ericoid, is a flowering plant species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. It typically grows in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats.

Ericoides benthamianum is often found in heathlands and rocky areas, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these environments.
