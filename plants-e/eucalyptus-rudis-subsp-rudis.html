Eucalyptus rudis subsp. rudis

Eucalyptus rudis subsp.

rudis, commonly known as the slender gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, often peeling bark.

The leaves are lance-shaped, usually with a greyish-green hue, and are arranged alternately along the branches. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, clustered in umbels, and attract a variety of pollinators.

This subspecies is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
