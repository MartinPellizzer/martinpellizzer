Eria rhynchostyloides

Eria rhynchostyloides, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed eria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
