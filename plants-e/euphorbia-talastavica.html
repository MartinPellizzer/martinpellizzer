Euphorbia talastavica

Euphorbia talastavica, commonly known as the Socotra candelabra, is a unique succulent plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its striking, tree-like appearance with a short, thick trunk and multiple branching stems. The plant features small, green, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are adapted to reduce water loss in its arid environment. Its flowers are clustered in yellowish-green cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Euphorbia talastavica is well-suited to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a resilient and visually distinctive plant.
