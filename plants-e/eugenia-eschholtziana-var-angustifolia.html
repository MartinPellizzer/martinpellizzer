Eugenia eschholtziana var. angustifolia

Eugenia eschholtziana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like fruits with a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
