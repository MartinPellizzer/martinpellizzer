Euphorbia euboea

Euphorbia euboea, commonly known as the Euboean spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Greek island of Euboea.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested. Euphorbia euboea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
