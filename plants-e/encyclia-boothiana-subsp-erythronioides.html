Encyclia boothiana subsp. erythronioides

Encyclia boothiana subsp.

erythronioides, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers with red veins.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color and bloom during the wet season.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
