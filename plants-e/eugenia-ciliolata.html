Eugenia ciliolata

Eugenia ciliolata, commonly known as the silky eucalyptus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.
