Erythrophleum africanum

Erythrophleum africanum, commonly known as African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red-orange flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The tree also has a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
