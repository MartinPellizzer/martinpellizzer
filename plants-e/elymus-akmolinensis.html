Elymus akmolinensis

Elymus akmolinensis, commonly known as the Akmolinsk wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leaf-like blades that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are the reproductive structures of grasses, and these are often tinged with purple or brown. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Elymus akmolinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
