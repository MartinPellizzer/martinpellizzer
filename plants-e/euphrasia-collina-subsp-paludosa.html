Euphrasia collina subsp. paludosa

Euphrasia collina subsp.

paludosa, commonly known as the bog dogwood, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It is characterized by its delicate, nodding yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
