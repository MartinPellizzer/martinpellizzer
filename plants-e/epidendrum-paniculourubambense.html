Epidendrum paniculourubambense

Epidendrum paniculourubambense, commonly known as the Redstem Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, red-brown flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth surface and a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

The orchid's unique floral structure and coloration make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical study.
