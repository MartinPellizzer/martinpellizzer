Eryngium depressum var. eudepressum

Eryngium depressum var. eudepressum, commonly known as the dwarf thistle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features compact, spiny foliage and produces blue to purple spherical flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette growth habit with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and drought-tolerant species within the Asteraceae family.
