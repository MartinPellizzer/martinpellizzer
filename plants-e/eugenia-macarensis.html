Eugenia macarensis

Eugenia macarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

Eugenia macarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
