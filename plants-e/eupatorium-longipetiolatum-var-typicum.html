Eupatorium longipetiolatum var. typicum

Eupatorium longipetiolatum var. typicum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its aromatic foliage and its role as a nectar source for pollinators.
