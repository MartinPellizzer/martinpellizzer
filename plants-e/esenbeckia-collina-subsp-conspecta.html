Esenbeckia collina subsp. conspecta

Esenbeckia collina subsp.

conspecta, commonly known as the coastal esenbeckia, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern South America. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in terminal clusters.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive component of coastal flora.
