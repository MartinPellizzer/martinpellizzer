Eriocaulon collinum

Eriocaulon collinum, commonly known as the hill marsh plant, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Eriocaulon collinum is an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
