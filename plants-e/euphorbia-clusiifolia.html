Euphorbia clusiifolia

Euphorbia clusiifolia, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.

Euphorbia clusiifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
