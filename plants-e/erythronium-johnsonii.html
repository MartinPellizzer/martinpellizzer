Erythronium johnsonii

Erythronium johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's dogtooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features showy, nodding flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive violet or purple hue at the base. The plant has a corm-like bulb that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive dormant periods. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Erythronium johnsonii thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
