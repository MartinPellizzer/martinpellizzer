Eria retusa

Eria retusa, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the soil.

Eria retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
