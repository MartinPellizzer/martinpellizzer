Echinocactus xiphacanthus

Echinocactus xiphacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and bright yellow spines that give it its common name. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
