Eriogonum sericoleucum

Eriogonum sericoleucum, commonly known as the whiteleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.

Eriogonum sericoleucum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
