Euphorbia taurinensis var. brachyceras

Euphorbia taurinensis var. brachyceras, commonly known as the Taurine spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southern regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, typical of many succulent species. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
