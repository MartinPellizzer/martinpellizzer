Echinocactus soebrensii

Echinocactus soebrensii, commonly known as the Soebren's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines, which provide protection against herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its greenish-gray body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
