Eurycarpus marinellii

Eurycarpus marinellii, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped fruits that contain numerous small seeds. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
