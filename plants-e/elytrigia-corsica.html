Elytrigia corsica

Elytrigia corsica, commonly known as Corsican grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across suitable habitats.

Elytrigia corsica plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
