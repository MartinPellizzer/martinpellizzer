Euphorbia cuphosperma

Euphorbia cuphosperma, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with thick, fleshy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by a glandular, sticky substance.

The fruit, which is a type of berry, is edible when ripe and has a tangy, citrus-like flavor.
