Eupatorium parviflorum var. monticola

Eupatorium parviflorum var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has slender, branched stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.
