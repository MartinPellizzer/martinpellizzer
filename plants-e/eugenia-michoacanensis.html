Eugenia michoacanensis

Eugenia michoacanensis, commonly known as the Michoacan huito, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
