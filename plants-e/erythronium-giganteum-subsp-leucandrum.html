Erythronium giganteum subsp. leucandrum

Erythronium giganteum subsp.

leucandrum, commonly known as the white trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, typically emerging from a bulbous underground stem.

The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often mottled with green and white patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is known for its early spring blooming period.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale coloration compared to the more commonly seen red-flowered E. giganteum.
