Eugenia ciarensis

Eugenia ciarensis, commonly known as the Ciarensis Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Eugenia ciarensis is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
