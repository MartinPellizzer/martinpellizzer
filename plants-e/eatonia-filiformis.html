Eatonia filiformis

Eatonia filiformis, commonly known as the slender eatonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it its scientific name, "filiformis," meaning thread-like in Latin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
