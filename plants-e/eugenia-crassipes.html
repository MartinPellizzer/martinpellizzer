Eugenia crassipes

Eugenia crassipes, commonly known as the water apple or mangostan, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, purple rind and a segmented, juicy white pulp. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

The fruit is valued for its flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
