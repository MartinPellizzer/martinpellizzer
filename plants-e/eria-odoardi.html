Eria odoardi

Eria odoardi, commonly known as the Odoardi's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its intricate floral structures and specialized pollination mechanisms.

It typically features slender, upright stems with a few to several leaves, and its flowers are usually small to medium in size, often with vibrant colors such as purple, pink, or white. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the surrounding substrate, often growing in epiphytic or terrestrial habitats.

Eria odoardi is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
