Echeveria elongata

Echeveria elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of violet or pink flowers during the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight.

Echeveria elongata is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
