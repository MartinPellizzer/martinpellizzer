Eustoma russellianum f. fisheri

Eustoma russellianum f. fisheri, commonly known as the Fischer's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals that resemble mist, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in both landscape and container settings.
