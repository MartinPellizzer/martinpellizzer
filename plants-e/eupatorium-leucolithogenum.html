Eupatorium leucolithogenum

Eupatorium leucolithogenum, commonly known as the white rock eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium leucolithogenum is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
