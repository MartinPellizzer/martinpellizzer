Epilobium cunninghamii

Epilobium cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain willow, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces showy, pink or red flowers in clusters. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Epilobium cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
