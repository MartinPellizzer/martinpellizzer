Eriocephalus ericoides

Eriocephalus ericoides, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Eriocephalus ericoides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
