Erythronium smithii

Erythronium smithii, commonly known as the Smith's dog-tooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features a bulbous underground stem and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and emerge before the flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
