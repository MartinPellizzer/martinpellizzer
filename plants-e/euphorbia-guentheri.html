Euphorbia guentheri

Euphorbia guentheri, commonly known as Guenther's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble yellowish-green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia guentheri is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
