Eupatorium benguetense

Eupatorium benguetense, commonly known as the Benguet eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Eupatorium benguetense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
