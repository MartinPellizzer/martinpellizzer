Elegia fusca

Elegia fusca, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers with dark centers. The plant typically grows in moist soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in prairie habitats.

Elegia fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
