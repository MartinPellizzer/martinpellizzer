Eugenia dyeriana var. oblonga

Eugenia dyeriana var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved satinash, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, acidic soils. The plant features lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. Following the flowers, it develops red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
