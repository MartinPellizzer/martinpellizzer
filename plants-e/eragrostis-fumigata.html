Eragrostis fumigata

Eragrostis fumigata, commonly known as the smokegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and produces fluffy, smoke-like seed heads that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Eragrostis fumigata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
