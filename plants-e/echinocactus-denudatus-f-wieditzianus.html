Echinocactus denudatus f. wieditzianus

Echinocactus denudatus f. wieditzianus, commonly known as the Wieditzianus cactus, is a rare and unique variant of the Echinocactus genus.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure, typical of many cacti in the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It is native to specific regions in Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
