Eragrostis mendocina

Eragrostis mendocina, commonly known as Mendo grass, is a perennial grass species native to California.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Eragrostis mendocina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
