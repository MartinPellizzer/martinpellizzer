Elaeagnus conferta var. malaccensis

Elaeagnus conferta var. malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca silverleaf, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or pale green, followed by edible berries that are orange-red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
