Eugenia vismiifolia

Eugenia vismiifolia, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its small white flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, while its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark purple.

Eugenia vismiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
