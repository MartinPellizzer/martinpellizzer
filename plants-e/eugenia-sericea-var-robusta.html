Eugenia sericea var. robusta

Eugenia sericea var. robusta, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
