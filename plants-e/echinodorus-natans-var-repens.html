Echinodorus natans var. repens

Echinodorus natans var. repens, commonly known as the wandering waterweed, is a popular aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, often forming dense mats. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is commonly used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and oxygenation properties. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, followed by the development of floating fruits.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a valuable and sometimes invasive species in aquatic ecosystems.
