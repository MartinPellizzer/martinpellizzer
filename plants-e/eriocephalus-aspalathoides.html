Eriocephalus aspalathoides

Eriocephalus aspalathoides, commonly known as the African Geranium, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features rounded, leathery leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
