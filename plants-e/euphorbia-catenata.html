Euphorbia catenata

Euphorbia catenata, commonly known as the chain euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy, pale green coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Euphorbia catenata is also known for its ability to store water in its stems, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry climates.
