Epipactis tremolsii subsp. latina

Epipactis tremolsii subsp.

latina, commonly known as the Latvian helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe, particularly Latvia and neighboring regions. It is a perennial herb with a rosette of broad, leathery leaves that form a dense basal clump.

The plant produces a tall, slender flowering stem bearing a single spike of greenish-white to pale yellow flowers, which are often hooded and have a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands, and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
