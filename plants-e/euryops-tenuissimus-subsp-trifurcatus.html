Euryops tenuissimus subsp. trifurcatus

Euryops tenuissimus subsp.

trifurcatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is a perennial species that produces numerous flowering stems, often with three branches, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
