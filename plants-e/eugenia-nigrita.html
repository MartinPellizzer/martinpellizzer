Eugenia nigrita

Eugenia nigrita, commonly known as black eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and clove. Its fruits are small, dark berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia nigrita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
