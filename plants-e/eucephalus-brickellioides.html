Eucephalus brickellioides

Eucephalus brickellioides, commonly known as the brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in the landscape.

Eucephalus brickellioides is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
