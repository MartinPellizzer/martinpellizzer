Erigeron shepardii

Erigeron shepardii, commonly known as Sheppard's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It has narrow, gray-green leaves and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
