Eurycaulis foetens

Eurycaulis foetens, commonly known as the stinking marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in swampy or marshy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its strong, unpleasant odor, it is often considered a nuisance in agricultural settings.
