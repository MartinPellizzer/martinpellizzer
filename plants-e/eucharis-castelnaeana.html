Eucharis castelnaeana

Eucharis castelnaeana, commonly known as the Castelnau's Amazon lily, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Aroid family, characterized by its distinctive spatulate leaves and showy, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often near water sources in the rainforest. Its botanical features include a short, rhizomatous stem and a single, large, waxy leaf that can reach up to 1 meter in length.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often followed by the development of a fruit that contains several seeds.
