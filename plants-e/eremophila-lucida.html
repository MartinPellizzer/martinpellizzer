Eremophila lucida

Eremophila lucida, commonly known as the slender eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its erect, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremophila lucida is often used in native gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
