Eryngium pandanifolium var. atrocephalum

Eryngium pandanifolium var. atrocephalum, commonly known as the black-headed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking blue to purple spiky flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by velvety, silver-green bracts. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, woolly coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
