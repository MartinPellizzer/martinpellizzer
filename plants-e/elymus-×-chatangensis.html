Elymus × chatangensis

Elymus × chatangensis, commonly known as the Chinese wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of its stems, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and as a forage crop.
