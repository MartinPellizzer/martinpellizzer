Erianthus pollinioides

Erianthus pollinioides, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green leaves and tall, feathery inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in water gardens and wetland restoration projects.

Erianthus pollinioides plays an important role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
