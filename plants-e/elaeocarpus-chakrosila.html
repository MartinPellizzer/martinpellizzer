Elaeocarpus chakrosila

Elaeocarpus chakrosila, commonly known as the Chakrosila elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, hard, woody fruits that are typically covered in a thick, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
