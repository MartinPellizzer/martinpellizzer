Erica ibityensis

Erica ibityensis, commonly known as the Madagascar heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is typically found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica ibityensis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
