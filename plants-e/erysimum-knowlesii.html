Erysimum knowlesii

Erysimum knowlesii, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are bright yellow with four petals, often arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
