Epidendrum socorrense

Epidendrum socorrense, commonly known as the Socorro orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Epidendrum socorrense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
