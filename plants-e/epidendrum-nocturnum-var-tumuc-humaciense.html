Epidendrum nocturnum var. tumuc-humaciense

Epidendrum nocturnum var. tumuc-humaciense, commonly known as the Tumuc-Humac night orchid, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its long, narrow pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, fragrant, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is noted for its unique nocturnal flowering pattern, making it a rare and sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
