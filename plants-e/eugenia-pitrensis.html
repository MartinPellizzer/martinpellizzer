Eugenia pitrensis

Eugenia pitrensis, commonly known as the Pitrense myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Eugenia pitrensis is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
