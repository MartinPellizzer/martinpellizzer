Eulophia farcta

Eulophia farcta, commonly known as the spotted eulophia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms.

Eulophia farcta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
