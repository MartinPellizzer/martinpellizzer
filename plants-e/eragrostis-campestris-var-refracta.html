Eragrostis campestris var. refracta

Eragrostis campestris var. refracta, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
