Erythranthe filicifolia

Erythranthe filicifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved erythranthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically arranged in racemes, and it produces small, spherical pods that contain the seeds.

Erythranthe filicifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
