Eleocharis kuroguwai

Eleocharis kuroguwai, commonly known as the Japanese spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence and small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and pond margins. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

Eleocharis kuroguwai is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to moist environments.
