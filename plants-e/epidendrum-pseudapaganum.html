Epidendrum pseudapaganum

Epidendrum pseudapaganum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces long, pendulous inflorescences with fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically has dark green, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, yellow, and green hues, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red.

Epidendrum pseudapaganum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
