Erysimum sivasicum

Erysimum sivasicum, commonly known as the Sivash mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Erysimum sivasicum is a member of the Brassicaceae family, known for its cruciform flowers and mustard oils.
