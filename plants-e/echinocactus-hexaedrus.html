Echinocactus hexaedrus

Echinocactus hexaedrus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in sharp spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Echinocactus hexaedrus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
