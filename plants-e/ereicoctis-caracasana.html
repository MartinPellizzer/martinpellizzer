Ereicoctis caracasana

Ereicoctis caracasana, commonly known as the Caracasana orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing structural support for the flowering stems.

The orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
