Elionurus ledermannii

Elionurus ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowering heads are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Elionurus ledermannii is valued for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
