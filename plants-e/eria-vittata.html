Eria vittata

Eria vittata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a contrasting greenish throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

Eria vittata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
