Erica brownleeae

Erica brownleeae, commonly known as the Brownlee's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths, heathers, and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, dense foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Erica brownleeae is often found in fynbos vegetation, a unique biome known for its biodiversity and endemic species.
