Eugenia salacifolia

Eugenia salacifolia, commonly known as the sour cherry or Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit yield.

Eugenia salacifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
