Eupatorium hypodictyon

Eupatorium hypodictyon, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely dissected, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in moist, partially shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Eupatorium hypodictyon is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
