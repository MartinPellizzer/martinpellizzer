Eremophila paisleyi subsp. glandulosa

Eremophila paisleyi subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
