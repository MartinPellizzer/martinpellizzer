Erigeron serpentarius

Erigeron serpentarius, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
