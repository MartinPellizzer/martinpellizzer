Eriosema rufum var. macrostachyum

Eriosema rufum var. macrostachyum, commonly known as the large-stalked eriosema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This variant is distinguished by its larger stamens and more robust growth compared to other varieties of Eriosema rufum.
