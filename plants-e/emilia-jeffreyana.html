Emilia jeffreyana

Emilia jeffreyana, commonly known as the Jeffrey's emilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. Emilia jeffreyana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
