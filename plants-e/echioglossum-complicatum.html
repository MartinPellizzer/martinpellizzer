Echioglossum complicatum

Echioglossum complicatum, commonly known as the twisted tongue orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a short, stocky rhizome and a single, narrow, grass-like leaf. The plant produces a single, elongated spike bearing several small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, twisted lip. It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia.

Echioglossum complicatum thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as pine rocklands and hammocks.
