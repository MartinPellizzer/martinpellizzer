Embothrium coccineum var. cavanillesii

Embothrium coccineum var. cavanillesii, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a deciduous shrub native to the central and southern regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, elliptical berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
