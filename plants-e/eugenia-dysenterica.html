Eugenia dysenterica

Eugenia dysenterica, commonly known as jambu or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark red to black berries that are highly aromatic. The plant has lanceolate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, white or pinkish flowers. Its fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
