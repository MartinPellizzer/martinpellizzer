Eragrostis apiculata

Eragrostis apiculata, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and spike-like inflorescence, which consists of small, two-lobed spikelets. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Its ornamental value has also led to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
