Erigeron flagellifolius

Erigeron flagellifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are typically white to pale yellow and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
