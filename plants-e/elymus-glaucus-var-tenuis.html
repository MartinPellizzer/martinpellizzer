Elymus glaucus var. tenuis

Elymus glaucus var. tenuis, commonly known as slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and have a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family.
