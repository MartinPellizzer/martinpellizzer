Echinocereus choranthus var. russanthus

Echinocereus choranthus var. russanthus, commonly known as the Russian Hedgehog Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by succulent enthusiasts.
