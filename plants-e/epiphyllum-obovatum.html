Epiphyllum obovatum

Epiphyllum obovatum, commonly known as the moon cactus or night bloomer, is a cactus species native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing plant with thick, fleshy stems that are typically green or red in color. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, often in shades of white, pink, or yellow. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and can be quite large, though they often wither as the plant matures.

Epiphyllum obovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering habits.
