Eulophia mweruensis

Eulophia mweruensis, commonly known as the Lake Mweru orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent sepals and petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Eulophia mweruensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
