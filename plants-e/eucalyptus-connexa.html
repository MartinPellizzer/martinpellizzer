Eucalyptus connexa

Eucalyptus connexa, commonly known as the broad-leaved malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Eucalyptus connexa is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands and heathlands.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
