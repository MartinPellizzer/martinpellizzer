Euphorbia antankara

Euphorbia antankara, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

Euphorbia antankara is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
