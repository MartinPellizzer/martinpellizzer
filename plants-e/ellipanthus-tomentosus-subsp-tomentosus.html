Ellipanthus tomentosus subsp. tomentosus

Ellipanthus tomentosus subsp.

tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly ellipanthus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a thick layer of fine, white hairs.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
