Erodium lebelii subsp. marcuccii

Erodium lebelii subsp.

marcuccii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to California. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is part of the Geraniaceae family and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
