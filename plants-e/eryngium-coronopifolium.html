Eryngium coronopifolium

Eryngium coronopifolium, commonly known as the crownleaf eryngium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and striking purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
