Euphorbia classenii

Euphorbia classenii, commonly known as the Blue Glow Euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, rounded shape with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Due to its striking appearance, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
