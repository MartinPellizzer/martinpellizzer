Evolvulus niveus

Evolvulus niveus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and have a delicate, trumpet-like shape.

Evolvulus niveus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooming habit.
