Ehretia monopyrena

Ehretia monopyrena, commonly known as the white-flowered ehretia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Ehretia monopyrena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
