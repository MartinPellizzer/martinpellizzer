Evia

Evia, commonly known as the "Island of the Gods," is a small island located in the Aegean Sea, Greece.

It is renowned for its lush, diverse flora, including various species of wildflowers, shrubs, and trees. The island's unique microclimate supports a rich variety of native plants, many of which are adapted to the rocky and coastal terrain. Evia is home to several endemic species, making it an important area for botanical study and conservation.

Its natural vegetation plays a crucial role in maintaining the island's ecological balance and scenic beauty.
