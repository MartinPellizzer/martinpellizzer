Embelia villosa var. reticulata

Embelia villosa var. reticulata, commonly known as the hairy embelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with elliptical leaves that have a rough texture. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
