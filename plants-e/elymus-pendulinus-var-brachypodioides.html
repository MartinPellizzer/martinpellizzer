Elymus pendulinus var. brachypodioides

Elymus pendulinus var. brachypodioides, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
