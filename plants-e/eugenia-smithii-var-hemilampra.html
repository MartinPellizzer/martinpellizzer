Eugenia smithii var. hemilampra

Eugenia smithii var. hemilampra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rainforest environments.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
