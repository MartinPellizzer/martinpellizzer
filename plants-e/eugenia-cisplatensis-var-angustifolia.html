Eugenia cisplatensis var. angustifolia

Eugenia cisplatensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf velvetwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
