Eutacta excelsa var. variegata-alba

Eutacta excelsa var. variegata-alba, commonly known as the white-variegated star sedge, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive white and green variegation, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in water gardens or as a landscape plant in humid climates.
