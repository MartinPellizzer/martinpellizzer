Echinocereus salm-dyckianus subsp. obscuriensis

Echinocereus salm-dyckianus subsp.

obscuriensis, commonly known as the dark-spined hedgehog cactus, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features a cylindrical stem with prominent, spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the summer months.

The subspecies is characterized by its darker, more robust spines compared to other members of the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often used in rock gardens or xeriscaping.
