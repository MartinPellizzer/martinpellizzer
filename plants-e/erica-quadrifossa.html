Erica quadrifossa

Erica quadrifossa, commonly known as the four-cup heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Erica quadrifossa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in garden settings.
