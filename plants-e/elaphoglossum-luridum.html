Elaphoglossum luridum

Elaphoglossum luridum, commonly known as the golden holly fern, is a small evergreen fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, arching fronds with a distinctive golden hue on the underside, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are released from fertile fronds that appear in late summer, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

Elaphoglossum luridum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
