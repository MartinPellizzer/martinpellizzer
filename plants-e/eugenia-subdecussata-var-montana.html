Eugenia subdecussata var. montana

Eugenia subdecussata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, ovate leaves that are often arranged in a decussate pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
