Eupatorium triangulare

Eupatorium triangulare, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium triangulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
