Eupatorium ageratifolium var. acuminatum

Eupatorium ageratifolium var. acuminatum, commonly known as narrowleaf boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in saturated soils.
