Elaeocarpus populneoides

Elaeocarpus populneoides, commonly known as the white sand elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are encased in a fleshy, yellowish pulp. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The species plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is often found in disturbed or secondary forest habitats.
