Erythrina orientalis

Erythrina orientalis, commonly known as the oriental flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, papery flowers that appear in clusters, typically in spring. The plant has palmate leaves composed of three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Erythrina orientalis is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
