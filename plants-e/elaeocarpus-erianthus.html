Elaeocarpus erianthus

Elaeocarpus erianthus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The fruit is a woody capsule that turns from green to reddish-brown as it matures, containing several seeds. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
