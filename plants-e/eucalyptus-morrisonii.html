Eucalyptus morrisonii

Eucalyptus morrisonii, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a tall evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a vibrant red or orange inner layer. The tree produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pink in color. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Eucalyptus morrisonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
