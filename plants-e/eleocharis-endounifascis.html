Eleocharis endounifascis

Eleocharis endounifascis, commonly known as the narrow-striated spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
