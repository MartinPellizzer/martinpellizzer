Eriogonum baileyi var. divaricatum

Eriogonum baileyi var. divaricatum, commonly known as the Bailey's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as chaparral and sagebrush communities. The plant features basal leaves that are often lobed or divided, and it produces small, clustered white to pale pink flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in low-water conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
