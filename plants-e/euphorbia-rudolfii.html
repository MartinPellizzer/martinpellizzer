Euphorbia rudolfii

Euphorbia rudolfii, commonly known as the Rudolf's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia rudolfii is also known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
