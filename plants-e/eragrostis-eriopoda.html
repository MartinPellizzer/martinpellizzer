Eragrostis eriopoda

Eragrostis eriopoda, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the United States, particularly found in the central and eastern regions.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a clump-forming habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
