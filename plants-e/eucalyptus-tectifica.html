Eucalyptus tectifica

Eucalyptus tectifica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system.

Eucalyptus tectifica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
