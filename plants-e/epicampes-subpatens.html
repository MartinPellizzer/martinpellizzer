Epicampes subpatens

Epicampes subpatens, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
