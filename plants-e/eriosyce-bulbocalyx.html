Eriosyce bulbocalyx

Eriosyce bulbocalyx, commonly known as the bulbous cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to its harsh desert environment.

Eriosyce bulbocalyx is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in dry conditions.
