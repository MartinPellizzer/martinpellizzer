Eruca vesicaria var. sativa

Eruca vesicaria var. sativa, commonly known as garden rocket or roquette, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately divided, dark green leaves that are often covered in small, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery, mustard-like flavor, making it a popular ingredient in salads and culinary dishes.

It thrives in cool climates and is often grown in Mediterranean regions for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
