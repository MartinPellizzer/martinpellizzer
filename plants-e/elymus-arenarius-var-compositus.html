Elymus arenarius var. compositus

Elymus arenarius var. compositus, commonly known as the sandgrass or coastal grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and coastal regions.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or rocky habitats. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small fauna.
