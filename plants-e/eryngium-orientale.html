Eryngium orientale

Eryngium orientale, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by silver-green bracts. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Eryngium orientale is known for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its striking appearance.
