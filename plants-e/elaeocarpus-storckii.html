Elaeocarpus storckii

Elaeocarpus storckii, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that are typically ellipsoidal in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
