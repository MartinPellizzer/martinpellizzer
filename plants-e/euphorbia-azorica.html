Euphorbia azorica

Euphorbia azorica, commonly known as the Azorean spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Azores.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a unique ability to store water in its thick, succulent leaves, which helps it survive in arid conditions. It is often found growing in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
