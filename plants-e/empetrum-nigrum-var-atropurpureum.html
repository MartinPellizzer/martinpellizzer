Empetrum nigrum var. atropurpureum

Empetrum nigrum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as black crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. The plant has tiny, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a mat-like appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and tundra environments.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
