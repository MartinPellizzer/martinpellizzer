Erica thymifolia

Erica thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica thymifolia produces fragrant, pink to reddish blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its aromatic leaves, reminiscent of thyme, make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
