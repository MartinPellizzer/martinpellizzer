Euklisia pulchella

Euklisia pulchella, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
