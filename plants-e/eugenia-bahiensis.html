Eugenia bahiensis

Eugenia bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Eugenia bahiensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
