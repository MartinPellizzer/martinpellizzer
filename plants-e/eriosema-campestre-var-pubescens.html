Eriosema campestre var. pubescens

Eriosema campestre var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy field eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, often glandular leaves that are typically alternate and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Fabaceae family.
