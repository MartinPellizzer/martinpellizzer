Elaeodendron nitidulum

Elaeodendron nitidulum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved elaeodendron, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in an alternate pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by the development of woody, capsule-like fruits. Elaeodendron nitidulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its rarity and aesthetic qualities.

Its conservation status is of concern due to habitat loss and limited natural distribution.
