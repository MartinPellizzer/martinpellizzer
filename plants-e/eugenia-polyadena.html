Eugenia polyadena

Eugenia polyadena, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are evergreen and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.
