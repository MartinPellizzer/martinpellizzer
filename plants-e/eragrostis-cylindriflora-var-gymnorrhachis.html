Eragrostis cylindriflora var. gymnorrhachis

Eragrostis cylindriflora var. gymnorrhachis, commonly known as the slender false lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has fine, wiry leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant, grass-like form.
