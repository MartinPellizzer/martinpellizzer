Euterpe panamensis

Euterpe panamensis, commonly known as the Panama banana, is a species of palm native to Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to bananas. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
