Eriogonum pyrolifolium var. pyrolifolium

Eriogonum pyrolifolium var. pyrolifolium, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, globular clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, while the stems are often leafless and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
