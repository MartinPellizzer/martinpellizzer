Exospermum stipitatum

Exospermum stipitatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved exospermum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Eucalyptaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its bark is smooth and peels off in strips, revealing a yellowish inner layer.

The species is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
