Eragrostiella bifaria var. secunda

Eragrostiella bifaria var. secunda, commonly known as the secondary slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features.
