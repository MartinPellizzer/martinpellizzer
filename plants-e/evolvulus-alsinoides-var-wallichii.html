Evolvulus alsinoides var. wallichii

Evolvulus alsinoides var. wallichii, commonly known as the Indian blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
