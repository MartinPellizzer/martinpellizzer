Echium micranthum var. exsertum

Echium micranthum var. exsertum, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in well-drained conditions.
