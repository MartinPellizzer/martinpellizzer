Elaeocarpus truncatus

Elaeocarpus truncatus, commonly known as the truncated elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, hard, woody fruits that are typically covered in a thick, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has limited uses in traditional medicine.
