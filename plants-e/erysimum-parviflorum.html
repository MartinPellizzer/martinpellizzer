Erysimum parviflorum

Erysimum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flower wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and produces small, oval-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
