Euonymus oliganthus

Euonymus oliganthus, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange leaves that turn vibrant colors in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Euonymus oliganthus is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in a variety of soil types.
