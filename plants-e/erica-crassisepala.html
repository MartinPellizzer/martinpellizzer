Erica crassisepala

Erica crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-stalked heath, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
