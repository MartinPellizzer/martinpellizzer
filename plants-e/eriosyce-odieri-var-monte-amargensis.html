Eriosyce odieri var. monte-amargensis

Eriosyce odieri var. monte-amargensis, commonly known as the Monte Amargue Eriosyce, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Peru.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and limited water availability. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are usually yellow or orange and are borne on short pedicels.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in dry gardens.
