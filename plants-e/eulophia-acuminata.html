Eulophia acuminata

Eulophia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Eulophia acuminata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
