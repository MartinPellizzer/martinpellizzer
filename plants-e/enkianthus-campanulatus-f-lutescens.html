Enkianthus campanulatus f. lutescens

Enkianthus campanulatus f. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a rare and striking variant of the Enkianthus genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, bell-shaped flowers that range from pale yellow to golden in color. Native to the mountainous regions of China, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of dark green, lance-shaped leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
