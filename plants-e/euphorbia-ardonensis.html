Euphorbia ardonensis

Euphorbia ardonensis, commonly known as the Armenian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and succulent leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique inflorescences. Euphorbia ardonensis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

It is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
