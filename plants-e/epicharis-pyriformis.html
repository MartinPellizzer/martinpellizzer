Epicharis pyriformis

Epicharis pyriformis, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow dudleya, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a powdery bloom. The plant produces yellow, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Epicharis pyriformis is drought-tolerant and is often used in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
