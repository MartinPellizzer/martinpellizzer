Erysimum nevadense subsp. fitzii

Erysimum nevadense subsp.

fitzii, commonly known as the Sierra wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its clustered, showy yellow flowers and hairy, basal leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open areas with full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh, high-altitude environments it inhabits.
