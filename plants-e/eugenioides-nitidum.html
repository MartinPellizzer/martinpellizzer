Eugenioides nitidum

Eugenioides nitidum, commonly known as the glossy eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are dark purple when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to warm, moist climates and suitable for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
