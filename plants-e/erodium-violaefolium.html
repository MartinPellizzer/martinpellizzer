Erodium violaefolium

Erodium violaefolium, commonly known as the violetleaf stormweed, is a small annual herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has small, violet-colored leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred structure.

It produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
