Echinopogon mckiei

Echinopogon mckiei, commonly known as the McKee's spiny grass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with bract-like structures that give it a spiky appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy surface that aids in reducing water loss.

Echinopogon mckiei is adapted to arid environments, showcasing features that enable it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
