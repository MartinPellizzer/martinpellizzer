Erophila × chavinii

Erophila × chavinii, commonly known as the hybrid water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Alismataceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
