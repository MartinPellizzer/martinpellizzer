Eriogonum gnaphalodes

Eriogonum gnaphalodes, commonly known as the graybark buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grayish bark and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with thread-like leaflets and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing food for wildlife.

Eriogonum gnaphalodes is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaped gardens.
