Elegia elephantina

Elegia elephantina, commonly known as the elephant's foot plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, and often hairy stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its stems make it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal.
