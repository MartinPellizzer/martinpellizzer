Eurybia subrepanda

Eurybia subrepanda, commonly known as the common sedum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its hardiness and ability to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Eurybia subrepanda is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its low maintenance requirements.
