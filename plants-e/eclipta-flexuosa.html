Eclipta flexuosa

Eclipta flexuosa, commonly known as wild sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Eclipta flexuosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
