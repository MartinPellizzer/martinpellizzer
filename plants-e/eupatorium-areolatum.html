Eupatorium areolatum

Eupatorium areolatum, commonly known as the clustered boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and wet meadows.

Eupatorium areolatum is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments and is a member of the Asteraceae family.
