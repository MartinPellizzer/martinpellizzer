Equisetum scirpoides var. pedunculatum

Equisetum scirpoides var. pedunculatum, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are typically found in moist, shaded environments. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

This variety is notable for its primitive morphology and its role in ecological succession in wetland ecosystems.
