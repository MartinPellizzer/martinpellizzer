Epidendrum examinis

Epidendrum examinis, commonly known as the silver-leafed epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum examinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
