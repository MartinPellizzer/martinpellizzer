Erodium malacoides var. trilobatum

Erodium malacoides var. trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, opposite leaves that have a distinctive three-lobed shape. The plant produces small pink or purple flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
