Eriachne gracilis

Eriachne gracilis, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems are often slightly curved and bear pale green to brown flowers.

Eriachne gracilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
