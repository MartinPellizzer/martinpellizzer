Epipactis palustris subsp. salina

Epipactis palustris subsp.

salina, commonly known as the salt marsh helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal wetlands. It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in marshes and tidal flats.

The plant has erect, hairy stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. Its flowers are showy, with a unique structure featuring a spurred lip and a hooded labellum.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
