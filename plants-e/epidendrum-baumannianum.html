Epidendrum baumannianum

Epidendrum baumannianum, commonly known as the Baumann's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. The plant features long, arching stems with numerous lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, displaying vibrant colors ranging from yellow to orange, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
