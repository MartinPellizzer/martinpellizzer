Echinocactus schmiedickeanus

Echinocactus schmiedickeanus, commonly known as the Schmiedicke's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which are typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
