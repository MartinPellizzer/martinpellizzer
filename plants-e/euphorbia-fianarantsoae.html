Euphorbia fianarantsoae

Euphorbia fianarantsoae, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
