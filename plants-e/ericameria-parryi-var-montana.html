Ericameria parryi var. montana

Ericameria parryi var. montana, commonly known as mountain ericameria, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-green foliage and yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky slopes and open woodlands at higher elevations.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
