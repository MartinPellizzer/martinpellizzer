Erica saptouensis

Erica saptouensis, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a species of heath plant native to the Western Cape province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its low, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Erica saptouensis is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region, with periods of drought and cold winters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
