Eragrostis paradoxa

Eragrostis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
