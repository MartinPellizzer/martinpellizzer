Epipactis atropurpurea var. leviconica

Epipactis atropurpurea var. leviconica, commonly known as the purple-flowered helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces erect, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its preference for partial to full shade.
