Euodia hortensis var. longifolia

Euodia hortensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces reddish-brown berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its aromatic leaves and bark contain compounds such as limonoids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile plant for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
