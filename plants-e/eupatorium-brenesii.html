Eupatorium brenesii

Eupatorium brenesii, commonly known as the Bahamian boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and branched.

Eupatorium brenesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
