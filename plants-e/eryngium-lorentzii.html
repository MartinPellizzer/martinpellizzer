Eryngium lorentzii

Eryngium lorentzii, commonly known as the mountain eryngo, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features striking blue to violet spiky flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly environments.

Eryngium lorentzii is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
