Eria pulchella

Eria pulchella, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eria pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
