Epacris rubra

Epacris rubra, commonly known as the red epacris, is an evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, tubular red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

Epacris rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
