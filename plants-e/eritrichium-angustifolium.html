Eritrichium angustifolium

Eritrichium angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
