Eulophia gastrodioides

Eulophia gastrodioides, commonly known as the stomach orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in the summer. The plant produces a greenish-yellow inflorescence with a distinct, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Eulophia gastrodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
