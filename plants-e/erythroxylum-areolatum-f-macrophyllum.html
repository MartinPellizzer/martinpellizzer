Erythroxylum areolatum f. macrophyllum

Erythroxylum areolatum f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf copal tree, is a variant of the Erythroxylum genus native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
