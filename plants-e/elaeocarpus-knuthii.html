Elaeocarpus knuthii

Elaeocarpus knuthii, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
