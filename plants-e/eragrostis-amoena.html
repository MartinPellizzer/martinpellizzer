Eragrostis amoena

Eragrostis amoena, commonly known as the silky lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to reddish spikelets that are typically arranged in loose, open panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis amoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
