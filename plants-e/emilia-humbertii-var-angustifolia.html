Emilia humbertii var. angustifolia

Emilia humbertii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emilia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun conditions.
