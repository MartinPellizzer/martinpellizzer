Echinocereus chiloensis

Echinocereus chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Chile and Peru.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spiny areoles, which bear clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its thick, water-storing stems.

Echinocereus chiloensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
