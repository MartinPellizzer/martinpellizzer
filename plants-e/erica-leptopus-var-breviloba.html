Erica leptopus var. breviloba

Erica leptopus var. breviloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It features small, dense clusters of needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in a fine, white wax. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and pale purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to harsh, sunny conditions.

Erica leptopus var. breviloba is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
