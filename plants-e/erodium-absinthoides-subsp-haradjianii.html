Erodium absinthoides subsp. haradjianii

Erodium absinthoides subsp.

haradjianii, commonly known as the Haradjian's common bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant produces small, triangular fruits known as schizocarps, which disperse via wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
