Elaeagnus wushanensis

Elaeagnus wushanensis, commonly known as the Wushan silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Elaeagnus wushanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
