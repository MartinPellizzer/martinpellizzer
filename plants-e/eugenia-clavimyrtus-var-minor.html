Eugenia clavimyrtus var. minor

Eugenia clavimyrtus var. minor, commonly known as the small-flowered myrtle, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
