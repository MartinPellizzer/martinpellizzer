Eugenia globosa

Eugenia globosa, commonly known as the globe guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers followed by round, red or yellow berries. These berries are edible, with a sweet, acidic flavor and are often used in jams and beverages.

Eugenia globosa is valued for its ornamental appearance and culinary uses in its native habitat.
