Eriocaulon rayatianum

Eriocaulon rayatianum, commonly known as the Rayatianum flat sedge, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often inconspicuous. Its root system helps in stabilizing the soil, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in certain regions.
