Eremophila pterocarpa subsp. pterocarpa

Eremophila pterocarpa subsp.

pterocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and succulent stems.

This subspecies is often found in sandy or rocky soils across the southeastern and southwestern regions of Australia.
