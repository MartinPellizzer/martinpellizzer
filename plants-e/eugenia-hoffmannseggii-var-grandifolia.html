Eugenia hoffmannseggii var. grandifolia

Eugenia hoffmannseggii var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
