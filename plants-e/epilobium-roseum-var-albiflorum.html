Epilobium roseum var. albiflorum

Epilobium roseum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
