Echinocereus paucispinus var. triglochidiatus

Echinocereus paucispinus var. triglochidiatus, commonly known as the silver needle cactus, is a spineless cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with a bluish-gray color and is covered in white, powdery wax that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attractive. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh desert conditions.
