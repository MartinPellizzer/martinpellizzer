Eleocharis albida var. berlandieri

Eleocharis albida var. berlandieri, commonly known as the white bulrush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This variant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, often found in wetland habitats. The plant has narrow, white to pale green leaves that form tussocks, and it blooms with tiny white flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wetland wildlife.
