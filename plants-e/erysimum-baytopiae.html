Erysimum baytopiae

Erysimum baytopiae, commonly known as the Baytop's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum baytopiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
