It is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds with a slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Elaphoglossum salvinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady gardens.
