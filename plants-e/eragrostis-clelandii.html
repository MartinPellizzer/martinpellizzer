Eragrostis clelandii

Eragrostis clelandii, commonly known as Cleland's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis clelandii is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
