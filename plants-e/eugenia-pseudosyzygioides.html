Eugenia pseudosyzygioides

Eugenia pseudosyzygioides, commonly known as the purple-leafed eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green to purple leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species in its native habitat.
