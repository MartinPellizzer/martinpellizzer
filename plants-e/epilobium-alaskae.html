Epilobium alaskae

Epilobium alaskae, commonly known as Alaska willow, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and alpine tundra. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish, and clustered in terminal spikes, blooming in early summer.

Epilobium alaskae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
