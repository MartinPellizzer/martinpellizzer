Elaphoglossum melanostictum

Elaphoglossum melanostictum, commonly known as the black-stained holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, spore-bearing sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in groups called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
