Eucalyptus decipiens

Eucalyptus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive eucalyptus, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of development. Its flowers are followed by woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Eucalyptus decipiens is a hardy species that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
