Eleogenus ovatus

Eleogenus ovatus, commonly known as the oval leaved eleogenus, is a lesser-known plant species within the Eleogenus genus.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the leaves. It is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying plant biodiversity.
