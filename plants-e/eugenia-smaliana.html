Eugenia smaliana

Eugenia smaliana, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright purple fruits that are highly valued for their sweet, tangy flavor and medicinal properties. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and small, clustered flowers.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
