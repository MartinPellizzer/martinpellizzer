Erysimum holboellii

Erysimum holboellii, commonly known as the Holboell's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Erysimum holboellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
