Eria pyrrhotricha

Eria pyrrhotricha, commonly known as the red-hair orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
