Euphorbia kangdingensis var. puberula

Euphorbia kangdingensis var. puberula, commonly known as the Kangding euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
