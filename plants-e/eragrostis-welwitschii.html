Eragrostis welwitschii

Eragrostis welwitschii, commonly known as Welwitsch's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savanna ecosystems.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and erosion control.
