Elaphoglossum diversifrons

Elaphoglossum diversifrons, commonly known as the variable holly fern, is a delicate fern species belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnate fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of silky, brownish hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its spores are released from small, clustered sorus structures on the underside of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum diversifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
