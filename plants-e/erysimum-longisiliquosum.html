Erysimum longisiliquosum

Erysimum longisiliquosum, commonly known as the long silique mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known mustard plants. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are upright and branched. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces long, slender pods called silique.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
