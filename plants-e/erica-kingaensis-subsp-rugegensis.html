Erica kingaensis subsp. rugegensis

Erica kingaensis subsp.

rugegensis, commonly known as the Kingaensis heath, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Erica kingaensis subsp.

rugegensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
