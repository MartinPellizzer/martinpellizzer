Eurybia picridifolia

Eurybia picridifolia, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eurybia picridifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
