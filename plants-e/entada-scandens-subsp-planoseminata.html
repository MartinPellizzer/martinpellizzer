Entada scandens subsp. planoseminata

Entada scandens subsp.

planoseminata, commonly known as the African sycamore or African trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, red or orange flowers that appear in long, drooping clusters.

The tree produces long, cylindrical pods containing flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and riverine areas.

The bark and seeds of this species have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
