Eugenia cabofriana

Eugenia cabofriana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cabofriana myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia cabofriana is also valued for its aromatic wood and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
