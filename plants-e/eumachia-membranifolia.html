Eumachia membranifolia

Eumachia membranifolia, commonly known as the paper-leaf eumachia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and thin leaves that give it its common name, which resemble paper in texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white or pale yellow, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Eumachia membranifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
