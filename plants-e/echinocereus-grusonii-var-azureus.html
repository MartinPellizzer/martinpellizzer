Echinocereus grusonii var. azureus

Echinocereus grusonii var. azureus, commonly known as the Azure Hedgehog Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its blue-green stems and spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, white coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
