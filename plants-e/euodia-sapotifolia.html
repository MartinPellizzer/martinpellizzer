Euodia sapotifolia

Euodia sapotifolia, commonly known as the Chinese sandpaper tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
