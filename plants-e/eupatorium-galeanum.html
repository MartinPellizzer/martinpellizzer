Eupatorium galeanum

Eupatorium galeanum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which range from white to pale blue, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Eupatorium galeanum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
