Eriope hypoleuca

Eriope hypoleuca, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eriope hypoleuca is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
