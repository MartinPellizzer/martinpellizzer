Erigeron viscidus

Erigeron viscidus, commonly known as Sticky Fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, sticky leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Erigeron viscidus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
