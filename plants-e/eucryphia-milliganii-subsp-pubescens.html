Eucryphia milliganii subsp. pubescens

Eucryphia milliganii subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent eucryphia, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand. It features prominently branched stems covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Its leaves are oval, hairy, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is well adapted to coastal environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
