Eriocaulon spruceanum f. fluitans

Eriocaulon spruceanum f. fluitans, commonly known as the floating erythronium, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant exhibits a rosette growth pattern with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters at the base of the leaves.

This species is adapted to still or slow-moving waters and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
