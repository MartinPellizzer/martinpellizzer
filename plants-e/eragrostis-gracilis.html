Eragrostis gracilis

Eragrostis gracilis, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa and the Americas.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a height ranging from 30 to 100 centimeters.

Eragrostis gracilis is often used in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and drought tolerance.
