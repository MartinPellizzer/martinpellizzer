Ericoila douglasiana

Ericoila douglasiana, commonly known as the Douglas's heath, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms in the spring or early summer.

Ericoila douglasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
