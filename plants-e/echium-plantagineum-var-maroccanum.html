Echium plantagineum var. maroccanum

Echium plantagineum var. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is an annual species that produces copious amounts of nectar, attracting various pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
