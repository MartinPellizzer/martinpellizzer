Evaiezoa hirta

Evaiezoa hirta, commonly known as the hairy evaiezoa, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Evaiezoa hirta is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
