Eriosema ramosum

Eriosema ramosum, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Eriosema ramosum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.
