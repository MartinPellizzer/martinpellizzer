Escobedia scabrifolia

Escobedia scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf escobedia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant's foliage is typically grayish-green with a rough, textured surface, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Escobedia scabrifolia produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season, adding a striking contrast to its desolate appearance.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and succulent enthusiasts alike.
