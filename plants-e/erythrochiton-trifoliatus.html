Erythrochiton trifoliatus

Erythrochiton trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaf erythrochiton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is typically found in tropical rainforests. It produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
