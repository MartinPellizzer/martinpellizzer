Eriogonum ochrocephalum var. angustum

Eriogonum ochrocephalum var. angustum, commonly known as narrowleaf buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to reddish-brown. The leaves are narrow and often hairy, adapting to arid environments. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological resilience.
