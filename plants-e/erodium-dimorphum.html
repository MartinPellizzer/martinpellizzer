Erodium dimorphum

Erodium dimorphum, commonly known as the common stinkcup, is a flowering plant native to western and southern Europe.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, cup-like structure.

Erodium dimorphum is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
