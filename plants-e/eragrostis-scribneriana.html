Eragrostis scribneriana

Eragrostis scribneriana, commonly known as Scribner's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
