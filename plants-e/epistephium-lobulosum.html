Epistephium lobulosum

Epistephium lobulosum, commonly known as the clustered tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Leguminosae family.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
