Eremopanax canalensis

Eremopanax canalensis, commonly known as the Canal Island emerald tree, is a rare and endemic plant species found only on the island of Canal in the Solomon Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and unique, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. Due to its limited distribution and habitat-specific requirements, Eremopanax canalensis is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat destruction.

Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure its survival in the wild.
