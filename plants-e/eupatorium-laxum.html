Eupatorium laxum

Eupatorium laxum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along streams, preferring moist to wet soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium laxum is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
