Erucastrum meruense subsp. balense

Erucastrum meruense subsp.

balense, commonly known as the East African wallflower, is a perennial herb native to East Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
