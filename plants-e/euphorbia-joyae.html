Euphorbia joyae

Euphorbia joyae, commonly known as the Joy's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, woody stem with clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.

Euphorbia joyae is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many other spurge species known for their medicinal and decorative uses.
