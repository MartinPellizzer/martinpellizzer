Erica exsurgens var. grandiflora

Erica exsurgens var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
