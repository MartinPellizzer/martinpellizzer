Erythraea caespitosa

Erythraea caespitosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pink or red flowers. Its botanical name reflects its habitat and growth form, with "caespitosa" referring to its tufted or clump-forming growth pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
