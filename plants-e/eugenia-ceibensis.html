Eugenia ceibensis

Eugenia ceibensis, commonly known as the ceiba tree, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a large, deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, buttressed trunk and a dense canopy. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
