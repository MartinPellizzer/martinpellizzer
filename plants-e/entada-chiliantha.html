Entada chiliantha

Entada chiliantha, commonly known as the Brazilian tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and long, slender pods. The tree produces a yellowish, acidic fruit that is used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its bark and seeds contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
