Eugenia cabelludo var. glazioviana

Eugenia cabelludo var. glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziovian wax myrtle, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
