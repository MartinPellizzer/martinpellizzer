Ellisia nyctelea

Ellisia nyctelea, commonly known as the night-flowering moonflower, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, yellowish seeds.
