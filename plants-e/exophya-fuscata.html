Exophya fuscata

Exophya fuscata, commonly known as the dark velvet fern, is a unique and intriguing member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures are concealed beneath the foliage, a feature that sets it apart from many other fern species.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
