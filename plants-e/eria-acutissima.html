Eria acutissima

Eria acutissima, commonly known as the sharp-leaved orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its elegant, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a sharp tip, giving it its common name.

Eria acutissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
