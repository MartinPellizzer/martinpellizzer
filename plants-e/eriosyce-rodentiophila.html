Eriosyce rodentiophila

Eriosyce rodentiophila, commonly known as the rodent-eating cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Despite its common name, it does not actually consume rodents but gets its name from the shape of its spines, which resemble the teeth of rodents.
