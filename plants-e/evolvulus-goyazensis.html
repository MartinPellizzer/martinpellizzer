Evolvulus goyazensis

Evolvulus goyazensis, commonly known as the Brazilian blue flower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

Evolvulus goyazensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
