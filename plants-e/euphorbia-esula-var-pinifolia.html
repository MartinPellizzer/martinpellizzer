Euphorbia esula var. pinifolia

Euphorbia esula var. pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive pine-like leaves that are narrow and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive potential in some areas, it is sometimes considered a problematic weed.
