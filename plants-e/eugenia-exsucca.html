Eugenia exsucca

Eugenia exsucca, commonly known as the common wax apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its small, waxy, apple-like fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
