Eucalyptus sparsiflora

Eucalyptus sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered eucalypt, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and smooth, often silver-gray bark. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Eucalyptus sparsiflora is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.
