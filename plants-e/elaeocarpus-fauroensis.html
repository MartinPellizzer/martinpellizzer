Elaeocarpus fauroensis

Elaeocarpus fauroensis, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in secondary vegetation.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
