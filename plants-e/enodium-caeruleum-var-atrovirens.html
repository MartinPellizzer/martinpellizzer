Enodium caeruleum var. atrovirens

Enodium caeruleum var. atrovirens, commonly known as the dark green enodium, is a variant of the enodium plant characterized by its deep green foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue and purple. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its foliage.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
