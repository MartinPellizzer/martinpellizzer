Erysimum cuspidatum

Erysimum cuspidatum, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its striking appearance and ecological value make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
