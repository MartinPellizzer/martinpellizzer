Erigeron diffusus

Erigeron diffusus, commonly known as the spreading fleabane, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Erigeron diffusus is often found in open fields, meadows, and disturbed areas, where it thrives in full sun.
