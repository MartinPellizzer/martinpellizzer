Eupatorium lucidum

Eupatorium lucidum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
