Elaterium coulteri

Elaterium coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's elaterium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds with a hard, angular coat. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
