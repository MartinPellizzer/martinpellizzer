Euchroma coccinea

Euchroma coccinea, commonly known as the red orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Euchroma coccinea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional practices.
