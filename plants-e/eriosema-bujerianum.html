Eriosema bujerianum

Eriosema bujerianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf eriosema, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Eriosema bujerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
