Eriogonum fendlerianum

Eriogonum fendlerianum, commonly known as the desert gourd or desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum fendlerianum is adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils.
