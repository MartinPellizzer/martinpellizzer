Echinodorus piauhyensis

Echinodorus piauhyensis, commonly known as the Piauhy waterweed, is a aquatic plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. It is often used in aquariums due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its aesthetic appeal.

Echinodorus piauhyensis is also valued for its role in water filtration and providing habitat for aquatic life.
