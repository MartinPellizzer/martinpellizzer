Erysimum stevenianum

Erysimum stevenianum, commonly known as the Steven's wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers, which are characteristic of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, with a distinct aromatic scent.

Erysimum stevenianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
