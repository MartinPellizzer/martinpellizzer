Eugenia flavida

Eugenia flavida, commonly known as the feijoa, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which is typically harvested when fully ripe, has a unique flavor combining tropical and citrus notes. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
