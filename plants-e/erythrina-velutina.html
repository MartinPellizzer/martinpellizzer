Erythrina velutina

Erythrina velutina, commonly known as the velvet flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, making it a visually stunning ornamental plant. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, sunny climates.

Erythrina velutina is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
