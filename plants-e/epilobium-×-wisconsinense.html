Epilobium × wisconsinense

Epilobium × wisconsinense, commonly known as the Wisconsin willow herb, is a hybrid plant species native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually reddish in color.

Epilobium × wisconsinense plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
