Erigeron alpinus var. polytrichus

Erigeron alpinus var. polytrichus, commonly known as the alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its flowers are blue to violet in color, attracting various pollinators.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
