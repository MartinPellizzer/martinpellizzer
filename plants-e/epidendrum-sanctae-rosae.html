Epidendrum sanctae-rosae

Epidendrum sanctae-rosae, commonly known as the Holy Rose Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure, producing multiple leaves and inflorescences. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive red lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and well-draining soil.

This species is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
