Epidendrum tuerckheimii

Epidendrum tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Epidendrum tuerckheimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
