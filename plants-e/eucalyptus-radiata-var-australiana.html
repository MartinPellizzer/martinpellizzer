Eucalyptus radiata var. australiana

Eucalyptus radiata var. australiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the radiata eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrubs with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
