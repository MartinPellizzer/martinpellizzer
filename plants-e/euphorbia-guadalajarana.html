Euphorbia guadalajarana

Euphorbia guadalajarana, commonly known as the Guadalajara spurge, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens in regions with warm, dry climates.
