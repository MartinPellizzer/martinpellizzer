Eucalyptus platypus subsp. congregata

Eucalyptus platypus subsp.

congregata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. It is commonly found in heathlands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
