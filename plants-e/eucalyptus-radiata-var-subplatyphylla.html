Eucalyptus radiata var. subplatyphylla

Eucalyptus radiata var. subplatyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often blue-gray in color, and it produces small, globular clusters of white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and fire resilience. It is often found in open woodlands and on sandy soils, and it plays an important ecological role in supporting native wildlife.

Its essential oils are also used in traditional and modern applications, including aromatherapy and medicinal uses.
