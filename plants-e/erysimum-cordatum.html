Erysimum cordatum

Erysimum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped, and it produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and resilience.
