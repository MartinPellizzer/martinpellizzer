Eriophorum × intercedens

Eriophorum × intercedens, commonly known as the tussock grass or common cottongrass, is a hybrid plant species within the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces fluffy, white seed heads that resemble cotton. The plant typically thrives in wet, boggy environments and is often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are sturdy and often reddish in color.

Eriophorum × intercedens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
