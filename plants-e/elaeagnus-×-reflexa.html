Elaeagnus × reflexa

Elaeagnus × reflexa, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, scale-like leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, which are consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen.

Elaeagnus × reflexa is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
