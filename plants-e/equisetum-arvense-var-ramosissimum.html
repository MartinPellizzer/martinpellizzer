Equisetum arvense var. ramosissimum

Equisetum arvense var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the field horsetail, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of needle-like leaves and a hollow, jointed stem that is typically green to yellow-green in color. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of the stem, which are important for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it an important component of certain wetland ecosystems.
