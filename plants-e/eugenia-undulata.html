Eugenia undulata

Eugenia undulata, commonly known as the wavy-eared guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that have a wavy margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is relatively low-maintenance and can be grown in well-drained soils.
