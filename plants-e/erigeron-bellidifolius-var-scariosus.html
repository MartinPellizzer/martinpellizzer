Erigeron bellidifolius var. scariosus

Erigeron bellidifolius var. scariosus, commonly known as the scaly fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features narrow, gray-green leaves with a distinctive scaly texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing both ray and disk florets.

The species is often found in chaparral and desert scrub environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.
