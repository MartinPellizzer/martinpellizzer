Epidendrum bradeanum

Epidendrum bradeanum, commonly known as the Bradean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it thrives in well-draining soil with moderate to high humidity.

Epidendrum bradeanum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
