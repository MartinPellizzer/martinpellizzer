Eleutherococcus trichodon

Eleutherococcus trichodon, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Hippocastanaceae and is valued for its adaptogenic properties.
