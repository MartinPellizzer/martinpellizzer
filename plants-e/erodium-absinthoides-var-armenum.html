Erodium absinthoides var. armenum

Erodium absinthoides var. armenum, commonly known as Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
