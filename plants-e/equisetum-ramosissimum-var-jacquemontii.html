Equisetum ramosissimum var. jacquemontii

Equisetum ramosissimum var. jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems that are typically green to brown in color. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stems, which are a distinctive feature of horsetails. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period.
