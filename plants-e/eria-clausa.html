Eria clausa

Eria clausa, commonly known as the closed eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often clustered in spikes. Its leaves are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Eria clausa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
