Euclea oppositifolia

Euclea oppositifolia, commonly known as the blue oat grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted, blue-green foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats. Its leaves are opposite in arrangement, giving it its scientific name, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Euclea oppositifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
