Eupatorium flaccidum

Eupatorium flaccidum, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its ability to thrive in moist or waterlogged soils.

Eupatorium flaccidum is also a valuable host plant for monarch butterflies.
