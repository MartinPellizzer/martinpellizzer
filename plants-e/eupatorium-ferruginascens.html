Eupatorium ferruginascens

Eupatorium ferruginascens, commonly known as iron-stem eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, often reddish-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

Eupatorium ferruginascens is often found in moist, open habitats such as swamps and woodland edges.
