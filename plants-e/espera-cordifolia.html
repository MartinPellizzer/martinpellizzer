Espera cordifolia

Espera cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf esperanza, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in landscaping.

Espera cordifolia is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
