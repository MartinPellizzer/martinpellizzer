Echites umbellatus subsp. crassipes

Echites umbellatus subsp.

crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved echites, is a member of the Apiaceae family. It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, umbrella-like inflorescences composed of small white or greenish flowers.

The subspecies is native to southern Africa and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are typically broad and pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
