Euphorbia portulacoides var. bridgesii

Euphorbia portulacoides var. bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridges' milkweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, fleshy stem with small, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and leaves.

This variety is known for its resilience and is often used in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.
