Eria lohitensis

Eria lohitensis, commonly known as the Lohit orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers, which are usually pale green or yellowish, bloom in summer and attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eria lohitensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
