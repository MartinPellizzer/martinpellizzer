Elaeagnus umbellata var. globosa

Elaeagnus umbellata var. globosa, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
