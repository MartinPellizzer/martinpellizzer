Eugenia aemilii

Eugenia aemilii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Eugenia aemilii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
