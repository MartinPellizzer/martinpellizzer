Echinochloa pithopus

Echinochloa pithopus, commonly known as rough fingergrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Echinochloa pithopus is an annual plant with a short life cycle, typically completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.
