Echinocactus sellowii var. macracanthus

Echinocactus sellowii var. macracanthus, commonly known as the Sowden's Hedgehog Cactus, is a distinctive cactus native to the arid regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
