Euphorbia persistentifolia

Euphorbia persistentifolia, commonly known as the persistentleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to arid environments. Its sap contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun.
