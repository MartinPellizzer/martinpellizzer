Eremanthus harleyi

Eremanthus harleyi, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow ray flowers that resemble small bells, giving it its common name. It typically grows in wetland areas and is often found in swamps or along the edges of ponds.

Eremanthus harleyi has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
