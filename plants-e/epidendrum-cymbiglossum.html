Epidendrum cymbiglossum

Epidendrum cymbiglossum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence is a raceme of numerous small, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Epidendrum cymbiglossum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
