Erigeron philippii var. coxii

Erigeron philippii var. coxii, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its blue to purple flower heads appear in summer, and the leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
