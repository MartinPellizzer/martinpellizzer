Erica trossula

Erica trossula, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Erica trossula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various small organisms.
