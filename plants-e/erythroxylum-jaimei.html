Erythroxylum jaimei

Erythroxylum jaimei, commonly known as the Jaimei copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, though it is not as well-known as other members of the Erythroxylum genus.
