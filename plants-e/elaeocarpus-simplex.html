Elaeocarpus simplex

Elaeocarpus simplex, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions for horticultural purposes.
