Echinolaena trinii

Echinolaena trinii, commonly known as the three-leaf burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in bracts that form a burr-like structure. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, with a basal rosette formation.

Echinolaena trinii is adapted to disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
