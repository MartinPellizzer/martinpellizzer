Echinocactus tricornis

Echinocactus tricornis, commonly known as the three-horned cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and the presence of three prominent spines at the top, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often borne on short, lateral branches. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
