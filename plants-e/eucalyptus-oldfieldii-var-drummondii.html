Eucalyptus oldfieldii var. drummondii

Eucalyptus oldfieldii var. drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in mallee and dry woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
