Ericoides luteoalbum

Ericoides luteoalbum, commonly known as the yellow-white heath, is a member of the Ericaceae family, characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and showy, clustered flowers.

This plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas. Its flowers, which are yellowish-white in color, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is noted for its slow growth and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain regions.
