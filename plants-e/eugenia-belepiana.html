Eugenia belepiana

Eugenia belepiana, commonly known as the Bismarck Island clove, is a small evergreen tree native to the Bismarck Archipelago in Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use due to its restricted natural range.
