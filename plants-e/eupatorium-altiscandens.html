Eupatorium altiscandens

Eupatorium altiscandens, commonly known as the tall boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium altiscandens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
