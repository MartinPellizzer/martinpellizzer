Erica grandinosa

Erica grandinosa, commonly known as the Cape heath or yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica grandinosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also valued for its resilience and low maintenance requirements in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
