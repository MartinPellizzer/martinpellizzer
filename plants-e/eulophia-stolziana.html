Eulophia stolziana

Eulophia stolziana, commonly known as the Stolz's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinct lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
