Eupatorium commersonii

Eupatorium commersonii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is characterized by its silvery-gray underside, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium commersonii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
