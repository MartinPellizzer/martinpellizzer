Ericoides deflexum

Ericoides deflexum, commonly known as the curled heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions make it well-suited for specific ecological niches.

Ericoides deflexum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native range.
