Epiphyllum violaceum

Epiphyllum violaceum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing cactus that produces large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant is native to Central America and has a stem that can grow up to several meters in length. Its leaves are usually modified into spines, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers are typically pink or purple and emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators.
