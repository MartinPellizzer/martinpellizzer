Eucyperus

Eucyperus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cyperaceae.

These plants are typically found in wetland environments, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Eucyperus species are characterized by their grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. They often form dense colonies, which help in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Some species of Eucyperus are used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
