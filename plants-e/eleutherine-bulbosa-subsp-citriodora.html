Eleutherine bulbosa subsp. citriodora

Eleutherine bulbosa subsp.

citriodora, commonly known as lemon-scented eleutherine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tuberous roots and slender, grass-like leaves that emit a distinct lemony fragrance.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique scent, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
