Edmondia pinifolia

Edmondia pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf edmondia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble those of pine trees, which help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by the development of follicles containing seeds. Edmondia pinifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh climates make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
