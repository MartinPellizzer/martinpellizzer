Eucalyptus polyanthemos

Eucalyptus polyanthemos, commonly known as the honey gum, is a tall, slender tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a bright yellow inner layer. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a glossy upper surface.

Eucalyptus polyanthemos is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native wildlife.
