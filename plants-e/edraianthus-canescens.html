Edraianthus canescens

Edraianthus canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf erysiphum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Edraianthus canescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate, daisy-like flowers and drought tolerance.
