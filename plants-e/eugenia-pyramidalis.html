Eugenia pyramidalis

Eugenia pyramidalis, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor, resembling a cross between a pineapple and a guava. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

The botanical features of Eugenia pyramidalis make it a popular ornamental and edible plant in gardens and commercial cultivation.
