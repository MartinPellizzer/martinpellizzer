Elytropappus spinulosus

Elytropappus spinulosus, commonly known as the spiny leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance. The plant features narrow, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Elytropappus spinulosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
