Eucalyptus interstans

Eucalyptus interstans, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia, specifically found in the southern regions of Western Australia. This plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. Eucalyptus interstans typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It produces small, spherical flower spikes that are usually pink or cream in color, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
