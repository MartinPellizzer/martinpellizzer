Eugenia serrasuela

Eugenia serrasuela, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pataua, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia serrasuela is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
