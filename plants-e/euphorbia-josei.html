Euphorbia josei

Euphorbia josei, commonly known as the Japanese spurge, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Euphorbia josei is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
