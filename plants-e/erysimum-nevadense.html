Erysimum nevadense

Erysimum nevadense, commonly known as the Sierra wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Erysimum nevadense is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages.
