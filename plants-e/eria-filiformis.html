Eria filiformis

Eria filiformis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its growth habit is epiphytic, meaning it often grows on trees or rocks rather than in the ground.

Eria filiformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
