Euphorbia buchananii

Euphorbia buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, which is characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with a branching pattern that allows it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Euphorbia buchananii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
