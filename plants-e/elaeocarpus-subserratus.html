Elaeocarpus subserratus

Elaeocarpus subserratus, commonly known as the serrated elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
