Erica platycodon

Erica platycodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Erica platycodon thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and resilience.
