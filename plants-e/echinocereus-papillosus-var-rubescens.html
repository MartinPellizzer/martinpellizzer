Echinocereus papillosus var. rubescens

Echinocereus papillosus var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles and bright red tips on its tubercles. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
