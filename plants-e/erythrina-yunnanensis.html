Erythrina yunnanensis

Erythrina yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan coral tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its seeds are enclosed in distinctive, spiny pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
