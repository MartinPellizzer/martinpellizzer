Eugenia leonanii

Eugenia leonanii, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil.
