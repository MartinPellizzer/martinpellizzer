Eremurus brachystemon

Eremurus brachystemon, commonly known as the desert candle, is a striking plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous yellow to orange-colored flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy, spiny bracts that protect the developing flower heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme drought conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
