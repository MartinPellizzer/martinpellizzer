Erysimum komarovii

Erysimum komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Erysimum komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
