Eurya domaensis

Eurya domaensis, commonly known as the Himalayan waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
