Eriogonum trachygonum subsp. wrightii

Eriogonum trachygonum subsp.

wrightii, commonly known as Wright's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
