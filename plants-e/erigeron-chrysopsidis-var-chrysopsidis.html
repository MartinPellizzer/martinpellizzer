Erigeron chrysopsidis var. chrysopsidis

Erigeron chrysopsidis var. chrysopsidis, commonly known as the golden blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
