Eryngium junceum subsp. juncifolium

Eryngium junceum subsp.

juncifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by pale green bracts.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often ornamental species in horticulture.
