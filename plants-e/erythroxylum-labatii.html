Erythroxylum labatii

Erythroxylum labatii, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a rough texture and can have a slight pubescence.

Erythroxylum labatii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
