Ericoides setulosum

Ericoides setulosum, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and the production of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in late spring.

Ericoides setulosum is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
