Erica capensis

Erica capensis, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Erica capensis is known for its resilience to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
