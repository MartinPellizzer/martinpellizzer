Echinops sphaerocephalus subsp. taygeteus

Echinops sphaerocephalus subsp.

taygeteus, commonly known as the Greek thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble small globes, which are a key feature of the echinops genus.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
