Elaeocarpus dalechampioides

Elaeocarpus dalechampioides, commonly known as the broadleaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically encased in a fleshy, reddish outer covering. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
