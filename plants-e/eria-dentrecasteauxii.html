Eria dentrecasteauxii

Eria dentrecasteauxii, commonly known as the toothed eria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
