Erigeron setchellii

Erigeron setchellii, commonly known as the Setchell's fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

Erigeron setchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
