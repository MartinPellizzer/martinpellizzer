Eugenia rigida

Eugenia rigida, commonly known as the rigid eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of eastern Australia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible fruits that are a food source for various wildlife.

Eugenia rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
