Eunomia oppositifolia

Eunomia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved eunomia, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves are paired along the stem at regular intervals. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, adding to its ornamental appeal. Eunomia oppositifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
