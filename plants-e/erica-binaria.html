Erica binaria

Erica binaria, commonly known as the two-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Erica binaria typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
