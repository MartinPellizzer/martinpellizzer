Emilia djalonensis

Emilia djalonensis, commonly known as the West African emilia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

Emilia djalonensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
