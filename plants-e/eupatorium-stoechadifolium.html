Eupatorium stoechadifolium

Eupatorium stoechadifolium, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often branching near the top. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in panicle-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Eupatorium stoechadifolium is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
