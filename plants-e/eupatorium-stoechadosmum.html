Eupatorium stoechadosmum

Eupatorium stoechadosmum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-to-purple inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
