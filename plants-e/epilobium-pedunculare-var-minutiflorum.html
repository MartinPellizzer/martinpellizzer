Epilobium pedunculare var. minutiflorum

Epilobium pedunculare var. minutiflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems that often grow in moist or wet habitats. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
