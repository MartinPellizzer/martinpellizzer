Echeveria × securifera

Echeveria × securifera, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a hybrid succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The leaves are covered in a fine, powdery coating called farina, which helps to reduce water loss. It produces vibrant clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring or summer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements.
