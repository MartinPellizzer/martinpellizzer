Eugenia izabalana

Eugenia izabalana, commonly known as the Izabal cherry or Izabal myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain a single seed, and they are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia izabalana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage.
