Elaeocarpus perim-kara

Elaeocarpus perim-kara, commonly known as the Perim-Kara elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds, typical of the genus Elaeocarpus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
