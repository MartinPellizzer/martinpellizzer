Eupatorium urticoides

Eupatorium urticoides, commonly known as boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Boneset is often found in moist habitats such as meadows, swamps, and stream banks.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported fever-reducing properties.
