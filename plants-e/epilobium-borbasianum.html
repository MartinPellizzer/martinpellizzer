Epilobium borbasianum

Epilobium borbasianum, commonly known as the Borbasian willowherb, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant features slender, often branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, clustered in dense spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
