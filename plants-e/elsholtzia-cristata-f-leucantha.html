Elsholtzia cristata f. leucantha

Elsholtzia cristata f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal medicine practices.
