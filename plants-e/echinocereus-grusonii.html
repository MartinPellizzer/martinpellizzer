Echinocereus grusonii

Echinocereus grusonii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branching stems and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that help it store water, a key adaptation for surviving in arid environments. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in horticultural displays.

Echinocereus grusonii prefers well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
