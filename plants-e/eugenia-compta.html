Eugenia compta

Eugenia compta, commonly known as the red-eyed wonder, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Eugenia compta thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical gardens.
