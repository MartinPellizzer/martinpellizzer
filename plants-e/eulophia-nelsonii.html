Eulophia nelsonii

Eulophia nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in two rows along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eulophia nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
