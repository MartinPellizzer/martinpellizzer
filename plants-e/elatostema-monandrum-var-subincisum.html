Elatostema monandrum var. subincisum

Elatostema monandrum var. subincisum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed elatostema, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often lobed or toothed, with a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces berries that are edible for some bird species.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
