Erodium jacquinianum subsp. bipinnatum

Erodium jacquinianum subsp.

bipinnatum, commonly known as the bipinnate erodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are finely divided into pinnate and bipinnate segments.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
