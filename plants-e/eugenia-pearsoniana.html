Eugenia pearsoniana

Eugenia pearsoniana, commonly known as the pearson's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit, which is small and reddish, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.
