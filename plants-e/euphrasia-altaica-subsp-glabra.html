Euphrasia altaica subsp. glabra

Euphrasia altaica subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth lady's tears, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
