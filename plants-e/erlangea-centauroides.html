Erlangea centauroides

Erlangea centauroides, commonly known as the centaur's shield, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique leaf shape resembles a shield, giving it its common name.

Erlangea centauroides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in drought conditions.
