Elatostema cupreoviride

Elatostema cupreoviride, commonly known as the copper-green elatostema, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually glossy and have a distinct copper-green coloration. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in the axils of the leaves.

Elatostema cupreoviride is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.
