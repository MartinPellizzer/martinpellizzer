Echium laevigatum

Echium laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Echium laevigatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
