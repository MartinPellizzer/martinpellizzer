Eremostachys napuligera

Eremostachys napuligera, commonly known as the Napuligera eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features a branched stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in a loose cluster and bloom in the summer months.

Eremostachys napuligera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
