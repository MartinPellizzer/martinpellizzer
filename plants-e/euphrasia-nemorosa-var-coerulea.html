Euphrasia nemorosa var. coerulea

Euphrasia nemorosa var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue wood betony, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with yellow markings, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in woodland or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
