Euphorbia massiliensis

Euphorbia massiliensis, commonly known as the Massiliensis Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a short, thick stem with prominent, spiny leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
