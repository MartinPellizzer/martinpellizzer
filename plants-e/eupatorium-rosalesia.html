Eupatorium rosalesia

Eupatorium rosalesia, commonly known as the Rosalesia eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features tall, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, pink to purple flower heads appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium rosalesia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
