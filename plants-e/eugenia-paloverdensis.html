Eugenia paloverdensis

Eugenia paloverdensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or paloverd, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
