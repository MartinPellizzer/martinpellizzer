Epidendrum funckianum

Epidendrum funckianum, commonly known as the Funck's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often arranged in racemes. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal among orchid enthusiasts.
