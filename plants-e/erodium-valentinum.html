Erodium valentinum

Erodium valentinum, commonly known as Valentine's parsley, is a small annual herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. Its seeds are tiny, ovoid, and often used as a spice in traditional cuisine.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family.
