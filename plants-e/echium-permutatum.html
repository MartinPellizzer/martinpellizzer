Echium permutatum

Echium permutatum, commonly known as the purple evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Echium permutatum is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
