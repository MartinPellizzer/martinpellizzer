Eria pudica

Eria pudica, commonly known as the sticky leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, waxy leaves that often have a sticky surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Eria pudica is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid environments.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
