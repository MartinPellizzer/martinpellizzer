Eugenia stigmatosa var. acutata

Eugenia stigmatosa var. acutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
