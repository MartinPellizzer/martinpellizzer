Echites obtusifolius

Echites obtusifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaved echites, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a sprawling growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a blunt tip, contributing to its common name.

Echites obtusifolius is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
