Epidendrum adenocarpon var. rosei

Epidendrum adenocarpon var. rosei, commonly known as the rose-flowered epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white to rose-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often epiphytic on trees or lithophytic on rocks. Its foliage consists of strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and waxy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
