Erica hebdomadalis

Erica hebdomadalis, commonly known as the seven-day heath, is a species of flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. Erica hebdomadalis typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, with a dormant period during the summer months.
