Enterolobium schomburgkii var. glaziovii

Enterolobium schomburgkii var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's burr legume, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers, which are often clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage and fruit structure.
