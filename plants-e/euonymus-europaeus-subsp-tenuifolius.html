Euonymus europaeus subsp. tenuifolius

Euonymus europaeus subsp.

tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woodbine, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are ornamental. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks.
