Erigeron anicularum var. latiusculus

Erigeron anicularum var. latiusculus, commonly known as the broadleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers, which bloom in the summer, are usually white or pale yellow with a central disk.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
