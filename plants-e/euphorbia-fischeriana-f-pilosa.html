Euphorbia fischeriana f. pilosa

Euphorbia fischeriana f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
