Eremophila calcicola

Eremophila calcicola, commonly known as the limestone emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils, particularly those rich in limestone, and is well adapted to withstand prolonged droughts.

Eremophila calcicola is an important component of the local flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
