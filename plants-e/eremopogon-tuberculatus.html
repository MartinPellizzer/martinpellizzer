Eremopogon tuberculatus

Eremopogon tuberculatus, commonly known as the scaly wheatgrass, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Eremopogon tuberculatus is an important component of its ecosystem, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
