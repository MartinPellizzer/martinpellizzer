Eremostachys laciniata var. rudbarica

Eremostachys laciniata var. rudbarica, commonly known as the Persian feather grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated or lobed edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to provide texture and movement in garden landscapes.
