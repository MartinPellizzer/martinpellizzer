Erigeron inornatus var. biolettii

Erigeron inornatus var. biolettii, commonly known as the Desert Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky areas, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in shades of blue and purple.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
