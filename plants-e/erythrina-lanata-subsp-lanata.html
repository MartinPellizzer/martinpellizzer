Erythrina lanata subsp. lanata

Erythrina lanata subsp.

lanata, commonly known as the woolly coral tree, is a species native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its striking red flowers and distinctive, velvety, hairy foliage.

The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

The bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or greyish hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.
