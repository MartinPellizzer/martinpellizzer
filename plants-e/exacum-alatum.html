Exacum alatum

Exacum alatum, commonly known as the winged ivy or spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue and purple. The leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
