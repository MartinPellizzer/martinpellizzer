Eucalyptus globulus subsp. bicostata

Eucalyptus globulus subsp.

bicostata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a subspecies of the common eucalyptus found primarily in southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom.

The plant produces small, globular flower buds that typically open to reveal white or pink flowers. Its bark is typically smooth and peels in strips, and it is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is known for its aromatic oils, which have various industrial and medicinal uses.
