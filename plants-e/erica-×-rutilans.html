Erica × rutilans

Erica × rutilans, commonly known as the golden heather, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange, depending on the cultivar. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Erica × rutilans is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and adaptability to various climates.
