Escallonia affinis

Escallonia affinis, commonly known as the Australian rose or scarlet glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in spring. Escallonia affinis is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of climates.
