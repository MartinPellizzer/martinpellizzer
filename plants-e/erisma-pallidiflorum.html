Erisma pallidiflorum

Erisma pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower erisma, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Erisma pallidiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
