Echinocactus fluctuosus

Echinocactus fluctuosus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, spherical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It has a slow growth rate and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Echinocactus fluctuosus is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
