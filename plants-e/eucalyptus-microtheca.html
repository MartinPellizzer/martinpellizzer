Eucalyptus microtheca

Eucalyptus microtheca, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to southern Australia, where it typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and rough, fissured bark. It produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Eucalyptus microtheca is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
