Euphrasia × difformis

Euphrasia × difformis, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a hybrid species within the Orobanchaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Euphrasia × difformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
