Elymus piperi

Elymus piperi, commonly known as western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, and it produces wind-pollinated flowers.

Elymus piperi is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
