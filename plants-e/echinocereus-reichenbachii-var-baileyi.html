Echinocereus reichenbachii var. baileyi

Echinocereus reichenbachii var. baileyi, commonly known as the Bailey's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a solitary, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that can grow up to 3 meters tall. Its spines are typically white to yellowish and are arranged in clusters along the ribs. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months.

It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid environments.
