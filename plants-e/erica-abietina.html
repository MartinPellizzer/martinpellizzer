Erica abietina

Erica abietina, commonly known as the dwarf heather, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica abietina thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging environmental conditions.
