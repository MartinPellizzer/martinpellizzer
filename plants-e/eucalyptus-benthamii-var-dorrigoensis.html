Eucalyptus benthamii var. dorrigoensis

Eucalyptus benthamii var. dorrigoensis, commonly known as the Dorrigo gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to the temperate rainforests of southeastern New South Wales.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pendulous, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, greyish bloom. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in globular inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. This variety is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in moist, cool environments with well-drained soil.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
