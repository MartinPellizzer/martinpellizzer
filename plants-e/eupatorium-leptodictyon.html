Eupatorium leptodictyon

Eupatorium leptodictyon, commonly known as slenderleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
