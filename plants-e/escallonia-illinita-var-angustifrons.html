Escallonia illinita var. angustifrons

Escallonia illinita var. angustifrons, commonly known as narrow-leaved escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for hedges or ornamental planting in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
