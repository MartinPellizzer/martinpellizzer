Epidendrum bothryanthum

Epidendrum bothryanthum, commonly known as the broad-leafed epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features long, arching stems that bear numerous lance-shaped leaves, which are often a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, three-lobed lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Epidendrum bothryanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a tropical or subtropical setting.
