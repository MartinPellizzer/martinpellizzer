Eriocaulon minutissimum

Eriocaulon minutissimum, commonly known as the minute spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers. This plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes covered with a fine, waxy coating.

Eriocaulon minutissimum is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
