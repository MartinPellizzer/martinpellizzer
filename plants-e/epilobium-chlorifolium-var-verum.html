Epilobium chlorifolium var. verum

Epilobium chlorifolium var. verum, commonly known as yellow willow or yellow stem.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and along the edges of ponds.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
