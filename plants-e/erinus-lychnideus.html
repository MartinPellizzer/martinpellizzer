Erinus lychnideus

Erinus lychnideus, commonly known as the evening snow or nightshade flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale purple flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a low, spreading habit with small, hairy leaves. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Erinus lychnideus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming pattern.
