Eriochilus dilatatus subsp. undulatus

Eriochilus dilatatus subsp.

undulatus, commonly known as the undulating eriochilus, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its undulating, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open heathlands and coastal areas.

This plant is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
