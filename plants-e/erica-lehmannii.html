Erica lehmannii

Erica lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late summer. Erica lehmannii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a container plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
