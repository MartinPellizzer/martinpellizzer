Echium fruticosum var. major

Echium fruticosum var. major, commonly known as the giant flax or large thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
