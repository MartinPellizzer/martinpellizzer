Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. ensifer

Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. ensifer, commonly known as the hairy echinofossulocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that provide insulation against extreme temperatures. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its fuzzy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized collections.
