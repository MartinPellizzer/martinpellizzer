Euphorbia tsukamotoi

Euphorbia tsukamotoi, commonly known as the Tsukamoto's spurge, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Euphorbia tsukamotoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in Asian herbal medicine.
