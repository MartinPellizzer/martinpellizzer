Erigeron hyoseroides

Erigeron hyoseroides, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing blue to purple ray florets and yellow disk florets.

Erigeron hyoseroides is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional purposes.
