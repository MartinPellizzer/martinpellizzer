Eucalyptus lehmannii subsp. lehmannii

Eucalyptus lehmannii subsp.

lehmannii, commonly known as the lemon-scented gum, is a species native to southern Australia. It is a tall, slender tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive lemon-like scent when crushed. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and they appear in clusters during the winter months.

This subspecies is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
