Eucalyptus leptopoda subsp. subluta

Eucalyptus leptopoda subsp.

subluta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is of interest for its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits in native landscaping.
