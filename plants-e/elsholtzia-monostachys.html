Elsholtzia monostachys

Elsholtzia monostachys, commonly known as the single-stemmed elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branching stems that often grow in clusters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Elsholtzia monostachys is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
