Echinops lasioclinius

Echinops lasioclinius, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its drought-resistant adaptations. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Echinops lasioclinius is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
