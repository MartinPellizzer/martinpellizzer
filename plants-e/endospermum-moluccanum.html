Endospermum moluccanum

Endospermum moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has a distinctive bark that is often fissured and peels in thin layers. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, and the fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.
