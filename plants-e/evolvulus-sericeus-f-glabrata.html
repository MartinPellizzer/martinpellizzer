Evolvulus sericeus f. glabrata

Evolvulus sericeus f. glabrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a variant of the species characterized by its smooth, hairless foliage.

This plant is a trailing herbaceous species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
