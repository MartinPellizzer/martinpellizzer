Escallonia grandiflora

Escallonia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered escallonia, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters and is well-suited for hedges or ornamental gardens. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.

Escallonia grandiflora is also valued for its fragrant leaves and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
