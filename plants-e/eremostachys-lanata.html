Eremostachys lanata

Eremostachys lanata, commonly known as the woolly eremostachys, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dry landscapes.
