Elatinoides spuria

Elatinoides spuria, commonly known as the slender elatin, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It belongs to the family Elatinaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by small, bead-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Elatinoides spuria plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
