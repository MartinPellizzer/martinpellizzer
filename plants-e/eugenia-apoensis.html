Eugenia apoensis

Eugenia apoensis, commonly known as the Apo mountain guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within montane forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, yellowish drupe with a sweet, fragrant flavor.

Eugenia apoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
