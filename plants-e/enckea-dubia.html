Enckea dubia

Enckea dubia, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for musical instruments and furniture. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red berries.

The tree is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
