Eriogonum plumatella var. palmeri

Eriogonum plumatella var. palmeri, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a short, branched stem and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its adaptability to drought conditions makes it a characteristic species of desert ecosystems.
