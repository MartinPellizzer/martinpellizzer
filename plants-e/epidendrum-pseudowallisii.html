Epidendrum pseudowallisii

Epidendrum pseudowallisii, commonly known as the Wallisii Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.

Epidendrum pseudowallisii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
