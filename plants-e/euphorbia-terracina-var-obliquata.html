Euphorbia terracina var. obliquata

Euphorbia terracina var. obliquata, commonly known as the Mediterranean spurge, is a shrub native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that are often covered in a waxy, pale green cuticle. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common weed in arid and semi-arid regions.
