Enkianthus sinohimalaicus

Enkianthus sinohimalaicus, commonly known as the Himalayan enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Enkianthus sinohimalaicus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
