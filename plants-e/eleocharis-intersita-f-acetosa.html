Eleocharis intersita f. acetosa

Eleocharis intersita f. acetosa, commonly known as the sourgrass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized growth habits.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps it retain moisture.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
