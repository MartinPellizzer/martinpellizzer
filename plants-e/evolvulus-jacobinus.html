Evolvulus jacobinus

Evolvulus jacobinus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
