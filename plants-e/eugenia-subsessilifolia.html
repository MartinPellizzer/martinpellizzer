Eugenia subsessilifolia

Eugenia subsessilifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or small-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often hairless. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red, edible fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
