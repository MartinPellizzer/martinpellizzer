Erica saturejoides

Erica saturejoides, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage composed of small, leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica saturejoides is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
