Eucalyptus redunca

Eucalyptus redunca, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance through its deep root system.

Eucalyptus redunca plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
