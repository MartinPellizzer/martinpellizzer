Erythrococca integrifolia

Erythrococca integrifolia, commonly known as the red cocoon tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. These fruits contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, woody capsule, which gives the plant its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
