Echinopsis rauschii

Echinopsis rauschii, commonly known as the Rausch's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, columnar stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically has a solitary stem with spines and produces clusters of white or yellow flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
