Eugenia caryophyllaea

Eugenia caryophyllaea, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but often considered a delicacy by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flowering season, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
