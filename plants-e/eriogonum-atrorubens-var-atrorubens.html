Eriogonum atrorubens var. atrorubens

Eriogonum atrorubens var. atrorubens, commonly known as the black-red buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking, showy purple to red flower clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is known for its resilience and is often used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
