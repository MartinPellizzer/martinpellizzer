Eucalyptus ravida

Eucalyptus ravida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pink.

Eucalyptus ravida plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
