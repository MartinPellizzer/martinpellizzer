Epipactis longibracteata

Epipactis longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bracted helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, linear leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent lips. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded woodlands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in acidic soils.

Epipactis longibracteata is often found in association with other orchids and is a valuable component of diverse forest ecosystems.
