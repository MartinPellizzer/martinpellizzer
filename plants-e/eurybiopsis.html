Eurybiopsis

Eurybiopsis, commonly known as the slender leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Eurybiopsis has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
