Echinocactus holopterus

Echinocactus holopterus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny exterior, which provides protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant has a thick, water-storing stem that allows it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the top of the plant during the growing season.

Echinocactus holopterus is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal watering once established.
