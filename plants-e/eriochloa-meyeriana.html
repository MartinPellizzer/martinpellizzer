Eriochloa meyeriana

Eriochloa meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that helps it conserve water.

Eriochloa meyeriana is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.
