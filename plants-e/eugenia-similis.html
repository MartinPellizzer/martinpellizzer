Eugenia similis

Eugenia similis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and tropical regions.

Eugenia similis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Indigenous Australian cultures.
