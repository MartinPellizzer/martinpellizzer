Eleocharis mohamadii

Eleocharis mohamadii, commonly known as the Mohamadi flatsedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of lakes. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its often waterlogged habitat.

Eleocharis mohamadii is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
