Epidendrum cearense

Epidendrum cearense, commonly known as the Cearense orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored floral spikes. The plant typically produces clusters of flowers that can range in color from white to yellow, red, or orange, depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Epidendrum cearense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
