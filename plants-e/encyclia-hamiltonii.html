Encyclia hamiltonii

Encyclia hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically during the rainy season. Its pseudobulbs are covered in a fibrous sheath, and the leaves are narrow and leathery.

Encyclia hamiltonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
