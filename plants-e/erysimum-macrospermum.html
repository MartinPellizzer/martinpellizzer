Erysimum macrospermum

Erysimum macrospermum, commonly known as the broad-seeded wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Erysimum macrospermum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
