Ericoides simpliciflorum

Ericoides simpliciflorum, commonly known as the simple-flowered heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the temperate regions of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes evergreens and shrubs adapted to acidic soils. The plant features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Ericoides simpliciflorum is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
