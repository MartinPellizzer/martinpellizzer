Eriogonum brevicaule var. bannockense

Eriogonum brevicaule var. bannockense, commonly known as the Bannock Basin buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Great Basin region of North America.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for pollinators and wildlife.
