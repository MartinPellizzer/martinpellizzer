Ericentrodea homogama

Ericentrodea homogama, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the sandy soils of coastal regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant features small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh, salty environment. Its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in spring.

Ericentrodea homogama plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
