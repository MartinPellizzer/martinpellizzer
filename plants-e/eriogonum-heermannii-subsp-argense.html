Eriogonum heermannii subsp. argense

Eriogonum heermannii subsp.

argense, commonly known as the Argentea buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
