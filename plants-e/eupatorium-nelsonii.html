Eupatorium nelsonii

Eupatorium nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has distinctive gray-green, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Nelson's boneset is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported fever-reducing properties.
