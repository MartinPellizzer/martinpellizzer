Eleocharis obtusa var. lanceolata

Eleocharis obtusa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to aquatic environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly rough texture.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
