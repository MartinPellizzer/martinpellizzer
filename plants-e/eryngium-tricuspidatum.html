Eryngium tricuspidatum

Eryngium tricuspidatum, commonly known as the thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and small blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its structure includes a tall, unbranched stem with alternate leaves that are usually narrow and pointed.

Eryngium tricuspidatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
