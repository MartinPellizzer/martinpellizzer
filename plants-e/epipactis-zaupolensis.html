Epipactis zaupolensis

Epipactis zaupolensis, commonly known as the Zaupolensian helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dark green, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip petal, typical of many orchids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Epipactis zaupolensis is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
