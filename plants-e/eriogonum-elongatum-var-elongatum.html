Eriogonum elongatum var. elongatum

Eriogonum elongatum var. elongatum, commonly known as the longstem buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically slender and branching. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
