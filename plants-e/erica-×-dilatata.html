Erica × dilatata

Erica × dilatata, commonly known as the Heath or Crossandra, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.

Erica × dilatata is also valued for its resilience to coastal conditions and drought tolerance.
