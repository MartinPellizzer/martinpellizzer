Euonymus maximowiczianus

Euonymus maximowiczianus, commonly known as the Korean spurge or Japanese euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Euonymus maximowiczianus is often used in landscaping for its dense growth habit and seasonal color changes.
