Erigeron hesperocallis

Erigeron hesperocallis, commonly known as the blanket flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Erigeron hesperocallis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
