Eulaliopsis binata

Eulaliopsis binata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass or Chinese silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles.

Eulaliopsis binata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
