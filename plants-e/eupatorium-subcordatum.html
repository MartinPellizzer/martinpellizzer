Eupatorium subcordatum

Eupatorium subcordatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside.

It is valued in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
