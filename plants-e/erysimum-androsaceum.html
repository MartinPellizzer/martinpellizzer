Erysimum androsaceum

Erysimum androsaceum, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Erysimum androsaceum thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
