Eugenioides racemosum

Eugenioides racemosum, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in racemes, giving it its scientific name. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fragrance of its flowers makes it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
