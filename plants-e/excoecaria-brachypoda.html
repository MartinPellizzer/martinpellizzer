Excoecaria brachypoda

Excoecaria brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with reddish or purple undersides. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
