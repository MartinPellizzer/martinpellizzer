Elatine hexandra proles major

Elatine hexandra proles major, commonly known as the common water starwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often submerged, and it produces tiny, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats on the water surface.

This species is often used in ecological studies due to its sensitivity to water quality changes.
