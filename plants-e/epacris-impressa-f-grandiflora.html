Epacris impressa f. grandiflora

Epacris impressa f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
