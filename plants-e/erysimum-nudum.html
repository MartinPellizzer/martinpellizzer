Erysimum nudum

Erysimum nudum, commonly known as the naked-stemed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species characterized by its simple, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellow or white flowers. The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Erysimum nudum is often found growing in rocky or disturbed areas, and it is valued for its ornamental flowers and ecological benefits.
