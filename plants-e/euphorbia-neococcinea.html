Euphorbia neococcinea

Euphorbia neococcinea, commonly known as the red milkweed or crimson spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a red or purple calyx. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its latex-producing species.

Euphorbia neococcinea is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
