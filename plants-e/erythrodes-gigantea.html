Erythrodes gigantea

Erythrodes gigantea, commonly known as the giant red fern, is a striking plant characterized by its large, velvety leaves that exhibit a deep red hue.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's foliage is often adorned with small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its unique coloration and robust growth make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, due to its specific ecological requirements, it is not commonly cultivated outside its native habitat.
