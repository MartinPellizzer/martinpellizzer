Epilobium × dovrense

Epilobium × dovrense, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Epilobium × dovrense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
