Erodium chium subsp. aragonense

Erodium chium subsp.

aragonense, commonly known as Aragon wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
