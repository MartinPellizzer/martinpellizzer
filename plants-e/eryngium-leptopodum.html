Eryngium leptopodum

Eryngium leptopodum, commonly known as the slender horse fennel, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pink flower heads that resemble small umbels. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Eryngium leptopodum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
