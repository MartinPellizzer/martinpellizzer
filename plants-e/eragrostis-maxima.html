Eragrostis maxima

Eragrostis maxima, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense spikes of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Eragrostis maxima is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
