Etlingera pilosa

Etlingera pilosa, commonly known as the hairy torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, red-orange flowers that resemble torches, and its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Etlingera pilosa thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually appealing addition to tropical gardens.
