Epilobium alpinum var. majus

Epilobium alpinum var. majus, commonly known as the Alpine willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.

Epilobium alpinum var. majus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude ecosystems.
