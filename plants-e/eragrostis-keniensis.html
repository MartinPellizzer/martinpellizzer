Eragrostis keniensis

Eragrostis keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to East Africa, particularly Kenya.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Eragrostis keniensis is adapted to low rainfall and is often used for forage by livestock in its native region.
