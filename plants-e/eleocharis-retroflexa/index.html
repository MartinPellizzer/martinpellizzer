Eleocharis Retroflexa

Eleocharis retroflexa is a small, grass-like plant commonly found in wetland environments. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other similar plants. This species is known for its delicate, spike-like inflorescence that often stands upright in shallow water. Its unique structure allows it to thrive in moist soils and contribute to ecosystem stability. Despite its modest appearance, Eleocharis retroflexa plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland habitats.
