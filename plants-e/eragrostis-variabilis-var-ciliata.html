Eragrostis variabilis var. ciliata

Eragrostis variabilis var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in both natural and cultivated environments.
