Ehretia punctata

Ehretia punctata, commonly known as the spotted ehretia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves that are often dotted with small, raised glands, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
