Eupatorium ciliatum

Eupatorium ciliatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, button-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have fine, silky hairs along the edges.
