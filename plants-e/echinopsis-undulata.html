Echinopsis undulata

Echinopsis undulata, commonly known as the undulating moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stem and is typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Echinopsis undulata is well-suited for arid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
