Euphorbia xbacensis

Euphorbia xbacensis, commonly known as the Red-veined Candelabra, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features a branched, tree-like structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces vibrant red and green leaves with prominent veins, adding to its ornamental appeal. It blooms with yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
