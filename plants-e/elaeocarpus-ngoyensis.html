Elaeocarpus ngoyensis

Elaeocarpus ngoyensis, commonly known as the Ngoye elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the dense rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of primary forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
