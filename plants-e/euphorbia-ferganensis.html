Euphorbia ferganensis

Euphorbia ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana spurge, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Euphorbia ferganensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
