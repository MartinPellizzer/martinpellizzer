Etlingera vestita

Etlingera vestita, commonly known as the white torch ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking white bracts that resemble torches, surrounding small yellow flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetlands and forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
