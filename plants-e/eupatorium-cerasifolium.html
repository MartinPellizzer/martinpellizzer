Eupatorium cerasifolium

Eupatorium cerasifolium, commonly known as red stem boneset, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.
