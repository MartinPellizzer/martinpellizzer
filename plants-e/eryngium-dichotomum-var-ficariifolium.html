Eryngium dichotomum var. ficariifolium

Eryngium dichotomum var. ficariifolium, commonly known as the ficaria-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that resemble the leaves of the ficus plant, giving it its common name. The plant produces blue to purple spiny flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its unique foliage and striking inflorescences make it a notable ornamental and ecological plant.
