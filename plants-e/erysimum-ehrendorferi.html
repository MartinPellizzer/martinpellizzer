Erysimum ehrendorferi

Erysimum ehrendorferi, commonly known as the Ehrendorfer's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

Erysimum ehrendorferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and hardy nature.
