Epidendrum anderssonii

Epidendrum anderssonii, commonly known as the Andes orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Epidendrum anderssonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
