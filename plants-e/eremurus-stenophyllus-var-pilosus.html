Eremurus stenophyllus var. pilosus

Eremurus stenophyllus var. pilosus, commonly known as the woolly spear thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flowering stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in a cylindrical spike.

It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
