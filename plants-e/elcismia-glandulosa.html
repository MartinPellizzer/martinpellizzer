Elcismia glandulosa

Elcismia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular elcismia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
