Erythroxylum gonoclados var. angustifolium

Erythroxylum gonoclados var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed copal tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, followed by small, woody fruits. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
