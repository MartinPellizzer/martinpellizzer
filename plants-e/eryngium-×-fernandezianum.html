Eryngium × fernandezianum

Eryngium × fernandezianum, commonly known as the fernandezianum thistle, is a hybrid plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-gray foliage and purple-blue flower heads that resemble a thistle. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, seed-filled spheres.

Eryngium × fernandezianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
