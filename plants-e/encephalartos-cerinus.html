Encephalartos cerinus

Encephalartos cerinus, commonly known as the Cape lily or the silver-leaf cycad, is a species of cycad native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy, silver-blue coating. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being cylindrical and the female cones being more compact and woody. Encephalartos cerinus is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
