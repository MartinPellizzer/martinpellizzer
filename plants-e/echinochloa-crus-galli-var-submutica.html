Echinochloa crus-galli var. submutica

Echinochloa crus-galli var. submutica, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa but now widespread in many parts of the world.

It is characterized by its flat, blade-like leaves and distinctive, spiky seed heads that resemble a chicken's comb, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, along roadsides, and in wetlands. It has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in cultivated areas.

Its rapid growth and high seed production contribute to its invasive potential in many ecosystems.
