Erythroxylum coca var. novogranatense

Erythroxylum coca var. novogranatense, commonly known as the Andean coca, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant contains alkaloids, most notably cocaine, which is found in its leaves. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for traditional and medicinal purposes.

Its ecological and cultural significance is deeply rooted in the Andean communities.
