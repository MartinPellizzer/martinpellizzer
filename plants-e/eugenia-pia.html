Eugenia pia

Eugenia pia, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia pia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
