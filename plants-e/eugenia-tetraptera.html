Eugenia tetraptera

Eugenia tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged eugenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple or black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

Eugenia tetraptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
