Eupatorium agrigaudium

Eupatorium agrigaudium, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white down. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and pollinator support.
