Eugenia wurdackii

Eugenia wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's clove, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent when crushed.

Eugenia wurdackii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
