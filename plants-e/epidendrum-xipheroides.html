Epidendrum xipheroides

Epidendrum xipheroides, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum xipheroides is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
