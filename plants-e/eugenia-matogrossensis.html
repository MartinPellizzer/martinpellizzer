Eugenia matogrossensis

Eugenia matogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and dark, glossy berries. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in the Amazon and Atlantic Forest regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

The fruit, which is edible, has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
