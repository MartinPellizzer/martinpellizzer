Eucalyptus paliformis

Eucalyptus paliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife, and it is adapted to dry, sandy soils.

Eucalyptus paliformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
