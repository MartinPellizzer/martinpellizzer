Englerophytum laurentii var. lundense

Englerophytum laurentii var. lundense, commonly known as the coastal dune morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops. It has adapted to saline and arid conditions, making it resilient to harsh coastal environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
