Eragrostis elliottii

Eragrostis elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Eragrostis elliottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
