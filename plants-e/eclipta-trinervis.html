Eclipta trinervis

Eclipta trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined eclipta, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically showy inflorescences. The plant has alternate, lanceolate leaves with three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. It produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, which are dispersed by wind.

Eclipta trinervis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
