Eriogonum gracile var. gracile

Eriogonum gracile var. gracile, commonly known as the slender buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in early summer.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
