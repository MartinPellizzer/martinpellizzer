Euploca pedicellaris

Euploca pedicellaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf euploca, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in wet, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight reddish tinge, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring.

Euploca pedicellaris is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
