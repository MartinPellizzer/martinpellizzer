Epiphyllum ruestii

Epiphyllum ruestii, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a climbing plant with fleshy, flat, and often brightly colored stems that resemble leaves. The plant produces showy, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
