Eugenioides macrophyllum

Eugenioides macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved eugenia, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy flowers.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pink, with a strong fragrance, and it produces small, edible berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
