Echinophora radians

Echinophora radians, commonly known as the radiate thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, woolly stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a radiate pattern, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and scrub habitats.

Echinophora radians is a perennial plant that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
