Euphorbia theriaca

Euphorbia theriaca, commonly known as the "bark-leaf euphorbia," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and reduced leaves, which are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It has a spiny, branched structure with a distinctive appearance that makes it stand out in its native habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability to arid conditions, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
