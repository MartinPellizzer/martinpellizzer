Euphorbia gollmeriana

Euphorbia gollmeriana, commonly known as the Mexican fire plant, is a succulent shrub native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its sap contains toxic compounds, so handling it requires caution.

Euphorbia gollmeriana is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and resilience.
