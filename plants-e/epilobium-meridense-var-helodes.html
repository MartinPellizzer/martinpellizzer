Epilobium meridense var. helodes

Epilobium meridense var. helodes, commonly known as the yellow willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its showy, yellowish-orange flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
