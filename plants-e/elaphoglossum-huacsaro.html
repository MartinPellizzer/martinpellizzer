Elaphoglossum huacsaro

Elaphoglossum huacsaro, commonly known as the Huasco fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a distinctive pattern on the underside of its leaves.

Elaphoglossum huacsaro is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
