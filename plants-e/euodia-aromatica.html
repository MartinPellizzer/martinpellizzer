Euodia aromatica

Euodia aromatica, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind or Chinese date, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round, brown fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in herbal remedies for centuries.

Euodia aromatica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
