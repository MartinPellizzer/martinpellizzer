Euclea urijiensis

Euclea urijiensis, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by elliptical, red or orange fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
