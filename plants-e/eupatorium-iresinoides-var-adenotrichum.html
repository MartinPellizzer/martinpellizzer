Eupatorium iresinoides var. adenotrichum

Eupatorium iresinoides var. adenotrichum, commonly known as the red-stemmed boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that often turn reddish with age, and it has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
