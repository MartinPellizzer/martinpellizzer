Eupatoriadelphus dubius

Eupatoriadelphus dubius, commonly known as the purple false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside, and it produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
