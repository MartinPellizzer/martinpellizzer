Evolvulus alsinoides f. albiflorus

Evolvulus alsinoides f. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and rapid growth.
