Euphorbia picachensis

Euphorbia picachensis, commonly known as the Picacho euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, spiny stems and clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting it to its harsh, dry environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
