Eriocaulon sinealaeum

Eriocaulon sinealaeum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Eriocaulon sinealaeum is relatively uncommon and is sometimes listed as a species of conservation concern due to habitat loss.
