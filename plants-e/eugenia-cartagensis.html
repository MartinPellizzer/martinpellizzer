Eugenia cartagensis

Eugenia cartagensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It is adapted to grow in coastal and tropical regions, often found in sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.
