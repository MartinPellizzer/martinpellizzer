Erianthus arundinaceus

Erianthus arundinaceus, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in riparian zones. Its fibrous stalks have been historically used for thatching and basket weaving.

Erianthus arundinaceus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
