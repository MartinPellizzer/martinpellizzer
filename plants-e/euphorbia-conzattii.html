Euphorbia conzattii

Euphorbia conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark is often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

Euphorbia conzattii is also noted for its ability to tolerate high temperatures and poor soil conditions.
