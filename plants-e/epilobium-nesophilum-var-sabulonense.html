Epilobium nesophilum var. sabulonense

Epilobium nesophilum var. sabulonense, commonly known as the sand dune willow herb, is a flowering plant native to coastal and sandy environments.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dune habitats along the Atlantic coast.

This species is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
