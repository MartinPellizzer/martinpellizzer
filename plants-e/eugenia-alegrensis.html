Eugenia alegrensis

Eugenia alegrensis, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-smelling fruits similar to guavas. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
