Epilobium watsonii var. occidentale

Epilobium watsonii var. occidentale, commonly known as the western willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval, and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are usually woody at the base.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
