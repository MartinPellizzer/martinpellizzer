Erigeron × paradoxus

Erigeron × paradoxus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Erigeron × paradoxus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
