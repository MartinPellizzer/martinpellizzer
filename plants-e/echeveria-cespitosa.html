Echeveria cespitosa

Echeveria cespitosa, commonly known as the Silver Ponytail, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features a compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern with fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces vibrant pink or purple flowers on tall stems during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Echeveria cespitosa is popular in rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
