Eragrostis biflora

Eragrostis biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Eragrostis biflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
