Eleocharis tuberculosa var. pubnicoensis

Eleocharis tuberculosa var. pubnicoensis, commonly known as the pubescent sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
