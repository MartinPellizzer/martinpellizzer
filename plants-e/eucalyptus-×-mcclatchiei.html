Eucalyptus × mcclatchiei

Eucalyptus × mcclatchiei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the winter months. Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for fuel and minor construction purposes.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought and high temperatures.
