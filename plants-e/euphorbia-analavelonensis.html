Euphorbia analavelonensis

Euphorbia analavelonensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It produces a milky sap that can be toxic and is often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
