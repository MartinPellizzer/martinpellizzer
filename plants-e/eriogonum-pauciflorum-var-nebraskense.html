Eriogonum pauciflorum var. nebraskense

Eriogonum pauciflorum var. nebraskense, commonly known as the Nebraska jointleaf buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Great Plains of North America.

It features a sprawling habit with gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive jointed appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is notable for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
