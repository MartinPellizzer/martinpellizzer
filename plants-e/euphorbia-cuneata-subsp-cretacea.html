Euphorbia cuneata subsp. cretacea

Euphorbia cuneata subsp.

cretacea, commonly known as the Crete spurges, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has adapted to arid environments with its succulent leaves and deep root system. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and diverse ecological roles.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
