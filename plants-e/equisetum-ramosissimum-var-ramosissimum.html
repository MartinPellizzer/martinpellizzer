Equisetum ramosissimum var. ramosissimum

Equisetum ramosissimum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stems, which are typical of its reproductive strategy. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of silica has contributed to its cultural significance.
