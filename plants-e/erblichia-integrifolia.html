Erblichia integrifolia

Erblichia integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and unlobed. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Erblichia integrifolia is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
