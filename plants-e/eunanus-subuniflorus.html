Eunanus subuniflorus

Eunanus subuniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower eunanus, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and a single, showy flower that blooms during the wet season. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its botanical structure includes a short stem with a single inflorescence at the tip.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
