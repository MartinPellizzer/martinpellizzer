Euphrasia pectinata var. subulata

Euphrasia pectinata var. subulata, commonly known as the slender yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, subulate (awl-shaped) leaves and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, hairy stems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its geographic range.
