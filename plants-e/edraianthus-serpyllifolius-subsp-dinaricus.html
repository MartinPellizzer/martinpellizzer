Edraianthus serpyllifolius subsp. dinaricus

Edraianthus serpyllifolius subsp.

dinaricus, commonly known as the Dinaric sandwort, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early summer. It is adapted to harsh, arid conditions and has a low-growing habit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
