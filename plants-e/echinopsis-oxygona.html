Echinopsis oxygona

Echinopsis oxygona, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

Echinopsis oxygona is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
