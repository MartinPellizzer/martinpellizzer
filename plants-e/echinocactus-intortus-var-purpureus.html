Echinocactus intortus var. purpureus

Echinocactus intortus var. purpureus, commonly known as the Purple Hedgehog Cactus, is a unique variant of the Echinocactus genus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

This plant is characterized by its dense, spiny, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with a striking purple hue, especially in the varietal form. The leaves are reduced to spines, and the plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its distinctive appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
