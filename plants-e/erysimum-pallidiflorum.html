Erysimum pallidiflorum

Erysimum pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a compact growth habit.

Erysimum pallidiflorum is often used in native plant gardens due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
