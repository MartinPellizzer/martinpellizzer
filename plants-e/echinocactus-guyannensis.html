Echinocactus guyannensis

Echinocactus guyannensis, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It has a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
