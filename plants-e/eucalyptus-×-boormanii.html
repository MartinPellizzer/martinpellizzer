Eucalyptus × boormanii

Eucalyptus × boormanii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Eucalyptus × boormanii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
