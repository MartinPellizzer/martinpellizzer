Epidendrum falcisepalum

Epidendrum falcisepalum, commonly known as the sickle-sepal epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, and often curved sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and yellow.

Epidendrum falcisepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
