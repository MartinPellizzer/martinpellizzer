Ericoides inflatum

Ericoides inflatum, commonly known as the inflated ericoid, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, inflated floral structures.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs, and is typically found in nutrient-poor soils such as those in heathlands or bogs. The plant exhibits a compact, shrub-like growth form with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are clustered in dense inflorescences, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Ericoides inflatum is adapted to cool, moist climates and plays a role in supporting local pollinators and soil stability in its native habitats.
