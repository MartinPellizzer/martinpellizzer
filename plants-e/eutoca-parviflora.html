Eutoca parviflora

Eutoca parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered eutoca, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Eutoca parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
