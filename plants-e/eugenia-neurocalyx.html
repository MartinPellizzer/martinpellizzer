Eugenia neurocalyx

Eugenia neurocalyx, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible, round, yellow or orange fruits that have a firm, waxy skin and a sweet, tart flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
