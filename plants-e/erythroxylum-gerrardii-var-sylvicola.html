Erythroxylum gerrardii var. sylvicola

Erythroxylum gerrardii var. sylvicola, commonly known as the swamp or river holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in wetland environments. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant produces small, round berries that are often consumed by wildlife.
