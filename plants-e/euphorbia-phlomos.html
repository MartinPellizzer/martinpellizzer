Euphorbia phlomos

Euphorbia phlomos, commonly known as the flax euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and it is often found in rocky or dry soils.

Euphorbia phlomos is noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
