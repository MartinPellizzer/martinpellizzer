Eusiphon longissimus

Eusiphon longissimus, commonly known as the long-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated nectar spur that extends from the base of the flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate light for photosynthesis. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by long-tongued insects such as bees and moths, which are able to reach the nectar deep within the spur.

The botanical features of Eusiphon longissimus make it an important component of its ecosystem, supporting specialized pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
