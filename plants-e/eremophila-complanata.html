Eremophila complanata

Eremophila complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to pink, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Eremophila complanata is often used in native gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
