Echinocactus echidne f. gilvus

Echinocactus echidne f. gilvus, commonly known as the golden echinocactus, is a unique succulent native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hair. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper drainage and sunlight.
