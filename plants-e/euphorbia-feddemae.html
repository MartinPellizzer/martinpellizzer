Euphorbia feddemae

Euphorbia feddemae, commonly known as the Sicilian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the island of Sicily.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
