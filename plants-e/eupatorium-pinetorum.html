Eupatorium pinetorum

Eupatorium pinetorum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

Eupatorium pinetorum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
