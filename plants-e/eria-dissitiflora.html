Eria dissitiflora

Eria dissitiflora, commonly known as the hairy-stalked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the rainy season. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eria dissitiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
