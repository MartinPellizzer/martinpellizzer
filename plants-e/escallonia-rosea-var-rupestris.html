Escallonia rosea var. rupestris

Escallonia rosea var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock escallonia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, leathery foliage that is dark green and glossy, providing year-round visual interest. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or red flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
