Eudema trichocarpa

Eudema trichocarpa, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow petals and dark central disk. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and branching.

Eudema trichocarpa is known for its resilience and is often used in native plant gardens.
