Eurybia muricata

Eurybia muricata, commonly known as the rough yarrow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.

Eurybia muricata is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
