Eupatorium intercostulatum

Eupatorium intercostulatum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, rough-hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
