Erigeron subasper

Erigeron subasper, commonly known as the rough fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture.
