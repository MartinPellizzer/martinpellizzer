Equilabium vesiculare

Equilabium vesiculare, commonly known as the bladderleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, inflated leaf-like structures that resemble bladders. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and pine flatwoods. Its flowers are small and white or pale green, often clustered in loose inflorescences.

The botanical features of Equilabium vesiculare make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
