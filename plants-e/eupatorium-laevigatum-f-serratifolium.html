Eupatorium laevigatum f. serratifolium

Eupatorium laevigatum f. serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated leaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. Native to the eastern United States, this plant thrives in moist, woodland environments. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
