Erythracanthus ruber

Erythracanthus ruber, commonly known as the red spurge.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water.

Its latex sap is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it a plant to handle with care.
