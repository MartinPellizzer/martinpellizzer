Euryblema anatonum

Euryblema anatonum, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and helps prevent soil erosion in desert landscapes.

Euryblema anatonum is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in saline soils.
