Ectrosia leporina var. longiglumis

Ectrosia leporina var. longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume ectrosia, is a variant of the ectrosia leporina species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated glumes, which are the bract-like structures that encase the flower spikes. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and ecological adaptations.
