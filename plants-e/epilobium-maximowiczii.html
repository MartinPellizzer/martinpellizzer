Epilobium maximowiczii

Epilobium maximowiczii, commonly known as the purple-flowered willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often shady habitats such as streambanks and woodland edges. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy, pink to purple flowers bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Epilobium maximowiczii is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
