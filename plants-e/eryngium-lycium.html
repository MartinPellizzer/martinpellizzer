Eryngium lycium

Eryngium lycium, commonly known as the spiked eryngium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in grassy areas and rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture.

Eryngium lycium is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for various pollinators.
