Elaeocarpus petiolosus

Elaeocarpus petiolosus, commonly known as the slender-leaved elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
