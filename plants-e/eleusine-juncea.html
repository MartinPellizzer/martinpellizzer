Eleusine juncea

Eleusine juncea, commonly known as lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a grass species that grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale green to brown flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its roots are fibrous and extensive, allowing it to thrive in various soil types and climates.

Eleusine juncea is also valued for its use in livestock feed and as a soil stabilizer in erosion-prone areas.
