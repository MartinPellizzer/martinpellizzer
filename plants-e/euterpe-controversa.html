Euterpe controversa

Euterpe controversa, commonly known as the pink-berried palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish fruits that are edible and often consumed by local communities. Euterpe controversa thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
