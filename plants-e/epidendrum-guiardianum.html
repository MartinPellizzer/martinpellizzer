Epidendrum guiardianum

Epidendrum guiardianum, commonly known as the Guianan Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences bearing numerous showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
