Epilobium angustifolium f. subverticillatum

Epilobium angustifolium f. subverticillatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a variant of the willowherb species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It typically features dense, whorled clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

This form is often found in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.
