Euphorbia avenia

Euphorbia avenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are the specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia avenia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
