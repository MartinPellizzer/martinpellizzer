Epilobium dominii

Epilobium dominii, commonly known as the Spanish heath, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Epilobium dominii is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
