Ericoides podophyllum

Ericoides podophyllum, commonly known as the dwarf gooseberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its berries, which are edible, are a food source for various wildlife species.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
