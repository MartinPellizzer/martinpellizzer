Empetrum nigrum

Empetrum nigrum, commonly known as crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to arctic and alpine regions.

It has small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in jams and jellies. The plant thrives in poor, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and tundra environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing year-round cover in harsh climates.

Crowberry plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
