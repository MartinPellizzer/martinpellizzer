Eremurus lactiflorus

Eremurus lactiflorus, commonly known as the milkflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which gives it its common name and contributes to its unique botanical properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

Eremurus lactiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its striking floral display.
