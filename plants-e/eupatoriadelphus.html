Eupatoriadelphus

Eupatoriadelphus, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant features aromatic, fuzzy leaves and produces small, white to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatoriadelphus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
