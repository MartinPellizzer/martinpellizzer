Eupatorium laevigatum f. subcapillatum

Eupatorium laevigatum f. subcapillatum, commonly known as the smooth blue woodruff, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
