Echites verrucosus

Echites verrucosus, commonly known as the rough echites, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually covered in a rough, waxy coating. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
