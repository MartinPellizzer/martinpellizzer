Eucalyptus tereticornis var. rostrata

Eucalyptus tereticornis var. rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the narrow-leafed gum.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine greyish bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is native to southern Australia and thrives in dry, sandy soils.

This variant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
