Euphorbia sultan-hassei

Euphorbia sultan-hassei, commonly known as the Sultan Hassani's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It features a short, woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to survive in dry, hot climates with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
