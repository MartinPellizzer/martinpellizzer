Euphrasia crassiuscula subsp. glandulifera

Euphrasia crassiuscula subsp.

glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular eyebright, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, typically from May to June.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with glandular dots, which give it its subspecific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
