Erica solandroides

Erica solandroides, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica solandroides is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardiness.

It produces small, elliptical leaves that are aromatic and can be used in traditional herbal remedies.
