Eremophila platythamnos

Eremophila platythamnos, commonly known as the broad-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spikes of pale pink to white flowers. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Eremophila platythamnos plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
