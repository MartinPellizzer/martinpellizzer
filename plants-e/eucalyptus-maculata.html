Eucalyptus maculata

Eucalyptus maculata, commonly known as the spotted gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark, which features patches of rough, flaky bark that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
