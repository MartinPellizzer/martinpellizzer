Evolvulus angustissimus

Evolvulus angustissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the early morning, hence its common name, and produces blue or violet-colored flowers. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Evolvulus angustissimus is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
