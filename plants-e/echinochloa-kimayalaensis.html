Echinochloa kimayalaensis

Echinochloa kimayalaensis, commonly known as the Kimayala panicgrass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ecological role and potential as a forage plant, it is of interest in botanical and agricultural studies.
