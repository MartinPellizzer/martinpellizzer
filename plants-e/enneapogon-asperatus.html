Enneapogon asperatus

Enneapogon asperatus, commonly known as the rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its stiff, rough-textured leaves and compact, cylindrical seed heads. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities.
