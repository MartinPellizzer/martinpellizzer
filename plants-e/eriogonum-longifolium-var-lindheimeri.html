Eriogonum longifolium var. lindheimeri

Eriogonum longifolium var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
