Eucalyptus conglobata subsp. perata

Eucalyptus conglobata subsp.

perata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and distinctive, urn-shaped flower buds.

The plant produces white or pink flowers that are clustered in groups of three, and it is known for its aromatic oils. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
