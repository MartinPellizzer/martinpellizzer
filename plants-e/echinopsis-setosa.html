Echinopsis setosa

Echinopsis setosa, commonly known as the "Lace Flower" or "Lace Cactus," is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.

Echinopsis setosa is a member of the Cactaceae family and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
