Ericoides polycomum

Ericoides polycomum, commonly known as the clustered ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is typically found in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact form and attractive blooms.

Ericoides polycomum is well adapted to harsh environments and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
