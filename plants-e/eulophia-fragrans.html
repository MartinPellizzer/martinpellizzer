Eulophia fragrans

Eulophia fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with erect, often branching stems that bear a profusion of showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that enhances its ornamental appeal. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural gardens and conservation efforts.
