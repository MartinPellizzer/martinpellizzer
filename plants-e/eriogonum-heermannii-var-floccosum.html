Eriogonum heermannii var. floccosum

Eriogonum heermannii var. floccosum, commonly known as the white-flowered buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs. It has basal leaves that are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Polygonaceae family and is known for its ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
