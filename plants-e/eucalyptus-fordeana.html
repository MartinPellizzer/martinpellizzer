Eucalyptus fordeana

Eucalyptus fordeana, commonly known as the Forde's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to low rainfall and can thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Eucalyptus fordeana is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
