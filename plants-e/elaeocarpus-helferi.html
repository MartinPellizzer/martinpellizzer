Elaeocarpus helferi

Elaeocarpus helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its attractive, glossy leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically blue or black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
