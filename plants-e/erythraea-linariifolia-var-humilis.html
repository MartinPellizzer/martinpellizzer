Erythraea linariifolia var. humilis

Erythraea linariifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf yellow wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
