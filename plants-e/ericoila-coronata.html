Ericoila coronata

Ericoila coronata, commonly known as the crowned heather, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its small, clustered flowers range in color from white to pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Ericoila coronata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
