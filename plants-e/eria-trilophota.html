Eria trilophota

Eria trilophota, commonly known as the three-ridged eria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
