Eugenia fernandopoana

Eugenia fernandopoana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-mato, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that give way to bright red berries. These berries are not only ornamental but also have traditional uses in local communities.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various bird and mammal species.
