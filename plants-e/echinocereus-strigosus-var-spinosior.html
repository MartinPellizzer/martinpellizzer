Echinocereus strigosus var. spinosior

Echinocereus strigosus var. spinosior, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are densely covered with sharp, backward-facing spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
