Echium calycinum subsp. arenarium

Echium calycinum subsp.

arenarium, commonly known as the sand echium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikes of blue to violet flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
