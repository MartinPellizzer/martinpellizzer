Epitrachys penicillata

Epitrachys penicillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are often submerged in water, contributing to its graceful appearance. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it attaches to submerged surfaces. Its fronds are typically divided into small, linear segments, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Epitrachys penicillata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
