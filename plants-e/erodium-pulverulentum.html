Erodium pulverulentum

Erodium pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty erodium, is a small annual herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and features delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a dusty appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering display.
