Eriosyce bicolor

Eriosyce bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

Eriosyce bicolor is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
