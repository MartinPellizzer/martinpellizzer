Eschweilera complanata

Eschweilera complanata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and is closely related to the genus Dalbergia. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its unique wood, which is highly sought after in musical instrument making.
