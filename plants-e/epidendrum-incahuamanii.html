Epidendrum incahuamanii

Epidendrum incahuamanii, commonly known as the Andean Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
