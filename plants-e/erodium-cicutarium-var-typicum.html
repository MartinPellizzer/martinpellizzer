Erodium cicutarium var. typicum

Erodium cicutarium var. typicum, commonly known as common bedstraw, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions.
