Eyrythalia caucasica

Eyrythalia caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian erythronium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive red throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
