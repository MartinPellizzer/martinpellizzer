Euryops imbricatus

Euryops imbricatus, commonly known as the golden bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Euryops imbricatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and hardy characteristics.
