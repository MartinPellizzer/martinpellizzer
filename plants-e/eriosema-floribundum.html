Eriosema floribundum

Eriosema floribundum, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds.

Eriosema floribundum is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and attractive floral display.
