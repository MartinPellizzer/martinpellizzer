Erica carneola

Erica carneola, commonly known as the purple heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, depending on the variety. Erica carneola is evergreen, with dense, leathery foliage that is often used in ornamental gardening.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
