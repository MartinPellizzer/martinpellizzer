Eranthemum semperflorens

Eranthemum semperflorens, commonly known as the perpetual flower or ever-blooming geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom continuously throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eranthemum semperflorens is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
