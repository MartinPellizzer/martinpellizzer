Eragrostis agrostoidea var. speciosa

Eragrostis agrostoidea var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, spike-like inflorescence that becomes more prominent during the flowering season. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish flowers that are typically arranged in dense, elongated clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping projects.
