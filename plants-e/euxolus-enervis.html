Euxolus enervis

Euxolus enervis, commonly known as the slender euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
