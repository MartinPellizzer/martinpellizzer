Eugenia bahorucana

Eugenia bahorucana, commonly known as the Madagascar guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, aromatic fruits that are similar in appearance to guavas but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
