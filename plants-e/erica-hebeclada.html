Erica hebeclada

Erica hebeclada, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica hebeclada thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
