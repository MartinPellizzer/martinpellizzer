Epilobium vogesiacum

Epilobium vogesiacum, commonly known as the Voges willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on both sides. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from June to August.

Epilobium vogesiacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
