Erica holocalycina

Erica holocalycina, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Erica holocalycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens for its winter flowering.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insects and birds.
