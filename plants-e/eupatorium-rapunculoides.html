Eupatorium rapunculoides

Eupatorium rapunculoides, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium rapunculoides is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
