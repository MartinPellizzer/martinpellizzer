Erochloe amabilis

Erochloe amabilis, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky foliage and produces small, blue-green spikelets that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

Erochloe amabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
