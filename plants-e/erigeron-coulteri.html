Erigeron coulteri

Erigeron coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Coulter's fleabane is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
