Escallonia × franciscana

Escallonia × franciscana, commonly known as the French escallonia, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern France and northern Spain, where it thrives in mild, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Escallonia × franciscana is relatively low-maintenance, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
