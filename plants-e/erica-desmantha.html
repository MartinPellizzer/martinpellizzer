Erica desmantha

Erica desmantha, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Erica desmantha is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh, coastal environments with high salt exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
