Erythraea pulchella var. ramosissima

Erythraea pulchella var. ramosissima, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
