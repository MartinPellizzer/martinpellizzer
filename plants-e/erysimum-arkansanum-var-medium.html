Erysimum arkansanum var. medium

Erysimum arkansanum var. medium, commonly known as the Arkansas pocket gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
