Erythroxylum pyrifolium var. laurel

Erythroxylum pyrifolium var. laurel, commonly known as the laurel erythroxylum, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically leathery and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by orange-red berries. Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Erythroxylaceae family.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
