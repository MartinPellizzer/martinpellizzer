Ericameria obovata

Ericameria obovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, obovate leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, herbaceous stems and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Ericameria obovata plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
