Echinops cathayanus

Echinops cathayanus, commonly known as the Persian globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble a globe, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its woody, shrub-like structure. Echinops cathayanus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in dry climates.
