Ericoides triphyllum

Ericoides triphyllum, commonly known as the sourwort, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in threes, giving it the species name "triphyllum.". The plant produces bell-shaped, white or yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in bogs and heathlands.

Ericoides triphyllum plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
