Echium sabulicola var. gussonei

Echium sabulicola var. gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's fireweed, is a perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and coastal winds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing nectar for pollinators.
