Eriophorum × rousseauianum

Eriophorum × rousseauianum, commonly known as the hybrid tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical tussocks formed by its grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

Eriophorum × rousseauianum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
