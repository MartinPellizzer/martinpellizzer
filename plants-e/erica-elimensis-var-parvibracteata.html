Erica elimensis var. parvibracteata

Erica elimensis var. parvibracteata, commonly known as the small-bract heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators and its resilience to coastal conditions.
