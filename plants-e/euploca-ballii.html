Euploca ballii

Euploca ballii, commonly known as the Ball's euploca, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Euploca ballii is adapted to thrive in arid and coastal environments, showcasing resilience to salt spray and drought.
