Eucalyptus anceps

Eucalyptus anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Western Australia and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract nectar-feeding insects and birds. Eucalyptus anceps is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
