Euphorbia hyssopifolia var. pulchella

Euphorbia hyssopifolia var. pulchella, commonly known as the pretty spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers surrounded by red bracts. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
