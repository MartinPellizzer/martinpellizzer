Eugenia atropunctata var. robusta

Eugenia atropunctata var. robusta, commonly known as the rough-leaved eugenia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in small, raised dots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in some cuisines.
