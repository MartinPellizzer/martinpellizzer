Echinofossulocactus confusus

Echinofossulocactus confusus, commonly known as the confused echinofossulocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and gardeners in dry climates.
