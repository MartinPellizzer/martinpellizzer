Eryngium tucano

Eryngium tucano, commonly known as the Tucano thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eryngium tucano is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
