Euphorbia leoncroizatii

Euphorbia leoncroizatii, commonly known as the lion's crozier euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
