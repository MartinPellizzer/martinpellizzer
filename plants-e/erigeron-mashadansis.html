Erigeron mashadansis

Erigeron mashadansis, commonly known as the Mashadan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

Erigeron mashadansis is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
