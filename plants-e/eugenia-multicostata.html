Eugenia multicostata

Eugenia multicostata, commonly known as the broad-leafed guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, broad, and leathery leaves that are often prominently veined. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits. Its fruit is typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a thick, smooth skin and a juicy, sweet pulp.

Eugenia multicostata is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in various regions.
