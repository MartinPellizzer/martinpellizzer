Erythrophleum cambodianum

Erythrophleum cambodianum, commonly known as Cambodian ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a keystone species in tropical forests and its potential for ecological and economic benefits.
