Eugenia brachysepala

Eugenia brachysepala, commonly known as the short-capped eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, berry-like drupe that is typically red when ripe.

Eugenia brachysepala is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
