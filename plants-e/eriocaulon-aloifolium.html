Eriocaulon aloifolium

Eriocaulon aloifolium, commonly known as the silver thread plant, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
