Euphorbia etuberculosa

Euphorbia etuberculosa, commonly known as the tuberculate spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps called tubercles. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers, which are typically surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. Euphorbia etuberculosa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also noted for its ability to store water in its stems, aiding in its survival in harsh environments.
