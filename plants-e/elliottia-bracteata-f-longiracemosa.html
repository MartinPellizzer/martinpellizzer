Elliottia bracteata f. longiracemosa

Elliottia bracteata f. longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme form of the mountain holly, is a variant of the mountain holly, a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, branched inflorescences that bear small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland habitats. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged oppositely along the stems.

The botanical interest of this form lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
