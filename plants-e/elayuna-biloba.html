Elayuna biloba

Elayuna biloba, commonly known as the Split-leaf Tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into two main sections, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody pods containing seeds. Elayuna biloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes a tall, slender trunk with a dense canopy of foliage.
