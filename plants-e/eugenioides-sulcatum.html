Eugenioides sulcatum

Eugenioides sulcatum, commonly known as the rough-leaved eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often marked with distinctive grooves or ridges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered together. Its fruit is a small, oval berry that turns from green to red when mature.

Eugenioides sulcatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
