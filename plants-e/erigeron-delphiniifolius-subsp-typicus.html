Erigeron delphiniifolius subsp. typicus

Erigeron delphiniifolius subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple ray florets and green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, white disk flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to arid environments, with a compact growth form and hairy foliage.
