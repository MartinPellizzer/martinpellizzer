Eucyperus alternifolius

Eucyperus alternifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paper plant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or pale in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Eucyperus alternifolius is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic ecosystems.
