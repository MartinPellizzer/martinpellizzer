Euphorbia inappendiculata

Euphorbia inappendiculata, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers, which are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
