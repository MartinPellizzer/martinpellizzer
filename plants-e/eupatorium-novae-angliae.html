Eupatorium novae-angliae

Eupatorium novae-angliae, commonly known as New England thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, making it an important plant in ecological habitats.
