Eragrostis megastachya f. nana

Eragrostis megastachya f. nana, commonly known as dwarf false panicgrass, is a compact, low-growing grass species native to parts of Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
