Eriocaulon lanceolatum

Eriocaulon lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant features slender, unbranched stems with whorled leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense spikes, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eriocaulon lanceolatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
