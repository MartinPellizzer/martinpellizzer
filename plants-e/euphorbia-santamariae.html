Euphorbia santamariae

Euphorbia santamariae, commonly known as the Santa Maria spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its spiny stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified floral structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
