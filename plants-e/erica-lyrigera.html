Erica lyrigera

Erica lyrigera, commonly known as the Lyreleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Erica lyrigera typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to coastal climates.
