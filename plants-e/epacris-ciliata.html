Epacris ciliata

Epacris ciliata, commonly known as the fringed epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a member of the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces spikes of tubular, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Epacris ciliata is often found in rocky or sandy soils in coastal and heathland environments.
