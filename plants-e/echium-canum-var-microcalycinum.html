Echium canum var. microcalycinum

Echium canum var. microcalycinum, commonly known as the dwarf fleabane, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in the summer months. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
