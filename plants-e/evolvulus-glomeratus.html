Evolvulus glomeratus

Evolvulus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Evolvulus glomeratus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering vines and herbs.
