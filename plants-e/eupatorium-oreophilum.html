Eupatorium oreophilum

Eupatorium oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of forests and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium oreophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
