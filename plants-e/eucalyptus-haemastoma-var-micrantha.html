Eucalyptus haemastoma var. micrantha

Eucalyptus haemastoma var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The flowers are typically small and grouped in clusters, producing a pale green to yellowish color. This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
