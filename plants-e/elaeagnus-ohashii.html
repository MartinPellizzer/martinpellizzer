Elaeagnus ohashii

Elaeagnus ohashii, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that ripen in the fall. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its tolerance to drought. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Elaeagnus ohashii is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
