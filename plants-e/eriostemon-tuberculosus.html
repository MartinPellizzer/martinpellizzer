Eriostemon tuberculosus

Eriostemon tuberculosus, commonly known as the mountain banksia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces striking, cone-like inflorescences that are often covered in small, tuberculate bumps, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in heathlands and open forests.

Eriostemon tuberculosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
