Eupatorium cydoniifolium

Eupatorium cydoniifolium, commonly known as the horseweed or sweet weed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
