Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. latum

Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. latum, commonly known as the broadflower goldfields, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and a low-growing habit.

This variety is distinguished by its broader flower heads compared to other varieties of Eriophyllum.
