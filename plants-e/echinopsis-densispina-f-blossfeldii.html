Echinopsis densispina f. blossfeldii

Echinopsis densispina f. blossfeldii, commonly known as the Blossfeld's dense spined cactus, is a unique hybrid of the Echinopsis genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are densely covered with white, radial spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and as a houseplant.
