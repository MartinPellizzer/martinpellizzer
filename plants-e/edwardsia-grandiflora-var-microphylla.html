Edwardsia grandiflora var. microphylla

Edwardsia grandiflora var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf edwardsia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, delicate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
