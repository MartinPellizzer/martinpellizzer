Erythroxylum campestre

Erythroxylum campestre, commonly known as the red-twig dogwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems, which are most prominent during the winter months. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by blue-black berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and turn red in the autumn.

Erythroxylum campestre is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant seasonal color changes.
