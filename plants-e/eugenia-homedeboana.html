Eugenia homedeboana

Eugenia homedeboana, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep red.
