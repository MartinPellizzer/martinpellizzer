Echinopsilon sedoides var. tetrandrus

Echinopsilon sedoides var. tetrandrus, commonly known as the four-threaded burrweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, spiky, bristly fruits that resemble burrs. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ecological role as a food source for various insects.

The species is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
