Erica bonplandiana

Erica bonplandiana, commonly known as the Bonpland's heath, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the spring and early summer. Erica bonplandiana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
