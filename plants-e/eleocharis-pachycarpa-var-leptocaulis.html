Eleocharis pachycarpa var. leptocaulis

Eleocharis pachycarpa var. leptocaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed spike rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as pond edges and shallow water areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
