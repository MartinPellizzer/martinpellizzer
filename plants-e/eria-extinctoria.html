Eria extinctoria

Eria extinctoria, commonly known as the fire extinguisher orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
