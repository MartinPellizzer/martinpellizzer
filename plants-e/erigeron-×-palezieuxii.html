Erigeron × palezieuxii

Erigeron × palezieuxii, commonly known as the Blueweed or Blue Mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and fluffy, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and pond edges. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetland environments.

Its flowers attract pollinators like butterflies and bees, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
