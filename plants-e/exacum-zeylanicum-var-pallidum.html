Exacum zeylanicum var. pallidum

Exacum zeylanicum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale-flowered African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compact, bushy growth with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. The flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically have a pale pink to white coloration, often with a slightly darker throat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
