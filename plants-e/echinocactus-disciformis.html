Echinocactus disciformis

Echinocactus disciformis, commonly known as the Mexican horse chestnut, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, which are followed by fruit that contains seeds. Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for desert conditions, and it is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.

Echinocactus disciformis is a hardy plant that requires minimal water and thrives in full sun.
