Erica × autumnalis

Erica × autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn heather, is a hybrid shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments. It blooms from late summer into autumn, making it a valuable plant for extending the flowering season in gardens.

Erica × autumnalis is also known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
