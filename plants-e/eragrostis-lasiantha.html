Eragrostis lasiantha

Eragrostis lasiantha, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves with a distinctive silky texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Eragrostis lasiantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
