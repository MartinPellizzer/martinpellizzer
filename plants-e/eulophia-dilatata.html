Eulophia dilatata

Eulophia dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leafed eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded forest environments. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid conditions.

Eulophia dilatata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
