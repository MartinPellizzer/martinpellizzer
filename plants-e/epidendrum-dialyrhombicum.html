Epidendrum dialyrhombicum

Epidendrum dialyrhombicum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple with a yellow throat. The plant has fleshy, strap-like leaves that help it store water in its natural habitat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Epidendrum dialyrhombicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
