Eucalyptus viminalis var. huberiana

Eucalyptus viminalis var. huberiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the narrow-leaf malleefowl eucalypt.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that typically open to reveal white or pale pink flowers. Its bark is typically fibrous and peels in strips, and it is commonly found in dry, sandy soils across southern Australia.

This variant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and heat.
