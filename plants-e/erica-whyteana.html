Erica whyteana

Erica whyteana, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica whyteana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form compact, attractive shrubs.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
