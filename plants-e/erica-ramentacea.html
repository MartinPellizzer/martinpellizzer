Erica ramentacea

Erica ramentacea, commonly known as the silver heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage covered in a fine layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring.

Erica ramentacea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
