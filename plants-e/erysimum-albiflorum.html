Erysimum albiflorum

Erysimum albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens. Its flowers, which range from white to pale yellow, bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's structure consists of a low-growing stem with alternate leaves and produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.
