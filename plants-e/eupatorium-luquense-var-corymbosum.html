Eupatorium luquense var. corymbosum

Eupatorium luquense var. corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
