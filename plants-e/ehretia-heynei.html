Ehretia heynei

Ehretia heynei, commonly known as the white-flowered ehretia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Ehretia heynei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage and floral display.
