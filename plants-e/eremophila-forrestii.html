Eremophila forrestii

Eremophila forrestii, commonly known as the Forest's eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking spikes of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, often pale or yellowish flowers that attract nectar-feeding insects.

Eremophila forrestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native landscapes.
