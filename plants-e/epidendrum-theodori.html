Epidendrum theodori

Epidendrum theodori, commonly known as the Theodore's Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous violet to purple blooms. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Epidendrum theodori is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
