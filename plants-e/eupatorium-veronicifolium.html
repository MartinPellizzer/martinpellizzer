Eupatorium veronicifolium

Eupatorium veronicifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium veronicifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
