Euphorbia magnicapsula var. lacertosa

Euphorbia magnicapsula var. lacertosa, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
