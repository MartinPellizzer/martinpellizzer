Eucharis × mastersii

Eucharis × mastersii, commonly known as the Amazon lily or white elephant ear, is a hybrid plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly the Amazon basin, and is characterized by its large, waxy, white leaves that resemble elephant ears. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, emerging from a spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Eucharis × mastersii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and unique floral display.
