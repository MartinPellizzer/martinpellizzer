Eugenia oligoneura

Eugenia oligoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, aromatic fruits similar to guavas. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Eugenia oligoneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
