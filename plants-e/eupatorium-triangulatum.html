Eupatorium triangulatum

Eupatorium triangulatum, commonly known as narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its triangular-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It is often found in the eastern and central United States, thriving in partial to full sunlight.

Its foliage and flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
