Erysimum capitatum subsp. lompocense

Erysimum capitatum subsp.

lompocense, commonly known as Lompoc wallflower, is a flowering plant native to California. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal grasslands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat woody.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
