Euphorbia herronii

Euphorbia herronii, commonly known as the Herron's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with a short, thick stem and dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Euphorbia herronii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
