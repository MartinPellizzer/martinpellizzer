Erodium cicutarium var. triviale

Erodium cicutarium var. triviale, commonly known as common stork's bill, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and produces small, bead-like fruits that are characteristic of the genus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
