Eria vaginata

Eria vaginata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, which helps in moisture retention.

Eria vaginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
