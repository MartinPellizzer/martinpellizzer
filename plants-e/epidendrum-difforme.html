Epidendrum difforme

Epidendrum difforme, commonly known as the variable epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other structures in its natural habitat. It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its adaptability and striking appearance make it a favored ornamental plant among orchid enthusiasts.
