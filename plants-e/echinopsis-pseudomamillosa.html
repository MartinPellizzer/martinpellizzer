Echinopsis pseudomamillosa

Echinopsis pseudomamillosa, commonly known as the "Pseudomamillosa Cactus," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines, which help to reduce water loss and protect against herbivores. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming at night to attract specific pollinators. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and unique adaptations, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and collections.
