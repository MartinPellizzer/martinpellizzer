Elaeocarpus clementis

Elaeocarpus clementis, commonly known as the Clement's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its growth habit is typically slow, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
