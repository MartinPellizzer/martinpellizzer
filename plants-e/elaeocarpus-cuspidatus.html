Elaeocarpus cuspidatus

Elaeocarpus cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Elaeocarpus cuspidatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
