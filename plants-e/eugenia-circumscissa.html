Eugenia circumscissa

Eugenia circumscissa, commonly known as the scarlet guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, citrus-scented berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The berries are rich in vitamin C and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
