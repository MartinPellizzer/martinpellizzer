Erodium staphylinum var. arenarium

Erodium staphylinum var. arenarium, commonly known as sand eryngo, is a small annual herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and has small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a hardy species capable of thriving in harsh environmental conditions.
