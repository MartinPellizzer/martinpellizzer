Erythrococca lasiococca

Erythrococca lasiococca, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used as a vegetable in some cuisines, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Erythrococca lasiococca thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
