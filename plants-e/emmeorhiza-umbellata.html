Emmeorhiza umbellata

Emmeorhiza umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in dense tufts at the base of the plant.

Emmeorhiza umbellata is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
