Euphorbia malachophylla

Euphorbia malachophylla, commonly known as the silver euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
