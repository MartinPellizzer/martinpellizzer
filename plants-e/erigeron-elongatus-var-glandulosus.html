Erigeron elongatus var. glandulosus

Erigeron elongatus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving the plant its specific variety name. It is native to North America and thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils.

This plant is often found in open areas and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
