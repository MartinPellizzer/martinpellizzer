Escallonia berberifolia

Escallonia berberifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has smooth, reddish bark and produces small, orange-yellow berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Escallonia berberifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
