Eleocharis austrotexana

Eleocharis austrotexana, commonly known as the Mexican spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a slightly rough texture.

Eleocharis austrotexana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
