Epidendrum micropus

Epidendrum micropus, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often pendulous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and usually display a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Epidendrum micropus is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
