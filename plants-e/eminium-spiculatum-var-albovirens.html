Eminium spiculatum var. albovirens

Eminium spiculatum var. albovirens, commonly known as the white-green spiky plant, is a unique succulent with a distinctive appearance.

It features slender, spiky stems that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The leaves are small, pointed, and typically have a pale green or white hue, contributing to its striking visual appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to harsh growing conditions.
