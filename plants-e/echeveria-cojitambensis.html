Echeveria cojitambensis

Echeveria cojitambensis, commonly known as the Cojitambo echeveria, is a succulent plant native to central Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
