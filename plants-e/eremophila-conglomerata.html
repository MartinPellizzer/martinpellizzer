Eremophila conglomerata

Eremophila conglomerata, commonly known as the clustered emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in poor, sandy soils. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.

Eremophila conglomerata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in local landscaping and restoration projects.
