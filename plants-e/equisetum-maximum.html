Equisetum maximum

Equisetum maximum, commonly known as the horse tail, is a primitive plant that belongs to the genus Equisetum.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and its distinctive spore-producing structures called strobila. This plant is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils near streams and wetlands. Equisetum maximum is a perennial plant that can grow up to several feet tall.

Its unique morphology and ancient lineage make it an important subject in the study of plant evolution.
