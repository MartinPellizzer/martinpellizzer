Eucalyptus amplifolia subsp. sessiliflora

Eucalyptus amplifolia subsp.

sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to western and southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, peeling bark on its trunk. It produces small, globular flower clusters, which are usually white or pale blue in color.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often thriving in sandy or rocky soils.
