Eriosema procumbens var. monophyllum

Eriosema procumbens var. monophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. The plant features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, and its leaves are narrowly elliptic with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
