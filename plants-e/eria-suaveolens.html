Eria suaveolens

Eria suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, greenish-white petals with a distinctive, sweet aroma that attracts pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Eria suaveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
