Erythroxylum bicolor

Erythroxylum bicolor, commonly known as the red bilva, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is known for its unique leaf structure, which has two distinct colors on each side. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The species is also notable for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
