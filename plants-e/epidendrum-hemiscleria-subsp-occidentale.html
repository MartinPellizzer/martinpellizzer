Epidendrum hemiscleria subsp. occidentale

Epidendrum hemiscleria subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the Western Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central America. It is characterized by its pendulous, often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

This subspecies is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
