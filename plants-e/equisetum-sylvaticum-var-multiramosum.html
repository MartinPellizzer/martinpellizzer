Equisetum sylvaticum var. multiramosum

Equisetum sylvaticum var. multiramosum, commonly known as the bristly horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, jointed stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stems, which are typically found in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of streams.

This variety is notable for its robust growth and its historical use in traditional medicine.
