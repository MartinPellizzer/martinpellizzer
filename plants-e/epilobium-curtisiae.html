Epilobium curtisiae

Epilobium curtisiae, commonly known as the dwarf willow, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of less than 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs.

Epilobium curtisiae is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
