Epilobium esquirolii

Epilobium esquirolii, commonly known as the Sierra buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, reddish-purple flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

Epilobium esquirolii is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, humid climates.
