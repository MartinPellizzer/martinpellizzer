Eugenia condensata

Eugenia condensata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *pau-ferro*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small, dark red drupe, is edible and has a tart flavor.

Eugenia condensata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
