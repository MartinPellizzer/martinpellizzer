Echinops persepolitanus

Echinops persepolitanus, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown, which give it its genus name "Echinops," meaning "spiny head.". The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, purple or blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Echinops persepolitanus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
