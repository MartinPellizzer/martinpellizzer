Euphorbia squamosa var. talyschensis

Euphorbia squamosa var. talyschensis, commonly known as the Talyschenskaya euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, protective layer. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
