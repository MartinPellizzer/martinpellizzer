Euphorbia rosea f. auricularia

Euphorbia rosea f. auricularia, commonly known as the auricular euphorbia, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and distinctive, ear-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to arid regions of southern Africa. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified floral structures typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
