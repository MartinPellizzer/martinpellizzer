Epacris rhombifolia

Epacris rhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers are typically pink or red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.
