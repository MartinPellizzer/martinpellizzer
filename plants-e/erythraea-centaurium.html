Erythraea centaurium

Erythraea centaurium, commonly known as the red centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Erythraea centaurium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
