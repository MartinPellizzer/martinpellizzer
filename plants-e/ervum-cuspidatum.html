Ervum cuspidatum

Ervum cuspidatum, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and a distinctive, spiny stem that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
