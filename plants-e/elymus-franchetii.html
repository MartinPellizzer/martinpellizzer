Elymus franchetii

Elymus franchetii, commonly known as the Chinese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Elymus franchetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
