Eulophia venusta

Eulophia venusta, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green to golden flowers arranged in a raceme, often with a distinct fragrance. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, typically growing in a rosette pattern.

Eulophia venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
