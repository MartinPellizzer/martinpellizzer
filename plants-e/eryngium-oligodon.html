Eryngium oligodon

Eryngium oligodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Eryngium oligodon is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
