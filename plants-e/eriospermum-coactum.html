Eriospermum coactum

Eriospermum coactum, commonly known as the stickyleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky secretion. The plant has small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments.

Eriospermum coactum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
