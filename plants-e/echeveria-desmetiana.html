Echeveria desmetiana

Echeveria desmetiana, commonly known as the Desert Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, and often bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container gardening.
