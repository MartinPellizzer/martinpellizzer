Euphorbia lockhartii

Euphorbia lockhartii, commonly known as the Lockhart's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous cylindrical, ribbed leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its leaves make it an interesting addition to xeriscapic gardens.
