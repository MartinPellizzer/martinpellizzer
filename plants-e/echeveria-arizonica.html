Echeveria arizonica

Echeveria arizonica, commonly known as Arizona stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces striking clusters of pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Echeveria arizonica is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
