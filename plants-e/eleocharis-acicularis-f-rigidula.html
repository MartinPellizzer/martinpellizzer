Eleocharis acicularis f. rigidula

Eleocharis acicularis f. rigidula, commonly known as the rigid spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of tightly packed, spikelet-like structures. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its structural rigidity.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
