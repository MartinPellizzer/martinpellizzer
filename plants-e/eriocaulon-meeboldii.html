Eriocaulon meeboldii

Eriocaulon meeboldii, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting diverse insect populations.
