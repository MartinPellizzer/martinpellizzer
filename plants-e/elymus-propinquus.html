Elymus propinquus

Elymus propinquus, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Elymus propinquus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
