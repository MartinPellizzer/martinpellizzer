Erigeron jamaicensis

Erigeron jamaicensis, commonly known as Jamaican fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
