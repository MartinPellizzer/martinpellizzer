Eugenia laxa

Eugenia laxa, commonly known as the small-leaved guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a yellowish color and is edible.

Eugenia laxa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic foliage.
