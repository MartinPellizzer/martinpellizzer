Euphorbia occidentaustralica

Euphorbia occidentaustralica, commonly known as the western austral euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique structure called a cyathium. Its leaves are typically narrow and may be reduced or absent in some varieties.

Euphorbia occidentaustralica is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
