Episcia purpusii

Episcia purpusii, commonly known as the Crimson Jewel, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Episcia purpusii is also known for its ability to produce offsets, making it a popular choice for propagation.
