Erodium ambiguum

Erodium ambiguum, commonly known as common stork's bill, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces distinctive, club-shaped seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
