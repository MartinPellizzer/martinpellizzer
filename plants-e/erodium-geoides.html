Erodium geoides

Erodium geoides, commonly known as the Australian catchfly, is a low-growing annual herb native to Australia.

It features small, pink to purple flowers and oval, hairy leaves. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Erodium geoides is adapted to withstand drought and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
