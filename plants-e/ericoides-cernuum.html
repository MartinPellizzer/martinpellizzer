Ericoides cernuum

Ericoides cernuum, commonly known as the drooping heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Ericoides cernuum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
