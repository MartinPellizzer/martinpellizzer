Eriogonum longifolium var. longidens

Eriogonum longifolium var. longidens, commonly known as the longleaf globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are clustered in globular heads, producing yellow to orange blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is characterized by its elongated leaves and is well adapted to drought conditions.
