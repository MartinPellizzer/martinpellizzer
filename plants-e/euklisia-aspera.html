Euklisia aspera

Euklisia aspera, commonly known as rough euklisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom throughout the growing season. Euklisia aspera thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
