Erigeron petrophilus var. petrophilus

Erigeron petrophilus var. petrophilus, commonly known as the rock eryngo, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators.
