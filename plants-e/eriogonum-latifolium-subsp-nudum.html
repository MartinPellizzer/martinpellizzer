Eriogonum latifolium subsp. nudum

Eriogonum latifolium subsp.

nudum, commonly known as the nakedleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Eriogonum latifolium subsp.

nudum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to arid environments.
