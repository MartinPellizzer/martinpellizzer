Epilobium tetragonum subsp. gillotii

Epilobium tetragonum subsp.

gillotii, commonly known as Gillot's willowherb, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect, often branched stems that are covered in glandular hairs.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges. It produces showy, pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, riverbanks, and wet meadows.
