Eupatorium prionophyllum var. asymmetrum

Eupatorium prionophyllum var. asymmetrum, commonly known as the asymmetrical eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in botanical studies and native plant gardens.
