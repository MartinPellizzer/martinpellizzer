Erinus pulchellus

Erinus pulchellus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its profuse display of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Erinus pulchellus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
