Euphorbia corollata

Euphorbia corollata, commonly known as the pink spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a low-growing, branched stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by colorful bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Euphorbia corollata is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation.
