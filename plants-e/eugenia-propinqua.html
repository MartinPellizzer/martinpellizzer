Eugenia propinqua

Eugenia propinqua, commonly known as the small-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe.

Eugenia propinqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
