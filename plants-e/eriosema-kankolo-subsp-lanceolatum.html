Eriosema kankolo subsp. lanceolatum

Eriosema kankolo subsp.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
