Euphorbia literata

Euphorbia literata, commonly known as the "narrowleaf spurge," is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, somewhat hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is drought-tolerant and can survive in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Euphorbia literata is also known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
