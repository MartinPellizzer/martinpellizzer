Eugenia roxburghii

Eugenia roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian bay leaf or Malabar kino, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are harvested for their essential oils, which have a strong, spicy aroma.

Eugenia roxburghii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
