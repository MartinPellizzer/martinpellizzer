Erica cerinthoides var. barbertona

Erica cerinthoides var. barbertona, commonly known as the Barberton heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its floral displays. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
