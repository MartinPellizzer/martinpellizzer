Erica elgonensis

Erica elgonensis, commonly known as the Elgon heath, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of East Africa, particularly around Mount Elgon.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the summer months. Erica elgonensis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates at high elevations.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various native insect and bird species.
