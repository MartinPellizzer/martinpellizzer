Euphorbia colorata

Euphorbia colorata, commonly known as the red-stemmed spurge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and yellowish-green leaves, which are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
