Euphorbia nigrispina

Euphorbia nigrispina, commonly known as the black-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its spines and toxic sap, it requires careful handling to avoid skin irritation.
