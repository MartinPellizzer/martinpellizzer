Eriolepis lanigera

Eriolepis lanigera, commonly known as the woolly leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on the underside of its fronds, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to botanical gardens and greenhouses.
