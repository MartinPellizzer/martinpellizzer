Eugenia louisae

Eugenia louisae, commonly known as the jamocha huckleberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible, dark purple berries that are rich in flavor and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Eugenia louisae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and provide habitat for wildlife.
