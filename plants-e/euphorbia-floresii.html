Euphorbia floresii

Euphorbia floresii, commonly known as the Floresian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the island of Flores in Indonesia.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by colorful bracts, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
