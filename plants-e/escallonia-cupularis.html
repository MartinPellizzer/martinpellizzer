Escallonia cupularis

Escallonia cupularis, commonly known as the Chilean fire bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Escallonia cupularis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
