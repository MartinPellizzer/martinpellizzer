Euphorbia pentlandii

Euphorbia pentlandii, commonly known as the Pentland's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
