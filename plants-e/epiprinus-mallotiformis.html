Epiprinus mallotiformis

Epiprinus mallotiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Epiprinus mallotiformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
