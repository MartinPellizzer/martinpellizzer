Echinops paniculatus

Echinops paniculatus, commonly known as the spiked centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features spiny bracts surrounding its yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in dry, sunny conditions.

Echinops paniculatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
