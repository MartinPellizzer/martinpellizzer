Eleocharis grandis

Eleocharis grandis, commonly known as the tall spike-rush, is a species of sedge that is native to various regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish in color. Eleocharis grandis is characterized by its erect, tall stem and is often found in shallow water or moist soil.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
