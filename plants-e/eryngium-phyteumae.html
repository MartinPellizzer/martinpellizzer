Eryngium phyteumae

Eryngium phyteumae, commonly known as the Greek thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to purple spiky flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silvery-gray, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine bristles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
