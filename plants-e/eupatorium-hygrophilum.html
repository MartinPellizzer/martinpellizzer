Eupatorium hygrophilum

Eupatorium hygrophilum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium hygrophilum is a valuable addition to native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
