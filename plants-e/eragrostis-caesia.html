Eragrostis caesia

Eragrostis caesia, commonly known as bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce fluffy seeds. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a clumping growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
