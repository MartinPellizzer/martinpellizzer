Eulophia sylviae

Eulophia sylviae, commonly known as the forest eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often near the forest floor. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in a raceme, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

Eulophia sylviae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
