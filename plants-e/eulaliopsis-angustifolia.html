Eulaliopsis angustifolia

Eulaliopsis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves with a narrow, linear shape and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical structure includes fibrous roots and a rhizomatous stem system, which aids in its spread and survival.

Eulaliopsis angustifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland ecosystems.
