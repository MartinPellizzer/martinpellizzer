Echinocactus horizonthalonius subsp. diabolicus

Echinocactus horizonthalonius subsp.

diabolicus, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height.

The plant features prominent, ribbed spines and produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique morphology and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
