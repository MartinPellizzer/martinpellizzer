Eriogonum loganum

Eriogonum loganum, commonly known as the Logan's Buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Eriogonum loganum is an important component of local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
