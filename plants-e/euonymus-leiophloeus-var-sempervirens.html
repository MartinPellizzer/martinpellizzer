Euonymus leiophloeus var. sempervirens

Euonymus leiophloeus var. sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen euonymus, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains vibrant throughout the year. The leaves are typically elliptical in shape with a smooth margin and a glossy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by red-orange berries that are attractive to birds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
