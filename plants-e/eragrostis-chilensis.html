Eragrostis chilensis

Eragrostis chilensis, commonly known as Chilean lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient.

Eragrostis chilensis is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
