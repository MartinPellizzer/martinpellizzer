Elaeocarpus serratus

Elaeocarpus serratus, commonly known as the serrated elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, blue-black berries that are edible for some bird species. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it often grows in coastal or lowland rainforest areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
