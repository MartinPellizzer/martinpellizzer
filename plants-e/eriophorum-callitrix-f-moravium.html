Eriophorum callitrix f. moravium

Eriophorum callitrix f. moravium, commonly known as the Moravian cottongrass, is a variant of the common cottongrass.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble cotton. This plant typically thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its adaptation to nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
