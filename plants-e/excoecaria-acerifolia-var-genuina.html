Excoecaria acerifolia var. genuina

Excoecaria acerifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the white-leaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically white or pale green, contrasting with its dark green veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
