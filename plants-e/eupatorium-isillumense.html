Eupatorium isillumense

Eupatorium isillumense, commonly known as the Florida ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

Eupatorium isillumense plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in natural habitats across Florida.
