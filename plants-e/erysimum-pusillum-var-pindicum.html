Erysimum pusillum var. pindicum

Erysimum pusillum var. pindicum, commonly known as the Pindus Brassica, is a flowering plant native to the Pindus Mountains in Greece.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant characterized by its compact stature and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or arid environments.

The flowers bloom early in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
