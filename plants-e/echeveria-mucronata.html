Echeveria mucronata

Echeveria mucronata, commonly known as the Pointed Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as bloom. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure to thrive.

Due to its compact size and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
