Eulophidium cordylinophyllum

Eulophidium cordylinophyllum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to South America.

It has broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing habitat for various aquatic life. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrates of still or slow-moving waters.

Eulophidium cordylinophyllum plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
