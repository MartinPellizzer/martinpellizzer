Eugenia lambertiana

Eugenia lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
