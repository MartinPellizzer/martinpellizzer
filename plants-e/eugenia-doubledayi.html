Eugenia doubledayi

Eugenia doubledayi, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Eugenia doubledayi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
