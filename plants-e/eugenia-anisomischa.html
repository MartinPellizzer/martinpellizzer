Eugenia anisomischa

Eugenia anisomischa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
