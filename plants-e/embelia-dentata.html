Embelia dentata

Embelia dentata, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
