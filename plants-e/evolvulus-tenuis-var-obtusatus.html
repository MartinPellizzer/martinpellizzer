Evolvulus tenuis var. obtusatus

Evolvulus tenuis var. obtusatus, commonly known as the bluntleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems that often grow along the ground. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other species of morning glories.

The varietal name 'obtusatus' refers to its blunt or rounded leaf shape, distinguishing it from other varieties of Evolvulus tenuis.
