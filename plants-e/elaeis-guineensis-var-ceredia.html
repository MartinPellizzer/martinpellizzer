Elaeis guineensis var. ceredia

Elaeis guineensis var. ceredia, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fruit that contain oil-rich seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight. Its cultivation is significant for the production of palm oil, which is used in various food and industrial products.

However, its expansion has raised concerns about environmental impact and biodiversity loss.
