Epidendrum alticola

Epidendrum alticola, commonly known as the highland epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surfaces of other plants without being parasitic. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its habitat typically includes humid, shaded environments at higher elevations.

Epidendrum alticola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
