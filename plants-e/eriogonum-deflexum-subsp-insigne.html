Eriogonum deflexum subsp. insigne

Eriogonum deflexum subsp.

insigne, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in open slopes and meadows.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
