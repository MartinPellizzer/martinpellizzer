Echinocactus wislizeni var. albispinus

Echinocactus wislizeni var. albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, white spines, which give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, typically blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
