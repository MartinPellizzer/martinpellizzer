Ecliptostelma molle

Ecliptostelma molle, commonly known as the soft-stemmed eclipta, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Ecliptostelma molle is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
