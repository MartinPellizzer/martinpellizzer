Erigeron bloomeri var. pubens

Erigeron bloomeri var. pubens, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant features dense, woolly silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. It produces small blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
