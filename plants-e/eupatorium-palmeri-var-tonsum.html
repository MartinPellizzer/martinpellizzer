Eupatorium palmeri var. tonsum

Eupatorium palmeri var. tonsum, commonly known as Palmer's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are often rough to the touch and have a strong, bitter scent.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
