Eucalyptus obconica

Eucalyptus obconica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often coated with a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, globular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Eucalyptus obconica is a hardy shrub or small tree that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
