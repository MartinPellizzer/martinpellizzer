Epidendrum anoglossoides

Epidendrum anoglossoides, commonly known as the narrow-lipped epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in summer and are known for their intricate, spurred petals.

Epidendrum anoglossoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
