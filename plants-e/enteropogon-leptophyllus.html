Enteropogon leptophyllus

Enteropogon leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
