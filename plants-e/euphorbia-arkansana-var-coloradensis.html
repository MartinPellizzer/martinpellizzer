Euphorbia arkansana var. coloradensis

Euphorbia arkansana var. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado prickly pear, is a succulent shrub native to the southern United States.

It features a thick, segmented stem covered in spiny, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water storage.

Its latex sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
