Echinocactus linkeanus

Echinocactus linkeanus, commonly known as the Mexican sunburst cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and prominent, ribbed structure, which helps it store water efficiently. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its spines are typically pale to dark green, providing both protection and a unique aesthetic.

Echinocactus linkeanus is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and popular choice for xeriscaping.
