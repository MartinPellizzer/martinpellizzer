Eriosoriopsis cycloloba

Eriosoriopsis cycloloba, commonly known as the cyclolobe rust fungus, is a plant pathogen that primarily affects the leaves of certain leguminous plants.

It belongs to the family Pucciniales and is characterized by its distinctive rust-like symptoms, including orange to reddish pustules on the leaf surfaces. The fungus overwinters as teliospores on infected plant debris, which germinate in the spring to produce basidiospores. These spores are dispersed by wind and infect new host plants, leading to the development of new rust colonies.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the growth patterns of its host plants, making it an important consideration in agricultural and ecological contexts.
