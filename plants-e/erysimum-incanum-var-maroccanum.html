Erysimum incanum var. maroccanum

Erysimum incanum var. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are typically grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
