Eulophia brunnea

Eulophia brunnea, commonly known as the brown orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of small, brownish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors and stream banks. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in variable conditions.

Eulophia brunnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
