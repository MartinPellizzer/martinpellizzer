Eupatorium weddellii

Eupatorium weddellii, commonly known as Weddell's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and white to pale blue in color.

Eupatorium weddellii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
