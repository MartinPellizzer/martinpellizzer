Eriogonum sphaerocephalum var. sphaerocephalum

Eriogonum sphaerocephalum var. sphaerocephalum, commonly known as the globe milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically woody and branched. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
