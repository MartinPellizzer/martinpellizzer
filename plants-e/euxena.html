Euxena

Euxena, commonly known as the common thornapple, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading shrub-like growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine pubescence. Euxena species produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, warty seeds.
