Erythroxylum armatum

Erythroxylum armatum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, elongated berries. These berries contain seeds and are often used in traditional medicine.

It is also known for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant foliage and fruit.
