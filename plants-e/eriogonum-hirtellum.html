Eriogonum hirtellum

Eriogonum hirtellum, commonly known as the hirtellum buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Eriogonum hirtellum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
