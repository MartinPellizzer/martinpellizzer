Erythrina bracteata

Erythrina bracteata, commonly known as the flame tree or red torch tree, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its striking red flowers and distinctive leaf structure.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features trifoliate leaves with long, slender stipules, and its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, often displaying a vibrant red or orange hue. Erythrina bracteata is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its showy blooms.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which enhances soil fertility.
