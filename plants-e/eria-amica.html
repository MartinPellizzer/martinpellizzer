Eria amica

Eria amica, commonly known as the friendship orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence produces clusters of small, colorful blossoms.

Eria amica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
