Epilobium brunnescens

Epilobium brunnescens, commonly known as brown willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and streambanks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
