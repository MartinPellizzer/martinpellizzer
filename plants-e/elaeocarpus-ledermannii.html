Elaeocarpus ledermannii

Elaeocarpus ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
