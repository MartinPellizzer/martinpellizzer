Euphorbia silenifolia

Euphorbia silenifolia, commonly known as the "silenifolia spurge," is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and the plant is known for its ability to store water in its stems, making it drought-tolerant.

Euphorbia silenifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
