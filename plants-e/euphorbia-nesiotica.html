Euphorbia nesiotica

Euphorbia nesiotica, commonly known as the Socotra cactus, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, tree-like structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a slow growth rate, making it a rare and striking species.

Euphorbia nesiotica is also notable for its potential medicinal properties and its role in the unique ecosystem of Socotra.
