Erodium pulverulentum subsp. pulverulentum

Erodium pulverulentum subsp.

pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty erodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small annual herb with slender stems and small, pale pink or white flowers.

The plant produces small, dry fruits called legumes, which contain seeds covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and distinctive, finely pubescent foliage.
