Escallonia coquimbensis var. salicifolia

Escallonia coquimbensis var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved escallonia, is a shrub native to Chile.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that resemble willow leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
