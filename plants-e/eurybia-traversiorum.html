Eurybia traversiorum

Eurybia traversiorum, commonly known as the slender New Zealand bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Eurybia traversiorum is often found in natural habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
