Erica mackayana

Erica mackayana, commonly known as the Mackay's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica mackayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
