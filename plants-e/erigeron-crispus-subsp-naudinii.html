Erigeron crispus subsp. naudinii

Erigeron crispus subsp.

naudinii, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

This subspecies is often found in the western United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
