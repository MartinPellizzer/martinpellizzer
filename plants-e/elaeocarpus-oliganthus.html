Elaeocarpus oliganthus

Elaeocarpus oliganthus, commonly known as the black elder or black nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers. The tree produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are encased in a hard, woody shell. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their nutritional content.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
