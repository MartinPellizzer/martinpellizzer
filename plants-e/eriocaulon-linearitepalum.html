Eriocaulon linearitepalum

Eriocaulon linearitepalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland and swampy habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Eriocaulaceae family. Its root system is adapted to waterlogged soils, allowing it to thrive in marshy environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
