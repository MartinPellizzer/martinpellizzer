Escallonia longidens

Escallonia longidens, commonly known as the long-stemmed escallonia, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Escallonia longidens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
