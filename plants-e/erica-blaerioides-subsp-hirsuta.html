Erica blaerioides subsp. hirsuta

Erica blaerioides subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
