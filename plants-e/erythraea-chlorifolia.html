Erythraea chlorifolia

Erythraea chlorifolia, commonly known as the green-leaved erythraea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive bright green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Erythraea chlorifolia thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
