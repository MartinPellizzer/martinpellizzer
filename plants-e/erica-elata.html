Erica elata

Erica elata, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Erica elata thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in harsh, cold climates.
