Eragrostis guianensis

Eragrostis guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Eragrostis guianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in suitable climates.
