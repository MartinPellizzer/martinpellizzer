Eupatorium halimifolium var. latifolium

Eupatorium halimifolium var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
