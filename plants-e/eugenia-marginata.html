Eugenia marginata

Eugenia marginata, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which is the most notable feature, is a bright red, segmented berry with a thick, colorful rind. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value and edible fruit make it both a horticultural and culinary interest.
