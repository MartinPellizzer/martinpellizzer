Erysimum cheiranthoides var. pygmaea

Erysimum cheiranthoides var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact foliage with small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants with minimal maintenance.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
