Eucalyptus leucoxylon var. piperita

Eucalyptus leucoxylon var. piperita, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the winter months. Its bark is often flaky or fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
