Entada scandens subsp. umbonata

Entada scandens subsp.

umbonata, commonly known as the African horned bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its palmate leaves and distinctive horn-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces yellow flowers and develops woody pods that contain the ornamental seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
