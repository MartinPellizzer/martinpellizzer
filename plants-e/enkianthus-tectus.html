Enkianthus tectus

Enkianthus tectus, commonly known as the Chinese flameflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Enkianthus tectus is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
