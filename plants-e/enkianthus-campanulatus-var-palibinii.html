Enkianthus campanulatus var. palibinii

Enkianthus campanulatus var. palibinii, commonly known as the Palibinii bellflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
