Echium armatum

Echium armatum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a distinctive, glandular-haired stem and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Echium armatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
