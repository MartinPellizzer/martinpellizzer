Erica ephemera

Erica ephemera, commonly known as the summer heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen foliage and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Erica ephemera thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is a short-lived perennial, typically flowering for only a few weeks during the summer months.
