Eriogonum ovalifolium var. celsum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. celsum, commonly known as the Celsum's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of oval, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This species is adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
