Eragrostis acutiflora

Eragrostis acutiflora, commonly known as the acute-flowered lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, awned florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence.

Eragrostis acutiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
