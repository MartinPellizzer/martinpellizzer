Erythrodes fissirostris

Erythrodes fissirostris, commonly known as the red-fissured orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and green coloration, with elongated, narrow leaves and a distinctive, spurred flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often with a sweet fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
