Eriogonum spergulinum subsp. reddingianum

Eriogonum spergulinum subsp.

reddingianum, commonly known as Redding's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to California. It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage and produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub ecosystems.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
