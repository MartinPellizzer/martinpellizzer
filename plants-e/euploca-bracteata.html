Euploca bracteata

Euploca bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Euploca bracteata is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
