Eremophila attenuata

Eremophila attenuata, commonly known as the slender emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila attenuata is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
