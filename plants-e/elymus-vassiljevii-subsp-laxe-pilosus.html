Elymus vassiljevii subsp. laxe-pilosus

Elymus vassiljevii subsp.

laxe-pilosus, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a silky texture.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
