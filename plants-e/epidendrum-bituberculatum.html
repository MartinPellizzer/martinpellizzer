Epidendrum bituberculatum

Epidendrum bituberculatum, commonly known as the two-ribbed epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that are elongated and often covered in a fibrous sheath. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, and yellow. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Epidendrum bituberculatum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
