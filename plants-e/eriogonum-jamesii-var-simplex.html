Eriogonum jamesii var. simplex

Eriogonum jamesii var. simplex, commonly known as the Great Basin mullein, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, erect stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage that is often fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower clusters in late summer, which attract a variety of pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
