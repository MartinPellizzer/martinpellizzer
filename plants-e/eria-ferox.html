Eria ferox

Eria ferox, commonly known as the sharp-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Eria ferox is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
