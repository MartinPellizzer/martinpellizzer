Ericoides lachnaeodes

Ericoides lachnaeodes, commonly known as the woolly erica, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Ericoides lachnaeodes thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, with drought tolerance and a slow growth rate.
