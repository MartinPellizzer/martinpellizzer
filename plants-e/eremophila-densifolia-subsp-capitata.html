Eremophila densifolia subsp. capitata

Eremophila densifolia subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in shrublands and open woodlands, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in suitable climates.
