Epidendrum bermejoense

Epidendrum bermejoense, commonly known as the Bermejo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in humid tropical environments. The plant produces striking, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
