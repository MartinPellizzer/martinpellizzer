Echeveria jepsonii

Echeveria jepsonii, commonly known as the Jepson's echeveria, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Echeveria jepsonii is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor and outdoor cultivation.
