Eremostachys popovii

Eremostachys popovii, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, aiding in water retention.

Eremostachys popovii is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
