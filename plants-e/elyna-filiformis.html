Elyna filiformis

Elyna filiformis, commonly known as the slender elyna, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the soil in its natural habitat.

Elyna filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
