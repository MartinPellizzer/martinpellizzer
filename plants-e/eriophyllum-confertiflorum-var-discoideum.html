Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. discoideum

Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. discoideum, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are linear and hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
