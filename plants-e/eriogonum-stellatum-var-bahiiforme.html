Eriogonum stellatum var. bahiiforme

Eriogonum stellatum var. bahiiforme, commonly known as the coastal buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to coastal and inland desert environments.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
