Embelia kerrii

Embelia kerrii, commonly known as the African star anise, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, aromatic seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to subtropical and tropical gardens.
