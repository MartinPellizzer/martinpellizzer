Eubotrys arvensis

Eubotrys arvensis, commonly known as field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eubotrys arvensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
