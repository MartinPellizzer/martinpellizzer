Eriosema afzelii

Eriosema afzelii, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Eriosema afzelii is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in its native habitat.
