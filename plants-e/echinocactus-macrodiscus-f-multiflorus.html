Echinocactus macrodiscus f. multiflorus

Echinocactus macrodiscus f. multiflorus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a unique variant of the standard echinocactus species.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny exterior and a compact, globular shape that resembles a star. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
