Eriocaulon hildebrandtii

Eriocaulon hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's spikerush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, swampy habitats, often forming dense mats in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are tiny and clustered, contributing to its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens and wetland restoration projects.
