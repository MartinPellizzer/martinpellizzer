Eragrostis concinna

Eragrostis concinna, commonly known as slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eragrostis concinna is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
