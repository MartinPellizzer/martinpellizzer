Euphrasia minima var. intermedia

Euphrasia minima var. intermedia, commonly known as the small whitlow grass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters and features delicate, hairy stems covered in small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating eye conditions and respiratory ailments.
