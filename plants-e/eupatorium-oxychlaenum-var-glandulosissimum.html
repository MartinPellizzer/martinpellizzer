Eupatorium oxychlaenum var. glandulosissimum

Eupatorium oxychlaenum var. glandulosissimum, commonly known as the glandular eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
