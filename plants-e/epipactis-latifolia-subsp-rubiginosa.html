Epipactis latifolia subsp. rubiginosa

Epipactis latifolia subsp.

rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-tongued helleborine, is a herbaceous perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features broad, pleated leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive red or orange lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it has a complex relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish markings on the lip and its slightly different morphology compared to other Epipactis species.
