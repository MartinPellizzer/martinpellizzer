Eragrostis lappula var. divaricata

Eragrostis lappula var. divaricata, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a variant of the fingergrass species native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, often branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
