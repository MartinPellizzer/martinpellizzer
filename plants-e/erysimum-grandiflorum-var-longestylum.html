Erysimum grandiflorum var. longestylum

Erysimum grandiflorum var. longestylum, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are aromatic and slightly succulent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract pollinators.
