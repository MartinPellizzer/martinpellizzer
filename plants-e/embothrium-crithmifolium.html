Embothrium crithmifolium

Embothrium crithmifolium, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Embothrium crithmifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
