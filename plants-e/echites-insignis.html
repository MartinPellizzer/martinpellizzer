Echites insignis

Echites insignis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Echites insignis is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
