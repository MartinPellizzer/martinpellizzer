Epipactis atrorubens subvar. viridiflora

Epipactis atrorubens subvar. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and its distinctive, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent purple veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has a complex root system and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
