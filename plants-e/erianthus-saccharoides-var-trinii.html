Erianthus saccharoides var. trinii

Erianthus saccharoides var. trinii, commonly known as the Trini giant bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height, making it one of the tallest grasses in its native habitat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its rapid growth and structural strength make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental use.
