Euphorbia exilispina

Euphorbia exilispina, commonly known as the slender-spined euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique flower-like structures. Its spiny leaves and stems serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Euphorbia exilispina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
