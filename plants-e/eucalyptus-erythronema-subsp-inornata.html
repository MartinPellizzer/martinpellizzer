Eucalyptus erythronema subsp. inornata

Eucalyptus erythronema subsp.

inornata, commonly known as the red gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that ranges from grey to reddish-brown.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge when young. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green to yellowish in color.

This species is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
