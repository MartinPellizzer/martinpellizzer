Elaeagnus conferta subsp. dendroidea

Elaeagnus conferta subsp.

dendroidea, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by small, edible berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
