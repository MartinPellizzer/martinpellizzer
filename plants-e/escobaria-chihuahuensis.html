Escobaria chihuahuensis

Escobaria chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Chihuahua Desert in northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It has a shallow root system that helps it absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

Escobaria chihuahuensis is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
