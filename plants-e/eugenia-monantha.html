Eugenia monantha

Eugenia monantha, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, dark purple berries. Eugenia monantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural gardens in warm climates.
