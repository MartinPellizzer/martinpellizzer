Eugenia byssacea

Eugenia byssacea, commonly known as the silky apple or red silky apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silky pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisine.

Eugenia byssacea is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
