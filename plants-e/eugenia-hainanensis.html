Eugenia hainanensis

Eugenia hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Hainan Island in China and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The fruit, which is similar to a guava, is yellow when ripe and contains a sweet, juicy pulp.
