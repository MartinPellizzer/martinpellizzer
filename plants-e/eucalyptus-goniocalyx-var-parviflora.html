Eucalyptus goniocalyx var. parviflora

Eucalyptus goniocalyx var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs and small, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The flowers are small and usually pale or creamy white, arranged in clusters. This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
