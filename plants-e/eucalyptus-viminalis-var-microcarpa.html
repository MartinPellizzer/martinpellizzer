Eucalyptus viminalis var. microcarpa

Eucalyptus viminalis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

The species is notable for its role in ecological systems and its use in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australian communities.
