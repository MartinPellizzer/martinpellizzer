Eria viridibracteata

Eria viridibracteata, commonly known as the green-bract eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Eria viridibracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
