Erycibe peguensis

Erycibe peguensis, commonly known as the Burmese star fruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble star-shaped berries. These fruits are edible and have a sweet, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
