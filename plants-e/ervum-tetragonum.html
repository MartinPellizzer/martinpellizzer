Ervum tetragonum

Ervum tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its square-shaped stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Ervum tetragonum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
