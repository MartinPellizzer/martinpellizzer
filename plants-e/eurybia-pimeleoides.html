Eurybia pimeleoides

Eurybia pimeleoides, commonly known as the yellow wood-sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Eurybia pimeleoides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
