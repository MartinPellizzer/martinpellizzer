Eulychnia acida var. elata

Eulychnia acida var. elata, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a compact, branching structure with fleshy, spiny leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

Eulychnia acida var. elata is well-suited for xeriscaping and requires minimal water once established.
