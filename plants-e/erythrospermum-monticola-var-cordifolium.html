Erythrospermum monticola var. cordifolium

Erythrospermum monticola var. cordifolium, commonly known as the mountain erythrospermum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green, and its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is evergreen, with a shrub-like growth habit, and it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
