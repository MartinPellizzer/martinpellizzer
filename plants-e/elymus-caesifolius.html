Elymus caesifolius

Elymus caesifolius, commonly known as the silver wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Elymus caesifolius is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects.
