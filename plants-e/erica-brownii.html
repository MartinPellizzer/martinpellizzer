Erica brownii

Erica brownii, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica brownii is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
