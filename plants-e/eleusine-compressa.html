Eleusine compressa

Eleusine compressa, commonly known as bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush, composed of tightly packed spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Eleusine compressa is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
