Echinopsis hertrichiana

Echinopsis hertrichiana, commonly known as the Hertrich's moon cactus, is a striking cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stem and vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths and bats.

Echinopsis hertrichiana is a popular choice for collectors due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
