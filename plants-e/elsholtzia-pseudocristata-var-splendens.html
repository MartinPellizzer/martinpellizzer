Elsholtzia pseudocristata var. splendens

Elsholtzia pseudocristata var. splendens, commonly known as the Splendid Elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
