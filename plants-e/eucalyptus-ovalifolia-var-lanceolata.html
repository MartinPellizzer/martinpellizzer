Eucalyptus ovalifolia var. lanceolata

Eucalyptus ovalifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pale yellow. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

The tree or shrub form varies depending on environmental conditions, and it plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
