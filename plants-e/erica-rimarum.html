Erica rimarum

Erica rimarum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and small trees known for their ability to thrive in acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica rimarum is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and it is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its dense growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
