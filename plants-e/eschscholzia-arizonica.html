Eschscholzia arizonica

Eschscholzia arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona poppy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces striking orange to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

The Arizona poppy is a symbol of the Sonoran Desert and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
