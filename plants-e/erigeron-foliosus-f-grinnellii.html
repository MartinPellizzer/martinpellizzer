Erigeron foliosus f. grinnellii

Erigeron foliosus f. grinnellii, commonly known as Grinnell's blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pollinator attractant and contributes to its ecological significance in arid environments.
