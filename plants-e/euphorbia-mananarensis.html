Euphorbia mananarensis

Euphorbia mananarensis, commonly known as the Mananara euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces a unique yellowish-green sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to the dry conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
