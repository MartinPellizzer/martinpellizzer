Epidendrum fasciola

Epidendrum fasciola, commonly known as the yellow-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are usually green with prominent yellow veins. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes, and are known for their fragrant scent.

Epidendrum fasciola is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
