Eria muscicola var. brevilinguis

Eria muscicola var. brevilinguis, commonly known as the short-leaved eria, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation and botanical gardens.
