Epidendrum tampense

Epidendrum tampense, commonly known as the Tampico orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and can bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum tampense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
