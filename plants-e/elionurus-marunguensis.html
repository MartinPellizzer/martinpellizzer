Elionurus marunguensis

Elionurus marunguensis, commonly known as the Marunguensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are usually green and covered with fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Elionurus marunguensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
