Eriogonum friscanum

Eriogonum friscanum, commonly known as the Friscanum buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing bright yellow to orange flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Eriogonum friscanum is an important plant for local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
