Echinocactus borchersii

Echinocactus borchersii, commonly known as the Borchers' cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent collections and rock gardens.
