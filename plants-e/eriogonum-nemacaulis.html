Eriogonum nemacaulis

Eriogonum nemacaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces numerous small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Eriogonum nemacaulis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
