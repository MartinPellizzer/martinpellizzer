Eucalyptus kingsmillii

Eucalyptus kingsmillii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus kingsmillii is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
