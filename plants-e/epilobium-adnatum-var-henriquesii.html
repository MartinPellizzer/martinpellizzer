Epilobium adnatum var. henriquesii

Epilobium adnatum var. henriquesii, commonly known as the Portuguese willow or Henriques' willow, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
