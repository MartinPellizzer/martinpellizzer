Eupatorium gracilicaule

Eupatorium gracilicaule, commonly known as slenderhead thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has slender, erect stems that are often hairy. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are small and white to pale blue, arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Eupatorium gracilicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
