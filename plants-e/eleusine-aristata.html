Eleusine aristata

Eleusine aristata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Eleusine aristata is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
