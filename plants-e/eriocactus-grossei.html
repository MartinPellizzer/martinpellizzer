Eriocactus grossei

Eriocactus grossei, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Its spines are thick and can be quite long, providing protection from herbivores.

Eriocactus grossei is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent stems and having a deep root system for drought tolerance.
