Euodia canalensis

Euodia canalensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands laurustinus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, showing adaptability to the island's unique climate. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

Euodia canalensis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
