Erica terniflora

Erica terniflora, commonly known as the three-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, evergreen leaves. It produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red.

Erica terniflora is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
