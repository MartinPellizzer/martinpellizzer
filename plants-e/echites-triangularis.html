Echites triangularis

Echites triangularis, commonly known as the three-angled spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a succulent appearance, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Echites triangularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
