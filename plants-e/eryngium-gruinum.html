Eryngium gruinum

Eryngium gruinum, commonly known as the lesser thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue-green leaves and purple-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract various pollinators.

Eryngium gruinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
