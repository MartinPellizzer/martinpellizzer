Euphorbia iharanae

Euphorbia iharanae, commonly known as the Iharana euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its succulent, spine-tipped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a thick stem that stores water. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
