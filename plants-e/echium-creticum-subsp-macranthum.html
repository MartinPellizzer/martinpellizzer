Echium creticum subsp. macranthum

Echium creticum subsp.

macranthum, commonly known as the giant thistle or Cretan fleur-de-lis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds enclosed in a hardened, spiky capsule. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
