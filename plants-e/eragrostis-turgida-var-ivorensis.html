Eragrostis turgida var. ivorensis

Eragrostis turgida var. ivorensis, commonly known as the Ivorian lovegrass, is a grass species native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like arrangement of small, two-lipped florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
