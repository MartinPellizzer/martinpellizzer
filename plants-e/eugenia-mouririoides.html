Eugenia mouririoides

Eugenia mouririoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or mouriri, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its flowers are white or pale pink, adding ornamental value.

The fruit develops in clusters and is an important food source for various wildlife species.
