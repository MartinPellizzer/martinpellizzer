Elymus ciliaris var. hackelianus

Elymus ciliaris var. hackelianus, commonly known as Hackel's beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with fine, silky hairs along the edges. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
