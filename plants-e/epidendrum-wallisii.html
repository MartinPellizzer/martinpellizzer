Epidendrum wallisii

Epidendrum wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features long, arching stems that can grow up to several meters in length, supporting numerous lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Epidendrum wallisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a home environment.
