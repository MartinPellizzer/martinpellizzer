Eugenia lundiana

Eugenia lundiana, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia lundiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
