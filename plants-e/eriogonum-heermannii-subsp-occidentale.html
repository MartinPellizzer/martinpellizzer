Eriogonum heermannii subsp. occidentale

Eriogonum heermannii subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the western buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky soils and dry environments, showing adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
