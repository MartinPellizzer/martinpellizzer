Epilobium adenocaulon

Epilobium adenocaulon, commonly known as sticky willow or woolly willow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Epilobium adenocaulon thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
