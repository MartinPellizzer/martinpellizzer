Effusiella tortilis

Effusiella tortilis, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Effusiella tortilis is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
