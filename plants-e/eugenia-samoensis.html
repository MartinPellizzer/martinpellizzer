Eugenia samoensis

Eugenia samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical Pacific islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a smooth, pinkish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some tropical regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
