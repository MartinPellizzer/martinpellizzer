Epipactis phyllanthes var. vectensis

Epipactis phyllanthes var. vectensis, commonly known as the green helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces erect stems that bear clusters of flowers.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded conditions and its ornamental value in gardens.
