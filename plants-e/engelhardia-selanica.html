Engelhardia selanica

Engelhardia selanica, commonly known as the mountain ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
