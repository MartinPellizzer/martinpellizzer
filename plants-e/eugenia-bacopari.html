Eugenia bacopari

Eugenia bacopari, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright purple fruits that are highly prized for their sweet, tangy flavor and medicinal properties. Eugenia bacopari thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in tropical horticulture and traditional medicine.
