Erysimum australe var. simplex

Erysimum australe var. simplex, commonly known as the Australian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with simple, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its compact size and vibrant color make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
