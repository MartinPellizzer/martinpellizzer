Ellipanthus peltatus

Ellipanthus peltatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved ellipanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting well to dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
