Euphorbia ipecacuanhae

Euphorbia ipecacuanhae, commonly known as the red-stemmed euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant has a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
