Erigeron villarsii var. villossus

Erigeron villarsii var. villossus, commonly known as the woolly fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale blue composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its scientific name reflects its distinctive woolly appearance and its classification within the Asteraceae family.
