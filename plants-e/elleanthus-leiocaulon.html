Elleanthus leiocaulon

Elleanthus leiocaulon, commonly known as the white-veined everlasting.

This plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions with its drought-resistant features. The leaves are usually narrow and may have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow, contributing to its ornamental value in dry gardens.
