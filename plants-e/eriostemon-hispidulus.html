Eriostemon hispidulus

Eriostemon hispidulus, commonly known as the rough-leaved eriostemon, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Eriostemon hispidulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
