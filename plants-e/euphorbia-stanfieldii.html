Euphorbia stanfieldii

Euphorbia stanfieldii, commonly known as the Stanfield's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a compact, low-growing form with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are its modified flower structures, often surrounded by colorful bracts. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Euphorbia stanfieldii is also noted for its ability to store water in its stems, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
