Eria decurrentipetala

Eria decurrentipetala, commonly known as the decurrent-petalled eria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers that are often clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
