Entandrophragma pseudocylindricum

Entandrophragma pseudocylindricum, commonly known as the African teak, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, cylindrical trunk and smooth, pale brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its durable, heavy wood used in construction and furniture.
