Equisetum virgatum

Equisetum virgatum, commonly known as the common horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, which are typically found in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats.

Equisetum virgatum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
