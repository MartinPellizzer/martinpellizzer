Eragrostis yucatana

Eragrostis yucatana, commonly known as Yucatan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for dryland environments. It is often used in landscaping for erosion control due to its hardy nature and ability to establish quickly.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that release lightweight seeds, aiding in its spread.
