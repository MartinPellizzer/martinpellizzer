Eschscholzia frutescens

Eschscholzia frutescens, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant with showy, bright orange or red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

The California poppy is also the state flower of California and is known for its medicinal properties.
