Eugenia stricta

Eugenia stricta, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically purple or red when ripe, with a thick, leathery skin. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
