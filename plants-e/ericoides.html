Ericoides

Ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Ericoides is known for its resilience to cold temperatures and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
