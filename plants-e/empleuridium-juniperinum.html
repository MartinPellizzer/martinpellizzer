Empleuridium juniperinum

Empleuridium juniperinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed, giving it a dense, shrub-like appearance. Its cones are small and berry-like, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Empleuridium juniperinum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
