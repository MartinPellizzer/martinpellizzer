Erica pubigera

Erica pubigera, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Erica pubigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
