Euphorbia lydenburgensis

Euphorbia lydenburgensis, commonly known as the Lydenburg spurge, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or green, which are characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
