Eugenia stephanii var. angustifolia

Eugenia stephanii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf velvetwood, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate with partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its dense foliage and year-round beauty.
