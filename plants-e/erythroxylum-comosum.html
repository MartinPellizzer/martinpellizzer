Erythroxylum comosum

Erythroxylum comosum, commonly known as the yellow copaiba, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and yellowish-brown resinous exudate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it notable for its potential medicinal and aromatic properties.
