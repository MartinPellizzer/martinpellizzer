Eucalyptus leucoxylon var. rostellata

Eucalyptus leucoxylon var. rostellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically white or pale yellow, which appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
