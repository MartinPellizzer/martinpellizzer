Eria recurvata

Eria recurvata, commonly known as the twisted eria, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of small, recurved flowers that bloom sequentially.

Eria recurvata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
