Eleocharis engelmannii var. monticola

Eleocharis engelmannii var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as meadows, streambanks, and marshes. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often clustered and covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
