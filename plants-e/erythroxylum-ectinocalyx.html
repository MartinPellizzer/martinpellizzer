Erythroxylum ectinocalyx

Erythroxylum ectinocalyx, commonly known as the yellow bell pepper plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bell-shaped fruits. These fruits are typically yellow or orange when mature and contain a single seed.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
