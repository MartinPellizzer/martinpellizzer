Eutrochium purpureum var. carolinianum

Eutrochium purpureum var. carolinianum, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its showy, clustered purple-pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

This species is also an important host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting the life cycle of this iconic insect.
