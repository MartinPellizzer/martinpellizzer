Echinocereus

Echinocereus, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a genus of flowering plants in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

These plants are native to the Americas, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. They are characterized by their spiny stems and often produce vibrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Echinocereus species are typically drought-tolerant and thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Their unique botanical features make them popular among cactus enthusiasts and suitable for xeriscaping.
