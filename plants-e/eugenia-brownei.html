Eugenia brownei

Eugenia brownei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a bright red skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Eugenia brownei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruit, which is popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
