Eupatorium pyramidale

Eupatorium pyramidale, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

Eupatorium pyramidale is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
