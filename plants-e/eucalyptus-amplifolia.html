Eucalyptus amplifolia

Eucalyptus amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed gum, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often coated with a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Eucalyptus amplifolia typically grows in open forest or woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses due to the presence of essential oils.
