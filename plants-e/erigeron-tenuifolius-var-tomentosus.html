Erigeron tenuifolius var. tomentosus

Erigeron tenuifolius var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
