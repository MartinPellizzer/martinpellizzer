Epimedium × cantabrigiense

Epimedium × cantabrigiense, commonly known as the barrenwort, is a deciduous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and attractive, shield-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, nodding yellow flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to naturalize and form dense ground covers.
