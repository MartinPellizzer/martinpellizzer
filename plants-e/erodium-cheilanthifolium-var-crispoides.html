Erodium cheilanthifolium var. crispoides

Erodium cheilanthifolium var. crispoides, commonly known as the curly erodium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are distinctive for their wavy, curled edges, which give it its common name.
