Elaeagia cubensis

Elaeagia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are clustered in groups of three, which are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
