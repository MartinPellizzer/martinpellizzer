Elongatia guttata

Elongatia guttata, commonly known as the spotted elongated fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like fronds that are often marked with distinctive spots or dots. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Elongatia guttata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
