Erythroxylum verruculosum

Erythroxylum verruculosum, commonly known as the roughleaf copal, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves with a rough, textured surface, and small, clustered flowers. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
