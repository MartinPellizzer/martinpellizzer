Eucalyptus gracilis var. erecta

Eucalyptus gracilis var. erecta, commonly known as the slender ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a blue-green hue, especially when young. It produces small, globular flower clusters in white or pale yellow, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and dry inland areas.
