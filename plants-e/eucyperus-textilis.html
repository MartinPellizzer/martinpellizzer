Eucyperus textilis

Eucyperus textilis, commonly known as the grass-palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark purple, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, tropical environments and is adapted to grow in swampy or marshy areas.
