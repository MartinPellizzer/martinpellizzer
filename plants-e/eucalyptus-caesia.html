Eucalyptus caesia

Eucalyptus caesia, commonly known as the grey box, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that is typically grey or pale brown in color. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale yellow, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is commonly found in heathlands and woodlands across southern Australia.
