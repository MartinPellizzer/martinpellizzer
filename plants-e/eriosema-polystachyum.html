Eriosema polystachyum

Eriosema polystachyum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sida, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features palmate leaves with a soft, velvety texture due to the dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
