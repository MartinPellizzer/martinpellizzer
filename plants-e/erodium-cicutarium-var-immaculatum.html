Erodium cicutarium var. immaculatum

Erodium cicutarium var. immaculatum, commonly known as the white bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and garden areas.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seed production.
