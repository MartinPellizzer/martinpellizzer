Eupatorium iridolepis

Eupatorium iridolepis, commonly known as the purplestem false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and roadsides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a distinctive purple or reddish tinge on the underside.

The showy blue to purple flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.
