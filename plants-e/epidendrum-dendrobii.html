Epidendrum dendrobii

Epidendrum dendrobii, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant typically grows as a sympodial herb with multiple pseudobulbs and long, linear leaves. Its flowers are showy, with a complex structure featuring a labellum that is often brightly colored.

Epidendrum dendrobii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
