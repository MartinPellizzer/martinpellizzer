Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus, commonly known as the Australian blue gum, is a tall evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Eucalyptus globulus is well-known for its essential oils, particularly eucalyptol, which have medicinal and industrial uses.

It thrives in temperate climates and is widely cultivated for its timber and aromatic properties.
