Ebenus tragacanthoides

Ebenus tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Mediterranean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Ebenus tragacanthoides is adapted to arid environments and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
