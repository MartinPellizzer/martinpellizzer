Eupatorium volkensii

Eupatorium volkensii, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Eupatorium volkensii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.
