Eremanthus crotonoides

Eremanthus crotonoides, commonly known as the Cape croton, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eremanthus crotonoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
