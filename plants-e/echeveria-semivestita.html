Echeveria semivestita

Echeveria semivestita, commonly known as the Mexican Firecracker, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves with a slight powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers in late spring or early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
