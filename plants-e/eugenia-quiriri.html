Eugenia quiriri

Eugenia quiriri, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Eugenia quiriri is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses, though it is less commonly cultivated outside its native range.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
