Elaeodendron trinitense

Elaeodendron trinitense, commonly known as the Trinidadian candlewood, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny, woody stems that store water, a typical adaptation to its arid habitat. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry forest areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
