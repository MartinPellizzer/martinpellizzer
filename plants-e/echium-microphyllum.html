Echium microphyllum

Echium microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including moths and beetles, due to their nocturnal blooming habit.

Echium microphyllum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique flowering pattern.
