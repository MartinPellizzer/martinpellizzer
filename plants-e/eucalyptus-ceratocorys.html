Eucalyptus ceratocorys

Eucalyptus ceratocorys, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs or small trees. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. Eucalyptus ceratocorys produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
