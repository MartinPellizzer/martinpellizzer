Eutrema parviflorum

Eutrema parviflorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf seagrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, delicate white flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. This species typically grows in shallow, saline waters and is often found in sandy or muddy substrates. Eutrema parviflorum plays an important role in stabilizing coastal sediments and providing habitat for marine life.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
