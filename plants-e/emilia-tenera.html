Emilia tenera

Emilia tenera, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Emilia tenera is known for its delicate, fern-like leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
