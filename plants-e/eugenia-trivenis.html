Eugenia trivenis

Eugenia trivenis, commonly known as the three-veined eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Eugenia trivenis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
