Eclipta alba

Eclipta alba, commonly known as false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides. It is an annual herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Eclipta alba is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
