Eriosema yerbalium f. puberulum

Eriosema yerbalium f. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy eriosema, is a variant of the species Eriosema yerbalium, which belongs to the Fabaceae family.

This plant is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "puberulum.". The leaves are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly pubescent as well. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months.

The plant is native to southern Africa and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.
