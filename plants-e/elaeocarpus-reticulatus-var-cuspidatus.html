Elaeocarpus reticulatus var. cuspidatus

Elaeocarpus reticulatus var. cuspidatus, commonly known as the spike-leaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single, hard seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
