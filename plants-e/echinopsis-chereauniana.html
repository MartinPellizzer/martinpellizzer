Echinopsis chereauniana

Echinopsis chereauniana, commonly known as the Chereaunian cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and prominent spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
