Eremanthus exsuccus

Eremanthus exsuccus, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, which helps it conserve water in arid conditions.

Eremanthus exsuccus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
