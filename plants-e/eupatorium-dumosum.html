Eupatorium dumosum

Eupatorium dumosum, commonly known as bushy thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

Eupatorium dumosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
