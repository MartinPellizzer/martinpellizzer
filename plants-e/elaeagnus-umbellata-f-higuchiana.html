Elaeagnus umbellata f. higuchiana

Elaeagnus umbellata f. higuchiana, commonly known as Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it suitable for various landscaping applications.
