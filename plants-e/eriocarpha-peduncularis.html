Eriocarpha peduncularis

Eriocarpha peduncularis, commonly known as the Brazilian burr acacia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
