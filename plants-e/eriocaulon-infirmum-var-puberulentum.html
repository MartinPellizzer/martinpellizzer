Eriocaulon infirmum var. puberulentum

Eriocaulon infirmum var. puberulentum, commonly known as the silky marsh grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a silky texture. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
