Ericala

Ericala, commonly known as the common heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Ericala thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
