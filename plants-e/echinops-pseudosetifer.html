Echinops pseudosetifer

Echinops pseudosetifer, commonly known as the spiked crownflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown, which are a key feature for its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by the distinctive spiky seed heads.

Echinops pseudosetifer is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
