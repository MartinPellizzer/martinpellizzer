Eugenia pseudopsidium var. portoricensis

Eugenia pseudopsidium var. portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in local horticulture.
