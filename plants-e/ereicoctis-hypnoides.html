Ereicoctis hypnoides

Ereicoctis hypnoides, commonly known as the creeping saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Ereicoctis hypnoides plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.
