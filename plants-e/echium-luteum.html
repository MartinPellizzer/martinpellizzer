Echium luteum

Echium luteum, commonly known as yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss. Its seed capsules contain numerous small, oily seeds that are used in various medicinal and industrial applications.

Echium luteum plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and practical uses.
