Eriophorum × medium f. candidum

Eriophorum × medium f. candidum, commonly known as the white cotton grass, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a hybrid species that exhibits characteristics of both Eriophorum angustifolium and Eriophorum vaginatum. The plant is notable for its dense, fluffy white inflorescences that resemble cotton, which are produced during the summer months. It typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and tundra regions.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
