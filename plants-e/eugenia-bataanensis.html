Eugenia bataanensis

Eugenia bataanensis, commonly known as the Bataan hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pink, with a distinctive red calyx that persists after flowering.

Eugenia bataanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
