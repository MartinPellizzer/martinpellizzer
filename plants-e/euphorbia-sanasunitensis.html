Euphorbia sanasunitensis

Euphorbia sanasunitensis, commonly known as the Sanasunit Desert Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
