Eurybia furcata f. elaciniata

Eurybia furcata f. elaciniata, commonly known as the silverleaf eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
