Eugenia kanalaensis

Eugenia kanalaensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain seeds.

Eugenia kanalaensis plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian forests and is also valued for its ornamental beauty.
