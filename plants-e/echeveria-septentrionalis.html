Echeveria septentrionalis

Echeveria septentrionalis, commonly known as the Northern Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to the United States, specifically Arizona and New Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white trichomes, giving it a frosty appearance. The plant produces tall, branching stems with clusters of vibrant yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and striking foliage make it a desirable addition to both outdoor and indoor collections.
