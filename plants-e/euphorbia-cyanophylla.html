Euphorbia cyanophylla

Euphorbia cyanophylla, commonly known as the blue-leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Euphorbia cyanophylla is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
