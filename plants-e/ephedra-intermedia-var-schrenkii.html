Ephedra intermedia var. schrenkii

Ephedra intermedia var. schrenkii, commonly known as the Schrenk's ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female structures. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.

The plant contains alkaloids, including ephedrine, which has historically been used in traditional medicine.
