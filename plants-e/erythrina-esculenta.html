Erythrina esculenta

Erythrina esculenta, commonly known as the Indian coral tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.

Additionally, its seeds and young pods are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
