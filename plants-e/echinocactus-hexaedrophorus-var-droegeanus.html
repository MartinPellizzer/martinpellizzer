Echinocactus hexaedrophorus var. droegeanus

Echinocactus hexaedrophorus var. droegeanus, commonly known as the Drogean hedgehog cactus, is a spineless cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a short, columnar stem that often branches into a dense, rounded form. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to drought conditions.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
