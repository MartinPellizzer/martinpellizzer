Eriachne mucronata var. villiculmis

Eriachne mucronata var. villiculmis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in botanical research.
