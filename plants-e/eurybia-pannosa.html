Eurybia pannosa

Eurybia pannosa, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime environments. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Eurybia pannosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to tolerate salty air and coastal conditions.
