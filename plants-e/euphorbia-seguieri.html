Euphorbia seguieri

Euphorbia seguieri, commonly known as the Seguier's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The sap of Euphorbia seguieri is toxic and can cause skin irritation, so care should be taken when handling it.
