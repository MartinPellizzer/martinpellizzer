Epilobium palustre var. virgatum

Epilobium palustre var. virgatum, commonly known as swamp willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, marshes, and other waterlogged areas. The plant features erect, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters. Its leaves are elliptical, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often found in association with other wetland flora.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
