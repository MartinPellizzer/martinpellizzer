Eupatorium texense

Eupatorium texense, commonly known as Texas boneset, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. It produces white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Its common name refers to the brittle, branching structure of its stems, which resemble bones.
