Echeveria decipiens

Echeveria decipiens, commonly known as the "False Echeveria," is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, often with a powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Echeveria decipiens is popular in rock gardens and as a container plant due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
