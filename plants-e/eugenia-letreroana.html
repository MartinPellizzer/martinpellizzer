Eugenia letreroana

Eugenia letreroana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or letreroana guava, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Eugenia letreroana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
