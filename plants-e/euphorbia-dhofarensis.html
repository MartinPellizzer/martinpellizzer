Euphorbia dhofarensis

Euphorbia dhofarensis, commonly known as the Dhofar euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the southern Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its leaves are small and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Euphorbia dhofarensis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
