Epiphyllum phyllanthus var. paraguayense

Epiphyllum phyllanthus var. paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguay night bloomer, is a striking cactus-like plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, segmented stems that resemble leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
