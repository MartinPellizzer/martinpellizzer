Eulophia gigantea

Eulophia gigantea, commonly known as the giant eulophia, is a striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with prominent, hairy sepals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Eulophia gigantea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
