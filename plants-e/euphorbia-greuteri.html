Euphorbia greuteri

Euphorbia greuteri, commonly known as the Mexican spurge, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
