Erica inaperta

Erica inaperta, commonly known as the narrowleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of pink or red flowers during the late summer and autumn months.

Erica inaperta is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
