Eragrostis pensylvanica

Eragrostis pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, prairies, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny grains of seed. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
