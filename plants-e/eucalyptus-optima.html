Eucalyptus optima

Eucalyptus optima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in winter. Eucalyptus optima is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, displaying drought tolerance and a ability to resprout from lignified stems after fire.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
