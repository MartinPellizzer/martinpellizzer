Eucalyptus sinuosa

Eucalyptus sinuosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallet, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of development. Its bark is smooth and peels in thin, papery layers, and it is often found in dry, open forests or woodlands.

Eucalyptus sinuosa is known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and for their insect-repelling properties.
