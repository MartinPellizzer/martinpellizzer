Eria pulvinata

Eria pulvinata, commonly known as the pulvinate orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is adorned with intricate patterns.

Eria pulvinata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
