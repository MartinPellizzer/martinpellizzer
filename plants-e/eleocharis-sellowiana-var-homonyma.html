Eleocharis sellowiana var. homonyma

Eleocharis sellowiana var. homonyma, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. This plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to submerged soils. Its rhizomes help it spread vegetatively, contributing to its ability to colonize aquatic environments.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
