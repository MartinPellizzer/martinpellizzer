Ebenus ferruginea

Ebenus ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty everlasting, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, branching stems covered in dense clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, rusty-colored down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is known for its long flowering period, extending from late spring to early autumn.
