Eriostemon tomentellus

Eriostemon tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils, often in heathland or open forests. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.

Eriostemon tomentellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
