Eugenia triflora

Eugenia triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that typically appear in clusters of three, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a slightly waxy texture.

Eugenia triflora is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
