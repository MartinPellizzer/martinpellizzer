Elaeocarpus nigropunctatus

Elaeocarpus nigropunctatus, commonly known as the black-punctured elaeocarp, is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and distinctive fruiting bodies. The fruit, which is typically a capsule, contains numerous small seeds surrounded by a fleshy, yellowish pulp. The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
