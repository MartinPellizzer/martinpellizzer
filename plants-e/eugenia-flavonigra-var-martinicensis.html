Eugenia flavonigra var. martinicensis

Eugenia flavonigra var. martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Martinique and nearby islands. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a mild aromatic scent when crushed.
