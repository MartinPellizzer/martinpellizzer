Eriogonum douglasii var. meridionale

Eriogonum douglasii var. meridionale, commonly known as the southern Douglas's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

This variety is part of the Polygonaceae family and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.
