Ericoides pubigera

Ericoides pubigera, commonly known as the hairy ericoid, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Ericoides pubigera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
