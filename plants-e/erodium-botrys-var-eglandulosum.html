Erodium botrys var. eglandulosum

Erodium botrys var. eglandulosum, commonly known as stickyweed or woolly geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered pink or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
