Eriosphaera dubia

Eriosphaera dubia, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent collectors.
