Echinocereus adustus var. schwarzii

Echinocereus adustus var. schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwaiger's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spine-tipped cactus with a compact, globular shape and dark green to blue-gray stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its hardy nature and minimal water requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
