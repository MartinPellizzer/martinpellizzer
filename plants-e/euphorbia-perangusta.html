Euphorbia perangusta

Euphorbia perangusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branching structure with narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are its unique floral structures. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia perangusta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
