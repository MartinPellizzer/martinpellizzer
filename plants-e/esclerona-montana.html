Esclerona montana

Esclerona montana, commonly known as the mountain hardy shrub, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Esclerona montana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
