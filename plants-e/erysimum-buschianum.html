Erysimum buschianum

Erysimum buschianum, commonly known as the Busch's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers with four petals, often arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem. Its leaves are linear and hairy, contributing to its ability to thrive in arid environments.

Erysimum buschianum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
