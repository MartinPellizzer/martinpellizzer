Entada monostachya

Entada monostachya, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, purple-pink flowers. The tree produces large, pod-like fruits that contain several hard, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Entada monostachya is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
