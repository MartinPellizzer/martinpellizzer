Eriogonum indictum

Eriogonum indictum, commonly known as the Indian paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, brush-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are often narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Eriogonum indictum plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
