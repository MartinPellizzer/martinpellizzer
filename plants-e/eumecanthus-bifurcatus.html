Eumecanthus bifurcatus

Eumecanthus bifurcatus, commonly known as the forked leaf orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bifurcated leaves that split into two distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are small but showy, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, Eumecanthus bifurcatus is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
