Eupatorium laete-virens var. angustius

Eupatorium laete-virens var. angustius, commonly known as narrowleaf white sweetflag, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. It is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and other aquatic habitats.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
