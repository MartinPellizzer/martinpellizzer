Echinopsis pugionacantha subsp. rossii

Echinopsis pugionacantha subsp.

rossii, commonly known as the Ross's night bloomer, is a succulent cactus native to southern Africa. It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique blooming habit and striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
