Escallonia spinulosa

Escallonia spinulosa, commonly known as the prickly escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It features dense, spiny foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or red flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its thorny structure provides natural protection, and it is often used in hedge planting.
