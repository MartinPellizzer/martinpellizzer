Elaeocarpus acronodia subsp. acronodia

Elaeocarpus acronodia subsp.

acronodia, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
