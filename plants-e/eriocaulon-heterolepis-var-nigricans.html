Eriocaulon heterolepis var. nigricans

Eriocaulon heterolepis var. nigricans, commonly known as blackstem spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spike-like clusters of small, white or pale flowers.

The variety *nigricans* is distinguished by its dark-colored stems, which contribute to its unique appearance and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
