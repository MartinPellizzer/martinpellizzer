Epidendrum mesocarpum

Epidendrum mesocarpum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or purple flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
