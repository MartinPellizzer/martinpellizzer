Epilobium amurense subsp. laetum

Epilobium amurense subsp.

laetum, commonly known as the silverleaf willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia. It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white or silver hairs, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, pink to red flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.
