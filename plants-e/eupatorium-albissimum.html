Eupatorium albissimum

Eupatorium albissimum, commonly known as white eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features showy, clustered white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium albissimum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
