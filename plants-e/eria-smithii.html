Eria smithii

Eria smithii, commonly known as the Smith's eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant blooms.

Eria smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
