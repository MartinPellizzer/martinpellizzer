Eryngium rostratum

Eryngium rostratum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads and gray-green, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Eryngium rostratum is considered an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
