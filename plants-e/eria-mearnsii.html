Eria mearnsii

Eria mearnsii, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a leguminous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common species in savanna ecosystems.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, it plays an important role in soil enrichment and ecological succession.
