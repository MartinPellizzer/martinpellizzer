Emilia juncea var. iringensis

Emilia juncea var. iringensis, commonly known as the Iringensis false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Emilia juncea var. iringensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
