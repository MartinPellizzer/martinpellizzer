Eriocaulon sikokianum var. lutchuense

Eriocaulon sikokianum var. lutchuense, commonly known as the Lutchuense spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in wetland and marshy environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it often grows in dense mats or colonies.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
