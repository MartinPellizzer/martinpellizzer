Elymus mollis subsp. interior

Elymus mollis subsp.

interior, commonly known as the inland feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, sandy soils, and disturbed areas. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of numerous spikelets, which are arranged in a loose, open structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
