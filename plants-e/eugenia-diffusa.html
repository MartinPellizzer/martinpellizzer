Eugenia diffusa

Eugenia diffusa, commonly known as the common guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-white fruits. The fruit has a thick, aromatic skin and a sweet, juicy pulp with numerous seeds.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
