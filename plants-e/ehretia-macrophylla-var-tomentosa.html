Ehretia macrophylla var. tomentosa

Ehretia macrophylla var. tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly elder, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woolly-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a soft, fuzzy texture. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, yellowish fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
