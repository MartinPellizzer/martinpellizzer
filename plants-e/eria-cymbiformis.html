Eria cymbiformis

Eria cymbiformis, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eria cymbiformis thrives in humid, warm environments with high light exposure.
