Eucalyptus tricarpa subsp. decora

Eucalyptus tricarpa subsp.

decora, commonly known as the decorative or ornamental eucalyptus, is a species native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or cream in hue. It is a medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with a slender trunk and a spreading crown.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
