Edrastima goreensis

Edrastima goreensis, commonly known as the Gorensis Euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes a diverse group of plants known for their unique floral structures and often milky sap. The plant features a short, woody stem with branching patterns that support its broad, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Edrastima goreensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience through its succulent-like foliage and deep root system.
