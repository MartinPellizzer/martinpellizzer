Erythroxylum havanense var. haitiense

Erythroxylum havanense var. haitiense, commonly known as Haitian copal or Haitian erythroxylum, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti and the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for various purposes, including medicinal applications. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas within forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
