Eugenia stictophylla

Eugenia stictophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Eugenia stictophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in indigenous Australian medicine.
