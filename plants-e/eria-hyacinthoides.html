Eria hyacinthoides

Eria hyacinthoides, commonly known as the orchid bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in the dry environments it inhabits.

Eria hyacinthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
