Eugenia umbellaris

Eugenia umbellaris, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet, tangy flavor. Eugenia umbellaris thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
