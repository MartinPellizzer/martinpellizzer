Encyclia ramonensis

Encyclia ramonensis, commonly known as the Ramon orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Encyclia ramonensis thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for botanical gardens and orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and unique appearance.
