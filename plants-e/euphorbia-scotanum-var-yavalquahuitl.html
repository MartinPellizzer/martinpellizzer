Euphorbia scotanum var. yavalquahuitl

Euphorbia scotanum var. yavalquahuitl, commonly known as the Yavalquahuitl euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stem for drought tolerance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
