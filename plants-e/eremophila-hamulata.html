Eremophila hamulata

Eremophila hamulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and spikes of pale pink to mauve flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and heat resistance. It produces small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Eremophila hamulata is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
