Euodia chunii

Euodia chunii, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.

However, it can become invasive in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
