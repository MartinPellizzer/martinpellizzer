Elaeagnus murakamiana

Elaeagnus murakamiana, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves, which are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Elaeagnus murakamiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
