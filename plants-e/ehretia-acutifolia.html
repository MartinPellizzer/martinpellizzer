Ehretia acutifolia

Ehretia acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ehretia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
