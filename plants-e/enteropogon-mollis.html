Enteropogon mollis

Enteropogon mollis, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to brown flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Botanically, it is noted for its fibrous root system and its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes.
