Erodium romanum var. caulescens

Erodium romanum var. caulescens, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed soils. The plant has small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spherical fruits called utricles, which contain seeds dispersed by wind.

The species is characterized by its slender, erect stems and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
