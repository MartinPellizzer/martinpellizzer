Ericoides oblongiflorum

Ericoides oblongiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered heath, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late spring and attract various pollinators.

Ericoides oblongiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
