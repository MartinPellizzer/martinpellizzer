Echites rubrovenosus

Echites rubrovenosus, commonly known as the red-veined echites, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red veins that run through its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a sprawling or climbing form depending on environmental conditions.

Echites rubrovenosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
