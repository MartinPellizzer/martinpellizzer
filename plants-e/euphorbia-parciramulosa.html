Euphorbia parciramulosa

Euphorbia parciramulosa, commonly known as the sparse-haired spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
