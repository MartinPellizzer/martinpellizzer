Eryngium balchanicum

Eryngium balchanicum, commonly known as the Balkan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Eryngium balchanicum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
