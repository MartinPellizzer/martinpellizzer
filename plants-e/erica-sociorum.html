Erica sociorum

Erica sociorum, commonly known as the common heath, is a shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. Erica sociorum produces clusters of pink or white flowers in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
