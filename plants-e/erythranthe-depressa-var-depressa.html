Erythranthe depressa var. depressa

Erythranthe depressa var. depressa, commonly known as the low erythranthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and delicate foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky stream banks. It features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.
