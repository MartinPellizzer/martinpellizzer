Elaeocarpus nooteboomii

Elaeocarpus nooteboomii, commonly known as the Nooteboom's elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, a feature typical of the genus Elaeocarpus. It is often found in lowland to montane forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
