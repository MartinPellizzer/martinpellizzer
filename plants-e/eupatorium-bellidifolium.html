Eupatorium bellidifolium

Eupatorium bellidifolium, commonly known as the showy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and stream banks, and can reach heights of up to six feet. Its showy, purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
