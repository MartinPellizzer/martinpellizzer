Erioneuron grandiflorum

Erioneuron grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered erioneuron, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Erioneuron grandiflorum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
