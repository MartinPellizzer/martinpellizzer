Erythronium pusaterii

Erythronium pusaterii, commonly known as the Siberian dog-tooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a bulbous root system and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Erythronium pusaterii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
