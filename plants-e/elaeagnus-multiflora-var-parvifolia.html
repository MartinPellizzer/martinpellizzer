Elaeagnus multiflora var. parvifolia

Elaeagnus multiflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible berries that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Elaeagnus multiflora var. parvifolia is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
