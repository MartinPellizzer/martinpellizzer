Eugenia inundata var. coriacea

Eugenia inundata var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal or riverine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
