Eugenia calycina

Eugenia calycina, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, while its fragrant flowers range in color from white to pink.

Eugenia calycina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
