Eleutherococcus gracilistylus var. major

Eleutherococcus gracilistylus var. major, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of East Asia.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 9 elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels. The plant produces red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It is valued for its adaptogenic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Araliaceae family.
