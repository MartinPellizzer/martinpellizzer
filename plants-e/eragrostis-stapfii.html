Eragrostis stapfii

Eragrostis stapfii, commonly known as the Stapf's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy underside.

Eragrostis stapfii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry conditions.
