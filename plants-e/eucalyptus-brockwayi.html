Eucalyptus brockwayi

Eucalyptus brockwayi, commonly known as the Brockway's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or grey. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It is well adapted to dry conditions and has a deep root system to access water.

Eucalyptus brockwayi is an important component of mallee ecosystems and provides habitat for various native wildlife.
