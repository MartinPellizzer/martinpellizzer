Eugenia pachakumachiana

Eugenia pachakumachiana, commonly known as the Andean myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the highlands of southern Peru and northern Bolivia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters, thriving in cool, humid climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
