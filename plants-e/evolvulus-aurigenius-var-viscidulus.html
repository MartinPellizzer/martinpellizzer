Evolvulus aurigenius var. viscidulus

Evolvulus aurigenius var. viscidulus, commonly known as the sticky morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped blue to purple flowers. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with a fine, sticky coating. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in waste areas.

Its rapid growth and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens.
