Erianthus fallax

Erianthus fallax, commonly known as the tall grass bluemink, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, panicle-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Erianthus fallax is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
