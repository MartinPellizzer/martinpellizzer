Epilobium roseum subsp. subsessile

Epilobium roseum subsp.

subsessile, commonly known as the slender willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy underside. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
