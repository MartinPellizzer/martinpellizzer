Eugenia leucophloea

Eugenia leucophloea, commonly known as the white-flowered eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions.
