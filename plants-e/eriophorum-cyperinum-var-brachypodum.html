Eriophorum cyperinum var. brachypodum

Eriophorum cyperinum var. brachypodum, commonly known as the shortpod sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, triangular leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as bogs, marshes, and wet meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This variety is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
