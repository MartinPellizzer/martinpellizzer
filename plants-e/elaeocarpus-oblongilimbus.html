Elaeocarpus oblongilimbus

Elaeocarpus oblongilimbus, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
