Eugenia pantagensis

Eugenia pantagensis, commonly known as the Pantagonese guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northwestern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and tropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
