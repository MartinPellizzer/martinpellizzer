Eriogonum fasciculatum var. obtusiflorum

Eriogonum fasciculatum var. obtusiflorum, commonly known as the blunt-flowered buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with basal leaves that are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
