Eurya emarginata

Eurya emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a distinctive notched or serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Eurya emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
