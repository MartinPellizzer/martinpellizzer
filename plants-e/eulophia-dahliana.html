Eulophia dahliana

Eulophia dahliana, commonly known as the Dahl's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers with pale yellow to green petals and a distinctive, often red or orange lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Eulophia dahliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
