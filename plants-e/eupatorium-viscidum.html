Eupatorium viscidum

Eupatorium viscidum, commonly known as sticky eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, often sticky stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as swamps and wetlands.

Its sticky secretions and aromatic foliage make it distinctive within its native range.
