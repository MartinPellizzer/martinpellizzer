Eriogonum tenellum var. tenellum

Eriogonum tenellum var. tenellum, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are typically borne in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
