Echinopsis adolfofriedrichii

Echinopsis adolfofriedrichii, commonly known as the Adolfo Friedrich cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has a ribbed structure with prominent areoles, from which both spines and flowers emerge. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This species is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
