Eucalyptus crebra

Eucalyptus crebra, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. Its bark is typically smooth and peels off in thin layers, revealing a mottled appearance.

Eucalyptus crebra is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
