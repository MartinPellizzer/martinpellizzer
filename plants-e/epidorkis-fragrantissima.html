Epidorkis fragrantissima

Epidorkis fragrantissima, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under the canopy of rainforests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Epidorkis fragrantissima is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its unique aromatic properties.
