Elaeocarpus cordifolius

Elaeocarpus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

It plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
