Eugenia exechusa var. uniflora

Eugenia exechusa var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in its native habitat.
