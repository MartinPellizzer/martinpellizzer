Eclipta alba f. erecta

Eclipta alba f. erecta, commonly known as white daisy or false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, white, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has serrated leaves and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
