Eriochloa texana

Eriochloa texana, commonly known as Texas bluestem, is a native grass species found in the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that turn golden in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Eriochloa texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
