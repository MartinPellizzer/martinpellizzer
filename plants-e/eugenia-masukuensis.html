Eugenia masukuensis

Eugenia masukuensis, commonly known as the Kikuyu myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia masukuensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
