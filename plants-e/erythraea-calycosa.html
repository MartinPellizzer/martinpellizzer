Erythraea calycosa

Erythraea calycosa, commonly known as the red calycous, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

Erythraea calycosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting display.
