Eriope crassipes var. acutifolia

Eriope crassipes var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eriope, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, woolly foliage that is typically green with a bluish tint, and its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it its specific epithet "acutifolia.". The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
