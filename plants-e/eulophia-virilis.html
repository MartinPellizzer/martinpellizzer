Eulophia virilis

Eulophia virilis, commonly known as the male orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green with a distinctive violet or purple lip. Eulophia virilis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
