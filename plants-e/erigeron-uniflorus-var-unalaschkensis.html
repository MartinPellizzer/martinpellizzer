Erigeron uniflorus var. unalaschkensis

Erigeron uniflorus var. unalaschkensis, commonly known as the Alaska fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, daisy-like blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in harsh, cold environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and is often found in alpine and tundra habitats.
