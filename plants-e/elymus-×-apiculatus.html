Elymus × apiculatus

Elymus × apiculatus, commonly known as the wheatgrass or goatgrass, is a hybrid grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional and modern herbal practices. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Elymus × apiculatus is also valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high concentration of vitamins and minerals.

Its seeds are sometimes used in food products and as a source of dietary fiber.
