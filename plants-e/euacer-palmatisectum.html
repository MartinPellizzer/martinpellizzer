Euacer palmatisectum

Euacer palmatisectum, commonly known as the broadleaf palm, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive leaf structure.

Its leaves are palmately divided, resembling a fan shape with multiple segments radiating from a central point. The plant exhibits a robust growth habit, often reaching significant heights in its natural habitat. The leaves are typically large, broad, and have a waxy surface that helps in water retention.

It thrives in humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
