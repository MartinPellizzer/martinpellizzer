Erysimum decumbens subsp. pyrenaicum

Erysimum decumbens subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean wallflower, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Pyrenees region. It features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

Its flowering period contributes to the ecological diversity of mountainous regions in Europe.
