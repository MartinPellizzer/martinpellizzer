Ehretia acuminata

Ehretia acuminata, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow before falling in the dry season. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Ehretia acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
