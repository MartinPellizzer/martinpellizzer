Elytrigia dschungarica

Elytrigia dschungarica, commonly known as the Central Asian grass or Dzhungarian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, temperate regions and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand arid conditions and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential resource for agricultural use.
