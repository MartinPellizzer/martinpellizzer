Euphorbia geyeri var. wheeleriana

Euphorbia geyeri var. wheeleriana, commonly known as Wheeler's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
