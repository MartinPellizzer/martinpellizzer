Eriogonum pharnaceoides var. cervinum

Eriogonum pharnaceoides var. cervinum, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
