Ericoides pellucidum

Ericoides pellucidum, commonly known as the translucent heath, is a rare and delicate plant species belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a waxy, translucent cuticle, giving it its common name. This plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in cool, moist environments such as heathlands or mountainous regions. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Ericoides pellucidum is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
