Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. tanacetifolium

Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. tanacetifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenbush, is a perennial herb native to California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for native wildlife.
