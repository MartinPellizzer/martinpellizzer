Eriochilus latifolius

Eriochilus latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf eriochilus, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a member of the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Eriochilus latifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
