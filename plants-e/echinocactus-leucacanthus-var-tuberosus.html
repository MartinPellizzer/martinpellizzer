Echinocactus leucacanthus var. tuberosus

Echinocactus leucacanthus var. tuberosus, commonly known as the Mexican giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems that are often ribbed and can grow quite large, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its striking appearance and unique features, it is a popular choice for collectors and desert gardeners.
