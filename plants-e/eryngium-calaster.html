Eryngium calaster

Eryngium calaster, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by silver-white bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eryngium calaster is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
