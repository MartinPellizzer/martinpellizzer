Eragrostis wiseana

Eragrostis wiseana, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Eragrostis wiseana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
