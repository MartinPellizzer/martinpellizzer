Ericoides andromediflorum

Ericoides andromediflorum, commonly known as the coastal heather, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the western coast of North America, particularly in regions with moist, acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are evergreen, needle-like, and arranged in dense clusters.

Ericoides andromediflorum thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.
