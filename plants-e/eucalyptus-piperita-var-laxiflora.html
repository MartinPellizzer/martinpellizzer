Eucalyptus piperita var. laxiflora

Eucalyptus piperita var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.

This variant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
