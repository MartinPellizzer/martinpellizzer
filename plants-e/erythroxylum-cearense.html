Erythroxylum cearense

Erythroxylum cearense, commonly known as the Cearense Coca, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, and it is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its botanical similarity to other coca species, it has been the subject of scientific study regarding its potential ecological and medicinal significance.
