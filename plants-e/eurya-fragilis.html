Eurya fragilis

Eurya fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant eurya, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.

Eurya fragilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
