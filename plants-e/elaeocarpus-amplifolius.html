Elaeocarpus amplifolius

Elaeocarpus amplifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
