Eschatogramme furcata

Eschatogramme furcata, commonly known as the forked eschatogram, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow from a central base, giving it its distinctive "forked" appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense tufts along the stem.

Eschatogramme furcata is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry climates.
