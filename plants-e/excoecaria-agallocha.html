Excoecaria agallocha

Excoecaria agallocha, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and distinctive, spiny stems. The plant is known for its ability to produce a latex sap, which has been historically used in the rubber industry. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, shady environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
