Eragrostis laniflora

Eragrostis laniflora, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
