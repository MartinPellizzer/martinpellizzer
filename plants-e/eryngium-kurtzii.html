Eryngium kurtzii

Eryngium kurtzii, commonly known as the mountain eryngo, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Eryngium kurtzii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
