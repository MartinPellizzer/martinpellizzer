Epilobium palustre var. davuricum

Epilobium palustre var. davuricum, commonly known as the marsh willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in northern regions.

It features erect, slender stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in elongated clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

This variant is adapted to moist soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.
