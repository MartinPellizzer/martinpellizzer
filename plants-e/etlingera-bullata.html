Etlingera bullata

Etlingera bullata, commonly known as the yellow torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow bracts that resemble torches, which are actually modified leaves. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color.

The plant prefers warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
