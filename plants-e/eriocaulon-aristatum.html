Eriocaulon aristatum

Eriocaulon aristatum, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green or pale in color.

Eriocaulon aristatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
