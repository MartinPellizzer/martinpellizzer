Elymus trinii

Elymus trinii, commonly known as the three-row wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Elymus trinii is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
