Erodium laciniatum

Erodium laciniatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, delicate appearance.

Erodium laciniatum is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
