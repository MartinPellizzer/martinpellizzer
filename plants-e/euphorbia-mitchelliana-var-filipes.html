Euphorbia mitchelliana var. filipes

Euphorbia mitchelliana var. filipes, commonly known as the Mitchell's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a unique appearance with its thread-like leaves and branching structure. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in rocky or sandy soils.
