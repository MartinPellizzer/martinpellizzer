Eragrostis multiflora subsp. leersioides

Eragrostis multiflora subsp.

leersioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
