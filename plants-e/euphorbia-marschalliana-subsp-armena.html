Euphorbia marschalliana subsp. armena

Euphorbia marschalliana subsp.

armena, commonly known as Armenian spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Caucasus region. It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow cyathia, which are its specialized inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
