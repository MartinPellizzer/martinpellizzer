Euphorbia ramofraga

Euphorbia ramofraga, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a woody stem with a distinctive branching pattern, often resembling a tree. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Euphorbia ramofraga is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid environments.
