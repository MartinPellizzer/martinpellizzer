Eragrostis suaveolens

Eragrostis suaveolens, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, pale green or brownish spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its aromatic leaves emit a sweet, honey-like scent when crushed, which is often used in traditional ceremonial practices.

Eragrostis suaveolens plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
