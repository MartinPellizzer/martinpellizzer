Eria fibuliformis

Eria fibuliformis, commonly known as the fibrous eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, fibrous pseudobulbs that support its narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and usually greenish or yellowish, with a delicate structure typical of many orchids.

Eria fibuliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
