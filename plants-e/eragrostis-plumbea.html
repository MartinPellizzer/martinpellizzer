Eragrostis plumbea

Eragrostis plumbea, commonly known as bluegrass or blue oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites.

Eragrostis plumbea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
