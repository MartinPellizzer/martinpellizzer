Epilobium alpinum var. clavatum

Epilobium alpinum var. clavatum, commonly known as the Alpine willow herb, is a perennial flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Epilobium alpinum var. clavatum is often found in mountain meadows and is a common component of alpine flora.
