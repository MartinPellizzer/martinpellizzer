Erigeron thunbergii

Erigeron thunbergii, commonly known as New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its fibrous root system helps it thrive in a variety of soil types, and it is often found in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native flora that supports local pollinators.
