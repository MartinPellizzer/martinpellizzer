Eremanthus elaeagnus

Eremanthus elaeagnus, commonly known as the silverleaf eremanthus, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eremanthus elaeagnus is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
