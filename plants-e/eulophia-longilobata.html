Eulophia longilobata

Eulophia longilobata, commonly known as the long-lipped eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the humid forest floor.

Eulophia longilobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
