Eugenia leptospermoides

Eugenia leptospermoides, commonly known as the slender-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, exhibiting a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
