Euphorbia mafingensis

Euphorbia mafingensis, commonly known as the Mafingensis spurge, is a succulent plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

Euphorbia mafingensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
