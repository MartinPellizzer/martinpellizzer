Eyrythalia septemfida

Eyrythalia septemfida, commonly known as the seven-lobed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive seven-lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its structure is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Eyrythalia septemfida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
