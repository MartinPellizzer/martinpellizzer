Echinocactus macrochele

Echinocactus macrochele, commonly known as the Mexican horse chestnut, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in prominent, sharp spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
