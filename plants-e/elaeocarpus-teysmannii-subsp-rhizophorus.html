Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp. rhizophorus

Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp.

rhizophorus, commonly known as the red ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
