Enterosora sprucei

Enterosora sprucei, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has leathery, dark green leaves that remain on the shrub throughout the year, providing a striking contrast to its white blossoms. Enterosora sprucei thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
