Eriogonum purpusii

Eriogonum purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland valleys. It has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum purpusii is an important plant for local wildlife, providing nectar for bees and other pollinators.
