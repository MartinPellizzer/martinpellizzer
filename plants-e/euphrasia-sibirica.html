Euphrasia sibirica

Euphrasia sibirica, commonly known as Siberian eyebright, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Siberia.

It features delicate, hairy stems that often grow in clusters, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent yellow spots, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its medicinal reputation.

Euphrasia sibirica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
