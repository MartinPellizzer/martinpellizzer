Erigeron maguirei var. harrisonii

Erigeron maguirei var. harrisonii, commonly known as the Harrison's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
