Eucalyptus sideroxylon var. minor

Eucalyptus sideroxylon var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by woody capsules. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ecological role and potential in horticulture.
