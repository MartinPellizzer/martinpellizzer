Eremophila graciliflora

Eremophila graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered emu bush, is a small shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can survive in poor soil, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Eremophila graciliflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.
