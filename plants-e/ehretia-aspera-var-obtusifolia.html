Ehretia aspera var. obtusifolia

Ehretia aspera var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf ehretia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
