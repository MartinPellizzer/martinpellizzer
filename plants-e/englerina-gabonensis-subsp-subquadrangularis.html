Englerina gabonensis subsp. subquadrangularis

Englerina gabonensis subsp.

subquadrangularis, commonly known as the Gabonese englerina, is a woody shrub or small tree native to Central Africa. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its quadrangular stem and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and crafts.
