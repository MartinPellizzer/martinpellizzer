Erigeron uniflorus subsp. aragonensis

Erigeron uniflorus subsp.

aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear a single flower at the tip.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral morphology.
