Eupatorium conoclinioides

Eupatorium conoclinioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium conoclinioides is a valuable plant for pollinators and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
