Epidendrum prancei

Epidendrum prancei, commonly known as the Prance's epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, and it prefers warm, humid environments with good air circulation.

Epidendrum prancei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
