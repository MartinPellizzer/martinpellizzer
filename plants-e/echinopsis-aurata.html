Echinopsis aurata

Echinopsis aurata, commonly known as the night bloomer cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense covering of spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Echinopsis aurata is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
