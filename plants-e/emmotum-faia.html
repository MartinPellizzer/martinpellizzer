Emmotum faia

Emmotum faia, commonly known as the golden leaf fern, is a unique plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow fronds that contrast sharply with its dark green stems. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is known for its intricate rhizome system that supports its growth in dense forest understories.
