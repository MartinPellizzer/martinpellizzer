Erythrophleum fordii

Erythrophleum fordii, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its rapid growth and striking foliage.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
