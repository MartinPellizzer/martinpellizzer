Eurya blumeana

Eurya blumeana, commonly known as the Blume's waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Eurya blumeana is also valued for its ability to attract birds and its use in traditional medicine.
