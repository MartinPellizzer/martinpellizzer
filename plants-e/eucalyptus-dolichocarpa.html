Eucalyptus dolichocarpa

Eucalyptus dolichocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, clustered in umbels, and are followed by woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds. This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

It plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
