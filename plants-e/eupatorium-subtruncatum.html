Eupatorium subtruncatum

Eupatorium subtruncatum, commonly known as narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodlands and stream banks, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments, and its foliage can be aromatic when crushed.
