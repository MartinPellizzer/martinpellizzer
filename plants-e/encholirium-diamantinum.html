Encholirium diamantinum

Encholirium diamantinum, commonly known as the diamond leaf or Brazilian diamond plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its striking, silver-white leaves that are covered in minute, diamond-shaped tubercles. The plant produces striking red flowers that emerge from a rosette of foliage, often in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
