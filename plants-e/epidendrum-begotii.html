Epidendrum begotii

Epidendrum begotii, commonly known as the Begotii orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Epidendrum begotii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
