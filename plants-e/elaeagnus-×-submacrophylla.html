Elaeagnus × submacrophylla

Elaeagnus × submacrophylla, commonly known as silverberry or sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves that provide ornamental value. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and adaptability.

Elaeagnus × submacrophylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
