Erica sordida

Erica sordida, commonly known as the gloomy heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant features dense, wiry stems covered in small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Erica sordida thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to harsh, coastal or rocky environments.
