Erythroxylum arrojadoi

Erythroxylum arrojadoi, commonly known as the red velvet plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of northern South America, particularly in countries like Venezuela and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its striking red, velvety bracts that surround its small yellow flowers. It typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface.
