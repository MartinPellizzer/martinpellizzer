Eritrichium graminifolium

Eritrichium graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate blue or purple flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and is typically short-lived, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.

Eritrichium graminifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
