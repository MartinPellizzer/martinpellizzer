Echium judaeum

Echium judaeum, commonly known as the Judean or Jerusalem thorn, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Echium judaeum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
