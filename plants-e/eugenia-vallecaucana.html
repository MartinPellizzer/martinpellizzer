Eugenia vallecaucana

Eugenia vallecaucana, commonly known as the Andean guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia vallecaucana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
