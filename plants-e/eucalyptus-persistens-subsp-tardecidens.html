Eucalyptus persistens subsp. tardecidens

Eucalyptus persistens subsp.

tardecidens, commonly known as the yellow box, is a medium to tall tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that becomes fibrous with age, and its lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy green. The flowers are typically small and white or pink, arranged in clusters, and the tree produces woody capsules as fruits.

It is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is adapted to withstand drought and fire.
