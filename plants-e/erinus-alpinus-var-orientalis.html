Erinus alpinus var. orientalis

Erinus alpinus var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
