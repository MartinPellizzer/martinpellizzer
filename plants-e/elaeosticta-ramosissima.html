Elaeosticta ramosissima

Elaeosticta ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf stinkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often glandular and emit a strong, unpleasant odor when crushed.

Elaeosticta ramosissima is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
