Eryngium davisii

Eryngium davisii, commonly known as the Davis thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by white to gray bracts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is typically found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Eryngium davisii is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
