Epipactis × graberi

Epipactis × graberi, commonly known as the hybrid marsh helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Epipactis × graberi is a natural hybrid between Epipactis palustris and Epipactis helleborine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in some regions.
