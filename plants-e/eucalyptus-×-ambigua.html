Eucalyptus × ambigua

Eucalyptus × ambigua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and heathlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
