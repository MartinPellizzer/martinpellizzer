Echinocactus ingens var. helophorus

Echinocactus ingens var. helophorus, commonly known as the Mexican giant hedgehog cactus, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its massive, spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features dense, white to yellow spines that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, appear at the top of the stems during the spring and summer months.

This variety is highly valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in arid gardens and greenhouses.
