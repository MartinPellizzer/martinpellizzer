Epipactis latifolia var. purpurea

Epipactis latifolia var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple broad-leaved helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-white flowers with purple markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds in capsules.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
