Erysimum murale

Erysimum murale, commonly known as wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found along walls or in dry, sunny locations. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
