Euonymus javanicus var. sphaerocarpus

Euonymus javanicus var. sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the globe-fruit euonymus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, spherical fruits that turn red when mature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and dark green coloration.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
