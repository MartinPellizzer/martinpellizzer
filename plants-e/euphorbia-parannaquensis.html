Euphorbia parannaquensis

Euphorbia parannaquensis, commonly known as the Paranna quillwort, is a unique member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and a distinct floral structure known as a cyathium. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers, which are surrounded by specialized bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
