Euphorbia parkeri

Euphorbia parkeri, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, waxy leaves that help it store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble small yellow flowers. Its sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Euphorbia parkeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
