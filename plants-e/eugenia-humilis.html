Eugenia humilis

Eugenia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
