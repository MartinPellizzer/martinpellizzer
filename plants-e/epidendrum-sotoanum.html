Epidendrum sotoanum

Epidendrum sotoanum, commonly known as the Soto's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features upright, hairy stems and produces clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, which helps in water retention.

Epidendrum sotoanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, humid conditions.
