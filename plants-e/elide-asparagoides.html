Elide asparagoides

Elide asparagoides, commonly known as the false asparagus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
