Eugenia cordata var. genuima

Eugenia cordata var. genuima, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink hibiscus.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to Brazil, characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for hedges or as a specimen plant in tropical gardens.
