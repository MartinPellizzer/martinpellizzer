Eucalyptus × insizwaensis

Eucalyptus × insizwaensis, commonly known as the Insizwa hybrid eucalyptus, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Eucalyptus bicolor and Eucalyptus tricarpa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of development. It is native to the eastern regions of South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous environments.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
