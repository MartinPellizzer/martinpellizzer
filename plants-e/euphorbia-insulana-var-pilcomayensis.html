Euphorbia insulana var. pilcomayensis

Euphorbia insulana var. pilcomayensis, commonly known as the Pilcomayo euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
