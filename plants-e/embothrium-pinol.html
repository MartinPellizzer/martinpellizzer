Embothrium pinol

Embothrium pinol, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a deciduous shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. Embothrium pinol thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited to coastal climates.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
