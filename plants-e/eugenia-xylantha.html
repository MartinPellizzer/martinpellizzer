Eugenia xylantha

Eugenia xylantha, commonly known as the red eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces small, edible berries.

Eugenia xylantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal properties.
