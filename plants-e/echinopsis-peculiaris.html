Echinopsis peculiaris

Echinopsis peculiaris, commonly known as the "Peculiar Cactus," is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically found in Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom at night. Echinopsis peculiaris is a succulent plant, storing water in its fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
