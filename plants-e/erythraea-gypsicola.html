Erythraea gypsicola

Erythraea gypsicola, commonly known as the gypsum erythraea, is a rare flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils, particularly those rich in gypsum. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Erythraea gypsicola is considered endangered in many regions.
