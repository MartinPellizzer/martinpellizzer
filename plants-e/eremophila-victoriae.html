Eremophila victoriae

Eremophila victoriae, commonly known as the Victorian emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Eremophila victoriae is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
