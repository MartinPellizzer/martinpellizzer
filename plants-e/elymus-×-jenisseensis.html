Elymus × jenisseensis

Elymus × jenisseensis, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which contain small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and tundra ecosystems.

Due to its hardiness and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and as a forage crop.
