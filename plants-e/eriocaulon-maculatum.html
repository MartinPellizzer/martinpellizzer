Eriocaulon maculatum

Eriocaulon maculatum, commonly known as the spotted flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green with distinctive reddish or purplish spots, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eriocaulon maculatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
