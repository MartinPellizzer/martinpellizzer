Elleanthus blatteus

Elleanthus blatteus, commonly known as the golden grass or yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bright yellow-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Elleanthus blatteus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
