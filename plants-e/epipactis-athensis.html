Epipactis athensis

Epipactis athensis, commonly known as the Cretan helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee, aiding in pollination by attracting insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Epipactis athensis is also known for its ability to grow in calcareous soils and is often found in areas with high humidity.
