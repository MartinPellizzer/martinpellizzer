Erysimum sisymbrioides

Erysimum sisymbrioides, commonly known as the California wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun. Its bright yellow or orange flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum sisymbrioides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
