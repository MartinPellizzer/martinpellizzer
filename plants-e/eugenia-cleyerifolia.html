Eugenia cleyerifolia

Eugenia cleyerifolia, commonly known as the Java apple or cleyera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Eugenia cleyerifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
