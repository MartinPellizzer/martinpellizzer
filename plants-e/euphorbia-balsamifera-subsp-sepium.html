Euphorbia balsamifera subsp. sepium

Euphorbia balsamifera subsp.

sepium, commonly known as the red milkweed tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its reddish bark and smooth, often grayish-green stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is known for its latex-like sap, which can be sticky and sometimes toxic.

This species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh conditions.
