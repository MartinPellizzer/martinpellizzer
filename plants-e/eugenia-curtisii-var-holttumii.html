Eugenia curtisii var. holttumii

Eugenia curtisii var. holttumii, commonly known as the Malayan cherry or Malayan eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and is often found in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, which is typically red or orange when mature, is edible and has a tart flavor.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
