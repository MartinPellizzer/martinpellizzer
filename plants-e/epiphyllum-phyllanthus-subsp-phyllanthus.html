Epiphyllum phyllanthus subsp. phyllanthus

Epiphyllum phyllanthus subsp.

phyllanthus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family. It is a climbing cactus native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are often segmented and covered in spines. Its flowers, which open at night, are large, showy, and typically white or pale pink.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique blooming pattern.
