Eugenia constanzae

Eugenia constanzae, commonly known as the Constantza's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, specifically Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
