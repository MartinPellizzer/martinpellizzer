Eugenia montigena

Eugenia montigena, commonly known as the mountain eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia montigena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
