Eria kalelotong

Eria kalelotong, commonly known as the "butterfly orchid," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy texture, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the air.

Eria kalelotong thrives in humid environments with indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
