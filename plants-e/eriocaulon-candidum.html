Eriocaulon candidum

Eriocaulon candidum, commonly known as the white threadgrass, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes, where it forms dense mats on the water surface. The plant is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Eriocaulon candidum plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
