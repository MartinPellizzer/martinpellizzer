Eupatorium odoratum

Eupatorium odoratum, commonly known as sweet weed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is often found near water sources. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium odoratum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
