Eupatorium rufescens var. glabratum

Eupatorium rufescens var. glabratum, commonly known as rough blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils, typically found in swampy areas or along creek banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family, contributing to its ecological importance in wetland habitats.
