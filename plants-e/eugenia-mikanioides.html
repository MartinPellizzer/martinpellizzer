Eugenia mikanioides

Eugenia mikanioides, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant's aromatic leaves and fruits contribute to its ecological and medicinal significance in its native habitat.
