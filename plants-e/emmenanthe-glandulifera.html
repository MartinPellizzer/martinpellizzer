Emmenanthe glandulifera

Emmenanthe glandulifera, commonly known as the sticky leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glandular-stippled leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its unique leaf structure and habitat preferences make it a notable species in moist, wooded areas.

Emmenanthe glandulifera is often found in shaded environments and is adapted to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
