Euphorbia bianoensis

Euphorbia bianoensis, commonly known as the Biano's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments, showcasing features like thick cuticles and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

Euphorbia bianoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
