Euphorbia pearsonii

Euphorbia pearsonii, commonly known as the Pearson's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or grayish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its stems and leaves.

Euphorbia pearsonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
