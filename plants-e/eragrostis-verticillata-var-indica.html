Eragrostis verticillata var. indica

Eragrostis verticillata var. indica, commonly known as Indian oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are often clustered in dense panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Its growth habit and morphology make it a notable species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
