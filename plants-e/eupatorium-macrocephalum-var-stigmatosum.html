Eupatorium macrocephalum var. stigmatosum

Eupatorium macrocephalum var. stigmatosum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, spire-like flower heads that range in color from white to deep purple, with prominent central cones. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
