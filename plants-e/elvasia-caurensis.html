It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow crevices and rocky outcrops, adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its habitat. The plant produces small, delicate orchid-like flowers with a unique coloration pattern, often featuring a combination of white and purple hues. Its root system is specialized for nutrient absorption from the mineral-rich soil, and it has a short flowering period during the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Elvasia caurensis is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native environment.
