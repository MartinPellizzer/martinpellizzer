Eupatorium arsenei

Eupatorium arsenei, commonly known as the Arsenic weed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium arsenei is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
