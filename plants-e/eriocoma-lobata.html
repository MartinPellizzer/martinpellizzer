Eriocoma lobata

Eriocoma lobata, commonly known as the woolly erica, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.

Eriocoma lobata is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
