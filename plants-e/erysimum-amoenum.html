Erysimum amoenum

Erysimum amoenum, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features basal rosettes of silvery-green, hairy leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum amoenum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
