Eugenia underwoodii

Eugenia underwoodii, commonly known as the Underwood's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, smooth, reddish-purple berry that is edible but not widely cultivated.

Eugenia underwoodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
