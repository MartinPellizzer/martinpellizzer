Erica caffra

Erica caffra, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Erica caffra is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ability to withstand salt spray makes it well-suited for coastal gardens and landscaping.
