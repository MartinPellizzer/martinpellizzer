Euphorbia rattanii

Euphorbia rattanii, commonly known as the rattan euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a unique structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia rattanii is also noted for its ability to store water in its stems, aiding in its survival in dry conditions.
