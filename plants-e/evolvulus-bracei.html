Evolvulus bracei

Evolvulus bracei, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its rapid growth and ability to reseed itself make it a common weed in desert regions.
