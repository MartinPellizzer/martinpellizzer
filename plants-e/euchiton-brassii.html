Euchiton brassii

Euchiton brassii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
