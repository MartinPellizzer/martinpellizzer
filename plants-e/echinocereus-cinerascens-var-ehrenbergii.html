Echinocereus cinerascens var. ehrenbergii

Echinocereus cinerascens var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Ehrenberg's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a compact, globular shape and a gray-green coloration. The plant features clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its muted foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
