Echium ambiguum

Echium ambiguum, commonly known as the bluemink or blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, rocky areas, and along roadsides.

Echium ambiguum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
