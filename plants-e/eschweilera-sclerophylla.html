Eschweilera sclerophylla

Eschweilera sclerophylla, commonly known as the Guianan wax palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red fruits containing edible seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
