Euphorbia tamanduana

Euphorbia tamanduana, commonly known as the Tamandua euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and distinctive leaf structure. The plant produces a unique type of inflorescence called a cyathium, which contains both male and female flowers. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
