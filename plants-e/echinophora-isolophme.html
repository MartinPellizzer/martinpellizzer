Echinophora isolophme

Echinophora isolophme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust taproot and upright stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of purple to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in a dense inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a rough texture, and are covered in small spines.

Echinophora isolophme is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
