Elwendia cylindrica

Elwendia cylindrica, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with purple, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to lavender in color, blooming in late summer. Elwendia cylindrica is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
