Echinochloa verticillata

Echinochloa verticillata, commonly known as slender false brome, is an annual grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively, it can become a weed in agricultural settings.
