Erica viguieri

Erica viguieri, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Erica viguieri thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscaping.
