Erycibe myriantha

Erycibe myriantha, commonly known as the white-veined hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with prominent veins. The flowers are showy, typically pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.
