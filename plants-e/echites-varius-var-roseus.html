Echites varius var. roseus

Echites varius var. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered echites, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, rose-like flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual appeal to gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for ornamental gardening.
