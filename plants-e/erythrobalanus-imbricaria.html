Erythrobalanus imbricaria

Erythrobalanus imbricaria, commonly known as the red-fruited balsa, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible. The plant is often found in moist, lowland forests and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for various purposes, including model building.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and unique fruiting habits.
