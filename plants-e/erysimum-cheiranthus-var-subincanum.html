Erysimum cheiranthus var. subincanum

Erysimum cheiranthus var. subincanum, commonly known as the silverleaf wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
