Eupatorium triste

Eupatorium triste, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium triste is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
