Echium valentinum

Echium valentinum, commonly known as Valentine's Day fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Boraginaceae family, known for its showy flowers and sometimes hairy stems.
