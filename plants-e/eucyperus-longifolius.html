Eucyperus longifolius

Eucyperus longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically long and linear, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by specialized leaf-like structures called bracts. It thrives in marshy or swampy areas, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Eucyperus longifolius is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its potential use in phytoremediation.
