Eugenia chepensis

Eugenia chepensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with glossy, dark green leaves.

Eugenia chepensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
