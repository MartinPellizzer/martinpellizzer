Eclipta alba var. erecta

Eclipta alba var. erecta, commonly known as white crownbeard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a head, surrounded by white, bract-like leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
