Entada bacillaris var. bacillaris

Entada bacillaris var. bacillaris, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise, is a large, robust plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
