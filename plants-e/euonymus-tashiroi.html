Euonymus tashiroi

Euonymus tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Euonymus tashiroi is also valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
