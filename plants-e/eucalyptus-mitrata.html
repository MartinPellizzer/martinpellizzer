Eucalyptus mitrata

Eucalyptus mitrata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as mallee and heathlands. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic uses.

Eucalyptus mitrata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
