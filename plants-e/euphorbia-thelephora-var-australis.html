Euphorbia thelephora var. australis

Euphorbia thelephora var. australis, commonly known as the Australian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and leaves that are typically alternate and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The plant contains latex, which can be irritating to the skin and should be handled with care.
