Echinops sabzevarensis

Echinops sabzevarensis, commonly known as the Sabzevar thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a globe. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

Echinops sabzevarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
