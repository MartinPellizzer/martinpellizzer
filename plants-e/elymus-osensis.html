Elymus osensis

Elymus osensis, commonly known as the Ossetian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Elymus osensis is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
