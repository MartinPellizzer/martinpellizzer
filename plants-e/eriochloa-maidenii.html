Eriochloa maidenii

Eriochloa maidenii, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-hued leaves and produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eriochloa maidenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
