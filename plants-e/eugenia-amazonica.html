Eugenia amazonica

Eugenia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Eugenia amazonica thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the rich, nutrient-dense soils of the Amazon basin.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the region's biodiversity and ecological balance.
