Episcia ochroleuca

Episcia ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-leaved wax plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, broad, waxy leaves that are typically bright yellow or orange in color. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually red or pink, often with contrasting yellow markings.

Episcia ochroleuca is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its vibrant foliage and ease of care.
