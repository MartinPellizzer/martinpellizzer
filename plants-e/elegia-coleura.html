Elegia coleura

Elegia coleura, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Elegia coleura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
