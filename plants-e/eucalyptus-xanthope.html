Eucalyptus xanthope

Eucalyptus xanthope, commonly known as the yellow box, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark that can range from grey to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically appearing in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive blue-green hue.

Eucalyptus xanthope is well adapted to dry conditions and is commonly found in heathlands and open forests.
