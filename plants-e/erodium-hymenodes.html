Erodium hymenodes

Erodium hymenodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a small annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and slightly hairy, with a distinctive, somewhat succulent texture.

Erodium hymenodes is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.
