Erythraea maritima subsp. discolor

Erythraea maritima subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers and silver-green foliage, which help it thrive in saline and sandy soils.

The subspecies discolor is distinguished by its slightly different flower coloration compared to other subspecies of Erythraea maritima. It typically grows in dune environments and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for various coastal organisms.
