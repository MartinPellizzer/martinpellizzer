Evelyna arpophyllostachys

Evelyna arpophyllostachys, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its elegant appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking, low-maintenance plant.
