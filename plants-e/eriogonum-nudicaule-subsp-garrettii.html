Eriogonum nudicaule subsp. garrettii

Eriogonum nudicaule subsp.

garrettii, commonly known as the Garrett's leafless buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its sparse, often absent foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats like sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes.
