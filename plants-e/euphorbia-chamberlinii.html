Euphorbia chamberlinii

Euphorbia chamberlinii, commonly known as the chamberlin's spurge, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features a compact, multi-stemmed structure with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and green, which are followed by distinctive, red-tipped seed capsules. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
