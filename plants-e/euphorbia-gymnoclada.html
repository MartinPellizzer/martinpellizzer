Euphorbia gymnoclada

Euphorbia gymnoclada, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick, and often branching stem covered in a smooth, waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its stem for survival in arid conditions.

Euphorbia gymnoclada is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
