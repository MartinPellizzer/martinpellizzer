Eryngium nudiflorum

Eryngium nudiflorum, commonly known as the nakedflower thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and its striking, thistle-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are composed of numerous small, tubular florets arranged in a spherical shape.

Eryngium nudiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
