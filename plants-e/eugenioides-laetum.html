Eugenioides laetum

Eugenioides laetum, commonly known as the golden eugenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Eugenioides laetum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
