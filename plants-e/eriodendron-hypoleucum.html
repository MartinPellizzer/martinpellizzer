Eriodendron hypoleucum

Eriodendron hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered eriodendron, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release seeds when mature.

Eriodendron hypoleucum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
