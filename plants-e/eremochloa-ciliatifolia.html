Eremochloa ciliatifolia

Eremochloa ciliatifolia, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually pale green to brown in color.

Eremochloa ciliatifolia is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
