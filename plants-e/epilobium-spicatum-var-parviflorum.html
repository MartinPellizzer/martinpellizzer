Epilobium spicatum var. parviflorum

Epilobium spicatum var. parviflorum, commonly known as narrow-leaved willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pink or reddish-purple flowers arranged in spikes. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
