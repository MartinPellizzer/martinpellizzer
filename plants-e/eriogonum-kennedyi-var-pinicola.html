Eriogonum kennedyi var. pinicola

Eriogonum kennedyi var. pinicola, commonly known as the Kennedy's buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

This variety is part of the large and diverse genus Eriogonum, which includes many species adapted to various ecological conditions.
