Evolvulus linifolius

Evolvulus linifolius, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant has hairy, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.

The seeds are encased in a distinctive, spiral-shaped capsule that opens to release the seeds.
