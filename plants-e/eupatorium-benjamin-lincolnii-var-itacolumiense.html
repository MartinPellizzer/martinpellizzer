Eupatorium benjamin-lincolnii var. itacolumiense

Eupatorium benjamin-lincolnii var. itacolumiense, commonly known as Itacolumi eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.

Its botanical name honors Benjamin Lincoln, a Revolutionary War general, and the Itacolumi region in Georgia.
