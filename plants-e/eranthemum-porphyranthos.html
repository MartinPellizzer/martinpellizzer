Eranthemum porphyranthos

Eranthemum porphyranthos, commonly known as the purple-flowered eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Eranthemum porphyranthos is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
