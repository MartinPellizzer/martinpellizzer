Encyclia microxanthina

Encyclia microxanthina, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in yellow or greenish-yellow hues. The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves that are usually arranged in two rows along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Encyclia microxanthina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
