Eriogonum flavum subsp. crassifolium

Eriogonum flavum subsp.

crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow to golden flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
