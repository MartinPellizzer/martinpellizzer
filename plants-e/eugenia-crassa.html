Eugenia crassa

Eugenia crassa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cacao tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
