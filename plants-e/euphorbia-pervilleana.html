Euphorbia pervilleana

Euphorbia pervilleana, commonly known as the Perville's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves. The plant produces a unique, cyathium-shaped inflorescence that contains small, yellow flowers surrounded by specialized bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
