Eugenia laurentii

Eugenia laurentii, commonly known as the Indian laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Eugenia laurentii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
