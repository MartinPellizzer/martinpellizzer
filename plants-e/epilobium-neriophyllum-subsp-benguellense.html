Epilobium neriophyllum subsp. benguellense

Epilobium neriophyllum subsp.

benguellense, commonly known as the Benguella willowherb, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial with erect, branching stems that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-gray appearance due to a dense covering of trichomes. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and are typically pink to reddish in color, with five petals.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, showing some degree of salt tolerance.
