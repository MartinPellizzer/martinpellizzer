Ericoila acuta

Ericoila acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leaved ericoila, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Ericoila acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
