Eriocaulon lanigerum

Eriocaulon lanigerum, commonly known as the woolly spike-rush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It is often found in moist, acidic soils in wetlands and marshy areas.

Eriocaulon lanigerum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
