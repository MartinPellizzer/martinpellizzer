Erythroxylum cambodianum

Erythroxylum cambodianum, commonly known as the Cambodian erythroxylum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that contain seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

While not as well-known as other members of its genus, it holds ecological significance in its native habitat.
