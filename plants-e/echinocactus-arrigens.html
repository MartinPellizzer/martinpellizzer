Echinocactus arrigens

Echinocactus arrigens, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Echinocactus arrigens is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and unique appearance.
