Elaeocarpus pulchellus var. pulchellus

Elaeocarpus pulchellus var. pulchellus, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and striking red or pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
