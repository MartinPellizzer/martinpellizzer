Eupatorium vernonioides

Eupatorium vernonioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features slender, branching stems and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its blue to purple flower clusters appear in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium vernonioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
