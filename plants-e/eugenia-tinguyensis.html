Eugenia tinguyensis

Eugenia tinguyensis, commonly known as the Andean huito, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and smooth, dark purple or black berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in cloud forests.

The berries are edible and have been used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
