Eriosema squarrosum var. latifolium

Eriosema squarrosum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a hairy stem and broad, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive species for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
