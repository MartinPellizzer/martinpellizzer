Euphorbia ambovombensis

Euphorbia ambovombensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers that are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
