Eurya pilosa

Eurya pilosa, commonly known as the hairy alder or hairy evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and typically grows in temperate forest environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, woody berries.

Eurya pilosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to shaded conditions.
