Eugenia hagendorffii

Eugenia hagendorffii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red mangosteen, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
