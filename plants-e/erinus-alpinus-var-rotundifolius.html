Erinus alpinus var. rotundifolius

Erinus alpinus var. rotundifolius, commonly known as the Alpine Erinus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the cultivar. The leaves are rounded and slightly hairy, giving the plant a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand cold climates.
