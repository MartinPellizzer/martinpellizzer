Eugenia pyrroclada var. columbiensis

Eugenia pyrroclada var. columbiensis, commonly known as the Columbia guava, is a variant of the guava species native to the western United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in arid climates.
