Elaphoglossum eriopus

Elaphoglossum eriopus, commonly known as the hairy holly fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Elaphoglossum eriopus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
