Eucalyptus trachybasis

Eucalyptus trachybasis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The essential oils from its leaves are also used in traditional and modern applications, including aromatherapy and medicinal purposes.
