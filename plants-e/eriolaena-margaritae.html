Eriolaena margaritae

Eriolaena margaritae, commonly known as the pearl-leaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often display a striking white or cream-colored margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by ornamental, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for tropical and subtropical ornamental planting.
