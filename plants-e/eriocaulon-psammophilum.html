Eriocaulon psammophilum

Eriocaulon psammophilum, commonly known as the sand-loving spikerush, is a flowering plant native to arid and sandy environments.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes or inland sandy habitats.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local ecosystems.
