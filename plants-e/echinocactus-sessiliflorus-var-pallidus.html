Echinocactus sessiliflorus var. pallidus

Echinocactus sessiliflorus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with blue or gray. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at the base of the spines. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
