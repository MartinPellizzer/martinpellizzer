Euploca uniflora

Euploca uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower euploca, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces a single, showy flower that is usually pink or purple in color, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to its arid environment.

Euploca uniflora is often found in dune habitats and is well-suited to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
