Erica sicula subsp. cyrenaica

Erica sicula subsp.

cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and produces showy pink or purple flowers in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
