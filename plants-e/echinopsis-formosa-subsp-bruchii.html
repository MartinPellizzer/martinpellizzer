Echinopsis formosa subsp. bruchii

Echinopsis formosa subsp.

bruchii, commonly known as the Bruch's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces showy, night-blooming flowers that range in color from pink to violet, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
