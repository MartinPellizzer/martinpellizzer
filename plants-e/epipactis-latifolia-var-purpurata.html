Epipactis latifolia var. purpurata

Epipactis latifolia var. purpurata, commonly known as the purple broad-leaf helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to moist woodland habitats in Europe.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple, and its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive purple lip. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
