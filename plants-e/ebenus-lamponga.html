Ebenus lamponga

Ebenus lamponga, commonly known as the redstem everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.

Ebenus lamponga thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
