Euphorianthus obtusatus

Euphorianthus obtusatus, commonly known as the blunt-leaved euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes a wide variety of shrubs and trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and interesting floral structures.
