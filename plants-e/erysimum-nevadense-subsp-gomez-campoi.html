Erysimum nevadense subsp. gomez-campoi

Erysimum nevadense subsp.

gomez-campoi, commonly known as the Gomez-Campoi wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and slopes.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
