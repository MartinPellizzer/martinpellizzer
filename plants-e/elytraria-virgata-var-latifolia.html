Elytraria virgata var. latifolia

Elytraria virgata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed elytraria, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in dune environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in coastal gardens.
