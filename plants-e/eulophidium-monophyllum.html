Eulophidium monophyllum

Eulophidium monophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It has small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to survive in areas with limited rainfall.

Eulophidium monophyllum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
