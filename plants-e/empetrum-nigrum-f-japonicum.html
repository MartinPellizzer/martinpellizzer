Empetrum nigrum f. japonicum

Empetrum nigrum f. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese crowberry, is a low-growing shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. The plant has dense, leathery leaves that help it thrive in harsh, rocky environments. It is adapted to cold climates and can be found in alpine and subarctic regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
