Eriosema zeyheri var. acuminatum

Eriosema zeyheri var. acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features wiry stems that are often branched and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a pointed tip, and are also hairy on both surfaces. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and it typically blooms from late winter to early spring.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along the edges of woodlands.
