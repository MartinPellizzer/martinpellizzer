Eupatorium ferrugineum var. paraguariense

Eupatorium ferrugineum var. paraguariense, commonly known as Paraguayan boneset, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features upright stems that are often reddish-brown and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have serrated margins.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
