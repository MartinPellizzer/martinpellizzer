Ericoides glomiflorum

Ericoides glomiflorum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually evergreen, leathery, and have a waxy texture.

Ericoides glomiflorum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
