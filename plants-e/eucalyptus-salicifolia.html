Eucalyptus salicifolia

Eucalyptus salicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in groups, often appearing in late winter to early spring. Eucalyptus salicifolia is a medium-sized tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, peeling bark.

It is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment.
