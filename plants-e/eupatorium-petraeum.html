Eupatorium petraeum

Eupatorium petraeum, commonly known as rock eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium petraeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
