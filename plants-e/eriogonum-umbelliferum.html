Eriogonum umbelliferum

Eriogonum umbelliferum, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has hairy, branching stems and small, narrow leaves.

Eriogonum umbelliferum plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often found in native plant gardens.
