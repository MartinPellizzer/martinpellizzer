Eupatorium pacificum

Eupatorium pacificum, commonly known as Pacific eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the western coast of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streamside areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
