Eugenia tocaiana

Eugenia tocaiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraseira*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and often consumed fresh or used in jams. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
