Eucalyptus gunnii var. ovata

Eucalyptus gunnii var. ovata, commonly known as the Tasmanian gum or ovate eucalyptus, is a medium-sized tree native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white and grey. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a pale green upper surface and a greyish underside. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
