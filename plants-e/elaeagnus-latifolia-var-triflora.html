Elaeagnus latifolia var. triflora

Elaeagnus latifolia var. triflora, commonly known as the silver leaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, reddish berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
