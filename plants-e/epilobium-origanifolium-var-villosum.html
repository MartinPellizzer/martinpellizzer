Epilobium origanifolium var. villosum

Epilobium origanifolium var. villosum, commonly known as woolly willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowering stems are often branched and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other showy flowering plants.
