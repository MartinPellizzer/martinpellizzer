Eragrostis gymnorrhachis

Eragrostis gymnorrhachis, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, prairies, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are slender and often appear in late spring to early summer.

Silky bluegrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
