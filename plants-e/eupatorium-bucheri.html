Eupatorium bucheri

Eupatorium bucheri, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Eupatorium bucheri is often found in moist woodlands and wet meadows, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
