Eragrostis chapelieri var. vexillaris

Eragrostis chapelieri var. vexillaris, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps reduce water loss.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
