Erythrina laeta

Erythrina laeta, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, pink, or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its woody structure supports its role as a landscape and ornamental plant. Erythrina laeta is also notable for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscaping.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
