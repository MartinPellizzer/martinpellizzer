Erica × sphenanthera

Erica × sphenanthera, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Erica vagans and Erica sphenanthera.

It is native to the coastal regions of South Africa, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in clusters and blooming during the summer months.

Erica × sphenanthera is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardiness and attractive appearance in coastal gardens.
