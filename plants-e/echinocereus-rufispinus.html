Echinocereus rufispinus

Echinocereus rufispinus, commonly known as the red-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in red, spiny areoles, which are typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
