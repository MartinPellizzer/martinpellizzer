Eryngium amethystinum f. trichophyllum

Eryngium amethystinum f. trichophyllum, commonly known as the purple thistle or velvet eryngium, is a striking perennial plant characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and showy blue-violet flower heads.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant's foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture, while its flowers are typically arranged in a spherical shape. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

This cultivar is a popular choice for rock gardens and borders due to its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
