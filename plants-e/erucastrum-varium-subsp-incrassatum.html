Erucastrum varium subsp. incrassatum

Erucastrum varium subsp.

incrassatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved winter cress, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It features a basal rosette of dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
