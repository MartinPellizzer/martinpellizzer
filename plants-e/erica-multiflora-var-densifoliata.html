Erica multiflora var. densifoliata

Erica multiflora var. densifoliata, commonly known as the dense-leaved heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, dark green foliage that is both narrow and leathery, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
