Erica irrorata

Erica irrorata, commonly known as the silver heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its profuse display of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant features needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Erica irrorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
