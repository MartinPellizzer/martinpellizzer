Echium tuberculatum f. micranthum

Echium tuberculatum f. micranthum, commonly known as the dwarf thimbleweed, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, upright stems and linear, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to native plant collections.
