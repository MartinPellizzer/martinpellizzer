Ernestia tenella var. tenella

Ernestia tenella var. tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, ascending stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
