Escallonia serrata

Escallonia serrata, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southern and eastern Australia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in spring. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Escallonia serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and adaptability.
