Eremophila adenotricha

Eremophila adenotricha, commonly known as the Woolly Emu Bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or mauve in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Eremophila adenotricha is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
