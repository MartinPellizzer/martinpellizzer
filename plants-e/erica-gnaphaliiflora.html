Erica gnaphaliiflora

Erica gnaphaliiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Erica gnaphaliiflora thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
