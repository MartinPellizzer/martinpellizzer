Eugenia ramosissima

Eugenia ramosissima, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Brazil.

It features dense, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. These flowers are followed by small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.
