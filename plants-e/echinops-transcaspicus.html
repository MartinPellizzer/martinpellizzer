Echinops transcaspicus

Echinops transcaspicus, commonly known as the Transcaspian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a spiky globe, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, appearing in early summer.

Echinops transcaspicus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and visual appeal.
