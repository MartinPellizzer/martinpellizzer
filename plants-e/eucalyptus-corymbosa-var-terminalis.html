Eucalyptus corymbosa var. terminalis

Eucalyptus corymbosa var. terminalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallet, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green to yellow in color. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin, papery layers.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is commonly found in coastal and sandy areas.
