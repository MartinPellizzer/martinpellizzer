Elatostema oblongifolium var. oblongifolium

Elatostema oblongifolium var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false shamrock, is a member of the Urticaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
