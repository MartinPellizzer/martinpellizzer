Eremophila scoparia

Eremophila scoparia, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Emu Bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, aromatic leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
