Euphorbia esula subsp. pseudocyparissias

Euphorbia esula subsp.

pseudocyparissias, commonly known as the Canadian tansy, is a perennial plant native to North America. It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like growth habit and produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters.

The plant has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and landscape management.
