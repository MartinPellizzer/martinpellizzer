Echinocactus cornigerus var. flavispinus

Echinocactus cornigerus var. flavispinus, commonly known as the golden spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, yellowish spines, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
