Erigeron variegatus

Erigeron variegatus, commonly known as the variegated fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually gray-green with white or silver markings, adding to its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in the summer, producing a showy display of yellow centers surrounded by white petals.
