Ephedra nevadensis subvar. paucibracteata

Ephedra nevadensis subvar. paucibracteata, commonly known as the Great Basin ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody capsules containing seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy soils and full sun.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it is also of interest for its potential pharmacological properties.
