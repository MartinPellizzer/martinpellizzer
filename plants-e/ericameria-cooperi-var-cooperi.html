Ericameria cooperi var. cooperi

Ericameria cooperi var. cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's ericameria, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
