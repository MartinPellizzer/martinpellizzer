Eumecanthus latericolor

Eumecanthus latericolor, commonly known as the golden-eyed orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green coloration, with intricate floral structures that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its delicate yet resilient nature, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
