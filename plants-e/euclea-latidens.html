Euclea latidens

Euclea latidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Euclea latidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
