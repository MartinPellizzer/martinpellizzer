Euphorbia serbica

Euphorbia serbica, commonly known as the Serbian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, milky sap and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Euphorbia serbica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
