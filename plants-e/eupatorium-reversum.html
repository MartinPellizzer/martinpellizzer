Eupatorium reversum

Eupatorium reversum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is also known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
