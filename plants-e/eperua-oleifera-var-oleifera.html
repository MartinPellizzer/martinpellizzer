Eperua oleifera var. oleifera

Eperua oleifera var. oleifera, commonly known as the oil tree, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a heavy, oily resin that is harvested for its aromatic properties. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
