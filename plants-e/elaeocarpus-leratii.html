Elaeocarpus leratii

Elaeocarpus leratii, commonly known as the New Caledonia elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the island of New Caledonia in the South Pacific and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
