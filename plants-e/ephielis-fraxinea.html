Ephielis fraxinea

Ephielis fraxinea, commonly known as the fragrant ash or sweet ash, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, fleshy fruits.

Ephielis fraxinea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
