Eucalyptus × taylorii

Eucalyptus × taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that typically range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across regions with a Mediterranean climate.
