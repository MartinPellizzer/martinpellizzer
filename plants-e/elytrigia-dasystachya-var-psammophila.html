Elytrigia dasystachya var. psammophila

Elytrigia dasystachya var. psammophila, commonly known as the sandgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
