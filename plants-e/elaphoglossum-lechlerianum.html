Elaphoglossum lechlerianum

Elaphoglossum lechlerianum, commonly known as the Lechler's holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
