Elaeocarpus cyaneus var. carneus

Elaeocarpus cyaneus var. carneus, commonly known as the pink-flowered elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with glossy, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
