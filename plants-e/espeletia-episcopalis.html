Espeletia episcopalis

Espeletia episcopalis, commonly known as the Bishop's espeletia, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and large, showy flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Espeletia episcopalis is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat, showcasing remarkable resilience.
