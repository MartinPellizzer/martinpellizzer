Echites glaucus

Echites glaucus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
