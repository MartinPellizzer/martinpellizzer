Empusa henryi

Empusa henryi, commonly known as the Henry's empress, is a rare and striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Empusa henryi is considered a valuable but endangered species in botanical conservation efforts.
