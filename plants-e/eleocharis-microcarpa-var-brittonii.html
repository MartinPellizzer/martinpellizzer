Eleocharis microcarpa var. brittonii

Eleocharis microcarpa var. brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's spike-rush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in areas with standing water or saturated soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often found in the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and the Gulf Coast region.
