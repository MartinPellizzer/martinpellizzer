Emblica Pacoensis

Paco emblica, scientifically known as Emblica pacoensis, is a rare and lesser-known species of the genus Emblica, native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia. This unique plant is characterized by its striking yellow fruit, which is both ornamental and potentially medicinal. While not as widely studied as its more famous relative, the Indian gooseberry or amla, Emblica pacoensis holds significant ecological value in its natural habitat. Its conservation status is of concern due to habitat loss and overharvesting. Researchers and botanists are increasingly interested in studying its properties to uncover its potential benefits for both medicine and biodiversity.
