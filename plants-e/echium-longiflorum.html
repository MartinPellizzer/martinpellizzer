Echium longiflorum

Echium longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered bluemink, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, elongated flower spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Echium longiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
