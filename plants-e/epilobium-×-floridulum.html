Epilobium × floridulum

Epilobium × floridulum, commonly known as the Florida willow herb, is a hybrid plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually erect and somewhat woody at the base.

Epilobium × floridulum plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
