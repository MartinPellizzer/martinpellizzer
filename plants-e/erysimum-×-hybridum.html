Erysimum × hybridum

Erysimum × hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Erysimum cheiranthoides and Erysimum telephium, resulting in a diverse range of cultivars with varying flower colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are showy, ranging from yellow to orange and red, and are borne in racemes.

Erysimum × hybridum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.
