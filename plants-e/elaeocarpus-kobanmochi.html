Elaeocarpus kobanmochi

Elaeocarpus kobanmochi, commonly known as the Japanese elaeocarp, is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Japan, particularly found in the Ryukyu Islands. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish-brown bark and a preference for humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
