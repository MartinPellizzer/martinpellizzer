Elephantopus carolinianus

Elephantopus carolinianus, commonly known as Carolina elephanthead, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bulbous stems that resemble elephant heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
