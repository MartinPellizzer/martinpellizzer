Eragrostis palmeri

Eragrostis palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and panicle-like inflorescence that produces small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and open grasslands. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in full sun.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native habitats.
