Eucalyptus × irbyi

Eucalyptus × irbyi, commonly known as the Sydney red gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey to reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant typically grows as a tall tree, reaching heights of up to 30 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, usually pink or pale red, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Eucalyptus × irbyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and along coastal areas for its hardiness and aesthetic features.
