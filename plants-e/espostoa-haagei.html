Espostoa haagei

Espostoa haagei, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height.

Espostoa haagei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
