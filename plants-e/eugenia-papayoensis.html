Eugenia papayoensis

Eugenia papayoensis, commonly known as the papaya tree, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to warm climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale pink, and it is primarily cultivated for its edible fruit.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional remedies.
