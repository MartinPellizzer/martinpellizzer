Eriogonum covilleanum

Eriogonum covilleanum, commonly known as the Coville's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Eriogonum covilleanum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
