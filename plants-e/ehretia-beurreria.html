Ehretia beurreria

Ehretia beurreria, commonly known as the Indian mahua, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultural practices.
