Euphorbia cremersii

Euphorbia cremersii, commonly known as the dwarf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces unique, yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
