Euphorbia volhynica

Euphorbia volhynica, commonly known as the Volhynian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, often spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic sap, handling the plant requires caution.
