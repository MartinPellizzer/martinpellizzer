Eriochloa subulifera

Eriochloa subulifera, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects in its native range.
