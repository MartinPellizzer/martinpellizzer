Erythrina costaricensis var. panamensis

Erythrina costaricensis var. panamensis, commonly known as the Panama coral tree, is a deciduous legume native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which bloom in dense clusters during the dry season. The plant has trifoliate leaves with pointed lobes and produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
