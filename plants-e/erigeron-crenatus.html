Erigeron crenatus

Erigeron crenatus, commonly known as the smooth blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

Erigeron crenatus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
