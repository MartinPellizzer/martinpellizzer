Eupatorium sillense

Eupatorium sillense, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium sillense is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
