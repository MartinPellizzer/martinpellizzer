Echinocactus supertextus

Echinocactus supertextus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem covered with prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

Echinocactus supertextus is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
