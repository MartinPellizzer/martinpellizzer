Eremaea asterocarpa subsp. brachyclada

Eremaea asterocarpa subsp.

brachyclada, commonly known as the Narrow-leafed Eremaea, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is well adapted to harsh, semi-arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
