Elaeocarpus pyriformis

Elaeocarpus pyriformis, commonly known as the oval-fruit elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lanceolate, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by distinctive, elongated, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color. The fruits are an important food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
