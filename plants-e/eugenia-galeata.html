Eugenia galeata

Eugenia galeata, commonly known as the Mexican wax tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that changes color from green to red as it matures.

Eugenia galeata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
