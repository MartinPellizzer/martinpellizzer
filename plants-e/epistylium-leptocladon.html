Epistylium leptocladon

Epistylium leptocladon, commonly known as the slender clade epistylium, is a colonial marine protist belonging to the order Rhizopodida.

It forms dense, branching colonies that are typically attached to surfaces in marine environments. The organism is characterized by its elongated, thread-like structure and the presence of fine, hair-like projections called rhopalia. It plays a significant role in marine ecosystems by contributing to the microbial loop and serving as a food source for various planktonic organisms.

Due to its delicate structure, it is often found in shallow coastal waters where light and nutrients are abundant.
