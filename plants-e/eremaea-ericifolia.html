Eremaea ericifolia

Eremaea ericifolia, commonly known as the heath eremaea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically small and white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

Eremaea ericifolia is well adapted to low-nutrient soils and is often found in coastal and inland sandy areas.
