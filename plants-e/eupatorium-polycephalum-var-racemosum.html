Eupatorium polycephalum var. racemosum

Eupatorium polycephalum var. racemosum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
