Echites manubriatus

Echites manubriatus, commonly known as the manatee's claw, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, claw-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its roots are sometimes harvested for their purported medicinal properties.

Echites manubriatus thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found near water bodies.
