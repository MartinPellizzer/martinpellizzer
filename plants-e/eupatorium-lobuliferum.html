Eupatorium lobuliferum

Eupatorium lobuliferum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It is known for its aromatic, slightly hairy stems and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
