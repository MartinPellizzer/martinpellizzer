Eugenia vernonioides

Eugenia vernonioides, commonly known as the New Guinea pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
