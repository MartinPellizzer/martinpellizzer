Elaeocarpus sericoloides var. diffusus

Elaeocarpus sericoloides var. diffusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale pink, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
