Eriogonum microtheca subsp. intricatum

Eriogonum microtheca subsp.

intricatum, commonly known as the intricate buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The subspecies intricatum is distinguished by its intricate leaf patterns and more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
