Epilobium fastigiatoramosum

Epilobium fastigiatoramosum, commonly known as the erect willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated edge. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

Epilobium fastigiatoramosum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
