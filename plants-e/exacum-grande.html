Exacum grande

Exacum grande, commonly known as the large-flowered ivy.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa, characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Exacum grande is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
