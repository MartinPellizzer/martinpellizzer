Espeletia pycnophylla subsp. llanaganatensis

Espeletia pycnophylla subsp.

llanaganatensis, commonly known as the Llanaganatensis heather, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America. It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, adapted for pollination by insects. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
