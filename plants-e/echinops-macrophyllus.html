Echinops macrophyllus

Echinops macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its flowers are blue to purple in color and bloom in the summer months.

Echinops macrophyllus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
