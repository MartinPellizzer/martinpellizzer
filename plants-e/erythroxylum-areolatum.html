Erythroxylum areolatum

Erythroxylum areolatum, commonly known as the areolate copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Mexico, Guatemala, and parts of the Amazon basin. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is also known for producing a milky sap, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
