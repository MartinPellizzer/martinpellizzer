Eugenia schippii

Eugenia schippii, commonly known as the Schippi's guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Eugenia schippii is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for xeriscaping in appropriate regions.
