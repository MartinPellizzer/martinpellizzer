Echinopsis ceroniana

Echinopsis ceroniana, commonly known as the Ceronian cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its short, spiny stems and dense clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a slow growth rate. Its flowers bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Echinopsis ceroniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
