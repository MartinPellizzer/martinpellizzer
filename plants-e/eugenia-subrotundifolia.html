Eugenia subrotundifolia

Eugenia subrotundifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

Eugenia subrotundifolia is also known for its hard, dense wood, which is highly sought after for furniture and musical instruments.
