Eryngium campestre var. campestre

Eryngium campestre var. campestre, commonly known as field thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by bracts resembling leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
