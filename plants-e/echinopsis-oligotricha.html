Echinopsis oligotricha

Echinopsis oligotricha, commonly known as the Pachyveria, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly in the arid regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are often covered in a layer of white, waxy hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
