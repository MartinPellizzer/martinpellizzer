Erica glandulipila

Erica glandulipila, commonly known as the glandular heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape province, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica glandulipila is adapted to Mediterranean climates, with a dormant summer period and active growth during the winter months.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
