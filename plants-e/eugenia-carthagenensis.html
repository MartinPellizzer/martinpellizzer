Eugenia carthagenensis

Eugenia carthagenensis, commonly known as the Carthage hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Eugenia carthagenensis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
