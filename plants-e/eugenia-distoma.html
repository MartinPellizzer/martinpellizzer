Eugenia distoma

Eugenia distoma, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia distoma*, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

The tree is also known for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.
