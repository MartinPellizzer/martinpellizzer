Eleusine conglomerata var. littoralis

Eleusine conglomerata var. littoralis, commonly known as coastal lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and in dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or bluish-green. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
