Elytraria virgata var. vahliana

Elytraria virgata var. vahliana, commonly known as the Vahli's bottle palm, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, leafy sheath. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern around the base. Its flowering occurs in clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, which are followed by ornamental, red-brown fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
