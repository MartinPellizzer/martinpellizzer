Erythrina parcellii

Erythrina parcellii, commonly known as the red torchwood tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive crimson flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The tree has compound leaves composed of three to five leaflets, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and seeds contain alkaloids, making it both ecologically and pharmacologically significant.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and savannah habitats.
