Elaeocarpus lenormandii

Elaeocarpus lenormandii, commonly known as the Lenormand's elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a thick, waxy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
