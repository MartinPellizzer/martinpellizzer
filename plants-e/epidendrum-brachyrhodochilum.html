Epidendrum brachyrhodochilum

Epidendrum brachyrhodochilum, commonly known as the short-lipped epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

This orchid prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
