Eugenia vigiensis

Eugenia vigiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while its flowers are white or pale pink, often appearing in clusters.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and slightly tart flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.
