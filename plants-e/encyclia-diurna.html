Encyclia diurna

Encyclia diurna, commonly known as the sun orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that open during the day, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns, and it produces small, green, fleshy fruits.

Encyclia diurna thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
