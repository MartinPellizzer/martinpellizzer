Erisma floribundum var. floribundum

Erisma floribundum var. floribundum, commonly known as the sweet william, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces multiple flowering stems that rise above the foliage. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erisma floribundum var. floribundum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in floral arrangements.
