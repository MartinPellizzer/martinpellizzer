Erysimum incanum var. pachycarpum

Erysimum incanum var. pachycarpum, commonly known as the woolly wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-gray foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
