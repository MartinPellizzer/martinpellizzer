Echinocactus crispatus

Echinocactus crispatus, commonly known as the zigzag cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems that are covered in white, fuzzy spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for desert gardens.
