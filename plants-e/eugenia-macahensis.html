Eugenia macahensis

Eugenia macahensis, commonly known as the Mexican guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and produces edible berries that are similar to guavas in taste and texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

The leaves are usually oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent.
