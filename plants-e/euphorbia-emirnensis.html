Euphorbia emirnensis

Euphorbia emirnensis, commonly known as the Emirnensis spurges, is a species of flowering plant in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey, specifically the Taurus Mountains. The plant is characterized by its succulent, often spiny stems and leaves, which are adapted to arid and rocky environments. Its flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the genus Euphorbia.

Euphorbia emirnensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
