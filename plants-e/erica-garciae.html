Erica garciae

Erica garciae, commonly known as the Garcia's heath, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Erica garciae is considered a rare and endemic species.
