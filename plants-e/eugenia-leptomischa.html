Eugenia leptomischa

Eugenia leptomischa, commonly known as the slender-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a fragrant aroma, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in local cuisines.

Eugenia leptomischa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
