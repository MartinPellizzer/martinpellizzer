Echium expansum

Echium expansum, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, inflated seed capsule that splits open to release its seeds. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.

Echium expansum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
