Eumachia insidens

Eumachia insidens, commonly known as the creeping marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It typically grows in dense mats, with slender, cylindrical stems that root at the nodes and form a carpet-like growth pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in spikes, which are often hidden among the foliage. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-poor soils.

Eumachia insidens plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
