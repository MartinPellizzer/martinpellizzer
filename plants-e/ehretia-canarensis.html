Ehretia canarensis

Ehretia canarensis, commonly known as the white-flowered ehretia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
