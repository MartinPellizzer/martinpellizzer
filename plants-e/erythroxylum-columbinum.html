Erythroxylum columbinum

Erythroxylum columbinum, commonly known as the Colombian copal tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for various purposes, including traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
