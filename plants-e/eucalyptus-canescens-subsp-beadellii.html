Eucalyptus canescens subsp. beadellii

Eucalyptus canescens subsp.

beadellii, commonly known as the Beadell's mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that peels in strips, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with a whitish bloom.

The flowers are typically pale green or yellow, arranged in clusters, and are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. This subspecies is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
