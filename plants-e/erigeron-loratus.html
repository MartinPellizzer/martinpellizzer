Erigeron loratus

Erigeron loratus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Erigeron loratus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
