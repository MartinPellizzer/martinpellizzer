Echites paniculatus

Echites paniculatus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers, which are typically trumpet-shaped and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
