Eugenia salamensis var. hiraeifolia

Eugenia salamensis var. hiraeifolia, commonly known as the Salam forest hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its ornamental, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, which bloom throughout the wet season. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
