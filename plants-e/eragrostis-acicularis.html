Eragrostis acicularis

Eragrostis acicularis, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Eragrostis acicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
