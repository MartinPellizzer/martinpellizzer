Eriogonum ursinum var. erubescens

Eriogonum ursinum var. erubescens, commonly known as the redleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive reddish tinge, especially when mature.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
