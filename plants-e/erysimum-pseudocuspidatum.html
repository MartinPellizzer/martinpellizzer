Erysimum pseudocuspidatum

Erysimum pseudocuspidatum, commonly known as the western wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces long, slender seed pods.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
