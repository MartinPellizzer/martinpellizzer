Eulophia ibityensis

Eulophia ibityensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its native habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it prefers well-drained soil in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Eulophia ibityensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
