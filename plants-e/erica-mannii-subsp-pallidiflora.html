Erica mannii subsp. pallidiflora

Erica mannii subsp.

pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered heath, is a shrub native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, pale pink or white flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period usually occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica mannii subsp.

pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
