Elaeocarpus largiflorens subsp. retinervis

Elaeocarpus largiflorens subsp.

retinervis, commonly known as the broad-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive reticulate venation pattern, which is a key feature of the subspecies. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
