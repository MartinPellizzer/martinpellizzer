Erica fausta

Erica fausta, commonly known as the summer heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This evergreen shrub typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually pink or red and are arranged in dense clusters.

Erica fausta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
