Eragrostis purpurascens

Eragrostis purpurascens, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often display a reddish or purple hue, especially under stress conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
