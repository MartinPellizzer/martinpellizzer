Erysimum aznavourii

Erysimum aznavourii, commonly known as the Armenian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Erysimum aznavourii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
