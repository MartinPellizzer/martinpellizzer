Erica fragrans

Erica fragrans, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica fragrans thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent.
