Ericoides lanuginosum

Ericoides lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and mountainous areas. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Ericoides lanuginosum plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
