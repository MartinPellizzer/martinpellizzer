Erica campanulata

Erica campanulata, commonly known as the bell heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained, acidic conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Erica campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
