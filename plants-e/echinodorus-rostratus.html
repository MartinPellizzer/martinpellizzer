Echinodorus rostratus

Echinodorus rostratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a popular aquatic plant native to South America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often dark green with a slight waxy coating, aiding in its survival in water environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which are followed by dark blue to black seeds. Echinodorus rostratus thrives in freshwater habitats and is often used in aquariums due to its hardy nature and ability to provide oxygen and shelter for aquatic life.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an effective choice for water gardeners seeking to maintain clear, balanced ecosystems.
