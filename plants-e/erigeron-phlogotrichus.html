Erigeron phlogotrichus

Erigeron phlogotrichus, commonly known as the fiery blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron phlogotrichus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
