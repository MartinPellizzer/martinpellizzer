Eucalyptus mooreana

Eucalyptus mooreana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to southern Australia, particularly in the states of South Australia and Victoria. The plant is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom. Eucalyptus mooreana produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which are typically followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
