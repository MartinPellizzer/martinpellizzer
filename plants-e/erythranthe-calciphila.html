Erythranthe calciphila

Erythranthe calciphila, commonly known as the limestone pink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. This species thrives in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid conditions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers.
