Eugenioides sessifolium

Eugenioides sessifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
