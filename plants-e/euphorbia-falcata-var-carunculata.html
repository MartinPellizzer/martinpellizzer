Euphorbia falcata var. carunculata

Euphorbia falcata var. carunculata, commonly known as the red-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia (cup-like structures) that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
