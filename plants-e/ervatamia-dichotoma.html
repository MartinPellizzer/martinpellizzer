Ervatamia dichotoma

Ervatamia dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 60 centimeters tall, characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Ervatamia dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
