Epacris pulchella var. genuina

Epacris pulchella var. genuina, commonly known as the pink epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink or red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
