Eugenia diplocampta

Eugenia diplocampta, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits. The fruit has a sweet, acidic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its shade and aesthetic value.
