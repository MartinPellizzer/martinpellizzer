Erica racemifera

Erica racemifera, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Erica racemifera thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various coastal wildlife.
