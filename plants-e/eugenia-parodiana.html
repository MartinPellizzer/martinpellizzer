Eugenia parodiana

Eugenia parodiana, commonly known as the Parodiana myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
