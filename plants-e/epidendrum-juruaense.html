Epidendrum juruaense

Epidendrum juruaense, commonly known as the Juruaense Vanda, is a tropical orchid native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and segmented, supporting its evergreen foliage.

Epidendrum juruaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
