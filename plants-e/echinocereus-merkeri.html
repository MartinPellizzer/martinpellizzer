Echinocereus merkeri

Echinocereus merkeri, commonly known as the Merker's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its stems are cylindrical and ribbed, with a dark green to blue-gray coloration.

Echinocereus merkeri is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
