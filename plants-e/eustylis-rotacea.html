Eustylis rotacea

Eustylis rotacea, commonly known as the red-stemmed flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Eustylis rotacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
