Erophila verna var. pinguis

Erophila verna var. pinguis, commonly known as the fat bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, which are usually pink to purple in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in temperate climates and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
