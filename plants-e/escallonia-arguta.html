Escallonia arguta

Escallonia arguta, commonly known as the hardy escallonia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries. Escallonia arguta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

It is also known for its tolerance to coastal environments and salt spray.
