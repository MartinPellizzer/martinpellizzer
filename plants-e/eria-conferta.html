Eria conferta

Eria conferta, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Eria conferta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
