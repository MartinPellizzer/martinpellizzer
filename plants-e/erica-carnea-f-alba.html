Erica carnea f. alba

Erica carnea f. alba, commonly known as the white heather, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
