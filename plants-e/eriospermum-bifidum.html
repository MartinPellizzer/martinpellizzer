Eriospermum bifidum

Eriospermum bifidum, commonly known as the bifid eriosperm, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, two-lobed capsule, which is a key feature of the species.

Eriospermum bifidum thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
