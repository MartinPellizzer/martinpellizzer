Eulophia welwitschii subsp. carsonii

Eulophia welwitschii subsp.

carsonii, commonly known as Carson's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

This subspecies is noted for its resilience to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for cultivation in dry environments.
