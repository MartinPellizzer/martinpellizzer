Epipactis erimae

Epipactis erimae, commonly known as the Erimae Helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent purple spots. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Epipactis erimae is a perennial herb that reproduces through rhizomes and produces a single flower spike each year.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Orchidaceae family.
