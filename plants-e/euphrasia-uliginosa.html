Euphrasia uliginosa

Euphrasia uliginosa, commonly known as bog gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and swampy areas. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Euphrasia uliginosa is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
