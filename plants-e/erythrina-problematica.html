Erythrina problematica

Erythrina problematica, commonly known as the crimson flame tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and showy, crimson-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Erythrina problematica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
