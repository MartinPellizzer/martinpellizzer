Erioglossum rubiginosum

Erioglossum rubiginosum, commonly known as the red-stemmed erioglossum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall distinctive appearance.

Erioglossum rubiginosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
