Ericoila bavarica

Ericoila bavarica, commonly known as the Bavarian heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Ericoila bavarica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
