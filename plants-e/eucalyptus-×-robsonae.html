Eucalyptus × robsonae

Eucalyptus × robsonae, commonly known as the Robson's malleable gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the cooler months. It is a small to medium-sized tree, usually reaching heights of up to 10 meters, and is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils.

Eucalyptus × robsonae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fragrant flowers.
