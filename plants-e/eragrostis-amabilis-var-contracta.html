Eragrostis amabilis var. contracta

Eragrostis amabilis var. contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, compact growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally used in landscaping and restoration projects.
