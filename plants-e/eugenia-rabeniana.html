Eugenia rabeniana

Eugenia rabeniana, commonly known as the Madagascar apple or wild guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature.

Eugenia rabeniana is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
