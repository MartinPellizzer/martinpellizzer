Erythroxylum campestre var. acutifolium

Erythroxylum campestre var. acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved copal tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with lanceolate, acuminate leaves that are typically glaucous underneath. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or red, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for its resins and medicinal properties.
