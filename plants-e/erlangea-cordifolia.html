Erlangea cordifolia

Erlangea cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf erlangea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green and have a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
