Echites pallidus

Echites pallidus, commonly known as the pale echites, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water retention.

The botanical characteristics of Echites pallidus make it a resilient plant suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
