Eriastrum diffusum

Eriastrum diffusum, commonly known as broadleaf eriocephalus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that attract various pollinators.

Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture that aids in water conservation.
