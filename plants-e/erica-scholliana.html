Erica scholliana

Erica scholliana, commonly known as the Scholliana heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica scholliana is a low-growing shrub, often forming dense mats that contribute to the unique fynbos vegetation of its habitat.

It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region, with drought-resistant features and a fire-prone ecosystem.
