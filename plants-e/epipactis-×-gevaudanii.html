Epipactis × gevaudanii

Epipactis × gevaudanii, commonly known as the Gevaudan hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Epipactis atrorubens and Epipactis palustris.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers with a distinctive greenish-yellow coloration. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy habitats across Europe, particularly in France and Germany. Its flowers have a unique structure with a spurred lip and a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Epipactis × gevaudanii is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
