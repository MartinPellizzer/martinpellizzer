Euphorbia lucida var. leiocarpa

Euphorbia lucida var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white-spined euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, glandular leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the specialized flower structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling and is often used in ornamental gardening.
