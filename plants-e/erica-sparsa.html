Erica sparsa

Erica sparsa, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright form and dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, pale pink to white flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in tight, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica sparsa is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
