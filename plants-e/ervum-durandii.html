Ervum durandii

Ervum durandii, commonly known as the Durand's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
