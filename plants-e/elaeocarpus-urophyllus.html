Elaeocarpus urophyllus

Elaeocarpus urophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological importance.
