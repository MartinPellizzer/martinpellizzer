Etlingera maingayi var. longibracteata

Etlingera maingayi var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract ginger, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are typical of the Zingiberaceae family. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The varietal name 'longibracteata' refers to the elongated bracts that surround its inflorescence.
