Earina aestivalis

Earina aestivalis, commonly known as the summer earina, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.
