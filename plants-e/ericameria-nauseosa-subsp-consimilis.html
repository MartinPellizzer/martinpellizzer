Ericameria nauseosa subsp. consimilis

Ericameria nauseosa subsp.

consimilis, commonly known as the yellow curlleaf mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to arid environments and contributes to its ecological role in native plant communities.
