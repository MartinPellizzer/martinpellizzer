Eulophia macrantha

Eulophia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and large, showy orchid flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces numerous small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters along the inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eulophia macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
