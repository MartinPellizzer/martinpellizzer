Eugenia kellermanii

Eugenia kellermanii, commonly known as Kellerman's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for its potential medicinal properties.
