Eucharidium grandiflorum var. roseum

Eucharidium grandiflorum var. roseum, commonly known as the roseum bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, rose-pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
