Epilobium glandulosum f. longifolium

Epilobium glandulosum f. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved willowherb, is a variant of the willowherb species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
