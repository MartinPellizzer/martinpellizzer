Echinopsis pentlandii var. tuberculata

Echinopsis pentlandii var. tuberculata, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in spiny, tuberculate structures that give it its common name. The plant produces striking white to pale blue flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
