Erigeron acris subsp. podolicus

Erigeron acris subsp.

podolicus, commonly known as the Podolic tickseed, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. It features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
