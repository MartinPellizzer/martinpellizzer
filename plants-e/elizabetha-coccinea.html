Elizabetha coccinea

Elizabetha coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Elizabetha coccinea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
