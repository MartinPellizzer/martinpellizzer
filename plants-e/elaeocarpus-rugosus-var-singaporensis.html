Elaeocarpus rugosus var. singaporensis

Elaeocarpus rugosus var. singaporensis, commonly known as the Singapore elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and opposite, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
