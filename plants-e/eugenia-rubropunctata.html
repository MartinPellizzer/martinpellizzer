Eugenia rubropunctata

Eugenia rubropunctata, commonly known as the red-spotted eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with reddish veins and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, often containing several seeds.

Eugenia rubropunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
