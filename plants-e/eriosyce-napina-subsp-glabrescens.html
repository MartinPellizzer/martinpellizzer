Eriosyce napina subsp. glabrescens

Eriosyce napina subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-flowered rock cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of short, woolly hairs that become less prominent with age.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted green color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
