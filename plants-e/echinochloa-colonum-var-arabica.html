Echinochloa colonum var. arabica

Echinochloa colonum var. arabica, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a highly competitive annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, blade-like leaves and spikelets that form dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, lawns, and wetlands. It has a rapid growth rate and can become a significant weed in crops such as rice and corn.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, rhizomatous stem that aids in its spread.
