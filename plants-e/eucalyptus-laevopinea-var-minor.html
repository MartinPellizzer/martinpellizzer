Eucalyptus laevopinea var. minor

Eucalyptus laevopinea var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are often yellow or cream in color. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin strips, revealing a mottled appearance.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry landscapes.
