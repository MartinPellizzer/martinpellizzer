Euphrasia fennica subsp. praecox

Euphrasia fennica subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early marsh eyebright, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent yellow spots, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
