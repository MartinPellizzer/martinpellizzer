Echinops psammophilus

Echinops psammophilus, commonly known as the sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its genus name "Echinops.". The plant thrives in sandy and gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, dry environments. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
