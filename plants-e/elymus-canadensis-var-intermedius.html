Elymus canadensis var. intermedius

Elymus canadensis var. intermedius, commonly known as intermediate Canada wildrye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

This species is often found in prairies, wetlands, and along riverbanks, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for wildlife.
