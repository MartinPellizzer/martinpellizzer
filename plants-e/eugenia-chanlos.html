Eugenia chanlos

Eugenia chanlos, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red fruits that are encased in a thick, bumpy rind. Its botanical features include a woody stem with smooth bark and a shallow root system adapted to wet, swampy environments.

The leaves are typically arranged oppositely and have a leathery texture.
