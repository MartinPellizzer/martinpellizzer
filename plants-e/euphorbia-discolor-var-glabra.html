Euphorbia discolor var. glabra

Euphorbia discolor var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, cylindrical stem covered in a smooth, waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is known for its ability to store water in its stem.

This variant is prized in horticulture for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
