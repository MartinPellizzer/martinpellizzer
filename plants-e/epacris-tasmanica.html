Epacris tasmanica

Epacris tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian heath, is an evergreen shrub native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained acidic soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing pink to purple blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Epacris tasmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
