Erica venustiflora

Erica venustiflora, commonly known as the showy heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Erica venustiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
