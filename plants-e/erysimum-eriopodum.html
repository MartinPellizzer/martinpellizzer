Erysimum eriopodum

Erysimum eriopodum, commonly known as the woolly wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features erect, branched stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a similar fuzzy coating.

Erysimum eriopodum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance.
