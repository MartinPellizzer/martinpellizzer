Erythroxylum daphnites

Erythroxylum daphnites, commonly known as the dwarf copal tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is valued for its role in local ecosystems and has some traditional uses in indigenous communities.
