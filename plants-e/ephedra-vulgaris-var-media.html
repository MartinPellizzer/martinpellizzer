Ephedra vulgaris var. media

Ephedra vulgaris var. media, commonly known as the middle ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has also been studied for its pharmacological properties.
