Eupatorium nepetifolium

Eupatorium nepetifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pale blue to lavender flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Eupatorium nepetifolium is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
