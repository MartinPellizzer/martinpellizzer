Eragrostis strigosa

Eragrostis strigosa, commonly known as hairy grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.

Eragrostis strigosa is also utilized in some regions for forage and erosion control.
