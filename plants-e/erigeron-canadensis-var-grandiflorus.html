Erigeron canadensis var. grandiflorus

Erigeron canadensis var. grandiflorus, commonly known as Canadian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open fields, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it is often found in the northeastern and central United States. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
