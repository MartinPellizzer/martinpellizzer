Eucephalus glabratus

Eucephalus glabratus, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, and its spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties.

Eucephalus glabratus is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
