Erianthus trinii

Erianthus trinii, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescence that resembles a plume. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Erianthus trinii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
