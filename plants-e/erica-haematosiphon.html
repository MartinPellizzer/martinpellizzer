Erica haematosiphon

Erica haematosiphon, commonly known as the blood-red heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink in color, which bloom in spring. Erica haematosiphon thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
