Eupatorium platychaetum

Eupatorium platychaetum, commonly known as roughhead thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically rough to the touch, with a distinctive serrated edge, and the plant often grows in moist to wet habitats. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.

The plant also serves as a host for certain butterfly species, contributing to local biodiversity.
