Eria velifera

Eria velifera, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, making it well-suited for humid environments.

Eria velifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
