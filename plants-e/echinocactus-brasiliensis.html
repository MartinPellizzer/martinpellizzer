Echinocactus brasiliensis

Echinocactus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brasiliensis Cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a spineless cactus that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a globular shape with a thick, ribbed stem that stores water. It produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
