Erythrodes mexicana

Erythrodes mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican redwood, is a rare and endangered species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Mexico.

It is a member of the Podocarpaceae family, characterized by its evergreen coniferous appearance and needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its slow growth rate and specific habitat requirements make it highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique botanical species from further decline.
