Eulophia chrysantha

Eulophia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage and produces fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive golden hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil and decaying organic matter.

Eulophia chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
