Euphorbia tshuiensis

Euphorbia tshuiensis, commonly known as the Tshuiensis spurge, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species capable of surviving in harsh conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
