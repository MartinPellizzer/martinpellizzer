Ephedra fragilis subsp. fragilis

Ephedra fragilis subsp.

fragilis, commonly known as the fragile ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically deciduous.

The plant produces green to yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody capsules containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Ephedra fragilis subsp.

fragilis is a member of the Ephedraceae family, which includes other species known for their medicinal properties.
