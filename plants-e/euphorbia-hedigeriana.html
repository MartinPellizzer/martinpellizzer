Euphorbia hedigeriana

Euphorbia hedigeriana, commonly known as the Desert Spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem with spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its stem and leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
