Epidendrum paniculorugulosum

Epidendrum paniculorugulosum, commonly known as the rugose panicled epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, rugose (rough) texture on the petals and sepals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate to high humidity for optimal growth.

This species is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
