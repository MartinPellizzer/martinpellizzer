Epidendrum arevaloides

Epidendrum arevaloides, commonly known as the Arevalo's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescence bearing numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Epidendrum arevaloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
