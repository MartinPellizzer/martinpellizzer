Eugenia gerrardii

Eugenia gerrardii, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or red cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.

Eugenia gerrardii is also sometimes cultivated as a hedge or screen due to its dense growth habit.
