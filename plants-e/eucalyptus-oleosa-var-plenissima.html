Eucalyptus oleosa var. plenissima

Eucalyptus oleosa var. plenissima, commonly known as the woolly box, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage composed of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white to grey pubescence. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

The species is noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
