Eriophorum pallidum

Eriophorum pallidum, commonly known as the slender cottongrass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white seed heads that resemble cotton, which are produced at the ends of its flowering stems. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs, fens, and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Eriophorum pallidum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing peat soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
