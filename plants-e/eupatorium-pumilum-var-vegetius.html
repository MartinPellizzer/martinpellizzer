Eupatorium pumilum var. vegetius

Eupatorium pumilum var. vegetius, commonly known as the small eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
