Eugenia blancoi

Eugenia blancoi, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and limestone soils.

Eugenia blancoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
