Eriogonum effusum var. divaricatum

Eriogonum effusum var. divaricatum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
