Eugenia praestigiosa

Eugenia praestigiosa, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
