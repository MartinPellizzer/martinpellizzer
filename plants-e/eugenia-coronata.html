Eugenia coronata

Eugenia coronata, commonly known as the crowned eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Eugenia coronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
