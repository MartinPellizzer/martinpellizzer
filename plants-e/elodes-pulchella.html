Elodes pulchella

Elodes pulchella, commonly known as the pretty waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are often green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that provide habitat for aquatic invertebrates. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water oxygenation and serving as a food source for various organisms.

Elodes pulchella is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
