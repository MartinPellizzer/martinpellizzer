Echium modestum var. wilczekianum

Echium modestum var. wilczekianum, commonly known as the Wilczek's thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, woolly stems and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
