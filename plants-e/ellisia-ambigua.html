Ellisia ambigua

Ellisia ambigua, commonly known as the sticky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that exude a sticky substance, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Ellisia ambigua is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
