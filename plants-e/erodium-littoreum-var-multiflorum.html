Erodium littoreum var. multiflorum

Erodium littoreum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the coastal stork's bill, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern California.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and small, clustered pink or reddish-purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed coastal habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal plant communities.
