Elaeagnus buisanensis

Elaeagnus buisanensis, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used for windbreaks and soil stabilization. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which are visited by pollinators.

The wood of Elaeagnus buisanensis is hard and durable, making it useful for various crafts and construction purposes.
