Epidendrum oncidioides

Epidendrum oncidioides, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, long-lasting flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum oncidioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
