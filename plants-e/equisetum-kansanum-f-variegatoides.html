Equisetum kansanum f. variegatoides

Equisetum kansanum f. variegatoides, commonly known as the variegated horsehair grass, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems that are often green with yellow or white variegation. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of the stem, which are typically dark brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
