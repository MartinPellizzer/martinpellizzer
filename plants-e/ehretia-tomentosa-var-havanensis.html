Ehretia tomentosa var. havanensis

Ehretia tomentosa var. havanensis, commonly known as the Havana hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
