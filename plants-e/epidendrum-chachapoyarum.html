Epidendrum chachapoyarum

Epidendrum chachapoyarum, commonly known as the Chachapoyas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of northern Peru.

It belongs to the Epidendraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of light green to yellowish-white flowers with a distinctive red or pink lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
