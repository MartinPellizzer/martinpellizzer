Erophila elongata

Erophila elongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and streams. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
