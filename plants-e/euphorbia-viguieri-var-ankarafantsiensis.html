Euphorbia viguieri var. ankarafantsiensis

Euphorbia viguieri var. ankarafantsiensis, commonly known as the Ankarafantsy Euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
