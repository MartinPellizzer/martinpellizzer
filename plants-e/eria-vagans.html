Eria vagans

Eria vagans, commonly known as the wandering eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom sequentially along the stem. Eria vagans thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
