Eugenia oleosa var. cyanocarpa

Eugenia oleosa var. cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-berried eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, intensely blue-black berries that are edible. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
