Epipactis violacea

Epipactis violacea, commonly known as the violet helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

The violet helleborine is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
