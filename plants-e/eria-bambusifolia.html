Eria bambusifolia

Eria bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its slender, bamboo-like rhizomes and delicate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its fronds are often arching and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Eria bambusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
