Echeveria turgida

Echeveria turgida, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces showy clusters of violet or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Echeveria turgida is popular as a houseplant and in rock gardens due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
