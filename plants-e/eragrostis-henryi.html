Eragrostis henryi

Eragrostis henryi, commonly known as Henry's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its adaptability makes it a useful plant for erosion control and ornamental gardening.
