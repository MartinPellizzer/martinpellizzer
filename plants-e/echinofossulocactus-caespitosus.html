Echinofossulocactus caespitosus

Echinofossulocactus caespitosus, commonly known as the clustered spiny barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that are covered in spines and are often clustered together. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
