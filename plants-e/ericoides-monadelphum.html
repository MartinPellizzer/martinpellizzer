Ericoides monadelphum

Ericoides monadelphum, commonly known as the red-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Ericoides monadelphum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
