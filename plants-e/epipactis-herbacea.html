Epipactis herbacea

Epipactis herbacea, commonly known as the common helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, pleated leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with purple or brown markings. Epipactis herbacea is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
