Eugenia sancarlosensis

Eugenia sancarlosensis, commonly known as the Sancarlos eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like guava and eucalyptus. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Following flowering, it develops small, round berries that are typically red or purple in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
