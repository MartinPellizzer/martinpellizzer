Eragrostis setifolia

Eragrostis setifolia, commonly known as slender beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Eragrostis setifolia is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
