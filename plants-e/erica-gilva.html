Erica gilva

Erica gilva, commonly known as Heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. Erica gilva has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine, white hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
