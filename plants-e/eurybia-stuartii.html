Eurybia stuartii

Eurybia stuartii, commonly known as the Stuart's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Eurybia stuartii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
