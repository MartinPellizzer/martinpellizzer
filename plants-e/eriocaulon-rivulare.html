Eriocaulon rivulare

Eriocaulon rivulare, commonly known as the river marsh grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of rivers, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually short and clustered, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Eriocaulon rivulare plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
