Eugenia hatschbachii

Eugenia hatschbachii, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Eugenia hatschbachii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
