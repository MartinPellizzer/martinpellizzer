Eriogonum spathulatum var. natum

Eriogonum spathulatum var. natum, commonly known as the broadleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It is a perennial species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
