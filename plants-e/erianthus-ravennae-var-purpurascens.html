Erianthus ravennae var. purpurascens

Erianthus ravennae var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered reed, is a variant of the reed grass native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive purple or reddish-purple inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils along rivers and lakes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
