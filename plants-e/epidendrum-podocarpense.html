Epidendrum podocarpense

Epidendrum podocarpense, commonly known as the Podocarp Forest Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features long, arching stems with numerous lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, with showy, spurred petals in shades of white, pink, and purple.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
