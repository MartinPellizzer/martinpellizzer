Elymus paboanus

Elymus paboanus, commonly known as the Pabuana grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Elymus paboanus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
