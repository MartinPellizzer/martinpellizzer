Epidendrum hitchcockii

Epidendrum hitchcockii, commonly known as the Hitchcock's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Epidendrum hitchcockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
