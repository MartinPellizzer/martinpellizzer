Ehretia setosa

Ehretia setosa, commonly known as the silky ehretia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes, adding a delicate appearance to the plant. It produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
