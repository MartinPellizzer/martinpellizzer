Euonymus europaeus

Euonymus europaeus, commonly known as wood spurge, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, appearing in early summer.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
