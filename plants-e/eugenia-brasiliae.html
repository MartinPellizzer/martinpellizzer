Eugenia brasiliae

Eugenia brasiliae, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage, which is used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
