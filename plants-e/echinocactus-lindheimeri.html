Echinocactus lindheimeri

Echinocactus lindheimeri, commonly known as the Lindheimer's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spiny exterior provides protection from herbivores, while its shallow root system enables it to absorb moisture efficiently.

Echinocactus lindheimeri is well adapted to desert climates and is often found in rocky soils and open plains.
