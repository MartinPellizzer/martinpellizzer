Ereicoctis juniperifolia

Ereicoctis juniperifolia, commonly known as the juniperleaf ericoid, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a compact, bushy form. It produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible berries in the summer.

Ereicoctis juniperifolia is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
