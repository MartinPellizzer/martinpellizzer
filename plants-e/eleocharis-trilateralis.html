Eleocharis trilateralis

Eleocharis trilateralis, commonly known as the three-angled sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Eleocharis trilateralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
