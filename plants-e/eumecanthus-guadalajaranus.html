Eumecanthus guadalajaranus

Eumecanthus guadalajaranus, commonly known as the Guadalajara orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
