Eugenia mandevillensis

Eugenia mandevillensis, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or the Brazilian cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient plant in tropical environments.
