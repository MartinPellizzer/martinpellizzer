Epimedium grandiflorum subsp. koreanum

Epimedium grandiflorum subsp.

koreanum, commonly known as the Korean barrenwort, is a perennial herb native to Korea and parts of China. It features rhizomatous growth and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish underside.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and early blooming habit.
