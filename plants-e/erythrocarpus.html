Erythrocarpus

Erythrocarpus, commonly known as the red-fruited plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and includes species such as Erythrocarpus sphacelatus, which is found in the coastal regions of New South Wales. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in heathland or scrub environments.

The genus is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to coastal and arid conditions.
