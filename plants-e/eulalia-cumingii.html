Eulalia cumingii

Eulalia cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Eulalia cumingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
