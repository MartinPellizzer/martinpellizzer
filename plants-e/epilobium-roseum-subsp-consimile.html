Epilobium roseum subsp. consimile

Epilobium roseum subsp.

consimile, commonly known as the pink willow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and woodland edges.

The plant features slender, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
