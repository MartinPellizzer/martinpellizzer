Echium vulgare var. estivalis

Echium vulgare var. estivalis, commonly known as summer evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
