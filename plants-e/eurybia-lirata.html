Eurybia lirata

Eurybia lirata, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Eurybia lirata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various pollinators.
