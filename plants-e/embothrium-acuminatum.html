Embothrium acuminatum

Embothrium acuminatum, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Embothrium acuminatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
