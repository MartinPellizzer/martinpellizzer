Erysimum cheiranthoides subsp. transiliense

Erysimum cheiranthoides subsp.

transiliense, commonly known as the Transilvanian wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found along mountainous areas and cliffs.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are bright yellow, borne in racemes, and are characteristic of the Brassicaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
