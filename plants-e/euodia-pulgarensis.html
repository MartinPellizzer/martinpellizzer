Euodia pulgarensis

Euodia pulgarensis, commonly known as the Mexican lauraceae, is a species of flowering plant in the laurel family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and parts of southern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Euodia pulgarensis is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
