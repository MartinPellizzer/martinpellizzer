Eriogonum prattenianum var. prattenianum

Eriogonum prattenianum var. prattenianum, commonly known as the Pratten's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is part of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive bract structures and ecological importance to local wildlife.
