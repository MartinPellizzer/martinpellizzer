Echeandia sinaloensis

Echeandia sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloa cholla, is a species of cactus native to the state of Sinaloa in Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and are often segmented. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Echeandia sinaloensis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions and limited water availability.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various desert species.
