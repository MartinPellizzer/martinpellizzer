Eria discolor

Eria discolor, commonly known as the silver orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, evergreen stems and delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, which gives it its common name.

Eria discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
