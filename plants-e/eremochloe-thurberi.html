Eremochloe thurberi

Eremochloe thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing minute, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eremochloe thurberi is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
