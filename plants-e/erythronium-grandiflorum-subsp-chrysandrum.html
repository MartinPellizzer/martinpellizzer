Erythronium grandiflorum subsp. chrysandrum

Erythronium grandiflorum subsp.

chrysandrum, commonly known as the golden bulb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe. It features a short, rhizomatous stem with a cluster of lance-shaped, dark green leaves at the base.

The plant produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
