Euphorbia baxanica

Euphorbia baxanica, commonly known as the Baxanica spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green or bluish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia baxanica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
