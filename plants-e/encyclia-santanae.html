Encyclia santanae

Encyclia santanae, commonly known as the Santana's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Encyclia santanae thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
