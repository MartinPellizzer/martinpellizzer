Eriogonum praebens var. divaricatum

Eriogonum praebens var. divaricatum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a fuzzy texture. This species is adapted to rocky and dry environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
