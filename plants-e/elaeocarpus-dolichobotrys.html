Elaeocarpus dolichobotrys

Elaeocarpus dolichobotrys, commonly known as the long-fruited elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, fleshy berries that are typically blue or black when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
