Echinops spinosissimus subsp. macrolepis

Echinops spinosissimus subsp.

macrolepis, commonly known as the large-lobed thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, spherical heads composed of numerous blue to purple bracts.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often branched and covered in stiff spines.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
