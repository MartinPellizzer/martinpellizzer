Eugenia tapirorum

Eugenia tapirorum, commonly known as the Tapirum myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, Eugenia tapirorum is considered endangered and is of interest for conservation efforts.
