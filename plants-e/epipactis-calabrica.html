Epipactis calabrica

Epipactis calabrica, commonly known as the Calabrian helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features a rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Epipactis calabrica is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
