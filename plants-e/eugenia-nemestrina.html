Eugenia nemestrina

Eugenia nemestrina, commonly known as the satinwood or red sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, reddish-brown berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia nemestrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
