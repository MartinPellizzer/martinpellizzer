Erythroxylum petiolatum

Erythroxylum petiolatum, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain elliptical seeds.

Erythroxylum petiolatum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings due to its adaptability to various soil types.
