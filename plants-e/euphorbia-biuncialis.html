Euphorbia biuncialis

Euphorbia biuncialis, commonly known as the two-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, woody stem with two opposite leaves at the top, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia biuncialis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
