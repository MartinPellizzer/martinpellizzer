Eugenia micropora

Eugenia micropora, commonly known as the small-fruited eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. It produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia micropora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
