Eremophila laanii

Eremophila laanii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, often pink or red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila laanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscaping.
