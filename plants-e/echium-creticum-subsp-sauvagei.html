Echium creticum subsp. sauvagei

Echium creticum subsp.

sauvagei, commonly known as the Cretan fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
