Echinops sintenisii

Echinops sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis' thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small globes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops sintenisii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
