Evolvulus frankenioides var. strictus

Evolvulus frankenioides var. strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
