Elegia vaginulata

Elegia vaginulata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its seeds are dispersed by water, contributing to its spread in aquatic environments.

Elegia vaginulata is also noted for its ability to tolerate flooding, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
