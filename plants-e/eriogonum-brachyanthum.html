Eriogonum brachyanthum

Eriogonum brachyanthum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Eriogonum brachyanthum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
