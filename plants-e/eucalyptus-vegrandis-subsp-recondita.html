Eucalyptus vegrandis subsp. recondita

Eucalyptus vegrandis subsp.

recondita, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a subspecies native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white bloom.

The flowers are small and usually grouped in clusters, producing a pale yellow to cream color. This subspecies is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for native fauna.
