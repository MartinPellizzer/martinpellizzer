Eriocaulon gracile var. kurzii

Eriocaulon gracile var. kurzii, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as marshes and ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale green flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
