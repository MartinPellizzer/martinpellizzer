Euphorbia montana var. robusta

Euphorbia montana var. robusta, commonly known as the Giant Spurge, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with distinctive yellowish-green sap. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by colorful bracts that give it a showy appearance. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This species is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
