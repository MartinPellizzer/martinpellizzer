Eria whitfordii

Eria whitfordii, commonly known as the Whitford's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Eria whitfordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
