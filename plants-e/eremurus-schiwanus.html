Eremurus schiwanus

Eremurus schiwanus, commonly known as the Schiwan's sasparilla, is a striking plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous yellow flowers. The plant produces large, showy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
