Encyclia ochracea

Encyclia ochracea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a greenish or reddish hue. Its leaves are typically dark green and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Encyclia ochracea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and greenhouses.
