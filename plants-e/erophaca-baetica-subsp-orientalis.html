Erophaca baetica subsp. orientalis

Erophaca baetica subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Mediterranean erophaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
