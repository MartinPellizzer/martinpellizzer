Ectrosiopsis curvifolia

Ectrosiopsis curvifolia, commonly known as the curved-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often ribbed and have a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Ectrosiopsis curvifolia is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
