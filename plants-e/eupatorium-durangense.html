Eupatorium durangense

Eupatorium durangense, commonly known as Durango boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions with a subtropical climate. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Eupatorium durangense plays an important role in its native ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
