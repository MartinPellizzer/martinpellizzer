Euphorbia fistulosa

Euphorbia fistulosa, commonly known as the red milkweed or prickly pear, is a succulent shrub native to South America.

It features a distinctive, spiny stem with a greenish-gray hue and produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soil.

Its structure and appearance make it a unique addition to xeriscapic gardens.
