Erithalis fruticosa var. odorifera

Erithalis fruticosa var. odorifera, commonly known as the fragrant erithalis, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its aromatic properties make it a notable species in both horticulture and herbal practices.
