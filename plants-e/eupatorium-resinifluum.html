Eupatorium resinifluum

Eupatorium resinifluum, commonly known as the resiniflorous boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has hairy, often glandular stems and leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, with white to pale purple petals, and it produces a sticky, resinous substance from its leaves.

Eupatorium resinifluum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
