Euphorbia javanica

Euphorbia javanica, commonly known as the Java spurge, is a succulent plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Euphorbia javanica is also noted for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
