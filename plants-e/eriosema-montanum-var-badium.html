Eriosema montanum var. badium

Eriosema montanum var. badium, commonly known as the mountain eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pale blue or violet, with a distinctive hairy texture on its leaves and stems. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

This species is often found in high-altitude grasslands and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
