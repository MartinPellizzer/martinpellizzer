Echinocactus paraguayensis

Echinocactus paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Paraguay and northeastern Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features spiny, cylindrical stems and produces yellow flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Echinocactus paraguayensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
