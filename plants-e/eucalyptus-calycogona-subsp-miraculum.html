Eucalyptus calycogona subsp. miraculum

Eucalyptus calycogona subsp.

miraculum, commonly known as the Miraculous Eucalyptus, is a rare and striking species native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped trunk and smooth, pale grey to white bark that peels in thin, papery strips.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is noted for its unique morphology and is considered a valuable component of its ecosystem.
