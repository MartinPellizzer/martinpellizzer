Eugenia xylopifolia

Eugenia xylopifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
