Eucosia viridiflora

Eucosia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered eucosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Eucosia viridiflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
