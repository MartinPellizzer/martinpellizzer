Elaeagnus davidi

Elaeagnus davidi, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Elaeagnus davidi is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
