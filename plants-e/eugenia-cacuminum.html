Eugenia cacuminum

Eugenia cacuminum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Eugenia cacuminum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Its botanical features make it a significant species in both horticulture and local ecosystems.
