Eulophia bulbinoides

Eulophia bulbinoides, commonly known as the bulb orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bulb-like tuber, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients and water. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually green or yellowish, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Eulophia bulbinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
