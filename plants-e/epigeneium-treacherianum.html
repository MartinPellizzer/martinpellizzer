Epigeneium treacherianum

Epigeneium treacherianum, commonly known as the climbing ivy or treacherous epigeneium, is a vine-like plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and aerial roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often hidden within a spadix, typical of the arum family. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.

Epigeneium treacherianum is often found in shaded, humid environments such as rainforests.
