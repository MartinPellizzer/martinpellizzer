Eragrostis eucampta

Eragrostis eucampta, commonly known as bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural applications.
