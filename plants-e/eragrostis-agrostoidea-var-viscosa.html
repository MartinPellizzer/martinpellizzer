Eragrostis agrostoidea var. viscosa

Eragrostis agrostoidea var. viscosa, commonly known as sticky lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered with fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
