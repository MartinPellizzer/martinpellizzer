Enchidium

Enchidium, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is a succulent plant native to South Africa, characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that emerge from a cup-like structure called a cyathium. Enchidium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Its resilience makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
