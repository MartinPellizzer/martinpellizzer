Echioides nigricans

Echioides nigricans, commonly known as the blackstem echioides, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to greenish flowers that appear in umbels. Its root system is fibrous and can be harvested for medicinal purposes.

Echioides nigricans is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an adaptable and resilient species.
