Eugenia sellowiana

Eugenia sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, which are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Eugenia sellowiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
