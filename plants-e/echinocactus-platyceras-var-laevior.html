Echinocactus platyceras var. laevior

Echinocactus platyceras var. laevior, commonly known as the Mexican sunburst cactus, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, spiny areoles that bear white, woolly bristles. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are often surrounded by a ring of spines. Its thick, ribbed structure helps it store water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
