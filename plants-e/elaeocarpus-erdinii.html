Elaeocarpus erdinii

Elaeocarpus erdinii, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically reddish or orange when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in some regions.
