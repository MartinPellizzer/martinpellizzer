Euaraliopsis ficifolia

Euaraliopsis ficifolia, commonly known as the fig-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a fig leaf. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, pointed leaflets.

Euaraliopsis ficifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
