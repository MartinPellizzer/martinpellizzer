Eruca sativa var. aurea

Eruca sativa var. aurea, commonly known as the golden rapeseed, is a variant of the common mustard plant.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, often used in salads or as a garnish.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which can be used in cooking or oil production.
