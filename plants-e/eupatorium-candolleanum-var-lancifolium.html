Eupatorium candolleanum var. lancifolium

Eupatorium candolleanum var. lancifolium, commonly known as the lancifoliate boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated margins and a distinct, aromatic scent.

It produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered heads, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
