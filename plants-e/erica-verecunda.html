Erica verecunda

Erica verecunda, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica verecunda is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its high biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
