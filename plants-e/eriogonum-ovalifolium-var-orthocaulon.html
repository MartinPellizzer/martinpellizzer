Eriogonum ovalifolium var. orthocaulon

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. orthocaulon, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid conditions.
