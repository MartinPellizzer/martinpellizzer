Epidendrum arnoldii

Epidendrum arnoldii, commonly known as the Arnold's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Epidendrum arnoldii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
