Erica cornuta

Erica cornuta, commonly known as the horned heath, is a flowering plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica cornuta produces small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late winter to early spring.

It is a hardy shrub that is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.
