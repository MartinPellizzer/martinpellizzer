Exocarpos boninensis

Exocarpos boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, round, waxy fruits that turn red when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by the distinctive, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a slightly aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
