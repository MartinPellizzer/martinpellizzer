Epilobium palustre var. virescens

Epilobium palustre var. virescens, commonly known as the green willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish-brown and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin and a glossy green underside. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish-purple, and arranged in spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.
