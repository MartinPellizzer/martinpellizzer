Eucalyptus salicola

Eucalyptus salicola, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often greyish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding insects and birds. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for local use in construction and fuel.

Eucalyptus salicola is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.
