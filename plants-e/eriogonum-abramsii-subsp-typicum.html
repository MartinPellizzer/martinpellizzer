Eriogonum abramsii subsp. typicum

Eriogonum abramsii subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the Abrams' buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, spikelike clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
