Euodia fraxinifolia

Euodia fraxinifolia, commonly known as the Chinese sandpaper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, aromatic wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
