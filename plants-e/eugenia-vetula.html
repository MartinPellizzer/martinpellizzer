Eugenia vetula

Eugenia vetula, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or the Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and produces small, bright red fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers are typically white or pink and have a delicate, fragrant aroma.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its high nutritional value.
