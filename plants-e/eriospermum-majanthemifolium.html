Eriospermum majanthemifolium

Eriospermum majanthemifolium, commonly known as the mountain eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

Eriospermum majanthemifolium is adapted to arid environments, with thick leaves and a deep root system to conserve water.
