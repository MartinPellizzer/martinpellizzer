Euonymus bungeanus

Euonymus bungeanus, commonly known as the Bunge's spindle-tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, spiny fruits that resemble tiny spindles. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by the unique, fleshy berries.

Euonymus bungeanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
