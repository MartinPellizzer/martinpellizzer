Erythrina gibbsiae

Erythrina gibbsiae, commonly known as the red coral tree, is a striking tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its distinctive, woody pods contain flat, winged seeds. Erythrina gibbsiae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as a small tree or shrub.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and public landscapes.
