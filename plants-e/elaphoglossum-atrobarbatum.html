Elaphoglossum atrobarbatum

Elaphoglossum atrobarbatum, commonly known as the black-tipped hawksbeard, is a fern species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. Elaphoglossum atrobarbatum thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on fern morphology and ecological adaptation.
