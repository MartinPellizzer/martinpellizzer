Eriogonum umbellatum subsp. polyanthum

Eriogonum umbellatum subsp.

polyanthum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry environments and is often found in the Sierra Nevada mountain range.
