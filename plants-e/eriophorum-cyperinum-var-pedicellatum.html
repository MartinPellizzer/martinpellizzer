Eriophorum cyperinum var. pedicellatum

Eriophorum cyperinum var. pedicellatum, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a tussock, with small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and bogs, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
