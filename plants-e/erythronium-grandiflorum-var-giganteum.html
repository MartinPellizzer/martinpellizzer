Erythronium grandiflorum var. giganteum

Erythronium grandiflorum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant dog's tooth violet, is a striking bulbous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe.

It features large, showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal that resembles a dog's tooth. The plant produces broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is sensitive to environmental changes.
