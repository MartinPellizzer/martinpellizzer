Eremophila oppositifolia

Eremophila oppositifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, opposite leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and heat resistance. It often grows in sandy or rocky soils and is commonly found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

Eremophila oppositifolia plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds, due to its nectar-rich flowers.
