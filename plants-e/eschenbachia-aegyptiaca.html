Eschenbachia aegyptiaca

Eschenbachia aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
