Eugenia quadriovulata

Eugenia quadriovulata, commonly known as the four-ovary eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a deep red or purple color.

Eugenia quadriovulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
