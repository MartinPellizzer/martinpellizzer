Exacum perfoliatum

Exacum perfoliatum, commonly known as the winter jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom even in cooler temperatures.
