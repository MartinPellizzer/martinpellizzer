Epilobium × biharicum

Epilobium × biharicum, commonly known as the Himalayan willow or Indian willow, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Onagraceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and forest edges.

Epilobium × biharicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
