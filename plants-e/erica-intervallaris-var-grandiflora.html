Erica intervallaris var. grandiflora

Erica intervallaris var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in winter, providing a striking contrast to the green foliage.

Erica intervallaris var. grandiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to coastal climates.
