Eucalyptus × bipileata

Eucalyptus × bipileata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid species within the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect shrubs with smooth, often glaucous bark. The leaves are narrow and linear, typically with a blue-green or greyish hue. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by woody capsules.

This species is native to southern Australia and is adapted to dry, sandy soils in coastal and inland regions.
