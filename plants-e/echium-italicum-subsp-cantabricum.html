Echium italicum subsp. cantabricum

Echium italicum subsp.

cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
