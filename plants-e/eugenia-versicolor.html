Eugenia versicolor

Eugenia versicolor, commonly known as the variable eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Eugenia versicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
