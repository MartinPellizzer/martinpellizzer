Erica viscaria subsp. longifolia

Erica viscaria subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western Mediterranean region. It features dense, compact foliage with narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
