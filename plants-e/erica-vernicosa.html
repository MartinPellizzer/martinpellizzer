Erica vernicosa

Erica vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica vernicosa is well adapted to acidic, sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
