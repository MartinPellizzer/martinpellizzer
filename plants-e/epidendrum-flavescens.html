Epidendrum flavescens

Epidendrum flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and prefers indirect light.

Epidendrum flavescens is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
