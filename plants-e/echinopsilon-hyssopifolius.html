Echinopsilon hyssopifolius

Echinopsilon hyssopifolius, commonly known as the woolly thornweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Echinopsilon hyssopifolius is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
