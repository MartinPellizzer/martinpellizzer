Eulophia capensis

Eulophia capensis, commonly known as the Cape eulophia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its erect, fibrous stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with three petals and a prominent lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Eulophia capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
