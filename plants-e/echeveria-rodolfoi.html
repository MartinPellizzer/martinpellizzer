Echeveria rodolfoi

Echeveria rodolfoi, commonly known as the Rodolfoi Graptopetalum, is a succulent native to the rocky and arid regions of Mexico.

It features a compact rosette of thick, fleshy, and often bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and requiring minimal watering.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
