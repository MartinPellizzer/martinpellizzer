Eugenia jambolana var. obtusifolia

Eugenia jambolana var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the Java cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with a leathery texture and a dark green color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, cherry-like fruits. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy fruiting display.

This variety is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
