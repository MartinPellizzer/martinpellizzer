Echinocactus valparaiso

Echinocactus valparaiso, commonly known as the Valparaiso cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and distinctive spines that provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
