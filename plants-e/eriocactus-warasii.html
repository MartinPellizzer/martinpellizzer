Eriocactus warasii

Eriocactus warasii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of pale yellow bristles that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red fruits containing edible seeds. Eriocactus warasii is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for desert gardens and as a houseplant.
