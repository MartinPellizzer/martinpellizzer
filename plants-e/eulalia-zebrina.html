Eulalia zebrina

Eulalia zebrina, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray appearance.

Eulalia zebrina is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique visual appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
