Eragrostis cilianensis subsp. pooides

Eragrostis cilianensis subsp.

pooides, commonly known as the Cilian grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its adaptability to arid conditions.
