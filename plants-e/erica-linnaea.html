Erica linnaea

Erica linnaea, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica linnaea thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
