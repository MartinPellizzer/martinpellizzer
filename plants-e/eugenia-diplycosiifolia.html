Eugenia diplycosiifolia

Eugenia diplycosiifolia, commonly known as the glossy apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
