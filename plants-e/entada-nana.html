Entada nana

Entada nana, commonly known as the dwarf African flame tree, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.
