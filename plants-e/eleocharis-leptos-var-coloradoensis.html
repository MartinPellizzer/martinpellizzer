Eleocharis leptos var. coloradoensis

Eleocharis leptos var. coloradoensis, commonly known as the Colorado sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

This variety is native to the western United States, particularly in Colorado, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils.
