Erythrochlamys nummularia

Erythrochlamys nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf erythrochlamys, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
