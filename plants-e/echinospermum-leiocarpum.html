Echinospermum leiocarpum

Echinospermum leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-husked echinocephalum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a dry, hairy capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

The leaves are typically hairy and alternate, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
