Erodium heliotropioides

Erodium heliotropioides, commonly known as the coastal heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that resemble those of the heliotrope, hence its common name. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Erodium heliotropioides is adapted to saline and wind-exposed environments, making it a characteristic species of coastal vegetation.
