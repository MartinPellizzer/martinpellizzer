Eleutherococcus wilsonii var. pilosulus

Eleutherococcus wilsonii var. pilosulus, commonly known as the hairy eleuthero, is a rare variant of the eleuthero plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, reddish-brown berries. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring.

This variant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often studied for its adaptability to harsh climates.
