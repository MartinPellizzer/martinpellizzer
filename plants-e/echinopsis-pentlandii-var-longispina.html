Echinopsis pentlandii var. longispina

Echinopsis pentlandii var. longispina, commonly known as the Long-spined Moon Cactus, is a succulent native to South America.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem with a single, spiky flower that blooms at night. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem adapted for storing water, typical of cacti in arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
