Elaeocarpus balgooyi

Elaeocarpus balgooyi, commonly known as the Balmahari or Balmahari tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense canopy of glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by round, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
