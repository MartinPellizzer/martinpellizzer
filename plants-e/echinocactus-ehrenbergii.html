Echinocactus ehrenbergii

Echinocactus ehrenbergii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of bright yellow spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny exterior. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
