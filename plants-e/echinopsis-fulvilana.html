Echinopsis fulvilana

Echinopsis fulvilana, commonly known as the yellow night bloomer, is a cacti species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
