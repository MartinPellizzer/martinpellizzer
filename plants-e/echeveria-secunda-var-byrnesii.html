Echeveria secunda var. byrnesii

Echeveria secunda var. byrnesii, commonly known as the Byrnes' Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of vibrant purple or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
