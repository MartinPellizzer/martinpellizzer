Eugenia hislopii

Eugenia hislopii, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Eugenia hislopii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
