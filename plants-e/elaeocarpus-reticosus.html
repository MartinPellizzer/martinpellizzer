Elaeocarpus reticosus

Elaeocarpus reticosus, commonly known as the reticulate elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, often with a fleshy aril that attracts birds and animals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The plant has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
