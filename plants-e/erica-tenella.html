Erica tenella

Erica tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf heath, is a dwarf shrub native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, leathery leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

Erica tenella is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh, sunny conditions.
