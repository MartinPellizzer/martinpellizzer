Epidendrum occidentale

Epidendrum occidentale, commonly known as the western epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing multiple stems that grow horizontally. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are usually pale green to white in color, with a subtle fragrance.

Epidendrum occidentale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
