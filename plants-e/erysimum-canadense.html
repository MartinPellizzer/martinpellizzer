Erysimum canadense

Erysimum canadense, commonly known as Canadian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. Its leaves are basal and alternate, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Erysimum canadense is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
