Eustoma maculatum

Eustoma maculatum, commonly known as the "bluebells of the desert," is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Eustoma maculatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
