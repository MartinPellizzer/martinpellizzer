Erigeron bellidioides var. khasicom

Erigeron bellidioides var. khasicom, commonly known as the Khasi fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.

This species is often found in the Khasi and Jaintia Hills, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
