Eria atrovinosa

Eria atrovinosa, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and striking dark purple to black veins. The plant produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eria atrovinosa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
