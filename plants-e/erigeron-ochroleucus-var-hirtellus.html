Erigeron ochroleucus var. hirtellus

Erigeron ochroleucus var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.
