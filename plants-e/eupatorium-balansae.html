Eupatorium balansae

Eupatorium balansae, commonly known as the Balansae boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, sandy soils. Its flowers are small and white to pale blue, often appearing in clusters, and it produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Eupatorium balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
