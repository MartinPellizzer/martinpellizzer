Epidendrum haematochilum

Epidendrum haematochilum, commonly known as the blood-leaf orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive dark red or black lip. The plant has long, arching stems that can reach several feet in length, supporting its large, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
