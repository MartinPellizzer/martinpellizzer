Elaeocarpus altisectus subsp. altisectus

Elaeocarpus altisectus subsp.

altisectus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines.

The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, woody fruits that are an important food source for various animals.

This subspecies is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
