Eunanus tricolor

Eunanus tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers, which typically display three distinct colors on each petal. The plant grows in rocky soils and prefers well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Eunanus tricolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
