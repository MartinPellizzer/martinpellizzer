Erica perspicua

Erica perspicua, commonly known as the clear heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its production of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Erica perspicua is known for its fragrant blooms and is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
