Eulophia ochracea

Eulophia ochracea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores nutrients, typical of many orchid species. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted for efficient photosynthesis.

Eulophia ochracea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
