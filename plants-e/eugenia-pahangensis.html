Eugenia pahangensis

Eugenia pahangensis, commonly known as the Pahang guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round, and have a smooth, thin skin, often containing a single or few seeds.

Eugenia pahangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral characteristics.
