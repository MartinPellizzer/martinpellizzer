Epacris alba-compacta

Epacris alba-compacta, commonly known as the white compact epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Epacris alba-compacta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
