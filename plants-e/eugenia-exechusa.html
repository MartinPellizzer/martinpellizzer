Eugenia exechusa

Eugenia exechusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or purple when mature, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Eugenia exechusa thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
