Epidendrum anatipedium

Epidendrum anatipedium, commonly known as the yellow-tipped epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with red markings and elongated, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Epidendrum anatipedium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
