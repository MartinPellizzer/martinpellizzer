Eleocharis pallida

Eleocharis pallida, commonly known as the pink spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that typically displays a pink or reddish coloration. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its small, spike-like clusters of flowers are adapted for wind pollination, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.

Eleocharis pallida is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate a range of water conditions.
