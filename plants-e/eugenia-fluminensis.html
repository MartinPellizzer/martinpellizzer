Eugenia fluminensis

Eugenia fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Eugenia fluminensis is also an important species in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
