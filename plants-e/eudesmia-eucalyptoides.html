Eudesmia eucalyptoides

Eudesmia eucalyptoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honey-myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the eastern regions of Australia, where it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, aromatic leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It is often found in coastal and forested areas, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
