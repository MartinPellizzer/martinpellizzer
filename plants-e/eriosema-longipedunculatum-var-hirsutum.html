Eriosema longipedunculatum var. hirsutum

Eriosema longipedunculatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are arranged in elongated peduncles. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.
