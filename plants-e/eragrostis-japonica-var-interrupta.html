Eragrostis japonica var. interrupta

Eragrostis japonica var. interrupta, commonly known as Japanese false brome, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
