Eugenia adenoclada

Eugenia adenoclada, commonly known as the velvetwood or black myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-green foliage and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Eugenia adenoclada thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
