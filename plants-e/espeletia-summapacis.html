Espeletia summapacis

Espeletia summapacis, commonly known as the Summapacis heather, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, showy flower heads that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or volcanic soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
