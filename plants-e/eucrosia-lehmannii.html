Eucrosia lehmannii

Eucrosia lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens across regions with similar climates.
