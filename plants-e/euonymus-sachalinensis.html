Euonymus sachalinensis

Euonymus sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, fleshy berries that turn from green to bright red as they mature. These berries are a key feature for identification and attract various bird species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
