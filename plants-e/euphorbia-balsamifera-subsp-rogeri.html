Euphorbia balsamifera subsp. rogeri

Euphorbia balsamifera subsp.

rogeri, commonly known as the Roger's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a thick, fleshy stem that can grow up to several meters in height, often branching out into a shrub-like form.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle, helping to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
