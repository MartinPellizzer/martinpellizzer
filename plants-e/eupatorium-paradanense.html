Eupatorium paradanense

Eupatorium paradanense, commonly known as the Caribbean boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium paradanense produces small white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
