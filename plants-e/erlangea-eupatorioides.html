Erlangea eupatorioides

Erlangea eupatorioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants with vibrant color.
