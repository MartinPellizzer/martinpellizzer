Erythroxylum sobraleanum

Erythroxylum sobraleanum, commonly known as the Sobraleanum coca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that contain the plant's psychoactive alkaloid, cocaine.

Due to its chemical composition, it is of both scientific and legal interest.
