Echinocereus dasyacanthus subsp. ctenoides

Echinocereus dasyacanthus subsp.

ctenoides, commonly known as the golden hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a ribbed stem and spiny areoles.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics include a dense covering of white to yellow spines and a tendency to form clumps over time.
