Euphorbia densa subsp. badghysi

Euphorbia densa subsp.

badghysi, commonly known as the Kashmir spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India. It is characterized by its dense, fleshy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce a milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
