Epidendrum ibaguense var. schomburgkii

Epidendrum ibaguense var. schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's Epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
