Enaimon

Enaimon, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals, aiding in seed dispersal. Enaimon thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, drought-tolerant plants.
