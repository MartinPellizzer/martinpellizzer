Euchlaena mexicana var. luxurians

Euchlaena mexicana var. luxurians, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a nuisance in certain ecosystems.
