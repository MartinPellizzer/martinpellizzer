Eremurus cappadocicus

Eremurus cappadocicus, commonly known as the Cappadocian candleplant, is a striking perennial plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Eremurus, which is part of the family Xanthorrhoeaceae. This plant is characterized by its tall, candle-like flower stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
