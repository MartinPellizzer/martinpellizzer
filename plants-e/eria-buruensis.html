Eria buruensis

Eria buruensis, commonly known as the Buru orchid, is a species of orchid native to the island of Buru in Indonesia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces compact, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Eria buruensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
