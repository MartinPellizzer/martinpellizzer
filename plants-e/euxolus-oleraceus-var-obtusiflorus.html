Euxolus oleraceus var. obtusiflorus

Euxolus oleraceus var. obtusiflorus, commonly known as the broad-flower eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are typically covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
