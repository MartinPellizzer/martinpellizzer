Eugenia nhanica

Eugenia nhanica, commonly known as the Vietnamese guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
