Epilobium hirsutum subsp. parviflorum

Epilobium hirsutum subsp.

parviflorum, commonly known as hairy willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, often disturbed habitats such as riverbanks, ditches, and wet meadows.

The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. Its small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
