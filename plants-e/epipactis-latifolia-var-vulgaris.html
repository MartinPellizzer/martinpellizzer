Epipactis latifolia var. vulgaris

Epipactis latifolia var. vulgaris, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It produces erect, branching stems that bear numerous flowers, which are pollinated by various insects.

This variant is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
