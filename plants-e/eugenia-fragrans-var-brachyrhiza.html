Eugenia fragrans var. brachyrhiza

Eugenia fragrans var. brachyrhiza, commonly known as the small-rooted clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-white flowers and small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested when they turn a deep red or purple color. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a dense root system adapted to humid, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic essential oils.
