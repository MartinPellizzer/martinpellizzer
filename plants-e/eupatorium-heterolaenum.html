Eupatorium heterolaenum

Eupatorium heterolaenum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar flowering plants.
