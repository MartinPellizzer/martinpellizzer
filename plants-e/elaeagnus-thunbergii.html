Elaeagnus thunbergii

Elaeagnus thunbergii, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, edible berries that appear in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Elaeagnus thunbergii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil improvement.
