Eriogonum deflexum var. multipedunculatum

Eriogonum deflexum var. multipedunculatum, commonly known as the many-stemmed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
