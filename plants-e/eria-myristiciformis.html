Eria myristiciformis

Eria myristiciformis, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinct, intricate structure.
