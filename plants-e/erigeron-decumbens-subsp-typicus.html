Erigeron decumbens subsp. typicus

Erigeron decumbens subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, sprawling stems that often lie close to the ground. Its flowers are small, white to blue-purple, and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It produces small, dry fruits called achene, which are dispersed by wind.
