Exacum pedunculatum var. petiolare

Exacum pedunculatum var. petiolare, commonly known as the Petiolate African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The leaves are typically velvety and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in controlled indoor environments.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
