Erysimum bocconei var. minus

Erysimum bocconei var. minus, commonly known as the lesser wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to yellow, bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
