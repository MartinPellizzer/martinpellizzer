Elymus caput-medusae

Elymus caput-medusae, commonly known as the "Medusa's Head" grass, is a tall perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and large, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a crown of spikes. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense clusters along the stems. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Elymus caput-medusae is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
