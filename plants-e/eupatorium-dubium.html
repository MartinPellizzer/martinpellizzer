Eupatorium dubium

Eupatorium dubium, commonly known as swamp boneset, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features tall, slender stems that are often covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
