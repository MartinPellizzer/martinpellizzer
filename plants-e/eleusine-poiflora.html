Eleusine poiflora

Eleusine poiflora, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and vibrant purple to pink flower spikes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers.

Eleusine poiflora is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
