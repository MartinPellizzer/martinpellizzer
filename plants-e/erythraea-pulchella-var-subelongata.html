Erythraea pulchella var. subelongata

Erythraea pulchella var. subelongata, commonly known as the long-stalked erythraea, is a variant of the erythraea plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated stems and distinctive, often showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silky hair.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and ecological adaptations.
