Euonymus europaeus subsp. latifolius

Euonymus europaeus subsp.

latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features opposite, oval to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that split open to reveal seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a versatile ornamental plant.

Its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for garden use.
