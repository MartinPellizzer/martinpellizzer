Ericoides recurvatum

Ericoides recurvatum, commonly known as the curly-stemmed ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its unique recurved stem structure and dense, leathery leaves are adaptations to its harsh, high-altitude habitat.

Ericoides recurvatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
