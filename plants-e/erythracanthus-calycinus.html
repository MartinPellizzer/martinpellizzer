Erythracanthus calycinus

Erythracanthus calycinus, commonly known as the red-capsulated sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Erythracanthus calycinus is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
