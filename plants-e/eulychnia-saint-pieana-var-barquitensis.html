Eulychnia saint-pieana var. barquitensis

Eulychnia saint-pieana var. barquitensis, commonly known as the Barquitensian candelabra cactus, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching structure and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and resilience.
