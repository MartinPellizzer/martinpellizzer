Erythraea angustifolia var. tenuiflora

Erythraea angustifolia var. tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved erythraea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
