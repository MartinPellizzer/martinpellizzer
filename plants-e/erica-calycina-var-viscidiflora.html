Erica calycina var. viscidiflora

Erica calycina var. viscidiflora, commonly known as the sticky-flowered heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. The leaves are narrow, leathery, and dark green, often covered in fine, sticky hairs. This variety is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.
