Epithecia trulla

Epithecia trulla, commonly known as the orange cup lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It features a distinctive orange to yellowish thallus with a smooth, crust-like surface. The plant's reproductive structures, called apothecia, are small and typically reddish-brown in color. It is often found on tree bark, stones, and soil in temperate regions.

Its chemical composition includes various secondary metabolites that contribute to its ecological role in nutrient cycling.
