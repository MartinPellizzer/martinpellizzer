Erysimum sikkimense

Erysimum sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its bright yellow or orange flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum sikkimense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
