Eriodendron guineense

Eriodendron guineense, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legumes as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
