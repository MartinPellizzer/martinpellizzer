Eugenia valerii

Eugenia valerii, commonly known as the velvetwood or white eugenia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown to black bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, dark purple to black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
