Eupatorium cordatum var. fraseri

Eupatorium cordatum var. fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a rough texture and distinctive, opposite arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant gardens.
