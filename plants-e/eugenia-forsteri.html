Eugenia forsteri

Eugenia forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which is a small, dark purple drupe, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
