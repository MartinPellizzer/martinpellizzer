Echinocactus polyraphis

Echinocactus polyraphis, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Echinocactus polyraphis is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.
