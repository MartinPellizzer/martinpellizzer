Encyclia wageneri

Encyclia wageneri, commonly known as the Wagener's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, narrow leaves and intricate flower structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Encyclia wageneri is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
