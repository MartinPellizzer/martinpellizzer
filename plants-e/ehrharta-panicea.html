Ehrharta panicea

Ehrharta panicea, commonly known as common lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Ehrharta panicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its hardy nature.
