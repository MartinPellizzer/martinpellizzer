Erucaria myagroides

Erucaria myagroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and has linear, hairless leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Erucaria myagroides is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is an important component of local vegetation in its native habitat.
