Erythrodes

Erythrodes, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their vibrant, often red or orange-colored flowers, which are typically spurred at the base. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. Erythrodes species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic, and they rely on humid environments for survival.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structure and ecological role in their native habitats.
