Echinops elymaiticus

Echinops elymaiticus, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Turkey. The plant is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown, which are composed of tightly packed bracts. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters.

Echinops elymaiticus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
