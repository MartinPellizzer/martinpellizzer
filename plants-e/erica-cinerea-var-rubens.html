Erica cinerea var. rubens

Erica cinerea var. rubens, commonly known as the red heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and coastal environments.
