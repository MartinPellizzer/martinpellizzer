Epidendrum talamancanum

Epidendrum talamancanum, commonly known as the Talamancan Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
