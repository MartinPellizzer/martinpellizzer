Echinodorus pubescens

Echinodorus pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf waterweed, is a popular aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively through runners.

Echinodorus pubescens is valued in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
