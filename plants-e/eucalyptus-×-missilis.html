Eucalyptus × missilis

Eucalyptus × missilis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for timber and fuel purposes.

Eucalyptus × missilis is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
