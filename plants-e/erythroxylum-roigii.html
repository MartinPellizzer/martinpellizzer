Erythroxylum roigii

Erythroxylum roigii, commonly known as the Roig's copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are typically elliptical, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

Erythroxylum roigii is also known for producing a resin that has been used traditionally for various purposes, including medicinal applications.
