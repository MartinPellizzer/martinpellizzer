Erycibe sapotacea

Erycibe sapotacea, commonly known as the red sapote, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces edible fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp that contains a single large seed. Its botanical features include smooth, grayish bark and a tendency to grow in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
