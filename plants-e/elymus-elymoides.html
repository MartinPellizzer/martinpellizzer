Elymus elymoides

Elymus elymoides, commonly known as blue wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian areas. Its flowering spikes are slender and spike-like, bearing small, spikelet-based flowers.

Elymus elymoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
