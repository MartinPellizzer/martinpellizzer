Eriocaulon aciphyllum

Eriocaulon aciphyllum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescences that bear tiny, spike-like clusters of flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and marshes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant's unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important component of its native habitats.
