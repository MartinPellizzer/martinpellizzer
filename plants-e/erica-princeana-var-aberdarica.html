Erica princeana var. aberdarica

Erica princeana var. aberdarica, commonly known as the Aberdare heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Aberdare Range in Kenya.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink during the rainy season. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
