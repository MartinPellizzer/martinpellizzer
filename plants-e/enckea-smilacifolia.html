Enckea smilacifolia

Enckea smilacifolia, commonly known as the creeping smilacaceous vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture, adapting to humid and shaded environments.

Enckea smilacifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
