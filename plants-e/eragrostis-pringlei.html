Eragrostis pringlei

Eragrostis pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Eragrostis pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
