Epipactis rubra lusus parviflora

Epipactis rubra lusus parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered helleborine, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit.

It features dark green, pleated leaves and produces pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often found in clusters along the stem. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.
