Eugenia hazompasika

Eugenia hazompasika, commonly known as the Madagascar guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native habitat.
