Eriogonum microtheca var. simpsonii

Eriogonum microtheca var. simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

Simpson's buckwheat is a perennial species that plays an important role in supporting local pollinator populations.
