Eupatorium phoenicolepis var. guatemalensis

Eupatorium phoenicolepis var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan boneset, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
