Eupatorium bupleurifolium var. microcephala

Eupatorium bupleurifolium var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct white midrib. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale blue, appearing in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
