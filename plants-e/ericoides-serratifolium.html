Ericoides serratifolium

Ericoides serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated leaf heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes evergreens and shrubs adapted to acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves with serrated edges, and it produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
