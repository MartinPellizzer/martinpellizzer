Euphrasia nemorosa subsp. alpina

Euphrasia nemorosa subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the Alpine eyebright, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive yellow markings, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance. Euphrasia nemorosa subsp.

alpina is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
