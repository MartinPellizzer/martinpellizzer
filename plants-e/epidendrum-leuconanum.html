Epidendrum leuconanum

Epidendrum leuconanum, commonly known as the white-veined epidendrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks. The plant features long, arching stems with numerous lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, white pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and a distinctive white lip, making it a popular ornamental species.

Epidendrum leuconanum thrives in humid, well-lit environments and requires good air circulation for optimal growth.
