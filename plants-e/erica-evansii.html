Erica evansii

Erica evansii, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant is well adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy coastal species. Erica evansii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
