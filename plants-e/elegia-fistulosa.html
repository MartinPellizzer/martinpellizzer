Elegia fistulosa

Elegia fistulosa, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is slender and often reddish in color.

Elegia fistulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
