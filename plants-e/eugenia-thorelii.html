Eugenia thorelii

Eugenia thorelii, commonly known as the Kew's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, dark purple drupe, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia thorelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
