Euonymus streptopterus

Euonymus streptopterus, commonly known as the red-veined or red-vein euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring deep green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Euonymus streptopterus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
