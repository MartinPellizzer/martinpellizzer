Eupatorium makinoi

Eupatorium makinoi, commonly known as Japanese boneset, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium makinoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
