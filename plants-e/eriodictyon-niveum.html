Eriodictyon niveum

Eriodictyon niveum, commonly known as white yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its white to pale blue flowers and gray-green foliage. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are aromatic and have been historically used by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.

Eriodictyon niveum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native environment.
