Elaphoglossum prainii

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, serrated segments. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, which are often covered with a layer of indusia.

Elaphoglossum prainii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
