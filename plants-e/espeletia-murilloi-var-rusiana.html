Espeletia murilloi var. rusiana

Espeletia murilloi var. rusiana, commonly known as the Rusian heather, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
