Eupatorium stipuliferum

Eupatorium stipuliferum, commonly known as the slender thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, and it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Eupatorium stipuliferum thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.
