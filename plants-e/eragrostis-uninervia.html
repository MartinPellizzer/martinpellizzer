Eragrostis uninervia

Eragrostis uninervia, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its resistance to drought.

Eragrostis uninervia is often used in landscaping and as a forage plant for livestock.
