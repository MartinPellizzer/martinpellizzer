Eremochloa helferi

Eremochloa helferi, commonly known as the desert grass or Helfer's grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Eremochloa helferi is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low precipitation and high temperatures.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
