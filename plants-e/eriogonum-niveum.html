Eriogonum niveum

Eriogonum niveum, commonly known as the white curlcup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white to pale green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eriogonum niveum is often found in open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
