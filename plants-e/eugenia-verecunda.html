Eugenia verecunda

Eugenia verecunda, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a bright red rind and a white, segmented flesh that is juicy and aromatic. The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
