Erica mundula

Erica mundula, commonly known as the small heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. Erica mundula produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
