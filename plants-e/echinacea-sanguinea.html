Echinacea sanguinea

Echinacea sanguinea, commonly known as the pink coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a sturdy stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped. Its flower heads consist of a central yellow disk surrounded by pinkish-red ray florets.

Echinacea sanguinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential immune-boosting properties.
