Eugenia congesta

Eugenia congesta, commonly known as the clustered eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, dark purple to black berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
