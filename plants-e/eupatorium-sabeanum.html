Eupatorium sabeanum

Eupatorium sabeanum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Eupatorium sabeanum is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
