Eugenia vicozensis var. sericea

Eugenia vicozensis var. sericea, commonly known as the silky eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

This plant is often grown for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
