Eugenia acapulcensis

Eugenia acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
