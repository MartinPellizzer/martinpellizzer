Eugenia fulvipes

Eugenia fulvipes, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
