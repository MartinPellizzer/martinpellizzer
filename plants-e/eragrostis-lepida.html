Eragrostis lepida

Eragrostis lepida, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale-green to brownish flowers.

Eragrostis lepida is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
