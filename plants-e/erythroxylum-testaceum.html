Erythroxylum testaceum

Erythroxylum testaceum, commonly known as the red coca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing fine, white seeds. It is notable for its alkaloid content, particularly cocaine, which has significant pharmacological and historical importance.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is also classified as a controlled substance in many countries.
