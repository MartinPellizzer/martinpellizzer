Eria junghuhnii

Eria junghuhnii, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Eria, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
