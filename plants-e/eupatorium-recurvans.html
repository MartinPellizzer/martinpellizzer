Eupatorium recurvans

Eupatorium recurvans, commonly known as slender boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in cylindrical heads, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium recurvans is often found in moist soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
