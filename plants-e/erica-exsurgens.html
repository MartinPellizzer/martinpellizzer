Erica exsurgens

Erica exsurgens, commonly known as the upright heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. Erica exsurgens thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a landscape plant due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
