Euphorbia tranzschelii

Euphorbia tranzschelii, commonly known as the Transkei spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
