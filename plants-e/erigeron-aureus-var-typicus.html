Erigeron aureus var. typicus

Erigeron aureus var. typicus, commonly known as the golden fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive silvery-green color.

The flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, making it a valuable plant for pollinators.
