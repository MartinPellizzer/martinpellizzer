Erica cordata

Erica cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica cordata is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes other heath and heather plants.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
