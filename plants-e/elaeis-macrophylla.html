Elaeis macrophylla

Elaeis macrophylla, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to western and central Africa and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and tall, straight trunk. The plant produces fruits that contain a high amount of oil, making it economically significant for palm oil production. It thrives in tropical climates with high rainfall and is often cultivated in monoculture plantations.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
