Enipea

Enipea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a reddish hue, which is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Enipea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique wood properties and ecological role in its native habitats.
