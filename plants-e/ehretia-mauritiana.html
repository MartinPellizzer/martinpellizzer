Ehretia mauritiana

Ehretia mauritiana, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense canopy of lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
