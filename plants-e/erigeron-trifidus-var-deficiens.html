Erigeron trifidus var. deficiens

Erigeron trifidus var. deficiens, commonly known as the sparseleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive trifid (three-lobed) appearance, contributing to its common name.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
