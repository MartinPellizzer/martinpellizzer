Eurya japonica f. microphylla

Eurya japonica f. microphylla, commonly known as the Japanese holly or dwarf Japanese holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. It is valued for its dense foliage and ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is particularly suitable for use as a hedge or in container gardening due to its manageable size and hardy nature.
