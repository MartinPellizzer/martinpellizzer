Erythrodes laticalcar

Erythrodes laticalcar, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins and its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. It requires well-drained soil and consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
