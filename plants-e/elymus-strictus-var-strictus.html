Elymus strictus var. strictus

Elymus strictus var. strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant.
