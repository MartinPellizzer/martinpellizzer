Eragrostis phyllacantha

Eragrostis phyllacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in degraded areas.
