Epidendrum restrepoanum

Epidendrum restrepoanum, commonly known as the Restrepo's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are borne on branching inflorescences and bloom during the rainy season.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
