Eucalyptus calcicultrix var. porosa

Eucalyptus calcicultrix var. porosa, commonly known as the porous eucalyptus, is a variant of the calcicultrix species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often appears flaky or peeling. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a grayish-green color and a rough texture. This variety thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

It is also known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
