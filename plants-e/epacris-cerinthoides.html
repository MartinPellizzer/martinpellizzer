Epacris cerinthoides

Epacris cerinthoides, commonly known as the pink epacris, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its striking pink or red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Epacris cerinthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
