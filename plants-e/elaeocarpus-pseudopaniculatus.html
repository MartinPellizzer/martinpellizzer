Elaeocarpus pseudopaniculatus

Elaeocarpus pseudopaniculatus, commonly known as the false panicle elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree belonging to the Elaeocarpaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia, and thrives in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a drupe, often containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy outer layer.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
