Embothrium wickhamii

Embothrium wickhamii, commonly known as Wickham's embothrium, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically Chile.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, and it produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Embothrium wickhamii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
