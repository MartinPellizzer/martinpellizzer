Erigeron gilensis

Erigeron gilensis, commonly known as Gil's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Erigeron gilensis is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
