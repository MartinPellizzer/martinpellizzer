Ericoides nitens

Ericoides nitens, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the cool, moist regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, dark-colored berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Ericoides nitens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in difficult environments.
