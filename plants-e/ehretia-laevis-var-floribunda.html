Ehretia laevis var. floribunda

Ehretia laevis var. floribunda, commonly known as the silky tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
