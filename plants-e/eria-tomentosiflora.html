Eria tomentosiflora

Eria tomentosiflora, commonly known as the woolly-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of multiple small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and wasps.

The orchid's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
