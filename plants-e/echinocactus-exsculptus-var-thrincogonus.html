Echinocactus exsculptus var. thrincogonus

Echinocactus exsculptus var. thrincogonus, commonly known as the Mexican golden barrel cactus, is a striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of bright yellow spines, which give it its common name. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem that can grow up to several feet in height, adapted to store water in its fleshy tissue. It produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in desert gardens and as a houseplant.
