Eleocharis platypus

Eleocharis platypus, commonly known as the broad-leaved spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, white or greenish flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and contribute to wetland ecosystem health.

Eleocharis platypus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
