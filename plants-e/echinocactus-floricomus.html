Echinocactus floricomus

Echinocactus floricomus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and distinctive yellow or orange flowers that resemble a Mexican hat. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that help it retain water in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Echinocactus floricomus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
