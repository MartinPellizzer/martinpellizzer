Epithecia coriacea

Epithecia coriacea, commonly known as the rough-skin lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Pertusariaceae.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery thallus that gives it a coriaceous texture, hence its scientific name. The lichen typically grows on bark, stone, or soil in temperate and subtropical regions. It is a secondary symbiont, formed by a mutualistic relationship between a green alga and a fungus.

Its morphology and habitat make it an important indicator species for environmental conditions.
