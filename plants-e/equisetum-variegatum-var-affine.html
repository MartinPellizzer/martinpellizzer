Equisetum variegatum var. affine

Equisetum variegatum var. affine, commonly known as the variable horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, segmented stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of the stems, which are distinct from the leaves. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its variability in color and form, making it a distinctive species within the horsetail genus.
