Eranthemum ciliatum

Eranthemum ciliatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
