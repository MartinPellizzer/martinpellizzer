Eupatorium mysorense

Eupatorium mysorense, commonly known as Indian fever vine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
