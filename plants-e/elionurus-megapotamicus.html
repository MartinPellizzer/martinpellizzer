Elionurus megapotamicus

Elionurus megapotamicus, commonly known as the South American thistle, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
