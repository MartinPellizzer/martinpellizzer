Ehretia cirrhosa

Ehretia cirrhosa, commonly known as the silky ehretia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
