Earina suaveolens

Earina suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in humid environments. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, while its flowers display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Earina suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
