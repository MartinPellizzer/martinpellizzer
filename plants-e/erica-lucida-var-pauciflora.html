Erica lucida var. pauciflora

Erica lucida var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in coastal areas.
