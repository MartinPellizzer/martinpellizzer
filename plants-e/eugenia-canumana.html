Eugenia canumana

Eugenia canumana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia canumana*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia canumana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
