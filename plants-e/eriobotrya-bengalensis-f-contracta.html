Eriobotrya bengalensis f. contracta

Eriobotrya bengalensis f. contracta, commonly known as the dwarf loquat, is a compact variant of the loquat plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smaller, rounded leaves and yellowish-orange fruit that ripen in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant.
