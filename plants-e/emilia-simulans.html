Emilia simulans

Emilia simulans, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems and small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. Emilia simulans thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
