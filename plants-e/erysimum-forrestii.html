Erysimum forrestii

Erysimum forrestii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill wallflower, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Nevada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers with four petals, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy.

Erysimum forrestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
