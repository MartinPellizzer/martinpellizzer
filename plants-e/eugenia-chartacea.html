Eugenia chartacea

Eugenia chartacea, commonly known as the wax myrtle or Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by dark purple to black berries. Eugenia chartacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in herbal remedies for various ailments.

Its adaptability to different soil types and climates makes it a common ornamental and invasive species in some regions.
