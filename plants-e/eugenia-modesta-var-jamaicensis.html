Eugenia modesta var. jamaicensis

Eugenia modesta var. jamaicensis, commonly known as Jamaican guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
