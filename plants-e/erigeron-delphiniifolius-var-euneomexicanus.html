Erigeron delphiniifolius var. euneomexicanus

Erigeron delphiniifolius var. euneomexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in arid landscapes.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
