Eugenia sooiana

Eugenia sooiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. Eugenia sooiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
