Erythroxylum retusum var. latifolium

Erythroxylum retusum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf copal tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its latex, which has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
