Epacris acuminata

Epacris acuminata, commonly known as the spike heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

Epacris acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
