Epilasia mirabilis

Epilasia mirabilis, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky inflorescence that resembles a cluster of tiny candles. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Epilasia mirabilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its distinctive appearance.
