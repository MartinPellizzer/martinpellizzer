Erigeron acris subsp. arctophilus

Erigeron acris subsp.

arctophilus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed areas.

The plant features a basal rosette of blue-gray leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
