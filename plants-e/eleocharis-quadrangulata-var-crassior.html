Eleocharis quadrangulata var. crassior

Eleocharis quadrangulata var. crassior, commonly known as the broad-leaved spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, triangular stems and broad, flat leaves that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, often found in wetland or marshy environments. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its structure and growth habit make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
