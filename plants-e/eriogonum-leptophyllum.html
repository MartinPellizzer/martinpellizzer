Eriogonum leptophyllum

Eriogonum leptophyllum, commonly known as desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in desert ecosystems.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
