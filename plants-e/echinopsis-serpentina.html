Echinopsis serpentina

Echinopsis serpentina, commonly known as the serpent's tail cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and succulent collectors.
