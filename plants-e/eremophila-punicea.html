Eremophila punicea

Eremophila punicea, commonly known as the redleaf eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremophila punicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
