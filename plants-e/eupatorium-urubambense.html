Eupatorium urubambense

Eupatorium urubambense, commonly known as the Urubamba eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
