Elaeocarpus osiae

Elaeocarpus osiae, commonly known as the Osie's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within lowland forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
