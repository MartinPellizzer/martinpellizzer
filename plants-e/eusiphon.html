Eusiphon

Eusiphon, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. Eusiphon species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

They also play a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
