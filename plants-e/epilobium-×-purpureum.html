Epilobium × purpureum

Epilobium × purpureum, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Epilobium × purpureum is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in waste areas, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
