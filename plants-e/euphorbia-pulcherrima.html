Euphorbia pulcherrima

Euphorbia pulcherrima, commonly known as poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant's true flowers are small and inconspicuous, hidden within the colorful structures. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant during the holiday season.

Euphorbia pulcherrima is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
