Eucalyptus tereticornis subsp. tereticornis

Eucalyptus tereticornis subsp.

tereticornis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallet, is a tall, slender tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, grey-green leaves and smooth, peeling bark.

The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and has been widely planted for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
