Erigeron farreri

Erigeron farreri, commonly known as the Sierra Valley blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and it thrives in regions with moderate to high elevations. Its blue to lavender flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
