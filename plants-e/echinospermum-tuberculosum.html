Echinospermum tuberculosum

Echinospermum tuberculosum, commonly known as the rough dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs.

Echinospermum tuberculosum is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
