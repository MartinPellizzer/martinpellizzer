Eugenia pastelillensis

Eugenia pastelillensis, commonly known as the pastelilla tree, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Following flowering, it produces small, round berries that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
