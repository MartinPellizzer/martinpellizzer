Eucalyptus conferruminata

Eucalyptus conferruminata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which are typically followed by woody capsules containing small seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

This species plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
