Eugenioides nudum

Eugenioides nudum, commonly known as the naked eugenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
