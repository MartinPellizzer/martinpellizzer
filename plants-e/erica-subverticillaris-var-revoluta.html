Erica subverticillaris var. revoluta

Erica subverticillaris var. revoluta, commonly known as the revolute heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.
