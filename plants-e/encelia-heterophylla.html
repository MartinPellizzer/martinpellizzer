Encelia heterophylla

Encelia heterophylla, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves vary in shape, being linear or lobed, depending on the environmental conditions.

Encelia heterophylla is a perennial shrub that plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
