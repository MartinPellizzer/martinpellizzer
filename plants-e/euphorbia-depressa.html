Euphorbia depressa

Euphorbia depressa, commonly known as the prostrate spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers clustered in structures called cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Euphorbia depressa is a hardy plant that is relatively drought-tolerant and can be grown in gardens as an ornamental species.
