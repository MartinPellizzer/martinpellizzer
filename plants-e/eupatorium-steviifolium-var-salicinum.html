Eupatorium steviifolium var. salicinum

Eupatorium steviifolium var. salicinum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pale blue to lavender flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
