Ehretia formosa var. oaxacana

Ehretia formosa var. oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico, particularly in the state of Oaxaca.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking, violet-blue berries that are highly ornamental and attract birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.
