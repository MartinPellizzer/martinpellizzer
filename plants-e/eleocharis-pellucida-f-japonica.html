Eleocharis pellucida f. japonica

Eleocharis pellucida f. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Its leaves are generally narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces tiny, pale green to brown flowers.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
