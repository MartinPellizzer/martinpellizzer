Euphorbia adinophylla

Euphorbia adinophylla, commonly known as the white-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, a common feature among the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
