Eugenia greggii

Eugenia greggii, commonly known as Gregg's huckleberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, edible berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia greggii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
