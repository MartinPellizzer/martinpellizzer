Epidendrum leonorae

Epidendrum leonorae, commonly known as the Leonora's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Epidendrum leonorae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
