Escallonia grahamiana

Escallonia grahamiana, commonly known as the Graham's escallonia, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Escallonia grahamiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
