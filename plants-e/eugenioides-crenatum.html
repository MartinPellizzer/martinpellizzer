Eugenioides crenatum

Eugenioides crenatum, commonly known as the rough-leaved eugenia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its serrated, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are rich in flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
