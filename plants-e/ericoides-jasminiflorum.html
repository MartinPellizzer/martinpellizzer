Ericoides jasminiflorum

Ericoides jasminiflorum, commonly known as the jasmine broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Ericoides jasminiflorum is often found in rocky or forested areas, particularly in the mountainous regions of its native range.
