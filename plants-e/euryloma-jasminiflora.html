Euryloma jasminiflora

Euryloma jasminiflora, commonly known as the jasmine-flowered euryloma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that resemble jasmine, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.
