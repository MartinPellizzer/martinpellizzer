Erica aestiva

Erica aestiva, commonly known as the common heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs and evergreens. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils. Erica aestiva produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring.

It is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, where it plays a vital role in local ecosystems.
