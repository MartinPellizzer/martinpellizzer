Echeveria diaboli

Echeveria diaboli, commonly known as the Devil's Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces showy, violet-pink flowers on tall, branching stalks during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
