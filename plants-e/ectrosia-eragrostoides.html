Ectrosia eragrostoides

Ectrosia eragrostoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant communities.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration projects.
