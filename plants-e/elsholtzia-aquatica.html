Elsholtzia aquatica

Elsholtzia aquatica, commonly known as water elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to moist habitats in China.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant thrives in wet or semi-aquatic environments, often found near streams or in marshy areas. Its botanical characteristics include a creeping rhizomatous root system and hairy stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
