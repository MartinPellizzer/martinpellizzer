Eucalyptus tereticornis var. cineolifera

Eucalyptus tereticornis var. cineolifera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the narrow-leafed gum.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is native to southeastern Australia and thrives in dry, sandy soils. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This variety is valued for its aromatic oils and is often used in traditional and commercial applications.
