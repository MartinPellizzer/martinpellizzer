Evolvulus alsinoides var. acapulcensis

Evolvulus alsinoides var. acapulcensis, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
