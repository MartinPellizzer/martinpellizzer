Elegia racemosa

Elegia racemosa, commonly known as the marsh heliotrope, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas of North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or pond margins.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
