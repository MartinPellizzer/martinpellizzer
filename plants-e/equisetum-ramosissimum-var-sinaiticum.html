Equisetum ramosissimum var. sinaiticum

Equisetum ramosissimum var. sinaiticum, commonly known as the Sinai horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, which are typically found in moist, calcareous soils. It is native to regions such as the Sinai Peninsula and parts of the Middle East.

This variety is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period.
