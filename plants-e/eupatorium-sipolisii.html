Eupatorium sipolisii

Eupatorium sipolisii, commonly known as the Sipolisi boneset, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments, typically near water bodies.

Eupatorium sipolisii is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
