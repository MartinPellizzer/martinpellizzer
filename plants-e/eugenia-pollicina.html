Eugenia pollicina

Eugenia pollicina, commonly known as the little apple tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia pollicina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
