Entada flexuosa

Entada flexuosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender pods that contain flat, seed-rich seeds. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

The wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
