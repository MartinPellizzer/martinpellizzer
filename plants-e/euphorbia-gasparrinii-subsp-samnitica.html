Euphorbia gasparrinii subsp. samnitica

Euphorbia gasparrinii subsp.

samnitica, commonly known as the Southern Italian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Italy. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
