Eugenia biflora

Eugenia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Eugenia biflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
