Epilobium amplectens

Epilobium amplectens, commonly known as the sticky willow or woolly willow, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive sticky texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually pink to reddish in color.

Epilobium amplectens plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
