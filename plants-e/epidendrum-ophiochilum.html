Epidendrum ophiochilum

Epidendrum ophiochilum, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers with dark red or purple markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and somewhat fleshy, adapted to store water.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting various pollinators.
