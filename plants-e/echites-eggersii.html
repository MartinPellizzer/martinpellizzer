Echites eggersii

Echites eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' cactus, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water for survival in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warm season, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its adaptations, including reduced leaves and thickened stems, are typical of xerophytic plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
