Euphorbia × cornubiensis

Euphorbia × cornubiensis, commonly known as the Cornish spurge, is a hybrid plant native to the south-west of England.

It is a low-growing, succulent perennial that typically forms dense mats of fleshy, green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and limestone areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
