Erica leptoclada

Erica leptoclada, commonly known as the slender heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen structure with dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves. It produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring.

Erica leptoclada thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
