Ericameria × uintahensis

Ericameria × uintahensis, commonly known as Uinta hybrid sage, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish to golden-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Ericameria × uintahensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
