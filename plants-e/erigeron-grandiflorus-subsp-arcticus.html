Erigeron grandiflorus subsp. arcticus

Erigeron grandiflorus subsp.

arcticus, commonly known as Arctic blueweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions of North America. It typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silvery-gray leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a blue-gray center. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to harsh climatic conditions, showcasing resilience to cold and strong winds.
