Enneapogon abyssinicus

Enneapogon abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian nine-awn grass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence with nine awns on each lemma. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like flowers.

This species is of interest for its ecological role and potential agricultural value in dryland areas.
