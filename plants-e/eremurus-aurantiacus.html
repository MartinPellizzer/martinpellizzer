Eremurus aurantiacus

Eremurus aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden torch or red torch, is a striking member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch, with bright orange to red flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Eremurus aurantiacus is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic appearance and resilience to harsh climates.
