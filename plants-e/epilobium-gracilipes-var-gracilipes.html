Epilobium gracilipes var. gracilipes

Epilobium gracilipes var. gracilipes, commonly known as slender willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in spikes, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family, characterized by its showy, often bilaterally symmetrical flowers.
