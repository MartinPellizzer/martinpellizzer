Eriocaulon selaginoides

Eriocaulon selaginoides, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, silver-veined leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
