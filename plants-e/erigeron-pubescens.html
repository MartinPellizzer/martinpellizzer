Erigeron pubescens

Erigeron pubescens, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Erigeron pubescens is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
