Entada rotundifolia

Entada rotundifolia, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, greyish-brown trunk and its distinctive, spurred calyx on its fruit. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain hard, angular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has cultural significance in various regions.
