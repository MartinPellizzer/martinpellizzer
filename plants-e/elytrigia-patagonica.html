Elytrigia patagonica

Elytrigia patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian needlegrass, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Elytrigia patagonica is adapted to grow in dry, often rocky or sandy soils, making it a common component of steppe and shrubland ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
