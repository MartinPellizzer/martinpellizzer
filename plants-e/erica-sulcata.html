Erica sulcata

Erica sulcata, commonly known as the serrated heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica sulcata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
