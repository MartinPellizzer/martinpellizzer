Eucalyptus acmenoides

Eucalyptus acmenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malletwood, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and typically occur in clusters, producing a pale green or yellowish hue.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable timber and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
