Eugenia rigidifolia

Eugenia rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, leathery leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
