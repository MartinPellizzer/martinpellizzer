Elaeocarpus bonii

Elaeocarpus bonii, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree's fruit is a woody, spherical capsule that turns orange-red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
