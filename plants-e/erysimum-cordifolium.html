Erysimum cordifolium

Erysimum cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with four-petaled flowers.

Erysimum cordifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
