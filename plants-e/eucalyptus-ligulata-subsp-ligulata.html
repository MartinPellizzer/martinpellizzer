Eucalyptus ligulata subsp. ligulata

Eucalyptus ligulata subsp.

ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, grey-green foliage and small, rounded leaves.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands and heathlands, often in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or cream in color.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
