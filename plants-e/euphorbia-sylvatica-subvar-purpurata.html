Euphorbia sylvatica subvar. purpurata

Euphorbia sylvatica subvar. purpurata, commonly known as the purple-stemmed spurge, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can be invasive in some regions.
