Ephedra alata var. decaisnei

Ephedra alata var. decaisnei, commonly known as the narrowleaf ephedra, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its jointed, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low precipitation and high temperatures.

Its stems contain alkaloids, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.
