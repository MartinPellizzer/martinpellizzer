Eugenia crassicaulis

Eugenia crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a deep green color. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Eugenia crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and fruit.
