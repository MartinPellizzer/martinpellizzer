Erinus lanceolatus

Erinus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved erinus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fluffy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Erinus lanceolatus is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
