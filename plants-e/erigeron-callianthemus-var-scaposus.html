Erigeron callianthemus var. scaposus

Erigeron callianthemus var. scaposus, commonly known as the slenderleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and stems that are often hairy. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
