Epacris vesta

Epacris vesta, commonly known as the pink epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and are borne in inflorescences at the tips of the stems. This species is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Epacris vesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
