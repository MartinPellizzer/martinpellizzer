Eulophia hebdingiana

Eulophia hebdingiana, commonly known as the Hebding's eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stem and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia hebdingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
