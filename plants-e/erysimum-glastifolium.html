Erysimum glastifolium

Erysimum glastifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, and they bloom in early spring.

Erysimum glastifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a wildflower in suitable climates.
