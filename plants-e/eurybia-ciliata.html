Eurybia ciliata

Eurybia ciliata, commonly known as the sandy pink or coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in coastal dunes and maritime environments. Its leaves are generally hairy and have a distinctive ciliate margin, giving it its scientific name.

Eurybia ciliata is valued for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
