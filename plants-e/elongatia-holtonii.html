Elongatia holtonii

Elongatia holtonii, commonly known as the Holton's elongated fern, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are often adorned with delicate, spore-producing structures called sori.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
