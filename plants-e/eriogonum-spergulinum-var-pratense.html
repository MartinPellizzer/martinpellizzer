Eriogonum spergulinum var. pratense

Eriogonum spergulinum var. pratense, commonly known as the plains buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The species is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
