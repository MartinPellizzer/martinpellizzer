Evax multicaulis var. drummondii

Evax multicaulis var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowering heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
