Erica hispidula

Erica hispidula, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage covered in short, stiff hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring.

Erica hispidula thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
