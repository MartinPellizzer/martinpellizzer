Elytrigia intermedia subsp. barbulata

Elytrigia intermedia subsp.

barbulata, commonly known as the rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
