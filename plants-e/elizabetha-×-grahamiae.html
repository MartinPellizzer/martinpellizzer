Elizabetha × grahamiae

Elizabetha × grahamiae, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a cross between Hibiscus elizabethae and Hibiscus grahamiae.

It is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing vibrant red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of large, waxy leaves that provide a striking contrast to the colorful blooms.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
