Eriogonum ciliatum var. foliosum

Eriogonum ciliatum var. foliosum, commonly known as the leafy curlleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
