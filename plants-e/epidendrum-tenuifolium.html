Epidendrum tenuifolium

Epidendrum tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of care.
