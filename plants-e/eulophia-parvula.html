Eulophia parvula

Eulophia parvula, commonly known as the dwarf eulophia, is a small orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

Eulophia parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in cultivation.
