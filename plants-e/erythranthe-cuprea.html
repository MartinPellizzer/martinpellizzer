Erythranthe cuprea

Erythranthe cuprea, commonly known as the copperweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Erythranthe cuprea is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
