Elaeocarpus macropus

Elaeocarpus macropus, commonly known as the large-fruited elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. The fruits are typically orange to red in color and contain a single seed, making them an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
