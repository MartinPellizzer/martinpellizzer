Echinocactus cinereus

Echinocactus cinereus, commonly known as the silver barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the stem. It has a deep root system that allows it to store water and survive in dry conditions.

Echinocactus cinereus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
