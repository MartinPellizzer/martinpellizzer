Euphorbia indistincta

Euphorbia indistincta, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant has a unique ability to produce a milky sap, which is a common characteristic among the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
