Eria maingayi

Eria maingayi, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stem. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Eria maingayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens.
