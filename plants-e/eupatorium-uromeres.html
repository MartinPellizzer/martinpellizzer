Eupatorium uromeres

Eupatorium uromeres, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium uromeres is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
