Echinocactus hamatacanthus

Echinocactus hamatacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense, spiny areoles that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble apples, which are edible and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This resilient plant is well adapted to desert conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental species.
