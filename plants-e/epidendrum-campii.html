Epidendrum campii

Epidendrum campii, commonly known as the Camp's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. Its pseudobulbs are often covered in a velvety coating, and the leaves are usually thick and fleshy.

Epidendrum campii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
