Ervum erectum

Ervum erectum, commonly known as the upright vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters. Ervum erectum is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.
