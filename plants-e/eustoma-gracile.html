Eustoma gracile

Eustoma gracile, commonly known as the small-flowered blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Eustoma gracile is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
