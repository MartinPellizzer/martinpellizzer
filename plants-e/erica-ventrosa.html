Erica ventrosa

Erica ventrosa, commonly known as the Heath or Heath Moor plant, is a species of flowering shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils typical of fynbos vegetation. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica ventrosa is characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage, which remains dense and leathery throughout the year.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
