Elaeocarpus ngii subsp. curtisii

Elaeocarpus ngii subsp.

curtisii, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarp, is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers.

The fruit is a woody drupe, typically containing a single seed, and is an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical rainforests and is often found in lowland to hill forest habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
