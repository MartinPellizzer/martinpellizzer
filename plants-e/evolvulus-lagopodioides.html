Evolvulus lagopodioides

Evolvulus lagopodioides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Evolvulus lagopodioides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
