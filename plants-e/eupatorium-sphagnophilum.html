Eupatorium sphagnophilum

Eupatorium sphagnophilum, commonly known as swamp boneset, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in bogs and swamps. The plant features erect, hairy stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

It produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
