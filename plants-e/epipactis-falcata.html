Epipactis falcata

Epipactis falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a tall flowering stem that bears clusters of greenish-yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Epipactis falcata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its flowers are pollinated by various insects, including bees and wasps, contributing to its ecological significance.
