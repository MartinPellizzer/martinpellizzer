Eucalyptus baileyana

Eucalyptus baileyana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallet, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It is a hardy, fast-growing tree that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Eucalyptus baileyana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
