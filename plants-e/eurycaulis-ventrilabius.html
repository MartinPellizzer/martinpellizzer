Eurycaulis ventrilabius

Eurycaulis ventrilabius, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Eurycaulis ventrilabius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
