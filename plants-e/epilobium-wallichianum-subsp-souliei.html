Epilobium wallichianum subsp. souliei

Epilobium wallichianum subsp.

souliei, commonly known as the Souchong willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, mountainous regions.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. Its botanical characteristics include showy, erect flowering stems and a preference for cool, temperate climates.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
