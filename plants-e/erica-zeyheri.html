Erica zeyheri

Erica zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. Erica zeyheri thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
