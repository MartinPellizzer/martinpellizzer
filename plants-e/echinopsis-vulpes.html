Echinopsis vulpes

Echinopsis vulpes, commonly known as the fennel cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and white, fuzzy spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract various pollinators. Echinopsis vulpes is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique form and resilience.
