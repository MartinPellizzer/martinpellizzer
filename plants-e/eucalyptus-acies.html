Eucalyptus acies

Eucalyptus acies, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in groups. Eucalyptus acies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and heat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems in which it is found.
