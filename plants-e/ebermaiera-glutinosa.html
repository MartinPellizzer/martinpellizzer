Ebermaiera glutinosa

Ebermaiera glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky ebermaiera, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that produce a sticky resin, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in green or reddish hues.

The resin produced by Ebermaiera glutinosa has been used traditionally by indigenous communities for various purposes, including medicinal applications.
