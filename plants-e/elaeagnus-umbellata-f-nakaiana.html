Elaeagnus umbellata f. nakaiana

Elaeagnus umbellata f. nakaiana, commonly known as the Nakaian silverberry, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and small, edible berries that turn red when mature. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are often clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various light conditions.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
