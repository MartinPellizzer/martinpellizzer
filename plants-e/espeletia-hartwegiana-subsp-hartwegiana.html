Espeletia hartwegiana subsp. hartwegiana

Espeletia hartwegiana subsp.

hartwegiana, commonly known as the Hartweg's espeletia, is a perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its rosette formation of thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to horticulturists and botanists.
