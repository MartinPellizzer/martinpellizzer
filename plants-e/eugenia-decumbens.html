Eugenia decumbens

Eugenia decumbens, commonly known as the Surinam cherry or yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Eugenia decumbens thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
