Eupatorium puberulum

Eupatorium puberulum, commonly known as rough eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has hairy, rough-textured leaves that give it its common name. Its showy, purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Eupatorium puberulum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
