Elaeagnus multiflora var. ovoidea

Elaeagnus multiflora var. ovoidea, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The variety ovoidea is distinguished by its egg-shaped fruits and compact growth habit.
