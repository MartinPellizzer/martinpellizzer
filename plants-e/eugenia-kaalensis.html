Eugenia kaalensis

Eugenia kaalensis, commonly known as the Kgalagadi eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible berries. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Eugenia kaalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
