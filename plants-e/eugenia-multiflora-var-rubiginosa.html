Eugenia multiflora var. rubiginosa

Eugenia multiflora var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-veined guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits similar to guavas. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
