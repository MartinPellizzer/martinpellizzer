Epidorkis fragrans

Epidorkis fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shady, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, emit a subtle, sweet aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
