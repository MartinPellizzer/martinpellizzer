Eugenioides macrocarpum

Eugenioides macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited eugenia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
