Elaeocarpus terminalioides

Elaeocarpus terminalioides, commonly known as the white-fruit elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, fleshy seeds, which are dispersed by animals. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.
