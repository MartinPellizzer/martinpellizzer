Eupatorium polystachyum

Eupatorium polystachyum, commonly known as the Chinese boneset, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its genus, Eupatorium, is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

Eupatorium polystachyum is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
