Entada polystachya

Entada polystachya, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods containing smooth, hard seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
