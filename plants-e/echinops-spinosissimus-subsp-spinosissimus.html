Echinops spinosissimus subsp. spinosissimus

Echinops spinosissimus subsp.

spinosissimus, commonly known as the spiny globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a spiky globe, which are a key feature of the genus Echinops.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and ecological restoration projects.
