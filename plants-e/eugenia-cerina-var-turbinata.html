Eugenia cerina var. turbinata

Eugenia cerina var. turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated cherry, is a variant of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, turbinated or turbinate-shaped fruits, which are typically smaller and more elongated than those of the standard variety. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit shape, making it a popular choice in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
