Euphorbia verticillata

Euphorbia verticillata, commonly known as the whorled spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested, and it has yellowish-green leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Euphorbia verticillata is a perennial herb that is relatively easy to grow in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 10.
