Enchylaena tomentosa var. leptophylla

Enchylaena tomentosa var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamarind, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
