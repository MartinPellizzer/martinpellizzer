Epidendrum schreineri

Epidendrum schreineri, commonly known as the Schreiner's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Epidendrum schreineri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
