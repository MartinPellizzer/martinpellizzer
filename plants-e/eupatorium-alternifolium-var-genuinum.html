Eupatorium alternifolium var. genuinum

Eupatorium alternifolium var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded habitats, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
