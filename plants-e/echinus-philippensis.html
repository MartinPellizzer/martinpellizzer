Echinus philippensis

Echinus philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped heads of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branching.

Echinus philippensis plays a role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small marine organisms.
