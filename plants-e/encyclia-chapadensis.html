Encyclia chapadensis

Encyclia chapadensis, commonly known as the Chapadense orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red spots. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
