Elaeagnus songarica

Elaeagnus songarica, commonly known as the Siberian silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils and tolerating cold climates. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Elaeagnus songarica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
