Eriocaulon leptophyllum

Eriocaulon leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescences that bear tiny, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eriocaulon leptophyllum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
