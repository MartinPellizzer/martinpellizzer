Eragrostis aethiopica

Eragrostis aethiopica, commonly known as Ethiopian lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, which helps it conserve water in arid environments.

Eragrostis aethiopica is often used for forage and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.
