Eulophia lagaligo

Eulophia lagaligo, commonly known as the Malabar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Eulophia lagaligo thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collections.
