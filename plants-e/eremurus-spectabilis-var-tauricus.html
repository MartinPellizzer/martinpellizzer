Eremurus spectabilis var. tauricus

Eremurus spectabilis var. tauricus, commonly known as the Tauric grassleek, is a striking perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental plant in botanical gardens and collections.
