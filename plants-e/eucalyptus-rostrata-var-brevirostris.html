Eucalyptus rostrata var. brevirostris

Eucalyptus rostrata var. brevirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beaked mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and distinctive, narrow beak-like buds. This plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its bark is often flaky and varies in color, while its flowers are typically white or pale blue.

The species is part of the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
