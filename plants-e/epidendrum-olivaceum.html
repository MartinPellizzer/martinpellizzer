Epidendrum olivaceum

Epidendrum olivaceum, commonly known as the Olive Epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically elliptical and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

Epidendrum olivaceum is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
