Escallonia sanguinea

Escallonia sanguinea, commonly known as the red-flowered escallonia, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant produces small, black, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal display of flowers and berries.
