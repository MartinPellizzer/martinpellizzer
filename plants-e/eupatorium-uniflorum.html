Eupatorium uniflorum

Eupatorium uniflorum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Eupatorium uniflorum is often used in native plant gardens for its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
