Eryngium amethystinum

Eryngium amethystinum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that are often tinged with white or pink. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Eryngium amethystinum is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
