Erica astroites var. minor

Erica astroites var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
