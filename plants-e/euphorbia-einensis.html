Euphorbia einensis

Euphorbia einensis, commonly known as the Einensis Spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, specifically found in South Africa's Eastern Cape province. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium.

Euphorbia einensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
