Erica socciflora

Erica socciflora, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth and small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It produces clusters of pink or white flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Erica socciflora is well adapted to sandy soils and salty sea air, making it a resilient species in coastal environments.
