Encyclia cordigera f. leucantha

Encyclia cordigera f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and sepals, which contrast with its greenish-yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant, showy display.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical research.
