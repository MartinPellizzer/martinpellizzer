Eulophia flaccida

Eulophia flaccida, commonly known as the drooping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and is characterized by its drooping, linear leaves. The plant produces racemose inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its botanical features include a distinct labellum and spurred petals, typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Eulophia flaccida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
