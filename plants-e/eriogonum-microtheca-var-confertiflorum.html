Eriogonum microtheca var. confertiflorum

Eriogonum microtheca var. confertiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
