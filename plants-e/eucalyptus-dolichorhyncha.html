Eucalyptus dolichorhyncha

Eucalyptus dolichorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The flowers are usually white or pale blue, arranged in clusters and attracting various pollinators. This plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is commonly found in heathlands and woodlands.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for native wildlife.
