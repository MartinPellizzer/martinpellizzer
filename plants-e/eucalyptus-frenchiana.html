Eucalyptus frenchiana

Eucalyptus frenchiana, commonly known as the French's gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia, where it typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.

The timber of Eucalyptus frenchiana is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
