Eugenia curvatopetiolata

Eugenia curvatopetiolata, commonly known as the curved petiole eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves, which have curved petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep red or purple color, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
