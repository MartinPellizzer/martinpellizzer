Epilobium obcordatum subsp. siskiyouense

Epilobium obcordatum subsp.

siskiyouense, commonly known as the Siskiyou willow, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its erect or decumbent stems and lanceolate to ovate leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and open woodlands.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
