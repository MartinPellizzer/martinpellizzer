Euphorbia petaloidea var. flagelliformis

Euphorbia petaloidea var. flagelliformis, commonly known as the whip-like euphorbia, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features elongated, thin stems that resemble whips or tendrils, which can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is known for its ability to store water in its stems, making it drought-tolerant.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its striking, otherworldly appearance and adaptability to arid environments.
