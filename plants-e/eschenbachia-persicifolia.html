Eschenbachia persicifolia

Eschenbachia persicifolia, commonly known as the peachleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth surface and a slightly serrated edge, often resembling the shape of a peach leaf. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along streambanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in moist environments.
