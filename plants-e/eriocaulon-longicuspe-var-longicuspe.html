Eriocaulon longicuspe var. longicuspe

Eriocaulon longicuspe var. longicuspe, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it relies on wind pollination.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
