Epilobium axillare

Epilobium axillare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, ascending stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom from June to August.

Epilobium axillare is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
