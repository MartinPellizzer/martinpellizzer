Eustoma silenifolium

Eustoma silenifolium, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
