Erysimum carduchorum

Erysimum carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum carduchorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
