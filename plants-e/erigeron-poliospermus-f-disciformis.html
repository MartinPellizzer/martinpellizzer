Erigeron poliospermus f. disciformis

Erigeron poliospermus f. disciformis, commonly known as the disciform hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils, and it is known for its showy, daisy-like appearance. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

This subspecies is often found in the western United States and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
