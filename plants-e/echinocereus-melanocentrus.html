Echinocereus melanocentrus

Echinocereus melanocentrus, commonly known as the black-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with cylindrical stems that are often covered in white to yellow spines. The flowers are typically red or pink, opening in the evening and persisting for several days. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant once established.
