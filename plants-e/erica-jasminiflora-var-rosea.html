Erica jasminiflora var. rosea

Erica jasminiflora var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, rose-pink flowers in spring, which are reminiscent of jasmine. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
