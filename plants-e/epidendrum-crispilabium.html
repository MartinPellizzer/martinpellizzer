Epidendrum crispilabium

Epidendrum crispilabium, commonly known as the crinkled orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the flowers are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Epidendrum crispilabium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
