Erica distorta

Erica distorta, commonly known as the twisted heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. It typically blooms in spring, producing pink or white flowers that attract pollinators.

Erica distorta is often grown in rock gardens or as a container plant due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
