Eugenia gustavioides

Eugenia gustavioides, commonly known as the Gustavii guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Eugenia gustavioides thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
