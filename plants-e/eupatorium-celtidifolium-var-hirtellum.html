Eupatorium celtidifolium var. hirtellum

Eupatorium celtidifolium var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy leaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist woodlands and thrives in partial shade.

Its showy white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
