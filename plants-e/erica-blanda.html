Erica blanda

Erica blanda, commonly known as the common heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically evergreen, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Erica blanda thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
