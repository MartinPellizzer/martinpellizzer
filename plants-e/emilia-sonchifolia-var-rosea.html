Emilia sonchifolia var. rosea

Emilia sonchifolia var. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf emilia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in clusters atop slender stems. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Emilia sonchifolia var. rosea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
