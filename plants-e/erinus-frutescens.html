Erinus frutescens

Erinus frutescens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy, gray-green leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Erinus frutescens is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
