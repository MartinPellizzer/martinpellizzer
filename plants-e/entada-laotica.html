Entada laotica

Entada laotica, commonly known as the Laos legume or Lao tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its timber, medicinal properties, and ornamental features.
