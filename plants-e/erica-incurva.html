Erica incurva

Erica incurva, commonly known as the bent heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months.

Erica incurva is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
