Elaeocarpus peduncularis

Elaeocarpus peduncularis, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

Elaeocarpus peduncularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
