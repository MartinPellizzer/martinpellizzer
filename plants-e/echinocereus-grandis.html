Echinocereus grandis

Echinocereus grandis, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, cylindrical stem and prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Echinocereus grandis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and striking visual appeal.
