Erica caffrorum var. luxurians

Erica caffrorum var. luxurians, commonly known as the Cape heath or sweet heath, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Erica caffrorum var. luxurians is also known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
