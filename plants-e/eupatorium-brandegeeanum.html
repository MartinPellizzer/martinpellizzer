Eupatorium brandegeeanum

Eupatorium brandegeeanum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine white hairs.

Eupatorium brandegeeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
