Euphorbia neopedunculata

Euphorbia neopedunculata, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in the Euphorbiaceae family. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
