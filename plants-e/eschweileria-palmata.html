Eschweileria palmata

Eschweileria palmata, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rosette of basal, waxy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or reddish tint. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage and flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant colors and long-lasting appearance.

Eschweileria palmata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
