Evolvulus karstenii

Evolvulus karstenii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is typically found in moist, tropical environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

The seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, inflated fruit that splits open when mature.
