Epidendrum werffii

Epidendrum werffii, commonly known as the Werff's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. Epidendrum werffii thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its flowers, which can be yellow, white, or pink, bloom in summer and are a popular attraction for orchid enthusiasts.
