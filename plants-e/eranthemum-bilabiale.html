Eranthemum bilabiale

Eranthemum bilabiale, commonly known as the two-lipped eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its inflorescence consists of clusters of two-lipped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
