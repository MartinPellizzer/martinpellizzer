Embelia tetrandra

Embelia tetrandra, commonly known as the Indian date tree or wild date tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy, red berries that are edible.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
