Eriosema proschii

Eriosema proschii, commonly known as the Cape mountain catchfly, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions.

Eriosema proschii is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.
