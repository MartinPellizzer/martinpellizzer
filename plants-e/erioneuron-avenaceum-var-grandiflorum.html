Erioneuron avenaceum var. grandiflorum

Erioneuron avenaceum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, grass-like foliage that often forms a low-growing mat. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
