Echinospermum simplex

Echinospermum simplex, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a prominent yellow central disk. The plant produces small, hairy seeds enclosed in a spiny capsule, which is a feature of its genus. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Echinospermum simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
