Erigeron vagus var. vagus

Erigeron vagus var. vagus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to North America and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and pond edges. The plant features slender, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and appear in clusters, producing a delicate, mist-like appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
