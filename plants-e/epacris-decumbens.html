Epacris decumbens

Epacris decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense, mat-forming structure. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in native plant landscaping.
