Endoleuca sphaerocephala

Endoleuca sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical seed head, which resembles a small globe, and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy as well.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
