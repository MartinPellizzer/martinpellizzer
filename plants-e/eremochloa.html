Eremochloa

Eremochloa, commonly known as the desert grass or eremochloa grass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the family Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Eremochloa species often have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, spike-like inflorescences. They are drought-tolerant and can thrive with minimal water, making them suitable for xeriscaping.

Their ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
