Endiplus bifidus

Endiplus bifidus, commonly known as the split-leaved endiplus, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive bifid leaves.

It belongs to the family Endiplusaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often divided into two lobes. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Endiplus bifidus is considered a valuable subject in botanical research.
