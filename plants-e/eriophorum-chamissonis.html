Eriophorum chamissonis

Eriophorum chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's cottongrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, fluffy seed heads that resemble cotton. The plant typically thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its leaves are grass-like and form a rosette near the ground, while the stems are short and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with the showy seed heads being the primary reproductive structure.
