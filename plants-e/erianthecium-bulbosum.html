Erianthecium bulbosum

Erianthecium bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous erianthus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its bulb-like rhizomes and tufted foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying wetland ecosystems.
