Erigeron peregrinus var. angustifolius

Erigeron peregrinus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small blue to purple flowers in the summer.
