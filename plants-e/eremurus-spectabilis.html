Eremurus spectabilis

Eremurus spectabilis, commonly known as the desert spear or giant sparrowgrass, is a striking plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, showy inflorescences. The plant produces long, narrow leaves and blooms with yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
