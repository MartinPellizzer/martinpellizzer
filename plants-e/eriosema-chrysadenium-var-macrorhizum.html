Eriosema chrysadenium var. macrorhizum

Eriosema chrysadenium var. macrorhizum, commonly known as the large-rooted yellow eriosema, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, fleshy root system that contributes to its common name. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are yellow and tubular, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooming habit.
