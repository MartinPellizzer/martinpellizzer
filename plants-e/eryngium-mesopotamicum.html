Eryngium mesopotamicum

Eryngium mesopotamicum, commonly known as the Mesopotamian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it thrive in arid environments. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert regions.

Eryngium mesopotamicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
