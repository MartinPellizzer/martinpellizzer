Eupatorium helianthemoides

Eupatorium helianthemoides, commonly known as the golden anise or swamp boneset, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and somewhat hairy.

Eupatorium helianthemoides plays an important role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
