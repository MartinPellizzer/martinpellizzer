Euphorbia lurida var. pringlei

Euphorbia lurida var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a unique cluster called a cyathium. Its bark is often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
