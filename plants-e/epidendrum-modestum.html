Epidendrum modestum

Epidendrum modestum, commonly known as the modest orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its branching stems and pseudobulbs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Epidendrum modestum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced growers.
