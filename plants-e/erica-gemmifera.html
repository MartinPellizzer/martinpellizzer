Erica gemmifera

Erica gemmifera, commonly known as the gemmy heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family (Ericaceae).

It is native to the western and southern regions of South Africa, where it typically grows in nutrient-poor, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white. Erica gemmifera is well adapted to coastal and mountainous environments, often thriving in areas with high sunlight exposure.

Its ability to form extensive clonal colonies through vegetative propagation makes it a significant component of its native ecosystems.
