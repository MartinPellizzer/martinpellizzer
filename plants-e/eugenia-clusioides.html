Eugenia clusioides

Eugenia clusioides, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
