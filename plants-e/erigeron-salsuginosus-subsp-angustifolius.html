Erigeron salsuginosus subsp. angustifolius

Erigeron salsuginosus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has linear, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow-centered flowers that bloom in the summer.

This subspecies is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
