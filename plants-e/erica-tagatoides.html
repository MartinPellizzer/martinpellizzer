Erica tagatoides

Erica tagatoides, commonly known as the Cape heath or silver leaf heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. Erica tagatoides thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
