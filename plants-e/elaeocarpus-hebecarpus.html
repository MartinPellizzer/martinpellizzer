Elaeocarpus hebecarpus

Elaeocarpus hebecarpus, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

The species is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological importance.
