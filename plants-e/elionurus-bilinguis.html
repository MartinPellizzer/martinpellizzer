Elionurus bilinguis

Elionurus bilinguis, commonly known as the two-language grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Elionurus bilinguis is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
