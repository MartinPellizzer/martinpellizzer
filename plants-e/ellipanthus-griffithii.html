Ellipanthus griffithii

Ellipanthus griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.
