Erysimum repandum var. aragonense

Erysimum repandum var. aragonense, commonly known as the Aragon poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are borne in racemes at the top of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
