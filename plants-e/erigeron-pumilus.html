Erigeron pumilus

Erigeron pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf fleabane, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features hairy, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Erigeron pumilus is often used in native plant gardens due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
