Echinocactus elegans

Echinocactus elegans, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is covered in bright yellow spines. The plant produces large, white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red fruits containing seeds. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
