Elaeocarpus hullettii

Elaeocarpus hullettii, commonly known as the Hullett's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
