Epilobium erubescens

Epilobium erubescens, commonly known as redstem willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in loose clusters.

Epilobium erubescens is known for its ecological value, providing nectar for bees and other pollinators.
