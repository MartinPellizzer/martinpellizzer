Eugenia gabonensis

Eugenia gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon ebony, is a deciduous tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The tree typically grows to a height of 20-30 meters, featuring elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Its small, white flowers are followed by black, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
