Eugenia euphlebia

Eugenia euphlebia, commonly known as the white eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The fruit, which is typically a small, round berry, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
