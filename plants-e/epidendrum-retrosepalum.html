Epidendrum retrosepalum

Epidendrum retrosepalum, commonly known as the pink-stemmed epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences and vibrant, showy flowers that often display a mix of pink, white, and purple hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

The sepals and petals are often adorned with intricate patterns, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
