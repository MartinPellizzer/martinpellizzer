Eriocaulon sphagnicola

Eriocaulon sphagnicola, commonly known as the peat moss fern, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in wetland environments. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic conditions typical of peat bogs and marshes. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize soil and contribute to the formation of peat.

Eriocaulon sphagnicola plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by supporting microbial and invertebrate life.
