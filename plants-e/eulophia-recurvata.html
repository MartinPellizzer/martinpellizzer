Eulophia recurvata

Eulophia recurvata, commonly known as the recurved eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive recurved petals and a prominent, club-shaped lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia recurvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
