Erodium aegyptiacum

Erodium aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian catchfly, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
