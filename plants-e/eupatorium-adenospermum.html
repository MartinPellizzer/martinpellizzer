Eupatorium adenospermum

Eupatorium adenospermum, commonly known as the Chinese eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its bark is rough and often fissured, and it has a woody stem with a distinctive flaky texture.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
