Eriogonum simpsonii var. floccosolanatum

Eriogonum simpsonii var. floccosolanatum, commonly known as the white-tasseled buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white seed heads that resemble small clouds, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.
