Eugenia gueinzii

Eugenia gueinzii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia gueinzii*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, cherry-like red fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a fragrant aroma, while its flowers are white and clustered.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
