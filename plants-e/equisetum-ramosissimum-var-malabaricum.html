Equisetum ramosissimum var. malabaricum

Equisetum ramosissimum var. malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, segmented stems and whorls of small, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It is notable for its ancient lineage, being one of the oldest groups of vascular plants.

The spore-bearing structures, called strobila, are often found at the top of the plant's stem.
