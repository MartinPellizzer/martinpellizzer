Eriogonum ovalifolium subsp. eximium

Eriogonum ovalifolium subsp.

eximium, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of oval, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is adapted to arid environments.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
