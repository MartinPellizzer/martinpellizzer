Erythrina aculeatissima

Erythrina aculeatissima, commonly known as the crimson torch tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often armed with sharp spines. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread in suitable habitats.
