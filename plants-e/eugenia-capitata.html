Eugenia capitata

Eugenia capitata, commonly known as the red raspberry guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. Eugenia capitata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in home gardens.
