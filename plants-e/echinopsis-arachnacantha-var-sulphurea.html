Echinopsis arachnacantha var. sulphurea

Echinopsis arachnacantha var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur-horned cactus, is a rare and striking succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and a unique, almost coral-like appearance due to its yellowish coloration. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled settings.
