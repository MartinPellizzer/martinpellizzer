Epidendrum parkinsonianum var. falcatum

Epidendrum parkinsonianum var. falcatum, commonly known as the banana orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves that resemble banana leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with numerous fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires warm, humid conditions to flourish.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
