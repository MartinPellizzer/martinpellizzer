Egeria matthewsii

Egeria matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that often root at the nodes and produce small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. It is often used in aquariums due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its aesthetic appearance.

Egeria matthewsii is also valued for its role in water purification and as a habitat for aquatic life.
