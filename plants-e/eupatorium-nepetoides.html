Eupatorium nepetoides

Eupatorium nepetoides, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features tall, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have serrated edges.

Boneset is often found in moist meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.
