Elaeagnus nokoensis

Elaeagnus nokoensis, commonly known as the Noko's silverleaf buckthorn, is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Elaeagnus nokoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
