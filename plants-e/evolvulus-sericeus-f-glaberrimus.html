Evolvulus sericeus f. glaberrimus

Evolvulus sericeus f. glaberrimus, commonly known as the silky blue morning glory, is a variant of the species characterized by its reduced pubescence compared to the typical form.

This plant is a herbaceous annual that produces small, blue or violet flowers with five petals. It has slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine, silky hair that is less pronounced in this form.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
