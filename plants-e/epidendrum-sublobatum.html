Epidendrum sublobatum

Epidendrum sublobatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has strap-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and somewhat leathery in texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain healthy growth.

Epidendrum sublobatum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
