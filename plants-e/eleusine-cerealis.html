Eleusine cerealis

Eleusine cerealis, commonly known as finger millet, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces edible seeds that are rich in calcium and protein, making it a valuable crop in many regions. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown in dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important staple food and forage crop in parts of Asia and Africa.
