Eragrostis patens var. congoensis

Eragrostis patens var. congoensis, commonly known as Congo lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and grasslands.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
