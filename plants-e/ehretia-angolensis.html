Ehretia angolensis

Ehretia angolensis, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive bell-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
