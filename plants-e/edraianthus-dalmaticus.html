Edraianthus dalmaticus

Edraianthus dalmaticus, commonly known as the Dalmatian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
