Erythroxylum tianguanum

Erythroxylum tianguanum, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain minute seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
