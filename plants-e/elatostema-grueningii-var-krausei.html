Elatostema grueningii var. krausei

Elatostema grueningii var. krausei, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
