Eucomis amaryllidifolia

Eucomis amaryllidifolia, commonly known as the pineapple lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a branched inflorescence with small, white or pale purple flowers that resemble a pineapple, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Eucomis amaryllidifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
