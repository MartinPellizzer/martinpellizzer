Eschweilera tetrapetala

Eschweilera tetrapetala, commonly known as the pink star orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers with a star-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to retain moisture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
