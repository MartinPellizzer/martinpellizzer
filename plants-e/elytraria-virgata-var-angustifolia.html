Elytraria virgata var. angustifolia

Elytraria virgata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elytraria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly known as grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
