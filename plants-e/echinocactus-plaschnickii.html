Echinocactus plaschnickii

Echinocactus plaschnickii, commonly known as the Plaschnick's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring or early summer, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its spines are modified leaves that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss through transpiration.

Echinocactus plaschnickii is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and unique member of the cactus family.
