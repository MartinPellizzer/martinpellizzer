Eulophia trilamellata

Eulophia trilamellata, commonly known as the three-lobed eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features three prominent, elongated petals and a spurred lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are broad, dark green, and arranged in a fan-like structure.

Eulophia trilamellata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
