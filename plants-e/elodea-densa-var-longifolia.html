Elodea densa var. longifolia

Elodea densa var. longifolia, commonly known as waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that emerge above the water surface, followed by small, dark brown fruits. Elodea densa var. longifolia is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams, where it plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems.

Its rapid growth makes it an important species for water oxygenation and habitat provision for aquatic life.
