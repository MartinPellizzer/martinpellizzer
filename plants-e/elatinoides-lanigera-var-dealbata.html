Elatinoides lanigera var. dealbata

Elatinoides lanigera var. dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered elatin, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Elatinaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has a prostrate growth habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is commonly found in coastal areas and wetlands.

This species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites, making it an important pioneer plant in ecological succession.
