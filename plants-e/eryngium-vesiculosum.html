Eryngium vesiculosum

Eryngium vesiculosum, commonly known as the bladder eryngi, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by bristly leaves. The plant produces small, bladder-like structures called pseudobulbs, which are used for storing water and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
