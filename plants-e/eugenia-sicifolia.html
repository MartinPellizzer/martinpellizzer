Eugenia sicifolia

Eugenia sicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Eugenia sicifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.
