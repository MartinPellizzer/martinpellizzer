Eugenia leonis

Eugenia leonis, commonly known as the lion's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Following flowering, it develops small, round berries that are typically red or purple in color.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
