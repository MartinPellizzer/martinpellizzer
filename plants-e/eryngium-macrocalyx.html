Eryngium macrocalyx

Eryngium macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems and large, blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant features silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, bristly texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.
