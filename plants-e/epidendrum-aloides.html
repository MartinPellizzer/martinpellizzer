Epidendrum aloides

Epidendrum aloides, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged along a single stem.

Epidendrum aloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
