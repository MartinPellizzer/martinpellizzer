Eriocoma pyramidata

Eriocoma pyramidata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring season. The plant has silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Eriocoma pyramidata is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
