Eleutherine palmifolia

Eleutherine palmifolia, commonly known as the elephant's foot or African star grass, is a striking tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, palm-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by distinctive red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
