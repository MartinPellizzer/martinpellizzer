Erica pohlmannii

Erica pohlmannii, commonly known as the Pohlmann's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica pohlmannii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal environments.
