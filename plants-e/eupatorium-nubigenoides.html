Eupatorium nubigenoides

Eupatorium nubigenoides, commonly known as cloud-like eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and other moist habitats, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium nubigenoides is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
