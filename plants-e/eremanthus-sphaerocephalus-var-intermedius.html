Eremanthus sphaerocephalus var. intermedius

Eremanthus sphaerocephalus var. intermedius, commonly known as the Intermediate Globehead Heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, rounded, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is well adapted to coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in its natural habitat.
