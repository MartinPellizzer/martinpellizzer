Erechtites glandulosus

Erechtites glandulosus, commonly known as the glossy sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features large, showy yellow flowers with dark centers, surrounded by a ring of green bracts. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and help reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
