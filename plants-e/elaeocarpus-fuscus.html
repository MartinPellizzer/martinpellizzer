Elaeocarpus fuscus

Elaeocarpus fuscus, commonly known as the black elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, hard, black fruits that are typically clustered in dense, spherical formations. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Elaeocarpus fuscus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
