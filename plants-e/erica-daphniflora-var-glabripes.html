Erica daphniflora var. glabripes

Erica daphniflora var. glabripes, commonly known as the glabrous heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage composed of small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica daphniflora var. glabripes is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in coastal areas due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
