Eryngium carthamoides

Eryngium carthamoides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to purple spiky flowers that resemble sea urchins, surrounded by silver-gray bracts. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often growing in coastal areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, silver-veined leaves that are aromatic and slightly fuzzy.

Eryngium carthamoides is commonly used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
