Elaeagia karstenii

Elaeagia karstenii, commonly known as the Karsten's candlewood, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Elaeagia karstenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
