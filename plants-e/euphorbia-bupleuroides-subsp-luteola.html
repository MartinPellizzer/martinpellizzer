Euphorbia bupleuroides subsp. luteola

Euphorbia bupleuroides subsp.

luteola, commonly known as the yellow spurge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often reduced to small, scale-like structures.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
