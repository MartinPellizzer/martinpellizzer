Edraianthus kitaibelii f. grandis

Edraianthus kitaibelii f. grandis, commonly known as the giant edraianthus, is a striking perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its blooms typically appear in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural collections.
