Eria glabra

Eria glabra, commonly known as the smooth eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth to the touch. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in late spring or early summer. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
