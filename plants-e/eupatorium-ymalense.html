Eupatorium ymalense

Eupatorium ymalense, commonly known as the Yucatan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium ymalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
