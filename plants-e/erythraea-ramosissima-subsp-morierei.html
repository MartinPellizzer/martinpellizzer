Erythraea ramosissima subsp. morierei

Erythraea ramosissima subsp.

morierei, commonly known as the Morier's erythraea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain wildlife. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with deep root systems and minimal water requirements.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local fauna.
