Eriochloa stevensii

Eriochloa stevensii, commonly known as stevens' panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Eriochloa stevensii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
