Epidendrum luizae

Epidendrum luizae, commonly known as the Luizae Vanda, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces erect, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant typically blooms with showy, fragrant flowers that are borne on terminal inflorescences, featuring a distinctive spurred lip. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange with a contrasting deep red or purple lip.

Epidendrum luizae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
