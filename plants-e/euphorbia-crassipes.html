Euphorbia crassipes

Euphorbia crassipes, commonly known as the thick-stemmed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems to survive drought conditions.

Euphorbia crassipes is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
