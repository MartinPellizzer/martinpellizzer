Echinofossulocactus robustus

Echinofossulocactus robustus, commonly known as the robust echinofossulocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
