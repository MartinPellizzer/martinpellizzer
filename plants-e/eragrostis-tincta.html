Eragrostis tincta

Eragrostis tincta, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a flowering grass species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple tassels on the inflorescence, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like flowers are typical of the grass family Poaceae.

Eragrostis tincta is adapted to full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
