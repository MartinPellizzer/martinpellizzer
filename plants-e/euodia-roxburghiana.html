Euodia roxburghiana

Euodia roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian laurier or Chinese laurier, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Euodia roxburghiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its glossy foliage.
