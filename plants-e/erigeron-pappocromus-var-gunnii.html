Erigeron pappocromus var. gunnii

Erigeron pappocromus var. gunnii, commonly known as the New Zealand bluebell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white pappus hairs that aid in seed dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
