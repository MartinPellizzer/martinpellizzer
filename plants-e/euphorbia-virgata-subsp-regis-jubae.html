Euphorbia virgata subsp. regis-jubae

Euphorbia virgata subsp.

regis-jubae, commonly known as the Royal Crown Spurge, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa. It features a distinctive appearance with its branching stems and clusters of yellowish-green flowers.

The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of arid environments. It produces small, gelatinous flowers that attract pollinators like bees and beetles.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be toxic if ingested.
