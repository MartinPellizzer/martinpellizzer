Exostema subcordatum var. buchii

Exostema subcordatum var. buchii, commonly known as the Buch's velvetleaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, often with a waxy coating. It is native to regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
