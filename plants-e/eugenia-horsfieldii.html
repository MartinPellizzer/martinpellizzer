Eugenia horsfieldii

Eugenia horsfieldii, commonly known as the Horsfield's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of soil types.

Eugenia horsfieldii prefers partial shade to full sun and is drought-tolerant once established.
