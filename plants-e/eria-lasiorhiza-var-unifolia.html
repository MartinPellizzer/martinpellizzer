Eria lasiorhiza var. unifolia

Eria lasiorhiza var. unifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, delicate flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or near streams.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in tropical botany and horticulture.
