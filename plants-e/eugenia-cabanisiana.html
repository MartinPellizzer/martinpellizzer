Eugenia cabanisiana

Eugenia cabanisiana, commonly known as the golden eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

Its fruit, a small, round berry, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
