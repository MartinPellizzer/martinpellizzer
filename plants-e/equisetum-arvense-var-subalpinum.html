Equisetum arvense var. subalpinum

Equisetum arvense var. subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine field horsetail, is a variant of the common horsetail that thrives in alpine and subalpine environments.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems and whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves. This plant is typically found in moist, nutrient-poor soils in mountainous regions. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing cones, which are distinctive and often clustered at the tips of the stems.

The plant exhibits a primitive morphology, reflecting its ancient lineage among vascular plants.
