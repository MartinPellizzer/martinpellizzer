Eleutherostemon laxus

Eleutherostemon laxus, commonly known as the looseflower eleutherostemon, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its showy, clustered inflorescence consists of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Eleutherostemon laxus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
