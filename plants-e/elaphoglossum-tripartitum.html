Elaphoglossum tripartitum

Elaphoglossum tripartitum, commonly known as the three-part holly fern, is a small, evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate pinnae that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique feature makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
