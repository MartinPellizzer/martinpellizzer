Escobaria lloydii

Escobaria lloydii, commonly known as Lloyd's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Escobaria lloydii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
