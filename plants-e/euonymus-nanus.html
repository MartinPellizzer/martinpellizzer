Euonymus nanus

Euonymus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf euonymus, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Euonymus nanus is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different light conditions.
