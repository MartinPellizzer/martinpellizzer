Erysimum friedrichii

Erysimum friedrichii, commonly known as the Friedrich's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

Erysimum friedrichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
