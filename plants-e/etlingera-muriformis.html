Etlingera muriformis

Etlingera muriformis, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
