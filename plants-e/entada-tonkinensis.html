Entada tonkinensis

Entada tonkinensis, commonly known as the African tamarind or Tonkin tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with root nodules, enhancing soil fertility. Its flowers are typically yellow or green, and the fruit pods contain flat, fibrous seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its timber and seeds in traditional practices.
