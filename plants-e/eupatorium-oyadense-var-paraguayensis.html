Eupatorium oyadense var. paraguayensis

Eupatorium oyadense var. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a serrated margin.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
