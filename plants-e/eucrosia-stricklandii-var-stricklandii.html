Eucrosia stricklandii var. stricklandii

Eucrosia stricklandii var. stricklandii, commonly known as the Strickland's golden lily, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Eucrosia stricklandii var. stricklandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
