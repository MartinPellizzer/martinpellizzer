Escallonia aculeata

Escallonia aculeata, commonly known as the coastal escallonia, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thorny stems and is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing year-round visual interest.

Escallonia aculeata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
