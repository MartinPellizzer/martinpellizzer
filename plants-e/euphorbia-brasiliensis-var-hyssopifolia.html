Euphorbia brasiliensis var. hyssopifolia

Euphorbia brasiliensis var. hyssopifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian spurge, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features a branched stem covered in soft, fuzzy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

However, it contains toxic sap that can cause skin irritation, so handling should be done with care.
