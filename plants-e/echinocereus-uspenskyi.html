Echinocereus uspenskyi

Echinocereus uspenskyi, commonly known as the Usanovsky's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is a small, solitary cactus with a globular or cylindrical stem that is typically covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb water efficiently in its arid environment.

Echinocereus uspenskyi is well-suited for cultivation in rocky, well-drained soils with minimal water.
