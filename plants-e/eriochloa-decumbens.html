Eriochloa decumbens

Eriochloa decumbens, commonly known as prairie dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, fluffy spikelets that are often carried on long, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for native plant gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
