Eupatorium sophioides

Eupatorium sophioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pale blue to lavender flower heads that resemble mist, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Eupatorium sophioides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
