Eurya conocarpa

Eurya conocarpa, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and typically dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Eurya conocarpa is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
