Erica magnifica

Erica magnifica, commonly known as the grand heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer. Erica magnifica thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant.
