Enemion raddeanum var. japonicum

Enemion raddeanum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese Enemion, is a small herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a smooth texture and bright green color. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, blooming in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
