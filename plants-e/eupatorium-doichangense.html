Eupatorium doichangense

Eupatorium doichangense, commonly known as the Doichang aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are blue to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium doichangense is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
