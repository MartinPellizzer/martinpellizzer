Eugenia discors

Eugenia discors, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and emit a strong, pleasant aroma when crushed. Eugenia discors is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and aromatic properties.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
