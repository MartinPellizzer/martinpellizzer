Empogona kirkii subsp. junodii

Empogona kirkii subsp.

junodii, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
