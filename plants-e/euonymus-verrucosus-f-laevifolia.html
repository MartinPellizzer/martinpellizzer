Euonymus verrucosus f. laevifolia

Euonymus verrucosus f. laevifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf woodbine, is a variant of the woodbine plant characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, typically found in woodland and moist habitats. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a distinctive, slightly lobed base.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
