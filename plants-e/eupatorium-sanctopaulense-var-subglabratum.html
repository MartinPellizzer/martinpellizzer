Eupatorium sanctopaulense var. subglabratum

Eupatorium sanctopaulense var. subglabratum, commonly known as the smooth eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with soft, glandular hairs that give it a slightly sticky texture. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense inflorescences, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along riverbanks.
