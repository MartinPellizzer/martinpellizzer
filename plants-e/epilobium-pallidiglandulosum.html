Epilobium pallidiglandulosum

Epilobium pallidiglandulosum, commonly known as the pale glandular willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as along stream banks and in meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems covered in pale glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin and are also hairy on both surfaces.

The flowers are pink to purple, clustered in elongated racemes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.
