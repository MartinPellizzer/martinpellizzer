Eleiodoxa xantholepis

Eleiodoxa xantholepis, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed euphorbia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, yellowish stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spherical seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
