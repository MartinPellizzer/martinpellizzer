Eucalyptus oocarpa

Eucalyptus oocarpa, commonly known as the red gum, is a tall, slender tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a reddish-brown color underneath. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray hue. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is known for its aromatic oils, which have various industrial and medicinal applications.
