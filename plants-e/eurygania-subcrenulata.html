Eurygania subcrenulata

Eurygania subcrenulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are often marked with distinctive wavy margins. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the day and close at night. Its roots system helps stabilize the sediment in shallow waters.

Eurygania subcrenulata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
