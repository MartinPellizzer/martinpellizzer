Epilobium glandulosum var. cardiophyllum

Epilobium glandulosum var. cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heart-leaved willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. The plant features showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate to heart-shaped, with a distinctive glandular surface that gives the species its scientific name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
