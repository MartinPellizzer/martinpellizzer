Eruca subbipinnata

Eruca subbipinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent aroma. The plant has lobed, hairy leaves that are often used in salads and as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread presence in temperate climates.
