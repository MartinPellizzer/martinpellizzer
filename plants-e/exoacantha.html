Exoacantha

Exoacantha, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plants typically grow as shrubs or small trees, featuring woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Exoacantha species are known for their ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions, making them well-suited for arid environments.

Their unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to their ecological significance.
