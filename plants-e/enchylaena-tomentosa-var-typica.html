Enchylaena tomentosa var. typica

Enchylaena tomentosa var. typica, commonly known as the silky mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Its unique texture and continuous blooming make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
