Escobaria chihuahuensis subsp. henricksonii

Escobaria chihuahuensis subsp.

henricksonii, commonly known as the Chihuahua prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the Chihuahua Desert in northern Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to dry conditions.

This subspecies is closely related to other species in the Escobaria genus, which are known for their ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
