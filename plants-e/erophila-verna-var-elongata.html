Erophila verna var. elongata

Erophila verna var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features slender, often hairy stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its small, pink to purple flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed edge, and are arranged alternately along the stem.
