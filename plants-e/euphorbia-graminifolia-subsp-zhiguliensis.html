Euphorbia graminifolia subsp. zhiguliensis

Euphorbia graminifolia subsp.

zhiguliensis, commonly known as the Zhiguliensis spurge, is a succulent plant native to China. It is characterized by its rosette-forming stems and leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in a cluster known as a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its foliage and growth pattern make it an attractive ornamental plant in dry gardens.
