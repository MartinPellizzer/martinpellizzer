Epipactis helleborine var. chlorantha

Epipactis helleborine var. chlorantha, commonly known as the green-flowered helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its distinctive, greenish-yellow flowers with a prominent lip. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant is perennial and reproduces through rhizomes, forming dense colonies over time.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
