Eugenia proba

Eugenia proba, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature.

Eugenia proba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
