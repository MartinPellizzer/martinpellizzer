Ericoides onosmiflorum

Ericoides onosmiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and leathery. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

Ericoides onosmiflorum is adapted to cool, moist climates and is often associated with rocky or sandy substrates.
