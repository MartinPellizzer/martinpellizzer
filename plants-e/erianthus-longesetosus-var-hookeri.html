Erianthus longesetosus var. hookeri

Erianthus longesetosus var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's silver grass, is a variant of the silver grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-tipped bristles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a silvery sheen that reflects light, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
