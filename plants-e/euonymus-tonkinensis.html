Euonymus tonkinensis

Euonymus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese spurge or Tonkin euonymus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Euonymus tonkinensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
