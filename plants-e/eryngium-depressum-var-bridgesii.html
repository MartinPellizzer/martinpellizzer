Eryngium depressum var. bridgesii

Eryngium depressum var. bridgesii, commonly known as the desert thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically basal, with a rough, hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The species is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
