Eutriana juncifolia

Eutriana juncifolia, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist to aquatic environments, often growing in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Eutriana juncifolia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
