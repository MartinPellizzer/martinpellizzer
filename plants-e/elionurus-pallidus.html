Elionurus pallidus

Elionurus pallidus, commonly known as the pale grassland aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Elionurus pallidus is an important nectar source for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
