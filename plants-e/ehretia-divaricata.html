Ehretia divaricata

Ehretia divaricata, commonly known as the Chinese mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
