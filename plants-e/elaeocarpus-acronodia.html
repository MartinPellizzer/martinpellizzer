Elaeocarpus acronodia

Elaeocarpus acronodia, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The tree produces woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed, which is often encased in a hard, persistent shell. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
