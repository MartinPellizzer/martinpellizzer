Eremopyrum triticeum

Eremopyrum triticeum, commonly known as the wheatgrass, is a species of grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Eremopyrum triticeum is often cultivated for its nutritional value, particularly in the production of sprouted grain products.
