Endlicheria bracteata

Endlicheria bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Endlicheria bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
