Eucalyptus petrensis

Eucalyptus petrensis, commonly known as the Sturt's Desert Peppermint, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fibrous bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming during spring. Eucalyptus petrensis is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils.

It is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and for their antiseptic properties.
