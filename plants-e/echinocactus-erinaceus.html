Echinocactus erinaceus

Echinocactus erinaceus, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical shape and thick, ribbed stems that are covered in spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Echinocactus erinaceus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
