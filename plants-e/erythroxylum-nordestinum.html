Erythroxylum nordestinum

Erythroxylum nordestinum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are highly attractive to birds. The plant is typically shrub-like or small tree-like, with simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The plant has some ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.
