Echinodorus pellucidus

Echinodorus pellucidus, commonly known as the glasswort waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often translucent, giving it its common name. This plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Echinodorus pellucidus is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.
