Elaeocarpus salomonensis

Elaeocarpus salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish pericarp. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
