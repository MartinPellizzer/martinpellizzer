Euphorbia georgiana

Euphorbia georgiana, commonly known as the Georgia spurge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has smooth, often hairy leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with latex-producing properties.

Euphorbia georgiana is often used in native plant gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
