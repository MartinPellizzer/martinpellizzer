Elymus × incertus

Elymus × incertus, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Elymus trachycaulus and Elymus lanceolatus, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. The plant has stiff, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, with spikelets that are typically awned. It is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its potential in forage and conservation grazing.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
