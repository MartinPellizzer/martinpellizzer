Eriosema molle

Eriosema molle, commonly known as the soft-hairy eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eriosema molle is typically found in grassy areas and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
