Elaeagia mariae

Elaeagia mariae, commonly known as the Mary's candlewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Elaeaginaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
