Euphorbia amygdaloides subsp. robbiae

Euphorbia amygdaloides subsp.

robbiae, commonly known as the Robbia's spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in late spring.

The plant has smooth, succulent stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
