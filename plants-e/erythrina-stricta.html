Erythrina stricta

Erythrina stricta, commonly known as the narrow erythrina, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

Erythrina stricta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
