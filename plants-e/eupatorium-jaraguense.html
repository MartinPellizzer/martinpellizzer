Eupatorium jaraguense

Eupatorium jaraguense, commonly known as Brazilian boneset, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Eupatorium jaraguense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
