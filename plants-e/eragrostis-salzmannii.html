Eragrostis salzmannii

Eragrostis salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often appear in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Eragrostis salzmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
