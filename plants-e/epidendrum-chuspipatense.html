Epidendrum chuspipatense

Epidendrum chuspipatense, commonly known as the Chuspipe Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in Ecuador and Peru.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a yellowish throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
