Eragrostis expansa

Eragrostis expansa, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and is commonly found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Eragrostis expansa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
