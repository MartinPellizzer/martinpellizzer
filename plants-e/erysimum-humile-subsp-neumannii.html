Erysimum humile subsp. neumannii

Erysimum humile subsp.

neumannii, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has compact, basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental choice for alpine gardens.
