Eupatorium salzmannianum

Eupatorium salzmannianum, commonly known as Salzmann's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Eupatorium salzmannianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
