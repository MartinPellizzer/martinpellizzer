Eriogonum pharnaceoides var. pharnaceoides

Eriogonum pharnaceoides var. pharnaceoides, commonly known as the silverleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. It has hairy stems and leaves that help reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in early spring and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
