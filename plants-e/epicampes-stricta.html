Epicampes stricta

Epicampes stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. The plant features simple, alternate, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.
