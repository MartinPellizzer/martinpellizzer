Eragrostis burttdavii

Eragrostis burttdavii, commonly known as Burtt's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its hardy and drought-tolerant nature.
