Eugenia irirensis

Eugenia irirensis, commonly known as the pink guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically pink or red in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

Eugenia irirensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
