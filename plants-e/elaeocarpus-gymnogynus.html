Elaeocarpus gymnogynus

Elaeocarpus gymnogynus, commonly known as the white sand elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it often grows in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
