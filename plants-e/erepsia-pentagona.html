Erepsia pentagona

Erepsia pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled erica, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family (Ericaceae).

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features small, evergreen leaves that are arranged in a distinctive five-angled pattern, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Erepsia pentagona is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
