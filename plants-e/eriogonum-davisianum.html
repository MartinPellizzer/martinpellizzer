Eriogonum davisianum

Eriogonum davisianum, commonly known as Davis' buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually unbranched.

Davis' buckwheat plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush communities.
