Eupatorium larcheanum

Eupatorium larcheanum, commonly known as the Larch Mountain eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium larcheanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
