Echeveria traskiae

Echeveria traskiae, commonly known as Trask's Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to the United States, specifically found in Texas.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, often with a powdery coating. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring or summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts and gardeners.
