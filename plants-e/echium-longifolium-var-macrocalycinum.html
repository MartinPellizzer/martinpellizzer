Echium longifolium var. macrocalycinum

Echium longifolium var. macrocalycinum, commonly known as the giant blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This variant is notable for its larger flower size compared to the typical Echium longifolium.
