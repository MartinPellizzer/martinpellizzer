Erigeron gilliesii

Erigeron gilliesii, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The showy blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
