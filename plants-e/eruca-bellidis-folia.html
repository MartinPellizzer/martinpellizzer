Eruca bellidis-folia

Eruca bellidis-folia, commonly known as the Mediterranean wallcress, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found along coastal areas. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly peppery taste, similar to that of mustard greens.

It produces small yellow flowers and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
