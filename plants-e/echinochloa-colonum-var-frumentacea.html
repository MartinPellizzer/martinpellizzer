Echinochloa colonum var. frumentacea

Echinochloa colonum var. frumentacea, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and high seed production make it a competitive plant in various ecosystems.
