Eurya theoides

Eurya theoides, commonly known as the Chinese holly or glossy holly, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Eurya theoides is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract wildlife.
