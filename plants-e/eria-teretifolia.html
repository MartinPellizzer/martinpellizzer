Eria teretifolia

Eria teretifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Eria teretifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
