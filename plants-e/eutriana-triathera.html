Eutriana triathera

Eutriana triathera, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cascading clusters of yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative element in landscape design.

The vine is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
