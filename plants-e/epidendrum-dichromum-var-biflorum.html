Epidendrum dichromum var. biflorum

Epidendrum dichromum var. biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking dichromatic flowers, which often display contrasting colors on the upper and lower petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The orchid produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
