Erica latiflora

Erica latiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica latiflora typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form attractive, compact shrubs.
