Epigeneium quinquecallosum

Epigeneium quinquecallosum, commonly known as the five-chambered epigeneium, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex floral structure and medicinal properties reported in traditional uses.
