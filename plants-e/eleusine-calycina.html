Eleusine calycina

Eleusine calycina, commonly known as finger millet, is an annual grass species native to Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces minute, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, triangular grains. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Eleusine calycina is valued for its nutritional content and is a staple crop in many regions.
