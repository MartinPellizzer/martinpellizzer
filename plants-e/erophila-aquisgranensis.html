Erophila aquisgranensis

Erophila aquisgranensis, commonly known as the water thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces purple to pink flowers in summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it can spread through both seeds and vegetative means.

Erophila aquisgranensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
