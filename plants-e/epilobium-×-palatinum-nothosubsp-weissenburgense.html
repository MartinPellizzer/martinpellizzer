Epilobium × palatinum nothosubsp. weissenburgense

Epilobium × palatinum nothosubsp.

weissenburgense, commonly known as the Weissenburg velvetleaf, is a hybrid plant species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its glandular-hairy stems and leaves, which are typically lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are pink to reddish-purple, arranged in terminal clusters, and are notable for their showy appearance. This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent or hybrid in the genus Epilobium, contributing to the genetic diversity of related species.
