Eragrostis boliviensis

Eragrostis boliviensis, commonly known as Bolivian lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, grass-like flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Eragrostis boliviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
