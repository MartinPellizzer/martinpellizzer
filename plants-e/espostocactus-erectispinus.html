Espostocactus erectispinus

Espostocactus erectispinus, commonly known as the erect-spined cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny exterior. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
