Echinocereus tuberosus

Echinocereus tuberosus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that bear vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to bloom.

Echinocereus tuberosus is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
