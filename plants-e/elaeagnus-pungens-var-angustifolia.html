Elaeagnus pungens var. angustifolia

Elaeagnus pungens var. angustifolia, commonly known as silver leaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
