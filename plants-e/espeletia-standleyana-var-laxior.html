Espeletia standleyana var. laxior

Espeletia standleyana var. laxior, commonly known as the "Laxior Globemallow," is a succulent shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and large, showy inflorescences that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has adapted to high-altitude environments with its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and cold conditions. Its growth habit is typically low and compact, forming a cushion-like structure that protects it from harsh weather.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
