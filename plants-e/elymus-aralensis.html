Elymus aralensis

Elymus aralensis, commonly known as the Aral Sea grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in saline soils near the Aral Sea region. Elymus aralensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas effectively.
