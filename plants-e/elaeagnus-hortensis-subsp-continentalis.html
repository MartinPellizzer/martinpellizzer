Elaeagnus hortensis subsp. continentalis

Elaeagnus hortensis subsp.

continentalis, commonly known as the European silverberry, is a shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant has a spreading growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and more compact form compared to other Elaeagnus species.
