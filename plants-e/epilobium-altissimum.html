Epilobium altissimum

Epilobium altissimum, commonly known as tall willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces showy pink to purple flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Epilobium altissimum is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivorous insects.
