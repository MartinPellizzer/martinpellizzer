Echinocactus sellowii var. turbinatus

Echinocactus sellowii var. turbinatus, commonly known as the Turbinated Barrel Cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical shape and distinctive, ribbed, spiny stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
