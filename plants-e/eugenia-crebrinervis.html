Eugenia crebrinervis

Eugenia crebrinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined eugenia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple to black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
