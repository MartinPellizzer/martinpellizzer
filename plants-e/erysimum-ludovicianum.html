Erysimum ludovicianum

Erysimum ludovicianum, commonly known as the eastern wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces bright yellow flowers with four petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Erysimum ludovicianum is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, where it blooms from late spring to early summer.
