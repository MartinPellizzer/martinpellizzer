Eugenia berlynensis

Eugenia berlynensis, commonly known as the Berlin guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red or purple berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.
