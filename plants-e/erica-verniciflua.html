Erica verniciflua

Erica verniciflua, commonly known as the sticky heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces a sticky resin that gives it its common name and has been used traditionally for various purposes. Erica verniciflua thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
