Elaeocarpus pycnanthus

Elaeocarpus pycnanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is relatively rare in the wild, making it a subject of conservation interest.
