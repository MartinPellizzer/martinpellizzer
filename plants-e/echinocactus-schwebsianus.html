Echinocactus schwebsianus

Echinocactus schwebsianus, commonly known as the Schwebes's cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that requires well-draining soil and full sun to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable specimen in botanical collections.
