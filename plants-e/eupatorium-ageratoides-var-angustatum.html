Eupatorium ageratoides var. angustatum

Eupatorium ageratoides var. angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.
