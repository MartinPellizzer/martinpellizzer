Eriogonum hieraciifolium f. atropurpureum

Eriogonum hieraciifolium f. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purpleleaf filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with a low, spreading growth habit and features slender, wiry stems. The leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green or purplish hue, which gives the plant its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense umbels, and are usually pink to purple in color.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
