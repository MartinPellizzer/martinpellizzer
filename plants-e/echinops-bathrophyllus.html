Echinops bathrophyllus

Echinops bathrophyllus, commonly known as the silver thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically silver or gray-green, covered in a fine layer of hairs that help reduce water loss.

Echinops bathrophyllus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
