Echinops freynianus

Echinops freynianus, commonly known as the Freyn's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical crown and pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Echinops freynianus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
