Echinomastus erectocentrus

Echinomastus erectocentrus, commonly known as the upright centrose mountain cactus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. Its foliage consists of small, fleshy leaves that help store water, and it produces edible fruits that are sometimes consumed by local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
