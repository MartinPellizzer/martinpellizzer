Elaeocarpus alaternoides

Elaeocarpus alaternoides, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers and oval-shaped, woody fruits. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
