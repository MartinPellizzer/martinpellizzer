Eulophia schlechteri

Eulophia schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinct lip, and it produces a single spike of blooms.

Eulophia schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
