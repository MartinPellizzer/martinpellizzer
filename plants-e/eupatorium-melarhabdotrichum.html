Eupatorium melarhabdotrichum

Eupatorium melarhabdotrichum, commonly known as the hairy eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces small, white to pale blue flowers.

Eupatorium melarhabdotrichum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
