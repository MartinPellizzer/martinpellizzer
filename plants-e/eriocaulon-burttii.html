Eriocaulon burttii

Eriocaulon burttii, commonly known as the Burtt's spike-moss, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomes form a network that helps anchor it in the substrate and facilitates vegetative propagation.

Eriocaulon burttii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to improve water quality in aquatic environments.
