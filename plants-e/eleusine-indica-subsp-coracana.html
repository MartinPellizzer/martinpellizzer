Eleusine indica subsp. coracana

Eleusine indica subsp.

coracana, commonly known as finger millet, is a small grain plant native to Africa and southern Asia. It is characterized by its tufted, grass-like foliage and spikelet inflorescences that bear small, oval-shaped seeds.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop for arid regions. Its seeds are rich in calcium and are often consumed as a porridge or flour.

The plant also has traditional uses in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
