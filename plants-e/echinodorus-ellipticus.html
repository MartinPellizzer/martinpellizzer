Echinodorus ellipticus

Echinodorus ellipticus, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often above the water surface. It is well adapted to both freshwater and brackish environments.

Echinodorus ellipticus is popular in aquariums and ponds due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to oxygenate water.
