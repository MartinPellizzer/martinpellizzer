Eremostachys discolor

Eremostachys discolor, commonly known as the pink desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
