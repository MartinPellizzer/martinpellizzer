Echeveria cante

Echeveria cante, commonly known as the Canary Island echeveria, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a slight powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with ample sunlight.

Echeveria cante is popular in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant due to its compact size and striking appearance.
