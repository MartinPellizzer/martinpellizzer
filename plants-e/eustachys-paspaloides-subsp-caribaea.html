Eustachys paspaloides subsp. caribaea

Eustachys paspaloides subsp.

caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean false brome, is a perennial grass native to the Caribbean region. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and coastal areas.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
