Echites mollissimus

Echites mollissimus, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name.

It is native to regions of South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Echites mollissimus is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
