Eupatorium huehuetecum

Eupatorium huehuetecum, commonly known as sacred herb or sacred plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine and spiritual practices among indigenous communities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
