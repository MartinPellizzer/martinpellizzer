Eugenia paucivenia

Eugenia paucivenia, commonly known as the small-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

Eugenia paucivenia is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
