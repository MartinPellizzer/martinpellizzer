Eugenia mogotensis

Eugenia mogotensis, commonly known as the Mogotense waxleaf, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible, dark purple berries that are used in local cuisines.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional remedies.
