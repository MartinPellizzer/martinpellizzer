Epidendrum elatius var. leopoldii

Epidendrum elatius var. leopoldii, commonly known as the Leopold's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
