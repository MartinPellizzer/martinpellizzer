Eugenia supraaxillaris

Eugenia supraaxillaris, commonly known as the mountain eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests or mountainous areas with cool, moist conditions. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that is sometimes used in local cuisines.

Botanically, it is characterized by its dense branching pattern and unique floral structure.
