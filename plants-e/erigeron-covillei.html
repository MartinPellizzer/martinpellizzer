Erigeron covillei

Erigeron covillei, commonly known as Coville's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Coville's fleabane is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
