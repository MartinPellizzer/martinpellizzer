Eucalyptus saligna var. parviflora

Eucalyptus saligna var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs or small trees with greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The flowers are small and usually pale or cream in color, appearing in clusters along the stems. This plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland regions with a temperate climate.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
