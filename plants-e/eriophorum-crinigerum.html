Eriophorum crinigerum

Eriophorum crinigerum, commonly known as the silky cottongrass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to wetland and bog environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy seed heads that resemble cotton, which are produced at the end of its flowering stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its soft texture. It thrives in nutrient-poor, waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing peatland ecosystems.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of its habitat.
