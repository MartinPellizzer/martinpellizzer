Eugenia lucida

Eugenia lucida, commonly known as the shining eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, urn-shaped fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

Eugenia lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
