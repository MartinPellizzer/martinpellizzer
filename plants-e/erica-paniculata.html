Erica paniculata

Erica paniculata, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heather plants. This species is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica paniculata typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
