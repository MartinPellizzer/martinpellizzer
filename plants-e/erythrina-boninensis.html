Erythrina boninensis

Erythrina boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets, and its stems are typically smooth and reddish in color. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
