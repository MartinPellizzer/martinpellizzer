Euclea natalensis subsp. obovata

Euclea natalensis subsp.

obovata, commonly known as Natal ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark.

The leaves are obovate in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
