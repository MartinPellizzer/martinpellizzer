Echinops latifolius f. angustilobus

Echinops latifolius f. angustilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is drought-tolerant. Its leaves are lance-shaped with narrow lobes, distinguishing it from the broader leaves of the typical variety.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and hardy nature.
