Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp. baileyi

Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp.

baileyi, commonly known as the Bailey's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
