Espeletia usubillagae

Espeletia usubillagae, commonly known as the Usubilla heather, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems. Its foliage consists of dense, woolly leaves that help insulate it from harsh weather conditions.

Espeletia usubillagae plays a role in local biodiversity and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
