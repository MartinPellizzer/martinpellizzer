Eleocharis palustris var. watsonii

Eleocharis palustris var. watsonii, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikes of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant is often found in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
