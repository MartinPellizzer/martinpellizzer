Eremostachys laevigata

Eremostachys laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf eremostachys, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are narrow and covered with a fine, silky hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Eremostachys laevigata is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in steppes and deserts.
