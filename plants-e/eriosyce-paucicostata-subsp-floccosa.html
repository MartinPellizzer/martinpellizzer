Eriosyce paucicostata subsp. floccosa

Eriosyce paucicostata subsp.

floccosa, commonly known as the woolly areca palm, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white to grayish hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant typically grows as a solitary stem with a short, thick base and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a slow growth rate, making it a striking addition to arid gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
