Echinocereus phoeniceus f. inermis

Echinocereus phoeniceus f. inermis, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem with distinctive red or pink spines that are shorter and less dense compared to the typical species. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects and bats. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
