Eulalia filifolia

Eulalia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Eulalia filifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its fine texture and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
