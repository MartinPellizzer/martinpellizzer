Erythrina castillejiflora

Erythrina castillejiflora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces distinctive seed pods that split open when mature. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
