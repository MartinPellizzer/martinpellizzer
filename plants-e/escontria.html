Escontria

Escontria, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and South America.

It belongs to the genus Maranta, which includes several species known for their ornamental foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by reddish berries that contain seeds. Its roots are often harvested for their starchy, edible tubers.

Escontria is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
