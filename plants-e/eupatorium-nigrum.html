Eupatorium nigrum

Eupatorium nigrum, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its name derives from the branching pattern of its stems, which resemble bones.

Eupatorium nigrum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
