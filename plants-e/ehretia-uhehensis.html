Ehretia uhehensis

Ehretia uhehensis, commonly known as the Nigerian Ehretia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
