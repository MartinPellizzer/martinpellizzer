Eupatorium brunneolum

Eupatorium brunneolum, commonly known as roughstem false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive grayish-green color.

The small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late summer.
