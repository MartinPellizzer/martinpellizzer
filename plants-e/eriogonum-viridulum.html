Eriogonum viridulum

Eriogonum viridulum, commonly known as the greenleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Eriogonum viridulum is an important plant for pollinators and is adapted to arid environments.
