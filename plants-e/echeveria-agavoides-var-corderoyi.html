Echeveria agavoides var. corderoyi

Echeveria agavoides var. corderoyi, commonly known as the Corderoy's Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers during the spring or early summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens or container planting due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
