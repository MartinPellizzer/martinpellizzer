Euhemus

Euhemus, commonly known as the red huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs and small trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a leathery texture.

Euhemus thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas.
