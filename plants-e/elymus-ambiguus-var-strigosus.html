Elymus ambiguus var. strigosus

Elymus ambiguus var. strigosus, commonly known as the hairy wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are often loosely arranged along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
