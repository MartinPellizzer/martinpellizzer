Erigeron pacificus

Erigeron pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific fleabane, is a herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
