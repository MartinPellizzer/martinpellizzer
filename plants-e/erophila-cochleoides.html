Erophila cochleoides

Erophila cochleoides, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant has slender, erect stems with opposite, narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in spring.

Erophila cochleoides is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species found in maritime environments.
