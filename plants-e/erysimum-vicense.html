Erysimum vicense

Erysimum vicense, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers and narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Erysimum vicense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms.
