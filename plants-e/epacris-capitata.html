Epacris capitata

Epacris capitata, commonly known as the clustered heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Epacris capitata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
