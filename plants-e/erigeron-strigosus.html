Erigeron strigosus

Erigeron strigosus, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
