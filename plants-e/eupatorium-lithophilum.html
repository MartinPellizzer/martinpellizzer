Eupatorium lithophilum

Eupatorium lithophilum, commonly known as the rock eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Eupatorium lithophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
