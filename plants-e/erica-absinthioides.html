Erica absinthioides

Erica absinthioides, commonly known as the silver heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica absinthioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
