Eupatorium chiapense

Eupatorium chiapense, commonly known as Chiapanensis boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Eupatorium chiapense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
