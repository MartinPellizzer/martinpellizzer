Euphorbia seguieriana var. gerardiana

Euphorbia seguieriana var. gerardiana, commonly known as the Gerard's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is a unique floral structure characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and striking foliage.
