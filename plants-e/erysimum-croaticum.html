Erysimum croaticum

Erysimum croaticum, commonly known as the Croatian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar characteristics. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It produces small, bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.
