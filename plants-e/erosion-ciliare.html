Erosion ciliare

Erosion ciliare, commonly known as the coastal ciliate, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including high salinity and strong winds. Its root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful in preventing erosion.

The species is often found in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines.
