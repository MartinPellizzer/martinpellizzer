Eugenia thouvenotiana

Eugenia thouvenotiana, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically bright red when ripe, encased in a thick, leathery rind. The fruit contains a segmented, juicy pulp that is prized for its sweet, tart flavor and aromatic qualities.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in warm climates for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
