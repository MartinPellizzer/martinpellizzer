Erysimum alliaceum

Erysimum alliaceum, commonly known as the garlic mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pungent odor, which resembles that of garlic, and its small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It grows up to 60 cm tall and is often found in woodland areas and along roadsides.

Erysimum alliaceum is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its potential as an invasive species in some regions.
