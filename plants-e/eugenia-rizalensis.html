Eugenia rizalensis

Eugenia rizalensis, commonly known as the Rizal's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is typically found in lowland and montane forests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in local cuisines.

This species plays an important role in its ecosystem as a food source for various wildlife species.
