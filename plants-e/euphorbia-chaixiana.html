Euphorbia chaixiana

Euphorbia chaixiana, commonly known as the Chaix's spurge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and sometimes succulent, aiding in water retention.

The flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia.
