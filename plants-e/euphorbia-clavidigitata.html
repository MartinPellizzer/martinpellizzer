Euphorbia clavidigitata

Euphorbia clavidigitata, commonly known as the fingered spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. It produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin.

Euphorbia clavidigitata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
