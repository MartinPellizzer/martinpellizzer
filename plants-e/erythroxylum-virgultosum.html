Erythroxylum virgultosum

Erythroxylum virgultosum, commonly known as the red balsa or yellow balsa, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and palmate leaves. The plant produces a lightweight, spongy wood that is used in various applications, including model building and as a substitute for balsa wood. The plant is also notable for its role in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in its native habitats.
