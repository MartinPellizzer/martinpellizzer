Euphorbia hepatica

Euphorbia hepatica, commonly known as the liverwort euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of a liver, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Euphorbia hepatica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
