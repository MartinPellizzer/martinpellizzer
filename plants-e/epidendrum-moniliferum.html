Epidendrum moniliferum

Epidendrum moniliferum, commonly known as the Necklace Orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are often dark green and glossy, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow and white, are arranged in a cascading pattern, making it a popular ornamental plant.
