Eleocharis palustris subsp. intersita

Eleocharis palustris subsp.

intersita, commonly known as the marsh spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments. It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems that bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
