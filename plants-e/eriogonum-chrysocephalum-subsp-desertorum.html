Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp. desertorum

Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp.

desertorum, commonly known as the Desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow to golden-orange flowers. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in its habitat.
