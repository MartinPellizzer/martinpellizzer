Epipactis viridans var. viridiflora

Epipactis viridans var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and its striking greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown patterns.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
