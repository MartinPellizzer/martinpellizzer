Erica caerulea

Erica caerulea, commonly known as the blue heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica caerulea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
