Eumorphanthus fragrans

Eumorphanthus fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, providing year-round visual interest.

The fragrance of its flowers attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
