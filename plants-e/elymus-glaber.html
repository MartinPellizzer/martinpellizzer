Elymus glaber

Elymus glaber, commonly known as stiff false wheat, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Elymus glaber is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
