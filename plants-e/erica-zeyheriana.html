Erica zeyheriana

Erica zeyheriana, commonly known as Zeyher's heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a notable ornamental plant in gardens.

Erica zeyheriana is also valued for its adaptability and resilience in Mediterranean climates.
