Escallonia alpina var. carmelitana

Escallonia alpina var. carmelitana, commonly known as Carmelita Escallonia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a subtle, spicy fragrance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
