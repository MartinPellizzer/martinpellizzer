Egeria chilensis

Egeria chilensis, commonly known as waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Hydrocharitaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant forms dense underwater mats, which provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. It is often found in freshwater lakes and slow-moving rivers.

Egeria chilensis can spread rapidly through vegetative reproduction, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in non-native environments.
