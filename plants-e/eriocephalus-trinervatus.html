Eriocephalus trinervatus

Eriocephalus trinervatus, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and the plant is known for its frost resistance.
