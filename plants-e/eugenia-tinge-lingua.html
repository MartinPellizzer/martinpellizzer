Eugenia tinge-lingua

Eugenia tinge-lingua, commonly known as the red-leafed myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often display a striking red or purple underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Eugenia tinge-lingua thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
