Euphorbia forolensis

Euphorbia forolensis, commonly known as the Forolensis spurge, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often thick and fleshy, storing water to survive in dry climates.

Euphorbia forolensis is notable for its striking yellow flowers, which appear in clusters and attract various pollinators.
