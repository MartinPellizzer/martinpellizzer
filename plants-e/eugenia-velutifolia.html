Eugenia velutifolia

Eugenia velutifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits. Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
