Echeveria × clavifolia

Echeveria × clavifolia, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a hybrid succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring or early summer.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
