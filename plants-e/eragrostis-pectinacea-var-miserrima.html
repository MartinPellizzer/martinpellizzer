Eragrostis pectinacea var. miserrima

Eragrostis pectinacea var. miserrima, commonly known as the weeping lovegrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
