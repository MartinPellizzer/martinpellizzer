Entada tuberosa var. pubescens

Entada tuberosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy or pubescent, giving it the varietal name *pubescens*. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers that are followed by woody pods containing spherical seeds. Its roots are known to be tuberous, which is a key botanical feature of the species.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
