Erysimum linariifolium

Erysimum linariifolium, commonly known as the linaria leafed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum linariifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
