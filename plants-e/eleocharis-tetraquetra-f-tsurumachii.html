Eleocharis tetraquetra f. tsurumachii

Eleocharis tetraquetra f. tsurumachii, commonly known as the Tsurumachi sedge, is a variant of the tetraquetra sedge species.

It is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and habitat preferences.
