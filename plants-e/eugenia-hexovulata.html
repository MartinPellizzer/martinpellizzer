Eugenia hexovulata

Eugenia hexovulata, commonly known as the Australian cherry or white myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, bright red berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for various purposes.
