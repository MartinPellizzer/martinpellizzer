Eryngium jaliscense

Eryngium jaliscense, commonly known as the Mexican thistle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ornamental gardening.

Eryngium jaliscense is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
