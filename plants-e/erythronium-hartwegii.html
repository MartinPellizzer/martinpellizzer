Erythronium hartwegii

Erythronium hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's dog's tooth violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces a single, upright stem bearing a showy, bell-shaped flower with bright yellow petals and a distinctive red or orange throat. It blooms in early spring, typically between March and May, and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
