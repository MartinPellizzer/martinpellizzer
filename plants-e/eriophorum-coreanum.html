Eriophorum coreanum

Eriophorum coreanum, commonly known as the Korean cottongrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces fluffy, white seed heads that resemble cotton. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are typically erect and slightly hairy.

Eriophorum coreanum thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas.
