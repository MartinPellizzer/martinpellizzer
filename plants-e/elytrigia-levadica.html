Elytrigia levadica

Elytrigia levadica, commonly known as the Levantine grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Elytrigia levadica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
