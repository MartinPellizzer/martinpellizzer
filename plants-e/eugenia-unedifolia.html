Eugenia unedifolia

Eugenia unedifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible red berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in coastal and tropical climates.
