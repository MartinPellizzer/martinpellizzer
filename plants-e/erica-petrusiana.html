Erica petrusiana

Erica petrusiana, commonly known as the Cape heath or African heather, is a species of flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and summer. Erica petrusiana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
