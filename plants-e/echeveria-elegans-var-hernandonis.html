Echeveria elegans var. hernandonis

Echeveria elegans var. hernandonis, commonly known as the Hernandonis Echeveria, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to Mexico.

It features vibrant, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a subtle powdery coating known as farina. The plant produces tall, branched stems with showy, pinkish-purple flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its striking appearance and easy care make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
