Eriogonum coloradense

Eriogonum coloradense, commonly known as the Colorado buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, spiny bracts and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often gray-green in color.

Eriogonum coloradense is an important component of local ecosystems and provides food for various pollinators.
