Eriocaulon spruceanum f. amphibium

Eriocaulon spruceanum f. amphibium, commonly known as the amphibious eriocaulon, is a unique variant of the genus Eriocaulon, characterized by its ability to thrive in both aquatic and terrestrial environments.

This plant exhibits a specialized morphology, with its leaves often submerged in water or partially emergent, adapting to fluctuating water levels. It is native to regions with moist, shaded habitats, such as wetlands and marshes, where it can tolerate periodic flooding. The plant's reproductive structures are typically found at the water's surface, facilitating pollination by wind or insects.
