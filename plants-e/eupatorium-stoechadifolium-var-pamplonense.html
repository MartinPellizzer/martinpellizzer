Eupatorium stoechadifolium var. pamplonense

Eupatorium stoechadifolium var. pamplonense, commonly known as the Pamplona eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pale blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of small, tubular florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
