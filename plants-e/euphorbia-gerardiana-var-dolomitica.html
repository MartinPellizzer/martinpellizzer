Euphorbia gerardiana var. dolomitica

Euphorbia gerardiana var. dolomitica, commonly known as the Dolomite spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

This variant is particularly noted for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
