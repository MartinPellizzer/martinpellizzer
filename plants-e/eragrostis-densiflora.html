Eragrostis densiflora

Eragrostis densiflora, commonly known as denseflower lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, South America, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Eragrostis densiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
