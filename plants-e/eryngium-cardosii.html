Eryngium cardosii

Eryngium cardosii, commonly known as the cardoon thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is part of the Asteraceae family.

Eryngium cardosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
