Euphorbia vittata

Euphorbia vittata, commonly known as the spotted spurge, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features bright green, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive yellowish-white spots along the midrib. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, which are specialized structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Euphorbia vittata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
