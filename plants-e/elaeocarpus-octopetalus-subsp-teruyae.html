Elaeocarpus octopetalus subsp. teruyae

Elaeocarpus octopetalus subsp.

teruyae, commonly known as the Philippine elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically encased in a fleshy, reddish or purple aril.

It is often found in lowland to montane forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
