Eucrosia eucrosioides var. rauhiana

Eucrosia eucrosioides var. rauhiana, commonly known as the Rauh's golden crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, typically from late winter to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries due to its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
