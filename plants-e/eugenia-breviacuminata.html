Eugenia breviacuminata

Eugenia breviacuminata, commonly known as the short-aculeate eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a reddish color.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
