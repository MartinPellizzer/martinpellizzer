Eleocharis subsphacelata

Eleocharis subsphacelata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, often acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
