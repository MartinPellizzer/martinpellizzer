Erythrina lithosperma var. armata

Erythrina lithosperma var. armata, commonly known as the Crimson Flame Acacia, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in dense, spherical clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is often fissured and covered in a rough, greyish texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
