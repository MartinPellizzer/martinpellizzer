Episcia cupreata

Episcia cupreata, commonly known as the copperleaf plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from bronze to deep green, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Episcia cupreata is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
