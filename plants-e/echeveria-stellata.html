Echeveria stellata

Echeveria stellata, commonly known as the star echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a powdery coating called farina. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Echeveria stellata is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
