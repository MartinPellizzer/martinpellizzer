Eucalyptus loxophleba subsp. lissophloia

Eucalyptus loxophleba subsp.

lissophloia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a subspecies of eucalyptus native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, greyish bark.

The plant typically grows in low shrubland and woodland habitats, often in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pink in color.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and fire.
