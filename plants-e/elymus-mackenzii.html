Elymus mackenzii

Elymus mackenzii, commonly known as Mackenzie's wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Elymus mackenzii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
