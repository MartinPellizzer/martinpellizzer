Eleocharis zinserlingii

Eleocharis zinserlingii, commonly known as the Zinserling's spike-rush, is a species of sedges native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, dry fruits that disperse via water or wind.

Eleocharis zinserlingii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
