Eragrostis vinicolor var. pallida

Eragrostis vinicolor var. pallida, commonly known as pale bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and delicate, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the growing season, making it a desirable ornamental plant.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil conditions.
