Elymus tzvelevii

Elymus tzvelevii, commonly known as Tzvelev's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blade-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Elymus tzvelevii is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
