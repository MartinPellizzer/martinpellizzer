Epilobium lobelii

Epilobium lobelii, commonly known as the mountain willow or lobel's willow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in alpine meadows. The plant features slender stems covered in fine hairs and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Epilobium lobelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
