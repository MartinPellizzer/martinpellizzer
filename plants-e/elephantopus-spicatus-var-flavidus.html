Elephantopus spicatus var. flavidus

Elephantopus spicatus var. flavidus, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it has a low-growing habit.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
