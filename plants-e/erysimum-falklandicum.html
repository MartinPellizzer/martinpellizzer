Erysimum falklandicum

Erysimum falklandicum, commonly known as Falkland Island wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Falkland Islands.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Erysimum falklandicum is well adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat, thriving in sandy and rocky soils.
