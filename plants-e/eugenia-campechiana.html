Eugenia campechiana

Eugenia campechiana, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or Campeche cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitats.
