Euphorbia dulcis subsp. purpurata

Euphorbia dulcis subsp.

purpurata, commonly known as the purple milkweed, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, upright stems that are often tinged with a reddish or purplish hue, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive central structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy choice for arid or rocky gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in xeriscaping.
