Eurya emarginata var. ryukyuensis

Eurya emarginata var. ryukyuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu Eurya, is a shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with acidic soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
