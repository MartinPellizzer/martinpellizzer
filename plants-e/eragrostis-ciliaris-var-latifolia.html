Eragrostis ciliaris var. latifolia

Eragrostis ciliaris var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This variety thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils. It is often used in turfgrass mixes for its fine texture and durability.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
