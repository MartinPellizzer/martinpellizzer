Elaphoglossum procurrens

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into lanceolate, serrated leaves. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Elaphoglossum procurrens thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
