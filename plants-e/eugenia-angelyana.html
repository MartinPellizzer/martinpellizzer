Eugenia angelyana

Eugenia angelyana, commonly known as the angel's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
