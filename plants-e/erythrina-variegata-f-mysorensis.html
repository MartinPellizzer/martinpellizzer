Erythrina variegata f. mysorensis

Erythrina variegata f. mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore coral tree, is a variant of the coral tree species native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual display to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and its woody structure supports climbing or shrub-like growth forms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a shade provider in urban settings.
