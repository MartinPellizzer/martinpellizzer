Erophila glabrescens

Erophila glabrescens, commonly known as the silky centipede plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
