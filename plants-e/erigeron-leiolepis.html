Erigeron leiolepis

Erigeron leiolepis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters of white to pale blue.

It serves as a valuable nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
