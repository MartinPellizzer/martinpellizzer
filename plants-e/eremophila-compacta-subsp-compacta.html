Eremophila compacta subsp. compacta

Eremophila compacta subsp.

compacta, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Emu Bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

This species is often used in native landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
