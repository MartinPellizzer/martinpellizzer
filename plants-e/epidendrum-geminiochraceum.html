Epidendrum geminiochraceum

Epidendrum geminiochraceum, commonly known as the twin-lobed epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated pseudobulbs covered in a layer of white to pale green sheath-like structures. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, with showy, fragrant blooms that range in color from white to pale yellow.

Epidendrum geminiochraceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
