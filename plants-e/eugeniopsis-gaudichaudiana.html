Eugeniopsis gaudichaudiana

Eugeniopsis gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage and showy blooms.
