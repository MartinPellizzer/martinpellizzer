Elephantopus elatus var. intermedius

Elephantopus elatus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate elephantopus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
