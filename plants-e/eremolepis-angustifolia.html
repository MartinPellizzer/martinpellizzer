Eremolepis angustifolia

Eremolepis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eremolepis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
