Erigeron rubricaulis

Erigeron rubricaulis, commonly known as the redstem fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are reddish in color, giving it its common name.

It produces small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
