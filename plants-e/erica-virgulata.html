Erica virgulata

Erica virgulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple. This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is well adapted to harsh climates and can tolerate drought and salt spray.

Erica virgulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
