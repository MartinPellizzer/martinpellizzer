Evolvulus glaber

Evolvulus glaber, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, opening in the morning and closing in the afternoon. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, inflated fruit that splits open when mature to release the seeds.

Evolvulus glaber is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
