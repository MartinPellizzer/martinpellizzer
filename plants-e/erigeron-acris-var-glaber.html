Erigeron acris var. glaber

Erigeron acris var. glaber, commonly known as stiffleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are generally stiff and hairy, giving it its common name.

The showy blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
