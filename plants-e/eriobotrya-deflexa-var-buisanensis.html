Eriobotrya deflexa var. buisanensis

Eriobotrya deflexa var. buisanensis, commonly known as the Bisanensis loquat, is a variant of the loquat species native to China.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in subtropical climates. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring. Its fruit, similar to the common loquat, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in gardens.
