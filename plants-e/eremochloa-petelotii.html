Eremochloa petelotii

Eremochloa petelotii, commonly known as the desert bamboo, is a perennial grass native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems and small, narrow leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces inflorescences that are often clustered and resemble panicles, typical of the grass family. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control in semi-arid climates.
