Empetrum nigrum subsp. nigrum

Empetrum nigrum subsp.

nigrum, commonly known as common crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features small, dark blue berries that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with prostrate stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are small, leathery, and dark green, providing dense ground cover.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.
