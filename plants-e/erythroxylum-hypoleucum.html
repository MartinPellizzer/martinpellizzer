Erythroxylum hypoleucum

Erythroxylum hypoleucum, commonly known as the white copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in lowland and mountainous areas, growing in humid, shaded environments.

The plant has historical significance in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
