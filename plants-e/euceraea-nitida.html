Euceraea nitida

Euceraea nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf euceraea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or pale green, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Euceraea nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
