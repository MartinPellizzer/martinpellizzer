Erysimum linifolium f. albiflorum

Erysimum linifolium f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered wall poppy, is a horticultural form of the common wall poppy.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.

The foliage is generally hairless, and the plant is known for its compact, upright growth habit.
