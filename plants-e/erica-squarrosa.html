Erica squarrosa

Erica squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Erica squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
