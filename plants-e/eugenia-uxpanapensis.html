Eugenia uxpanapensis

Eugenia uxpanapensis, commonly known as the Mexican wax plum, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
