Euphorbia seguieriana var. latifolia

Euphorbia seguieriana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive dry conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
