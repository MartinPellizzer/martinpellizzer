Erythronium sajanense var. argenteum

Erythronium sajanense var. argenteum, commonly known as the silver-flowered dog's tooth violet, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a silvery or pale green hue, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variant is a subspecies of Erythronium sajanense, which is part of the Colocasiaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a plant of conservation interest.
