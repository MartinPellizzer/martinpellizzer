Elaeocarpus miriensis

Elaeocarpus miriensis, commonly known as the Miriensis Elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of New Guinea and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Elaeocarpus miriensis typically grows as a medium to large tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters, and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as both a habitat provider and a contributor to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
