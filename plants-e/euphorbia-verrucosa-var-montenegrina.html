Euphorbia verrucosa var. montenegrina

Euphorbia verrucosa var. montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegrin spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Balkan region.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
