Eulophia vanoverberghii

Eulophia vanoverberghii, commonly known as the Vanoverbergh's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomes and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eulophia vanoverberghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
