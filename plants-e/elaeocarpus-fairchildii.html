Elaeocarpus fairchildii

Elaeocarpus fairchildii, commonly known as the Fairchild's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, edible seeds. This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

It plays a significant role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining forest biodiversity.
