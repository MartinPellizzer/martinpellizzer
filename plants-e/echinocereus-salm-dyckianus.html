Echinocereus salm-dyckianus

Echinocereus salm-dyckianus, commonly known as the Salm-Dyck hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and areoles, which are specialized structures for hosting flowers and branches. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its adaptations include succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Echinocereus salm-dyckianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
