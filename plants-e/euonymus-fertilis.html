Euonymus fertilis

Euonymus fertilis, commonly known as the common euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and colorful fruits, making it a popular choice in landscaping.

Euonymus fertilis is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in a variety of soil types.
