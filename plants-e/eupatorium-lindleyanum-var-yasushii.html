Eupatorium lindleyanum var. yasushii

Eupatorium lindleyanum var. yasushii, commonly known as the Japanese eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
