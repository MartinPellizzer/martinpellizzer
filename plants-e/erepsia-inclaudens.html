Erepsia inclaudens

Erepsia inclaudens, commonly known as the clustered ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, leathery leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces bell-shaped, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Erepsia inclaudens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
