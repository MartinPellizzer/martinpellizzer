Eriogonum effusum var. foliosum

Eriogonum effusum var. foliosum, commonly known as the leafy wild buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental qualities in native plant gardens.
