Epidendrum fraudulentum

Epidendrum fraudulentum, commonly known as the false orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
