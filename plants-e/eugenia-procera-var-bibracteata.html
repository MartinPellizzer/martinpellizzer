Eugenia procera var. bibracteata

Eugenia procera var. bibracteata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia bibracteata*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and ornamental purposes.
