Embelia rugosa

Embelia rugosa, commonly known as the Chinese starfruit or wild starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange-red fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include aromatic leaves and a distinctive, clustered fruiting habit.

Embelia rugosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
