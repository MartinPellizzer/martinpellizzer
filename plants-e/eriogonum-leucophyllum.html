Eriogonum leucophyllum

Eriogonum leucophyllum, commonly known as silverleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Silverleaf buckwheat is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its ecological importance to pollinators and wildlife.
