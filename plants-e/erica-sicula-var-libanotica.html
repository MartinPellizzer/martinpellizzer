Erica sicula var. libanotica

Erica sicula var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand dry and rocky conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
