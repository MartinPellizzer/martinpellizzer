Epilobium tournefortii

Epilobium tournefortii, commonly known as the Sierra buckthorn, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.

Epilobium tournefortii is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.
