Eupatorium ceratophyllum

Eupatorium ceratophyllum, commonly known as the spiked eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy. Its most distinctive feature is the showy, pale blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer.

Eupatorium ceratophyllum is often found in wetlands and moist soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
