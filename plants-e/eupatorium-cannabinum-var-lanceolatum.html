Eupatorium cannabinum var. lanceolatum

Eupatorium cannabinum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as hemp agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its name reflects its historical association with hemp and its use in herbal remedies.
