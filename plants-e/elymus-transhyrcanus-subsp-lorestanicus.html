Elymus transhyrcanus subsp. lorestanicus

Elymus transhyrcanus subsp.

lorestanicus, commonly known as the Lorestan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces inflorescences composed of spikelets that are often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
