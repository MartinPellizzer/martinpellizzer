Erigeron alpinus var. major

Erigeron alpinus var. major, commonly known as alpine blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine gardens.
