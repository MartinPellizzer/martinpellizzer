Exochanthus pleianthus

Exochanthus pleianthus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
