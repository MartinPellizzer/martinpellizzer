Eragrostis amurensis

Eragrostis amurensis, commonly known as Amur lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

However, it can also become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
