Elaeocarpus confertifolius

Elaeocarpus confertifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
