Equisetum laevigatum var. scabrellum

Equisetum laevigatum var. scabrellum, commonly known as the rough horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered in rough, scaly texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. It produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stems, which are essential for reproduction.

This variety is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period.
