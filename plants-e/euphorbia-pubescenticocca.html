Euphorbia pubescenticocca

Euphorbia pubescenticocca, commonly known as the hairy euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem covered in short, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
