Euphorbia abdulghafooriana

Euphorbia abdulghafooriana, commonly known as the Abdulghafoor's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and fleshy, green stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of yellow and green, typically clustered in a structure called a cyathium. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a milky sap that can be toxic.

Euphorbia abdulghafooriana is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low moisture conditions.
