Eugenia incrassata

Eugenia incrassata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Barriguda*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of eastern Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Eugenia incrassata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
