Eulophidium petiolatum

Eulophidium petiolatum, commonly known as the slender leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, exhibiting a grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, aiding in soil stabilization.
