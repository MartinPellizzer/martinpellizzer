Erigeron Abyssinicus

Erigeron abyssinicus is a striking member of the Asteraceae family known for its vibrant blue and purple flower heads. Native to the highlands of Ethiopia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its daisy-like appearance makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and ecological value. The plant's ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds to its ecological significance. Despite its ornamental appeal, Erigeron abyssinicus remains relatively obscure in mainstream horticulture.
