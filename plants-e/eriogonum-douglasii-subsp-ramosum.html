Eriogonum douglasii subsp. ramosum

Eriogonum douglasii subsp.

ramosum, commonly known as Douglas's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
