Eugenia guayaquilensis

Eugenia guayaquilensis, commonly known as the Guayaquil wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
