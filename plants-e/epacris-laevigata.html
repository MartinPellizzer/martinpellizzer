Epacris laevigata

Epacris laevigata, commonly known as the smooth epacris, is an evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red and are borne in terminal spikes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
