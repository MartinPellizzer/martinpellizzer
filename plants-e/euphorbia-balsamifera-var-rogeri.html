Euphorbia balsamifera var. rogeri

Euphorbia balsamifera var. rogeri, commonly known as the Roger's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
