Euphorbia cyathophora var. graminifolia

Euphorbia cyathophora var. graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
