Eucalyptus subangusta subsp. pusilla

Eucalyptus subangusta subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malleeflower, is a small shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as mallee woodlands. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought resistance and low water requirements.

This subspecies is of interest for its ornamental value and potential ecological roles in native landscapes.
