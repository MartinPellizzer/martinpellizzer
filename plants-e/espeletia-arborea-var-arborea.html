Espeletia arborea var. arborea

Espeletia arborea var. arborea, commonly known as the tree heather, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and alpine landscapes.
