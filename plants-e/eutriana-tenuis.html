Eutriana tenuis

Eutriana tenuis, commonly known as the slender eutriana, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy areas near rivers. It plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat for insects and small aquatic life.

Due to its preference for wet environments, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
