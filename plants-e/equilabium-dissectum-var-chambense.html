Equilabium dissectum var. chambense

Equilabium dissectum var. chambense, commonly known as the Chambon's dissected bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often with intricate patterns. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are finely dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
