Eriocaulon rhodae

Eriocaulon rhodae, commonly known as the red-stemmed spike-moss, is a small, terrestrial moss belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of leaves that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and pointed, with a reddish hue when mature.

Eriocaulon rhodae plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various small organisms.
