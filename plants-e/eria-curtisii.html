Eria curtisii

Eria curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and unique floral structure. Eria curtisii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
