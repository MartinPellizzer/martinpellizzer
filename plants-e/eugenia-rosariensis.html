Eugenia rosariensis

Eugenia rosariensis, commonly known as the rose apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
