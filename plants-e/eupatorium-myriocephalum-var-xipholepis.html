Eupatorium myriocephalum var. xipholepis

Eupatorium myriocephalum var. xipholepis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features tall, branched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
