Epidendrum stamfordianum var. lawrenceanum

Epidendrum stamfordianum var. lawrenceanum, commonly known as the Lawrenceanum Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that bear clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements in a suitable growing environment.
