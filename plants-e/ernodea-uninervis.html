Ernodea uninervis

Ernodea uninervis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a slender, climbing stem with pinnately compound leaves that have a single leaflet. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing nodules, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

The plant is often used as a forage crop and in traditional medicine.
