Eupatorium sagittatum var. deltophyllum

Eupatorium sagittatum var. deltophyllum, commonly known as the arrowhead eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
