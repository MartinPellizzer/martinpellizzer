Epilobium × brunnatum

Epilobium × brunnatum, commonly known as the brown willow herb, is a hybrid species within the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, often branched stems that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that are arranged in terminal clusters, typical of the genus Epilobium. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Epilobium × brunnatum is typically found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including wetlands and roadside areas.
