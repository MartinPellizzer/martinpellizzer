Eupatorium pachanoi

Eupatorium pachanoi, commonly known as the sacred plant of the Andes, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of southern Peru and northern Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional Andean practices.
