Eupatorium betoniciforme var. hypoleucum

Eupatorium betoniciforme var. hypoleucum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
