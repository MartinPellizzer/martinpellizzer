Eulophia merrillii

Eulophia merrillii, commonly known as the Merrill's eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia merrillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
