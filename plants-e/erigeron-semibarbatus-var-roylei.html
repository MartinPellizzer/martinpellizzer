Erigeron semibarbatus var. roylei

Erigeron semibarbatus var. roylei, commonly known as the Himalayan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of white to pale blue.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
