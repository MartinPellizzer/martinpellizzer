Elymus kuramensis

Elymus kuramensis, commonly known as Kuram grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Elymus kuramensis is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its natural distribution across its habitat.
