Epidendrum pedicellare

Epidendrum pedicellare, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, often with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum pedicellare is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
