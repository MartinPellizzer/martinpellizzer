Eriocaulon pubescens

Eriocaulon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

The flowers are small and clustered, with a pale greenish or brownish color, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
