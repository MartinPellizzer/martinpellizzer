Euphorbia mitchelliana var. oblongifolia

Euphorbia mitchelliana var. oblongifolia, commonly known as Mitchell's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features a short, woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are its unique flower-like structures. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and distinctive foliage.
