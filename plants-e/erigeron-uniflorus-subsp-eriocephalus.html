Erigeron uniflorus subsp. eriocephalus

Erigeron uniflorus subsp.

eriocephalus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
