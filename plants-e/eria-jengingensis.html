Eria jengingensis

Eria jengingensis, commonly known as the Jenging orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences with a distinctive coloration.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Eria jengingensis is considered a valuable subject in botanical research and conservation efforts.
