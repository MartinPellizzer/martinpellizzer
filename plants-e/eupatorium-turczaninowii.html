Eupatorium turczaninowii

Eupatorium turczaninowii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
