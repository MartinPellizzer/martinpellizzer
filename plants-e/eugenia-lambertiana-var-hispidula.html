Eugenia lambertiana var. hispidula

Eugenia lambertiana var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential ornamental use.
