Elytrigia strigosa subsp. aegilopoides

Elytrigia strigosa subsp.

aegilopoides, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
