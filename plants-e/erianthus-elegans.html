Erianthus elegans

Erianthus elegans, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Erianthus elegans thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.
