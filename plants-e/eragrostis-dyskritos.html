Eragrostis dyskritos

Eragrostis dyskritos, commonly known as the dwarf false wheatgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny grains. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient to harsh conditions. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils, and its growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control in degraded landscapes.

The species is of interest in ecological and agricultural studies due to its potential for sustainable land management.
