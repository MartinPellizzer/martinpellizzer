Eupatorium pictum

Eupatorium pictum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its showy appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Eupatorium pictum also serves as a host plant for several species of butterflies and is valued for its ecological benefits.
