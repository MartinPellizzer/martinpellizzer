Elaeocarpus lecardii

Elaeocarpus lecardii, commonly known as the LeCard's elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit is a woody, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
