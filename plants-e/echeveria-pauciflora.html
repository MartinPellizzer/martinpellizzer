Echeveria pauciflora

Echeveria pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved echeveria, is a compact, succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are narrow and pointed, often with a powdery coating. The plant produces small, pale pink to lavender flowers on tall stems during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its low maintenance and attractive appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
