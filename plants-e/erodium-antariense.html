Erodium antariense

Erodium antariense, commonly known as the Antarctic catchfly, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, delicate leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits called capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
