Eremopogon delavayi

Eremopogon delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's false rye, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in alpine meadows or rocky soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinct flowering structure that resembles a spikelet.

Eremopogon delavayi is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
