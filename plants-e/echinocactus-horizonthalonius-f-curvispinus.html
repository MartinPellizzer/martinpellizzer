Echinocactus horizonthalonius f. curvispinus

Echinocactus horizonthalonius f. curvispinus, commonly known as the Mexican spineless cactus, is a unique variation of the standard horizonthalonius species.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. Its spineless appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in arid gardens.

This cultivar is native to Mexico and thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.
