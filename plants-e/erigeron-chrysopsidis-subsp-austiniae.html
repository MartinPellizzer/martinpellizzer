Erigeron chrysopsidis subsp. austiniae

Erigeron chrysopsidis subsp.

austiniae, commonly known as Austin's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the central Texas region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its foliage is generally gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.
