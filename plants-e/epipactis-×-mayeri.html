Epipactis × mayeri

Epipactis × mayeri, commonly known as the hybrid marsh helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a hybrid between Epipactis palustris and Epipactis helleborine, characterized by its greenish-yellow flowers with dark purple markings. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its hybrid origin and ecological adaptability.
