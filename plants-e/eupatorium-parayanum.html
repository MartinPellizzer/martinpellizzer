Eupatorium parayanum

Eupatorium parayanum, commonly known as the Parayanum dewflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Eupatorium parayanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
