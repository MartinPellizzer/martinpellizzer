Elaeocarpus comptonii

Elaeocarpus comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or purple when mature, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
