Erica rastellum

Erica rastellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica rastellum typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
