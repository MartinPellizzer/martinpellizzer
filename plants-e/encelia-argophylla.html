Encelia argophylla

Encelia argophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Encelia argophylla is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant once established.
