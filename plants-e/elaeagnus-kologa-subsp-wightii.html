Elaeagnus kologa subsp. wightii

Elaeagnus kologa subsp.

wightii, commonly known as the Indian silver bush, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, edible fruits that are used in local cuisines.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in degraded landscapes.
