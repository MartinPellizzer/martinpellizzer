Eugenia rubropurpurea

Eugenia rubropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leafed guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple leaves, which contrast with its small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, though not widely cultivated, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
