Eupatorium squarrulosum

Eupatorium squarrulosum, commonly known as rough eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. Its showy, pale blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer and are arranged in clusters.

Eupatorium squarrulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
