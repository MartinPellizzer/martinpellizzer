Eccoilopus cotulifer

Eccoilopus cotulifer, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a sprawling or upright form depending on environmental conditions.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
