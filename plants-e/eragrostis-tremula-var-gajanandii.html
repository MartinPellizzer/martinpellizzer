Eragrostis tremula var. gajanandii

Eragrostis tremula var. gajanandii, commonly known as the Gajanandii lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
