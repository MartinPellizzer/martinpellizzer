Elymus rigidulus

Elymus rigidulus, commonly known as prairie grass or stiff wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Elymus rigidulus thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies, meadows, and disturbed areas.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
