Echinopsis densispina f. haematantha

Echinopsis densispina f. haematantha, commonly known as the red-flowered echinopsis, is a striking cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and vibrant red flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique coloration.
