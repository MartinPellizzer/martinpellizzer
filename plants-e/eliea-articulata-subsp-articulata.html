Eliea articulata subsp. articulata

Eliea articulata subsp.

articulata, commonly known as the articulated eliea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa. It is characterized by its unique, segmented stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
