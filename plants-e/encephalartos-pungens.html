Encephalartos pungens

Encephalartos pungens, commonly known as the Natal cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically appearing on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
