Erianthus rufus

Erianthus rufus, commonly known as the red grass or red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, tufted inflorescences that often display a reddish hue. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib and a rough texture.

Erianthus rufus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
