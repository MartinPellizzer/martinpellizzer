Eucalyptus intertexta var. diminuta

Eucalyptus intertexta var. diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flowers that are typically cream or pale yellow, which appear in clusters. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and heat.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
