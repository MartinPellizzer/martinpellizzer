Euphorbia triflora subsp. kerneri

Euphorbia triflora subsp.

kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's milkweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, aiding in survival in arid environments.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its milky sap, characteristic of the Euphorbia genus, contains toxic compounds that can cause skin irritation.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
