Erica comorensis subsp. anjurensis

Erica comorensis subsp.

anjurensis, commonly known as the Comoros heath, is a shrub native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments. Erica comorensis subsp.

anjurensis is often used in local horticulture and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
