Eupatorium ravenii

Eupatorium ravenii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, purple flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and swampy areas.

Eupatorium ravenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
