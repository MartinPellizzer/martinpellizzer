Euphorbia korshinskyi

Euphorbia korshinskyi, commonly known as the Mexican fire plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiky, branching structure. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems and leaves.

Euphorbia korshinskyi is often cultivated for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
