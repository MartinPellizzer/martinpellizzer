Eulophidium gracillimum

Eulophidium gracillimum, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in late spring. Eulophidium gracillimum is typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and pine flatwoods.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
