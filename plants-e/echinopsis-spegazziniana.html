Echinopsis spegazziniana

Echinopsis spegazziniana, commonly known as the Spegazzini's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a spiny exterior with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Echinopsis spegazziniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
