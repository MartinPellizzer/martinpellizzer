Eugenia emirnensis var. podocarpifolia

Eugenia emirnensis var. podocarpifolia, commonly known as the Emirnensis myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, dark purple berries.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
