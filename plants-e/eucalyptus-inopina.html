Eucalyptus inopina

Eucalyptus inopina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The flowers are usually white or cream and are arranged in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding insects and birds. This plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid environments of its native range.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
