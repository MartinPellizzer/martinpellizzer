Eriogonum brevicaule var. promiscuum

Eriogonum brevicaule var. promiscuum, commonly known as the sticky leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in harsh conditions.
