Elymus panormitanus

Elymus panormitanus, commonly known as the Mediterranean wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Elymus panormitanus is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient component of Mediterranean vegetation.
