Echinodorus glaucus

Echinodorus glaucus, commonly known as the blue velvet waterweed, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often a vibrant blue-green color, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in slow to moderately flowing freshwater environments, making it a popular choice for aquariums. It is known for its ability to purify water by absorbing excess nutrients.

Echinodorus glaucus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ease of care in both natural and artificial aquatic settings.
