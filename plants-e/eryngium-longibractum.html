Eryngium longibractum

Eryngium longibractum, commonly known as the narrow bract eryngium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It features striking spiky, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by stiff, leaf-like bracts. The plant has a robust root system and grows in moist to wet soils, often found in prairies and wetlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture.

Eryngium longibractum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
