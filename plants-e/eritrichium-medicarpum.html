Eritrichium medicarpum

Eritrichium medicarpum, commonly known as the medicinal chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has alternate, hairy leaves that are oval to lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Historically, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
