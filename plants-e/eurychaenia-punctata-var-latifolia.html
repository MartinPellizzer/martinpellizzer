Eurychaenia punctata var. latifolia

Eurychaenia punctata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically broad and oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
