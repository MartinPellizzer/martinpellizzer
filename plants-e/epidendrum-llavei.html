Epidendrum llavei

Epidendrum llavei, commonly known as the Llave's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in racemes, and are fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
