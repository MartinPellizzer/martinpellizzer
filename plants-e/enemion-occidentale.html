Enemion occidentale

Enemion occidentale, commonly known as the western pimpernel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Enemion occidentale is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
