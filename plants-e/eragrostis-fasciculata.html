Eragrostis fasciculata

Eragrostis fasciculata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
