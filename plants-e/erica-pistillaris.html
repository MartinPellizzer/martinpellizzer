Erica pistillaris

Erica pistillaris, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other shrubs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Erica pistillaris thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
