Erodium glabellum

Erodium glabellum, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Erodium glabellum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
