Echinopsis ancistrophora var. kratochviliana

Echinopsis ancistrophora var. kratochviliana, commonly known as the Kratochvil's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is typically covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habits, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
