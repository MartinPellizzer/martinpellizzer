Eryngium proteiflorum

Eryngium proteiflorum, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiky, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Eryngium proteiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
