Elymus parishii

Elymus parishii, commonly known as Parish's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions.

Elymus parishii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
