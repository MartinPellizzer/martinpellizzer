Echites lappulaceus

Echites lappulaceus, commonly known as the lappula, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It has a short life cycle and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
