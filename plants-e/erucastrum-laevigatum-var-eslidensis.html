Erucastrum laevigatum var. eslidensis

Erucastrum laevigatum var. eslidensis, commonly known as the Eslandian cress, is a variant of the broadleaf cress species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and field margins. Its fruits are short, cylindrical siliques containing numerous small, oval seeds.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and ecological adaptations.
