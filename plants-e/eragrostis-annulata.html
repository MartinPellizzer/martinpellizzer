Eragrostis annulata

Eragrostis annulata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis annulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
