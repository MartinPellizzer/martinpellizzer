Eulophia epiphytica

Eulophia epiphytica, commonly known as the rock orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in nutrient-poor environments. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

It requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth and flowering.
