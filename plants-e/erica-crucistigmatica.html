Erica crucistigmatica

Erica crucistigmatica, commonly known as the cross-stem heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like form and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica crucistigmatica is adapted to Mediterranean climates, with a dormant period during the wet season and active growth during the dry season.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
