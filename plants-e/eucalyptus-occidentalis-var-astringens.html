Eucalyptus occidentalis var. astringens

Eucalyptus occidentalis var. astringens, commonly known as the Sydney red gum, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is a tall, slender tree with smooth, peeling bark that reveals a red or pinkish inner layer. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside, while the flowers are typically white or pale blue. This variant is known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and for their antiseptic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.
