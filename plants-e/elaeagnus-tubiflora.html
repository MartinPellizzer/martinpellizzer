Elaeagnus tubiflora

Elaeagnus tubiflora, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Elaeagnus tubiflora has a shallow root system that can help stabilize soil in arid environments.
