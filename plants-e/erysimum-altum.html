Erysimum altum

Erysimum altum, commonly known as the tall wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often hairy as well.

Erysimum altum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often found growing along roadsides and in rocky areas.
