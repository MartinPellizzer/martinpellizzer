Eugenia cristaensis

Eugenia cristaensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, though they are less commonly cultivated than other guava species.

Its ornamental value is often appreciated in gardens due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
