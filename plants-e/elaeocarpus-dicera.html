Elaeocarpus dicera

Elaeocarpus dicera, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits with a bright red color. Its fruit is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, making it a valuable resource in some regions.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
