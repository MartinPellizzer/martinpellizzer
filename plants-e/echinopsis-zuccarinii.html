Echinopsis zuccarinii

Echinopsis zuccarinii, commonly known as the Zuccarini's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, columnar stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.

Echinopsis zuccarinii is relatively easy to care for, making it a favored plant among cactus enthusiasts.
