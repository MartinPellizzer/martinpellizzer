Erica isaloensis

Erica isaloensis, commonly known as the Isalo heath, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Erica isaloensis is adapted to the unique climatic conditions of its native habitat, making it a distinctive example of Madagascar's flora.
