Erica cinerea var. numidica

Erica cinerea var. numidica, commonly known as the Numidian heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
