Eria braddonii

Eria braddonii, commonly known as Braddon's orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with red or purple markings, and it produces a single, elongated spike of blooms.

Eria braddonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens.
