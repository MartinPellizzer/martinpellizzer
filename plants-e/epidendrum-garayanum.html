Epidendrum garayanum

Epidendrum garayanum, commonly known as the Guatemalan orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from white to yellow and red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Epidendrum garayanum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
