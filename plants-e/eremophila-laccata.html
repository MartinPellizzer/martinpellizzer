Eremophila laccata

Eremophila laccata, commonly known as the scarlet eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Eremophila laccata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
