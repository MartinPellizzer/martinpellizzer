Epiphyllum ackermannii f. candidum

Epiphyllum ackermannii f. candidum, commonly known as the white-flowered night blooming cereus, is a hybrid cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that grow horizontally or vertically, often forming a shrub-like structure. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night and emit a sweet fragrance, typically in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with bright, indirect light.

This species is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
