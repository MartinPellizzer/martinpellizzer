Eremophila latrobei subsp. latrobei

Eremophila latrobei subsp.

latrobei, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its grey-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats.

The species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
