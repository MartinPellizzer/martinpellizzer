Epilobium glandulosum subsp. maximowiczii

Epilobium glandulosum subsp.

maximowiczii, commonly known as the glandular willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as streambanks, meadows, and wetlands.

The plant features slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with glandular dots. Its showy, pink to purple flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
