Erophila boerhaavii var. linearifolia

Erophila boerhaavii var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in shallow water. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in muddy or sandy soils along the edges of ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and wildlife.
