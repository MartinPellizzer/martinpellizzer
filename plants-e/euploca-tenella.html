Euploca tenella

Euploca tenella, commonly known as the slender euploca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Euploca tenella is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
