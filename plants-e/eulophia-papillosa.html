Eulophia papillosa

Eulophia papillosa, commonly known as the papillate eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, showy flowers arranged in a raceme.

Eulophia papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
