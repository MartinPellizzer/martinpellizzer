Epidendrum labiatum var. pallidum

Epidendrum labiatum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale-lipped epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces long, pendant inflorescences. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a distinctive lip that is often lighter in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
