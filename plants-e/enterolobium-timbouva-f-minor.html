Enterolobium timbouva f. minor

Enterolobium timbouva f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf timbouva tree, is a small to medium-sized leguminous shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in reforestation efforts.
