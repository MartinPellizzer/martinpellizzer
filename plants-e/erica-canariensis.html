Erica canariensis

Erica canariensis, commonly known as the Canary heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly referred to as the heath or heather family. The plant features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica canariensis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
