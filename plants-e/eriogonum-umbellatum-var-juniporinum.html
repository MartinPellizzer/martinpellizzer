Eriogonum umbellatum var. juniporinum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. juniporinum, commonly known as the Juniperleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, needle-like leaves that resemble juniper foliage, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
