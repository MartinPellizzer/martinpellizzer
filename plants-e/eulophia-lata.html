Eulophia lata

Eulophia lata, commonly known as the broad-leafed eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are often found in symbiotic relationships with specific fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Eulophia lata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
