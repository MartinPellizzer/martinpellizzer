Eulophia dissimilis

Eulophia dissimilis, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that resemble other orchid species, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and shrublands. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, with petals and sepals that are often brightly colored.

Eulophia dissimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
