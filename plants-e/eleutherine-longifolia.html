Eleutherine longifolia

Eleutherine longifolia, commonly known as the purple lily or elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a tuberous root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical name reflects its long, slender leaves and its historical significance in herbal remedies.
