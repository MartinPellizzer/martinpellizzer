Eriogonum nudum var. pauciflorum

Eriogonum nudum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the sparse-flowered buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
