Eriocaulon nipponicum var. glaberrimum

Eriocaulon nipponicum var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the Japanese hairgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. The plant is native to Japan and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and grassy, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other varieties within the species.
