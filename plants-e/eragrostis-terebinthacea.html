Eragrostis terebinthacea

Eragrostis terebinthacea, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Eragrostis terebinthacea is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
