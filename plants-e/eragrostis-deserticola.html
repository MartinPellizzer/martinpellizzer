Eragrostis deserticola

Eragrostis deserticola, commonly known as desert lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.

Due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil, it plays a significant role in ecological restoration projects.
