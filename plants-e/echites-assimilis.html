Echites assimilis

Echites assimilis, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that transition into spherical seed heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
