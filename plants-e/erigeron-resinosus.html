Erigeron resinosus

Erigeron resinosus, commonly known as resinous fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are white to pale blue in color.

It produces a sticky resin that can be observed on its stems and leaves, contributing to its common name.
