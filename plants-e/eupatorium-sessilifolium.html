Eupatorium sessilifolium

Eupatorium sessilifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, sessile leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
