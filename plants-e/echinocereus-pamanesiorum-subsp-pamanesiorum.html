Echinocereus pamanesiorum subsp. pamanesiorum

Echinocereus pamanesiorum subsp.

pamanesiorum, commonly known as the Pamanesio Desert Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a ribbed stem and spines that are typically white to yellow in color.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by insects and bats. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in harsh climates.
