Euphorbia amygdaloides f. purpurata

Euphorbia amygdaloides f. purpurata, commonly known as the Purple Leaf Spurge, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features distinctive, waxy, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a purple or reddish tinge, especially in cooler weather. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by a unique, cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, visually appealing plants.
