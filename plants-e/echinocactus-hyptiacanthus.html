Echinocactus hyptiacanthus

Echinocactus hyptiacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of bright yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
