Euphorbia macdonaldii var. macdonaldii

Euphorbia macdonaldii var. macdonaldii, commonly known as the MacDonald's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that is often branched and covered in a layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
