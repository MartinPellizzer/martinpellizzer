Eremostachys diversifolia var. subvillosa

Eremostachys diversifolia var. subvillosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf eremostachys, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, branching stems covered in dense, soft hairs that help reduce water loss. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to further minimize evaporation. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in rock gardens or xeriscaping.
