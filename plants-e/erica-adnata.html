Erica adnata

Erica adnata, commonly known as the silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant features small, dense, evergreen leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

Erica adnata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
