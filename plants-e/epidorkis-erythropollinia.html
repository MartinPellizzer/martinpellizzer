Epidorkis erythropollinia

Epidorkis erythropollinia, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The orchid's unique botanical features make it a popular specimen among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
