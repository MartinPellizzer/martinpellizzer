Eugenia ilalensis

Eugenia ilalensis, commonly known as the Philippine myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in local traditional medicine.

Eugenia ilalensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
