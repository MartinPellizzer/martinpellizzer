Epacris impressa var. leucantha

Epacris impressa var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-stemmed epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or acidic soils in coastal heathlands and is adapted to full sun.

It is a perennial shrub that is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
