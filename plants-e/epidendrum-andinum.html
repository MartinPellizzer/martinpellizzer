Epidendrum andinum

Epidendrum andinum, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Epidendrum andinum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
