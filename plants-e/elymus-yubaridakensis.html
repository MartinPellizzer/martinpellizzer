Elymus yubaridakensis

Elymus yubaridakensis, commonly known as the Yubaridak grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. Its flowering heads are spike-like and contain small, wind-pollinated florets.

Elymus yubaridakensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
