Eupatorium magdalenense

Eupatorium magdalenense, commonly known as Magdalena boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium magdalenense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
