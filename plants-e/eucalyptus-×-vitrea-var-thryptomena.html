Eucalyptus × vitrea var. thryptomena

Eucalyptus × vitrea var. thryptomena, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid species within the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It is native to southern Australia and thrives in sandy or clay soils with good drainage.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
