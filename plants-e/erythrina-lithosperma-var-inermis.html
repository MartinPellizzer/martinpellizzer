Erythrina lithosperma var. inermis

Erythrina lithosperma var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth erythrina, is a deciduous legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and trifoliate leaves with reddish veins. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its pods are long and cylindrical, containing several seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
