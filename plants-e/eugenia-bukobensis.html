Eugenia bukobensis

Eugenia bukobensis, commonly known as the Bukoban ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, dark purple berries. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a pleasant aromatic scent when crushed.

Eugenia bukobensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
