Euphorbia gueinzii

Euphorbia gueinzii, commonly known as the Gueinz's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous branches that form a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually spine-tipped and adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Euphorbia gueinzii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
