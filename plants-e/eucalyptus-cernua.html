Eucalyptus cernua

Eucalyptus cernua, commonly known as the weeping eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, which bloom during the cooler months. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it valuable for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The tree is well adapted to dry and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging climates.
