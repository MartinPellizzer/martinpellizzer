Eriogonum spergulinum var. reddingianum

Eriogonum spergulinum var. reddingianum, commonly known as Redding's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
