Etorloba caerulea

Etorloba caerulea, commonly known as the bluebell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment. Etorloba caerulea thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
