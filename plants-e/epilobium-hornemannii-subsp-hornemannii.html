Epilobium hornemannii subsp. hornemannii

Epilobium hornemannii subsp.

hornemannii, commonly known as the hornemannii willowherb, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish-purple, and arranged in dense spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
