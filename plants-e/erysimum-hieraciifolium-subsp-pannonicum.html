Erysimum hieraciifolium subsp. pannonicum

Erysimum hieraciifolium subsp.

pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian wallflower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The leaves are pinnately divided, resembling those of the common groundsel, and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
