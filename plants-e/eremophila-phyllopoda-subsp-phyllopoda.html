Eremophila phyllopoda subsp. phyllopoda

Eremophila phyllopoda subsp.

phyllopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife. Eremophila phyllopoda subsp.

phyllopoda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
