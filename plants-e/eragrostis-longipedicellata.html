Eragrostis longipedicellata

Eragrostis longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-sided lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its flowers are usually wind-pollinated, and the seeds are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across arid and semi-arid regions.

Eragrostis longipedicellata is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in sandy or rocky soils.
