Eupatorium purpureum f. tegulosum

Eupatorium purpureum f. tegulosum, commonly known as the purple stem eupatorium, is a variant of the purple stem aster.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged stems and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pinkish flowers that attract pollinators.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
