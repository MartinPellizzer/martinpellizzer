Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. confusus

Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. confusus, commonly known as the fuzzy spine cactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that provide insulation and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized collections.
