Epilobium palustre var. grammadophyllum

Epilobium palustre var. grammadophyllum, commonly known as narrow-leaf willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist soils, often found in marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 cm wide. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in dense spikes, blooming from June to August.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
