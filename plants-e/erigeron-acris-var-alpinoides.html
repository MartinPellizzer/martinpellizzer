Erigeron acris var. alpinoides

Erigeron acris var. alpinoides, commonly known as the alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
