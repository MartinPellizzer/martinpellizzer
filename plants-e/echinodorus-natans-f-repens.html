Echinodorus natans f. repens

Echinodorus natans f. repens, commonly known as the wandering waterweed, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, often forming dense mats. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is commonly found in ponds and lakes. It is an effective oxygenator and is often used in aquatic gardening and pond ecosystems.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a valuable and potentially invasive species in certain water bodies.
