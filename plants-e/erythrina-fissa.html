Erythrina fissa

Erythrina fissa, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, feathery clusters. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five leaflets, which are often trifoliate in appearance. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release its seeds.

Erythrina fissa is native to parts of Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
