Eupatorium rotundifolium var. cordigerum

Eupatorium rotundifolium var. cordigerum, commonly known as the heartleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
