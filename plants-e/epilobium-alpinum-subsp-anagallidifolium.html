Epilobium alpinum subsp. anagallidifolium

Epilobium alpinum subsp.

anagallidifolium, commonly known as the alpine willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, often acidic soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. Its small, pinkish to purple flowers appear in spikes, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
