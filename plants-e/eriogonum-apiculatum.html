Eriogonum apiculatum

Eriogonum apiculatum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum apiculatum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.
