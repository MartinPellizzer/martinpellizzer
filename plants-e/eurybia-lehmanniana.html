Eurybia lehmanniana

Eurybia lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eurybia lehmanniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.
