Eugenia lumboy

Eugenia lumboy, commonly known as the white eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
