Euphorbia scyphadena

Euphorbia scyphadena, commonly known as the red-veined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, though the true flowers are small and inconspicuous. Its leaves are typically narrow and may have a reddish or purple hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Euphorbia scyphadena is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
