Echinops babatagensis

Echinops babatagensis, commonly known as the Babatagan thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a globe. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Echinops babatagensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
