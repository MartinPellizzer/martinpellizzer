Epidendrum chaparense

Epidendrum chaparense, commonly known as the Chapare orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
