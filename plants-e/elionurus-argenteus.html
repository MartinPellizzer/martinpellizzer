Elionurus argenteus

Elionurus argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, silver-tomentose leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. Elionurus argenteus is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is not widely cultivated or used for commercial purposes.
