Epidendrum uribeianum

Epidendrum uribeianum, commonly known as the Uribes orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale pink blossoms that bloom in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes grown as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
