Enchysia erinoides

Enchysia erinoides, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its succulent leaves and ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils make it well adapted to desert conditions.

Enchysia erinoides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
