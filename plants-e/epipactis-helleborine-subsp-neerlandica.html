Epipactis helleborine subsp. neerlandica

Epipactis helleborine subsp.

neerlandica, commonly known as the Dutch helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe, including the Netherlands and Belgium. It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive purple markings.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and meadows. It produces a single spike of flowers, which can bear up to 20 blooms, each with a three-lobed lip.

This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments and is often found in association with other orchid species.
