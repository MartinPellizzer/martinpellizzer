Euphorbia barbellata f. denudata

Euphorbia barbellata f. denudata, commonly known as the naked euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and reduced leaves, which are often absent or highly modified. The plant produces striking yellow-green cyathia, which are its specialized flower-like structures. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
