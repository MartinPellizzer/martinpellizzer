Encephalartos verschaffeltii

Encephalartos verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Van der Walt's cycad, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of pinnate, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It is a slow-growing, long-lived species that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
