Eugenia corrientina

Eugenia corrientina, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia corrientina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
