Erigeron foetidus

Erigeron foetidus, commonly known as stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and have a strong, unpleasant odor, which is a key identifying feature.

The flowers are small and white or pale blue, blooming in late summer to early fall.
