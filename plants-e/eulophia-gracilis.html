Eulophia gracilis

Eulophia gracilis, commonly known as the slender eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, upright stem and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white with yellow or brown markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Eulophia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
