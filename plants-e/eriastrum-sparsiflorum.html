Eriastrum sparsiflorum

Eriastrum sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparseflower eriastrum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. This annual plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats.

Eriastrum sparsiflorum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
