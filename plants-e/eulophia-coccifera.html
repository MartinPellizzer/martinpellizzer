Eulophia coccifera

Eulophia coccifera, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers with deep red veins contrasting against a white or pale pink background. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and wetlands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Eulophia coccifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
