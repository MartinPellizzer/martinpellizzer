Eugenia cumbreana

Eugenia cumbreana, commonly known as the Andean guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor, similar to guavas. It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
