Ericoila verna

Ericoila verna, commonly known as the spring heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and produces bell-shaped, white or pink flowers in early spring. Ericoila verna thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
