Erica robynsiana

Erica robynsiana, commonly known as Robyn's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica robynsiana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form compact, shrub-like forms.

It is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils in coastal and sandy environments.
