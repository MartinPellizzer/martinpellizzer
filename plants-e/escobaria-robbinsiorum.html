Escobaria robbinsiorum

Escobaria robbinsiorum, commonly known as Robbin's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its compact, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are typically borne on short, hairy stems. Escobaria robbinsiorum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and ability to store water in its leaves make it a hardy and resilient species.
