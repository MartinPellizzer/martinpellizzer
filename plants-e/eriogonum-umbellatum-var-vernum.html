Eriogonum umbellatum var. vernum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. vernum, commonly known as the spring buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

It is an important plant for pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
