Euryomyrtus diffusa

Euryomyrtus diffusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The leaves are used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and have a distinctive, spicy scent.
