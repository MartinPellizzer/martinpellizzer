Eugenia mananquil

Eugenia mananquil, commonly known as the Mananquil cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small red or purple drupe, is edible and has a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.
