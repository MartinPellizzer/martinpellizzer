Eremurus candidus

Eremurus candidus, commonly known as the white torchflower, is a striking perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous white or pale yellow florets. The plant produces large, showy flowers that bloom in early summer, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Eremurus candidus is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic visual impact.
