Echinocactus ourselianus

Echinocactus ourselianus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
