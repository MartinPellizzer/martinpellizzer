Eupatorium grandidentatum

Eupatorium grandidentatum, commonly known as the broad-toothed eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments. Its leaves are broad, toothed, and often have a distinct, fragrant scent.

Eupatorium grandidentatum produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
