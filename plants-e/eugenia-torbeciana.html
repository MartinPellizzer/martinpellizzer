Eugenia torbeciana

Eugenia torbeciana, commonly known as the Malabar cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.
