Euterpe acuminata

Euterpe acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that develop into drupes, which are typically consumed by various bird species. Euterpe acuminata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.

However, it faces threats from habitat loss and overharvesting in the wild.
