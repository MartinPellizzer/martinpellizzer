Encephalartos gratus

Encephalartos gratus, commonly known as the Gratus cycad, is a species of cycad native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like, dark green leaves. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being cylindrical and the female cones being more rounded. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Encephalartos gratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
