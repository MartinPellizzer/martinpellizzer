Eucalyptus alaticaulis

Eucalyptus alaticaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, arranged in clusters, and are pollinated by insects.

Eucalyptus alaticaulis plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various native animals.
