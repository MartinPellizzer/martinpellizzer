Epacris impressa f. ceriflora

Epacris impressa f. ceriflora, commonly known as the waxflower, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
