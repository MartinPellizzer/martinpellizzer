Eugenia ombrophila

Eugenia ombrophila, commonly known as the "sugar apple" or "sweet tooth," is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a yellowish-green skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp that contains numerous seeds. Eugenia ombrophila thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

It is valued not only for its edible fruits but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
