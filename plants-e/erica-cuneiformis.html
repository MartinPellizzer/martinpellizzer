Erica cuneiformis

Erica cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a hardy evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of pink or white bell-shaped flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

Erica cuneiformis is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in coastal areas.
