Eucalyptus incrassata var. goniantha

Eucalyptus incrassata var. goniantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale yellow to cream in color. It is a hardy shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height.

The species is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in landscaping for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
