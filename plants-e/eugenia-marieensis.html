Eugenia marieensis

Eugenia marieensis, commonly known as Marie's evergreen guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
