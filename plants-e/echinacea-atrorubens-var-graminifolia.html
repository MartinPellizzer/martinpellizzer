Echinacea atrorubens var. graminifolia

Echinacea atrorubens var. graminifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a distinctive, spiky cone at the center of its flower head, typical of the genus Echinacea. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
