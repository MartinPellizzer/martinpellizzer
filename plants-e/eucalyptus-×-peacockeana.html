Eucalyptus × peacockeana

Eucalyptus × peacockeana, commonly known as the Peacocke's gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and distinctive, pendulous white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a greyish-green hue with a rough texture. This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
