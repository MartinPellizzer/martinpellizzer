Eriogonum umbellatum var. aureum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. aureum, commonly known as golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often used in native plant landscaping.
