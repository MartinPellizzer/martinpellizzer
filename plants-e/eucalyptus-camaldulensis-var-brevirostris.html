Eucalyptus camaldulensis var. brevirostris

Eucalyptus camaldulensis var. brevirostris, commonly known as the narrow-barked river gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, rounded to lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often greyish bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found near water sources such as rivers and swamps.

This species is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
