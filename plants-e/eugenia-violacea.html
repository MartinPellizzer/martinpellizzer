Eugenia violacea

Eugenia violacea, commonly known as the violet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a pleasant fragrance, while its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are edible.

Eugenia violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
