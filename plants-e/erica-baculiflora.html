Erica baculiflora

Erica baculiflora, commonly known as the slender-stemmed heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Erica baculiflora typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
