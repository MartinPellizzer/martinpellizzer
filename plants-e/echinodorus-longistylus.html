Echinodorus longistylus

Echinodorus longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled elodea, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found above the water surface. Echinodorus longistylus is often used in aquariums due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.

Its roots help stabilize sediment in aquatic environments, making it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.
