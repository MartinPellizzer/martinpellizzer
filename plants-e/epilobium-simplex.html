Epilobium simplex

Epilobium simplex, commonly known as slender willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish-purple, and arranged in spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Epilobium simplex is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is often found in disturbed or riparian areas.
