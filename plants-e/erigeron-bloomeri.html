Erigeron bloomeri

Erigeron bloomeri, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its blue to violet flower heads bloom in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant often forms low, spreading mats.
