Esenbeckia alata

Esenbeckia alata, commonly known as the Brazilian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a stem that is covered in fine hairs.

Esenbeckia alata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
