Eryngium microcephalum

Eryngium microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by white to gray bracts. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its unique appearance makes it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.
