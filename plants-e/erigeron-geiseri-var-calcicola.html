Erigeron geiseri var. calcicola

Erigeron geiseri var. calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss.
