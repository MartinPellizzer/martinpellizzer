Excoecaria biglandulosa var. sellowiana

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's croton, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
