Elatostema novae-britanniae

Elatostema novae-britanniae, commonly known as the New Britain elatostema, is a member of the Elatostemaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies, and is native to the island of New Britain in the Pacific.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on tropical flora and plant adaptations.
