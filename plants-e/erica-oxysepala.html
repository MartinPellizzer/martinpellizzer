Erica oxysepala

Erica oxysepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in sandy, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica oxysepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
