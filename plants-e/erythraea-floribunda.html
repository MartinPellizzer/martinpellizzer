Erythraea floribunda

Erythraea floribunda, commonly known as the red-flowered erythraea, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red blooms, which appear in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with woody stems that become more pronounced with age. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Erythraea floribunda is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
