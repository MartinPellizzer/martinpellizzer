Elymus stebbinsii subsp. septentrionalis

Elymus stebbinsii subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern United States and southern Canada. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces inflorescences that are spike-like, composed of dense clusters of spikelets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
