Echites campanulatus

Echites campanulatus, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Echites campanulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
