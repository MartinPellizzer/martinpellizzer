Enkianthus perulatus var. japonicus

Enkianthus perulatus var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing an attractive backdrop to its vibrant blossoms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
