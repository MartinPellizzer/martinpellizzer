Elsholtzia kachinensis var. petiolata

Elsholtzia kachinensis var. petiolata, commonly known as the Kachin elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, minty aroma. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically arranged in whorls along the stem.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional herbal applications.
