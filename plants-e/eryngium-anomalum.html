Eryngium anomalum

Eryngium anomalum, commonly known as the strange thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Eryngium anomalum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
