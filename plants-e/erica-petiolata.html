Erica petiolata

Erica petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica petiolata typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs.
