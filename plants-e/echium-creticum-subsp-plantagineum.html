Echium creticum subsp. plantagineum

Echium creticum subsp.

plantagineum, commonly known as the Cretan fleur-de-lis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August.

The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
