Eugenia heterochroa

Eugenia heterochroa, commonly known as the variable myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or purple when mature. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring contrasting shades of green, which adds to its ornamental value.

Eugenia heterochroa is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
