Euphorbia brasiliensis f. pruinosa

Euphorbia brasiliensis f. pruinosa, commonly known as the Pruinose Spurge, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling to avoid skin irritation.
