Eugenia caudata

Eugenia caudata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Eugenia caudata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
