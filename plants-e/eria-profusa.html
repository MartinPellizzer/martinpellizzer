Eria profusa

Eria profusa, commonly known as the spreading eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often creeping stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eria profusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens.
