Eugenia purpurea

Eugenia purpurea, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, purple-skinned fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a unique fruit structure with a segmented, leathery rind.

Eugenia purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
