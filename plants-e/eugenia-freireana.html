Eugenia freireana

Eugenia freireana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit yield.

Eugenia freireana is also sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
