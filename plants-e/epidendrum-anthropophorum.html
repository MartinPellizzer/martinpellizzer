Epidendrum anthropophorum

Epidendrum anthropophorum, commonly known as the "human-eater orchid," is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest understories. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that are often red, orange, or yellow, with a unique, showy appearance. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

The orchid is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and greenhouses.
