Euodia ruticarpa var. officinalis

Euodia ruticarpa var. officinalis, commonly known as Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds such as lignans and flavonoids, which are valued for their medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental and therapeutic uses.
