Erymophyllum ramosum subsp. involucratum

Erymophyllum ramosum subsp.

involucratum, commonly known as the woolly sunhemp, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, providing a unique texture and appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for cultivation in harsh environments.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
