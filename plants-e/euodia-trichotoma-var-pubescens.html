Euodia trichotoma var. pubescens

Euodia trichotoma var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sour-tasting fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
