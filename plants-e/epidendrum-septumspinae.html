Epidendrum septumspinae

Epidendrum septumspinae, commonly known as the Spiny Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, branching stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
