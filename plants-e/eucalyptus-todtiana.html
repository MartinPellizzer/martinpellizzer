Eucalyptus todtiana

Eucalyptus todtiana, commonly known as the Tottin's gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is a medium to tall tree, typically reaching heights of up to 30 meters, with a straight trunk and a spreading crown. The bark is smooth and often exhibits a grey or white color, shedding in strips as the tree matures. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The flowers are small and white or pale blue, clustered in umbels, and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.
