Ehrharta laxiflora

Ehrharta laxiflora, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Ehrharta laxiflora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
