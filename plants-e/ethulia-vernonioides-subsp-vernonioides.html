Ethulia vernonioides subsp. vernonioides

Ethulia vernonioides subsp.

vernonioides, commonly known as the Vernonioides false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and produce small, fluffy seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
