Echinocereus armatusatus

Echinocereus armatusatus, commonly known as the Arizona hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spiny, tuberculate structures that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, which are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Echinocereus armatusatus is a hardy, low-maintenance plant often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
