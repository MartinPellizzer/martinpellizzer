Eriosema stolzii

Eriosema stolzii, commonly known as the velvetleaf eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a robust root system and produces erect, hairy stems that bear alternate, pinnately compound leaves with a velvety texture. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
