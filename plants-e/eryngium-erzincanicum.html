Eryngium erzincanicum

Eryngium erzincanicum, commonly known as the Erzincan thistle, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Eryngium erzincanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
