Erica carniula

Erica carniula, commonly known as the Carniola heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Erica carniula thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or heathland environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
