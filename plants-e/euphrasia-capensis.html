Euphrasia capensis

Euphrasia capensis, commonly known as the Cape Hedge Mustard, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Euphrasia capensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
