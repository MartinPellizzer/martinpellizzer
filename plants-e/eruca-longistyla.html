Eruca longistyla

Eruca longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled rocket, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, green, elongated leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Eruca longistyla is noted for its peppery flavor, making it a potential edible plant in certain regions.
