Eugenia kuhlmanniana

Eugenia kuhlmanniana, commonly known as the Kuhlmann's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

Eugenia kuhlmanniana is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
