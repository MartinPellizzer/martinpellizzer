Echinolobium sennoides

Echinolobium sennoides, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Echinolobium sennoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
