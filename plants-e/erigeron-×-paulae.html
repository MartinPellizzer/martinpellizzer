Erigeron × paulae

Erigeron × paulae, commonly known as the Blueweed or Blue Mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy blue-gray foliage and showy white to pale blue flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads.

Erigeron × paulae is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for pollinators.
