Elymus glaucus var. jepsonii

Elymus glaucus var. jepsonii, commonly known as Jepson's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to California.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
