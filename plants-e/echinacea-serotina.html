Echinacea serotina

Echinacea serotina, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple flower heads and hairy, greenish-gray stems. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinacea serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
