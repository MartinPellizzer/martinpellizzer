Embothrium mucronatum

Embothrium mucronatum, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
