Elaeocarpus brachyandrus

Elaeocarpus brachyandrus, commonly known as the short-stalked elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody fruits that are typically spherical or egg-shaped. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting local biodiversity.
