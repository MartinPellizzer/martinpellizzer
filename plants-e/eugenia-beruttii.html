Eugenia beruttii

Eugenia beruttii, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
