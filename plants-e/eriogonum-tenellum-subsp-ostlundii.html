Eriogonum tenellum subsp. ostlundii

Eriogonum tenellum subsp.

ostlundii, commonly known as the desert buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States. It features small, clustered white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in desert landscapes.
