Erythrina variegata var. orientalis

Erythrina variegata var. orientalis, commonly known as the Oriental Flame Tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.
