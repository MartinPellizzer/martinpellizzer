Eucalyptus conglomerata

Eucalyptus conglomerata, commonly known as the broad-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fibrous bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue. It is commonly found in heathlands and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Eucalyptus conglomerata is an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
