Erica penicilliformis

Erica penicilliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Erica penicilliformis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
