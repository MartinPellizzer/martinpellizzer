Excoecaria biglandulosa var. leptadenia

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. leptadenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
