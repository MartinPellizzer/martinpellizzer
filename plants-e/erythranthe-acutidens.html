Erythranthe acutidens

Erythranthe acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved erythranthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Erythranthe acutidens is often found in meadows and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
