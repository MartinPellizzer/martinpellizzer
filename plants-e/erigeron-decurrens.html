Erigeron decurrens

Erigeron decurrens, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and marshes, and it features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
