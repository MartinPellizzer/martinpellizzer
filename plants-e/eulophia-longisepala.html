Eulophia longisepala

Eulophia longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled eulophia, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces striking, fragrant orchid flowers with a distinctive sepals and petals structure. It typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Eulophia longisepala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
