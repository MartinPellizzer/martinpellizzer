Elaeocarpus miquelii

Elaeocarpus miquelii, commonly known as the Philippine satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a thick, waxy coating. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and decorative items.

Elaeocarpus miquelii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.
