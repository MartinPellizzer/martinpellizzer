Erigeron peregrinus var. callianthemus

Erigeron peregrinus var. callianthemus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy purple or pink flower heads, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
