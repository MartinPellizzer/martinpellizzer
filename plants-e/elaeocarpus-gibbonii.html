Elaeocarpus gibbonii

Elaeocarpus gibbonii, commonly known as the Gibbon's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, woody fruits that are typically encased in a fleshy, red or yellow pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
