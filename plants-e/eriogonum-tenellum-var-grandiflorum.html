Eriogonum tenellum var. grandiflorum

Eriogonum tenellum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the largeflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has soft, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
