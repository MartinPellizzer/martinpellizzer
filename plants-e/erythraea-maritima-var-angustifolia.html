Erythraea maritima var. angustifolia

Erythraea maritima var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sea erythraea, is a coastal plant adapted to saline and sandy environments.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in width. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dune ecosystems along the Atlantic coast.

This variety is notable for its resilience to harsh maritime conditions.
