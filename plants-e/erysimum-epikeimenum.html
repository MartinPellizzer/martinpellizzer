Erysimum epikeimenum

Erysimum epikeimenum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Erysimum epikeimenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
