Euphoria noronhiana

Euphoria noronhiana, commonly known as the Brazilian euphoria, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually elliptical in shape. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.
