Erythroxylum ulei var. ulei

Erythroxylum ulei var. ulei, commonly known as the Brazilian peppercorn, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it has been introduced to other parts of the world.
