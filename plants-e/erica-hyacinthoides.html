Erica hyacinthoides

Erica hyacinthoides, commonly known as the Heath Hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the western and southern regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a range of colors from white to deep purple. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Erica hyacinthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
