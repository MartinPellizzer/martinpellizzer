Eucalyptus × affinis

Eucalyptus × affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Eucalyptus camaldulensis and Eucalyptus crebra.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs with smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a blue-green coloration, and they are covered in a fine, white bloom. This species is native to southern Australia, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.

Eucalyptus × affinis is well adapted to arid conditions and is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil quality.
