Eugenia malabarica

Eugenia malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar plum, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas across southern India and Sri Lanka.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
