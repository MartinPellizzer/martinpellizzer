Evolvulus tenuis var. tenuis

Evolvulus tenuis var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens and as a ground cover.
