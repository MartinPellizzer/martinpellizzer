Erica melliflua

Erica melliflua, commonly known as the honeyheather, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica melliflua is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
