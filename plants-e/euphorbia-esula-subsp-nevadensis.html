Euphorbia esula subsp. nevadensis

Euphorbia esula subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and basal rosettes of fleshy, lobed leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in high-elevation environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a resilient and adaptable species within its native range.
