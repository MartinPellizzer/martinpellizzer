Eugenioides tinctorium

Eugenioides tinctorium, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is known for its lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been traditionally used in dyeing and medicinal applications.

The tree is also notable for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a valuable but endangered species in its natural habitat.
