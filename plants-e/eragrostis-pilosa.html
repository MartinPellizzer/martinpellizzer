Eragrostis pilosa

Eragrostis pilosa, commonly known as hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower structures.

Eragrostis pilosa is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
