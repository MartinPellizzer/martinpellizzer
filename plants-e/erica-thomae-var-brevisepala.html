Erica thomae var. brevisepala

Erica thomae var. brevisepala, commonly known as the short-stalked heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in coastal gardens.
