Elaeocarpus pulleanus

Elaeocarpus pulleanus, commonly known as the purple-leaf elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green to purple leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a dense canopy and thick foliage.

Elaeocarpus pulleanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
