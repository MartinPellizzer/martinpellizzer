Echinocactus fiedlerianus

Echinocactus fiedlerianus, commonly known as the Fiedler's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to dry environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Its slow growth rate makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
