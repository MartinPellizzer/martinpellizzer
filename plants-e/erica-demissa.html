Erica demissa

Erica demissa, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western and southern regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, white waxy coating. Erica demissa produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
