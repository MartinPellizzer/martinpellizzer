Euterpe stenophylla

Euterpe stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf babaçu, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clustered, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar to those of the babaçu palm, though smaller in size. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species requiring conservation efforts.
