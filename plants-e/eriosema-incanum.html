Eriosema incanum

Eriosema incanum, commonly known as the white-hairy eriosema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eriosema incanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
