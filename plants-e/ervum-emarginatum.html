Ervum emarginatum

Ervum emarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a climbing habit, often twining around other vegetation, and produces elongated, slender pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.

Ervum emarginatum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings.
