Eupatorium ganophyllum

Eupatorium ganophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium ganophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
