Eupatorium nummularia

Eupatorium nummularia, commonly known as white snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a creeping rhizome and produces clusters of small, white, button-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating, and the flowers bloom from late summer to early fall.

It is often used in herbal medicine, though it can be toxic to livestock.
