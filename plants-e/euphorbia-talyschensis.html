Euphorbia talyschensis

Euphorbia talyschensis, commonly known as the Talyschense Spurge, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, Euphorbia talyschensis is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
