Endospermum macrophyllum

Endospermum macrophyllum, commonly known as the Australian wattlevine, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and contain an endosperm that provides nourishment to the developing embryo.
