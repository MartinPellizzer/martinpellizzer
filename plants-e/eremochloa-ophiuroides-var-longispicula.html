Eremochloa ophiuroides var. longispicula

Eremochloa ophiuroides var. longispicula, commonly known as the long-bristled spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and swampy environments in eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear long, bristly spikes, which give it its scientific name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, poorly drained soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
