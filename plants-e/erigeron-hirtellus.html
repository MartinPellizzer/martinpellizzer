Erigeron hirtellus

Erigeron hirtellus, commonly known as Hirtell's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Hirtell's fleabane is often found in the northeastern and mid-Atlantic regions of North America.
