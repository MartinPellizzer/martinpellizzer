Epilobium propinquum

Epilobium propinquum, commonly known as narrowleaf willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Epilobium propinquum is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other willowherbs and flowering plants with showy, often bilaterally symmetric flowers.
