Echeveria steyermarkii

Echeveria steyermarkii, commonly known as the Mexican snowball, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among collectors and gardeners who grow it in arid or semi-arid climates.
