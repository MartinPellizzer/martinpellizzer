Eriophorum polystachion f. gynodynamis

Eriophorum polystachion f. gynodynamis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cotton grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, fluffy flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wet meadows, often forming dense stands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The inflorescence is particularly notable for its ability to disperse seeds effectively through wind, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
