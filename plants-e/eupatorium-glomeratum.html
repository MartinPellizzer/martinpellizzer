Eupatorium glomeratum

Eupatorium glomeratum, commonly known as clustered boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in dense heads, giving it its common name.

It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
