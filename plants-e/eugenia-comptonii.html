Eugenia comptonii

Eugenia comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, pinkish-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in some tropical and subtropical regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
