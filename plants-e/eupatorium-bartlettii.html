Eupatorium bartlettii

Eupatorium bartlettii, commonly known as Bartlett's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium bartlettii is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
