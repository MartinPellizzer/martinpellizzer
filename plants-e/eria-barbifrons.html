Eria barbifrons

Eria barbifrons, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a distinctive, hairy lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a unique, fringed appearance.

Eria barbifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
