Etlingera corneri

Etlingera corneri, commonly known as the Hainan torch ginger, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Hainan Island in China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking red and orange inflorescences, which resemble torches, hence its common name. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.
