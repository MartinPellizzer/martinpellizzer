Elyna filifolia

Elyna filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf elyna, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Elyna filifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
