Ehretia tomentosa

Ehretia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly evergreen, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly grayish-white pubescence covering its young shoots and leaves. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
