Eremurus spectabilis subsp. regelii

Eremurus spectabilis subsp.

regelii, commonly known as the Regel's sasparilla, is a striking perennial plant native to Central Asia. It features tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, adorned with large, showy yellow-green inflorescences.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into spiky, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
