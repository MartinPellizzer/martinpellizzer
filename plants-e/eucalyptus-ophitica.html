Eucalyptus ophitica

Eucalyptus ophitica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and usually white or pink, arranged in clusters.

Eucalyptus ophitica plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
