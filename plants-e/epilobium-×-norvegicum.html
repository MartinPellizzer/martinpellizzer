Epilobium × norvegicum

Epilobium × norvegicum, commonly known as Norwegian willow herb, is a hybrid plant species native to northern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland edges and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers in the summer. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Onagraceae family.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
