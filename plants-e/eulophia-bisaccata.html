Eulophia bisaccata

Eulophia bisaccata, commonly known as the two-pouched eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with two distinct, swollen leaf bases that give it its common name. The plant produces tall, slender stems with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in a raceme, and have a distinctive, intricate structure.

Eulophia bisaccata is often found in shaded, moist habitats such as forest understories.
