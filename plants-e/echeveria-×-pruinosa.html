Echeveria × pruinosa

Echeveria × pruinosa, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that often develop a bluish hue when exposed to full sun. The plant produces clusters of violet or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and striking color make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
