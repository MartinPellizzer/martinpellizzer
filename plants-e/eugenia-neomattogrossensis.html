Eugenia neomattogrossensis

Eugenia neomattogrossensis, commonly known as the Mattogrossense Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Brazil, particularly in the state of Mato Grosso. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It produces edible berries that are typically consumed raw or used in local cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
