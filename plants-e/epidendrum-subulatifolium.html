Epidendrum subulatifolium

Epidendrum subulatifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This epiphytic orchid is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
