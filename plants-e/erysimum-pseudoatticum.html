Erysimum pseudoatticum

Erysimum pseudoatticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers are yellow or orange and bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
