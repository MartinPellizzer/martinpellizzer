Equisetum hyemale f. doellii

Equisetum hyemale f. doellii, commonly known as the Christmas fern, is a perennial fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, evergreen fronds that resemble a Christmas tree, hence its common name. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing both sporangia and strobili, which are specialized structures for spore production. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

The fern's ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with significant botanical interest.
