Euphorbia pekinensis subsp. fauriei

Euphorbia pekinensis subsp.

fauriei, commonly known as the Peking spurge, is a succulent plant native to China. It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
