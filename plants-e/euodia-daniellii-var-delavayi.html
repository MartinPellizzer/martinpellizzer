Euodia daniellii var. delavayi

Euodia daniellii var. delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact foliage consisting of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.

This variety is closely related to other Euodia species and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
