Eruca silvestris

Eruca silvestris, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbages and broccoli. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped and have a peppery, mustard-like flavor.

Eruca silvestris is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
