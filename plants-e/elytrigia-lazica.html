Elytrigia lazica

Elytrigia lazica, commonly known as the Lazica grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in mountainous areas and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a rough texture.

Elytrigia lazica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
