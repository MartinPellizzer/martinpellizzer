Eremia peltata

Eremia peltata, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Eremia peltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy or rocky soils.
