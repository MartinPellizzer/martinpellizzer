Euphorbia salsicola

Euphorbia salsicola, commonly known as the candlestick euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, upright stem that resembles a candlestick, often adorned with small, green, leaf-like structures. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
