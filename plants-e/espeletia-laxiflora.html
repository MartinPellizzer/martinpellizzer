Espeletia laxiflora

Espeletia laxiflora, commonly known as the "Páramo Giant," is a striking plant native to the páramo ecosystems of South America.

It is characterized by its large, rosette-like leaves that are thick and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, pale flowers that bloom in the cool, high-altitude environments where it thrives. Its leaves are adapted to retain moisture and protect against intense UV radiation.

Espeletia laxiflora plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various invertebrates and birds.
