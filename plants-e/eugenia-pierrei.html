Eugenia pierrei

Eugenia pierrei, commonly known as the Malabar cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia pierrei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
