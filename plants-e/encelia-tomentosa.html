Encelia tomentosa

Encelia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, white to grayish foliage and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, ragweed, and sunflowers. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Encelia tomentosa is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
