Englerophytum natalense

Englerophytum natalense, commonly known as Natal ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Englerophytum natalense is often found in rocky areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
