Eugenia albiflora

Eugenia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
