Eria stricta

Eria stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
