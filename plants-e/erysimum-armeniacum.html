Erysimum armeniacum

Erysimum armeniacum, commonly known as Armenian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Turkey and the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and upright flowering stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
