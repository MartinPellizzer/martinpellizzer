Eragrostis appressa

Eragrostis appressa, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescences that resemble spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis appressa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
