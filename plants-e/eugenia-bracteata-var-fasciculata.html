Eugenia bracteata var. fasciculata

Eugenia bracteata var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered clove or fasciculate eugenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, dark berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage and flowers.
