Eriogonum shockleyi var. packardiae

Eriogonum shockleyi var. packardiae, commonly known as the Packard's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The plant has narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is particularly adapted to the harsh conditions of its native range, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
