Echinocereus rigidissimus

Echinocereus rigidissimus, commonly known as the rigid hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red berries. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant's structure and appearance make it a striking addition to desert gardens and rockeries.
