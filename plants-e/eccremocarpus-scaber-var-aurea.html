Eccremocarpus scaber var. aurea

Eccremocarpus scaber var. aurea, commonly known as the golden glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that supports its climbing growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscaping.
