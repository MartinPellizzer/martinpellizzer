Erica alticola

Erica alticola, commonly known as the Alpine Heather, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Erica alticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to alpine environments.
