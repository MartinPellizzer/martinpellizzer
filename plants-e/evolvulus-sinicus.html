Evolvulus sinicus

Evolvulus sinicus, commonly known as the Chinese blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

Evolvulus sinicus is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily in gardens.
