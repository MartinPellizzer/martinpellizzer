Eleutherococcus evodiifolius var. gracilis

Eleutherococcus evodiifolius var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender-leaved Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Asia.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their adaptogenic properties.

It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and requires a cool, temperate climate for optimal growth.
