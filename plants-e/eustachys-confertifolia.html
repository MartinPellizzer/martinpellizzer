Eustachys confertifolia

Eustachys confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems in its native habitats.
