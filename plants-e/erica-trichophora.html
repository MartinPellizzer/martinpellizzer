Erica trichophora

Erica trichophora, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and its dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. Erica trichophora produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to harsh, sunny environments.
