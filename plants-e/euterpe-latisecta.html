Euterpe latisecta

Euterpe latisecta, commonly known as the broad-leafed palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant typically grows as a solitary trunk with a crown of fronds, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Euterpe latisecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
