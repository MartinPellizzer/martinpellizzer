Eugenia candolleana

Eugenia candolleana, commonly known as the mountain guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia candolleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native habitat.
