Erythrina sandwicensis

Erythrina sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its structure is adapted to the tropical climate of the islands. It plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian ecosystems, providing nectar for native birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in other tropical regions.
