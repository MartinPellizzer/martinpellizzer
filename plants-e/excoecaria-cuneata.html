Excoecaria cuneata

Excoecaria cuneata, commonly known as the croton or Indian rubber plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Excoecaria cuneata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
