Eupatorium mutiscuense

Eupatorium mutiscuense, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are often aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
