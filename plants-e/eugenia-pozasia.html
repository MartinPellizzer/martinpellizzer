Eugenia pozasia

Eugenia pozasia, commonly known as the New Guinea blueberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, which is typically blue or purple when ripe, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor.

Eugenia pozasia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries in its native habitat.
