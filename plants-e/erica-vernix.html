Erica vernix

Erica vernix, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica vernix is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.
