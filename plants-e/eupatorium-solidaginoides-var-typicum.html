Eupatorium solidaginoides var. typicum

Eupatorium solidaginoides var. typicum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a potential source of medicinal compounds.
