Emilia drummondii

Emilia drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's emilia, is an annual flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Emilia drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
