Elaeagnus fruticosa

Elaeagnus fruticosa, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in the fall. The plant has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and is often used in erosion control. It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Elaeagnus fruticosa is a hardy species that provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.
