Elymus mollis subsp. villosissimus

Elymus mollis subsp.

villosissimus, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America. It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
