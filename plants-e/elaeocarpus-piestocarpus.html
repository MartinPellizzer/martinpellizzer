Elaeocarpus piestocarpus

Elaeocarpus piestocarpus, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, spherical fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential medicinal studies.
