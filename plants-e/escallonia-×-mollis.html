Escallonia × mollis

Escallonia × mollis, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Escallonia × mollis is also valued for its winter hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
