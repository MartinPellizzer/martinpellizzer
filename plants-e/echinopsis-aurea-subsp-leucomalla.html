Echinopsis aurea subsp. leucomalla

Echinopsis aurea subsp.

leucomalla, commonly known as the white-flowered moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and a striking white to pale yellow flower that blooms at night.

The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments with its succulent stems that store water. It produces a single, showy flower that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
