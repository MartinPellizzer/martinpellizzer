Euphorbia schimperiana

Euphorbia schimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to store water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
