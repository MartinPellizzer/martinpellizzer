Eritrichium gnaphalioides

Eritrichium gnaphalioides, commonly known as the woolly mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, and its foliage is covered in dense, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.

Eritrichium gnaphalioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy, resilient nature.
