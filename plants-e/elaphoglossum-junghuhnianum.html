Elaphoglossum junghuhnianum

Elaphoglossum junghuhnianum, commonly known as the Junghuhn's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, serrated fronds that are typically arching or upright, depending on the growth conditions. The plant produces spores in clusters on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are often covered with a layer of fine, hairy structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its intricate leaf structure and spore-producing mechanisms make it an important subject in the study of fern morphology and ecology.
