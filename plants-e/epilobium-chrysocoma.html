Epilobium chrysocoma

Epilobium chrysocoma, commonly known as golden willow or yellow willow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as riverbanks and meadows. The plant features slender, willow-like stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually yellow or orange in color.

Epilobium chrysocoma is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
