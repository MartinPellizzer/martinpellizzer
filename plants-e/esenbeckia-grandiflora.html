Esenbeckia grandiflora

Esenbeckia grandiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a yellow or orange berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Esenbeckia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
