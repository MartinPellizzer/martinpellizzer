Echium cephaloideum

Echium cephaloideum, commonly known as the "blueweed" or "woolly thistle," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silver-gray, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It grows up to 1.5 meters tall and is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Echium cephaloideum is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
