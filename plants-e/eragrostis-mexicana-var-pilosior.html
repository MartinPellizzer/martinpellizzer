Eragrostis mexicana var. pilosior

Eragrostis mexicana var. pilosior, commonly known as hairy mexican lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a dense covering of short hairs. The leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green coloration and a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small clusters of flowering structures.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common component of dry grasslands and disturbed soils.
