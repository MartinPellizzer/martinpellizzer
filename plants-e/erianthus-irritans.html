Erianthus irritans

Erianthus irritans, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and upright growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, bract-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
