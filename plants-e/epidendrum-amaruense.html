Epidendrum amaruense

Epidendrum amaruense, commonly known as the Amaru orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red streaks.

Epidendrum amaruense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
