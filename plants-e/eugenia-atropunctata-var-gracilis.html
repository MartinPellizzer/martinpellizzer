Eugenia atropunctata var. gracilis

Eugenia atropunctata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender satinwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. It produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The tree is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
