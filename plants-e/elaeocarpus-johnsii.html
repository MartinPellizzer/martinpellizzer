Elaeocarpus johnsii

Elaeocarpus johnsii, commonly known as the New Guinea elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the humid forests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that enclose a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to mid-elevation rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
