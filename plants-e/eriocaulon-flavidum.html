Eriocaulon flavidum

Eriocaulon flavidum, commonly known as the yellow spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Eriocaulon flavidum plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
