Eria torricellensis

Eria torricellensis, commonly known as the Torricelli's orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted for efficient water absorption.

The flowers exhibit a range of colors, from white to pale pink, and are often clustered in inflorescences.
