Eryngium triquetrum var. xauense

Eryngium triquetrum var. xauense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and narrow, gray-green leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
