Espeletia hartwegiana var. vegasana

Espeletia hartwegiana var. vegasana, commonly known as the Velvetleaf Tree of Life, is a striking plant native to the Andean highlands of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, daisy-like inflorescences that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to store water in its arid native environment.

This species is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
