Eragrostis ledermannii

Eragrostis ledermannii, commonly known as Ledermann's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
