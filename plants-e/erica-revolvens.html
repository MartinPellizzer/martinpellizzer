Erica revolvens

Erica revolvens, commonly known as the Cape heath or narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and early summer. Erica revolvens thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs.
