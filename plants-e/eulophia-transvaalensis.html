Eulophia transvaalensis

Eulophia transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Eulophia transvaalensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
