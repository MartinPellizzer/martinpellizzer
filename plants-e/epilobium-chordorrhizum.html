Epilobium chordorrhizum

Epilobium chordorrhizum, commonly known as the heart-root willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and opposite leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

Epilobium chordorrhizum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
