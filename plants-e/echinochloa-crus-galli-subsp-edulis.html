Echinochloa crus-galli subsp. edulis

Echinochloa crus-galli subsp.

edulis, commonly known as lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions. It has erect, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a weed and a forage plant in various ecosystems.
