Eucalyptus peninsularis

Eucalyptus peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular eucalyptus, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, peeling bark that ranges from gray to white. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This plant thrives in coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.
