Echinocactus erectus

Echinocactus erectus, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features a spiny, segmented stem with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Echinocactus erectus is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal rainfall.
