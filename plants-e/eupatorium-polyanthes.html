Eupatorium polyanthes

Eupatorium polyanthes, commonly known as the meadow dewflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy.

Eupatorium polyanthes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
