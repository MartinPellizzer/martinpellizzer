Euthalis lucida

Euthalis lucida, commonly known as the whiteflower false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Euthalis lucida has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
