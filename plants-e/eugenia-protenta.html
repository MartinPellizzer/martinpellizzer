Eugenia protenta

Eugenia protenta, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which is typically purple or red when mature, has a thick, leathery rind and a segmented, juicy interior. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and potential medicinal purposes.
