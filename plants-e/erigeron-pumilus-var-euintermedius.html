Erigeron pumilus var. euintermedius

Erigeron pumilus var. euintermedius, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with fine, grass-like leaves and daisy-like blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
