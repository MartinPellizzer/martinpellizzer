Erythronium americanum subsp. harperi

Erythronium americanum subsp.

harperi, commonly known as the Harp's dog's tooth violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces a single upright stem bearing a showy, bell-shaped flower with a distinctive purple-pink hue. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, typically between March and May.

The subspecies is characterized by its smaller size and more compact growth compared to other Erythronium species.
