Eremophila gilesii

Eremophila gilesii, commonly known as the Grey Kangaroo Bush, is a shrub native to Australia, particularly found in South Australia and the Northern Territory.

It is characterized by its silver-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and a compact growth habit. It produces small, winged fruits that disperse its seeds by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Eremophila gilesii plays a role in providing habitat and nectar for local wildlife, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
