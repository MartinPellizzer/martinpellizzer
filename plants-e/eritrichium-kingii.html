Eritrichium kingii

Eritrichium kingii, commonly known as the King's eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, delicate blue to violet flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in open meadows and sagebrush steppe.

Eritrichium kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
