Elleanthus scopula

Elleanthus scopula, commonly known as the narrowleaf elleanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming during the warmer months.

Elleanthus scopula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
