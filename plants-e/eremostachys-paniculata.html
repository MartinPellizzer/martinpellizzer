Eremostachys paniculata

Eremostachys paniculata, commonly known as the desert gentian, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eremostachys paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
