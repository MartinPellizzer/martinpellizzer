Eragrostis contrerasii

Eragrostis contrerasii, commonly known as the Mexican lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
