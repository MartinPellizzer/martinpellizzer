Epicladium boothianum var. erythronioides

Epicladium boothianum var. erythronioides, commonly known as the red-leafed marsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in eastern North America.

It features striking, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color, giving it its common name. It grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 30 inches, and thrives in moist, acidic soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
