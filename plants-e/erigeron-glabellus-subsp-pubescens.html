Erigeron glabellus subsp. pubescens

Erigeron glabellus subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
