Eriophyllum lanatum var. leucophyllum

Eriophyllum lanatum var. leucophyllum, commonly known as silver woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Oregon.

It is characterized by its soft, white to grayish-white fuzzy leaves and yellow ray florets. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
