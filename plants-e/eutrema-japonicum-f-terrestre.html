Eutrema japonicum f. terrestre

Eutrema japonicum f. terrestre, commonly known as the Japanese sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
