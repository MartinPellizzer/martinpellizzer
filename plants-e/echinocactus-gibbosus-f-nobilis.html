Echinocactus gibbosus f. nobilis

Echinocactus gibbosus f. nobilis, commonly known as the noble hedgehog cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a globular shape, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the tips of the stems. Its fleshy, water-storing stems are adapted to survive in hot, dry environments.

This cultivar is prized for its unique appearance and is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
