Elaeocarpus pauciflorus

Elaeocarpus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Elaeocarpus pauciflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
