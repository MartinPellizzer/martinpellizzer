Encelia hispida

Encelia hispida, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and spherical, yellow-flowered heads that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its adaptations include a deep root system and waxy leaf surfaces to reduce water loss.

Encelia hispida plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
