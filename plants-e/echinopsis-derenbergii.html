Echinopsis derenbergii

Echinopsis derenbergii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiny, globular shape and is often cultivated as a decorative plant. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments.

Echinopsis derenbergii requires minimal water and thrives in well-draining soil.
