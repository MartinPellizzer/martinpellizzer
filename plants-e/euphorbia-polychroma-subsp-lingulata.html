Euphorbia polychroma subsp. lingulata

Euphorbia polychroma subsp.

lingulata, commonly known as the red-veined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, ribbed stems that display a striking red or purple coloration, especially under stress or in bright light.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
