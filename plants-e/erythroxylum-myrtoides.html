Erythroxylum myrtoides

Erythroxylum myrtoides, commonly known as the "pink pepper" or "Peruvian pepper tree," is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that develop into elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves and stems contain alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
