Erica multiflexuosa

Erica multiflexuosa, commonly known as the many-branched heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit with numerous small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple, during the spring and early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica multiflexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as part of native plant landscapes.
