Ephedra przewalskii

Ephedra przewalskii, commonly known as the Chinese ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It contains the alkaloid ephedrine, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties and conservation status, it is also of interest in botanical and pharmacological research.
