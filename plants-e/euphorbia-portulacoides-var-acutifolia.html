Euphorbia portulacoides var. acutifolia

Euphorbia portulacoides var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf milkplant, is a succulent shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features a compact, cushion-like growth form with dense clusters of small, spiny leaves that are typically green to gray-green in color. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and well adapted to rocky, arid environments.

Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
