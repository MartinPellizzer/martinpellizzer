Eragrostis blepharostachya

Eragrostis blepharostachya, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of dryland ecosystems.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion and contributes to soil stabilization in degraded areas.
