Erucastrum cossonianum

Erucastrum cossonianum, commonly known as the Cossonian cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The leaves are pinnately divided and have a peppery aroma, similar to that of mustard plants.
