Eulalia concinna

Eulalia concinna, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Eulalia concinna is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
