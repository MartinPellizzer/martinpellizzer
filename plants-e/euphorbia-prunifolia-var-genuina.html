Euphorbia prunifolia var. genuina

Euphorbia prunifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the Prunefolia Spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, spiny stems and produces small, yellowish-green花朵 that are clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

The plant is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
