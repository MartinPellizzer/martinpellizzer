Eremochloa ciliaris var. gigantea

Eremochloa ciliaris var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is notable for its height, sometimes reaching over 2 meters, making it a striking feature in natural or cultivated landscapes.
