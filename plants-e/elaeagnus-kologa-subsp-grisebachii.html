Elaeagnus kologa subsp. grisebachii

Elaeagnus kologa subsp.

grisebachii, commonly known as the grayleaf silverberry, is a shrub native to China. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in a dense layer of fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
