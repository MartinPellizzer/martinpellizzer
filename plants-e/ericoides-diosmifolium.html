Ericoides diosmifolium

Ericoides diosmifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Ericoides diosmifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
