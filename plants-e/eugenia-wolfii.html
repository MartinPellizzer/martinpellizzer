Eugenia wolfii

Eugenia wolfii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or wolfii myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain a single seed, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia wolfii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
