Eugenia portoricensis var. brevipes

Eugenia portoricensis var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed wax apple, is a species native to Puerto Rico.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible, waxy berries that are yellow when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystem.
