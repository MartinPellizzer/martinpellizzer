Euonymus europaeus var. subvelutinus

Euonymus europaeus var. subvelutinus, commonly known as the European spindletree, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes contribute to its aesthetic appeal in landscaped settings.
