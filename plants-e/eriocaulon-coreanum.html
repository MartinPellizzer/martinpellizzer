Eriocaulon coreanum

Eriocaulon coreanum, commonly known as the Korean thread-leaf sedge, is a small perennial herb native to Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it is often found in low-light conditions.

Eriocaulon coreanum plays a role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
