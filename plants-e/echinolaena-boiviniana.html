Echinolaena boiviniana

Echinolaena boiviniana, commonly known as the Boivin's burrgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, spiky inflorescences that bear bracts. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds that are encased in a hairy, spiny structure, aiding in its propagation. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in coastal areas.

Its morphology and reproductive strategies make it well-adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
