Elaeagnus numajiriana

Elaeagnus numajiriana, commonly known as the Kashmir honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its silvery-haired leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Elaeagnus numajiriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
