Erythranthe thermalis

Erythranthe thermalis, commonly known as the thermal sedum, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It is adapted to cold, rocky environments and requires well-drained soil.
