Erigeron bellidiastrum var. bellidiastrum

Erigeron bellidiastrum var. bellidiastrum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-blue leaves and produces clusters of small, daisy-like white flowers with a blue center. Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive, silvery appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
