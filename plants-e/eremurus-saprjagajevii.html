Eremurus saprjagajevii

Eremurus saprjagajevii, commonly known as the Siberian Spearwort, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has fibrous roots and sturdy, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue.

Eremurus saprjagajevii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.
