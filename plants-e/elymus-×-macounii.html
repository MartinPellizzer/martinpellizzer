Elymus × macounii

Elymus × macounii, commonly known as Macoun's wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and forest edges. Its flowering heads are spike-like, containing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Elymus × macounii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
