Ebenus elliptica

Ebenus elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense cylindrical inflorescences, and come in shades of pink, purple, or white.

Ebenus elliptica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
