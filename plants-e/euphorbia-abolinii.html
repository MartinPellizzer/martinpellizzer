Euphorbia abolinii

Euphorbia abolinii, commonly known as the Persian spurge, is a succulent plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy tissues.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
