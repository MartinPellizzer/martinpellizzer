Epipactis microphylla var. nuda

Epipactis microphylla var. nuda, commonly known as the naked-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are often hairless, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

Epipactis microphylla var. nuda is a perennial herb that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
