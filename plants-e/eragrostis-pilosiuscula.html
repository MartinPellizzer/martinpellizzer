Eragrostis pilosiuscula

Eragrostis pilosiuscula, commonly known as silky fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Silky fescue is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
