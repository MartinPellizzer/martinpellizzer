Elytrigia varnensis

Elytrigia varnensis, commonly known as Varnen's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Elytrigia varnensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
