Eleusine inaequalis

Eleusine inaequalis, commonly known as rough false brome, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, slightly rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Eleusine inaequalis thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
