Eupatorium mairetianum

Eupatorium mairetianum, commonly known as the Mairetianum eupatorium, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium mairetianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
