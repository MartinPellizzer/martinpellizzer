Excoecaria cochinchinensis var. formosana

Excoecaria cochinchinensis var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan croton, is a species native to Taiwan.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces orange-red berries that contain seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
