Eupatorium setiferum

Eupatorium setiferum, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple to pink flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense, spiky inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Eupatorium setiferum is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.
