Eulophia deflexa

Eulophia deflexa, commonly known as the drooping eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, rhizomatous stem and a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces a single, elongated inflorescence bearing several showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinct, fragrant odor. Its blooms typically appear in late spring to early summer, and the plant thrives in shaded, moist forest environments.

Eulophia deflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
