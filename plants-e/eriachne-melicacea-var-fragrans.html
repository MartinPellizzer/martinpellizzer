Eriachne melicacea var. fragrans

Eriachne melicacea var. fragrans, commonly known as the sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, pale-green blades that emit a mild, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and woodland margins.

Due to its fragrant properties, it has been traditionally used by Indigenous communities for various cultural and medicinal purposes.
