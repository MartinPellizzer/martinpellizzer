Eleocharis kirkii

Eleocharis kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its morphology and habitat make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
