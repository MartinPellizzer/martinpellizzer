Eugenia argentea

Eugenia argentea, commonly known as silver eucalyptus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its lance-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic wood and has been used in traditional medicine.
