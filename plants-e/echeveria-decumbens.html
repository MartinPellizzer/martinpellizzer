Echeveria decumbens

Echeveria decumbens, commonly known as the Mexican rose, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
