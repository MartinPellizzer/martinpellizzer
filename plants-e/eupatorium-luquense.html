Eupatorium luquense

Eupatorium luquense, commonly known as the Luquense boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and swampy areas. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

Eupatorium luquense produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
