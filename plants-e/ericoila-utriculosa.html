Ericoila utriculosa

Ericoila utriculosa, commonly known as the bladderleaf ericoid, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in spring. Ericoila utriculosa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, moist environment.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its compact form and delicate floral display.
