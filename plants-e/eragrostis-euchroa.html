Eragrostis euchroa

Eragrostis euchroa, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Eragrostis euchroa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soils.
