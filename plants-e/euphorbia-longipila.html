Euphorbia longipila

Euphorbia longipila, commonly known as the long-stemmed spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height, with a smooth, greenish-brown surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick stems.

Euphorbia longipila is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
