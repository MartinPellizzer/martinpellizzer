Epacris fasciculata

Epacris fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Epacris fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
