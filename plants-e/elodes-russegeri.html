Elodes russegeri

Elodes russegeri, commonly known as the Russian waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that often form dense mats in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, though it is more commonly spread through vegetative reproduction. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often arranged in whorls along the stem.

Elodes russegeri plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
