Eucalyptus georgei subsp. fulgida

Eucalyptus georgei subsp.

fulgida, commonly known as the golden eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green.

The flowers are small, white or cream, and are arranged in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and seasonal flowering.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
