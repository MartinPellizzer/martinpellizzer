Entada burkei

Entada burkei, commonly known as the African breadnut, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. Its bark and seeds have been traditionally used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The plant is also valued for its timber and ornamental appeal.
