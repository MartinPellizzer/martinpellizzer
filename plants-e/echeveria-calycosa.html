Echeveria calycosa

Echeveria calycosa, commonly known as the Snowy Echeveria, is a species of succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the spring or summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Echeveria calycosa is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
