Erigeron reinanus

Erigeron reinanus, commonly known as the lesser fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with white ray florets.

It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
