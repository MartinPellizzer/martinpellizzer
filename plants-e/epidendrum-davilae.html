Epidendrum davilae

Epidendrum davilae, commonly known as the Pink Lady Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink and white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its pseudobulbs store water, allowing it to thrive in moist but well-drained soil.

Epidendrum davilae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
