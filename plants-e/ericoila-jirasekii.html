Ericoila jirasekii

Ericoila jirasekii, commonly known as the Jirasek's heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, and it produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink. Ericoila jirasekii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
