Evolvulus martii

Evolvulus martii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

Evolvulus martii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and easy care requirements.
