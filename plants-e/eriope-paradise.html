Eriope paradise

Eriope paradise, commonly known as the paradise eriope, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
