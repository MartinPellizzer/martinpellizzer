Erianthus angustifolius var. coloratus

Erianthus angustifolius var. coloratus, commonly known as the colorful narrowleaf grass, is a striking ornamental grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems with narrow, blade-like leaves that display vibrant color changes throughout the seasons. The plant produces fluffy, silver-white plumes in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage and seasonal coloration make it a popular choice for landscaping and native plant gardens.
