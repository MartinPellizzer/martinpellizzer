Euphorbia sipolisii

Euphorbia sipolisii, commonly known as the Sipolii spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with a short, thick stem and fleshy, spiny leaves that are typically green or bluish-green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thickened stems and leaves.

Euphorbia sipolisii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
