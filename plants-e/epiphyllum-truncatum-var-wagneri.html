Epiphyllum truncatum var. wagneri

Epiphyllum truncatum var. wagneri, commonly known as the Wagner's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a climbing plant with fleshy, leaf-like stems that are often covered in a layer of whitish hair. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that typically open at night, showcasing a range of colors from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique blooming habit and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners interested in night-blooming plants.
