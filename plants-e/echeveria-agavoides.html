Echeveria agavoides

Echeveria agavoides, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container planting.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
