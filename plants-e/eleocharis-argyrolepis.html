Eleocharis argyrolepis

Eleocharis argyrolepis, commonly known as silverleaf spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, white or pale-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered with a fine, silvery bloom.

Eleocharis argyrolepis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wetland wildlife.
