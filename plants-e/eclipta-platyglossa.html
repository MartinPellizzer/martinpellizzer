Eclipta platyglossa

Eclipta platyglossa, commonly known as blue false fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Eclipta platyglossa is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
