Eriocaulon buergerianum

Eriocaulon buergerianum, commonly known as the Buergerianum rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of China, India, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in wetland environments, often growing in marshes or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with a dense, compact growth habit that helps it survive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
