Euphrasia × brevipila var. glaberrima

Euphrasia × brevipila var. glaberrima, commonly known as the hairy eyebright, is a hybrid species within the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it the common name, and it has a compact, upright growth habit.

This variety is often found in the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.
