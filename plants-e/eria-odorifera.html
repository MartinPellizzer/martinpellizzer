Eria odorifera

Eria odorifera, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its most notable characteristic is the production of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

Eria odorifera is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
