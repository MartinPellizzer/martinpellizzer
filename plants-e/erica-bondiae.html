Erica bondiae

Erica bondiae, commonly known as the Bond's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica bondiae typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
