Elaeagnus × pyramidalis

Elaeagnus × pyramidalis, commonly known as silver berry or pyramidal silverberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that turn red in late summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in landscaping.
