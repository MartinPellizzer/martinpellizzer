Eperua rubiginosa

Eperua rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stained eperua, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces pods containing flat, leathery seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential pharmacological properties.

Eperua rubiginosa is often found in moist lowland forests and plays a role in local ecosystems.
