Erica alveiflora

Erica alveiflora, commonly known as the sand erica, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica alveiflora thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
