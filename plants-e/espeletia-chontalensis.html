Espeletia chontalensis

Espeletia chontalensis, commonly known as the Chontalensis heather, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or volcanic soils and thrives in cool, dry climates. It is well adapted to high-altitude environments, often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
