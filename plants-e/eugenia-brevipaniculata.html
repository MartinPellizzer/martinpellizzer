Eugenia brevipaniculata

Eugenia brevipaniculata, commonly known as the short-bracted eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia brevipaniculata is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
