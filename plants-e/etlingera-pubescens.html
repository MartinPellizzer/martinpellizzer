Etlingera pubescens

Etlingera pubescens, commonly known as the hairy torch ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking red or orange inflorescence, which resembles a torch, and its hairy leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
