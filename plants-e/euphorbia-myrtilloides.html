Euphorbia myrtilloides

Euphorbia myrtilloides, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the Euphorbia genus and can be toxic if ingested. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
