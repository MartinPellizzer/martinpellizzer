Euphorbia heishuiensis

Euphorbia heishuiensis, commonly known as the Heishui spurge, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native region.
