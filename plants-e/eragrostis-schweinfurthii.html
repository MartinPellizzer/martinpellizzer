Eragrostis schweinfurthii

Eragrostis schweinfurthii, commonly known as Schwielfurth's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky soils, exhibiting a clump-forming habit with dense, tufted foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous minute, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eragrostis schweinfurthii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
