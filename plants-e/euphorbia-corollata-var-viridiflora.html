Euphorbia corollata var. viridiflora

Euphorbia corollata var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered spurge, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with succulent, somewhat fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters known as cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
