Erigeron obovatus

Erigeron obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. Its leaves are broadly ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and branching.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
