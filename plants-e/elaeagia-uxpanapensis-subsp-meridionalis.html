Elaeagia uxpanapensis subsp. meridionalis

Elaeagia uxpanapensis subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Mexico. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface.

The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and scrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
