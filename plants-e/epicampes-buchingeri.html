Epicampes buchingeri

Epicampes buchingeri, commonly known as the Buchinger's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
