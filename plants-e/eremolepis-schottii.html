Eremolepis schottii

Eremolepis schottii, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Eremolepis schottii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
