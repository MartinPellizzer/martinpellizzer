Eugenia suffruticosa var. latifolia

Eugenia suffruticosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved evergreen, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink and appear in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
