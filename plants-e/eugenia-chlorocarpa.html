Eugenia chlorocarpa

Eugenia chlorocarpa, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers and dark, glossy leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Eugenia chlorocarpa is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
