Eragrostis amanda

Eragrostis amanda, commonly known as Amanda's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
