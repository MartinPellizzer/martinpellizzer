Etlingera hyalina

Etlingera hyalina, commonly known as the glassy ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically green or yellowish in color.

Etlingera hyalina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
