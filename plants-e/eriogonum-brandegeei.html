Eriogonum brandegeei

Eriogonum brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegge's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green, hairy, and adapted to arid conditions, making it well-suited for dry environments.

Eriogonum brandegeei plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
