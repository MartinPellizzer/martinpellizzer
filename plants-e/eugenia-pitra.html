Eugenia pitra

Eugenia pitra, commonly known as the Java apple or wild apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical ecosystems.
