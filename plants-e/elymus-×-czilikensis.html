Elymus × czilikensis

Elymus × czilikensis, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. This plant is native to regions of Central Asia and is known for its adaptability to various soil types and climatic conditions. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Additionally, Elymus × czilikensis has been studied for its potential as a forage crop and its role in sustainable agriculture.
