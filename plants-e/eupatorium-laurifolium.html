Eupatorium laurifolium

Eupatorium laurifolium, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are often hairy.

Eupatorium laurifolium is often used in herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
