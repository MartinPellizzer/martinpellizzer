Eupatorium glandulosissimum

Eupatorium glandulosissimum, commonly known as the sticky eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its flowering stems are usually hairy and often branched, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium glandulosissimum is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
