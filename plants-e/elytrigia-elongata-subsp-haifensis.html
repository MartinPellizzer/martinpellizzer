Elytrigia elongata subsp. haifensis

Elytrigia elongata subsp.

haifensis, commonly known as the Haifa grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and parts of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
