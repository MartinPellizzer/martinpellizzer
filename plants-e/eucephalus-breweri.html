Eucephalus breweri

Eucephalus breweri, commonly known as the Brewer's bluebelle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eucephalus breweri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
