Erigeron acris subsp. macrophyllus

Erigeron acris subsp.

macrophyllus, commonly known as the Showy Blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
