Eugenia arbutifolia var. obscura

Eugenia arbutifolia var. obscura, commonly known as the darkleaf evergreen, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its fruits are small, dark berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
