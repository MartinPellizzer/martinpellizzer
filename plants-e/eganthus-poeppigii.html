Eganthus poeppigii

Eganthus poeppigii, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that resemble snowflakes, which bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has small, narrow leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Eganthus poeppigii is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
