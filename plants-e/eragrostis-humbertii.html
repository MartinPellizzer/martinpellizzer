Eragrostis humbertii

Eragrostis humbertii, commonly known as Humbert's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in landscaping and restoration projects.
