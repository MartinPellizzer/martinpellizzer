Eriogonum spathulatum var. panguicense

Eriogonum spathulatum var. panguicense, commonly known as the Panguis Basin buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species suited to desert environments. It is often found in grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and along rocky outcrops.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
