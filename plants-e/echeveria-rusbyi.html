Echeveria rusbyi

Echeveria rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces showy, violet-pink flowers on tall stems during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its compact form.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
