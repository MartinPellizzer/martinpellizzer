Eurya obliquifolia

Eurya obliquifolia, commonly known as the zigzag eurya, is a species of evergreen shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, India, and Myanmar. The plant is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that often have a zigzag or serrated margin. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible.

Eurya obliquifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and seasonal floral display.
