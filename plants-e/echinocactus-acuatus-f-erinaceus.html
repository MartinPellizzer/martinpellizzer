Echinocactus acuatus f. erinaceus

Echinocactus acuatus f. erinaceus, commonly known as the erinaceus cactus, is a unique hybrid plant originating from a cross between Echinocactus acuatus and Echinocactus horstii.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often adorned with a distinctive white or pale blue waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It is native to the arid regions of Mexico and thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in cactus gardens and as a houseplant.
