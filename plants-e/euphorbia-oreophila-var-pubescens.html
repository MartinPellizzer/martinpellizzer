Euphorbia oreophila var. pubescens

Euphorbia oreophila var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mountain spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
