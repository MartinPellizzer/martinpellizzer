Echinospermum polymorphum var. minimum

Echinospermum polymorphum var. minimum, commonly known as the dwarf echinospermum, is a compact, flowering plant native to South America.

It features small, hairy leaves and produces delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive, spiky central structure. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries due to its compact size and vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to botanical collections and gardens.
