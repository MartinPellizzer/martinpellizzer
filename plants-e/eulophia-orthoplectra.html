Eulophia orthoplectra

Eulophia orthoplectra, commonly known as the erect-leaved eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a rhizome that produces multiple pseudobulbs. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces spike-like inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers.

The botanical characteristics of Eulophia orthoplectra make it a notable species within the orchid family, valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
