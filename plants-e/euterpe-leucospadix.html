Euterpe leucospadix

Euterpe leucospadix, commonly known as the white-spotted palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, yellow fruits containing a single seed. Euterpe leucospadix is adapted to humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
