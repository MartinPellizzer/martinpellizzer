Erigeron stenophyllus var. tetrapleuris

Erigeron stenophyllus var. tetrapleuris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are linear and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are small, white to pale blue, and bloom in late summer.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and is often found in grassland and chaparral habitats.
