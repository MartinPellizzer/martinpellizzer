Eugenia neotristis

Eugenia neotristis, commonly known as the New Guinea cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit, which is a small, red, cherry-like berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

Eugenia neotristis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
