Euphorbia anacampseros var. anacampseros

Euphorbia anacampseros var. anacampseros, commonly known as the Canary Island spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features thick, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Its slow growth and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
