Erodium daucoides subsp. macrocalyx

Erodium daucoides subsp.

macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are schizocarps, releasing small, kidney-shaped seeds when mature.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.
