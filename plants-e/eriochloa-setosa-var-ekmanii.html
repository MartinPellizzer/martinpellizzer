Eriochloa setosa var. ekmanii

Eriochloa setosa var. ekmanii, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Botanically, it is noted for its distinctive floral structure and adaptability to various soil types.
