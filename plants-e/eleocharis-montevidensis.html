Eleocharis montevidensis

Eleocharis montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Uruguay and parts of Argentina. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
