Erica jasminoides

Erica jasminoides, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, range in color from white to pale yellow, often resembling jasmine in scent.

Erica jasminoides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
