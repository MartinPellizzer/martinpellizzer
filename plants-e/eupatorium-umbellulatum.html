Eupatorium umbellulatum

Eupatorium umbellulatum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed, contributing to its common name. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Eupatorium umbellulatum is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
