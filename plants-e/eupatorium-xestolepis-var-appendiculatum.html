Eupatorium xestolepis var. appendiculatum

Eupatorium xestolepis var. appendiculatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features upright stems covered with stiff, glandular hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clustered umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
