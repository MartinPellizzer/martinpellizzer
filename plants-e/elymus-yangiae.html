Elymus yangiae

Elymus yangiae, commonly known as the Yunnan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool and temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with a pale blue or greenish hue.

Elymus yangiae is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
