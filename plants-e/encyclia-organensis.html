Encyclia organensis

Encyclia organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains orchid, is a species of orchid native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically produces 3–5 lance-shaped leaves and has a single, erect stem with 3–6 elliptical flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
