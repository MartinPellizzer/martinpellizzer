Euphorbia akmanii

Euphorbia akmanii, commonly known as the Desert Spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features a thick, cylindrical stem covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Euphorbia akmanii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
