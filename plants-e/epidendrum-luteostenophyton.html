Epidendrum luteostenophyton

Epidendrum luteostenophyton, commonly known as the golden-veined epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in humid tropical environments. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

The species is noted for its adaptability to a range of light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
