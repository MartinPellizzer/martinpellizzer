Eriocaulon hamiltonianum var. minimum

Eriocaulon hamiltonianum var. minimum, commonly known as the dwarf spike-rush, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed, forming a rosette-like structure. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters at the tips of the stems.

It is characterized by its compact size and is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its aesthetic appeal.
