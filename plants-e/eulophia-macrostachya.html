Eulophia macrostachya

Eulophia macrostachya, commonly known as the giant eulophia, is a striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that can reach several feet in length, featuring numerous fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks in tropical rainforests. Its pseudobulbs are robust and store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
