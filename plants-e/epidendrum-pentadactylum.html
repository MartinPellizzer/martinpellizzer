Epidendrum pentadactylum

Epidendrum pentadactylum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Amazon, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Epidendrum pentadactylum is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
