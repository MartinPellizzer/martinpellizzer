Eugenia tumida

Eugenia tumida, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruit. Eugenia tumida thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
