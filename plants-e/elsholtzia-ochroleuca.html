Elsholtzia ochroleuca

Elsholtzia ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pale yellow to white, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Elsholtzia ochroleuca is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.
