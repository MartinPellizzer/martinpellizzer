Euphorbia sogdiana

Euphorbia sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Euphorbia sogdiana is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
