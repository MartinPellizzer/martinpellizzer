Ehretia andrieuxii

Ehretia andrieuxii, commonly known as the Andrieux's ehretia, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
