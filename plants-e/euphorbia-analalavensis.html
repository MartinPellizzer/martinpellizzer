Euphorbia analalavensis

Euphorbia analalavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
