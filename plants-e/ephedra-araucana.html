Ephedra araucana

Ephedra araucana, commonly known as the Andean ephedra, is a shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves, which are adapted to the harsh, dry environments it inhabits. The plant produces yellow-green flowers and is often found in rocky or alpine regions. It belongs to the Ephedraceae family, which is closely related to the Ginkgoales order.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally by indigenous communities for various purposes.
