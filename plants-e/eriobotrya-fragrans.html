Eriobotrya fragrans

Eriobotrya fragrans, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy white flowers in early spring, which are followed by sweet, aromatic fruits resembling small pears. The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
