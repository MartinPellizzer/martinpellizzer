Eugenioides martinicense var. paraguayense

Eugenioides martinicense var. paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguayan myrtle, is a species native to South America, particularly Paraguay and northern Argentina.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a compact, shrub-like growth habit.
