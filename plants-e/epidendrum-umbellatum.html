Epidendrum umbellatum

Epidendrum umbellatum, commonly known as the Umbrella Epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-draining soil. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Epidendrum umbellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a tropical or greenhouse setting.
