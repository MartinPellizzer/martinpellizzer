Eryngium rechingeri

Eryngium rechingeri, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust, upright stem covered in coarse, hairy foliage. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Eryngium rechingeri is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.
