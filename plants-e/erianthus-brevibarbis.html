Erianthus brevibarbis

Erianthus brevibarbis, commonly known as the short-barbed bluestem, is a tall, perennial grass native to the United States, particularly in the southern and central regions.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue to purplish flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.

Erianthus brevibarbis is also used in some regions for forage and as a component in native plant gardens.
