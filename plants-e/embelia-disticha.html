Embelia disticha

Embelia disticha, commonly known as the two-branched embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by elliptical, woody berries. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and fragrant foliage.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
