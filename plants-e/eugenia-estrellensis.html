Eugenia estrellensis

Eugenia estrellensis, commonly known as the Estrella guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-purple fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
