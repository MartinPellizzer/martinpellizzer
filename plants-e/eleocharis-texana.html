Eleocharis texana

Eleocharis texana, commonly known as Texas spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Eleocharis texana plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and aiding in water filtration.
