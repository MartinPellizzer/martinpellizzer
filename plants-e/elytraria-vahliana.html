Elytraria vahliana

Elytraria vahliana, commonly known as the Madagascar grass or Vahli's grass, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Elytraria vahliana is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its native habitat, making it a distinctive component of Madagascar's flora.
