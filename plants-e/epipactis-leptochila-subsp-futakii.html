Epipactis leptochila subsp. futakii

Epipactis leptochila subsp.

futakii, commonly known as the Japanese helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan. It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces erect spikes of greenish-yellow flowers with prominent, hooded sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a long flowering period, often from late spring to early autumn.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact inflorescence compared to the typical form of *Epipactis leptochila*.
