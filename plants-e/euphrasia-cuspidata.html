Euphrasia cuspidata

Euphrasia cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped eyebright, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers with two upper petals and two lower ones, arranged in a whorl. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Euphrasia cuspidata is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
