Eugenia itahypensis

Eugenia itahypensis, commonly known as the Itahy poinsettia, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, while its flowers are small and white, clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season.
