Eupatorium subintegerrimum var. aliciae

Eupatorium subintegerrimum var. aliciae, commonly known as Alicia's eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
