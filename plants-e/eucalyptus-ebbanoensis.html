Eucalyptus ebbanoensis

Eucalyptus ebbanoensis, commonly known as the Ebbanoensis gumtree, is a species of eucalyptus native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It is a tall tree, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are lance-shaped, glossy green, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. Its flowers are small and white or pale blue, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The tree is valued for its timber and has cultural significance to the local Indigenous communities.
