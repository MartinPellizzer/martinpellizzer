Elephantopus hirtiflorus

Elephantopus hirtiflorus, commonly known as hairy-flowered elephantopus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as fields and along riverbanks. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
