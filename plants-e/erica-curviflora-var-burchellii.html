Erica curviflora var. burchellii

Erica curviflora var. burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
