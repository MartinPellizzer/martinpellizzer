Eupatorium pilosum

Eupatorium pilosum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, often hairy, and have a distinctive rough texture.

Eupatorium pilosum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
