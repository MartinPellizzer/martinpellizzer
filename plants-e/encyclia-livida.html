Encyclia livida

Encyclia livida, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a terrestrial plant that typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinct, fragrant scent. Its flowers are usually borne on a single, erect stem and bloom during the dry season.

Encyclia livida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
