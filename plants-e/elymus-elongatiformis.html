Elymus elongatiformis

Elymus elongatiformis, commonly known as the long-form grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Elymus elongatiformis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological restoration projects.
