Eliokarmos namaquanulus

Eliokarmos namaquanulus, commonly known as the Namibian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Namib Desert.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the rainy season. It grows close to the ground, forming dense clusters that help reduce water loss.

Its unique adaptations make it well-suited for extreme desert environments.
