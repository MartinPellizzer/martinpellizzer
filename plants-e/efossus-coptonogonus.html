Efossus coptonogonus

Efossus coptonogonus, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight and reduces water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring. Its stems are upright and branched, often forming dense shrubs.

Efossus coptonogonus is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
