It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into small, pinnae with a serrated margin. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in clusters called sori. Elaphoglossum williamsiorum is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and scientific study.
