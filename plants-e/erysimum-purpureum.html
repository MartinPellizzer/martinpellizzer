Erysimum purpureum

Erysimum purpureum, commonly known as the purple wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that produces showy, violet-purple flowers in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are usually upright and branched. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
