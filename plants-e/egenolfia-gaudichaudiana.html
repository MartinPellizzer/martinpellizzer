Egenolfia gaudichaudiana

Egenolfia gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Egenolfia gaudichaudiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
