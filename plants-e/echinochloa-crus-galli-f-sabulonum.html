Echinochloa crus-galli f. sabulonum

Echinochloa crus-galli f. sabulonum, commonly known as the sandspur grass, is a variant of the common barnyard grass.

It is a annual grass species characterized by its distinctive spiny seed heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a prostrate or erect growth habit.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
