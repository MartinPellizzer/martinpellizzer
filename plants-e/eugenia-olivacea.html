Eugenia olivacea

Eugenia olivacea, commonly known as the olive leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Eugenia olivacea is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
