Eupatorium divaricatum

Eupatorium divaricatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, gray-green foliage and clusters of tiny, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
