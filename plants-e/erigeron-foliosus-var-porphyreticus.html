Erigeron foliosus var. porphyreticus

Erigeron foliosus var. porphyreticus, commonly known as the Porphyretic Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and compact growth habit.
