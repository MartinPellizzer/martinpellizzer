Erica bracteata

Erica bracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, leathery leaves.

Erica bracteata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
