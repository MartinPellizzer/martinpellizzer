Eleotis brazzae

Eleotis brazzae, commonly known as the Brazza's eleotrid, is a small freshwater fish native to the rivers of Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Eleotridae, which includes various species of sleeper gobies. This species is characterized by its slender body and elongated fins, which aid in its agile movement through aquatic vegetation. Eleotis brazzae typically inhabits slow-moving waters with dense plant cover, where it can hide from predators.

Its diet consists mainly of small invertebrates and detritus, making it an important component of the aquatic ecosystem.
