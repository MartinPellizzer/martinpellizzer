Eubotrys elongata

Eubotrys elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features elongated, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with five petals arranged in a bell-like shape. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Eubotrys elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
