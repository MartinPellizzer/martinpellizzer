Euphorbia itampolensis

Euphorbia itampolensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces a unique sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its structure and appearance make it a distinctive member of the euphorbia genus.
