Elymus tsukushiensis var. transiens

Elymus tsukushiensis var. transiens, commonly known as the Japanese basket grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
