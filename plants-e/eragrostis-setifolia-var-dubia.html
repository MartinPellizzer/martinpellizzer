Eragrostis setifolia var. dubia

Eragrostis setifolia var. dubia, commonly known as the slender false wheatgrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
