Euphrasia holliana

Euphrasia holliana, commonly known as the Christmas or Holy Night carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, glandular-hairy stems that often branch near the base. The plant produces small, showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of red, pink, or white, and a distinctive yellow or orange throat. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface.

Euphrasia holliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
