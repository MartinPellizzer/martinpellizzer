Euphorbia hyssopifolia var. paraguayensis

Euphorbia hyssopifolia var. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features a branched stem with smooth, grayish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
