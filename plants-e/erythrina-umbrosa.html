Erythrina umbrosa

Erythrina umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, flame-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Erythrina umbrosa thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
