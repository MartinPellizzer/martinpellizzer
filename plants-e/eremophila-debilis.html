Eremophila debilis

Eremophila debilis, commonly known as the slender emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the plant family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink in color, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Eremophila debilis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for drought-prone regions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
