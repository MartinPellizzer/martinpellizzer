Euonymus europaeus f. atropurpureus

Euonymus europaeus f. atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple euonymus, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Europe.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a distinctive purplish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red, four-lobed capsules that persist into winter. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its foliage provides year-round interest, making it a popular choice for garden borders and foundation plantings.
