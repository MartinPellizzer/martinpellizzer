Eriogonum jamesii var. flavescens

Eriogonum jamesii var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and mountain slopes. It has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
