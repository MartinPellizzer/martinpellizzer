Eleocharis ovata var. obtusa

Eleocharis ovata var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Eleocharis ovata var. obtusa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
