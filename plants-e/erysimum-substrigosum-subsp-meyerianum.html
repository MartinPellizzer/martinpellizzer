Erysimum substrigosum subsp. meyerianum

Erysimum substrigosum subsp.

meyerianum, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
