Euphorbia togakusensis

Euphorbia togakusensis, commonly known as the Togakushi spurge, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and distinctive yellow-orange cyathia. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained soil. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
