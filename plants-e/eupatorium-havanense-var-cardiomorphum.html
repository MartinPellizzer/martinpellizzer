Eupatorium havanense var. cardiomorphum

Eupatorium havanense var. cardiomorphum, commonly known as the Cuban feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shady habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
