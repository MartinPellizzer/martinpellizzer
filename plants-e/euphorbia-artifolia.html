Euphorbia artifolia

Euphorbia artifolia, commonly known as the artificial euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that resemble artificial plastic tubing, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in a cluster known as a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
