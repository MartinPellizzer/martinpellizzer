Evolvulus linifolius var. grandiflorus

Evolvulus linifolius var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for containers, hanging baskets, and ground covers.
