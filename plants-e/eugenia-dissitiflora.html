Eugenia dissitiflora

Eugenia dissitiflora, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its fruits are small, round, and edible, with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia dissitiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits in home gardens.
