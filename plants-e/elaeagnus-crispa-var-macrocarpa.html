Elaeagnus crispa var. macrocarpa

Elaeagnus crispa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

Additionally, it has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
