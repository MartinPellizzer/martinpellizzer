Euphorbia shirensis

Euphorbia shirensis, commonly known as the Shireland spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branched stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters known as cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
