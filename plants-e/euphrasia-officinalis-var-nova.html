Euphrasia officinalis var. nova

Euphrasia officinalis var. nova, commonly known as the Nova Crown Vetch, is a variant of the common eyebright plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported soothing properties, particularly for eye conditions.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a flowering period that extends from spring to early summer.
