Eucalyptus alba var. alba

Eucalyptus alba var. alba, commonly known as the white ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white to greyish bark that peels in thin, fibrous strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a waxy coating. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
