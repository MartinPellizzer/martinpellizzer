Echinocactus gibbosus var. cerebriformis

Echinocactus gibbosus var. cerebriformis, commonly known as the brain-shaped cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, brain-like appearance, with ribbed stems that form a dense, spiny structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged exterior. It is well adapted to dry climates, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
