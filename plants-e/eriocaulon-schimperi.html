Eriocaulon schimperi

Eriocaulon schimperi, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, often found in areas with standing water or high humidity. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Eriocaulon schimperi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
