Eupatorium purpureum var. foliosum

Eupatorium purpureum var. foliosum, commonly known as purple stem false dragonhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, lavender to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
