Erigeron alpinus subsp. uniflorus

Erigeron alpinus subsp.

uniflorus, commonly known as the alpine blueweed, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, blue to violet flowering heads.

The plant typically produces a single flower per head, which is a distinguishing feature of the subspecies. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
