Eriosyce subgibbosa var. clavata

Eriosyce subgibbosa var. clavata, commonly known as the Clavate Eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
