Erythrina platyphylla

Erythrina platyphylla, commonly known as the flatleaf coral tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright red flowers that bloom in dense, umbrella-like clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three broad, elliptical leaflets. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

Erythrina platyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
