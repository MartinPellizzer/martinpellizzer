Eremophila racemosa

Eremophila racemosa, commonly known as the clustered emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its erect, woody structure and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers range in color from pink to red, and it produces small, elliptical, woody fruits.

Eremophila racemosa is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
