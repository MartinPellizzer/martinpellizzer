Eugenia pilotantha

Eugenia pilotantha, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. These flowers are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia pilotantha is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
