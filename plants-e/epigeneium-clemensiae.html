Epigeneium clemensiae

Epigeneium clemensiae, commonly known as the slender epigeneium, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with a distinctive, fleshy spathe.

Epigeneium clemensiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
