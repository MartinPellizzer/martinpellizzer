Elymus angulatus

Elymus angulatus, commonly known as the angular wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, angular stems that often have a distinctive zigzag appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets, and its leaves are typically narrow and hairy. Elymus angulatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
