Elaeagnus conferta subsp. mollis

Elaeagnus conferta subsp.

mollis, commonly known as silverleaf elaeagnus, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early spring. Its roots are capable of fixing nitrogen, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
