Eleocharis maculosa subsp. fuscopurpurea

Eleocharis maculosa subsp.

fuscopurpurea, commonly known as purple-spored spike-rush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear purple or reddish-brown spikelets.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in shallow water or saturated soil. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often reddish in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
