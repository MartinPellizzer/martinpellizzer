Eugenia deflexa

Eugenia deflexa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or small-leaved guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing a single seed. Eugenia deflexa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
