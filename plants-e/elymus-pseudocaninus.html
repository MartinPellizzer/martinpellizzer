Elymus pseudocaninus

Elymus pseudocaninus, commonly known as the false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets at the ends of its stems, which are typically green to purplish in color. Elymus pseudocaninus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
