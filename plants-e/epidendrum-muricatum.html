Epidendrum muricatum

Epidendrum muricatum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and clusters of dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Epidendrum muricatum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
