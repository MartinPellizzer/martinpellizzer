Erigeron pumilus subsp. pumilus

Erigeron pumilus subsp.

pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf fleabane, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically reaches a height of less than 10 centimeters, with a mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.
