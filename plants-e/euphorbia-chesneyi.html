Euphorbia chesneyi

Euphorbia chesneyi, commonly known as the Desert Caper, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features thick, ribbed stems and produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its stems for survival in harsh environments.

Euphorbia chesneyi is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to extreme weather conditions.
