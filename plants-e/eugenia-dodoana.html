Eugenia dodoana

Eugenia dodoana, commonly known as the Dodoana myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Eugenia dodoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
