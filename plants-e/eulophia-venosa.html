Eulophia venosa

Eulophia venosa, commonly known as the veined eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green leaves with prominent veins and its clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are often pollinated by insects, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Eulophia venosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
