Eulophia macowanii

Eulophia macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's eulophia, is a striking orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with strap-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, showy flowers with intricate patterns of purple, white, and yellow. Its flowers are typically 5 to 7 cm in diameter, with a unique, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Eulophia macowanii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
