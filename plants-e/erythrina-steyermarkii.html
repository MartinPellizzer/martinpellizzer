Erythrina steyermarkii

Erythrina steyermarkii, commonly known as the scarlet coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces distinctive, woody pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

Erythrina steyermarkii is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
