Eupatorium symphyopappus var. pohlianum

Eupatorium symphyopappus var. pohlianum, commonly known as the Pohlian's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, a common trait among members of its family. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.
