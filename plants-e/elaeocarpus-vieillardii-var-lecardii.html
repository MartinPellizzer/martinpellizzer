Elaeocarpus vieillardii var. lecardii

Elaeocarpus vieillardii var. lecardii, commonly known as the Lecard's elaeocarp, is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
