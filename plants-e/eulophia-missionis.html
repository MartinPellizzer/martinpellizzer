Eulophia missionis

Eulophia missionis, commonly known as the Mission orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are linear and grass-like. Eulophia missionis thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is a relatively hardy species, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
