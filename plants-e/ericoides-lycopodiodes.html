Ericoides lycopodiodes

Ericoides lycopodiodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf clubmoss, is a species of lycopod that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophyte produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila.

Ericoides lycopodiodes plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
