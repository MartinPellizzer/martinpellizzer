Eupatorium cordatum

Eupatorium cordatum, commonly known as heartleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
