Erysimum passgalense

Erysimum passgalense, commonly known as the Turkish wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it an attractive addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
