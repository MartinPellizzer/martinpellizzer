Eragrostis nigra var. trachycarpa

Eragrostis nigra var. trachycarpa, commonly known as blackgrass or rough-seeded blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dark-colored stems and rough, hairy texture on its leaves and seed heads. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, dark brown seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
