Eriocaulon pectinatum

Eriocaulon pectinatum, commonly known as the feather tassel sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized reproductive structures.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that resemble tassels, which are typical of sedges. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that aids in its survival in waterlogged soils.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
