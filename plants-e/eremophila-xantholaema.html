Eremophila xantholaema

Eremophila xantholaema, commonly known as the yellow emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Eremophila xantholaema is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
