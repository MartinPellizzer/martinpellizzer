Epilobium × crassicaule

Epilobium × crassicaule, commonly known as broad-leaved willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30–100 cm and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitats.
