Ecbolium thymifolium

Ecbolium thymifolium, commonly known as the thyme-leaved eryngo, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that resemble those of thyme, and its small, yellowish composite flowers. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments. The plant is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
