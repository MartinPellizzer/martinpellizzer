Erica nudiflora

Erica nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. Erica nudiflora thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
