Eupatorium foeniculaceum var. traganthes

Eupatorium foeniculaceum var. traganthes, commonly known as the narrowleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
