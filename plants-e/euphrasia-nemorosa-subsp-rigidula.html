Euphrasia nemorosa subsp. rigidula

Euphrasia nemorosa subsp.

rigidula, commonly known as the rigid marsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or yellow-green in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is distinguished by its stiff, upright stems and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
