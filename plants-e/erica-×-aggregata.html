Erica × aggregata

Erica × aggregata, commonly known as the Heath or Erica, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Erica × aggregata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
