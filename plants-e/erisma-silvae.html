Erisma silvae

Erisma silvae, commonly known as the silverleaf erisma, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are coated in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in early spring. Erisma silvae thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to native plant collections.
