Erythroxylum luculentum

Erythroxylum luculentum, commonly known as the golden leaf coca, is a species of flowering plant in the Erythroxylaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, red-brown fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is notable for its potential traditional uses and its ecological role in its native habitat.
