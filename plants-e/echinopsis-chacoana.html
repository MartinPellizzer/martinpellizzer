Echinopsis chacoana

Echinopsis chacoana, commonly known as the Chacoan night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its columnar stem and spiny appearance. The plant produces large, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Echinopsis chacoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
