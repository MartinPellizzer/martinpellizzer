Euphorbia chrysocoma var. glaucophylla

Euphorbia chrysocoma var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.

Its slow growth rate makes it suitable for container gardening and landscape design in water-efficient settings.
