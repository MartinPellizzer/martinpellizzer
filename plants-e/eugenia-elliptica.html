Eugenia elliptica

Eugenia elliptica, commonly known as the oval guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp. Eugenia elliptica thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
