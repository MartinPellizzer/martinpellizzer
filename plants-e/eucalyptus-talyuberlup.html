Eucalyptus talyuberlup

Eucalyptus talyuberlup, commonly known as the Talyuberlup mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that often reveals a fibrous inner layer. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue.

The Talyuberlup mallee is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
