Elaeocarpus makinoi

Elaeocarpus makinoi, commonly known as the Makino's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, often used in traditional medicine. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
