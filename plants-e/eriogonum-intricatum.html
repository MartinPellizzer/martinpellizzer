Eriogonum intricatum

Eriogonum intricatum, commonly known as the intricate buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Eriogonum intricatum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush ecosystems.
