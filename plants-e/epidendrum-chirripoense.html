Epidendrum chirripoense

Epidendrum chirripoense, commonly known as the Chirripo orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of Costa Rica and Panama.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at elevations between 1,200 and 2,500 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth surface and a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Epidendrum chirripoense is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
