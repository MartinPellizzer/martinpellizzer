Euphorbia mayana

Euphorbia mayana, commonly known as the Mayan spurge, is a succulent plant native to Central America.

It features a branched stem with smooth, greenish-gray bark and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and dry forests, adapting well to tropical and subtropical climates.

Its latex contains toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
