Epidendrum platycardium

Epidendrum platycardium, commonly known as the Broad-leafed Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white or silver hair. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum platycardium is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardeners.
