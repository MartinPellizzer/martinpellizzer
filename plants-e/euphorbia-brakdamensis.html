Euphorbia brakdamensis

Euphorbia brakdamensis, commonly known as the Brakdam euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are usually spiny or lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Euphorbia brakdamensis is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes.
