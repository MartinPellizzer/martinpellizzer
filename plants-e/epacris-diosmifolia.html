Epacris diosmifolia

Epacris diosmifolia, commonly known as the silver-leafed epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically silver-white in color, providing a striking contrast to the pink or purple blooms. This species thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in open forests or heathlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
