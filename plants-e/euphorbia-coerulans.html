Euphorbia coerulans

Euphorbia coerulans, commonly known as the blue snowplant, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with dense clusters of blue-gray leaves that give it a frosty appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
