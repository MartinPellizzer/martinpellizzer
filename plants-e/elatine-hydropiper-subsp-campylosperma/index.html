Elatine Hydropiper Subsp. Campylosperma

Elatine hydropiper subsp. campylosperma is a lesser-known aquatic plant that thrives in wetland environments. It belongs to the Elatinaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and unique reproductive structures. This subspecies is often found in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its ability to grow in nutrient-poor soils makes it an important indicator of water quality. Despite its modest appearance, it plays a vital role in supporting local ecosystems.
