Ericameria diffusa

Ericameria diffusa, commonly known as sticky goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Ericameria diffusa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and use in erosion control and ornamental gardening.
