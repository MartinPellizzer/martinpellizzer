Epacris waltonii

Epacris waltonii, commonly known as the pink epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually from summer to autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Epacris waltonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
