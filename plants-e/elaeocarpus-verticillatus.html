Elaeocarpus verticillatus

Elaeocarpus verticillatus, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves that are arranged in whorls of three to five around the stems. The plant produces showy white or pale pink flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental horticulture and ecological restoration.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
