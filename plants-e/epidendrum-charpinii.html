Epidendrum charpinii

Epidendrum charpinii, commonly known as the Charpin's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Epidendrum charpinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow under proper conditions.
