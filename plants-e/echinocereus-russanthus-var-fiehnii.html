Echinocereus russanthus var. fiehnii

Echinocereus russanthus var. fiehnii, commonly known as the Fiehn's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a spiny, ribbed stem that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in xeriscaped gardens.
