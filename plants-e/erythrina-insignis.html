Erythrina insignis

Erythrina insignis, commonly known as the flame tree or red bean tree, is a tropical leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Erythrina insignis thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and nitrogen-fixing properties.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various pollinators and wildlife.
