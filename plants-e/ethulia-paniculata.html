Ethulia paniculata

Ethulia paniculata, commonly known as the panicled ethulia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Ethulia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
