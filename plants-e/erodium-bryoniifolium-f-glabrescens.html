Erodium bryoniifolium f. glabrescens

Erodium bryoniifolium f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous bedstraw, is a variant of the bedstraw species characterized by its reduced pubescence.

It is a low-growing annual herb native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, where it thrives in full sun.
