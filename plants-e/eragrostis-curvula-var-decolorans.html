Eragrostis curvula var. decolorans

Eragrostis curvula var. decolorans, commonly known as the pale lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that often exhibit a bluish-green hue and a distinctive blue-gray sheen. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
