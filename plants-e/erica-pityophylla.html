Erica pityophylla

Erica pityophylla, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen habit. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, often covering the entire plant in a delicate display. Erica pityophylla thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
