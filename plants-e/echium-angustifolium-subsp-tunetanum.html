Echium angustifolium subsp. tunetanum

Echium angustifolium subsp.

tunetanum, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
