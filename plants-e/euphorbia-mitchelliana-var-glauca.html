Euphorbia mitchelliana var. glauca

Euphorbia mitchelliana var. glauca, commonly known as Mitchell's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a branched stem system with a thick, waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The leaves are typically small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its drought tolerance. It produces unique, spiny cyathia (modified flowers) that are often yellow or greenish in color.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
