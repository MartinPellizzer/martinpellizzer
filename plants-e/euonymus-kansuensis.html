Euonymus kansuensis

Euonymus kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red, inflated fruits that split open to reveal seeds. It is typically found in rocky or mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
