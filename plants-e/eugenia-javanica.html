Eugenia javanica

Eugenia javanica, commonly known as the Java guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically pink to red in color when ripe. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.
