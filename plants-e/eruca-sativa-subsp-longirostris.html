Eruca sativa subsp. longirostris

Eruca sativa subsp.

longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pinnately divided, lobed leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods, giving it the subspecies name "longirostris.".

This species is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to mustard greens.
