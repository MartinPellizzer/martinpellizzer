Eruca prolongoi

Eruca prolongoi, commonly known as the longleaf rocket, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often growing in moist, calcareous soils. The plant features a basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves, which are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, and it produces small, cylindrical siliques containing numerous seeds.

Eruca prolongoi is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
