Elettaria costata

Elettaria costata, commonly known as cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and spikes of small, white to pale green flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic seeds enclosed in three-sided, triangular pods. Cardamom is cultivated for its seeds, which are widely used as a spice and in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
