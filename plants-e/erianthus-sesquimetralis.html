Erianthus sesquimetralis

Erianthus sesquimetralis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Erianthus sesquimetralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
