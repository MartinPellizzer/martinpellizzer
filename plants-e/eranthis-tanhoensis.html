Eranthis tanhoensis

Eranthis tanhoensis, commonly known as the Tanhoen's winter jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, producing clusters of bright yellow flowers in late winter or early spring. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a valuable early nectar source for pollinators.
