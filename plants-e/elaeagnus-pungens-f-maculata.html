Elaeagnus pungens f. maculata

Elaeagnus pungens f. maculata, commonly known as the variegated silverberry, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety leaves that often have a striking variegation of yellow and green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red-orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
