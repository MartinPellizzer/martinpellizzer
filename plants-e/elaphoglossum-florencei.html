Elaphoglossum florencei

Elaphoglossum florencei, commonly known as Florence's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a unique pattern along the margins of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
