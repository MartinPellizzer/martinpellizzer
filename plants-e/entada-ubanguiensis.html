Entada ubanguiensis

Entada ubanguiensis, commonly known as the African hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Central and East Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a rounded crown. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.
