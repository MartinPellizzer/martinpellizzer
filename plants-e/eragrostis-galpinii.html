Eragrostis galpinii

Eragrostis galpinii, commonly known as Galpin's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it conserve moisture.

Eragrostis galpinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
