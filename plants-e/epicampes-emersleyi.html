Epicampes emersleyi

Epicampes emersleyi, commonly known as the Desert Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes. Its leaves are usually small and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
