Erucaria cakiloidea

Erucaria cakiloidea, commonly known as the Cakiloidean plant, is a species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Erucaria cakiloidea is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique appearance and resilience.
