Ervum ervoides

Ervum ervoides, commonly known as the wild vetch, is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Ervum ervoides is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
