Epitrachys nemoralis

Epitrachys nemoralis, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a shrub native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, preferring moist, well-drained conditions. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. Its berries are black, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for birds.

Epitrachys nemoralis is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
