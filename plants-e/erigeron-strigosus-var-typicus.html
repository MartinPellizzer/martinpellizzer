Erigeron strigosus var. typicus

Erigeron strigosus var. typicus, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its woolly, gray-green foliage and daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. It is an annual or biennial species that thrives in full sun.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
