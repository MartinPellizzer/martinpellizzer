Eryngium tricuspidatum var. bocconei

Eryngium tricuspidatum var. bocconei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by white to pale blue bracts, which are typical of the eryngium genus. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in gardens and natural landscapes.
