Eucalyptus disclusa

Eucalyptus disclusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus disclusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
