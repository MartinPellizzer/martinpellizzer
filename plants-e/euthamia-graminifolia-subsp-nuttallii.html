Euthamia graminifolia subsp. nuttallii

Euthamia graminifolia subsp.

nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish to golden flowers.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and grass-like, giving the plant its species name "graminifolia.". It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along streambanks.

This subspecies is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
