Englerella

Englerella, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plants typically have alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Englerella species are native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Argentina.

They are often cultivated in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
