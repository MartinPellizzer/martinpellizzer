Erica leucotrachela subsp. monicae

Erica leucotrachela subsp.

monicae, commonly known as the Monica's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
