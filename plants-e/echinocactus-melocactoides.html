Echinocactus melocactoides

Echinocactus melocactoides, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of spines, which protect it from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
