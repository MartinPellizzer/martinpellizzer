Eriochloa mollis

Eriochloa mollis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

Eriochloa mollis is often found in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
