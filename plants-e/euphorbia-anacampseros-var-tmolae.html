Euphorbia anacampseros var. tmolae

Euphorbia anacampseros var. tmolae, commonly known as the Tmolus euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spineless stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
