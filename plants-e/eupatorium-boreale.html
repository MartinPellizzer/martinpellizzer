Eupatorium boreale

Eupatorium boreale, commonly known as northern boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
