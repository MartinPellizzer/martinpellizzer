Eulophia saundersiae

Eulophia saundersiae, commonly known as the Saunders' eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia saundersiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
