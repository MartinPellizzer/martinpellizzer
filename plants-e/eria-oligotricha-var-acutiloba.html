Eria oligotricha var. acutiloba

Eria oligotricha var. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spike, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
