Erodium chium f. murcicum

Erodium chium f. murcicum, commonly known as the Murcian velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant native to southern Europe.

It is a small annual herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in areas with Mediterranean climates. It has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name.

This subspecies is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
