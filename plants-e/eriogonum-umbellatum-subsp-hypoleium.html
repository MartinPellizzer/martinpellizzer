Eriogonum umbellatum subsp. hypoleium

Eriogonum umbellatum subsp.

hypoleium, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
