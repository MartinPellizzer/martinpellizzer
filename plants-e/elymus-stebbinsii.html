Elymus stebbinsii

Elymus stebbinsii, commonly known as Stebbins' needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Elymus stebbinsii is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
