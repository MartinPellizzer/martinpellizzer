Endocomia macrocoma

Endocomia macrocoma, commonly known as the giant cupuaçu, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its fruit, similar to a cupuaçu, is edible and has a sweet, creamy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
