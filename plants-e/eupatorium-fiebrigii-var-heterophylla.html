Eupatorium fiebrigii var. heterophylla

Eupatorium fiebrigii var. heterophylla, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in composite heads, which bloom from late summer to fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
