Echium italicum var. siculum

Echium italicum var. siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and ornamental horticulture.
