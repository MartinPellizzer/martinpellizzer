Eugenia pinariensis

Eugenia pinariensis, commonly known as the Pinariense myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eugenia pinariensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
