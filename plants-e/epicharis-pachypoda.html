Epicharis pachypoda

Epicharis pachypoda, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, featuring a distinctive spider-like appearance due to its elongated petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a rare and ecologically significant species.

Epicharis pachypoda plays a vital role in its ecosystem, supporting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
