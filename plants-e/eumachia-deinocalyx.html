Eumachia deinocalyx

Eumachia deinocalyx, commonly known as the giant red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eumachia deinocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.
