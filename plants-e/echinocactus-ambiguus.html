Echinocactus ambiguus

Echinocactus ambiguus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive crown of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that help it store water and reduce water loss. Its roots are shallow, allowing it to absorb moisture from light rains.

Echinocactus ambiguus is well adapted to harsh desert environments and requires minimal care when grown in suitable conditions.
