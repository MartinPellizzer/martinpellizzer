Eupatorium gilbertii

Eupatorium gilbertii, commonly known as Gilbert's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
