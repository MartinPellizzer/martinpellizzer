Eupatorium album var. typicum

Eupatorium album var. typicum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that are often branched and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
