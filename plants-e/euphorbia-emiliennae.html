Euphorbia emiliennae

Euphorbia emiliennae, commonly known as the Emilianna's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium, which is a typical feature of the Euphorbia genus. Its growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping, while its striking appearance adds ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
