Elaphoglossum lepidopodum

Elaphoglossum lepidopodum, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lanceolate leaves with a finely divided structure, characteristic of the Elaphoglossaceae family. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ability to retain moisture.

Elaphoglossum lepidopodum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
