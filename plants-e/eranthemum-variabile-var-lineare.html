Eranthemum variabile var. lineare

Eranthemum variabile var. lineare, commonly known as the linear eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
