Eschscholzia minuscula

Eschscholzia minuscula, commonly known as the dwarf California poppy, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a wildflower and its historical use in traditional medicine.
