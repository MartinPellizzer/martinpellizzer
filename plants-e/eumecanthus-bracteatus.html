Eumecanthus bracteatus

Eumecanthus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
