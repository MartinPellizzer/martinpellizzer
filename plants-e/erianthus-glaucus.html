Erianthus glaucus

Erianthus glaucus, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

Erianthus glaucus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
