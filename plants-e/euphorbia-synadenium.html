Euphorbia synadenium

Euphorbia synadenium, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, branching stems and distinctive, yellowish-green, fleshy fruits that resemble small cucumbers. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which can be toxic if ingested. Its leaves are typically small, oval, and covered in fine hairs.

Euphorbia synadenium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought-resistant properties.
