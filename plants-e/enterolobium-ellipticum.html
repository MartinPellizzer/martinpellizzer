Enterolobium ellipticum

Enterolobium ellipticum, commonly known as the velvet bean tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces long, pendulous pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The tree is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
