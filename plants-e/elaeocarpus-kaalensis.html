Elaeocarpus kaalensis

Elaeocarpus kaalensis, commonly known as the Kailani tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

Elaeocarpus kaalensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests.
