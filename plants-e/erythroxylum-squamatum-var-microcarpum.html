Erythroxylum squamatum var. microcarpum

Erythroxylum squamatum var. microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf copaiba, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and unique floral structures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic, resinous seeds.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its medicinal properties due to the presence of essential oils in its bark and leaves.
