Ericoides lehmannii

Ericoides lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's ericoid, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
