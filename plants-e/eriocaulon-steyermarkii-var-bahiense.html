Eriocaulon steyermarkii var. bahiense

Eriocaulon steyermarkii var. bahiense, commonly known as the Bahia flat sedge, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has a short rhizome system and produces spikelets that are typically clustered at the base of the plant.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
