Eurotia ceratoides f. tragacanthoides

Eurotia ceratoides f. tragacanthoides, commonly known as the tragacanth-like globemallow, is a variant of the globemallow plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular seed pods and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals, and it produces a sticky, resinous substance in its pods.

This form is notable for its unique morphological features that distinguish it from the typical species.
