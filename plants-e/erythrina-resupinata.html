Erythrina resupinata

Erythrina resupinata, commonly known as the crimson glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets, and its stems are typically woody with a reddish hue. Erythrina resupinata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
