Echeveria tamaulipana

Echeveria tamaulipana, commonly known as the Tamaulipan Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers in the spring, which emerge from the center of the rosette. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
