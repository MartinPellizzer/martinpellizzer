Eupatorium ejidense

Eupatorium ejidense, commonly known as Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium ejidense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
