Erica ventricosa var. meyeriana

Erica ventricosa var. meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's heath, is a flowering plant native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, evergreen foliage that thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the winter months. It is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
