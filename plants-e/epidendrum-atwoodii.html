Epidendrum atwoodii

Epidendrum atwoodii, commonly known as the Atwood's Epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features a sympodial growth pattern, with each stem producing a new growth from the base of the previous one. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a yellow lip, and it blooms during the late spring to early summer.

Epidendrum atwoodii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
