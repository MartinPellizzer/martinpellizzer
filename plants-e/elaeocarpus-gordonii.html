Elaeocarpus gordonii

Elaeocarpus gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white to pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
