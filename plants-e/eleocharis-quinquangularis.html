Eleocharis quinquangularis

Eleocharis quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eleocharis quinquangularis is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies.
