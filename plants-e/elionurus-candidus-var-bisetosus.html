Elionurus candidus var. bisetosus

Elionurus candidus var. bisetosus, commonly known as the white-haired grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
