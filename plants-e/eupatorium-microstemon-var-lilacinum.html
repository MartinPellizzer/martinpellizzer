Eupatorium microstemon var. lilacinum

Eupatorium microstemon var. lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac stem eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, lilac-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
