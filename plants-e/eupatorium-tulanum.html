Eupatorium tulanum

Eupatorium tulanum, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Eupatorium tulanum is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
