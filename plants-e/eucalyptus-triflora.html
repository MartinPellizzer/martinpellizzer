Eucalyptus triflora

Eucalyptus triflora, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which appear in clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Eucalyptus triflora plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various native animals.
