Erythroxylum ambiguum

Erythroxylum ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous erythroxylum, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that contain seeds, and the plant is notable for its potential medicinal properties.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its chemical composition.
