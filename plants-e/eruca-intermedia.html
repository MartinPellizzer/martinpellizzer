Eruca intermedia

Eruca intermedia, commonly known as wild rocket or Italian rocket, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

It is valued for its peppery, edible leaves and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
