Echinops amoenus

Echinops amoenus, commonly known as the Persian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiky, spherical seed heads that resemble small, fuzzy spheres, which are a striking feature of the plant. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a vibrant touch to its dry habitat. Echinops amoenus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
