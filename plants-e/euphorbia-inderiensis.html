Euphorbia inderiensis

Euphorbia inderiensis, commonly known as the Inderien euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a branched stem system with fleshy, often spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions through its deep root system.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
