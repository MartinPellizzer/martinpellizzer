Eurybia alpicola

Eurybia alpicola, commonly known as alpine sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Eurybia alpicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in challenging mountainous conditions.
