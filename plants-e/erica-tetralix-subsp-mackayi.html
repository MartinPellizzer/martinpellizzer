Erica tetralix subsp. mackayi

Erica tetralix subsp.

mackayi, commonly known as the Mackay heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the Scottish Highlands. It belongs to the heather family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorland environments. It plays a vital role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
