Eupodium cicutifolium

Eupodium cicutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Eupodium cicutifolium is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
