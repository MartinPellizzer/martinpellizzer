Eupatorium hyssopifolium var. laciniatum

Eupatorium hyssopifolium var. laciniatum, commonly known as the cutleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often branching near the top. The leaves are deeply lobed and serrated, giving the plant its distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. It produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

This plant thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
