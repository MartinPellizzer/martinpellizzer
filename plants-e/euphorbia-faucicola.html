Euphorbia faucicola

Euphorbia faucicola, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

Euphorbia faucicola is also known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
