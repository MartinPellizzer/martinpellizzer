Erysimum nuratense

Erysimum nuratense, commonly known as the Spanish wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Erysimum nuratense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
