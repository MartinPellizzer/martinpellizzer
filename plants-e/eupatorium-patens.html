Eupatorium patens

Eupatorium patens, commonly known as common boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its medicinal uses and serves as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
