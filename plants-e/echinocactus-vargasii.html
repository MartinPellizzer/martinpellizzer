Echinocactus vargasii

Echinocactus vargasii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of bright yellow spines. The plant has a thick, water-storing stem that helps it survive in dry environments. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are typically yellow or orange and are borne at the top of the plant.

Echinocactus vargasii is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.
