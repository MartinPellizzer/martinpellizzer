Ethulia vernonioides

Ethulia vernonioides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in spring and are followed by small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Ethulia vernonioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
