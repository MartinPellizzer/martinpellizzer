Echinosepala biseta

Echinosepala biseta, commonly known as the hairy echinosepala, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, often covered in fine hairs.

Echinosepala biseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
