Erodium cinerascens

Erodium cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-colored bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, with small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution across its native range.
