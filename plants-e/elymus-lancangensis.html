Elymus lancangensis

Elymus lancangensis, commonly known as the Lancang wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It typically grows in high-altitude meadows and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Elymus lancangensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
