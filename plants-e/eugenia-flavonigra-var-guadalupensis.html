Eugenia flavonigra var. guadalupensis

Eugenia flavonigra var. guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupensis Eugenia, is a species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, which bloom during the spring and summer months.

The fruit is a small, round berry that changes from green to red as it matures, often attracting various wildlife species.
