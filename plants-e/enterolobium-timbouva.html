Enterolobium timbouva

Enterolobium timbouva, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits that enclose the seeds, which are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which enhance soil fertility. Its growth is slow, and it can reach heights of up to 40 meters, making it a significant component of the rainforest canopy.

The tree plays a crucial role in the ecosystem, supporting a wide variety of wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
