Eugenia elliotii

Eugenia elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters and features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Eugenia elliotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
