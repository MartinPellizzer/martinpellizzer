Eriophorum chamissonis subsp. albidum

Eriophorum chamissonis subsp.

albidum, commonly known as the white tussock grass, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its dense, white to light brown tufts of flowering heads that resemble small clouds.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, nutrient-poor soils in wetlands and bogs. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, scale-like leaves.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
