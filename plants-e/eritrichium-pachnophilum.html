Eritrichium pachnophilum

Eritrichium pachnophilum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-eared cress, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Eritrichium pachnophilum plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.
