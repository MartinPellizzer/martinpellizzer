Erythroxylum kunthianum var. subsessile

Erythroxylum kunthianum var. subsessile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coca, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by oval, red-brown capsules containing coca leaves. These leaves contain alkaloids, particularly cocaine, which has been historically used for various purposes.

It is often cultivated for its psychoactive properties and has cultural and economic significance in certain regions.
