Epilobium alpinum f. lactiflorum

Epilobium alpinum f. lactiflorum, commonly known as the alpine willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Onagraceae and is characterized by its slender stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that are clustered in terminal racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
