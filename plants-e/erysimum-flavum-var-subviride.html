Erysimum flavum var. subviride

Erysimum flavum var. subviride, commonly known as the green-flowered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow or greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
