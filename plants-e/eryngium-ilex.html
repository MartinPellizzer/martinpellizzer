Eryngium ilex

Eryngium ilex, commonly known as the sea holly, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium ilex is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
