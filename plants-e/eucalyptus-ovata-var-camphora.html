Eucalyptus ovata var. camphora

Eucalyptus ovata var. camphora, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. This plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale blue in color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.
