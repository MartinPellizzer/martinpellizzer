Euphorbia valeryae

Euphorbia valeryae, commonly known as the Valery's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has a spiny texture and produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
