Eryngium variifolium

Eryngium variifolium, commonly known as the variable eryngo, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads and its compound, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly seed heads.

Eryngium variifolium is often found in the eastern and central regions of the United States.
