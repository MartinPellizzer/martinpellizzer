Eria microphylla

Eria microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
