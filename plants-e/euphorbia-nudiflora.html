Euphorbia nudiflora

Euphorbia nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branching stem covered in soft, blue-green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are its modified flowers surrounded by leaf-like structures. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia nudiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
