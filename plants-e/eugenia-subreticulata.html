Eugenia subreticulata

Eugenia subreticulata, commonly known as the reticulated eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

The fruit, which is small and reddish, is edible and contains a single seed.
