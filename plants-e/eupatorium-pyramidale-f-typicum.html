Eupatorium pyramidale f. typicum

Eupatorium pyramidale f. typicum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
