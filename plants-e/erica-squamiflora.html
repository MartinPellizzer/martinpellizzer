Erica squamiflora

Erica squamiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in acidic conditions. Erica squamiflora produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
