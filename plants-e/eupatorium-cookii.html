Eupatorium cookii

Eupatorium cookii, commonly known as Cook's eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Eupatorium cookii is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
