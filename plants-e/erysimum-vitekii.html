Erysimum vitekii

Erysimum vitekii, commonly known as the Vitek's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other garden and wild plants. The plant features basal and stem leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the family.

Erysimum vitekii is often found growing in dry, sunny areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
