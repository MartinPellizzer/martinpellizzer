Endospermum peltatum

Endospermum peltatum, commonly known as the Crown Daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence composed of yellow ray florets and purple disk florets. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while the fruit is a small, dry capsule containing numerous seeds.

The Crown Daisy is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
