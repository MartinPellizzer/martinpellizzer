Eugenia muricata var. guyanensis

Eugenia muricata var. guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana apple or Guyana myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
