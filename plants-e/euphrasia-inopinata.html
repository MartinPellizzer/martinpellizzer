Euphrasia inopinata

Euphrasia inopinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eyebright, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with prominent white markings, which are characteristic of the genus Euphrasia. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Euphrasia inopinata is valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat eye conditions and other ailments.
