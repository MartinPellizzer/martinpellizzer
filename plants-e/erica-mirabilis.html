Erica mirabilis

Erica mirabilis, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, creating a striking visual display. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica mirabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
