Erigeron uniflorus var. glabrescens

Erigeron uniflorus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually hairy but become smooth as the plant matures, giving it its varietal name.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
