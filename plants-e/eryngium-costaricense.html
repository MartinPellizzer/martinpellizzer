Eryngium costaricense

Eryngium costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny, blue-green leaves and produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry forest understories.

Eryngium costaricense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
