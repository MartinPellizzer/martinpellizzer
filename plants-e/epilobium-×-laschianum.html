Epilobium × laschianum

Epilobium × laschianum, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a hybrid species within the Onagraceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pink to reddish, with four petals and numerous stamens, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and roadside areas.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open habitats, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
