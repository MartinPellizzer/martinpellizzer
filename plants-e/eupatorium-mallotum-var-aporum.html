Eupatorium mallotum var. aporum

Eupatorium mallotum var. aporum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers surrounded by bracts, giving it a coneflower appearance.
