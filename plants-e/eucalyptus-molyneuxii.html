Eucalyptus molyneuxii

Eucalyptus molyneuxii, commonly known as the molyneux's eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark that can range from white to grey in color. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale blue, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in heathlands and open forests.
