Eriochloa nelsonii var. papillosa

Eriochloa nelsonii var. papillosa, commonly known as the silky false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in soft, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil, it is also valued for ecological restoration efforts.
