Eupatorium oregonum

Eupatorium oregonum, commonly known as Oregon feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Oregon feverfew is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
