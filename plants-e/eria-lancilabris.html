Eria lancilabris

Eria lancilabris, commonly known as the slender-leafed eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Eria lancilabris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
