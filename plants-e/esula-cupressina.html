Esula cupressina

Esula cupressina, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family, which includes cypresses, junipers, and redwoods. The tree is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. Its bark is thick and reddish-brown, often flaking in small plates.

Esula cupressina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its drought tolerance and year-round visual interest.
