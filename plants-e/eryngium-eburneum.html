Eryngium eburneum

Eryngium eburneum, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, spiny, spherical flower heads that are typically white or pale blue in color. The plant has silver-gray foliage with hairy surfaces, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Eryngium eburneum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
