Eragrostis swallenii

Eragrostis swallenii, commonly known as the Swallen's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Eragrostis swallenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
