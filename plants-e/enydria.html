Enydria

Enydria, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant has a short blooming period, typically during the late summer months, and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its harsh habitat.

Despite its beauty, Enydria is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
