Erica hirtiflora var. hirtiflora

Erica hirtiflora var. hirtiflora, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with fine, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with salt spray and strong winds.

Erica hirtiflora var. hirtiflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.
