Eruca vesicaria var. longistyla

Eruca vesicaria var. longistyla, commonly known as the giant wallcress, is a variant of the common wallcress.

It is a biennial or perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features elongated, thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
