Encyclia linkiana

Encyclia linkiana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive lip that often has a contrasting color. Encyclia linkiana thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular species among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
