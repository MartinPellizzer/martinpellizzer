Eugenia nandarivatensis

Eugenia nandarivatensis, commonly known as the Nandariva myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
