Eremostachys macrophylla

Eremostachys macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved eremostachys, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is considered a valuable species for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
