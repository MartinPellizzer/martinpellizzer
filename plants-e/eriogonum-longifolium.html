Eriogonum longifolium

Eriogonum longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is an important nectar source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Eriogonum longifolium is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in arid regions.
