Euphorbia hyberna subsp. insularis

Euphorbia hyberna subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the Balearic spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Balearic Islands. It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green.

The plant has adapted to the arid and coastal environments of its native habitat, developing thick leaves to store water. It produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the Euphorbia genus.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is considered a rare and endemic species.
