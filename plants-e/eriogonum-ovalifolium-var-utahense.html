Eriogonum ovalifolium var. utahense

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. utahense, commonly known as Utah's desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
