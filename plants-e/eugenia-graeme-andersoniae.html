Eugenia graeme-andersoniae

Eugenia graeme-andersoniae, commonly known as the Anderson's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
