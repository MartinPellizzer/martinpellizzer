Ehretia acuminata var. grandifolia

Ehretia acuminata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the largeflowered ehretia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glabrous and have a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring or early summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
