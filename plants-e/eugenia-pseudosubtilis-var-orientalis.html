Eugenia pseudosubtilis var. orientalis

Eugenia pseudosubtilis var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant in warm regions.
