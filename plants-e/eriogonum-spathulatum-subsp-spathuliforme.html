Eriogonum spathulatum subsp. spathuliforme

Eriogonum spathulatum subsp.

spathuliforme, commonly known as the broad-leafed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers.

The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

It is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
