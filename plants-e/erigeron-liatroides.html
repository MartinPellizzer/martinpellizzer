Erigeron liatroides

Erigeron liatroides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, soft appearance.
