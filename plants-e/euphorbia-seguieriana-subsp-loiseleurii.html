Euphorbia seguieriana subsp. loiseleurii

Euphorbia seguieriana subsp.

loiseleurii, commonly known as the Loiseleur's spurge, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, ribbed stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant has spiny leaves and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-resistant. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
