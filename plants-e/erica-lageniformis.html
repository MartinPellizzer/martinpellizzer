Erica lageniformis

Erica lageniformis, commonly known as the bottlebrush heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking, bottle-shaped flower clusters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Erica lageniformis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is a hardy, evergreen shrub that is well-suited to coastal and sunny environments.
