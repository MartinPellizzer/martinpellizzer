Eryngium yuccifolium var. synchaetum

Eryngium yuccifolium var. synchaetum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable species in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
