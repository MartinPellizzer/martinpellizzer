Eupatorium sotarense

Eupatorium sotarense, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and features finely divided, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and white to pale blue, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium sotarense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties.
