Erysimum hieraciifolium subsp. denticulatum

Erysimum hieraciifolium subsp.

denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features basal leaves that are pinnately divided with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically arranged in loose racemes. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and garden cultivation.
