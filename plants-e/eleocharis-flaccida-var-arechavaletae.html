Eleocharis flaccida var. arechavaletae

Eleocharis flaccida var. arechavaletae, commonly known as the arechavaletae sedge, is a variant of the flaccida sedge native to certain regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are linear and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
