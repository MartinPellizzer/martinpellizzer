Epicampes argentea

Epicampes argentea, commonly known as silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Epicampes argentea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
