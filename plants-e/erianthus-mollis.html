Erianthus mollis

Erianthus mollis, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Erianthus mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
