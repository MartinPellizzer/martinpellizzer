Eriogonum dichroanthum

Eriogonum dichroanthum, commonly known as the two-colored buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned flowers, which range from white to pink or purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Eriogonum dichroanthum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its ecosystem.
