Encyclia patens

Encyclia patens, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in seasonal environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in soil.

Encyclia patens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
