Eugenia myrtifolia

Eugenia myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and have a slight waxy texture. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by edible, dark purple berries.

Eugenia myrtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
