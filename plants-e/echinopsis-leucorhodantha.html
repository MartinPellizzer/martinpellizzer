Echinopsis leucorhodantha

Echinopsis leucorhodantha, commonly known as the White-flowered Moon cactus, is a succulent native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular, ribbed stem that stores water. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its nocturnal blooming habit makes it a unique addition to desert gardens.

Echinopsis leucorhodantha requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive, making it well-suited for arid environments.
