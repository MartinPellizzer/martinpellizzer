Epidendrum citroserpens

Epidendrum citroserpens, commonly known as the Lemon-scented Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This epiphytic orchid prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
