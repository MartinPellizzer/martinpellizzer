Eucalyptus vegrandis subsp. vegrandis

Eucalyptus vegrandis subsp.

vegrandis, commonly known as the broad-leaved malleeflower, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous underneath.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding insects and birds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is of interest for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
