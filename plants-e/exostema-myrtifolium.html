Exostema myrtifolium

Exostema myrtifolium, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical, glossy leaflets that give it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a slight fragrance when crushed.
