Ehrharta festucacea

Ehrharta festucacea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Ehrharta festucacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
