Echinops microcephalus

Echinops microcephalus, commonly known as the small-headed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of its distinctive, globe-shaped fruits. Echinops microcephalus is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
