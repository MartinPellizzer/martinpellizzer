Elaeagnus conferta subsp. javanica

Elaeagnus conferta subsp.

javanica, commonly known as the Javan silverleaf, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple pairs of leaflets.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
