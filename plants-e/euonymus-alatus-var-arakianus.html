Euonymus alatus var. arakianus

Euonymus alatus var. arakianus, commonly known as the winged euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems that resemble the wings of a bird, giving it its common name. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
