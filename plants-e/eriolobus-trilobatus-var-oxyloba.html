Eriolobus trilobatus var. oxyloba

Eriolobus trilobatus var. oxyloba, commonly known as the oxyloba variant of the three-lobed eriolobus, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, opposite leaves with three lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release minute, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland understories.
