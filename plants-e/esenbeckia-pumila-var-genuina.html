Esenbeckia pumila var. genuina

Esenbeckia pumila var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or Brazilian cherry, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
