Eugenia cachoeirensis

Eugenia cachoeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cupuaçu tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces a unique fruit known as the cupuaçu, which has a creamy, buttery texture and a complex flavor profile. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
