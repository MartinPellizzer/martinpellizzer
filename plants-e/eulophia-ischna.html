Eulophia ischna

Eulophia ischna, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typical of many orchid species. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Eulophia ischna is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
