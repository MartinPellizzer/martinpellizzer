Eriocaulon sikokianum

Eriocaulon sikokianum, commonly known as the Japanese marsh grass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

Eriocaulon sikokianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or bog gardens.
