Elymus retroflexus

Elymus retroflexus, commonly known as the rough bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage that becomes more grayish with age. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters, and its inflorescence is often described as being somewhat resemble a bottlebrush. Elymus retroflexus thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils, and is often found in the eastern and central United States.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
