Eriogonum tenellum var. leptocladon

Eriogonum tenellum var. leptocladon, commonly known as the slender-leaved buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with showy bracts that attract pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar and habitat for various insects.
