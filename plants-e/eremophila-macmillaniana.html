Eremophila macmillaniana

Eremophila macmillaniana, commonly known as the MacMillan's eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of showy pink or red flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Eremophila macmillaniana is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
