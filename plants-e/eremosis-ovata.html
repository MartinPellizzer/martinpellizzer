Eremosis ovata

Eremosis ovata, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a thick, succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Eremosis ovata is well adapted to hot, dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
