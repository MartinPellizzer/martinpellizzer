Evolvulus alsinoides var. procumbens

Evolvulus alsinoides var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing, trailing annual that produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread along the ground. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
