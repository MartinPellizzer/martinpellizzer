Euphorbia coalcomanensis

Euphorbia coalcomanensis, commonly known as the Coalcoman euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
