Eugenia umbelliflora

Eugenia umbelliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or the sweetshrub, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Eugenia umbelliflora thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
