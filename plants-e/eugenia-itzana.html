Eugenia itzana

Eugenia itzana, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or Mexican myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia itzana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
