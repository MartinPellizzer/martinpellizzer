Eleocharis parvula var. anachaeta

Eleocharis parvula var. anachaeta, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a small, grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous tiny spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is relatively uncommon and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
