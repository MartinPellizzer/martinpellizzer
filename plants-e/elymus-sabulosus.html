Elymus sabulosus

Elymus sabulosus, commonly known as sandreed grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, wiry stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland dunes. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Elymus sabulosus is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
