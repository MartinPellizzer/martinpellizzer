Eriosema tisserantii var. angustifolium

Eriosema tisserantii var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, especially along the veins. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin and a hairy texture. Its small, white to pale purple flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months.

This plant is often found in grassy areas and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
