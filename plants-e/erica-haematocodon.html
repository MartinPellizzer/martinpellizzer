Erica haematocodon

Erica haematocodon, commonly known as the bloodroot heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica haematocodon typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation, a unique biome known for its rich biodiversity.
