Erysimum kunzeanum var. maroccanum

Erysimum kunzeanum var. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
