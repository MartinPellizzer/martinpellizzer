Euphorbia missurica var. petaloidea

Euphorbia missurica var. petaloidea, commonly known as the showy euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
