Eucalyptus lamprocarpa

Eucalyptus lamprocarpa, commonly known as the silver-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant typically grows as a multi-stemmed shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It produces small, globular flower buds that open to reveal white or pale yellow flowers, which are attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
