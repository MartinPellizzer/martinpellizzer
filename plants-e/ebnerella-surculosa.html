Ebnerella surculosa

Ebnerella surculosa, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features basal leaves that are succulent and often covered in a fine, white powder. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or reddish in color.

Ebnerella surculosa is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
