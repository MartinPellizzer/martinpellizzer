Elaeocarpus philippinensis

Elaeocarpus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
