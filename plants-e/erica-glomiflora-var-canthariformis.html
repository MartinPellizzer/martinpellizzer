Erica glomiflora var. canthariformis

Erica glomiflora var. canthariformis, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness in coastal gardens.
