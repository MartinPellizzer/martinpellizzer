Eugenia duthieana

Eugenia duthieana, commonly known as the Duthie's eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
