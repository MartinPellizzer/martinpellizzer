Echinops lineari-lancifolius

Echinops lineari-lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-linear thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in late summer, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Echinops lineari-lancifolius is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
