Eulalia quadrinervis var. wightii

Eulalia quadrinervis var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and low moisture conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and taxonomic research.
