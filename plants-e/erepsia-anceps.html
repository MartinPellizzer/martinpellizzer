Erepsia anceps

Erepsia anceps, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Erepsia anceps is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
