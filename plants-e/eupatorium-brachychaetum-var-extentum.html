Eupatorium brachychaetum var. extentum

Eupatorium brachychaetum var. extentum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine and is a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
