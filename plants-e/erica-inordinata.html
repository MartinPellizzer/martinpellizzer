Erica inordinata

Erica inordinata, commonly known as the scented heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Erica inordinata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
