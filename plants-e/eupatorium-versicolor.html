Eupatorium versicolor

Eupatorium versicolor, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple and white, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive silvery or grayish underside.

Eupatorium versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
