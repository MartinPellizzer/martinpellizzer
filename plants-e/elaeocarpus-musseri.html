Elaeocarpus musseri

Elaeocarpus musseri, commonly known as the Musser's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds. It is typically found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
