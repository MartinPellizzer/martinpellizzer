Erigeron leschenaultii

Erigeron leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is usually grayish-green with hairy leaves, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.
