Erianthus formosanus var. pollinioides

Erianthus formosanus var. pollinioides, commonly known as the Pollinioides grass, is a variant of the Erianthus formosanus species native to Taiwan.

It is a tall, perennial grass characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along streambanks and in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
