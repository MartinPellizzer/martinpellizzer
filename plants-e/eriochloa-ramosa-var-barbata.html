Eriochloa ramosa var. barbata

Eriochloa ramosa var. barbata, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread aggressively in moist, disturbed soils.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
