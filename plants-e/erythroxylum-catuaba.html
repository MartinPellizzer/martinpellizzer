Erythroxylum catuaba

Erythroxylum catuaba, commonly known as catuaba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and roots have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their purported stimulant and aphrodisiac properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
