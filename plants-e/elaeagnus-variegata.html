Elaeagnus variegata

Elaeagnus variegata, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in the fall. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its capacity for nitrogen fixation through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and fragrant flowers.

Elaeagnus variegata is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
