Eragrostis crassa

Eragrostis crassa, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. The plant produces dense, tufted clumps with long, flat leaves that are typically blue-green in color. Its flowering spikes are often tinged with purple, adding to its ornamental value.

Eragrostis crassa is drought-tolerant and is often used in erosion control and as a landscape plant in xeriscaped gardens.
