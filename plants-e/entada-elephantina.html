Entada elephantina

Entada elephantina, commonly known as the elephant tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, thick, woody trunk and large, compound leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in long, drooping racemes. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod, which can remain on the tree for several years.

Entada elephantina is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
