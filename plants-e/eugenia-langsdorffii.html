Eugenia langsdorffii

Eugenia langsdorffii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or langsdorff's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, often with a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor, making it a popular ingredient in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
