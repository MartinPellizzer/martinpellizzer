Elaphoglossum beccarianum

Elaphoglossum beccarianum, commonly known as the Beccarian holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds with a distinctive holly-like appearance, featuring a central vein and small, clustered sori on the underside. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's spores are released from the sori, which are protected by a thin, membranous covering.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
