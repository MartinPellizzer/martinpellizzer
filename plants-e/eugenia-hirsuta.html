Eugenia hirsuta

Eugenia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy wax apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of aromatic oils in its foliage.

Eugenia hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
