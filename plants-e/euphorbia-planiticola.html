Euphorbia planiticola

Euphorbia planiticola, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its stems are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Euphorbia planiticola is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
