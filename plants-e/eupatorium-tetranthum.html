Eupatorium tetranthum

Eupatorium tetranthum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

White boneset is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
