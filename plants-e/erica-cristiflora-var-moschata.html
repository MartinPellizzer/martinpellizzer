Erica cristiflora var. moschata

Erica cristiflora var. moschata, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
