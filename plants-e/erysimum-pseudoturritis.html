Erysimum pseudoturritis

Erysimum pseudoturritis, commonly known as the Turkish wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention.

Erysimum pseudoturritis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
