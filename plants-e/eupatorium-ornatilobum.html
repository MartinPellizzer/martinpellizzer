Eupatorium ornatilobum

Eupatorium ornatilobum, commonly known as the ornate lobed eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant's ornamental value comes from its vibrant purple to pink flower clusters, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
