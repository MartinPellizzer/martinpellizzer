Elaeocarpus palembanicus

Elaeocarpus palembanicus, commonly known as the Palembang elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
