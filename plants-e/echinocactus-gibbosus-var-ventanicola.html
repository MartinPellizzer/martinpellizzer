Echinocactus gibbosus var. ventanicola

Echinocactus gibbosus var. ventanicola, commonly known as the Mexican golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, barrel-shaped stem that is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stem. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
