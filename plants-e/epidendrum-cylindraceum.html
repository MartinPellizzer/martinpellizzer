Epidendrum cylindraceum

Epidendrum cylindraceum, commonly known as the cylindrical epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its flowers are often arranged in a cylindrical shape, giving the species its common name.

Epidendrum cylindraceum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
