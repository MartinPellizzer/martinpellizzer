Erianthus violaceus

Erianthus violaceus, commonly known as the violet panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Erianthus violaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
