Eragrostis alba

Eragrostis alba, commonly known as white lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small white flowers.

Eragrostis alba is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
