Echinopsis haynii

Echinopsis haynii, commonly known as the "Pinky Star" cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, radial spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Echinopsis haynii is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
