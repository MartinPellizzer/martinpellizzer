Elaeocarpus eymae

Elaeocarpus eymae, commonly known as the Eyma's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
