Epidendrum gymnopodum

Epidendrum gymnopodum, commonly known as the naked foot epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant features a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Epidendrum gymnopodum thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is often found in forest understories.
