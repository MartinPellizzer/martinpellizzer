Eugenioides platyphyllum

Eugenioides platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
