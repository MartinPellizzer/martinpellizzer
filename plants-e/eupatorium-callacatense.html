Eupatorium callacatense

Eupatorium callacatense, commonly known as the purple false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often aromatic, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that disperse its seeds.

Eupatorium callacatense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
