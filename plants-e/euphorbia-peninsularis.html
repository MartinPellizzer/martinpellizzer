Euphorbia peninsularis

Euphorbia peninsularis, commonly known as the Canary Islands spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a distinctive, branched stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are its unique floral structures, rather than traditional flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves and stems.

Euphorbia peninsularis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardiness.
