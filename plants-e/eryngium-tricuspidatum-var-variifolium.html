Eryngium tricuspidatum var. variifolium

Eryngium tricuspidatum var. variifolium, commonly known as the variable thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by white or pale blue bracts. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, with some variation in shape among individuals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
