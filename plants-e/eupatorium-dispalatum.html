Eupatorium dispalatum

Eupatorium dispalatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium dispalatum is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
