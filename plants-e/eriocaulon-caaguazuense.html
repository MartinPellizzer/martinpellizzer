Eriocaulon caaguazuense

Eriocaulon caaguazuense, commonly known as the caaguazu grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which includes various aquatic and semi-aquatic grasses. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense covering of fine hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
