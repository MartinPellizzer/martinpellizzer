Eriogonum umbellatum var. modocense

Eriogonum umbellatum var. modocense, commonly known as Modoc wild buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is often found in the Modoc Plateau region of California and Oregon.
