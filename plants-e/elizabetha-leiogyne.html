Elizabetha leiogyne

Elizabetha leiogyne, commonly known as the broadleaf waterhyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has broad, waxy leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, and it produces small, pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its rapid growth rate makes it both a valuable resource for water purification and a potential invasive species in non-native habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it an important subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
