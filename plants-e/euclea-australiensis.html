Euclea australiensis

Euclea australiensis, commonly known as the Australian euphorbia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant has a distinctive, spiny appearance and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap.

Euclea australiensis is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental features.
