Elodea iowensis

Elodea iowensis, commonly known as Iowa waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the United States, particularly in the Midwest.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often branching at the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which develop underwater before emerging above the water surface. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, arranged alternately along the stem, and they are often used in aquariums for their aesthetic value and ability to oxygenate water.

Elodea iowensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small fish.
