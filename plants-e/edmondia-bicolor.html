Edmondia bicolor

Edmondia bicolor, commonly known as the twin-flowered edmondia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers in pairs. The plant typically blooms in spring and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a short growth cycle and is adapted to arid environments.

Edmondia bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
