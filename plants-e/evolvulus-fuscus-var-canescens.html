Evolvulus fuscus var. canescens

Evolvulus fuscus var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
