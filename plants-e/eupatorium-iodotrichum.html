Eupatorium iodotrichum

Eupatorium iodotrichum, commonly known as the coastal eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in sandy, coastal areas and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has a distinctive aromatic scent and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various insects and birds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
