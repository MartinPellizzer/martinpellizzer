Escallonia discolor

Escallonia discolor, commonly known as the variable escallonia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Escalloniaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with dense, leathery foliage. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Escallonia discolor is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
