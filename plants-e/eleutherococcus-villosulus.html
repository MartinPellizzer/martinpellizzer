Eleutherococcus villosulus

Eleutherococcus villosulus, commonly known as the hairy eleuthero, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early summer, followed by small, red berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with partial shade.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
