Epilobium alpinum var. behringianum

Epilobium alpinum var. behringianum, commonly known as the Behring's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
