Eremopyrum orientale var. lasianthum

Eremopyrum orientale var. lasianthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy surface. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered, bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy component of steppe and desert ecosystems.
