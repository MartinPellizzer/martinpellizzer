Epidendrum mathildae

Epidendrum mathildae, commonly known as the Mathilde's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically has strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum mathildae is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
