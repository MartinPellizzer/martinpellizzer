Enkianthus nudipes

Enkianthus nudipes, commonly known as the nakedstem enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

Enkianthus nudipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
