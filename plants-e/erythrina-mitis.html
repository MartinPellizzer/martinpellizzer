Erythrina mitis

Erythrina mitis, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense, torch-like clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often woody and thorny. Erythrina mitis is a fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
