Erica oliveri

Erica oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Erica oliveri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
