Eupatorium conyzoides f. angustiflorum

Eupatorium conyzoides f. angustiflorum, commonly known as narrow-flowered false fleabane, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of small white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators.
