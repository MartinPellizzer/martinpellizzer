Equilabium parvum

Equilabium parvum, commonly known as the small-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact stature and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Equilabium parvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
