Eugenia illepida

Eugenia illepida, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and small, edible berries. The leaves are narrow and glossy, often with a slight aromatic scent. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional medicine.
