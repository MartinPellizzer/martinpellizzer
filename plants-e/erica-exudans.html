Erica exudans

Erica exudans, commonly known as the sticky heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Erica exudans is known for its sticky resinous secretions, which give it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
