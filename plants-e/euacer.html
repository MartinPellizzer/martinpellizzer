Euacer

Euacer, commonly known as the sacred flame plant, is a unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom during the rainy season. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, dark green leaves that have a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers emit a mild, spicy fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
