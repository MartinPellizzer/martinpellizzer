Eryngium × embergeri

Eryngium × embergeri, commonly known as the blueweed or sea holly, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet spiky flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters and are visited by various pollinators.

Eryngium × embergeri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
