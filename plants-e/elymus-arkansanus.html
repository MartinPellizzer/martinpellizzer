Elymus arkansanus

Elymus arkansanus, commonly known as Arkansas wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Elymus arkansanus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
