Euosma micrantha

Euosma micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered euosma, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted and have a fragrant aroma. It produces small, white, clustered flowers that are typically followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Euosma micrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded woodland environments.
