Eulalia wightii var. latifolia

Eulalia wightii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pampas grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and large, feathery plumes that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in certain environments.
