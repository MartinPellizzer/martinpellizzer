Eremosyne pectinata

Eremosyne pectinata, commonly known as the silky eremosyne, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with deep roots and a low profile to reduce water loss.

Eremosyne pectinata is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of local flora in its native habitat.
