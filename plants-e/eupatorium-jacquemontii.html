Eupatorium jacquemontii

Eupatorium jacquemontii, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium jacquemontii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
