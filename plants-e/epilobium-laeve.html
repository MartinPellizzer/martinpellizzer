Epilobium laeve

Epilobium laeve, commonly known as smooth willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as stream banks, wet meadows, and woodland edges. The plant features upright stems that are smooth and often reddish in color, and it produces showy pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

Epilobium laeve is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other plants like willows and evening primroses.
