Erophila verna subsp. vulgaris

Erophila verna subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
