Eupatorium mendezii

Eupatorium mendezii, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
