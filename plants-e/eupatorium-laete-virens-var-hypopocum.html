Eupatorium laete-virens var. hypopocum

Eupatorium laete-virens var. hypopocum, commonly known as the green eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive silvery-green hue.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
