Eryngium hemisphaericum

Eryngium hemisphaericum, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that resemble small globes, typically ranging in color from blue to purple. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is usually unbranched and somewhat woody. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
