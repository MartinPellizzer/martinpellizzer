Equisetum fluviatile var. leptocladon

Equisetum fluviatile var. leptocladon, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a variant of the water horsetail, characterized by its slender, wiry stems and reduced leaves.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wet, marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are often submerged in water. Its primitive structure reflects its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor aquatic habitats.
