Echinocereus fendleri var. ledingii

Echinocereus fendleri var. ledingii, commonly known as the Ledingii prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny areoles, which bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to desert conditions.
