Eurycaulis atjehensis

Eurycaulis atjehensis, commonly known as the Atjeh endemic plant, is a unique species native to the island of Sumatra in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to its restricted range and habitat specificity, Eurycaulis atjehensis is considered a rare and potentially vulnerable species.
