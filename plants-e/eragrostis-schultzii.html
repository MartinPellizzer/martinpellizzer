Eragrostis schultzii

Eragrostis schultzii, commonly known as Schultze's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Eragrostis schultzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
