Eria chrysobractea

Eria chrysobractea, commonly known as the golden bract eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and striking yellow bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, fragrant orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among plant enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of care.
