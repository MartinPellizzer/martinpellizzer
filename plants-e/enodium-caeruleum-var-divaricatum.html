Enodium caeruleum var. divaricatum

Enodium caeruleum var. divaricatum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-white underneath, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
