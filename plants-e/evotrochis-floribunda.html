Evotrochis floribunda

Evotrochis floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of silky hair.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
