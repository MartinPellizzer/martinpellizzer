Eragrostis viscosa var. pilosissima

Eragrostis viscosa var. pilosissima, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
