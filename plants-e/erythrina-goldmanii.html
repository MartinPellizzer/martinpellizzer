Erythrina goldmanii

Erythrina goldmanii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
