Erica vespertina

Erica vespertina, commonly known as the evening heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it well-suited for coastal and rocky habitats. Its dense foliage provides habitat for various insects and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant's compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
