Ericoides irregularis

Ericoides irregularis, commonly known as the irregular ericoid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Ericoides irregularis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
