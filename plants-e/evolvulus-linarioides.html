Evolvulus linarioides

Evolvulus linarioides, commonly known as common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a trailing herbaceous plant with heart-shaped leaves and blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means make it a notable species in ecological studies.
