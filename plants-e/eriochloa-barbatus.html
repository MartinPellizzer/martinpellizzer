Eriochloa barbatus

Eriochloa barbatus, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often adorned with bristly hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Eriochloa barbatus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common species in ecological studies and landscape restoration projects.
