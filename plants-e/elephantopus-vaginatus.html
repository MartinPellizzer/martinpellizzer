Elephantopus vaginatus

Elephantopus vaginatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or broadleaf elephantopus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, which contribute to its common name. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months.

Elephantopus vaginatus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
