Eutrochium maculatum

Eutrochium maculatum, commonly known as spotted Joe-Pye weed, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, pinkish-purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and often displays white or pale spots, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and marshes.

Its root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically valuable in wetland environments.
