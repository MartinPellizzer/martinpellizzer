Erysimum squarrosum

Erysimum squarrosum, commonly known as the rough wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments, making it well adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

Erysimum squarrosum is often found in open woodlands, slopes, and along stream banks.
