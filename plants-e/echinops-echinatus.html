Echinops echinatus

Echinops echinatus, commonly known as the Thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, spherical seed heads that are covered in bristly hairs. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, blue or purple flowers in early summer, which are followed by the distinctive seed heads.

Echinops echinatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
