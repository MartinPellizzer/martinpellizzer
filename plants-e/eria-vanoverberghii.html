Eria vanoverberghii

Eria vanoverberghii, commonly known as the Van Overbergh's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
