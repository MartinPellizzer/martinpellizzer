Elytraria madagascariensis

Elytraria madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Madagascar. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Elytraria madagascariensis is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or savanna habitats.
