Eriochloa polystachya var. annulata

Eriochloa polystachya var. annulata, commonly known as the ringed bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces tall, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along creek banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
