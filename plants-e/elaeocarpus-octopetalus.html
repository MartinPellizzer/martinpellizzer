Elaeocarpus octopetalus

Elaeocarpus octopetalus, commonly known as the eight-petaled elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its distinctive flowers with eight petals. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically dark blue or black when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
