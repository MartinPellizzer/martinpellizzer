Echinodorus rostratus var. lanceolatus

Echinodorus rostratus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spathiphyllum, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length, giving it a dramatic visual presence in aquariums. The plant produces striking white to pale pink flowers that emerge from a spathe, adding both aesthetic and ecological value. It thrives in moderate to high light conditions and requires stable water parameters for optimal growth.

Its versatility makes it a popular choice for both freshwater and brackish water environments.
