Ephedra saxatilis var. sikkimensis

Ephedra saxatilis var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spine-like stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to grow in arid, rocky environments. It is part of the Ephedraceae family and contains alkaloids such as ephedrine, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also of interest in pharmacological research.
