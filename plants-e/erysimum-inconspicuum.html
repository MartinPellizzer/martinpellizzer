Erysimum inconspicuum

Erysimum inconspicuum, commonly known as the inconspicuous wallflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features a compact, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and hairy, often covered in fine pubescence.

Erysimum inconspicuum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, commonly found in rocky or disturbed areas.
