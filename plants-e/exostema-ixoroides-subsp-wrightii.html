Exostema ixoroides subsp. wrightii

Exostema ixoroides subsp.

wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's exostema, is a species native to specific regions of Central America. It is a perennial herb characterized by its fibrous roots and upright stems.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a distinct coloration that aids in pollination.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
