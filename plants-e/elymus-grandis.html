Elymus grandis

Elymus grandis, commonly known as giant oat, is a tall perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its robust growth and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Elymus grandis is valued for its ecological benefits, providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
