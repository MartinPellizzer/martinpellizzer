Erica stenantha

Erica stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pink. Erica stenantha thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
