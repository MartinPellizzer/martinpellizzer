Epipactis viridans f. humilis

Epipactis viridans f. humilis, commonly known as the slender green helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spike, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows.

This orchid is known for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations with fungi, which aid in nutrient uptake.
