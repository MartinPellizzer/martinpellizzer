Euphorbia pseudochamaesyce f. pilosa

Euphorbia pseudochamaesyce f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, branching stems covered in dense, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically small, oval, and also hairy, contributing to its overall textured look. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.
