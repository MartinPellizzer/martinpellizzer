Eremaea blackwelliana

Eremaea blackwelliana, commonly known as the Blackwell's eremaea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, which are often clustered in groups. It is well adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.

Eremaea blackwelliana is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of local flora in arid ecosystems.
