Erigeron decoloratus

Erigeron decoloratus, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage and grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to attract pollinators.

Erigeron decoloratus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
