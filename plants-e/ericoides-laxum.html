Ericoides laxum

Ericoides laxum, commonly known as the slender ericoid, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces clusters of pink or white bell-shaped flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Ericoides laxum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
