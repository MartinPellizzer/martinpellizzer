Ephedra tibetica

Ephedra tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan ephedra, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its jointed, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant contains alkaloids, particularly ephedrine, which has been historically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has attracted significant scientific and commercial interest.
