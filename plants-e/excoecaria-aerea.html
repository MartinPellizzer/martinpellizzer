Excoecaria aerea

Excoecaria aerea, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its large, palmate leaves. The leaves are typically bright green with a waxy surface, and the plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
