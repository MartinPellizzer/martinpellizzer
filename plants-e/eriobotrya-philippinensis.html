Eriobotrya philippinensis

Eriobotrya philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine loquat, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by yellow-orange berries in the summer. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and economic significance.
