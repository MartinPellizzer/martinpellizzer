Epipactis helleborine subsp. moratoria

Epipactis helleborine subsp.

moratoria, commonly known as the Moratorium Helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and a distinctive, showy inflorescence with greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial species that produces a single, erect stem bearing several flowers.

The subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in certain ecological interactions.
