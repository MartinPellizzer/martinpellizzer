Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. lancifer

Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. lancifer, commonly known as the lance-leafed echinofossulocactus, is a rare and striking cactus species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles, and its distinctive lance-shaped leaves that are often mistaken for spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.
