Evolvulus anomalus

Evolvulus anomalus, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Evolvulus anomalus is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
