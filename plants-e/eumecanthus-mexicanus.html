Eumecanthus mexicanus

Eumecanthus mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Eumecanthus within the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The Mexican sunflower is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
