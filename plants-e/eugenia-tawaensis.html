Eugenia tawaensis

Eugenia tawaensis, commonly known as the Tawa tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

The Tawa tree plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
