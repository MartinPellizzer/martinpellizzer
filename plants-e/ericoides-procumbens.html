Ericoides procumbens

Ericoides procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate ericoid, is a low-growing shrub native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in the spring and attract various pollinators.

Ericoides procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
