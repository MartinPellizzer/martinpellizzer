Eriogonum strictum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum strictum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the slender buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
