Echidnopsis multangula

Echidnopsis multangula, commonly known as the multibranched milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its milky sap is a notable feature, containing toxic compounds that deter herbivores.
