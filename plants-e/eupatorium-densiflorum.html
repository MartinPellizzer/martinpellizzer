Eupatorium densiflorum

Eupatorium densiflorum, commonly known as denseflower eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
