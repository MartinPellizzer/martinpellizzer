Eupatorium angulicaule

Eupatorium angulicaule, commonly known as angular eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist to moderately wet soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowering stems are often branched and hairy. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale purple, blooming in late summer to early fall.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar flowering plants such as daisies and sunflowers.
