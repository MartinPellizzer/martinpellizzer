Elaeodendron croceum var. triandrum

Elaeodendron croceum var. triandrum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered elaeodendron, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
