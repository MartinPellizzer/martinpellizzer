Eupatorium ibaquense var. indiscretum

Eupatorium ibaquense var. indiscretum, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
