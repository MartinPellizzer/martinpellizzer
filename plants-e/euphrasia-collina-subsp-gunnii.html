Euphrasia collina subsp. gunnii

Euphrasia collina subsp.

gunnii, commonly known as the mountain eyebright, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has hairy, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This subspecies is often found in alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
