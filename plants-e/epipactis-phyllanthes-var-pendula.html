Epipactis phyllanthes var. pendula

Epipactis phyllanthes var. pendula, commonly known as the hanging marsh helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-shaped leaves and produces a single, erect flowering stem. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
