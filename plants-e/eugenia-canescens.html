Eugenia canescens

Eugenia canescens, commonly known as the hairy eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are covered in fine, whitish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in small inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a red or purple color, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
