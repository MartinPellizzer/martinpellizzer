Ehretia volubilis

Ehretia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing ehretia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a woody vine characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
