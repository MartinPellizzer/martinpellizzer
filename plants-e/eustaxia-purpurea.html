Eustaxia purpurea

Eustaxia purpurea, commonly known as the purple eustaxia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Eustaxia purpurea is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
