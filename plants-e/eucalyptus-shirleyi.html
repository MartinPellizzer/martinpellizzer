Eucalyptus shirleyi

Eucalyptus shirleyi, commonly known as Shirley's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grey or white bark, which peels in thin, flaky layers. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale yellow to cream in color, blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Eucalyptus shirleyi is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
