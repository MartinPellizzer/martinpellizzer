Eupatorium soratae

Eupatorium soratae, commonly known as the white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along streams, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium soratae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
