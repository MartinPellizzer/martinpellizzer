Eupatorium loeseneri

Eupatorium loeseneri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Eupatorium loeseneri is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
