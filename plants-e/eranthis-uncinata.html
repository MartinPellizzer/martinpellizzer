Eranthis uncinata

Eranthis uncinata, commonly known as the hooked winter aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring. The plant features showy yellow flowers with prominent, hooked sepals that give it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Eranthis uncinata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
