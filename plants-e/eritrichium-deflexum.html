Eritrichium deflexum

Eritrichium deflexum, commonly known as the bentleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive downward curve, giving it its common name.

Eritrichium deflexum is often found in prairies and open woodlands, where it thrives in full sun.
