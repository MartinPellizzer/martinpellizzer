Eutheta

Eutheta, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescences that resemble flowers but are actually modified stems called cyathia. The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking, unusual appearance.
