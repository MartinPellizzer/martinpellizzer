Epidendrum oldemanii

Epidendrum oldemanii, commonly known as the Oldeman's orchid, is a species native to Central America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces numerous pseudobulbs. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with a yellow lip and a violet-blue upper sepal, often arranged in a raceme.

Epidendrum oldemanii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a tropical or greenhouse environment.
