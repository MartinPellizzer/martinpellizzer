Eugenia ovalis

Eugenia ovalis, commonly known as the oval guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits resembling small guavas. The fruit is juicy and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible produce.
