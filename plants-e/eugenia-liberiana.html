Eugenia liberiana

Eugenia liberiana, commonly known as the Jamaican star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a star-shaped calyx, which turns from green to yellow as it ripens. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, juicy pulp that has a mild, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native regions.
