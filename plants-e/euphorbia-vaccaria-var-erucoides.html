Euphorbia vaccaria var. erucoides

Euphorbia vaccaria var. erucoides, commonly known as the yellow-spined spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, upright stems that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, often with a slight reddish tinge.

Euphorbia vaccaria var. erucoides is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
