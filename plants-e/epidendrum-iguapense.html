Epidendrum iguapense

Epidendrum iguapense, commonly known as the Iguapense orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Amazon rainforests of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, long-lasting flowers in shades of yellow and green, often arranged in racemes. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Epidendrum iguapense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
