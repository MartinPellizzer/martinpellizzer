Elaeocarpus altisectus

Elaeocarpus altisectus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into narrow, elongated segments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody drupe, containing a single seed, and is an important food source for various wildlife.

Elaeocarpus altisectus is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
