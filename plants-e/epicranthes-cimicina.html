Epicranthes cimicina

Epicranthes cimicina, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming during the winter months. The plant has a rosette of dark green, velvety leaves that help it conserve moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The plant's unique blooming period and attractive appearance make it a valued ornamental species.
