Eria pubescens var. lanata

Eria pubescens var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
