Echioglossum muticum

Echioglossum muticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually pale blue or violet, and are clustered in racemes.

Echioglossum muticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
