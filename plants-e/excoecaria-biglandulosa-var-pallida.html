Excoecaria biglandulosa var. pallida

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. pallida, commonly known as the white-leaf croton, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and indoor collections.
