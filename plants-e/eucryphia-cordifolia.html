Eucryphia cordifolia

Eucryphia cordifolia, commonly known as the silver honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features attractive, heart-shaped leaves that turn red in autumn, adding vibrant seasonal color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and often has a silvery sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eucryphia cordifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
