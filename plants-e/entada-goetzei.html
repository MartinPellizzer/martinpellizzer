Entada goetzei

Entada goetzei, commonly known as the African hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods that contain seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in various cultures.
