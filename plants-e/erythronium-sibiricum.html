Erythronium sibiricum

Erythronium sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian dog's-tooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It features a corm-like structure and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive red or purple base. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are basal and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Erythronium sibiricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
