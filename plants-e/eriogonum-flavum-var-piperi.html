Eriogonum flavum var. piperi

Eriogonum flavum var. piperi, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and hillsides. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family, which includes many plants with nitrogen-fixing abilities.
