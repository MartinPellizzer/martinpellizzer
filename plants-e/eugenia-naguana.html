Eugenia naguana

Eugenia naguana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cinnamomum* species, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The tree thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid environment to flourish.
