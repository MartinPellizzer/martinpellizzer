Eriosyce heinrichiana subsp. intermedia

Eriosyce heinrichiana subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate ornamental quality. Its growth is slow, and it forms a compact rosette structure that makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled settings to ensure its survival.
