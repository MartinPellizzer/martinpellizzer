Elaeagnus umbellata f. argyrea

Elaeagnus umbellata f. argyrea, commonly known as silverleaf oleaster, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
