Echinocactus hexaedrophorus var. subcostatus

Echinocactus hexaedrophorus var. subcostatus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stem that is often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, branched stems. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular choice for succulent collections and xeriscaping.
