Euphorbia baliola

Euphorbia baliola, commonly known as the Bali spurge, is a succulent plant native to the island of Bali in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, which is a common trait among species in its family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia baliola is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
