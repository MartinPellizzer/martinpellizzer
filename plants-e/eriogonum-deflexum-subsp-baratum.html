Eriogonum deflexum subsp. baratum

Eriogonum deflexum subsp.

baratum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces showy, pale pink to white flowers in late spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

Its subspecies status reflects geographic and morphological distinctions from other Eriogonum species.
