Erica intricata

Erica intricata, commonly known as the intricate heath, is a shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in spring. Erica intricata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
