Eragrostis brachypodon

Eragrostis brachypodon, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Eragrostis brachypodon plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
