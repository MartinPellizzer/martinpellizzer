Eucephalus albus

Eucephalus albus, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
