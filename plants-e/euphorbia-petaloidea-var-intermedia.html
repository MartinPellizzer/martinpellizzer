Euphorbia petaloidea var. intermedia

Euphorbia petaloidea var. intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, giving it a floral appearance despite being a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant has a short, thick stem with smooth, waxy leaves that are often green or bluish-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by the showy bracts.

This species is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
