Erigeron compositus var. grandiflorus

Erigeron compositus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the largeflower blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it is often found in the Midwest region. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting native insect populations and its use in restoration projects.
