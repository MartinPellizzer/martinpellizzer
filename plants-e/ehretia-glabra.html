Ehretia glabra

Ehretia glabra, commonly known as the smooth ehretia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often blooming in spring. Its fruits are small drupes that mature to a reddish color, containing a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
