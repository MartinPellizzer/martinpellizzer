Euclea dewinteri

Euclea dewinteri, commonly known as the dewinter's euclea, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, often blooming in late summer.

Euclea dewinteri is notable for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
