Eugenia brunneopubescens

Eugenia brunneopubescens, commonly known as the brown-hairy eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a dense layer of brownish pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in small inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark purple color, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
