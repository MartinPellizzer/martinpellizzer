Echinostachys vanhoutteana

Echinostachys vanhoutteana, commonly known as the Van Houtte's tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that resemble small, fuzzy cones. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, giving it a unique and eye-catching appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a silver-blue hue that adds visual interest to the landscape.
