Epilobium montanum var. verticillatum

Epilobium montanum var. verticillatum, commonly known as mountain willow or clustered willow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils.
