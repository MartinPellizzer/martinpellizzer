Eupatorium bicolor

Eupatorium bicolor, commonly known as purple boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, two-toned flowers—pinkish-white at the base and violet at the tip—that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
