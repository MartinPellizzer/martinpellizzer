Encyclia ovulum

Encyclia ovulum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking yellow flowers with a greenish throat. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Encyclia ovulum thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical forests.
