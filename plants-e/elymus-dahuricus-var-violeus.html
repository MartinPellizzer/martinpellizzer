Elymus dahuricus var. violeus

Elymus dahuricus var. violeus, commonly known as the violet tamarisk, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of violet-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
