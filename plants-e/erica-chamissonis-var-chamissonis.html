Erica chamissonis var. chamissonis

Erica chamissonis var. chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's heath, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
