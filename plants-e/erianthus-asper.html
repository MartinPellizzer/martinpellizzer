Erianthus asper

Erianthus asper, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy texture and bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Erianthus asper is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
