Eryngium spinosepalum

Eryngium spinosepalum, commonly known as the spinose-sepal eryngium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like structures that surround its small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
