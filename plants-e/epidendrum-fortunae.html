Epidendrum fortunae

Epidendrum fortunae, commonly known as the Fortune's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting lip. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in seasonal climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing structural support for the flowering stems.

Epidendrum fortunae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
