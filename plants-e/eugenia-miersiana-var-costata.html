Eugenia miersiana var. costata

Eugenia miersiana var. costata, commonly known as the Costate Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its narrow, ribbed leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for food.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
