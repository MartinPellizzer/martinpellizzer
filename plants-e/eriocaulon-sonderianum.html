Eriocaulon sonderianum

Eriocaulon sonderianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikerush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like flower clusters that emerge from water or moist soil. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its reproductive structures consist of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, three-lobed seeds.

Eriocaulon sonderianum plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
