Erica simulans

Erica simulans, commonly known as the common heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica simulans thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens and as a ground cover in suitable climates.
