Euphorbia bimensis

Euphorbia bimensis, commonly known as the two-week euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems to survive dry periods.

Euphorbia bimensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
