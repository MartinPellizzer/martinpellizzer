Eupatorium adamantium var. minor

Eupatorium adamantium var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue-gray flowers. The plant produces tiny, tubular florets that are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is a low-maintenance plant that is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
