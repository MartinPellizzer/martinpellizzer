Eugenia hilariana

Eugenia hilariana, commonly known as the red bell pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark red, fleshy fruits that resemble peppers. The leaves are opposite, elliptic, and covered with a fine, silky hair.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a distinct aromatic scent when the leaves are crushed.
