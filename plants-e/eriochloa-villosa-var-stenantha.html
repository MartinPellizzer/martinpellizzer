Eriochloa villosa var. stenantha

Eriochloa villosa var. stenantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and habitat for wildlife.
