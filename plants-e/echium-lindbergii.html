Echium lindbergii

Echium lindbergii, commonly known as Lindberg's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height.

Echium lindbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
