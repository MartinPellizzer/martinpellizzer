Eroeda laevis

Eroeda laevis, commonly known as the smooth erica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eroeda laevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
