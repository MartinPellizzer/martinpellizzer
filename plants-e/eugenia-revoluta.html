Eugenia revoluta

Eugenia revoluta, commonly known as the red bell pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, bell-shaped fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Eugenia revoluta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural collections.
