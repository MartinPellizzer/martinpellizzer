Eugenia mandevillensis var. perratonii

Eugenia mandevillensis var. perratonii, commonly known as the Perratonii Eugenia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange when mature, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and potential medicinal properties.
