Eurybia chrysophylla

Eurybia chrysophylla, commonly known as the goldenleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Eurybia chrysophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
