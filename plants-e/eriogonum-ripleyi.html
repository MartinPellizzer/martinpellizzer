Eriogonum ripleyi

Eriogonum ripleyi, commonly known as Ripleys' buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and crevices, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and oval-shaped, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Eriogonum ripleyi plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in mixed shrublands and chaparral ecosystems.
