Elymus caucasicus

Elymus caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Elymus caucasicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in natural habitats.
