Elaeocarpus rutengii

Elaeocarpus rutengii, commonly known as the Java elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or purple when mature, containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is relatively rare in the wild, making it a subject of conservation interest.
