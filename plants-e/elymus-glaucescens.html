Elymus glaucescens

Elymus glaucescens, commonly known as blue wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with blue or purple hues.

Elymus glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
