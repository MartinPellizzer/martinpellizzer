Erica cristiflora

Erica cristiflora, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments. Erica cristiflora is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique, flattened inflorescence gives it a distinctive appearance, making it a popular ornamental plant.
