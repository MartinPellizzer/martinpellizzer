Eulalia irritans subsp. egregia

Eulalia irritans subsp.

egregia, commonly known as the showy grass, is a perennial grass native to South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and strikingly ornamental inflorescences that are often reddish or purplish in color.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

It is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
