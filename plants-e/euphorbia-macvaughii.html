Euphorbia macvaughii

Euphorbia macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
