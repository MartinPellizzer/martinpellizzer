Eupatorium iresinoides var. villosum

Eupatorium iresinoides var. villosum, commonly known as hairy boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and meadows.
