Eragrostis raynaliana

Eragrostis raynaliana, commonly known as Raynalian lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
