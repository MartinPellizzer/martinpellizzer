Eriochloa trichopus var. glabrata

Eriochloa trichopus var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth eriochloa, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
