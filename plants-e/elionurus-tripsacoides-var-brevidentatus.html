Elionurus tripsacoides var. brevidentatus

Elionurus tripsacoides var. brevidentatus, commonly known as the narrow-toothed grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with small, sharp teeth along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in grassland and savanna habitats, often in areas with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its native range.
