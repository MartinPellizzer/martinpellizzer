Eremaea brevifolia

Eremaea brevifolia, commonly known as the slender eremaea, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to low rainfall conditions.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in drought-tolerant gardens.
