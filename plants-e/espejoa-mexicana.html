Espejoa mexicana

Espejoa mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican thornapple, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that contain seeds. Espejoa mexicana is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low water availability, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
