Eupatorium foeniculaceum var. lateriflorum

Eupatorium foeniculaceum var. lateriflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and pollinator support.
