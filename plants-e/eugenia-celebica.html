Eugenia celebica

Eugenia celebica, commonly known as the Java apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its oval, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Eugenia celebica is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

It also has traditional medicinal applications in some cultures.
