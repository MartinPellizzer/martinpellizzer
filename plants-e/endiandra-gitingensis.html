Endiandra gitingensis

Endiandra gitingensis, commonly known as the Gitingensis Endiandra, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has compound leaves with alternate arrangement. Its flowers are small and usually greenish-yellow, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
