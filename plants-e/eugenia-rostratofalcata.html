Eugenia rostratofalcata

Eugenia rostratofalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia rostratofalcata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
