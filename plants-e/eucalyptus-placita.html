Eucalyptus placita

Eucalyptus placita, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in summer. Eucalyptus placita is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and salt.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various native animals.
