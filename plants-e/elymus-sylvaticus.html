Elymus sylvaticus

Elymus sylvaticus, commonly known as the wood grass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces dense clusters of spikelets along its length. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

Elymus sylvaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
