Erodium gruinum var. cuneatum

Erodium gruinum var. cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
