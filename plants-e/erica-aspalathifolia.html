Erica aspalathifolia

Erica aspalathifolia, commonly known as the rooibos plant, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, with drought-resistant properties. Its leaves are harvested for the production of rooibos tea, which is renowned for its unique flavor and health benefits.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a key species in local ecosystems and its economic importance in the tea industry.
