Eupatorium lozanoi

Eupatorium lozanoi, commonly known as the Lozano's thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically opposite, with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads, often displaying shades of white, pink, or purple.

Eupatorium lozanoi is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
