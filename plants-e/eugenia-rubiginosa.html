Eugenia rubiginosa

Eugenia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Eugenia rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
