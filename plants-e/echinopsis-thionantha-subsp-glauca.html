Echinopsis thionantha subsp. glauca

Echinopsis thionantha subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the Blue Ghost Cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to blue-gray spines.

The plant produces large, showy white or pale blue flowers during the cooler months, typically in winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and unique features, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
