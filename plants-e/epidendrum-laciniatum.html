Epidendrum laciniatum

Epidendrum laciniatum, commonly known as the Shaved Lip Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of pendulous, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often have a serrated or notched appearance, contributing to its common name.

Epidendrum laciniatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
