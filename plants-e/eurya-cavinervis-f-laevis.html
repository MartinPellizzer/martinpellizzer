Eurya cavinervis f. laevis

Eurya cavinervis f. laevis, commonly known as the smooth-veined eurya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
