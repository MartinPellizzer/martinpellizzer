Echinopsis eyriesii var. triumphans

Echinopsis eyriesii var. triumphans, commonly known as the Queen of the Night, is a striking cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its dramatic appearance and unique blooming behavior.
