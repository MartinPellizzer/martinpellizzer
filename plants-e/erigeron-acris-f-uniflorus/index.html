Erigeron Acris F. Uniflorus

Erigeron acris f. uniflorus is a unique variant of the common fleabane plant known for its striking single flower form. This plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in open, sunny habitats across North America. Its pale blue to lavender flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant's compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays. Its rarity and distinctive appearance make it a fascinating subject for botanists and gardeners alike.
