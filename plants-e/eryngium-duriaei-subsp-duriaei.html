Eryngium duriaei subsp. duriaei

Eryngium duriaei subsp.

duriaei, commonly known as the red spurflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. It produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
