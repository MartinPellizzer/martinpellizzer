Ereicoctis hyssopifolia

Ereicoctis hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense, mist-like clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ereicoctis hyssopifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
