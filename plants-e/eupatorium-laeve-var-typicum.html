Eupatorium laeve var. typicum

Eupatorium laeve var. typicum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, slightly hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
