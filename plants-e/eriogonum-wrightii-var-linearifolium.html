Eriogonum wrightii var. linearifolium

Eriogonum wrightii var. linearifolium, commonly known as the longleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It is a perennial shrub that produces small, edible seeds, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
