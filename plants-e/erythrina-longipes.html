Erythrina longipes

Erythrina longipes, commonly known as the longipes coral tree, is a striking tropical plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which are clustered in dense panicles and attract various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often woody and covered in a thick, waxy cuticle. Erythrina longipes is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
