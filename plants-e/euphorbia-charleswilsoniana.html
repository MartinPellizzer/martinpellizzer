Euphorbia charleswilsoniana

Euphorbia charleswilsoniana, commonly known as the Wilson's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin.

This species is valued for its resilience and unique appearance in xeriscaping and desert gardens.
