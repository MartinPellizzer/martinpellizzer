Eremophila bowmanii var. latifolia

Eremophila bowmanii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
