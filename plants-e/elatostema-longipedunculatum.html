Elatostema longipedunculatum

Elatostema longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed elatostema, is a member of the Urticaceae family, characterized by its small, herbaceous structure.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and short, hairy stalks. Its flowering occurs in clusters, producing tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, making it a common component of understory vegetation.
