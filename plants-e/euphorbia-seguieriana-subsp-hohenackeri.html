Euphorbia seguieriana subsp. hohenackeri

Euphorbia seguieriana subsp.

hohenackeri, commonly known as the Hohenacker's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright, branching structure and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
