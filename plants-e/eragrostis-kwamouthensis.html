Eragrostis kwamouthensis

Eragrostis kwamouthensis, commonly known as Kwamouth grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and erosion control in dry regions.
