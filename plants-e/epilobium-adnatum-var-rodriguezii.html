Epilobium adnatum var. rodriguezii

Epilobium adnatum var. rodriguezii, commonly known as Rodriguez's willowherb, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its habitat.
