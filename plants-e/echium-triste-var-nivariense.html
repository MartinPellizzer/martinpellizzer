Echium triste var. nivariense

Echium triste var. nivariense, commonly known as the snowy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.
