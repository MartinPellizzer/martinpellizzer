Euphorbia viminea f. barringtonensis

Euphorbia viminea f. barringtonensis, commonly known as the Barrington's milkweed, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, branched stem that is often covered in a waxy, bluish-green cuticle. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are typically narrow and may be reduced or absent in some varieties.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
