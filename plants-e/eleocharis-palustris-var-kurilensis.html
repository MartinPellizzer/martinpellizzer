Eleocharis palustris var. kurilensis

Eleocharis palustris var. kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril marsh sedge, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the Kuril Islands and parts of eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy or aquatic environments. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
