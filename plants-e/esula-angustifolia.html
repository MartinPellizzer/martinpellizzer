Esula angustifolia

Esula angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf esula, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or swampy areas.

Esula angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
