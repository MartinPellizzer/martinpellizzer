Ebermaiera minarum

Ebermaiera minarum, commonly known as the Minarum Tree, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, bark-like surface that helps it retain moisture in its humid environment. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Ebermaiera minarum plays an important ecological role by providing shelter and food for various rainforest animals.
