Eragrostis pooides f. umbrosa

Eragrostis pooides f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving lovegrass, is a variant of the lovegrass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it suitable for woodland or shaded garden settings. Its compact form and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for ground cover in humid climates.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically pale green to brown, adding subtle visual interest during the flowering season.
