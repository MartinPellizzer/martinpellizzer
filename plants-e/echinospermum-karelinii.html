Echinospermum karelinii

Echinospermum karelinii, commonly known as the Karakul lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Echinospermum karelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
