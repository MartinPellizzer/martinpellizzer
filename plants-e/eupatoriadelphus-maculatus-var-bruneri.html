Eupatoriadelphus maculatus var. bruneri

Eupatoriadelphus maculatus var. bruneri, commonly known as the spotted ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in late summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
