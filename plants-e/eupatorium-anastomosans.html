Eupatorium anastomosans

Eupatorium anastomosans, commonly known as the clustered thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a branched stem covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, clustered flower heads that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium anastomosans thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
