Epidendrum atypicum

Epidendrum atypicum, commonly known as the Atypical Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Epidendrum atypicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
