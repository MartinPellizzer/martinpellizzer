Euphorbia montana var. trifaris

Euphorbia montana var. trifaris, commonly known as the three-lobed spurge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in a unique cyathium structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular plant among gardeners interested in cultivating native or hardy plants.
