Eriobotrya kwangsiensis

Eriobotrya kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi loquat, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy white flowers in early spring, followed by yellow-orange berries that ripen in late summer.

It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
