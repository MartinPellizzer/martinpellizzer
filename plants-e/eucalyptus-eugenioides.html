Eucalyptus eugenioides

Eucalyptus eugenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallet, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Eucalyptus eugenioides is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
