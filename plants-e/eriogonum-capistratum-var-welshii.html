Eriogonum capistratum var. welshii

Eriogonum capistratum var. welshii, commonly known as the Welsh's buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and crevices, demonstrating its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
