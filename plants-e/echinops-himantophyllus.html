Echinops himantophyllus

Echinops himantophyllus, commonly known as the Afghan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin, giving it its genus name "Echinops.". The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments.

Its structure and appearance make it a striking addition to xeriscaped gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
