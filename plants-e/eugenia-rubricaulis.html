Eugenia rubricaulis

Eugenia rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown stems that contrast with its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
