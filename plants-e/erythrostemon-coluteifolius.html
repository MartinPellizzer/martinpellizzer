Erythrostemon coluteifolius

Erythrostemon coluteifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved erythrostemon, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a woody stem and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
