Eugenia vismioides

Eugenia vismioides, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by edible, yellow to orange fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when fully ripe and are used in various culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
