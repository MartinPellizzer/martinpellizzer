Epacris gnidioides

Epacris gnidioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved epacris, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Epacris gnidioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
