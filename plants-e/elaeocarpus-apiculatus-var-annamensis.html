Elaeocarpus apiculatus var. annamensis

Elaeocarpus apiculatus var. annamensis, commonly known as the Annamese elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
