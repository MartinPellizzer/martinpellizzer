Eucalyptus simmondsii

Eucalyptus simmondsii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The flowers are small, white or cream, and appear in clusters, often during the winter months. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and heat.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
