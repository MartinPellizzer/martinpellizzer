Erodium guicciardii

Erodium guicciardii, commonly known as the California morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and open fields.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
