Erica spiraeiflora

Erica spiraeiflora, commonly known as the spiral heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces both male and female flowers on the same plant, making it monoecious.

Erica spiraeiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
