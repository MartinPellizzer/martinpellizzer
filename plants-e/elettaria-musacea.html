Elettaria musacea

Elettaria musacea, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its underground rhizomes are the primary part used, containing essential oils responsible for its distinctive fragrance.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic properties, it is valued in traditional medicine and perfumery.
