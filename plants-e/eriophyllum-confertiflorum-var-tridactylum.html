Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. tridactylum

Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. tridactylum, commonly known as the coastal goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing in open habitats such as dunes and grasslands. It has small, scale-like leaves and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
