Euphorbia antonii

Euphorbia antonii, commonly known as the "Coral Plant," is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar.

It features striking, branching stems covered in dense, fleshy, greenish-yellow leaves that resemble coral branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which give it its ornamental appeal. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid environments.
