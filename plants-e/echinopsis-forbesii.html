Echinopsis forbesii

Echinopsis forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' night bloomers, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming behavior, it is a popular choice for cultivated cactus gardens.
