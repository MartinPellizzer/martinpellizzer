Eragrostis kuschelii

Eragrostis kuschelii, commonly known as the Kuschel's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to bluish-gray florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Eragrostis kuschelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
