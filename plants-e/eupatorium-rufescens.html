Eupatorium rufescens

Eupatorium rufescens, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, pale purple to white composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. It blooms from late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators including bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium rufescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
