Erica occulta

Erica occulta, commonly known as the hidden heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, dense foliage. The plant produces delicate white or pale pink flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Erica occulta thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, compact growth.
