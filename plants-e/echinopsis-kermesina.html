Echinopsis kermesina

Echinopsis kermesina, commonly known as the Kermesina cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its short, ribbed stems and dense clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces spiny areoles with clusters of white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Echinopsis kermesina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
