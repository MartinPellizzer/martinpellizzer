Eriophorum angustifolium f. uniceps

Eriophorum angustifolium f. uniceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cotton grass, is a variant of the cotton grass genus characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant that typically thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments such as bogs and wet meadows. The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal, a common feature among members of the Cyperaceae family. Its morphology includes a basal rosette of leaves with a central stem bearing the inflorescence.

This form is distinguished by its reduced number of inflorescence branches, giving it a more compact appearance compared to other cotton grass species.
