Eremurus × ludmillae

Eremurus × ludmillae, commonly known as the hybrid eremurus or the "bishop's candle," is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Eremurus, which is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae). The plant is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a candle, consisting of numerous yellow to orange ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Eremurus × ludmillae is valued for its ornamental display and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral appearance.
