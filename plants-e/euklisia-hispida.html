Euklisia hispida

Euklisia hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf chenille, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
