Eriogonum vimineum subsp. polycladon

Eriogonum vimineum subsp.

polycladon, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces numerous yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe.

This subspecies is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.
