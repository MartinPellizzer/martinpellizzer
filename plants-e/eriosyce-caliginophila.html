Eriosyce caliginophila

Eriosyce caliginophila, commonly known as the cloud forest cactus, is a species native to the Andean cloud forests of South America.

It is a low-growing, leafless cactus that produces clusters of white, woolly flowers. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dense, spiny stems and is adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its habitat. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found at higher elevations.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
