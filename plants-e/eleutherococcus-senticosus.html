Eleutherococcus senticosus

Eleutherococcus senticosus, commonly known as Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of eastern Russia, northeastern China, and Korea.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces red berries that contain the seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its roots and stems are the primary parts used for medicinal purposes, containing various bioactive compounds.

It is valued for its adaptogenic properties, which are believed to enhance physical and mental performance.
