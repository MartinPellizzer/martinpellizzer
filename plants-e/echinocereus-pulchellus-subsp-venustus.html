Echinocereus pulchellus subsp. venustus

Echinocereus pulchellus subsp.

venustus, commonly known as the Venus's Thorn, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or purple hues.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
