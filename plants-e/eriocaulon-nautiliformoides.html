Eriocaulon nautiliformoides

Eriocaulon nautiliformoides, commonly known as the nautiliformoid sea grass, is a unique aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that form dense underwater mats, often found in shallow, brackish or freshwater environments. The plant exhibits a specialized morphology adapted for underwater growth, with reduced leaves and a rhizomatous root system. It plays an important role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
