Erodium atlanticum var. cossonii

Erodium atlanticum var. cossonii, commonly known as the coastal bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to coastal regions of southern California.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or coastal habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of coastal environments.
