Elymus hispidus var. epiroticus

Elymus hispidus var. epiroticus, commonly known as the Epirotic needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
