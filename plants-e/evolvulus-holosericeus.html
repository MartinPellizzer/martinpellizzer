Evolvulus holosericeus

Evolvulus holosericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

The silver-gray coloration of its leaves gives it its common name and makes it visually distinctive.
