Erodium cicutarium var. nadorense

Erodium cicutarium var. nadorense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink or purplish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface.

The seeds are small and covered in a sticky substance, aiding in their dispersal by adhering to animals or clothing.
