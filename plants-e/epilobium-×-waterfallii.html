Epilobium × waterfallii

Epilobium × waterfallii, commonly known as the waterfall willow herb, is a hybrid species within the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often cascading stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in terminal clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and woodland edges.
