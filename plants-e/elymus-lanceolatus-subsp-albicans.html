Elymus lanceolatus subsp. albicans

Elymus lanceolatus subsp.

albicans, commonly known as white wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, grayish-green leaves and tall, branched inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its white-tinged foliage and the presence of awns on its florets.
