Elymus formosanus var. pubiger

Elymus formosanus var. pubiger, commonly known as the hairy formosan wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often bear a bluish-green hue. The plant produces spikelets that are usually arranged in dense clusters, with leathery, awned glumes. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, often covered with fine hairs.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist to mesic habitats.
