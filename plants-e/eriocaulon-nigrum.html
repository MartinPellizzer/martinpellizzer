Eriocaulon nigrum

Eriocaulon nigrum, commonly known as black rush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces dark, berry-like fruits.

Eriocaulon nigrum plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
