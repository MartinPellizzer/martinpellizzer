Erica auricularis

Erica auricularis, commonly known as the auricle heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica auricularis typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
