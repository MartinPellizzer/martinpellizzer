Erysimum scabrosum

Erysimum scabrosum, commonly known as the rough wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are covered in a rough, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Erysimum scabrosum is known for its vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring and early summer.
