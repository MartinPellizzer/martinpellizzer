Erodium cicutarium var. viscosum

Erodium cicutarium var. viscosum, commonly known as sticky bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its sticky, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a glandular surface that produces a sticky secretion. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its seeds are encased in a hairy, pod-like fruit that bursts open to disperse seeds upon maturity.
