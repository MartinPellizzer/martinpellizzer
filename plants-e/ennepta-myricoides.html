Ennepta myricoides

Ennepta myricoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it often has a spicy, resinous scent.

Ennepta myricoides is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
