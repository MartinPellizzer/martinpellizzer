Eucalyptus amygdalina var. radiata

Eucalyptus amygdalina var. radiata, commonly known as the lemon-scented gum, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is a tall tree with smooth, peeling bark that reveals a fibrous underlayer. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive lemon-like fragrance when crushed. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
