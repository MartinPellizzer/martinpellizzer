Epilobium coloratum f. minus

Epilobium coloratum f. minus, commonly known as the dwarf willow or pink willow, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of only a few inches, making it a compact and ornamental species. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
