Eionitis psammophila var. hirtella

Eionitis psammophila var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
