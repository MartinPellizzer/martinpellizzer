Eugenia widgrenii

Eugenia widgrenii, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature, though they are not as commonly consumed as other Eugenia species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant.
