Eugenia cartilaginea

Eugenia cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, which is typically small and yellowish, is edible and has a mild flavor.

Eugenia cartilaginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
