Elatine triandra subsp. americana

Elatine triandra subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American waterstarwort, is a small aquatic plant native to North America. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams.

The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length, and it produces tiny, star-shaped flowers that are often submerged or floating. It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and is often found in nutrient-rich waters.

This subspecies is part of the Elatinaceae family and is adapted to thrive in wetland habitats.
