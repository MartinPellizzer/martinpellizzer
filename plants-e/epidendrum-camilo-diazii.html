Epidendrum camilo-diazii

Epidendrum camilo-diazii, commonly known as the Camilo-Diaz Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks in tropical forests. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a contrasting lip, and it blooms during the warmer months.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
