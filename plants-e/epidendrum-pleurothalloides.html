Epidendrum pleurothalloides

Epidendrum pleurothalloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
