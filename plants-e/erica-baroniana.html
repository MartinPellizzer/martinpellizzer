Erica baroniana

Erica baroniana, commonly known as the Baronne's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica baroniana is a low-growing shrub, often forming dense mats, and is well adapted to Mediterranean-type climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
