Eremophila yinnetharrensis

Eremophila yinnetharrensis, commonly known as the Yinnetharren eremophila, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces striking spikes of pink to red flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. It has adapted to survive in dry conditions with small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss.

Eremophila yinnetharrensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
