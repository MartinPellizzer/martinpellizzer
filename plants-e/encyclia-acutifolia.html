Encyclia acutifolia

Encyclia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically pointed at the tips. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive green lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Encyclia acutifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
