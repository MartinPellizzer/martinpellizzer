Ericoides carinatum

Ericoides carinatum, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a shrub native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes evergreen shrubs and small trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ericoides carinatum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
