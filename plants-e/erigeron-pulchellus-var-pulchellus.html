Erigeron pulchellus var. pulchellus

Erigeron pulchellus var. pulchellus, commonly known as the showy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing vibrant blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often found in native plant gardens.
