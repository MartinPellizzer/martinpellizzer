Eleocharis glaucescens

Eleocharis glaucescens, commonly known as the blue spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often bear small, blue-green spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into dark, spherical berries.

Eleocharis glaucescens plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
