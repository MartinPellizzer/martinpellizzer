Erodium aguilellae

Erodium aguilellae, commonly known as the coastal stork's-bill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Erodium aguilellae is adapted to tolerate salt spray and is an important part of the local flora in its native range.
