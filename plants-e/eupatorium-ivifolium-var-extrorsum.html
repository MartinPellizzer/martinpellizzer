Eupatorium ivifolium var. extrorsum

Eupatorium ivifolium var. extrorsum, commonly known as the creeping white snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that spreads by rhizomes, forming dense mats.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
