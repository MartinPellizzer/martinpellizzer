Eucalyptus recta

Eucalyptus recta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The tree typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, spherical flower buds that are often pink or red in color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic oils and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
