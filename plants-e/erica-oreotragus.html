Erica oreotragus

Erica oreotragus, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions. Erica oreotragus is known for its ability to thrive in areas with high altitude and low rainfall.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
