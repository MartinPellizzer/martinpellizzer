Erigeron × engadensis

Erigeron × engadensis, commonly known as the Engadine fleabane, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically feature white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Erigeron × engadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
