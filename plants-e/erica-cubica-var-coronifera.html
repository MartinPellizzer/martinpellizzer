Erica cubica var. coronifera

Erica cubica var. coronifera, commonly known as the heather or dwarf heather, is a low-growing shrub native to the British Isles.

It features compact, evergreen foliage with needle-like leaves that are dark green and aromatic. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
