Eryngium triquetrum

Eryngium triquetrum, commonly known as the three-angled thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. Its flowers are composed of small, tubular florets surrounded by bracts, and it produces small, triangular seeds.

Eryngium triquetrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
