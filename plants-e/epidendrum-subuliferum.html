Epidendrum subuliferum

Epidendrum subuliferum, commonly known as the needle-leaf epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in spikes, and come in various colors such as white, yellow, and pink.

Epidendrum subuliferum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
