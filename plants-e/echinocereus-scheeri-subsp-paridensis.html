Echinocereus scheeri subsp. paridensis

Echinocereus scheeri subsp.

paridensis, commonly known as the Paridens cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, white spines and small, rounded areoles.

The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
