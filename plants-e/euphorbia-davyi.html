Euphorbia davyi

Euphorbia davyi, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with its spiny stems and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia davyi is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
