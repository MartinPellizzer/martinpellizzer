Erica myriadenia

Erica myriadenia, commonly known as the many-leaved heath, is a species native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the summer months. Erica myriadenia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, with periods of drought tolerance.
