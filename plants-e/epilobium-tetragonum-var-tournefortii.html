Epilobium tetragonum var. tournefortii

Epilobium tetragonum var. tournefortii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and disturbed sites.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively, it can become invasive in certain environments.
