Epidendrum cirrhochilum

Epidendrum cirrhochilum, commonly known as the curl-lip epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
