Erioblastus flexuosus

Erioblastus flexuosus, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or low shrubs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Erioblastus flexuosus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
