Evolvulus bahiensis

Evolvulus bahiensis, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
