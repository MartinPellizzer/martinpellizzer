Erica phylicoides

Erica phylicoides, commonly known as the Cape heath or narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit with small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica phylicoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
