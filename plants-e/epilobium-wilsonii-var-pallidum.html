Epilobium wilsonii var. pallidum

Epilobium wilsonii var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale willowherb, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a stout stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and capsules as its fruiting structure.

It is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
