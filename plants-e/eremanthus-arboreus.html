Eremanthus arboreus

Eremanthus arboreus, commonly known as the tree eremanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions.

Eremanthus arboreus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
