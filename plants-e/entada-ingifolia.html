Entada ingifolia

Entada ingifolia, commonly known as the African honeycomb tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hollow, segmented pods that resemble a honeycomb. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and large, compound leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces a sweet, sticky sap that has been traditionally used in various cultural practices.

The plant is also valued for its hard, heavy wood and its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils.
