Eryngium bourgatii var. bourgatii

Eryngium bourgatii var. bourgatii, commonly known as the blue thistle or sea holly, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, silver-green bracts. The plant has a robust, upright stem with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
