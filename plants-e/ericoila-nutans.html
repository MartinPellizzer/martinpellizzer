Ericoila nutans

Ericoila nutans, commonly known as the nodding ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Ericoila nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
