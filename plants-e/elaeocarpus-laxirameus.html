Elaeocarpus laxirameus

Elaeocarpus laxirameus, commonly known as the slender elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that enclose a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
