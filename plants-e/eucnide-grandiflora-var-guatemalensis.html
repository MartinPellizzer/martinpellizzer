Eucnide grandiflora var. guatemalensis

Eucnide grandiflora var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
