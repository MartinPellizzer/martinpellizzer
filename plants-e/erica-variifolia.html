Erica variifolia

Erica variifolia, commonly known as the variable heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica variifolia is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
