Erythroxylum flavicans

Erythroxylum flavicans, commonly known as the yellow mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers and palmate leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds covered in fine hairs.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant blooms.
