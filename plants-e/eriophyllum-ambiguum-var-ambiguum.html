Eriophyllum ambiguum var. ambiguum

Eriophyllum ambiguum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the Coast Goldfields, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for coastal gardens.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
