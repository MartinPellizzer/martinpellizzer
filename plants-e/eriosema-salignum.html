Eriosema salignum

Eriosema salignum, commonly known as the silverleaf eriosema, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
