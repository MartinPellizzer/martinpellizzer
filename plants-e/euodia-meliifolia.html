Euodia meliifolia

Euodia meliifolia, commonly known as the honeycomb tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its bark is smooth and often has a distinctive honeycomb-like pattern, giving it its common name.

Euodia meliifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
