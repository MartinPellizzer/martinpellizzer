Encyclia belizensis subsp. parviflora

Encyclia belizensis subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the Parviflora Encyclia, is a species of orchid native to Central America. It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow.

The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
