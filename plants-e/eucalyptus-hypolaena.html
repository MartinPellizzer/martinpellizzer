Eucalyptus hypolaena

Eucalyptus hypolaena, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pink or white, which bloom during the cooler months. Eucalyptus hypolaena is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
