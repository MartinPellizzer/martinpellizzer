Euphorbia minuta

Euphorbia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance.

Euphorbia minuta is also known for its latex sap, which can be irritant to the skin.
