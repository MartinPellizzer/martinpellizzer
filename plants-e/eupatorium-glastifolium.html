Eupatorium glastifolium

Eupatorium glastifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Eupatorium glastifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
