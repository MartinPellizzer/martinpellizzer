Erigeron berteroanus var. prostratus

Erigeron berteroanus var. prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate blueweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
