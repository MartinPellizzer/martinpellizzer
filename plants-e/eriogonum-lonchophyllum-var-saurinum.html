Eriogonum lonchophyllum var. saurinum

Eriogonum lonchophyllum var. saurinum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive drought.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
