Evolvulus araucanus

Evolvulus araucanus, commonly known as the Chilean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Evolvulus araucanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in warm, sunny environments.
