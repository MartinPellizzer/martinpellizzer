Eriogonum compositum var. pilicaule

Eriogonum compositum var. pilicaule, commonly known as the hairyleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
