Elaeagnus triflora subsp. polymorpha

Elaeagnus triflora subsp.

polymorpha, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in spring. Its fruits are small, black berries that ripen in late summer.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is known for its ability to thrive in sandy or well-drained soils.
