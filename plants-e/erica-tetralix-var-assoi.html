Erica tetralix var. assoi

Erica tetralix var. assoi, commonly known as the assoi heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has leathery, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Erica tetralix var. assoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
