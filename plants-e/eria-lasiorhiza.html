Eria lasiorhiza

Eria lasiorhiza, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
