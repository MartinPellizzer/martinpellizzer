Epidendrum iridicolor

Epidendrum iridicolor, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often fragrant flowers that can range from white to purple, with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, and it produces numerous spikes of flowers during the growing season.

Epidendrum iridicolor is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
