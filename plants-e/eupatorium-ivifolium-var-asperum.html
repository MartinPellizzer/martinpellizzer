Eupatorium ivifolium var. asperum

Eupatorium ivifolium var. asperum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties used by Indigenous peoples.

Its name reflects both its physical appearance and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
