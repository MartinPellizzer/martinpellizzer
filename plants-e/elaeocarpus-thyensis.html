Elaeocarpus thyensis

Elaeocarpus thyensis, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a thick, woody husk. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
