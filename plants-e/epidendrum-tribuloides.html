Epidendrum tribuloides

Epidendrum tribuloides, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. It requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.

Epidendrum tribuloides is valued for its unique appearance and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
