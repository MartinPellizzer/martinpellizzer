Eucalyptus goniantha subsp. notactites

Eucalyptus goniantha subsp.

notactites, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often greyish-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower buds that typically open to reveal white or pale pink flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and heat.

The species is also valued for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in various traditional and commercial applications.
