Eugenia hydrophila

Eugenia hydrophila, commonly known as the water myrtle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Eugenia hydrophila plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
