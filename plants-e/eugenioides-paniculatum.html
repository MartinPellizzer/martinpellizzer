Eugenioides paniculatum

Eugenioides paniculatum, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with long, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a small, woody pod that contains several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
