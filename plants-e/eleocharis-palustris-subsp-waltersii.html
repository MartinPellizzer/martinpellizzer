Eleocharis palustris subsp. waltersii

Eleocharis palustris subsp.

waltersii, commonly known as the Walterson's spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and shallow water areas.

The plant features cylindrical, leaf-like stems that are often covered in small, scale-like structures. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of tiny, three-lobed flowers that are usually green to reddish in color.

This subspecies is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
