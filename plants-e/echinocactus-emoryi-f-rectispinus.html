Echinocactus emoryi f. rectispinus

Echinocactus emoryi f. rectispinus, commonly known as the Emory's cactus or the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a variety of the Emory cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance characterized by its pale green to blue-green stems and dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels at the areoles. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and having a slow growth rate.

This cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
