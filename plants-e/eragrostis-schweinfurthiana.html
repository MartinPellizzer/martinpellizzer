Eragrostis schweinfurthiana

Eragrostis schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinct blue-green color, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Eragrostis schweinfurthiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
