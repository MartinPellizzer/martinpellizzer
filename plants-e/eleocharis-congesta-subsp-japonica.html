Eleocharis congesta subsp. japonica

Eleocharis congesta subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often clustered together, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This species is native to East Asia and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
