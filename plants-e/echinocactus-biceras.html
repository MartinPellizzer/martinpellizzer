Echinocactus biceras

Echinocactus biceras, commonly known as the two-horned cactus, is a species native to the Mexican states of Chihuahua and Coahuila.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and prominent spines. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale green, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Echinocactus biceras is valued for its striking appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
