Epidendrum weddelii

Epidendrum weddelii, commonly known as the Weddell's Epidendrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves, which are typically dark green and strap-shaped. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are usually yellow or greenish with purple or red markings.

Epidendrum weddelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
