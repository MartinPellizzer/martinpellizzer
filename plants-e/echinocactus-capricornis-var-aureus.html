Echinocactus capricornis var. aureus

Echinocactus capricornis var. aureus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny, cylindrical stem that resembles a barrel, with a distinctive golden to yellowish-green color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
