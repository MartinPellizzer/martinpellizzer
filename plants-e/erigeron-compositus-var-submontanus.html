Erigeron compositus var. submontanus

Erigeron compositus var. submontanus, commonly known as the Sierra fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and slopes, often at higher elevations. Its foliage is generally gray-green with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms in yellow to white hues.

The species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in mountainous regions.
