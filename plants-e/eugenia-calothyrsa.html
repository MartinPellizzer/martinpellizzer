Eugenia calothyrsa

Eugenia calothyrsa, commonly known as the rock apple or Australian cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red, cherry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Eugenia calothyrsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a source of food for wildlife.
