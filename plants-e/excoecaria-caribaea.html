Excoecaria caribaea

Excoecaria caribaea, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant's roots are used in traditional medicine and its name derives from the Latin word "excoecare," meaning to excavate, referring to its root structure.
