Eugenia pantanalensis

Eugenia pantanalensis, commonly known as the Pantanal Eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pantanal wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is adapted to the seasonal flooding of its native habitat.

Due to its unique ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
