Elaeagnus cumingii

Elaeagnus cumingii, commonly known as the silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in the fall. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Elaeagnus cumingii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
