Epilobium confertifolium var. tenuipes

Epilobium confertifolium var. tenuipes, commonly known as narrow-leaved willowherb, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils, often in tundra or alpine environments. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, narrow, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are showy, pink to red, and arranged in spikes, blooming in late summer.

It is an important plant in its ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
