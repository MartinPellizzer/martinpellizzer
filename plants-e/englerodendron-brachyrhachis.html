Englerodendron brachyrhachis

Englerodendron brachyrhachis, commonly known as the short-stemmed huito, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves with opposite arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional construction and crafts.
