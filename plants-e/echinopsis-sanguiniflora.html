Echinopsis sanguiniflora

Echinopsis sanguiniflora, commonly known as the blood flower, is a cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and striking red or pink flowers that bloom at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bats and moths, which are attracted to its strong fragrance.

Echinopsis sanguiniflora is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
