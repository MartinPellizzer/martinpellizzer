Eugenia limbosa

Eugenia limbosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and dry forest areas. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are woody capsules containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Eugenia limbosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
