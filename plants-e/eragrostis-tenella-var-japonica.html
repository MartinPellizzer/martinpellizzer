Eragrostis tenella var. japonica

Eragrostis tenella var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese wiregrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a versatile choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
