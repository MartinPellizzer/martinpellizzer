Erinacea erinacea

Erinacea erinacea, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that resemble the quills of a hedgehog. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Erinacea erinacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
