Eremurus giselae

Eremurus giselae, commonly known as the Giselae's torchflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Eremurus, which is part of the family Asteraceae, commonly known as the sunflower family. This plant is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch, composed of numerous yellow to orange ray florets. Eremurus giselae typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually impressive addition to botanical gardens and arid landscape designs.
