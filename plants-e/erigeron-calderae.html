Erigeron calderae

Erigeron calderae, commonly known as the Caldeira blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture.

Erigeron calderae is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
