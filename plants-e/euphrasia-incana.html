Euphrasia incana

Euphrasia incana, commonly known as the hairy eyebright, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its common name.

Euphrasia incana is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to support eye health and treat various ailments.
