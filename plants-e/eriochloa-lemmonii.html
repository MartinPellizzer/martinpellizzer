Eriochloa lemmonii

Eriochloa lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale blue florets. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and heat resistance.

Eriochloa lemmonii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry regions.
