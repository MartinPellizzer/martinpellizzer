Elaeocarpus subvillosus

Elaeocarpus subvillosus, commonly known as the silky elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited uses in traditional medicine.
