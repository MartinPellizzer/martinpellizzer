Eugenia pruinosa

Eugenia pruinosa, commonly known as the silver leaf eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several elliptical seeds.

Eugenia pruinosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of soil types.
