Espeletia occulta subsp. glossophylla

Espeletia occulta subsp.

glossophylla, commonly known as the "Cock's-comb" or "Cock's-hat," is a striking plant native to the high Andes of South America. It is characterized by its dense, tufted rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that form a compact, cushion-like structure.

The plant produces tall, spires of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in harsh, high-altitude environments with strong sunlight and cool temperatures.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens and botanical collections.
