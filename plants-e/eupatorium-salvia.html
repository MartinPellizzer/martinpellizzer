Eupatorium salvia

Eupatorium salvia, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, serrated, and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

Boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for treating fever and digestive issues.
