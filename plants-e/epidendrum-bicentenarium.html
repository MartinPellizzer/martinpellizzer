Epidendrum bicentenarium

Epidendrum bicentenarium, commonly known as the bicentennial orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the surrounding soil.

Epidendrum bicentenarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
