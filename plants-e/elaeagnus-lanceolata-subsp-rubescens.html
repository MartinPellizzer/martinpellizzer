Elaeagnus lanceolata subsp. rubescens

Elaeagnus lanceolata subsp.

rubescens, commonly known as the red-fruited silverberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, red-orange fruits that mature in late summer.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
