Erysimum capitatum

Erysimum capitatum, commonly known as the clustered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Erysimum capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
