Eucalyptus ligustrina

Eucalyptus ligustrina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs or small trees. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. Eucalyptus ligustrina is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
