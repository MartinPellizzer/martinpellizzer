Eucalyptus gamophylla

Eucalyptus gamophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The flowers are usually small and arranged in clusters, producing a distinctive honey-scented nectar that attracts various pollinators. The bark of this plant is often flaky and peels away in strips, revealing a smooth, pale underbark.

It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
