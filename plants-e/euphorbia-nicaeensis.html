Euphorbia nicaeensis

Euphorbia nicaeensis, commonly known as the Sicilian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
