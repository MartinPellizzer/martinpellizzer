Eria conrardii var. fimbriata

Eria conrardii var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed eria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
