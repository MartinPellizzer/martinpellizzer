Eugenia arbutifolia

Eugenia arbutifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often found in coastal areas and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
