Erica caledonica

Erica caledonica, commonly known as the Scottish heather, is a flowering plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers, blueberries, and azaleas. This evergreen shrub typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local wildlife.
