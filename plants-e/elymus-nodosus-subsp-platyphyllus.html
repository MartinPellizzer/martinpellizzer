Elymus nodosus subsp. platyphyllus

Elymus nodosus subsp.

platyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its flat, wide leaves and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles.

Botanically, it is notable for its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
