Eucalyptus buprestium

Eucalyptus buprestium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are often pink or red, which develop into woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Eucalyptus buprestium is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
