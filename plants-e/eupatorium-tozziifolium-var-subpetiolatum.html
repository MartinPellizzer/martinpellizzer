Eupatorium tozziifolium var. subpetiolatum

Eupatorium tozziifolium var. subpetiolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is part of the Asteraceae family. It produces fluffy, seed-filled structures that aid in wind pollination.

This variety is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
