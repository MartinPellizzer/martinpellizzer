Epimenidion hyacinthoides

Epimenidion hyacinthoides, commonly known as the bluebell of the Mediterranean, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Epimenidion hyacinthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
