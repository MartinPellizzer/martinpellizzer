Euanthe sanderiana f. immaculata

Euanthe sanderiana f. immaculata, commonly known as the white-flowered sanders' euanthe, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a pale yellow lip, which distinguish it from its more colorful relatives. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often during the summer months, and it requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
