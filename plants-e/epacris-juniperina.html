Epacris juniperina

Epacris juniperina, commonly known as the Juniper Bower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Epacris juniperina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
