Erysimum teppneri

Erysimum teppneri, commonly known as the Teppner's wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, brightly colored flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is aromatic and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
