Eleutherococcus leucorrhizus var. fulvescens

Eleutherococcus leucorrhizus var. fulvescens, commonly known as the yellow root.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, particularly China and Korea. The plant is characterized by its yellowish-brown rhizomes, which are the primary part used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.
