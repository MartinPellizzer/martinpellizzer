Eriocephala claviceps

Eriocephala claviceps, commonly known as the clustered false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a fibrous root system and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eriocephala claviceps is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
