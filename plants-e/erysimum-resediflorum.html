Erysimum resediflorum

Erysimum resediflorum, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, elongated leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it forms dense mats.

Erysimum resediflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
