Erythroxylum macrophyllum

Erythroxylum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces elliptical, woody capsules that contain the seeds.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
