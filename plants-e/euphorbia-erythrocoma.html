Euphorbia erythrocoma

Euphorbia erythrocoma, commonly known as the red-stemmed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are typically reddish in color, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually small and may be reduced or absent in some varieties.

Euphorbia erythrocoma is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.
