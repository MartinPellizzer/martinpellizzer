Eragrostis megastachya var. verrucosa

Eragrostis megastachya var. verrucosa, commonly known as the rough false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a rough, scaly texture. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy surface that contributes to its distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet cluster, which is typically reddish-brown in color.

This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in regions with low rainfall.
