Erythroxylum pyrifolium

Erythroxylum pyrifolium, commonly known as the red leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and green foliage, which is often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value rather than for its medicinal properties.
