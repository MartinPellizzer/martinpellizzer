Euphorbia fontqueriana

Euphorbia fontqueriana, commonly known as the Fontqueriana spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
