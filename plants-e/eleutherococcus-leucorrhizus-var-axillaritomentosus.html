Eleutherococcus leucorrhizus var. axillaritomentosus

Eleutherococcus leucorrhizus var. axillaritomentosus, commonly known as the white-root eleuthero, is a variant of the eleuthero plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white roots, which are often harvested for their medicinal properties. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its chemical composition, which includes various bioactive compounds used in traditional medicine.
