Euphorbia zamudioi

Euphorbia zamudioi, commonly known as the Zamudio's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
