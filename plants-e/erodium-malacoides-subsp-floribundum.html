Erodium malacoides subsp. floribundum

Erodium malacoides subsp.

floribundum, commonly known as the clustered bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often prostrate stems that produce clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine, silky hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
