Eragrostis brownii var. schistacea

Eragrostis brownii var. schistacea, commonly known as the schistaceous lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soils.

This grass is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
