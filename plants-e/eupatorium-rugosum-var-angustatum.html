Eupatorium rugosum var. angustatum

Eupatorium rugosum var. angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in flat-topped inflorescences, blooming in late summer.

It is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
