Eutrochium maculatum var. bruneri

Eutrochium maculatum var. bruneri, commonly known as showy blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, clustered purple to blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish or purplish hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It serves as a valuable nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
