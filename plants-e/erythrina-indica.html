Erythrina indica

Erythrina indica, commonly known as the Indian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom in dense clusters during the warmer months. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its distinctive pods, which contain seeds, are elongated and often covered in fine hairs.

Erythrina indica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
