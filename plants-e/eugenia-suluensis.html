Eugenia suluensis

Eugenia suluensis, commonly known as the Sulu myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface with a strong, spicy scent.

Eugenia suluensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
