Eragrostiella brachyphylla

Eragrostiella brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
