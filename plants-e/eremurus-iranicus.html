Eremurus iranicus

Eremurus iranicus, commonly known as the Iranian goat's beard, is a striking perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences that resemble a goat's beard, composed of numerous white to pale yellow florets. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater. It produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Eremurus iranicus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
