Euryops fruticulosa

Euryops fruticulosa, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling dandelion flowers, hence its common name. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Euryops fruticulosa is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
