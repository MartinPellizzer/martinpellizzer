Erythroxylum emarginatum var. angustifolium

Erythroxylum emarginatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved copal tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with elliptical, dark green leaves that have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody capsules containing small, black seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
