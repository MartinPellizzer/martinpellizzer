Erica mackayi f. plena

Erica mackayi f. plena, commonly known as the Mackay's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
