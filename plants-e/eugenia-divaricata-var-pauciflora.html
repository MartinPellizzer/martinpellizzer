Eugenia divaricata var. pauciflora

Eugenia divaricata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered roughshrub, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or white, blooming in the summer months.

The fruit is a small, reddish drupe that ripens in the fall and is often consumed by various bird species.
