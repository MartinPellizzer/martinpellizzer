Eupatorium trifidum

Eupatorium trifidum, commonly known as three-lobed thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are trifid, meaning they are divided into three parts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
