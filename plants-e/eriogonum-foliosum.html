Eriogonum foliosum

Eriogonum foliosum, commonly known as the leafy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often found in coastal and inland regions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eriogonum foliosum is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
