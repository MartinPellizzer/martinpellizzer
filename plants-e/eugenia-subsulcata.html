Eugenia subsulcata

Eugenia subsulcata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often gland-dotted. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that can be yellow or red when mature.

Eugenia subsulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage and edible fruits.
