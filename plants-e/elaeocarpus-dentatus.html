Elaeocarpus dentatus

Elaeocarpus dentatus, commonly known as the serrated elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and dense crown, making it suitable for ornamental purposes in tropical gardens.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in some regions.
