Erigeron bonariensis var. angustifolius

Erigeron bonariensis var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
