Eurya hellwigii

Eurya hellwigii, commonly known as the dwarf waxleaf eurya, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a waxy coating that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and as a ground cover in woodland settings.
