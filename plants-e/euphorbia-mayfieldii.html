Euphorbia mayfieldii

Euphorbia mayfieldii, commonly known as Mayfield's spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Euphorbia mayfieldii is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
