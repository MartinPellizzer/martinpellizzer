Erigeron persicus

Erigeron persicus, commonly known as Persian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, providing a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in summer, adding a delicate beauty to its natural habitat.
