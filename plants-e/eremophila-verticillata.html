Eremophila verticillata

Eremophila verticillata, commonly known as the mountain eremophila, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eremophila verticillata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens.
