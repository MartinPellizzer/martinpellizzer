Euphorbia standleyi

Euphorbia standleyi, commonly known as the Standley's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to store water in its stems.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
