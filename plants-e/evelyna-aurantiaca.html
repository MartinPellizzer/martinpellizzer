Evelyna aurantiaca

Evelyna aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Evelyna aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
