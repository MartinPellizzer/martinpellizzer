Eurya ponapensis

Eurya ponapensis, commonly known as the Caroline Islands eurya, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain several seeds.

Eurya ponapensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky areas.
