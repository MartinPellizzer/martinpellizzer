Eugenia caurensis

Eugenia caurensis, commonly known as the Ceará guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil, specifically the state of Ceará, and grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It produces small, edible fruits that are similar to guavas but have a distinct flavor.

Eugenia caurensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
