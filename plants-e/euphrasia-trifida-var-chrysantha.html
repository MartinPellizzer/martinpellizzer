Euphrasia trifida var. chrysantha

Euphrasia trifida var. chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.
