Epidendrum macasense

Epidendrum macasense, commonly known as the Macas orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment.

Epidendrum macasense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
