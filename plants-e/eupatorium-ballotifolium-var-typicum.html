Eupatorium ballotifolium var. typicum

Eupatorium ballotifolium var. typicum, commonly known as the white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Due to its historical use in herbal medicine, it is also known as the "herb of the sick.".
