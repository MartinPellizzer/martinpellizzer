Erigeron pulchellus var. typicus

Erigeron pulchellus var. typicus, commonly known as the showy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, producing daisy-like blooms in shades of white to pink.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
