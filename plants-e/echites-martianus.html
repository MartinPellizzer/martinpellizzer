Echites martianus

Echites martianus, commonly known as the Martian devil, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers during the rainy season, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. Its leaves are typically small and spiny, serving as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Echites martianus is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient example of botanical adaptation.
