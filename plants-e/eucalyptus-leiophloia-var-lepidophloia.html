Eucalyptus leiophloia var. lepidophloia

Eucalyptus leiophloia var. lepidophloia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. This plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is a shrub or small tree that often forms dense thickets, providing habitat for various wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional and modern applications.
