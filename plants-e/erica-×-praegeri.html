Erica × praegeri

Erica × praegeri, commonly known as the hybrid heath, is a species resulting from the cross between Erica cinerea and Erica tetralix.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink or white blossoms that attract pollinators. Erica × praegeri thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and moorland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
