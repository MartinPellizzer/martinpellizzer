Echium italicum subsp. siculum

Echium italicum subsp.

siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking, spires of blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
