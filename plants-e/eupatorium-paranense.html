Eupatorium paranense

Eupatorium paranense, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium paranense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
