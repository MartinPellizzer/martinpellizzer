Eugenia arthroopoda

Eugenia arthroopoda, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or the pink fairy apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers and red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant fragrance, while its fruits are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
