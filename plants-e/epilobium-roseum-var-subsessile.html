Epilobium roseum var. subsessile

Epilobium roseum var. subsessile, commonly known as the slender willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom in early summer.

The leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
