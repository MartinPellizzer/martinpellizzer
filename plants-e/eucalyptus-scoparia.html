Eucalyptus scoparia

Eucalyptus scoparia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spherical flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are typically clustered in groups. Eucalyptus scoparia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and resilience.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and for ornamental purposes.
