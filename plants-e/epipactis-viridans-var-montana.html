Epipactis viridans var. montana

Epipactis viridans var. montana, commonly known as the alpine helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as meadows and forests. It has a complex root system and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude environments.
