Eruca alba

Eruca alba, commonly known as white garden cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks. Its seeds are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to that of mustard.

Eruca alba is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
