Euphorbia terracina var. variicornis

Euphorbia terracina var. variicornis, commonly known as the variable-spined spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a thick, branched stem covered in spiny, glandular hairs. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-gray hue. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia.

This plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
