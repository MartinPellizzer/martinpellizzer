Erigeron subspicatus var. leiocarpus

Erigeron subspicatus var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the whiteleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its holarctic distribution and ecological role in supporting local wildlife make it an important species in its native range.
