Erigeron dusenii f. viridis

Erigeron dusenii f. viridis, commonly known as the greenleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

The flowers are usually pale blue to white, and they bloom in the summer months.
