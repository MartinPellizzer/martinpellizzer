Erycina hyalinobulbon

Erycina hyalinobulbon, commonly known as the glassy bulb orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent bulb-like structures that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid environments. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Erycina hyalinobulbon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
