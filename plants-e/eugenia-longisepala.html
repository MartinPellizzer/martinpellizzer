Eugenia longisepala

Eugenia longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, smooth, egg-shaped berry that turns red when mature.

Eugenia longisepala is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
