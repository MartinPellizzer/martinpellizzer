Eupatorium thyrsigerum

Eupatorium thyrsigerum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of small, blue to purple florets that attract pollinators.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
