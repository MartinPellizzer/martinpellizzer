Eremogone glaucescens

Eremogone glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green eremogone, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and features small, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are often borne on short pedicels. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Eremogone glaucescens is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it thrives with minimal water availability.
