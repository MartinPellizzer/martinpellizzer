Eucalyptus cephalocarpa

Eucalyptus cephalocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its bark is often flaky and varies in color from grey to brown.

Eucalyptus cephalocarpa is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in mallee woodlands.
