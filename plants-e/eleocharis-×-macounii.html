Eleocharis × macounii

Eleocharis × macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's spikerush, is a hybrid grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its morphology includes narrow leaves and a distinctive flowering structure that aids in wind pollination.

Eleocharis × macounii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
