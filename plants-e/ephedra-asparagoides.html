Ephedra asparagoides

Ephedra asparagoides, commonly known as the Chinese ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of China.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It contains alkaloids such as ephedrine, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also subject to regulation in many countries.
