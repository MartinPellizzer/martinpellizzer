Eugenia leytensis

Eugenia leytensis, commonly known as the Leyte guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
