Eriogonum tenellum var. ramosissimum

Eriogonum tenellum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the slender buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is part of the Polygonaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
