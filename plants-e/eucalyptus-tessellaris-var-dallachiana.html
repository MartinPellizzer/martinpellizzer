Eucalyptus tessellaris var. dallachiana

Eucalyptus tessellaris var. dallachiana, commonly known as the Dallachy's mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
