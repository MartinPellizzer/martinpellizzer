Eryngium loesenerianum

Eryngium loesenerianum, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive spiny, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by stiff, silver-green bracts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
