Euodia pubifolia

Euodia pubifolia, commonly known as the hairy euodia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its leaves and fruits contain essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Euodia pubifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
