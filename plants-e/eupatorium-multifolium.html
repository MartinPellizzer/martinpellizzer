Eupatorium multifolium

Eupatorium multifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Eupatorium multifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
