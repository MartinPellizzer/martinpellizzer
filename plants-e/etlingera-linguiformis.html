Etlingera linguiformis

Etlingera linguiformis, commonly known as the tongue-shaped torch ginger, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow, tongue-like leaves and striking red or orange inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The flowers are typically borne on tall, leafless stalks, adding visual interest to gardens and landscapes.
