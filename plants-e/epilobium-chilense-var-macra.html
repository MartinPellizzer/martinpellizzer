Epilobium chilense var. macra

Epilobium chilense var. macra, commonly known as Chilean willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions and along streams.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
