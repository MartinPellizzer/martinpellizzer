Erigeron acris subsp. kamtschaticus

Erigeron acris subsp.

kamtschaticus, commonly known as the Kamtschatic mountain fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features a basal rosette of silvery-gray, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
