Echeveria uxorum

Echeveria uxorum, commonly known as the Mexican rose or silver rose, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Echeveria uxorum is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of care.
