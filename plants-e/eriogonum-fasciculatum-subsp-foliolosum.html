Eriogonum fasciculatum subsp. foliolosum

Eriogonum fasciculatum subsp.

foliolosum, commonly known as the leafy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and small, often hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for pollinators and wildlife.
