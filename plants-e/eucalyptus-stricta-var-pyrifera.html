Eucalyptus stricta var. pyrifera

Eucalyptus stricta var. pyrifera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The flowers are usually small and clustered in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, often displaying shades of white or pink. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various native fauna.
