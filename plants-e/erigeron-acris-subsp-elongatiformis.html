Erigeron acris subsp. elongatiformis

Erigeron acris subsp.

elongatiformis, commonly known as the slenderleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a hairy texture, and its flowers are blue to purple in color, appearing in clusters.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
