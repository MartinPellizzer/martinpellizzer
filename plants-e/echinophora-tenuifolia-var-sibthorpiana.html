Echinophora tenuifolia var. sibthorpiana

Echinophora tenuifolia var. sibthorpiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a hardy, drought-resistant species in Mediterranean climates.
