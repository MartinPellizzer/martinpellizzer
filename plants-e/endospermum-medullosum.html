Endospermum medullosum

Endospermum medullosum, commonly known as the honeycomb tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hollow, and segmented trunk that resembles a honeycomb. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. Its unique morphology makes it an important component of the ecosystems in which it grows.

The endosperm of its seeds plays a crucial role in the plant's reproductive cycle.
