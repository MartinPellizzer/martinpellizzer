Echium confusum var. decipiens

Echium confusum var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive echium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
