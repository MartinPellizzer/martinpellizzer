Erigeron multiradiatus var. salicifolius

Erigeron multiradiatus var. salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, willow-like leaves and produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and as a component of native plant communities.
