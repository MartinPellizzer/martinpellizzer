Euphorbia neglecta

Euphorbia neglecta, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, handling the plant requires caution to avoid skin irritation.
