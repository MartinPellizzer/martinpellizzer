Epilobium calycinum

Epilobium calycinum, commonly known as the purple willow-herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, often disturbed habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its showy flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes and bloom in the summer months.

Epilobium calycinum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
