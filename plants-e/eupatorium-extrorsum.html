Eupatorium extrorsum

Eupatorium extrorsum, commonly known as wild chervil, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats. It has a long flowering period, usually from late spring to early fall, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Eupatorium extrorsum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
