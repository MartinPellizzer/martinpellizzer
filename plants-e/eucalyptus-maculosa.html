Eucalyptus maculosa

Eucalyptus maculosa, commonly known as the spotted gum, is a medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that often displays a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a rough, serrated edge. It produces small, globular clusters of white flowers that bloom during the spring and summer months.

The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in forests and woodlands.
