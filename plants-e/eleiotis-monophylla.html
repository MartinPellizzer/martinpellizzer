Eleiotis monophylla

Eleiotis monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf eleiotis, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its singular, large, waxy leaf that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eleiotis monophylla is considered a vulnerable species.
