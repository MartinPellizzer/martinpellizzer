Eucalyptus × rameliana var. trabutii

Eucalyptus × rameliana var. trabutii, commonly known as the Trabut's mallee, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white, grey, and green. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
