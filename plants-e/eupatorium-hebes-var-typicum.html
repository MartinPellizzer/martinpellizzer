Eupatorium hebes var. typicum

Eupatorium hebes var. typicum, commonly known as New Zealand thornapple, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
