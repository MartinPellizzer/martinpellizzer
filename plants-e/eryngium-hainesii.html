Eryngium hainesii

Eryngium hainesii, commonly known as Haines' thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features spiny, blue-green foliage and produces striking purple to violet flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Eryngium hainesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
