Eupatorium myrianthum

Eupatorium myrianthum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium myrianthum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
