Ehrharta longifolia

Ehrharta longifolia, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or blue-green inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.

Ehrharta longifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
