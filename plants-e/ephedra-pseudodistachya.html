Ephedra pseudodistachya

Ephedra pseudodistachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It contains ephedrine, a compound with historical use in traditional medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also of interest in pharmacological research.
