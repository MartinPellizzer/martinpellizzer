Eriobotrya cordata

Eriobotrya cordata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit, which resembles a pear, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flesh. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
