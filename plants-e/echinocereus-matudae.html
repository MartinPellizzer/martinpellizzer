Echinocereus matudae

Echinocereus matudae, commonly known as the Matudae cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water once established.

Echinocereus matudae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in xeriscaping.
