Eriosema zeyheri var. latifolium

Eriosema zeyheri var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
