Echinoschoenus beyrichii

Echinoschoenus beyrichii, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and spiky, bract-like leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune environments. Its morphology includes a compact growth habit with a basal rosette of leaves.

Echinoschoenus beyrichii is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.
