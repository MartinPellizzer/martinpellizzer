Eleotis

Eleotis, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its edible, leafy stems and leaves. The plant thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found in rice fields or along waterways. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for cultivation in many parts of Asia.

Eleotis is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its use in traditional medicine.
