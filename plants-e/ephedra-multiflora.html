Ephedra multiflora

Ephedra multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its stems contain ephedrine, a compound used in traditional medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also classified as a controlled substance in many countries.
