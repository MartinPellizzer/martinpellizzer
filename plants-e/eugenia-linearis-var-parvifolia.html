Eugenia linearis var. parvifolia

Eugenia linearis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
