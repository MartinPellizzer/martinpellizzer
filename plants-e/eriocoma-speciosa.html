Eriocoma speciosa

Eriocoma speciosa, commonly known as the showy eriocoma, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of pink or white, and bloom in the spring.

Eriocoma speciosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
