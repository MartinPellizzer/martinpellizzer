Eragrostis rogersii

Eragrostis rogersii, commonly known as Rogers' lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Rogers' lovegrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
