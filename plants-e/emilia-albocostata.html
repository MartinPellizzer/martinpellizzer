Emilia albocostata

Emilia albocostata, commonly known as the white-costly emilia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Emilia albocostata is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
