Echinocereus chloranthus var. cylindricus

Echinocereus chloranthus var. cylindricus, commonly known as the blue barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive bluish-green stem that often has a cylindrical shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stem. It is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is known for its slow growth and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
