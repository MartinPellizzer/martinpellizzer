Eupatorium reticulatum

Eupatorium reticulatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, slightly hairy foliage.
