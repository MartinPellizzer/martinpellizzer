Epilobium × rivulicola

Epilobium × rivulicola, commonly known as the riverweed or common willow herb, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, sometimes sprawling growth habit and is often found in moist habitats such as streambanks and wet meadows. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or reddish-purple flowers. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Epilobium × rivulicola is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators and serving as a food source for herbivorous insects.
