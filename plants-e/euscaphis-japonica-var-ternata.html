Euscaphis japonica var. ternata

Euscaphis japonica var. ternata, commonly known as Japanese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
