Eupatorium rugosum

Eupatorium rugosum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a serrated edge.

Eupatorium rugosum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
