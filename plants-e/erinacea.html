Erinacea

Erinacea, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the deserts and arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plants are characterized by their spiny, segmented stems and clusters of small, colorful flowers. Erinacea species typically thrive in full sun and well-draining soil, making them suitable for xeriscaping.

They are popular among cactus enthusiasts for their unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
