Erodium cicutarium var. viscidum

Erodium cicutarium var. viscidum, commonly known as sticky bedstraw, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in cultivated fields. It has a low-growing habit with slender stems and is often found in temperate climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
