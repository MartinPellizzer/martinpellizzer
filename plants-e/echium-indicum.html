Echium indicum

Echium indicum, commonly known as the common thimbleweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive blue to violet flower spike that resembles a thimble, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echium indicum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
