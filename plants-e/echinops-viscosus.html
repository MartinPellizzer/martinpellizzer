Echinops viscosus

Echinops viscosus, commonly known as the sticky globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Echinops viscosus is adapted to full sun and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.
