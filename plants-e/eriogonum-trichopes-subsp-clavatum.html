Eriogonum trichopes subsp. clavatum

Eriogonum trichopes subsp.

clavatum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a valuable plant for native landscaping.
