Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp. albertii

Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp.

albertii, commonly known as the Albert's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a small, spiny cactus that typically grows to a height of about 15 centimeters.

The plant features cylindrical stems covered with clusters of sharp, reddish-brown spines. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in the spring and lasting for several days.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
