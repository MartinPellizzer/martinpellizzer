Erysimum bocconei

Erysimum bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vivid splash of color to its natural surroundings.

Erysimum bocconei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
