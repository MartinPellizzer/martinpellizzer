Euodia viticina

Euodia viticina, commonly known as the Chinese yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Euodia viticina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
