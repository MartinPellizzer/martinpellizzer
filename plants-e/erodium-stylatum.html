Erodium stylatum

Erodium stylatum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pinkish to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has slender stems and oval, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
