Erigeron ciliatus

Erigeron ciliatus, commonly known as the woolly fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides, and it thrives in well-drained soils. The showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
