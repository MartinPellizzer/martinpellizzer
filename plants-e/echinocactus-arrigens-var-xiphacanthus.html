Echinocactus arrigens var. xiphacanthus

Echinocactus arrigens var. xiphacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
