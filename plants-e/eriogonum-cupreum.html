Eriogonum cupreum

Eriogonum cupreum, commonly known as the copper globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its globe-shaped, copper-colored bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush communities. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence.

Eriogonum cupreum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
