Eremophila neglecta

Eremophila neglecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry landscapes. It produces small, often pink or red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila neglecta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
