Eria ramosissima

Eria ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or undergrowth. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and can be slightly hairy, contributing to its common name.

Eria ramosissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
