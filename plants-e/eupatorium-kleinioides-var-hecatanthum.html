Eupatorium kleinioides var. hecatanthum

Eupatorium kleinioides var. hecatanthum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
