Eriope salviifolia

Eriope salviifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
