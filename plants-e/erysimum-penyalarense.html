Erysimum penyalarense

Erysimum penyalarense, commonly known as the Azores wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Azores Islands.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It features basal and stem leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape.

Erysimum penyalarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
