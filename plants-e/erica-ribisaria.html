Erica ribisaria

Erica ribisaria, commonly known as the purple heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Erica ribisaria thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
