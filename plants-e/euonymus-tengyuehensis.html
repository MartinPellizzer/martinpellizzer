Euonymus tengyuehensis

Euonymus tengyuehensis, commonly known as the Tengyuehensis spindle tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often reddish, bark and its alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and seasonal color changes.
