Eucalyptus volcanica

Eucalyptus volcanica, commonly known as the Mount Stromlo eucalypt, is a species native to Australia, specifically found in the ACT region.

It is a tall tree with smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin, fibrous strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow.

The plant is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
