Eria cooperi

Eria cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's eria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Eria cooperi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
