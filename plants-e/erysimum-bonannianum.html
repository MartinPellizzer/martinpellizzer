Erysimum bonannianum

Erysimum bonannianum, commonly known as the Bonannia wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum bonannianum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
