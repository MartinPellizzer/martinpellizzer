Euonymus japonicus var. macrophyllus

Euonymus japonicus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as Japanese spurge or large-leaf euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, inflated seed capsules that persist through the winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
