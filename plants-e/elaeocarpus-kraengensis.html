Elaeocarpus kraengensis

Elaeocarpus kraengensis, commonly known as the Kraengensis elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
