Espeletia × coloradarum

Espeletia × coloradarum, commonly known as the Colorado Giant Heath, is a hybrid plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
