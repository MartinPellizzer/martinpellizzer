Emmenanthe pusilla

Emmenanthe pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf pincushion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of tiny, pink to white flowers that resemble miniature pincushions, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiny bracts around its flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.
