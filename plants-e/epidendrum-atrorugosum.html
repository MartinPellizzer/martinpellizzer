Epidendrum atrorugosum

Epidendrum atrorugosum, commonly known as the black-veined epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with prominent black veins, and its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It produces long, pendulous inflorescences with numerous blooms, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

Epidendrum atrorugosum is relatively easy to care for, requiring bright indirect light and moderate watering.
