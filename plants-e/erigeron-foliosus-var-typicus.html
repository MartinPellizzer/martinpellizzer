Erigeron foliosus var. typicus

Erigeron foliosus var. typicus, commonly known as the leafy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. Its flowers are blue to purple in color and bloom in the summer months.

It is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
