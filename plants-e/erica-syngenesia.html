Erica syngenesia

Erica syngenesia, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Erica syngenesia is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is highly tolerant of coastal salt spray.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various coastal wildlife.
