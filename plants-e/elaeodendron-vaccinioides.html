Elaeodendron vaccinioides

Elaeodendron vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Elaeodendron vaccinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its compact growth habit.
