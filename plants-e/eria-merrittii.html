Eria merrittii

Eria merrittii, commonly known as the Merritt's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread gradually over time. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it prefers shaded, moist environments.

Eria merrittii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
