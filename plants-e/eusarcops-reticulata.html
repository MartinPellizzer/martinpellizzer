Eusarcops reticulata

Eusarcops reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, net-like vein patterns on its leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Eusarcops reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
