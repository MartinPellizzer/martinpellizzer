Eriophyllum oppositifolium

Eriophyllum oppositifolium, commonly known as the Coast Goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves arranged oppositely along its stems. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, forming dense clusters at the tips of the branches.

Eriophyllum oppositifolium is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
