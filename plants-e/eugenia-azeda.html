Eugenia azeda

Eugenia azeda, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, dark purple fruits that are highly valued for their sweet, tangy flavor and nutritional content. Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant and a source of natural compounds used in traditional medicine.

The species is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
