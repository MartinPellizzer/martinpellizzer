Epilobium novae-zeylandiae

Epilobium novae-zeylandiae, commonly known as New Zealand willow, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, cool environments such as stream banks and wetlands. The plant features slender, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and usually have five petals, with a pink to reddish hue.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
