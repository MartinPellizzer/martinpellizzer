Echinopsis simplex

Echinopsis simplex, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, adding to its striking appearance. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil surface.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
