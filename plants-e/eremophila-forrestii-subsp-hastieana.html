Eremophila forrestii subsp. hastieana

Eremophila forrestii subsp.

hastieana, commonly known as the Northern River Red Gum, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
