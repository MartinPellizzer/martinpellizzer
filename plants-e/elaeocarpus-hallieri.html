Elaeocarpus hallieri

Elaeocarpus hallieri, commonly known as the New Zealand hakea, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp. It is often found in coastal and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Elaeocarpus hallieri is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in local ecosystems.
