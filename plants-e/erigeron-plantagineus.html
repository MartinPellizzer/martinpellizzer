Erigeron plantagineus

Erigeron plantagineus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and roadsides.

Erigeron plantagineus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
