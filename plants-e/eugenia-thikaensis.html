Eugenia thikaensis

Eugenia thikaensis, commonly known as the East African myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small white or pink flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Eugenia thikaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local communities.
