Eupatorium coelocaule

Eupatorium coelocaule, commonly known as the slender eupatorium, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, often displaying a range of colors from white to purple.

Eupatorium coelocaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
