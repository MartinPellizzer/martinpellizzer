Enkianthus subsessilis var. angustifolius

Enkianthus subsessilis var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, which bloom in early spring. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
