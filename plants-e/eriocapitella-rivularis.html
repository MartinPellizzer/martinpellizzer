Eriocapitella rivularis

Eriocapitella rivularis, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows near water sources such as rivers and streams. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Eriocapitella rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
