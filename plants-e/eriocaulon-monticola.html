Eriocaulon monticola

Eriocaulon monticola, commonly known as the mountain marsh grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in wetland and swampy habitats. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are tiny and clustered in spikelets.

Eriocaulon monticola plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
