Erigeron pyrrhopappus

Erigeron pyrrhopappus, commonly known as the redstem fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
