Eurya subintegra var. pubisepala

Eurya subintegra var. pubisepala, commonly known as the pubisepal eurya, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers with hairy sepals, which are characteristic of its varietal name. The berries are typically red or orange when mature and are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
