Erica chamissonis var. polyantha

Erica chamissonis var. polyantha, commonly known as the Polyantha heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
