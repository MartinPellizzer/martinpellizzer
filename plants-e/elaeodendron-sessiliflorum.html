Elaeodendron sessiliflorum

Elaeodendron sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flower elaeodendron, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and distinctive foliage composed of thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the rainy season. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in terms of adaptation to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
