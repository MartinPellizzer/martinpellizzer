Eugenia stenosepaloides

Eugenia stenosepaloides, commonly known as the narrow-sepal Eugenia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
