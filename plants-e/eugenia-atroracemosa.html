Eugenia atroracemosa

Eugenia atroracemosa, commonly known as black rafia or black rafia tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northern Australia and is characterized by its dark, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round, black fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and wet tropical regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian cultures.
