Eugenia bissei

Eugenia bissei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red cherry tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Eugenia bissei thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural gardens.
