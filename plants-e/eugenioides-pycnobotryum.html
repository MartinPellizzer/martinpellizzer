Eugenioides pycnobotryum

Eugenioides pycnobotryum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathland or scrub environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate salt spray and drought conditions.
