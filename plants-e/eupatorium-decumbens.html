Eupatorium decumbens

Eupatorium decumbens, commonly known as wild blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium decumbens is often found in wetlands and is known for its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
