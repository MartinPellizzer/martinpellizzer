Erica lanata

Erica lanata, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica lanata is well adapted to dry conditions and is a popular plant in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
