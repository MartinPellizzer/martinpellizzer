Eugenia grossa

Eugenia grossa, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Eugenia grossa thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative shrub.
