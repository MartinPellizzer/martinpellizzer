Eritrichium baicalense

Eritrichium baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal Eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Eritrichium baicalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
