Eugenia spathophylla

Eugenia spathophylla, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fruit.
