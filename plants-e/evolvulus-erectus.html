Evolvulus erectus

Evolvulus erectus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate to ascending growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, hence its common name.
