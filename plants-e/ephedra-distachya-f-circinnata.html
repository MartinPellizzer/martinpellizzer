Ephedra distachya f. circinnata

Ephedra distachya f. circinnata, commonly known as the curled-stem ephedra, is a unique variant of the ephedra plant characterized by its distinct, spirally twisted stems.

This plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Its small, scale-like leaves are typically reduced to minute structures, and it produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is notable for its ability to survive in harsh environments due to its efficient water conservation mechanisms.

It is also of interest in botanical studies for its morphological adaptations to extreme climates.
