Eucalyptus rudis subsp. cratyantha

Eucalyptus rudis subsp.

cratyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically small and white or pink, arranged in clusters, and are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
