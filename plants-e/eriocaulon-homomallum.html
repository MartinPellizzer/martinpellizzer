Eriocaulon homomallum

Eriocaulon homomallum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh grass, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically near the base of the plant. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
