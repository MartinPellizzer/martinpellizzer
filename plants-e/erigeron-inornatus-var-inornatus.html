Erigeron inornatus var. inornatus

Erigeron inornatus var. inornatus, commonly known as the slender fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has slender stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing a showy display of white to pale yellow petals.
