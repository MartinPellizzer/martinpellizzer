Eremostachys nuda f. simplex

Eremostachys nuda f. simplex, commonly known as the simple leaf eremostachys, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and linear, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

This variety is prized in xeriscaping for its low maintenance and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
