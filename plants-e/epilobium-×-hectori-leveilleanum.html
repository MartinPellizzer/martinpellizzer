Epilobium × hectori-leveilleanum

Epilobium × hectori-leveilleanum, commonly known as the Scottish heather, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is native to the British Isles and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats.
