Eremophila macdonnellii var. macrocarpa

Eremophila macdonnellii var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Macdonnell Emu Bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its grey-green foliage and showy pink or red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for low-water gardens. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
