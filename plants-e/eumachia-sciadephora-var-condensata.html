Eumachia sciadephora var. condensata

Eumachia sciadephora var. condensata, commonly known as the dense-leaved mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often densely packed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds. It is characterized by its hemiparasitic nature, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from the host while also performing photosynthesis.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
