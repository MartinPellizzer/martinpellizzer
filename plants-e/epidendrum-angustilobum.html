Epidendrum angustilobum

Epidendrum angustilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum angustilobum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in tropical gardens.
