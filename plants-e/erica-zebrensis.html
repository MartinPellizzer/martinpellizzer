Erica zebrensis

Erica zebrensis, commonly known as the zebra heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with striking silver and green variegation. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red during the summer months. Erica zebrensis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
