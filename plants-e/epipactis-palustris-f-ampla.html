Epipactis palustris f. ampla

Epipactis palustris f. ampla, commonly known as the broad-leaved marsh helleborine, is a herbaceous perennial orchid native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It features dark green, broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and marshes. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
