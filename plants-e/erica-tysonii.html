Erica tysonii

Erica tysonii, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western coast of Africa.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes other heath and heather plants.

Erica tysonii is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
