Eucalyptus elata

Eucalyptus elata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs or small trees with smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a bluish-green hue and a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular clusters of white or cream flowers, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
