Eriosyce curvispina subsp. aconcaguensis

Eriosyce curvispina subsp.

aconcaguensis, commonly known as the Aconcagua torch cactus, is a species native to the coastal regions of Chile. It is characterized by its columnar stems that are often covered in dense, white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in cactus gardens.
