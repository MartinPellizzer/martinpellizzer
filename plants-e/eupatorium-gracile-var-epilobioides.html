Eupatorium gracile var. epilobioides

Eupatorium gracile var. epilobioides, commonly known as narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in moist, rich soils along streambanks and in woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
