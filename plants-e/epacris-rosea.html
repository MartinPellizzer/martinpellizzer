Epacris rosea

Epacris rosea, commonly known as the pink epacris, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive bilabiate (two-lip) structure, and are arranged in spikes along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

Epacris rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in native gardens.
