Erigeron koidzumii

Erigeron koidzumii, commonly known as the Koidzumi's blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silver-gray foliage with hairy texture, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Erigeron koidzumii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
