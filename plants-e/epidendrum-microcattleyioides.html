Epidendrum microcattleyioides

Epidendrum microcattleyioides, commonly known as the miniature cattleya orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a compact, sympodial growth habit with fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically in late spring or early summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its delicate appearance and ease of care, it is well-suited for beginners in the world of orchid growing.
