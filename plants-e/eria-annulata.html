Eria annulata

Eria annulata, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow. Eria annulata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
