Eupatorium lanigerum var. typicum

Eupatorium lanigerum var. typicum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to support pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
