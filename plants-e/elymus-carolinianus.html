Elymus carolinianus

Elymus carolinianus, commonly known as Carolina wildrye, is a tall, perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to six feet. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated.
