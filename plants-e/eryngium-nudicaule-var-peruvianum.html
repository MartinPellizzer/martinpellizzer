Eryngium nudicaule var. peruvianum

Eryngium nudicaule var. peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-to-purple flower heads that resemble a thistle, surrounded by a series of bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
