Ericameria humilis

Ericameria humilis, commonly known as the low bush ericameria, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, yellowish-green leaves and produces fluffy, yellow to golden-orange flowers in the spring. It is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

Ericameria humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
