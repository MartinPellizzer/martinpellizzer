Epidendrum leucochilum

Epidendrum leucochilum, commonly known as the white-lipped epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, often pendulous inflorescences and flowers that feature a distinctive white lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
