Ecbolium linneanum

Ecbolium linneanum, commonly known as the yellow tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are small, linear, and often covered in fine hairs. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, water-scarce conditions.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
