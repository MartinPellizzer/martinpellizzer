Ericoides hibbertium

Ericoides hibbertium, commonly known as the Hibbert's heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ericoides hibbertium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
