Echinops chodzha-mumini

Echinops chodzha-mumini, commonly known as the Afghan thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a globe. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Echinops chodzha-mumini is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
