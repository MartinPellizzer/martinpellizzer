Erythranthe purpurea

Erythranthe purpurea, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the flowers are pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Erythranthe purpurea is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
