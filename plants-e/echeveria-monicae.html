Echeveria monicae

Echeveria monicae, commonly known as "Monica's Echeveria," is a species of succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers on tall stalks during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and attractive appearance, it is often used in horticultural displays and as a decorative plant.
