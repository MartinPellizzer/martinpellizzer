Erodium cicutarium var. primulaceum

Erodium cicutarium var. primulaceum, commonly known as the common stork's bill, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has slender, branched stems and pinnately trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its fruits are distinctive, resembling small, curved bills, which give it its common name.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
