Euphorbia cooperi var. calidicola

Euphorbia cooperi var. calidicola, commonly known as the Cooper's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Euphorbia cooperi var. calidicola is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
