Eulophia exilis

Eulophia exilis, commonly known as the slender eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, slender flower spike that bears a single, fragrant flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive, lip-like structure.

Eulophia exilis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
