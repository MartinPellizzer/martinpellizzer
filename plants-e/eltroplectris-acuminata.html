Eltroplectris acuminata

Eltroplectris acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies such as ponds, streams, and swamps. The plant has a slender, leafy stem with elongated, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Eltroplectris acuminata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its specialized pollination mechanisms and adaptations to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
