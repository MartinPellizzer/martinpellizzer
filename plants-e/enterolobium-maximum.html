Enterolobium maximum

Enterolobium maximum, commonly known as the Brazilian bean tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a large, fast-growing legume that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional dishes. Its leaves are compound and provide shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental and agroforestry purposes.

The plant also has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
