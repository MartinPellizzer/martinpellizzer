Endomelas alata

Endomelas alata, commonly known as the winged balm, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features slender, upright stems with distinctive wing-like structures along the leaf midribs, giving it its common name. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
