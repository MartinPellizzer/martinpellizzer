Echinops sphaerocephalus var. koelzii

Echinops sphaerocephalus var. koelzii, commonly known as the globular thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that are densely packed with small, white flowers. The plant has silver-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
