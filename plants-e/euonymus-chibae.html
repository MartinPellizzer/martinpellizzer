Euonymus chibae

Euonymus chibae, commonly known as the Japanese spurge or Japanese euonymus, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is often cultivated with caution.
