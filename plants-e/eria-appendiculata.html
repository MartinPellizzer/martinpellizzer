Eria appendiculata

Eria appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are linear and often covered with a waxy cuticle, which helps in moisture retention.

Eria appendiculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
