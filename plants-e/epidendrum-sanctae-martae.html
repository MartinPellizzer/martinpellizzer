Epidendrum sanctae-martae

Epidendrum sanctae-martae, commonly known as the Santa Marta orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its flowering season occurs during the wet season, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty and unique floral characteristics.

It is a relatively rare species, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
