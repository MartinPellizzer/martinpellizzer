Eryngium nudicaule var. pinnatifidum

Eryngium nudicaule var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts. The plant has narrow, pinnatifid (deeply lobed) leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
