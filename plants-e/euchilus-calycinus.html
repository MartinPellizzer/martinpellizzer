Euchilus calycinus

Euchilus calycinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Euchilus calycinus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
