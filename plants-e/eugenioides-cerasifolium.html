Eugenioides cerasifolium

Eugenioides cerasifolium, commonly known as the cherry-leafed eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that resemble those of cherry trees, hence its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
