Encyclia fragrans

Encyclia fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette formation.

Encyclia fragrans thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.
