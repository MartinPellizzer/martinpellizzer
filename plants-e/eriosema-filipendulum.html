Eriosema filipendulum

Eriosema filipendulum, commonly known as the silverleaf eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eriosema filipendulum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
