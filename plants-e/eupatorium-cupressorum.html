Eupatorium cupressorum

Eupatorium cupressorum, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has aromatic, hairy leaves that are dark green in color. Its showy, purple-tinged flower heads bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators.

The plant is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
