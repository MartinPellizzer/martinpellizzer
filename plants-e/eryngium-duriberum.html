Eryngium duriberum

Eryngium duriberum, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by bracts resembling a crown. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eryngium duriberum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
