Epipactis royleana

Epipactis royleana, commonly known as the Royle's helleborine, is an orchid species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Epipactis royleana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
