Erianthus purpurascens

Erianthus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple reed grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, purple-tinted inflorescence. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and landscaping projects.
