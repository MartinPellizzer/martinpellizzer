Eulophia pocsii

Eulophia pocsii, commonly known as the Pocsii orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

Eulophia pocsii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
