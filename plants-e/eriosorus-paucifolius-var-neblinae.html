Eriosorus paucifolius var. neblinae

Eriosorus paucifolius var. neblinae, commonly known as the Neblina fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, leathery fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the cool, misty conditions of its high-altitude habitat. Its unique morphology and restricted distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful protection to ensure its survival.
