Echinopsis reichenbachiana

Echinopsis reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, spiny stems that often display a variety of colors, including green, blue, and purple. The plant produces white or pink flowers during the warmer months, typically in the summer. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
