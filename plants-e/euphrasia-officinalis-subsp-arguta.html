Euphrasia officinalis subsp. arguta

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

arguta, commonly known as the hairy eyebright, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and shaded areas, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features small, yellowish-orange flowers with prominent yellow markings, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces that give it its common name. Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

arguta is valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat eye ailments and other inflammatory conditions.
