Echinocereus coccineus subsp. rosei

Echinocereus coccineus subsp.

rosei, commonly known as the roseate hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring red to pink spines and a dense covering of white to pale blue hairs.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid climates.
