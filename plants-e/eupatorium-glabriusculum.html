Eupatorium glabriusculum

Eupatorium glabriusculum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine, silky hairs.

It is often used in traditional medicine and attracts various pollinators due to its flowering structure.
