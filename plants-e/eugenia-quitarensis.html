Eugenia quitarensis

Eugenia quitarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia quitarensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
