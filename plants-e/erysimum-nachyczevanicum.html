Erysimum nachyczevanicum

Erysimum nachyczevanicum, commonly known as the Armenian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
