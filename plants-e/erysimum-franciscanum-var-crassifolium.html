Erysimum franciscanum var. crassifolium

Erysimum franciscanum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to California.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, a characteristic adaptation to its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
