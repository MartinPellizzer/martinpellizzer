Euphorbia wangii

Euphorbia wangii, commonly known as the Wang's spurge, is a succulent plant native to China.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are its primary ornamental feature. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
