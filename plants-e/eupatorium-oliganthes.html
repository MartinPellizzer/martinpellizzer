Eupatorium oliganthes

Eupatorium oliganthes, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to moist to wet soils.

Eupatorium oliganthes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
