Eragrostis elegantula

Eragrostis elegantula, commonly known as slender false brome, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
