Epacris tomentosa

Epacris tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces showy spikes of pink or red tubular flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, particularly in regions with a temperate climate.
