Elaeocarpus obovatus subsp. obovatus

Elaeocarpus obovatus subsp.

obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on the upper surface.

The tree produces small, white flowers followed by distinctive, woody fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit is typically slow, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
