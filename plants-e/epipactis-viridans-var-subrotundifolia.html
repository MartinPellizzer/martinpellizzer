Epipactis viridans var. subrotundifolia

Epipactis viridans var. subrotundifolia, commonly known as the green helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive lip that is usually darker in color. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly rounded, broad leaves compared to the typical form of E. viridans.
