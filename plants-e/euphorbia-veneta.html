Euphorbia veneta

Euphorbia veneta, commonly known as the Venetian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. Euphorbia veneta is known for its succulent leaves and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
