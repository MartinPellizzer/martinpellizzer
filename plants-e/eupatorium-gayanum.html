Eupatorium gayanum

Eupatorium gayanum, commonly known as the Sri Lankan boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium gayanum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
