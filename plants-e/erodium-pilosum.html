Erodium pilosum

Erodium pilosum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
