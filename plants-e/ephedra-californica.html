Ephedra californica

Ephedra californica, commonly known as the California ephedra, is a dioecious shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, scale-like leaves and produces yellowish-green cones that contain the plant's characteristic alkaloids. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert regions. Its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in height.

Ephedra californica is notable for its medicinal uses and historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
