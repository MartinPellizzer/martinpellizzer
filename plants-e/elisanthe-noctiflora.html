Elisanthe noctiflora

Elisanthe noctiflora, commonly known as the night blooming elisanthe, is a flowering plant characterized by its unique nocturnal blooming habit.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to arid regions of South America. The plant features striking, showy flowers that open at dusk and close by dawn, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its foliage is typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in its native environment.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a tubular shape that facilitates pollination by specific insects.
