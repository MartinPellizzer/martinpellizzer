Eugenia andamanica

Eugenia andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
