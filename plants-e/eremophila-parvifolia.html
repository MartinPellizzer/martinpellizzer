Eremophila parvifolia

Eremophila parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to red tubular flowers, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suited to dry conditions.

Eremophila parvifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
