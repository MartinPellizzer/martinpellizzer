Eupatorium theifolium var. subellipticum

Eupatorium theifolium var. subellipticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false dragonhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
