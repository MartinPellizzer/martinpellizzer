Eufragia vivianii

Eufragia vivianii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
