Eragrostis kossinskyi

Eragrostis kossinskyi, commonly known as the Kossinsky's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
