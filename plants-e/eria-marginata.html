Eria marginata

Eria marginata, commonly known as the marginated eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinct greenish hue at the base. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eria marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
