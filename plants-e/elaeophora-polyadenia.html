Elaeophora polyadenia

Elaeophora polyadenia, commonly known as the silky milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Elaeophora polyadenia is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
