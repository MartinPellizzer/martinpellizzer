Eriobotrya buisanensis

Eriobotrya buisanensis, commonly known as the Bisan apple, is a species of evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces edible, yellowish-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in subtropical regions.
