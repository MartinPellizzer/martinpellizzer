Echinocereus floresii

Echinocereus floresii, commonly known as the Floresii cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its adaptations include reduced leaf surfaces and a deep root system to conserve water.

Echinocereus floresii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
