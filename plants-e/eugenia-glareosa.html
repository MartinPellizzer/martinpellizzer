Eugenia glareosa

Eugenia glareosa, commonly known as the shining eugenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that are often fragrant. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Eugenia glareosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
