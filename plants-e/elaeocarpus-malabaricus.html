Elaeocarpus malabaricus

Elaeocarpus malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense evergreen forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited uses in traditional medicine.
