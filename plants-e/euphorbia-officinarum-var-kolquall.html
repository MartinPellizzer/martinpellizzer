Euphorbia officinarum var. kolquall

Euphorbia officinarum var. kolquall, commonly known as the Kolquall euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
