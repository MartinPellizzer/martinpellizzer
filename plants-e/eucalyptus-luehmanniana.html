Eucalyptus luehmanniana

Eucalyptus luehmanniana, commonly known as the yellow box, is a medium to large deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey to yellowish bark that peels in thin strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, typically in spring, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
