Earlia

Earlia, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Earlia in the family Acanthaceae, and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Earlia blooms in spring and summer, producing clusters of bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
