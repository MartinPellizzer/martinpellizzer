Echinocactus dadakii

Echinocactus dadakii, commonly known as the Mexican sunburst cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems that form a spherical shape, often with a yellowish or greenish hue. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short, hairy stems. Echinocactus dadakii is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is a slow-growing cactus that requires minimal water and full sunlight to thrive.
