Echinocereus sarissophorus

Echinocereus sarissophorus, commonly known as the long-spine hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny areoles, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stem structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its adaptations, such as reduced leaves and thick stems, help it conserve water in harsh desert conditions.
