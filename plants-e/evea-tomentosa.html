Evea tomentosa

Evea tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Evea tomentosa is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
