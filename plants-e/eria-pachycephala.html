Eria pachycephala

Eria pachycephala, commonly known as the thick-headed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. This plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive thickened base on the petals.

The plant is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
