Elaeocarpus acmosepalus

Elaeocarpus acmosepalus, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, woody drupes.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
