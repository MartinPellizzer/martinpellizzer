Epicampes lanata

Epicampes lanata, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, white seeds.

Epicampes lanata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
