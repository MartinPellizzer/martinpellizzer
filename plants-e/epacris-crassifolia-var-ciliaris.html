Epacris crassifolia var. ciliaris

Epacris crassifolia var. ciliaris, commonly known as the woolly epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces spikes of tubular, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
