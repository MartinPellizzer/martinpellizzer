Eugenia contrerasii

Eugenia contrerasii, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or white cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia contrerasii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
