Elaeocarpus takolensis

Elaeocarpus takolensis, commonly known as the Khammuen elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Cambodia and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruits are woody and contain a single seed, which is an adaptation for dispersal by animals.

The species plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
