Eruca vesicaria subsp. pinnatifida

Eruca vesicaria subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rocket, is a biennial or perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, pinnately divided leaves that are hairy and have a peppery aroma.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

This species is valued both as a wild edible and for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
