Echeveria longissima subsp. longissima

Echeveria longissima subsp.

longissima, commonly known as the Dragon Head Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico. It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are pointed and have a waxy texture.

The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its compact form.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
