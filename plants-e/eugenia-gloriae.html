Eugenia gloriae

Eugenia gloriae, commonly known as the Glory Eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Eugenia gloriae make it a popular choice for gardeners and horticulturists in tropical and subtropical regions.
