Erica cupressifolia

Erica cupressifolia, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, particularly in Namibia and South Africa. This evergreen shrub typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring dense, compact foliage with needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer.

Erica cupressifolia is well adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.
