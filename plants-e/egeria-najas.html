Egeria najas

Egeria najas, commonly known as water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. Egeria najas is often found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
