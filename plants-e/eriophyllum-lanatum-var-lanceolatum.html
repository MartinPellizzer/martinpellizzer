Eriophyllum lanatum var. lanceolatum

Eriophyllum lanatum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved common goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are linear and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting pollinators.
