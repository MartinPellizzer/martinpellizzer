Euphrasia parviflora var. glabrescens

Euphrasia parviflora var. glabrescens, commonly known as the small-flowered eyebright, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters and features slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent dark veins, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
