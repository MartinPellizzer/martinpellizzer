Eupatorium urolepis

Eupatorium urolepis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, lavender-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower structure.
