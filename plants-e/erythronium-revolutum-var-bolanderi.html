Erythronium revolutum var. bolanderi

Erythronium revolutum var. bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's fawn lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a reddish tinge. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance in suitable growing conditions.
