Eryngium bupleuroides

Eryngium bupleuroides, commonly known as blue eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

Eryngium bupleuroides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for pollinators.
