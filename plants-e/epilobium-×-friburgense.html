Epilobium × friburgense

Epilobium × friburgense, commonly known as the Friburg velvetleaf, is a hybrid species within the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Epilobium × friburgense is often found in temperate regions of Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
