Elatine hexandra subsp. macropoda

Elatine hexandra subsp.

macropoda, commonly known as the broad-leaf water starwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and wetlands.

The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, with four petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and is often used as an indicator of water quality.
