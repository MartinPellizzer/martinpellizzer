Eucalyptus moorei var. latiuscula

Eucalyptus moorei var. latiuscula, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Stringy Gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves that are often ribbed and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that typically range in color from white to pink, followed by small, woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
