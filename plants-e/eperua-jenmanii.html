Eperua jenmanii

Eperua jenmanii, commonly known as the Jenman's eperua, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of northern Australia and parts of New Guinea.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The tree produces pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional crafts and construction.

Eperua jenmanii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
