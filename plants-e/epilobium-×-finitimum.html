Epilobium × finitimum

Epilobium × finitimum, commonly known as the Scottish heath.

It is a hybrid species native to the British Isles, characterized by its erect growth habit and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Epilobium × finitimum plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various invertebrates.
