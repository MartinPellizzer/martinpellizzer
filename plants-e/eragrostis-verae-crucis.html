Eragrostis verae-crucis

Eragrostis verae-crucis, commonly known as the cross-veined lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with prominent cross-veins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
