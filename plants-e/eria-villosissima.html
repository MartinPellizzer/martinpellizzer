Eria villosissima

Eria villosissima, commonly known as the hairy eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
