Eriogonum umbellatum subsp. ferrissii

Eriogonum umbellatum subsp.

ferrissii, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It has basal and sometimes upright leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to dry, sunny environments and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.
