Eryngium × hybridum

Eryngium × hybridum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by silver-green bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and well-drained soils, exhibiting drought tolerance. Its foliage consists of blue-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eryngium × hybridum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in sunny, dry conditions.
