Exomicrum grandifolium

Exomicrum grandifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf exomicrum, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
