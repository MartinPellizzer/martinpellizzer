Eriocaulon argenteum

Eriocaulon argenteum, commonly known as silver spikegrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
