Erythroxylum firmum

Erythroxylum firmum, commonly known as the red mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red flowers and hairy, broad leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences and are known for their vibrant color and ornamental value.

Erythroxylum firmum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
