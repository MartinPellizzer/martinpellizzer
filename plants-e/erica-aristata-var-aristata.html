Erica aristata var. aristata

Erica aristata var. aristata, commonly known as the upright heath, is a dwarf shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features compact, evergreen foliage with needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This hardy plant is well adapted to coastal conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
