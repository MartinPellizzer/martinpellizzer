Erysimum pirinicum

Erysimum pirinicum, commonly known as the Pirin Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Erysimum pirinicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
