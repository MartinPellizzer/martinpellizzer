Eugenioides obtusum

Eugenioides obtusum, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
