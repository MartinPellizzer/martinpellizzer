Emilia

Emilia, commonly known as Emilia campanulata, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in clusters, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Emilia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
