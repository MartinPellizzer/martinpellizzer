Eupatorium phanioides

Eupatorium phanioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swamps or along stream banks.

Eupatorium phanioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
