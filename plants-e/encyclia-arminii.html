Encyclia arminii

Encyclia arminii, commonly known as the Armin's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces a single, erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a distinct greenish-blue lip.

Encyclia arminii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
