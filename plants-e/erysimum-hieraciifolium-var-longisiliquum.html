Erysimum hieraciifolium var. longisiliquum

Erysimum hieraciifolium var. longisiliquum, commonly known as the long silique wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, slender pods called silique, which contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
