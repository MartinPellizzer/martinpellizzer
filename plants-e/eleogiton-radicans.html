Eleogiton radicans

Eleogiton radicans, commonly known as the creeping elephant's foot, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months. Its root system is extensive, helping it anchor in rocky or sandy soils.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and unique appearance.
