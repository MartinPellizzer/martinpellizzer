Excoecaria bicalcarata

Excoecaria bicalcarata, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, green, often waxy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its roots are the primary part used, containing a starchy substance that can be processed into a flour-like powder.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
