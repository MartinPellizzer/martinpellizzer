Erianthus speciosus

Erianthus speciosus, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Erianthus speciosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize wetland soils.
