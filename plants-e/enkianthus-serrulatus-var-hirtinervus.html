Enkianthus serrulatus var. hirtinervus

Enkianthus serrulatus var. hirtinervus, commonly known as the hairy-veined enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves with prominent, hairy veins. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and showy floral display.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
