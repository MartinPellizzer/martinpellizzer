Erica unicolor subsp. georgensis

Erica unicolor subsp.

georgensis, commonly known as the Georgian heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves.

The plant produces vivid pink or red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in short inflorescences. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
