Elytrigia pontica subsp. turcica

Elytrigia pontica subsp.

turcica, commonly known as the Turkish bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland habitats.

The plant has slender, creeping stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually clustered in dense, cylindrical clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for erosion control.
