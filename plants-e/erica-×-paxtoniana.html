Erica × paxtoniana

Erica × paxtoniana, commonly known as the Cross-leaved Heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Erica × paxtoniana is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
