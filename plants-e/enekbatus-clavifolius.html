Enekbatus clavifolius

Enekbatus clavifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed enekbatus, is a species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Due to its resilience and unique adaptations, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
