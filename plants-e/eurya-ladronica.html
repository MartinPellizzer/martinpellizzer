Eurya ladronica

Eurya ladronica, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs and small trees. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright blue berries in the fall. These berries are edible for birds and some mammals but are generally not consumed by humans.

The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a slightly glossy texture.
