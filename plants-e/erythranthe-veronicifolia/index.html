Erythranthe Veronicifolia

Erythranthe veronicifolia is a striking member of the Brassicaceae family known for its vibrant purple flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of western North America, this plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Its common name, "veronicifolia," refers to its leaf shape resembling that of the shrub veronica. The plant is often found in alpine meadows and is admired for its delicate appearance. Due to its beauty and ecological significance, it is a popular subject for botanists and gardeners alike.
