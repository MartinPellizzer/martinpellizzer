Erythrina piscidioides

Erythrina piscidioides, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, woody pod.

Erythrina piscidioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
