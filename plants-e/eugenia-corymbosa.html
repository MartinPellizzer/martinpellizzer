Eugenia corymbosa

Eugenia corymbosa, commonly known as the Java apple or rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit that resembles a hairy coconut. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in corymbs, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
