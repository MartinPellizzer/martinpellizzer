Epidendrum diosanense

Epidendrum diosanense, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit.

Epidendrum diosanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
