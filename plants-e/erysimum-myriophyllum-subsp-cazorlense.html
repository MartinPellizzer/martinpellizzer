Erysimum myriophyllum subsp. cazorlense

Erysimum myriophyllum subsp.

cazorlense, commonly known as the Cazorla erysimum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in Mediterranean climates. It has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.
