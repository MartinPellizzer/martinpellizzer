Eleocharis nodulosa var. tenuis

Eleocharis nodulosa var. tenuis, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often densely packed, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences composed of tiny, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in regions with high humidity and waterlogged soils.

This species plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and contributing to soil stabilization.
