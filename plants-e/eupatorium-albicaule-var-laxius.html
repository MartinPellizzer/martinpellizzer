Eupatorium albicaule var. laxius

Eupatorium albicaule var. laxius, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
