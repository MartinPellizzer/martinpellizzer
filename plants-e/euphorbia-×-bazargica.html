Euphorbia × bazargica

Euphorbia × bazargica, commonly known as the Persian spurge, is a hybrid plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens in regions with dry climates.
