Eugenia subspinulosa

Eugenia subspinulosa, commonly known as the rough eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
