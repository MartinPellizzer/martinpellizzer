Echeandia reflexa

Echeandia reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed echeandia, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curled, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Echeandia reflexa is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
