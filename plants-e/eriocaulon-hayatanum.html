Eriocaulon hayatanum

Eriocaulon hayatanum, commonly known as the Hayata's spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, with a dense covering of fine, silky hairs.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, often arranged in spike-like clusters, typical of the genus Eriocaulon.
