Euphrasia officinalis subvar. stricta

Euphrasia officinalis subvar. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eyebright, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, and the plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in treating eye conditions and respiratory ailments.
