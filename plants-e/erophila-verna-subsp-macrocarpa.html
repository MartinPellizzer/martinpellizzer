Erophila verna subsp. macrocarpa

Erophila verna subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to purple flowers in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its subspecies is distinguished by its larger fruiting structures compared to other members of the species.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
