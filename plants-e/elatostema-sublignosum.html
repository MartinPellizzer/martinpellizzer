Elatostema sublignosum

Elatostema sublignosum, commonly known as the creeping elatostema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with fleshy, often reddish stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a waxy surface.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
