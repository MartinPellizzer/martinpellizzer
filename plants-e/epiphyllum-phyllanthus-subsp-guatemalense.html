Epiphyllum phyllanthus subsp. guatemalense

Epiphyllum phyllanthus subsp.

guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family. It is a climbing cactus that produces large, showy flowers that open at night.

The plant is native to Central America and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its stems are flattened and segmented, and it has spiny areoles.

The flowers are typically pink to red in color and emit a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.
