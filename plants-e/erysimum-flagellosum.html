Erysimum flagellosum

Erysimum flagellosum, commonly known as the desert wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, bloom in early spring.

Erysimum flagellosum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
