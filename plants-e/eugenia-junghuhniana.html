Eugenia junghuhniana

Eugenia junghuhniana, commonly known as the Java apple or Java cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves are typically used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.
