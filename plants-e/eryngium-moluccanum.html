Eryngium moluccanum

Eryngium moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-violet flower heads and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Eryngium moluccanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
