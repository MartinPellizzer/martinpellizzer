Equisetum lacustre

Equisetum lacustre, commonly known as water horsetail, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Equisetaceae.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of stems and leaves, which are typically small and scale-like. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and lakes. Its stems are hollow and contain silica, giving them a rigid, needle-like structure.

Equisetum lacustre plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in aquatic habitats.
