Eurystyles standleyi

Eurystyles standleyi, commonly known as the Standley's euphorbia, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico, characterized by its thick, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense inflorescences, which are typical of the genus Eurystyles. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
