Eugenia tomentosa

Eugenia tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet apple or tamarillo, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible, bright yellow or orange berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its botanical features include opposite leaves, woody stems, and a preference for warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
