Eugenia fragrans var. obovata

Eugenia fragrans var. obovata, commonly known as the fragrant guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often obovate in shape, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, aromatic fruits. Its botanical features include a dense, compact growth habit and a preference for warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of culinary and medicinal uses.
