Eryngium discolor

Eryngium discolor, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium discolor is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
