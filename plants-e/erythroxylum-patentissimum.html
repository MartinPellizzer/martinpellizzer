Erythroxylum patentissimum

Erythroxylum patentissimum, commonly known as the Amazonian copaiba, is a shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain aromatic, resinous seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often found in humid, tropical environments.
