Eurybia linearifolia

Eurybia linearifolia, commonly known as slender bellflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces small, seed-like fruits called cypselae, which are dispersed by wind.

Eurybia linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
