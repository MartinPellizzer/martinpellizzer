Eurystigma clavatum

Eurystigma clavatum, commonly known as the club-stemmed eurystigma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features a distinctive, club-shaped stem that is often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique morphology and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
