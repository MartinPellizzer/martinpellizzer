Eragrostis monodii

Eragrostis monodii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often pale green or brown, and it is well adapted to drought conditions. Eragrostis monodii thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems and a potential candidate for restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
