Eleocharis czernjajevi

Eleocharis czernjajevi, commonly known as the Czernjajew's spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to parts of Asia, including China and Russia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eleocharis czernjajevi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
