Eminium rauwolffii

Eminium rauwolffii, commonly known as the golden star, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features vibrant yellow flowers arranged in a star-like pattern, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a sought-after addition to botanical collections.
