Erythrina sandwicensis var. luteosperma

Erythrina sandwicensis var. luteosperma, commonly known as the yellow-fruited coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its fruit is a pod that turns yellow when mature. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its native habitat.

The species is also notable for its role in traditional Hawaiian culture and ecological significance.
