Elodea potamogeton

Elodea potamogeton, commonly known as water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It has long, branching stems that are typically less than 1 meter in length, and it produces small, green flowers that emerge above the water surface. The plant's leaves are narrow, ribbon-like, and arranged in whorls around the stem, aiding in efficient photosynthesis in aquatic conditions. Elodea potamogeton plays a crucial role in water ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms and improving water quality through oxygenation.

It is also used in biological studies due to its simple structure and ease of cultivation.
