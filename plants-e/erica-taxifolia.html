Erica taxifolia

Erica taxifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. Erica taxifolia is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in maritime climates.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
