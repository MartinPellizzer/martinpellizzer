Evax lusitanica

Evax lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its foliage is covered in coarse, bristly hairs, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Evax lusitanica is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
