Eriachne burkittii var. hirsutissima

Eriachne burkittii var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy eriachne, is a grass-like plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in stabilizing soils in native ecosystems.
