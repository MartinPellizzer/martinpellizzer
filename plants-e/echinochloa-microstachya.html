Echinochloa microstachya

Echinochloa microstachya, commonly known as small-stalked barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale green flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils, wetlands, and agricultural fields, where it can become a weed.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common plant in various ecological and agricultural settings.
