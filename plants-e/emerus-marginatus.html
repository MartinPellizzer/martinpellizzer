Emerus marginatus

Emerus marginatus, commonly known as the marginate emerus, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct margin. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in local ecosystems.
