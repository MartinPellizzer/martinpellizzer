Erica interrupta

Erica interrupta, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a dwarf shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of bright pink or white flowers in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica interrupta is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
