Eragrostis surreyana

Eragrostis surreyana, commonly known as Surrey grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
