Erysimum grandiflorum subsp. mediohispanicum

Erysimum grandiflorum subsp.

mediohispanicum, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often along roadsides and in dry, sunny areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the stems are branched and covered in fine pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
