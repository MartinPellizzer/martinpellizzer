Euphorbia amarifontana

Euphorbia amarifontana, commonly known as the red-veined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and may have a reddish or purplish hue, adding to its ornamental value.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.
