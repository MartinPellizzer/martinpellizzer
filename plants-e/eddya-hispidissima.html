Eddya hispidissima

Eddya hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy eddy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
