Eupatorium anisochroma

Eupatorium anisochroma, commonly known as purplestem false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, lavender to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium anisochroma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
