Echeveria nayaritensis

Echeveria nayaritensis, commonly known as the Nayarit echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white to grayish hairs. The leaves are often bluish-green in color and can vary in shape depending on the specific cultivar. It produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the spring or summer months.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
