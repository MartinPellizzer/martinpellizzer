Eugenia rosmarinifolia var. latifolia

Eugenia rosmarinifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved clove or broad-leaved eugenia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark red, clove-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
