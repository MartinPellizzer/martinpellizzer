Eurybia lamprophylla

Eurybia lamprophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name.

Eurybia lamprophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
