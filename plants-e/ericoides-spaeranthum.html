Ericoides spaeranthum

Ericoides spaeranthum, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, producing pink to purple blooms that attract pollinators.

Ericoides spaeranthum is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
