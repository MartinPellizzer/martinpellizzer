Eria longilabris

Eria longilabris, commonly known as the long-lipped orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with long, narrow lips that are often a vibrant pink or purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eria longilabris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
