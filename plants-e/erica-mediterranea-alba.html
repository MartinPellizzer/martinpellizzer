Erica mediterranea alba

Erica mediterranea alba, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun, making it well adapted to rocky and coastal environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Erica mediterranea alba plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
