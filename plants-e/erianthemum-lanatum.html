Erianthemum lanatum

Erianthemum lanatum, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its hairy stems and leaves provide a unique texture, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Erianthemum lanatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
