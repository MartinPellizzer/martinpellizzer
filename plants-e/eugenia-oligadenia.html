Eugenia oligadenia

Eugenia oligadenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin layers, while its wood is hard and durable.

Eugenia oligadenia is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.
