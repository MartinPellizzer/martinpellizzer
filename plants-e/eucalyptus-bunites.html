Eucalyptus bunites

Eucalyptus bunites, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub or small tree form, often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color, and it produces small, globular flower buds that range from yellow to red. Eucalyptus bunites is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.

Its bark is fibrous and peels in strips, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
