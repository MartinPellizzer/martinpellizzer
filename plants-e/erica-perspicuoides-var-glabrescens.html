Erica perspicuoides var. glabrescens

Erica perspicuoides var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The variety 'glabrescens' is distinguished by its smoother, less hairy leaves compared to other varieties of Erica perspicuoides.
