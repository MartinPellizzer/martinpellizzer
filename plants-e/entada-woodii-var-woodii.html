Entada woodii var. woodii

Entada woodii var. woodii, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, followed by spherical, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
