Erodium crenatum f. speciosum

Erodium crenatum f. speciosum, commonly known as the ornamental bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.

The species is native to regions of Europe and the Mediterranean, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
