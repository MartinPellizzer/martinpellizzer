Epilobium angustifolium f. macrophyllum

Epilobium angustifolium f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved willowherb, is a variant of the narrow-leaved willowherb.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its lance-shaped, often broad leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are generally larger and more elongated compared to the typical species, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.
