Erica turbiniflora var. aristata

Erica turbiniflora var. aristata, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in areas with high salt exposure. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand coastal conditions and limited water availability.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
