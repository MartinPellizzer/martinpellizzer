Elaeocarpus suaveolens

Elaeocarpus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
