Eremothera boothii subsp. condensata

Eremothera boothii subsp.

condensata, commonly known as the Desert Evening Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes and are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
