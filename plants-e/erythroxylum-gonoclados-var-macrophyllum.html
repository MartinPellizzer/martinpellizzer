Erythroxylum gonoclados var. macrophyllum

Erythroxylum gonoclados var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leafed copal tree, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
