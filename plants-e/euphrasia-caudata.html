Euphrasia caudata

Euphrasia caudata, commonly known as the trailing eyebright, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, trailing stems that produce small, yellowish-green flowers with two lips. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, hairy textures.

Euphrasia caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
