Euphorbia luzoniensis

Euphorbia luzoniensis, commonly known as the Luzon spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
