Eriocaulon karaavalense

Eriocaulon karaavalense, commonly known as the Karavale rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in moist lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall resemblance to other rush species.

Eriocaulon karaavalense plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
