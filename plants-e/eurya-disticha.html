Eurya disticha

Eurya disticha, commonly known as the two-branched evergreen holly, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. Eurya disticha thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing addition to gardens and landscapes.
