Eugenia kunstleri

Eugenia kunstleri, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The tree is also valued for its ability to withstand coastal conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
