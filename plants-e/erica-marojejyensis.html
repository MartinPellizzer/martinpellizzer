Erica marojejyensis

Erica marojejyensis, commonly known as the Marojejy heath, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
