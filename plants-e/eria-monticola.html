Eria monticola

Eria monticola, commonly known as the mountain eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its flowers are small and often pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Eria monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
