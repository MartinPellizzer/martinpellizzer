Echinospermum deflexum var. floribundum

Echinospermum deflexum var. floribundum, commonly known as the Woolly Sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow ray flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, hard seeds that are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in its native habitat.
