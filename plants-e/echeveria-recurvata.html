Echeveria recurvata

Echeveria recurvata, commonly known as the recurved echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight.

Due to its compact size and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
