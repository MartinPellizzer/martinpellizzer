Echinocactus pentlandii

Echinocactus pentlandii, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is a spineless cactus with a globular shape and a thick, ribbed stem that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at the top of the stems. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments with minimal rainfall.

Echinocactus pentlandii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
