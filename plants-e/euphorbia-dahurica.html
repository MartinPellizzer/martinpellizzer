Euphorbia dahurica

Euphorbia dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian spurge, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by colorful bracts. Its latex is toxic and can cause skin irritation, which is a characteristic feature of many euphorbias.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
