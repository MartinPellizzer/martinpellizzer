Eryngium thessalonicum

Eryngium thessalonicum, commonly known as the Thessaloniki thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features striking blue to purple spiked flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silvery-gray, spiny foliage that is both ornamental and drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
