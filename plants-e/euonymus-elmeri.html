Euonymus elmeri

Euonymus elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, inflated seed capsules that persist into the winter. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its low maintenance requirements.
