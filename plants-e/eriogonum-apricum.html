Eriogonum apricum

Eriogonum apricum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped.

Eriogonum apricum is a member of the legume family and is adapted to arid environments.
