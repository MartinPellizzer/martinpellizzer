Erithalis pentagonia

Erithalis pentagonia, commonly known as the five-angled erithalis, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
