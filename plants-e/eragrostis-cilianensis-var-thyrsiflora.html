Eragrostis cilianensis var. thyrsiflora

Eragrostis cilianensis var. thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Cilian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
