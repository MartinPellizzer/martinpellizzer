Epilobium petraeum

Epilobium petraeum, commonly known as the rock willow or mountain willow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in early summer.

Epilobium petraeum is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
