Erigeron ciliaris

Erigeron ciliaris, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually hairy and gray-green, with a distinctive silvery border along the edges.
