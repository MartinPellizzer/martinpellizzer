Equisetum ramosissimum var. boivinii

Equisetum ramosissimum var. boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's horse-tail, is a variant of the horse-tail plant native to southern Africa.

It is a spore-bearing plant that belongs to the Equisetaceae family, characterized by its whorled arrangement of branches and a distinctive, segmented stem. The plant produces strobili, or cone-like structures, at the tips of its branches, which contain the spores for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in damp woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ancient lineage and unique morphology make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
