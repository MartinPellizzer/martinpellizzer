Eugenia mato

Eugenia mato, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible red berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in jams, jellies, and traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
