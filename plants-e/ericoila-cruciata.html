Ericoila cruciata

Ericoila cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Ericoila cruciata is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
