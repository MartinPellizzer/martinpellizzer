Eugenia marlierioides

Eugenia marlierioides, commonly known as the pink-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, attracting various wildlife.

Eugenia marlierioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
