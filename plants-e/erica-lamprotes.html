Erica lamprotes

Erica lamprotes, commonly known as the silver heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, dense foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica lamprotes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
