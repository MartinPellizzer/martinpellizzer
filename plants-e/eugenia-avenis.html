Eugenia avenis

Eugenia avenis, commonly known as the silverleaf eucalyptus, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, hard seeds.

Eugenia avenis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to grow in sandy or well-drained soils.
