Erica aggregata

Erica aggregata, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western Cape region of South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and rocky habitats. It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes other heath and heather plants.

Erica aggregata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
