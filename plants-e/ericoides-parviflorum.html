Ericoides parviflorum

Ericoides parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered heath, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly in California.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and azaleas. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. Its flowers are tiny, bell-shaped, and usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Ericoides parviflorum thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
