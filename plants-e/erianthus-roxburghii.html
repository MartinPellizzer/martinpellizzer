Erianthus roxburghii

Erianthus roxburghii, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, producing a distinctive appearance.

Erianthus roxburghii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
