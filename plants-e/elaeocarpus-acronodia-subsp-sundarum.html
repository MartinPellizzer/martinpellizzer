Elaeocarpus acronodia subsp. sundarum

Elaeocarpus acronodia subsp.

sundarum, commonly known as the Malabar elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers.

The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forests and undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
