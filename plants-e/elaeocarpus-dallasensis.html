Elaeocarpus dallasensis

Elaeocarpus dallasensis, commonly known as the Dallas elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the wet forests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that enclose a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
