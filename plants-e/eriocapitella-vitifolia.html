Eriocapitella vitifolia

Eriocapitella vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf eriocapitella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with elliptical leaves that are often slightly hairy. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
