Eucalyptus macrocarpa subsp. macrocarpa

Eucalyptus macrocarpa subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, grey-green foliage and smooth, often peeling bark.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.
