Eucalyptus gunnii var. rubida

Eucalyptus gunnii var. rubida, commonly known as the crimson gum, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its distinctive crimson flowers and smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
