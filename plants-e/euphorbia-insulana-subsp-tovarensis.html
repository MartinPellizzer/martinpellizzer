Euphorbia insulana subsp. tovarensis

Euphorbia insulana subsp.

tovarensis, commonly known as the Tovarensis spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Canary Islands. It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
