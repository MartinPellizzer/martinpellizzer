Eragrostis friesii

Eragrostis friesii, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and compact growth habit, making it visually distinct. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Eragrostis friesii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
