Euonymus phellomanus

Euonymus phellomanus, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Euonymus phellomanus is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in a variety of soil conditions.
