Eucalyptus forrestiana subsp. dolichorhyncha

Eucalyptus forrestiana subsp.

dolichorhyncha, commonly known as the slender-leafed mallees, is a subshrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance.

The flowers are typically small and arranged in clusters, producing a distinctive yellow or cream color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is part of the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
