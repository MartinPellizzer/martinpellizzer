Eucalyptus eximia

Eucalyptus eximia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green leaves and small, globular buds. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces white or pale pink flowers, which are clustered in groups of up to seven.

Eucalyptus eximia is often found in open woodlands and is an important component of the local flora.
