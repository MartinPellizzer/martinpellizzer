Eragrostis platystachys

Eragrostis platystachys, commonly known as broad-stalked lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often silver-haired leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid and arid environments.

Eragrostis platystachys is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
