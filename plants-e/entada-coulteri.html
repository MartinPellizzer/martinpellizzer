Entada coulteri

Entada coulteri, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used traditionally in various cultures for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its timber and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
