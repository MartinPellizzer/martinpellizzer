Escobedia peduncularis

Escobedia peduncularis, commonly known as the coral tree or coral bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, fleshy flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, aiding in water retention.

Escobedia peduncularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and unique floral display.
