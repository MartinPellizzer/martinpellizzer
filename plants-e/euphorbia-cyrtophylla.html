Euphorbia cyrtophylla

Euphorbia cyrtophylla, commonly known as the Cape Hottentot, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branched stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Euphorbia cyrtophylla is also known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
