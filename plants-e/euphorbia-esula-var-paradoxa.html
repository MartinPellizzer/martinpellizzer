Euphorbia esula var. paradoxa

Euphorbia esula var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Canadian tansy, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant has a milky latex sap that can cause skin irritation. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
