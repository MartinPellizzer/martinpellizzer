Eupatorium scopulorum

Eupatorium scopulorum, commonly known as rock eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
