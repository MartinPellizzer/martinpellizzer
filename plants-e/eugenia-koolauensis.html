Eugenia koolauensis

Eugenia koolauensis, commonly known as the Koolau tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark purple berries. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

The Koolau tree is often found in moist, mesic forests and plays a role in the ecological diversity of Hawaii's native flora.
