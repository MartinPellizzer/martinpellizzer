Eupatorium japonicum var. tripartitum

Eupatorium japonicum var. tripartitum, commonly known as Japanese false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
