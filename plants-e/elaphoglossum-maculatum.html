Elaphoglossum maculatum

Elaphoglossum maculatum, commonly known as the spotted maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that often display a striking contrast of green and silver. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and stout, supporting the growth of its delicate, lace-like leaves.

Elaphoglossum maculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
