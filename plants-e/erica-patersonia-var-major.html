Erica patersonia var. major

Erica patersonia var. major, commonly known as the greater heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
