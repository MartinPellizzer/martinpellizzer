Entandrophragma candolleana

Entandrophragma candolleana, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Bombacaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy, yellow aril. Its seeds are used in the production of shea butter, a valuable natural product in cosmetics and food industries.

The plant is also important for local communities and plays a role in traditional medicine.
