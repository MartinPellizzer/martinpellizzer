Elymus elymoides subsp. californicus

Elymus elymoides subsp.

californicus, commonly known as California wild rye, is a perennial grass native to western North America. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
