Erica cubitalis

Erica cubitalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreens. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and leathery. Erica cubitalis produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
