Eucalyptus melliodora var. murrurundi

Eucalyptus melliodora var. murrurundi, commonly known as the Murrurundi malleeflower, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and small, aromatic leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage and floral display.
