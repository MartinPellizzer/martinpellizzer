Eugenia kwangtungensis

Eugenia kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to southern China, particularly Guangdong province, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its aromatic foliage and edible fruits.
