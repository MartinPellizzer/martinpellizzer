Erodium texanum

Erodium texanum, commonly known as Texas catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and dry habitats.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread across open areas.
