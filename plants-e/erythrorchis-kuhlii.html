Erythrorchis kuhlii

Erythrorchis kuhlii, commonly known as the Kuhli's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, pseudobulb-like stem and produces showy, red-brown flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by beetles and flies, which are attracted to its strong scent.

Erythrorchis kuhlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
