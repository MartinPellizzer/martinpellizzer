Elymus kamoji var. intermedius

Elymus kamoji var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.
