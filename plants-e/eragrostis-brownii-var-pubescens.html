Eragrostis brownii var. pubescens

Eragrostis brownii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy brown lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit, often forming compact clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous minute, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The hairy pubescence on its stems and leaves gives it a distinctive appearance and helps reduce water loss.
