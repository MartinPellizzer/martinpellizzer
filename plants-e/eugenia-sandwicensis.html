Eugenia sandwicensis

Eugenia sandwicensis, commonly known as the ʻōhiʻa lehua, is a native Hawaiian plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

The ʻōhiʻa lehua plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian ecosystems and is culturally significant to Native Hawaiians.
