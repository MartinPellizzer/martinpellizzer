Erigeron rigidus

Erigeron rigidus, commonly known as rigid fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

Erigeron rigidus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
