Eugenia puncticulata

Eugenia puncticulata, commonly known as the punctuated eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive dotted pattern on the underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, edible drupe that is typically consumed raw or used in local cuisine.
