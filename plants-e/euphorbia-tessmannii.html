Euphorbia tessmannii

Euphorbia tessmannii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
