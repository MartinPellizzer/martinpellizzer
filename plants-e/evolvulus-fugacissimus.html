Evolvulus fugacissimus

Evolvulus fugacissimus, commonly known as the fast-fading morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and short life cycle. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
