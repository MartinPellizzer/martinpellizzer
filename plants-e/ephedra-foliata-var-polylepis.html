Ephedra foliata var. polylepis

Ephedra foliata var. polylepis, commonly known as the "Polylepis Ephedra," is a shrub-like plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
