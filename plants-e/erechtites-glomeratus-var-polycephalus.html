Erechtites glomeratus var. polycephalus

Erechtites glomeratus var. polycephalus, commonly known as the clustered eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that produce clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer. The plant has serrated, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, rounded flower heads that give it a unique appearance.
