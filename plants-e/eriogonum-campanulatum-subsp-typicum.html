Eriogonum campanulatum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum campanulatum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the bellflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
