Encyclia edithiana

Encyclia edithiana, commonly known as the Edith's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, green to reddish-brown pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with intricate patterns, and they bloom during the cooler months.

Encyclia edithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
