Elaeocarpus blascoi

Elaeocarpus blascoi, commonly known as the Blasco's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
