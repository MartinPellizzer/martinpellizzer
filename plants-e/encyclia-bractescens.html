Encyclia bractescens

Encyclia bractescens, commonly known as the bracteate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive greenish-yellow color and a hooded lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Encyclia bractescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
