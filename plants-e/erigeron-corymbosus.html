Erigeron corymbosus

Erigeron corymbosus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides, reaching heights of up to 2 feet. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are generally hairy and lance-shaped, with a slightly aromatic scent.
