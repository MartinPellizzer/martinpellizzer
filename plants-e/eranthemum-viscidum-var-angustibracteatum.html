Eranthemum viscidum var. angustibracteatum

Eranthemum viscidum var. angustibracteatum, commonly known as narrow-bract eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered with a sticky substance, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
