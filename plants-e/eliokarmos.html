Eliokarmos

Eliokarmos, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clustered fruit. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its rapid growth rate. Its fruit, known as the "fig," is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The tree also plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
