Eulophia eleogena

Eulophia eleogena, commonly known as the golden eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, arranged in a compact rosette.

Eulophia eleogena thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
