Encyclia gravida

Encyclia gravida, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with flowers that have three sepals and three petals, one of which is showy and lip-like. Encyclia gravida thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
