Eragrostis nigra var. cochinchinensis

Eragrostis nigra var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, slender leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale-colored florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is adapted to warm climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it both a useful forage crop and an invasive weed in some regions.
