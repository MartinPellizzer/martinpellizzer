Epipactis persica subsp. pontica

Epipactis persica subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic Persian helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
