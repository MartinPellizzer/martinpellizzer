Elaeagia subspicata

Elaeagia subspicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom in spring. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Elaeagia subspicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water environments.
