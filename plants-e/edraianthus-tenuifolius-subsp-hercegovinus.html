Edraianthus tenuifolius subsp. hercegovinus

Edraianthus tenuifolius subsp.

hercegovinus, commonly known as the Hercegovina grassvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its delicate foliage and is often found in open, sunny environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
