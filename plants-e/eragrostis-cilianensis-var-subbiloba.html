Eragrostis cilianensis var. subbiloba

Eragrostis cilianensis var. subbiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential candidate for erosion control.
