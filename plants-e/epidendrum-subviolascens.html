Epidendrum subviolascens

Epidendrum subviolascens, commonly known as the violet-tinged epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of violet and white. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually dark green and somewhat fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its native habitat.

Epidendrum subviolascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
