Echinocactus ebenacanthus

Echinocactus ebenacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that is covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, typically at the top of the stem. Its thick, ribbed structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The golden barrel cactus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
