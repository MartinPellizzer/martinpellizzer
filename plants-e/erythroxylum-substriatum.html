Erythroxylum substriatum

Erythroxylum substriatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical research.
