Euphorbia pilosa var. longifolia

Euphorbia pilosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to parts of Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with dense clusters of spiny, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study and cultivation.
