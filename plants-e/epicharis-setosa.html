Epicharis setosa

Epicharis setosa, commonly known as the hairy epicharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Epicharis setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
