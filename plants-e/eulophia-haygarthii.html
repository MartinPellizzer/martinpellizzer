Eulophia haygarthii

Eulophia haygarthii, commonly known as the Haygarth's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on the forest floor or along stream banks. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Eulophia haygarthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
