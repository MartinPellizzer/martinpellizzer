Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp. fitchii

Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp.

fitchii, commonly known as Fitch's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in spines and are often green in color.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments with deep roots and thick, water-storing stems.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
