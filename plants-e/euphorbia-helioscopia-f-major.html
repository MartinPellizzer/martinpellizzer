Euphorbia helioscopia f. major

Euphorbia helioscopia f. major, commonly known as the sun spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with bright yellow cyathia that resemble flowers. The plant features smooth, green, lobed leaves and produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant appearance.
