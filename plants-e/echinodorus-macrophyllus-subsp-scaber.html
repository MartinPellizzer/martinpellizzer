Echinodorus macrophyllus subsp. scaber

Echinodorus macrophyllus subsp.

scaber, commonly known as the broad-leaf waterweed, is a aquatic plant native to South America. It is characterized by its large, broad, and often waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant typically has a short rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the water surface. It thrives in slow-moving to still freshwater environments with moderate to high light exposure.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is popular in aquascaping and pond decoration.
