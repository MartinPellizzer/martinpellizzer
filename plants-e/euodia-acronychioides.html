Euodia acronychioides

Euodia acronychioides, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that split open to release seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.

Its leaves and fruits have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
