Euphorbia winkleri

Euphorbia winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features a short, thick stem with fleshy, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry climates with minimal rainfall.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
