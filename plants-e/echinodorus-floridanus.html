Echinodorus floridanus

Echinodorus floridanus, commonly known as the Florida swordplant, is a species of aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. This plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments with moderate to high light exposure. It is often found in ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams.

Echinodorus floridanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to help improve water quality by absorbing nutrients.
