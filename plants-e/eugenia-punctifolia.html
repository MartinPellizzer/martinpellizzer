Eugenia punctifolia

Eugenia punctifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry with a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Eugenia punctifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
