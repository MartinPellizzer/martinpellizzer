Eugenia dichroma

Eugenia dichroma, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Eugenia dichroma thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
