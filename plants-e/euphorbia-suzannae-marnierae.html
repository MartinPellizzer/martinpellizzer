Euphorbia suzannae-marnierae

Euphorbia suzannae-marnierae, commonly known as the Marnier's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
