Eugenia pseudopsidium

Eugenia pseudopsidium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or small cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a fragrant scent when crushed.

Eugenia pseudopsidium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
