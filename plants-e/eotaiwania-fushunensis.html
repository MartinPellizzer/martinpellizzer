Eotaiwania fushunensis

Eotaiwania fushunensis, commonly known as the Fushun fossil cycad, is an extinct plant species belonging to the Cycadophyta group.

It is notable for its well-preserved fossil records found in the Fushun coal mines of China. The plant's botanical features include a trunk with a distinctive ribbed structure and large, pinnate leaves. It thrived during the Paleogene period and is considered a significant indicator of ancient tropical climates.

Its study provides valuable insights into the evolution of early seed plants and paleoenvironmental conditions.
