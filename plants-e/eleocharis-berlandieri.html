Eleocharis berlandieri

Eleocharis berlandieri, commonly known as the desert spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often sparsely branched, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its reproductive structures are wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Eleocharis berlandieri is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
