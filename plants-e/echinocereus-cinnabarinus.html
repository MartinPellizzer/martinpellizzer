Echinocereus cinnabarinus

Echinocereus cinnabarinus, commonly known as the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny areoles, which are typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments with minimal rainfall.

Echinocereus cinnabarinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
