Eupatorium modestum

Eupatorium modestum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Eupatorium modestum thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
