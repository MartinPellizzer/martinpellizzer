Eria bidentata

Eria bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, creeping rhizome and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and have a distinctive two-toothed appearance at the base, which gives it its scientific name.

Eria bidentata is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
