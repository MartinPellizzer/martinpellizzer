Eumachia podocephala

Eumachia podocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eumachia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are usually pale green to white in color.

Eumachia podocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
