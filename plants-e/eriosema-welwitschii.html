Eriosema welwitschii

Eriosema welwitschii, commonly known as the velvetleaf eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Eriosema welwitschii is often found in grassland and woodland habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
