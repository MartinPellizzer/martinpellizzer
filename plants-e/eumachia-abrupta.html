Eumachia abrupta

Eumachia abrupta, commonly known as the abrupt mountain sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Eumachia abrupta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
