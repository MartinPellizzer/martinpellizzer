Encelia mexicana

Encelia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican tarplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
