Eulalia viminea var. variabilis

Eulalia viminea var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features slender, wiry stems that produce feathery, silver-green inflorescences, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with blades that can range from narrow to broader in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
