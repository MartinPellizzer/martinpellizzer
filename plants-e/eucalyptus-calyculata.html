Eucalyptus calyculata

Eucalyptus calyculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters and attract various pollinators. The bark is fibrous and peels in strips, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
