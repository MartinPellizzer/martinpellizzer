Erianthus giganteus var. compactus

Erianthus giganteus var. compactus, commonly known as the dwarf switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown or tan in color. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping and as a bioenergy crop due to its high biomass yield.
