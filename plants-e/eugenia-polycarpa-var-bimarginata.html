Eugenia polycarpa var. bimarginata

Eugenia polycarpa var. bimarginata, commonly known as the red-leaf eugenia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often display red or purple margins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The berries are not typically consumed by humans but are used in traditional medicine in some regions.
