Eriophorum caespitosum

Eriophorum caespitosum, commonly known as tussock grass or cotton grass, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and is often found in moist or wetland habitats. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that appear in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Eriophorum caespitosum is an important component of peatland ecosystems and plays a role in soil stabilization.
