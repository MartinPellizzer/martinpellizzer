Eriocaulon modestum f. grandifolium

Eriocaulon modestum f. grandifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf marsh plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically broad and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly textured surface. This plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
