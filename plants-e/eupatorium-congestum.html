Eupatorium congestum

Eupatorium congestum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
