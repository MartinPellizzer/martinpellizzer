Erica vallis-gratiae

Erica vallis-gratiae, commonly known as the valley heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heather family (Ericaceae).

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa, particularly in the Cape Floristic Region. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica vallis-gratiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
