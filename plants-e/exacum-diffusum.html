Exacum diffusum

Exacum diffusum, commonly known as the spreading blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
