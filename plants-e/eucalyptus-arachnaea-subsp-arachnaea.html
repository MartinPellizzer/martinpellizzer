Eucalyptus arachnaea subsp. arachnaea

Eucalyptus arachnaea subsp.

arachnaea, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark that peels in thin, papery layers.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a white underside. It produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically pale yellow or cream in color.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
