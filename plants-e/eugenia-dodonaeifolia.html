Eugenia dodonaeifolia

Eugenia dodonaeifolia, commonly known as the Java apple or red apple tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Eugenia dodonaeifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
