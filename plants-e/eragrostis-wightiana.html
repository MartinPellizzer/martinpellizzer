Eragrostis wightiana

Eragrostis wightiana, commonly known as Wight's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Eragrostis wightiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
