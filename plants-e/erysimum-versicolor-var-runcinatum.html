Erysimum versicolor var. runcinatum

Erysimum versicolor var. runcinatum, commonly known as the curly yellow mustache plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its distinctive, curly, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
