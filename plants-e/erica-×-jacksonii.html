Erica × jacksonii

Erica × jacksonii, commonly known as the Jackson's heath, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Erica × jacksonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
