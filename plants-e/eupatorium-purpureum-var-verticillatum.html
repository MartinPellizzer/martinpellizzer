Eupatorium purpureum var. verticillatum

Eupatorium purpureum var. verticillatum, commonly known as purple false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
