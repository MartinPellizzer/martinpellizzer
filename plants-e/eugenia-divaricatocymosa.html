Eugenia divaricatocymosa

Eugenia divaricatocymosa, commonly known as the variable cyme eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in cymes, and produce small, edible berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
