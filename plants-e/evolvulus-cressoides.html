Evolvulus cressoides

Evolvulus cressoides, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 20 to 40 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, round seeds.

Evolvulus cressoides is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in warm, sunny environments.
