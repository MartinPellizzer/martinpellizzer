Elaeagnus multiflora var. obovoidea

Elaeagnus multiflora var. obovoidea, commonly known as the silverberry or sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible, reddish berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
