Ericoides triceps

Ericoides triceps, commonly known as the three-lobed heath, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are typically leathery and arranged in a compact, shrub-like form. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are often pink or white in color. Ericoides triceps thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in temperate regions with cool, moist climates.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
