Erigeron sublyratus

Erigeron sublyratus, commonly known as the silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Erigeron sublyratus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
