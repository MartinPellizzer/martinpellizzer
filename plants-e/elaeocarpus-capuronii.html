Elaeocarpus capuronii

Elaeocarpus capuronii, commonly known as the Capuron's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a thick, waxy coating. It is often found in lowland to montane rainforests, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
