Eucalyptus brachycalyx

Eucalyptus brachycalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and distinctive, often clustered flower buds. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It produces white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is valued for its resilience and is often used in reforestation and land rehabilitation efforts.
