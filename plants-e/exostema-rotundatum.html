Exostema rotundatum

Exostema rotundatum, commonly known as the roundleaf orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, often clustered growth habit. The plant features fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention in its humid, shaded habitat. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink.

Exostema rotundatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
