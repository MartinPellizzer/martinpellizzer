Eupatorium kleinioides var. lasiolepis

Eupatorium kleinioides var. lasiolepis, commonly known as the silky blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
