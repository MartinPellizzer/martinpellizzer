Enchysia secunda var. glabrata

Enchysia secunda var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf enchysia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, smooth, and lance-shaped leaves that are often glabrous, giving it its common name. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it a subject of interest in horticulture and ecological studies.
