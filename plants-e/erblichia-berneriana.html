Erblichia berneriana

Erblichia berneriana, commonly known as the Berner's lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces white or pale pink petals that are often speckled with darker markings. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
