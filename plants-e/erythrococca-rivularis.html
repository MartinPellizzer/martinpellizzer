Erythrococca rivularis

Erythrococca rivularis, commonly known as the red cocculus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces striking red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
