Elaeocarpus stenophyllus

Elaeocarpus stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are typically blue or black in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
