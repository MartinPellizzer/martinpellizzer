Eria ringens

Eria ringens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Eria ringens is known for its delicate foliage and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in both indoor and outdoor settings.
