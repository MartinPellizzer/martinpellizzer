Echeveria platyphylla

Echeveria platyphylla, commonly known as the Blue Jelly Plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-gray leaves that are thick and waxy, adapted for storing water. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
