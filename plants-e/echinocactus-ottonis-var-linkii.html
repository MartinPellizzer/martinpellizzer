Echinocactus ottonis var. linkii

Echinocactus ottonis var. linkii, commonly known as the Mexican lion's mouth, is a striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem covered with bright green, ribbed segments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its spines are typically white to yellow, providing both protection and a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
