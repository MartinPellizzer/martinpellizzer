Eucalyptus morrisbyi

Eucalyptus morrisbyi, commonly known as the Mornington Island gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark that ranges from grey to white in color. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and typically have a grey-green hue with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small, white or cream, and are arranged in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators.

The tree is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
