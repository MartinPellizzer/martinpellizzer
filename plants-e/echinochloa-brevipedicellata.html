Echinochloa brevipedicellata

Echinochloa brevipedicellata, commonly known as small-stemmed needlegrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, agricultural fields, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is often considered a weed in cultivated areas but plays a role in stabilizing soil in natural habitats.
