Eriope obovata var. obovata

Eriope obovata var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf eriope, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variant is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
