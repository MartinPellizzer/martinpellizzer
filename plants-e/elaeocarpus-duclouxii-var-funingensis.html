Elaeocarpus duclouxii var. funingensis

Elaeocarpus duclouxii var. funingensis, commonly known as the Funing elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that are typically spherical or ellipsoid in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
