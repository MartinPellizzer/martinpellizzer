Elcismia prorepens

Elcismia prorepens, commonly known as the creeping elcismia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, clustered white to pale yellow flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Elcismia prorepens is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
