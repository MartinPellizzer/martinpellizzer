Eupatorium pseudofastigiatum var. crenata

Eupatorium pseudofastigiatum var. crenata, commonly known as the smooth blue eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
