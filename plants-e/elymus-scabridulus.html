Elymus scabridulus

Elymus scabridulus, commonly known as rough wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, serrated leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

Elymus scabridulus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
