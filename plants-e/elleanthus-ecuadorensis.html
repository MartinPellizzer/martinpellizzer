Elleanthus ecuadorensis

Elleanthus ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian elleanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

Elleanthus ecuadorensis is valued both for its ecological role and its potential contributions to pharmacological research.
