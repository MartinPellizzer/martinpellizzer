Epithema violaceum

Epithema violaceum, commonly known as the violet epithema, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Epithema violaceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
