Eriobotrya chinensis

Eriobotrya chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens and orchards.
