Erica saxigena

Erica saxigena, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom in spring.

Erica saxigena is often used in rock gardens and as a hardy ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
