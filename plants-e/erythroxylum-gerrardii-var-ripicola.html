Erythroxylum gerrardii var. ripicola

Erythroxylum gerrardii var. ripicola, commonly known as the rock copal or rock mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
