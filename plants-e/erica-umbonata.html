Erica umbonata

Erica umbonata, commonly known as the Cape heath or mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers. Erica umbonata is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Its flowering period usually occurs in spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
