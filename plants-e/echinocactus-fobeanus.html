Echinocactus fobeanus

Echinocactus fobeanus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing cactus characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles. The plant features bright green, ribbed stems that can develop a golden hue when exposed to sunlight. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Echinocactus fobeanus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
