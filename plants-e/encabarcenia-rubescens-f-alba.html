Encabarcenia rubescens f. alba

Encabarcenia rubescens f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered red-leafed shrub, is a botanical variant of the species Encabarcenia rubescens.

It is characterized by its striking contrast between the deep red leaves and the pale white flowers. This plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
