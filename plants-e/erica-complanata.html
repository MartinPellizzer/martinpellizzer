Erica complanata

Erica complanata, commonly known as the flat heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high acidity. Erica complanata is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
