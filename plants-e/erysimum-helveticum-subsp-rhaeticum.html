Erysimum helveticum subsp. rhaeticum

Erysimum helveticum subsp.

rhaeticum, commonly known as the Rhaetic wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are basal, pinnately lobed, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
