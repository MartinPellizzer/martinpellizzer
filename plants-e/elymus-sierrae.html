Elymus sierrae

Elymus sierrae, commonly known as Sierra wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and along streambanks.

Elymus sierrae plays an important ecological role by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
