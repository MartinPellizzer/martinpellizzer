Eugenia austrocaledonica

Eugenia austrocaledonica, commonly known as the Kermadec guava, is a species of flowering plant native to the Kermadec Islands in the South Pacific.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland forest habitats. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature.

Eugenia austrocaledonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also important in local ecosystems.
