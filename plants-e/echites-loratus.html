Echites loratus

Echites loratus, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Echites loratus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
