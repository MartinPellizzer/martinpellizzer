Entada scandens var. discosperma

Entada scandens var. discosperma, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red-and-yellow flowers. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain flat, disc-shaped seeds, giving it its specific epithet "discosperma.". Its bark and wood have been used traditionally for various purposes, including carving and medicinal applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
