Ericoides virginale

Ericoides virginale, commonly known as the Virginia heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and bogs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Ericoides virginale plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
