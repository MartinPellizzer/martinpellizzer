Eugenia canelonensis

Eugenia canelonensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural purposes in warm climates.

Eugenia canelonensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
