Eleusine distans

Eleusine distans, commonly known as false lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish flowers. It is adapted to grow in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes other grasses.
