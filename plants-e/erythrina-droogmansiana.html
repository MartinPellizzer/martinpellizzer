Erythrina droogmansiana

Erythrina droogmansiana, commonly known as the Droogmans' flame tree, is a deciduous legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking crimson flowers that bloom in dense, umbrella-like clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color. It produces long, slender pods that contain several flat, elliptical seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
