Elaeocarpus subinteger

Elaeocarpus subinteger, commonly known as the smooth-leafed elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, smooth-textured leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The genus Elaeocarpus is known for its ornamental value and some species have been used in traditional medicine.
