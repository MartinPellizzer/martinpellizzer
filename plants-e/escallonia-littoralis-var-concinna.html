Escallonia littoralis var. concinna

Escallonia littoralis var. concinna, commonly known as the coastal escallonia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers in spring, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice for landscaping in maritime climates.
