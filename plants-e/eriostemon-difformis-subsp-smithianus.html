Eriostemon difformis subsp. smithianus

Eriostemon difformis subsp.

smithianus, commonly known as the Smith's rough-hairbush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its rough, glandular leaves that are often covered in short, stiff hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rainforest areas.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
