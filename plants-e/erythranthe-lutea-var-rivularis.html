Erythranthe lutea var. rivularis

Erythranthe lutea var. rivularis, commonly known as the yellow rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in early spring.

It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
