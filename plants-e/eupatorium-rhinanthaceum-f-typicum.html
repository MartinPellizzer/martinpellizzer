Eupatorium rhinanthaceum f. typicum

Eupatorium rhinanthaceum f. typicum, commonly known as the common thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
