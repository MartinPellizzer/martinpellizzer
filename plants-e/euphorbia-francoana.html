Euphorbia francoana

Euphorbia francoana, commonly known as the Franco's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with unique floral structures. The plant is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
