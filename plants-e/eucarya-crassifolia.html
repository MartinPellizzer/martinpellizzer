Eucarya crassifolia

Eucarya crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf eucarya, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its robust root system allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Eucarya crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
