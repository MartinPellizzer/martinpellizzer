Eugenia noumeensis

Eugenia noumeensis, commonly known as the New Caledonia myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia noumeensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique appearance and aromatic properties.
