It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a finely divided, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in groups called sorus.

Elaphoglossum glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
