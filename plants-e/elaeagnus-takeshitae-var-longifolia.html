Elaeagnus takeshitae var. longifolia

Elaeagnus takeshitae var. longifolia, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
