Euphorbia emodi

Euphorbia emodi, commonly known as the Kashmir spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by colorful bracts. It is adapted to grow in high-altitude environments with poor soil conditions.

Euphorbia emodi is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
