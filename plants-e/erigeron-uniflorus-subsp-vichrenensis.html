Erigeron uniflorus subsp. vichrenensis

Erigeron uniflorus subsp.

vichrenensis, commonly known as the Vichrenian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
