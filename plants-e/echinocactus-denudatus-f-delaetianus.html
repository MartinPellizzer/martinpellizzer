Echinocactus denudatus f. delaetianus

Echinocactus denudatus f. delaetianus, commonly known as the Delaetian Hedgehog Cactus, is a unique variant of the species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny tubercles that give it a distinct appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
