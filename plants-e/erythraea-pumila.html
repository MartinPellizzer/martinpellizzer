Erythraea pumila

Erythraea pumila, commonly known as the dwarf erythraea, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping it conserve water in dry conditions.

Erythraea pumila is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact size and delicate flowers.
