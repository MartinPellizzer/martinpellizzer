Eurybia illita

Eurybia illita, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, clump-forming structure. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Eurybia illita thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
