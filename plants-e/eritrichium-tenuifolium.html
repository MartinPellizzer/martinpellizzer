Eritrichium tenuifolium

Eritrichium tenuifolium, commonly known as slenderleaf alpine sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features a dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in harsh alpine environments, tolerating cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.

Eritrichium tenuifolium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine wildlife.
