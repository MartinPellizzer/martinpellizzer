Echinocereus persolutus

Echinocereus persolutus, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its spines are short and often covered in a whitish bloom, which helps to reduce water loss.

Echinocereus persolutus is well adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant for desert gardens.
