Euphorbia aeruginosa

Euphorbia aeruginosa, commonly known as the blue euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow cyathia, which are its modified flower structures. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color, contributing to its striking appearance.

Euphorbia aeruginosa is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
