Eatonia

Eatonia, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and emit a subtle, sweet fragrance.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Eatonia is highly sought after in horticulture and conservation efforts.
