Eucalyptus insulana

Eucalyptus insulana, commonly known as the Lord Howe Island gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the subtropical regions of Lord Howe Island.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy on top and greyish underneath. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale green to white in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several elliptical seeds.

Eucalyptus insulana is a unique species adapted to the specific ecological conditions of its island habitat.
