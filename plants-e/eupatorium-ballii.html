Eupatorium ballii

Eupatorium ballii, commonly known as the Ball's thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Eupatorium ballii is also an important plant for pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
