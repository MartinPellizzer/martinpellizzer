Eugenia multipunctata

Eugenia multipunctata, commonly known as the spotted eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and humid climates. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Eugenia multipunctata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
