Elaeocarpus gracilipes

Elaeocarpus gracilipes, commonly known as the white sand apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and smooth, pale bark. The plant produces small, round fruits with a hard, woody shell enclosing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Elaeocarpus gracilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
