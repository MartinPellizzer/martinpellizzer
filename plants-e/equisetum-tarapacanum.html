Equisetum tarapacanum

Equisetum tarapacanum, commonly known as the Atacama horsetail, is a unique plant species native to the arid regions of Chile.

It is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant exhibits a primitive morphology, with a distinct distinction between the stem and the sporophylls, which are modified leaves that bear spores. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to extreme environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and valuable species in its native habitat.
