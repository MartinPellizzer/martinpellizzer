Ehretia resinosa

Ehretia resinosa, commonly known as the resin tree or white mahogany, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
