Eria amplectens

Eria amplectens, commonly known as the clinging orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous roots that help it adhere to tree trunks and branches. The plant produces striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, forming a rosette structure that helps retain moisture.

Eria amplectens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
