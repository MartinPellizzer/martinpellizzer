Elaeocarpus longlingensis

Elaeocarpus longlingensis, commonly known as the Longling elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
