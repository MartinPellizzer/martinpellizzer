Elaeocarpus nitidus var. velutinus

Elaeocarpus nitidus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a velvety texture on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers followed by distinctive, woody fruits that enclose a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
