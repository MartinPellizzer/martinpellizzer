Epilobium × pubescens

Epilobium × pubescens, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, with four petals. It is typically found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides.

Epilobium × pubescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in challenging environments.
