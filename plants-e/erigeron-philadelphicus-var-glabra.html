Erigeron philadelphicus var. glabra

Erigeron philadelphicus var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows and roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety 'glabra' is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems compared to other varieties of Erigeron philadelphicus.
