Eriospermum thyrsoideum

Eriospermum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the mountain erythronium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces a tall, unbranched flowering stem that bears a single, showy, white to pale yellow flower with six petals. Its flowering period typically occurs in early spring, and it is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
