Elaeocarpus coloides subsp. coloides

Elaeocarpus coloides subsp.

coloides, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland tropical forests and is valued for its durable, aromatic timber.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
