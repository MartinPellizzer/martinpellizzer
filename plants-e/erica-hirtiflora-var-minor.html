Erica hirtiflora var. minor

Erica hirtiflora var. minor, commonly known as the lesser heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
