Erica cretica

Erica cretica, commonly known as the Heath or Heath Erica, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Erica cretica thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
