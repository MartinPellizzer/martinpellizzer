Euterpe globosa

Euterpe globosa, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or buriti palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of sweet, edible fruits. The fruits are often consumed raw or used in traditional remedies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
