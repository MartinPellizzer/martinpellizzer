Eugenia cassapensis

Eugenia cassapensis, commonly known as the Java apple or water apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically round or oblong, with a smooth, thin skin and a juicy, acidic flesh. Its roots are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
