Eugenia fortis

Eugenia fortis, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible, red, berry-like fruits that are often used in culinary applications.

Eugenia fortis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
