Elliottia racemosa

Elliottia racemosa, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture.

Elliottia racemosa plays an important ecological role in its native environment by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
