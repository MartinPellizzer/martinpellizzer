Eupatorium gracillimum

Eupatorium gracillimum, commonly known as slender boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Eupatorium gracillimum is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
