Euphorbia chamaerrhodos var. hirsuta

Euphorbia chamaerrhodos var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, spiny stems that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the characteristic flower-like structures of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
