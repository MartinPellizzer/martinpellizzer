Echium vulgare var. carneum

Echium vulgare var. carneum, commonly known as the red poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, papery petals and a distinctive, spiny capsule that contains numerous seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of natural dye.
