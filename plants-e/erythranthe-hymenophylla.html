Erythranthe hymenophylla

Erythranthe hymenophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf erythranthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in specialized habitats and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
