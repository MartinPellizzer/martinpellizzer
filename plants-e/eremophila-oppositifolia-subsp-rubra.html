Eremophila oppositifolia subsp. rubra

Eremophila oppositifolia subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, opposite leaves and showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by birds, particularly honeyeaters.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
