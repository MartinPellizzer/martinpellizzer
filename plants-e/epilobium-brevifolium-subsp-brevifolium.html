Epilobium brevifolium subsp. brevifolium

Epilobium brevifolium subsp.

brevifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils in woodland edges, meadows, and streambanks.

The plant features erect, branching stems that are covered in short, glandular hairs. Its leaves are narrowly elliptic to lanceolate, with a hairy surface and a pointed tip.

Small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in dense spikes, blooming from June to August.
