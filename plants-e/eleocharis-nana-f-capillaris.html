Eleocharis nana f. capillaris

Eleocharis nana f. capillaris, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, cylindrical stems that often form tight clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, white to pale brown flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Its delicate appearance and adaptability make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
