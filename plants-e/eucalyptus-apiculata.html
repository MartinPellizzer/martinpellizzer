Eucalyptus apiculata

Eucalyptus apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or cream in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Eucalyptus apiculata is well adapted to drought conditions and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
