Elatine hydropiper subsp. campylosperma

Elatine hydropiper subsp.

campylosperma, commonly known as water pepper, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Elatinaceae. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered with a waxy coating. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
