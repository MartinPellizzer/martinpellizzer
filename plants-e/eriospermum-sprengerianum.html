Eriospermum sprengerianum

Eriospermum sprengerianum, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Eriospermum sprengerianum is also known for its ability to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.
