Ericameria riskindii

Ericameria riskindii, commonly known as the Riskind's goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has aromatic foliage with a resinous scent, and its leaves are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Ericameria riskindii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
