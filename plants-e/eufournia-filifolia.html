Eufournia filifolia

Eufournia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Eufournia filifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and resilience.
