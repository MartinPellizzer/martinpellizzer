Entada parvifolia

Entada parvifolia, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The tree produces long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, seed-rich seeds. Its roots and seeds contain toxic compounds, which have been used traditionally in various cultural practices.

The plant is also valued for its durable wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
