Eriocephala schumanniana subsp. claviceps

Eriocephala schumanniana subsp.

claviceps, commonly known as the Schumann's eriocephala, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and features small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically produces pale yellow to reddish-brown blooms, which are often covered in fine hairs. Native to South America, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
