Echinolaena polystachya

Echinolaena polystachya, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, spikelet-like flowers, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

Echinolaena polystachya is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
