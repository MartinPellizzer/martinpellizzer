Eupatorium alternifolium subsp. crassipes

Eupatorium alternifolium subsp.

crassipes, commonly known as broadleaf eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
