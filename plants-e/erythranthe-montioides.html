Erythranthe montioides

Erythranthe montioides, commonly known as the Sierra stonecress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. It has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate, and its stems are often branched.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
