Eucalyptus kombolgiensis

Eucalyptus kombolgiensis, commonly known as the Kombolgie eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green or cream in color.

This species is often found in wet sclerophyll forests and is valued for its aromatic oils and ecological role in its habitat.
