Eugenia tapiaka

Eugenia tapiaka, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red, segmented fruits. The fruit's pericarp contains a white, aromatic flesh that is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
