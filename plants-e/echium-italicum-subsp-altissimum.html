Echium italicum subsp. altissimum

Echium italicum subsp.

altissimum, commonly known as the tall evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
