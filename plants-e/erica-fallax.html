Erica fallax

Erica fallax, commonly known as the evergreen heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and summer. Erica fallax thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
