Eucalyptus ebbanoensis subsp. glauciramula

Eucalyptus ebbanoensis subsp.

glauciramula, commonly known as the Grey-leafed Mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

The flowers are typically small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and are pollinated by insects. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
