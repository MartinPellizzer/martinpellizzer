Elaeocarpus raphiolepidiifolius

Elaeocarpus raphiolepidiifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
