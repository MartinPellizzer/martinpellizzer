Eugenia expallens

Eugenia expallens, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

Eugenia expallens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
