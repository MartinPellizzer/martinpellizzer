Epilobium anagallidifolium var. pseudoscaposum

Epilobium anagallidifolium var. pseudoscaposum, commonly known as the slender willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The flowers are typically small, with four petals, and are often pink or reddish in color. This variant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
