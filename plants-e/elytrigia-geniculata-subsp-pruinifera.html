Elytrigia geniculata subsp. pruinifera

Elytrigia geniculata subsp.

pruinifera, commonly known as the pruinose bentgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in moist, acidic habitats such as bogs and fens, where it thrives in nutrient-poor conditions.
