Echinocactus bodenbenderianus

Echinocactus bodenbenderianus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a slow growth rate and the ability to store water in its thick stem.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
