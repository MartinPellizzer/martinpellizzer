Erythronium maculatum

Erythronium maculatum, commonly known as the spotted crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features a rosette of heart-shaped, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellowish spots. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Erythronium maculatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and early blooming habit.
