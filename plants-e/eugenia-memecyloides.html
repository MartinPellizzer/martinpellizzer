Eugenia memecyloides

Eugenia memecyloides, commonly known as the little apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
