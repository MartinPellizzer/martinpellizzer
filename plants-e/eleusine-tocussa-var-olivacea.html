Eleusine tocussa var. olivacea

Eleusine tocussa var. olivacea, commonly known as the olive bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fibrous roots make it suitable for erosion control and soil stabilization in degraded lands.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
