Eryngium billardierei var. meiocephalum

Eryngium billardierei var. meiocephalum, commonly known as the narrow-headed thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It features spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flower structure.
