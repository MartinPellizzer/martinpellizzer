Eugenia leptalea

Eugenia leptalea, commonly known as the slender satinwood, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
