Erica parkeri

Erica parkeri, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica parkeri produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
