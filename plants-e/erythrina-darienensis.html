Erythrina darienensis

Erythrina darienensis, commonly known as the Darien erythrina, is a striking tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vivid red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences and attract a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its structure is typical of the Erythrina genus, featuring thorn-like structures on the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has potential uses in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
