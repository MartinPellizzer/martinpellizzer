Eupatorium silphiifolium

Eupatorium silphiifolium, commonly known as purpletop, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and prairies.

Eupatorium silphiifolium is also known for its medicinal uses by indigenous peoples.
