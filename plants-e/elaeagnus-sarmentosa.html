Elaeagnus sarmentosa

Elaeagnus sarmentosa, commonly known as silver berry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, fragrant leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Elaeagnus sarmentosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
