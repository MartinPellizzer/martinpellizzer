Ericoides massonii

Ericoides massonii, commonly known as the Masson's ericoid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ericoides massonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
