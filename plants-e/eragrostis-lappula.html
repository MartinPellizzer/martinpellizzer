Eragrostis lappula

Eragrostis lappula, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and woodland edges. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
