Eriope macrostachya var. amazonica

Eriope macrostachya var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian eriope, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a water-holding structure typical of bromeliads. The plant produces showy, red-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This variety is well adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of the Amazonian understory, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils.
