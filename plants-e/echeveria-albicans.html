Echeveria albicans

Echeveria albicans, commonly known as the white echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of pale pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
