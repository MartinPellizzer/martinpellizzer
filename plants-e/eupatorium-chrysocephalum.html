Eupatorium chrysocephalum

Eupatorium chrysocephalum, commonly known as goldenhead, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features showy, yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
