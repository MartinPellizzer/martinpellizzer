Equisetum variegatum var. wilsonii

Equisetum variegatum var. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's variable horsetail, is a variant of the variable horsetail, a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems that are typically green with some yellow or brownish pigmentation. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, which are often hidden beneath the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its ancient lineage and unique morphology make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
