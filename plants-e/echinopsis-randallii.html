Echinopsis randallii

Echinopsis randallii, commonly known as the Randall's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical or cylindrical shape and its ability to produce vibrant, showy flowers during the warmer months. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and they bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Echinopsis randallii is popular among cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
