Eleocharis flavescens var. thermalis

Eleocharis flavescens var. thermalis, commonly known as the thermal sedge, is a flowering plant native to regions with warm, wet climates.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is distinguished by its ability to tolerate high temperatures, making it a characteristic species of thermal environments.
