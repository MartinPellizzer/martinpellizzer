Erysimum comatum var. drenowskii

Erysimum comatum var. drenowskii, commonly known as the Drenowsky's wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
