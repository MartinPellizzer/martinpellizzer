Excoecaria biglandulosa var. stenophylla

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its distinctive foliage and unique botanical features.

It possesses opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glaucous and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, orange-red capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically found in lowland tropical regions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking leaf coloration.
