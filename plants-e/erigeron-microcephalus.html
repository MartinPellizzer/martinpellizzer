Erigeron microcephalus

Erigeron microcephalus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its small, blue to violet flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
