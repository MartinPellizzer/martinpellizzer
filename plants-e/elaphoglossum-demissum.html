Elaphoglossum demissum

Elaphoglossum demissum, commonly known as the slender holly fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are usually less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a compact growth habit with a short, creeping rhizome and produces spore-bearing sporangia on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and shaded garden settings.
