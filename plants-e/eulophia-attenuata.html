Eulophia attenuata

Eulophia attenuata, commonly known as the slender eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, creeping rhizome and a rosette of linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences bearing small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and mountainous regions.

Eulophia attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
