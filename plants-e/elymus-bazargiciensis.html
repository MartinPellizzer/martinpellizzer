Elymus bazargiciensis

Elymus bazargiciensis, commonly known as the Bazargic grass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, linear leaves. This plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Elymus bazargiciensis is often found in alpine meadows and on slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing panicle-like inflorescences that contribute to its ecological significance.
