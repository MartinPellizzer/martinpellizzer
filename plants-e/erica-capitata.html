Erica capitata

Erica capitata, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica capitata is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
