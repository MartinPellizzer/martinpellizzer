Eugenia mascarensis

Eugenia mascarensis, commonly known as the Mascarene guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically sour and have a high acidity level. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
