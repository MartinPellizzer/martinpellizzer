Erythrodes robusta

Erythrodes robusta, commonly known as the robust redwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces woody capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous seeds.

Erythrodes robusta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
