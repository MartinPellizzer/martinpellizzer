Eupatorium laevigatum f. lilacinum

Eupatorium laevigatum f. lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac eupatorium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, lavender-colored flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
