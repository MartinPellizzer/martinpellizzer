Erythrina glabrescens

Erythrina glabrescens, commonly known as the flame tree or the Australian flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its striking red-orange flowers.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is native to regions of Australia and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Erythrina glabrescens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
