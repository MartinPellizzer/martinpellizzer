Eulychnia breviflora subsp. iquiquensis

Eulychnia breviflora subsp.

iquiquensis, commonly known as the Iquique cactus, is a species native to the coastal regions of northern Chile. It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows in sandy soils along the Pacific coast.

The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and moths. Its structure is adapted to withstand strong coastal winds and salt spray.

The subspecies is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in arid coastal environments.
