Epilobium pycnostachyum

Epilobium pycnostachyum, commonly known as sticky willow or woolly willow, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its glandular-hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, sticky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Epilobium pycnostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
