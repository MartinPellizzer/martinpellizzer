Echinocactus acuatus var. tetracanthus

Echinocactus acuatus var. tetracanthus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical shape and dense, spiny ribs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique structure and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and collections.
