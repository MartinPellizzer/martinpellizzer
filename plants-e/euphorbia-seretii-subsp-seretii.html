Euphorbia seretii subsp. seretii

Euphorbia seretii subsp.

seretii, commonly known as the Seret's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and sparse, fleshy leaves that help it store water.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
