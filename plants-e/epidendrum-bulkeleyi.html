Epidendrum bulkeleyi

Epidendrum bulkeleyi, commonly known as the Bulkeley's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in seasonal environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip, and it blooms during the spring and summer months.

Epidendrum bulkeleyi is often found in forest understories, where it thrives in humid and shaded conditions.
