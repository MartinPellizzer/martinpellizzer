Erucastrum virgatum subsp. virgatum

Erucastrum virgatum subsp.

virgatum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
