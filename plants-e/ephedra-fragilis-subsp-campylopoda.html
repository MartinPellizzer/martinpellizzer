Ephedra fragilis subsp. campylopoda

Ephedra fragilis subsp.

campylopoda, commonly known as the slender ephedra, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
