Eupatorium buchii

Eupatorium buchii, commonly known as the white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetlands and moist soils, making it a common sight in swampy areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
