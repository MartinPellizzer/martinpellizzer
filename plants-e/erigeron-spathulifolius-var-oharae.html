Erigeron spathulifolius var. oharae

Erigeron spathulifolius var. oharae, commonly known as Ohara's narrow-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and spoon-shaped, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
