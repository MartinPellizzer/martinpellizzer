Eupatorium stigmatosum var. violaceum

Eupatorium stigmatosum var. violaceum, commonly known as purple false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
