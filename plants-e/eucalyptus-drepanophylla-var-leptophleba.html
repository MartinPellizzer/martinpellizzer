Eucalyptus drepanophylla var. leptophleba

Eucalyptus drepanophylla var. leptophleba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are usually red or purple, which are followed by woody capsules containing the seeds. It is a hardy shrub or small tree that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
