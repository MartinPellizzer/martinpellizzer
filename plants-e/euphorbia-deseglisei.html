Euphorbia deseglisei

Euphorbia deseglisei, commonly known as the Desert Spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss.

Euphorbia deseglisei is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
