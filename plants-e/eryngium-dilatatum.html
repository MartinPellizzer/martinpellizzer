Eryngium dilatatum

Eryngium dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its habitat includes dry, rocky soils in open woodlands and meadows.

Eryngium dilatatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
