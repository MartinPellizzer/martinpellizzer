Erica montis-hominis

Erica montis-hominis, commonly known as the man's mountain heath, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica montis-hominis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
