Euphorbia viminea f. carolensis

Euphorbia viminea f. carolensis, commonly known as Carolinian threadleaf spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and thread-like, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.
