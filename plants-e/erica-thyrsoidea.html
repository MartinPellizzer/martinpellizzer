Erica thyrsoidea

Erica thyrsoidea, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen.

Erica thyrsoidea is well adapted to poor, sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
