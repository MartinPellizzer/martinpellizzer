Eryngium odoratum

Eryngium odoratum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, velvety hairs.

Eryngium odoratum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant nature.
