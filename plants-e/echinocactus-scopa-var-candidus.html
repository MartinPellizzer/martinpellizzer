Echinocactus scopa var. candidus

Echinocactus scopa var. candidus, commonly known as the white-pearled cactus, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with prominent, white spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiky form. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
