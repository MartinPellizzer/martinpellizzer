Eugenia tierneyana

Eugenia tierneyana, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces edible, red, berry-like fruits that are a source of nectar for birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Eugenia tierneyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and seasonal floral display.
