Excoecaria arguta

Excoecaria arguta, commonly known as the Madagascar arrowroot or arrowleaf, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch, making it an economically important species in its native region.
