Erythronium revolutum var. watsonii

Erythronium revolutum var. watsonii, commonly known as Watson's fawn lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive green throat. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variety is noted for its early spring blooming habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
