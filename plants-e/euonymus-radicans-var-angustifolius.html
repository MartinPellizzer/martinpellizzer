Euonymus radicans var. angustifolius

Euonymus radicans var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved trailing twiner, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover slopes or trellises.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for erosion control and ground cover in woodland settings.
