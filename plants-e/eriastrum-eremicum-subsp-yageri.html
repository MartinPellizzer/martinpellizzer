Eriastrum eremicum subsp. yageri

Eriastrum eremicum subsp.

yageri, commonly known as Yager's eriole, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, glandular-stemmed leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
