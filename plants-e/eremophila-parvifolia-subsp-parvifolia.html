Eremophila parvifolia subsp. parvifolia

Eremophila parvifolia subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are often pink or red in color. It is part of the Myrtaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
