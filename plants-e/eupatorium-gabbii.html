Eupatorium gabbii

Eupatorium gabbii, commonly known as Gabb's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wet meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium gabbii is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
