Eugenia maingayi

Eugenia maingayi, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often in moist tropical forests.

Eugenia maingayi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
