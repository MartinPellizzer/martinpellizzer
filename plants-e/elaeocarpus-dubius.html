Elaeocarpus dubius

Elaeocarpus dubius, commonly known as the white sand apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit, which is edible, has a tough outer shell and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
