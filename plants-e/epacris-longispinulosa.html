Epacris longispinulosa

Epacris longispinulosa, commonly known as the long-spined epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in heathlands and sclerophyll forests. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are pollinated by birds and insects.
