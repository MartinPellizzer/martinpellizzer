Eulophia vera

Eulophia vera, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, often branched stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Eulophia vera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
