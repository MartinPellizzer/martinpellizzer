Erica densifolia

Erica densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact foliage and small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica densifolia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
