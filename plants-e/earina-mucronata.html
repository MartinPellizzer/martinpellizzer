Earina mucronata

Earina mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.

Earina mucronata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
