Eryngium cuneifolium

Eryngium cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eryngium cuneifolium is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
