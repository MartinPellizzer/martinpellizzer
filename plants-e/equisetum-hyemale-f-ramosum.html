Equisetum hyemale f. ramosum

Equisetum hyemale f. ramosum, commonly known as the scaly horse-hair fern, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of stiff, scaly, and often branching stems. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of the stems, which are typically dark brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Paleozoic era.
