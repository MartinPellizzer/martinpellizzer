Erica multiflora subsp. hyblaea

Erica multiflora subsp.

hyblaea, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife. Erica multiflora subsp.

hyblaea is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscaping.
