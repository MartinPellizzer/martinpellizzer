Epimedium alpinum var. rubrum

Epimedium alpinum var. rubrum, commonly known as the red-leaf barrenwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-red foliage, which contrasts with its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and hardy nature.
