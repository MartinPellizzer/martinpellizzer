Eragrostis megastachya var. densiflora

Eragrostis megastachya var. densiflora, commonly known as dense-flowered weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, drooping inflorescences that give it a weeping appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, typically in shades of green to brown, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a fine texture that makes it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
