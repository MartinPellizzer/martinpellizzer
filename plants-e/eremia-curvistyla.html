Eremia curvistyla

Eremia curvistyla, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy and gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically fleshy and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Eremia curvistyla plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.
