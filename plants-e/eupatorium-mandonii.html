Eupatorium mandonii

Eupatorium mandonii, commonly known as Mandon's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has serrated leaves and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat fevers and digestive issues.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
