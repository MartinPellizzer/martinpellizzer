Eria bicristata

Eria bicristata, commonly known as the two-crowned eria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and features small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eria bicristata is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
