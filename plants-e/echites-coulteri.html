Echites coulteri

Echites coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's echites, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

The species is characterized by its unique fruit structure, which consists of a spiny capsule containing several seeds.
