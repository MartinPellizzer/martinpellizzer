Eucalyptus thamnoides

Eucalyptus thamnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and small, globular flower clusters that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its essential oils, particularly eucalyptol, are used in traditional and modern medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Eucalyptus thamnoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
