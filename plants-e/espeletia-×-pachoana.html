Espeletia × pachoana

Espeletia × pachoana, commonly known as the Pachonia, is a hybrid plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or volcanic soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Espeletia × pachoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
