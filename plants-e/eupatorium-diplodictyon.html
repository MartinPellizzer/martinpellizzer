Eupatorium diplodictyon

Eupatorium diplodictyon, commonly known as the slender boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a height ranging from 1 to 3 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are pale blue to white and are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.
