Equisetum telmateia var. ramulosum

Equisetum telmateia var. ramulosum, commonly known as the ramose horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, segmented stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of the stem, which are clustered in a distinctive, cone-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with unique botanical features.
