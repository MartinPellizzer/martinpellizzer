Erythrina guatemalensis

Erythrina guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan coral tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically smooth and woody. Erythrina guatemalensis is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.

It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
