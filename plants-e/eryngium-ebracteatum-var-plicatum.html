Eryngium ebracteatum var. plicatum

Eryngium ebracteatum var. plicatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers surrounded by bracts.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
