Eucalyptus × beaselyi

Eucalyptus × beaselyi, commonly known as the Sydney peppermint, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green leaves that release a strong peppermint scent when crushed. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale pink, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and for essential oil production.
