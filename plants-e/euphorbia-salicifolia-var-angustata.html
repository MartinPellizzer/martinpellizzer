Euphorbia salicifolia var. angustata

Euphorbia salicifolia var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is a common feature among the Euphorbia genus. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often ornamental species in native plant gardens.
