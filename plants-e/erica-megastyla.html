Erica megastyla

Erica megastyla, commonly known as the giant heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in spring. Erica megastyla is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
