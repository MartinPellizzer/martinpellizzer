Eugenia acutissima

Eugenia acutissima, commonly known as the purple-leafed myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep purple or bronze color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia acutissima is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
