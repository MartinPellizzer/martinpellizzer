Echinocactus denudatus f. scheidelianus

Echinocactus denudatus f. scheidelianus, commonly known as the Scheidelianus echinocactus, is a unique variation of the basic echinocactus species.

It is characterized by its reduced spines and a more compact, globular shape compared to the typical form. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem that is often covered in a layer of white, waxy bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its distinct appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized plant in botanical collections.
