Epidendrum harrisonianum

Epidendrum harrisonianum, commonly known as the Harrison's Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, often found growing on trees in humid tropical environments. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a contrasting lip. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Epidendrum harrisonianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
