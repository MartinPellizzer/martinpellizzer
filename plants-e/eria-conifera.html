Eria conifera

Eria conifera, commonly known as the dwarf pine, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its coniferous appearance, with needle-like leaves and a compact growth habit. The plant typically grows at elevations above 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cold, rocky environments. Its cones are small and typically contain a single seed, which is dispersed by wind.

Eria conifera plays a significant role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat and is valued for its resilience in extreme conditions.
