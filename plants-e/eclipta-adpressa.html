Eclipta adpressa

Eclipta adpressa, commonly known as false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 60 cm tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
