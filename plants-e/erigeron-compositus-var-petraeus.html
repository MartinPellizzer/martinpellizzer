Erigeron compositus var. petraeus

Erigeron compositus var. petraeus, commonly known as the rock eryngo, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as cliffs and slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
