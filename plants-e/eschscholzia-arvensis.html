Eschscholzia arvensis

Eschscholzia arvensis, commonly known as the field poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces slender, erect stems and has palmately lobed leaves.

The flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by capsule fruits that release seeds when mature.
