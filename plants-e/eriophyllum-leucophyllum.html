Eriophyllum leucophyllum

Eriophyllum leucophyllum, commonly known as the white woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish-white cobweb-like foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its drought tolerance.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
