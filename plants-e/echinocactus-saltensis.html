Echinocactus saltensis

Echinocactus saltensis, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. Its thick, ribbed structure helps it store water, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Echinocactus saltensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
