Eupatorium oyadense

Eupatorium oyadense, commonly known as the Oyadense Boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Puerto Rico and the Virgin Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white to pale blue in color.

Eupatorium oyadense is known for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
