Eugenia cloiselii

Eugenia cloiselii, commonly known as the cloiselii guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Eugenia cloiselii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in home gardens.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
