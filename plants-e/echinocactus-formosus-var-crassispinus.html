Echinocactus formosus var. crassispinus

Echinocactus formosus var. crassispinus, commonly known as the Crassispine Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, sharp spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
