Erysimum ochroleucum proles pyrenaicum

Erysimum ochroleucum proles pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It features basal and stem leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are usually yellow or pale yellow, blooming in early spring.
