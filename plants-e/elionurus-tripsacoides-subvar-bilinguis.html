Elionurus tripsacoides subvar. bilinguis

Elionurus tripsacoides subvar. bilinguis, commonly known as the two-lobed panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
