Eugenia bagensis var. angustifolia

Eugenia bagensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red or purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
