Eranthis uncinata f. puberula

Eranthis uncinata f. puberula, commonly known as the hairy corydalis, is a botanical variant of the corydalis genus.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features showy, early spring blooms in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it the "puberula" designation.

This form is often found in woodland and meadow habitats, where it thrives in partial shade.
