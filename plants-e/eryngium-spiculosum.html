Eryngium spiculosum

Eryngium spiculosum, commonly known as the spiny eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually covered in small, stiff spines.

Eryngium spiculosum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for beneficial insects.
