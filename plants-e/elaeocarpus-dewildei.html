Elaeocarpus dewildei

Elaeocarpus dewildei, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
