Erigeron keredjensis

Erigeron keredjensis, commonly known as the Keredj fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
