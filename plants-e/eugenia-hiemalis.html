Eugenia hiemalis

Eugenia hiemalis, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is typically grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Eugenia hiemalis is often used in landscaping for its year-round beauty and ability to thrive in humid climates.
