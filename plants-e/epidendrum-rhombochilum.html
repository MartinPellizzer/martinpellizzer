Epidendrum rhombochilum

Epidendrum rhombochilum, commonly known as the rhombic-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, typically with a waxy texture.

Epidendrum rhombochilum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
