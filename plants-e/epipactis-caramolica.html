Epipactis caramolica

Epipactis caramolica, commonly known as the Caramolica helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and its striking, pale green to yellowish flowers with prominent purple markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky slopes. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits a complex structure with a long, slender rhizome.

Epipactis caramolica is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, often forming dense colonies in its native environment.
