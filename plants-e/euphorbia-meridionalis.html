Euphorbia meridionalis

Euphorbia meridionalis, commonly known as the southern spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
