Eugenia sipaliwinensis

Eugenia sipaliwinensis, commonly known as the Sierra Leone ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical, with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, the species is also of interest in conservation efforts.
