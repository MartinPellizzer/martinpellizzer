Evolvulus frankenioides var. subglaber

Evolvulus frankenioides var. subglaber, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial herb with hairy, glandular stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to produce numerous small, showy flowers throughout the growing season.
