Eremophila glabra subsp. albicans

Eremophila glabra subsp.

albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands and heathlands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and landscaping.
