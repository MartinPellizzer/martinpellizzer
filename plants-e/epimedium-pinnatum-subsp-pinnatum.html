Epimedium pinnatum subsp. pinnatum

Epimedium pinnatum subsp.

pinnatum, commonly known as the barrenwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and distinctive, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are borne on upright, branched inflorescences. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is commonly found in woodlands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
