Epidendrum molinae

Epidendrum molinae, commonly known as the Molina's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along riverbanks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Epidendrum molinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
