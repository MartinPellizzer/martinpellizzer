Erica betsileana

Erica betsileana, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It produces small, dark blue or black berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Erica betsileana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
