Emilia palhinhana

Emilia palhinhana, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Emilia palhinhana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
