Erica plumigera

Erica plumigera, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact form and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Erica plumigera thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
