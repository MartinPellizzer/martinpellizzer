Epipactis muelleri

Epipactis muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and wetlands. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
