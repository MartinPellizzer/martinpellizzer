Epidendrum ballonii

Epidendrum ballonii, commonly known as the balloon orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of showy, balloon-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has fleshy, strap-like leaves that help store water, adapting it to its often humid and shaded habitat. It thrives in well-draining potting media and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum ballonii is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
