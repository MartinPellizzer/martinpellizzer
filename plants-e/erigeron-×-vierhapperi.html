Erigeron × vierhapperi

Erigeron × vierhapperi, commonly known as the Blueweed or Blue Mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray foliage and showy white to blue-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

It is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
