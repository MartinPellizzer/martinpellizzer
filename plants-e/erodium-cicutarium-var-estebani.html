Erodium cicutarium var. estebani

Erodium cicutarium var. estebani, commonly known as the Mexican bedstraw, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pink or red flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the genus Erodium. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions of the Americas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its adaptability, it is often considered an invasive species in certain areas.
