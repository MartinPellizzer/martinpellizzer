Emerus longifolius

Emerus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved emerus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a fibrous root system adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
