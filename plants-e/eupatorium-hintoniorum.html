Eupatorium hintoniorum

Eupatorium hintoniorum, commonly known as Hinton's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.

Eupatorium hintoniorum is known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
