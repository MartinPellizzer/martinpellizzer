Echinocactus napinus

Echinocactus napinus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a ribbed structure with spines that provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture efficiently during rare rainfall events.

Echinocactus napinus is well-suited to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
