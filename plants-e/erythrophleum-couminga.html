Erythrophleum couminga

Erythrophleum couminga, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers. The tree produces woody pods containing flat, oval seeds, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
