Eugenia itaguahiensis

Eugenia itaguahiensis, commonly known as the Itaguai orange or Itaguai myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, specifically found in Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, smooth, orange-colored drupe that is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor.

It is often used in local traditional medicine and as a source of natural flavoring.
