Eragrostis dekindtii

Eragrostis dekindtii, commonly known as the De Kindt's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.

Eragrostis dekindtii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
