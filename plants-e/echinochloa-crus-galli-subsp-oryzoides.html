Echinochloa crus-galli subsp. oryzoides

Echinochloa crus-galli subsp.

oryzoides, commonly known as false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown spikelets. It is often found in disturbed soils, wetlands, and agricultural fields.

This species is considered a weed in rice paddies and can compete with crops for nutrients and water.
