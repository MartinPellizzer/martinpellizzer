Erythrocarpus spicatus

Erythrocarpus spicatus, commonly known as the red-fruited sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by water or animals. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in estuarine or tidal environments.

Erythrocarpus spicatus plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
