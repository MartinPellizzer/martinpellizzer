Eugenia pinifolia

Eugenia pinifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are narrow and pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Eugenia pinifolia is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
