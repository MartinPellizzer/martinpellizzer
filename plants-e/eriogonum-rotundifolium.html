Eriogonum rotundifolium

Eriogonum rotundifolium, commonly known as the roundleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
