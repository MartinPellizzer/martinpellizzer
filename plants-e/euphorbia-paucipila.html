Euphorbia paucipila

Euphorbia paucipila, commonly known as the desert euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Euphorbia paucipila is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
