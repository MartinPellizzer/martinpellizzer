Epidendrum krugii

Epidendrum krugii, commonly known as the Krug's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Epidendrum krugii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
