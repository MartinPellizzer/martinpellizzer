Erodium × fallax

Erodium × fallax, commonly known as the hybrid common stork's bill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a distinctive, twisted, spiral-shaped capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Erodium × fallax is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
