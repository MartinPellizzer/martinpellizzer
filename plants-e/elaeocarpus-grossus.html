Elaeocarpus grossus

Elaeocarpus grossus, commonly known as the giant elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
