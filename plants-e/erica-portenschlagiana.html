Erica portenschlagiana

Erica portenschlagiana, commonly known as the Alpine heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family, Ericaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly the Alps. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Erica portenschlagiana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
