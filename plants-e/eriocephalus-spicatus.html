Eriocephalus spicatus

Eriocephalus spicatus, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Eriocephalus spicatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
