Eugenioides ferrugineum

Eugenioides ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with a reddish-brown underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, reddish-purple berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
