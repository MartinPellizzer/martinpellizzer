Erysimum saxosum

Erysimum saxosum, commonly known as the saxifrage mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found growing in rocky or calcareous soils. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically basal, simple, and slightly hairy.

Erysimum saxosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
