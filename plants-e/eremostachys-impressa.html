Eremostachys impressa

Eremostachys impressa, commonly known as the wrinkled goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wrinkled leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
