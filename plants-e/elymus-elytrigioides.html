Elymus elytrigioides

Elymus elytrigioides, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open areas, meadows, and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
