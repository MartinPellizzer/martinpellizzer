Excoecaria farinosa

Excoecaria farinosa, commonly known as the farinose croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by orange or red berries. It is adapted to thrive in humid, well-drained soils and can tolerate partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it may be invasive in some areas.
