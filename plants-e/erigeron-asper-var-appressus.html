Erigeron asper var. appressus

Erigeron asper var. appressus, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy ray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it a distinctive texture.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
