Erythrostemon fimbriatus

Erythrostemon fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its flowers are borne on elongated, spikelike inflorescences and bloom in late summer.

Erythrostemon fimbriatus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
