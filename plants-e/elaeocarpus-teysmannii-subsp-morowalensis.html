Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp. morowalensis

Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp.

morowalensis, commonly known as the Morowalensi elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it often grows in lowland rainforests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
