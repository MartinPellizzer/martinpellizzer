Erica bibracteata

Erica bibracteata, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica bibracteata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
