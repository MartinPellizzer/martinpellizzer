Euonymus gracilis angustifolius

Euonymus gracilis angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, bright orange to red berries in the fall. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is relatively low maintenance once established.
