Eriogonum flavum var. androsaceum

Eriogonum flavum var. androsaceum, commonly known as the yellow flax or Androsace-like eriogonum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are generally hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
