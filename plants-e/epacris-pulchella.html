Epacris pulchella

Epacris pulchella, commonly known as the pink epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, bloom in spring and are attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Epacris pulchella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively hardy in suitable climates.
