Eupatorium turbacense var. ovatifolia

Eupatorium turbacense var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovate-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
