Elaeagnus tsukubana

Elaeagnus tsukubana, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Elaeagnus tsukubana is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing properties.
