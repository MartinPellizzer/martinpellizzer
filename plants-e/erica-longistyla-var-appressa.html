Erica longistyla var. appressa

Erica longistyla var. appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compact, dense foliage with narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form attractive ground covers in suitable climates.
