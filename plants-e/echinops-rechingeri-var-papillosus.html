Echinops rechingeri var. papillosus

Echinops rechingeri var. papillosus, commonly known as the hairy thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble thimbles. The plant produces yellow to orange ray flowers, which are typically shorter than those of other echinops species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
