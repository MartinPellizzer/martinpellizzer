Eleotis buraeavii

Eleotis buraeavii, commonly known as the Buraeavii water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and rice fields. The plant has feathery, delicate fronds that float on the water surface, with roots hanging below. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, muddy substrates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
