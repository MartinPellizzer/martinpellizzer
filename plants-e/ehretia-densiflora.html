Ehretia densiflora

Ehretia densiflora, commonly known as the thick-leaved ehretia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
