Eriocaulon guineense

Eriocaulon guineense, commonly known as Guinea spikegrass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a small, herbaceous plant typically found in wet or marshy habitats across tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear whorled clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine, silky bloom, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eriocaulon guineense plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
