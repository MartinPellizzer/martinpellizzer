Eupatorium chinense var. tripartitum

Eupatorium chinense var. tripartitum, commonly known as Chinese boneset, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties.
