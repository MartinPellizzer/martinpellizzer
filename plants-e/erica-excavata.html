Erica excavata

Erica excavata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments. Erica excavata produces pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
