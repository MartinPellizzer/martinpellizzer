Erica granulata

Erica granulata, commonly known as the granulate heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Erica granulata typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
