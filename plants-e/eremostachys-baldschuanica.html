Eremostachys baldschuanica

Eremostachys baldschuanica, commonly known as the Balkhash wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Eremostachys baldschuanica is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
