Epilobium puberulum

Epilobium puberulum, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, erect stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Epilobium puberulum is known for its ecological value, providing nectar for bees and other pollinators.
