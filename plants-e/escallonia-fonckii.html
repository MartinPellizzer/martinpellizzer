Escallonia fonckii

Escallonia fonckii, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Escalloniaceae and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Escallonia fonckii is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal and arid environments.
