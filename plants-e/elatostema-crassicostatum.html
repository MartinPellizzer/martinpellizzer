Elatostema crassicostatum

Elatostema crassicostatum, commonly known as the broad-costate elatostema, is a member of the Urticaceae family, characterized by its small, succulent leaves.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a prostrate or creeping growth habit with hairy stems and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, typical of the genus Elatostema.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its role in local ecosystems as a ground cover plant.
