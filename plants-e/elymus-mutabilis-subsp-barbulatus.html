Elymus mutabilis subsp. barbulatus

Elymus mutabilis subsp.

barbulatus, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a soft, silky texture, which contribute to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and ornamental landscapes.
