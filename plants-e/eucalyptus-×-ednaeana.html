Eucalyptus × ednaeana

Eucalyptus × ednaeana, commonly known as the Ednaeana gum tree, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Eucalyptus camaldulensis and Eucalyptus tereticornis.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark that peels in thin, fibrous layers. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a dull green upper surface with a whitish underside. It produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from pale yellow to cream.

This species is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.
