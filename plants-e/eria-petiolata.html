Eria petiolata

Eria petiolata, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often pendulous stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive lip.

Eria petiolata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
