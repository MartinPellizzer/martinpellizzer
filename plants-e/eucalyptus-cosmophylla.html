Eucalyptus cosmophylla

Eucalyptus cosmophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eucalypt, is a tall tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to greyish hairs. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. Its bark is smooth and peels in thin, papery strips, and it is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands.

The species is known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
