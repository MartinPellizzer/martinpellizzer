Eugenia arnoldsonii

Eugenia arnoldsonii, commonly known as the Arnoldson's guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
