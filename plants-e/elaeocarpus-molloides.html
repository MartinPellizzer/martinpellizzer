Elaeocarpus molloides

Elaeocarpus molloides, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically enclosed in a fleshy, woody capsule. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
