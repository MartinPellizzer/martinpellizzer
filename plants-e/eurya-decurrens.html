Eurya decurrens

Eurya decurrens, commonly known as the narrowleaf eurya, is a species of evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. Eurya decurrens typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
