Elaeagnus thunbergii var. coreana

Elaeagnus thunbergii var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, silvery-white leaves with a distinctive glandular texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. It is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and autumn coloration, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
