Epidendrum xanthinum

Epidendrum xanthinum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features long, arching stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. This species thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its striking floral display makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
