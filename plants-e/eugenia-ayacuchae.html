Eugenia ayacuchae

Eugenia ayacuchae, commonly known as the Ayacuchan guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, similar to a guava, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Due to its ornamental value and unique characteristics, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
