Epidendrum melistagoides

Epidendrum melistagoides, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear numerous showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Epidendrum melistagoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
