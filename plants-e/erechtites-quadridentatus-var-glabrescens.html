Erechtites quadridentatus var. glabrescens

Erechtites quadridentatus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and often have a slightly hairy underside.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced hairiness compared to the typical form, giving it a more refined appearance.
