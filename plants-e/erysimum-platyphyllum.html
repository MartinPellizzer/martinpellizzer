Erysimum platyphyllum

Erysimum platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum platyphyllum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
