Euphorbia masirahensis

Euphorbia masirahensis, commonly known as the Masirah spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with unique defensive mechanisms.

Euphorbia masirahensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight.
