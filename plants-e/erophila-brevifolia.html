Erophila brevifolia

Erophila brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb with a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers surrounded by pale green bracts, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Erophila brevifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
