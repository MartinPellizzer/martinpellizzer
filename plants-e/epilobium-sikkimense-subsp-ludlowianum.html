Epilobium sikkimense subsp. ludlowianum

Epilobium sikkimense subsp.

ludlowianum, commonly known as the Sikkim willow, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal. It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
