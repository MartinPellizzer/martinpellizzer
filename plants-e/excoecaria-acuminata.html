Excoecaria acuminata

Excoecaria acuminata, commonly known as the Cannonball Tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, greenish fruit that turns red when mature. The plant has thick, waxy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The fruit is edible when ripe but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.
