Epilobium japonicum

Epilobium japonicum, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Epilobium japonicum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
