Eryngium elegans var. microcephalum

Eryngium elegans var. microcephalum, commonly known as the dwarf thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features compact, spiny stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant.
