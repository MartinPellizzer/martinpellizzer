Echinocactus odieri var. mebbesii

Echinocactus odieri var. mebbesii, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often spiny and covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
