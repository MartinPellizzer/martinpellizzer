Esterhuysenia mucronata

Esterhuysenia mucronata, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Esterhuysenia mucronata is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
