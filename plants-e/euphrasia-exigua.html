Euphrasia exigua

Euphrasia exigua, commonly known as the small yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular with prominent blue or yellow corollas.

Euphrasia exigua is often found in open meadows and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
