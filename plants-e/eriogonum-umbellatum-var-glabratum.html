Eriogonum umbellatum var. glabratum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. glabratum, commonly known as the white sand buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other buckwheat species.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence compared to other varieties of Eriogonum umbellatum.
