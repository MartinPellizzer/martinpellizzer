Eryngium tricuspidatum subsp. bocconei

Eryngium tricuspidatum subsp.

bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are composed of numerous small, tubular florets surrounded by a series of bracts.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
