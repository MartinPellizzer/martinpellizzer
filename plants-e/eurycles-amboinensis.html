Eurycles amboinensis

Eurycles amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom throughout the year.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
