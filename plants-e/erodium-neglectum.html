Erodium neglectum

Erodium neglectum, commonly known as common eryngo, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer, and its leaves are typically lobed or toothed. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, dry fruits that disperse seeds by wind.

Erodium neglectum is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
