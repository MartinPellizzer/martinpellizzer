Elymus nutans var. condensatus

Elymus nutans var. condensatus, commonly known as the dense foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are tightly clustered, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush communities.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems and a potential forage species.
