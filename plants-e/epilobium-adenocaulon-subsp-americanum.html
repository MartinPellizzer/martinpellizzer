Epilobium adenocaulon subsp. americanum

Epilobium adenocaulon subsp.

americanum, commonly known as the American willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and forest edges.

The plant features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are erect with a slightly woody base.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
