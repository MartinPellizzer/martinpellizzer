Euphorbia luticola

Euphorbia luticola, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through features such as thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Euphorbia luticola is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species.
