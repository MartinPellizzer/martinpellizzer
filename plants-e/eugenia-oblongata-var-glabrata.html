Eugenia oblongata var. glabrata

Eugenia oblongata var. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark red berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
