Eupatorium missionum

Eupatorium missionum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
