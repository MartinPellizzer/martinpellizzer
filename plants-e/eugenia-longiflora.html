Eugenia longiflora

Eugenia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The fruit is a small, round berry that changes color from green to red as it matures.
