Embamma

Embamma, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
