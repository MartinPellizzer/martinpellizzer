Excoecaria biglandulosa var. lanceolata

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain toxic compounds, making it potentially hazardous to livestock and humans.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
