Enneastemon ochroleucus

Enneastemon ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered false gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade. Its bright yellow petals are surrounded by blue-green calyces, creating a striking contrast.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
