Eugenia azurensis

Eugenia azurensis, commonly known as the blue eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is small, fleshy, and usually contains several seeds, making it an important food source for local wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some areas.
