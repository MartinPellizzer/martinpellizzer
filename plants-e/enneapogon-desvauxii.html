Enneapogon desvauxii

Enneapogon desvauxii, commonly known as the desert grass or desert lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It plays a role in stabilizing soils in degraded landscapes and is often used in restoration ecology.

Enneapogon desvauxii is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its potential as a forage plant for livestock.
