Euphorbia sturii

Euphorbia sturii, commonly known as the Sturii's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and distinctive yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
