Epilobium pyrricholophum f. macrocarpum

Epilobium pyrricholophum f. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded willow herb, is a botanical variant of the willow herb genus.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy pink to purple flowers in the summer. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, hairy seeds.

This subspecies is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
