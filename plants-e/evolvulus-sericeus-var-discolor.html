Evolvulus sericeus var. discolor

Evolvulus sericeus var. discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative ground cover. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its rapid growth and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice among gardeners.
