Epidendrum brevicaule

Epidendrum brevicaule, commonly known as the short-stemmed epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and relatively short, sturdy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are usually dark green and somewhat fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum brevicaule is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
