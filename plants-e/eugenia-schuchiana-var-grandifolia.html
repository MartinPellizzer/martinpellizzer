Eugenia schuchiana var. grandifolia

Eugenia schuchiana var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered myrtle, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, tropical forest environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The fruit, which is a small, red, berry-like drupe, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic foliage, making it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
