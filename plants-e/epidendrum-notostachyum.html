Epidendrum notostachyum

Epidendrum notostachyum, commonly known as the orange-tinged epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
