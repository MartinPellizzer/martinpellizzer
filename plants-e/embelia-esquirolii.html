Embelia esquirolii

Embelia esquirolii, commonly known as the Mexican embelia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with small elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
