Eugenia bergiana

Eugenia bergiana, commonly known as the African cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
