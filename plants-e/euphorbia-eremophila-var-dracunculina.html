Euphorbia eremophila var. dracunculina

Euphorbia eremophila var. dracunculina, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its stems and leaves.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and rock gardens in dry climates.
