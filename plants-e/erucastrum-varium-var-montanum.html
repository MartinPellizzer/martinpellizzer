Erucastrum varium var. montanum

Erucastrum varium var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain rockcress, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
