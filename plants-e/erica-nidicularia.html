Erica nidicularia

Erica nidicularia, commonly known as the cushion heath, is a dwarf shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of small, needle-like leaves that form a compact, mat-like structure. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Erica nidicularia is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
