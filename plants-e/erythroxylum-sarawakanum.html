Erythroxylum sarawakanum

Erythroxylum sarawakanum, commonly known as the Sarawak euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to Borneo.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
