Euphorbia kerneri

Euphorbia kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with thick, waxy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
