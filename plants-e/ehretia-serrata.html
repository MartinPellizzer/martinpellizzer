Ehretia serrata

Ehretia serrata, commonly known as the serrated ehretia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
