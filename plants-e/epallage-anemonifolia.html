Epallage anemonifolia

Epallage anemonifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf or anemonifoliate epallage, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate leaf structure.

Its leaves are divided into numerous small, delicate lobes that resemble the petals of an anemone, giving it its common name. The plant typically features a compact, bushy growth habit with a central stem that supports the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

The botanical interest of Epallage anemonifolia lies in its complex leaf morphology and its role in certain ecological niches.
