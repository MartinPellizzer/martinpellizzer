Eugenia batucaryensis

Eugenia batucaryensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
