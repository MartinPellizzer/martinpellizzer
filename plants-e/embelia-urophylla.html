Embelia urophylla

Embelia urophylla, commonly known as the white-flowered embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
