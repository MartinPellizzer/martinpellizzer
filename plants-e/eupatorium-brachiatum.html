Eupatorium brachiatum

Eupatorium brachiatum, commonly known as narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Eupatorium brachiatum is often found in areas with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
