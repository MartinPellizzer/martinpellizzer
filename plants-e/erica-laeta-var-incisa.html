Erica laeta var. incisa

Erica laeta var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica laeta var. incisa is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
