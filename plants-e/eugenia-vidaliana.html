Eugenia vidaliana

Eugenia vidaliana, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
