Eupatorium erodiifolium var. canescens

Eupatorium erodiifolium var. canescens, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, swamps, and stream banks. The plant features opposite, rough-textured, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
