Eragrostis nairii

Eragrostis nairii, commonly known as the Nair's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a tufted growth habit with slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers, characteristic of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Eragrostis nairii is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in low-nutrient environments, making it a resilient plant in arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
