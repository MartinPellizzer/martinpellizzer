Eleutherococcus giraldii f. hispidus

Eleutherococcus giraldii f. hispidus, commonly known as the hairy eleutherococcus, is a variant of the eleutherococcus plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
