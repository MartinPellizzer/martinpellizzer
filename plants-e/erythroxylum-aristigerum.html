Erythroxylum aristigerum

Erythroxylum aristigerum, commonly known as the red-veined euphorbia, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red veins on its leaves. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Erythroxylum aristigerum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
