Ericoides variabile

Ericoides variabile, commonly known as the variable heath, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ericoides variabile is often found in coastal dunes and sandy woodlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
