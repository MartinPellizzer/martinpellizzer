Echeveria calderoniae

Echeveria calderoniae, commonly known as the "Calderonia Echeveria," is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to Mexico.

It features vibrant, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a powdery coating known as farina. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
