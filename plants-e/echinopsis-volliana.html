Echinopsis volliana

Echinopsis volliana, commonly known as the Volliana cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive spiny crown and a short, thick stem. The plant features white to pale blue flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Echinopsis volliana is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
