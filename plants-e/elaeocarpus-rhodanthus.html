Elaeocarpus rhodanthus

Elaeocarpus rhodanthus, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer. The tree produces woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in warm climates.
