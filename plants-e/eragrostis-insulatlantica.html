Eragrostis insulatlantica

Eragrostis insulatlantica, commonly known as the Caribbean grass, is a species of grass native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control in tropical and subtropical regions.
