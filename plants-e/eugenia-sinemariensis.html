Eugenia sinemariensis

Eugenia sinemariensis, commonly known as the Sinemari mango, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically small, with a thick, fibrous skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
