Eulophia citrina

Eulophia citrina, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.

Eulophia citrina is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its elegant and delicate appearance.
