Erinus verticillatus

Erinus verticillatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Erinus verticillatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
