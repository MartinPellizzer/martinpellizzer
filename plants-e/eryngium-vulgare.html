Eryngium vulgare

Eryngium vulgare, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by finely divided, silver-green leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, dry soils, and disturbed sites, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its unique appearance has made it a popular choice in ornamental gardening.

Eryngium vulgare is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
