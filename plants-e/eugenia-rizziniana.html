Eugenia rizziniana

Eugenia rizziniana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia rizziniana*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, cherry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is well-suited for gardens and landscapes in warm regions.
