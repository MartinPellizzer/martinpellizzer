Echinopsis tarijensis subsp. bertramiana

Echinopsis tarijensis subsp.

bertramiana, commonly known as the Bertram's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a globular shape, often covered in a layer of white, powdery bloom.

The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its compact size and ornamental value.

This subspecies is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-draining soil with minimal water.
