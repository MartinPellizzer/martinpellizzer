Erigeron howardii

Erigeron howardii, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with white ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Erigeron howardii is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
