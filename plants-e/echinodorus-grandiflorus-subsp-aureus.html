Echinodorus grandiflorus subsp. aureus

Echinodorus grandiflorus subsp.

aureus, commonly known as the golden elodea, is a aquatic plant native to South America. It features long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green with a golden or yellowish tint.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in spadices, which are characteristic of the Araceae family. It thrives in freshwater environments with moderate to high light and nutrient-rich substrates.

This species is popular in aquariums due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
