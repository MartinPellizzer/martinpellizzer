Eugenia axillaris var. cozumelensis

Eugenia axillaris var. cozumelensis, commonly known as the Cozumel cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Cozumel in the Mexican Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in subtropical regions.
