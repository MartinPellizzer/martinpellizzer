Erythea aculeata

Erythea aculeata, commonly known as the prickly erythea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns orange when mature.

Erythea aculeata is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
