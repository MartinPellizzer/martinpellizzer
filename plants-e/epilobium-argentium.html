Epilobium argentium

Epilobium argentium, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Epilobium argentium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
