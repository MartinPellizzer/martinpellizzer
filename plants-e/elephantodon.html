Elephantodon

Elephantodon, commonly known as the elephant's tusk plant, is a unique member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and fleshy leaves that resemble the shape of an elephant's tusk, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unusual appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
