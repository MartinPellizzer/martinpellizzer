Eria clemensiae

Eria clemensiae, commonly known as the Clements' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Eria clemensiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
