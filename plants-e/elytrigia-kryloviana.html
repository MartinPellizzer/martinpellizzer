Elytrigia kryloviana

Elytrigia kryloviana, commonly known as Krylov's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for forage or ornamental purposes.
