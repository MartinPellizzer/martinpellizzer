Euphrasia officinalis var. neglecta

Euphrasia officinalis var. neglecta, commonly known as the lesser eyebright, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat eye conditions and respiratory ailments.
