Erodium ciconium var. erectum

Erodium ciconium var. erectum, commonly known as the upright bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases tiny seeds.

The upright bedstraw is often used in herbal medicine for its purported sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
