Eragrostis jacobsiana

Eragrostis jacobsiana, commonly known as Jacobs' lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Jacobs' lovegrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
