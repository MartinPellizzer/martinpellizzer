Eremocharis integrifolia

Eremocharis integrifolia, commonly known as the velvet tunicate orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, showy flowers with a velvety texture and a distinctive purple hue. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem and lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy coating. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Eremocharis integrifolia is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
