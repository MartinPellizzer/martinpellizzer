Euphorbia nevadensis var. carpetana

Euphorbia nevadensis var. carpetana, commonly known as the Nevada spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts. The plant has a distinctive milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its foliage is typically gray-green, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.
