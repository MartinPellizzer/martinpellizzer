Eugenia delicatula

Eugenia delicatula, commonly known as the fragrant guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically sweet and fragrant, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eugenia delicatula is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
