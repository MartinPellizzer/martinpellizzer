Eriocaulon siamense var. hexagynum

Eriocaulon siamense var. hexagynum, commonly known as the Siam spikegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, showcasing its adaptation to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
