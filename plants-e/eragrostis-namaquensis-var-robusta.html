Eragrostis namaquensis var. robusta

Eragrostis namaquensis var. robusta, commonly known as the Namibian lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fine-textured foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are often hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its robust root system.
