Eupatorium espinosarum var. subintegrifolium

Eupatorium espinosarum var. subintegrifolium, commonly known as the Florida spiderwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
