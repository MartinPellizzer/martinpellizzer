Eupatorium delpechianum

Eupatorium delpechianum, commonly known as the Delpech's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium delpechianum is known for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
