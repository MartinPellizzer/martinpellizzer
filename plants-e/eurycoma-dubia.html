Eurycoma dubia

Eurycoma dubia, commonly known as the Malayan velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland tropical rainforests. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
