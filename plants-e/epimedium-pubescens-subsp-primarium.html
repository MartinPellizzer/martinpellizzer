Epimedium pubescens subsp. primarium

Epimedium pubescens subsp.

primarium, commonly known as the Chinese barrenwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern China. It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on leafless stems. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often covered with fine pubescence.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
