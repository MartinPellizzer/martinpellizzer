Eragrostis amabilis var. riparia

Eragrostis amabilis var. riparia, commonly known as river lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in riparian zones, thriving along riverbanks and moist soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it a delicate appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
