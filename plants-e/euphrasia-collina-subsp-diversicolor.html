Euphrasia collina subsp. diversicolor

Euphrasia collina subsp.

diversicolor, commonly known as the yellow evening-primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday.

The plant typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly soils in prairies and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The species is known for its short stature, usually reaching heights of less than 30 centimeters.
