Epipactis atromarginata

Epipactis atromarginata, commonly known as the black-margin helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and a tall, erect stem that bears a single, showy flower. The plant has a complex root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are typically green or brownish with a distinctive dark margin, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Epipactis atromarginata is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, often forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
