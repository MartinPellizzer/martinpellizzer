Eria compressa

Eria compressa, commonly known as the flat-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are compressed and narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eria compressa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
