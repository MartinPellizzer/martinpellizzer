Elaeocarpus sadikanensis

Elaeocarpus sadikanensis, commonly known as the Sadikan's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically encased in a fleshy, edible pericarp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary forest habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
