Echeveria laxa var. cymosa

Echeveria laxa var. cymosa, commonly known as the "Cymosa Echeveria," is a compact, low-growing succulent with rosettes of fleshy, pale green to blue-gray leaves.

The leaves are typically spoon-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in arid conditions. It produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the spring or early summer. Native to Mexico, this variety thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its slow growth rate and attractive foliage make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
