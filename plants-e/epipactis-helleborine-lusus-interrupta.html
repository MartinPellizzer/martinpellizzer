Epipactis helleborine lusus interrupta

Epipactis helleborine lusus interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a unique inflorescence with pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to grow in shaded, moist environments, often in woodlands or along stream banks. It has a complex life cycle involving a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.

The specific epithet "lusus interrupta" refers to the irregular or interrupted pattern of its floral structure.
