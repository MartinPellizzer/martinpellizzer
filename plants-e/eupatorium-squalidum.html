Eupatorium squalidum

Eupatorium squalidum, commonly known as swamp boneset, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium squalidum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.
