Epidendrum resectum

Epidendrum resectum, commonly known as the Resected Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Epidendrum resectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
