Echinocystis marah

Echinocystis marah, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant produces smooth, green, hairy fruits that turn yellow as they mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a trailing growth habit.

Echinocystis marah is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks.
