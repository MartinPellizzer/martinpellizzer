Echinocactus gladiatus

Echinocactus gladiatus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny exterior, which provides protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant features a thick, segmented stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in the spring and are produced at the top of the stem.

Echinocactus gladiatus is well adapted to desert conditions, with a slow growth rate and the ability to thrive with minimal water.
