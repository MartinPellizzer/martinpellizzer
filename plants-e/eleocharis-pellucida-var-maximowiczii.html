Eleocharis pellucida var. maximowiczii

Eleocharis pellucida var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the glassworts, is a species of sedge that is characterized by its translucent, glass-like stems.

It is native to wetland environments and thrives in marshy areas with saturated soils. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in spikes and are usually wind-pollinated.

This variety is notable for its unique appearance and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
