Epacris vilmoreana

Epacris vilmoreana, commonly known as the pink epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Epacris vilmoreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
