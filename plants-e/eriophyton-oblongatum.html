Eriophyton oblongatum

Eriophyton oblongatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus.

Eriophyton oblongatum is often found in open, sunny areas such as fields and roadsides.
