Eurya pumila

Eurya pumila, commonly known as the smoothleaf eurya, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Eurya pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
