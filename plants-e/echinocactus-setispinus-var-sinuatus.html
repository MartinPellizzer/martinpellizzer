Echinocactus setispinus var. sinuatus

Echinocactus setispinus var. sinuatus, commonly known as the Sinuous Hedgehog Cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny areoles, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiky structure. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

This variant is distinguished by its sinuous, undulating stem shape, which sets it apart from other members of the Echinocactus genus.
