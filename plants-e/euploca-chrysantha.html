Euploca chrysantha

Euploca chrysantha, commonly known as the golden euploca, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tint when young.

Euploca chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
