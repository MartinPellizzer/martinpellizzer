Erysimum capitatum var. lompocense

Erysimum capitatum var. lompocense, commonly known as Lompoc wallflower, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, elongated leaves and has a mat-forming structure that helps it thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to full sun and dry conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
