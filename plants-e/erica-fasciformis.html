Erica fasciformis

Erica fasciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica fasciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
