Euonymus roseoperulatus

Euonymus roseoperulatus, commonly known as the rose-leaf woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, rose-pink flowers that appear in late spring, followed by red-orange berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its unique foliage and colorful fruits make it a standout plant in the botanical garden.
