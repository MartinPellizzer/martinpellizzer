Euphorbia jaliscensis var. durangensis

Euphorbia jaliscensis var. durangensis, commonly known as the Durango euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the highlands of Durango, Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapted to its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

This species is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
