Eriogonum salinum

Eriogonum salinum, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in saline soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Eriogonum salinum plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
