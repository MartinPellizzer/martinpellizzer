Eugenia oligandra var. macnabiana

Eugenia oligandra var. macnabiana, commonly known as the MacNab's guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically pink to red in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
