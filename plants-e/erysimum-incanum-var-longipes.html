Erysimum incanum var. longipes

Erysimum incanum var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its long, slender stems distinguish it from other varieties within the Erysimum genus.
