Euonymus japonicus latifolius-albovariegatus

Euonymus japonicus latifolius-albovariegatus, commonly known as the variegated Japanese spurge, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that display striking white and green variegation. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, adding visual interest to gardens.
