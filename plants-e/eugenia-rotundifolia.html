Eugenia rotundifolia

Eugenia rotundifolia, commonly known as the Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, rounded leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is renowned for its edible, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
