Euphorbia postii

Euphorbia postii, commonly known as the dwarf crown-of-thorns, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun.

Euphorbia postii is also known for its spiny appearance, which serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
