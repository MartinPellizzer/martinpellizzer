Echinocactus pampeanus var. rubellianus

Echinocactus pampeanus var. rubellianus, commonly known as the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spiny cactus native to Argentina.

It features a globular stem covered in bright green to reddish-brown spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
