Eremophila bowmanii subsp. bowmanii

Eremophila bowmanii subsp.

bowmanii, commonly known as Bowman's eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and a compact growth habit. It often grows in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This subspecies is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators.
