Erysimum cheiranthoides subsp. altum

Erysimum cheiranthoides subsp.

altum, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features basal rosettes of linear, glaucous leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowering period is short, typically lasting only a few weeks, and it is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
