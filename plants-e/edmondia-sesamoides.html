Edmondia sesamoides

Edmondia sesamoides, commonly known as the desert broom, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Edmondia sesamoides plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
