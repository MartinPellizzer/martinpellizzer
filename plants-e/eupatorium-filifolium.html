Eupatorium filifolium

Eupatorium filifolium, commonly known as silverleaf eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pale blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has slender, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium filifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
