Eriogonum jamesii subsp. bakeri

Eriogonum jamesii subsp.

bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has hairy stems and leaves that are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh conditions and plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
