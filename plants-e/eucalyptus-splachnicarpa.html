Eucalyptus splachnicarpa

Eucalyptus splachnicarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallet gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The tree produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin, fibrous strips, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is commonly found in forested areas and along riverbanks.
