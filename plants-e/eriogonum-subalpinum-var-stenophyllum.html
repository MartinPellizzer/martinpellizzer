Eriogonum subalpinum var. stenophyllum

Eriogonum subalpinum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

This variety is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
