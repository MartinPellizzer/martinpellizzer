Eulophia roseolabia

Eulophia roseolabia, commonly known as the pink-lipped eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a pinkish-lavender lip and greenish-white petals. The plant grows as an epiphytic orchid, often found clinging to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Eulophia roseolabia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
