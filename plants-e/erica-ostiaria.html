Erica ostiaria

Erica ostiaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related species. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Erica ostiaria is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
