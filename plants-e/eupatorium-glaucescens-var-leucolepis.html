Eupatorium glaucescens var. leucolepis

Eupatorium glaucescens var. leucolepis, commonly known as the white eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its overall textured appearance.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
