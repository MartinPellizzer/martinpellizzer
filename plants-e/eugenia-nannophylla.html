Eugenia nannophylla

Eugenia nannophylla, commonly known as the little-leaf eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
