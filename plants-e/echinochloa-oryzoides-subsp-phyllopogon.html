Echinochloa oryzoides subsp. phyllopogon

Echinochloa oryzoides subsp.

phyllopogon, commonly known as ricegrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three bracteate spikelets. It is often found growing in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural fields.

Botanically, it is notable for its morphological similarity to other Echinochloa species, which can make identification challenging without careful examination.
