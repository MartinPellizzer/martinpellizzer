Erodium crenatum var. glanduliferum

Erodium crenatum var. glanduliferum, commonly known as sticky weed or glandular erodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a distinctive glandular surface that produces a sticky secretion. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban areas.
