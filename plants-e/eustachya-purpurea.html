Eustachya purpurea

Eustachya purpurea, commonly known as purple grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clumps of foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear purple or pinkish flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to gardens.

Eustachya purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
