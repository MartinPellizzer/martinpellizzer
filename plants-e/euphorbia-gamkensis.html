Euphorbia gamkensis

Euphorbia gamkensis, commonly known as the Karoo euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces a unique cyathium, a cup-like structure that houses its small, inconspicuous flowers. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stem to survive dry conditions.

Euphorbia gamkensis is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
