Erica ampullacea var. obbata

Erica ampullacea var. obbata, commonly known as the Cape heath or narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

Erica ampullacea var. obbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators.
