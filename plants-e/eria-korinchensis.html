Eria korinchensis

Eria korinchensis, commonly known as the korinch's orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical and slightly fleshy, adapted to retain moisture.

Eria korinchensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
