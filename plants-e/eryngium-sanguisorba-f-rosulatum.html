Eryngium sanguisorba f. rosulatum

Eryngium sanguisorba f. rosulatum, commonly known as the Rosulate Cornflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial with a rosette of dark green, slightly hairy leaves.

It produces striking, spiny, blue-violet flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils. Its unique foliage and showy inflorescence make it a desirable ornamental plant.

It is relatively low maintenance and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
