Epacris crassifolia

Epacris crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved epacris, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Epacris crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
