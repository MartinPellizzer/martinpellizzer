Eupatorium clematideum

Eupatorium clematideum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fluffy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Eupatorium clematideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
