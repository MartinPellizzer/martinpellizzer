Elaeocarpus sphaericus

Elaeocarpus sphaericus, commonly known as the globe elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and wet tropical rainforest areas. The plant is characterized by its spherical, woody fruit, which is often covered in a thick, leathery layer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
