Eugenia pseudosubtilis var. platyphylla

Eugenia pseudosubtilis var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink and appear in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
