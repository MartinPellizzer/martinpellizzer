Eragrostis cenolepis

Eragrostis cenolepis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in areas with moderate to low rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
