Eupatorium quasitripartitum

Eupatorium quasitripartitum, commonly known as the three-parted thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant.

Eupatorium quasitripartitum is often found in moist woodlands and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance to pollinators.
