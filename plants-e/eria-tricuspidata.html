Eria tricuspidata

Eria tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-toothed eria, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the roots are fibrous and adapted for epiphytic growth.

Eria tricuspidata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is found in shaded, humid environments.
