Euphrasia minima subsp. font-queri

Euphrasia minima subsp.

font-queri, commonly known as the dwarf eyebright, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodland or scrub habitats.

The plant features delicate, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers with prominent dark veins. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat eye conditions and other ailments.
