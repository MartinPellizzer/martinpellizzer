Eriocaulon filifolium

Eriocaulon filifolium, commonly known as the thread-leaved spikerush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its roots are often submerged, while the stems and leaves remain above water.

Eriocaulon filifolium plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
