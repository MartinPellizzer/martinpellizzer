Eucalyptus latisinensis

Eucalyptus latisinensis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale green or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in shrublands and woodlands, forming part of the mallee ecosystem.

Eucalyptus latisinensis plays a significant role in providing habitat for native wildlife and is valued for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
