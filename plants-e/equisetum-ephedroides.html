Equisetum ephedroides

Equisetum ephedroides, commonly known as the creeping whipstalk, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, scale-like leaves and a hollow, jointed stem that resembles a whip. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its fossil record suggests it has remained relatively unchanged for millions of years.

Equisetum ephedroides is also notable for containing alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
