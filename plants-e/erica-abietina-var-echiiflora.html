Erica abietina var. echiiflora

Erica abietina var. echiiflora, commonly known as the Alpine heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen. It produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is adapted to poor, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
