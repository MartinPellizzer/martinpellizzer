Erucago monspeliaca

Erucago monspeliaca, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
