Echinops pungens var. transcaucasicus

Echinops pungens var. transcaucasicus, commonly known as the Transcaucasian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features a compact, spiny plant with a short, woody stem and dense, spherical blue-gray flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grayish-green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
