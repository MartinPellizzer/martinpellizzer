Eremophila jucunda subsp. pulcherrima

Eremophila jucunda subsp.

pulcherrima, commonly known as the pretty emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces vibrant pink or red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native landscapes.
