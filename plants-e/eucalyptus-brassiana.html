Eucalyptus brassiana

Eucalyptus brassiana, commonly known as the red gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils.

Eucalyptus brassiana is often found in dry forests and woodlands, where it plays an important ecological role.
