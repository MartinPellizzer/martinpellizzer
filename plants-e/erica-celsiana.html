Erica celsiana

Erica celsiana, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Erica celsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as part of natural landscapes.
