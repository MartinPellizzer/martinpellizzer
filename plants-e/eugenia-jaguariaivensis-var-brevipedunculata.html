Eugenia jaguariaivensis var. brevipedunculata

Eugenia jaguariaivensis var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
