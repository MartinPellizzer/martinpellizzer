Eugenia plicatula

Eugenia plicatula, commonly known as the wrinkled guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often marked with distinctive white veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Eugenia plicatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
