Elaeocarpus ngii

Elaeocarpus ngii, commonly known as the Bornean elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Borneo.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Elaeocarpus ngii is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also noted for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
