Ectotropis alpina

Ectotropis alpina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold and harsh conditions.

Ectotropis alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
