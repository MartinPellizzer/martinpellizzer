Eragrostis poculiformis

Eragrostis poculiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluestem, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Eragrostis poculiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
