Elaeocarpus dolichostylus subsp. dolichostylus

Elaeocarpus dolichostylus subsp.

dolichostylus, commonly known as the long-styled elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region. It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers with four to five petals, followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds. Its flowers are often white or pale pink, and the species is notable for its ornamental value in horticulture.

The subspecies is particularly valued for its unique floral structure and potential ecological significance in its native habitats.
