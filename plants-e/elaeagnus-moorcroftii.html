Elaeagnus moorcroftii

Elaeagnus moorcroftii, commonly known as the Kashmir silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Elaeagnus moorcroftii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration.
