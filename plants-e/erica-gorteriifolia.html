Erica gorteriifolia

Erica gorteriifolia, commonly known as the silver-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with silvery-gray leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring.

Erica gorteriifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
