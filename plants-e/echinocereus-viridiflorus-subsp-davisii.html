Echinocereus viridiflorus subsp. davisii

Echinocereus viridiflorus subsp.

davisii, commonly known as the Davis's green-flowered cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing, mound-forming cactus with ribbed stems and clusters of bright green flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring spines for protection and reduced leaves to minimize water loss. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to green, with a distinctive appearance that sets it apart from other cacti in the genus.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
