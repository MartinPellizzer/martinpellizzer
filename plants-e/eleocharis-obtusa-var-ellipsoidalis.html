Eleocharis obtusa var. ellipsoidalis

Eleocharis obtusa var. ellipsoidalis, commonly known as the broad-ellipsoid sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, often ribbed stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib. This variety is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in wet or seasonally flooded soils.

It is often found in marshy areas, wetlands, and along the edges of water bodies.
