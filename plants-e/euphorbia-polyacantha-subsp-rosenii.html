Euphorbia polyacantha subsp. rosenii

Euphorbia polyacantha subsp.

rosenii, commonly known as the Rosenvale euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it blooms in late spring or early summer.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
