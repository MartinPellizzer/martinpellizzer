Erigeron politus subsp. berlinii

Erigeron politus subsp.

berlinii, commonly known as the Berliner's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

The species is known for its showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
