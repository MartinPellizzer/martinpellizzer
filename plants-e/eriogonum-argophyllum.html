Eriogonum argophyllum

Eriogonum argophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other members like sorrels and knotweeds.

Eriogonum argophyllum plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as habitat for various insects and small animals.
