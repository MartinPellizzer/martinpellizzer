Euphorbia gladiosa

Euphorbia gladiosa, commonly known as the swordleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy texture that helps it conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
