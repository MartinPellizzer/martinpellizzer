Erythrophleum chlorostachys

Erythrophleum chlorostachys, commonly known as the green-stemmed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greenish-barked trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing flat, elongated seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
