Eremocharis ferreyrae

Eremocharis ferreyrae, commonly known as the Ferreyra's spider lily, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, spider-like flowers with white petals and a prominent green throat. The plant is adapted to humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive. It blooms in the spring and is prized for its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered endangered and is often grown in specialized botanical gardens.
