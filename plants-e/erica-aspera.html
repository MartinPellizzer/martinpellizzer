Erica aspera

Erica aspera, commonly known as the rough heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Erica aspera thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
