Euphorbia nicaeensis subsp. hispanica

Euphorbia nicaeensis subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the specialized inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling.
