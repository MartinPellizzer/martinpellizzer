Euonymus tanakae

Euonymus tanakae, commonly known as the Japanese euonymus, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth texture. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters during the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are ornamental.

Euonymus tanakae is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
