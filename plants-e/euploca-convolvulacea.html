Euploca convolvulacea

Euploca convolvulacea, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows along the ground, forming a dense mat of foliage. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or white, blooming in the early morning. Its stems are hairy and can climb or trail along the ground, often rooting at the nodes.

Euploca convolvulacea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
