Encyclia lorata

Encyclia lorata, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Encyclia lorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
