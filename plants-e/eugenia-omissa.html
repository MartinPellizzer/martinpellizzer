Eugenia omissa

Eugenia omissa, commonly known as the pink eucalyptus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or sandy soils.

Eugenia omissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
