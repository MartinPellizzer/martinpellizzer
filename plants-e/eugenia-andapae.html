Eugenia andapae

Eugenia andapae, commonly known as the Andapae myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, shaded environments. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss, Eugenia andapae is considered a vulnerable species.
