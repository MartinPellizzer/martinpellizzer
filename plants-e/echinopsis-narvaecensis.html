Echinopsis narvaecensis

Echinopsis narvaecensis, commonly known as the Narvaecensis cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bats and insects. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in low-nutrient soils.

Echinopsis narvaecensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in desert ecosystems.
