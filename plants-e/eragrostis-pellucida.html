Eragrostis pellucida

Eragrostis pellucida, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils, and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Eragrostis pellucida is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
