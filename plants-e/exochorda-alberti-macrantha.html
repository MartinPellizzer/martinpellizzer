Exochorda alberti macrantha

Exochorda alberti macrantha, commonly known as the Alberta rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features attractive, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, rounded form with greyish-green foliage that provides ornamental interest throughout the growing season. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes, and the plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a versatile choice for landscape design.
