Eriosema pycnanthum var. pycnanthum

Eriosema pycnanthum var. pycnanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eriosema, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is often found in open grasslands and along the edges of wooded areas.
