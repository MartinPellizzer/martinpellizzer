Echinopsis leucotricha

Echinopsis leucotricha, commonly known as the white spines cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, silky spines that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, nocturnal flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments with thick, water-storing stems.

Echinopsis leucotricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
