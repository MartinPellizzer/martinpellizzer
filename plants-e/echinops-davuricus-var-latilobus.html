Echinops davuricus var. latilobus

Echinops davuricus var. latilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and silver-gray, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom during the summer months.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
