Erica campylophylla

Erica campylophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica campylophylla is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments, showing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
