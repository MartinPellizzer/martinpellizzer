Epilobium inornatum var. brevipes

Epilobium inornatum var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed willow herb, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, pink to red, and arranged in spike-like clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
