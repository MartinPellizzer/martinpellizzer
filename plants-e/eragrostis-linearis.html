Eragrostis linearis

Eragrostis linearis, commonly known as slender barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eragrostis linearis is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
