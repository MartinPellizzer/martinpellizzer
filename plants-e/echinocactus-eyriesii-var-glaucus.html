Echinocactus eyriesii var. glaucus

Echinocactus eyriesii var. glaucus, commonly known as the Blue Echinocactus, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces striking blue-green flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
