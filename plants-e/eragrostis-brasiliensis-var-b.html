Eragrostis brasiliensis var. b

Eragrostis brasiliensis var. b, commonly known as Brazilian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
