Eriogonum ochroleucum var. macropodum

Eriogonum ochroleucum var. macropodum, commonly known as the large-footed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are upright and branched.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
