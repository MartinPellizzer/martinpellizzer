Elaphoglossum boquetense

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. Elaphoglossum boquetense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
