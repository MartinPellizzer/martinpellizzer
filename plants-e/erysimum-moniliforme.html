Erysimum moniliforme

Erysimum moniliforme, commonly known as the clustered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in late spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum moniliforme is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
