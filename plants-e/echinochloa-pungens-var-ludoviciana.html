Echinochloa pungens var. ludoviciana

Echinochloa pungens var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the Texas panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
