Erigeron lassenianus var. lassenianus

Erigeron lassenianus var. lassenianus, commonly known as the Lassen's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fine, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
