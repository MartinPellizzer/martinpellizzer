Euphorbia dracunculoides subsp. dracunculoides

Euphorbia dracunculoides subsp.

dracunculoides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It features dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia.

The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of arid environments. It produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
