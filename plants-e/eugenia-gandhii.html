Eugenia gandhii

Eugenia gandhii, commonly known as the Indian clove or Malabar clove, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, reddish-brown flowers and small, edible berries. The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.

Eugenia gandhii is also noted for its ornamental value due to its glossy foliage and seasonal flowering.
