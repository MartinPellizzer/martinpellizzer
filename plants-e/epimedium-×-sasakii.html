Epimedium × sasakii

Epimedium × sasakii, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, foliage that turns vibrant red in the fall. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.

Epimedium × sasakii thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
