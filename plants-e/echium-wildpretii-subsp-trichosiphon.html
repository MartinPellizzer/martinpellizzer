Echium wildpretii subsp. trichosiphon

Echium wildpretii subsp.

trichosiphon, commonly known as the Trichosiphon larkspur, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
