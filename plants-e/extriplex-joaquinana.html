Extriplex joaquinana

Extriplex joaquinana, commonly known as the Joaquin's saltbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common component of desert and coastal ecosystems.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources and survive in harsh, dry conditions.
