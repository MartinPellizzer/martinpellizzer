Epidendrum echinocarpon

Epidendrum echinocarpon, commonly known as the spiny-fruit epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

The fruit is distinctive, being a spiny capsule that opens to release the seeds.
