Eucalyptus × lateritica

Eucalyptus × lateritica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, making it a resilient component of native ecosystems.

Eucalyptus × lateritica plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
