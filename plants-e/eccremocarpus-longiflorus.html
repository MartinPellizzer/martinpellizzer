Eccremocarpus longiflorus

Eccremocarpus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces unique, spiral-shaped fruits that twist as they mature, a feature known as "twisting fruit.". It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its visual appeal.
