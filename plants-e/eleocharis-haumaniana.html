Eleocharis haumaniana

Eleocharis haumaniana, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into dry, three-lobed fruits.

Its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels makes it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
