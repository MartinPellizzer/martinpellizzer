Echinops grossheimii

Echinops grossheimii, commonly known as the Cape Thistle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the distinctive spiny fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Echinops grossheimii is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
