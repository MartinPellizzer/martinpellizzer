Elymus laxe-pilosus

Elymus laxe-pilosus, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, silky-haired leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
