Echeveria colorata f. colorata

Echeveria colorata f. colorata, commonly known as the Colorata Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purplish hue. The leaves are covered in a fine, powdery coating called farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring or summer.

This variety is prized for its compact growth and striking coloration, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
