Encyclia rufa

Encyclia rufa, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with a dense cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are borne on a single stem and consist of showy, red-tipped petals and sepals, often with a yellow or green throat.

Encyclia rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or greenhouses.
