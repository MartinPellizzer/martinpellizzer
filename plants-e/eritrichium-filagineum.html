Eritrichium filagineum

Eritrichium filagineum, commonly known as the filaginous eritrichium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
