Eriogonum elongatum var. areorivum

Eriogonum elongatum var. areorivum, commonly known as the redstem buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, reddish stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This species plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
