Epidendrum rousseauae

Epidendrum rousseauae, commonly known as the Rousseau's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are usually pink or purple in color, with a distinct, fragrant scent.

Epidendrum rousseauae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
