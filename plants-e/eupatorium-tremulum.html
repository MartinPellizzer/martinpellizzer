Eupatorium tremulum

Eupatorium tremulum, commonly known as shiveringweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its tendency to tremble or shake when touched, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium tremulum is valued in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
