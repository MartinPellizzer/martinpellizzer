Euphorbia scopulorum

Euphorbia scopulorum, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, gray-green stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-shaped structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens.
