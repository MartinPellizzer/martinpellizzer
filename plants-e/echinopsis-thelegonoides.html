Echinopsis thelegonoides

Echinopsis thelegonoides, commonly known as the Paraguay night beauty, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a ribbed structure with spines that provide protection against herbivores. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Echinopsis thelegonoides is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
