Echinolobium obscurum

Echinolobium obscurum, commonly known as the dark globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense cluster. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, dark blue or purple flowers during the late spring to early summer.

Echinolobium obscurum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
