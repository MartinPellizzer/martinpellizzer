Euphorbia nevadensis var. acutifolia

Euphorbia nevadensis var. acutifolia, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada spurge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, spine-tipped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is adapted to arid environments. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
