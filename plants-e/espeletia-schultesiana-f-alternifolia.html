Espeletia schultesiana f. alternifolia

Espeletia schultesiana f. alternifolia, commonly known as the cloud forest heather, is a flowering plant native to the Andean cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a capitulum. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and is adapted to the unique microclimate of its native habitat.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in its ecosystem.
