Epilobium adenocaulon f. umbrosum

Epilobium adenocaulon f. umbrosum, commonly known as the shadowy willow herb, is a variant of the willow herb plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
