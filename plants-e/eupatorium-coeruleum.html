Eupatorium coeruleum

Eupatorium coeruleum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside.

Eupatorium coeruleum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
