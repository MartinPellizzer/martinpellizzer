Eriosema longifolium var. pedunculatum

Eriosema longifolium var. pedunculatum, commonly known as the long-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a softly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky outcrops.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
