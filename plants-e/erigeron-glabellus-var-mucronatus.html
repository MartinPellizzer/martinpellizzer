Erigeron glabellus var. mucronatus

Erigeron glabellus var. mucronatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic plant of the sagebrush steppe ecosystem.
