Erysimum piasezkii

Erysimum piasezkii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its foliage consists of lobed, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments.
