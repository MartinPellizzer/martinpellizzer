Euthamia californica

Euthamia californica, commonly known as California goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow spheres. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces showy, bright yellow blooms from late summer to early autumn.

Euthamia californica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
