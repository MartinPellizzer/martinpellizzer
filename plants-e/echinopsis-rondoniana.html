Echinopsis rondoniana

Echinopsis rondoniana, commonly known as the Rondonian cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and produces white to pale pink flowers during the rainy season. The plant has a ribbed structure with spines that provide protection from herbivores. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
