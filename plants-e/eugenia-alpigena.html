Eugenia alpigena

Eugenia alpigena, commonly known as the alpine guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It thrives in high-altitude environments, typically above 3,000 meters, where it forms dense shrubs.

The fruit, though not widely cultivated, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
