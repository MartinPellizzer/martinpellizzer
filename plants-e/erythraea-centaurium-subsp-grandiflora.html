Erythraea centaurium subsp. grandiflora

Erythraea centaurium subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered red centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender stems that grow upright, often reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant produces showy, bright red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
