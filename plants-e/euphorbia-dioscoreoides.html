Euphorbia dioscoreoides

Euphorbia dioscoreoides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation and is used in traditional medicine.

Euphorbia dioscoreoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
