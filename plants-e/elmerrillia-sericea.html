Elmerrillia sericea

Elmerrillia sericea, commonly known as the silky elmerrillia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Elmerrillia sericea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful form.
