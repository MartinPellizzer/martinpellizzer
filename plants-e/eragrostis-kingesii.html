Eragrostis kingesii

Eragrostis kingesii, commonly known as King's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fine texture that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Eragrostis kingesii is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
