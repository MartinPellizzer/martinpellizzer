Erica esterhuyseniae var. trimera

Erica esterhuyseniae var. trimera, commonly known as the Knysna sand erica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in winter. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
