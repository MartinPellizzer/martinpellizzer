Erica virescens

Erica virescens, commonly known as the green heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This species typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring.

Erica virescens is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.
