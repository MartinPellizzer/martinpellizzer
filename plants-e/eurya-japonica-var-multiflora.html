Eurya japonica var. multiflora

Eurya japonica var. multiflora, commonly known as Japanese holly or Japanese evergreen, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for garden landscapes and hedges.
