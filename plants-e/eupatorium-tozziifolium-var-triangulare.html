Eupatorium tozziifolium var. triangulare

Eupatorium tozziifolium var. triangulare, commonly known as the triangular eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are characterized by their triangular shape and serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
