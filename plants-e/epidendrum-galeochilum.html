Epidendrum galeochilum

Epidendrum galeochilum, commonly known as the orchid with the "chalice-like" lip, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

Epidendrum galeochilum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, humid environments.
