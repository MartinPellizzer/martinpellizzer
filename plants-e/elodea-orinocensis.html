Elodea orinocensis

Elodea orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco waterweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color. Elodea orinocensis is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
