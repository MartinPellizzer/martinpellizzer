Euphrasia arctica var. obtusata

Euphrasia arctica var. obtusata, commonly known as Arctic toothwort, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or tundra environments. Its root system is tuberous, allowing it to survive harsh winter conditions.

Euphrasia arctica var. obtusata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.
