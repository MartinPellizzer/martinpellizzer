Euphorbia saccharata

Euphorbia saccharata, commonly known as the sugar euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It has a spiny exterior and contains a toxic sap that can cause skin irritation.

Euphorbia saccharata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
