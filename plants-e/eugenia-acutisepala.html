Eugenia acutisepala

Eugenia acutisepala, commonly known as the sharp-sepaled eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or purple color, attracting various bird species.
