Elaphoglossum chodatii

Elaphoglossum chodatii, commonly known as the Chodat's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lanceolate fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Elaphoglossum chodatii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
