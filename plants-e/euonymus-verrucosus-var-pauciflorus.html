Euonymus verrucosus var. pauciflorus

Euonymus verrucosus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the scarlet euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and small, yellow-green leaves that turn orange in the fall. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
