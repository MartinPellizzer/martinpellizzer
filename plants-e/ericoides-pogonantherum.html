Ericoides pogonantherum

Ericoides pogonantherum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes evergreen shrubs and trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant features small, leathery leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside, providing a unique visual characteristic. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early spring.

Ericoides pogonantherum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.
