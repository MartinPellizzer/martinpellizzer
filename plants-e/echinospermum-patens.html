Echinospermum patens

Echinospermum patens, commonly known as the hairy sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has adapted to thrive in arid conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in a spiny pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Echinospermum patens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
