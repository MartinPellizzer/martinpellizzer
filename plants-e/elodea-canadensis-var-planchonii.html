Elodea canadensis var. planchonii

Elodea canadensis var. planchonii, commonly known as Canadian waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and whorled arrangement of narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
