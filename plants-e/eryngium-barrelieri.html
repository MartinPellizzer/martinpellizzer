Eryngium barrelieri

Eryngium barrelieri, commonly known as the African thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that resemble a crown, surrounded by sharp, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly seed heads.

Eryngium barrelieri is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
