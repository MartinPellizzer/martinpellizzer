Echinocereus engelmannii subsp. fasciculatus

Echinocereus engelmannii subsp.

fasciculatus, commonly known as the lace cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a short, thick stem and clusters of spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
