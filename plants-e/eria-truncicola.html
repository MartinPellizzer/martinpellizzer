Eria truncicola

Eria truncicola, commonly known as the truncated eria, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure, with showy, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
