Erythranthe lutea var. variegata

Erythranthe lutea var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated yellow evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a variegated pattern of green and white, adding visual interest. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardens.
