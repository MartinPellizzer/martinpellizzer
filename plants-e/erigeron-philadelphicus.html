Erigeron philadelphicus

Erigeron philadelphicus, commonly known as New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open areas such as meadows and marshes. It features a robust root system and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its foliage is generally gray-green with rough, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture.
