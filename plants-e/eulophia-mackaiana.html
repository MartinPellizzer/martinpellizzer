Eulophia mackaiana

Eulophia mackaiana, commonly known as the Mackaian orchid, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure and multiple pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Eulophia mackaiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
