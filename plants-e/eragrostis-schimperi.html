Eragrostis schimperi

Eragrostis schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to drought conditions.

Eragrostis schimperi is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
