Eragrostis arundinacea

Eragrostis arundinacea, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Silky bluegrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
