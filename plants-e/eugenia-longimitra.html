Eugenia longimitra

Eugenia longimitra, commonly known as the long-stemmed guava, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface.

Eugenia longimitra is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
