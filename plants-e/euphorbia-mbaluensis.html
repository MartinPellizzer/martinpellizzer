Euphorbia mbaluensis

Euphorbia mbaluensis, commonly known as the Mbalu's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia mbaluensis is also known for its ability to store water in its stems, which helps it survive in dry conditions.
