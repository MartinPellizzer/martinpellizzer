Erythrina sousae

Erythrina sousae, commonly known as the Red Mimosas, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its bright red, fluffy flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often covered in soft, hairy tissue. Erythrina sousae is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
