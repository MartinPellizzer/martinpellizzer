Evolvulus goyazensis var. penicillatus

Evolvulus goyazensis var. penicillatus, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
