Eruca sativa subsp. lativalvis

Eruca sativa subsp.

lativalvis, commonly known as broad-leafed garden cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are broad and often hairy.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in racemes, which are typically followed by elongated, cylindrical siliques containing numerous seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as a leaf vegetable or for its seeds.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Eruca genus and shares similar ecological and botanical traits.
