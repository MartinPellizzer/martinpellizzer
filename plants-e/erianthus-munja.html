Erianthus munja

Erianthus munja, commonly known as wild millet, is a grass species native to various parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging conditions.

Erianthus munja is often used as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional value and adaptability.
