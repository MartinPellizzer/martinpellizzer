Erysimum bakeri

Erysimum bakeri, commonly known as Baker's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are bright yellow with four petals and are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Erysimum bakeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
