Erica sessiliflora var. claviflora

Erica sessiliflora var. claviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
