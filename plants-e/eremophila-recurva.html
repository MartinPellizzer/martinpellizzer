Eremophila recurva

Eremophila recurva, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and fire resilience. It produces small, often pale or yellowish flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native landscapes.
