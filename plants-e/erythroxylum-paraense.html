Erythroxylum paraense

Erythroxylum paraense, commonly known as the Amazonian coca, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain elliptical seeds. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is adapted to thrive in warm, moist environments.

Due to its botanical significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to researchers and conservationists.
