Eriogonum ovalifolium var. cyclophyllum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. cyclophyllum, commonly known as the cyclophyll mountain buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elliptical, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This variety is part of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive bracts and achene fruits.
