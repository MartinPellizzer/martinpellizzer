Emmotum nudum

Emmotum nudum, commonly known as the naked stem plant, is a unique species within the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves on its stems, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in pink or white hues.

Emmotum nudum is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in forested areas.
