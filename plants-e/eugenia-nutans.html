Eugenia nutans

Eugenia nutans, commonly known as the hanging guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its drooping, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, aromatic berries. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Eugenia nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
