Eugenia balbisiana var. triflora

Eugenia balbisiana var. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered guava, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that typically appear in clusters of three, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and fruit yield.
