Eremophila subfloccosa subsp. lanata

Eremophila subfloccosa subsp.

lanata, commonly known as the woolly emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
