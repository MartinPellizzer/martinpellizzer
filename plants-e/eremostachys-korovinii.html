Eremostachys korovinii

Eremostachys korovinii, commonly known as the Korovin's eremostachys, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Eremostachys korovinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xerophytic gardens.
