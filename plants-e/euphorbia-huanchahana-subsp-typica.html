Euphorbia huanchahana subsp. typica

Euphorbia huanchahana subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Huancas Valley spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Peruvian Andes. It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to its arid environment.

The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are its specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
