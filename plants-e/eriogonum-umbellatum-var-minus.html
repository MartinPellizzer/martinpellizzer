Eriogonum umbellatum var. minus

Eriogonum umbellatum var. minus, commonly known as the smallflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact stature and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
