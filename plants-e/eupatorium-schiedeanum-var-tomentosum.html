Eupatorium schiedeanum var. tomentosum

Eupatorium schiedeanum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a height ranging from 1 to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive hairy texture.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties.
