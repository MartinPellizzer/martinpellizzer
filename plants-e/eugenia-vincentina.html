Eugenia vincentina

Eugenia vincentina, commonly known as the Vincent's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round, and turn from green to red or purple as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
