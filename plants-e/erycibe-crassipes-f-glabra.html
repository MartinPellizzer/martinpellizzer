Erycibe crassipes f. glabra

Erycibe crassipes f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed erycibe, is a variant of the erycibe plant native to tropical regions.

It is a shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
