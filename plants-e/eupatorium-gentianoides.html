Eupatorium gentianoides

Eupatorium gentianoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium gentianoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
