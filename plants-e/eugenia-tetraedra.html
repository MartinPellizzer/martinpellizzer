Eugenia tetraedra

Eugenia tetraedra, commonly known as the four-sided eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit, which is typically small and fleshy, contains several seeds.

Eugenia tetraedra is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
