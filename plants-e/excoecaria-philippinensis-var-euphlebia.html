Excoecaria philippinensis var. euphlebia

Excoecaria philippinensis var. euphlebia, commonly known as the Philippine croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its green to reddish-brown stems and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.
