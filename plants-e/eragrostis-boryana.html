Eragrostis boryana

Eragrostis boryana, commonly known as the Bory's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showing drought tolerance.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
