Escobaria vivipara var. deserti

Escobaria vivipara var. deserti, commonly known as the desert livefoot, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. Its unique reproductive strategy involves the development of live young, or "livefoot," on the stems, which eventually detach and grow independently.

This adaptation enhances its survival in nutrient-poor, arid soils.
