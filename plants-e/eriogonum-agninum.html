Eriogonum agninum

Eriogonum agninum, commonly known as the San Benito sage, is a flowering plant native to California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its gray-green leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure. It belongs to the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Eriogonum agninum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
