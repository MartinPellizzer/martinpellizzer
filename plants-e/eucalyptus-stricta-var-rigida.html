Eucalyptus stricta var. rigida

Eucalyptus stricta var. rigida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, often clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. This plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional and modern applications.
