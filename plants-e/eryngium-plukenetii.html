Eryngium plukenetii

Eryngium plukenetii, commonly known as the prairie eryngium, is a flowering plant native to the central and northern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant features striking blue to purple spiky flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Eryngium plukenetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
