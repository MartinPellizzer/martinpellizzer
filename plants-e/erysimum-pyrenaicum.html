Erysimum pyrenaicum

Erysimum pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of basal rosettes of serrated, lance-shaped leaves.

Erysimum pyrenaicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
