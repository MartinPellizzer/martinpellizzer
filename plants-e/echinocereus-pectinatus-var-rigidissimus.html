Echinocereus pectinatus var. rigidissimus

Echinocereus pectinatus var. rigidissimus, commonly known as the rigid hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a compact, rounded shape and a dense covering of white to pale blue hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively easy to care for, though it requires minimal water to prevent root rot.
