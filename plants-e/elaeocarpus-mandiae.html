Elaeocarpus mandiae

Elaeocarpus mandiae, commonly known as the Mandi elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of India and Indonesia. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Elaeocarpus mandiae is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
