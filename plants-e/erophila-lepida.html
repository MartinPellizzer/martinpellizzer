Erophila lepida

Erophila lepida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved erophila, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Erophila lepida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
