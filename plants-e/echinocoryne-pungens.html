Echinocoryne pungens

Echinocoryne pungens, commonly known as the spiked corymbia, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescences that bear small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Echinocoryne pungens is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
