Euryops serra

Euryops serra, commonly known as the serrated bushveld daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it its common name, while its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months.

Euryops serra is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
