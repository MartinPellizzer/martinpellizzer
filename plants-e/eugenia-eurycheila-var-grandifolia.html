Eugenia eurycheila var. grandifolia

Eugenia eurycheila var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-fruited eugenia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Following flowering, it bears bright red, edible berries that are highly prized for their flavor and nutritional value.

This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
