Eremophila parvifolia subsp. auricampa

Eremophila parvifolia subsp.

auricampa, commonly known as the golden eremophila, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptation.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry garden environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
