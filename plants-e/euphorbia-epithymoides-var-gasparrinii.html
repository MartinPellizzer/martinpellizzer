Euphorbia epithymoides var. gasparrinii

Euphorbia epithymoides var. gasparrinii, commonly known as the Gasparrini's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiny margins and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts and collectors.
