Eriogonum tiehmii

Eriogonum tiehmii, commonly known as the Tiehm's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and canyons. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces seeds in small, dry capsules.

Eriogonum tiehmii is often found in chaparral and woodland ecosystems, contributing to local biodiversity.
