Eupatorium laeve var. macrophyllum

Eupatorium laeve var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often found in moist to wet habitats, including wetlands and along stream banks.
