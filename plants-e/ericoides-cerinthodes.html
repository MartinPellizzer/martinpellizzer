Ericoides cerinthodes

Ericoides cerinthodes, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and small trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ericoides cerinthodes thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
