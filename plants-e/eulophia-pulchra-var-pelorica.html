Eulophia pulchra var. pelorica

Eulophia pulchra var. pelorica, commonly known as the pink-flowered eulophia, is a striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that bear multiple lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
