Endlicheria impressa

Endlicheria impressa, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant produces small, green to brownish flowers with intricate patterns on the petals, which are often adorned with dark markings. Endlicheria impressa thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
