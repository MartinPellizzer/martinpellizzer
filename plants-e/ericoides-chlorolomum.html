Ericoides chlorolomum

Ericoides chlorolomum, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It possesses small, scale-like leaves that are typically green, though they may exhibit a yellowish hue under certain conditions. The plant produces white, clustered flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its berries, which are usually bright red, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments, making it a common sight in forest understories.
