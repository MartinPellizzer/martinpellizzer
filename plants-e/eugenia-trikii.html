Eugenia trikii

Eugenia trikii, commonly known as the Malabar cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia trikii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and medicinal properties.
