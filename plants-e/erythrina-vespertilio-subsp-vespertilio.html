Erythrina vespertilio subsp. vespertilio

Erythrina vespertilio subsp.

vespertilio, commonly known as the evening bat erythrina, is a striking legume native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and its distinctive pod fruits contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local cultures.
