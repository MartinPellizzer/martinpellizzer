Eria porphyroglossa

Eria porphyroglossa, commonly known as the purple-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple lips and greenish-white petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Eria porphyroglossa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
