Eupatorium selerianum

Eupatorium selerianum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, lavender-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium selerianum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
