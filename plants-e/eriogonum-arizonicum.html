Eriogonum arizonicum

Eriogonum arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eriogonum arizonicum is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
