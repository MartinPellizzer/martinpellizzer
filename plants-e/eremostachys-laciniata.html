Eremostachys laciniata

Eremostachys laciniata, commonly known as the shredded stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided or lobed, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens.

Its unique morphology and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert landscapes.
