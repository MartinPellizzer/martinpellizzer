Eryngium tricuspidatum subsp. occidentalis

Eryngium tricuspidatum subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western thistle, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers surrounded by bracts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
