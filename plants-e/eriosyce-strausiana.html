Eriosyce strausiana

Eriosyce strausiana, commonly known as the Straus's cholla, is a species of cactus native to central Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions and high sunlight exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated in desert gardens for its ornamental value.
