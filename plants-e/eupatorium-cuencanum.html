Eupatorium cuencanum

Eupatorium cuencanum, commonly known as the Cuencanum eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium cuencanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
