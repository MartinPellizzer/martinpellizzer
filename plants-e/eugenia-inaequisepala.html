Eugenia inaequisepala

Eugenia inaequisepala, commonly known as the unequal-sepal eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves with a smooth texture. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a red or yellow color, attracting various bird species.
