Eragrostis hypnoides

Eragrostis hypnoides, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in native plant gardens.
