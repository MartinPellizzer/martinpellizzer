Epilobium anatolicum subsp. anatolicum

Epilobium anatolicum subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian willowherb, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. Its flowers are arranged in spikes, with pink to purple petals that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
