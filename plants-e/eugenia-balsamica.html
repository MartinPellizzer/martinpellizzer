Eugenia balsamica

Eugenia balsamica, commonly known as the balsam apple, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Eugenia balsamica thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
