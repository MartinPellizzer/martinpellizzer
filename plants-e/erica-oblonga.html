Erica oblonga

Erica oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heaths. The plant is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica oblonga typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in South Africa.
