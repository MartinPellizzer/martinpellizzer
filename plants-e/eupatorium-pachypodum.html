Eupatorium pachypodum

Eupatorium pachypodum, commonly known as the Mexican feverfew, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clustered, small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy roots and is often found in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium pachypodum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
