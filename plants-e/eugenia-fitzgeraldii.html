Eugenia fitzgeraldii

Eugenia fitzgeraldii, commonly known as the Fitzgerald's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
