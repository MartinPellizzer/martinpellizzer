Embothrium coccineum var. obovatum

Embothrium coccineum var. obovatum, commonly known as the red-flowered coca or red-flowered embothrium, is a striking shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are typically obovate, hence the variety name, and are glossy with a slightly waxy texture. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally in some regions for its medicinal properties.
