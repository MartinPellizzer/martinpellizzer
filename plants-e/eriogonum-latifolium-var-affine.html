Eriogonum latifolium var. affine

Eriogonum latifolium var. affine, commonly known as the broadleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
