Eriosema heterophyllum var. parviflorum

Eriosema heterophyllum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
