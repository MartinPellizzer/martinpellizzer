Epipremnum foraminiferum

Epipremnum foraminiferum, commonly known as the satin pothos, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a fast-growing species that can climb or trail, making it popular as a houseplant.

Its ability to purify indoor air and adapt to various growing conditions contributes to its widespread cultivation.
