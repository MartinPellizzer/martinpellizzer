Eriosyce islayensis subsp. grandis

Eriosyce islayensis subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the Islay giant cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of southern Chile. It is characterized by its large, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height, covered in dense, white spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are typically borne at the stem tips. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the study of desert flora.
