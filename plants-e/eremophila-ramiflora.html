Eremophila ramiflora

Eremophila ramiflora, commonly known as the slender eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia, particularly found in the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays an important role in providing nectar for native pollinators such as honeyeaters and butterflies.

Eremophila ramiflora is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
