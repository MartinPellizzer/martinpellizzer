Erythraea vulgaris var. quadrifida

Erythraea vulgaris var. quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leaf erythraea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
