Eragrostis purpureopedicellata

Eragrostis purpureopedicellata, commonly known as purple-stemmed lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often display a reddish or purple hue, especially under stress or during certain growth stages. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Eragrostis purpureopedicellata is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
