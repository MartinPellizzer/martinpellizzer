Erythrina viarum

Erythrina viarum, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a deciduous legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often resembling torches. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets, and its stems are often thorny. It produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Erythrina viarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
