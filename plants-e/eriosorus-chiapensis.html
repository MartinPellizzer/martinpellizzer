Eriosorus chiapensis

Eriosorus chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical forests. Its spores are released from small, clustered sorus structures on the underside of the fronds.

Eriosorus chiapensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
