Eremophila pterocarpa

Eremophila pterocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila pterocarpa is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
