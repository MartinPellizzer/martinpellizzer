Erioscirpus comosus

Erioscirpus comosus, commonly known as the woolly burrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of fine hairs on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Erioscirpus comosus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
