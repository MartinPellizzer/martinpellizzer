Eugenia hauthalii

Eugenia hauthalii, commonly known as the Hauthal's wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, encased in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Eugenia hauthalii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
