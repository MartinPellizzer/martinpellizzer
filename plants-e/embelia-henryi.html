Embelia henryi

Embelia henryi, commonly known as the Chinese embelia or wild pepper, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of forests and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture and emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

The berries are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
