Eupatorium odoratissimum

Eupatorium odoratissimum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium odoratissimum is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils.
