Eucalyptus robusta var. bivalvis

Eucalyptus robusta var. bivalvis, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironbark, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often broader at the base. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that transition from green to red or yellow as they mature. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for various invertebrates and small mammals.

This variant is well adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
