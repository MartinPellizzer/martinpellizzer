Euphorbia cheiradenia

Euphorbia cheiradenia, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable plant in its native habitat.
