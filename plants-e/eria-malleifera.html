Eria malleifera

Eria malleifera, commonly known as the hairy leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown in gardens and botanical collections.
