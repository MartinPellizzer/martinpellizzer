Erodium botrys var. genuinum

Erodium botrys var. genuinum, commonly known as the common stickyweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are tiny, dry capsules that release minute seeds, aiding in its widespread distribution.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.
