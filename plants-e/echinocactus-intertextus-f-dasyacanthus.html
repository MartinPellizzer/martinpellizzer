Echinocactus intertextus f. dasyacanthus

Echinocactus intertextus f. dasyacanthus, commonly known as the thick-spined hedgehog cactus, is a unique variant of the Echinocactus genus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically cylindrical in shape, with a ribbed structure that helps it store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its distinctive features, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in succulent gardens.
