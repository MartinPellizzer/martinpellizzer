Eustachya coerulea

Eustachya coerulea, commonly known as the bluegrass grass or bluegrass reed, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple flowers. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Eustachya coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
