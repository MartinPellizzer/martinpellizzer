Erigeron canadensis var. strictus

Erigeron canadensis var. strictus, commonly known as Canadian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in the summer months.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators.
