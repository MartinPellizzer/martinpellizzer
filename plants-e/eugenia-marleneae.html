Eugenia marleneae

Eugenia marleneae, commonly known as the Marlene's evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The fruit, which is typically reddish-purple when mature, is edible and has a tart flavor.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
