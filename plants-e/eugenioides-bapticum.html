Eugenioides bapticum

Eugenioides bapticum, commonly known as the sacred balm of Gilead, is a shrub native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its essential oils, which have been used traditionally for their soothing and healing effects. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate to flourish.

The bark and leaves are often harvested for their aromatic compounds and therapeutic applications.
