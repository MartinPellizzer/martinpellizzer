Eupatorium irrasum

Eupatorium irrasum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

White boneset is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
