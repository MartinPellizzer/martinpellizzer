Eucalyptus umbellata var. media

Eucalyptus umbellata var. media, commonly known as the small-leaf mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly coating. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus umbellata var. media is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
