Euphorbia ivanjohnstonii f. longifolia

Euphorbia ivanjohnstonii f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved ivanjohnstonii euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically elongated, adapted to store water in its native environment. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
