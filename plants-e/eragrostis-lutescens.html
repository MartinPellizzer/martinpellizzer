Eragrostis lutescens

Eragrostis lutescens, commonly known as yellow lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a dull green color. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates.

Eragrostis lutescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
