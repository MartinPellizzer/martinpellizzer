Eucalyptus intertexta var. magna

Eucalyptus intertexta var. magna, commonly known as the large-flowered ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white, grey, and green. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
