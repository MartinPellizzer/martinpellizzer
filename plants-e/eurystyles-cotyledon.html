Eurystyles cotyledon

Eurystyles cotyledon, commonly known as the cotyledon plant, is a member of the Aizoaceae family.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

The cotyledon plant is popular as a decorative succulent due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
