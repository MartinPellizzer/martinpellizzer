Eupatorium silenifolium

Eupatorium silenifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue-gray foliage and fluffy, mist-like flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny to partially shaded areas, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its showy flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Eupatorium silenifolium is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
