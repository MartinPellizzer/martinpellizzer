Erythrina ankaranensis

Erythrina ankaranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a striking leguminous plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its sturdy, often thorny stems support its showy floral display. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Erythrina ankaranensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits in local ecosystems.
