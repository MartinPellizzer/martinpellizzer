Echinops kafirniganus

Echinops kafirniganus, commonly known as the Kafirnigan thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Echinops kafirniganus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and drought tolerance.
