Epilobium × ninckii

Epilobium × ninckii, commonly known as the hybrid willowherb, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Epilobium × ninckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
