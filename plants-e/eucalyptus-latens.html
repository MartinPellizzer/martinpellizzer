Eucalyptus latens

Eucalyptus latens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, greyish-brown branches and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in umbels. Eucalyptus latens typically grows in sandy or loamy soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in shrublands and woodlands.
