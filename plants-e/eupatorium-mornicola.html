Eupatorium mornicola

Eupatorium mornicola, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and erect.

Eupatorium mornicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
