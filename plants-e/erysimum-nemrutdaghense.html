Erysimum nemrutdaghense

Erysimum nemrutdaghense, commonly known as the Nemrutdagh Wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and distinctive seed pods. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bright yellow or orange flowers bloom in late spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
