Eliokarmos neomaculatus

Eliokarmos neomaculatus, commonly known as the New Spotted Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
