Eupatorium aschenbornianum

Eupatorium aschenbornianum, commonly known as the Aschenborn's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall.

It is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
