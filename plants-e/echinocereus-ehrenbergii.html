Echinocereus ehrenbergii

Echinocereus ehrenbergii, commonly known as the blue agave, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, and its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
