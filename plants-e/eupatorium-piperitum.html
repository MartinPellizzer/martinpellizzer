Eupatorium piperitum

Eupatorium piperitum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist, rich soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium piperitum is known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
