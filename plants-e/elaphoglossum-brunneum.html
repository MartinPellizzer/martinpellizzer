Elaphoglossum brunneum

Elaphoglossum brunneum, commonly known as the brown fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bronze to brown in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered spore sacs on the underside of its fronds, which are typical of fern reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Elaphoglossum brunneum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
