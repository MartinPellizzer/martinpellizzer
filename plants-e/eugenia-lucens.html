Eugenia lucens

Eugenia lucens, commonly known as the golden eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, spherical fruits. Eugenia lucens is often found in open forests and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
