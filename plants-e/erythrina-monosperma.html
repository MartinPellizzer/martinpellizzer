Erythrina monosperma

Erythrina monosperma, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical legume native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its pods contain a single seed, giving it the species name *monosperma*. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
