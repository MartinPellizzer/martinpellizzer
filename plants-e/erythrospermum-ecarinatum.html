Erythrospermum ecarinatum

Erythrospermum ecarinatum, commonly known as the weeping erythrospermum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its pendulous, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erythrospermum ecarinatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
