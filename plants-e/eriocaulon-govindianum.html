Eriocaulon govindianum

Eriocaulon govindianum, commonly known as the Indian marsh grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its small, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. This species typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.

The plant is also noted for its ability to tolerate both freshwater and brackish conditions.
