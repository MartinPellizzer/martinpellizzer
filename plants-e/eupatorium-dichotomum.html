Eupatorium dichotomum

Eupatorium dichotomum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
