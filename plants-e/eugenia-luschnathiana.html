Eugenia luschnathiana

Eugenia luschnathiana, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eugenia luschnathiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
