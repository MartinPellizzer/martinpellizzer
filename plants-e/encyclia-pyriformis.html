Encyclia pyriformis

Encyclia pyriformis, commonly known as the oval orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering. The plant produces a single, lance-shaped leaf and a raceme of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive, boat-shaped lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Encyclia pyriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
