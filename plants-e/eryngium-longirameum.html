Eryngium longirameum

Eryngium longirameum, commonly known as the long-stemmed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by white to pale blue bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust, upright stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Eryngium longirameum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
