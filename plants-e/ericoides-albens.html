Ericoides albens

Ericoides albens, commonly known as the silver heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact, dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ericoides albens thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
