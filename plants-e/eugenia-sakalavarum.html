Eugenia sakalavarum

Eugenia sakalavarum, commonly known as the Malabar apple or wild guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish-green fruits that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
