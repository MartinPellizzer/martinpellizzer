Eucalyptus balladoniensis

Eucalyptus balladoniensis, commonly known as the Balladoon gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that becomes flaky with age, and its lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
