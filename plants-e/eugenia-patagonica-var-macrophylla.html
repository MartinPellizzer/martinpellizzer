Eugenia patagonica var. macrophylla

Eugenia patagonica var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Patagonian guava, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, aromatic berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in local cuisines.
