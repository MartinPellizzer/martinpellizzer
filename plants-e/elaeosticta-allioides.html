Elaeosticta allioides

Elaeosticta allioides, commonly known as the garlic mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant has kidney-shaped leaves and produces small white or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it can thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native plants.
