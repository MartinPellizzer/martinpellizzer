Erica pillansii subsp. pillansii

Erica pillansii subsp.

pillansii, commonly known as the Pillans's heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region, with its flowering period occurring during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.
