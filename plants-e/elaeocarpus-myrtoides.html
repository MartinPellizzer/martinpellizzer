Elaeocarpus myrtoides

Elaeocarpus myrtoides, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
