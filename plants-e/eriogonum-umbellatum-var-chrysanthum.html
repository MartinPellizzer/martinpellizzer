Eriogonum umbellatum var. chrysanthum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. chrysanthum, commonly known as yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
