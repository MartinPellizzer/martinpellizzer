Exacum aphyllum

Exacum aphyllum, commonly known as the naked-flower ivy, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden among its foliage. The plant produces clusters of pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.
