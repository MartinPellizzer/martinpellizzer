Eugenia casearioides

Eugenia casearioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or casearia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
