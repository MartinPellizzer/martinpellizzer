Euphorbia richardsiae subsp. richardsiae

Euphorbia richardsiae subsp.

richardsiae, commonly known as the Richards' spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a thick, fleshy stem that can grow up to several meters in height, often with a branching structure.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its stem, making it drought-tolerant.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
