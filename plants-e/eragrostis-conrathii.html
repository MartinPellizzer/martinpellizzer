Eragrostis conrathii

Eragrostis conrathii, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, typically with two to three flowering branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis conrathii is often used in landscaping and as a forage plant due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
