Encholirium fragae

Encholirium fragae, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces showy, colorful blooms during the spring season.

Encholirium fragae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
