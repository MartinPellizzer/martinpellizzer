Epidendrum forcipatum

Epidendrum forcipatum, commonly known as the clawed epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its inflorescence is a spike bearing numerous small, clustered blooms.

Epidendrum forcipatum is valued in horticulture for its vibrant colors and relatively easy care requirements.
