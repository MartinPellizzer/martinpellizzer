Euonymus oxyphyllus

Euonymus oxyphyllus, commonly known as the glossy euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Euonymus oxyphyllus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
