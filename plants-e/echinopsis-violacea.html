Echinopsis violacea

Echinopsis violacea, commonly known as the violet moon cactus, is a cacti species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. It produces a dense cluster of spines that provide protection from herbivores.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and collectors.
