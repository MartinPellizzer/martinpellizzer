Euxolus lividus

Euxolus lividus, commonly known as the violetleaf euphorbia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically a deep violet or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cup-like structure, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
