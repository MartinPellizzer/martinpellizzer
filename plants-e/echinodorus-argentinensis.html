Echinodorus argentinensis

Echinodorus argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically light green to bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments. It is often used in aquariums due to its ability to tolerate a range of water conditions.

Echinodorus argentinensis is also known for its moderate growth rate and ease of care, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced aquatic gardeners.
