Eugenia venezuelensis

Eugenia venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some tropical regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
