Echinocactus setispinus var. orcuttii

Echinocactus setispinus var. orcuttii, commonly known as the Orcutt's hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically greenish-gray in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, branched inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
