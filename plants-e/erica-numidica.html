Erica numidica

Erica numidica, commonly known as the Numidian heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Erica numidica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
