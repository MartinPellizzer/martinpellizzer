Eragrostis vulgaris

Eragrostis vulgaris, commonly known as common lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Eragrostis vulgaris is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
