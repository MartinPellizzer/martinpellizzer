Etlingera stenophylla

Etlingera stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related species. The plant is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and clusters of bright red flowers that resemble torches. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a glossy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
