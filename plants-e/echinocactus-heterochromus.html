Echinocactus heterochromus

Echinocactus heterochromus, commonly known as the blue hibiscus cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in blue-gray spines. The plant produces showy, violet or blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
