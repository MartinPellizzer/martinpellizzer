Ebingeria elegans

Ebingeria elegans, commonly known as the elegant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom above the water surface, often with a subtle pink or purple hue. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic life. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow waters and absorbing nutrients from the sediment.

Ebingeria elegans plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
