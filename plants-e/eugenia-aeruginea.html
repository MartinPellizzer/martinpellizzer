Eugenia aeruginea

Eugenia aeruginea, commonly known as the silver leaf eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Eugenia aeruginea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
