Eruca vesicaria subvar. hispida

Eruca vesicaria subvar. hispida, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and small, kidney-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a slender, elongated silique that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
