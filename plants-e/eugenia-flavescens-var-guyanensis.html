Eugenia flavescens var. guyanensis

Eugenia flavescens var. guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana apple or Guyana cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It produces small, yellow to orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is used in local cuisines and has potential medicinal properties.
