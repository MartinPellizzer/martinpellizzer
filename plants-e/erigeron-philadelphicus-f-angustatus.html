Erigeron philadelphicus f. angustatus

Erigeron philadelphicus f. angustatus, commonly known as narrowleaf fleabane, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
