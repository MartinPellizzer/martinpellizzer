Erica irregularis

Erica irregularis, commonly known as the irregular heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family Ericaceae.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica irregularis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs.
