Erythronium sibiricum subsp. sulevii

Erythronium sibiricum subsp.

sulevii, commonly known as the Siberian dog's tooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues.

The plant produces a single, showy, bell-shaped flower with a distinctive purple to pink coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows.

Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
