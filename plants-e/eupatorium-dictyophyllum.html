Eupatorium dictyophyllum

Eupatorium dictyophyllum, commonly known as the clustered thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium dictyophyllum is typically found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands.
