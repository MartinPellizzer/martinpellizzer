Elsholtzia major

Elsholtzia major, commonly known as the tall elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils contain various volatile compounds, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine.

Elsholtzia major is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
