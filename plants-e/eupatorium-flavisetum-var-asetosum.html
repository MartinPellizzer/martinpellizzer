Eupatorium flavisetum var. asetosum

Eupatorium flavisetum var. asetosum, commonly known as the yellow woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The flowering period usually occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
