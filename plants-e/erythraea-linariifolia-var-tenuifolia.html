Erythraea linariifolia var. tenuifolia

Erythraea linariifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved erythraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

This variant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
