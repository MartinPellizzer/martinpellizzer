Eupatorium occidentale var. decemplex

Eupatorium occidentale var. decemplex, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, flat-topped clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are gray-green in color and often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to wet soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued in native gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
