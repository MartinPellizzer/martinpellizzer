Ephebopogon

Ephebopogon, commonly known as the slender grass.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and tapering, often with a bluish-green hue.

Ephebopogon produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.
