Epidendrum luceroae

Epidendrum luceroae, commonly known as the Lucero's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, hairy leaves that are often dark green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum luceroae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
