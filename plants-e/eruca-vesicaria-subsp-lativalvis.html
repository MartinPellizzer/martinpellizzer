Eruca vesicaria subsp. lativalvis

Eruca vesicaria subsp.

lativalvis, commonly known as broad-leafed wall rocket, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found growing along roadsides and in rocky areas. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a popular ingredient in salads and traditional cuisines.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger leaf size compared to other Eruca species.
