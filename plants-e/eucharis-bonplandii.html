Eucharis bonplandii

Eucharis bonplandii, commonly known as the Bonplandian amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It is commonly cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Eucharis bonplandii typically produces offsets, allowing for propagation through division of the rhizomes.
