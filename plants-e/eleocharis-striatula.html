Eleocharis striatula

Eleocharis striatula, commonly known as the stripe spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a distinct striated pattern, contributing to its common name.

Eleocharis striatula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
