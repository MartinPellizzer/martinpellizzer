Equisetum variegatum var. pseudoelongatum

Equisetum variegatum var. pseudoelongatum, commonly known as the variable horsetail, is a variant of the common horsetail.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its jointed, segmented stems and whorled arrangement of leaves. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its stems are typically green or yellow-green, and the leaves are small, scale-like, and often inconspicuous.

This variety is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.
