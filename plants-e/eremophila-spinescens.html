Eremophila spinescens

Eremophila spinescens, commonly known as the spinous emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, often attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Eremophila spinescens is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ornamental value.
