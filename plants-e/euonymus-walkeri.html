Euonymus walkeri

Euonymus walkeri, commonly known as the burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage that turns brilliant orange in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth margin, typically ranging from 2 to 5 inches in length. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
