Eryngium humile var. gigantophyllum

Eryngium humile var. gigantophyllum, commonly known as the giant sea holly, is a striking perennial plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It features spiky, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by silver-blue bracts, creating a dramatic appearance. The plant has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and hardy growth habits make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
