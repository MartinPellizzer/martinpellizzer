Euphorbia demnatensis

Euphorbia demnatensis, commonly known as the Demnat's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the semi-arid regions of North Africa.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its succulent stems and leaves, which are typically covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish in color, surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Euphorbia demnatensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
