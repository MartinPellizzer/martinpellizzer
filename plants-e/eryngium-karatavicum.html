Eryngium karatavicum

Eryngium karatavicum, commonly known as the Karatavicum thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features spiny, blue-green leaves and produces striking purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble thistle-like structures. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium karatavicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
