Eria conrardii

Eria conrardii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Eria conrardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
