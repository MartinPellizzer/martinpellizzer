Evolvulus echioides var. minor

Evolvulus echioides var. minor, commonly known as the lesser wood sorrel, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens. Its rapid growth and ability to self-seed make it both a weed and a hardy garden plant.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is part of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).
