Elatine triandra var. orientalis

Elatine triandra var. orientalis, commonly known as the Oriental waterwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters, and it produces small, elliptical seeds.

This variety is often used in water gardens and as a decorative plant in aquatic environments.
