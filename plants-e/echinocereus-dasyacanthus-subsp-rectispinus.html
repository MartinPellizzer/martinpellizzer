Echinocereus dasyacanthus subsp. rectispinus

Echinocereus dasyacanthus subsp.

rectispinus, commonly known as the Mexican hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and is typically found in rocky, arid environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal watering, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
