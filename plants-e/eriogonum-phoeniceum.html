Eriogonum phoeniceum

Eriogonum phoeniceum, commonly known as the redstem globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, reddish stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Eriogonum phoeniceum is an important species for pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
