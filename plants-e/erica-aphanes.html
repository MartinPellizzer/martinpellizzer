Erica aphanes

Erica aphanes, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and dense growth habit. The plant typically produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Erica aphanes thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
