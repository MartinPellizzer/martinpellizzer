Echinopsis pentlandii var. gracilispina

Echinopsis pentlandii var. gracilispina, commonly known as the slender-spined moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and as a houseplant.
