Euphorbia flavicoma subsp. giselae

Euphorbia flavicoma subsp.

giselae, commonly known as the Giselae Spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
