Eriogonum brevicaule var. huberi

Eriogonum brevicaule var. huberi, commonly known as the Huber's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience to arid conditions.
