Elegia fastigata

Elegia fastigata, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a compact, cushion-like formation.

Elegia fastigata thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
