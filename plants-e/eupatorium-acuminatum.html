Eupatorium acuminatum

Eupatorium acuminatum, commonly known as narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in white or pale pink, which appear in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks.

Eupatorium acuminatum is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it plays a role in supporting local wildlife and ecological diversity.
