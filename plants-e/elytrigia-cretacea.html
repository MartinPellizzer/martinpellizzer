Elytrigia cretacea

Elytrigia cretacea, commonly known as the Cretan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Elytrigia cretacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
