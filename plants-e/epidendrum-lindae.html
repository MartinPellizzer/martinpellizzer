Epidendrum lindae

Epidendrum lindae, commonly known as Lind's Epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its roots are adapted to grow in loose, well-draining soil. Epidendrum lindae is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.

It prefers bright, indirect light and consistent moisture to thrive.
