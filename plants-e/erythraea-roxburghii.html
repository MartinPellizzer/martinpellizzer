Erythraea roxburghii

Erythraea roxburghii, commonly known as the red sanders, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a legume containing several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Due to overharvesting, it is now listed as an endangered species.
