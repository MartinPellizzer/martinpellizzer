Erodium verbenifolium

Erodium verbenifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf bird's-beak, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in dry, disturbed soils such as along roadsides and in arid environments.

Erodium verbenifolium is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
