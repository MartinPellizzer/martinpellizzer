Epilobium × blockianum

Epilobium × blockianum, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, riverbanks, and wet meadows.

Epilobium × blockianum is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
