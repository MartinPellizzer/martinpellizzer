Eryngium intermedium

Eryngium intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking blue to purple spiky flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant has a robust root system and hairy, gray-green leaves that are often lobed or toothed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Eryngium intermedium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
