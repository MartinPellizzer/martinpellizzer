Eupatorium lanigerum var. oppositifolium

Eupatorium lanigerum var. oppositifolium, commonly known as the oppositeleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
