Eria rimannii

Eria rimannii, commonly known as the Riemann's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which are typically fragrant and bloom during the wet season. Eria rimannii is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
