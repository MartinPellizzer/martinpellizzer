Eleocharis acicularis subsp. bonariensis

Eleocharis acicularis subsp.

bonariensis, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its reproductive structures consist of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, dark brown achenes.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
