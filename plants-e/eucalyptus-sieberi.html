Eucalyptus sieberi

Eucalyptus sieberi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and flaky, peeling bark. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in winter. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue.

Eucalyptus sieberi is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.
