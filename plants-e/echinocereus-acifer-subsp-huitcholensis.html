Echinocereus acifer subsp. huitcholensis

Echinocereus acifer subsp.

huitcholensis, commonly known as the Huichol cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it resilient to drought. It is often found in rocky soils and is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is named after the Huichol people, who are indigenous to the region where it is found.
