Eulophia bakeri

Eulophia bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and produces inflorescences with multiple showy blooms. Eulophia bakeri thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the diverse orchid flora of its native habitat.
