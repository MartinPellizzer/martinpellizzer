Eucalyptus pseudoglobulus

Eucalyptus pseudoglobulus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The flowers are typically small and arranged in clusters, producing a distinctive yellow-green color. This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
