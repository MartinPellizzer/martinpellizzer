Eugenia ravenii

Eugenia ravenii, commonly known as the Raven's Wood or black myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia ravenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a rich history in traditional medicine.
