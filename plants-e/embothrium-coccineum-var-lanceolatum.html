Embothrium coccineum var. lanceolatum

Embothrium coccineum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
