Erigeron armenus

Erigeron armenus, commonly known as the Armenian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are often pale blue or white.

Erigeron armenus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
