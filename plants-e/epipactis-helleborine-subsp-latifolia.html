Epipactis helleborine subsp. latifolia

Epipactis helleborine subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers with a prominent lip.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The subspecies latifolia is distinguished by its wider leaves compared to other subspecies of Epipactis helleborine.
