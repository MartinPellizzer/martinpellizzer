Eremocharis triradiata

Eremocharis triradiata, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with long, slender stems and pale green leaves that resemble the shape of a ghost. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Eremocharis triradiata is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
