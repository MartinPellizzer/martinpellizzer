Epidendrum amplexicaule

Epidendrum amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping epidendrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a unique appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in spikes and can range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety.

Epidendrum amplexicaule is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
