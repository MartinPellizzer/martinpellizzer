Erythraea portensis

Erythraea portensis, commonly known as the Port Island flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats near the coast.

Erythraea portensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
