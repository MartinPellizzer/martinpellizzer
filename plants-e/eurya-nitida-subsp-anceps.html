Eurya nitida subsp. anceps

Eurya nitida subsp.

anceps, commonly known as the glossy buckel-leaf tea, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, black berries that mature in late summer to early autumn.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shaded garden settings.
