Epilobium riphaeum

Epilobium riphaeum, commonly known as water thimble or narrow-leaved willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. The plant has slender, often hairy stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and usually have a glandular underside.

The flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense terminal spikes, and bloom from June to August.
