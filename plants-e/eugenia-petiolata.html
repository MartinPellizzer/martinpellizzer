Eugenia petiolata

Eugenia petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish or red fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Eugenia petiolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in home gardens.
