Eugenia firma

Eugenia firma, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia firma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
