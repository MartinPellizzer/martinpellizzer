Erophila corsica

Erophila corsica, commonly known as the Corsican sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Erophila corsica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
