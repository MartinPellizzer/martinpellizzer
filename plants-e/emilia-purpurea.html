Emilia purpurea

Emilia purpurea, commonly known as common emilia, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine white down, giving them a soft appearance.

Emilia purpurea is often grown as a ornamental plant for its delicate blooms and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
