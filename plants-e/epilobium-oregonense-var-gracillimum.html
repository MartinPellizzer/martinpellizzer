Epilobium oregonense var. gracillimum

Epilobium oregonense var. gracillimum, commonly known as Oregon willow, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers, which range in color from pink to red, are arranged in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
