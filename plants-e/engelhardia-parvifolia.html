Engelhardia parvifolia

Engelhardia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved engelhardia, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs. Its flowers are small and white or pale pink, often clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
