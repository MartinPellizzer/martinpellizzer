Eugenia congolensis

Eugenia congolensis, commonly known as the Congo guava, is a small evergreen tree native to central Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
