Eremurus aschersonii

Eremurus aschersonii, commonly known as the desert candle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Eremurus aschersonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
