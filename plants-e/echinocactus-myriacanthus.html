Echinocactus myriacanthus

Echinocactus myriacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, which is typically a bright yellow or greenish-yellow color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
