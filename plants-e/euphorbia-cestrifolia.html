Euphorbia cestrifolia

Euphorbia cestrifolia, commonly known as the woolly spurge, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia cestrifolia is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
