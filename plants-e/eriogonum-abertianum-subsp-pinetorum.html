Eriogonum abertianum subsp. pinetorum

Eriogonum abertianum subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the Sierra Valley buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its pale green to yellowish leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
