Erysimum grandiflorum var. ramosum

Erysimum grandiflorum var. ramosum, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
