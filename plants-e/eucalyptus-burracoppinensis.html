Eucalyptus burracoppinensis

Eucalyptus burracoppinensis, commonly known as the Burra burra, is a species of eucalyptus native to the wet tropics of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically mid to dark green in color. The plant produces small, globular white flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences at the ends of branches. It is a medium to tall tree, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters, and is adapted to thrive in humid, subtropical climates.

The species is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
