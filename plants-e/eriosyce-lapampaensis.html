Eriosyce lapampaensis

Eriosyce lapampaensis, commonly known as the Lapampan Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Peru.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular, spiny stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Eriosyce lapampaensis is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
