Eriosyce curvispina subsp. robusta

Eriosyce curvispina subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the robusta cholla, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in dense clusters of spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its robust growth habit and ability to store water in its stems make it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in dry climates.
