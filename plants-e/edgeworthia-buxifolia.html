Edgeworthia buxifolia

Edgeworthia buxifolia, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.
