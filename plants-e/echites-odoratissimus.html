Echites odoratissimus

Echites odoratissimus, commonly known as the sweet-smelling echites, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
