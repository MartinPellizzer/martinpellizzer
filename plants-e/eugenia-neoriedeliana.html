Eugenia neoriedeliana

Eugenia neoriedeliana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia neoriedeliana*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy green appearance.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
