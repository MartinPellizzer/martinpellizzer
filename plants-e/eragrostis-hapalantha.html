Eragrostis hapalantha

Eragrostis hapalantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its dense root system.
