Emilia aurita

Emilia aurita, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Emilia aurita grows up to 60 cm tall and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

It is cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional herbal medicine.
