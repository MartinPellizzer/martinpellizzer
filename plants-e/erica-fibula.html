Erica fibula

Erica fibula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Erica fibula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
