Ericoides wendlandianum

Ericoides wendlandianum, commonly known as the Wendland's erica, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
