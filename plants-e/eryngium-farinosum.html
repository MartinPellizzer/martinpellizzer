Eryngium farinosum

Eryngium farinosum, commonly known as the powdered thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and fuzzy, grayish foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into thistle-like bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eryngium farinosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
