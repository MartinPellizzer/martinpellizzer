Eragrostis plumosa

Eragrostis plumosa, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a soft, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive texture.

Eragrostis plumosa is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
