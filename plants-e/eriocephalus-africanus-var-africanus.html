Eriocephalus africanus var. africanus

Eriocephalus africanus var. africanus, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, silver-green leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, and its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
