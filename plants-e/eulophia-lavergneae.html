Eulophia lavergneae

Eulophia lavergneae, commonly known as the Lavergne's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. The plant produces striking, elongated flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Eulophia lavergneae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
