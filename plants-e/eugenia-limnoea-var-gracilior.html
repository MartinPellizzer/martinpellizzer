Eugenia limnoea var. gracilior

Eugenia limnoea var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender water myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Eugenia limnoea var. gracilior plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic life.
