Erysimum lycaonicum

Erysimum lycaonicum, commonly known as the Lycaonia wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Erysimum lycaonicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
