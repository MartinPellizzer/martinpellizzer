Erigeron uniflorus subsp. typicus

Erigeron uniflorus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the single-flower fleabane, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States. It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces a single, showy blue to purple flower atop a slender stem. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in dry to mesic environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
