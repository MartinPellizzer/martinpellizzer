Echinochloa zizanioides

Echinochloa zizanioides, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a widespread annual grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its flat, blade-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence, which contains small, two-lobed spikelets. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, making it a common weed. It has a robust root system that allows it to spread rapidly and compete aggressively with crops.

Due to its adaptability and rapid growth, it is considered a significant weed in many parts of the world.
