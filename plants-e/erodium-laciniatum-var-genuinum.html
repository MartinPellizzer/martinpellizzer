Erodium laciniatum var. genuinum

Erodium laciniatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
