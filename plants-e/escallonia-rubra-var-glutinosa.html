Escallonia rubra var. glutinosa

Escallonia rubra var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky escallonia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or red flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable.
