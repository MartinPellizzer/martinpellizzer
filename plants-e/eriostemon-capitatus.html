Eriostemon capitatus

Eriostemon capitatus, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
