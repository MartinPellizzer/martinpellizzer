Euonymus alatus f. subtriflorus

Euonymus alatus f. subtriflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered winged euonymus, is a variant of the winged euonymus, characterized by its unique floral structure.

This plant is a deciduous shrub native to China, where it thrives in woodland and scrub habitats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive winged appearance along the petioles. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters of three, giving it its common name.

It produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals, contributing to its ecological role.
