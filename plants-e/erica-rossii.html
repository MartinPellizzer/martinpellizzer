Erica rossii

Erica rossii, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter, adding a striking contrast to its evergreen foliage. Erica rossii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
