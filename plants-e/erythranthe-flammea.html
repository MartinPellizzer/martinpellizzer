Erythranthe flammea

Erythranthe flammea, commonly known as the fireweed or redstem filaree, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, bright red flowers. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces quickly.
