Eupatorium purpureum

Eupatorium purpureum, commonly known as purple boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, lavender-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Purple boneset is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
