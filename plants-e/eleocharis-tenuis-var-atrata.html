Eleocharis tenuis var. atrata

Eleocharis tenuis var. atrata, commonly known as blackstem spikegrass, is a variant of the slender spikegrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps. The plant is characterized by its dark-colored stems, which distinguish it from other varieties of Eleocharis. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing structures.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
