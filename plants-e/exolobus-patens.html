Exolobus patens

Exolobus patens, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Exolobus patens thrives in coastal and sandy soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
