Eragrostis ciliata

Eragrostis ciliata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that are often covered with delicate cilia, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow cluster of small spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

Eragrostis ciliata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can also serve as forage for livestock in certain regions.
