Eucalyptus absita

Eucalyptus absita, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub or tree form. The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color, and it produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters. Eucalyptus absita is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a Mediterranean climate.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
