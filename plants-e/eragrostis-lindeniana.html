Eragrostis lindeniana

Eragrostis lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindenberg's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and panicle-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some drought tolerance.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
