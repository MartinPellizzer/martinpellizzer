Erythroxylum hypericifolium

Erythroxylum hypericifolium, commonly known as the redleaf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often red or purple in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are follicles that contain seeds.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
