Ervatamia pubescens var. superba

Ervatamia pubescens var. superba, commonly known as the hairy ervatamia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features hairy, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and edible plants.
