Elymus sibiricus var. pilosifolius

Elymus sibiricus var. pilosifolius, commonly known as the silky threadgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as meadows and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
