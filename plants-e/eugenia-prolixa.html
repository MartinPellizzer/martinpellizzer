Eugenia prolixa

Eugenia prolixa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia prolixa*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eugenia prolixa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage.
