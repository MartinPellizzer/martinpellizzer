Euphorbia mancinella

Euphorbia mancinella, commonly known as the sweet euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a short, thick stem with clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves and stem.

Like many euphorbias, it produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin.
