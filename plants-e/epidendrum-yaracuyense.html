Epidendrum yaracuyense

Epidendrum yaracuyense, commonly known as the Yaracuyense orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually colorful, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
