Eremophila splendens

Eremophila splendens, commonly known as the splendour bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit and often displays a range of flower colors, including red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal and semi-arid regions.

Eremophila splendens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
