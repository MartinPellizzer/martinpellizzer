Eriogonum sulcatum var. argense

Eriogonum sulcatum var. argense, commonly known as the Argenteum buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
