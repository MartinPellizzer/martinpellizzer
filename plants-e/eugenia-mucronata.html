Eugenia mucronata

Eugenia mucronata, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to coastal regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in brackish water environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, reddish drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia mucronata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
