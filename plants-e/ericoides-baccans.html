Ericoides baccans

Ericoides baccans, commonly known as the red-fruited heath, is a shrub native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants adapted to nutrient-poor soils. The plant features small, evergreen leaves and produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its growth is typically dense and compact, forming thickets that provide habitat for small animals.

Ericoides baccans thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.
