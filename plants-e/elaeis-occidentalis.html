Elaeis occidentalis

Elaeis occidentalis, commonly known as the oil palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and tall, straight trunk. The plant produces clusters of fruits that contain the oil-rich seeds, which are the primary source of palm oil. Its cultivation is economically significant in many tropical regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Arecaceae family and is closely related to other palm species.
