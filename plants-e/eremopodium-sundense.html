Eremopodium sundense

Eremopodium sundense, commonly known as the Sundanese eremopodium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Eremopodaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a short, erect stem and waxy, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss.

Eremopodium sundense is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
