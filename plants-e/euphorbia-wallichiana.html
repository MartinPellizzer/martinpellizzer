Euphorbia wallichiana

Euphorbia wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan spurge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, stem-like leaves and a distinctive inflorescence called a cyathium. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers surrounded by modified leaves known as bracts. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Euphorbia wallichiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
