Eupatorium inaequidens

Eupatorium inaequidens, commonly known as the roughleaf thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically rough to the touch, hence its common name, and are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
