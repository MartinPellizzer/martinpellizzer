Eupatorium inulifolium f. lasiophlebium

Eupatorium inulifolium f. lasiophlebium, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming from midsummer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
