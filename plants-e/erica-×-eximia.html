Erica × eximia

Erica × eximia, commonly known as the scented heath, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica × eximia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
