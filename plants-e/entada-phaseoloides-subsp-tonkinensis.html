Entada phaseoloides subsp. tonkinensis

Entada phaseoloides subsp.

tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin legume or Tonkin bean, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, woody layer. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of leguminous protein.
