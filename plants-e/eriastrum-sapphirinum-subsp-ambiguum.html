Eriastrum sapphirinum subsp. ambiguum

Eriastrum sapphirinum subsp.

ambiguum, commonly known as the blueflower eriastrum, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
