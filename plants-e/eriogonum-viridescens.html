Eriogonum viridescens

Eriogonum viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with branching stems that often root at the nodes.

Eriogonum viridescens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with limited rainfall.
