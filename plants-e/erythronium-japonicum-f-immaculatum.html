Erythronium japonicum f. immaculatum

Erythronium japonicum f. immaculatum, commonly known as the Japanese dog's tooth violet, is a striking bulbous plant native to Japan.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale lavender, with a distinctive, tooth-like pattern on the petals. The plant has a short, sturdy stem that supports the blooms, which appear in early spring. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that emerge in early spring as well.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and elegant appearance.
