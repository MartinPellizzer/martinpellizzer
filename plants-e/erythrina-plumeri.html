Erythrina plumeri

Erythrina plumeri, commonly known as the flame bean, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine. Erythrina plumeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a distinctive and recognizable species in its native habitat.
