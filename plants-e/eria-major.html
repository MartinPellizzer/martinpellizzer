Eria major

Eria major, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Eria major is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
