Eriogonum caespitosum var. alyssoides

Eriogonum caespitosum var. alyssoides, commonly known as the Alyssum-like Buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
