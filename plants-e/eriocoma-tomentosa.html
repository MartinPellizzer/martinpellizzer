Eriocoma tomentosa

Eriocoma tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly eriocoma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white to grayish hairs, which gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny conditions. Its small, pinkish to white flowers are clustered in dense heads, and the leaves are narrow, often covered in a similar hairy coating.

Eriocoma tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
