Eugenia paniala

Eugenia paniala, commonly known as the Paniala clove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, dark red berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Eugenia paniala make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
