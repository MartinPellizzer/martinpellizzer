Erysimum hieraciifolium subsp. durum

Erysimum hieraciifolium subsp.

durum, commonly known as the Alpine wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a fern-like appearance, and it produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates. It is often found in open slopes and meadows at high elevations.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Brassicaceae family.
