Eugenia bernieri var. lateriticolor

Eugenia bernieri var. lateriticolor, commonly known as the lateritic color eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that often exhibit a striking variegation of colors, including shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or lateritic landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
