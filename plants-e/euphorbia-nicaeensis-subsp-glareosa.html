Euphorbia nicaeensis subsp. glareosa

Euphorbia nicaeensis subsp.

glareosa, commonly known as the Sicilian spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, ribbed stems and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiny, leafless stems and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
