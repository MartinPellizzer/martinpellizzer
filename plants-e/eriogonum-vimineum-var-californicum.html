Eriogonum vimineum var. californicum

Eriogonum vimineum var. californicum, commonly known as the California buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its wiry, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
