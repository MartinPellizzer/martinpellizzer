Echinocactus ancylacanthus

Echinocactus ancylacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its spines are modified leaves, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss through transpiration.

Echinocactus ancylacanthus is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus gardens.
