Elaeagnus argentea subsp. rotundifolia

Elaeagnus argentea subsp.

rotundifolia, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding plant life.
