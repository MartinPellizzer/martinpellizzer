Eurybia erubescens

Eurybia erubescens, commonly known as the red stem bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a reddish tinge. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Eurybia erubescens is valued in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
