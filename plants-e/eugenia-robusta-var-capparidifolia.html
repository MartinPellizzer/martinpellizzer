Eugenia robusta var. capparidifolia

Eugenia robusta var. capparidifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture and prominent venation. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit, which is small and berry-like, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor.
