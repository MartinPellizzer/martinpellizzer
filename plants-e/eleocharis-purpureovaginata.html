Eleocharis purpureovaginata

Eleocharis purpureovaginata, commonly known as purple false rice, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are often pink or purple, giving it a striking appearance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
