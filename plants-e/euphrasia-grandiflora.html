Euphrasia grandiflora

Euphrasia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered eyebright, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Euphrasia grandiflora is often found in the genus Euphrasia, which includes many species with similar medicinal and ornamental uses.
