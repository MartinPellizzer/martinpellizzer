Euxolus lineatus

Euxolus lineatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed euphorbia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a succulent stem that stores water, adapting it to survive in dry conditions.

The species is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
