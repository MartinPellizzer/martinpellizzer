Eugenioides brongniartianum

Eugenioides brongniartianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy evergreen leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and fruit-bearing qualities.

Eugenioides brongniartianum is also known for its aromatic foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
