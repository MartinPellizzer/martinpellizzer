Erythrina × fluminensis

Erythrina × fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that appear in dense clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its woody structure and rapid growth make it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Erythrina × fluminensis also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
