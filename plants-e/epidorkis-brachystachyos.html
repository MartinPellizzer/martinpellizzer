Epidorkis brachystachyos

Epidorkis brachystachyos, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
