Erysimum bocconei var. henriquesii

Erysimum bocconei var. henriquesii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, high-altitude environments. It has linear to lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit.

This variety is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.
