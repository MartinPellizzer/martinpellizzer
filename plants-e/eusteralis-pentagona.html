Eusteralis pentagona

Eusteralis pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal shape, which is reflected in its scientific name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eusteralis pentagona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
