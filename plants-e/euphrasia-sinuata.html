Euphrasia sinuata

Euphrasia sinuata, commonly known as the zigzag gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight sinusoidal shape, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Euphrasia sinuata is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
