Ericoila loureiroi

Ericoila loureiroi, commonly known as the Brazilian heath, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, dark purple or black berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
