Epidendrum lamellatum

Epidendrum lamellatum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Amazon, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinct lip. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
