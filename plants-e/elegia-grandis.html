Elegia grandis

Elegia grandis, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a thick, spongy stem that helps it float on the water's surface while anchoring it to the bottom. Its flowers are typically white or pink, blooming at night and emitting a mild fragrance to attract pollinators.

Elegia grandis plays an important ecological role in its native wetlands by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water filtration.
