Echium albereanum

Echium albereanum, commonly known as the Alpine evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Echium albereanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
