Echinopsis × climaxantha

Echinopsis × climaxantha, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Echinopsis chamaecaulis and Echinopsis subdenudata.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and a distinctive, often brightly colored crown. The plant is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent collectors and desert gardens.
