Echinocactus hamatacanthus var. longihamatus

Echinocactus hamatacanthus var. longihamatus, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and prominent, spiny areoles that bear both radial and central spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. It is a slow-growing succulent that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
