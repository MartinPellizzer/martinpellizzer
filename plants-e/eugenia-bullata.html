Eugenia bullata

Eugenia bullata, commonly known as the broad-leaved eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in heathlands and open forests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white bloom. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are clustered in spikes, attracting a variety of pollinators.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
