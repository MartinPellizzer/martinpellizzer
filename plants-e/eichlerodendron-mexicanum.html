Eichlerodendron mexicanum

Eichlerodendron mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican wax palm, is a tall, slender palm native to southern Mexico.

It features a single, unbranched trunk that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets that are covered in a waxy cuticle. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by clustered, spherical fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
