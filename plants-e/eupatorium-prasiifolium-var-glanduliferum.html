Eupatorium prasiifolium var. glanduliferum

Eupatorium prasiifolium var. glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular greenleaf thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with glandular dots on their surfaces. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clustered inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
