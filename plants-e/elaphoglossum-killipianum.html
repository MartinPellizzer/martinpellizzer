Elaphoglossum killipianum

Elaphoglossum killipianum, commonly known as the Killipian holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a finely divided structure, typical of many ferns in the genus Elaphoglossum. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for studies on fern morphology and ecological adaptation.
