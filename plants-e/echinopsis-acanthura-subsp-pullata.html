Echinopsis acanthura subsp. pullata

Echinopsis acanthura subsp.

pullata, commonly known as the Pachyveria, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
