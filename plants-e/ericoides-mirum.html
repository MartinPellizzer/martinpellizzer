Ericoides mirum

Ericoides mirum, commonly known as the fairy moss, is a small, delicate plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and its ability to thrive in acidic, nutrient-poor soils. The plant features tiny, scale-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are often pink or white. Ericoides mirum is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rock crevices.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a distinctive member of the ericoid mosses.
