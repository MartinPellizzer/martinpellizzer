Epilobium variabile

Epilobium variabile, commonly known as variable willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

Epilobium variabile is an annual or biennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
