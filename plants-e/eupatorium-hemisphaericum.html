Eupatorium hemisphaericum

Eupatorium hemisphaericum, commonly known as the redroot verbena, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
