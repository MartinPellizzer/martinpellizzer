Erianthus saccharoides var. angustifolius

Erianthus saccharoides var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved sugarcane grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and high biomass production, making it suitable for forage and biofuel applications. Its leaves are typically bluish-green in color and have a distinct, fibrous texture.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential in sustainable agriculture.
