Epacris campanulata var. rosea

Epacris campanulata var. rosea, commonly known as the pink bell heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, evergreen stems and clusters of tubular, pink or rose-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly honeyeaters, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
