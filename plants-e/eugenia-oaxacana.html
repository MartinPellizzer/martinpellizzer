Eugenia oaxacana

Eugenia oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Eugenia oaxacana is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
