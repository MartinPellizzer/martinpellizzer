Eranthemum malabaricum

Eranthemum malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar balm, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Eranthemum malabaricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
