Eupatorium trinervum

Eupatorium trinervum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Eupatorium trinervum is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
