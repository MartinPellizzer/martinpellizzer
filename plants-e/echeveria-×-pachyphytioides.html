Echeveria × pachyphytioides

Echeveria × pachyphytioides, commonly known as the "Blue Echeveria," is a hybrid succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white trichomes, giving it a powdery appearance. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers during the spring or summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact size and striking foliage make it a desirable ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
