Euphorbia xiangxiui

Euphorbia xiangxiui, commonly known as the Xiangxiui spurge, is a succulent plant native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Euphorbia xiangxiui is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
