Epipactis dolabripetala

Epipactis dolabripetala, commonly known as the broad-lobed helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is known for its complex pollination mechanisms, involving specific insect species.
