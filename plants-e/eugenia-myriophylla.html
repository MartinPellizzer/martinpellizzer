Eugenia myriophylla

Eugenia myriophylla, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange, which bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
