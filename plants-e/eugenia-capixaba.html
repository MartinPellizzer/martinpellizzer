Eugenia capixaba

Eugenia capixaba, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or capixaba cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic wood and traditional medicinal uses.
