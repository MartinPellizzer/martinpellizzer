Eucalyptus confertiflora

Eucalyptus confertiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved mallee, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, greyish-brown bark that peels in thin, fibrous strips. The plant produces clusters of small, creamy-white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and often have a silver-white underside.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting a high degree of drought tolerance.
