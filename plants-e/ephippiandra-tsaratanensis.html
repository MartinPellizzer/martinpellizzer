Ephippiandra tsaratanensis

Ephippiandra tsaratanensis, commonly known as the Madagascar starfish plant, is a unique succulent native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often adorned with white or yellow markings. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its adaptations to the arid conditions of its native habitat include thick, fleshy leaves that store water and a slow growth rate.

Ephippiandra tsaratanensis is a sought-after species among succulent enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and rarity.
