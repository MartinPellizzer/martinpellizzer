Eugenia paranaguensis

Eugenia paranaguensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraria*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical climates.

Eugenia paranaguensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
