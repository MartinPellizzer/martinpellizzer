Eria ovilis

Eria ovilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Eria ovilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
