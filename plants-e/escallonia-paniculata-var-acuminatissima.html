Escallonia paniculata var. acuminatissima

Escallonia paniculata var. acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Escallonia paniculata var. acuminatissima prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
