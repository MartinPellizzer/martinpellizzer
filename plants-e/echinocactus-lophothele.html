Echinocactus lophothele

Echinocactus lophothele, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by spiny, spherical fruits. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and as a houseplant.
