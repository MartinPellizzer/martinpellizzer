Eucarya acuminata

Eucarya acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed eucarya, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a pointed apex, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. Its fibrous root system helps it thrive in well-drained soils, and it is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

Eucarya acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering display.
