Eucalyptus virgata var. obtusiflora

Eucalyptus virgata var. obtusiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which appear in winter and early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
