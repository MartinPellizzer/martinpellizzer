Eupatorium hakonense

Eupatorium hakonense, commonly known as the Hawaiian feverfew, is a perennial herb native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Hawaiian medicine.

Its growth habit and floral structure make it a distinctive member of the Eupatorium genus.
