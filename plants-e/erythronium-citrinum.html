Erythronium citrinum

Erythronium citrinum, commonly known as the yellow trout lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or green. The plant produces a single, upright flower with a white or pale yellow perianth and a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is known for its early spring blooming period.

The leaves and flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns, which are characteristic of the genus Erythronium.
