Erodium malacoides subvar. microphyllum

Erodium malacoides subvar. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved common erodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
