Eriogonum sphaerocephalum var. fasciculifolium

Eriogonum sphaerocephalum var. fasciculifolium, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged in fascicles, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
