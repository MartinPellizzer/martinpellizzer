Erica pulvinitormis

Erica pulvinitormis, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This plant is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica pulvinitormis thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salt exposure.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
