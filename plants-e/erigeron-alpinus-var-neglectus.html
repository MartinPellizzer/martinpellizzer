Erigeron alpinus var. neglectus

Erigeron alpinus var. neglectus, commonly known as the alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to violet, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.
