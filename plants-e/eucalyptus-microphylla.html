Eucalyptus microphylla

Eucalyptus microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Eucalyptus microphylla is often found in shrublands and is an important component of the local flora in its habitat.
