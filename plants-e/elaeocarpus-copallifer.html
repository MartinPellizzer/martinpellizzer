Elaeocarpus copallifer

Elaeocarpus copallifer, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp. Its bark is thick and rough, often with a reddish or brownish hue, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
