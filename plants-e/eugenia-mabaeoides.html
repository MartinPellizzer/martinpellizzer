Eugenia mabaeoides

Eugenia mabaeoides, commonly known as the Mabae's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
