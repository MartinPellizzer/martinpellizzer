Euphorbia sieboldiana f. peninsularis

Euphorbia sieboldiana f. peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular spurge, is a succulent plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features a compact, shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique floral structures. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
