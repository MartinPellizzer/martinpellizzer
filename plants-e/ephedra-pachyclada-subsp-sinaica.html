Ephedra pachyclada subsp. sinaica

Ephedra pachyclada subsp.

sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in hot, dry climates and is adapted to poor, sandy soils. Ephedra pachyclada subsp.

sinaica is notable for its chemical composition, containing ephedrine, a compound with historical medicinal uses.
