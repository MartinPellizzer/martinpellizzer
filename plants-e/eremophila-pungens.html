Eremophila pungens

Eremophila pungens, commonly known as the spiked emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers range in color from pink to red and are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches.

Eremophila pungens is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
