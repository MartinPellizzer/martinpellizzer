Eugenia fulva

Eugenia fulva, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often followed by yellow or red berries. Eugenia fulva is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
