Euphorbia kimmerica

Euphorbia kimmerica, commonly known as the Kimmerica spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, branching stem covered in small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are cup-shaped structures containing numerous tiny flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
