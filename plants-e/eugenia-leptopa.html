Eugenia leptopa

Eugenia leptopa, commonly known as the slender eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other notable plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, round drupe, is typically red or yellow when ripe and contains a single seed.

Eugenia leptopa is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and potential medicinal properties.
