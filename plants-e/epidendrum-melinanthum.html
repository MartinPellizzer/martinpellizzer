Epidendrum melinanthum

Epidendrum melinanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Epidendrum melinanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
