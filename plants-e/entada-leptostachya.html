Entada leptostachya

Entada leptostachya, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise plant, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing flat, seed-rich seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems where it is native.
