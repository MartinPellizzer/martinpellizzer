Eryngium leptophyllum

Eryngium leptophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Eryngium leptophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flower structure.
