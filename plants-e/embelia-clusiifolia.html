Embelia clusiifolia

Embelia clusiifolia, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, globose berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
