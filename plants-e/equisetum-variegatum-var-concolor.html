Equisetum variegatum var. concolor

Equisetum variegatum var. concolor, commonly known as the green scouring rush, is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, segmented stems covered in small, scale-like structures.

It is a primitive vascular plant that retains primitive features such as spore-producing cones and a lack of true leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in wetlands. Its stems are used in traditional medicine and as a natural cleaning agent due to their abrasive texture.

The varietal form concolor distinguishes it by having a uniform green coloration throughout the stem, unlike other varieties with more pronounced color variations.
