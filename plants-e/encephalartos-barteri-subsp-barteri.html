Encephalartos barteri subsp. barteri

Encephalartos barteri subsp.

barteri, commonly known as the Barter's cycad, is a species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of pinnate, dark green leaves.

The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically appearing on separate plants. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
