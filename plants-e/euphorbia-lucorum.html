Euphorbia lucorum

Euphorbia lucorum, commonly known as the limestone spurge, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in a cyathium.

Euphorbia lucorum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
