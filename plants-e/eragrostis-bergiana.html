Eragrostis bergiana

Eragrostis bergiana, commonly known as Berg's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Eragrostis bergiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
