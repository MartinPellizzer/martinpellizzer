Echeveria rosaeformis

Echeveria rosaeformis, commonly known as the Rosy Form Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically pink to red in color, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
