Eulophia lutea

Eulophia lutea, commonly known as the yellow eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a rhizome that produces multiple pseudobulbs. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in a spike and often fragrant.

Eulophia lutea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
