Erica × vilmoriana

Erica × vilmoriana, commonly known as the Cape heath or the honey-scented heath, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded conditions. Erica × vilmoriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for gardens in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
