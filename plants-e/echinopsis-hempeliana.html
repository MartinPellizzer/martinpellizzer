Echinopsis hempeliana

Echinopsis hempeliana, commonly known as the "Hempel's moon cactus," is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a small, solitary plant with a short, thick stem and a crown of spiny, ribbed stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are often nocturnal in bloom. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Echinopsis hempeliana is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
