Elaeocarpus tectorius

Elaeocarpus tectorius, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often edible and contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical regions.
