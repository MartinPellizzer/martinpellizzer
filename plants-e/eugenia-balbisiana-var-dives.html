Eugenia balbisiana var. dives

Eugenia balbisiana var. dives, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple, segmented fruit that is highly valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit's outer rind, known as the pericarp, is thick and contains a rich, complex flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.
