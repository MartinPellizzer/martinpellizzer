Epidendrum variegatum var. lineatum

Epidendrum variegatum var. lineatum, commonly known as the Variegated Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with a mix of green, white, and pink hues, which adds to its ornamental value. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
