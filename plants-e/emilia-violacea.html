Emilia violacea

Emilia violacea, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. Emilia violacea is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
