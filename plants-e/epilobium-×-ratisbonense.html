Epilobium × ratisbonense

Epilobium × ratisbonense, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 60–120 cm, featuring erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, riverbanks, and in wet meadows.
