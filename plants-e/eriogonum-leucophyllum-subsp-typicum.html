Eriogonum leucophyllum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum leucophyllum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the whiteleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant has narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
