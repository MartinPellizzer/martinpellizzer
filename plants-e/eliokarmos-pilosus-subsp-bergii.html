Eliokarmos pilosus subsp. bergii

Eliokarmos pilosus subsp.

bergii, commonly known as the Berg's hairy elokarmos, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are typically covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
