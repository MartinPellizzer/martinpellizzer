Eupatorium dissectum

Eupatorium dissectum, commonly known as New England thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and finely dissected leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium dissectum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
