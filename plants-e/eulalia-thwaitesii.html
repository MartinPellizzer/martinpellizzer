Eulalia thwaitesii

Eulalia thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thwaites' grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. This perennial herb is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grassy slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems, though it is relatively uncommon in cultivation.
