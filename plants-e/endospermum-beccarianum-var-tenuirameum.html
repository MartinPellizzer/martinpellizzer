Endospermum beccarianum var. tenuirameum

Endospermum beccarianum var. tenuirameum, commonly known as the slender-leafed ghost gum, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its endosperm is well-developed, providing nourishment to the developing embryo.

This species is native to northern Australia and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
