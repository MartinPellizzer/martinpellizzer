Entada arenaria

Entada arenaria, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by long, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include a woody stem, bipinnate leaves, and a unique seed structure.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.
