Erigeron acris var. viridifolius

Erigeron acris var. viridifolius, commonly known as the greenleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving the plant its distinctive green foliage. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows.

This variety is often found in the central and eastern United States.
