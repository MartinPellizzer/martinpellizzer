Eriogonum corymbosum var. corymbosum

Eriogonum corymbosum var. corymbosum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
