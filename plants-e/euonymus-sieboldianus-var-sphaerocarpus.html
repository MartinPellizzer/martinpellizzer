Euonymus sieboldianus var. sphaerocarpus

Euonymus sieboldianus var. sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy dark green surface. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
