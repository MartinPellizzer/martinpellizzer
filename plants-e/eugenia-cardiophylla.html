Eugenia cardiophylla

Eugenia cardiophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are typically red when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
