Echinops aberdaricus

Echinops aberdaricus, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small globes, which give it its common name. The plant has silver-gray foliage and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Echinops aberdaricus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
