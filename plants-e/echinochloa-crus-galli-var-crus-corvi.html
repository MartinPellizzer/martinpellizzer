Echinochloa crus-galli var. crus-corvi

Echinochloa crus-galli var. crus-corvi, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense, branching panicles, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is commonly found in disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and agricultural areas.

This species is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
