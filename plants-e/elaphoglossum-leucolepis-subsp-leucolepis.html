Elaphoglossum leucolepis subsp. leucolepis

Elaphoglossum leucolepis subsp.

leucolepis, commonly known as the silver-stemmed holly fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family. It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of silvery scales.

The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous short, upright shoots.

The spore-bearing structures, or sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a thin, translucent indusium.
