Eremanthus pallidisetus

Eremanthus pallidisetus, commonly known as the pale-hairy eremanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive pale or hairy underside, contributing to its unique appearance. It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
