Epiamomum pungens

Epiamomum pungens, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant contains fine hairs that release an irritating chemical when touched, causing a skin reaction in humans. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Epiamomum pungens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
