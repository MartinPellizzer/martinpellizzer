Echinopsis poco var. albiflora

Echinopsis poco var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a succulent, adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
