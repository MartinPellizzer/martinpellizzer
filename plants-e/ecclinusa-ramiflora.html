Ecclinusa ramiflora

Ecclinusa ramiflora, commonly known as the branching-flower plant, is a unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that resemble small, delicate umbrellas. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop for its vibrant flowers. Ecclinusa ramiflora blooms throughout the year, with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
