Eryngium pseudothorifolium

Eryngium pseudothorifolium, commonly known as the false thorned eryngium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads that resemble a crown, surrounded by bracts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Eryngium pseudothorifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
