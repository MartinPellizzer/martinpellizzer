Epilobium × palustriflorum

Epilobium × palustriflorum, commonly known as swamp willow or marsh willow, is a hybrid species within the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and is typically found in wetland environments such as marshes and riverbanks. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

Epilobium × palustriflorum is known for its adaptability to moist soils and its role in stabilizing aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
