Eupatorium squalidum var. typicum

Eupatorium squalidum var. typicum, commonly known as swamp boneset, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with slender stems that are often reddish in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers in the summer months. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is well adapted to moist soil conditions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
