Evolvulus cuspidatus

Evolvulus cuspidatus, commonly known as the prickly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Evolvulus cuspidatus is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.
