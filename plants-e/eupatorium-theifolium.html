Eupatorium theifolium

Eupatorium theifolium, commonly known as false boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
