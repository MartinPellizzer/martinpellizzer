Elatine hydropiper subsp. gyrosperma

Elatine hydropiper subsp.

gyrosperma, commonly known as water pepper, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Elatinaceae. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces tiny, greenish flowers. Its fruits are small, spherical seeds that are dispersed by water, aiding in its spread.

This species is adapted to moist environments and is often found in temperate regions.
