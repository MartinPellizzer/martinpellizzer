Echinocactus mammulosus var. hircina

Echinocactus mammulosus var. hircina, commonly known as the hircine hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny cylindrical stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels at the stem tips. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to hot, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Due to its striking appearance and unique morphology, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in succulent gardens.
