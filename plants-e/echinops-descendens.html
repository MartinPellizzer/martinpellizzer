Echinops descendens

Echinops descendens, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky seed heads that resemble small globes, which are a key feature in its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of its distinctive seed heads. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Echinops descendens is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
