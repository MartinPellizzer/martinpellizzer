Ephedra fragilis f. disperma

Ephedra fragilis f. disperma, commonly known as the brittle ephedra, is a subspecies of the ephedra plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to extreme drought conditions.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has also been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
