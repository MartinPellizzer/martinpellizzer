Erysimum stoechadifolium

Erysimum stoechadifolium, commonly known as the Scottish wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal environments. It features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowers are usually pink or white.

Erysimum stoechadifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
