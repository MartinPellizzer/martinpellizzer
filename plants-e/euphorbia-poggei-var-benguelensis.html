Euphorbia poggei var. benguelensis

Euphorbia poggei var. benguelensis, commonly known as the Benguela euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny foliage, which help it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-shaped structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
