Eugenia ignota

Eugenia ignota, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or wild cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Eugenia ignota thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
