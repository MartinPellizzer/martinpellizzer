Echinospartum barnadesii subsp. dorsisericeum

Echinospartum barnadesii subsp.

dorsisericeum, commonly known as the spiny desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
