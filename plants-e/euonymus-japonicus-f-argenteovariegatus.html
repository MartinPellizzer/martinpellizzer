Euonymus japonicus f. argenteovariegatus

Euonymus japonicus f. argenteovariegatus, commonly known as the silver-veined Japanese spurge, is a varietal form of the Japanese spurge plant.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring bright white to cream-colored veins against a dark green background. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a smooth texture, adding visual interest to garden landscapes. This plant is evergreen, maintaining its ornamental appearance throughout the year.

It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used as a decorative shrub in woodland gardens.
