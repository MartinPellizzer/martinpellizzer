Eulophia plicata

Eulophia plicata, commonly known as the pleated orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pleated, grass-like leaves and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and marshes. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of flowers with pale yellow to white petals and sepals.

Eulophia plicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
