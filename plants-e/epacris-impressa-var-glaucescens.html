Epacris impressa var. glaucescens

Epacris impressa var. glaucescens, commonly known as the Blue Heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, blue or violet flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish or greyish hue, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
