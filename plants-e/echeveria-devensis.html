Echeveria devensis

Echeveria devensis, commonly known as the Mexican snowflake, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces striking violet or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in horticultural displays and as a decorative plant.
