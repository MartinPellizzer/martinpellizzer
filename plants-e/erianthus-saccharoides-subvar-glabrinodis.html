Erianthus saccharoides subvar. glabrinodis

Erianthus saccharoides subvar. glabrinodis, commonly known as the smooth-node sugarcane grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its potential in biofuel production and as a forage crop due to its high biomass yield.
