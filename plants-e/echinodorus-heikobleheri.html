Echinodorus heikobleheri

Echinodorus heikobleheri, commonly known as the Bornean swordplant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a metallic sheen. This plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense underwater mats.

Echinodorus heikobleheri is often cultivated in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
