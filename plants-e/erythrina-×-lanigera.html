Erythrina × lanigera

Erythrina × lanigera, commonly known as the Crimson Flame Tree, is a hybrid species within the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with compound leaves that are palmately arranged and have a feathery appearance. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue, while its wood is valued for its durability and workability.

Erythrina × lanigera is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
