Eucalyptus triplex

Eucalyptus triplex, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and its lance-shaped, often grey-green leaves. Eucalyptus triplex produces small white or pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which are typically followed by woody capsules containing small, brown seeds.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and plays an important role in the ecological stability of its native habitat.
