Eugenia lancetillae

Eugenia lancetillae, commonly known as the lanceleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
