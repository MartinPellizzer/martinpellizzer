Eugenia jamaicana

Eugenia jamaicana, commonly known as Jamaican guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically pink to red when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia jamaicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
