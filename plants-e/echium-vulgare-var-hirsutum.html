Echium vulgare var. hirsutum

Echium vulgare var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking spikes of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas.

Echium vulgare var. hirsutum is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
