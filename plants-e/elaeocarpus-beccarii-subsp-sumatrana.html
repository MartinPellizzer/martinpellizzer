Elaeocarpus beccarii subsp. sumatrana

Elaeocarpus beccarii subsp.

sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
