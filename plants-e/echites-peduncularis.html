Echites peduncularis

Echites peduncularis, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
