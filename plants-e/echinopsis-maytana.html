Echinopsis maytana

Echinopsis maytana, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its succulent stem. Its flowers are typically white, pink, or purple and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

Echinopsis maytana is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and striking appearance.
