Euonymus bulgaricus

Euonymus bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae and is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.

Euonymus bulgaricus is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
