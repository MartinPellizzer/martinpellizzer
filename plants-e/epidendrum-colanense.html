Epidendrum colanense

Epidendrum colanense, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces showy, dark violet to purple blooms that are often adorned with white or pink markings. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Epidendrum colanense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
