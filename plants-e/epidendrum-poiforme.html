Epidendrum poiforme

Epidendrum poiforme, commonly known as the Orchid of the Form, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features elongated, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent, showy sepals and petals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Epidendrum poiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
