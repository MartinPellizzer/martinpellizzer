Erica innocens

Erica innocens, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Erica innocens thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
