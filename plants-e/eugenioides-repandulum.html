Eugenioides repandulum

Eugenioides repandulum, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, warm climate for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
