Erythroxylum perpusillum

Erythroxylum perpusillum, commonly known as the dwarf copal tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
