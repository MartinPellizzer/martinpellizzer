Epidendrum propinquum

Epidendrum propinquum, commonly known as the close relative of the epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with pseudobulbs that support its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a two-ranked pattern along the stems.

Epidendrum propinquum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
