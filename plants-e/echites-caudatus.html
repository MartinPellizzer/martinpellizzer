Echites caudatus

Echites caudatus, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional remedies.
