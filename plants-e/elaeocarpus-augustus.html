Elaeocarpus augustus

Elaeocarpus augustus, commonly known as the golden apple wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow-orange fruits resembling small apples. Its fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, edible aril, making it an important food source for various wildlife species.

The wood of Elaeocarpus augustus is hard and durable, historically valued for construction and carving purposes.
