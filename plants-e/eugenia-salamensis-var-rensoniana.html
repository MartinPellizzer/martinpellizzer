Eugenia salamensis var. rensoniana

Eugenia salamensis var. rensoniana, commonly known as the Renson's guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the humid lowland forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically pink to red in color when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
