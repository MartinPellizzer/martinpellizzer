Erythranthus coriaceus

Erythranthus coriaceus, commonly known as the rough erythranthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red or pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erythranthus coriaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
