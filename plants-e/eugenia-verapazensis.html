Eugenia verapazensis

Eugenia verapazensis, commonly known as the Verapaz myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
