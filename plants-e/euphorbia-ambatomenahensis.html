Euphorbia ambatomenahensis

Euphorbia ambatomenahensis, commonly known as the Ambatomena spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in its arid native environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often sought after by plant enthusiasts and conservationists.
