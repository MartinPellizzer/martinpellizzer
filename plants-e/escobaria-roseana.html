Escobaria roseana

Escobaria roseana, commonly known as the roseate liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of fleshy, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small, green, and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Escobaria roseana is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
