Eragrostis rubens var. contracta

Eragrostis rubens var. contracta, commonly known as red false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often appear reddish in color. The leaves are narrow, slightly rolled, and have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
