Elaeocarpus bakaianus

Elaeocarpus bakaianus, commonly known as the Bakaianus elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
