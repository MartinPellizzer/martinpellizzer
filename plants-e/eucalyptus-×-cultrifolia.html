Eucalyptus × cultrifolia

Eucalyptus × cultrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a hybrid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in spikes. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
