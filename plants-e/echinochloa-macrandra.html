Echinochloa macrandra

Echinochloa macrandra, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is an annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in certain ecosystems.
