Elymus dentatus var. kashmiricus

Elymus dentatus var. kashmiricus, commonly known as Kashmir wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescence, which typically bear small, awned florets. The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
