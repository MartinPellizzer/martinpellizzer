Elaeocarpus timorensis

Elaeocarpus timorensis, commonly known as the Timor elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, warm climates with high rainfall.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
