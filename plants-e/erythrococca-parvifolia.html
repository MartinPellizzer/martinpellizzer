Erythrococca parvifolia

Erythrococca parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved erythrococca, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain small, black seeds.

Erythrococca parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
