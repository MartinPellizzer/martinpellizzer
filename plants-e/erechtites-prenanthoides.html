Erechtites prenanthoides

Erechtites prenanthoides, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a bluish-gray coloration. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small blue to purple flowers.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
