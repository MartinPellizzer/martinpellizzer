Eria inornata

Eria inornata, commonly known as the plain eria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and bloom during the rainy season.

Eria inornata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its graceful appearance and ease of care.
