Eustachys floridana

Eustachys floridana, commonly known as Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Eustachys floridana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
