Equisetum × robertsii

Equisetum × robertsii, commonly known as the hybrid horse tail, is a sterile, non-woody plant that belongs to the Equisetaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Equisetum arvense and Equisetum hyemale, characterized by its spore-bearing cones and whorled leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its stems are segmented and jointed, with a distinctive appearance that resembles the tail of a horse, hence its common name.

Equisetum × robertsii is primarily used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
