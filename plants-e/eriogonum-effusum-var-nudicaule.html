Eriogonum effusum var. nudicaule

Eriogonum effusum var. nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stemmed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and may have a slight pubescence.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
