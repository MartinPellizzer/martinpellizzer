Epiphyllum cartagense

Epiphyllum cartagense, commonly known as the Cartagena night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing cactus that produces large, showy flowers that open at night. Native to Central America, it thrives in humid environments and often grows on trees or rocks. The plant has flat, fleshy stems that store water, and its leaves are typically reduced to spines.

Its blooming period occurs in the evening, making it a popular choice for nocturnal gardens.
