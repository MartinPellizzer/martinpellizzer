Eulophia tayloriana

Eulophia tayloriana, commonly known as the Taylor's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water and nutrients.

Eulophia tayloriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
