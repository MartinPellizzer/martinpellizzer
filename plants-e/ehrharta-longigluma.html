Ehrharta longigluma

Ehrharta longigluma, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, two-lobed flowers.

Ehrharta longigluma is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
