Esenbeckia alata var. laevis

Esenbeckia alata var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth esenbeckia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
