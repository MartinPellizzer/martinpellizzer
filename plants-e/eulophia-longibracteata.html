Eulophia longibracteata

Eulophia longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, and the bracts are notably elongated.

Eulophia longibracteata thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
