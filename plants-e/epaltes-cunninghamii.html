Epaltes cunninghamii

Epaltes cunninghamii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flower clusters that appear in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Epaltes cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
