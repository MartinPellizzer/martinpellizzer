Echinocactus mammillarioides

Echinocactus mammillarioides, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
