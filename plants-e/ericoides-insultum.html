Ericoides insultum

Ericoides insultum, commonly known as the hairy heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Ericoides insultum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
