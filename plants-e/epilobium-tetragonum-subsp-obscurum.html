Epilobium tetragonum subsp. obscurum

Epilobium tetragonum subsp.

obscurum, commonly known as the dark willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has square stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
