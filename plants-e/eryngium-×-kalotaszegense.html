Eryngium × kalotaszegense

Eryngium × kalotaszegense, commonly known as the Hungarian thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and is native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It produces small, white ray florets surrounded by dense, bristly disc florets, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Eryngium × kalotaszegense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
