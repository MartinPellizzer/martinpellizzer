Elaeagnus nagasakiana

Elaeagnus nagasakiana, commonly known as the Nagasaki silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Elaeagnus nagasakiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in difficult environments.
