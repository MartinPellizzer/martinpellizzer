Ephedra americana f. rosea

Ephedra americana f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered ephedra, is a variant of the American ephedra.

It is a shrub-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features small, scale-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow-green flowers. Its stems are woody and often branched, with a distinctive reddish-brown color.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
