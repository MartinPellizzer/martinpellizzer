Eriophorum polystachion f. elegans

Eriophorum polystachion f. elegans, commonly known as the elegant tussock sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce small, three-lobed flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
