Ervum montevidense

Ervum montevidense, commonly known as the Uruguayan cowpea, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, curved pods and are often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
