Eranthemum nervosum

Eranthemum nervosum, commonly known as the veined eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that have prominent veins. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
