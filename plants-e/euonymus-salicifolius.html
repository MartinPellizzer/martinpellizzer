Euonymus salicifolius

Euonymus salicifolius, commonly known as the leafy spurge or burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green on top and grayish-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are clustered in pairs, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.

Euonymus salicifolius is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
