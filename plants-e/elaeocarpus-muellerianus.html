Elaeocarpus muellerianus

Elaeocarpus muellerianus, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Elaeocarpus muellerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
