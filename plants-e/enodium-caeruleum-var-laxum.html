Enodium caeruleum var. laxum

Enodium caeruleum var. laxum, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer and are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant features a robust root system and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens with careful attention to soil and climate conditions.
