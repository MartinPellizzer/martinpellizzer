Eufragia viscosa var. suavis

Eufragia viscosa var. suavis, commonly known as sweet sticky eufragia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its sticky secretions are a notable botanical feature, contributing to its unique ecological interactions.

The variety suavis is distinguished by its more intense floral scent compared to other forms of Eufragia viscosa.
