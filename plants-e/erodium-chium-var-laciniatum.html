Erodium chium var. laciniatum

Erodium chium var. laciniatum, commonly known as the cut-leaved horse bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The leaves are finely divided, giving the plant a feathery appearance.
