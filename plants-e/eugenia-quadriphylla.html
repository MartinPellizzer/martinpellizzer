Eugenia quadriphylla

Eugenia quadriphylla, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit yield, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
