Echinocactus obvallatus var. lancifer

Echinocactus obvallatus var. lancifer, commonly known as the Mexican lion's mouth cactus, is a succulent native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with prominent, rib-like structures that help it store water efficiently. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are typically reddish-brown and can be quite sharp, providing protection from herbivores.

This variety is well-suited for arid climates and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
