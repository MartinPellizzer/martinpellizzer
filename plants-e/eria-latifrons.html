Eria latifrons

Eria latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fronded eria, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
