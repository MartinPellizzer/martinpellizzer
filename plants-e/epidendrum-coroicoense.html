Epidendrum coroicoense

Epidendrum coroicoense, commonly known as the Coroico orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which can reach lengths of up to 30 cm. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Epidendrum coroicoense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
