Encyclia vitellina

Encyclia vitellina, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying environmental conditions.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
