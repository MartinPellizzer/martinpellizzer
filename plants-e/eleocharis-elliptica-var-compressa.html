Eleocharis elliptica var. compressa

Eleocharis elliptica var. compressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, compressed stems and small, clustered spikelets that are typically borne in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. This plant is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
