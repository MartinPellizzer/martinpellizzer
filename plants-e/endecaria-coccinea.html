Endecaria coccinea

Endecaria coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered false hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically bright red or orange, with five to seven lobes, and are borne in clusters at the ends of branches. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
