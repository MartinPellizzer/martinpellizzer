Echinopsis glaucina

Echinopsis glaucina, commonly known as the White-Flowered Cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces striking, large white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths.

It is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
