Euphorbia friedrichiae subsp. pofadderensis

Euphorbia friedrichiae subsp.

pofadderensis, commonly known as the Pofadder euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its characteristic flower-like structures. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh, dry conditions.
