Ermania incana

Ermania incana, commonly known as the hairy ermania, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Ermania incana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
