Eleocharis filiculmis

Eleocharis filiculmis, commonly known as the rice sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale brown flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic wildlife.
