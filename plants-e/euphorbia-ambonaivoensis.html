Euphorbia ambonaivoensis

Euphorbia ambonaivoensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in its arid native environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in a cluster known as a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It has a spiny appearance, with some species exhibiting a unique branching pattern.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
