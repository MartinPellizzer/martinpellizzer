Eriosphaera catipes

Eriosphaera catipes, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is appreciated by many succulent enthusiasts.
