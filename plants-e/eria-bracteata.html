Eria bracteata

Eria bracteata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Eria bracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
