Echeveria paniculata

Echeveria paniculata, commonly known as the tree echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a powdery coating known as farina. The plant produces striking inflorescences of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is widely appreciated by succulent enthusiasts.
