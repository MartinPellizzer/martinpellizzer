Erysimum krynitzkii

Erysimum krynitzkii, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in coastal or alpine environments. It produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers from spring to early summer, and its leaves are narrow and linear.

Erysimum krynitzkii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
