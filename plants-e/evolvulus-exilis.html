Evolvulus exilis

Evolvulus exilis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated as a decorative ground cover. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Evolvulus exilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
