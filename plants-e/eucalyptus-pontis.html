Eucalyptus pontis

Eucalyptus pontis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia, specifically found in the southern regions of Western Australia. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months.

Eucalyptus pontis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
