Eulophia leonensis

Eulophia leonensis, commonly known as the Leon's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically features long, strap-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats. Its inflorescence is a raceme that bears numerous small to medium-sized blooms.

Eulophia leonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
