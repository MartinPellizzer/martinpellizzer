Eugenia polyphylla var. obovata

Eugenia polyphylla var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed eugenia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its glossy, obovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
