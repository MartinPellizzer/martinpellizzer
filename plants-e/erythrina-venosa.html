Erythrina venosa

Erythrina venosa, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often woody and thorny. Erythrina venosa is native to parts of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
