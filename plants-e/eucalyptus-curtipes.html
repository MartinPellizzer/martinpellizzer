Eucalyptus curtipes

Eucalyptus curtipes, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Eucalyptus curtipes is adapted to withstand drought and fire, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
