Elvira atripliciformis

Elvira atripliciformis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Elvira atripliciformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
