Ericoides squarrosum

Ericoides squarrosum, commonly known as the squarrosum heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Ericoides squarrosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
