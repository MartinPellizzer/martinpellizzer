Enneapogon spathaceus

Enneapogon spathaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a dense, tufted growth habit.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
