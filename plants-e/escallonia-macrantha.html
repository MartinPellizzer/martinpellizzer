Escallonia macrantha

Escallonia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans.

Escallonia macrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
