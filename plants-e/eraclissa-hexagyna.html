Eraclissa hexagyna

Eraclissa hexagyna, commonly known as the six-sided leaf plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hexagonal leaf shape, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Eraclissa hexagyna is also noted for its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.
