Eupatorium perfoliatum var. truncatum

Eupatorium perfoliatum var. truncatum, commonly known as stiffstem boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive rough texture. It produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This plant is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, and it is known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
