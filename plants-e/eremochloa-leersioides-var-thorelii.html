Eremochloa leersioides var. thorelii

Eremochloa leersioides var. thorelii, commonly known as the Thorelian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
