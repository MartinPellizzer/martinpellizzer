Eupatorium scaberrimum

Eupatorium scaberrimum, commonly known as rough eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its leaves are rough to the touch, with a serrated margin and a distinctive grayish-green color.

It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
