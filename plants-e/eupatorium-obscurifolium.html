Eupatorium obscurifolium

Eupatorium obscurifolium, commonly known as blackleaf thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually white to pale purple and are arranged in dense clusters.

The leaves are dark green on top and silvery white underneath, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
