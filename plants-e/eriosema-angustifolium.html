Eriosema angustifolium

Eriosema angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
