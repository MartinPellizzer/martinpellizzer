Euphorbia dulcis subsp. incompta

Euphorbia dulcis subsp.

incompta, commonly known as the sweet euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features a distinctive, branched stem with a waxy, greenish-gray appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and green, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
