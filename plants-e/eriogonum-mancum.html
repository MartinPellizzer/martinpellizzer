Eriogonum mancum

Eriogonum mancum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Eriogonum mancum is a member of the Polygonaceae family and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
