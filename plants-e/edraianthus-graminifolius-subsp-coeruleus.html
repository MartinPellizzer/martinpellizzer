Edraianthus graminifolius subsp. coeruleus

Edraianthus graminifolius subsp.

coeruleus, commonly known as the blue-flowered edraianthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
