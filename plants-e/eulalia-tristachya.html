Eulalia tristachya

Eulalia tristachya, commonly known as the silky grass or silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky leaves and produces dense, fluffy seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Eulalia tristachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
