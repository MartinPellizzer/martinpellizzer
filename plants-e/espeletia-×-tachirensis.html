Espeletia × tachirensis

Espeletia × tachirensis, commonly known as the Tachirean heather, is a hybrid plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and dense clusters of small, often woolly leaves. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and is adapted to rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in alpine flora.
