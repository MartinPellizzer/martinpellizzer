Eurycaulis diffusus

Eurycaulis diffusus, commonly known as the spreading bladderwort, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its floating leaves and specialized bladder-like structures.

These bladders are used to trap small aquatic organisms, which the plant then digests for nutrients. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. The plant's roots are typically submerged, while its stems and leaves remain above the water surface.

Eurycaulis diffusus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
