Euphorbia ambroseae var. spinosa

Euphorbia ambroseae var. spinosa, commonly known as the spinous euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves, which help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
