Embelia nagushia

Embelia nagushia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
