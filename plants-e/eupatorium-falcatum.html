Eupatorium falcatum

Eupatorium falcatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Eupatorium falcatum is often found in wetlands and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
