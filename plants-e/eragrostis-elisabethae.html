Eragrostis elisabethae

Eragrostis elisabethae, commonly known as Elizabeth's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bend at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with delicate, awned florets that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
