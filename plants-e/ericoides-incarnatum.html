Ericoides incarnatum

Ericoides incarnatum, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, and it produces small, oval berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Ericoides incarnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
