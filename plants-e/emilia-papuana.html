Emilia papuana

Emilia papuana, commonly known as the papuan emilia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that are often surrounded by pale green bracts. Emilia papuana typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.
