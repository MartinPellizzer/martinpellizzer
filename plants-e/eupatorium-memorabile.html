Eupatorium memorabile

Eupatorium memorabile, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
