Elymus laevis

Elymus laevis, commonly known as the smooth wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, leaf-like blades and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is well adapted to a range of environmental conditions. Elymus laevis has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it an ecologically valuable species.

Its morphology and growth habits make it a common component of native plant communities in its range.
