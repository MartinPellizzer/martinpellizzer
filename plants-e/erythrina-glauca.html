Erythrina glauca

Erythrina glauca, commonly known as the blue erythrina, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is often smooth with a reddish-brown color. Erythrina glauca is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
