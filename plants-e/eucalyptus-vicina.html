Eucalyptus vicina

Eucalyptus vicina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from green to pink, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is typically found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Eucalyptus vicina is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment, showing resilience to drought and fire.
