Esenbeckia densiflora

Esenbeckia densiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fruit that resembles a star, hence its common name, with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Botanically, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes other plants with similar milky sap and unique floral structures.
