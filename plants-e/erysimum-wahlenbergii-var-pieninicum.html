Erysimum wahlenbergii var. pieninicum

Erysimum wahlenbergii var. pieninicum, commonly known as the Pienin eryngium, is a flowering plant native to the Pieniny Mountains in Poland.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive, spiky seed heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of basal leaves with a hairy texture, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountainous regions.
