Encyclia dichroma var. alba

Encyclia dichroma var. alba, commonly known as the white-tipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white and green flowers with a distinctive white tip on the lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often with a waxy coating to retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
