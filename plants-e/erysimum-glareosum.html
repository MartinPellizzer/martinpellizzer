Erysimum glareosum

Erysimum glareosum, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, semi-double flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal and upright stems with linear, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum glareosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
