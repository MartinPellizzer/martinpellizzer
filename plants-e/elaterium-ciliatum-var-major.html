Elaterium ciliatum var. major

Elaterium ciliatum var. major, commonly known as the large-fruited sandmat, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated capsule that contains numerous small seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.
