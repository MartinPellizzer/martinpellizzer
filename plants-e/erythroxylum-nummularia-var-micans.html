Erythroxylum nummularia var. micans

Erythroxylum nummularia var. micans, commonly known as the silverleaf copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are small, rounded, and often covered in fine, soft hairs.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing seeds.
