Eleocharis erythropoda

Eleocharis erythropoda, commonly known as red spike rush, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of reddish-brown spikelets. This species typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
