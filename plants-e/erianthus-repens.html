Erianthus repens

Erianthus repens, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a striking appearance. The plant thrives in moist environments, often found along riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Erianthus repens is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
