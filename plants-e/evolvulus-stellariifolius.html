Evolvulus stellariifolius

Evolvulus stellariifolius, commonly known as starleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive star-like pattern on the underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the morning, hence the name "morning glory.".

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
