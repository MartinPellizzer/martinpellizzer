Eria hypophaea

Eria hypophaea, commonly known as the slender spike orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Eria hypophaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
