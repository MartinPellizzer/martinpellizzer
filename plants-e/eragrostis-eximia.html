Eragrostis eximia

Eragrostis eximia, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in dry conditions and its adaptability to various soil types.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
