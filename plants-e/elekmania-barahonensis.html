Elekmania barahonensis

Elekmania barahonensis, commonly known as the Barahonensis Elekmania, is a unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem, though it remains relatively understudied.
