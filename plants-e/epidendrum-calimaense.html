Epidendrum calimaense

Epidendrum calimaense, commonly known as the Calima orchid, is a species of orchid native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in humid tropical forests. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red with yellow markings. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Epidendrum calimaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
