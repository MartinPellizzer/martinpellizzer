Elaeocarpus eumundi

Elaeocarpus eumundi, commonly known as the Eumundi satinash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the subtropical rainforests of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a soft, fleshy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of dense rainforest vegetation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
