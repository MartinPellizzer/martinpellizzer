Erythrospermum mauritanum var. verticillatum

Erythrospermum mauritanum var. verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered erythrospermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, spreading foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that are clustered in whorls, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a glossy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
