Erica benguelensis var. albescens

Erica benguelensis var. albescens, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.

The variety 'albescens' is distinguished by its pale white flowers, setting it apart from other varieties of Erica benguelensis.
