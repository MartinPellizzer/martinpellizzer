Eriospermum lanceifolium var. brevipedicellarum

Eriospermum lanceifolium var. brevipedicellarum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eriosperm, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

This variant is notable for its shorter pedicels compared to other varieties within the species.
