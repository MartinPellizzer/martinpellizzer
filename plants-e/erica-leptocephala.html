Erica leptocephala

Erica leptocephala, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many evergreen shrubs and small trees. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure.

Erica leptocephala plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
