Eucalyptus racemosa subsp. racemosa

Eucalyptus racemosa subsp.

racemosa, commonly known as the yellow box, is a medium to tall tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark that ranges from grey to yellowish in color.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive blue-green hue when mature. It produces creamy-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators.

This species is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
