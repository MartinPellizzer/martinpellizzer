Erophila quadruplex

Erophila quadruplex, commonly known as four-leaf sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically red-tinged and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Erophila quadruplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
