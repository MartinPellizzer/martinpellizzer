Epilobium franciscanum

Epilobium franciscanum, commonly known as the Franciscan willow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Epilobium franciscanum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
