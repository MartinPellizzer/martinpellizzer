Eucalyptus pauciflora subsp. pauciflora

Eucalyptus pauciflora subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the snow gum, is a hardy evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels in thin layers, revealing a pale greenish-white underlayer.

The tree produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside, adapted to reduce water loss in cold, dry conditions.

This subspecies is well-suited to alpine and subalpine environments, where it plays an important ecological role.
