Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp. obtusa

Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp.

obtusa, commonly known as the river red gum, is a tall, deciduous tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a reddish-brown color underneath.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a greyish-green appearance. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale yellow or white.

This subspecies is well adapted to riparian environments and is often found along riverbanks and floodplains.
