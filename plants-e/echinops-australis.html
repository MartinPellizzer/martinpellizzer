Echinops australis

Echinops australis, commonly known as the Australian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Echinops australis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
