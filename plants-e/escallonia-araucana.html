Escallonia araucana

Escallonia araucana, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its dark red to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Escallonia araucana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
