Eugenia cymosa var. concinna

Eugenia cymosa var. concinna, commonly known as the dwarf satinwood, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
