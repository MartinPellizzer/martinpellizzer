Erica laxa

Erica laxa, commonly known as the slender heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and attractive foliage.
