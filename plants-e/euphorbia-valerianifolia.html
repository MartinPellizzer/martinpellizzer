Euphorbia valerianifolia

Euphorbia valerianifolia, commonly known as the Valerian leaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental appeal and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
