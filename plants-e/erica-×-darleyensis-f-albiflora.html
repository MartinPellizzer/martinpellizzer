Erica × darleyensis f. albiflora

Erica × darleyensis f. albiflora, commonly known as the White-Flowered Heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to southern England.

It features dense, compact growth with narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
