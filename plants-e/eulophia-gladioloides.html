Eulophia gladioloides

Eulophia gladioloides, commonly known as the sword orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and striking, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with a distinctive lip, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Eulophia gladioloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
