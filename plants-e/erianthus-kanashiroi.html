Erianthus kanashiroi

Erianthus kanashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-white plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Erianthus kanashiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
