Eragrostis ciliaris var. clarkei

Eragrostis ciliaris var. clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
