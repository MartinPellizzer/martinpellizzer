Eriophorum Callitrix F. Moravium

Eriophorum callitrix f. moravium is a rare and intriguing plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is a variant of the common tussock sedge, known for its distinctive morphological features. This subspecies is primarily found in specific habitats across central Europe, particularly in the Moravian region. Its unique ecological niche makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Understanding its biology and distribution is essential for preserving biodiversity in its native environment.
