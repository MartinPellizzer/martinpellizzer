Euterpe brevivaginata

Euterpe brevivaginata, commonly known as the short-shoot banana, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, clustered inflorescence and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically consumed by wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
