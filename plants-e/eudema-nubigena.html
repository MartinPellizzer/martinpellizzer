Eudema nubigena

Eudema nubigena, commonly known as the cloud mist eudema, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves that are adapted to collect moisture from the humid air. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in the nutrient-poor soils of its habitat.

Due to its unique adaptations, Eudema nubigena is considered an important species for ecological studies in tropical ecosystems.
