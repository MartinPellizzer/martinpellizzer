Elymus corsicus

Elymus corsicus, commonly known as Corsican wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species.

Elymus corsicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native environment.
