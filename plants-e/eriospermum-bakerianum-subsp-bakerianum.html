Eriospermum bakerianum subsp. bakerianum

Eriospermum bakerianum subsp.

bakerianum, commonly known as the Baker's eriosperm, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
