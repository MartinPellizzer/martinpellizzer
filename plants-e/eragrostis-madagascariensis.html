Eragrostis madagascariensis

Eragrostis madagascariensis, commonly known as Madagascar lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and have a slightly hairy texture, typical of many grass species.

Eragrostis madagascariensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
