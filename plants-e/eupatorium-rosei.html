Eupatorium rosei

Eupatorium rosei, commonly known as Rosey thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale pink flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Eupatorium rosei is typically found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in moist wooded areas.
