Erysimum pseudorhaeticum subsp. pseudorhaeticum

Erysimum pseudorhaeticum subsp.

pseudorhaeticum, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its compact habit and bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically features basal rosettes of linear to oblanceolate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spring. It is native to the alpine regions of Europe and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh mountain environments.
