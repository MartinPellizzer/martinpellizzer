Euclea crispa

Euclea crispa, commonly known as the rough euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough, and often hairy leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. Euclea crispa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
