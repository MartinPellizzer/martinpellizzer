Eriobotrya seguinii

Eriobotrya seguinii, commonly known as the Seguin loquat, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by yellow-orange, fleshy berries that are edible.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
