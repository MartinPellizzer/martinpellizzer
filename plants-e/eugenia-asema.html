Eugenia asema

Eugenia asema, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, glossy leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
