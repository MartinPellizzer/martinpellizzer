Eryngium gramineum

Eryngium gramineum, commonly known as the common thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny leaves and blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Eryngium gramineum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
