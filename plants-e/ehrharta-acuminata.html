Ehrharta acuminata

Ehrharta acuminata, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, spike-like seed heads.

Ehrharta acuminata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
