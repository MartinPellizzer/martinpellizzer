Epiphyllum bridgesii

Epiphyllum bridgesii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that resemble leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night and emit a strong fragrance. These flowers are typically white or pale pink and can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

Epiphyllum bridgesii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
