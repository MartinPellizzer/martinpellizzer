Enteropogon minutus

Enteropogon minutus, commonly known as the dwarf false oat, is a small grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
