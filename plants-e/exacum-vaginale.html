Exacum vaginale

Exacum vaginale, commonly known as the velvet leaf or African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indirect light. It prefers well-draining soil and moderate moisture, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
