Elatine hungarica

Elatine hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian waterwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branched stems that are often submerged, with small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

Elatine hungarica is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
