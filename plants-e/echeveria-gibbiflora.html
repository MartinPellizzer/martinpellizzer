Echeveria gibbiflora

Echeveria gibbiflora, commonly known as the Pineapple Echeveria, is a species of flowering plant in the Crassulaceae family.

It is native to the Mexican state of Oaxaca and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces striking inflorescences with red bracts and yellow flowers, typically blooming in spring. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its thick, fleshy leaves, making it drought-tolerant.

Echeveria gibbiflora is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
