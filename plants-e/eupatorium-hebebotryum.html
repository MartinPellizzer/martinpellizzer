Eupatorium hebebotryum

Eupatorium hebebotryum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing pale blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators.

Eupatorium hebebotryum is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting native insect populations.
