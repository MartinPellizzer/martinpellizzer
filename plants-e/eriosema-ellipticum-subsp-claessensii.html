Eriosema ellipticum subsp. claessensii

Eriosema ellipticum subsp.

claessensii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
