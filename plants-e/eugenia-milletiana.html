Eugenia milletiana

Eugenia milletiana, commonly known as the African cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
