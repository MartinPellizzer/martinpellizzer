Erysimum cheiranthoides var. laxa

Erysimum cheiranthoides var. laxa, commonly known as the lax flowered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are pinnately lobed, with a slightly hairy surface, and the stems are often prostrate or creeping. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
