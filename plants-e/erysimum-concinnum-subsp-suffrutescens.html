Erysimum concinnum subsp. suffrutescens

Erysimum concinnum subsp.

suffrutescens, commonly known as the purple rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate and a flowering stem with showy, violet to purple flowers. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
