Eucalyptus × oxypoma

Eucalyptus × oxypoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Eucalyptus camaldulensis and Eucalyptus crebra.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, rounded buds. This plant typically grows in sandy or saline soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
