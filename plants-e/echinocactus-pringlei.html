Echinocactus pringlei

Echinocactus pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a spiny exterior. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
