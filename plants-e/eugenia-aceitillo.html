Eugenia aceitillo

Eugenia aceitillo, commonly known as the wild guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its foliage is dense, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Eugenia aceitillo thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
