Elaeagnus formosensis

Elaeagnus formosensis, commonly known as the Taiwan silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
