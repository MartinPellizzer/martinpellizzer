Epilobium billardiereanum subsp. intermedium

Epilobium billardiereanum subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Australia. It typically grows in moist, often shady environments such as forest floors and wetlands.

The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from late winter to early spring.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
