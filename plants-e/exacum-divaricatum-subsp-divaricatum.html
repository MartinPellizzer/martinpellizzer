Exacum divaricatum subsp. divaricatum

Exacum divaricatum subsp.

divaricatum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is a compact, evergreen perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white.

The plant has a short, upright stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers bloom throughout the year in warm climates, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
