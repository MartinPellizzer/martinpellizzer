Enydra

Enydra, commonly known as watergrass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant typically features slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Enydra species are known for their ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and are often used in wetland restoration projects.

Their rapid growth and dense coverage make them effective for stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in aquatic environments.
