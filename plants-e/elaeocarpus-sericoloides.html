Elaeocarpus sericoloides

Elaeocarpus sericoloides, commonly known as the small-leaved elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that enclose a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
