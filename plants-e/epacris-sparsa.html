Epacris sparsa

Epacris sparsa, commonly known as the sparsely flowering epacris, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. Epacris sparsa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
