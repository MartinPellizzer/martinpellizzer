Eriocaulon candango

Eriocaulon candango, commonly known as the Brazilian star sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by bracts.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
