Erica aghillana

Erica aghillana, commonly known as the Highland heath Erica, is a species of heath plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils, often found in heathland and moorland environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica aghillana is well adapted to harsh climatic conditions, including cold temperatures and strong winds.

Its dense growth habit makes it an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
