Elaeocarpus obtusus subsp. krukovii

Elaeocarpus obtusus subsp.

krukovii, commonly known as the Krukovii satinash, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
