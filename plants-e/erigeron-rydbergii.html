Erigeron rydbergii

Erigeron rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Rydberg's fleabane is often found in prairie ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
