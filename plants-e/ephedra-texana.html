Ephedra texana

Ephedra texana, commonly known as Texas ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow-green flowers and is adapted to arid environments. It contains alkaloids, including ephedrine, which has historically been used in traditional medicine.

Due to its chemical composition, it is also of interest in botanical and pharmacological research.
