Echium lasiophyllum

Echium lasiophyllum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple or blue-violet flower heads. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Echium lasiophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
