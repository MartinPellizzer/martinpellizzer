Eyrythalia angustifolia

Eyrythalia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved erythronium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and elongated, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with six petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
