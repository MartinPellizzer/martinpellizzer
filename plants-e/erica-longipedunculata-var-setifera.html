Erica longipedunculata var. setifera

Erica longipedunculata var. setifera, commonly known as the slender-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety setifera is distinguished by its elongated peduncles and fine, silky leaves.
