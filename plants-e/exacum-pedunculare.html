Exacum pedunculare

Exacum pedunculare, commonly known as the clustered violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

The flowers are typically borne on short peduncles, giving the plant its common name.
