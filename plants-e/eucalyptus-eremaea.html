Eucalyptus eremaea

Eucalyptus eremaea, commonly known as the sturt's eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Eucalyptus eremaea is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
