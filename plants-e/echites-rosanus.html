Echites rosanus

Echites rosanus, commonly known as the red-veined dayflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red with contrasting white veins. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the rainy season.
