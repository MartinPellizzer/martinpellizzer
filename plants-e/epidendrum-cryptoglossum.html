Epidendrum cryptoglossum

Epidendrum cryptoglossum, commonly known as the cryptoglossum orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually elliptical and slightly fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments.

The flowers bloom in spring and are known for their subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
