Encholirium inerme

Encholirium inerme, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its unique appearance and colorful blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Encholirium inerme is also noted for its ability to store water in its thick stems, allowing it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
