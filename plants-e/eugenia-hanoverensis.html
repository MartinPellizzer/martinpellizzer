Eugenia hanoverensis

Eugenia hanoverensis, commonly known as the Hanover guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in subtropical gardens and for local consumption.
