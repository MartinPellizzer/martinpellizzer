Erica actaea

Erica actaea, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Erica actaea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
