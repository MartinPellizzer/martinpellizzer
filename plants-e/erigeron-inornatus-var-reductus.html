Erigeron inornatus var. reductus

Erigeron inornatus var. reductus, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are typically white or pale blue.

It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.
