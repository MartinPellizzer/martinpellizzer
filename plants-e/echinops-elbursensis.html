Echinops elbursensis

Echinops elbursensis, commonly known as the Elburz thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer, followed by the development of the distinctive spiny fruits.

Echinops elbursensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
