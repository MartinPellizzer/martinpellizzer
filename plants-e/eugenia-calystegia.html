Eugenia calystegia

Eugenia calystegia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Eugenia calystegia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
