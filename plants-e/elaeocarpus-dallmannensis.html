Elaeocarpus dallmannensis

Elaeocarpus dallmannensis, commonly known as the Dallmann's elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by round, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
