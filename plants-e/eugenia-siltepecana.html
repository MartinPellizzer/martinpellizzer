Eugenia siltepecana

Eugenia siltepecana, commonly known as the Mexican huito, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid lowland forests.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in local cuisines.
