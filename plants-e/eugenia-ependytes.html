Eugenia ependytes

Eugenia ependytes, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible, purple or reddish berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The botanical features of Eugenia ependytes make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
