Epidendrum calyptrochilum

Epidendrum calyptrochilum, commonly known as the velvet lip orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Epidendrum calyptrochilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
