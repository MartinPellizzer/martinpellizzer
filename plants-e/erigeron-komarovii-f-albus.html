Erigeron komarovii f. albus

Erigeron komarovii f. albus, commonly known as the white fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy white ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract various pollinators. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its foliage is generally hairy and gray-green, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
