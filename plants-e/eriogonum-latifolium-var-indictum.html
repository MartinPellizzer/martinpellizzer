Eriogonum latifolium var. indictum

Eriogonum latifolium var. indictum, commonly known as the broadleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.

The species is often found in California and Oregon, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
