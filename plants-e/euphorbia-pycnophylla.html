Euphorbia pycnophylla

Euphorbia pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and its dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick leaves and stems.

Euphorbia pycnophylla is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
