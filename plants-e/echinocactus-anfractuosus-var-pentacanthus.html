Echinocactus anfractuosus var. pentacanthus

Echinocactus anfractuosus var. pentacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a slow-growing cactus with a distinctive star-shaped appearance due to its ribbed, spiny structure. The plant features a short, thick stem with prominent, spiny tubercles that give it a unique and rugged appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking form and ability to withstand harsh desert conditions.
