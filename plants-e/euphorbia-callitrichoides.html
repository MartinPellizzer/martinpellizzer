Euphorbia callitrichoides

Euphorbia callitrichoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Its foliage is usually narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance in its native habitat.
