Elatine williamsii

Elatine williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' waterwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. This species thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands, where it often forms dense mats on the water's surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish or reddish, and appear in clusters.

Elatine williamsii is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed aquatic habitats.
