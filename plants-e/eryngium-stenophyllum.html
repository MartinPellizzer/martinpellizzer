Eryngium stenophyllum

Eryngium stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Eryngium stenophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
