Espostoa lanata subsp. lanata

Espostoa lanata subsp.

lanata, commonly known as the woolly espostoa, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid habitats, often at high altitudes. Its subspecies lanata is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage and robust stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
