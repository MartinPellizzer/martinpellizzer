Erigeron aurantiacus

Erigeron aurantiacus, commonly known as the orange hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with bright orange to yellow petals and dark brown centers. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and arranged in a basal rosette.

Erigeron aurantiacus thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
