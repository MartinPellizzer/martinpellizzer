Eugenia breteleri

Eugenia breteleri, commonly known as the Congo guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
