Eriogonum effusum var. limbatum

Eriogonum effusum var. limbatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive leaf venation and often hairy surfaces.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
