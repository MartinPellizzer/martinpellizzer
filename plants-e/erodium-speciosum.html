Erodium speciosum

Erodium speciosum, commonly known as the showy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or reddish-purple flowers that bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and open woodlands.

Erodium speciosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
