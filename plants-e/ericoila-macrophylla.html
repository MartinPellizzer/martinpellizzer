Ericoila macrophylla

Ericoila macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Ericoila macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
