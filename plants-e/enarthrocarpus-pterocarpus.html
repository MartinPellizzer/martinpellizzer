Enarthrocarpus pterocarpus

Enarthrocarpus pterocarpus, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods. Its botanical features include a deep root system and the ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is also notable for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
