Erythrococca stolziana

Erythrococca stolziana, commonly known as the red cocoon plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, fleshy fruits that resemble small capsules, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with small, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
