Eupatorium smithii

Eupatorium smithii, commonly known as Smith's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
