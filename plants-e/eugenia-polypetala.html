Eugenia polypetala

Eugenia polypetala, commonly known as the fragrant eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Eugenia polypetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
