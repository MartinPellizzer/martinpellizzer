Eragrostis pooides var. barrielieri

Eragrostis pooides var. barrielieri, commonly known as the Barriel's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid and arid environments.

This grass is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
