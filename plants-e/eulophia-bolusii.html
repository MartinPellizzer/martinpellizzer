Eulophia bolusii

Eulophia bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing clusters of white to pale yellow flowers with a distinct lip. Eulophia bolusii prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, often growing in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
