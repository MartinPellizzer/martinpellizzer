Eucalyptus coronata

Eucalyptus coronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale or creamy white, which bloom during the cooler months. Its bark is often flaky and peels in strips, revealing a smooth, pale underlayer.

Eucalyptus coronata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
