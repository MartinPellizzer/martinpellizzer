Eucalyptus rhodantha

Eucalyptus rhodantha, commonly known as the red gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. Eucalyptus rhodantha is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both dry and moist conditions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
