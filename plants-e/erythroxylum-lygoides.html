Erythroxylum lygoides

Erythroxylum lygoides, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that contain the seeds. It is a member of the Erythroxylaceae family, which includes other species known for their latex and medicinal properties.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
