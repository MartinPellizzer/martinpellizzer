Euploca maxima

Euploca maxima, commonly known as the giant euploca, is a striking tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it adapted to arid environments.

Euploca maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in traditional practices.
