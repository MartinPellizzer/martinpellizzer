Eleusine coracana subsp. africana

Eleusine coracana subsp.

africana, commonly known as African finger millet, is a small-grained cereal grass native to Africa. It is characterized by its tufted inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its seeds are harvested for human consumption and animal feed.

It plays an important role in food security in many parts of sub-Saharan Africa.
