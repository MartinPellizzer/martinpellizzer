Eucalyptus macrorhyncha

Eucalyptus macrorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia, where it thrives in sandy or loamy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and small, woody flowers that typically bloom in winter. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant shrub that plays an important role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in rehabilitation projects and as a ornamental plant in dry climates.
