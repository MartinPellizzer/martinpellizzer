Erechtites ambiguus

Erechtites ambiguus, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, rich soils. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are hairy and somewhat succulent.

Erechtites ambiguus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
