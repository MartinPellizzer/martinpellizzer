Escallonia glabrata

Escallonia glabrata, commonly known as the smooth escallonia, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in mild climates.

Escallonia glabrata is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance garden plant.
