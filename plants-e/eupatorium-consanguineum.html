Eupatorium consanguineum

Eupatorium consanguineum, commonly known as bloodweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively.

Eupatorium consanguineum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
