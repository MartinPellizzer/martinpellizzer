Eleocharis arcuata

Eleocharis arcuata, commonly known as the curved spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes help stabilize the soil, contributing to the ecological health of wetland habitats.

Eleocharis arcuata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich environments.
