Eragrostis minor subsp. mimica

Eragrostis minor subsp.

mimica, commonly known as mimosa grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
