Esenbeckia febrifuga

Esenbeckia febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antipyretic effects.

Esenbeckia febrifuga is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
