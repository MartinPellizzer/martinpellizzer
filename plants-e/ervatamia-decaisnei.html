Ervatamia decaisnei

Ervatamia decaisnei, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of North America.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that disperse easily in the wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and drought-resistant foliage.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
