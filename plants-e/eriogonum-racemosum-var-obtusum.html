Eriogonum racemosum var. obtusum

Eriogonum racemosum var. obtusum, commonly known as the softleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive seed structures.

This variety is distinguished by its broader, more rounded leaf shape compared to other varieties of Eriogonum racemosum.
