Eremophila leonhardiana

Eremophila leonhardiana, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Emu Bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces vibrant purple or red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila leonhardiana is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
