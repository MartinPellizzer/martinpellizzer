Echinops cyanocephalus

Echinops cyanocephalus, commonly known as the blue globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, radial petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its seed pods are spiny and contain small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

Echinops cyanocephalus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
