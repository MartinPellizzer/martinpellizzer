Euthamia graminifolia var. galetorum

Euthamia graminifolia var. galetorum, commonly known as the coastal goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or along the edges of salt marshes. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize coastal soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
