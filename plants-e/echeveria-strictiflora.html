Echeveria strictiflora

Echeveria strictiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is popular as a decorative houseplant and for rock garden displays.
