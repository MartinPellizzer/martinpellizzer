Eriogonum ramosissimum

Eriogonum ramosissimum, commonly known as the Mojave prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, branched stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and rocky soils.

Eriogonum ramosissimum is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
