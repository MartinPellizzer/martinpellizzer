Erysimum lazistanicum

Erysimum lazistanicum, commonly known as the Lazistan mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cabbage and mustard. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching.

Erysimum lazistanicum is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
