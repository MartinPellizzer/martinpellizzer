Erica intonsa

Erica intonsa, commonly known as the Cape heath or sweet heather, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica intonsa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs.
