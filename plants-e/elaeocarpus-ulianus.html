Elaeocarpus ulianus

Elaeocarpus ulianus, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically elliptical in shape.

The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
