Eugenia pallida

Eugenia pallida, commonly known as the white eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, smooth-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Eugenia pallida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
