Eupatorium liatrideum var. venulosum

Eupatorium liatrideum var. venulosum, commonly known as the veined eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and its flowers are typically white to pale blue, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
