Elodes axillaris

Elodes axillaris, commonly known as the axillary elodea, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often grow in waterlogged soils or shallow freshwater environments. The plant produces small, green, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with small, greenish-white blooms that appear in the summer months.

Elodes axillaris is often used in pond ecosystems for its ability to provide habitat for aquatic organisms and help stabilize sediment.
