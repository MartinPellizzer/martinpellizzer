Enterosora parietina

Enterosora parietina, commonly known as the wall bladder, is a climbing plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and bean-like pods that contain seeds. The plant is typically found growing on walls and rocky surfaces, where it adheres using its tendrils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Enterosora parietina is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is considered a pioneer species in disturbed habitats.
