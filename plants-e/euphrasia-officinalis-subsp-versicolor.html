Euphrasia officinalis subsp. versicolor

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. This subspecies is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional herbal medicine.
