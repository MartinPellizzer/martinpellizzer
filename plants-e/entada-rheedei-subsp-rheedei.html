Entada rheedei subsp. rheedei

Entada rheedei subsp.

rheedei, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a source of tannins.
