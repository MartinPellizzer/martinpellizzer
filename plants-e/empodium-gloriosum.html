Empodium gloriosum

Empodium gloriosum, commonly known as the gloryflower, is a striking member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that resemble small fireworks, hence its common name. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Empodium gloriosum thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
