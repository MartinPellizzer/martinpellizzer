Eremophila congesta

Eremophila congesta, commonly known as the clustered emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremophila congesta is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
