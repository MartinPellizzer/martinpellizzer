Erica cothurnalis

Erica cothurnalis, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Erica cothurnalis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the rich biodiversity of the region.
