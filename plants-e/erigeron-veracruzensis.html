Erigeron veracruzensis

Erigeron veracruzensis, commonly known as the Veracruz fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in summer. It grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its foliage is usually hairy and aromatic, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
