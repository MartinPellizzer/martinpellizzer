Euonymus macrocarpus

Euonymus macrocarpus, commonly known as the Australian boxwood, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that consists of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and year-round foliage.

The leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems, contributing to its neat appearance.
