Eucalyptus vesiculosa

Eucalyptus vesiculosa, commonly known as the smooth-barked gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a greyish-blue hue.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually in yellow or greenish-yellow inflorescences, and are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.
