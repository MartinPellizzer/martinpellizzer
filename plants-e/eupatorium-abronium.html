Eupatorium abronium

Eupatorium abronium, commonly known as redroot eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along streambanks.

Eupatorium abronium is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
