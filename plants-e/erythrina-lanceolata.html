Erythrina lanceolata

Erythrina lanceolata, commonly known as the flame tree or the Australian wattle, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its striking red or orange flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of Australia and Southeast Asia. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds.

Erythrina lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
