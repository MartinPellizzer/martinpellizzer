Eugenia gayana

Eugenia gayana, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
