Erechtites gardnerianus

Erechtites gardnerianus, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are usually hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Erechtites gardnerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
