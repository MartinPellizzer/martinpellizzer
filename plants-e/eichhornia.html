Eichhornia

Eichhornia, commonly known as water hyacinth, is an aquatic plant native to South America.

It features floating leaves with long, thin stems and produces showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense populations in water bodies. Its roots are fibrous and can absorb nutrients from the water, sometimes improving water quality.

However, it can also become invasive, outcompeting native species and clogging waterways.
