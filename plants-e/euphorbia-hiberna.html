Euphorbia hiberna

Euphorbia hiberna, commonly known as the Irish buttercup, is a perennial plant native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick stems and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Despite its attractive appearance, it contains toxic sap that can cause skin irritation.
