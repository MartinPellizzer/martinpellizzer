Eupatorium hyssopifolium var. angustissimum

Eupatorium hyssopifolium var. angustissimum, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, sunny habitats and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
