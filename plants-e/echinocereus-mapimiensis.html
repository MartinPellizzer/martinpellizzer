Echinocereus mapimiensis

Echinocereus mapimiensis, commonly known as the Mapimi cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Echinocereus mapimiensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
