Erithalis fruticosa subsp. odorifera

Erithalis fruticosa subsp.

odorifera, commonly known as the fragrant erithalis, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas. It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its pleasant scent.
