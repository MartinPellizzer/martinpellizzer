Euphorbia erythrodon

Euphorbia erythrodon, commonly known as the red-veined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has a distinctive red or purple vein pattern on its leaves, which gives it its common name. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia erythrodon is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
