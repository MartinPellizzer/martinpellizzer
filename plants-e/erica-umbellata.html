Erica umbellata

Erica umbellata, commonly known as the heather bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica umbellata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
