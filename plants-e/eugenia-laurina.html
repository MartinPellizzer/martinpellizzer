Eugenia laurina

Eugenia laurina, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or laurina, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia laurina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
