Eucalyptus × blackburniana

Eucalyptus × blackburniana, commonly known as the Sydney peppermint, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often tinged with grey or blue on the underside. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and medicinal applications.

The tree is also valued for its fast growth and adaptability to various soil types.
