Erysimum hyacinthoides

Erysimum hyacinthoides, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to gardens.
