Eugenia brevipedunculata

Eugenia brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

The fruit is small, fleshy, and often contains a single seed, making it an important food source for various wildlife species.
