Encephalocarpus

Encephalocarpus, commonly known as the "brain nut," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of a single species, Encephalocarpus macrophyllus, which is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, woody, brain-like seed pods that encase the seeds. These pods are typically large, hard, and covered in a thick, leathery layer.

The botanical features of Encephalocarpus make it unique among legumes, with its specialized seed structure playing a crucial role in its ecological and historical significance.
