Eupatorium conyzoides

Eupatorium conyzoides, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is fibrous, and it often thrives in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and roadsides.

Eupatorium conyzoides is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
