Eremophila forrestii subsp. capensis

Eremophila forrestii subsp.

capensis, commonly known as the Cape emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive emu-like seed pods.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
