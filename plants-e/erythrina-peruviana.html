Erythrina peruviana

Erythrina peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian torch tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, racemose inflorescences. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and unique seed pods.

Erythrina peruviana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
