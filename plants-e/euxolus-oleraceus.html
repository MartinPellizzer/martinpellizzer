Euxolus oleraceus

Euxolus oleraceus, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, and it is known for its edible leaves, though they should be consumed in moderation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
