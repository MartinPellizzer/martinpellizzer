Escallonia revoluta var. berteroana

Escallonia revoluta var. berteroana, commonly known as the Bertero's escallonia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to southern South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in spring. Its ornamental value comes from its dense foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
