Eucalyptus forsythii

Eucalyptus forsythii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, greyish pubescence. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters and attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in open woodlands or on sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
