Eragrostis minor subsp. angusta

Eragrostis minor subsp.

angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in groups, with small, bristly florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
