Elytrigia repens subsp. elongatiformis

Elytrigia repens subsp.

elongatiformis, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and wetlands.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is characterized by its elongated, linear leaves and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
