Epischoenus adnatus

Epischoenus adnatus, commonly known as the slender water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, often with three petals.

Epischoenus adnatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and waterfowl.
