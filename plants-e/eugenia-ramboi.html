Eugenia ramboi

Eugenia ramboi, commonly known as the red-fruit eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round, and typically turn a deep red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
