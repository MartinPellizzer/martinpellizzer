Ericoides appressum

Ericoides appressum, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact, moss-like appearance. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, white waxy coating. It produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring.

Ericoides appressum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
