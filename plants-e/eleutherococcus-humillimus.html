Eleutherococcus humillimus

Eleutherococcus humillimus, commonly known as dwarf Asian ginseng, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, with the roots and stems used in traditional medicine.
