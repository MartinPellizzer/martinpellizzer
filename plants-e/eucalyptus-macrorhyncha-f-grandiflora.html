Eucalyptus macrorhyncha f. grandiflora

Eucalyptus macrorhyncha f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ghost gum, is a variant of the ghost gum species native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous blue-green in color. The plant produces large, showy white flowers in clusters, which bloom during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
