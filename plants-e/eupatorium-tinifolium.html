Eupatorium tinifolium

Eupatorium tinifolium, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Sweet everlasting is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
