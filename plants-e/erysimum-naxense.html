Erysimum naxense

Erysimum naxense, commonly known as the Canary Islands wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Erysimum naxense is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to the region's mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers.
