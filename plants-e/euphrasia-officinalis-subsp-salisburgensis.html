Euphrasia officinalis subsp. salisburgensis

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

salisburgensis, commonly known as the Salzburg eyebright, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers with prominent yellow markings.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential benefits to eye health.
