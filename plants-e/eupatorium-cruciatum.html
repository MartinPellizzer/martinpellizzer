Eupatorium cruciatum

Eupatorium cruciatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Eupatorium cruciatum is a popular choice for native gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
