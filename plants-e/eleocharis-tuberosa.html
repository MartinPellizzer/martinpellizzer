Eleocharis tuberosa

Eleocharis tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant is notable for its underground tubers, which are often used as a food source in some regions. Its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Eleocharis tuberosa plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
