Erica connivens

Erica connivens, commonly known as the common heath, is a shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually pink or purple and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Erica connivens is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
