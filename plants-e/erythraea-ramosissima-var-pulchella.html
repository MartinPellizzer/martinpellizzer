Erythraea ramosissima var. pulchella

Erythraea ramosissima var. pulchella, commonly known as the pink-flowered erythraea, is a shrub characterized by its dense, branched growth habit.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or reddish-pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
