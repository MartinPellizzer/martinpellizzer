Euphorbia exigua var. pycnophylla

Euphorbia exigua var. pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are densely packed, giving it a bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
