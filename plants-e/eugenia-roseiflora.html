Eugenia roseiflora

Eugenia roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, fragrant pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is small, edible, and often used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.
