Emilia longifolia

Emilia longifolia, commonly known as the blueweed or blueflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval, green fruits that contain numerous seeds. Emilia longifolia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is also known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
