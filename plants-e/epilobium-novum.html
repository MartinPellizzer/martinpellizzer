Epilobium novum

Epilobium novum, commonly known as the New Zealand willow, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. Epilobium novum thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora.
