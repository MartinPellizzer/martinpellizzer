Echinocactus setispinus var. robustus

Echinocactus setispinus var. robustus, commonly known as the robustus variation of the fish spines cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are densely covered with sharp, spiny bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
