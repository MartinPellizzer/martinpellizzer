Euonymus macropterus

Euonymus macropterus, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, inflated seed pods that persist through winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its striking foliage makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking seasonal color.
