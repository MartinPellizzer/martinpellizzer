Eugenia lasiothecia

Eugenia lasiothecia, commonly known as the woolly eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, smooth, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Eugenia lasiothecia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
