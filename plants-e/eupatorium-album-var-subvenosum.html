Eupatorium album var. subvenosum

Eupatorium album var. subvenosum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a distinct veining pattern. It prefers moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is known for its medicinal uses and is a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
