Euphrasia tatarica var. tenuis

Euphrasia tatarica var. tenuis, commonly known as slender tawny cowslip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with distinctive dark spots. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
