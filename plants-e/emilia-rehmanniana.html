Emilia rehmanniana

Emilia rehmanniana, commonly known as the blueweed or blueflower, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Emilia rehmanniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
