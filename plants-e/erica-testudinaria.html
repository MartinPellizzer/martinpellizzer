Erica testudinaria

Erica testudinaria, commonly known as the African heath or the bear's foot heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact, cushion-like formation. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and typically pink or red, appearing in clusters during the winter months.

Erica testudinaria is well adapted to harsh, arid conditions and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
