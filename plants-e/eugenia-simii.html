Eugenia simii

Eugenia simii, commonly known as the Java apple or wild guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. Eugenia simii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a strong aromatic scent.
