Ervum suberviforme

Ervum suberviforme, commonly known as the narrow-leafed vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain several small, rounded seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Ervum suberviforme is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
