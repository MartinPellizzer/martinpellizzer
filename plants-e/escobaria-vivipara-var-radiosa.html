Escobaria vivipara var. radiosa

Escobaria vivipara var. radiosa, commonly known as the radiate live-bearing sandwort, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces live young through vegetative reproduction, a unique adaptation that aids in its survival in arid environments. Its leaves are typically covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
