Eugenia urdanetensis

Eugenia urdanetensis, commonly known as the Urdanet holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
