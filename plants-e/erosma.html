Erosma

Erosma, commonly known as the "scented beauty," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plants typically have trifoliate leaves and produce small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Erosma species are native to various regions in Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

They are often grown in gardens for their ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with moderate sunlight.
