Erica velleriflora

Erica velleriflora, commonly known as the silky heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica velleriflora typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form compact, attractive shrubs.
