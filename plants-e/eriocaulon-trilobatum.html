Eriocaulon trilobatum

Eriocaulon trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its flowering structures are usually green or reddish, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Eriocaulon trilobatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to colonize disturbed wetland areas.
