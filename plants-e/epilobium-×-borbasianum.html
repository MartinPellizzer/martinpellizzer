Epilobium × borbasianum

Epilobium × borbasianum, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Epilobium × borbasianum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
