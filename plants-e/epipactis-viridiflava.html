Epipactis viridiflava

Epipactis viridiflava, commonly known as the green-flowered helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, upright flowering stem. Its flowers are greenish-yellow with a distinctive yellow lip, and they bloom from June to August.

Epipactis viridiflava is a perennial herb that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, showcasing its ecological complexity.
