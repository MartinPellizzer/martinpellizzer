Erythrina poianthes var. subinermis

Erythrina poianthes var. subinermis, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a striking tropical legume native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red floral clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has pinnate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces distinctive, woody pods that contain flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
