Eupatorium hieronymi

Eupatorium hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymus' boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium hieronymi is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
