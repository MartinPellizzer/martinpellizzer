Euphorbia glareosa var. pannonica

Euphorbia glareosa var. pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian spurge, is a succulent shrub native to Central Europe.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and unique floral structures.
