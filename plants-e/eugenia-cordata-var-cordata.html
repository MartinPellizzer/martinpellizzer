Eugenia cordata var. cordata

Eugenia cordata var. cordata, commonly known as the "evergreen waxleaf," is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that remain vibrant throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its dense foliage and long-lasting leaves, which provide year-round visual interest.
