Ehretia dicksonii var. japonica

Ehretia dicksonii var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a delicate, snow-like appearance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and attractive foliage.
