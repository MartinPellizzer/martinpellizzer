Erica × krameri

Erica × krameri, commonly known as the coastal heath or coastal Erica, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Erica × krameri is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
