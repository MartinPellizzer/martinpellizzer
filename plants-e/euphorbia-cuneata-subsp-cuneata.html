Euphorbia cuneata subsp. cuneata

Euphorbia cuneata subsp.

cuneata, commonly known as the prairie spurge, is a perennial plant native to the central United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers surrounded by red bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Euphorbia cuneata subsp.

cuneata is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought.
