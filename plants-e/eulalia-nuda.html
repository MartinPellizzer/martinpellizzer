Eulalia nuda

Eulalia nuda, commonly known as the naked eulalia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Eulalia nuda is often found in wetland or marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
