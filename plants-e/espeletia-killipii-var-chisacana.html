Espeletia killipii var. chisacana

Espeletia killipii var. chisacana, commonly known as the Chisacana heath, is a plant native to the Andean highlands of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense rosettes of silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically white or pale purple, adapting to the harsh, cold climate of its habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
