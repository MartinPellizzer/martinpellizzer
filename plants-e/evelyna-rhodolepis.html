Evelyna rhodolepis

Evelyna rhodolepis, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Evelyna rhodolepis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
