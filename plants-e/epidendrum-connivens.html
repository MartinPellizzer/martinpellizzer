Epidendrum connivens

Epidendrum connivens, commonly known as the orchid with the pink and white flowers, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are often pink or white with a yellow or green throat, and it blooms throughout the year in suitable conditions.

Epidendrum connivens is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
