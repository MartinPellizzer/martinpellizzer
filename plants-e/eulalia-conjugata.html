Eulalia conjugata

Eulalia conjugata, commonly known as the common marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Eulalia conjugata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
