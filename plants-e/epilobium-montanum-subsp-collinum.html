Epilobium montanum subsp. collinum

Epilobium montanum subsp.

collinum, commonly known as the alpine willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pink to reddish, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late summer.

It is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils in harsh environments.
