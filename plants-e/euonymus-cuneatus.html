Euonymus cuneatus

Euonymus cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by bright red, spherical fruits that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
