Erica cuscutiformis

Erica cuscutiformis, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance. Erica cuscutiformis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
