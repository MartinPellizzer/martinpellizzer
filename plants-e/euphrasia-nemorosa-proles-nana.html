Euphrasia nemorosa proles nana

Euphrasia nemorosa proles nana, commonly known as the dwarf wood betony, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of less than 10 centimeters, with slender stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular and have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure at the base.

It thrives in woodland or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.
