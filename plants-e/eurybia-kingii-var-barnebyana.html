Eurybia kingii var. barnebyana

Eurybia kingii var. barnebyana, commonly known as the Barneby's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct grayish-green color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
