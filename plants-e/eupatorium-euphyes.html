Eupatorium euphyes

Eupatorium euphyes, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

Eupatorium euphyes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
