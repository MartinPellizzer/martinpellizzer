Euphorbia falcata subsp. rubra

Euphorbia falcata subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It features thick, fleshy stems that are typically reddish in color, which helps it store water and reduce transpiration.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and may have a waxy coating to further reduce water loss.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and drought conditions.
