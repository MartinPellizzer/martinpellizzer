Erica wangfatiana

Erica wangfatiana, commonly known as the Wangfatian heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Erica wangfatiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long blooming period.
