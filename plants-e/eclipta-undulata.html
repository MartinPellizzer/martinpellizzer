Eclipta undulata

Eclipta undulata, commonly known as the zigzag ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive, zigzag-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often serrated. Its yellow flower heads appear in clusters, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and full sun conditions.

Eclipta undulata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
