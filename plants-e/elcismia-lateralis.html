Elcismia lateralis

Elcismia lateralis, commonly known as the silverleaf elcismia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Elcismia lateralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
