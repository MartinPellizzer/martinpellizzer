Echinopsis formosa subsp. randallii

Echinopsis formosa subsp.

randallii, commonly known as the Randall's moon cactus, is a succulent species native to the arid regions of South America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems.

The plant produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
