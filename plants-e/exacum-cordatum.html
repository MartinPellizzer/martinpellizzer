Exacum cordatum

Exacum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of about 15 to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Exacum cordatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
