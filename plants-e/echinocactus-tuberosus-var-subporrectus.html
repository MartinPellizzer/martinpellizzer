Echinocactus tuberosus var. subporrectus

Echinocactus tuberosus var. subporrectus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
