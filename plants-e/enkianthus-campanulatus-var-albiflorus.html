Enkianthus campanulatus var. albiflorus

Enkianthus campanulatus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
