Eulophia pottsii

Eulophia pottsii, commonly known as the Potts' eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces inflorescences with numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia pottsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
