Eugenia pseudoglauca

Eugenia pseudoglauca, commonly known as the glossy waxwood, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
