Eria curranii

Eria curranii, commonly known as the Curran's orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. Eria curranii is often found in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains a relatively rare and specialized species.
