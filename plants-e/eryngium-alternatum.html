Eryngium alternatum

Eryngium alternatum, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by silver-gray bracts. The plant has a rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found growing along sea shores and coastal dunes.

Eryngium alternatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
