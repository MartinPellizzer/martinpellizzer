Erysimum cuspidatum f. pumilum

Erysimum cuspidatum f. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a compact, mat-forming plant native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
