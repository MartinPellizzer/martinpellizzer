Eupatorium punctulatum

Eupatorium punctulatum, commonly known as spotted beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, open woods or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a nectar source for pollinators like butterflies and bees.
