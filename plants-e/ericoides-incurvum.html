Ericoides incurvum

Ericoides incurvum, commonly known as the curly ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats. It is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Ericoides incurvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
