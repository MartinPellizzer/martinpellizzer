Eriosyce napina subsp. lembckei

Eriosyce napina subsp.

lembckei, commonly known as the Lembcke's stone cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Argentina. It features a ribbed stem with prominent areoles, from which it produces white, woolly flowers.

The plant has a dense covering of pale hairs, which helps protect it from intense sunlight and dehydration. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
