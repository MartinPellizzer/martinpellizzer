Elaeocarpus lagunensis

Elaeocarpus lagunensis, commonly known as the Philippine elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.

This plant is often found in lowland to montane forests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
