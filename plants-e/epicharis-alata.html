Epicharis alata

Epicharis alata, commonly known as the winged begonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive wing-like structures on its stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Epicharis alata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
