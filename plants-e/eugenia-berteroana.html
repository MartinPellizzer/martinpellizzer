Eugenia berteroana

Eugenia berteroana, commonly known as the Andean guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Eugenia berteroana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
