Elytrigia sinuata

Elytrigia sinuata, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Elytrigia sinuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
