Eragrostis refracta

Eragrostis refracta, commonly known as the rigid false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple hues.

Eragrostis refracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
