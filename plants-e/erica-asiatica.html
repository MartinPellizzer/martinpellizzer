Erica asiatica

Erica asiatica, commonly known as the Asian heath, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes evergreen shrubs and is known for its acidophilic soil preferences. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica asiatica is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
