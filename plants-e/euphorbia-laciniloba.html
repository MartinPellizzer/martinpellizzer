Euphorbia laciniloba

Euphorbia laciniloba, commonly known as the lacey spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive, branching stem covered in small, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
