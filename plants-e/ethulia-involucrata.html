Ethulia involucrata

Ethulia involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Ethulia involucrata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
