Elaeagnus undulata

Elaeagnus undulata, commonly known as the silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Elaeagnus undulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It also has a history of use in traditional medicine.
