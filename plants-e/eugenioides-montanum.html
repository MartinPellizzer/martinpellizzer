Eugenioides montanum

Eugenioides montanum, commonly known as the mountain eugenia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small white or pink flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or forested areas.

Eugenioides montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
