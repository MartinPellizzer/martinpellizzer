Erica arborea

Erica arborea, commonly known as the tree heath, is a flowering plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round coverage.

Erica arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
