Elaeagnus umbellata var. longicarpa

Elaeagnus umbellata var. longicarpa, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, edible berries that turn red when mature. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fragrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.

The variety longicarpa is distinguished by its longer, more elongated fruits compared to the typical form.
