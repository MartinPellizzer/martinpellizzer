Euonymus japonicus var. subinteger

Euonymus japonicus var. subinteger, commonly known as the Japanese spindletree, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and entire leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that persist into the winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and horticulture.
