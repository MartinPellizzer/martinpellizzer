Episcia bryogeton

Episcia bryogeton, commonly known as the mossy heart plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of moss-like growth, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually come in shades of red, orange, or yellow, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
