Elsholtzia argyi var. nipponica

Elsholtzia argyi var. nipponica, commonly known as Chinese catnip or Japanese elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
