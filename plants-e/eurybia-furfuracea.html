Eurybia furfuracea

Eurybia furfuracea, commonly known as the wooly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, disk-like flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eurybia furfuracea is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
