Erica curviflora var. splendens

Erica curviflora var. splendens, commonly known as the Splendour Heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
