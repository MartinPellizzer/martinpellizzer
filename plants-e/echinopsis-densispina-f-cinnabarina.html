Echinopsis densispina f. cinnabarina

Echinopsis densispina f. cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-spined echinopsis, is a unique cactus species native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are often tinged with a reddish hue, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking white or yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
