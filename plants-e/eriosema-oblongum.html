Eriosema oblongum

Eriosema oblongum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, with a slightly hairy upper surface.

Eriosema oblongum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
