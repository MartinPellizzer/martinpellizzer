Eria reptans

Eria reptans, commonly known as the creeping fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, with long, horizontal stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, delicate fronds that are typically divided into linear segments. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Eria reptans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
