Encyclia guianensis

Encyclia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan Encyclia, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as tropical forests, where it attaches itself to trees or rocks. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with dark markings, and the leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted for water storage.

Encyclia guianensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
