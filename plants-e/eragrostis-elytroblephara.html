Eragrostis elytroblephara

Eragrostis elytroblephara, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming habit with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib and a rough texture.

Eragrostis elytroblephara is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
