Erigeron dusenii f. purpurascens

Erigeron dusenii f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive purplish tinge, while its flowers are small and white to pale blue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
