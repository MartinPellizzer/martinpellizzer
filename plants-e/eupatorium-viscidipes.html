Eupatorium viscidipes

Eupatorium viscidipes, commonly known as sticky eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, sticky stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its sticky secretions and aromatic foliage make it distinctive among native plants.
