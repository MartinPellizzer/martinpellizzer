Eriochloa fatmensis

Eriochloa fatmensis, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
