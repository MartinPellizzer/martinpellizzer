Ehretia duplicata

Ehretia duplicata, commonly known as the double-leaf ehretia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, orange-colored pulp.

Ehretia duplicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
