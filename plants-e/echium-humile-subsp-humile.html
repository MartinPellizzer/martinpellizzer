Echium humile subsp. humile

Echium humile subsp.

humile, commonly known as the lesser thimbleweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often covered with a dense layer of fine, white to greyish hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy habitats.
