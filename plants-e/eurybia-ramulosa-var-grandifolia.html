Eurybia ramulosa var. grandifolia

Eurybia ramulosa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, ray-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
