Encyclia × tudiana

Encyclia × tudiana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Encyclia cochleata and Encyclia tucumanensis.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its upright, branching structure. Its flowers are typically yellow to orange, with a distinctive greenish throat and showy, spurred petals.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
