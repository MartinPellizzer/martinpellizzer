Epilobium hirsutum var. foliis-variegatis

Epilobium hirsutum var. foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow or pink markings. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in terminal spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a distinctive and hardy plant.
