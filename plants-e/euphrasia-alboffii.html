Euphrasia alboffii

Euphrasia alboffii, commonly known as the white-eyed toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually yellow with white markings, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Euphrasia alboffii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
