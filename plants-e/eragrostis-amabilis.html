Eragrostis amabilis

Eragrostis amabilis, commonly known as the sweet grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

Eragrostis amabilis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
