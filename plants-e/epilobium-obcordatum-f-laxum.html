Epilobium obcordatum f. laxum

Epilobium obcordatum f. laxum, commonly known as the broad-leaved willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, often hairy leaves and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils along stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
