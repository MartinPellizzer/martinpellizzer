Erica velitaris var. parvibracteata

Erica velitaris var. parvibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bracted heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils in coastal or mountainous regions.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
