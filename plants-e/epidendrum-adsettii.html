Epidendrum adsettii

Epidendrum adsettii, commonly known as the Adsett's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, producing multiple shoots from its underground stem. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with yellow and pink markings, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Epidendrum adsettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and relatively low maintenance requirements.
