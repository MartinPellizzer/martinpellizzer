Eupatorium polycephalum var. typicum

Eupatorium polycephalum var. typicum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, violet-purple flower heads that resemble small cones, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a robust root system. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
