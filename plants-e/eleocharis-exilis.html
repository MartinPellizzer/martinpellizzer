Eleocharis exilis

Eleocharis exilis, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a species of grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it a common inhabitant of marshes, swales, and other moist habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Eleocharis exilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
