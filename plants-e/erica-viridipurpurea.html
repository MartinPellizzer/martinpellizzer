Erica viridipurpurea

Erica viridipurpurea, commonly known as the green-purple heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it grows in sandy soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from green to purple, depending on the variety. It is a low-growing shrub with dense, evergreen foliage that provides habitat for various insects and birds.

Erica viridipurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
