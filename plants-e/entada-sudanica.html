Entada sudanica

Entada sudanica, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The bark and seeds contain compounds that have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
