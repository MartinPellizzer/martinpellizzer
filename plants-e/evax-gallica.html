Evax gallica

Evax gallica, commonly known as the French lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit.

Evax gallica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
