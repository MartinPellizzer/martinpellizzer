Eremophila clarkei

Eremophila clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like structure with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Eremophila clarkei is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
