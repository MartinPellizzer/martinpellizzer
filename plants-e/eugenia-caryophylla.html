Eugenia caryophylla

Eugenia caryophylla, commonly known as the clove tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, red-brown flower buds that are harvested and dried to produce the spice clove. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically pink or white and are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits.

Eugenia caryophylla is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
