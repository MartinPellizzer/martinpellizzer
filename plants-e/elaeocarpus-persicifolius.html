Elaeocarpus persicifolius

Elaeocarpus persicifolius, commonly known as the persimmon leaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often mistaken for persimmon leaves due to their similar appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
