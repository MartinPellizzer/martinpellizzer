Eugenia simpsonii

Eugenia simpsonii, commonly known as the Simpson's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters and features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia simpsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
