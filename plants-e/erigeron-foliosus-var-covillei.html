Erigeron foliosus var. covillei

Erigeron foliosus var. covillei, commonly known as Coville's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and blue to purple in color, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Coville's blueweed is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub ecosystems.
