Epacris pinoidea

Epacris pinoidea, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Epacris pinoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
