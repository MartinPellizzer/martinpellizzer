Erythroxylum argentinum var. calophyllum

Erythroxylum argentinum var. calophyllum, commonly known as the Argentine erythroxylum, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a shrub or small tree belonging to the Erythroxylaceae family, characterized by its elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that contain seeds, and it is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry or semi-arid regions.
