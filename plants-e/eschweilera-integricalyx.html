Eschweilera integricalyx

Eschweilera integricalyx, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tall evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces woody pods containing multiple seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is a keystone species in the Amazon ecosystem.
