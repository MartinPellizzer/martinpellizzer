Eleocharis palustris subsp. lindbergii

Eleocharis palustris subsp.

lindbergii, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas, and is often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and usually shorter than the inflorescence.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
