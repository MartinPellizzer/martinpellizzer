Epacris impressa var. reineckei

Epacris impressa var. reineckei, commonly known as the Common Heath, is a species of flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in clusters along the stems. It has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
