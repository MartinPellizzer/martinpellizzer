Erysimum kurdicum

Erysimum kurdicum, commonly known as the Kurdish wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Erysimum kurdicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
