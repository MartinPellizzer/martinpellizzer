Eupatorium splendens

Eupatorium splendens, commonly known as the showy boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Eupatorium splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
