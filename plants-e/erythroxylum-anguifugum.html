Erythroxylum anguifugum

Erythroxylum anguifugum, commonly known as the creeping yellow balm, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Erythroxylum anguifugum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
