Eugenia porphyrocarpa

Eugenia porphyrocarpa, commonly known as the purple-fruited eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, purple-black berries. These berries are often used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.

Eugenia porphyrocarpa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, subtropical climate.
