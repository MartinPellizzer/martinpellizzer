Eugenia conceptionis

Eugenia conceptionis, commonly known as the Concepcion guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the humid forests of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Eugenia conceptionis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit and floral characteristics make it a notable species in tropical horticulture.
