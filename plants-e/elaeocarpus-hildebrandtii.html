Elaeocarpus hildebrandtii

Elaeocarpus hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of Hawaii and the Marquesas.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
