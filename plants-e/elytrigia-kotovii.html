Elytrigia kotovii

Elytrigia kotovii, commonly known as the Kotov's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Elytrigia kotovii is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
