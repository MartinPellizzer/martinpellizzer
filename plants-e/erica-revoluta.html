Erica revoluta

Erica revoluta, commonly known as the revolute heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Erica revoluta typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high sunlight exposure.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
