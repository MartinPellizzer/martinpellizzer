Eriachne pindanica

Eriachne pindanica, commonly known as the Pindan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. The plant has narrow, erect leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showing drought tolerance and resilience to salt spray.

Eriachne pindanica plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
