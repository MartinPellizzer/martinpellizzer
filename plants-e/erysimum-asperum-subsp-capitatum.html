Erysimum asperum subsp. capitatum

Erysimum asperum subsp.

capitatum, commonly known as the rough wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
