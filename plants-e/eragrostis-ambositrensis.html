Eragrostis ambositrensis

Eragrostis ambositrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
