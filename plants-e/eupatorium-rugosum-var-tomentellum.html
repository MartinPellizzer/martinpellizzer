Eupatorium rugosum var. tomentellum

Eupatorium rugosum var. tomentellum, commonly known as rough eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a rugged appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in fine, fuzzy hairs.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and providing habitat for various insects.
