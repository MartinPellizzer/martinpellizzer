Eupatorium farinosum

Eupatorium farinosum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its foliage and flowering period attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium farinosum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
