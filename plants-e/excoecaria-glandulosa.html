Excoecaria glandulosa

Excoecaria glandulosa, commonly known as the croton or Indian rubber plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
