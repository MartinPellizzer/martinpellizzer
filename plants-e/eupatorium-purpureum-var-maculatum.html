Eupatorium purpureum var. maculatum

Eupatorium purpureum var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted Joe-Pye weed, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, fluffy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to support pollinators like butterflies and bees.
