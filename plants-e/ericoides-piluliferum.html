Ericoides piluliferum

Ericoides piluliferum, commonly known as the pill-bearing heath, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its unique feature is the production of small, capsule-like structures that resemble pills, giving it its common name.

Ericoides piluliferum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
