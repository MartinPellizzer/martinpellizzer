Echinocactus alamosanus

Echinocactus alamosanus, commonly known as the Alamos prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its robust, ribbed stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has spiny areoles and produces edible fruits, which are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species within its native habitat.
