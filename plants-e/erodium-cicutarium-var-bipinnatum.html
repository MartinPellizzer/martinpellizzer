Erodium cicutarium var. bipinnatum

Erodium cicutarium var. bipinnatum, commonly known as the twin-leaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided into two pairs of pinnate lobes. The plant produces small, pink or reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This annual herb is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means.
