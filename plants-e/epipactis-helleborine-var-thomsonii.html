Epipactis helleborine var. thomsonii

Epipactis helleborine var. thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is a perennial herb that produces a single, erect stem with a single flower.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
