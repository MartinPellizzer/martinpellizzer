Elettaria floribunda

Elettaria floribunda, commonly known as the Indian cardamom, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by the aromatic, pod-like fruits containing the spice known as cardamom. Elettaria floribunda is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery, medicine, and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional and commercial contexts.
