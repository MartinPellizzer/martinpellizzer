Euphorbia gerardiana var. laconia

Euphorbia gerardiana var. laconia, commonly known as the Laconian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and may be covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
