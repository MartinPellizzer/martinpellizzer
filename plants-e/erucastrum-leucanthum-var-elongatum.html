Erucastrum leucanthum var. elongatum

Erucastrum leucanthum var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed white cress, is a variant of the white cress species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white, cruciform flowers. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found near streams, ditches, and wet meadows.

This plant is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
