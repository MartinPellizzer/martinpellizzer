Epibaterium carolinum

Epibaterium carolinum, commonly known as the Carolina epibaterium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has slender, ascending stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
