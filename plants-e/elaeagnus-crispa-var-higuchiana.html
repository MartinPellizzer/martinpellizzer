Elaeagnus crispa var. higuchiana

Elaeagnus crispa var. higuchiana, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Elaeagnus crispa var. higuchiana is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
