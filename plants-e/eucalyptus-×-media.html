Eucalyptus × media

Eucalyptus × media, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a hybrid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its multi-stemmed growth habit and rough, fibrous bark that peels in strips. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-gray hue, and they are typically aromatic. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, and it is known for its hardiness in arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus × media is widely used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
