Elaeocarpus kaniensis

Elaeocarpus kaniensis, commonly known as Kani hill ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by small, round fruits that are encased in a hard, woody capsule.
