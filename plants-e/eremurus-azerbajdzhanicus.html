Eremurus azerbajdzhanicus

Eremurus azerbajdzhanicus, commonly known as the Azeri spire flower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Eremurus, which is part of the family Asteraceae, commonly known as the sunflower family. The plant is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in regions with mild winters.
