Euphorbia tithymaloides subsp. padifolia

Euphorbia tithymaloides subsp.

padifolia, commonly known as the blue-green euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a branching stem system with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by colorful bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
