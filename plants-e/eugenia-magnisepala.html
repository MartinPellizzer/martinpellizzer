Eugenia magnisepala

Eugenia magnisepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, a small, fleshy berry, is edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
