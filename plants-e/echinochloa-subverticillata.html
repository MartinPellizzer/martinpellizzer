Echinochloa subverticillata

Echinochloa subverticillata, commonly known as slender barnyardgrass, is an annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is a common weed in agricultural fields.

Botanically, it is closely related to other species in the genus Echinochloa, which are known for their ecological and agricultural significance.
