Eugenia apodantha

Eugenia apodantha, commonly known as the slender eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia apodantha is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
