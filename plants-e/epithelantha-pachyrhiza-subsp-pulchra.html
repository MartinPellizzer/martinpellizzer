Epithelantha pachyrhiza subsp. pulchra

Epithelantha pachyrhiza subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the "beautiful root" or "Mexican torch cactus," is a succulent plant native to central Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are typically green or reddish in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
