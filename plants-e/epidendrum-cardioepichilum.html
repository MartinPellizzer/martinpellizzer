Epidendrum cardioepichilum

Epidendrum cardioepichilum, commonly known as the Crimson Spotted Epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a sympodial orchid that typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, often found in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or red flowers. Its inflorescence is usually terminal, with numerous small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
