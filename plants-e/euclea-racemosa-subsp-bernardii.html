Euclea racemosa subsp. bernardii

Euclea racemosa subsp.

bernardii, commonly known as the Natal flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, adding vibrant color to its habitat.

The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its striking floral display.
