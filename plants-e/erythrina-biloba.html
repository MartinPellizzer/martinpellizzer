Erythrina biloba

Erythrina biloba, commonly known as the red trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its bark is often marked with fissures. Erythrina biloba is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a spreading canopy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
