Erodium bicolor

Erodium bicolor, commonly known as the common stork's bill, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, spurred seed pods that resemble a stork's bill, giving it its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy inflorescences.
