Erythroxylum intermedium

Erythroxylum intermedium, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of habitats including disturbed soils and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.
