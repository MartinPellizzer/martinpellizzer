Elaeagnus multiflora var. hortensis

Elaeagnus multiflora var. hortensis, commonly known as the silverberry or sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces fragrant flowers in early spring, which are visited by pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
