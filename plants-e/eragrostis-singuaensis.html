Eragrostis singuaensis

Eragrostis singuaensis, commonly known as the Singuena grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid conditions, with deep roots that help it survive drought.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and potentially useful for erosion control.
