Erica caroliniana

Erica caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina heath, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in spring, and the plant is often used in native plant gardens.

Erica caroliniana is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
