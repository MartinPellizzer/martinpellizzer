Elymus kronokensis var. dasyphyllus

Elymus kronokensis var. dasyphyllus, commonly known as the Alaska wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are typically narrow and blue-green in color, with a distinctive rough texture. This variety is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
