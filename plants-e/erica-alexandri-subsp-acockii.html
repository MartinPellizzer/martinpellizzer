Erica alexandri subsp. acockii

Erica alexandri subsp.

acockii, commonly known as the Acock's heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green and evergreen throughout the year.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
