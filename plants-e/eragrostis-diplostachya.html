Eragrostis diplostachya

Eragrostis diplostachya, commonly known as narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for dryland environments.

Eragrostis diplostachya is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
