Erodiophyllum

Erodiophyllum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and is an important part of the local flora.

Erodiophyllum is also known for its ability to stabilize sand dunes and prevent erosion.
