Erythrodes sutricalcar

Erythrodes sutricalcar, commonly known as the red-stemmed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically features succulent, fleshy stems that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Erythrodes sutricalcar is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
