Eugenia neomyrtifolia

Eugenia neomyrtifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or myrtle leaf eugenia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia neomyrtifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
