Eugenia conglobata

Eugenia conglobata, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple, spiny fruit that contains segmented, white, edible arils. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, and it produces small, white flowers that attract pollinators.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, tart flavor and is often used in culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
