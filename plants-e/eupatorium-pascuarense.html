Eupatorium pascuarense

Eupatorium pascuarense, commonly known as the marsh thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small, white to pale blue, and are arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called umbels.

Eupatorium pascuarense is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
