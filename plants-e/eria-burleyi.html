Eria burleyi

Eria burleyi, commonly known as Burley's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Eria burleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
