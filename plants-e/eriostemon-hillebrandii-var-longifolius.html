Eriostemon hillebrandii var. longifolius

Eriostemon hillebrandii var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved eriostemon, is a species native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
