Erythroxylum coca var. spruceanum

Erythroxylum coca var. spruceanum, commonly known as the Andean coca, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing coca seeds. It is known for containing alkaloids, particularly cocaine, which has both traditional and medicinal uses.

This variety is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in traditional practices and for the production of coca tea.
