Ensete fecundum

Ensete fecundum, commonly known as the giant banana plant, is a striking member of the Musaceae family native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It can grow up to 6 meters tall with large, broad, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers followed by edible, banana-like fruits. Its rhizomes are also utilized in local medicine and as a source of fiber.

Ensete fecundum plays a significant ecological and cultural role in its native region.
