Eriophorum virginicum var. album

Eriophorum virginicum var. album, commonly known as the white tussock sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, white-tipped cylindrical inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and bogs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

The species is often found in nutrient-poor soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
