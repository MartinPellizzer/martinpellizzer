Erysimum incanum f. maroccanum

Erysimum incanum f. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan wallflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Morocco.

It features upright stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with four petals. The plant is characterized by its frost-resistant nature and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for Mediterranean climates and xeriscaping.
