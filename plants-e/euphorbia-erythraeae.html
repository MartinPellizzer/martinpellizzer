Euphorbia erythraeae

Euphorbia erythraeae, commonly known as the Red Sand Thorn, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It features a branching structure with spiny, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers, which are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, further minimizing water loss.

Euphorbia erythraeae is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
