Epacris hyacinthiflora

Epacris hyacinthiflora, commonly known as the hyacinth honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing showy, violet to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Epacris hyacinthiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
