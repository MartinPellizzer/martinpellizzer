Elodea ernstiae

Elodea ernstiae, commonly known as the Ernst's waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are often submerged, though they may emerge slightly above the water surface. Elodea ernstiae thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.
