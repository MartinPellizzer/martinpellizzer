Eupatorium iodotrichum f. rivetii

Eupatorium iodotrichum f. rivetii, commonly known as the Rivet's blueweed, is a rare plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands, showcasing its preference for humid environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
