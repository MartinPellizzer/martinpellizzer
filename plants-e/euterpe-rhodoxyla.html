Euterpe rhodoxyla

Euterpe rhodoxyla, commonly known as the red-wood palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
