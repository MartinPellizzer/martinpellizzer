Erepsia dregeana

Erepsia dregeana, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that often display a mix of purple, white, and green hues. The plant typically blooms in winter, producing intricate orchid-like flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are long and narrow, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Erepsia dregeana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
