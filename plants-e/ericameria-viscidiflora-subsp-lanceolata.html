Ericameria viscidiflora subsp. lanceolata

Ericameria viscidiflora subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf ericameria, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Ericameria viscidiflora subsp.

lanceolata is often found in chaparral and scrubland habitats.
