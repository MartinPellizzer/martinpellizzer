Eupatorium monardifolium

Eupatorium monardifolium, commonly known as purpletop verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its striking purple flowers, which appear in late summer, attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Eupatorium monardifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
