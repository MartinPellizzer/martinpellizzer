Ericameria parryi var. affinis

Ericameria parryi var. affinis, commonly known as the Parry's goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive, resinous scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Ericameria parryi var. affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
