Erythrina crista-galli var. inermis

Erythrina crista-galli var. inermis, commonly known as the scarlet coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking red, feathery inflorescences that resemble a crown, which bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five elliptical leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
