Erinus glabratus

Erinus glabratus, commonly known as the silverleaf erinus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for xeriscaping.

Erinus glabratus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
