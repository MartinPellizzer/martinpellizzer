Escobaria sneedii subsp. albicolumnaria

Escobaria sneedii subsp.

albicolumnaria, commonly known as the white-columned liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often tinged with white or pale blue.

The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
