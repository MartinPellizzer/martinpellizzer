Elaeocarpus barbulatus

Elaeocarpus barbulatus, commonly known as the hairy elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically covered in fine hairs.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
