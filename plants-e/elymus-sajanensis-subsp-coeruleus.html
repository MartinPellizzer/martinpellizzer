Elymus sajanensis subsp. coeruleus

Elymus sajanensis subsp.

coeruleus, commonly known as the blue wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, which are usually blue or purplish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and resilience.
