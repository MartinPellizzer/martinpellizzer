Eupatorium cannabis-folium

Eupatorium cannabis-folium, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and feathery foliage. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sunny environments. Its leaves are often mistaken for cannabis leaves due to their similar appearance, leading to its common name.

Eupatorium cannabis-folium is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
