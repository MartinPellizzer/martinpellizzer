Elaeocarpus forbesii subsp. ridsdalei

Elaeocarpus forbesii subsp.

ridsdalei, commonly known as the Ridsdale's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role within its native habitat.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in horticulture.
