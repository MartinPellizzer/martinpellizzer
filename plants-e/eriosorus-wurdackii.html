Eriosorus wurdackii

Eriosorus wurdackii, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a soft, moss-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its distinctive appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
