Ervum nemorale

Ervum nemorale, commonly known as the wood vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are often dispersed by animals.

Ervum nemorale plays an important role in ecological systems, supporting various insect species and contributing to soil fertility.
