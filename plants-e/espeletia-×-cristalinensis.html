Espeletia × cristalinensis

Espeletia × cristalinensis, commonly known as the cristalina, is a hybrid plant native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces showy, daisy-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
