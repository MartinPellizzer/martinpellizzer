Erigeron olympicus

Erigeron olympicus, commonly known as Olympic blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance.
