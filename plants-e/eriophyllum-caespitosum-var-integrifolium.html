Eriophyllum caespitosum var. integrifolium

Eriophyllum caespitosum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the Coast Goldfields, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has linear, often hairy leaves that are typically entire, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
