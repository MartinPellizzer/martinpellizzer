Eria fastigiatifolia

Eria fastigiatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features linear, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting mix. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper humidity and light conditions.

Eria fastigiatifolia is a member of the Orchidaceae family, which includes many other species of ornamental and commercially valuable plants.
