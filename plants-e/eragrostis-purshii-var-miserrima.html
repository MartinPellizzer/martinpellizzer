Eragrostis purshii var. miserrima

Eragrostis purshii var. miserrima, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, hairless leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is adapted to arid conditions with deep root systems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
