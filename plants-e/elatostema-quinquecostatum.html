Elatostema quinquecostatum

Elatostema quinquecostatum, commonly known as the five-costate elatostema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features small, ovate leaves with prominent venation patterns, often with five distinct costae, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces tiny, capsule-like fruits.

Elatostema quinquecostatum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
