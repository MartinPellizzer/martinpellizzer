Erigeron bonariensis

Erigeron bonariensis, commonly known as the New Zealand fleabane, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
