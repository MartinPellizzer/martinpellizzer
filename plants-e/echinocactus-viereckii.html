Echinocactus viereckii

Echinocactus viereckii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and desert gardens.
