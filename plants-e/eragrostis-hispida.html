Eragrostis hispida

Eragrostis hispida, commonly known as hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
