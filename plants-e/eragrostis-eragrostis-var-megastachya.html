Eragrostis eragrostis var. megastachya

Eragrostis eragrostis var. megastachya, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense, fluffy panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are distinctive and often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
