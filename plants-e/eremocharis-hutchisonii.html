Eremocharis hutchisonii

Eremocharis hutchisonii, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the high altitudes of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of white or pale pink blossoms that are highly ornamental. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or protected environments.
