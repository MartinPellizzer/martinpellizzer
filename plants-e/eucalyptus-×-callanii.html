Eucalyptus × callanii

Eucalyptus × callanii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically silver or grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale yellow, which appear during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping projects.
