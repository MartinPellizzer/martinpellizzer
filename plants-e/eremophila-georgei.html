Eremophila georgei

Eremophila georgei, commonly known as George's erymanth, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to low rainfall and well-drained soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Eremophila georgei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
