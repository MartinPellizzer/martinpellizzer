Eria trichotaenia

Eria trichotaenia, commonly known as the hairy spike orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name.

Eria trichotaenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
