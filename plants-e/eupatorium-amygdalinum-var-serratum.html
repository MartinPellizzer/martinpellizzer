Eupatorium amygdalinum var. serratum

Eupatorium amygdalinum var. serratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
