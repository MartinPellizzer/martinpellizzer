Euphorbia loiseleurii

Euphorbia loiseleurii, commonly known as the Blue Glow euphorbia, is a striking succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, waxy leaves that range from pale blue to deep green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
