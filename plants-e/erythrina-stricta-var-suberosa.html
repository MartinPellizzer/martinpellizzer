Erythrina stricta var. suberosa

Erythrina stricta var. suberosa, commonly known as the red torchwood or red flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright red flowers that bloom in dense, clustered inflorescences, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Additionally, it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules, contributing to soil fertility.
