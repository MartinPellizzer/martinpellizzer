Erica viminalis

Erica viminalis, commonly known as the heath or cross-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths, heathers, and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white wax. Its flowers are typically pale pink or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Erica viminalis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.
