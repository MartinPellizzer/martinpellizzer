Euphorbia caput-medusae

Euphorbia caput-medusae, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a caudex, or thickened stem, that resembles a crown, with numerous spiny branches radiating outward. The plant produces small, yellowish-green花朵 that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling to avoid skin irritation.
