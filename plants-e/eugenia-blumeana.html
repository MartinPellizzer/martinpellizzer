Eugenia blumeana

Eugenia blumeana, commonly known as the Blume's guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically followed by edible, pinkish-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves and fruits have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
