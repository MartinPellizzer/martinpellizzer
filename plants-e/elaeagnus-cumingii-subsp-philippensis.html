Elaeagnus cumingii subsp. philippensis

Elaeagnus cumingii subsp.

philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine silverberry, is a shrub native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
