Eugenia succulenta

Eugenia succulenta, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Eugenia succulenta is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
