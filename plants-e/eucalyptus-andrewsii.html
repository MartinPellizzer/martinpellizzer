Eucalyptus andrewsii

Eucalyptus andrewsii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or cream in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus andrewsii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
