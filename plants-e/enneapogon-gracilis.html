Enneapogon gracilis

Enneapogon gracilis, commonly known as slender false lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Enneapogon gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
