Eryngium campestre var. axicum

Eryngium campestre var. axicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture and a distinct central stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
