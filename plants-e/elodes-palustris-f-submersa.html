Elodes palustris f. submersa

Elodes palustris f. submersa, commonly known as the submerged waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are typically green to reddish in color, and it lacks true roots, instead relying on its stem for anchoring. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, though they may emerge during certain times of the year. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.

Elodes palustris f. submersa is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
