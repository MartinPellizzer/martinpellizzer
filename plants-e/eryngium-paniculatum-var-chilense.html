Eryngium paniculatum var. chilense

Eryngium paniculatum var. chilense, commonly known as the Chilean thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows in coastal and arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
