Eriogonum flavum subsp. piperi

Eriogonum flavum subsp.

piperi, commonly known as the yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers.

The plant typically produces bright yellow blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant found in various desert and foothill regions.
