Elaeagnus angustifolia var. normalis

Elaeagnus angustifolia var. normalis, commonly known as silver leaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by reddish-brown, elongated fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
