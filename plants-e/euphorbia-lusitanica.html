Euphorbia lusitanica

Euphorbia lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in late spring, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Euphorbia lusitanica is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and diverse growth forms.
