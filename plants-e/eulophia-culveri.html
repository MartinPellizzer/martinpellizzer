Eulophia culveri

Eulophia culveri, commonly known as Culver's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically has a single, erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in racemes and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

Eulophia culveri is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swamps.
