Eriogonum saxatile var. stokesiae

Eriogonum saxatile var. stokesiae, commonly known as Stokes' sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

It is a member of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
