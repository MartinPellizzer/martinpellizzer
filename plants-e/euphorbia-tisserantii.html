Euphorbia tisserantii

Euphorbia tisserantii, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a distinctive appearance with its branching stems that resemble coral, covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
