Eucalyptus pulverulenta var. lanceolata

Eucalyptus pulverulenta var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub form and its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically grey-green in color and have a rough, fibrous texture. This plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
