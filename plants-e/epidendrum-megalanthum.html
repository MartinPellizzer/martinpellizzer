Epidendrum megalanthum

Epidendrum megalanthum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it a standout in any collection. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to retain moisture.

Epidendrum megalanthum is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper light and humidity conditions.
