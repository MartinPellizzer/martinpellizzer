Euphorbia ritchiei subsp. ritchiei

Euphorbia ritchiei subsp.

ritchiei, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a succulent shrub native to Mexico. It features thick, ribbed stems and leaves that are often blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces striking yellow flowers surrounded by red bracts, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
