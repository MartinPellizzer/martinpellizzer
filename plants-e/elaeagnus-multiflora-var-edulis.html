Elaeagnus multiflora var. edulis

Elaeagnus multiflora var. edulis, commonly known as the edible silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-green leaves and small, edible fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used for erosion control. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries in the fall.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional medicine.
