Echinocereus viridiflorus var. davisii

Echinocereus viridiflorus var. davisii, commonly known as the Davis's green-flowered cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

This variety is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.
