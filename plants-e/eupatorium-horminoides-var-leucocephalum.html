Eupatorium horminoides var. leucocephalum

Eupatorium horminoides var. leucocephalum, commonly known as white headplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale purple flower heads that resemble small umbrellas. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, preferring moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive white or grayish underside.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
