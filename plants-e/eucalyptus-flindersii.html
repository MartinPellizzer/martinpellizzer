Eucalyptus flindersii

Eucalyptus flindersii, commonly known as Flinders' malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers, typically in clusters, during the cooler months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Eucalyptus flindersii is often found in mallee woodlands and is an important component of the local flora.
