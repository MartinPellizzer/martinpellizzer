Elytropus heterophyllus

Elytropus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable elytropus, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which can range from broad to narrow and often exhibits different shapes depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, which are followed by small, round fruits.

Due to its adaptability and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
