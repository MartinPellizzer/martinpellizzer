Euonymus alatus f. angustatus

Euonymus alatus f. angustatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf burning bush, is a cultivar of the burning bush plant.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tinge during the growing season. In autumn, the foliage turns vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal color changes.
