Eucalyptus gracilis var. viminea

Eucalyptus gracilis var. viminea, commonly known as the slender ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, often pendulous leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white or cream-colored flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
