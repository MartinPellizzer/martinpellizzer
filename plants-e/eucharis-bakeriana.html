Eucharis bakeriana

Eucharis bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Eucharis bakeriana is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
