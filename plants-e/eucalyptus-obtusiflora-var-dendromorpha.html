Eucalyptus obtusiflora var. dendromorpha

Eucalyptus obtusiflora var. dendromorpha, commonly known as the rough-barked apple, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, globular flower buds that are usually pale green or yellowish in color.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
