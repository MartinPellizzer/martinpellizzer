Erophila rurivaga

Erophila rurivaga, commonly known as the field eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Erophila rurivaga is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
