Eria elmeri

Eria elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. Eria elmeri typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitats.
