Erica senilis

Erica senilis, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Erica senilis thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
