Enkianthus pendulus

Enkianthus pendulus, commonly known as the weeping enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique floral display and graceful appearance make it a distinctive addition to woodland settings.
