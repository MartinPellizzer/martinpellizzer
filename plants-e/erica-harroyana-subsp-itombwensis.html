Erica harroyana subsp. itombwensis

Erica harroyana subsp.

itombwensis, commonly known as the Itombwensis heath, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acidophilous plants adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. Its flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in spring, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
