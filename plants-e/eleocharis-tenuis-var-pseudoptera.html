Eleocharis tenuis var. pseudoptera

Eleocharis tenuis var. pseudoptera, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This variety is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
