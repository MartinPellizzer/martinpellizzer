Echinopsis quehlii

Echinopsis quehlii, commonly known as the Queen of the Night cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, a trait that gives it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
