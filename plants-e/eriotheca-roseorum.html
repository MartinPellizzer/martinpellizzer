Eriotheca roseorum

Eriotheca roseorum, commonly known as the pink hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Eriotheca roseorum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
