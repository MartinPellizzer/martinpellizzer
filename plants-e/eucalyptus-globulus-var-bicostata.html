Eucalyptus globulus var. bicostata

Eucalyptus globulus var. bicostata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the common gum tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, spherical flower buds that turn from green to red as they mature. It is typically found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some uses in horticulture and traditional medicine.
