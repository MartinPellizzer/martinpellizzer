Echinopsis knuthiana

Echinopsis knuthiana, commonly known as the Knuth's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stems and distinctive white spines that give it a moon-like appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a popular choice for succulent gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.

Echinopsis knuthiana requires well-draining soil and minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
