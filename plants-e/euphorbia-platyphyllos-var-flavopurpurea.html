Euphorbia platyphyllos var. flavopurpurea

Euphorbia platyphyllos var. flavopurpurea, commonly known as the Purple-veined Spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with prominent purple veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking foliage, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
