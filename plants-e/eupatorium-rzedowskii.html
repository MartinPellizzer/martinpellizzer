Eupatorium rzedowskii

Eupatorium rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing clusters of small white to pale blue flowers.

Eupatorium rzedowskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
