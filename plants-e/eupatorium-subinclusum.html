Eupatorium subinclusum

Eupatorium subinclusum, commonly known as narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Eupatorium subinclusum is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its medicinal properties remain under study.
