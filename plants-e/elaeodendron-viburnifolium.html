Elaeodendron viburnifolium

Elaeodendron viburnifolium, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and are typically pink or white, blooming in clusters.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
