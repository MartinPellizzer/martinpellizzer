Eriophyllum wallacei var. rubellum

Eriophyllum wallacei var. rubellum, commonly known as the Wallace's golden bush, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is notable for its vibrant color and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
