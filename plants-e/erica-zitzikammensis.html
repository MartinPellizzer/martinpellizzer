Erica zitzikammensis

Erica zitzikammensis, commonly known as the Zitzmann's heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heather. This plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica zitzikammensis is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
