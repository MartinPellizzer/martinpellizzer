Elymus sinosubmuticus

Elymus sinosubmuticus, commonly known as the Chinese false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
