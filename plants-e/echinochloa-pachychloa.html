Echinochloa pachychloa

Echinochloa pachychloa, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or disturbed soils and is often found in rice paddies. It has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

E. pachychloa is an important weed in agricultural systems and has been studied for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
