Eugenia eggersii

Eugenia eggersii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant bears edible red berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in culinary applications. Eugenia eggersii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
