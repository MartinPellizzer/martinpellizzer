Eria glabrifolia

Eria glabrifolia, commonly known as the hairy leaf eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
