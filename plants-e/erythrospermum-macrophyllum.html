Erythrospermum macrophyllum

Erythrospermum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf erythrospermum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, exhibiting a woody structure with dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy texture, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Erythrospermum macrophyllum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
