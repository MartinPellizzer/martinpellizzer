Eclipta thermalis

Eclipta thermalis, commonly known as the tropical ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eclipta thermalis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
