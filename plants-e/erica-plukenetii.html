Erica plukenetii

Erica plukenetii, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a hardy evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically evergreen, and it produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Erica plukenetii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
