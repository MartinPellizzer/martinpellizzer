Ephedra botryoides

Ephedra botryoides, commonly known as the clustered ephedra, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert habitats.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has also been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
