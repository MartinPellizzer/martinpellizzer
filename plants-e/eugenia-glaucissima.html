Eugenia glaucissima

Eugenia glaucissima, commonly known as the waxleaf eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
