Eriocoma elegans

Eriocoma elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf eriocoma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Eriocoma elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
