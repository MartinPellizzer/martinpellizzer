Elaeocarpus bilobatus

Elaeocarpus bilobatus, commonly known as the two-lobed elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that are typically covered in a fleshy, yellowish pulp. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
