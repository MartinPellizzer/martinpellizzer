Elaeagnus lanceolata var. grandifolia

Elaeagnus lanceolata var. grandifolia, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its autumn foliage, which turns a striking red or orange.
