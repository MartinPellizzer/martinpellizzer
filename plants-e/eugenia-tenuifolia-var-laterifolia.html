Eugenia tenuifolia var. laterifolia

Eugenia tenuifolia var. laterifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
