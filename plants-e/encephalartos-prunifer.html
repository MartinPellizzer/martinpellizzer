Encephalartos prunifer

Encephalartos prunifer, commonly known as the silver-leaf cycad, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves that are typically a striking silver-green color. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It is a slow-growing, long-lived plant that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
