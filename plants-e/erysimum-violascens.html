Erysimum violascens

Erysimum violascens, commonly known as the violet broom or violaceous broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is typically herbaceous, growing to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.
