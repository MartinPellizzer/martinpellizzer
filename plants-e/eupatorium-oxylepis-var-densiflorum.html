Eupatorium oxylepis var. densiflorum

Eupatorium oxylepis var. densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks. Its showy, clustered inflorescences attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
