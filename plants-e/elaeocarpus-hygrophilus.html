Elaeocarpus hygrophilus

Elaeocarpus hygrophilus, commonly known as the water elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
