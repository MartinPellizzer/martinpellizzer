Eriogonum trichopes subsp. glaucum

Eriogonum trichopes subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as the grayleaf buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
