Erodium involucratum

Erodium involucratum, commonly known as the clustered bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, dry fruits called legumes. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an attractive species for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
