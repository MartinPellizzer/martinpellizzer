Elaeocarpus mamasii

Elaeocarpus mamasii, commonly known as the Mamasii elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that are typically covered in a thick, woody husk. It is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems, where it plays a role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
