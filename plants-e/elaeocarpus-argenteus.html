Elaeocarpus argenteus

Elaeocarpus argenteus, commonly known as silver ash or silver tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Elaeocarpus argenteus is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in some regions.
