Eugenia teffensis

Eugenia teffensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red or black berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia teffensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
