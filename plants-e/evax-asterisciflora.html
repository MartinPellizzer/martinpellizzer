Evax asterisciflora

Evax asterisciflora, commonly known as the star-flowered evax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Evax asterisciflora is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often thriving in sandy or rocky soils.
