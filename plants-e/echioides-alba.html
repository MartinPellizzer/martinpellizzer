Echioides alba

Echioides alba, commonly known as the white echium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright stem and striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Echioides alba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
