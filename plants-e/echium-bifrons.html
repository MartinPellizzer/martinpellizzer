Echium bifrons

Echium bifrons, commonly known as the double-headed eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, often forming dense clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Echium bifrons is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
