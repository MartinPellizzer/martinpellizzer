Epiphyllum phyllanthus subsp. hookeri

Epiphyllum phyllanthus subsp.

hookeri, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a striking epiphytic cactus native to Brazil. It features flattened, segmented stems that are often brightly colored, with flowers that bloom in winter.

The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its long-lasting blooms and unique appearance.

Its ability to store water in its stems makes it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
