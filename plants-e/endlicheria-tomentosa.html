Endlicheria tomentosa

Endlicheria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly endlicheria, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage that covers its stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
