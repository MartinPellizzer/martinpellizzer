Epilobium bongardii

Epilobium bongardii, commonly known as Bongard's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Epilobium bongardii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a valuable plant for wildlife habitat.
