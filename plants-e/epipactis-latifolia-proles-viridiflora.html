Epipactis latifolia proles viridiflora

Epipactis latifolia proles viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered broad-leaf helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and its unique greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers with a distinctive lip.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
