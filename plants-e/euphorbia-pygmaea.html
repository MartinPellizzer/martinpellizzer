Euphorbia pygmaea

Euphorbia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.

However, it contains toxic sap, so caution is advised when handling it.
