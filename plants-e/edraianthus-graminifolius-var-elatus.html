Edraianthus graminifolius var. elatus

Edraianthus graminifolius var. elatus, commonly known as the tall grassleaf edraianthus, is a rare and striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
