Eryngium puberulentum

Eryngium puberulentum, commonly known as the rough eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads. Eryngium puberulentum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
