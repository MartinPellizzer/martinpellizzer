Eugenia anastomosans

Eugenia anastomosans, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia anastomosans*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, reddish-purple drupe that ripens in the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
