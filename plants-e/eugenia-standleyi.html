Eugenia standleyi

Eugenia standleyi, commonly known as the Mexican tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are initially green and turn yellow or red when ripe. The fruits are edible, with a tart flavor, and are often used in traditional cuisine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
