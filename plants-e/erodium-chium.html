Erodium chium

Erodium chium, commonly known as stinkcup, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its seeds are encased in a fleshy, orange-colored pith that aids in seed dispersal.

Erodium chium is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.
