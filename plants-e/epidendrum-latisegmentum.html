Epidendrum latisegmentum

Epidendrum latisegmentum, commonly known as the broad-segmented epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple stems. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to pale green pubescence. Its flowers are borne in racemes and are typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shade gardens and greenhouses.
