Episcia cordata

Episcia cordata, commonly known as the "Heartleaf" or "Wax plant," is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often brightly colored. It produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Episcia cordata is typically grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
