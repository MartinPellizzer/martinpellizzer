Eritrichium kamtschaticum

Eritrichium kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Kamtschatskan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and delicate foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eritrichium kamtschaticum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
