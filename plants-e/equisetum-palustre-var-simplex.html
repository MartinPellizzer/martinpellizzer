Equisetum palustre var. simplex

Equisetum palustre var. simplex, commonly known as the marsh horsetail, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a hollow, jointed stem. This plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in marshy or swampy habitats. It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments and is often found in temperate regions.

The plant reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called strobila.
