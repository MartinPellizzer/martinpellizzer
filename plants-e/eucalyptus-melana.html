Eucalyptus melana

Eucalyptus melana, commonly known as black gum tree, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy bark that gives it its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
