Echinodorus palifolius var. latifolius

Echinodorus palifolius var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf cabomba, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often bright green and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the water surface, adding a decorative element to aquatic environments. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with moderate to high light conditions.

This variety is popular in aquariums and ponds due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
