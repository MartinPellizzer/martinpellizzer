Elsholtzia byeonsanensis

Elsholtzia byeonsanensis, commonly known as the Korean elsholtzia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a fragrant scent when crushed.

Elsholtzia byeonsanensis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in temperate climates with ample sunlight.
