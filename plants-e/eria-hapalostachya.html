Eria hapalostachya

Eria hapalostachya, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed eria, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive hairy appearance on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eria hapalostachya is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
