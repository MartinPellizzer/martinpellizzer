Eupatorium catharinense

Eupatorium catharinense, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported antipyretic and analgesic properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of neotropical flora.
