Erica recurvifolia

Erica recurvifolia, commonly known as the bent-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and recurved, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Erica recurvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
