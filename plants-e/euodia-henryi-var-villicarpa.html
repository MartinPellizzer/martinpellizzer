Euodia henryi var. villicarpa

Euodia henryi var. villicarpa, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
