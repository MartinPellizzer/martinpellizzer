Epidendrum porphyroglossum var. sulphureum

Epidendrum porphyroglossum var. sulphureum, commonly known as the Sulphur-flowered Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange flowers that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated, dark green, and often have a waxy texture.

This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
