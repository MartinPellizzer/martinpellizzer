Echeveria yalmanantlanensis

Echeveria yalmanantlanensis, commonly known as the Yalmanantlan echewera, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure composed of fleshy, often blue-green or pinkish leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is appreciated by both horticultural enthusiasts and collectors.
