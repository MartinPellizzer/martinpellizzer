Eucalyptus laevopinea var. turbinata

Eucalyptus laevopinea var. turbinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the winter months. Its bark is often flaky or peeling, and it is commonly found in dry, sandy soils.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native flora in those regions.
