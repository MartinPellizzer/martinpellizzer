Euphorbia capansa

Euphorbia capansa, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny, spherical fruit that encloses the seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in a crown-like structure. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Euphorbia capansa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
