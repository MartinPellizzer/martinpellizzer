Englerophytum gigantifolium

Englerophytum gigantifolium, commonly known as the giant-leaf englerophyte, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable species for studying desert plant resilience.
