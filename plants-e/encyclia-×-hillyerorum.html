Encyclia × hillyerorum

Encyclia × hillyerorum, commonly known as the Hillyer’s orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Encyclia cochleata and Encyclia fraterna.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America, particularly in regions of Mexico and Guatemala. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that support its evergreen foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred lip, and bloom during the late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
