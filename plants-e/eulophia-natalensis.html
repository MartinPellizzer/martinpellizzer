Eulophia natalensis

Eulophia natalensis, commonly known as Natal's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in grasslands and forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive white lip, and they bloom during the warmer months.

Eulophia natalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
