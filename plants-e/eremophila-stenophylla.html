Eremophila stenophylla

Eremophila stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spikes of tubular, pink to red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for native pollinators.

Eremophila stenophylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
