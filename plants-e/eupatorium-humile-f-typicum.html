Eupatorium humile f. typicum

Eupatorium humile f. typicum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
