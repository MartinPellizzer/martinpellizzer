Erica laevis

Erica laevis, commonly known as the smooth heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, appearing in spring and early summer. Erica laevis is well adapted to harsh, rocky environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
