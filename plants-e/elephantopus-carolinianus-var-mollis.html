Elephantopus carolinianus var. mollis

Elephantopus carolinianus var. mollis, commonly known as Carolina elephantweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it often grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
