Erysimum adcumbens

Erysimum adcumbens, commonly known as the creeping wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant with bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming habit, with branches that lie close to the ground, making it suitable for ground cover. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often prostrate.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
