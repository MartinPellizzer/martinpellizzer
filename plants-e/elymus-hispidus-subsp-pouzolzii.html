Elymus hispidus subsp. pouzolzii

Elymus hispidus subsp.

pouzolzii, commonly known as the Sierra fescue, is a perennial grass native to western North America. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
