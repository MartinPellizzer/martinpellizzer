Eleocharis cubangensis

Eleocharis cubangensis, commonly known as the Cubango sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly found in wetland and marshy habitats. The plant typically features slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its moist environments.

Eleocharis cubangensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
