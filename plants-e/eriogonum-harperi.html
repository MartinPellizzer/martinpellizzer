Eriogonum harperi

Eriogonum harperi, commonly known as the Harp's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Eriogonum harperi is an important component of local ecosystems and provides nectar for pollinators.
