Eugenia micrandra

Eugenia micrandra, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark red berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
