Eupatorium paneroi

Eupatorium paneroi, commonly known as the Paneroi blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium paneroi is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds.
