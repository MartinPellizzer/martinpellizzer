Epipactis phyllanthes subsp. pendula

Epipactis phyllanthes subsp.

pendula, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that hang from the plant, often in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in calcareous soils.
