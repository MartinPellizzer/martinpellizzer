Echinocactus longihamatus var. bicolor

Echinocactus longihamatus var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored hedgehog cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are adorned with prominent, sharp spines. The plant exhibits a unique coloration pattern, with a greenish-gray body and red or pinkish spines, creating a visually appealing contrast. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and as a houseplant.
