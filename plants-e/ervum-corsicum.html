Ervum corsicum

Ervum corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a leguminous species, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Ervum corsicum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
