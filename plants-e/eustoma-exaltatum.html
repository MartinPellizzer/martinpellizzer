Eustoma exaltatum

Eustoma exaltatum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Eustoma exaltatum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
