Epidendrum rhacoglossum

Epidendrum rhacoglossum, commonly known as the yellow-tipped epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with red markings and long, strap-like petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence is usually erect, bearing numerous flowers that bloom in clusters.

Epidendrum rhacoglossum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
