Eupatorium atromontanum

Eupatorium atromontanum, commonly known as black bones, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Eupatorium atromontanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
