Elaeocarpus pendulus

Elaeocarpus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging elaeocarp, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its drooping inflorescences and small, fleshy fruits that are often brightly colored. Its leaves are typically alternate and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

Elaeocarpus pendulus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
