Eriocaulon silveirae

Eriocaulon silveirae, commonly known as the Brazilian spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescence. Native to Brazil, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features tiny, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in compact clusters.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its wetland habitat, with rhizomes that help anchor it in soft, waterlogged soils.
