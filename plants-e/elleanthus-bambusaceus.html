Elleanthus bambusaceus

Elleanthus bambusaceus, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble bamboo, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Elleanthus bambusaceus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
