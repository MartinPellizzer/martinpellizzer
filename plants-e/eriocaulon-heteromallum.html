Eriocaulon heteromallum

Eriocaulon heteromallum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its reproductive structures consist of tiny, white to greenish flowers arranged in dense inflorescences.

Eriocaulon heteromallum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
