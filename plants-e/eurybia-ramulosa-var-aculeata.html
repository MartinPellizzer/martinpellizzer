Eurybia ramulosa var. aculeata

Eurybia ramulosa var. aculeata, commonly known as the hooked frostweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its stems are often armed with stiff, hooked bracts, which give it its common name.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
