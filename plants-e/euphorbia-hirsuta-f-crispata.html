Euphorbia hirsuta f. crispata

Euphorbia hirsuta f. crispata, commonly known as the curly spurge, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, branched stems and distinctive leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically small, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. Its most notable feature is the curling, wavy edges of the leaves, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. Native to arid regions, it is adapted to survive in dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

This plant is often cultivated as a decorative succulent due to its unique appearance and low maintenance needs.
