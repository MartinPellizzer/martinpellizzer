Eragrostis carazensis

Eragrostis carazensis, commonly known as the Andean lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it conserve water in its native habitat.

Eragrostis carazensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for livestock.
