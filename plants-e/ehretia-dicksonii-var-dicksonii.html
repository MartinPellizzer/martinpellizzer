Ehretia dicksonii var. dicksonii

Ehretia dicksonii var. dicksonii, commonly known as the Mexican mahogany, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
