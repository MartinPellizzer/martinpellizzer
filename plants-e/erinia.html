Erinia

Erinia, commonly known as the common ergot, is a parasitic plant that infects the spikes of rye and other grasses.

It produces a dark, hard sclerotium that contains alkaloids, which can be toxic to humans and animals. The plant belongs to the Claviceps genus and is part of the fungal kingdom, not a true plant. Its presence can lead to ergotism, a serious illness affecting the nervous system.

Erinia has historical significance in both agriculture and medicine due to its complex chemical composition.
