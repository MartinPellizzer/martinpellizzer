Episcia villosa

Episcia villosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf or woolly episcia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white, pink, or red.

Episcia villosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
