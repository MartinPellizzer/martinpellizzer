Ericoides dumosum

Ericoides dumosum, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its compact growth habit and ability to form dense thickets make it an important component of its native ecosystems.

Ericoides dumosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
