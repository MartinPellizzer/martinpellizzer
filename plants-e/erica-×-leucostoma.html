Erica × leucostoma

Erica × leucostoma, commonly known as the white heath, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to form thickets.

Erica × leucostoma is also valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
