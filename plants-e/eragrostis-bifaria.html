Eragrostis bifaria

Eragrostis bifaria, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Eragrostis bifaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
