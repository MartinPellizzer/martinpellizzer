Echinopsis arachnacantha

Echinopsis arachnacantha, commonly known as the spider-tailed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces striking, night-blooming flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and conservation efforts.
