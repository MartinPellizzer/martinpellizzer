Echium strictum var. lineolatum

Echium strictum var. lineolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thornweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
