Eriogonum parishii

Eriogonum parishii, commonly known as Parc's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum parishii is an important nectar source for local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
