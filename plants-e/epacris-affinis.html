Epacris affinis

Epacris affinis, commonly known as the common epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Epacris affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
