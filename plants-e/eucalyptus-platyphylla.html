Eucalyptus platyphylla

Eucalyptus platyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed malleeflower, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is often found in dry, sandy soils in coastal and inland regions.

Eucalyptus platyphylla is a shrub or small tree that plays an important role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
