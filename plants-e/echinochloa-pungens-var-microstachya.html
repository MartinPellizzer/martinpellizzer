Echinochloa pungens var. microstachya

Echinochloa pungens var. microstachya, commonly known as small-stemmed barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tightly clustered at the top of the plant. The leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, with a prominent midrib. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is frequently found in agricultural fields and wetlands.

This species is known for its rapid growth and can become a competitive weed in various ecosystems.
