Echioides picta

Echioides picta, commonly known as the painted echium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Echioides picta is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
