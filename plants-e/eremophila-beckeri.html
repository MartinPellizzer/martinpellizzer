Eremophila beckeri

Eremophila beckeri, commonly known as the Becker's emu bush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its upright, woody structure and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Eremophila beckeri is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
