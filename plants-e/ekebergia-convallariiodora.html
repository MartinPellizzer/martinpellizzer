Ekebergia convallariiodora

Ekebergia convallariiodora, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
