Eupatorium maculatum var. urticifolium

Eupatorium maculatum var. urticifolium, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are hairy and often reddish in color. The plant produces showy, purple-to-pink composite flowers that bloom from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a distinctive white or pale green vein pattern.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
