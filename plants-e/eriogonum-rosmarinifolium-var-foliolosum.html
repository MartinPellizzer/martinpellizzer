Eriogonum rosmarinifolium var. foliolosum

Eriogonum rosmarinifolium var. foliolosum, commonly known as the leafy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its striking silver-green foliage. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
