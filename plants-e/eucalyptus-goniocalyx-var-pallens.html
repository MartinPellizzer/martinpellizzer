Eucalyptus goniocalyx var. pallens

Eucalyptus goniocalyx var. pallens, commonly known as the white-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its pale grey to white bark, which is fibrous and peels in strips, giving the tree a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered with a fine white bloom, contributing to its unique visual appeal. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green or yellow, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and hardy species in its native habitat.
