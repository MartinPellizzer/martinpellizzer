Edwardia cauliflora

Edwardia cauliflora, commonly known as the orchid tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble orchids, which bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Edwardia cauliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
