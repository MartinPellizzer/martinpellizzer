Eria reptans var. matsudae

Eria reptans var. matsudae, commonly known as the Matsudae's climbing orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and linear, grass-like leaves that form dense mats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
