Emmeorhiza brasiliensis

Emmeorhiza brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its spiny stems and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

Emmeorhiza brasiliensis is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.
