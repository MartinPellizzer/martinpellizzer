Eremopappus pulchellus

Eremopappus pulchellus, commonly known as the beautiful eremopappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in the spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
