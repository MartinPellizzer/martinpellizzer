Eriocaulon gibbosum var. brevifolium

Eriocaulon gibbosum var. brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and pond margins. The plant has compact, tufted growth with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, white to pale green flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
