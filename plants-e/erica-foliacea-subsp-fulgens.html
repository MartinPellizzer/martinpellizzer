Erica foliacea subsp. fulgens

Erica foliacea subsp.

fulgens, commonly known as the fire heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers during the summer months.

The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Erica foliacea subsp.

fulgens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
