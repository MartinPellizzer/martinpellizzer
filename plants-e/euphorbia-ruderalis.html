Euphorbia ruderalis

Euphorbia ruderalis, commonly known as the common spurge, is a flowering plant in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant has a milky, toxic sap that can cause skin irritation. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in a structure called a cyathium.

Euphorbia ruderalis is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and its ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.
