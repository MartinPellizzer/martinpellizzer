Erica codonodes

Erica codonodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Erica codonodes is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardiness and attractive appearance.
