Euonymus longipes

Euonymus longipes, commonly known as the longipes woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

Euonymus longipes is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
