Echinodorus alpestris

Echinodorus alpestris, commonly known as the alpine arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and can tolerate a range of water conditions. Echinodorus alpestris is often used in aquariums due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

It produces small, white flowers that emerge above the water surface, adding to its ornamental value.
