Erica filamentosa

Erica filamentosa, commonly known as the thread-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dense clusters of evergreen leaves. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica filamentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic beauty.
