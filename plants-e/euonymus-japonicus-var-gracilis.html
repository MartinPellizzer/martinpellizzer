Euonymus japonicus var. gracilis

Euonymus japonicus var. gracilis, commonly known as Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that transition from green to red as they mature, producing showy, berry-like fruits. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a slight pubescent texture. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique fruiting display makes it a notable addition to gardens and landscapes.
