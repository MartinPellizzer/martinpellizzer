Elaeocarpus williamsianus

Elaeocarpus williamsianus, commonly known as the Williams's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
