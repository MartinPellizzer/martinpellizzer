Erigeron × chanousii

Erigeron × chanousii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and fluffy, white to pale blue flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, herbaceous stems.

Erigeron × chanousii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
