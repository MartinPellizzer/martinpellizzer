Echium creticum subsp. algarbiense

Echium creticum subsp.

algarbiense, commonly known as the Algarbi poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
