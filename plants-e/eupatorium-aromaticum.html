Eupatorium aromaticum

Eupatorium aromaticum, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Eupatorium aromaticum is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
