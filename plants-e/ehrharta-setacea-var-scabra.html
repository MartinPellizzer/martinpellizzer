Ehrharta setacea var. scabra

Ehrharta setacea var. scabra, commonly known as rough fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its wiry, hairy stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
