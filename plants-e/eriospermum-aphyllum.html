Eriospermum aphyllum

Eriospermum aphyllum, commonly known as the naked eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with only a few small, scale-like structures present. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
