Euonymus occidentalis subsp. parishii

Euonymus occidentalis subsp.

parishii, commonly known as the Pacific euonymus, is a shrub native to western North America. It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually elliptic to ovate leaves that are often dark green above and lighter below.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, spherical fruits that are visually striking. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
