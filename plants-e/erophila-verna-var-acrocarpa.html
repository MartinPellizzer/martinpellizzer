Erophila verna var. acrocarpa

Erophila verna var. acrocarpa, commonly known as early meadow cranesbill, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
