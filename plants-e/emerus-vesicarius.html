Emerus vesicarius

Emerus vesicarius, commonly known as the bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are often submerged, while its stems and roots are anchored in the substrate.

The plant's unique trapping mechanism allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments by supplementing its nutrient intake through prey.
