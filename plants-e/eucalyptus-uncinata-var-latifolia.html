Eucalyptus uncinata var. latifolia

Eucalyptus uncinata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved malleeflower, is a variant of the malleeflower species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to grey pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to white in color, arranged in clusters. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

This species is often found in mallee woodlands and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
