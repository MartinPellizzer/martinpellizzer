Eucalyptus polycarpa

Eucalyptus polycarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which are followed by woody, cylindrical capsules. Eucalyptus polycarpa is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
