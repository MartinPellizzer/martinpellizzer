Echinocactus schlechtendalii

Echinocactus schlechtendalii, commonly known as the Schlechtendal's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Echinocactus schlechtendalii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and striking appearance.
