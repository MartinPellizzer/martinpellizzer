Echinochloa jaliscana

Echinochloa jaliscana, commonly known as Mexican barnyardgrass, is a species of annual grass native to Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. It is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields, making it a common weed in many regions.

Its rapid growth and high seed production contribute to its invasive potential in various ecosystems.
