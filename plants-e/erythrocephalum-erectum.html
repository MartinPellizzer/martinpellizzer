Erythrocephalum erectum

Erythrocephalum erectum, commonly known as the erect swamp everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flower heads that resemble small globes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erythrocephalum erectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
