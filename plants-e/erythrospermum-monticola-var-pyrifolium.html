Erythrospermum monticola var. pyrifolium

Erythrospermum monticola var. pyrifolium, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated in gardens across the country.
