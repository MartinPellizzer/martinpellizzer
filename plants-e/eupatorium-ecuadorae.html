Eupatorium ecuadorae

Eupatorium ecuadorae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and montane grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Eupatorium ecuadorae is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
