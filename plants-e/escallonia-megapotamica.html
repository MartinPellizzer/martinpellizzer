Escallonia megapotamica

Escallonia megapotamica, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Escallonia megapotamica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like birds and bees.
