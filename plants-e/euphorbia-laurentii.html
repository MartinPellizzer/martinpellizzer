Euphorbia laurentii

Euphorbia laurentii, commonly known as the "Crown of Thorns," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with fleshy, green stems covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often small and scale-like, reducing water loss in arid environments.

Euphorbia laurentii is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
