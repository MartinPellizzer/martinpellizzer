Epipactis neglecta

Epipactis neglecta, commonly known as the common helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with showy, greenish-yellow flowers. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Epipactis neglecta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in association with other orchid species.
