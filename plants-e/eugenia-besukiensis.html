Eugenia besukiensis

Eugenia besukiensis, commonly known as the Besuk Island clove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the island of Bali in Indonesia and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
