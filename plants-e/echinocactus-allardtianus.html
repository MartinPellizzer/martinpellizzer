Echinocactus allardtianus

Echinocactus allardtianus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and extreme weather. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant's unique appearance and resilience make it a notable example of desert flora.
