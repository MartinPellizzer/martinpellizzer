Erythrodes polygonata

Erythrodes polygonata, commonly known as the red-veined polygon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often colorful flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with prominent red veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

Erythrodes polygonata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and seasonal blooms.
