Eugenia complanata

Eugenia complanata, commonly known as the flat-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically flat and smooth. It produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that is edible and often used in local cuisines.
