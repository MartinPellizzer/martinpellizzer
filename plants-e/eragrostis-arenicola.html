Eragrostis arenicola

Eragrostis arenicola, commonly known as sandblain grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, reflecting its ability to reduce water loss.

Eragrostis arenicola is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in some regions for forage or erosion control.
