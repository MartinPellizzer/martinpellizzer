Eryngium amethystinum var. transiens

Eryngium amethystinum var. transiens, commonly known as the transitory purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, spiny, purple-blue flower heads that resemble thistles, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, foamy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a notable addition to wildflower gardens.
