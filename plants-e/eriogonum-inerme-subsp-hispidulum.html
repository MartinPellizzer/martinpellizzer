Eriogonum inerme subsp. hispidulum

Eriogonum inerme subsp.

hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with low precipitation.
