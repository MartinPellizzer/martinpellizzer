Eugenia subrufa

Eugenia subrufa, commonly known as the red-leaf eugenia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green to reddish-brown leaves that change color with the seasons. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Eugenia subrufa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
