Elaeocarpus longifolius

Elaeocarpus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody, round fruits that are typically covered in a thick, hairy layer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
