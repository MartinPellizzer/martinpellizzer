Eriosolena pendula

Eriosolena pendula, commonly known as the hanging mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Eriosolena pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
