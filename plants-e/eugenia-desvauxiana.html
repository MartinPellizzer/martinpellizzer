Eugenia desvauxiana

Eugenia desvauxiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia desvauxiana*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and medicinal properties.
