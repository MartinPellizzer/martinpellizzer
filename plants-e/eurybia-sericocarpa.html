Eurybia sericocarpa

Eurybia sericocarpa, commonly known as silverleaf sunflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Eurybia sericocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
