Erysimum caboverdeanum

Erysimum caboverdeanum, commonly known as the Cape Verde wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Erysimum caboverdeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
