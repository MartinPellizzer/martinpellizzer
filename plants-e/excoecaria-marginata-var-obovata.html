Excoecaria marginata var. obovata

Excoecaria marginata var. obovata, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, obovate leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive red or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its bark is thick and often marked with vertical stripes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
