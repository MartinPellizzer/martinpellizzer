Enceliopsis covillei

Enceliopsis covillei, commonly known as Coville's globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

Enceliopsis covillei plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a rare and sensitive species.
