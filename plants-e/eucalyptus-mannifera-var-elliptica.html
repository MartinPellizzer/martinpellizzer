Eucalyptus mannifera var. elliptica

Eucalyptus mannifera var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stringy gum, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, greyish-brown felt. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically cream to pale yellow in color. Its bark is fibrous and peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands.
