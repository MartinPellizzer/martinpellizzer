Eberlanzia intricata

Eberlanzia intricata, commonly known as the intricate eberlanzia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Eberlanzia intricata is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
