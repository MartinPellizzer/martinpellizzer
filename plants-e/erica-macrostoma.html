Erica macrostoma

Erica macrostoma, commonly known as the large-stem heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica macrostoma is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its high biodiversity.

Its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions makes it a resilient and ecologically significant species.
