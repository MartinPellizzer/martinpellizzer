Eriogonum filicaule

Eriogonum filicaule, commonly known as the broadleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and sagebrush steppe. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is adapted to arid environments.

Eriogonum filicaule plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
