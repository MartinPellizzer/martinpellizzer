Erechtites megalocarpus

Erechtites megalocarpus, commonly known as the giant ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with rough textures and serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Erechtites megalocarpus is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
