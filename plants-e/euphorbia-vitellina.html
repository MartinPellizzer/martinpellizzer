Euphorbia vitellina

Euphorbia vitellina, commonly known as the yellow-spined spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and yellowish-green sap, which can be toxic if ingested. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
