Echinopsis pectinata var. laevior

Echinopsis pectinata var. laevior, commonly known as the "Moon Cactus," is a succulent native to South America.

It features a short, thick stem with a dense covering of white spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear pale yellow to white flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
