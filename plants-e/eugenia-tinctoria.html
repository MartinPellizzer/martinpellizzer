Eugenia tinctoria

Eugenia tinctoria, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor. Eugenia tinctoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the medicinal properties of its leaves and fruits.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is commonly found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
