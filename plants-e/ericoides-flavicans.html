Ericoides flavicans

Ericoides flavicans, commonly known as the yellow-flowered heath, is a shrub native to the wet tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes evergreen shrubs and trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
