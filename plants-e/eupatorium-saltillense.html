Eupatorium saltillense

Eupatorium saltillense, commonly known as the saltillense thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Eupatorium saltillense produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.
