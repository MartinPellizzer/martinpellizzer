Eugenia capensis subsp. nyassensis

Eugenia capensis subsp.

nyassensis, commonly known as the Nyassan guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Africa. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
