Erica decipiens

Erica decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family, Ericaceae.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape province, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica decipiens is known for its compact growth habit and ability to form dense, evergreen shrubs.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal and sandy environments.
