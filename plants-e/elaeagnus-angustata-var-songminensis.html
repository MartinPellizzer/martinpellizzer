Elaeagnus angustata var. songminensis

Elaeagnus angustata var. songminensis, commonly known as the Songmin silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, scale-like hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, reddish berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
