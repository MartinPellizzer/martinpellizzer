Eugenia squamifera

Eugenia squamifera, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, tart fruits resembling small cherries. Eugenia squamifera thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.
