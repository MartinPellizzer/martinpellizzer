Enarthrocarpus strangulatus f. brachycarpus

Enarthrocarpus strangulatus f. brachycarpus, commonly known as the narrow-fruit strangler fig, is a member of the Moraceae family.

It is a tropical tree characterized by its climbing habit and ability to form dense canopies. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The species is native to Southeast Asia and is known for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
