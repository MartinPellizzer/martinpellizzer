Eupatorium alternifolium f. oblongifolium

Eupatorium alternifolium f. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thoroughwort, is a variant of the thoroughwort plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads. The plant typically features clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its foliage and flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
