Elaeocarpus grumosus

Elaeocarpus grumosus, commonly known as the clustered elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically covered in a thick, fleshy covering. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture and prominent veins.

The clustered elaeocarp is often found in lowland and hill forests, thriving in humid, warm climates.
