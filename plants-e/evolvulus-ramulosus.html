Evolvulus ramulosus

Evolvulus ramulosus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soil.

Evolvulus ramulosus is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners.
