Eupatorium laevigatum f. crenatifolium

Eupatorium laevigatum f. crenatifolium, commonly known as the hairy boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its serrated, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
