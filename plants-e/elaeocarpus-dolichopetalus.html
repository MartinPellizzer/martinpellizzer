Elaeocarpus dolichopetalus

Elaeocarpus dolichopetalus, commonly known as the long-petaled elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant's fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
