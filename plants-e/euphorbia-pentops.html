Euphorbia pentops

Euphorbia pentops, commonly known as the five-lobed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with several branches, and its most distinctive characteristic is its five-lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy, white coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Euphorbia pentops is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves to survive dry conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
