Eugenia storkii

Eugenia storkii, commonly known as the Stork's bill or Storksbill, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, often containing a single seed. Eugenia storkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.
