Erythroxylum gerrardii subsp. ankaranense

Erythroxylum gerrardii subsp.

ankaranense, commonly known as the Ankaranense copal tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody capsules containing aromatic resinous seeds. It is adapted to dry, tropical climates and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant's resin has been traditionally used for various purposes, including incense and medicinal applications.
