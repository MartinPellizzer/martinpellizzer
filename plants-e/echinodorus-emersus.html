Echinodorus emersus

Echinodorus emersus, commonly known as the broadleaf waterweed, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that float on the water surface or extend partially out of it. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread rapidly in aquatic environments. It thrives in slow to moderately flowing freshwater habitats with soft to moderately hard water.

Echinodorus emersus is often used in pond and aquarium settings for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
