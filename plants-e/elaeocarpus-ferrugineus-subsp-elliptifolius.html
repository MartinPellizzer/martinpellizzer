Elaeocarpus ferrugineus subsp. elliptifolius

Elaeocarpus ferrugineus subsp.

elliptifolius, commonly known as the rusty elaeocarpus, is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family. It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and rusty brown beneath.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in lowland and hill forest environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local communities.
