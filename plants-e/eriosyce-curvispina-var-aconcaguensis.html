Eriosyce curvispina var. aconcaguensis

Eriosyce curvispina var. aconcaguensis, commonly known as the Aconcagua cholla, is a succulent cactus native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It features a cylindrical stem with spines that are curved and often reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring season, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
