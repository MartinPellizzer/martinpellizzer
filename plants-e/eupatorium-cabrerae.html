Eupatorium cabrerae

Eupatorium cabrerae, commonly known as the Cuban boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Cuba.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and white or pale purple.

Eupatorium cabrerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
