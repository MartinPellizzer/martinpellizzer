Epidendrum tungurahuae

Epidendrum tungurahuae, commonly known as the Tungurahua Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Ecuador.

This terrestrial orchid features a sympodial growth habit with a short, horizontal rhizome and a few elongated, fleshy pseudobulbs. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy green surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
