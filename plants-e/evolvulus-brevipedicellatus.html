Evolvulus brevipedicellatus

Evolvulus brevipedicellatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed blue morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often twining.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
