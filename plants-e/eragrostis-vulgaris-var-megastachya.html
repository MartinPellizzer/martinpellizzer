Eragrostis vulgaris var. megastachya

Eragrostis vulgaris var. megastachya, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to golden flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose, open cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive, feathery appearance.
