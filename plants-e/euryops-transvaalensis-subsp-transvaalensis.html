Euryops transvaalensis subsp. transvaalensis

Euryops transvaalensis subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flower heads with a central disk.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
