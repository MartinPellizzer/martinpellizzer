Encyclia ceratistes

Encyclia ceratistes, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, horn-like structures on its flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its inflorescence bears several showy, greenish-yellow flowers.

Encyclia ceratistes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
