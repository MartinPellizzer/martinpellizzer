Euosma cordifolia

Euosma cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf euosma, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem structure.

Euosma cordifolia is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland habitats.
