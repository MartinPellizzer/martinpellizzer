Ervum cappadocicum

Ervum cappadocicum, commonly known as Cappadocian vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Ervum cappadocicum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
