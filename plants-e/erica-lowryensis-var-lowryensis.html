Erica lowryensis var. lowryensis

Erica lowryensis var. lowryensis, commonly known as Lowry's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen and adapted to the harsh coastal climate.

This plant is often found in open habitats such as dunes and coastal scrub, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
