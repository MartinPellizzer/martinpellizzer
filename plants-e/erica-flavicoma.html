Erica flavicoma

Erica flavicoma, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.

Erica flavicoma is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.
