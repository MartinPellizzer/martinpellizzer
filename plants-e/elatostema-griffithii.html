Elatostema griffithii

Elatostema griffithii, commonly known as the creeping snowflake, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Elatostema griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
