Echinocereus hancockii

Echinocereus hancockii, commonly known as the Hancock's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spine-tipped cactus that typically forms dense clusters of cylindrical stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and desert gardens.
