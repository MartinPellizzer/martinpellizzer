Erythrina kassneri

Erythrina kassneri, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a reddish hue. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Erythrina kassneri also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
