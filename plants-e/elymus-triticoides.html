Elymus triticoides

Elymus triticoides, commonly known as western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Elymus triticoides is valued for its drought tolerance and is used in ecological restoration projects.
