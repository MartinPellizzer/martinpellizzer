Echinopsis saltensis var. nealeana

Echinopsis saltensis var. nealeana, commonly known as the Nealeana cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming during the cooler months. It is a slow-growing cactus that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
