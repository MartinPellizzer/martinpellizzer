Eriogonum trachygonum var. subscaposum

Eriogonum trachygonum var. subscaposum, commonly known as the roughleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and has a low water requirement.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a hardy plant for xeriscaping.
