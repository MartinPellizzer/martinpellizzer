Eleocharis brachycarpa

Eleocharis brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited spike-rush, is a species of grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, a common feature in many Cyperaceae species.

Eleocharis brachycarpa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
