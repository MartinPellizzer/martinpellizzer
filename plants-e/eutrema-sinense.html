Eutrema sinense

Eutrema sinense, commonly known as the Chinese sandmat, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a fibrous root system and a mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Eutrema sinense is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
