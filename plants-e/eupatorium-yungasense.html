Eupatorium yungasense

Eupatorium yungasense, commonly known as Yungas eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, and is adapted to the cooler, humid conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium yungasense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
