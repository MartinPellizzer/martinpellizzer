Epimedium macranthum f. violaceum

Epimedium macranthum f. violaceum, commonly known as the purple-flowered barberry, is a striking perennial plant native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-pink flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its unique foliage and showy blooms make it a prized addition to shade gardens.
