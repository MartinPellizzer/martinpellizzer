Eurygania melliflora

Eurygania melliflora, commonly known as the sweet-scented bladdernut, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often gland-dotted. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
