Eugenia magoana

Eugenia magoana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia magoana*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Eugenia magoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
