Elytrigia intermedia subsp. graeca

Elytrigia intermedia subsp.

graeca, commonly known as Greek intermediate grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its robust root system and adaptability to poor soils.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
