Ervum giganteum

Ervum giganteum, commonly known as the giant vetch, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant has a robust root system and can fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. Its stems are often hairy and can grow up to several meters in length.

It is valued in agriculture for its forage qualities and ability to improve soil fertility.
