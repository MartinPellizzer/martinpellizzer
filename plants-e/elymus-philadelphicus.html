Elymus philadelphicus

Elymus philadelphicus, commonly known as redtop grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces spikelets that are usually pink to reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including wetlands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Elymus philadelphicus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
