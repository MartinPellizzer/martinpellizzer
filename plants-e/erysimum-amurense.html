Erysimum amurense

Erysimum amurense, commonly known as the Siberian wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erysimum amurense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
