Erysimum asperum var. bealianum

Erysimum asperum var. bealianum, commonly known as the rough wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
