Elymus donianus subsp. virescens

Elymus donianus subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the green wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
