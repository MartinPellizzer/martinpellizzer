Eugenia calycina var. herbacea

Eugenia calycina var. herbacea, commonly known as the soft-leaved guava, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thin, herbaceous leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, aromatic fruits similar to guavas. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
