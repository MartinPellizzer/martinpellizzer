Euterpe pisifera

Euterpe pisifera, commonly known as the Brazilian pineapple plant, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers followed by edible, spiky fruits resembling pineapples. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is an important food source for local communities.

Euterpe pisifera plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
