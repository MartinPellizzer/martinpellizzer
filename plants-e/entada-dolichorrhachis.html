Entada dolichorrhachis

Entada dolichorrhachis, commonly known as the long-stemmed African bean, is a member of the Fabaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, and it is native to tropical Africa.

The tree is valued for its timber and traditional uses, though it has limited horticultural appeal.
