Etlingera goliathensis

Etlingera goliathensis, commonly known as the giant flame bamboo, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its inflorescence consists of vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual impact.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in warm climates.
