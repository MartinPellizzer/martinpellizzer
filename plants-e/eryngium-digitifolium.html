Eryngium digitifolium

Eryngium digitifolium, commonly known as the fingerleaf eryngium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Eryngium digitifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flower structure.
