Eurya pusilla

Eurya pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf waxleaf eurya, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Eurya pusilla is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects to the garden.
