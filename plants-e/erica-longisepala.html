Erica longisepala

Erica longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Erica longisepala is well adapted to harsh, coastal environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
