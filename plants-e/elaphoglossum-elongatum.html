Elaphoglossum elongatum

Elaphoglossum elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters in length. The leaves are pinnate, with small, ovate pinnae that are often slightly curled at the edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fern-like appearance.
