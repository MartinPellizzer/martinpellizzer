Eupatorium bertholdii

Eupatorium bertholdii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and pond edges, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Eupatorium bertholdii is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local pollinators.
