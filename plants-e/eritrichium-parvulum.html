Eritrichium parvulum

Eritrichium parvulum, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white trichomes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.

Eritrichium parvulum is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers.
