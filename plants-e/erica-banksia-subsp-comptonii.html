Erica banksia subsp. comptonii

Erica banksia subsp.

comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia. It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and distinctive, cone-like inflorescences.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
