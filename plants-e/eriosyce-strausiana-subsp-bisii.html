Eriosyce strausiana subsp. bisii

Eriosyce strausiana subsp.

bisii, commonly known as the Desert Candelabra Cactus, is a perennial plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its columnar stem with dense, woolly spines and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has a shallow root system adapted to the arid environment, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. Its unique morphology includes a branching pattern that resembles a candelabra, giving it its common name.

It plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing shelter and food for desert wildlife.
