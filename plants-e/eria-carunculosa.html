Eria carunculosa

Eria carunculosa, commonly known as the red-rooted orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred orchid flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in some regions.
