Euxolus rhombeus

Euxolus rhombeus, commonly known as the diamond leaf, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It prefers well-drained, alkaline soils and full sun exposure, making it a challenging plant to cultivate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
