Euphorbia papillifolia var. polyandra

Euphorbia papillifolia var. polyandra, commonly known as the pink spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
