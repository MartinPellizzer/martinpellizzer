Euclinia longiflora

Euclinia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered euclinia, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that often display vibrant colors, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Euclinia longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
