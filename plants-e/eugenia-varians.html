Eugenia varians

Eugenia varians, commonly known as the variable eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round, and typically turn red when mature.

Eugenia varians is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
