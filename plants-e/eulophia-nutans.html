Eulophia nutans

Eulophia nutans, commonly known as the drooping eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces pseudobulbs and leafy shoots. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves and produces racemes of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a long, slender spur and are often pollinated by moths.

Eulophia nutans thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
