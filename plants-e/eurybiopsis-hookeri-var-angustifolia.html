Eurybiopsis hookeri var. angustifolia

Eurybiopsis hookeri var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eurybiopsis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
