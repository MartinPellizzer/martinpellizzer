Eleocharis acicularis subsp. yokoscensis

Eleocharis acicularis subsp.

yokoscensis, commonly known as the Yokosuka needle sedge, is a slender, grass-like plant native to Japan. It is characterized by its narrow, spike-like inflorescence and tiny, spikelet-bearing clusters.

The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
