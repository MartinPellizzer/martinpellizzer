Eulophia lamellata

Eulophia lamellata, commonly known as the lace orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Eulophia lamellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
