Eriogonum flavum var. muticum

Eriogonum flavum var. muticum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces seed pods that are inflated and covered in fine hairs.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
