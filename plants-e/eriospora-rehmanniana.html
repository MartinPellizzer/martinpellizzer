Eriospora rehmanniana

Eriospora rehmanniana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Eriospora rehmanniana is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climatic conditions.
