Eleutherococcus melanocarpus

Eleutherococcus melanocarpus, commonly known as black stemmed arrowhead, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Eleutherococcus melanocarpus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in herbal medicine.
