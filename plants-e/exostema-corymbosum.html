Exostema corymbosum

Exostema corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered waxplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce clusters of small, waxy, white flowers. The plant has succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also noted for its resilience and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
