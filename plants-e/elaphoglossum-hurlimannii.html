It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Elaphoglossum hurlimannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
