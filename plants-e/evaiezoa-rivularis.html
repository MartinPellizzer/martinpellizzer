Evaiezoa rivularis

Evaiezoa rivularis, commonly known as the river ivy, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a ground cover in ornamental gardens.
