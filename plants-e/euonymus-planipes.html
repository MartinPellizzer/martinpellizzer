Euonymus planipes

Euonymus planipes, commonly known as the flatleaf woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and dark green on top with a lighter underside. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant's unique leaf shape and ornamental berries make it a notable species in botanical studies and garden settings.
