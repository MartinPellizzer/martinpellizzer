Elionurus agropyroides

Elionurus agropyroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass or slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Elionurus agropyroides is characterized by its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
