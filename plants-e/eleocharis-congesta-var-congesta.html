Eleocharis congesta var. congesta

Eleocharis congesta var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in temperate climates. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, often contributing to soil stabilization.

The species is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
