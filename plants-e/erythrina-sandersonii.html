Erythrina sandersonii

Erythrina sandersonii, commonly known as the red sandersonii or sanderson's flame tree, is a tropical legume native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom in dense, racemose inflorescences. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

Erythrina sandersonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.
