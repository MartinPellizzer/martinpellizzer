Eugenia fragrans var. genuina

Eugenia fragrans var. genuina, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round, red or yellow berries that are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
