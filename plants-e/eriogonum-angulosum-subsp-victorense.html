Eriogonum angulosum subsp. victorense

Eriogonum angulosum subsp.

victorense, commonly known as the Sierra madre buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as hillsides and open woodlands.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
