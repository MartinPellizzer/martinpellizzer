Elytraria crenata

Elytraria crenata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous areas, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming throughout the year in some regions. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
