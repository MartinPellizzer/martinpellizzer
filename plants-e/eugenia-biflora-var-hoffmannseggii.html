Eugenia biflora var. hoffmannseggii

Eugenia biflora var. hoffmannseggii, commonly known as the Hoffmannsegg's eugenia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature. This plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
