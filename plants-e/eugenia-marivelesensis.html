Eugenia marivelesensis

Eugenia marivelesensis, commonly known as the Mariveles eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Luzon, where it grows in lowland to montane rainforests. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by local wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered endangered and is of interest for conservation efforts.
