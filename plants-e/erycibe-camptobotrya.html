Erycibe camptobotrya

Erycibe camptobotrya, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers with yellow centers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
