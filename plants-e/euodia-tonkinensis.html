Euodia tonkinensis

Euodia tonkinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese cinnamon or tonkin cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing elliptical seeds. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Euodia tonkinensis is valued for its unique scent and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
