Eucalyptus × barmedmanensis

Eucalyptus × barmedmanensis, commonly known as the Barmedman gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in umbels, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This species is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
