Eriocaulon plumale

Eriocaulon plumale, commonly known as the featherleaf spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that often bear dense clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are linear and often have a feathery appearance, contributing to its common name.

Eriocaulon plumale plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
