Erythroxylum microphyllum

Erythroxylum microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf copal tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact size, typically reaching heights of only 1 to 2 meters, with dense, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
