Echinocactus fischeri

Echinocactus fischeri, commonly known as the fisher's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
