Erysimum mongolicum

Erysimum mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Erysimum mongolicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
