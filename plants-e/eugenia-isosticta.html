Eugenia isosticta

Eugenia isosticta, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its striking red, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture, and it produces small, edible fruits that are yellow when ripe.

Eugenia isosticta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.
