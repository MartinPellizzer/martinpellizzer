Epidendrum galeatum

Epidendrum galeatum, commonly known as the Spotted Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with a vibrant purple or red coloration, and are arranged in a raceme.

Epidendrum galeatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
