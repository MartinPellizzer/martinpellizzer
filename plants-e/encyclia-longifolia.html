Encyclia longifolia

Encyclia longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and large, showy white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of fragrant, elongated petals. Its leaves are linear and arranged in two rows along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Encyclia longifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
