Euclea natalensis subsp. rotundifolia

Euclea natalensis subsp.

rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its elliptic to ovate leaves that are often rounded at the base.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
