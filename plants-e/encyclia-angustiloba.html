Encyclia angustiloba

Encyclia angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
