Ericoides fragrans

Ericoides fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ericoides fragrans is native to regions with temperate climates, particularly in parts of Europe and Asia.
