Eremophila citrina

Eremophila citrina, commonly known as the lemon bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for drought-tolerant gardens.

Eremophila citrina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
