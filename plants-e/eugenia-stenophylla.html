Eugenia stenophylla

Eugenia stenophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant red flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often consumed fresh or processed into jams and juices.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
