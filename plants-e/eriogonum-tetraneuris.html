Eriogonum tetraneuris

Eriogonum tetraneuris, commonly known as the four-veined buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that have four distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Eriogonum tetraneuris is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive seed structures and ecological importance to pollinators.
