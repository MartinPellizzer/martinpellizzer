Euphorbia esula subsp. paradoxa

Euphorbia esula subsp.

paradoxa, commonly known as the Canadian tansy, is a perennial plant native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems that are often covered in a powdery white coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is considered invasive in some regions.
