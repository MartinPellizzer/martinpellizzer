Erica trassula

Erica trassula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica trassula typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained, acidic conditions.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique type of biome in South Africa.
