Eupatorium badium

Eupatorium badium, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium badium is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
