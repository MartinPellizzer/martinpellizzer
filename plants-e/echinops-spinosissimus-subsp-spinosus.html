Echinops spinosissimus subsp. spinosus

Echinops spinosissimus subsp.

spinosus, commonly known as the spiny globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small globes, which are a key feature of the genus Echinops.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by the development of its distinctive, spiky fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
