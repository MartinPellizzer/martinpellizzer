Embothrium dentatum

Embothrium dentatum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its bright red flowers, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Embothrium dentatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
