Ehretia cortesia

Ehretia cortesia, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeard tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
