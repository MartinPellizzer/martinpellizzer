Eurybia furcata

Eurybia furcata, commonly known as the greenish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white to pale blue in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eurybia furcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
