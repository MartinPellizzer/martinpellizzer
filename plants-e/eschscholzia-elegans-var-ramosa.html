Eschscholzia elegans var. ramosa

Eschscholzia elegans var. ramosa, commonly known as the bushy poppy, is a variant of the California poppy characterized by its dense, branched stems and clusters of bright orange flowers.

It is native to California and thrives in well-drained soils, preferring full sun. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to naturalize, making it a popular choice for wildflower gardens. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety is particularly noted for its compact growth habit and showy, vibrant blooms.
