Erythronium carolinianum

Erythronium carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina dogbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a reddish or greenish tinge. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

Erythronium carolinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
