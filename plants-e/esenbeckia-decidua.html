Esenbeckia decidua

Esenbeckia decidua, commonly known as the Brazilian peppercorn, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, peppercorn-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
