Erigeron litoralis var. luxurians

Erigeron litoralis var. luxurians, commonly known as salt marsh fleabane, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of North America.

It thrives in saline or brackish wetlands, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and fluctuating water levels.

This variety is distinguished by its dense flowering and robust growth, making it a characteristic component of coastal vegetation.
