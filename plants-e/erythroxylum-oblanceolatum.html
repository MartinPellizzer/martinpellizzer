Erythroxylum oblanceolatum

Erythroxylum oblanceolatum, commonly known as the pinkleaf copal, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive pinkish or reddish hue on the underside. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.

This species is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
