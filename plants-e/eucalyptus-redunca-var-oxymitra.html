Eucalyptus redunca var. oxymitra

Eucalyptus redunca var. oxymitra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant produces woody capsules as fruit, containing several seeds.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
