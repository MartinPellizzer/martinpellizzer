Elymus neogaeus

Elymus neogaeus, commonly known as the narrowleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Elymus neogaeus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
