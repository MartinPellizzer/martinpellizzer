Epidendrum laurelense

Epidendrum laurelense, commonly known as the Laurel Epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forests or along stream banks. The plant features long, arching stems with numerous lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, with showy, colorful blooms that range from white to pink and purple.

Epidendrum laurelense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
