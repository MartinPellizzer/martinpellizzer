Eupatorium purpureum var. album

Eupatorium purpureum var. album, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
