Ebnerella denudata

Ebnerella denudata, commonly known as the naked stem orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often showy flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant has a slender, often leafless stem, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose inflorescence and are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms.

Ebnerella denudata is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological niche.
