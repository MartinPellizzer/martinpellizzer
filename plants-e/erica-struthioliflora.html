Erica struthioliflora

Erica struthioliflora, commonly known as the silver-leaved heath, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring and early summer.

Erica struthioliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
