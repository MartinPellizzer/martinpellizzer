Echinospartum ibericum

Echinospartum ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in Mediterranean-type climates.
