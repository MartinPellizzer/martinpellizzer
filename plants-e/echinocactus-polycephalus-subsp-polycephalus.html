Echinocactus polycephalus subsp. polycephalus

Echinocactus polycephalus subsp.

polycephalus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of bright yellow spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
