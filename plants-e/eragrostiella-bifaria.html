Eragrostiella bifaria

Eragrostiella bifaria, commonly known as the two-lobed lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib, and its flowers are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

Eragrostiella bifaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
