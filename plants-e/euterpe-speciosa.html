Euterpe speciosa

Euterpe speciosa, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or buriti palm, is a tall, slender palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-orange fruits that are edible. The plant thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found near rivers and floodplains. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used by indigenous communities for centuries.

The plant's resilience and adaptability make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
