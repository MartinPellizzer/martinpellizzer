Echinopsis celsiana

Echinopsis celsiana, commonly known as the Celsian night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stems and dense covering of spines. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a shallow root system and thick, water-storing stems.

Echinopsis celsiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering behavior.
