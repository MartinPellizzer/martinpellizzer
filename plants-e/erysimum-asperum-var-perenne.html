Erysimum asperum var. perenne

Erysimum asperum var. perenne, commonly known as the hoary whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are often followed by narrow, elongated seed pods. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
