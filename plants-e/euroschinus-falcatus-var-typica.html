Euroschinus falcatus var. typica

Euroschinus falcatus var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the family Myrtaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of northeastern Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other tropical regions.
