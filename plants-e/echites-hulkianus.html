Echites hulkianus

Echites hulkianus, commonly known as the giant hulkian spiderwort, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forest understories.

Its unique morphology and vibrant blooms make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
