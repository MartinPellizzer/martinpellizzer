Echinochloa phyllopogon subsp. oryzicola

Echinochloa phyllopogon subsp.

oryzicola, commonly known as rice grass or wild rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and rice paddies, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by water, contributing to its wide distribution.

This species is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and ecological restoration projects.
