Eritrichium hispidum var. leiocarpum

Eritrichium hispidum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the soft-hairy eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers with five white petals. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
