Echinops pathanorum

Echinops pathanorum, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its genus name "Echinops," meaning "spiny head.". The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by the distinctive seed heads that persist through the winter. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Echinops pathanorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
