Eucalyptus × purpurascens var. petiolaris

Eucalyptus × purpurascens var. petiolaris, commonly known as the purple malletwood, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple or pink flower buds that are often more showy than the actual flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly rough upper surface. This plant prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open forests or woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
