Eupatorium pharcidodes var. pentanthum

Eupatorium pharcidodes var. pentanthum, commonly known as purple boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
