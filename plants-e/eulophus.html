Eulophus

Eulophus, commonly known as the European ash, is a deciduous tree belonging to the family Oleaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits called samaras. Eulophus is native to Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for furniture and cabinetry.
