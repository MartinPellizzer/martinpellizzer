Escallonia hispida

Escallonia hispida, commonly known as the rough escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are red and edible, often used in culinary applications.

Escallonia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
