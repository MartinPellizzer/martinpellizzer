Euonymus tsoi subsp. brevipes

Euonymus tsoi subsp.

brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed boxwood, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit.

The leaves are typically oval to elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
