Echinops niveus

Echinops niveus, commonly known as the white thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are covered in white bracts. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by the distinctive white, woolly seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, rocky habitats.

Echinops niveus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
