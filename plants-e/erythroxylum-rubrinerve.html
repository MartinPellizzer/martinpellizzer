Erythroxylum rubrinerve

Erythroxylum rubrinerve, commonly known as the red-veined erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its distinctive red veins running through its broad, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
