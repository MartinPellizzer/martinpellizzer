Eugenioides jucundum

Eugenioides jucundum, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
