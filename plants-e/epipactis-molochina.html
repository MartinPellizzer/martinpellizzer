Epipactis molochina

Epipactis molochina, commonly known as the Moloch orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rosette of linear, grass-like leaves that form a basal clump. The plant produces a tall flowering stem bearing several showy, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers with intricate patterns. Its flowers are typically pollinated by specific insects, such as flies and beetles, due to their unique coloration and scent.

Epipactis molochina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
