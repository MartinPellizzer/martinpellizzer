Erysimum aureum

Erysimum aureum, commonly known as the golden wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
