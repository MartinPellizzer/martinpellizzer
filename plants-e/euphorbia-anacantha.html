Euphorbia anacantha

Euphorbia anacantha, commonly known as the spiny euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, spiny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Euphorbia anacantha is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
