Euonymus japonicus var. longifolius

Euonymus japonicus var. longifolius, commonly known as the Japanese spindletree, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, inflated fruits that split to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its foliage provides seasonal color changes, adding visual interest to gardens throughout the year.
