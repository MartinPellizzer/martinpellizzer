Eupatorium amygdalinum var. dissitiflorum

Eupatorium amygdalinum var. dissitiflorum, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy inflorescences and aromatic foliage.
