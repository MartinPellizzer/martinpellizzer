Evolvulus boninensis

Evolvulus boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, hairy leaves and has a preference for coastal and sandy habitats. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, and it blooms throughout the growing season.

Evolvulus boninensis is considered a rare and endemic species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
