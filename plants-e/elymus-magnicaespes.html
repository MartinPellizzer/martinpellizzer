Elymus magnicaespes

Elymus magnicaespes, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and dense, grass-like foliage that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems, and its flowers are typically small and wind-pollinated. Elymus magnicaespes is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage crop for livestock.
