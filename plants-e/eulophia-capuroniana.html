Eulophia capuroniana

Eulophia capuroniana, commonly known as the Capuron's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often near streams or in undergrowth. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with purple or brown markings, and it produces a single spike of blooms.

Eulophia capuroniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
