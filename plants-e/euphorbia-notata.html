Euphorbia notata

Euphorbia notata, commonly known as the yellow-spined spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a distinctive yellow-orange spine-tipped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to skin. Euphorbia notata typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
