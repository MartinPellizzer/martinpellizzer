Encelia montana

Encelia montana, commonly known as the California bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces striking blue or violet tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland chaparral habitats.

Encelia montana is adapted to dry climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
