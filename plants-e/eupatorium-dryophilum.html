Eupatorium dryophilum

Eupatorium dryophilum, commonly known as the dry soil eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually pink to purple in color and attract various pollinators.

Eupatorium dryophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
