Euphorbia cuneata var. spinescens

Euphorbia cuneata var. spinescens, commonly known as the spiny euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the southern United States.

It features a branched, spiny stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered with small spines. It produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
