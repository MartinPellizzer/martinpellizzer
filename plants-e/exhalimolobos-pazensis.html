Exhalimolobos pazensis

Exhalimolobos pazensis, commonly known as the Pazentis alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Ecuador.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the cold and arid conditions of its habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
