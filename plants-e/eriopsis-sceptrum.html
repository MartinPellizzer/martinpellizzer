Eriopsis sceptrum

Eriopsis sceptrum, commonly known as the desert star or false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with silvery-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in spring, and the plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eriopsis sceptrum is adapted to survive in poor soils and is often found in disturbed or sandy habitats.
