Eugenia lancifolia

Eugenia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves with a pointed tip and a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible, aromatic fruits similar to guavas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and medicinal properties.
