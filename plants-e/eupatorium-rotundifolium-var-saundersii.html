Eupatorium rotundifolium var. saundersii

Eupatorium rotundifolium var. saundersii, commonly known as the Saunders' boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are hairy and often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are rounded and have a distinctive rough texture, with serrated edges.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
