Euphorbia coghlanii

Euphorbia coghlanii, commonly known as the Coghlan's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.

Euphorbia coghlanii is also known for its ability to store water in its stems, making it well adapted to harsh, dry climates.
