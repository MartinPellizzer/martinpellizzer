Eremurus spectabilis f. inderiensis

Eremurus spectabilis f. inderiensis, commonly known as the Kashmiri sparrowgrass, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear numerous yellowish-green to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has fibrous roots and a rosette of basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of hardy plants.
