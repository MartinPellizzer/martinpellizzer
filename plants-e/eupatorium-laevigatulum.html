Eupatorium laevigatulum

Eupatorium laevigatulum, commonly known as smooth eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a smooth texture.

Eupatorium laevigatulum is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
