Excoecaria robusta

Excoecaria robusta, commonly known as the croton or Indian rubber plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that vary in color from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Excoecaria robusta is also noted for its ability to tolerate low light conditions and moderate watering.
