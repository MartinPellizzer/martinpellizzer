Elaeagnus retrostyla

Elaeagnus retrostyla, commonly known as the silverleaf elaeagnus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of climates.

Elaeagnus retrostyla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
