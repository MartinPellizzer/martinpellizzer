Eugenia laruotteana

Eugenia laruotteana, commonly known as the Laruottea myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, edible seeds.

Eugenia laruotteana is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
