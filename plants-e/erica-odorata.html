Erica odorata

Erica odorata, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Erica odorata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
