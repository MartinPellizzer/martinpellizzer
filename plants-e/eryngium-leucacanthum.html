Eryngium leucacanthum

Eryngium leucacanthum, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by white, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium leucacanthum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
