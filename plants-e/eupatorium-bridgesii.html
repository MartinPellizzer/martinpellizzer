Eupatorium bridgesii

Eupatorium bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridgewater eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium bridgesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
