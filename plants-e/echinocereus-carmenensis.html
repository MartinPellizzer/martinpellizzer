Echinocereus carmenensis

Echinocereus carmenensis, commonly known as Carmen's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is a small, columnar cactus that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features spiny, ribbed stems and produces yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
