Erica abietina subsp. aurantiaca

Erica abietina subsp.

aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden heath, is a dwarf shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bright orange-red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
