Epidendrum × spathiporphyreum

Epidendrum × spathiporphyreum, commonly known as the Purple Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Epidendrum spathaceum and Epidendrum porphyreum.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical environments. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces striking, violet to purple flowers in dense spikes. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a branching pattern with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental cultivation and botanical study.
