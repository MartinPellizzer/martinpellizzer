Eupatorium betonicum var. subintegrum

Eupatorium betonicum var. subintegrum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in herbal medicine.
