Elaeocarpus amabilis

Elaeocarpus amabilis, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, round fruits with a hard, woody shell enclosing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
