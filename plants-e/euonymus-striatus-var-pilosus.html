Euonymus striatus var. pilosus

Euonymus striatus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing addition to gardens.
