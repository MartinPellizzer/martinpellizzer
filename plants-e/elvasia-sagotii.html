Elvasia sagotii

Elvasia sagotii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or coastal landscapes. Its unique appearance and ecological importance have made it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.

However, due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in many parts of its natural range.
