Eulophia stewartiae

Eulophia stewartiae, commonly known as Stewart's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering structure. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme, and are characterized by their intricate, spurred petals.

Eulophia stewartiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
