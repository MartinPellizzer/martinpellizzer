Eriochloa sundaica

Eriochloa sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas.

Eriochloa sundaica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
