Eriosema volubile

Eriosema volubile, commonly known as the sticky vine or velvet vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
