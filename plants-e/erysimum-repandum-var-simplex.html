Erysimum repandum var. simplex

Erysimum repandum var. simplex, commonly known as the common wallflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
