Echinopsis densispina f. setosa

Echinopsis densispina f. setosa, commonly known as the woolly night bloomer, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, white spines that give it a woolly appearance, and its stems are typically short and cylindrical. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, usually in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
