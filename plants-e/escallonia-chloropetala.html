Escallonia chloropetala

Escallonia chloropetala, commonly known as the green-leafed escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural settings.
