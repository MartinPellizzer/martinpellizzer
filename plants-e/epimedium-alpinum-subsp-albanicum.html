Epimedium alpinum subsp. albanicum

Epimedium alpinum subsp.

albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian barrenwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage consisting of leathery, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in panicle-like inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
