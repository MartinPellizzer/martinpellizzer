Euphorbia jaliscensis

Euphorbia jaliscensis, commonly known as the Jalisco euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
