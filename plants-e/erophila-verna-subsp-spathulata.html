Erophila verna subsp. spathulata

Erophila verna subsp.

spathulata, commonly known as the early spring bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or violet, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
