Echinochloa polystachya var. spectabilis

Echinochloa polystachya var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and striking, spike-like inflorescences that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced during the growing season.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
