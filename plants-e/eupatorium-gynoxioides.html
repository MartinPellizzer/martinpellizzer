Eupatorium gynoxioides

Eupatorium gynoxioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late summer.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
