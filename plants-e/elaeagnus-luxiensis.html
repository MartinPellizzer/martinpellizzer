Elaeagnus luxiensis

Elaeagnus luxiensis, commonly known as the goldenflower buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or disturbed areas.

Elaeagnus luxiensis has potential ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
