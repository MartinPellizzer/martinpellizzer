Epidendrum serruliferum

Epidendrum serruliferum, commonly known as the serrated leaf epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that are often serrated along the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with clusters of fragrant, violet or white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum serruliferum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
