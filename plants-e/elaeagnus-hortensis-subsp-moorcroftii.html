Elaeagnus hortensis subsp. moorcroftii

Elaeagnus hortensis subsp.

moorcroftii, commonly known as the silverberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its silvery, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
