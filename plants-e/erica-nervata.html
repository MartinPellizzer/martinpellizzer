Erica nervata

Erica nervata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica nervata typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
