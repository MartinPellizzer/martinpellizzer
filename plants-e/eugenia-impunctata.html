Eugenia impunctata

Eugenia impunctata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, cherry-like fruits. The fruits are edible, often consumed raw or used in jams and beverages, and are a key component of the Brazilian diet.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
