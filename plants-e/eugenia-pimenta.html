Eugenia pimenta

Eugenia pimenta, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a spicy or peppery flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
