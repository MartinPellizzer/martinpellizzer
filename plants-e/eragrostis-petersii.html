Eragrostis petersii

Eragrostis petersii, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are typically narrow, smooth, and have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in erosion control.
