Eupatorium callilepis

Eupatorium callilepis, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported fever-reducing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
