Euphorbia phylloclada

Euphorbia phylloclada, commonly known as the leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often mistaken for leaves, a feature known as phylloclades. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, surrounded by a cup-shaped structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
