Eupatorium spiraeifolium

Eupatorium spiraeifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
