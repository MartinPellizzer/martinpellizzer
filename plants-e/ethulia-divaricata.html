Ethulia divaricata

Ethulia divaricata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

Ethulia divaricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
