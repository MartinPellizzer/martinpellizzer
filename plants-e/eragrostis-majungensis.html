Eragrostis majungensis

Eragrostis majungensis, commonly known as the Majungensis lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and grasslands.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated as a low-maintenance ornamental plant.
