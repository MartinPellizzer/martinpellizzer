Erigeron × stanleyi

Erigeron × stanleyi, commonly known as the Stanley's fleabane, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically range from white to pale blue in color. The plant produces ray florets that are usually sterile, while the central disk florets are fertile and produce small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Erigeron × stanleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
