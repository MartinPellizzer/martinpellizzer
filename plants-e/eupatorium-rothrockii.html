Eupatorium rothrockii

Eupatorium rothrockii, commonly known as Rothrock's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its flowering period occurs in the late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators.
