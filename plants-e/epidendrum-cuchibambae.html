Epidendrum cuchibambae

Epidendrum cuchibambae, commonly known as the Cuchibamba orchid, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting purple or brown lip. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, and the plant thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Epidendrum cuchibambae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
