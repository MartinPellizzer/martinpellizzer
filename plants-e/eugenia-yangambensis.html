Eugenia yangambensis

Eugenia yangambensis, commonly known as the Yangambi plum, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when mature, resembling plums in appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forest growth.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
