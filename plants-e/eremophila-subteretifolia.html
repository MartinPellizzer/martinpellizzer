Eremophila subteretifolia

Eremophila subteretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, often pink or red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila subteretifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
