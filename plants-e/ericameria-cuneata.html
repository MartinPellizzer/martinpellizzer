Ericameria cuneata

Ericameria cuneata, commonly known as narrowleaf ericameria, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush ecosystems.

Ericameria cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
