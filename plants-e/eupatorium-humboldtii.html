Eupatorium humboldtii

Eupatorium humboldtii, commonly known as Humboldt's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clustered umbels, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Eupatorium humboldtii is typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas.
