Eulophia tenuiscapa

Eulophia tenuiscapa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia tenuiscapa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
