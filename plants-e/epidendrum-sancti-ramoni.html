Epidendrum sancti-ramoni

Epidendrum sancti-ramoni, commonly known as the Saint-Ramon orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The flowers often display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.
