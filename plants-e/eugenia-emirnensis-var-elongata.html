Eugenia emirnensis var. elongata

Eugenia emirnensis var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated eugenia, is a species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark purple when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities.
