Echinochloa pungens var. coarctata

Echinochloa pungens var. coarctata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in regions with a semi-arid climate.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
