Esenbeckia pilocarpoides

Esenbeckia pilocarpoides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
