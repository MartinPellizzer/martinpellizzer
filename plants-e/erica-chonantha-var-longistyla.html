Erica chonantha var. longistyla

Erica chonantha var. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. Its unique floral structure features elongated stamens and a distinctive, showy appearance that attracts pollinators.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
