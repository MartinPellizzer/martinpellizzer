Echinocactus horripilus

Echinocactus horripilus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and slow growth rate make it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
