Eucalyptus incurva

Eucalyptus incurva, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Eucalyptus incurva produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale green or yellowish in color.

It is an important component of the ecosystems in which it is found, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
