Erica tenuibractea

Erica tenuibractea, commonly known as the narrow-bract heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Erica tenuibractea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, compact shrubs.
