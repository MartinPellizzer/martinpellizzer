Eleocharis gonzaleziae

Eleocharis gonzaleziae, commonly known as the Gonzalez's spike rush, is a species of sedges native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in nutrient-poor soils. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, which is common among sedges.

The species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
