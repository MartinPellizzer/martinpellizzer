Erophila verna var. decipiens

Erophila verna var. decipiens, commonly known as the false penny-cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
