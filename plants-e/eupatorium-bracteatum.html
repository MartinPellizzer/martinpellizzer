Eupatorium bracteatum

Eupatorium bracteatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium bracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
