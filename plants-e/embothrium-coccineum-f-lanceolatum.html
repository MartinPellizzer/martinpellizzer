Embothrium coccineum f. lanceolatum

Embothrium coccineum f. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate firetree, is a variant of the firetree species native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its striking red flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions. Its botanical features include compound leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
