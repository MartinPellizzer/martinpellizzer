Erica chlamydiflora

Erica chlamydiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica chlamydiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
