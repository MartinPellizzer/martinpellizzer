Erinacea anthyllis subsp. anthyllis

Erinacea anthyllis subsp.

anthyllis, commonly known as the creeping eryngo, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, mat-forming habit, featuring silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Erinacea anthyllis subsp.

anthyllis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in dry garden landscapes.
