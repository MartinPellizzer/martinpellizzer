Eriosyce curvispina subsp. curvispina

Eriosyce curvispina subsp.

curvispina, commonly known as the curved-spined eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are often covered in a layer of white, woolly hair.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
