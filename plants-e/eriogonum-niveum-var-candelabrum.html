Eriogonum niveum var. candelabrum

Eriogonum niveum var. candelabrum, commonly known as the snowbrush or candlestick eriogonum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its distinctive branched inflorescence, which resembles a candlestick, giving it its common name.
