Eucalyptus falcata

Eucalyptus falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia, particularly found in the states of New South Wales and Victoria. This plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Eucalyptus falcata is a shrub or small tree that often grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
