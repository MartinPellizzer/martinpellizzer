Erica octonaria

Erica octonaria, commonly known as the eight-branched heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

Erica octonaria is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
