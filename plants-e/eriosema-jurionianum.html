Eriosema jurionianum

Eriosema jurionianum, commonly known as the mountain bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in racemose inflorescences, and bloom during the cooler months.

Eriosema jurionianum is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
