Erica lutea

Erica lutea, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica lutea is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it forms dense shrubs.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
