Ephedra polygonoides

Ephedra polygonoides, commonly known as the Chinese ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of China and Mongolia.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It contains ephedrine, a compound historically used in traditional medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties and potential for misuse, it is regulated in many countries.
