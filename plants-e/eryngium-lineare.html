Eryngium lineare

Eryngium lineare, commonly known as narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Eryngium lineare is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
