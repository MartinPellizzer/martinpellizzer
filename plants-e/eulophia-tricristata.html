Eulophia tricristata

Eulophia tricristata, commonly known as the three-ridged eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a single, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with clustered, showy flowers that have three prominent ridges on the lip. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a contrasting purple or brown lip.

Eulophia tricristata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
