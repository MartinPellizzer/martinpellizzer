Eugenia angustisepala

Eugenia angustisepala, commonly known as the narrow-lobed eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
