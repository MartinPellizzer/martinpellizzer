Elegia asperiflora var. lacerata

Elegia asperiflora var. lacerata, commonly known as the rough-leaved elegia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique foliage and compact growth habit make it a desirable ornamental plant in arid environments.
