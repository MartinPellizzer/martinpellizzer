Erica solandra var. mollis

Erica solandra var. mollis, commonly known as the soft erica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and requires full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature, it is suitable for coastal gardens and can withstand light frost.
