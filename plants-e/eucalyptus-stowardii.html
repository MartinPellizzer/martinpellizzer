Eucalyptus stowardii

Eucalyptus stowardii, commonly known as the Stoward's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in mallee woodlands.

Eucalyptus stowardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
