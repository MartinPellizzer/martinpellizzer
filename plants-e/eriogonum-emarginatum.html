Eriogonum emarginatum

Eriogonum emarginatum, commonly known as the fringed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are often lobed or notched, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush communities.

Eriogonum emarginatum is a hardy species that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
