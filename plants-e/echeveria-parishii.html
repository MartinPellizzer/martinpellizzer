Echeveria parishii

Echeveria parishii, commonly known as the Mexican snowball, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Echeveria parishii is popular among gardeners for its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
