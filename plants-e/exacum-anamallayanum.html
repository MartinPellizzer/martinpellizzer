Exacum anamallayanum

Exacum anamallayanum, commonly known as the Malabar violet, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Exacum anamallayanum is valued both for its beauty and its traditional uses in local medicine.
