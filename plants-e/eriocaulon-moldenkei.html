Eriocaulon moldenkei

Eriocaulon moldenkei, commonly known as the dwarf rice sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and flooded soils, where it thrives in consistently moist conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in its habitat.

Eriocaulon moldenkei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
