Eupatorium vernosissimum

Eupatorium vernosissimum, commonly known as early eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
