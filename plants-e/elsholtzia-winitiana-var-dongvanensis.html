Elsholtzia winitiana var. dongvanensis

Elsholtzia winitiana var. dongvanensis, commonly known as the Dongvan mountain hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and it blooms during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
