Epilobium canescens var. glabrescens

Epilobium canescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
