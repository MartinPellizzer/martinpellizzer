Erinus laciniatus

Erinus laciniatus, commonly known as the laceleaf erinus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erinus laciniatus is valued for its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period.
