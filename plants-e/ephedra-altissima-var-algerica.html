Ephedra altissima var. algerica

Ephedra altissima var. algerica, commonly known as the Algerian ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in regions with low precipitation.

This variety is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in stabilizing desert landscapes.
