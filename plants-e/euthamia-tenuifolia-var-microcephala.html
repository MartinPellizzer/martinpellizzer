Euthamia tenuifolia var. microcephala

Euthamia tenuifolia var. microcephala, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. It is often found in the western United States and parts of Canada.

Its compact growth habit and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
