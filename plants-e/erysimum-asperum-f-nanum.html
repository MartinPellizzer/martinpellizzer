Erysimum asperum f. nanum

Erysimum asperum f. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a compact, low-growing plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
