Eryngium glomeratum

Eryngium glomeratum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by silver-gray bracts. The plant has upright, branching stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Eryngium glomeratum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
