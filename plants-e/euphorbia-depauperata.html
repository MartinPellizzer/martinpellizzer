Euphorbia depauperata

Euphorbia depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Euphorbia depauperata is also known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.
