Elaeagnus multiflora f. jucundicocca

Elaeagnus multiflora f. jucundicocca, commonly known as the sweetleaf dogwood, is a cultivar of the Elaeagnus multiflora species.

It is characterized by its silvery-white foliage, which is covered in dense, fine hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, edible fruits. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This variety is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
