Eugenia dibrachiata

Eugenia dibrachiata, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red fruits with a thick, white rind and juicy, sweet-tart pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The botanical features of Eugenia dibrachiata make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
