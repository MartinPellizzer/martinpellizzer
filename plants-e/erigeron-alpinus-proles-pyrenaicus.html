Erigeron alpinus proles pyrenaicus

Erigeron alpinus proles pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at higher elevations with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white to pale blue in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
