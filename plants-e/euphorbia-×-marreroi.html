Euphorbia × marreroi

Euphorbia × marreroi, commonly known as the hybrid euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, surrounded by colorful bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
