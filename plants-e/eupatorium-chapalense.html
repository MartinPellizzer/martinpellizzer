Eupatorium chapalense

Eupatorium chapalense, commonly known as the Mexican thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a strong, often unpleasant odor.

Eupatorium chapalense produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
