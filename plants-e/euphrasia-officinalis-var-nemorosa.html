Euphrasia officinalis var. nemorosa

Euphrasia officinalis var. nemorosa, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veined pattern and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is characterized by its erect stems and is often found growing in clusters.

Botanically, it belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
