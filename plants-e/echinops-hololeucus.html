Echinops hololeucus

Echinops hololeucus, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are covered in white, silky bracts. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom during the summer months.

Echinops hololeucus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
