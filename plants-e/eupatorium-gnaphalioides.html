Eupatorium gnaphalioides

Eupatorium gnaphalioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flowers that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Eupatorium gnaphalioides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
