Elaeagnus umbellata subsp. magna

Elaeagnus umbellata subsp.

magna, commonly known as silverberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery, hirsute leaves and small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in the fall.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces fragrant flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
