Eriogonum arachnoideum

Eriogonum arachnoideum, commonly known as the spider-scale buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that resembles spider silk, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Eriogonum arachnoideum is a perennial shrub that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
