Erechtites valerianifolius f. prenanthoides

Erechtites valerianifolius f. prenanthoides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that resemble small cones, surrounded by narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is native to the eastern United States and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
