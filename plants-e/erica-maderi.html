Erica maderi

Erica maderi, commonly known as the Iberian heather, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other heath and heather plants. The plant features small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Erica maderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
