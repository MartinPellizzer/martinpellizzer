Epacris transparens

Epacris transparens, commonly known as the transparent epacris, is an evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
