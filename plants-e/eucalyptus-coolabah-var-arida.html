Eucalyptus coolabah var. arida

Eucalyptus coolabah var. arida, commonly known as the red box, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped leaves which are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pink flowers, which appear during the winter months. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.

The species is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have various industrial and medicinal uses.
