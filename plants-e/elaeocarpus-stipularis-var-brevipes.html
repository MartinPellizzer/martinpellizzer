Elaeocarpus stipularis var. brevipes

Elaeocarpus stipularis var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed elaeocarpus, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruiting characteristics.
