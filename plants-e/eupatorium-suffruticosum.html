Eupatorium suffruticosum

Eupatorium suffruticosum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
