Euphorbia francoisii var. crassicaulis

Euphorbia francoisii var. crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are often reduced to small spines, which help reduce water loss.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
