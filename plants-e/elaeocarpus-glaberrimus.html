Elaeocarpus glaberrimus

Elaeocarpus glaberrimus, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and dull underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
