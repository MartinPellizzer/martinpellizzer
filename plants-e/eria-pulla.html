Eria pulla

Eria pulla, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their vibrant color and ornamental value.

Eria pulla requires specific care, including high humidity and well-draining soil, to thrive.
