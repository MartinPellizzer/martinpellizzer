Eucalyptus gunnii var. glauca

Eucalyptus gunnii var. glauca, commonly known as the silvergunnii, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-blue bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It is a hardy species that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional and modern applications.
