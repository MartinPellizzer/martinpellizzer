Echinops dissectus

Echinops dissectus, commonly known as the Persian crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are prominent in late summer and autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by the development of its distinctive, thistle-like bracts. Echinops dissectus is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and xeriscaping.
