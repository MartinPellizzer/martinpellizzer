Eugenia perparvifolia

Eugenia perparvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

Eugenia perparvifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
