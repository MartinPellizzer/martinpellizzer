Echinocactus exsculptus var. tenuispinus

Echinocactus exsculptus var. tenuispinus, commonly known as the narrow-spined hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its distinctive, narrow spines that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
