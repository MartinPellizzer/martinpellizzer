Erigeron vagus var. madsenii

Erigeron vagus var. madsenii, commonly known as the Madsen's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small blue to purple flowers in the summer.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a component of native plant communities.
