Eugenia eucaudata

Eugenia eucaudata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically reddish-brown when mature. These fruits contain a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody shell.

Eugenia eucaudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
