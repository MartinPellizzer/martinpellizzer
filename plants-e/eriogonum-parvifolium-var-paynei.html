Eriogonum parvifolium var. paynei

Eriogonum parvifolium var. paynei, commonly known as Payne's buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are clustered in dense, spikelike inflorescences. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in arid environments.
