Erica trachysantha

Erica trachysantha, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica trachysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
