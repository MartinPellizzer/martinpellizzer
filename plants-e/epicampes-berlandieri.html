Epicampes berlandieri

Epicampes berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that mature into white, fluffy seeds, similar to those of the common dandelion. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
