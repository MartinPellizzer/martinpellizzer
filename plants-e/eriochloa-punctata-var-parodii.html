Eriochloa punctata var. parodii

Eriochloa punctata var. parodii, commonly known as the Parodii false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear dense clusters of spikelets. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
