Eucalyptus benthamii

Eucalyptus benthamii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by woody capsules containing seeds. Eucalyptus benthamii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as thick, waxy leaves.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
