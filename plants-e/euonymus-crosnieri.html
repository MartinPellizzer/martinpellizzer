Euonymus crosnieri

Euonymus crosnieri, commonly known as the Corsican spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. Euonymus crosnieri is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
