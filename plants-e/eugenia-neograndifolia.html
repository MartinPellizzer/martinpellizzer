Eugenia neograndifolia

Eugenia neograndifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white eugenia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia neograndifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
