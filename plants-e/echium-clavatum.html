Echium clavatum

Echium clavatum, commonly known as the purpleweed or clustered evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of striking violet or blue-purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Echium clavatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
