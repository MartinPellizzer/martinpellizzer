Escobedia parvifolia

Escobedia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved escobedia, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth form and fleshy, spiny stems. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

Escobedia parvifolia is often found growing on rocky soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged periods of drought.
