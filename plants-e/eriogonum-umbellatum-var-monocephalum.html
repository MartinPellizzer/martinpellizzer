Eriogonum umbellatum var. monocephalum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. monocephalum, commonly known as the single-head buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flower heads. The plant typically produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and disturbed sites.

This variety is distinguished by its single, dense flower cluster at the end of each stem, giving it its common name.
