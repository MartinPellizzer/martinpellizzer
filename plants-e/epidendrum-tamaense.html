Epidendrum tamaense

Epidendrum tamaense, commonly known as the Tamaean orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying environmental conditions.

Epidendrum tamaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
