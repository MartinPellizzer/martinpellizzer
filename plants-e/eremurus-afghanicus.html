Eremurus afghanicus

Eremurus afghanicus, commonly known as the Afghan goat's beard, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the genus Eremurus, which is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae. The plant is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that resemble a beard, hence its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils at high altitudes.

Eremurus afghanicus is a perennial plant that produces large, showy yellow or orange flowers, making it a notable ornamental species.
