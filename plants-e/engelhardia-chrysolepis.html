Engelhardia chrysolepis

Engelhardia chrysolepis, commonly known as the golden-leaved engelhardia, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts vividly with its dark green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Engelhardia chrysolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
