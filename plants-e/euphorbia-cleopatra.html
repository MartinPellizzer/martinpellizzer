Euphorbia cleopatra

Euphorbia cleopatra, commonly known as Cleopatra's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, which is a common feature among species in the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable plant in its native habitat.
