Echium caudatum

Echium caudatum, commonly known as the viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Echium caudatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
