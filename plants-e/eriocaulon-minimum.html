Eriocaulon minimum

Eriocaulon minimum, commonly known as the small haircap moss, is a small, creeping moss that typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

It has a dense, cushion-like growth form with small, ovate leaves that are usually pale green to reddish in color. The leaves are covered with a fine layer of hair-like structures, giving the plant its characteristic appearance. It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in other damp, shaded areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
