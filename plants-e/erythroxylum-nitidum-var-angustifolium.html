Erythroxylum nitidum var. angustifolium

Erythroxylum nitidum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved copal tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for various purposes, including incense and medicinal applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
