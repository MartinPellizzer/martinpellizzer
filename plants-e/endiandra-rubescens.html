Endiandra rubescens

Endiandra rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered endiandra, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
