Eupatorium pomaderrifolium

Eupatorium pomaderrifolium, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium pomaderrifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
