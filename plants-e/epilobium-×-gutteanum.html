Epilobium × gutteanum

Epilobium × gutteanum, commonly known as the spotted willow herb, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, often disturbed habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and roadside ditches.

Epilobium × gutteanum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
