Epidendrum suturatum

Epidendrum suturatum, commonly known as the Suturatum Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, branching stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinct lip, and it produces fleshy, greenish-brown pseudobulbs that store water.

Epidendrum suturatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
