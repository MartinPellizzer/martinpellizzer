Euphorbia muirii

Euphorbia muirii, commonly known as the Muir's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
