Elytrigia amgunensis

Elytrigia amgunensis, commonly known as the Amgun grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Elytrigia amgunensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid regions and is of interest for ecological and agricultural research.
