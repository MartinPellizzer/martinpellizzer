Elymus secalinus

Elymus secalinus, commonly known as goatgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields.

Elymus secalinus is also cultivated as a forage crop in some agricultural regions.
