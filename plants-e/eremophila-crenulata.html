Eremophila crenulata

Eremophila crenulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its grey-green, narrow leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry climates. It often grows in sandy or rocky soils and is commonly found in coastal areas.

Eremophila crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting native wildlife.
