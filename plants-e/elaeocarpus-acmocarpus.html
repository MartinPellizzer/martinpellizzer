Elaeocarpus acmocarpus

Elaeocarpus acmocarpus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers followed by spherical, red or purple fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy texture.

The tree is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
