Echium pallidum

Echium pallidum, commonly known as the pale evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of pale violet to white flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Echium pallidum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
