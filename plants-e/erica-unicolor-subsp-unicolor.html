Erica unicolor subsp. unicolor

Erica unicolor subsp.

unicolor, commonly known as the single-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces solitary, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Erica unicolor subsp.

unicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and unique floral display.
