Elaeocarpus obtusus subsp. apiculatus

Elaeocarpus obtusus subsp.

apiculatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarpus, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests.

The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a woody, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
