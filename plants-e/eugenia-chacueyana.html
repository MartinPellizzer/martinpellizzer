Eugenia chacueyana

Eugenia chacueyana, commonly known as the Chacueyana myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia chacueyana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
