Euphorbia portlandica

Euphorbia portlandica, commonly known as the Portland spurge, is a succulent plant native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has a distinctive milky sap that can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Euphorbia portlandica is often found in coastal and sandy environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
