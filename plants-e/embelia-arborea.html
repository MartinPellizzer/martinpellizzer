Embelia arborea

Embelia arborea, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
