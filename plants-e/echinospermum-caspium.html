Echinospermum caspium

Echinospermum caspium, commonly known as the Caspian echinops, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed head that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to dry climates. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, blooming in the summer months, followed by the development of its unique, spiky fruit.
