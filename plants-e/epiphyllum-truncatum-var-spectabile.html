Epiphyllum truncatum var. spectabile

Epiphyllum truncatum var. spectabile, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a striking epiphytic cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that grow horizontally, often resembling leaves, and its ability to store water efficiently. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. Its blooming period is usually in the summer months, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
