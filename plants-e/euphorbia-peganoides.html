Euphorbia peganoides

Euphorbia peganoides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, green, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.

Euphorbia peganoides thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
