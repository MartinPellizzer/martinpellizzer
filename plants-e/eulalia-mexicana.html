Eulalia mexicana

Eulalia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps of silver-green foliage and delicate, feathery seed heads that sway gracefully in the wind. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to provide seasonal interest with its soft, plume-like inflorescences.

Eulalia mexicana is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
