Epiphyllum phyllanthus subsp. rubrocoronatum

Epiphyllum phyllanthus subsp.

rubrocoronatum, commonly known as the red-capped night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family. It is a climbing cactus that produces large, showy flowers that open at night.

The plant is native to Mexico and has a stem that is often covered in sharp, hooked spines. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive red or orange crown.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
