Escallonia chlorophylla

Escallonia chlorophylla, commonly known as the green escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that remains lush throughout the year. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Escallonia chlorophylla thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
