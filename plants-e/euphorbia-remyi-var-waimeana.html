Euphorbia remyi var. waimeana

Euphorbia remyi var. waimeana, commonly known as the Waimea euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and coastal environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
