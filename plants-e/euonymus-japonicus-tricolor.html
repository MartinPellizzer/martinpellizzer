Euonymus japonicus tricolor

Euonymus japonicus tricolor, commonly known as the tri-color euonymus, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features three distinct colors on the same leaf: green, white, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, and the plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

Euonymus japonicus tricolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
