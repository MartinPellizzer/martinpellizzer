Eugenia mooniana var. gracilis

Eugenia mooniana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but often have a mild, sweet taste. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
