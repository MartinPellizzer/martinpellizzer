Eucalyptus conglobata

Eucalyptus conglobata, commonly known as the red box, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and pendulous, globular clusters of pale yellow flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry climates.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have various industrial and medicinal applications.
