Epiphyllum pumilum

Epiphyllum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing cactus that originates from Central and South America. The plant has flat, leaf-like stems that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, often in shades of white or pale pink.

Despite its delicate appearance, it is relatively easy to care for in a suitable environment.
