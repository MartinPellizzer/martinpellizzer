Erechtites argutus var. obovatus

Erechtites argutus var. obovatus, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with rough, hairy foliage. The leaves are broad, ovate to obovate in shape, with a serrated margin and a velvety texture. Its showy, purple-to-red flower heads appear in late summer, forming clusters at the top of the plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
