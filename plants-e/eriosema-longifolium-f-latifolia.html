Eriosema longifolium f. latifolia

Eriosema longifolium f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved eriosema, is a variant of the species Eriosema longifolium, characterized by its broader leaves compared to the typical form.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous traits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. Its foliage is generally hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.
