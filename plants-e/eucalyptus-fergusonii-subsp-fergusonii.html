Eucalyptus fergusonii subsp. fergusonii

Eucalyptus fergusonii subsp.

fergusonii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The flowers are usually pale or creamy white, arranged in clusters, and appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage and resilience.
