Erigeron serotinus

Erigeron serotinus, commonly known as late blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, appearing in late summer to early fall.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
