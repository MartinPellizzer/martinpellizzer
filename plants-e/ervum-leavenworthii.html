Ervum leavenworthii

Ervum leavenworthii, commonly known as the Leavenworth pea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes characterized by their pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy pod and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Ervum leavenworthii is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
