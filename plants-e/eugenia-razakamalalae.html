Eugenia razakamalalae

Eugenia razakamalalae, commonly known as the Madagascar nutmeg, is a small evergreen tree native to the eastern rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive red or purple berries that contain a single seed, and it is closely related to other Eugenia species found in tropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
