Elymus multiflorus subsp. kingianus

Elymus multiflorus subsp.

kingianus, commonly known as King's wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a prominent midrib.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
