Elymus thoroldianus var. laxiusculus

Elymus thoroldianus var. laxiusculus, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and forage in arid environments.
