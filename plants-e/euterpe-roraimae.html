Euterpe roraimae

Euterpe roraimae, commonly known as the Roraima palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to those of other palm species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in swampy or inundated areas.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
