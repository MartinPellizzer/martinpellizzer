Eucalyptus guilfoylei

Eucalyptus guilfoylei, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that ranges from grey to brown in color. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically pale yellow to cream in color.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
