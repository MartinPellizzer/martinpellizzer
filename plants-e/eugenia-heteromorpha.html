Eugenia heteromorpha

Eugenia heteromorpha, commonly known as the variable guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. Its fruit, which is typically edible, varies in size and shape, contributing to its common name.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
