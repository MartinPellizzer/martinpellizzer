Etoxoe

Etoxoe, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

Etoxoe thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
