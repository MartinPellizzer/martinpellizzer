Eugenia bakeri

Eugenia bakeri, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant is known for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season when its flowers bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Eugenia bakeri is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
