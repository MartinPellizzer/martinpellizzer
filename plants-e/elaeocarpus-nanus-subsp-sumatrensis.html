Elaeocarpus nanus subsp. sumatrensis

Elaeocarpus nanus subsp.

sumatrensis, commonly known as the dwarf elaeocarpus, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that enclose a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its ornamental value and potential ecological importance make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
