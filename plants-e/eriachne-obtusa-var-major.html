Eriachne obtusa var. major

Eriachne obtusa var. major, commonly known as the broad-leafed grass or broad-leafed eriochloa, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
