Erysimum × mailhoi

Erysimum × mailhoi, commonly known as the winter flowering wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, rounded pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its early blooming and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

Erysimum × mailhoi prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
