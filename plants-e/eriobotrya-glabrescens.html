Eriobotrya glabrescens

Eriobotrya glabrescens, commonly known as the loquat, is a deciduous small tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are typically harvested in late winter to early spring. The flowers are white or pink, with five petals, and are followed by the fruit after a period of pollination and fruit development.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
