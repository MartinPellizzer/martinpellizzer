Eupatorium discolor

Eupatorium discolor, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies.

Eupatorium discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
