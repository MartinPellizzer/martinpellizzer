Erianthus griffithii

Erianthus griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's bluestem, is a tall, perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and panicle-like inflorescence that blooms in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its fibrous roots contribute to soil stabilization, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.

Erianthus griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for use in erosion control.
