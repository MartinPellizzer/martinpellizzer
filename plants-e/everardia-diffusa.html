Everardia diffusa

Everardia diffusa, commonly known as the silky everardia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Everardia diffusa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
