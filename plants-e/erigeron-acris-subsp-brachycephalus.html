Erigeron acris subsp. brachycephalus

Erigeron acris subsp.

brachycephalus, commonly known as the short-headed blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit.

The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
