Eucalyptus tricarpa

Eucalyptus tricarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which appear in clusters. Eucalyptus tricarpa is a hardy shrub or small tree that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
