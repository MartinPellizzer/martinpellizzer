Echiochilon cyananthum

Echiochilon cyananthum, commonly known as the blue-flowered grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique floral coloration and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
