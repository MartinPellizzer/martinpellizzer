Eugenia flavescens var. parvifolia

Eugenia flavescens var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, subacid fruits that are typically small and round, with a thin, waxy skin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
