Eria pachystachya

Eria pachystachya, commonly known as the giant orchid or large-leaf orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, helping to retain moisture in its native habitat. Eria pachystachya is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
