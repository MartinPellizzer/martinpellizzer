Equisetum telmateia var. hillii

Equisetum telmateia var. hillii, commonly known as the hillii variety of the scouring rush, is a non-vascular plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinct, jointed stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its reproductive structures, called strobili, are cone-like and contain spores.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
