Eukrania suecica

Eukrania suecica, commonly known as the Swedish cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern Europe.

It features small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by bright red berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and bogs.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
