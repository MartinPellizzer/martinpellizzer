Euclea natalensis subsp. capensis

Euclea natalensis subsp.

capensis, commonly known as the Natal euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the Eastern Cape and KwaZulu-Natal regions.

The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
