Epidendrum virgatum

Epidendrum virgatum, commonly known as the Virgin's Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their beauty and longevity.
