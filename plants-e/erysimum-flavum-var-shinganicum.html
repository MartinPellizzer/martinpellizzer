Erysimum flavum var. shinganicum

Erysimum flavum var. shinganicum, commonly known as the Shingana wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
