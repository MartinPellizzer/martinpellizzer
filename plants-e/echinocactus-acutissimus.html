Echinocactus acutissimus

Echinocactus acutissimus, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
