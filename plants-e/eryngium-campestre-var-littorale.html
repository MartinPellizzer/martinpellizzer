Eryngium campestre var. littorale

Eryngium campestre var. littorale, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and silver-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in salt marshes and dunes. Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and ecological restoration projects.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
