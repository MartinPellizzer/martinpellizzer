Endopogon viscosus var. viscosissimus

Endopogon viscosus var. viscosissimus, commonly known as the sticky endopogon, is a variant of the Endopogon viscosus species.

It is a grass-like plant characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is notable for its sticky secretions, which are produced on its leaves and stems. It typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and disturbed areas.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
