Erigeron subandinus

Erigeron subandinus, commonly known as the Andean fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Erigeron subandinus is often used in traditional medicine by local communities for its purported health benefits.
