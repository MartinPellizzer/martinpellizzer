Epilobium wilsonii

Epilobium wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Epilobium wilsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
