Elodes drumondii

Elodes drumondii, commonly known as the waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, flexible stems that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often branching out and bearing small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface. Elodes drumondii plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.

It is often used in pond and aquarium settings due to its rapid growth and ability to control algae.
