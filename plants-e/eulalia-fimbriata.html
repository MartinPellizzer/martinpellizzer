Eulalia fimbriata

Eulalia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a distinctive fringed edge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eulalia fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
