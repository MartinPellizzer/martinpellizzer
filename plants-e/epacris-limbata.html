Epacris limbata

Epacris limbata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, evergreen stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of tubular, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils in open forests and heathlands.

Epacris limbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
