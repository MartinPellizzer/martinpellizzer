Eugenia spicata var. cordata

Eugenia spicata var. cordata, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The fruit is a small, edible drupe that is sometimes used in local cuisines.
