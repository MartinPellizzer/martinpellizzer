Elaeagnus crispa var. longicarpa

Elaeagnus crispa var. longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, elongated fruits that mature in the fall. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces yellowish flowers in early spring. It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil restoration efforts.
