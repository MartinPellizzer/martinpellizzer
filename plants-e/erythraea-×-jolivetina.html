Erythraea × jolivetina

Erythraea × jolivetina, commonly known as the hybrid erythraea, is a rare and intriguing plant resulting from the cross between Erythraea minor and Erythraea ramosa.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and unique genetic makeup, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
