Escallonia angustifolia

Escallonia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf escallonia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or red flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red berries. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Escallonia angustifolia prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
