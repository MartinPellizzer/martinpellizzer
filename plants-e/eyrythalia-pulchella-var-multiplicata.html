Eyrythalia pulchella var. multiplicata

Eyrythalia pulchella var. multiplicata, commonly known as the multibract erythranthe, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant features specialized bracts that are often more conspicuous than the actual flowers, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
