Elionurus tripsacoides subvar. sericeus

Elionurus tripsacoides subvar. sericeus, commonly known as the silky panic grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
