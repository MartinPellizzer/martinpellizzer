Equisetum multiforme var. variegatum

Equisetum multiforme var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated horse tail, is a variant of the common horsetail.

It is a perennial plant that belongs to the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, jointed stems. The plant features whorled leaves that are often green with yellow or white variegation, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and its ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical studies.
