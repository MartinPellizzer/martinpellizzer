Erythroxylum parishii

Erythroxylum parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay rose or Paraguay erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in parts of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
