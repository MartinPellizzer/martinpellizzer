Erigeron concinnus var. subglaber

Erigeron concinnus var. subglaber, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation across arid environments.
