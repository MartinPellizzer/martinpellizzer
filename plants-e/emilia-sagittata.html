Emilia sagittata

Emilia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It grows in dry, open habitats like meadows and roadsides, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

Emilia sagittata is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.
