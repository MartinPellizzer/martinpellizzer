Elaphoglossum glabrescens

Elaphoglossum glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum glabrescens is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
