Euonymus barberi

Euonymus barberi, commonly known as the red-edged euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring bright green leaves with red margins that become more pronounced in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in both natural and cultivated settings.
