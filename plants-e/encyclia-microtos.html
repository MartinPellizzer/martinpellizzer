Encyclia microtos

Encyclia microtos, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a species native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, often in humid, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its inflorescence typically consists of a few blooms, each with three petals and three sepals.

Encyclia microtos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
