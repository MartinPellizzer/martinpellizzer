Euphorbia hainanensis

Euphorbia hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan spurge, is a flowering plant native to the island of Hainan in China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and clustered, small greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its latex is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle.

Euphorbia hainanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystem.
