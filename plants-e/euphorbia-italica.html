Euphorbia italica

Euphorbia italica, commonly known as the Italian spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features a distinctive appearance with its spiky, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems, making it drought-tolerant. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many other spurge species known for their milky sap.

Euphorbia italica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
