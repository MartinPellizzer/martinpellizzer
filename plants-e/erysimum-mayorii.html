Erysimum mayorii

Erysimum mayorii, commonly known as the California wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

Erysimum mayorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
