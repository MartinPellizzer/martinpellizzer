Eupatorium azangaroense

Eupatorium azangaroense, commonly known as the Azangaro feverfew, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium azangaroense is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Andean medicine for its purported fever-reducing effects.
