Erianthus articulatus

Erianthus articulatus, commonly known as the jointed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Erianthus articulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration.
