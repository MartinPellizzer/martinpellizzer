Erysimum pallidum

Erysimum pallidum, commonly known as the pale mustang grass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
