Espeletia × gritaensis

Espeletia × gritaensis, commonly known as the Cumbre de la Vieja, is a hybrid plant native to the Andes mountains of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flower heads that bloom in the spring. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
