Eucalyptus × forthiana

Eucalyptus × forthiana, commonly known as the Sydney peppermint, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinct minty scent when crushed.

The flowers are small and white or pale blue, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.
