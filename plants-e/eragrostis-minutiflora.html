Eragrostis minutiflora

Eragrostis minutiflora, commonly known as slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, pale green flowers that appear in panicles. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a clumping growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in dry conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
