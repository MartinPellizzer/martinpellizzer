Episcia tessellata

Episcia tessellata, commonly known as the mosaic begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that often display intricate patterns resembling mosaic designs, which are a key feature of its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red, orange, or yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. Episcia tessellata thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
