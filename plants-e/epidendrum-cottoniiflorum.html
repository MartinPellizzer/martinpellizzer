Epidendrum cottoniiflorum

Epidendrum cottoniiflorum, commonly known as the cottony-flowered epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendant, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
