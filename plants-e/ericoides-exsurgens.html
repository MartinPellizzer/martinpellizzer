Ericoides exsurgens

Ericoides exsurgens, commonly known as the ascending heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes plants adapted to acidic soils and nutrient-poor environments. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen, and its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Ericoides exsurgens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to coastal and mountainous habitats.
