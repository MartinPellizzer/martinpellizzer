Eragrostis glanvillei

Eragrostis glanvillei, commonly known as Glanville's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray sheen that helps reduce water loss.

Eragrostis glanvillei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
