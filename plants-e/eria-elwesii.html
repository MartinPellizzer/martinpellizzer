Eria elwesii

Eria elwesii, commonly known as the Elwes' orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white with yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
