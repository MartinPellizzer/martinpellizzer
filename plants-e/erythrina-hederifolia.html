Erythrina hederifolia

Erythrina hederifolia, commonly known as the creeping coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody stems are often covered in a layer of bark that can vary in color and texture.

Erythrina hederifolia is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
