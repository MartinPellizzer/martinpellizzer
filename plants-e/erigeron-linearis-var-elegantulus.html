Erigeron linearis var. elegantulus

Erigeron linearis var. elegantulus, commonly known as the elegant blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is an annual or biennial herb with a low-growing, spreading habit.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
