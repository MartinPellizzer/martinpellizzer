Eryngium luzulifolium f. typicum

Eryngium luzulifolium f. typicum, commonly known as the alpine thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and purple-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in compact, spherical clusters.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh alpine conditions.
