Entada bequaertii

Entada bequaertii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant features striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is smooth and light gray, while its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
