Elaeocarpus pubescens

Elaeocarpus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a dense pubescence on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological importance.
