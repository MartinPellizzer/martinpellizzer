Eragrostis longiramea

Eragrostis longiramea, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects for soil stabilization.
