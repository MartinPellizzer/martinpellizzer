Entada phaneroneura

Entada phaneroneura, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and smooth, grey bark. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is valued for its durable wood used in construction and furniture.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in traditional medicine.
