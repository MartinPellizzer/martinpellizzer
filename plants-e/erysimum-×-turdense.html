Erysimum × turdense

Erysimum × turdense, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Erysimum × turdense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
