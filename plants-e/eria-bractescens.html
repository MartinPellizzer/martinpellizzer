Eria bractescens

Eria bractescens, commonly known as the hairy leech orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, leech-like appearance due to its elongated, hairy pseudobulbs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Eria bractescens is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance.
