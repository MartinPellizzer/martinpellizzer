Evolvulus alsinoides var. sericeus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. sericeus, commonly known as silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
