Eulophia chrysoglossoides

Eulophia chrysoglossoides, commonly known as the golden-tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering. The plant produces linear, grass-like leaves and bears inflorescences with showy, yellow to orange flowers that have a distinctive, lip-like structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Eulophia chrysoglossoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
