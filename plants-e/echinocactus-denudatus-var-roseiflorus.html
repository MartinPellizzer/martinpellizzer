Echinocactus denudatus var. roseiflorus

Echinocactus denudatus var. roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in spiny, greenish-gray ribs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant pink to rose-colored flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
