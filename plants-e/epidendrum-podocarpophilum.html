Epidendrum podocarpophilum

Epidendrum podocarpophilum, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the winter season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The plant is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
