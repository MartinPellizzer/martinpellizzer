Echinopsis peruviana subsp. puquiensis

Echinopsis peruviana subsp.

puquiensis, commonly known as the Puquio cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Peru. It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the cooler months.

The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its growth is slow, and it can reach heights of up to two meters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in low-maintenance gardens.
