Elaeocarpus submonoceras subsp. lasionyx

Elaeocarpus submonoceras subsp.

lasionyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its lanceolate leaves, which are usually glossy and arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
