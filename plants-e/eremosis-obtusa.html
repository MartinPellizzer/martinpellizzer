Eremosis obtusa

Eremosis obtusa, commonly known as the bluntleaf eremosis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows from a fibrous root system and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is considered a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
