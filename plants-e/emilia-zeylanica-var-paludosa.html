Emilia zeylanica var. paludosa

Emilia zeylanica var. paludosa, commonly known as the marsh emilia, is a variant of the Emilia genus native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, pale blue or lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, swampy areas and thrives in regions with high humidity. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Emilia zeylanica var. paludosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
