Eupatorium formosanum var. quasitripartitum

Eupatorium formosanum var. quasitripartitum, commonly known as the Taiwan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
