Erica incarnata

Erica incarnata, commonly known as the common heather, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica incarnata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats.

Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are evergreen throughout the year.
