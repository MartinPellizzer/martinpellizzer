Erigeron strigosus var. beyrichii

Erigeron strigosus var. beyrichii, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

This variety is often found in the western United States and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
