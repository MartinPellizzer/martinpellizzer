Eugenia neesiana

Eugenia neesiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when mature, with a thin, leathery skin. Eugenia neesiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
