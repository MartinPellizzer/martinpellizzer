Echinocactus hossei

Echinocactus hossei, commonly known as the Mexican sunburst cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne in clusters at the top of the stems. Its spines are typically yellow to orange in color, providing both protection and a striking visual feature.

Echinocactus hossei is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for arid environments.
