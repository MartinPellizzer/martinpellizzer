Epidendrum strobiliferum

Epidendrum strobiliferum, commonly known as the Christmas Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, spike-like inflorescence that bears numerous fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple lip.

Epidendrum strobiliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
