Erodium malvaceum

Erodium malvaceum, commonly known as the small globemallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its small, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, round seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
