Ebenus plumosa subsp. plumosa

Ebenus plumosa subsp.

plumosa, commonly known as the feathered eveningflower, is a member of the Boraginaceae family. It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.
