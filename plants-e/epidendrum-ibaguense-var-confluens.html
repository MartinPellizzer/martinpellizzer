Epidendrum ibaguense var. confluens

Epidendrum ibaguense var. confluens, commonly known as the Ibaguense orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

The species is appreciated for its aesthetic value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
