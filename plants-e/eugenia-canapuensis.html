Eugenia canapuensis

Eugenia canapuensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian peppertree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a garden plant in tropical regions.
