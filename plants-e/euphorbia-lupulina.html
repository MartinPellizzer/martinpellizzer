Euphorbia lupulina

Euphorbia lupulina, commonly known as the yellow-spined spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are surrounded by leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Euphorbia lupulina is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
