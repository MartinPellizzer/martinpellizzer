Eugenia urceolata

Eugenia urceolata, commonly known as the urn-shaped eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. Eugenia urceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
