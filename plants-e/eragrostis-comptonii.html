Eragrostis comptonii

Eragrostis comptonii, commonly known as Compton's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
