Erythrophleum lasianthum

Erythrophleum lasianthum, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive red flowers. The tree produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
