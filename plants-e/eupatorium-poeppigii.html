Eupatorium poeppigii

Eupatorium poeppigii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery or grayish underside.

Eupatorium poeppigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
