Elymus canadensis f. calvescens

Elymus canadensis f. calvescens, commonly known as Canada wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a prominent midrib.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
