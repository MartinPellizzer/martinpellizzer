Eryngium siculum

Eryngium siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and grayish, hairy leaves. The plant belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Eryngium siculum typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
