Eragrostis sessilispica

Eragrostis sessilispica, commonly known as slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, spike-like inflorescences that are typically sessile, meaning they lack a stalk. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among grasses. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Eragrostis sessilispica is often found in grasslands and open habitats, where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock.
