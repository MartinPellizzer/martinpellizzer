Ehretia buxifolia var. microphylla

Ehretia buxifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved boxleaf ehretia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and compact growth habit.
