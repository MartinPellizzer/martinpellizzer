Encyclia oncidioides var. mooreana

Encyclia oncidioides var. mooreana, commonly known as Moore's orchid, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers with three petals and a distinctive lip. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of the diverse orchid flora in its native habitat.
