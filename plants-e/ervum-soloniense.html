Ervum soloniense

Ervum soloniense, commonly known as the broad bean or fava bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds. It has a long history of agricultural use and is valued for its nutritional content.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
