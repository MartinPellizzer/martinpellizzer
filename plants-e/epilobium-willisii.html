Epilobium willisii

Epilobium willisii, commonly known as Will's willowherb, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish and hairy.

Epilobium willisii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
