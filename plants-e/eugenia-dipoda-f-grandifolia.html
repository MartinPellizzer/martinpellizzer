Eugenia dipoda f. grandifolia

Eugenia dipoda f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-fruited eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Myrtaceae family, valued for both ornamental and practical uses.
