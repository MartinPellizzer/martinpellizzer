Ercilla

Ercilla, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, and it produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

Ercilla is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological and cultural significance in its region.
