Elettaria cardamomum zeylanicum

Elettaria cardamomum zeylanicum, commonly known as cardamom, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is cultivated for its aromatic, greenish-black seed pods. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture.
