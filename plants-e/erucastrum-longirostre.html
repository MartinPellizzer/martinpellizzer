Erucastrum longirostre

Erucastrum longirostre, commonly known as the long-beaked erucoid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are elongated and often hairy. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology.
