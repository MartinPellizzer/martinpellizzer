Eryngium raulinii

Eryngium raulinii, commonly known as the Raulin's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium raulinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
