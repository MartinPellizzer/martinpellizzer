Eriophorum russeolum var. aquatile

Eriophorum russeolum var. aquatile, commonly known as the water tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form tufts, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in peat bogs and fens.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
