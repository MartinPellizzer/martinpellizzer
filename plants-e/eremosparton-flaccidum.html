Eremosparton flaccidum

Eremosparton flaccidum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. Its fibrous stems and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Eremosparton flaccidum plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local biodiversity.
