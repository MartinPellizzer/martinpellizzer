Euodia palawensis

Euodia palawensis, commonly known as the Philippine lauraceae, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, while its fruits are small, round, and reddish when mature.

Euodia palawensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
