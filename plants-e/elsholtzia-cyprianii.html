Elsholtzia cyprianii

Elsholtzia cyprianii, commonly known as the Cyprian's woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to purple and are arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

Elsholtzia cyprianii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
