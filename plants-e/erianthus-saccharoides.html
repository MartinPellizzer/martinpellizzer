Erianthus saccharoides

Erianthus saccharoides, commonly known as sugarcane grass, is a tall perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust growth, with long, narrow leaves and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant is closely related to sugarcane and has potential for use in biofuel production due to its high biomass yield. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its fibrous stalks are also used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber in some regions.
