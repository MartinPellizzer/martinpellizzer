Enckea discolor

Enckea discolor, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and is highly valued for its aesthetic and acoustic properties. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
