Eria gracilis

Eria gracilis, commonly known as the slender eria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Eria gracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
