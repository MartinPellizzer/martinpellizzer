Eremanthus seidelii

Eremanthus seidelii, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eremanthus seidelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
