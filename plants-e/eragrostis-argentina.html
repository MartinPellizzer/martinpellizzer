Eragrostis argentina

Eragrostis argentina, commonly known as Argentine lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis argentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
