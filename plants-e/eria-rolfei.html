Eria rolfei

Eria rolfei, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Eria, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant prefers warm, humid environments and thrives in well-draining soil with partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Eria rolfei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
