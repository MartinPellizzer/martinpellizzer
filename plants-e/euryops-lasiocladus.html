Euryops lasiocladus

Euryops lasiocladus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Euryops lasiocladus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
