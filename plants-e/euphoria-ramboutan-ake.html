Euphoria ramboutan-ake

Euphoria ramboutan-ake, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit that resembles a hairy coconut. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are pollinated by insects. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, sweet pulp.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
