Echinops tenuissimus

Echinops tenuissimus, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces spherical, spiny seed heads that are often mistaken for small globes or decorative objects. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Echinops tenuissimus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
