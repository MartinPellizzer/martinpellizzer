Eyrythalia auriculata

Eyrythalia auriculata, commonly known as the golden earleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in the spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eyrythalia auriculata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
