Eriocaulon argyraeum

Eriocaulon argyraeum, commonly known as silver rush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems covered in a dense, silvery-white layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its small, grass-like leaves are arranged in a tight spiral, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and adaptability to wet environments.
