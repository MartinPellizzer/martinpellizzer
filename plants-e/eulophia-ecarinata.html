Eulophia ecarinata

Eulophia ecarinata, commonly known as the hooked eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that are typically greenish-white with reddish markings. The plant has a pseudobulbous structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, adapting to its tropical and subtropical habitats. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme, and the species is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Eulophia ecarinata prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
