Equisetum variegatum f. alpestre

Equisetum variegatum f. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine variagated horsetail, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its segmented, hollow stems that are often green with a reddish tint. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, typical of its reproductive strategy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental appearance and its historical use in traditional medicine.
