Elaeodendron buchananii

Elaeodendron buchananii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Elaeodendron buchananii is known for its hard, durable wood, which is highly valued for construction and furniture-making.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
