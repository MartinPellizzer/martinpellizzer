Eupatorium subalternifolium

Eupatorium subalternifolium, commonly known as the lesser boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties.

The plant is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
