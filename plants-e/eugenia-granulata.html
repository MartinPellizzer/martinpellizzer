Eugenia granulata

Eugenia granulata, commonly known as the granulated eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in some cuisines.
