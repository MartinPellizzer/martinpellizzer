Elaeocarpus leptostachyus

Elaeocarpus leptostachyus, commonly known as the slender-stalked elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and showy white flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
