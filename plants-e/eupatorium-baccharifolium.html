Eupatorium baccharifolium

Eupatorium baccharifolium, commonly known as the sand cordgrass, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments and salt marshes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium baccharifolium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local wildlife.
