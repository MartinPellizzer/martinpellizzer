Esula falcata

Esula falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, pinnately compound fronds that often curve in a sickle shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a striking addition to shaded gardens.
