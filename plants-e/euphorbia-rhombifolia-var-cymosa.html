Euphorbia rhombifolia var. cymosa

Euphorbia rhombifolia var. cymosa, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features fleshy, rhombic-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
