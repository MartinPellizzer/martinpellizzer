Echinocactus salmii

Echinocactus salmii, commonly known as the Salm's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on the upper part of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
