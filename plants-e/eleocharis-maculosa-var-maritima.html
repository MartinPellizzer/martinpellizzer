Eleocharis maculosa var. maritima

Eleocharis maculosa var. maritima, commonly known as salt marsh spike-rush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It typically grows in coastal marshes and tidal flats, where it thrives in brackish or slightly saline water. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or brownish in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in dense clusters near the water's edge.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
