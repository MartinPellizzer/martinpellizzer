Elaeagnus multiflora var. tsukubana

Elaeagnus multiflora var. tsukubana, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, red berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and winter interest.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
