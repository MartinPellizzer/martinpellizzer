Echinochloa erythrosperma

Echinochloa erythrosperma, commonly known as red-seeded barnyardgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its invasive potential in agricultural areas. It typically grows in moist soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in rice paddies and other cultivated lands.
