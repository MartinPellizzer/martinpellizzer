Erianthus griffithii var. trichophyllus

Erianthus griffithii var. trichophyllus, commonly known as the hairy lemongrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and a distinctive, aromatic, lemon-scented foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

This variety is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.
