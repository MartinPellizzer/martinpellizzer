Erythroxylum suberosum var. denudatum

Erythroxylum suberosum var. denudatum, commonly known as the naked-stemmed copal tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, followed by small, fleshy fruits.

It is known for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
