Evolvulus sericeus var. commersonii

Evolvulus sericeus var. commersonii, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a trailing vine with slender stems that can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

The species is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.
