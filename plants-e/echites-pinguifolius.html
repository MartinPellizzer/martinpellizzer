Echites pinguifolius

Echites pinguifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved echites, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its striking foliage, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
