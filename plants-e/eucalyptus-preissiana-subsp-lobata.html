Eucalyptus preissiana subsp. lobata

Eucalyptus preissiana subsp.

lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often greyish bark.

The plant typically grows as a multi-stemmed shrub or small tree, adapting to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
