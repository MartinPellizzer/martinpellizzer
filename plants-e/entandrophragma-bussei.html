Entandrophragma bussei

Entandrophragma bussei, commonly known as African teak, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. The plant has a straight trunk and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by winged fruits. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also cultivated for timber production.
