Echinopsis mamillosa var. hystrichoides

Echinopsis mamillosa var. hystrichoides, commonly known as the hystrichoides cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a spineless cactus characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its blooming period occurs during the warmer months, usually from spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance and ability to withstand drought conditions.
