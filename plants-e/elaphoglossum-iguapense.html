Elaphoglossum iguapense

Elaphoglossum iguapense, commonly known as the Iguape fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds with a distinctive, intricate venation pattern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas, where it thrives in the understory. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori on the underside of its leaves, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Elaphoglossum iguapense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
