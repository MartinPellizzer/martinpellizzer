Echinopsis pentlandii var. maximiliana

Echinopsis pentlandii var. maximiliana, commonly known as the Golden Lion Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It is a slow-growing cactus that requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habits, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
