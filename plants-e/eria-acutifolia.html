Eria acutifolia

Eria acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
