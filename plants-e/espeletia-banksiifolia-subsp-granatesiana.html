Espeletia banksiifolia subsp. granatesiana

Espeletia banksiifolia subsp.

granatesiana, commonly known as the Granate Espeletia, is a species native to the Andean highlands of South America. It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and dense, silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces showy, violet-colored inflorescences that bloom in the spring. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
