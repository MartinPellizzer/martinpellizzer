Eugenia wilsonii

Eugenia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a reddish tint when young.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
