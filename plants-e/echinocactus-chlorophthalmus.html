Echinocactus chlorophthalmus

Echinocactus chlorophthalmus, commonly known as the blue-eyed cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and distinctive blue-gray spines that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting to the harsh environmental conditions.

Echinocactus chlorophthalmus is a slow-growing plant that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
