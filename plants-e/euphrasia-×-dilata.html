Euphrasia × dilata

Euphrasia × dilata, commonly known as the long-flowered eyebright, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers with prominent, showy eyespots. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, downy hairs. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
