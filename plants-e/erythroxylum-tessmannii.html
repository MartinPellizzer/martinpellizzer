Erythroxylum tessmannii

Erythroxylum tessmannii, commonly known as the Tessmann's erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain elliptical, shiny seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its morphological features and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
