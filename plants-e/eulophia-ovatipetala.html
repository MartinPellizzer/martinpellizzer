Eulophia ovatipetala

Eulophia ovatipetala, commonly known as the broad-leafed eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces striking, elongated inflorescences with flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
