Elymus pendulosus

Elymus pendulosus, commonly known as the weeping wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its slender, blue-green leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Elymus pendulosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
