Erysimum longifolium var. curvifolium

Erysimum longifolium var. curvifolium, commonly known as the bent-leaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are elongated and often curved, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
