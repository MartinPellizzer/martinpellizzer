Eryngium polyrhizum

Eryngium polyrhizum, commonly known as the many-rooted thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system, which contributes to its hardiness and ability to thrive in various soil conditions. It is often found in dry meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Eryngium polyrhizum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
