Epilobium × glanduligerum

Epilobium × glanduligerum, commonly known as the sticky willow or glandular willow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Onagraceae and is characterized by its glandular-hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its adaptability makes it a common sight in various habitats across its range.
