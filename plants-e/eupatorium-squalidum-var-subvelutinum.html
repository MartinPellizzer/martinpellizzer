Eupatorium squalidum var. subvelutinum

Eupatorium squalidum var. subvelutinum, commonly known as the rough eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardening.
