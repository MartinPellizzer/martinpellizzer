Epilobium virgatum

Epilobium virgatum, commonly known as sticky willow or silverweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in sticky hairs, which give it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a silvery underside.

The flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in spikes, blooming in late spring to early summer.
