Excoecaria pachyphylla

Excoecaria pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean islands.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
