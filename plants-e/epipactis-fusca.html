Epipactis fusca

Epipactis fusca, commonly known as the yellow helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a cluster of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. Epipactis fusca is typically found in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

It is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
