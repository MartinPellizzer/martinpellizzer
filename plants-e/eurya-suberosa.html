Eurya suberosa

Eurya suberosa, commonly known as the roughleaf eurya, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Eurya suberosa typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
