Eugenia stapfiana

Eugenia stapfiana, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark, glossy leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Eugenia stapfiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
