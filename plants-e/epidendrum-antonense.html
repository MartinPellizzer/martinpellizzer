Epidendrum antonense

Epidendrum antonense, commonly known as the Anton's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or purple lip. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and covered in a thick, leathery sheath.

Epidendrum antonense is typically found in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories.
