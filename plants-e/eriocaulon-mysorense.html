Eriocaulon mysorense

Eriocaulon mysorense, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including India and Malaysia. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous sheath at the base.

Eriocaulon mysorense plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
