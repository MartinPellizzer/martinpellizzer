Eupatorium perfoliatum f. laciniatum

Eupatorium perfoliatum f. laciniatum, commonly known as the cutleaf boneset, is a variant of the boneset plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
