Elsholtzia cristata var. angustifolia

Elsholtzia cristata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It features wiry, branched stems that often grow close to the ground, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its aromatic leaves and stems contain essential oils, making it a plant of interest for herbal and medicinal uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, adapting well to harsh environmental conditions.
