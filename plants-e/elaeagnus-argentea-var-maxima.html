Elaeagnus argentea var. maxima

Elaeagnus argentea var. maxima, commonly known as silverleaf elaeagnus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically yellow or pale green. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The fruit is a small, edible berry that is often used in culinary applications.
