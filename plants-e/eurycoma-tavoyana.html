Eurycoma tavoyana

Eurycoma tavoyana, commonly known as the Tavoyan tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red fruits containing elliptical seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forests, thriving in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional uses.
