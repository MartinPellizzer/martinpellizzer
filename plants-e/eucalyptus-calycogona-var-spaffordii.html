Eucalyptus calycogona var. spaffordii

Eucalyptus calycogona var. spaffordii, commonly known as the Spafford's mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped, grey-green leaves. The plant produces small, globular white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
