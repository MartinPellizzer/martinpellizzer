Erysimum tetovense

Erysimum tetovense, commonly known as the Tetovense Wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to its natural surroundings.

Erysimum tetovense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
