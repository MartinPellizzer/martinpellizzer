Eupatorium hunzigeri

Eupatorium hunzigeri, commonly known as the golden anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium hunzigeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
