Eriobotrya laoshanica

Eriobotrya laoshanica, commonly known as the Lao Mountain loquat, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by yellow-orange berries in late summer. Its fruits are edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
