Ebenus foliosa

Ebenus foliosa, commonly known as the leafy euphorbia, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a succulent, branching stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
