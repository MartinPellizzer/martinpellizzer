Erianthus coarctatus var. elliottianus

Erianthus coarctatus var. elliottianus, commonly known as the Elliot's panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
