Eucalyptus haemastoma

Eucalyptus haemastoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallet gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The tree produces small, spherical flower buds that are often pink or red in hue. Its bark is smooth and peels off in thin, papery strips.

This species is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is valued for its ornamental and timber qualities.
