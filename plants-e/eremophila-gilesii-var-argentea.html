Eremophila gilesii var. argentea

Eremophila gilesii var. argentea, commonly known as the silver-leaf eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
