Erodium jacquinianum var. annuum

Erodium jacquinianum var. annuum, commonly known as the annual bedstraw, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in arid and semi-arid regions.
