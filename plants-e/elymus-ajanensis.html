Elymus ajanensis

Elymus ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajan grass, is a perennial grass species native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Elymus ajanensis is adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
