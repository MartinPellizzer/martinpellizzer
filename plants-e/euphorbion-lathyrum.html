Euphorbion lathyrum

Euphorbion lathyrum, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in yellowish-green cyathia, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

It is often found in woodland floors and along stream banks, where it thrives in rich, loamy soil.
