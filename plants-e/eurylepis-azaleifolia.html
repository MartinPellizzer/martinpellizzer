Eurylepis azaleifolia

Eurylepis azaleifolia, commonly known as the rock azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other shrubs adapted to acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
