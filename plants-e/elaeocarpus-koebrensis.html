Elaeocarpus koebrensis

Elaeocarpus koebrensis, commonly known as the Java elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
