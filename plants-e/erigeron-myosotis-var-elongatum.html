Erigeron myosotis var. elongatum

Erigeron myosotis var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its slender stems and delicate appearance make it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens and natural landscapes.
