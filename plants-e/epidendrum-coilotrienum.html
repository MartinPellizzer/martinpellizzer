Epidendrum coilotrienum

Epidendrum coilotrienum, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive purple veining on the petals and sepals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
