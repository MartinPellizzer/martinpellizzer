Erysimum × cheiri subsp. inexpectans

Erysimum × cheiri subsp.

inexpectans, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silvery-green leaves and has a preference for well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging growing conditions.
