Eupatorium marquezianum

Eupatorium marquezianum, commonly known as Marquez's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
