Eulophia burkei

Eulophia burkei, commonly known as the Burkill's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a single inflorescence with several flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forested areas.

Eulophia burkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
