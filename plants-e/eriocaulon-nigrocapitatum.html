Eriocaulon nigrocapitatum

Eriocaulon nigrocapitatum, commonly known as black-capped spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers.

The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with a fine layer of silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
