Eriogonum lachnogynum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum lachnogynum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows.

The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and hairy, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes. It produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
