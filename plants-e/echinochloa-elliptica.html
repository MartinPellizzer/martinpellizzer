Echinochloa elliptica

Echinochloa elliptica, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically green and smooth on the upper surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and wetlands.

Echinochloa elliptica is an annual plant that plays a role in both ecological systems and weed management in cultivated areas.
