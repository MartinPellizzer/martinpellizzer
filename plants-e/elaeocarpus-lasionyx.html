Elaeocarpus lasionyx

Elaeocarpus lasionyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a narrow shape, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
