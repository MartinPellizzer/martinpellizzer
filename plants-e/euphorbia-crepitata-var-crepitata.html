Euphorbia crepitata var. crepitata

Euphorbia crepitata var. crepitata, commonly known as the crackling spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, clustered yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, which is a common feature among the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
