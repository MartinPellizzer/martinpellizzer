Erysimum rarifolium

Erysimum rarifolium, commonly known as the slender wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features slender, hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Erysimum rarifolium is adapted to rocky and sunny environments, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.
