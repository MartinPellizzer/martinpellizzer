Ericoides coccineum

Ericoides coccineum, commonly known as the red erica, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly in the Cape Province.

Ericoides coccineum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover in woodland settings.
