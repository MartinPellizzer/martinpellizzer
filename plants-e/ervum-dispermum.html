Ervum dispermum

Ervum dispermum, commonly known as the two-seeded vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain two seeds, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ervum dispermum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
