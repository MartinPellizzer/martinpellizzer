Eleocharis lankana subsp. mohamadii

Eleocharis lankana subsp.

mohamadii, commonly known as the Mohamadii spike rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of spikelets.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Its morphology includes narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
