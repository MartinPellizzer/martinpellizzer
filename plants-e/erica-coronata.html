Erica coronata

Erica coronata, commonly known as the Crown Heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related species. This evergreen shrub typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or white, appearing in spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
