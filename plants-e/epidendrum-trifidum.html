Epidendrum trifidum

Epidendrum trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, branching stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive three-lobed lip, which is a characteristic feature of the genus.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
