Eryngium pentechinum

Eryngium pentechinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that resemble a crown. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Eryngium pentechinum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
