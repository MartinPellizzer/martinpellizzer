Eugenia coibensis

Eugenia coibensis, commonly known as the Coiba guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry with a thin, yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
