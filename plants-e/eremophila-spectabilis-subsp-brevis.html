Eremophila spectabilis subsp. brevis

Eremophila spectabilis subsp.

brevis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for low-water gardens. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
