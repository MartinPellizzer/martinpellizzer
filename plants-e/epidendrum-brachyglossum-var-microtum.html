Epidendrum brachyglossum var. microtum

Epidendrum brachyglossum var. microtum, commonly known as the miniature tongue orchid, is a compact epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features short, narrow pseudobulbs with small, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is favored by orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
