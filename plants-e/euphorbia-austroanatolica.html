Euphorbia austroanatolica

Euphorbia austroanatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian spurge, is a succulent plant native to Turkey.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous cylindrical, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves and stem for survival.

Euphorbia austroanatolica is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and diverse growth forms.
