Exocarya sclerioides

Exocarya sclerioides, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features woody stems with rough, scaly bark and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Exocarya sclerioides is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots and a drought-resistant structure.
