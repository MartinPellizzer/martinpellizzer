Eupatorium rhomboideum

Eupatorium rhomboideum, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a robust root system and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Eupatorium rhomboideum is often found in wetland ecosystems and is known for its ability to thrive in poorly drained soils.
