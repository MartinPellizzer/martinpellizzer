Equisetum × litorale var. tortuosum

Equisetum × litorale var. tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted field horsetail, is a hybrid variety of horsetail that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and a hollow, jointed stem. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and tidal zones. Its leaves are small, needle-like, and often have a distinctive twisted appearance.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
