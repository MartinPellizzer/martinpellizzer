Erysimum altaicum var. viride

Erysimum altaicum var. viride, commonly known as the green alpine mustard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

This variety is distinguished by its distinctively green foliage, which contrasts with the yellow flowers, making it a notable plant in its habitat.
