Epilobium transandinum

Epilobium transandinum, commonly known as the Andean willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Epilobium transandinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
