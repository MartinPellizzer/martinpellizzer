Eriogonum kennedyi var. purpusii

Eriogonum kennedyi var. purpusii, commonly known as Kennedy's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive, often lobed shape.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
