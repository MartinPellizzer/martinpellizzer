Euphorbia × pasteurii

Euphorbia × pasteurii, commonly known as the pasteur's spurge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia. The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin, a common trait among many species in the Euphorbia genus. It is native to regions of Europe and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Euphorbia × pasteurii prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
