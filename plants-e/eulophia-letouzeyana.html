Eulophia letouzeyana

Eulophia letouzeyana, commonly known as the Letouzey's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, arranged in a raceme along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Eulophia letouzeyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
