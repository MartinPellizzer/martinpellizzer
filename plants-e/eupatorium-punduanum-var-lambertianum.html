Eupatorium punduanum var. lambertianum

Eupatorium punduanum var. lambertianum, commonly known as Lambert's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodlands and wetlands, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features opposite, serrated leaves and produces small, clustered white flowers in the late summer. It is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is characterized by its sturdy stems and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
