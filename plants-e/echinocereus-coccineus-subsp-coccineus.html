Echinocereus coccineus subsp. coccineus

Echinocereus coccineus subsp.

coccineus, commonly known as the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a small, spineless cactus with a distinctive red or pinkish hue on its stems and flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange and bloom in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
