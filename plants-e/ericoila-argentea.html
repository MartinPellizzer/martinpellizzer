Ericoila argentea

Ericoila argentea, commonly known as silver heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It features small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained acidic soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Ericoila argentea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive ground cover.
