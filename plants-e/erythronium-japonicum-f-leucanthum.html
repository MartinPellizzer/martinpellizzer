Erythronium japonicum f. leucanthum

Erythronium japonicum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese dog's mercury, is a rare and striking variant of the typical Erythronium japonicum.

This plant is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast sharply with its dark green, arrow-shaped leaves. It is a perennial herb native to Japan, typically growing in shaded, moist woodlands. The plant produces a rosette of basal leaves and a single flowering stem that emerges in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest for plant enthusiasts and researchers.
