Erythrina variegata f. picta

Erythrina variegata f. picta, commonly known as the variegated flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a variant of the common flame tree, characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
