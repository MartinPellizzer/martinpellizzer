Erica alopecuroides

Erica alopecuroides, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, wiry foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is well-suited to coastal and sandy environments.

Erica alopecuroides is a hardy plant that is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative ground cover.
