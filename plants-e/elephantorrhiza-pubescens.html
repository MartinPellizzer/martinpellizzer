Elephantorrhiza pubescens

Elephantorrhiza pubescens, commonly known as the African elephant's foot, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy base that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Its unique morphology makes it well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions.
