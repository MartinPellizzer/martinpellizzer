Epidendrum acuminatum

Epidendrum acuminatum, commonly known as the Acuminatum Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically has elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a compact rosette. It thrives in humid, well-draining environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum acuminatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
