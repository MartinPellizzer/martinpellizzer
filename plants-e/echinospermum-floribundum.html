Echinospermum floribundum

Echinospermum floribundum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive conical flower structure and its tendency to produce multiple flowers.

Echinospermum floribundum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
