Euphorbia chrysophylla

Euphorbia chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green, sometimes with a yellowish or golden tint. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
