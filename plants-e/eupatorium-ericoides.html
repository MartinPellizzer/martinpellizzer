Eupatorium ericoides

Eupatorium ericoides, commonly known as swamp boneset, is a perennial herb native to wetland areas of North America.

It features erect, hairy stems that are often reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Eupatorium ericoides thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks.
