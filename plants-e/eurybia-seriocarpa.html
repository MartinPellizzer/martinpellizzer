Eurybia seriocarpa

Eurybia seriocarpa, commonly known as the showy golden aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features basal leaves that are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. Its flowering heads are composed of numerous small florets, with the central disk surrounded by ray florets that give it a showy appearance.

Eurybia seriocarpa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
