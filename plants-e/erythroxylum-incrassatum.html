Erythroxylum incrassatum

Erythroxylum incrassatum, commonly known as the thick-leaved erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several elliptical seeds.

This species is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles.
