Erica polifolia

Erica polifolia, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring and early summer. Erica polifolia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
