Erica atromontana

Erica atromontana, commonly known as the black mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, dark purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Erica atromontana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment.
