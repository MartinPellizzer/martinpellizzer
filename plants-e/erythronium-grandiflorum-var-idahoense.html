Erythronium grandiflorum var. idahoense

Erythronium grandiflorum var. idahoense, commonly known as the Idaho trout lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive mottled pattern and produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest floors and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is known for its early spring blooming period.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
