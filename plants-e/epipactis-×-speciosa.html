Epipactis × speciosa

Epipactis × speciosa, commonly known as the hybrid lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a prominent, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers are often pink or white and have a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Epipactis × speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
