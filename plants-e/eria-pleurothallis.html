Eria pleurothallis

Eria pleurothallis, commonly known as the pleurothallis orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining, loose potting mix.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its delicate appearance and resilience.
