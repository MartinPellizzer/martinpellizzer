Ericoides cylindricum

Ericoides cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. Native to regions with acidic soils, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained environments such as heaths and bogs. Its growth habit is low and compact, often forming dense mats or shrubs.

The plant's botanical features make it well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and cold climates.
