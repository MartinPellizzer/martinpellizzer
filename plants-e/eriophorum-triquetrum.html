Eriophorum triquetrum

Eriophorum triquetrum, commonly known as the three-angled cottongrass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular stem and fluffy, white or pale pink flower heads that resemble cotton. This plant typically thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its leaves are grass-like and often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Eriophorum triquetrum plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and provides habitat for various small organisms.
