Echinocactus capricornis

Echinocactus capricornis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that help it store water, adapting to its natural arid environment. Its flowers typically come in shades of red, pink, and white, and it is a popular ornamental plant.

Echinocactus capricornis thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
