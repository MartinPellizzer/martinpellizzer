Echium australe subsp. macranthum

Echium australe subsp.

macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
