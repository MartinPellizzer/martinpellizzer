Erianthera neesiana

Erianthera neesiana, commonly known as the pink flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

Erianthera neesiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
