Encabarcenia

Encabarcenia, commonly known as the "Spanish thistle," is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant features silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Encabarcenia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical uses in herbal medicine.
