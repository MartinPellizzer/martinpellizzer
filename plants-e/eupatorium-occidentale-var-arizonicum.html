Eupatorium occidentale var. arizonicum

Eupatorium occidentale var. arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona feverfew, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are hairy and often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called cymes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a serrated margin.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.
