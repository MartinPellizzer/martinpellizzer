Elymus strigosus

Elymus strigosus, commonly known as rough false wheat, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open woodlands.

Elymus strigosus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
