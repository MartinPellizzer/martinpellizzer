Erica andromediflora

Erica andromediflora, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It features dense, leathery foliage that is well adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring. Erica andromediflora thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

It is an important plant in coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
