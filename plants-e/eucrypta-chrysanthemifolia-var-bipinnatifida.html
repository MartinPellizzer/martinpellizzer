Eucrypta chrysanthemifolia var. bipinnatifida

Eucrypta chrysanthemifolia var. bipinnatifida, commonly known as the golden fern, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnatifid leaves that give it a feathery appearance, resembling a delicate fern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique foliage and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
