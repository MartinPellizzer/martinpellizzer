Euphrasia timbali

Euphrasia timbali, commonly known as the Timbali eyebright, is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Euphrasia timbali is often found in woodland areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
