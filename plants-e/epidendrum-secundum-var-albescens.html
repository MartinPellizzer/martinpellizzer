Epidendrum secundum var. albescens

Epidendrum secundum var. albescens, commonly known as the White-flowered Epidendrum, is a bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in terminal inflorescences. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
