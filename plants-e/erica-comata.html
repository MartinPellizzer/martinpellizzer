Erica comata

Erica comata, commonly known as the silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica comata is known for its compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer.
