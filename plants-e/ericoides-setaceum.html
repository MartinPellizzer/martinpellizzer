Ericoides setaceum

Ericoides setaceum, commonly known as the slender ericoid, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, moist environments.

Ericoides setaceum is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing rocky terrain.
