Eucalyptus consideniana

Eucalyptus consideniana, commonly known as the Mount Lofty gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin flakes. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. This tree produces small, globular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color.

It is well adapted to the dry, inland climates of its native region.
