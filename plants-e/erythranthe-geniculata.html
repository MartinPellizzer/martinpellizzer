Erythranthe geniculata

Erythranthe geniculata, commonly known as the Sierra stonecress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Erythranthe geniculata is a perennial herb that is adapted to cold climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
