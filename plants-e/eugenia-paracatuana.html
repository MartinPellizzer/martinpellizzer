Eugenia paracatuana

Eugenia paracatuana, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, aromatic berries.

Its fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
