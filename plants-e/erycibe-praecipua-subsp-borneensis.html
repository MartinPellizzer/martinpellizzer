Erycibe praecipua subsp. borneensis

Erycibe praecipua subsp.

borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo. It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland rainforests.

The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and distinctive, star-shaped fruits that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
