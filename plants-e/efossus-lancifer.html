Efossus lancifer

Efossus lancifer, commonly known as the narrow-leaved efossus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, which bloom in the early morning. Efossus lancifer is often found growing in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it thrives with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
