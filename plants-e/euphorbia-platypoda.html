Euphorbia platypoda

Euphorbia platypoda, commonly known as the flat-topped spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia platypoda is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
