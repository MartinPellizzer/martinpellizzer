Elymus

Elymus, commonly known as goatgrass, is a genus of grasses in the family Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in temperate regions and are characterized by their tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. Elymus species often thrive in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides. They are known for their ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making them adaptable to various habitats.

Some species of Elymus are cultivated for forage or as ornamental plants.
