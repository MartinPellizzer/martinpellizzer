Euphorbia tirucalli var. rhipsaloides

Euphorbia tirucalli var. rhipsaloides, commonly known as the African milk tree or pencil cactus, is a succulent shrub native to Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with its cylindrical, segmented stems that resemble small pencils, often covered in a waxy, greenish coating. The plant is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its green stems. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange or red berries.

This species is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and irritating to the skin.
