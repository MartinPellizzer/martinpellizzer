Erica ewerana

Erica ewerana, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. Erica ewerana is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
