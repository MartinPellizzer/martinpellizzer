Erica cruenta var. mutica

Erica cruenta var. mutica, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Erica cruenta var. mutica is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
