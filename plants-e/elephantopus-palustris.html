Elephantopus palustris

Elephantopus palustris, commonly known as marsh elephantopus, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often near water bodies, and thrives in environments with high humidity. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
