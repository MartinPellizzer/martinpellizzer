Eriocaulon tuyamae

Eriocaulon tuyamae, commonly known as the Japanese spikerush, is a small perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Japan.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which is characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habits. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, forming dense clusters with slender, erect stems. Its flowering structures are simple, with small white or pale green flowers that bloom in summer.

Eriocaulon tuyamae is often found in marshes, rice fields, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
