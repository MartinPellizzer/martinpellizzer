Euphrasia × brevipila f. congesta

Euphrasia × brevipila f. congesta, commonly known as the clustered eyebright, is a hybrid species within the genus Euphrasia, which belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae.

This plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers, which bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
