Eriogonum angulosum var. victorense

Eriogonum angulosum var. victorense, commonly known as the Victor Valley buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
