Eria dagamensis

Eria dagamensis, commonly known as the Dagam orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its elegant, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers, which are usually greenish-yellow or white, bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators.

Eria dagamensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
