Erigeron howellii

Erigeron howellii, commonly known as Howell's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Howell's fleabane is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
