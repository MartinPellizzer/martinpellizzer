Evolvulus sericatus

Evolvulus sericatus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces delicate, funnel-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Evolvulus sericatus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread rapidly.
