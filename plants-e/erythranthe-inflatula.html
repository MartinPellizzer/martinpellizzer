Erythranthe inflatula

Erythranthe inflatula, commonly known as the inflated beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, inflated bracts that resemble a beard. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Erythranthe inflatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
