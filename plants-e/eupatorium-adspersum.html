Eupatorium adspersum

Eupatorium adspersum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported antipyretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
