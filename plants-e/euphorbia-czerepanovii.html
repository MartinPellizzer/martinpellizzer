Euphorbia czerepanovii

Euphorbia czerepanovii, commonly known as the Czerepanov's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous cylindrical, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive periods of drought.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
