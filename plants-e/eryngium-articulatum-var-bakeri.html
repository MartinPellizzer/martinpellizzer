Eryngium articulatum var. bakeri

Eryngium articulatum var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and distinctive, thistle-like flower heads that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its structure consists of a basal rosette of leaves with a central stem that bears the flowering heads.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
