Eupatorium tetrastichum

Eupatorium tetrastichum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium tetrastichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
