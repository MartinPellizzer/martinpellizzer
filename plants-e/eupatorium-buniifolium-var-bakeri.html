Eupatorium buniifolium var. bakeri

Eupatorium buniifolium var. bakeri, commonly known as Baker's blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
