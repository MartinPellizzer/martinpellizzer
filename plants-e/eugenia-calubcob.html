Eugenia calubcob

Eugenia calubcob, commonly known as the Java apple or *Syzygium jambolanum*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature, containing a single seed. These berries are edible, though the pulp is often tart and the seeds are not typically consumed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
