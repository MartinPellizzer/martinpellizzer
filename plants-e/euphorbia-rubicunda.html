Euphorbia rubicunda

Euphorbia rubicunda, commonly known as the red euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with dense, fleshy leaves that are typically red or purple in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
