Eschscholzia californica var. tenuifolia

Eschscholzia californica var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf California poppy, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Nevada.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is a hardy annual that plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental beauty.
