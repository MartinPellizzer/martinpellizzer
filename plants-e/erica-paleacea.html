Erica paleacea

Erica paleacea, commonly known as the silver heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing shrub form and produces small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Erica paleacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
