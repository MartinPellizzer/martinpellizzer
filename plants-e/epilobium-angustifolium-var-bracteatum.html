Epilobium angustifolium var. bracteatum

Epilobium angustifolium var. bracteatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved willowherb, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and roadside areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
