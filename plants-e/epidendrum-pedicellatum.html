Epidendrum pedicellatum

Epidendrum pedicellatum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Forest, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The flowers, which come in shades of white, pink, and red, are highly ornamental and attract various pollinators.
