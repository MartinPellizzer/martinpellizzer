Espeletia arborea var. lancifolia

Espeletia arborea var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tree heather, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree with dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

This plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems.
