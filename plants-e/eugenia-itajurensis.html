Eugenia itajurensis

Eugenia itajurensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers and dark purple, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, and it is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Eugenia itajurensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
