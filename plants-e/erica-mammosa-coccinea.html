Erica mammosa coccinea

Erica mammosa coccinea, commonly known as the red heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. Erica mammosa coccinea is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
