Euphorbia tripartita

Euphorbia tripartita, commonly known as the three-part euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branching stem with waxy, green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia tripartita is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
