Echium creticum var. sauvagei

Echium creticum var. sauvagei, commonly known as the Cretan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, spiny bracts that protect its seed capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
