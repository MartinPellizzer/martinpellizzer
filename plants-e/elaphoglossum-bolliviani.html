Elaphoglossum bolliviani

Elaphoglossum bolliviani, commonly known as the Bollivian holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lanceolate fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by indusia. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Elaphoglossum bolliviani is valued for its ornamental foliage and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
