Eugenia pasacaensis

Eugenia pasacaensis, commonly known as the Pasaca guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are followed by edible, fragrant fruits resembling guavas in appearance. Eugenia pasacaensis is typically found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential horticultural use.
