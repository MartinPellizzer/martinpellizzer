Eugenia pellucida

Eugenia pellucida, commonly known as the silverleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in small inflorescences.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
