Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. viridescens

Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. viridescens, commonly known as the green-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
