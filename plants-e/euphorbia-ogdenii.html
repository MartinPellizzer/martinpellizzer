Euphorbia ogdenii

Euphorbia ogdenii, commonly known as the Ogden's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features a distinctive, branching stem structure with small, waxy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
