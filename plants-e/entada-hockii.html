Entada hockii

Entada hockii, commonly known as the African hornbeam, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Entada hockii is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in various applications.
