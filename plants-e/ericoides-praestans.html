Ericoides praestans

Ericoides praestans, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor, acidic soils and thrives in coastal environments with high humidity and salt spray.

Its dense foliage provides habitat for various insects and birds, contributing to the ecological balance of its native habitat.
