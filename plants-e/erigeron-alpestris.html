Erigeron alpestris

Erigeron alpestris, commonly known as the alpine fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high altitudes. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Erigeron alpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
