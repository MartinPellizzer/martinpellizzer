Eupatorium verbenaceum var. rhodolepis

Eupatorium verbenaceum var. rhodolepis, commonly known as the pink eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
