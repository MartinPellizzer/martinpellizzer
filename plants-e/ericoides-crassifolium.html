Ericoides crassifolium

Ericoides crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and forest edges.

Ericoides crassifolium plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
