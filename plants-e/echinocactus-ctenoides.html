Echinocactus ctenoides

Echinocactus ctenoides, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, covered with areoles that bear clusters of white or yellowish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor collections.
