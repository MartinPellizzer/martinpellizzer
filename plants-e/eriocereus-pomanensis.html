Eriocereus pomanensis

Eriocereus pomanensis, commonly known as the Pomanensis Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its columnar stem and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
