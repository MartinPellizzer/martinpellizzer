Eragrostis abrumpens

Eragrostis abrumpens, commonly known as the brokengrass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eragrostis abrumpens is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
