Euphorbia bachmannii

Euphorbia bachmannii, commonly known as the Bachmann's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, branched stem covered in small, spiny structures that serve as a defense mechanism. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
