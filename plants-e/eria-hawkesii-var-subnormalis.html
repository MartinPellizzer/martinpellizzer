Eria hawkesii var. subnormalis

Eria hawkesii var. subnormalis, commonly known as the Hawkes' eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid with slender, often curved pseudobulbs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose potting media.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
