Echinodorus martii var. uruguayensis

Echinodorus martii var. uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly serrated edge. This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of water conditions. It is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and water-cleaning properties.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge above the water surface.
