Eugenia assimilis

Eugenia assimilis, commonly known as the Java apple or wild guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. The fruits are typically yellow or red when ripe and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia assimilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
