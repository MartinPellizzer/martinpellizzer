Erodium lebelii subsp. lebelii

Erodium lebelii subsp.

lebelii, commonly known as the Lebel's common bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, opposite leaves.

The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, along roadsides, and in cultivated areas.

The species is notable for its ability to spread through seeds and its role in local ecosystems.
