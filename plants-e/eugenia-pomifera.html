Eugenia pomifera

Eugenia pomifera, commonly known as the feijoa, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It produces a unique, aromatic fruit with a white, fleshy pulp surrounding black seeds. The plant has glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

The feijoa is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
