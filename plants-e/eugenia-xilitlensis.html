Eugenia xilitlensis

Eugenia xilitlensis, commonly known as the Mexican guava, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface, and the fruit is usually a small, yellow or red berry.

Eugenia xilitlensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
