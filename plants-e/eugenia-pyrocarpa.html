Eugenia pyrocarpa

Eugenia pyrocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit production.

Eugenia pyrocarpa is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
