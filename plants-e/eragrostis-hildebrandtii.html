Eragrostis hildebrandtii

Eragrostis hildebrandtii, commonly known as Hildebrandt's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Eragrostis hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
