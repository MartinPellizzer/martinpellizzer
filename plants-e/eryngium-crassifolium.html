Eryngium crassifolium

Eryngium crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eryngium crassifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
