Eupatorium cannabinum var. syriacum

Eupatorium cannabinum var. syriacum, commonly known as Syrian hemp nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is often found growing in association with other herbaceous plants in its native habitat.
