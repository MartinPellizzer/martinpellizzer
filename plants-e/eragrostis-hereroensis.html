Eragrostis hereroensis

Eragrostis hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and usually pale green or brown.

Eragrostis hereroensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid environments.
