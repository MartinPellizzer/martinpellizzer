Erisma splendens

Erisma splendens, commonly known as the golden eriska, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Erisma splendens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
