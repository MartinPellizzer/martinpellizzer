Encyclia advena

Encyclia advena, commonly known as the adventitious orchid, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a distinctive yellow lip.

Encyclia advena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
