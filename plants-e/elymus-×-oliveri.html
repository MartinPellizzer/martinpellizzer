Elymus × oliveri

Elymus × oliveri, commonly known as the purple wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and panicles that often display a distinctive purplish hue. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Elymus × oliveri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
