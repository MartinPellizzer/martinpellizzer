Erysimum frutescens

Erysimum frutescens, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum frutescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
