Erythroxylum dekindtii

Erythroxylum dekindtii, commonly known as the De Kindt's copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is also known for its resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local biodiversity.
