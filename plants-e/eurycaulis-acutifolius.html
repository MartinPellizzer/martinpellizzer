Eurycaulis acutifolius

Eurycaulis acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved eurycaulis, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand salty sea air and coastal winds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
