Eriogonum filiforme

Eriogonum filiforme, commonly known as the slenderleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.

Eriogonum filiforme is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
