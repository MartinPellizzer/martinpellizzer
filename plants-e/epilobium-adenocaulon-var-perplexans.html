Epilobium adenocaulon var. perplexans

Epilobium adenocaulon var. perplexans, commonly known as the perplexing willow herb, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with showy pink or red petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
