Erithalis acuminata

Erithalis acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved erithalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.
