Eriochloa stapfiana

Eriochloa stapfiana, commonly known as the Stapf's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often leafless above the base, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered and covered in fine hairs.

Eriochloa stapfiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and resilience.
