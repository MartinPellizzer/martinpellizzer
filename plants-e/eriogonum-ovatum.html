Eriogonum ovatum

Eriogonum ovatum, commonly known as the broadleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its ovate, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Eriogonum ovatum plays an important role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
