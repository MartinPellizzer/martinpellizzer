Ernodea serratifolia

Ernodea serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery fronds that are deeply serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its reproductive structures are small, spore-bearing cones that develop at the base of the fronds.

Ernodea serratifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
