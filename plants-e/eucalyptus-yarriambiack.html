Eucalyptus yarriambiack

Eucalyptus yarriambiack, commonly known as the Yarriambiack mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its gnarled, multi-stemmed form and rough, fibrous bark that peels in strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery-green underside, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators.

This hardy plant is well-suited to dry conditions and plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
