Entada plumeri

Entada plumeri, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering. Its bark is thick and fissured, often with a reddish or brownish hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various cultures.
