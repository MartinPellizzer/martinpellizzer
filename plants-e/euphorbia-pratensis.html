Euphorbia pratensis

Euphorbia pratensis, commonly known as the meadow spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually green with a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant contains latex, which can be irritating to the skin.
