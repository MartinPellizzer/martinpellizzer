Erica speciosa var. hirtella

Erica speciosa var. hirtella, commonly known as the Hirtella heather, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This variety is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically leathery and evergreen, providing year-round interest.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
