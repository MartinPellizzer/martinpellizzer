Erythroxylum australe

Erythroxylum australe, commonly known as the Australian copal tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing elliptical, shiny seeds. It is typically found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

The species is of interest for its potential ecological roles and unique botanical features.
