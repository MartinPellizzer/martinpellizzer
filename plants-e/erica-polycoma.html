Erica polycoma

Erica polycoma, commonly known as the many-flowered heath, is a hardy evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple during the summer months. Erica polycoma is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought resistance.
