Elymus mollis var. japonicus

Elymus mollis var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese soft grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its delicate appearance.
