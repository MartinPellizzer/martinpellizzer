Erica vestita coccinea

Erica vestita coccinea, commonly known as the red heather, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family, Ericaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in late summer and autumn. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, forming a low, mat-like growth habit.

Erica vestita coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
