Eriosema griseum

Eriosema griseum, commonly known as the rough eriosema, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eriosema griseum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
