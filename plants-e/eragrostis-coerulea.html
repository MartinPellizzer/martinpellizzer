Eragrostis coerulea

Eragrostis coerulea, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.

Its ornamental value has also led to its use in landscaping and restoration projects.
