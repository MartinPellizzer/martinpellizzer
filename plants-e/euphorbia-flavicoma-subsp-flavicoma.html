Euphorbia flavicoma subsp. flavicoma

Euphorbia flavicoma subsp.

flavicoma, commonly known as the yellow-haired euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and sparse, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or green. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
