Eulophia helleborina

Eulophia helleborina, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive purple lip. The plant typically blooms in winter or early spring, making it a rare winter-flowering orchid. Its leaves are usually broad and sword-shaped, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Eulophia helleborina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
