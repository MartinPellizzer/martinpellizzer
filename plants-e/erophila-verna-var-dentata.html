Erophila verna var. dentata

Erophila verna var. dentata, commonly known as the early vernal eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and waste areas. It produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are usually rough and toothed, giving the plant its specific variety name, dentata.
