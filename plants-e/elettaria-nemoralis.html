Elettaria nemoralis

Elettaria nemoralis, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, round fruits containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their strong, spicy scent.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
