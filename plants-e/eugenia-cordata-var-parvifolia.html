Eugenia cordata var. parvifolia

Eugenia cordata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved wax myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a fragrant, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its dense growth habit and aromatic foliage make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial.
