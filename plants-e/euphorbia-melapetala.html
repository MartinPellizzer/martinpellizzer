Euphorbia melapetala

Euphorbia melapetala, commonly known as the white-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its leaves are typically small and succulent, storing water to aid in survival.

Euphorbia melapetala is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
