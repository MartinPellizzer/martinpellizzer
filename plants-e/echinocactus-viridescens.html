Echinocactus viridescens

Echinocactus viridescens, commonly known as the green prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green, with clusters of spines along the ridges. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
