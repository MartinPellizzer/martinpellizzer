Echinodorus repens

Echinodorus repens, commonly known as the wandering water plant, is a popular aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, making it ideal for background placement in aquariums. The plant thrives in moderate to high light conditions and requires regular nutrient supplementation to maintain its vibrant green color. It is a fast-growing species that spreads through rhizomes, often forming dense underwater mats.

Echinodorus repens is also valued for its ability to help oxygenate water and provide shelter for aquatic life.
