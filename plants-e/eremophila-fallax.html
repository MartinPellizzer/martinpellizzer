Eremophila fallax

Eremophila fallax, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, often attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Eremophila fallax is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
