Eryngium medium

Eryngium medium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by silver-white bracts. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Eryngium medium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
