Erigeron acris var. major

Erigeron acris var. major, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It has finely divided, hairy leaves and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, making it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
