Eriochloa villosa f. distachya

Eriochloa villosa f. distachya, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and hairy texture, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in pairs, a feature that distinguishes it from other related species.

The botanical features of Eriochloa villosa f. distachya make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
