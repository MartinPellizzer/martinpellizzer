Erigeron acris var. serotinus

Erigeron acris var. serotinus, commonly known as the late blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed areas. It has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
