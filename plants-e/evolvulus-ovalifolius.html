Evolvulus ovalifolius

Evolvulus ovalifolius, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in warm, sunny environments.
