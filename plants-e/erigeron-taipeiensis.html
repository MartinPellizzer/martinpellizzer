Erigeron taipeiensis

Erigeron taipeiensis, commonly known as Taipei fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
