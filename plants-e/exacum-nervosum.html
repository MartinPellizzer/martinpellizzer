Exacum nervosum

Exacum nervosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Exacum nervosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various growing conditions.
