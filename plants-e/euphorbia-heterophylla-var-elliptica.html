Euphorbia heterophylla var. elliptica

Euphorbia heterophylla var. elliptica, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use, though caution is needed due to its irritant properties.
