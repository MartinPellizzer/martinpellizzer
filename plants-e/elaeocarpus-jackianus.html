Elaeocarpus jackianus

Elaeocarpus jackianus, commonly known as the Jack's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that enclose a single seed.

It is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
