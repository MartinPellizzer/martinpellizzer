Eriogonum ovalifolium var. depressum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. depressum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as hillsides and meadows. The plant features basal leaves that are oval-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pale pink flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native range.
