Erodium guicciardii var. glanduliferum

Erodium guicciardii var. glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular erodium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, dry pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
