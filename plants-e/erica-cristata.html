Erica cristata

Erica cristata, commonly known as the cristate heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained, acidic conditions. Erica cristata is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to coastal climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.
