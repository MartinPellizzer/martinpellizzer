Elymus neoborealis

Elymus neoborealis, commonly known as the northern wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and rocky soils.

Elymus neoborealis is often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and along the edges of lakes and rivers.
