Euphrasia collina subsp. tasmanica

Euphrasia collina subsp.

tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open heathland or coastal areas. Its leaves are linear and hairy, and it produces numerous small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
