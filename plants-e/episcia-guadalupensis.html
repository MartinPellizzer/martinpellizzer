Episcia guadalupensis

Episcia guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupensis waxplant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a low, trailing herb with succulent, fleshy leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Episcia guadalupensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in hanging baskets and as a houseplant.
