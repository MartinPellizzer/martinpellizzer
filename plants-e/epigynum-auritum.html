Epigynum auritum

Epigynum auritum, commonly known as the ear-shaped nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive ear-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet flowers. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for food but is studied for its potential medicinal compounds.
