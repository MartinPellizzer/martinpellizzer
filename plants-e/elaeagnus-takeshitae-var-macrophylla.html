Elaeagnus takeshitae var. macrophylla

Elaeagnus takeshitae var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and small, edible fruits that resemble berries. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its tolerance to harsh climates. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which are pollinated by insects.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
