Eucalyptus corynocalyx

Eucalyptus corynocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs or small trees with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in groups, producing a distinctive honey-scented nectar that attracts various pollinators. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions with its deep root system.

Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for fuel and minor construction purposes.
