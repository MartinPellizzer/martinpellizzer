Euphorbia diuretica

Euphorbia diuretica, commonly known as the diuretic spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its upright growth and branching structure. The plant features yellowish-green sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Euphorbia diuretica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses.
