Entandrophragma platanoides

Entandrophragma platanoides, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, light grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy timber used in furniture and construction.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
