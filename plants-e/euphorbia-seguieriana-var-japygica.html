Euphorbia seguieriana var. japygica

Euphorbia seguieriana var. japygica, commonly known as the Seguier's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental appearance make it a desirable addition to arid and semi-arid landscapes.
