Elaeocarpus chelonimorphus

Elaeocarpus chelonimorphus, commonly known as the turtle-shell elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, thick, leathery leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The tree produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is rough and fissured, and the plant is noted for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Elaeocarpus chelonimorphus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
