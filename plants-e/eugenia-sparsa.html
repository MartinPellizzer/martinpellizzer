Eugenia sparsa

Eugenia sparsa, commonly known as the sparse eugenia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green, with a slight pubescence on the underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries.

Eugenia sparsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
