Elaphoglossum minutissimum

Elaphoglossum minutissimum, commonly known as the minute harts-tongue fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its small size and intricate structure, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
