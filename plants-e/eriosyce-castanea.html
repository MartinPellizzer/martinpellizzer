Eriosyce castanea

Eriosyce castanea, commonly known as the chestnut cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiny stems that resemble the texture of chestnut bark. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often hidden among the dense foliage. Eriosyce castanea is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
