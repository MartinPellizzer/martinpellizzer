Edithea rupicola

Edithea rupicola, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the warm seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dry woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
