Euphrasia salisburgensis var. minuta

Euphrasia salisburgensis var. minuta, commonly known as the Salzburg sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It typically features small, delicate yellow flowers with prominent dark markings, which are characteristic of the genus Euphrasia. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, high-altitude conditions. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized flora species.
