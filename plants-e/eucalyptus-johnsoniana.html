Eucalyptus johnsoniana

Eucalyptus johnsoniana, commonly known as the Johnson's malleeflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of grey and white. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Eucalyptus johnsoniana is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
