Eugenia flavescens var. guianensis

Eugenia flavescens var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan satinash, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities. Its fruit, a small, yellowish drupe, is edible but less commonly consumed by humans.

The species is also notable for its role in local ecosystems and its potential use in traditional medicine.
