Eupatorium vallincola var. brevipilum

Eupatorium vallincola var. brevipilum, commonly known as the short-hair eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in umbels. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
