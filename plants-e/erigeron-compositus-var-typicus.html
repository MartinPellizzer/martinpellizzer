Erigeron compositus var. typicus

Erigeron compositus var. typicus, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed areas. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are generally hairy and alternate along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
