Eugenia trinervia

Eugenia trinervia, commonly known as the red-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-purple berries. Eugenia trinervia thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
