Epidendrum incisum

Epidendrum incisum, commonly known as the serrated epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, arching stems that bear clusters of fragrant, pendulous flowers. The plant typically has broad, elliptical leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or yellow markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting mix.

Epidendrum incisum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
