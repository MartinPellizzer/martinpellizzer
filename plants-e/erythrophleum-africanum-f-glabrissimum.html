Erythrophleum africanum f. glabrissimum

Erythrophleum africanum f. glabrissimum, commonly known as the African velvetwood, is a variant of the African ironwood tree.

It is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical pods containing flat, brown seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in construction and furniture-making.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and soil stabilization.
