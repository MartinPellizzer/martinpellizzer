Echinochloa crus-galli var. zelayensis

Echinochloa crus-galli var. zelayensis, commonly known as yellow grass or wild millet, is a annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to compete with crops, making it a common weed in many regions.
