Elaeagnus angustata

Elaeagnus angustata, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular branches and small, silvery-gray leaves that reflect sunlight, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Elaeagnus angustata also has a reputation for nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility in its environment.
