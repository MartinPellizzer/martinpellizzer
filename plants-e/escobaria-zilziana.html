Escobaria zilziana

Escobaria zilziana, commonly known as Zilz's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rosette-forming growth pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it highly drought-resistant.

Escobaria zilziana is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
