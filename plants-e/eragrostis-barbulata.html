Eragrostis barbulata

Eragrostis barbulata, commonly known as hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Eragrostis barbulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
