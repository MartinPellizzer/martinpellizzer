Emilia abyssinica var. macroglossa

Emilia abyssinica var. macroglossa, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
