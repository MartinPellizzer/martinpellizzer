Eucalyptus howittiana

Eucalyptus howittiana, commonly known as Howitt's mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Eucalyptus howittiana is an important component of the local flora and plays a role in supporting native wildlife.
