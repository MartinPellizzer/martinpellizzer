Eucalyptus virginea

Eucalyptus virginea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia, particularly in the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia. This plant is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Eucalyptus virginea produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically during the winter months.

It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
