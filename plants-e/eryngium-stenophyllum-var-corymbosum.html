Eryngium stenophyllum var. corymbosum

Eryngium stenophyllum var. corymbosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by bracts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
