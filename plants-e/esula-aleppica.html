Esula aleppica

Esula aleppica, commonly known as the Aleppo thorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

Esula aleppica is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
