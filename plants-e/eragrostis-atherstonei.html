Eragrostis atherstonei

Eragrostis atherstonei, commonly known as the Atherstone's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
