Eleocharis caribaea var. achlamydea

Eleocharis caribaea var. achlamydea, commonly known as the Caribbean sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in the Caribbean region.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

It is an important component of the local flora, supporting various wildlife species.
