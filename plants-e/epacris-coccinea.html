Epacris coccinea

Epacris coccinea, commonly known as the crimson epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its striking red or orange-red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained acidic soils. It produces small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Epacris coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
