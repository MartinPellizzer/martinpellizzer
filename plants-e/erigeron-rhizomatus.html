Erigeron rhizomatus

Erigeron rhizomatus, commonly known as the creeping blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a fibrous root system and grows in dense mats, often forming a low-growing ground cover. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration.
