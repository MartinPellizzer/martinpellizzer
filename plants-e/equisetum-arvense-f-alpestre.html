Equisetum arvense f. alpestre

Equisetum arvense f. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine field horsetail, is a variant of the common field horsetail.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, jointed stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are typically found in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, humid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecological systems.
