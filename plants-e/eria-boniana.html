Eria boniana

Eria boniana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

Eria boniana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine.
