Euphorbia officinarum var. hernandez-pachecoi

Euphorbia officinarum var. hernandez-pachecoi, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a branched stem system with a thick, fleshy trunk and sparse, upright leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems and leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry conditions.
