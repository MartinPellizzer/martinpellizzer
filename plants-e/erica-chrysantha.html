Erica chrysantha

Erica chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica chrysantha is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in the Cape region of South Africa.

Its small, clustered flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
