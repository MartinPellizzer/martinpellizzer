Eucalyptus lansdowneana subsp. albopurpurea

Eucalyptus lansdowneana subsp.

albopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-veined ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark that peels in thin strips, revealing a lighter underbark.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive white or purple vein pattern, which gives it its common name. It produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically white or pale blue, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
