Elodes glandulosum

Elodes glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered with small, glandular dots, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are long and ribbon-like, arranged in whorls around the stem.

Elodes glandulosum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
