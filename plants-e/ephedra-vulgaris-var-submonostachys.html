Ephedra vulgaris var. submonostachys

Ephedra vulgaris var. submonostachys, commonly known as the Chinese ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly containing ephedrine, a compound used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Due to its medicinal value, it has been heavily harvested, leading to conservation concerns.
