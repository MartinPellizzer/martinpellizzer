Epithema brunonis

Epithema brunonis, commonly known as the black epithema, is a rare and unique plant species native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Epithema brunonis is known for its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, making it a challenging species to cultivate outside its natural environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
