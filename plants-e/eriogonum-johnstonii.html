Eriogonum johnstonii

Eriogonum johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tough, seed-like fruits.

Eriogonum johnstonii is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.
