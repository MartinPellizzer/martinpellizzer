Enchylaena tomentosa

Enchylaena tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly salsify, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and stout, fleshy roots. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and riverbanks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
