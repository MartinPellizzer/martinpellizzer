Epipactis ovata

Epipactis ovata, commonly known as the ovate helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate-shaped leaves and a distinctive inflorescence of greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have a unique structure with a spurred lip that serves as a nectar source for pollinators.

Epipactis ovata is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments and is often found in association with other orchid species.
