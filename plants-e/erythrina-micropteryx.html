Erythrina micropteryx

Erythrina micropteryx, commonly known as the narrow-winged flame tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, narrow leaflets and produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and ability to thrive in warm climates.

The species also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
