Euploca diversifolia

Euploca diversifolia, commonly known as the variable euploca, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes and colors, which vary depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Euploca diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
