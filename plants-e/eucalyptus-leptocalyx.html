Eucalyptus leptocalyx

Eucalyptus leptocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of development. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional and modern applications.
