Ebnerella tacubayensis

Ebnerella tacubayensis, commonly known as the Tacubaya cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a limited distribution, primarily found in the state of Puebla.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted range, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
