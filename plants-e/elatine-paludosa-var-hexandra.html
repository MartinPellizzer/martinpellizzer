Elatine paludosa var. hexandra

Elatine paludosa var. hexandra, commonly known as the marsh waterwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant found in wetland habitats.

It belongs to the Elatinaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in temperate regions. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variant is distinguished by its hexagonal stem structure, which gives it its specific epithet "hexandra.".
