Epacris ceriflora

Epacris ceriflora, commonly known as the candleflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its showy, candle-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Epacris ceriflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
