Erysimum sylvaticum

Erysimum sylvaticum, commonly known as the woodland wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
