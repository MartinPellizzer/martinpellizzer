Euphorbia lamarckii subsp. regis-jubae

Euphorbia lamarckii subsp.

regis-jubae, commonly known as the Royal Crown Euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a crown, giving it its common name.

The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers surrounded by specialized leaves. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
