Euphorbia arahaka

Euphorbia arahaka, commonly known as the Arahaka spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to touch or ingest.

Euphorbia arahaka is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
