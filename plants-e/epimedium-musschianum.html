Epimedium musschianum

Epimedium musschianum, commonly known as the barrenwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that remains throughout the year. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are borne on short, upright pedicels. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread slowly and form compact clumps.

Epimedium musschianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
