Eriobotrya glabrata

Eriobotrya glabrata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and fruit.

The species is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
