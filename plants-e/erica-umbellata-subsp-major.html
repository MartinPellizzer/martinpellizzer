Erica umbellata subsp. major

Erica umbellata subsp.

major, commonly known as the greater heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in full sun.

The subspecies is noted for its hardiness and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
