Exostema ellipticum

Exostema ellipticum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it is also a common weed in agricultural settings.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge in certain ecosystems.
