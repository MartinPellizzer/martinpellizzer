Eupatorium bracteatum var. pinnatifidum

Eupatorium bracteatum var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, blue to purple flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
