Eulophia inaequalis

Eulophia inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and several leaves arranged along its stem. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive labellum that sets it apart from other orchid species.

Eulophia inaequalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
