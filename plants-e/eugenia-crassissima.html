Eugenia crassissima

Eugenia crassissima, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or candeia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is known for its edible red berries, which are rich in antioxidants and used in local cuisines. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
