Erythrina × blakei

Erythrina × blakei, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a hybrid species within the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods that contain flattened seeds.

Erythrina × blakei is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
