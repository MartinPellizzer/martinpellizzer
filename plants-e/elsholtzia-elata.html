Elsholtzia elata

Elsholtzia elata, commonly known as the tall elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments due to its cold hardiness.

Elsholtzia elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
