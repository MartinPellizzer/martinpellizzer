Erica lichtensteinii

Erica lichtensteinii, commonly known as the Lichtenstein's heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica lichtensteinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
