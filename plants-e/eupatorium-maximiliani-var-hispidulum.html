Eupatorium maximiliani var. hispidulum

Eupatorium maximiliani var. hispidulum, commonly known as roughside eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural use.
