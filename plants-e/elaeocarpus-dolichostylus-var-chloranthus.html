Elaeocarpus dolichostylus var. chloranthus

Elaeocarpus dolichostylus var. chloranthus, commonly known as the green-flowered elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically green or white in color. The plant produces woody fruits that enclose a single seed, a feature common among members of its family. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
