Eulophia pedicellata

Eulophia pedicellata, commonly known as the clustered spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often near streams or in mountainous regions. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with a dense cluster of erect, linear leaves. Its flowering stems bear a raceme of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with prominent purple markings.

The orchid is characterized by its specialized pollination mechanism, attracting specific insect pollinators.
