Echinopsis formosa subsp. kieslingii

Echinopsis formosa subsp.

kieslingii, commonly known as the Kiesling's moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The plant produces striking, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
