Eugenia chrysophylloides

Eugenia chrysophylloides, commonly known as the golden-leaved eucalyptus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves, which contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
