Eritrichium maackii

Eritrichium maackii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive violet hue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of down.

Eritrichium maackii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in cooler climates.
