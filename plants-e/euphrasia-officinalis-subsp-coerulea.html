Euphrasia officinalis subsp. coerulea

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

coerulea, commonly known as blue-eyed Mary, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that resemble tiny eyes, hence its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and a distinctive yellowish-green throat. Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

coerulea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
