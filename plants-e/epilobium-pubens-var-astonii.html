Epilobium pubens var. astonii

Epilobium pubens var. astonii, commonly known as the Aston's willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland edges and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and glandular, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense spike-like clusters, and bloom from June to August.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in wildlife gardens to support pollinators.
