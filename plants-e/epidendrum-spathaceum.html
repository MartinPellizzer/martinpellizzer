Epidendrum spathaceum

Epidendrum spathaceum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a dense layer of white tomentum.

Epidendrum spathaceum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
