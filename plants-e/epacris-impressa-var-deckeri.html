Epacris impressa var. deckeri

Epacris impressa var. deckeri, commonly known as the Kangaroo Island heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
