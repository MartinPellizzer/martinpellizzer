Eria muscicola var. oblonga

Eria muscicola var. oblonga, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
