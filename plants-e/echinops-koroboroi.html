Echinops koroboroi

Echinops koroboroi, commonly known as the Koroboroi thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Its structure and appearance make it a distinctive and visually striking plant in its native habitat.
