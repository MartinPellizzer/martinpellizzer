Eugenia dealbata

Eugenia dealbata, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Eugenia dealbata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
