Erica walkeria var. praestans

Erica walkeria var. praestans, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica walkeria var. praestans is also appreciated for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements in garden settings.
