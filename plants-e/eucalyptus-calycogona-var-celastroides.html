Eucalyptus calycogona var. celastroides

Eucalyptus calycogona var. celastroides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters and attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
