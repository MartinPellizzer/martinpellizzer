Eragrostis manikensis

Eragrostis manikensis, commonly known as the Manikensis lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
