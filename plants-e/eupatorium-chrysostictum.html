Eupatorium chrysostictum

Eupatorium chrysostictum, commonly known as the golden stem eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium chrysostictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
