Eupatorium sideritidis

Eupatorium sideritidis, commonly known as the Greek feverfew, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Eupatorium sideritidis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
