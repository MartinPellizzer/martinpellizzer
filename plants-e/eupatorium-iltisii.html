Eupatorium iltisii

Eupatorium iltisii, commonly known as the Iltis' boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Eupatorium iltisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
