Euphorbia guillemetii

Euphorbia guillemetii, commonly known as the Guillemet's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with prominent, fleshy leaves that are usually green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the modified flower structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Euphorbia guillemetii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
