Evolvulus sericeus var. angustifolius

Evolvulus sericeus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluecurl, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
