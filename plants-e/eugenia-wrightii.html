Eugenia wrightii

Eugenia wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, citrus-scented fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in its native habitats.
