Euonymus arakianus

Euonymus arakianus, commonly known as the Armenian Spurge, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that persist into the winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Euonymus arakianus is also valued for its role in traditional medicine in its native region.
