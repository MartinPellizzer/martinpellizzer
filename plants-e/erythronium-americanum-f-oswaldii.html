Erythronium americanum f. oswaldii

Erythronium americanum f. oswaldii, commonly known as the Oswalt's dog's mercury, is a rare variant of the American dog's mercury.

It is a bulbous perennial herb native to the eastern United States, characterized by its distinctive, nodding, yellow flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments and has a short growing season.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
