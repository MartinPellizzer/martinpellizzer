Epidendrum brachypodum

Epidendrum brachypodum, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with numerous erect or spreading leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered in inflorescences, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Epidendrum brachypodum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
