Eucalyptus leucoxylon var. pluriflora

Eucalyptus leucoxylon var. pluriflora, commonly known as the red gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue when young, turning grey-green with age. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in umbels, attracting a variety of pollinators.

This species is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands.
