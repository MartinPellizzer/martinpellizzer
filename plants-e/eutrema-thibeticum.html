Eutrema thibeticum

Eutrema thibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia, including Tibet, Mongolia, and parts of China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and has a low, spreading growth habit.

Due to its unique ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
