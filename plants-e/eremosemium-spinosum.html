Eremosemium spinosum

Eremosemium spinosum, commonly known as the spiny eremosemium, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in sharp, spiny projections, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Eremosemium spinosum is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xerophytic landscapes.
