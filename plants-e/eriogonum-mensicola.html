Eriogonum mensicola

Eriogonum mensicola, commonly known as the moonflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Eriogonum mensicola is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like sorrel and buckwheat.
