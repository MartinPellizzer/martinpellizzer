Eucnide xylinea

Eucnide xylinea, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Its bark is fibrous and has been used traditionally in some cultures for various purposes.

Eucnide xylinea is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
