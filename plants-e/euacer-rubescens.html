Euacer rubescens

Euacer rubescens, commonly known as the scarlet euacer, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red petals that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its roots are fibrous and help it thrive in well-drained soils.

Euacer rubescens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
