Eupatorium brasiliense

Eupatorium brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian boneset, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as marshes and wetlands, and it is often found in regions with high humidity. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and have been used in traditional medicine.

Eupatorium brasiliense plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
