Erysimum raineri

Erysimum raineri, commonly known as the Rainer wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers. The plant has basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum raineri is noted for its long blooming period, typically from spring to early summer.
