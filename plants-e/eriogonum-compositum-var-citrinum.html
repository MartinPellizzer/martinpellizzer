Eriogonum compositum var. citrinum

Eriogonum compositum var. citrinum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. It has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, spherical seed pods.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.
