Elaeocarpus borneensis

Elaeocarpus borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
