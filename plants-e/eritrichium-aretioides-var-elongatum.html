Eritrichium aretioides var. elongatum

Eritrichium aretioides var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height, often adorned with lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
