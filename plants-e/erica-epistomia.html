Erica epistomia

Erica epistomia, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica epistomia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
