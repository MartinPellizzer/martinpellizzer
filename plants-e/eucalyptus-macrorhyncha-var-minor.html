Eucalyptus macrorhyncha var. minor

Eucalyptus macrorhyncha var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive, bottle-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

The species is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have various industrial and medicinal applications.
