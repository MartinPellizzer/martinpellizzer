Euphorbia edgeworthii

Euphorbia edgeworthii, commonly known as the Edgeworth's spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green花朵 that are clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dry landscapes.
