Erigeron hispanicus

Erigeron hispanicus, commonly known as Spanish fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its foliage is usually gray-green with hairy leaves, and it is often used in herbal remedies for its purported medicinal properties.
