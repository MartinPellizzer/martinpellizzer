Echinopsis purpureopilosa

Echinopsis purpureopilosa, commonly known as the purple-hair cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and vibrant purple to pink flowers. The plant features dense, woolly hair covering its stems, which help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Echinopsis purpureopilosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
