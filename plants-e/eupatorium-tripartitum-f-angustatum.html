Eupatorium tripartitum f. angustatum

Eupatorium tripartitum f. angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct, slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
