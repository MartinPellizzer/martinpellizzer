Erysimum ochroleucum proles ascendens

Erysimum ochroleucum proles ascendens, commonly known as the ascending white-flowered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found on coastal cliffs and in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, while the stems are woody and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long-lasting blooms.
