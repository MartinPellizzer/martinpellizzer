Euphorbia mitchelliana var. longiloba

Euphorbia mitchelliana var. longiloba, commonly known as the Mitchell's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branching stem system with numerous small, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers, often surrounded by colorful bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
