Eucalyptus pachycalyx subsp. waajensis

Eucalyptus pachycalyx subsp.

waajensis, commonly known as the Northern Territory ironbark, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often appears flaky or scaly, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a grey-green hue and a slightly hairy underside. It produces small, globular flower spikes that are usually pale or creamy white in color.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and heat.
