Epilobium campestre

Epilobium campestre, commonly known as common willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers appear in clusters at the ends of the stems, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Epilobium campestre is often found in wet or damp environments and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
