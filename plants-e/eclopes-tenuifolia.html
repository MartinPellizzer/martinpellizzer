Eclopes tenuifolia

Eclopes tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf eclopes, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
