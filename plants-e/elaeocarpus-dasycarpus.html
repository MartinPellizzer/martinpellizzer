Elaeocarpus dasycarpus

Elaeocarpus dasycarpus, commonly known as the thick-shelled elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, thick-walled fruits that are typically red or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its timber and is also studied for its potential ecological roles in tropical ecosystems.
