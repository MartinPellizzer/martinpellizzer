Echinocactus myriostigma

Echinocactus myriostigma, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, lateral branches. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular choice for succulent collections.
