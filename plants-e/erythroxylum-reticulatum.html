Erythroxylum reticulatum

Erythroxylum reticulatum, commonly known as the red nettle or Brazilian nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptic with serrated edges and can be aromatic when crushed.

Erythroxylum reticulatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
