Eclipta erecta var. diffusa

Eclipta erecta var. diffusa, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and blue to purple in color.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
