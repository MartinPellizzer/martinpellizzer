Eugenia arthroopoda var. menabeensis

Eugenia arthroopoda var. menabeensis, commonly known as the Menabe ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
