Eriogonum undulatum

Eriogonum undulatum, commonly known as the crested buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-crested leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like sorrel and buckwheat.

Eriogonum undulatum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in xerophytic environments.
