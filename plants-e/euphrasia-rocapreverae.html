Euphrasia rocapreverae

Euphrasia rocapreverae, commonly known as the Cape York bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Cape York Peninsula in northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a small herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, blue to purple flowers resembling bells, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathland environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Euphrasia rocapreverae is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and is of significant ecological and botanical interest.
