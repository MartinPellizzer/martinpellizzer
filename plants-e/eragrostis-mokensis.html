Eragrostis mokensis

Eragrostis mokensis, commonly known as Mokensis lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Eragrostis mokensis is noted for its adaptability to dry and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
