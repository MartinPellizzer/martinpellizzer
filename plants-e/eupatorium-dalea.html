Eupatorium dalea

Eupatorium dalea, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, preferring moist, sunny environments.

Eupatorium dalea is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
