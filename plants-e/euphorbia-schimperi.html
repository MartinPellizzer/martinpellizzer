Euphorbia schimperi

Euphorbia schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian spurge, is a succulent plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
