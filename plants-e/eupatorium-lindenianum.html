Eupatorium lindenianum

Eupatorium lindenianum, commonly known as the Lindens' eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks, preferring moist, rich soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium lindenianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
