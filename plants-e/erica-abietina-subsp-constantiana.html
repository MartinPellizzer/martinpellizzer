Erica abietina subsp. constantiana

Erica abietina subsp.

constantiana, commonly known as the Alpine heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen. Erica abietina subsp.

constantiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine flora and fauna.
