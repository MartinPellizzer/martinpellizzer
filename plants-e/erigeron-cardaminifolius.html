Erigeron cardaminifolius

Erigeron cardaminifolius, commonly known as the rock eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.

Erigeron cardaminifolius is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.
