Eucalyptus kybeanensis

Eucalyptus kybeanensis, commonly known as the Kybean mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that open to reveal white or pale blue flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Eucalyptus kybeanensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
