Epipactis latifolia var. pycnostachys

Epipactis latifolia var. pycnostachys, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often more elaborate than the other petals.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments and is often found in association with other orchid species.
