Epilobium junceum subf. microphyllum

Epilobium junceum subf.

microphyllum, commonly known as narrowleaf willowherb, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in spikes at the top of the plant. The plant thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, streambanks, and marshes.

Its flowering period usually occurs in the summer months, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
