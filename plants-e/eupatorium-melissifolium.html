Eupatorium melissifolium

Eupatorium melissifolium, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it historically significant in traditional herbal practices.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
