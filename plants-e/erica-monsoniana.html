Erica monsoniana

Erica monsoniana, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica monsoniana is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom in spring and are usually pink or white in color.
