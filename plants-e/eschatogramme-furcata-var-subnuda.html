Eschatogramme furcata var. subnuda

Eschatogramme furcata var. subnuda, commonly known as the naked forked grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
