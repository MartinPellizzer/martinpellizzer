Ehretia kaessneri

Ehretia kaessneri, commonly known as the whiteflower ehretia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Ehretia kaessneri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
