Erodium birandianum

Erodium birandianum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is part of the Geraniaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
