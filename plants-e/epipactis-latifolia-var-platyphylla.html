Epipactis latifolia var. platyphylla

Epipactis latifolia var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and its striking, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments with adequate moisture.
