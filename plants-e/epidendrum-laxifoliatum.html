Epidendrum laxifoliatum

Epidendrum laxifoliatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often hairy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces pendulous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, yellow, and pink, during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This species is popular among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
