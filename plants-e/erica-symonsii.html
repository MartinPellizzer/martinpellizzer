Erica symonsii

Erica symonsii, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

Erica symonsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
