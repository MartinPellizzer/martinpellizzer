Erythrina maxima

Erythrina maxima, commonly known as the crimson flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has pinnate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds. Its growth habit includes a tall, often multi-stemmed structure with a dense canopy.

Erythrina maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
