Eriosema brachybotrys

Eriosema brachybotrys, commonly known as the small-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
