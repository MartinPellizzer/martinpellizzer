Evolvulus scoparioides

Evolvulus scoparioides, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual with prostrate or trailing growth habit, characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are followed by spherical, dry fruits containing several seeds.

Evolvulus scoparioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and brief floral display.
