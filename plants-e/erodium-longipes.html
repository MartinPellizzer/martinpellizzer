Erodium longipes

Erodium longipes, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Erodium longipes is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
