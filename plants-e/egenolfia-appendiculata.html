Egenolfia appendiculata

Egenolfia appendiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, succulent stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Egenolfia appendiculata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
