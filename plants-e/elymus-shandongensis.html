Elymus shandongensis

Elymus shandongensis, commonly known as the Shandong needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland environments. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often slightly hairy.

Elymus shandongensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
