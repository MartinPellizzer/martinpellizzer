Epilobium chilense var. latifolia

Epilobium chilense var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf willow herb, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often broad and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
