Evaiezoa esholtzi

Evaiezoa esholtzi, commonly known as the Esholt's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, showy purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine, white to grayish hairs.

Evaiezoa esholtzi is known for its adaptability to dry, rocky soils and its ability to thrive in full sun.
