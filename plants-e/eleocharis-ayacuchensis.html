Eleocharis ayacuchensis

Eleocharis ayacuchensis, commonly known as the Ayacuchensis spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
