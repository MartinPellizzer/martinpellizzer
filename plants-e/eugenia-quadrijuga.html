Eugenia quadrijuga

Eugenia quadrijuga, commonly known as the four-way eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, attracting various birds and small mammals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
