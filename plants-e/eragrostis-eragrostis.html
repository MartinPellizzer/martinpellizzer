Eragrostis eragrostis

Eragrostis eragrostis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions outside its native range.
