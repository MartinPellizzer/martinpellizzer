Eriogonum heermannii subsp. typicum

Eriogonum heermannii subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the Heermann's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has small, narrow leaves and is well adapted to arid and rocky environments. It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. Eriogonum heermannii subsp.

typicum plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
