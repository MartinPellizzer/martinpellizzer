Eupatorium duodecimiflorum

Eupatorium duodecimiflorum, commonly known as the twelve-flowered boneset, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
