Erythrodes lutea

Erythrodes lutea, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has succulent-like leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to shallow soil layers, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Erythrodes lutea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
