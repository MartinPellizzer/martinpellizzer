Elaeocarpus apiculatus

Elaeocarpus apiculatus, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and mountainous forests.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
