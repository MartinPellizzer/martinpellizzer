Eleocharis nipponica

Eleocharis nipponica, commonly known as Japanese spike-rush, is a species of sedges native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as rice fields and wetlands. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in shallow water or moist soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
