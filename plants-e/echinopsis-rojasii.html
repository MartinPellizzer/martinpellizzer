Echinopsis rojasii

Echinopsis rojasii, commonly known as the Rojasii cactus, is a species native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, columnar stem and prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Echinopsis rojasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
