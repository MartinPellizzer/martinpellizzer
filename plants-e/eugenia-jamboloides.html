Eugenia jamboloides

Eugenia jamboloides, commonly known as the Java apple or Java plum, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruit.
