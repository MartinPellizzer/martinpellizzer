Erica sodalis

Erica sodalis, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Erica sodalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
