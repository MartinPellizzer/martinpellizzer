Euonymus xylocarpus

Euonymus xylocarpus, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for hedges and garden landscapes.
