Erigeron biramosus

Erigeron biramosus, commonly known as the two-branched fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive branching pattern, especially on the lower part of the plant.

The showy white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
