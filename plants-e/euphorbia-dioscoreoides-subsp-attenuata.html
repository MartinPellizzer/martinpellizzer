Euphorbia dioscoreoides subsp. attenuata

Euphorbia dioscoreoides subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the slender-stemmed spurge, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences called cyathia. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and typically have a waxy texture. Euphorbia dioscoreoides subsp.

attenuata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
