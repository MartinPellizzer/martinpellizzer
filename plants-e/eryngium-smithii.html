Eryngium smithii

Eryngium smithii, commonly known as Smith's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eryngium smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
