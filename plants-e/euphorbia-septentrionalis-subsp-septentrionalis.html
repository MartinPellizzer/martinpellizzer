Euphorbia septentrionalis subsp. septentrionalis

Euphorbia septentrionalis subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern spurge, is a perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. Euphorbia septentrionalis subsp.

septentrionalis is often found in tundra and alpine meadows where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
