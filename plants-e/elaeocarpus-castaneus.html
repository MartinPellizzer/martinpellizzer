Elaeocarpus castaneus

Elaeocarpus castaneus, commonly known as the broadleaf chestnut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are white or pale pink. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release smooth, chestnut-like seeds. The plant is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

It has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
