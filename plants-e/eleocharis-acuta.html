Eleocharis acuta

Eleocharis acuta, commonly known as the sharp spike rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that helps it withstand waterlogged conditions.

Eleocharis acuta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
