Excoecaria orientalis var. daitoinsularis

Excoecaria orientalis var. daitoinsularis, commonly known as the Christmas tree or the Natal hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive red or orange center. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically lobed and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance and seasonal blooming pattern.
