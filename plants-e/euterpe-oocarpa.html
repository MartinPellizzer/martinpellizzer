Euterpe oocarpa

Euterpe oocarpa, commonly known as the açaí palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish flowers, which eventually develop into dark purple, nutrient-rich berries. The fruit is the primary economic product of the plant, valued for its high antioxidant content and use in food and supplements.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
