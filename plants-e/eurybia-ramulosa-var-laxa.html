Eurybia ramulosa var. laxa

Eurybia ramulosa var. laxa, commonly known as the laxa goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
