Echinocactus acanthostephes

Echinocactus acanthostephes, commonly known as the Mexican lion's tail, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on elongated, tubular bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
