Eragrostis thyrsoidea

Eragrostis thyrsoidea, commonly known as silverleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and slender, erect stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in native plant landscapes.
