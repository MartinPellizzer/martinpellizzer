Echeveria longiflora

Echeveria longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight.

Echeveria longiflora is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its compact size and ornamental appeal.
