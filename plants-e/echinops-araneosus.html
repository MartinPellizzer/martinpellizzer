Echinops araneosus

Echinops araneosus, commonly known as the spider flower or desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a spider's web, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Echinops araneosus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptive features make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
