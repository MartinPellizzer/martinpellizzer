Eucalyptus stuartiana var. amblycorys

Eucalyptus stuartiana var. amblycorys, commonly known as the Stuart's mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, occurring in clustered umbels, and are attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It prefers well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
