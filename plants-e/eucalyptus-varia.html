Eucalyptus varia

Eucalyptus varia, commonly known as the variable eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the eastern and southern regions of Australia, where it typically grows in dry, open forests and woodlands. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale yellow, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eucalyptus varia is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
