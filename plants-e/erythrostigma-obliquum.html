Erythrostigma obliquum

Erythrostigma obliquum, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are linear and often arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Erythrostigma obliquum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
