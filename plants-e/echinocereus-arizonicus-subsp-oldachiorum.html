Echinocereus arizonicus subsp. oldachiorum

Echinocereus arizonicus subsp.

oldachiorum, commonly known as the Arizona hedgehog cactus, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spiny, tuberculate structures that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects and bats. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in xerophytic gardens.
