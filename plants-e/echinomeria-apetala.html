Echinomeria apetala

Echinomeria apetala, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with soft, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. Its flowers are typically yellow, with no petals, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Echinomeria apetala is adapted to survive in harsh, sunny environments with minimal rainfall.
