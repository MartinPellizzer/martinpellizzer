Eucalyptus × purpurascens var. petiolulata

Eucalyptus × purpurascens var. petiolulata, commonly known as the purple-leafed gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple to reddish-brown foliage, which contrasts with its smooth, peeling bark. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 8 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
