Eleusine marginata

Eleusine marginata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silver or white margin. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish spikelets that are typically arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Eleusine marginata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
