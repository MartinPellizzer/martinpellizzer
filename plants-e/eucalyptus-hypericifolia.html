Eucalyptus hypericifolia

Eucalyptus hypericifolia, commonly known as the silver dollar tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark that peels in thin, papery strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
