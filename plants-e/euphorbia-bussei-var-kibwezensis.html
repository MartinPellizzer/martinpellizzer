Euphorbia bussei var. kibwezensis

Euphorbia bussei var. kibwezensis, commonly known as the Kibweze euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually small and may be covered with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This variant is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
