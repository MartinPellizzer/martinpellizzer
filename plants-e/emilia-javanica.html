Emilia javanica

Emilia javanica, commonly known as the velvet flower or blue bonnet, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Emilia javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
