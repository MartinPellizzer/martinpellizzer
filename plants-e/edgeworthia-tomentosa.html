Edgeworthia tomentosa

Edgeworthia tomentosa, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant's leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, woolly texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The Japanese snowbell is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Chinese and Japanese gardens.
