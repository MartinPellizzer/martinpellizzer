Emphysopus gunnii

Emphysopus gunnii, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the coastal regions of Antarctica and sub-Antarctic islands.

It thrives in harsh, cold environments with strong winds and limited nutrients, showcasing remarkable adaptability to extreme conditions. The plant has slender, tufted leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against wind damage. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy in its arctic habitat.

Emphysopus gunnii plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its extreme environment.
