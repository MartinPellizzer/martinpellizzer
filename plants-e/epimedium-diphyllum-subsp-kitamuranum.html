Epimedium diphyllum subsp. kitamuranum

Epimedium diphyllum subsp.

kitamuranum, commonly known as the Japanese barrenwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are borne on short, upright pedicels. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
