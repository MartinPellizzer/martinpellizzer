Erica gossypioides

Erica gossypioides, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are dense, leathery, and covered in fine white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Erica gossypioides is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
