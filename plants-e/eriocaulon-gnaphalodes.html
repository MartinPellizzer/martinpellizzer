Eriocaulon gnaphalodes

Eriocaulon gnaphalodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and wetland restoration projects.
