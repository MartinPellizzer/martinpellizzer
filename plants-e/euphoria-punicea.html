Euphoria punicea

Euphoria punicea, commonly known as the crimson euphoria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Euphoria punicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
