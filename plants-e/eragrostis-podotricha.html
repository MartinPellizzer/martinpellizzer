Eragrostis podotricha

Eragrostis podotricha, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to warm temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Silky fingergrass is often found in regions with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
