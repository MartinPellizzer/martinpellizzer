Epidendrum imbricatum

Epidendrum imbricatum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and can thrive in a variety of light conditions. It is well-suited for cultivation as a houseplant or in a greenhouse due to its adaptability.

Epidendrum imbricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
