Eugenia velutiflora

Eugenia velutiflora, commonly known as the velvet-flowered eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
