Erysimum pusillum subsp. rechingeri

Erysimum pusillum subsp.

rechingeri, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it conserve moisture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
