Eugenia peregrina

Eugenia peregrina, commonly known as the wild pineapple or pineapple guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit is a small, yellow or orange berry with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Eugenia peregrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
