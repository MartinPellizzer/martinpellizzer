Erica manifesta var. campanulata

Erica manifesta var. campanulata, commonly known as the bell heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the heather family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Erica manifesta var. campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
