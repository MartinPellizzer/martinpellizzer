Eragrostis bipinnata

Eragrostis bipinnata, commonly known as the two-feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive, finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Eragrostis bipinnata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
