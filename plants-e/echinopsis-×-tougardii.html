Echinopsis × tougardii

Echinopsis × tougardii, commonly known as the Tougard's moon cactus, is a hybrid cactus species resulting from the cross between Echinopsis chamaecaulis and Echinopsis terscheckii.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, fleshy stems that are typically green or blue-green in color, often with a white or pale waxy coating. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, yellow, or red, depending on the parent species. It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in well-draining soil with ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
