Epidendrum asplundii

Epidendrum asplundii, commonly known as the Asplund's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a rhizomatous structure with multiple stems and is known for its clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Epidendrum asplundii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
