Erica tetralix f. mollis

Erica tetralix f. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compact, leathery leaves that are softer and more pubescent compared to other Erica species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
