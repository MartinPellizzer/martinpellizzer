Euphorbia taihsiensis

Euphorbia taihsiensis, commonly known as the Taihsi spurge, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling.
