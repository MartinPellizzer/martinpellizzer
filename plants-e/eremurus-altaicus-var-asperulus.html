Eremurus altaicus var. asperulus

Eremurus altaicus var. asperulus, commonly known as the Siberian Spearwort, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a spear, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
