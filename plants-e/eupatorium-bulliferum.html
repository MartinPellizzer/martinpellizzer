Eupatorium bulliferum

Eupatorium bulliferum, commonly known as the showy eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Eupatorium bulliferum is often found in moist habitats like swamps and wet meadows.
