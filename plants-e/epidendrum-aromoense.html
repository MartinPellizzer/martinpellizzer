Epidendrum aromoense

Epidendrum aromoense, commonly known as the fragrant epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often epiphytic on trees or rocks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral fragrance.
