Erythrina raja

Erythrina raja, commonly known as the crimson flame tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has feathery, trifoliate leaves and produces long, slender pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a notable addition to tropical gardens and landscapes.
