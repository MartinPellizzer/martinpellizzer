Ericoides humifusum

Ericoides humifusum, commonly known as the lowland heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its leathery, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, nutrient-poor soils, often in heathlands or moorland environments. It plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.

Ericoides humifusum is well adapted to harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
