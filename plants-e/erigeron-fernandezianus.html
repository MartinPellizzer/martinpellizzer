Erigeron fernandezianus

Erigeron fernandezianus, commonly known as the Fernandez's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.
