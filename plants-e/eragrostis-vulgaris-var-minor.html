Eragrostis vulgaris var. minor

Eragrostis vulgaris var. minor, commonly known as dwarf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, tufted culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
