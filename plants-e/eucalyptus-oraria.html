Eucalyptus oraria

Eucalyptus oraria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrubs with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The flowers are typically small and white or pale pink, appearing in clusters along the stems. This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various native animals.
