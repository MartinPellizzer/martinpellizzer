Euphorbia angulata f. nemoralis

Euphorbia angulata f. nemoralis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a perennial plant characterized by its succulent, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to regions of southern Africa. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia, which are typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
