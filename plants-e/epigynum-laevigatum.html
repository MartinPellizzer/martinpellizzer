Epigynum laevigatum

Epigynum laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth epigynum, is a lesser-known plant species with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that provide a unique texture compared to other similar species. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer months.

Epigynum laevigatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
