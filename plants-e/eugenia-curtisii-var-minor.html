Eugenia curtisii var. minor

Eugenia curtisii var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a compact evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
