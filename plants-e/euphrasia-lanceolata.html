Euphrasia lanceolata

Euphrasia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf eyebright, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and dark green. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, often forming clusters at the tips of the stems.

Euphrasia lanceolata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
