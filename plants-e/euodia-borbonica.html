Euodia borbonica

Euodia borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar laurier or star anise tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that mature to a dark purple color. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and fruits, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Euodia borbonica is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
