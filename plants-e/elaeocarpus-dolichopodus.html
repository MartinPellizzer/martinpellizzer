Elaeocarpus dolichopodus

Elaeocarpus dolichopodus, commonly known as the long-fruited elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody, elongated fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
