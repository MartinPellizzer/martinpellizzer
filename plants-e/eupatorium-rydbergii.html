Eupatorium rydbergii

Eupatorium rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and marshes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium rydbergii is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects and birds.
