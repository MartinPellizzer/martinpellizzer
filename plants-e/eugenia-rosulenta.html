Eugenia rosulenta

Eugenia rosulenta, commonly known as the rose-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, rose-colored leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe.

Eugenia rosulenta is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
