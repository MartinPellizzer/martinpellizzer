Echites obtusus

Echites obtusus, commonly known as the broadleaf echites, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences, and the plant is known for its aromatic foliage.

Echites obtusus is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
