Eryngium andicola

Eryngium andicola, commonly known as the Andean thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is often branched and rigid.

Eryngium andicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
