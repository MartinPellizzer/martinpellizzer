Elaeocarpus oreogena

Elaeocarpus oreogena, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the highland regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. This species is valued for its ornamental potential and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.
