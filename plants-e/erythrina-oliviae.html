Erythrina oliviae

Erythrina oliviae, commonly known as the Olivia's flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks.

Erythrina oliviae is also known for its ability to thrive in coastal areas due to its tolerance to salt spray.
