Ervilia singarensis

Ervilia singarensis, commonly known as the Singarensis orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a slender, upright stem and a single, showy flower. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, producing a pale yellow or greenish-white flower with intricate patterns. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the humid, shaded environments of its native habitat.

Ervilia singarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
