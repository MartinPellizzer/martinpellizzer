Eupatorium michelianum

Eupatorium michelianum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
