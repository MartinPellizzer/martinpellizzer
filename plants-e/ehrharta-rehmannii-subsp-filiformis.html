Ehrharta rehmannii subsp. filiformis

Ehrharta rehmannii subsp.

filiformis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
