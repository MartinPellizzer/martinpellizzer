Eupatorium retrofractum

Eupatorium retrofractum, commonly known as the fragrant weed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Eupatorium retrofractum is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
