Epilobium rigidum var. canescens

Epilobium rigidum var. canescens, commonly known as hairy willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant features erect, often glandular-stipitate stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface and a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered flowers with pink to reddish petals.

The species is known for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivorous insects.
