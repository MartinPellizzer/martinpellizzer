Epipactis gigantea var. thunbergii

Epipactis gigantea var. thunbergii, commonly known as the giant helleborine, is a striking orchid native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer, featuring a distinctive greenish-yellow lip with intricate patterns. The plant prefers moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in rich, loamy soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

The species is notable for its complex pollination mechanisms, involving specific insect species that are attracted to its nectar-rich flowers.
