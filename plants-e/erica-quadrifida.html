Erica quadrifida

Erica quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Erica quadrifida is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
