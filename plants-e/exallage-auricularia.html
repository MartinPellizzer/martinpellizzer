Exallage auricularia

Exallage auricularia, commonly known as the ear-shaped lichen, is a foliose lichen characterized by its leaf-like thallus.

It typically grows on tree bark, stones, or soil in temperate regions. The plant has a complex structure with a cortex, medulla, and algae-containing layers. Its color ranges from pale yellow to brown, depending on the substrate and environmental conditions.

It plays a role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
