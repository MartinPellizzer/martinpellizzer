Eriogonum latifolium subsp. decurrens

Eriogonum latifolium subsp.

decurrens, commonly known as the broadleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy and grayish-green in appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its hardy nature in arid environments.
