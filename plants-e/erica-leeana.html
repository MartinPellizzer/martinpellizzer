Erica leeana

Erica leeana, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing on sandy or rocky soils. Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient species in maritime climates.

Erica leeana plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
