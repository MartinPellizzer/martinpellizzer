Eupatorium hyrcanicum

Eupatorium hyrcanicum, commonly known as the Persian feverfew, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Eupatorium hyrcanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
