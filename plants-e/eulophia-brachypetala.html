Eulophia brachypetala

Eulophia brachypetala, commonly known as the short-lip eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces racemes of small, showy orchid flowers with distinctive spurred petals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia brachypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
