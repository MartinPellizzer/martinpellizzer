Eritrichium leiocarpum

Eritrichium leiocarpum, commonly known as the white hair vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy stems and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, smooth, yellowish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Eritrichium leiocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils.
