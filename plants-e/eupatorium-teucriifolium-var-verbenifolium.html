Eupatorium teucriifolium var. verbenifolium

Eupatorium teucriifolium var. verbenifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in native plant gardens for its attractive appearance and ecological benefits.
