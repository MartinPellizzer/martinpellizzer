Euphorbia cylindrica

Euphorbia cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, cylindrical stem that is often ribbed and covered in a thick layer of wax. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
