Ericoides cymosum

Ericoides cymosum, commonly known as the clustered ericoid, is a low-growing shrub native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Ericoides cymosum is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical research.
