Eugenia angustifolia var. angustissima

Eugenia angustifolia var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
