Echinocystis scabrida

Echinocystis scabrida, commonly known as the rough puffer gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States and parts of Central America. The plant produces large, spiny fruits that are typically harvested when mature. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

The species is notable for its unique, puffy, spiky fruit structure that distinguishes it from other gourds.
