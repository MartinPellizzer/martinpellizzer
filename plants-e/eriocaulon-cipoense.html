Eriocaulon cipoense

Eriocaulon cipoense, commonly known as the Cipo sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in wetland and marshy environments. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. It plays a role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
