Eryngium lazae

Eryngium lazae, commonly known as the Laza thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust, upright stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Eryngium lazae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
