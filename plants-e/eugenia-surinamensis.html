Eugenia surinamensis

Eugenia surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam apple, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
