Emilia ambifaria

Emilia ambifaria, commonly known as the double-flowered emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellow flowers with a distinctive double petal structure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Emilia ambifaria is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
