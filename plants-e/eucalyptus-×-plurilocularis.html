Eucalyptus × plurilocularis

Eucalyptus × plurilocularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of development. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and fire resistance.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
