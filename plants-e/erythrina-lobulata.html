Erythrina lobulata

Erythrina lobulata, commonly known as the flame tree or red bean tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain flat, bean-like seeds. Erythrina lobulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
