Eriogonum annuum var. pauciflorum

Eriogonum annuum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its basal leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as gravelly slopes and open woodlands. It produces small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
