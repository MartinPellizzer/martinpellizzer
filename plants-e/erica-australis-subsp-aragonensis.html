Erica australis subsp. aragonensis

Erica australis subsp.

aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon heath, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is adapted to Mediterranean climates, exhibiting drought tolerance and resistance to salt spray.

This subspecies plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
