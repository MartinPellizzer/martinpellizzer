Eupatorium bruneri

Eupatorium bruneri, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and opposite, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
