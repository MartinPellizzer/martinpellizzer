Echinocereus coccineus subsp. transpecosensis

Echinocereus coccineus subsp.

transpecosensis, commonly known as the Transpecos Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the rocky hills of Texas and New Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 30 inches tall.

The plant produces vibrant red flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and bats. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and conservation efforts.
