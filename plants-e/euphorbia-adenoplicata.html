Euphorbia adenoplicata

Euphorbia adenoplicata, commonly known as the white-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a distinctive white or pale green spines along its stems, which provide protection against herbivores. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Euphorbia adenoplicata is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appearance.
