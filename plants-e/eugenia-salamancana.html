Eugenia salamancana

Eugenia salamancana, commonly known as the Salamanca guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to guavas but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible yield.

Eugenia salamancana is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
