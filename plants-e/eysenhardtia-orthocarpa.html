Eysenhardtia orthocarpa

Eysenhardtia orthocarpa, commonly known as the California coffeeberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, red, berry-like fruits that mature in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
