Euphorbia lingiana

Euphorbia lingiana, commonly known as the Ling's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and distinctive yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Euphorbia lingiana is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
