Euphorbia regina

Euphorbia regina, commonly known as the Queen's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, branched stem covered in a thick layer of wax, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, giving it an eye-catching appearance. Its leaves are typically fleshy and alternate along the stem, and it is known for its ability to store water efficiently.

Euphorbia regina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
