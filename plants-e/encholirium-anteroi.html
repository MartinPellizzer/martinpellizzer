Encholirium anteroi

Encholirium anteroi, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a striking bromeliad native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround a cluster of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Encholirium anteroi is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
