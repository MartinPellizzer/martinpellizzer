Euchresta tenuifolia

Euchresta tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf echinocactus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to desert landscapes.
