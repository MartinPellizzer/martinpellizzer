Echinocactus arizonicus

Echinocactus arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spiny cactus that typically grows to a height of about 10 inches. The plant features a ribbed stem with prominent, white to yellow spines that provide protection from herbivores. It produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Echinocactus arizonicus is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
