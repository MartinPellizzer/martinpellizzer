Eugenia laevifolia

Eugenia laevifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
