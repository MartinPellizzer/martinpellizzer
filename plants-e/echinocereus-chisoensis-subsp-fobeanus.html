Echinocereus chisoensis subsp. fobeanus

Echinocereus chisoensis subsp.

fobeanus, commonly known as the Chisos Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the Chisos Mountains in Texas. It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular or cylindrical shape, often featuring a ribbed structure.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.
