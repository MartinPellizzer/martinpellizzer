Epidendrum rupestre

Epidendrum rupestre, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or in rocky soils, exhibiting a lithophytic or epiphytic growth pattern. Its leaves are usually fleshy and dark green, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Epidendrum rupestre is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
