Eugenia curtiflora

Eugenia curtiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia curtiflora is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
