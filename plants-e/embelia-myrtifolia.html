Embelia myrtifolia

Embelia myrtifolia, commonly known as the Indian embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
