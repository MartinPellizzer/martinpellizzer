Elionurus tripsacoides var. ciliaris

Elionurus tripsacoides var. ciliaris, commonly known as the ciliate grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with hairy margins. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed areas, showing adaptability to various soil types. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
