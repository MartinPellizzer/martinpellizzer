Euodia dubia

Euodia dubia, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces black, red, and white berries, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils with antimicrobial properties.

Euodia dubia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal applications.
