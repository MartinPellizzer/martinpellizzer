Episcia pulchella

Episcia pulchella, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep green to shades of purple and red. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Episcia pulchella is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
