Eugenia pruniformis

Eugenia pruniformis, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange when mature. Eugenia pruniformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and year-round flowering.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
