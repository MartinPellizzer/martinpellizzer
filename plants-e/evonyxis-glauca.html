Evonyxis glauca

Evonyxis glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with a spreading habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Evonyxis glauca is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
