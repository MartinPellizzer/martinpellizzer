Euphorbia esula var. parvifolia

Euphorbia esula var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and has small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils.

It is often found in disturbed areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
