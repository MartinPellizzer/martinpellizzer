Eugenia domestica

Eugenia domestica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, sweet flavor. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
