Ericoides involvens

Ericoides involvens, commonly known as the curled heath, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces bell-shaped, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Ericoides involvens typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in shaded forest environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
