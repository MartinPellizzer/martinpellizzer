Echinodorus cordifolius f. lanceolatus

Echinodorus cordifolius f. lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a variant of the broad-leaf waterweed species.

It is a semi-aquatic plant native to South America, often found in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant features long, narrow lanceolate leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, and it thrives in moderate to high light conditions.

This variety is popular in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.
