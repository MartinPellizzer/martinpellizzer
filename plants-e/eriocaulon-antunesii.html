Eriocaulon antunesii

Eriocaulon antunesii, commonly known as the Antunes' hairgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. This species typically grows in wet, marshy environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures include small, clustered flowers that are usually inconspicuous and borne on short, erect stalks.

Eriocaulon antunesii plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is of interest to botanists studying aquatic and semi-aquatic flora.
