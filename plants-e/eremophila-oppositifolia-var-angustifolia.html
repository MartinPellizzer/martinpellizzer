Eremophila oppositifolia var. angustifolia

Eremophila oppositifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
