Eupatorium filifolium var. genuina

Eupatorium filifolium var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slight hairy texture. This plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
