Epidendrum caluerorum

Epidendrum caluerorum, commonly known as the Caluera Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. The plant features erect, branching stems and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Epidendrum caluerorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
