Echinospermum calathicarpum

Echinospermum calathicarpum, commonly known as the hairy burr daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers surrounded by a series of bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains small, hard seeds.

Echinospermum calathicarpum is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
