Euchaetis avisylvana

Euchaetis avisylvana, commonly known as the silverleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
