Euphorbia carinulata

Euphorbia carinulata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin, typical of many euphorbias. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
