Erica prestoniensis

Erica prestoniensis, commonly known as the Preston's heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica prestoniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.
