Eugenia brachycalyx

Eugenia brachycalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in heathlands and coastal scrub.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a valued plant in native gardens.
