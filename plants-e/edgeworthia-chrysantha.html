Edgeworthia chrysantha

Edgeworthia chrysantha, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and its unique, lantern-shaped, orange-red bracts that appear in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated, narrow fruits. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its distinctive foliage and colorful bracts make it a visually striking addition to the landscape.
