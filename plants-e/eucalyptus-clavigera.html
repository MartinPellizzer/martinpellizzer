Eucalyptus clavigera

Eucalyptus clavigera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. Eucalyptus clavigera is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
