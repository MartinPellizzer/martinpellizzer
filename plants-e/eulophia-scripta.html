Eulophia scripta

Eulophia scripta, commonly known as the script lily, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a tall, branching inflorescence that bears numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the summer months.

Eulophia scripta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
