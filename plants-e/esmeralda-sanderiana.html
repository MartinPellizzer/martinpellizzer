Esmeralda sanderiana

Esmeralda sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's esmeralda, is a rare and striking plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Due to its limited habitat and specific environmental needs, it is considered a conservation concern.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
