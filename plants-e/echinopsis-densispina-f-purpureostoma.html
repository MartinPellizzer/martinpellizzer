Echinopsis densispina f. purpureostoma

Echinopsis densispina f. purpureostoma, commonly known as the Purple-stemmed Hedgehog Cactus, is a unique cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are often tinged with a striking purple hue, especially on the stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and colorful stems, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
