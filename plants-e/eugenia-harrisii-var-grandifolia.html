Eugenia harrisii var. grandifolia

Eugenia harrisii var. grandifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
