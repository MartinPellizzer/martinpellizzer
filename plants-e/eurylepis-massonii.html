Eurylepis massonii

Eurylepis massonii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
