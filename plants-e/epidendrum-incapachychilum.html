Epidendrum incapachychilum

Epidendrum incapachychilum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian Epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of Ecuador.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, stocky rhizome and numerous pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
