Epidendrum bidens

Epidendrum bidens, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces two-lobed, yellowish-green to brownish-purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Epidendrum bidens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
