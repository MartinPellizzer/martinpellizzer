Eremophila jucunda subsp. jucunda

Eremophila jucunda subsp.

jucunda, commonly known as the slender eremophila, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia. It typically grows to a height of about 1 meter and has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to red, usually blooming in spring. It is adapted to low rainfall and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suited to dry environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
