Elsholtzia capituligera

Elsholtzia capituligera, commonly known as the mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine environments and is well adapted to cold, dry climates.

Elsholtzia capituligera is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
