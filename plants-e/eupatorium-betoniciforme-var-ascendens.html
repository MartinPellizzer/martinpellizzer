Eupatorium betoniciforme var. ascendens

Eupatorium betoniciforme var. ascendens, commonly known as the ascending boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and wet meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
