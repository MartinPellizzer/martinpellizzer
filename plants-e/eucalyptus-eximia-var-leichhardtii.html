Eucalyptus eximia var. leichhardtii

Eucalyptus eximia var. leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fibrous bark and lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to greyish hairs. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pale yellow, which open to reveal creamy-white flowers. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, and is often found in open woodlands or on sandy soils.

The species is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional and commercial applications.
