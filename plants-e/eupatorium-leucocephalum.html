Eupatorium leucocephalum

Eupatorium leucocephalum, commonly known as white eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological habitats.
