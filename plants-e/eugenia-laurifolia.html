Eugenia laurifolia

Eugenia laurifolia, commonly known as the shining evergreen or silverleaf eugenia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Eugenia laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its dense foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
