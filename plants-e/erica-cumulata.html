Erica cumulata

Erica cumulata, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to acidic and nutrient-poor environments, making it a hardy plant in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica cumulata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
