Erythrocephalum marginatum

Erythrocephalum marginatum, commonly known as the coastal flax or sea daisies, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its bright pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, often forming dense clusters. The plant has narrow, silver-green leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Erythrocephalum marginatum is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
