Encyclia sceptra

Encyclia sceptra, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-colored flowers with prominent green veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

Encyclia sceptra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique floral display.
