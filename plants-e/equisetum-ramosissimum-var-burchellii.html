Equisetum ramosissimum var. burchellii

Equisetum ramosissimum var. burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's horse-tail, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Equisetaceae.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a tall, spore-bearing stem that emerges in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a trait shared with other ancient plant species.

Its morphology reflects its evolutionary history as one of the oldest lineages of land plants.
