Elymus aemulans

Elymus aemulans, commonly known as the western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Elymus aemulans plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
