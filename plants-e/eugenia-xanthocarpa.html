Eugenia xanthocarpa

Eugenia xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits. The flowers are fragrant and white, often appearing in clusters, and the fruit has a mild, sweet flavor with a slight tartness.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and culinary uses.
