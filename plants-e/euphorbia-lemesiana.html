Euphorbia lemesiana

Euphorbia lemesiana, commonly known as the Lemesos euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its latex contains toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
