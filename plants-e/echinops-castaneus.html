Echinops castaneus

Echinops castaneus, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small, fuzzy balls. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by the distinctive seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial.

Echinops castaneus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
