Eugenia limnoea

Eugenia limnoea, commonly known as the water myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows near water bodies, such as swamps and wetlands, and thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating. Its flowers are fragrant and white or pale pink, clustered in small groups, and it produces small, edible berries.

Eugenia limnoea plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
