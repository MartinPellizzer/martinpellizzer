Eragrostis dumasiana

Eragrostis dumasiana, commonly known as Dumas' lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
