Erysimum dubium var. pyrenaicum

Erysimum dubium var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and the flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
