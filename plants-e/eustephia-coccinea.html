Eustephia coccinea

Eustephia coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its striking floral display attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
