Eulophia caloptera

Eulophia caloptera, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and produces multiple leaves that are often broad and fleshy. The plant has showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological studies.
