Evax palaestina

Evax palaestina, commonly known as the Palestinian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, a capsule, contains several seeds and is often used in traditional medicine.

However, it is toxic and should not be ingested.
