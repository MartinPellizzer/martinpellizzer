Euonymus acanthocarpus var. laxus

Euonymus acanthocarpus var. laxus, commonly known as the laxus euonymus, is a variant of the euonymus plant characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is a deciduous shrub native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly textured surface. Its flowers are inconspicuous, but the plant produces vibrant red berries that persist into winter, adding ornamental value.

Euonymus acanthocarpus var. laxus is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
