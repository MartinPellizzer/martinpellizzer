Evolvulus thymiflorus

Evolvulus thymiflorus, commonly known as the thyme-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of thyme. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, colorful plant.
