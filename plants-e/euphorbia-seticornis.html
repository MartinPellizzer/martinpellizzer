Euphorbia seticornis

Euphorbia seticornis, commonly known as the horned spurges, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to arid environments. It produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin.

Euphorbia seticornis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
