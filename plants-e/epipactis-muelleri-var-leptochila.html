Epipactis muelleri var. leptochila

Epipactis muelleri var. leptochila, commonly known as the slender-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, preferring rich, organic soils. It has a complex root system that helps it absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

This orchid is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
