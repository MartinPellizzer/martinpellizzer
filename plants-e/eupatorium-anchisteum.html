Eupatorium anchisteum

Eupatorium anchisteum, commonly known as New Jersey tea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
