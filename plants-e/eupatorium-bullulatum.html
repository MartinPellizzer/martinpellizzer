Eupatorium bullulatum

Eupatorium bullulatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-to-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
