Erica phillipsii

Erica phillipsii, commonly known as the Phillips' heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica phillipsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
