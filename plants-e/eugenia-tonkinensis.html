Eugenia tonkinensis

Eugenia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The wood of Eugenia tonkinensis is also valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts.
