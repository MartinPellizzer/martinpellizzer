Eupatorium glyptophlebium

Eupatorium glyptophlebium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage that turns a striking blue-gray color as it matures. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall, consist of small white to pale blue florets arranged in dense, mist-like clusters.

Eupatorium glyptophlebium is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges.
