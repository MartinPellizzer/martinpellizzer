Euphorbia nevadensis subsp. nevadensis

Euphorbia nevadensis subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It is adapted to rocky soils and is often found in desert and foothill habitats.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions with minimal water requirements.
