Eulophia candida

Eulophia candida, commonly known as the white eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Eulophia candida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
