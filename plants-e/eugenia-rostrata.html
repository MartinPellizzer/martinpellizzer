Eugenia rostrata

Eugenia rostrata, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 10 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, tangy flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
