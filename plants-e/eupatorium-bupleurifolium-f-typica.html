Eupatorium bupleurifolium f. typica

Eupatorium bupleurifolium f. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Eupatorium genus.
