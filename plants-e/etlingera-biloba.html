Etlingera biloba

Etlingera biloba, commonly known as the white torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking white or pale pink flowers that resemble torches. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a glossy surface.

Etlingera biloba is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
