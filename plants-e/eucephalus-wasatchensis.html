Eucephalus wasatchensis

Eucephalus wasatchensis, commonly known as the Wasatch bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eucephalus wasatchensis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
