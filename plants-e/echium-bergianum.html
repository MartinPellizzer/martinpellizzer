Echium bergianum

Echium bergianum, commonly known as the Alpine evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Echium bergianum is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
