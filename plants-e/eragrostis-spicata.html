Eragrostis spicata

Eragrostis spicata, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months, and it is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Eragrostis spicata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
