Eucyperus martianus

Eucyperus martianus, commonly known as the Martian sedges, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eucyperus martianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
