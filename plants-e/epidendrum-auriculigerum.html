Epidendrum auriculigerum

Epidendrum auriculigerum, commonly known as the auriculate epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a velvety appearance. The flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

This species is popular among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
