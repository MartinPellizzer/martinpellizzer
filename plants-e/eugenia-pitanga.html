Eugenia pitanga

Eugenia pitanga, commonly known as Surinam cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C and commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications. Eugenia pitanga thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
