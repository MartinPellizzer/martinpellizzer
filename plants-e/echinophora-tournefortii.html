Echinophora tournefortii

Echinophora tournefortii, commonly known as the desert hedgehog.

It is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are typically small and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

Echinophora tournefortii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
