Eritrichium persicum

Eritrichium persicum, commonly known as Persian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Eritrichium persicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
