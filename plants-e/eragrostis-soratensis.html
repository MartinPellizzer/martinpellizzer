Eragrostis soratensis

Eragrostis soratensis, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is drought-tolerant and can thrive in a variety of climatic conditions.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
