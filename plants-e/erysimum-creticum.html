Erysimum creticum

Erysimum creticum, commonly known as the Cretan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow with four petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky or sandy terrain.

The species is notable for its resilience and ability to bloom in harsh environmental conditions.
