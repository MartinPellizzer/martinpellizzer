Eriophorum chamissonis var. albidum

Eriophorum chamissonis var. albidum, commonly known as the white tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are typically pale or white in color, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, slender, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Cyperaceae family, sharing similar structural and ecological traits.
