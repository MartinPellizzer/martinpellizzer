Eriosema tuberosum

Eriosema tuberosum, commonly known as the African potato or *Cape groundnut*, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, tuberous roots and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Eriosema tuberosum is also valued as a forage plant for livestock in arid regions.
