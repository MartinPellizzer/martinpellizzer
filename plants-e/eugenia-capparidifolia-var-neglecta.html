Eugenia capparidifolia var. neglecta

Eugenia capparidifolia var. neglecta, commonly known as the small-leaved clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
