Eragrostis vulgaris subsp. barrelieri

Eragrostis vulgaris subsp.

barrelieri, commonly known as Barrelier's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control.
