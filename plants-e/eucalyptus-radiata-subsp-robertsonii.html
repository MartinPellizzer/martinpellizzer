Eucalyptus radiata subsp. robertsonii

Eucalyptus radiata subsp.

robertsonii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically narrow and have a distinct yellowish-green color.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale blue to white in color. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and unique foliage.
