Eriogonum niveum var. suksdorfii

Eriogonum niveum var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the White-flowered Buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is part of the Polygonaceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
