Echites hispidus

Echites hispidus, commonly known as the hairy echites, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a sturdy, branched stem covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Echites hispidus is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
