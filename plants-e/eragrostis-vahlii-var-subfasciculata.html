Eragrostis vahlii var. subfasciculata

Eragrostis vahlii var. subfasciculata, commonly known as the narrow-panicled lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, tufted stems with dense, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often clustered in fascicles, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This grass is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
