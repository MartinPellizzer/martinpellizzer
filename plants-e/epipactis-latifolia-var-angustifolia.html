Epipactis latifolia var. angustifolia

Epipactis latifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are spurred at the base and have a distinctive, intricate structure typical of orchids.

It is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its ornamental value in garden settings.
