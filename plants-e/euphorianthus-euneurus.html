Euphorianthus euneurus

Euphorianthus euneurus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with unique floral structures. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant's sap is often milky and can be toxic, a common trait among members of its family.
