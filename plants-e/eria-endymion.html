Eria endymion

Eria endymion, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, velvety leaves and striking, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in acidic soils. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Eria endymion is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in its natural habitat.
