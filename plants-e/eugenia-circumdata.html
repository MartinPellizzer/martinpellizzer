Eugenia circumdata

Eugenia circumdata, commonly known as the red-eyed treefrog plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that are often surrounded by red calyces, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

Eugenia circumdata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
