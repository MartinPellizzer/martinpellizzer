Erythrodes parvula

Erythrodes parvula, commonly known as the small red orchid, is a delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and vibrant red petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small, usually less than 2 centimeters in diameter, and bloom in clusters.

Erythrodes parvula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
