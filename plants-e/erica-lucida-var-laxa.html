Erica lucida var. laxa

Erica lucida var. laxa, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has wiry, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
