Eria halconensis

Eria halconensis, commonly known as the Halconensis orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant blooms. The plant typically produces small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pendulous flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
