Eupatorium hymenolepis

Eupatorium hymenolepis, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Eupatorium hymenolepis is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
