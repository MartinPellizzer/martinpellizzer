Eupatorium baccharoides var. fratris

Eupatorium baccharoides var. fratris, commonly known as the Fraternal eupatorium, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to aquatic environments.
