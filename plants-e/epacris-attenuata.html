Epacris attenuata

Epacris attenuata, commonly known as the slender epacris, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and sunny conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Epacris attenuata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
