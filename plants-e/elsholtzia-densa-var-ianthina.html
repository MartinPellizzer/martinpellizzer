Elsholtzia densa var. ianthina

Elsholtzia densa var. ianthina, commonly known as the dark-flowered elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or dark violet in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for botanical gardens and native plant collections.
