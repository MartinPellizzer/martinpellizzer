Erigeron semibarbatus var. monocephalus

Erigeron semibarbatus var. monocephalus, commonly known as the single-head fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in xeriscaped gardens.
