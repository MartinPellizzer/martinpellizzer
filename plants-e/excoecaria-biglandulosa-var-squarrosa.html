Excoecaria biglandulosa var. squarrosa

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The leaves are typically serrated and have a rough texture, while the stems are woody and often branched.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
