Eria acervata

Eria acervata, commonly known as the clustered spike orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs and produces racemes of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Eria acervata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouse environments due to its specific humidity and temperature requirements.
