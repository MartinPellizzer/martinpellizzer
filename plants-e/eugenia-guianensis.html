Eugenia guianensis

Eugenia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan evergreen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
