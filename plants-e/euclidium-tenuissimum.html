Euclidium tenuissimum

Euclidium tenuissimum, commonly known as the slender euclidium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires careful protection.
