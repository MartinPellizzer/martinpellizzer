Euphorbia zygophylloides var. cymulosa

Euphorbia zygophylloides var. cymulosa, commonly known as the Cymulosa euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences containing yellowish flowers surrounded by modified leaves. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
