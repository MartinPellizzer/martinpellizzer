Epilobium hornemannii f. album

Epilobium hornemannii f. album, commonly known as the white willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features showy, white to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
