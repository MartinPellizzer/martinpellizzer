Euphrasia gracilis f. atropurpurea

Euphrasia gracilis f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-eyed gentian, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features delicate, slender stems that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers with striking purple eyes. The flowers are typically blue or purple in color, with prominent dark veins that create a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, downy hairs.
