Elaeocarpus stipularis var. rejangensis

Elaeocarpus stipularis var. rejangensis, commonly known as the Rejang elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
