Elymus tauri subsp. pertenuis

Elymus tauri subsp.

pertenuis, commonly known as the slender taurian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and mountainous areas.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are usually covered with fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small clusters of flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
