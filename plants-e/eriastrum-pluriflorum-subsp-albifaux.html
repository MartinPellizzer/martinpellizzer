Eriastrum pluriflorum subsp. albifaux

Eriastrum pluriflorum subsp.

albifaux, commonly known as the white-flowered eriastrum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
