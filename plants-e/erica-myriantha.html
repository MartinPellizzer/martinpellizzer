Erica myriantha

Erica myriantha, commonly known as the Cape rosemary, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Erica myriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aromatic leaves and seasonal blooms.

It thrives in full sun and requires minimal water once established.
