Elephantopus scaber var. plurisetus

Elephantopus scaber var. plurisetus, commonly known as the rough elephantopus, is a variant of the elephantopus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
