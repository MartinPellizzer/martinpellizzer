Epipactis microphylla

Epipactis microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and inconspicuous, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by flies, which are attracted to its scent and color.

Epipactis microphylla is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance.
