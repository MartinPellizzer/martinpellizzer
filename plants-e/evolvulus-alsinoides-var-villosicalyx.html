Evolvulus alsinoides var. villosicalyx

Evolvulus alsinoides var. villosicalyx, commonly known as hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features include alternate leaves and a fibrous root system, making it adaptable to various environments.
