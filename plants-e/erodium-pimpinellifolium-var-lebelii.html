Erodium pimpinellifolium var. lebelii

Erodium pimpinellifolium var. lebelii, commonly known as the Lebel's common bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its seed pods split open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
