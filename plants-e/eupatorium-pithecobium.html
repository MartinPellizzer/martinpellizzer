Eupatorium pithecobium

Eupatorium pithecobium, commonly known as the golden anise or yellow woodruff, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and unique floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
