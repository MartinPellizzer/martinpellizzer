Eleocharis disciformis

Eleocharis disciformis, commonly known as the flat-spike sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. Eleocharis disciformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
