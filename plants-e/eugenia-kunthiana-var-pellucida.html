Eugenia kunthiana var. pellucida

Eugenia kunthiana var. pellucida, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The variety pellucida is distinguished by its particularly white flowers and is a popular choice for tropical gardens.
