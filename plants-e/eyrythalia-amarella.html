Eyrythalia amarella

Eyrythalia amarella, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features delicate, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
