Eriolaena malvacea

Eriolaena malvacea, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal and forested areas.

Eriolaena malvacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
