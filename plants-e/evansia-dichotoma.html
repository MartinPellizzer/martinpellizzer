Evansia dichotoma

Evansia dichotoma, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle floral display to the desert landscape. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to quickly absorb moisture from infrequent rainfall.

Evansia dichotoma plays an important ecological role by stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
