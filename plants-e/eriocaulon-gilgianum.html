Eriocaulon gilgianum

Eriocaulon gilgianum, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and aquatic or wetland habitat.

It typically grows in marshy areas, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often reddish or purplish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.

Eriocaulon gilgianum plays a vital role in filtering water and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
