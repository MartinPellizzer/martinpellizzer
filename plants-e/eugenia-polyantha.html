Eugenia polyantha

Eugenia polyantha, commonly known as the rose apple or Java apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Eugenia polyantha is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
