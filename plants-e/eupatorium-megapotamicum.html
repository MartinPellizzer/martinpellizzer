Eupatorium megapotamicum

Eupatorium megapotamicum, commonly known as the Andean thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery underside, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Eupatorium megapotamicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
