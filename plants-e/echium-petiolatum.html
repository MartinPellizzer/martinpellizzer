Echium petiolatum

Echium petiolatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Echium petiolatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
