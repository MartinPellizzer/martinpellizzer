Echinophora orientalis

Echinophora orientalis, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are hairy and often lobed, while its flowers are yellow and surrounded by spiny structures.

Echinophora orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
