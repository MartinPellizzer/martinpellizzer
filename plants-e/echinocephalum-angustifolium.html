Echinocephalum angustifolium

Echinocephalum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf echinocephalum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its narrow, linear leaves are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.
