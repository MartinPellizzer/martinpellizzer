Elaterium carthagenense var. cordatum

Elaterium carthagenense var. cordatum, commonly known as the heart-shaped elaterium, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
