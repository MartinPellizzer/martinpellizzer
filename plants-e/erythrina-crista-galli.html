Erythrina crista-galli

Erythrina crista-galli, commonly known as the crown-of-thorns or the Indian lantern, is a striking tropical legume native to Africa, the Americas, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant has feathery, trifoliate leaves and produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Erythrina crista-galli is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
