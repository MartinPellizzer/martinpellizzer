Eragrostis serpula

Eragrostis serpula, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense, low-growing clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Eragrostis serpula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and soil-stabilizing properties.
