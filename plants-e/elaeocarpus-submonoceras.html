Elaeocarpus submonoceras

Elaeocarpus submonoceras, commonly known as the narrow-cupped elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, cup-shaped fruits. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, edible aril, which attracts various bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental features and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
