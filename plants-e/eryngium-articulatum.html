Eryngium articulatum

Eryngium articulatum, commonly known as the prickly eryngium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, artichoke-like appearance with blue to purple bracts and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are segmented and somewhat woody.

Eryngium articulatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
