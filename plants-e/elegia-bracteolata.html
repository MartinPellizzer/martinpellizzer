Elegia bracteolata

Elegia bracteolata, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers have three petals and six stamens, which are typical of the Iridaceae family.

Elegia bracteolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
