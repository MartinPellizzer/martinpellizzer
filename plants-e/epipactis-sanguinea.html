Epipactis sanguinea

Epipactis sanguinea, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a tall, erect stem with several leaves and a cluster of striking red or pink flowers. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often darker in color, aiding in pollination by insects.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
