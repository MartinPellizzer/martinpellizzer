Eleocharis flaccida var. fuscescens

Eleocharis flaccida var. fuscescens, commonly known as the black spike sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikes that are typically dark purple to black in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered at the tips of the spikes.

This variant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
