Eucalyptus raveretiana

Eucalyptus raveretiana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions.

Eucalyptus raveretiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
