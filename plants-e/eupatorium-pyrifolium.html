Eupatorium pyrifolium

Eupatorium pyrifolium, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Eupatorium pyrifolium is often found in wetland habitats and is considered a valuable component of native plant communities.
