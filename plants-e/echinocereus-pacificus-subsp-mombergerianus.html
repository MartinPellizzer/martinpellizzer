Echinocereus pacificus subsp. mombergerianus

Echinocereus pacificus subsp.

mombergerianus, commonly known as the Momberger's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which bear clusters of white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by various wildlife species.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh desert conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
