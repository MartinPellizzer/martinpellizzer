Eriocaulon amanoanum

Eriocaulon amanoanum, commonly known as the Amano's spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its native range.
