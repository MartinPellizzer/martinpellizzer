Eucalyptus preissiana subsp. preissiana

Eucalyptus preissiana subsp.

preissiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters and attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The bark is fibrous and peels off in strips, and the wood is strong and durable.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
