Euphorbia anthonyi

Euphorbia anthonyi, commonly known as Anthony's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
