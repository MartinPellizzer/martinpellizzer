Eugenia concava

Eugenia concava, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia concava is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
