Epilobium tetragonum var. subsessile

Epilobium tetragonum var. subsessile, commonly known as narrowleaf willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant features slender, quadrangular stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It has a fibrous root system and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
