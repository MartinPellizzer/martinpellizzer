Eupatorium glischrum

Eupatorium glischrum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
