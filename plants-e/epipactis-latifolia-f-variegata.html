Epipactis latifolia f. variegata

Epipactis latifolia f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated broad-leaved helleborine, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that often display striking variegation patterns of green and white. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent, hood-like structures and spurred petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex hybrid origin and its role in orchid taxonomy.
