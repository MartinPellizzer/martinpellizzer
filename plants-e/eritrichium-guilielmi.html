Eritrichium guilielmi

Eritrichium guilielmi, commonly known as the William's alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant has small, hairy leaves and thrives in cool, high-altitude environments. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers.
