Erica parviflora var. ternifolia

Erica parviflora var. ternifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
