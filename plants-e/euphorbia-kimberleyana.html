Euphorbia kimberleyana

Euphorbia kimberleyana, commonly known as the Kimberley spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Kimberley region of Western Australia.

It features a short, thick stem with a spiny crown and produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its ability to store water in its fleshy stems. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is known for its resilience in harsh, dry conditions.

Euphorbia kimberleyana is often found in rocky outcrops and is a characteristic species of the region's unique flora.
