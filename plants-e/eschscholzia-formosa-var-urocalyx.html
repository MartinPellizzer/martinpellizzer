Eschscholzia formosa var. urocalyx

Eschscholzia formosa var. urocalyx, commonly known as the Sierra California poppy, is a variant of the California poppy native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, preferring full sun. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is known for its resilience and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
