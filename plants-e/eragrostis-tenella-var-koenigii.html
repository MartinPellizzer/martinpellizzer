Eragrostis tenella var. koenigii

Eragrostis tenella var. koenigii, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-blue blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its striking foliage.
