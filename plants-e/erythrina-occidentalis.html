Erythrina occidentalis

Erythrina occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Flame Tree, is a deciduous legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Botanically, it is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic root nodules.
