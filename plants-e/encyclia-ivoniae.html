Encyclia ivoniae

Encyclia ivoniae, commonly known as the Ivory Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with intricate patterns and a pleasant, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hair.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Encyclia ivoniae is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
