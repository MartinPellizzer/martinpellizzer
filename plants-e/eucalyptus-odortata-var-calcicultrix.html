Eucalyptus odortata var. calcicultrix

Eucalyptus odortata var. calcicultrix, commonly known as the coastal mallee eucalypt, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It is well adapted to harsh, saline soils and is often found in coastal and inland sandy areas.

This species is notable for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its use in traditional and contemporary herbal applications.
