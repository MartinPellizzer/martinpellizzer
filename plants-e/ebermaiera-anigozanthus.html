Ebermaiera anigozanthus

Ebermaiera anigozanthus, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a fiery display during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
