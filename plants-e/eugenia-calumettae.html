Eugenia calumettae

Eugenia calumettae, commonly known as the Calumetta guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The fruit is a small, yellow or orange berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
