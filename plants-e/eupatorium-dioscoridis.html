Eupatorium dioscoridis

Eupatorium dioscoridis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Eupatorium dioscoridis is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators.
