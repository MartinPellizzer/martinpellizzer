Eriogonum cernuum subsp. viminale

Eriogonum cernuum subsp.

viminale, commonly known as the trailing buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a velvety texture with a grayish-green color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
