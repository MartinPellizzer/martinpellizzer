Echinocereus rectispinus

Echinocereus rectispinus, commonly known as the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically reddish in color, especially at the tips. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
