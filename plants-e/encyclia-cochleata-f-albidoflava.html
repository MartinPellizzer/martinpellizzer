Encyclia cochleata f. albidoflava

Encyclia cochleata f. albidoflava, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a hybrid form of the orchid species Encyclia cochleata.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which are typically smaller than those of the typical form. The plant is native to Central and South America, where it grows in humid, shaded environments. It has a pseudobulbous structure with strap-like leaves and produces flowers on erect stems.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
