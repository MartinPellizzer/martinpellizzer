Elaeagnus arborea var. maculata

Elaeagnus arborea var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted silver bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage with distinctive red or purple spots on the leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental choice for gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular plant for landscaping and ecological restoration.
