Eugenia glazioviana f. parvifolia

Eugenia glazioviana f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved gloryberry, is a compact shrub native to Brazil.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fruit.

The species is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove.
