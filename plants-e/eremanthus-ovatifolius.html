Eremanthus ovatifolius

Eremanthus ovatifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf eremanthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, making it an attractive addition to gardens.

Eremanthus ovatifolius is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
