Eucalyptus calcicultrix

Eucalyptus calcicultrix, commonly known as the white box eucalypt, is a species native to southern Australia.

It is a medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, white bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale yellow.

Eucalyptus calcicultrix is adapted to arid conditions and plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
