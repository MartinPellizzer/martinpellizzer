Erythrina panamensis

Erythrina panamensis, commonly known as the Panama coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red bracts that surround its small flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Erythrina panamensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun and well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
