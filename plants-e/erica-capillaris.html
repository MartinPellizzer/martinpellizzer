Erica capillaris

Erica capillaris, commonly known as the heather, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs and evergreens. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Erica capillaris is known for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
