Eriosyce crispa var. atroviridis

Eriosyce crispa var. atroviridis, commonly known as the black-green eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green to dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and conservation programs.
