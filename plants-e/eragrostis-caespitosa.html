Eragrostis caespitosa

Eragrostis caespitosa, commonly known as slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It forms dense, clump-like growths and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open fields.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
