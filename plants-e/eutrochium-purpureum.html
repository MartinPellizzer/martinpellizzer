Eutrochium purpureum

Eutrochium purpureum, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features tall, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals surrounding a central cone of brown seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
