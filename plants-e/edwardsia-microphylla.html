Edwardsia microphylla

Edwardsia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved Edwardsia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure.

Edwardsia microphylla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
