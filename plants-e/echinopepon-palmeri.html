Echinopepon palmeri

Echinopepon palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's prickly pear, is a cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, paddle-shaped stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its structure is adapted to arid environments, with thick stems that store water and reduce transpiration.

Echinopepon palmeri plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
