Eugenia parkeri var. ibitensis

Eugenia parkeri var. ibitensis, commonly known as the Ibitauba or Ibitauba cherry, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of the Amazonian ecosystem.
