Erysimum brevirostre

Erysimum brevirostre, commonly known as the narrow-beaked erinaceum, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a rough texture and a strong, peppery aroma.

Erysimum brevirostre is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
