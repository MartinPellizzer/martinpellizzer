Erysimum flavum var. subincanum

Erysimum flavum var. subincanum, commonly known as the pale yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This variety is a cultivar of Erysimum flavum, distinguished by its pale yellow petals and shorter stature compared to the typical form.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
