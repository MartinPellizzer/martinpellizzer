Epipactis pycnostachys

Epipactis pycnostachys, commonly known as the dense-stalked helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, hairy coating. Its flowers are greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip, and they bloom in late summer.

Epipactis pycnostachys is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
