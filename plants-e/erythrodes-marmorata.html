Erythrodes marmorata

Erythrodes marmorata, commonly known as the marbled orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, marbled pattern on its leaves and flowers, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pink and purple.

Erythrodes marmorata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
