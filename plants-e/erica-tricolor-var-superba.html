Erica tricolor var. superba

Erica tricolor var. superba, commonly known as the superba heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
