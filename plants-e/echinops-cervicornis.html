Echinops cervicornis

Echinops cervicornis, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Echinops cervicornis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
