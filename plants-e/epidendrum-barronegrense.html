Epidendrum barronegrense

Epidendrum barronegrense, commonly known as the Barronegrense Orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its elongated pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often during the rainy season. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms.
