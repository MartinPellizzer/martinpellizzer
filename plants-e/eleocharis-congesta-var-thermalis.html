Eleocharis congesta var. thermalis

Eleocharis congesta var. thermalis, commonly known as the thermal sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to hot spring environments.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in wet, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near geothermal areas. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is adapted to high temperatures and is a unique indicator species of thermal habitats.
