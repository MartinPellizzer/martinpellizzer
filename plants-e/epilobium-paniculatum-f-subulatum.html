Epilobium paniculatum f. subulatum

Epilobium paniculatum f. subulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow herb, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in areas with partial shade.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
