Epidendrum humboldtii

Epidendrum humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in water retention.

Epidendrum humboldtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
