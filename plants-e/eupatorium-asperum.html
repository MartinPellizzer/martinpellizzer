Eupatorium asperum

Eupatorium asperum, commonly known as rough eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Eupatorium asperum is often found in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

It is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
