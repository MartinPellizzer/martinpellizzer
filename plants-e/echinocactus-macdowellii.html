Echinocactus macdowellii

Echinocactus macdowellii, commonly known as the Macdowell's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on elongated pedicels. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within the Cactaceae family.
