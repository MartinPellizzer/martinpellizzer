Eustoma exaltatum subsp. russellianum

Eustoma exaltatum subsp.

russellianum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens.

The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long bloom period and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
