Eugenia ligustrina

Eugenia ligustrina, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. The fruit has a tart flavor and is often used in culinary applications or as a medicinal plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
