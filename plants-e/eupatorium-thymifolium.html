Eupatorium thymifolium

Eupatorium thymifolium, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and showy white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves and flowers are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.
