Eugenia macrosepala

Eugenia macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep red color.

Eugenia macrosepala is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
