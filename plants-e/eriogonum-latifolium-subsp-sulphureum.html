Eriogonum latifolium subsp. sulphureum

Eriogonum latifolium subsp.

sulphureum, commonly known as the sulphur flax, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the spring.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
