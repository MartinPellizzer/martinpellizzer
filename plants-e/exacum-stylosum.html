Exacum stylosum

Exacum stylosum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its ability to produce clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has fleshy, dark green leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in bright, indirect light. It is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Exacum stylosum prefers well-draining soil and moderate watering to prevent root rot.
