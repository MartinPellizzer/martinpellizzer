Erythroxylum suberosum

Erythroxylum suberosum, commonly known as the roughleaf copal, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It is often found in humid, mountainous environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

Due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
