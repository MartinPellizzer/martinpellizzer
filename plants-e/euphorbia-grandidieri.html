Euphorbia grandidieri

Euphorbia grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the Euphorbiaceae family.
