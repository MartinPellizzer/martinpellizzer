Eragrostis lacunaria

Eragrostis lacunaria, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in open areas such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray appearance, and its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread.

Eragrostis lacunaria is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.
