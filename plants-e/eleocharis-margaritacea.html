Eleocharis margaritacea

Eleocharis margaritacea, commonly known as the pearl sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant produces tiny white or pale blue flowers that are often clustered together, giving it a delicate, ornamental appearance. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and pond margins.

Eleocharis margaritacea plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
