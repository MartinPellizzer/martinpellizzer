Erica pulchelliflora

Erica pulchelliflora, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica pulchelliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
