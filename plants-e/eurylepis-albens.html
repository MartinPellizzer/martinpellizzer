Eurylepis albens

Eurylepis albens, commonly known as the white-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
