Eleusine indica var. tristachya

Eleusine indica var. tristachya, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, spreading growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it both a pioneer species and a potential weed.
