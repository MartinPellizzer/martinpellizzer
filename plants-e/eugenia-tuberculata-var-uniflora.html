Eugenia tuberculata var. uniflora

Eugenia tuberculata var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slight waxy coating. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage and unique floral display.
