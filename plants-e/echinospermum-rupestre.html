Echinospermum rupestre

Echinospermum rupestre, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and rocky environments. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a schizocarp with small, spiky seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Echinospermum rupestre is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand periods of drought.
