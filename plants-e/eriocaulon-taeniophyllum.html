Eriocaulon taeniophyllum

Eriocaulon taeniophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flat sedge, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes many other sedges and related plants.
