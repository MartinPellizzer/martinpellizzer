Erica tetralix var. watsonii

Erica tetralix var. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
