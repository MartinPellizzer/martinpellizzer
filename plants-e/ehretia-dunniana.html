Ehretia dunniana

Ehretia dunniana, commonly known as the dwarf mahogany, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
