Eryngium irgangii

Eryngium irgangii, commonly known as the mountain eryngium, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

Eryngium irgangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
