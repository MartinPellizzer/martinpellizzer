Erigeron compositus var. glabratus

Erigeron compositus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous appearance compared to other forms of Erigeron compositus.
