Eranthemum insularum

Eranthemum insularum, commonly known as the island eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its small, showy flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eranthemum insularum is often found in moist, well-drained habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
