Eurybia gunniana var. microcephala

Eurybia gunniana var. microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
