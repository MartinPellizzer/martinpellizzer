Epipactis pendula var. minor

Epipactis pendula var. minor, commonly known as the lesser hanging helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are oval and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, dark brown seeds in capsules.

This orchid is noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
