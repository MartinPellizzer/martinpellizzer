Eulophia sclerophylla

Eulophia sclerophylla, commonly known as the hard-leaved eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive pattern of markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
