Epidendrum caveroi

Epidendrum caveroi, commonly known as the Cavero's Epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred lip.

Epidendrum caveroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
