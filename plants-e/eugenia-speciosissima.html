Eugenia speciosissima

Eugenia speciosissima, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense foliage and showy inflorescences.

Eugenia speciosissima is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
