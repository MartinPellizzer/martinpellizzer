Erica bruniifolia var. barbigera

Erica bruniifolia var. barbigera, commonly known as the Barbed Heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is distinguished by its barbed, hairy leaves, which give it its specific epithet.
