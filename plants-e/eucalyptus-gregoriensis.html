Eucalyptus gregoriensis

Eucalyptus gregoriensis, commonly known as the Sturt's Desert Peppermint, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically pale blue or white in color. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Eucalyptus gregoriensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
