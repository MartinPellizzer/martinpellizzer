Eriogonum polycladon var. crispum

Eriogonum polycladon var. crispum, commonly known as the curlyleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
