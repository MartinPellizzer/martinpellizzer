Eulophia lonchophylla

Eulophia lonchophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia lonchophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
