Elymus ciliaris subsp. amurensis

Elymus ciliaris subsp.

amurensis, commonly known as Amur needlegrass, is a grass species native to eastern Asia, particularly found in regions of Russia and China. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and fine, needle-shaped leaves that are often hairy along the edges.

This subspecies typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as riverbanks, marshes, and wet meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
