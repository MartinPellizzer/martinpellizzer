Eremitis grandiflora

Eremitis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered eremitis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
