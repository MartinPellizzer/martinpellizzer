Elaeochytris

Elaeochytris, commonly known as the oil-producing chytrid, is a genus of chytrid fungi that plays a unique role in aquatic ecosystems.

These microorganisms are characterized by their ability to produce lipids, which are essential for their survival in nutrient-poor environments. Elaeochytris species are typically found in freshwater habitats, where they contribute to the microbial food web. Their life cycle involves both free-living and parasitic stages, depending on the species.

The study of Elaeochytris has gained attention due to its potential applications in biotechnology, particularly in the production of renewable biofuels.
