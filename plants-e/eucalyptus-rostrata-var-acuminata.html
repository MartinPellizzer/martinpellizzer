Eucalyptus rostrata var. acuminata

Eucalyptus rostrata var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, acutely pointed leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often found in heathlands and open forests. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale yellow.

This variant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
