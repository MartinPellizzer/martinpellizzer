Eucalyptus calycogona var. gracilis

Eucalyptus calycogona var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale yellow. It is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, growing in low shrublands or open woodlands.

This species is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in revegetation projects due to its resilience.
