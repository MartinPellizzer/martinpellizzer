Erica lacuniflora

Erica lacuniflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. This plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica lacuniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators.
