Erysimum czernjajevi

Erysimum czernjajevi, commonly known as the Czernjajev's wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Erysimum czernjajevi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows.
