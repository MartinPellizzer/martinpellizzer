Eriogonum nudum var. sulphureum

Eriogonum nudum var. sulphureum, commonly known as the sulphur flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow to golden yellow inflorescences, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small organisms.
