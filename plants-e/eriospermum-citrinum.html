Eriospermum citrinum

Eriospermum citrinum, commonly known as the yellow eriosperm, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, erect stems. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a slow growth rate, making it a rare and delicate species.
