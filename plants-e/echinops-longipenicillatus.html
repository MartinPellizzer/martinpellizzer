Echinops longipenicillatus

Echinops longipenicillatus, commonly known as the long-beaked thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are covered in bristly structures. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, elongated seed capsules. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
