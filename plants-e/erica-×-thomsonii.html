Erica × thomsonii

Erica × thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's heath, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica × thomsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and attractive floral display.
