Erica propinqua

Erica propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, particularly in South Africa. This plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, evergreen foliage, and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica propinqua thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
