Echinops elatus

Echinops elatus, commonly known as the blue thistle or desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking spherical seed heads that turn blue or purple as they mature. The plant has silver-gray foliage with soft, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the distinctive spiky seed heads.

Echinops elatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
