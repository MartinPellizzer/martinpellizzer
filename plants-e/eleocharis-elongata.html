Eleocharis elongata

Eleocharis elongata, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often exceed 1 meter in height, making it a notable feature in wetland habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
