Eragrostis unioloides var. ongiemensis

Eragrostis unioloides var. ongiemensis, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with low rainfall.
