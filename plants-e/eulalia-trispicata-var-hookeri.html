Eulalia trispicata var. hookeri

Eulalia trispicata var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's bulrush, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant features tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
