Erigeron strigosus var. eligulatus

Erigeron strigosus var. eligulatus, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are blue to purple in color, appearing in late summer to early fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
