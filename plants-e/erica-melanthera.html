Erica melanthera

Erica melanthera, commonly known as the black-throated heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers. Its dark purple to black flowers appear in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Erica melanthera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
