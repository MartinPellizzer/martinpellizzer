Erythrophleum densiflorum

Erythrophleum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower legume, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with numerous small leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

Erythrophleum densiflorum is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
