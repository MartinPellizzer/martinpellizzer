Erodium virescens

Erodium virescens, commonly known as green everlasting or green deadnettle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, greenish-white flowers with two lips, typical of the tribe Plantagneae. The plant has slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Erodium virescens is known for its ability to self-seed and persist in various habitats.
