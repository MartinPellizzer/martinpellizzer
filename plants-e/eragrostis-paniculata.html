Eragrostis paniculata

Eragrostis paniculata, commonly known as weeping lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, panicle-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. It is often found in open, disturbed areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Eragrostis paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful appearance.
