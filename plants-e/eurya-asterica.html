Eurya asterica

Eurya asterica, commonly known as the star-leaf eurya, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often have a star-like pattern of veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Eurya asterica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
