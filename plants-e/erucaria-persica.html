Erucaria persica

Erucaria persica, commonly known as Persian erucaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Erucaria persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
