Eriosyce jussieui

Eriosyce jussieui, commonly known as the Jussieui Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny, globular structure that resembles a pincushion. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

Eriosyce jussieui is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique shape and resilience.
