Echinocactus macracanthus

Echinocactus macracanthus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense, spiny stems that provide protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

Echinocactus macracanthus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
