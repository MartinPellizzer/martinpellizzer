Eragrostis campestris

Eragrostis campestris, commonly known as broadleaf wildrye, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its erect, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, and it blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers. Eragrostis campestris is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields, where it thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
