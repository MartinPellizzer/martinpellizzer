Eurycentrum atroviride

Eurycentrum atroviride, commonly known as the black-veined beauty, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its deep green, velvety leaves with dark green veins that create a distinctive visual contrast. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

Due to its limited natural range and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticultural collections.
