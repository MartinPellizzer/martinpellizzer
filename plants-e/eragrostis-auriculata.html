Eragrostis auriculata

Eragrostis auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense tufts, often reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its dense root system.
