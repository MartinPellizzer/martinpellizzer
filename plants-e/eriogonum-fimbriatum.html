Eriogonum fimbriatum

Eriogonum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Eriogonum fimbriatum is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
