Erica suffulta

Erica suffulta, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica suffulta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
