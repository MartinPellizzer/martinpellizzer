Elatine senegalensis

Elatine senegalensis, commonly known as the African waterwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, often greenish-white in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Elatine senegalensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, moist environments.
