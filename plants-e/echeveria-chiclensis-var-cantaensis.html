Echeveria chiclensis var. cantaensis

Echeveria chiclensis var. cantaensis, commonly known as the Cantaensis Echeveria, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color with a powdery coating called farina. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its coloration.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
