Equisetum hyemale f. attenuatum

Equisetum hyemale f. attenuatum, commonly known as the brittle scouring rush, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, segmented stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stems, which are typically found in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

This fern-like plant is known for its historical use in scouring and as a source of silica.
