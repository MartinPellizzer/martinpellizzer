Elaeocarpus linearifolius

Elaeocarpus linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that enclose a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited economic use.
