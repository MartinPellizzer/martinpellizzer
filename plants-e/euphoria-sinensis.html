Euphoria sinensis

Euphoria sinensis, commonly known as Chinese euphorbia, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a stem that is often branched and covered in a waxy, greenish coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Euphoria sinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
