Elaeocarpus gillespieanus

Elaeocarpus gillespieanus, commonly known as the Gillespie's elaeocarp, is a flowering plant species native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
