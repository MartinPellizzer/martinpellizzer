Eugenia kiaerskoviana

Eugenia kiaerskoviana, commonly known as the Kieperskoi cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia kiaerskoviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
