Entada myriadenia

Entada myriadenia, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and bipinnately compound leaves. Its fruit is a long, slender pod containing several seeds surrounded by a fibrous, edible pulp.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
