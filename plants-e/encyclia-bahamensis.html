Encyclia bahamensis

Encyclia bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clustered, showy flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Encyclia bahamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
