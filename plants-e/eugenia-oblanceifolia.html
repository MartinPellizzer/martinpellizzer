Eugenia oblanceifolia

Eugenia oblanceifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and aromatic properties.

Eugenia oblanceifolia is also known for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
