Eragrostis lamarckii

Eragrostis lamarckii, commonly known as slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and drooping inflorescences that give it a weeping appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing small, pale green to brown spikelets.

Eragrostis lamarckii is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
