Encelia suffrutescens

Encelia suffrutescens, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with bright orange to red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a hairy stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and desert regions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species adapted to arid environments.
