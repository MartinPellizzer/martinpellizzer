Erepsia cyathiformis

Erepsia cyathiformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped eremalche, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy, spiny stems that form a compact, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, which are followed by distinctive, cup-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
