Eriocaulon lamarckii

Eriocaulon lamarckii, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Eriocaulon lamarckii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
