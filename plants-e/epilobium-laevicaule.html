Epilobium laevicaule

Epilobium laevicaule, commonly known as smooth willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are typically hairless or only slightly hairy. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.
