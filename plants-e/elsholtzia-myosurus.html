Elsholtzia myosurus

Elsholtzia myosurus, commonly known as the mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to violet flowers appear in whorled clusters along the stem, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Elsholtzia myosurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
