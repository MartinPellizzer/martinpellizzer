Eugenia biflora var. mini

Eugenia biflora var. mini, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Following flowering, it bears small, round, yellow-orange fruits with a wax-like coating.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
