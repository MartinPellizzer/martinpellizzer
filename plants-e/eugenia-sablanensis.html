Eugenia sablanensis

Eugenia sablanensis, commonly known as the Sablan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Guam.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a fragrant scent.

Eugenia sablanensis is adapted to the tropical climate of Guam and plays a role in the local ecosystem.
