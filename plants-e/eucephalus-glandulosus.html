Eucephalus glandulosus

Eucephalus glandulosus, commonly known as the stickyhead goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular-covered foliage that gives it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Eucephalus glandulosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
