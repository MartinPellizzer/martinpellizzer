Echinops sulaimanii

Echinops sulaimanii, commonly known as the Sulaiman thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin. The plant typically grows in rocky and dry environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces yellow flowers during the summer months.

Echinops sulaimanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
