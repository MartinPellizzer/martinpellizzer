Echinochloa crus-galli var. sieberiana

Echinochloa crus-galli var. sieberiana, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail, which is typically yellow to golden in color. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and is often found in agricultural areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variant is closely related to the common foxtail, E. crus-galli, and is often considered a weed in cultivated lands.
