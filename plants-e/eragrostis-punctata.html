Eragrostis punctata

Eragrostis punctata, commonly known as speckled lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, spikelet-based flowers that are often tinged with brown or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis punctata is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
