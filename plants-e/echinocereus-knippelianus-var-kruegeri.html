Echinocereus knippelianus var. kruegeri

Echinocereus knippelianus var. kruegeri, commonly known as the Krueger's hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem with a greenish-gray color and a dense covering of white to pale blue hairs. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its slow growth and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
