Eleocharis cryptica

Eleocharis cryptica, commonly known as the cryptic spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, cylindrical spikes of flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. Eleocharis cryptica is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its ability to thrive in fluctuating water levels makes it a resilient and adaptive species.
