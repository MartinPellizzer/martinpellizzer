Eugenia cambucarana

Eugenia cambucarana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions.
