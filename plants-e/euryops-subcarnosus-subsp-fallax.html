Euryops subcarnosus subsp. fallax

Euryops subcarnosus subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky habitats, displaying a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flower heads are surrounded by pale blue or white ray florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and hardiness.
