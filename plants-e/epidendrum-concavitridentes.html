Epidendrum concavitridentes

Epidendrum concavitridentes, commonly known as the concave-toothed epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and greenhouses.
