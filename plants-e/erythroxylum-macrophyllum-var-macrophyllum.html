Erythroxylum macrophyllum var. macrophyllum

Erythroxylum macrophyllum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant copal tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ecological role and traditional uses in local communities.
