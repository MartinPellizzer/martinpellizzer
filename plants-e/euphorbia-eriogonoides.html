Euphorbia eriogonoides

Euphorbia eriogonoides, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a branched stem system with thick, fleshy leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

Its botanical structure is adapted to conserve water, making it well-suited for desert environments.
