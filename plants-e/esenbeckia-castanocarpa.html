Esenbeckia castanocarpa

Esenbeckia castanocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or purple passionflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
