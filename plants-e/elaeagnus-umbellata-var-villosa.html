Elaeagnus umbellata var. villosa

Elaeagnus umbellata var. villosa, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for various landscaping applications.

Silverberry is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
