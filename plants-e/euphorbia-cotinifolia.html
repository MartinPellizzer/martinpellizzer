Euphorbia cotinifolia

Euphorbia cotinifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright red bracts that resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by greenish seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its unique appearance and seasonal display make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
