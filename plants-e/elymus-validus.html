Elymus validus

Elymus validus, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically blooming in late summer. Elymus validus is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
