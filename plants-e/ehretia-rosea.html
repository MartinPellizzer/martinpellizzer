Ehretia rosea

Ehretia rosea, commonly known as the rosewood tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, orange-red fruits. It is valued for its hard, aromatic wood and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
