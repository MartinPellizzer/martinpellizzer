Eleocharis leptocaulis

Eleocharis leptocaulis, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it a common inhabitant of marshes, swamps, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

Eleocharis leptocaulis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
