Euphorbia apicata

Euphorbia apicata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, globe-shaped fruit that resembles a small lantern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in a crown-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Euphorbia apicata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
