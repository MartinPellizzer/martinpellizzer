Eremophila interstans subsp. interstans

Eremophila interstans subsp.

interstans, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It is well-suited for drought-tolerant landscaping due to its low water requirements.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native wildlife.
