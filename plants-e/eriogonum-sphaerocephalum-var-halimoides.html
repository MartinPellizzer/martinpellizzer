Eriogonum sphaerocephalum var. halimoides

Eriogonum sphaerocephalum var. halimoides, commonly known as the silverleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, clustered white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
