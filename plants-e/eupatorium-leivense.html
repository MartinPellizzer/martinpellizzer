Eupatorium leivense

Eupatorium leivense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Eupatorium leivense is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
