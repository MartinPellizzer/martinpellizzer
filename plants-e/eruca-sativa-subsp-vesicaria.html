Eruca sativa subsp. vesicaria

Eruca sativa subsp.

vesicaria, commonly known as rocket or arugula, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features rosettes of pinnately lobed, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow, cruciform flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seeds are elongated and ridged, typically maturing in late summer or early autumn.

It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its peppery, edible leaves.
