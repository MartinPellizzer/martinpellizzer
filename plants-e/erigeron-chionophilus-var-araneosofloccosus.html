Erigeron chionophilus var. araneosofloccosus

Erigeron chionophilus var. araneosofloccosus, commonly known as the snow-soft fleur-de-lis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly foliage and delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure consists of a central yellow disk surrounded by pale blue to white ray florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
