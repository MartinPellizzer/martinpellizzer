Epacris impressa var. breviflora

Epacris impressa var. breviflora, commonly known as the slender leek-orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a member of the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in spring and are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

The variety breviflora is distinguished by its shorter floral tubes compared to other varieties of Epacris impressa.
