Erica pabstii

Erica pabstii, commonly known as the Pabst heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in spring. Erica pabstii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
