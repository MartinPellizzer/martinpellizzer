Eugenia racemiflora

Eugenia racemiflora, commonly known as the clustered clove or fragrant eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.

Eugenia racemiflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
