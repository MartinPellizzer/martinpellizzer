Erythraea bianoris

Erythraea bianoris, commonly known as the Crimson Veil Orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its vivid red petals with intricate white veins, giving it a unique and ornate appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its flowering season occurs during the late spring to early summer months, producing fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to climate change, Erythraea bianoris is considered an endangered species.
