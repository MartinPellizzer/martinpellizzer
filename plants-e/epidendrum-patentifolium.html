Epidendrum patentifolium

Epidendrum patentifolium, commonly known as the showy epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, and often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
