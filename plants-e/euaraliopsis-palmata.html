Euaraliopsis palmata

Euaraliopsis palmata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it forms dense mats on the water surface. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption from the surrounding water.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate a range of water conditions, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems.
