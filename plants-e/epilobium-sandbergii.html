Epilobium sandbergii

Epilobium sandbergii, commonly known as sandberg's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as along roadsides and in wet meadows. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is often found in prairie and wetland habitats, contributing to ecological diversity in these environments.
