Elaeagnus praematura

Elaeagnus praematura, commonly known as the silver berry or early silver bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer, attracting various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Elaeagnus praematura is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
