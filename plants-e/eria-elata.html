Eria elata

Eria elata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Eria elata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
