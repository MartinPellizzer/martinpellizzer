Eugenia teyu-iba

Eugenia teyu-iba, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Eugenia teyu-iba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
