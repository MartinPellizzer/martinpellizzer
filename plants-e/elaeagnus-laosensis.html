Elaeagnus laosensis

Elaeagnus laosensis, commonly known as the Laos silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, powdery hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Elaeagnus laosensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil stabilization efforts.
