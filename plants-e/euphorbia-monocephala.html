Euphorbia monocephala

Euphorbia monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are covered in a thick layer of pale green to grayish-white bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
