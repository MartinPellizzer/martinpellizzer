Elatine anagalloides

Elatine anagalloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterwort, is a small aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, opposite leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. Elatine anagalloides is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed aquatic habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
