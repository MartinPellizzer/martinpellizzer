Elaeocarpus brunneotomentosus

Elaeocarpus brunneotomentosus, commonly known as the brown-tomentose elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, brownish pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, spherical fruits that are often covered in a hairy coating.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
