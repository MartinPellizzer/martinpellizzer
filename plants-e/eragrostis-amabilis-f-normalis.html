Eragrostis amabilis f. normalis

Eragrostis amabilis f. normalis, commonly known as the common silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and delicate, silky texture, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
