Eucalyptus × rariflora

Eucalyptus × rariflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub form and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery bloom. The plant produces small, globular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to cream, typically blooming during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and for land rehabilitation purposes.
