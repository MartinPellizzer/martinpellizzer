Erigeron engelmannii subsp. davisii

Erigeron engelmannii subsp.

davisii, commonly known as Engelmann's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
