Echinocactus mariposensis

Echinocactus mariposensis, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
