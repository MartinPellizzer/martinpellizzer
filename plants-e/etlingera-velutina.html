Etlingera velutina

Etlingera velutina, commonly known as the velvet ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its medicinal potential.
