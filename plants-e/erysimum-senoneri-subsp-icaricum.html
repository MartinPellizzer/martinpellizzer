Erysimum senoneri subsp. icaricum

Erysimum senoneri subsp.

icaricum, commonly known as the Icaric Mountains mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Icaric Mountains in northern Italy. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow or white flowers in early spring, and its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
