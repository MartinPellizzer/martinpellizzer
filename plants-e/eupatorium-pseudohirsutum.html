Eupatorium pseudohirsutum

Eupatorium pseudohirsutum, commonly known as the roughstem snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has hairy, often glandular stems and leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a pale blue or purple coloration.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
