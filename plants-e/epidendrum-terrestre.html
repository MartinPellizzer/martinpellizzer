Epidendrum terrestre

Epidendrum terrestre, commonly known as the terrestrial orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent lips, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
