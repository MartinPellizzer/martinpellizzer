Eclipta alba f. longifolia

Eclipta alba f. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf eclipta, is a variant of the common eclipta, characterized by its elongated leaves and distinctive flowering structure.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and may have a slightly hairy texture.

The botanical features of this plant make it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
