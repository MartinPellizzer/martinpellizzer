Euonymus wrayi

Euonymus wrayi, commonly known as the red-edged euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that turn bright red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Euonymus wrayi is often used in landscaping for its vibrant foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
