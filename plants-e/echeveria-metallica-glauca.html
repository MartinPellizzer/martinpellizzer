Echeveria metallica glauca

Echeveria metallica glauca, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, violet-pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

Due to its attractive appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
