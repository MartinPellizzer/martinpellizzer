Erysimum maclovianum

Erysimum maclovianum, commonly known as the Maclovian wallflower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow flowers with four petals. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
