Eupatorium pharcidodes var. sororopanense

Eupatorium pharcidodes var. sororopanense, commonly known as the Florida blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas. It has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
