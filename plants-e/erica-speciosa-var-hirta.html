Erica speciosa var. hirta

Erica speciosa var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Erica speciosa var. hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
