Eulophia florulenta

Eulophia florulenta, commonly known as the showy eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its often humid environment.

Eulophia florulenta is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
