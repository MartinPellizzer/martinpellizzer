Eriogonum deflexum var. baratum

Eriogonum deflexum var. baratum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and often have a grayish or fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
