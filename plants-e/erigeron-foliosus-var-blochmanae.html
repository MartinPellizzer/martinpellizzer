Erigeron foliosus var. blochmanae

Erigeron foliosus var. blochmanae, commonly known as the Blochman's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky habitats. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
