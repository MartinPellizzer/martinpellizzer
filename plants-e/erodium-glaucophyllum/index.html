Erodium Glaucophyllum

Erodium glaucophyllum is a small flowering plant native to southern California and parts of Nevada. It is commonly known as the bluebells or the lesser bedstraw. This species thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland habitats. Its delicate blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species and is protected in several areas.
