Echinopsis aurea subsp. aurea

Echinopsis aurea subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden echinopsis, is a cactus species native to South America. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers are often pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

This species is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
