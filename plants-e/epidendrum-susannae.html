Epidendrum susannae

Epidendrum susannae, commonly known as the Susan's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, and its pseudobulbs are covered in a dense layer of white to brown hair. Epidendrum susannae thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate to high humidity for optimal growth.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
