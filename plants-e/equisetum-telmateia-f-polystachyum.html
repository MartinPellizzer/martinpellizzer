Equisetum telmateia f. polystachyum

Equisetum telmateia f. polystachyum, commonly known as the giant horsetail, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are essential for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with unique botanical features.
