Eria puberula

Eria puberula, commonly known as the hairy eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Eria puberula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow under proper conditions.
