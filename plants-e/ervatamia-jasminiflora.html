Ervatamia jasminiflora

Ervatamia jasminiflora, commonly known as the sweet-scented germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a jasmine-like aroma. The plant has aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ervatamia jasminiflora is valued in traditional medicine for its purported tonic and antispasmodic properties.
