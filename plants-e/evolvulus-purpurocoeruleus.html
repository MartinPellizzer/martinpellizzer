Evolvulus purpurocoeruleus

Evolvulus purpurocoeruleus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.
