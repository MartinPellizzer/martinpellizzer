Epiphyllum latifrons

Epiphyllum latifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved night bloomer, is a climbing cactus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Epiphyllum latifrons is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering behavior.
