Erythroxylum exaltatum

Erythroxylum exaltatum, commonly known as the red torch plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flames, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are typically alternate and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant, eye-catching appearance.
