Eroeda muirii

Eroeda muirii, commonly known as the Muir's erica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Eroeda muirii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
