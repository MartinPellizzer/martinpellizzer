Eryngium billardierei subsp. kotschyi

Eryngium billardierei subsp.

kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi thistle, is a perennial herb native to western Australia. It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by pale green bracts.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structures.
