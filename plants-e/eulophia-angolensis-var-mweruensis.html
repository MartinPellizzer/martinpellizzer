Eulophia angolensis var. mweruensis

Eulophia angolensis var. mweruensis, commonly known as the Mweru eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to central Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect inflorescence bearing numerous showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, while the roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

This orchid is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
