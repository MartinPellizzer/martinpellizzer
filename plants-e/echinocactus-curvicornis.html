Echinocactus curvicornis

Echinocactus curvicornis, commonly known as the narrow-horned cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and a distinctive, narrow horn at the top of each stem. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Echinocactus curvicornis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
