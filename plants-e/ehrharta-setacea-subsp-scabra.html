Ehrharta setacea subsp. scabra

Ehrharta setacea subsp.

scabra, commonly known as the rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving it a distinctive texture.

It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands.
