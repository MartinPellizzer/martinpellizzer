Eugenia napiformis

Eugenia napiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia napiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
