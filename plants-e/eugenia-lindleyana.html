Eugenia lindleyana

Eugenia lindleyana, commonly known as the Java apple or purple apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit.

Eugenia lindleyana is also cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in perfumery.
