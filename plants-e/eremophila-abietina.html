Eremophila abietina

Eremophila abietina, commonly known as the silver-leaf eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Eremophila abietina is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
