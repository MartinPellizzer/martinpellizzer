Eurya persicifolia

Eurya persicifolia, commonly known as the peachleaf eurya, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that resemble peach leaves in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Eurya persicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
