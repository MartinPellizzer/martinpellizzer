Evelyna conifera

Evelyna conifera, commonly known as the silver pine, is a coniferous tree native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are coated in a silvery wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces cones that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Evelyna conifera typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
