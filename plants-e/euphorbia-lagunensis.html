Euphorbia lagunensis

Euphorbia lagunensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features a thick, woody stem with spiny leaves and produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves. It is often found in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a notable species in the flora of the Canary Islands.
