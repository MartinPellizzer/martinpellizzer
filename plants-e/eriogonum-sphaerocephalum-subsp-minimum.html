Eriogonum sphaerocephalum subsp. minimum

Eriogonum sphaerocephalum subsp.

minimum, commonly known as the small-headed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems and small, glandular leaves.

The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
