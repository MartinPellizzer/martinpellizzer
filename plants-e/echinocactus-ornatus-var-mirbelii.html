Echinocactus ornatus var. mirbelii

Echinocactus ornatus var. mirbelii, commonly known as the Mirbel's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which serve as protective adaptations against herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth rate and unique morphology, it is a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
