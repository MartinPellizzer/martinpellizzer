Eremopyrum bourgaei

Eremopyrum bourgaei, commonly known as the desert grass or Bourgae's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Eremopyrum bourgaei is well-suited to harsh climates and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
