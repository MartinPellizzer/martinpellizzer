Erysimum lusitanicum

Erysimum lusitanicum, commonly known as the Portuguese wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, basal leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
