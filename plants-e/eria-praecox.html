Eria praecox

Eria praecox, commonly known as the early orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Eria praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its delicate floral display.
