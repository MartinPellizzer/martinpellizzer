Eugenia seriatopedunculata

Eugenia seriatopedunculata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia seriatopedunculata*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
