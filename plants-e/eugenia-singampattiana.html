Eugenia singampattiana

Eugenia singampattiana, commonly known as the Singampattinam eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its fruits are small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.
