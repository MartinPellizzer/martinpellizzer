Eupatorium tripartitum

Eupatorium tripartitum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, woodland environments.

Eupatorium tripartitum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
