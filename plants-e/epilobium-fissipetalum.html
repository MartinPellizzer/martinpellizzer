Epilobium fissipetalum

Epilobium fissipetalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often boggy habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pinkish-purple flowers in clusters. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals that split at the tip, giving it the species name *fissipetalum*.

It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
