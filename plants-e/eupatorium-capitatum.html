Eupatorium capitatum

Eupatorium capitatum, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy white to pale purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium capitatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
