Erica consobrina

Erica consobrina, commonly known as the common heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils, often in heathland or coastal areas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica consobrina is part of the Ericaceae family, which includes other heather plants.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
