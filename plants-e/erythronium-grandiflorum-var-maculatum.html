Erythronium grandiflorum var. maculatum

Erythronium grandiflorum var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted dog's-tongue, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often spotted or streaked with white or pale green. The plant produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of white, yellow, or pale pink, often with a distinctive red or orange spot at the base of the petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
