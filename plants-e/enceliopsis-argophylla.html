Enceliopsis argophylla

Enceliopsis argophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant is known for its silvery-white leaves, which give it its common name, and its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
