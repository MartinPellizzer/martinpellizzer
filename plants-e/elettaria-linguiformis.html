Elettaria linguiformis

Elettaria linguiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces aromatic, dark brown pods containing aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique aroma and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
