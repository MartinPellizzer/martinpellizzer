Euonymus fortunei f. gracilis

Euonymus fortunei f. gracilis, commonly known as the creeping euonymus, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. It is valued for its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature.
