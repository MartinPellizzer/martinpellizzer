Epidendrum philowercklei

Epidendrum philowercklei, commonly known as the Philowercklei orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its inflorescence is often adorned with a distinctive, hairy structure called a bract.

Epidendrum philowercklei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
