Excoecaria laurocerasus var. laurifolia

Excoecaria laurocerasus var. laurifolia, commonly known as the Indian laurel or laurifolia croton, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
