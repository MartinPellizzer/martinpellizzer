Echinops bovei

Echinops bovei, commonly known as the Bove's thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical heads of flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

Echinops bovei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
