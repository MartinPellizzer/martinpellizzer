Evolvulus pilosus

Evolvulus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Evolvulus pilosus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing growth patterns.
