Eugenia longifolia

Eugenia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by edible, red or purple berries. Eugenia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
