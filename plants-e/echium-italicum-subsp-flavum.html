Echium italicum subsp. flavum

Echium italicum subsp.

flavum, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting various pollinators.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a raceme, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
