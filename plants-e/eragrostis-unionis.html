Eragrostis unionis

Eragrostis unionis, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the plant. It is characterized by its ability to form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Botanically, Eragrostis unionis is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its potential use in restoration projects.
