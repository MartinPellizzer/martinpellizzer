Eriogonum kennedyi subsp. typicum

Eriogonum kennedyi subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the Kennedy's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. It produces small, dry fruits that contain one or two seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators and wildlife.
