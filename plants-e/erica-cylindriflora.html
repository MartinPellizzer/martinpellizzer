Erica cylindriflora

Erica cylindriflora, commonly known as the cylindrical heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica cylindriflora produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
