Eruca sativa var. hispida

Eruca sativa var. hispida, commonly known as "Rapeseed mustard," is a biennial or annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with small, hairy lobes. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by long, slender pods containing small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its oil-rich seeds.

This variety is also valued for its culinary uses, particularly in traditional Mediterranean cuisine.
