Excoecaria bicolor var. purpurascens

Excoecaria bicolor var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf croton, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage, which features shades of green, yellow, and purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens for its aesthetic value.
