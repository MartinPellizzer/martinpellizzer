Erica lysimachiiflora

Erica lysimachiiflora, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons, and is adapted to grow in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in sunny, coastal environments and is often found in sand dunes and rocky outcrops.

Erica lysimachiiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
