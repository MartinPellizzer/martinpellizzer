Echium sabulicola var. briquetii

Echium sabulicola var. briquetii, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and saline environments.
