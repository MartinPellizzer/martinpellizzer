Erythronium dens-canis subsp. albiflorum

Erythronium dens-canis subsp.

albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered dog's tooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features a rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces a single, upright stem bearing a single, large, white or pale yellow flower with a distinctive, spurred petal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
