Euphorbia dilobadena

Euphorbia dilobadena, commonly known as the two-lobed spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts. The plant has a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia dilobadena is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.
