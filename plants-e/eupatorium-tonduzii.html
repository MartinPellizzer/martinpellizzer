Eupatorium tonduzii

Eupatorium tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduzii boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium tonduzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
