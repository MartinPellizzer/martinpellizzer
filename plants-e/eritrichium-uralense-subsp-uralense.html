Eritrichium uralense subsp. uralense

Eritrichium uralense subsp.

uralense, commonly known as the Ural bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often on rocky slopes and meadows.

The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-shaped flowers.
