Eupatorium tranninense

Eupatorium tranninense, commonly known as the Trinidadian boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Eupatorium tranninense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
