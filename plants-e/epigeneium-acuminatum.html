Epigeneium acuminatum

Epigeneium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed epigeneium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Epigeneium acuminatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
