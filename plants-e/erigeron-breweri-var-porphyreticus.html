Erigeron breweri var. porphyreticus

Erigeron breweri var. porphyreticus, commonly known as the Porphyritic Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

The showy blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
