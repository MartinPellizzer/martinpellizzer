Eucalyptus terebra

Eucalyptus terebra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. It is often found in shrublands and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Eucalyptus terebra plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various native animals.
