Eugenia clarensis

Eugenia clarensis, commonly known as the Clarendon guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet fruits similar to guavas. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia clarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in suitable environments.
