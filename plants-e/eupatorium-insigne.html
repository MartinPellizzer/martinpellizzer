Eupatorium insigne

Eupatorium insigne, commonly known as the showy eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often branching near the top. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Eupatorium insigne is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as swamps and wetlands.
