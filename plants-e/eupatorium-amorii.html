Eupatorium amorii

Eupatorium amorii, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, open woods or along stream banks.

Eupatorium amorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
