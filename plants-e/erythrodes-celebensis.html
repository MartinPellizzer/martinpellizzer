Erythrodes celebensis

Erythrodes celebensis, commonly known as the Celebes redwood, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the island of Sulawesi in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with smooth, glossy bark and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pale pink. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and deforestation.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this distinctive botanical species.
