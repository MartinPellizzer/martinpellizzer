Echinophora caspica

Echinophora caspica, commonly known as the Caspian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Echinophora caspica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
