Ericameria filifolia

Ericameria filifolia, commonly known as silverleaf ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. It has small, narrow leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Ericameria filifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
