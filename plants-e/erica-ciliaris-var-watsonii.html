Erica ciliaris var. watsonii

Erica ciliaris var. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, needle-like leaves that are typically silver-green in color, with a compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
