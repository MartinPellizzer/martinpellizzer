Eriogonum sericeum

Eriogonum sericeum, commonly known as silverleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Eriogonum sericeum is an important plant for pollinators and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
