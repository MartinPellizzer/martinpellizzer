Endotropis roylei

Endotropis roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool and moist climates. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a velvety texture.

The botanical features of Endotropis roylei make it an important species in the study of alpine flora and traditional medicine.
