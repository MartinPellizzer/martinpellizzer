Eriogonum vimineum var. densum

Eriogonum vimineum var. densum, commonly known as the denseleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is part of the Polygonaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and ecological importance to pollinators.
