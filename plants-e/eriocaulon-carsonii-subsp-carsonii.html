Eriocaulon carsonii subsp. carsonii

Eriocaulon carsonii subsp.

carsonii, commonly known as Carson's spike-rush, is a member of the Eriocaulaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, often in the western United States. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, white flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
