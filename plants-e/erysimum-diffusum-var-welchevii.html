Erysimum diffusum var. welchevii

Erysimum diffusum var. welchevii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of California.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is well adapted to the cool, moist climates of its native range.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
