Eremophila latrobei subsp. glabra

Eremophila latrobei subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth eremophila, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically smooth and hairless.

The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
