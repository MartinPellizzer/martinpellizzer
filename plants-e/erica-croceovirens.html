Erica croceovirens

Erica croceovirens, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments. Erica croceovirens is known for its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
