Erigeron spathulatus var. nanulus

Erigeron spathulatus var. nanulus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms in the summer months.

The species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
