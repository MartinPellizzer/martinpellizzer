Echium coincyanum

Echium coincyanum, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Echium coincyanum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
