Eria latifolia

Eria latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are broad and often covered with a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in spikes, with a pale greenish-yellow color.

Eria latifolia thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
