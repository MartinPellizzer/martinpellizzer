Euphorbia viridula

Euphorbia viridula, commonly known as green milkweed, is a succulent plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its sap contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation.

Euphorbia viridula is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
