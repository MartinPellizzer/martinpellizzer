Eubotryoides grayana f. tschonoskii

Eubotryoides grayana f. tschonoskii, commonly known as the Tschonoskii's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated inflorescence and translucent, waxy flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on the trunks of trees or in rocky crevices. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and specialized roots adapted for nutrient absorption from the air.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in both horticultural and conservation contexts.
