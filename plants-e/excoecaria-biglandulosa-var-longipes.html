Excoecaria biglandulosa var. longipes

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features distinctive, waxy leaves with prominent veins and a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
