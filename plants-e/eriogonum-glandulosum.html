Eriogonum glandulosum

Eriogonum glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its leaves are often glandular, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Eriogonum glandulosum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is a common species in sagebrush ecosystems.
