Eremophila kochii

Eremophila kochii, commonly known as the Kangaroo Island emu plant, is a shrub native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a slow growth rate. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for native pollinators.

Eremophila kochii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
