Epidendrum usurpator

Epidendrum usurpator, commonly known as the orchid thief, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers, which can be white, yellow, or pink, bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, Epidendrum usurpator is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
