Eragrostis chalarothyrsos

Eragrostis chalarothyrsos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often blue or purple in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its adaptability to wet environments makes it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
