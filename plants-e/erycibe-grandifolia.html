Erycibe grandifolia

Erycibe grandifolia, commonly known as the giant cupuaçu, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a fruit similar to the cupuaçu, which is valued for its high nutritional content. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-draining soil.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is often cultivated in agroforestry systems.
