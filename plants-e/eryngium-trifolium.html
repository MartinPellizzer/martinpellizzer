Eryngium trifolium

Eryngium trifolium, commonly known as the three-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flowers and its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its flowers bloom in summer and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Eryngium trifolium is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
