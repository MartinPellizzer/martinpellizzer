Eulophia pillansii

Eulophia pillansii, commonly known as the Pillans' eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, often white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eulophia pillansii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
