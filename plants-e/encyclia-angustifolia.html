Encyclia angustifolia

Encyclia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, spurred orchid flowers in shades of yellow and green, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure includes a single, short stem with several flowering shoots emerging from the base.
