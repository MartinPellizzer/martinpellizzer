Elymus fontqueri

Elymus fontqueri, commonly known as the Sierra fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

Elymus fontqueri is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in high-elevation environments.
