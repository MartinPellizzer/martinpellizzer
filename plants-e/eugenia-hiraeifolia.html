Eugenia hiraeifolia

Eugenia hiraeifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or hibiscus tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eugenia hiraeifolia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and along roadsides.
