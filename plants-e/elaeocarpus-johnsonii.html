Elaeocarpus johnsonii

Elaeocarpus johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
