Ericoides axilliflorum

Ericoides axilliflorum, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats.

Ericoides axilliflorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
