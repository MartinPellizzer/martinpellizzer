Erythroxylum subglaucescens

Erythroxylum subglaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Mexico, Guatemala, and parts of the Amazon basin. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
