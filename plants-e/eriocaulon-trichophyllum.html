Eriocaulon trichophyllum

Eriocaulon trichophyllum, commonly known as the silky spikerush, is a member of the Poales order and belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white flowers, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in wetland habitats, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its preference for moist environments, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
