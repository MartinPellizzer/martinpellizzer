Erigeron concinnus var. concinnus

Erigeron concinnus var. concinnus, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.
