Eria virescens

Eria virescens, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight glaucous sheen. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Eria virescens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and subtle floral display.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
