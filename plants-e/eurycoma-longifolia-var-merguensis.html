Eurycoma longifolia var. merguensis

Eurycoma longifolia var. merguensis, commonly known as the Merguensian Tualang, is a variant of the Tualang tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen nature, with elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in tropical rainforests and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
