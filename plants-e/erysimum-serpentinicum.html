Erysimum serpentinicum

Erysimum serpentinicum, commonly known as the serpentine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in poor, calcareous soils, often found on rocky outcrops and coastal areas. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.

Erysimum serpentinicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to colonize harsh environments.
