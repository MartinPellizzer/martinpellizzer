Emilia crepidioides

Emilia crepidioides, commonly known as the blueweed or bluecup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in dry, rocky soils. Emilia crepidioides is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
