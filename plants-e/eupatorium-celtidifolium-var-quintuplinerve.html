Eupatorium celtidifolium var. quintuplinerve

Eupatorium celtidifolium var. quintuplinerve, commonly known as the five-veined boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with five prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clustered heads, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine highlight its ecological and cultural significance.
