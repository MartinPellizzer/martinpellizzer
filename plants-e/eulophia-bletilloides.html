Eulophia bletilloides

Eulophia bletilloides, commonly known as the Bletilla orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often along stream banks or in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing numerous small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Eulophia bletilloides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in suitable conditions.
