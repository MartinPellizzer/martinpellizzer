Embelia

Embelia, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or embelia, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It consists of several species, with Embelia ribes being the most well-known. These plants are native to tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. They are shrubs or small trees with aromatic leaves and small, fleshy fruits.

The fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
