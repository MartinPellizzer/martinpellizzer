Elaphoglossum carolinense

Elaphoglossum carolinense, commonly known as Carolina maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.
