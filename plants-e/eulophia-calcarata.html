Eulophia calcarata

Eulophia calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or in forest understories.

Eulophia calcarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
