Echites auriculatus

Echites auriculatus, commonly known as the auriculate echites, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are characterized by their auriculate (ear-shaped) base, giving the plant its common name.

Echites auriculatus is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
