Eleutherococcus sieboldianus

Eleutherococcus sieboldianus, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the Far East of Russia and parts of China.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months, followed by red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
