Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp. simulata

Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp.

simulata, commonly known as the river red gum, is a subspecies native to Australia, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and drooping branches. It is a deciduous tree with smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white and grey.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in inflorescences. This subspecies is adapted to a wide range of soil types but thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water sources.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
