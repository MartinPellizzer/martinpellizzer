Eugenia palawanensis

Eugenia palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature.

Eugenia palawanensis is typically found in lowland to montane forests, often in areas with high humidity and well-drained soil.
