Exacum inflatum

Exacum inflatum, commonly known as the waxflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub habit and produces clusters of small, waxy, pink or white flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Exacum inflatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a potted plant.
