Erysimum cardaminoides

Erysimum cardaminoides, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage is basal, with lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Erysimum cardaminoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soil.
