Ereicoctis filiformis

Ereicoctis filiformis, commonly known as the slender ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is native to regions of South America, particularly in the Andes, where it is often found in moist, mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
