Eriogonum heermannii var. occidentale

Eriogonum heermannii var. occidentale, commonly known as the western bush poppy, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright red, papery flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and is often found in rocky soils and arid environments. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
