Euphorbia klokovii

Euphorbia klokovii, commonly known as the Klovkov's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-shaped structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its stems and leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
