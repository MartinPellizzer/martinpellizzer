Eragrostis cylindriflora var. gymnorrachis

Eragrostis cylindriflora var. gymnorrachis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a tufted growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
