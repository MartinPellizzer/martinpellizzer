Excoecaria albicans

Excoecaria albicans, commonly known as the white croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically green with a white or yellowish underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
