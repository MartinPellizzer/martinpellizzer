Eriophorum × medium f. rufescens

Eriophorum × medium f. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed cotton grass, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a hybrid species that exhibits characteristics of both Eriophorum vaginatum and Eriophorum angustifolium. The plant is known for its distinctive reddish-brown stems and fluffy, white seed heads that appear in late summer. It typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
