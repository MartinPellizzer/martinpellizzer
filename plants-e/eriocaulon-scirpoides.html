Eriocaulon scirpoides

Eriocaulon scirpoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spicate inflorescences that bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that contribute to wetland vegetation structure.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
