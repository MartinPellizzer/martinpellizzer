Eleocharis melanosperma

Eleocharis melanosperma, commonly known as black-berried spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy areas in the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries, which are a key feature in its common name. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies that help stabilize shoreline ecosystems.

Eleocharis melanosperma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
