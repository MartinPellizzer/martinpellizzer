Echinopsis kieslingii

Echinopsis kieslingii, commonly known as the Kiesling's cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a spiny exterior, with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Echinopsis kieslingii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
