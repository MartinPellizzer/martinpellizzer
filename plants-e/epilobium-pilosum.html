Epilobium pilosum

Epilobium pilosum, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as along stream banks and roadsides. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive hairy appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a fuzzy texture, while its flowers are pink to reddish in color and appear in clusters.

Epilobium pilosum is often found in wetland habitats and is known for its ecological value to pollinators.
