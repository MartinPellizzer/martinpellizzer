Epiphyllum acuminatum

Epiphyllum acuminatum, commonly known as the night bloomer or moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing plant with fleshy, thornless stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, often in shades of white, pink, or red. Its leaves are alternate and elliptical, typically turning into spines as the plant matures.

Epiphyllum acuminatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking floral display and unique growth habit.
