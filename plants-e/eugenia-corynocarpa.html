Eugenia corynocarpa

Eugenia corynocarpa, commonly known as the red-berried eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, red or purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subcoastal regions.

Eugenia corynocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal flowers.
