Eugenia apiocarpa

Eugenia apiocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or wild cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark red to black fruits that resemble cherries. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by its edible berries. The leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Eugenia apiocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
