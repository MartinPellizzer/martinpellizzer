Epilobium × nebrodense

Epilobium × nebrodense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, such as those found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
