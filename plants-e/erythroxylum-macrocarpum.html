Erythroxylum macrocarpum

Erythroxylum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in humid, lowland forests.

Erythroxylum macrocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
