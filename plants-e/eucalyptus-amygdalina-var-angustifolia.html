Eucalyptus amygdalina var. angustifolia

Eucalyptus amygdalina var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf apple gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its bark is typically smooth and peels off in strips, revealing a mottled pattern beneath.

This species is adapted to dry and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant in arid regions.
