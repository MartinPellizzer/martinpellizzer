Eugenia moonioides

Eugenia moonioides, commonly known as the moonflower eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
