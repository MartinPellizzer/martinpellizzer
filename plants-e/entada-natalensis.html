Entada natalensis

Entada natalensis, commonly known as the Natal doublepod, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
