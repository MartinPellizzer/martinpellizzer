Elaeocarpus heptadactylus

Elaeocarpus heptadactylus, commonly known as the seven-finger elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like leaves with seven prominent veins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance in its native habitats.
