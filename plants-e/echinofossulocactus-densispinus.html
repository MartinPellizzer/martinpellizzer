Echinofossulocactus densispinus

Echinofossulocactus densispinus, commonly known as the dense-spined fissured cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered with dense, sharp spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and desert gardens.
