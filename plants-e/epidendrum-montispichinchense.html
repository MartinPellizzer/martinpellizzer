Epidendrum montispichinchense

Epidendrum montispichinchense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically has strap-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
