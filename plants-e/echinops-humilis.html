Echinops humilis

Echinops humilis, commonly known as the Persian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, spherical seed heads that resemble small, fuzzy balls. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils. It blooms in late summer with blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops humilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
