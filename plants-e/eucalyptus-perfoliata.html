Eucalyptus perfoliata

Eucalyptus perfoliata, commonly known as the slender-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically pink or cream in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Eucalyptus perfoliata is a hardy shrub or small tree that plays an important role in local ecosystems.
