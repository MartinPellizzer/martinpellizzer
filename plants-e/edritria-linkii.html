Edritria linkii

Edritria linkii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a soft, almost ethereal appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique foliage and showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Edritria linkii thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
