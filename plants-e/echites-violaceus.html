Echites violaceus

Echites violaceus, commonly known as the violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces slender, winding vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers are typically about 5-7 cm in diameter and are known for their fragrant scent.

Echites violaceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
