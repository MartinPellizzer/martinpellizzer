Eusteralis tomentosa var. gracilis

Eusteralis tomentosa var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender woolly bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy gray-green foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
