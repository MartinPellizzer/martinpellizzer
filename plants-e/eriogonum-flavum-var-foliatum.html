Eriogonum flavum var. foliatum

Eriogonum flavum var. foliatum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lobed and can vary in shape depending on the environment.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid landscapes.
