Evalthe

Evalthe, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a sacred plant in various cultures, particularly in Hinduism and Buddhism.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, buoyant stalks that rise from water. Its leaves are broad and circular, often floating on the water's surface.

Evalthe is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
