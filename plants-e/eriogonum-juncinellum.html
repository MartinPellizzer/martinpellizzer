Eriogonum juncinellum

Eriogonum juncinellum, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Eriogonum juncinellum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
