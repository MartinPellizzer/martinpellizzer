Erysimum olympicum

Erysimum olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic poppy, is a striking annual plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
