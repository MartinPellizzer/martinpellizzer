Eudema thlaspiformis

Eudema thlaspiformis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils.

Eudema thlaspiformis is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
