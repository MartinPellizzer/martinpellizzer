Erysimum parnassi

Erysimum parnassi, commonly known as the Parnassian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Erysimum parnassi is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
