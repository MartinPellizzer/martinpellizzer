Echinocactus horizonthalonius var. nicholii

Echinocactus horizonthalonius var. nicholii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and bright yellow spines that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
