Eria inamoena

Eria inamoena, commonly known as the orchid of the forest, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Eria inamoena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
