Euphorbia ambacensis

Euphorbia ambacensis, commonly known as the Ambacensis spurge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes a wide variety of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
