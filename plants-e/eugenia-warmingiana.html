Eugenia warmingiana

Eugenia warmingiana, commonly known as the Java apple or yellow apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Eugenia warmingiana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
