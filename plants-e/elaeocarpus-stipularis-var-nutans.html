Elaeocarpus stipularis var. nutans

Elaeocarpus stipularis var. nutans, commonly known as the hanging elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines, and is typically found in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its drooping inflorescences and small, clustered flowers, which are often white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.

It plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
