Eroteum

Eroteum, commonly known as the "love plant," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences that resemble flowers but are actually modified stems called cyathia. The plants typically have smooth, waxy leaves and produce small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by brightly colored bracts. Eroteum species are native to tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant and unique floral displays.
