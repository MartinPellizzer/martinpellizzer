Eupatorium perezioides

Eupatorium perezioides, commonly known as Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium perezioides is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
