Eria linearifolia

Eria linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf erythrina, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Eria linearifolia typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
