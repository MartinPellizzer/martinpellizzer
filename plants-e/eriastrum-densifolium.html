Eriastrum densifolium

Eriastrum densifolium, commonly known as denseleaf eriocephalus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are densely packed, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
