Elaphoglossum dombeyanum

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.
