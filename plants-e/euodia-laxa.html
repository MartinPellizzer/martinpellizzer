Euodia laxa

Euodia laxa, commonly known as Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, berry-like fruits. Euodia laxa is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
