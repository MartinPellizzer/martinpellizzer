Escallonia bellidifolia

Escallonia bellidifolia, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a flowering shrub native to southern South America.

It features dense, glossy green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is well adapted to coastal and dry environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Escallonia bellidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
