Euphorbia rupicola

Euphorbia rupicola, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and yellow-orange cyathia, which are its specialized flower structures. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found growing on cliffs or among rocks, where it can withstand harsh conditions.

Euphorbia rupicola is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is popular among succulent enthusiasts.
