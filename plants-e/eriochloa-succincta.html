Eriochloa succincta

Eriochloa succincta, commonly known as rough false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense stands.

Eriochloa succincta is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
