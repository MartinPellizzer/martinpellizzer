Euploca strigosa

Euploca strigosa, commonly known as the rough euploca, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the spring and summer months.
