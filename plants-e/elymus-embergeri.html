Elymus embergeri

Elymus embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, with small, awned florets that are characteristic of the wheatgrass genus. Elymus embergeri thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks, and is often found in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
