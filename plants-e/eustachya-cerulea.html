Eustachya cerulea

Eustachya cerulea, commonly known as blue grass or blue oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale blue seed heads that add ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Eustachya cerulea is often used in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
