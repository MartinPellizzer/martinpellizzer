Eugenia bojeri

Eugenia bojeri, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, reddish-brown drupe that is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

Eugenia bojeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
