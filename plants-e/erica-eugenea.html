Erica eugenea

Erica eugenea, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants adapted to nutrient-poor soils. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica eugenea is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
