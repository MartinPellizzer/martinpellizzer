Eremophila galeata

Eremophila galeata, commonly known as the mountain eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Eremophila galeata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-water environments.
