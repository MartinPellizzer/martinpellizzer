Euphorbia corollata var. paniculata

Euphorbia corollata var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled spurge, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems that are covered in a distinctive white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers surrounded by fleshy, leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture with a subtle greenish hue.

This species is adapted to moist, open habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
