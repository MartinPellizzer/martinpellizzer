Erysimum rechingeri

Erysimum rechingeri, commonly known as the Armenian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, demonstrating a preference for well-drained soils. Its bright yellow or orange flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum rechingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
