Eugenia gigantifolia

Eugenia gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces small, round, red berries that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant is also popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
