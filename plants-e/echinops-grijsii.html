Echinops grijsii

Echinops grijsii, commonly known as the blue glass globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue-gray flower heads that resemble small globes, surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by the distinctive seed heads that persist through the winter. Echinops grijsii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant.
