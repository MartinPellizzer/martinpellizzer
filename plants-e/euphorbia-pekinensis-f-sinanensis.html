Euphorbia pekinensis f. sinanensis

Euphorbia pekinensis f. sinanensis, commonly known as the Sinanensis spurge, is a rare and striking variant of the Euphorbia pekinensis species.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, and often branched stems that display a range of colors from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often the main attraction for botanists and horticulturists. It is native to China and is typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
