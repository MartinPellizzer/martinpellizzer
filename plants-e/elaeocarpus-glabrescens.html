Elaeocarpus glabrescens

Elaeocarpus glabrescens, commonly known as the silky laurier, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are a source of food for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
