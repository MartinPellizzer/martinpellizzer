Erigeron × woronowii

Erigeron × woronowii, commonly known as the hybrid fleabane, is a perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing a range of colors from white to pink.

Erigeron × woronowii is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, thriving in a variety of soil types.
