Echinocereus engelmannii

Echinocereus engelmannii, commonly known as the Engelmann's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and spiny, tuberculate areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its structure and growth habits make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
