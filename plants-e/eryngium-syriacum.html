Eryngium syriacum

Eryngium syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by white to pale blue bracts. The plant has a robust root system and is characterized by its upright, branched stems. Its foliage consists of narrow, grayish-green leaves with a rough texture.

Eryngium syriacum is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
