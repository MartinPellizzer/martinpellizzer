Elaeocarpus obovatus

Elaeocarpus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf elaeocarpus, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
