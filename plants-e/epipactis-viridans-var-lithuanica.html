Epipactis viridans var. lithuanica

Epipactis viridans var. lithuanica, commonly known as the Lithuanian green helleborine, is a rare orchid species native to parts of Europe, particularly Lithuania.

It is a terrestrial herb with erect, leafy stems and is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, and the flowers are arranged in a raceme with showy, spurred petals.

This variety is notable for its limited distribution and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
