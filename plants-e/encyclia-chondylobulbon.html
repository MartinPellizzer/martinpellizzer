Encyclia chondylobulbon

Encyclia chondylobulbon, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in seasonal environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, humid conditions.

Encyclia chondylobulbon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
