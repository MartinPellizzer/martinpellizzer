Exomicrum insculptum

Exomicrum insculptum, commonly known as the sculptured fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the temperate rainforests of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, lance-shaped fronds that exhibit a unique pattern of raised ridges and grooves. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing cones, are located on the underside of its leaves.
