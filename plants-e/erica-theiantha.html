Erica theiantha

Erica theiantha, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica theiantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
