Echinochloa compressa

Echinochloa compressa, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. It is an annual weed that can compete with crops for nutrients and water.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some areas.
