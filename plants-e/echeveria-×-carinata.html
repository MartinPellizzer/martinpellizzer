Echeveria × carinata

Echeveria × carinata, commonly known as the hybrid echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray or green in color. The leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss. It produces vibrant pink or red flowers in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
