Eremophila websteri

Eremophila websteri, commonly known as the Webster's emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright shrub form. The plant produces striking spikes of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Eremophila websteri is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
