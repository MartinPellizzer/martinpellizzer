Epilobium acinifolium

Epilobium acinifolium, commonly known as sticky willow or red willow, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant has slender, willow-like branches and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red. Its leaves are often covered in a sticky substance, which gives it its common name.

Epilobium acinifolium is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
