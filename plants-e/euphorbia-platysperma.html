Euphorbia platysperma

Euphorbia platysperma, commonly known as the broad-seeded spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Euphorbia platysperma is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
