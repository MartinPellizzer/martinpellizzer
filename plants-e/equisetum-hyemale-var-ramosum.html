Equisetum hyemale var. ramosum

Equisetum hyemale var. ramosum, commonly known as the Scouring Rush, is a primitive fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of stiff, needle-like leaves and a tall, spore-bearing stem that emerges in early spring. The plant is notable for its lack of true leaves and its reliance on spores for reproduction, a feature common to ancient plant lineages. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its historical use in cleaning metal surfaces has given it the common name "Scouring Rush.".
