Eriochloa lemmonii var. minor

Eriochloa lemmonii var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy surface, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
