Emilia graminea

Emilia graminea, commonly known as the golden dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind, similar to dandelions. Emilia graminea thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
