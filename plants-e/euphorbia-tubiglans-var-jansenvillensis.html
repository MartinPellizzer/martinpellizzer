Euphorbia tubiglans var. jansenvillensis

Euphorbia tubiglans var. jansenvillensis, commonly known as the Jansenvillensis Spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by a unique, modified leaf-like structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for collectors and gardeners in arid climates.
