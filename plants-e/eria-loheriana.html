Eria loheriana

Eria loheriana, commonly known as the Lohr's orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow blooms with intricate petal structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Eria loheriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
