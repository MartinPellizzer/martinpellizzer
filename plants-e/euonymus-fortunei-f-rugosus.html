Euonymus fortunei f. rugosus

Euonymus fortunei f. rugosus, commonly known as the warty euonymus, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its distinctive, warty texture on the stems and leaves.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It is native to China and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and hardiness.

Euonymus fortunei f. rugosus thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
