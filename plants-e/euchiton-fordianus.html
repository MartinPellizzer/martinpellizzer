Euchiton fordianus

Euchiton fordianus, commonly known as the Ford's spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Euchiton fordianus is often found in woodlands and along stream banks in its native range.
