Epidendrum geraldoi

Epidendrum geraldoi, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It features long, slender pseudobulbs that support its evergreen, strap-like leaves. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
