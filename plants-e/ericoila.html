Ericoila

Ericoila, commonly known as the marsh heather, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae.

These plants are typically found in moist, acidic soils in temperate regions, often in heathlands or bogs. Ericoila species are characterized by their small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. They are adapted to nutrient-poor environments and often form dense mats or shrubs.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in horticulture for their ornamental value.
