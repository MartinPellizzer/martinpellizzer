Eupatorium photinum

Eupatorium photinum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine, downy hairs.

Eupatorium photinum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.
