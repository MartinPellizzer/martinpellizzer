Eriogonum tenellum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum tenellum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the slender buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink.

The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
