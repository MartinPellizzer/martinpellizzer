Etlingera belalongensis

Etlingera belalongensis, commonly known as the Belalong torch ginger, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking red and yellow floral bracts that resemble torches, hence its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.

The leaves are broad, sword-shaped, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.
