Eugenia neocaledonica

Eugenia neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonia myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces berries that range in color from red to black, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas of its native habitat.
