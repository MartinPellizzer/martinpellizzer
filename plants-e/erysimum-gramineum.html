Erysimum gramineum

Erysimum gramineum, commonly known as the grass-like wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Erysimum gramineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
