Eulophia analavelensis

Eulophia analavelensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in the summer months. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Eulophia analavelensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
