Eriocaulon parvulum

Eriocaulon parvulum, commonly known as the small bulbous rush, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense mats near the water's edge. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Eriocaulon parvulum is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
