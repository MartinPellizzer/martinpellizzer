Elettaria solaris

Elettaria solaris, commonly known as the golden spice plant, is a tropical herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, elongated fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves and seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their essential oils.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
