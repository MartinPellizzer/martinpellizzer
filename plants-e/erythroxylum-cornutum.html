Erythroxylum cornutum

Erythroxylum cornutum, commonly known as the hairy copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains toxic seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
