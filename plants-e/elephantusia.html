Elephantusia

Elephantusia, commonly known as the giant elephant plant, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its enormous, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to several feet in diameter. The plant produces strikingly large, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in humid, warm environments with high light exposure.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
