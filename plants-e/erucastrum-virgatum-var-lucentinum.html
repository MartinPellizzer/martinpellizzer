Erucastrum virgatum var. lucentinum

Erucastrum virgatum var. lucentinum, commonly known as the lucentinum cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, bright green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of streams.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
