Episcia longipetiolata

Episcia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled wax plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to flourish.
