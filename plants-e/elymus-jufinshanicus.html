Elymus jufinshanicus

Elymus jufinshanicus, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
