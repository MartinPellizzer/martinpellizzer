Erica lusitanica subsp. cantabrica

Erica lusitanica subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
