Erigeron ingae var. innocentius

Erigeron ingae var. innocentius, commonly known as the Innocent's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pollinator attractant and a component of local biodiversity.
