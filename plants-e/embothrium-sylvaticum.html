Embothrium sylvaticum

Embothrium sylvaticum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, often with a reddish or purplish hue.

Embothrium sylvaticum is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
