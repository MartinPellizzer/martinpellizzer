Eugenia lumilla

Eugenia lumilla, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a waxy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
