Eleocharis fassettii

Eleocharis fassettii, commonly known as the Florida sedge, is a grass-like plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often wind-pollinated. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its ability to thrive in saturated soils makes it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
