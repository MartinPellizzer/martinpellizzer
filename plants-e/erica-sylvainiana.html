Erica sylvainiana

Erica sylvainiana, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica sylvainiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
