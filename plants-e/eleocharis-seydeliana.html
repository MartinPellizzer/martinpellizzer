Eleocharis seydeliana

Eleocharis seydeliana, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical stems and small, clustered spikelets that bear tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Eleocharis seydeliana is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed wetland areas.
