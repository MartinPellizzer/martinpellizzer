Elaphandra verbesinoides

Elaphandra verbesinoides, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Elaphandra verbesinoides is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in perfumes, incense, and traditional medicines.
