Eryngium humile var. brevibracteatum

Eryngium humile var. brevibracteatum, commonly known as the dwarf thistle or small bract eryngium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features compact, spiny foliage and produces small, blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its short, branched stems. Its botanical features include basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
