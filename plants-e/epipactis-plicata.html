Epipactis plicata

Epipactis plicata, commonly known as the wrinkled helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its pleated, dark green leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with purple markings. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces erect, flowering stems that bear several flowers arranged in a spike.

It is often found in association with other orchids and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
