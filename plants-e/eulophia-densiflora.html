Eulophia densiflora

Eulophia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered eulophia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Eulophia densiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and long-lasting blooms.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturalists.
