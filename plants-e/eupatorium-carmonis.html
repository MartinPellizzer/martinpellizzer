Eupatorium carmonis

Eupatorium carmonis, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium carmonis is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
