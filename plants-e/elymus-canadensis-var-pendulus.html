Elymus canadensis var. pendulus

Elymus canadensis var. pendulus, commonly known as the drooping Canada wildrye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery inflorescence and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
