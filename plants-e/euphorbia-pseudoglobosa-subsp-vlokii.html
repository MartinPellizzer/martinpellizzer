Euphorbia pseudoglobosa subsp. vlokii

Euphorbia pseudoglobosa subsp.

vlokii, commonly known as the Vlok's globe thorn, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. Its leaves are often reduced to small, spiny structures, contributing to its defensive adaptations.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
