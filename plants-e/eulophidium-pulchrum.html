Eulophidium pulchrum

Eulophidium pulchrum, commonly known as the golden starbursts, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small starbursts, which bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental species. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green.

Eulophidium pulchrum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
