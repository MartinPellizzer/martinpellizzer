Eleusine tocussa var. melanocarpa

Eleusine tocussa var. melanocarpa, commonly known as black-eyed Susan grass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble small, dark-eyed sunflowers. The plant produces small, pale green to brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental and agricultural plant.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in traditional practices.
