Echeveria vulcanicola

Echeveria vulcanicola, commonly known as the Volcano Echeveria, is a succulent native to the volcanic regions of Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its compact shape.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
