Epilobium hirsutum var. africanum

Epilobium hirsutum var. africanum, commonly known as African willow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. It is characterized by its erect stem and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
