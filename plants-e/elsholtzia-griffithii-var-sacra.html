Elsholtzia griffithii var. sacra

Elsholtzia griffithii var. sacra, commonly known as sacred elsholtzia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and emit a strong, mint-like fragrance when crushed.

The flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, often displaying shades of purple or blue.
