Elymus × saundersii

Elymus × saundersii, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, blue-green foliage. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Elymus × saundersii is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
