Elaeocarpus validus

Elaeocarpus validus, commonly known as the white tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, yellowish fruits that have a tangy, acidic flavor. Its fruit is used in traditional cuisines and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Elaeocarpus validus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.
