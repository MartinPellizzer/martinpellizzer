Eremophila duttonii

Eremophila duttonii, commonly known as the Dutton's eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Eremophila duttonii is often found in rocky soils and is a valuable component of local ecosystems.
