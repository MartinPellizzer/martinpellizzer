Eugenia belloi

Eugenia belloi, commonly known as the Andean beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Following flowering, it bears small, round, blue-black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia belloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
