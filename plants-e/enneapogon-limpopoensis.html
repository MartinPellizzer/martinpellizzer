Enneapogon limpopoensis

Enneapogon limpopoensis, commonly known as the Limpopo grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Enneapogon limpopoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
