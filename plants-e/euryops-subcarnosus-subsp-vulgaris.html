Euryops subcarnosus subsp. vulgaris

Euryops subcarnosus subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb with hairy, branching stems and bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
