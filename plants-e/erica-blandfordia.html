Erica blandfordia

Erica blandfordia, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica blandfordia is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late winter to early spring, adding color to gardens during cooler months.
