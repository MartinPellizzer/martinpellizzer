Eucalyptus paralimnetica

Eucalyptus paralimnetica, commonly known as the Limnetic Gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow. It thrives in wetland or swampy environments, often growing near water bodies.

Eucalyptus paralimnetica is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
