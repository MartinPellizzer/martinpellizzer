Erica hemisphaerica

Erica hemisphaerica, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact, bushy form makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Erica hemisphaerica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
