Echinospermum fremontii

Echinospermum fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's echinochrome, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features hairy, branching stems and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Echinospermum fremontii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
