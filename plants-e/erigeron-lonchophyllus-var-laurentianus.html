Erigeron lonchophyllus var. laurentianus

Erigeron lonchophyllus var. laurentianus, commonly known as Laurentian blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and finely divided, silvery-gray leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
