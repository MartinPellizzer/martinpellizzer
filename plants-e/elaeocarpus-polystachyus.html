Elaeocarpus polystachyus

Elaeocarpus polystachyus, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain bird species. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is also cultivated in horticulture.
