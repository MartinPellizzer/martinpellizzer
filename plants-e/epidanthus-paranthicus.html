Epidanthus paranthicus

Epidanthus paranthicus, commonly known as the Paranthic Epidanthus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
