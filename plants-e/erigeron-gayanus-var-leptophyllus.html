Erigeron gayanus var. leptophyllus

Erigeron gayanus var. leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.
