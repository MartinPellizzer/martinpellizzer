Eremostachys paropamisica

Eremostachys paropamisica, commonly known as the Pamir spiny grass, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green or blue-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its harsh habitat.
