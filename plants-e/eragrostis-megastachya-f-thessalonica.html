Eragrostis megastachya f. thessalonica

Eragrostis megastachya f. thessalonica, commonly known as the Thessalonica false panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
