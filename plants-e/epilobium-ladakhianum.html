Epilobium ladakhianum

Epilobium ladakhianum, commonly known as the Ladakh willow herb, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to cold and harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems in its native range.
