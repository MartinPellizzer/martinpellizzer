Erythroxylum steyermarkii

Erythroxylum steyermarkii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, red berries and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different environmental conditions.
