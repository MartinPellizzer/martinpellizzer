Erica massonii var. massonii

Erica massonii var. massonii, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vivid pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
