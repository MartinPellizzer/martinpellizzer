Erythrina ovalifolia

Erythrina ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf coral tree, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its striking red flowers and compound leaves.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its trifoliate leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and the plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Erythrina ovalifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
