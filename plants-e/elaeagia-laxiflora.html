Elaeagia laxiflora

Elaeagia laxiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Elaeagia laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
