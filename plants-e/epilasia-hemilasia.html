Epilasia hemilasia

Epilasia hemilasia, commonly known as the narrowleaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along streambanks. It has slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.
