Echinops taeckholmianus

Echinops taeckholmianus, commonly known as the Taeckholm's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small, white balls, which appear in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, adapted to reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by the development of its distinctive seed heads.

Echinops taeckholmianus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
