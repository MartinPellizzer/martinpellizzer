Eria reinwardtii

Eria reinwardtii, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding surfaces.

Eria reinwardtii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
