Eupatorium jugipaniculatum

Eupatorium jugipaniculatum, commonly known as sweetee weed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces fluffy, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It has heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium jugipaniculatum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
