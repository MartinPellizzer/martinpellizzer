Elingamita

Elingamita, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal in appearance. The plant produces small, delicate white flowers that bloom at night, emitting a faint, sweet fragrance. Elingamita is adapted to survive in extreme conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its rarity and striking appearance, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
