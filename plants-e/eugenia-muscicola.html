Eugenia muscicola

Eugenia muscicola, commonly known as the shrub eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
