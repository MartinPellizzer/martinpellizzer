Eupatorium laete-virens var. steviifolium

Eupatorium laete-virens var. steviifolium, commonly known as the stevia-leaved thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
