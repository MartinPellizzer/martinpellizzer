Echinopsis pentlandii var. vitellina

Echinopsis pentlandii var. vitellina, commonly known as the yellow echinopsis, is a cacti species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant has spiny areoles and is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It requires minimal water and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for succulent collectors.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal in both ornamental and horticultural settings.
