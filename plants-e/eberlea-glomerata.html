Eberlea glomerata

Eberlea glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eberlea glomerata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
