Euterpe edulis

Euterpe edulis, commonly known as the açaí palm, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The açaí berry is the most economically important part of the plant, widely consumed as a superfood.

Due to its ecological and economic significance, it plays a vital role in both local and global markets.
