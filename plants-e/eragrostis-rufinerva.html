Eragrostis rufinerva

Eragrostis rufinerva, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that turn reddish-purple at the tips. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis rufinerva is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
