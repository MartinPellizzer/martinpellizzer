Euphorbia linifolia

Euphorbia linifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellowish-green flower clusters. The plant is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Euphorbia linifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
