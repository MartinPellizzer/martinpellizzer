Echinopus cyrenaicus

Echinopus cyrenaicus, commonly known as the Cape hedgehog, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are usually small, hairy, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Echinopus cyrenaicus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
