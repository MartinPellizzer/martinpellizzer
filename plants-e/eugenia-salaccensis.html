Eugenia salaccensis

Eugenia salaccensis, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a bright red outer rind and a segmented, white, juicy flesh. The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits.

Its cultivation is significant in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
