Eugenia bella

Eugenia bella, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers partial to full sun.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
