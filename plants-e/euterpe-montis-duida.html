Euterpe montis-duida

Euterpe montis-duida, commonly known as the mountain banana, is a species of palm native to the high-altitude regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, tropical climate.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
