Echinopsis ayopayana

Echinopsis ayopayana, commonly known as the Ayopayana cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
