Eritrichium glomeratum var. virgatum

Eritrichium glomeratum var. virgatum, commonly known as the blue-eyed weed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed edges. It prefers sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
