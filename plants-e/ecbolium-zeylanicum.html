Ecbolium zeylanicum

Ecbolium zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian broom or Ceylon broom, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka and southern India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are pods that split open to release small, rounded seeds.

The plant is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.
