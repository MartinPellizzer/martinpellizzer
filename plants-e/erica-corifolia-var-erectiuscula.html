Erica corifolia var. erectiuscula

Erica corifolia var. erectiuscula, commonly known as the upright heath, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves that are typically evergreen and covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces delicate pink or purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in compact, terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in horticultural settings.
