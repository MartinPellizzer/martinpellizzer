Epacris impressa var. alborosea

Epacris impressa var. alborosea, commonly known as the white-veined heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during spring and summer. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It has narrow, evergreen leaves and is often found in coastal regions.
