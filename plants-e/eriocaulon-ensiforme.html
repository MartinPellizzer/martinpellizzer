Eriocaulon ensiforme

Eriocaulon ensiforme, commonly known as the narrow leaf rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense stands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and linear in shape. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces minute seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Eriocaulon ensiforme plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
