Eucalyptus lenziana

Eucalyptus lenziana, commonly known as the Lenz's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Eucalyptus lenziana is often found in mallee woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
