Ephedra ferganensis

Ephedra ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana ephedra, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and contains alkaloids, particularly ephedrine, which have medicinal uses. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been historically used in traditional medicine.
