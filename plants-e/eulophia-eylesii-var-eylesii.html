Eulophia eylesii var. eylesii

Eulophia eylesii var. eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and long, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme and often have a pleasant fragrance.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
