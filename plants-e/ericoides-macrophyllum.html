Ericoides macrophyllum

Ericoides macrophyllum, commonly known as the bigleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Ericoides macrophyllum is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
