Euphorbia schiedeana

Euphorbia schiedeana, commonly known as the Schiede's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cluster called a cyathium. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
