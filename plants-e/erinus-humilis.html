Erinus humilis

Erinus humilis, commonly known as the common erinus, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender, often blooming in clusters. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette or along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erinus humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
