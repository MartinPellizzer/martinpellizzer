Encyclia bennettii

Encyclia bennettii, commonly known as Bennett's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are borne on erect, branched inflorescences, and the species is noted for its ornamental value.

Encyclia bennettii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
