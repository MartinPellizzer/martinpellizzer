Elymus churchii

Elymus churchii, commonly known as Church's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often tinged with brown or purple hues. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Elymus churchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
