Epigaea repens var. glabrifolia

Epigaea repens var. glabrifolia, commonly known as Allegheny rosebay, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its compact form and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.
