Eugenia pacifica

Eugenia pacifica, commonly known as the Alaska snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically white or pale green, turning red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Eugenia pacifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
