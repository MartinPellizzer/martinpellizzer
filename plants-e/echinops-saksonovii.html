Echinops saksonovii

Echinops saksonovii, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

Echinops saksonovii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
