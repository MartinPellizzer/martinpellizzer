Eucalyptus wimmerensis subsp. grata

Eucalyptus wimmerensis subsp.

grata, commonly known as the Wimmera gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. It produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
