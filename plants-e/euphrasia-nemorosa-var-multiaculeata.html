Euphrasia nemorosa var. multiaculeata

Euphrasia nemorosa var. multiaculeata, commonly known as the many-toothed eyebright, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. Its hairy stems and clusters of tiny flowers make it distinctive in its habitat.

It is often found in association with other woodland plants and is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family.
