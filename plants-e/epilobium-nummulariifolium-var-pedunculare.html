Epilobium nummulariifolium var. pedunculare

Epilobium nummulariifolium var. pedunculare, commonly known as the purple willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near waterways or in wet meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
