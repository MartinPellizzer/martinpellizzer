Eupatorium albescens

Eupatorium albescens, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is known for its composite flower heads.
