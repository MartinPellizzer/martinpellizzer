Epipactis muelleri var. dunensis

Epipactis muelleri var. dunensis, commonly known as the Mueller's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Central and Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and forest margins. It is a perennial species that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
