Eugenia paradoxa

Eugenia paradoxa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or the pink guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, pinkish-red fruits that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its resilience to pests and diseases.
