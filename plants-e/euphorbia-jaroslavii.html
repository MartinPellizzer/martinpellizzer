Euphorbia jaroslavii

Euphorbia jaroslavii, commonly known as the Jaroslav's spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Euphorbia jaroslavii is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
