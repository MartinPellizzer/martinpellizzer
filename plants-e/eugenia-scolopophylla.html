Eugenia scolopophylla

Eugenia scolopophylla, commonly known as the golden apple myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Eugenia scolopophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
