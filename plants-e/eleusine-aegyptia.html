Eleusine aegyptia

Eleusine aegyptia, commonly known as Egyptian lovegrass, is an annual grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in disturbed soils.
