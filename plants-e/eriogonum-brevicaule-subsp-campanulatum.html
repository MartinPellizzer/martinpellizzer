Eriogonum brevicaule subsp. campanulatum

Eriogonum brevicaule subsp.

campanulatum, commonly known as the Sierra bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs. It produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
