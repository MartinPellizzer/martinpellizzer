Epipactis procera

Epipactis procera, commonly known as the tall helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, supporting a cluster of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soils.

Epipactis procera is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
