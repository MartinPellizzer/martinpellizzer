Eupatorium pulchellum var. angustifolium

Eupatorium pulchellum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and serrated, often with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
