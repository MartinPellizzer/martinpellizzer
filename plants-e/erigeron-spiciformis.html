Erigeron spiciformis

Erigeron spiciformis, commonly known as the spiciform hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a spiciform (spike-like) inflorescence.

Erigeron spiciformis is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
