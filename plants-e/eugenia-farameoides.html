Eugenia farameoides

Eugenia farameoides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it requires careful management to prevent ecological disruption.
