Euphrasia serotina var. sicula

Euphrasia serotina var. sicula, commonly known as Sicilian eyebright, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Sicilian eyebright is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
