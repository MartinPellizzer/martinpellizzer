Evotella carnosa

Evotella carnosa, commonly known as the red velvet orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Evotella carnosa is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
