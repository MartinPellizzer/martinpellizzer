Erica sicula subsp. bocquetii

Erica sicula subsp.

bocquetii, commonly known as the Sicilian heath, is a shrub native to the island of Sicily. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its floral display, which occurs in spring, consists of pink or reddish hues, making it a notable ornamental plant.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
