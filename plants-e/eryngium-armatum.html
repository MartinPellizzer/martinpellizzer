Eryngium armatum

Eryngium armatum, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, spiky blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant has a rough, often grayish-blue foliage with stiff, spiny leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
