Eriocoma bracteata

Eriocoma bracteata, commonly known as the silverleaf eriocoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires careful watering to prevent root rot.
