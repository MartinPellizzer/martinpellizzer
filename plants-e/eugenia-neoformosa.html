Eugenia neoformosa

Eugenia neoformosa, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often fragrant and have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia neoformosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
