Erica multiflora f. alba

Erica multiflora f. alba, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a ground cover.

The white flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a striking contrast to the dark green leaves.
