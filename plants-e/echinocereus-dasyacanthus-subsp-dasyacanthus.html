Echinocereus dasyacanthus subsp. dasyacanthus

Echinocereus dasyacanthus subsp.

dasyacanthus, commonly known as the desert night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom at night.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal moths and bats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
