Elytrigia intermedia

Elytrigia intermedia, commonly known as intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It is well adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and forage production.
