Eragrostiella nardoides

Eragrostiella nardoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in dense, compact clusters.

Eragrostiella nardoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
