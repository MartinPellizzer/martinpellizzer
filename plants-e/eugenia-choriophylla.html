Eugenia choriophylla

Eugenia choriophylla, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in perfumery and herbal remedies.
