Elymus × insularis

Elymus × insularis, commonly known as the island wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including dry meadows, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

Elymus × insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
