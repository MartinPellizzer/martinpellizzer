Eleocharis brainii

Eleocharis brainii, commonly known as the brain grass, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swampy areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in certain cultures.

Botanically, it is classified within the Cyperaceae family, which includes over 5,000 species of sedges.
