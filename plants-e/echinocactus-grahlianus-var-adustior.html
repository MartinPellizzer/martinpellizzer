Echinocactus grahlianus var. adustior

Echinocactus grahlianus var. adustior, commonly known as the desert rose or the red echinocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
