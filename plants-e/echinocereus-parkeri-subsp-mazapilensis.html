Echinocereus parkeri subsp. mazapilensis

Echinocereus parkeri subsp.

mazapilensis, commonly known as the Mazapil cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring spiny stems and a compact growth habit.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and resilient plant for desert environments.
