Elytropappus cinerascens

Elytropappus cinerascens, commonly known as the grayleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or gray pubescence. Its flowering heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray flowers.

Elytropappus cinerascens is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
