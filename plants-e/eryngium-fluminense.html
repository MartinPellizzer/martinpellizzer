Eryngium fluminense

Eryngium fluminense, commonly known as Brazilian thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant features spiny, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eryngium fluminense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
