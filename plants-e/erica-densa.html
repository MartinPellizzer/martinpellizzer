Erica densa

Erica densa, commonly known as the dense heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and produces vibrant pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Erica densa is well adapted to harsh environments, including high winds and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
