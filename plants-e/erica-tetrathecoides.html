Erica tetrathecoides

Erica tetrathecoides, commonly known as the four-ovary heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Erica tetrathecoides typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
