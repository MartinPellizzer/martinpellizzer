Eria zollingeri

Eria zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces showy, often white or pale pink flowers with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Eria zollingeri is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
