Emilia integrifolia

Emilia integrifolia, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has feathery, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Emilia integrifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
