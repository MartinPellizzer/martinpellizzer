Eugenia theodori-wolfii

Eugenia theodori-wolfii, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
