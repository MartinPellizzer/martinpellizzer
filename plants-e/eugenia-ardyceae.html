Eugenia ardyceae

Eugenia ardyceae, commonly known as the Australian satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic oils and often fragrant flowers. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Eugenia ardyceae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal floral displays.
