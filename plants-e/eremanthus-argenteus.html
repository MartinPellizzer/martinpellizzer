Eremanthus argenteus

Eremanthus argenteus, commonly known as silver eremanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Eremanthus argenteus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
