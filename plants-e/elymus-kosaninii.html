Elymus kosaninii

Elymus kosaninii, commonly known as the Kosanin's wild rye, is a perennial grass native to eastern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are usually awned, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Elymus kosaninii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
