Eremurus persicus

Eremurus persicus, commonly known as the Persian giant feathergrass, is a striking plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, feathery plumes of yellow-green flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a landscape accent.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to botanical collections.
