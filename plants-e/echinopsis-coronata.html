Echinopsis coronata

Echinopsis coronata, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening at night and closing by dawn.

Echinopsis coronata is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
