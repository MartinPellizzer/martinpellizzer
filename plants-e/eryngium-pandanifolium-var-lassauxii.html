Eryngium pandanifolium var. lassauxii

Eryngium pandanifolium var. lassauxii, commonly known as the lace fern eryngium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that are clustered in dense, spiky inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
