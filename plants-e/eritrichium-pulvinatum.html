Eritrichium pulvinatum

Eritrichium pulvinatum, commonly known as the purple alpine sandwort, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to purple. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are usually narrow and pointed. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
