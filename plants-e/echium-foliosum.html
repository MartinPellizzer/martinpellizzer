Echium foliosum

Echium foliosum, commonly known as the leafy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Echium foliosum is a member of the Boraginaceae family, which is known for its showy flowers and sometimes hairy foliage.
