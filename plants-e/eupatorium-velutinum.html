Eupatorium velutinum

Eupatorium velutinum, commonly known as downy boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features dark green, opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
