Encyclia aemula

Encyclia aemula, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Encyclia aemula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
