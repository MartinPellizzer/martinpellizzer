Erysimum favargeri

Erysimum favargeri, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
