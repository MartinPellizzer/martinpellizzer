Eugenia hemilampra

Eugenia hemilampra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
