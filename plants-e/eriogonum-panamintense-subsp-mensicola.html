Eriogonum panamintense subsp. mensicola

Eriogonum panamintense subsp.

mensicola, commonly known as the Panamint mountain buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the Mojave Desert in California and Nevada. It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments with high temperatures and low precipitation. It plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and provides nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
