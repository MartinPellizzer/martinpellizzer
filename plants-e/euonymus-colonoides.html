Euonymus colonoides

Euonymus colonoides, commonly known as the Florida euonymus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive orange-red capsules containing seeds. Its foliage provides valuable habitat for various insects and small animals.

Euonymus colonoides is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to different soil types.
