Embelia cyrtobotrya

Embelia cyrtobotrya, commonly known as the Indian embelia or wild pepper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, elliptic, and have a slightly rough texture with a strong, pungent aroma.

Embelia cyrtobotrya is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
