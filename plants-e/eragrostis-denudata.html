Eragrostis denudata

Eragrostis denudata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands.

Eragrostis denudata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
