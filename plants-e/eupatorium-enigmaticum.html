Eupatorium enigmaticum

Eupatorium enigmaticum, commonly known as the enigmatic thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, pinkish-white flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
