Eupatorium desmophyllum

Eupatorium desmophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small, white to pale blue flowers that attract pollinators.

Eupatorium desmophyllum is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
