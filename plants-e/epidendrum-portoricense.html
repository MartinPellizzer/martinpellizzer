Epidendrum portoricense

Epidendrum portoricense, commonly known as the Caribbean orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with long, slender stems that produce numerous leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Epidendrum portoricense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
