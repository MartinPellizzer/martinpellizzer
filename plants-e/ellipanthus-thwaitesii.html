Ellipanthus thwaitesii

Ellipanthus thwaitesii, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit with small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ellipanthus thwaitesii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
