Eleocharis dulcis

Eleocharis dulcis, commonly known as water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to Asia and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous root system, which is the edible part consumed by humans. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds. Its leaves are slender and grass-like, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The botanical structure of Eleocharis dulcis is well adapted to its wetland habitat, supporting both ecological and agricultural significance.
