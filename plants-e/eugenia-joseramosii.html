Eugenia joseramosii

Eugenia joseramosii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers and red, cherry-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, making it a valued plant in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
