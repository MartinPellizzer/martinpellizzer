Eupatorium roseorum

Eupatorium roseorum, commonly known as rose swamp milkweed, is a flowering plant native to wetlands in North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in marshy areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, contributing to its ecological importance.

Eupatorium roseorum is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
