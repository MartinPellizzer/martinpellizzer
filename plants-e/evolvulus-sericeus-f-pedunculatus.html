Evolvulus sericeus f. pedunculatus

Evolvulus sericeus f. pedunculatus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a variant of the silverleaf morning glory, characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage.

It is a flowering plant native to the Americas, typically growing as an annual herb with trailing stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a velvety texture. It produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
