Eurycaulis croceocentrus

Eurycaulis croceocentrus, commonly known as the golden-veined evergreen, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow veins that run through its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and well adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Eurycaulis croceocentrus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
