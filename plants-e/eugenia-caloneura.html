Eugenia caloneura

Eugenia caloneura, commonly known as the narrow-veined eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
