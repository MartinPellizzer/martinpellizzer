Eulophia siamensis

Eulophia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a short rhizome and a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with several showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red veins.

Eulophia siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
