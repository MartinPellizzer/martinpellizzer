Euphorbia symmetrica

Euphorbia symmetrica, commonly known as the symmetrical spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, symmetrical arrangement of fleshy, green stems that form a rosette shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its specialized flower-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia symmetrica is often cultivated for its ornamental appearance and resilience.
