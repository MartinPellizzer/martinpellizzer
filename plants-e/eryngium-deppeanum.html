Eryngium deppeanum

Eryngium deppeanum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are grayish-green with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

Eryngium deppeanum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
