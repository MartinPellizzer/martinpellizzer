Euodia simulans

Euodia simulans, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Euodia simulans is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
