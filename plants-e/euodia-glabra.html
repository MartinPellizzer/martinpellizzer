Euodia glabra

Euodia glabra, commonly known as Chinese prickly ash, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, red or orange berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Euodia glabra is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruiting.
