Eryngium sparganioides

Eryngium sparganioides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shores and dunes. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly seed heads.

Eryngium sparganioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
