Elymus mendocinus

Elymus mendocinus, commonly known as the Mendocino wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring cool, temperate climates. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Elymus mendocinus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
