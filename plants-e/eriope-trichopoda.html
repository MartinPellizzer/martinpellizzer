Eriope trichopoda

Eriope trichopoda, commonly known as the hairy eriope, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
