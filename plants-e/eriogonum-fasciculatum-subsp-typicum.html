Eriogonum fasciculatum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum fasciculatum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its clustered, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
