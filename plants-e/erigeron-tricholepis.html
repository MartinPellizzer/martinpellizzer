Erigeron tricholepis

Erigeron tricholepis, commonly known as the silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Erigeron tricholepis is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to support local pollinators.
