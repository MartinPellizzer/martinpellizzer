Entada zeylanica

Entada zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain flat, seed-rich seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to thrive in various soil types makes it adaptable to different tropical environments.
