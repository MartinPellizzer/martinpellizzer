Eugenia perpunctata

Eugenia perpunctata, commonly known as the spotted eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often marked with small, white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and botanical collections.
