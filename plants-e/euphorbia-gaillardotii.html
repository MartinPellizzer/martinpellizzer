Euphorbia gaillardotii

Euphorbia gaillardotii, commonly known as the Gaillardot's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with prominent, ribbed leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes.
