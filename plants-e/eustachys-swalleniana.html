Eustachys swalleniana

Eustachys swalleniana, commonly known as the swamp panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Eustachys swalleniana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
