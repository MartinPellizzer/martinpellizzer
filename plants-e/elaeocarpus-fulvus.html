Elaeocarpus fulvus

Elaeocarpus fulvus, commonly known as the yellow-leafed elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are usually yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
