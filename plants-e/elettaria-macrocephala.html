Elettaria macrocephala

Elettaria macrocephala, commonly known as greater cardamom, is a member of the ginger family, Zingiberaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly India and Indonesia. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are typically greenish-white in color.

The aromatic seeds, which are the primary commercial product, are enclosed in three-winged fruits and are used extensively in cooking and traditional medicine.
