Euxolus crispus

Euxolus crispus, commonly known as the curled euphorbia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features succulent, often hairy stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a distinctive crinkled or wavy margin.

Euxolus crispus is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges or grassy areas.
