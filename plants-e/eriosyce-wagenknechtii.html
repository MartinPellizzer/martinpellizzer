Eriosyce wagenknechtii

Eriosyce wagenknechtii, commonly known as the Wagenknecht's cholla, is a species of cactus native to Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh desert conditions with its water-storing stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a notable species within its native habitat.
