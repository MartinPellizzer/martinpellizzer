Eriogonum orthocaulon

Eriogonum orthocaulon, commonly known as the white-topped buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and is often found in arid environments.

Eriogonum orthocaulon plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
