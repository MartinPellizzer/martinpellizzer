Euodia madagascariensis

Euodia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar laurier cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rutaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Euodia madagascariensis thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
