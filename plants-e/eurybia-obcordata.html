Eurybia obcordata

Eurybia obcordata, commonly known as the spoon-leaved Aster, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Eurybia obcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
