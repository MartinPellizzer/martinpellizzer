Eupatorium ceriferum

Eupatorium ceriferum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and forest edges.

Eupatorium ceriferum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.
