Euphorbia inundata

Euphorbia inundata, commonly known as the water spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic and irritating to the skin. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Euphorbia inundata is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
