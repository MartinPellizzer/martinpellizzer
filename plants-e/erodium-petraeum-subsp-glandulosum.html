Erodium petraeum subsp. glandulosum

Erodium petraeum subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular erodium, is a small annual plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant has small, oval leaves that are covered in glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are pink to purple in color and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
