Eucalyptus canobolensis

Eucalyptus canobolensis, commonly known as the Canobolense eucalyptus, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically white or pale yellow.

This eucalyptus is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.
