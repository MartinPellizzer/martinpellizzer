Euphorbia sajanensis

Euphorbia sajanensis, commonly known as the Sajan euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
