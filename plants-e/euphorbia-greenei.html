Euphorbia greenei

Euphorbia greenei, commonly known as Greene's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing a compact, shrub-like form with thick, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by colorful bracts that attract pollinators.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
