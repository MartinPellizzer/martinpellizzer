Eucalyptus eremicola subsp. peeneri

Eucalyptus eremicola subsp.

peeneri, commonly known as the Peener's mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to greyish pubescence.

The flowers are typically pale or creamy white, arranged in clusters, and are produced in late summer to early autumn. The plant is adapted to low rainfall and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
