Echeveria rauschii

Echeveria rauschii, commonly known as the Rauschii Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces showy, pinkish-purple flowers during the spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
