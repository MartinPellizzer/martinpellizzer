Evolvulus bahamensis

Evolvulus bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
