Eria polystachya

Eria polystachya, commonly known as the hairy leech orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.

Eria polystachya is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
