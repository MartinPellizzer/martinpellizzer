Eugenia mattosii

Eugenia mattosii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia* species, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia mattosii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
