Eriogonum brevicaule var. laxifolium

Eriogonum brevicaule var. laxifolium, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a basal rosette, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
