Erymophyllum tenellum

Erymophyllum tenellum, commonly known as the slender ericaceous plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
