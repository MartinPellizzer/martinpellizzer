Epidendrum bitriangulare

Epidendrum bitriangulare, commonly known as the three-angled epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique triangular-shaped pseudobulbs and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with three prominent sepals and petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum bitriangulare is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
