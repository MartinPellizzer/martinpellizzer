Eugenia patagonica

Eugenia patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
