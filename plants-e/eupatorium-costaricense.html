Eupatorium costaricense

Eupatorium costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

Eupatorium costaricense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
