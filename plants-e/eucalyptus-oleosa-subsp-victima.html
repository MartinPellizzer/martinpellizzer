Eucalyptus oleosa subsp. victima

Eucalyptus oleosa subsp.

victima, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a medium-sized tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are usually pale or creamy white. Its essential oils, particularly those containing cineole, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
