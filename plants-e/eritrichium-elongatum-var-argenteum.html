Eritrichium elongatum var. argenteum

Eritrichium elongatum var. argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its striking appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
