Eucalyptus androsaemifolia

Eucalyptus androsaemifolia, commonly known as the Androsaemifolia gum tree, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark that displays a range of colors, from white to grey. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue, and it has lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color. Eucalyptus androsaemifolia is a hardy tree that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
