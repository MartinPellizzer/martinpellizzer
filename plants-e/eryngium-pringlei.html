Eryngium pringlei

Eryngium pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple-blue flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are tubular and are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Eryngium pringlei is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
