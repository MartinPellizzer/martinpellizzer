Eupatorium dichroum

Eupatorium dichroum, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and opposite, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium dichroum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
