Ehrharta erecta

Ehrharta erecta, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Ehrharta erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a ground cover.
