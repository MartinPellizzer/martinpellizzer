Epiphyllum costaricense

Epiphyllum costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing cactus native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica. The plant features flat, fleshy stems that are often segmented and covered in white or light green spines. Its most striking feature is its large, showy flowers that open at night and last only a few hours.

The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
