Eriogonum luteolum

Eriogonum luteolum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Its small, rounded leaves and showy blooms make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
