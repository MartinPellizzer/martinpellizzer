Enkianthus uniflorus

Enkianthus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink or red flowers, which are often borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and remain green throughout the growing season.

Enkianthus uniflorus thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
