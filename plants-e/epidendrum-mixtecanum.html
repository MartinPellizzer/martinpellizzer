Epidendrum mixtecanum

Epidendrum mixtecanum, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with reddish markings, and it produces a strong, pleasant fragrance.

Epidendrum mixtecanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
