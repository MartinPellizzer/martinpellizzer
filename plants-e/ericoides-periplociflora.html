Ericoides periplociflora

Ericoides periplociflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf ericoides, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or rocky slopes. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to efficiently absorb nutrients from poor soils.

The plant also produces small, dark berries that serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
