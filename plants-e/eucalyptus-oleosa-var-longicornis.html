Eucalyptus oleosa var. longicornis

Eucalyptus oleosa var. longicornis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery coating. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters and attracting various pollinators. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its aromatic oils have been used in traditional and modern applications, including medicinal and aromatic purposes.
