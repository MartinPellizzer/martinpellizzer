Espeletia banksiifolia

Espeletia banksiifolia, commonly known as the Banks' espeletia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces showy, cone-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It is adapted to high altitudes and is often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
