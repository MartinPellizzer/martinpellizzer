Eupatorium callilepis var. oligocephalum

Eupatorium callilepis var. oligocephalum, commonly known as the narrow-headed eupatorium, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is adapted to moist, acidic soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
