Encholirium agavoides

Encholirium agavoides, commonly known as the Agave-like Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing numerous showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is also of interest to botanists and conservationists.
