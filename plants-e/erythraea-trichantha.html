Erythraea trichantha

Erythraea trichantha, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
