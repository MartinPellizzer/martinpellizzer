Eriosema scioanum var. meruense

Eriosema scioanum var. meruense, commonly known as the East African eriosema, is a perennial herb native to eastern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in horticultural and ecological contexts.
