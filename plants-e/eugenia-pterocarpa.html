Eugenia pterocarpa

Eugenia pterocarpa, commonly known as the Australian satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in coastal and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
