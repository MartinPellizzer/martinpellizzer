Eragrostis ambohibengensis

Eragrostis ambohibengensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, grassy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
