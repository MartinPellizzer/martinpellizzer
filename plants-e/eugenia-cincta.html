Eugenia cincta

Eugenia cincta, commonly known as the waxleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eugenia cincta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
