Eccremocarpus lobbianus

Eccremocarpus lobbianus, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are covered in a fleshy, sticky coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
