Euphorbia chasmophila

Euphorbia chasmophila, commonly known as the crevice euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces striking yellowish-green cyathia, which are its specialized inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or rocky environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
