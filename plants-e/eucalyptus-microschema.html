Eucalyptus microschema

Eucalyptus microschema, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, aromatic flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known eucalyptus species. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus microschema is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
