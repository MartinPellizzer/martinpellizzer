Exacum cubense

Exacum cubense, commonly known as the Cuban bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Cuba.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Exacum cubense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
