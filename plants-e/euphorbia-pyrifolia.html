Euphorbia pyrifolia

Euphorbia pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the summer. The plant has alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are usually gray-green in color. It is adapted to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Euphorbia pyrifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
