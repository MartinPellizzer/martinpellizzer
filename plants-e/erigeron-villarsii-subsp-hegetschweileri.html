Erigeron villarsii subsp. hegetschweileri

Erigeron villarsii subsp.

hegetschweileri, commonly known as the Alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
