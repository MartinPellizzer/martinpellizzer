Elaeocarpus serrulatus

Elaeocarpus serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces distinctive, woody fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential pharmacological properties.

Elaeocarpus serrulatus plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
