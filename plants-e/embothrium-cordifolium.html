Embothrium cordifolium

Embothrium cordifolium, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
