Euphrasia × vernalis

Euphrasia × vernalis, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a hybrid plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with dark veins. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. It produces a single flower per stem, which blooms in early spring.

Euphrasia × vernalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
