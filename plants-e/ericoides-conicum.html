Ericoides conicum

Ericoides conicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Ericoides conicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
