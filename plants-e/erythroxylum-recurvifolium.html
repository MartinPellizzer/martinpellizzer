Erythroxylum recurvifolium

Erythroxylum recurvifolium, commonly known as the curly-leaved erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is related to the genus Erythroxylum, which includes the coca plant.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
