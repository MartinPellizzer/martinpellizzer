Eugenia peroblata

Eugenia peroblata, commonly known as the white-leaved eucalyptus, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white tomentum. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Eugenia peroblata is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands, where it contributes to the local ecosystem.
