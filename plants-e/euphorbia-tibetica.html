Euphorbia tibetica

Euphorbia tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan spurge, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It features a distinctive appearance with its thick, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to harsh, arid environments and has a slow growth rate.

Euphorbia tibetica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging conditions.
