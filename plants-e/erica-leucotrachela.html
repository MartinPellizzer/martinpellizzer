Erica leucotrachela

Erica leucotrachela, commonly known as the white-streaked heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in areas with high sunlight exposure. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica leucotrachela is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
