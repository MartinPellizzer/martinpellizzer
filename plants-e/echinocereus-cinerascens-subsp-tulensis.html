Echinocereus cinerascens subsp. tulensis

Echinocereus cinerascens subsp.

tulensis, commonly known as the Tulip Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
