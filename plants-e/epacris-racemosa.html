Epacris racemosa

Epacris racemosa, commonly known as the clustered heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces showy, pink to red tubular flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Epacris racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
