Eriogonum hylophilum

Eriogonum hylophilum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum hylophilum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in high-elevation environments.
