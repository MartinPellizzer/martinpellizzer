Evolvulus alsinoides var. philippinensis

Evolvulus alsinoides var. philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a herbaceous annual with prostrate or trailing growth habit, featuring small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed.
