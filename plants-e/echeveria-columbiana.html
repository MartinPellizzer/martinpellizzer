Echeveria columbiana

Echeveria columbiana, commonly known as the Columbian rose.

It is a succulent plant native to Mexico, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a fine, powdery coating. The leaves are usually blue-gray or green, with a slight bluish tint, and can grow up to 4 inches in length. It produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months.

This species is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
