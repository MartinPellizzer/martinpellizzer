Eucalyptus × brachyphylla

Eucalyptus × brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and often forms dense, shrub-like growth. The plant typically grows in heathland and sandy soils, adapting well to arid and coastal environments. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale blue.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental features.
