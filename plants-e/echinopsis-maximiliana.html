Echinopsis maximiliana

Echinopsis maximiliana, commonly known as the giant night-blooming cactus, is a species native to the deserts of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
