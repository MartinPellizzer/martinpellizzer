Eragrostis lilliputiana

Eragrostis lilliputiana, commonly known as the dwarf lovegrass, is a small annual grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble those of other lovegrasses. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, making it one of the smallest members of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
