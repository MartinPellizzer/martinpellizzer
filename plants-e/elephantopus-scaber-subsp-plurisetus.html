Elephantopus scaber subsp. plurisetus

Elephantopus scaber subsp.

plurisetus, commonly known as the hairy elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its large, hairy, fleshy leaves that resemble the foot of an elephant, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
