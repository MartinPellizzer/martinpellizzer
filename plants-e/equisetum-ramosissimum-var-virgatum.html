Equisetum ramosissimum var. virgatum

Equisetum ramosissimum var. virgatum, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a perennial plant characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered in small, scale-like structures.

It belongs to the Equisetaceae family and is known for its primitive, ancient appearance, resembling fossilized plants from the Carboniferous period. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, which are typically found near the ground level. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it an aggressive colonizer in suitable habitats.
