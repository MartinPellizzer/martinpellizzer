Euphorbia conferta

Euphorbia conferta, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It contains a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Euphorbia conferta is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun.
