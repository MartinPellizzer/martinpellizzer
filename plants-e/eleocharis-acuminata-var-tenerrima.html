Eleocharis acuminata var. tenerrima

Eleocharis acuminata var. tenerrima, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a wetland plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It typically grows in marshy or shallow aquatic environments, thriving in areas with standing water or saturated soil. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are often surrounded by bracts. It is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate varying water levels and nutrient conditions.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in suitable habitats.
