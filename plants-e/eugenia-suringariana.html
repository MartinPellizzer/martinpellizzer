Eugenia suringariana

Eugenia suringariana, commonly known as the Suringa tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
