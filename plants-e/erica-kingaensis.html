Erica kingaensis

Erica kingaensis, commonly known as Kinga's heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer. Erica kingaensis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
