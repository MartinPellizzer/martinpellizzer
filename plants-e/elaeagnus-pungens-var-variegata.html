Elaeagnus pungens var. variegata

Elaeagnus pungens var. variegata, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking visual interest in the landscape.
