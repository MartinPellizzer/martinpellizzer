Echinochloa zonalis

Echinochloa zonalis, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its wide, flat blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Echinochloa zonalis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
