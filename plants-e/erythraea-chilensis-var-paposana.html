Erythraea chilensis var. paposana

Erythraea chilensis var. paposana, commonly known as the Chilean erythraea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of central Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to the arid and coastal climate of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
