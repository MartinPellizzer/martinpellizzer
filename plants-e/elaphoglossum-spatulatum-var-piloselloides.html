Elaphoglossum spatulatum var. piloselloides

Elaphoglossum spatulatum var. piloselloides, commonly known as the hairy-leaf holly fern, is a variant of the spatulatum fern species.

It is a delicate, evergreen fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are characterized by their finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, hairy texture. This variety is native to parts of Asia and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in humid climates.
