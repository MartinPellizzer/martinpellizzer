Eugenia valsuganana

Eugenia valsuganana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit production.
