Echenais ferox

Echenais ferox, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in dense forests. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its aggressive spreading habit.
