Emilia kilwensis

Emilia kilwensis, commonly known as the Kilwensian emilia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers with a distinctive, sweet scent. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Emilia kilwensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
