Eriophyllum nevinii

Eriophyllum nevinii, commonly known as the Nevin's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to California, specifically found in the coastal regions of Santa Barbara County.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters and dry summers. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in the spring, and it produces fluffy, white to grayish seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Eriophyllum nevinii is considered a rare and endemic species, making it a subject of interest for conservation efforts.
