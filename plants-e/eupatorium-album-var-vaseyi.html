Eupatorium album var. vaseyi

Eupatorium album var. vaseyi, commonly known as White Boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

White Boneset is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
