Eupatorium stenolepis

Eupatorium stenolepis, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium stenolepis is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine, though its medicinal properties are not well-documented.
