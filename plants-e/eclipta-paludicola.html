Eclipta paludicola

Eclipta paludicola, commonly known as swamp eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in marshy or pond edge habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white in color.

Eclipta paludicola is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
