Eriachne pallescens var. muelleri

Eriachne pallescens var. muelleri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass or Mueller's eriachne, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves that are usually pale green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This grass is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
