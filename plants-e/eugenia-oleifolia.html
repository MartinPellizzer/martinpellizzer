Eugenia oleifolia

Eugenia oleifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It is known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common ornamental plant in warm climates.
