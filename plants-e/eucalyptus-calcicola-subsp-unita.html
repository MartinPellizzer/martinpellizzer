Eucalyptus calcicola subsp. unita

Eucalyptus calcicola subsp.

unita, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white to greyish bloom.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale or yellowish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Eucalyptus calcicola subsp.

unita is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
