Euphorbia sepulta

Euphorbia sepulta, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as cliffs and rocky slopes.

Euphorbia sepulta is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
