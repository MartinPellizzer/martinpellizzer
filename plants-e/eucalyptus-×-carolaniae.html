Eucalyptus × carolaniae

Eucalyptus × carolaniae, commonly known as the hybrid gum tree, is a species resulting from the cross between Eucalyptus camaldulensis and Eucalyptus robusta.

It is a medium to tall tree, typically reaching heights of 10 to 20 meters, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are lance-shaped, often with a silver underside, and the flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. The bark is smooth and peels in strips, revealing a mottled appearance, and the tree is well adapted to a range of soil types and climatic conditions.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
