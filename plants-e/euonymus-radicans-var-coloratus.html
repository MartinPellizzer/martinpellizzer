Euonymus radicans var. coloratus

Euonymus radicans var. coloratus, commonly known as the red-leaf creeper, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant foliage, which turns red in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy surface and a striking coloration that varies from green to red.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover slopes or retaining walls.
