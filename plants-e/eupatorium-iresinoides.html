Eupatorium iresinoides

Eupatorium iresinoides, commonly known as black snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, dark brown fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.
