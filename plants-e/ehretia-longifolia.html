Ehretia longifolia

Ehretia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf ehretia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, greenish-brown fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
