Eschweilera grata

Eschweilera grata, commonly known as the golden wax palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Eschweilera grata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
