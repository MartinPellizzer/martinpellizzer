Elaeocarpus obtusus

Elaeocarpus obtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically green to reddish in color. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often marked with shallow fissures.

Elaeocarpus obtusus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
