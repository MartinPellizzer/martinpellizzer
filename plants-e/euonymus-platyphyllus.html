Euonymus platyphyllus

Euonymus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, inflated seed capsules that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Euonymus platyphyllus is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
