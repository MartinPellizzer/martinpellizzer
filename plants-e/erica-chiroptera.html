Erica chiroptera

Erica chiroptera, commonly known as the bat's ear heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica chiroptera typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open, sunny environments.

It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region, with periods of drought tolerance.
