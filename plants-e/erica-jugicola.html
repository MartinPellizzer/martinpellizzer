Erica jugicola

Erica jugicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and evergreen plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters. Erica jugicola thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, often growing in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
