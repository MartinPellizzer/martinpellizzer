Eugenia floribunda

Eugenia floribunda, commonly known as the red-eyed woodberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible red berries that are popular in local cuisines. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and showy blooms, making it a desirable plant for gardens.

Eugenia floribunda is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
