Eucalyptus tereticornis var. bancroftii

Eucalyptus tereticornis var. bancroftii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the narrow-leafed mallee eucalyptus.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which are typically followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is native to southern Australia, particularly in regions with dry, sandy soils.

This species is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions and plays an important role in its native ecosystems.
