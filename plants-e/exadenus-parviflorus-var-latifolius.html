Exadenus parviflorus var. latifolius

Exadenus parviflorus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf exadenus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
