Euphorbia hopetownensis

Euphorbia hopetownensis, commonly known as the Hope Town euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and sparse, upright leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a cup-shaped structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
