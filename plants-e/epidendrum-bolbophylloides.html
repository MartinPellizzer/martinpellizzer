Epidendrum bolbophylloides

Epidendrum bolbophylloides, commonly known as the bulbophyll orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and upright, branching stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
