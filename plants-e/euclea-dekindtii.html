Euclea dekindtii

Euclea dekindtii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The wood of Euclea dekindtii is highly valued for its strength and durability, though its use is often limited due to its slow growth and conservation status.
