Elytrigia intermedia subsp. latronum

Elytrigia intermedia subsp.

latronum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its role in ecological restoration and as a forage crop for livestock.
