Erica bracteolaris

Erica bracteolaris, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate beauty to its evergreen foliage. Erica bracteolaris thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
