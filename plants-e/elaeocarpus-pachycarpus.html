Elaeocarpus pachycarpus

Elaeocarpus pachycarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruited elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, woody fruits that are typically ellipsoid in shape. The plant produces flowers that are usually white or pale pink, and it has a dense, evergreen canopy with elliptical leaves.

Elaeocarpus pachycarpus is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological importance.
