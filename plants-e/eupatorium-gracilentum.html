Eupatorium gracilentum

Eupatorium gracilentum, commonly known as slender boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Eupatorium gracilentum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
