Erigeron alaskanus f. albiflorus

Erigeron alaskanus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the Alaska fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The foliage is covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.
