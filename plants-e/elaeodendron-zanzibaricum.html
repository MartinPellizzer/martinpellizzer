Elaeodendron zanzibaricum

Elaeodendron zanzibaricum, commonly known as the Zanzibar elaeodendron, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and thrives in coastal environments with high salinity. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

The Zanzibar elaeodendron is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
