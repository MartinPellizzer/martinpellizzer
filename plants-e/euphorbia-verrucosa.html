Euphorbia verrucosa

Euphorbia verrucosa, commonly known as the疣茎大戟, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems covered in small, warty protrusions. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
