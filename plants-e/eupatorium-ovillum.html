Eupatorium ovillum

Eupatorium ovillum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Eupatorium ovillum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
