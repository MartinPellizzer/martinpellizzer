Equisetum sylvaticum f. tardatum

Equisetum sylvaticum f. tardatum, commonly known as the late wood rush, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are typically found in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Paleozoic era, making it one of the oldest living plant groups.
