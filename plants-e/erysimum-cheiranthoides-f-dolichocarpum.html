Erysimum cheiranthoides f. dolichocarpum

Erysimum cheiranthoides f. dolichocarpum, commonly known as the long-seed wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and elongated, cylindrical seed pods, which distinguish it from other forms of the species. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to regions of southern Europe. It thrives in full sun and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in both horticulture and ecological studies.
