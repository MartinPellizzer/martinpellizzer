Eriogonum minimum

Eriogonum minimum, commonly known as the dwarf mullein, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 inches and features dense, fuzzy gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.

Eriogonum minimum is an important plant for pollinators and is well adapted to arid environments.
