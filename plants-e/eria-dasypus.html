Eria dasypus

Eria dasypus, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eria dasypus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
