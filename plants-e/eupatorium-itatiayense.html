Eupatorium itatiayense

Eupatorium itatiayense, commonly known as the Itatiaya feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, flat clusters.

Eupatorium itatiayense is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
