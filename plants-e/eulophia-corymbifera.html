Eulophia corymbifera

Eulophia corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered helmet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a sympodial growth pattern.

Eulophia corymbifera is valued for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
