Ericoides purpureum

Ericoides purpureum, commonly known as the purple ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a dense, mat-like formation.

Ericoides purpureum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
