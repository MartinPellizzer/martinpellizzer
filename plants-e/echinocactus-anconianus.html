Echinocactus anconianus

Echinocactus anconianus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, and it produces yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
