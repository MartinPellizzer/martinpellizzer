Eugenia stereophylla

Eugenia stereophylla, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the wet tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Eugenia stereophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
