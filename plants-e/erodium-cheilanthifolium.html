Erodium cheilanthifolium

Erodium cheilanthifolium, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, fern-like leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in dry landscapes.
