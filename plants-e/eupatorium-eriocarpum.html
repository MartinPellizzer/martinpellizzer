Eupatorium eriocarpum

Eupatorium eriocarpum, commonly known as roughhead boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

Eupatorium eriocarpum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
