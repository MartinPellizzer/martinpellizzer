Eggersia

Eggersia, commonly known as the glassy-winged sharpshooter, is a species of planthopper native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its transparent wings and slender body, which allow it to blend with its environment. The plant plays a significant role in the transmission of plant pathogens, particularly Xylella fastidiosa, which causes severe diseases in crops. Eggersia feeds on the xylem sap of various plants, including citrus, grapevines, and ornamental species.

Its life cycle and behavior are closely linked to the health and productivity of the plants it inhabits.
