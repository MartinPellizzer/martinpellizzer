Echeveria bracteosa

Echeveria bracteosa, commonly known as the silver rose echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray or silver in color, with a powdery coating. The plant produces striking pink or red flowers on tall stalks during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Echeveria bracteosa is popular in rock gardens and as a potted plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
