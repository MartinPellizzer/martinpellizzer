Eriosorus velleus

Eriosorus velleus, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly appearance, which gives it a soft, velvety texture. The plant's foliage consists of small, overlapping leaves that form a compact rosette. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Despite its delicate appearance, it is a resilient species with a distinctive botanical structure.
