Eugenia mattogrossensis

Eugenia mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Amazonian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
