Eugenia chlorantha

Eugenia chlorantha, commonly known as the white eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the wet tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Eugenia chlorantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
