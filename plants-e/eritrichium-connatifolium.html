Eritrichium connatifolium

Eritrichium connatifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color, with five petals arranged in a bell shape. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Eritrichium connatifolium is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
