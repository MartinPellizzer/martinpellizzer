Eupatorium angustatum

Eupatorium angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Eupatorium angustatum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
