Eugenia virgata

Eugenia virgata, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Eugenia virgata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
