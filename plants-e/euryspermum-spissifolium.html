Euryspermum spissifolium

Euryspermum spissifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved euryspermum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
