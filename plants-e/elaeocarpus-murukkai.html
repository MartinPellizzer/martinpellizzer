Elaeocarpus murukkai

Elaeocarpus murukkai, commonly known as the Malabar elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically clustered together, containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody capsule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
