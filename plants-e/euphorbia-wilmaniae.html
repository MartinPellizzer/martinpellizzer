Euphorbia wilmaniae

Euphorbia wilmaniae, commonly known as the Wilman's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
