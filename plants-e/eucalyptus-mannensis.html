Eucalyptus mannensis

Eucalyptus mannensis, commonly known as the malleeflower eucalypt, is a medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or grey. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in umbels. Eucalyptus mannensis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
