Echium vulgare subsp. vulgare

Echium vulgare subsp.

vulgare, commonly known as Common Viper's Bugloss, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces a tall, branching stem with numerous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, inflated capsule that splits open when mature to release the seeds.
