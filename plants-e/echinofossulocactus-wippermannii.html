Echinofossulocactus wippermannii

Echinofossulocactus wippermannii, commonly known as the Wippermann's spiny barrel cactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that is covered in prominent, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water requirements.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat needs, it is often cultivated in specialized collections and conservation programs.
