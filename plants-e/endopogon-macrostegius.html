Endopogon macrostegius

Endopogon macrostegius, commonly known as the large-stemmed sandgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is well adapted to sandy soils, allowing it to thrive in environments with low water availability.

Endopogon macrostegius plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
