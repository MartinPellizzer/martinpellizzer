Echeveria prunina

Echeveria prunina, commonly known as the Prunina Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to lavender flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
