Echinochloa madagascariensis

Echinochloa madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar panicgrass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a rough texture.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
