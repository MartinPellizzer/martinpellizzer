Echinopsis Dobeana

Echinopsis dobeana, scientifically known as Echinopsis dobeana, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America. This unique cactus is characterized by its short, spiny stems and vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom at night. It thrives in harsh desert environments, adapting to extreme temperatures and minimal rainfall. Due to its rarity and beauty, it has become a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this species from habitat loss and over-collection.
