Elymus farctus

Elymus farctus, commonly known as the fatstem wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a rough texture.

Elymus farctus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
