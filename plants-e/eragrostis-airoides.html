Eragrostis airoides

Eragrostis airoides, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, purple to pinkish flower heads, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Eragrostis airoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and floral display.
