Eucalyptus calycogona subsp. trachybasis

Eucalyptus calycogona subsp.

trachybasis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee and woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
