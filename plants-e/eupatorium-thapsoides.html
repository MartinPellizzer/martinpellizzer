Eupatorium thapsoides

Eupatorium thapsoides, commonly known as gray goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a branched stem and is often found in the eastern United States.

Eupatorium thapsoides is a valuable plant for pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
