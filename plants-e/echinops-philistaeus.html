Echinops philistaeus

Echinops philistaeus, commonly known as the Palestine thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of the Middle East, including Israel and Jordan. The plant is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a spiky ball, which are a feature of the genus Echinops. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by the development of its distinctive seed heads.

Echinops philistaeus is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry and harsh conditions.
