Eremurus × gypsaceus

Eremurus × gypsaceus, commonly known as the hybrid tumbleweed, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Eremurus przewalskii and Eremurus gyps.

It is a member of the genus Eremurus, which belongs to the family Asteraceae, commonly known as the sunflower family. This plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow or orange flower heads that resemble dandelions. It is native to the arid regions of Central Asia and is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
