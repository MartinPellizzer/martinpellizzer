Eragrostis montufarii

Eragrostis montufarii, commonly known as the mountain false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountain meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
