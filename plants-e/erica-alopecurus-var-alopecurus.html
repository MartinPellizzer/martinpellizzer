Erica alopecurus var. alopecurus

Erica alopecurus var. alopecurus, commonly known as the heath cinquefoil, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a dense, mat-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
