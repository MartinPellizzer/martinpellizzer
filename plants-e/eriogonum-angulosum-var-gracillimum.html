Eriogonum angulosum var. gracillimum

Eriogonum angulosum var. gracillimum, commonly known as the slender leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like sorrel and knotweed.

This variety is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.
