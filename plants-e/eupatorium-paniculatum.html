Eupatorium paniculatum

Eupatorium paniculatum, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium paniculatum is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
