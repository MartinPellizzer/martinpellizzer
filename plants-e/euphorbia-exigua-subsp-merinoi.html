Euphorbia exigua subsp. merinoi

Euphorbia exigua subsp.

merinoi, commonly known as the Merino's prickly pear, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
