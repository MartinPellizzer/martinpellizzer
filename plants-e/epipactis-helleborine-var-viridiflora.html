Epipactis helleborine var. viridiflora

Epipactis helleborine var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces greenish-yellow to greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The species is characterized by its unique floral structure and its ability to form mycorrhizal associations with fungi.
