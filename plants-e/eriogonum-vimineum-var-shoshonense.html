Eriogonum vimineum var. shoshonense

Eriogonum vimineum var. shoshonense, commonly known as the Shoshone buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has slender, wiry stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and open woodland habitats.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
