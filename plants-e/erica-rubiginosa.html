Erica rubiginosa

Erica rubiginosa, commonly known as the red heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in early spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Erica rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
