Euphorbia meganaesos

Euphorbia meganaesos, commonly known as the giant euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems for survival.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
