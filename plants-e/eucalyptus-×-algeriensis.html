Eucalyptus × algeriensis

Eucalyptus × algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian gum tree, is a hybrid species native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, aromatic flowers that range from white to pink, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Eucalyptus × algeriensis is valued for its timber and has been introduced to other regions for ornamental and industrial purposes.
