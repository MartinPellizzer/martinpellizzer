Eugenia tetramera

Eugenia tetramera, commonly known as the four-parted eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls of four. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
