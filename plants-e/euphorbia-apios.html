Euphorbia apios

Euphorbia apios, commonly known as the red-veined euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features succulent, ribbed stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive reddish vein pattern.

The plant is known for its ability to store water in its stems, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.
