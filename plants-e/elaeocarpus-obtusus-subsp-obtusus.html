Elaeocarpus obtusus subsp. obtusus

Elaeocarpus obtusus subsp.

obtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant features simple, opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces woody, spherical fruits that enclose a single seed.

The subspecies is characterized by its broad, acuminate leaves and is often found in lowland rainforests.
