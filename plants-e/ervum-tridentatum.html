Ervum tridentatum

Ervum tridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring. Its trifoliate structure and flowering pattern are key botanical features that aid in its identification.

Ervum tridentatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
