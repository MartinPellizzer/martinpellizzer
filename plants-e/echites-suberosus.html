Echites suberosus

Echites suberosus, commonly known as the roughleaf echites, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its stems are often woody and can become thick and swollen, storing water.

Echites suberosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
