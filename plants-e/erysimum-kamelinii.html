Erysimum kamelinii

Erysimum kamelinii, commonly known as the Kamelin's wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are borne on elongated pedicels. Its flowering period usually occurs between March and May, depending on the local climate.

Erysimum kamelinii is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in full sun conditions.
