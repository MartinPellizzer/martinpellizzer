Euphorbia perrierioides

Euphorbia perrierioides, commonly known as the Perrier's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive in dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
