Euphorbia triaculeata

Euphorbia triaculeata, commonly known as the three-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with spiny, triangular leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
