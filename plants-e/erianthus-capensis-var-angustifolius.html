Erianthus capensis var. angustifolius

Erianthus capensis var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sugarcane grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and tapering. The plant grows in tall, dense clumps and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.

Its fibrous roots make it a useful plant for erosion control and as a forage crop.
