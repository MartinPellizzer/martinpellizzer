Elytrigia dasystachya subsp. albicans

Elytrigia dasystachya subsp.

albicans, commonly known as the white-stemmed false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often whitish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
