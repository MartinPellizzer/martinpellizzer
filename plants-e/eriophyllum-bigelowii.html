Eriophyllum bigelowii

Eriophyllum bigelowii, commonly known as Bigelow's woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, white to grayish woolly covering on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Eriophyllum bigelowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
