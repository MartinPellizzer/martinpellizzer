Eragrostis congesta

Eragrostis congesta, commonly known as clustered lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Eragrostis congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
