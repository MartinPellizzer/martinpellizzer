Erioscirpus microstachyus

Erioscirpus microstachyus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically inconspicuous, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Erioscirpus microstachyus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms and aiding in water filtration.
