Echium pyramidatum

Echium pyramidatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its unique conical flower head is composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echium pyramidatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
