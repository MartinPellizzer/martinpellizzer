Ephedra distachya var. caspia

Ephedra distachya var. caspia, commonly known as the Caspian ephedra, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its jointed, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

This variety is closely related to other ephedra species and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
