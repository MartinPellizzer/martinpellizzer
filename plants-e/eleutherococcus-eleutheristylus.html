Eleutherococcus eleutheristylus

Eleutherococcus eleutheristylus, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of Eastern Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, pinnately compound leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic properties, though its botanical classification remains a subject of debate among taxonomists.
