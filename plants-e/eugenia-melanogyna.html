Eugenia melanogyna

Eugenia melanogyna, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, black or dark purple drupe, which is edible and has a tart, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia melanogyna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
