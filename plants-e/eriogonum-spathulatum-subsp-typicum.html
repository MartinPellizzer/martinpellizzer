Eriogonum spathulatum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum spathulatum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the broadleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.

The subspecies typicum is distinguished by its more robust growth and broader leaves compared to other subspecies.
