Esula ararica

Esula ararica, commonly known as the Araric tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits containing edible seeds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in various soil types and climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
