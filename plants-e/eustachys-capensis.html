Eustachys capensis

Eustachys capensis, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grass species.
