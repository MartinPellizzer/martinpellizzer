Excoecaria biglandulosa var. cuneata

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. cuneata, commonly known as the cuneate spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its sap contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
