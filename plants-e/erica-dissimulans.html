Erica dissimulans

Erica dissimulans, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, white waxy coating. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are usually pink or white and are clustered in small, rounded inflorescences.

Erica dissimulans is well adapted to poor, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
