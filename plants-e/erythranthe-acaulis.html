Erythranthe acaulis

Erythranthe acaulis, commonly known as the cushion mouse-ear, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosette of fleshy, silver-gray leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple during the summer months. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
