Eucomis punctata

Eucomis punctata, commonly known as the spikesedge or forest daisy, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, compact base. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences topped with clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. Its foliage and blooms are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegant appearance.

Eucomis punctata thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
