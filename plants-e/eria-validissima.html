Eria validissima

Eria validissima, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
