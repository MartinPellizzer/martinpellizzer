Erysimum pulchellum var. calvertii

Erysimum pulchellum var. calvertii, commonly known as the Calvert's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
