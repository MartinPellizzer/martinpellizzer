Eulophia zanzibarica

Eulophia zanzibarica, commonly known as the Zanzibar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Zanzibar in Tanzania.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with numerous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a slight purple tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Eulophia zanzibarica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a symbol of the unique biodiversity of the East African archipelago.
