Euphorbia pteroneura var. typica

Euphorbia pteroneura var. typica, commonly known as the feathered spurge, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, spiny stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of succulents. It produces a milky sap that can be irritant to the skin.

This species is often cultivated for its unique appearance and hardy nature in arid environments.
