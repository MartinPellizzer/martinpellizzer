Eragrostis maypurensis var. meratiana

Eragrostis maypurensis var. meratiana, commonly known as the Maypure grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed areas, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in such environments.

Its botanical features make it useful for soil stabilization and as a forage plant in certain regions.
