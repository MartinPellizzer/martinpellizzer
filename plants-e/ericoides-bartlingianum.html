Ericoides bartlingianum

Ericoides bartlingianum, commonly known as the Bartling's ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late summer.

Ericoides bartlingianum is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
