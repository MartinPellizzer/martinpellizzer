Erythrina dybowskii

Erythrina dybowskii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a vivid display during the flowering season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Erythrina dybowskii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its ornate appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.
