Eleocharis acicularis var. acicularis

Eleocharis acicularis var. acicularis, commonly known as the needle sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common species in marshes and pond edges. Its reproductive structures are adapted for dispersal by water, aiding in its spread across aquatic habitats.

Eleocharis acicularis var. acicularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
