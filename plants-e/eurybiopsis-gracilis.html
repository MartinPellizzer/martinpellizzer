Eurybiopsis gracilis

Eurybiopsis gracilis, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and unique adaptations, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
