Ervum australe

Ervum australe, commonly known as the Australian vetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a leguminous species that belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its flowers are usually small and yellow or white, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Ervum australe is used in some ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
