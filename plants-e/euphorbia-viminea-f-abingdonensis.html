Euphorbia viminea f. abingdonensis

Euphorbia viminea f. abingdonensis, commonly known as the Abingdon spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a branching stem system with a distinctive, cylindrical shape and a gray-green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, berry-like structures. It is characterized by its ability to store water in its fleshy stems, making it drought-tolerant.

This cultivar is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
