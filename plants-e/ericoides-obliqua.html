Ericoides obliqua

Ericoides obliqua, commonly known as the slender heath, is a shrub native to the wetter regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces bell-shaped, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is adapted to acidic soils, and it is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Ericoides obliqua is an important component of local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
