Erianthus monstieri

Erianthus monstieri, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

Erianthus monstieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
