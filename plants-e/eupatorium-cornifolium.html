Eupatorium cornifolium

Eupatorium cornifolium, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its serrated, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium cornifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
