Eulophia carrii

Eulophia carrii, commonly known as the Carri's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically has a single pseudobulb with a few leaves and bears a spike of small, clustered flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia carrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
