Eupatorium cymuliferum

Eupatorium cymuliferum, commonly known as the clustered boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have serrated edges.

Eupatorium cymuliferum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.
