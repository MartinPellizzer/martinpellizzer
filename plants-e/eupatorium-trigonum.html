Eupatorium trigonum

Eupatorium trigonum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-to-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is commonly found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
