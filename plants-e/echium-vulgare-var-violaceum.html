Echium vulgare var. violaceum

Echium vulgare var. violaceum, commonly known as the purpleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of striking violet to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
