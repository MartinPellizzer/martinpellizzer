Eragrostis interrupta var. laxiflora

Eragrostis interrupta var. laxiflora, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
