Erigeron alpicola var. heterotrichus

Erigeron alpicola var. heterotrichus, commonly known as the alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to violet flowering heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

This variety is distinguished by its unique leaf arrangement and floral structure compared to other members of the Erigeron genus.
