Etlingera aurantia

Etlingera aurantia, commonly known as the golden torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that resemble torches, which are modified leaves used to attract pollinators. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are often hidden within the showy bracts.

Etlingera aurantia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
