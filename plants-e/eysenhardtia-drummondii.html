Eysenhardtia drummondii

Eysenhardtia drummondii, commonly known as the Mexican coffee tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
