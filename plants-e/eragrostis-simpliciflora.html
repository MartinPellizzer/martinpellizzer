Eragrostis simpliciflora

Eragrostis simpliciflora, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
