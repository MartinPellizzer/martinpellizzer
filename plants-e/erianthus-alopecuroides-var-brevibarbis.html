Erianthus alopecuroides var. brevibarbis

Erianthus alopecuroides var. brevibarbis, commonly known as the short-barbed bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for use in native plant gardens and restoration projects.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
