Echinocactus wippermannii

Echinocactus wippermannii, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny, cylindrical fruits. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
