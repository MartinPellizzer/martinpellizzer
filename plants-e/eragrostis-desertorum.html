Eragrostis desertorum

Eragrostis desertorum, commonly known as desert lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to reddish in color. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Due to its resilience, it is also used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
