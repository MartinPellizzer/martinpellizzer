Eugenia poliensis

Eugenia poliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces fragrant white flowers and has glossy, elliptical leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
