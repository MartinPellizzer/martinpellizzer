Eclipta erecta var. brachypoda

Eclipta erecta var. brachypoda, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
