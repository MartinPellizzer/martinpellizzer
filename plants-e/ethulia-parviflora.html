Ethulia parviflora

Ethulia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ethulia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. Ethulia parviflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
