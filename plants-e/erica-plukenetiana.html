Erica plukenetiana

Erica plukenetiana, commonly known as the Cape heath or African heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica plukenetiana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
