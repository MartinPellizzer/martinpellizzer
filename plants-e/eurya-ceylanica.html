Eurya ceylanica

Eurya ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon eurya, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen or deciduous leaves depending on the climate. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, leathery berries. The leaves are usually elliptical with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and the plant has a compact growth habit.
