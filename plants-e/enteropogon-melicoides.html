Enteropogon melicoides

Enteropogon melicoides, commonly known as the dwarf false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is notable for its potential use in ecological restoration and as a source of biomass.
