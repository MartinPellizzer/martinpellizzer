Echites palustris

Echites palustris, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, sandy soils. It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Echites palustris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
