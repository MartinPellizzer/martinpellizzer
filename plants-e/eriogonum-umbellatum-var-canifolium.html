Eriogonum umbellatum var. canifolium

Eriogonum umbellatum var. canifolium, commonly known as the sharpleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, sharply toothed leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
