Elaeocarpus salicifolius

Elaeocarpus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that resemble willow leaves, hence its common name. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.
