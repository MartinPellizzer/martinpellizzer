Euphorbia blepharadena

Euphorbia blepharadena, commonly known as the eye-leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, leaf-like structures that resemble eyes, which are actually modified stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the specialized flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
