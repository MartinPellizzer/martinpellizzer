Euphorbia subsalsa subsp. otzenii

Euphorbia subsalsa subsp.

otzenii, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It features a short, woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that help store water, adapting to its harsh environment.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and ability to survive in extreme conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
