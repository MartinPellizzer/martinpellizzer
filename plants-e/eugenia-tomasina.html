Eugenia tomasina

Eugenia tomasina, commonly known as the Tasmanian guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
