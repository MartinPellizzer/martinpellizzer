Euphorbia garuana

Euphorbia garuana, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in complex inflorescences called cyathia. Its leaves are typically narrow and may have a reddish or purplish hue.

Euphorbia garuana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry woodlands and rocky areas.
