Eupatorium cuspidatum

Eupatorium cuspidatum, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has serrated, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Boneset is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and blooms from late summer to early fall.
