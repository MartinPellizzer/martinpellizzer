Eschscholzia californica var. maritima

Eschscholzia californica var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal poppy, is a variant of the California poppy adapted to coastal environments.

It is a herbaceous annual plant with slender, branching stems and vibrant orange to red flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in saline conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found along ocean shores and dunes.

This variety is notable for its resilience to coastal winds and salt spray.
