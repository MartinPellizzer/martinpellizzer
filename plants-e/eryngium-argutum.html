Eryngium argutum

Eryngium argutum, commonly known as the prickly eryngo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green leaves and spherical, spiny flower heads that resemble a sea urchin. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually lavender to blue in color and bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Eryngium argutum is often used in wildflower gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
