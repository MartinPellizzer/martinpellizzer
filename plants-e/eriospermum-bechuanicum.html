Eriospermum bechuanicum

Eriospermum bechuanicum, commonly known as the Bechuanian eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Eriospermum bechuanicum is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in shrubland or rocky habitats.
