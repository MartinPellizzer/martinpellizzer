Elwendia salsa

Elwendia salsa, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage covered in fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Elwendia salsa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions and a valuable plant for ecological restoration in desert landscapes.
