Erigeron peregrinus f. subvillosus

Erigeron peregrinus f. subvillosus, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely hairy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a soft, downy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
