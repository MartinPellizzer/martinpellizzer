Eupatorium charpinii

Eupatorium charpinii, commonly known as the New Jersey tea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium charpinii is also noted for its historical use in herbal medicine.
