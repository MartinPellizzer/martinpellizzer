Eremocharis

Eremocharis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with long, slender stems and waxy, pale green leaves that resemble the shape of a ghost. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer or early autumn. Eremocharis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich substrates to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized greenhouses or botanical gardens.
