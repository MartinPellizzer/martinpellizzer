Echeveria nevadensis

Echeveria nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada echeveria, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces vibrant purple to pink flowers during the summer months, typically on tall, branching stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
