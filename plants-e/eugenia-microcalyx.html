Eugenia microcalyx

Eugenia microcalyx, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, glossy green leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia microcalyx is also valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
