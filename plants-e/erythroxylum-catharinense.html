Erythroxylum catharinense

Erythroxylum catharinense, commonly known as the Brazilian coffee tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, which are used for propagation. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and research purposes.
