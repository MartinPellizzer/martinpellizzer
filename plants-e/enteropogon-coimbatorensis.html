Enteropogon coimbatorensis

Enteropogon coimbatorensis, commonly known as the Coimbatore grass, is a species of grass native to the southern regions of India.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, pale to brownish flowers.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native environment.
