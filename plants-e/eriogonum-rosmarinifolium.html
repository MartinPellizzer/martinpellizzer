Eriogonum rosmarinifolium

Eriogonum rosmarinifolium, commonly known as lemony goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a lemony scent when crushed. The plant produces clusters of yellow to golden-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

Eriogonum rosmarinifolium is a hardy, drought-tolerant shrub that plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
