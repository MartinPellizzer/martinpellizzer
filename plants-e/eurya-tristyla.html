Eurya tristyla

Eurya tristyla, commonly known as the black elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and turn red in the autumn.

Eurya tristyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal color changes.
