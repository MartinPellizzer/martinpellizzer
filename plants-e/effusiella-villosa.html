Effusiella villosa

Effusiella villosa, commonly known as the hairy effusiella, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its common name and helps in reducing water loss. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Effusiella villosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
