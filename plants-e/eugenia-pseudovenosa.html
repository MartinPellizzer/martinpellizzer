Eugenia pseudovenosa

Eugenia pseudovenosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or velvet cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
