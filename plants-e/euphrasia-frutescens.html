Euphrasia frutescens

Euphrasia frutescens, commonly known as the purple-eyed grass, is a small herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, upright stems with small, clustered leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is the presence of tiny, purple-tinged flowers that bloom in early spring.

Euphrasia frutescens is often found in moist soils and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
