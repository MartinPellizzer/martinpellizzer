Elsholtzia cristata f. saxatilis

Elsholtzia cristata f. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock rose or alpine elsholtzia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, aromatic leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in hue, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun and arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh alpine environments.
