Eucrosia stricklandii

Eucrosia stricklandii, commonly known as the Strickland's euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Eucrosia stricklandii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
