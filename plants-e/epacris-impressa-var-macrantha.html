Epacris impressa var. macrantha

Epacris impressa var. macrantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
