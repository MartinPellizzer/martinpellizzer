Eurybia myrsinoides var. serrata

Eurybia myrsinoides var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated golden saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
