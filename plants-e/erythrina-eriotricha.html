Erythrina eriotricha

Erythrina eriotricha, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five elliptical leaflets, and its bark is typically smooth and reddish in color. Erythrina eriotricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
