Eulophia gilletiana

Eulophia gilletiana, commonly known as the giant eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia gilletiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
