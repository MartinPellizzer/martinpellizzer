Eryngium corniculatum

Eryngium corniculatum, commonly known as the thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as prairies and open woodlands.

Eryngium corniculatum is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
