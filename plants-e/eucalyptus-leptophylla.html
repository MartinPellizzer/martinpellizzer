Eucalyptus leptophylla

Eucalyptus leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in heathlands and woodlands, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.

Eucalyptus leptophylla is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have various traditional and commercial uses.
