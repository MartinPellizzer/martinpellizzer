Elymus lazicus subsp. attenuatiglumis

Elymus lazicus subsp.

attenuatiglumis, commonly known as the slender-leaved wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, glabrous spikelets and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
