Erica doliiformis

Erica doliiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white, pink, or red, during the summer months.

Erica doliiformis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in South Africa.
