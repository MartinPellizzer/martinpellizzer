Euphorbia falcata var. mucronata

Euphorbia falcata var. mucronata, commonly known as the sharpleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
