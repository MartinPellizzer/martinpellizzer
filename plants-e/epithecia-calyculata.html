Epithecia calyculata

Epithecia calyculata, commonly known as the yellow cup lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Trapeliaceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange apothecia, which are typically cup-shaped and surrounded by a distinct marginal rim. The thallus is foliose, meaning it has a leaf-like structure, and is often found on tree bark or soil in temperate regions. This lichen is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
