Epidendrum inaguense

Epidendrum inaguense, commonly known as the Inagua Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean island of Inagua in the Bahamas.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are characterized by their vibrant colors, often ranging from red to orange.

Epidendrum inaguense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
