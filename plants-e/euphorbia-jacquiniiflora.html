Euphorbia jacquiniiflora

Euphorbia jacquiniiflora, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branched stem system that resembles a ladder, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in umbels, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
