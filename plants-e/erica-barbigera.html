Erica barbigera

Erica barbigera, commonly known as the tufted heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica barbigera typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
