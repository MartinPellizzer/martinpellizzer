Euonymus javanicus var. talungensis

Euonymus javanicus var. talungensis, commonly known as the Talungensis spindlewood, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
