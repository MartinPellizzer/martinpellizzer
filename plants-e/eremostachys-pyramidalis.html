Eremostachys pyramidalis

Eremostachys pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal eremostachys, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and fleshy, gray-green leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping in dry climates.
