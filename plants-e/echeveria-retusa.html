Echeveria retusa

Echeveria retusa, commonly known as the Wooly Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features dense, fuzzy gray-green leaves that form a rosette shape, giving it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces upright, branching flower stalks that bear clusters of pink or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its compact form.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique texture and color.
