Eleocharis paracicularis

Eleocharis paracicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland and marsh environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Eleocharis paracicularis plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and waterfowl.
