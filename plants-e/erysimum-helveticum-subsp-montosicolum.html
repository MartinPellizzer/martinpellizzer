Erysimum helveticum subsp. montosicolum

Erysimum helveticum subsp.

montosicolum, commonly known as the Alpine wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
