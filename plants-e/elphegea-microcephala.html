Elphegea microcephala

Elphegea microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf elphegea, is a small herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with compact, rounded foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

Elphegea microcephala is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.
