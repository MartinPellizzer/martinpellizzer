Erysimum franchetii

Erysimum franchetii, commonly known as the Franchet's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species of mustard and cress. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Erysimum franchetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
