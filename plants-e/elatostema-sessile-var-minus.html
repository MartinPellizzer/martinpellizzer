Elatostema sessile var. minus

Elatostema sessile var. minus, commonly known as the small-leaf false shamrock, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in diameter, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is characterized by a waxy surface and a tendency to curl slightly at the edges.
