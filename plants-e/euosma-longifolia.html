Euosma longifolia

Euosma longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf euodia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are drupes, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Euosma longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
