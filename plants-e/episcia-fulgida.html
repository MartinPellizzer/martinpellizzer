Episcia fulgida

Episcia fulgida, commonly known as the golden wax plant, is a tropical perennial native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, waxy, and often brightly colored foliage, which can range from green to yellow and red. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Episcia fulgida is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
