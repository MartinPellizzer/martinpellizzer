Eupatorium luzoniense

Eupatorium luzoniense, commonly known as the Luzon blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Eupatorium luzoniense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
