Eucalyptus umbellata var. dealbata

Eucalyptus umbellata var. dealbata, commonly known as the white gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white to grey bark that peels in thin, fibrous strips. The leaves are lance-shaped, usually grey-green in color, and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

It is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
