Erica cooperi

Erica cooperi, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, evergreen foliage, and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.

Erica cooperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
