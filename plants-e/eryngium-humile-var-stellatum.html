Eryngium humile var. stellatum

Eryngium humile var. stellatum, commonly known as the star eryngo, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, spiny leaves and produces spherical flower heads that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative plant.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
