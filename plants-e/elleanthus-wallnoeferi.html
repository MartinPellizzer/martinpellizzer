Elleanthus wallnoeferi

Elleanthus wallnoeferi, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant has small, pale green leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers. It is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for construction and tool-making.

Elleanthus wallnoeferi is also adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
