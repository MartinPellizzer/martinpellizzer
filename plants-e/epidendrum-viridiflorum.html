Epidendrum viridiflorum

Epidendrum viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in humid tropical environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow lips. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability make it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
