Eugenia filipes

Eugenia filipes, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a round, reddish-purple berry that contains a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
