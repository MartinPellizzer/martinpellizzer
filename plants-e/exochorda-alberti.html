Exochorda alberti

Exochorda alberti, commonly known as the Alberta honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its stems are often reddish-brown and can be somewhat woody with age.

Exochorda alberti is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
