Eucalyptus singularis

Eucalyptus singularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin, papery layers, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus singularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
