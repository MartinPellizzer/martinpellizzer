Eremophila acrida

Eremophila acrida, commonly known as the rough eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Eremophila acrida is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
