Erodium cicutarium subsp. pallidiflorum

Erodium cicutarium subsp.

pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower bedstraw, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

The species is part of the Geraniaceae family, characterized by its distinctive floral structure and often hairy stems.
