Epipactis striata

Epipactis striata, commonly known as the spotted helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and its striking, pale green or yellowish flowers with dark brown markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a complex life cycle involving mycorrhizal associations with fungi, which are essential for its nutrient uptake.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
