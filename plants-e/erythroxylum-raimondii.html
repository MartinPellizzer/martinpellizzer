Erythroxylum raimondii

Erythroxylum raimondii, commonly known as the Peruvian belladonna, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador, and is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces capsule-like fruits that contain elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
