Erica jumellei

Erica jumellei, commonly known as the Jumelle's heather, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica jumellei typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form compact, attractive shrubs.
