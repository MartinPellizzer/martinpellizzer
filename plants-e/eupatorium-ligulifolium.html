Eupatorium ligulifolium

Eupatorium ligulifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium ligulifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
