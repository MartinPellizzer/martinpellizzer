Eupatorium alternans

Eupatorium alternans, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is often finely divided, with a silver-gray underside that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Eupatorium alternans is valued in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
