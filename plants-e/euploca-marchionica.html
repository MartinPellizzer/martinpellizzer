Euploca marchionica

Euploca marchionica, commonly known as the coastal euploca, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and saline environments, demonstrating a high tolerance for salt and wind. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. It is often found in dunes and other coastal habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, maritime conditions.
