Elymus alaskanus

Elymus alaskanus, commonly known as Alaska needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the northwestern United States and Alaska.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spikelets that bear small, awned lemmas. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

Elymus alaskanus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
