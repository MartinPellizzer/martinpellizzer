Euonymus verrucosoides

Euonymus verrucosoides, commonly known as the roughleaf euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, ovate leaves with a rough, waxy texture and a distinctive yellow-green hue in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, inflated seed capsules that persist through winter. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Euonymus verrucosoides is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its resistance to pests and diseases.
