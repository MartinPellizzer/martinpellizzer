Eugenia bahiana

Eugenia bahiana, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, and juicy berries.

Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, while the plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
