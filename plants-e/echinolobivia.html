Echinolobivia

Echinolobivia, commonly known as the spiny lobelia, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and showy blue to purple flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a rough texture.

Echinolobivia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
