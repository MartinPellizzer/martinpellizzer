Ericoides cruentum

Ericoides cruentum, commonly known as the crimson heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

Ericoides cruentum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
