Erophila sabulosa

Erophila sabulosa, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky areas. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects.

Erophila sabulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
