Echeveria pulvinata var. leucotricha

Echeveria pulvinata var. leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features dense, compact rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves with white or silver hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are thick and water-storing, adapted to arid environments. It produces upright flower stalks that bear clusters of pink to reddish flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in well-draining soil.
