Eugenia mirandae

Eugenia mirandae, commonly known as the Miranda's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are followed by small, round berries that are edible. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Eugenia mirandae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in its native region.
