Eriosema simplicifolium var. micranthum

Eriosema simplicifolium var. micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered eriosema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of botanical circles.
