Erisma floribundum

Erisma floribundum, commonly known as the sweet william or garden eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Erisma floribundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
