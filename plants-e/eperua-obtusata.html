Eperua obtusata

Eperua obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf eperua, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is valued for its hard wood and ornamental potential.

Eperua obtusata thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
