Eugenia johorensis

Eugenia johorensis, commonly known as the Malabar chestnut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
