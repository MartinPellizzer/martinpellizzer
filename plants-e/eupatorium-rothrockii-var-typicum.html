Eupatorium rothrockii var. typicum

Eupatorium rothrockii var. typicum, commonly known as Rothrock's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves. It is a member of the Eupatorium genus, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

Its foliage and flowering period make it a distinctive plant in its native habitat.
