Echites multinervius

Echites multinervius, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers, which bloom in the early morning. The plant has a climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers have five petals and a prominent spur.

Echites multinervius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
