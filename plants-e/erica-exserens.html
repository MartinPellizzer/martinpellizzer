Erica exserens

Erica exserens, commonly known as the silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Erica exserens is well adapted to coastal climates and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
