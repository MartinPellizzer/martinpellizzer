Eupatorium suratense

Eupatorium suratense, commonly known as Surat blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and riverbanks, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its blue to purple flower heads are often clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, and it has lance-shaped, hairy leaves.

Eupatorium suratense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
