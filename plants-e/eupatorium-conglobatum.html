Eupatorium conglobatum

Eupatorium conglobatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue-to-purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Eupatorium conglobatum is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
