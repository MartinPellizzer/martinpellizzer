Eriachne brevifolia

Eriachne brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved eriachne, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces inflorescences that are spike-like, bearing tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Eriachne brevifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
