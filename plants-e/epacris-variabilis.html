Epacris variabilis

Epacris variabilis, commonly known as the variable heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Epacris variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
