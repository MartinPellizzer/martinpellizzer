Escallonia spiraeoides

Escallonia spiraeoides, commonly known as the Australian spiraea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or white flowers in the spring, which are borne on long, slender stems. Its leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Escallonia spiraeoides is well-suited to coastal areas and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
