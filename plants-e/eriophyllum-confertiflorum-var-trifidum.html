Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. trifidum

Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in coastal scrub and grassland habitats. Its leaves are typically trifid, meaning they are divided into three parts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
