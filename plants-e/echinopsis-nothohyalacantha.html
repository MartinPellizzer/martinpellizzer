Echinopsis nothohyalacantha

Echinopsis nothohyalacantha, commonly known as the red-tipped moon cactus, is a cactus species native to the dry regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant red spines that contrast with its green body. The plant produces showy, night-blooming flowers in shades of yellow and orange, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
