Epidendrum chogoncolonchense

Epidendrum chogoncolonchense, commonly known as the Chogon Colonchense orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant features a rhizomatous structure with elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are showy, with colorful petals and a distinctive lip, which is a characteristic feature of many orchid species.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
