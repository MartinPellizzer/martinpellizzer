Evolvulus glomeratus var. lanceolatus

Evolvulus glomeratus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant is a climbing vine that produces showy blooms, often seen in gardens and natural habitats. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means.
