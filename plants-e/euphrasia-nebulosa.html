Euphrasia nebulosa

Euphrasia nebulosa, commonly known as the cloudy toadflax, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
