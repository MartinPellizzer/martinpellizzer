Elaeagnus ferruginea subsp. sumatrana

Elaeagnus ferruginea subsp.

sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, scale-like hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by elongated, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
