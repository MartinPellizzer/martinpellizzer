Ericoides desmanthum

Ericoides desmanthum, commonly known as the small heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and heathers. The plant typically forms dense mats of dark green foliage with small, leathery leaves. It produces bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer.

Ericoides desmanthum thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.
