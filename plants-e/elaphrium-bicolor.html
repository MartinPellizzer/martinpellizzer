Elaphrium bicolor

Elaphrium bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored elaphrium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting leaf colors, with the upper surface being a deep green and the underside a pale white or silver. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale blue, adding to its ornamental appeal. Elaphrium bicolor thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique foliage and compact growth habit make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
