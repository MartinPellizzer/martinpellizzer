Euphorbia santapaui

Euphorbia santapaui, commonly known as the Santa Paula euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to California, specifically the Santa Paula area.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its unique sap and diverse growth forms.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
