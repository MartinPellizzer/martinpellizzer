Eugenia kangeanensis

Eugenia kangeanensis, commonly known as the Kangean cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Indonesian island of Kangean and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and red, edible berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant aroma when crushed.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
