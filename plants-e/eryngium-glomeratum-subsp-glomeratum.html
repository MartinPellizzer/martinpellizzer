Eryngium glomeratum subsp. glomeratum

Eryngium glomeratum subsp.

glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features striking blue to purple spiked flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has a robust root system and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
