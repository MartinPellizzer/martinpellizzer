Erigeron yellowstonensis

Erigeron yellowstonensis, commonly known as the Yellowstone fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Wyoming.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellow to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Erigeron yellowstonensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in harsh alpine environments.
