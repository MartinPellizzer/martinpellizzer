Euphorbia macropus

Euphorbia macropus, commonly known as the giant spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems and leaves.

Euphorbia macropus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
