Eragrostis interrupta

Eragrostis interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis interrupta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
