Eyrythalia campestris

Eyrythalia campestris, commonly known as the field erythronium, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often marked with purple or red streaks. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive red or purple throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Eyrythalia campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
