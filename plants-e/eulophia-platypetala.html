Eulophia platypetala

Eulophia platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, and the inflorescence is a raceme of showy, lip-like flowers.

Eulophia platypetala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
