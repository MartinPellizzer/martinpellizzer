Eryngium amethystinodes

Eryngium amethystinodes, commonly known as the amethyst thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles, with a distinctive amethyst-colored central disc. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Eryngium amethystinodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
