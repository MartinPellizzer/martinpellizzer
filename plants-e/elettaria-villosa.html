Elettaria villosa

Elettaria villosa, commonly known as the hairy cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in spikes, and it produces elongated, hairy pods containing aromatic seeds.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cardamom.
