Eragrostis capensis

Eragrostis capensis, commonly known as Cape buffalo grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense growth habit, making it well-suited for dry and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
