Eragrostis villosipes

Eragrostis villosipes, commonly known as silky lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Eragrostis villosipes is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
