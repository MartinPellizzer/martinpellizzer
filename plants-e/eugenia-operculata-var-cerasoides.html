Eugenia operculata var. cerasoides

Eugenia operculata var. cerasoides, commonly known as the cherry gum or red gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pinkish flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
