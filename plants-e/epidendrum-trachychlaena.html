Epidendrum trachychlaena

Epidendrum trachychlaena, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Epidendrum trachychlaena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
