Eleocharis albibracteata

Eleocharis albibracteata, commonly known as the white bract sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
