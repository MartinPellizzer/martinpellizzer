Escallonia glutinosa

Escallonia glutinosa, commonly known as sticky escallonia, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Escallonia glutinosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
