Epipactis magnibracteata

Epipactis magnibracteata, commonly known as the large-bracted helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy greenish-yellow flowers with prominent, fleshy bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, dark brown capsules as its fruit.

Epipactis magnibracteata is a perennial herb that blooms from June to August, showcasing its unique botanical features.
