Eremophila linearis

Eremophila linearis, commonly known as the slender emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila linearis is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
