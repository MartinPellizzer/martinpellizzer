Eugenia pseudoverticillata

Eugenia pseudoverticillata, commonly known as the false verticillate eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences and small, aromatic leaves. It produces white or pale pink flowers that are often used in ornamental horticulture.

The fruit is typically small and edible, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
