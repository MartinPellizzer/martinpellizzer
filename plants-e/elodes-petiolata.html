Elodes petiolata

Elodes petiolata, commonly known as the spiny elodea, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often slightly serrated. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Elodes petiolata thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
