Ervum woronowii

Ervum woronowii, commonly known as the Russian bean or Woronow's bean, is a climbing legume native to Central Asia.

It produces small, pale green to yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant's pod-like fruits are elongated and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Ervum woronowii is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
