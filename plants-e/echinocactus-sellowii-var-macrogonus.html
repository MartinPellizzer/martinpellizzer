Echinocactus sellowii var. macrogonus

Echinocactus sellowii var. macrogonus, commonly known as the Sowden's hedgehog cactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
