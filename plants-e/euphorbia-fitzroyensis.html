Euphorbia fitzroyensis

Euphorbia fitzroyensis, commonly known as Fitzroy's spurge, is a striking succulent native to Australia.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often green or red, depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
