Ericoides broadleyanum

Ericoides broadleyanum, commonly known as the Broadley's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or moorland environments.

Ericoides broadleyanum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
