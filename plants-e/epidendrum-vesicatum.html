Epidendrum vesicatum

Epidendrum vesicatum, commonly known as the vesicata orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive veined pattern on the petals. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette, allowing it to capture moisture from the air.

Epidendrum vesicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
