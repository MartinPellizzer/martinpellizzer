Emblica chayamaritiae

Emblica chayamaritiae, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round, green berries that turn orange when ripe, containing a high concentration of vitamin C. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional properties.

The fruit is widely used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine and as a dietary supplement.
