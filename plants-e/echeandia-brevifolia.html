Echeandia brevifolia

Echeandia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved Echeandia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters that bloom in the spring.

Echeandia brevifolia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
