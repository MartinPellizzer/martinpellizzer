Elaeagnus pungens subsp. subpungens

Elaeagnus pungens subsp.

subpungens, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
