Eugenioides acutum

Eugenioides acutum, commonly known as the sharp-leafed eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves with a pointed tip, and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenioides acutum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
