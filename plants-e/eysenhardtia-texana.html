Eysenhardtia texana

Eysenhardtia texana, commonly known as Texas spongewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
