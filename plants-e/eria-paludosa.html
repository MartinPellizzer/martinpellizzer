Eria paludosa

Eria paludosa, commonly known as the marsh eryngo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and have a distinctive rough texture.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming from late spring to early autumn.
