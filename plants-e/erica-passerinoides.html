Erica passerinoides

Erica passerinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny or partially shaded environments. Erica passerinoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form compact, attractive shrubs.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
