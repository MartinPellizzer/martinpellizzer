Eleogenus capitatus var. minor

Eleogenus capitatus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser golden gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
