Echinochloa rotundiflora

Echinochloa rotundiflora, commonly known as roundleaf barnyardgrass, is an annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, where it can become a weed. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, water, or through attachment to animals, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a problematic weed in many parts of the world.
