Eragrostis grandis var. polyantha

Eragrostis grandis var. polyantha, commonly known as the purple threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of spikelets that bear small, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces a distinctive seed head that is often used in ornamental gardening.

The species is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.
