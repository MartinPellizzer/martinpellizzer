Eupatorium psiadiifolium var. tereticaule

Eupatorium psiadiifolium var. tereticaule, commonly known as narrow-leaved eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
