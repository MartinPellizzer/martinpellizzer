Erythraea spicata var. australis

Erythraea spicata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian erythraea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen.

This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suited for coastal and sandy soils.
