Eriocaulon periyarense

Eriocaulon periyarense, commonly known as the Periyar grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which includes various species of marsh and swamp grasses. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, tufted growth habit.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Eriocaulaceae family.
