Eucryphia patagonica

Eucryphia patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian candlebush, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-green bark and clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Eucryphia patagonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
