Erysimum crassistylum subsp. verresianum

Erysimum crassistylum subsp.

verresianum, commonly known as the Sierra velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fuzzy, heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves and more robust flowering compared to other members of the Erysimum genus.
