Encholirium piresianum

Encholirium piresianum, commonly known as the Piresia orchid, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
