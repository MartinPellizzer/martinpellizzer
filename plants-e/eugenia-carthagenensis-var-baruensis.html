Eugenia carthagenensis var. baruensis

Eugenia carthagenensis var. baruensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
