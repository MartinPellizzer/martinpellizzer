Eulophia camporum

Eulophia camporum, commonly known as the Camp's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in seasonal environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

Eulophia camporum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.
