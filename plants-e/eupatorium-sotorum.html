Eupatorium sotorum

Eupatorium sotorum, commonly known as black snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and bottomlands. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are small and clustered, often with a reddish or purplish hue.

Eupatorium sotorum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
