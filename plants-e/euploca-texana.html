Euploca texana

Euploca texana, commonly known as the Texas euploca, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by dry, capsule-like fruits.

Euploca texana is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in xeriscaped landscapes.
