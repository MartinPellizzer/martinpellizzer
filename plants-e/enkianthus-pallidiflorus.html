Enkianthus pallidiflorus

Enkianthus pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Enkianthus pallidiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
