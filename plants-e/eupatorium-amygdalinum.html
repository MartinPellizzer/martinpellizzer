Eupatorium amygdalinum

Eupatorium amygdalinum, commonly known as sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that appear in late summer. The plant produces small, spiky, spherical fruits called galls, which contain seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Eupatorium amygdalinum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
