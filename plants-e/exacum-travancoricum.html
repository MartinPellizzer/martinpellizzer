Exacum travancoricum

Exacum travancoricum, commonly known as the Travancore morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial with trailing stems and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
