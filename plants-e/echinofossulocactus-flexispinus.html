Echinofossulocactus flexispinus

Echinofossulocactus flexispinus, commonly known as the flexible spiny barrel cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is ribbed and covered with prominent, hooked spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons, which are often borne in clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
