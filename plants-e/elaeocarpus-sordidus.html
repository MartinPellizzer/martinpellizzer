Elaeocarpus sordidus

Elaeocarpus sordidus, commonly known as the black laurier, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some economic importance in local communities.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
