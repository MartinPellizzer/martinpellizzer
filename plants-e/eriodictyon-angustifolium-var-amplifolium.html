Eriodictyon angustifolium var. amplifolium

Eriodictyon angustifolium var. amplifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf yerba santa, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, resinous scent. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub ecosystems.
