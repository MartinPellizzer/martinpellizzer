Eurybiopsis hookeri var. scabra

Eurybiopsis hookeri var. scabra, commonly known as the rough eurybiopsis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
