Eugenia pseudocaryophyllus

Eugenia pseudocaryophyllus, commonly known as the Java clove or clove tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown flower buds that are harvested and dried to produce clove essential oil. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, white, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Eugenia pseudocaryophyllus is cultivated primarily for its clove spice, which is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine.
