Eria elisheae

Eria elisheae, commonly known as the Elishean orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within forests. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its root system is adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Eria elisheae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
