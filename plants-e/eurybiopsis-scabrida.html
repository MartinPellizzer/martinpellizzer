Eurybiopsis scabrida

Eurybiopsis scabrida, commonly known as the rough eurybiopsis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
