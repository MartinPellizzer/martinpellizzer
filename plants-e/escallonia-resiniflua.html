Escallonia resiniflua

Escallonia resiniflua, commonly known as the resinous escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a sticky resin, which gives it its common name and has been used historically in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and attractive appearance make it a valued plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
