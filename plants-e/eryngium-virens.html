Eryngium virens

Eryngium virens, commonly known as the green thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically gray-green in color with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Eryngium virens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
