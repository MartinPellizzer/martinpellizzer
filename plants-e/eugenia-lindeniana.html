Eugenia lindeniana

Eugenia lindeniana, commonly known as the red eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Eugenia lindeniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
