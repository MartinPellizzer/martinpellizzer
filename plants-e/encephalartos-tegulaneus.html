Encephalartos tegulaneus

Encephalartos tegulaneus, commonly known as the Natal knobbed pine, is a species of cycad native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, trunk-like stem and a crown of stiff, dark green, pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its leaves are often covered in small, knob-like projections, giving it its common name.

Encephalartos tegulaneus is a slow-growing plant that prefers well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to warm, dry climates.
