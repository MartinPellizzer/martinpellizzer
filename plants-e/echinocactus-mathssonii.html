Echinocactus mathssonii

Echinocactus mathssonii, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

Echinocactus mathssonii is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
