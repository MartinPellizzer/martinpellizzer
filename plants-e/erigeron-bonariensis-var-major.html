Erigeron bonariensis var. major

Erigeron bonariensis var. major, commonly known as the Blueweed or Blue Mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

This species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
