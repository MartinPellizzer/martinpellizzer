Eugenia salicifolia

Eugenia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are similar in taste to guavas but are less commonly consumed.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its potential medicinal properties.
