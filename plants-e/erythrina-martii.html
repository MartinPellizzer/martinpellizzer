Erythrina martii

Erythrina martii, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three elliptic to ovate leaflets. Its woody pods contain flat, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Erythrina martii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
