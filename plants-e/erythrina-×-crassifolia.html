Erythrina × crassifolia

Erythrina × crassifolia, commonly known as the coral tree, is a hybrid legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has compound leaves with three to seven leaflets and produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value.

Erythrina × crassifolia is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
