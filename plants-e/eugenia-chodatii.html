Eugenia chodatii

Eugenia chodatii, commonly known as the Chodat's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in humid tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

Eugenia chodatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
