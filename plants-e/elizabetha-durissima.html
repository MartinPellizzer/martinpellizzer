Elizabetha durissima

Elizabetha durissima, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, waxy leaves and its showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in partial shade with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to bloom during the Christmas season.

Elizabetha durissima is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
