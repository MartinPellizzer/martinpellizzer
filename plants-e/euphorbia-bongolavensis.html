Euphorbia bongolavensis

Euphorbia bongolavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick stems.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
