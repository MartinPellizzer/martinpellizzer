Eragrostis equitans

Eragrostis equitans, commonly known as the common lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open areas such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Eragrostis equitans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its dense root system.
