Eupatorium trisectum

Eupatorium trisectum, commonly known as narrowleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are finely toothed and often have a hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of small, white, composite flowers.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
