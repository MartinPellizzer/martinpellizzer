Eugenia microphylla

Eugenia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, small leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Eugenia microphylla is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
