Eragrostis intermedia var. praetermissa

Eragrostis intermedia var. praetermissa, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed spikelets. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in natural and agricultural settings.
