Eulophia seleensis var. tenuiscapa

Eulophia seleensis var. tenuiscapa, commonly known as the slender-leafed eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, often on tree trunks or among leaf litter. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
