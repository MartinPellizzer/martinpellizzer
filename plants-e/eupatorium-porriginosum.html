Eupatorium porriginosum

Eupatorium porriginosum, commonly known as boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antipyretic and diaphoretic effects.
