Echinocactus hexaedrophorus var. labouretianus

Echinocactus hexaedrophorus var. labouretianus, commonly known as the Mexican lion's tail, is a succulent cactus native to the Sierra Madre mountains of Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange spines and a compact, globular shape. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rocky or well-drained soils.
