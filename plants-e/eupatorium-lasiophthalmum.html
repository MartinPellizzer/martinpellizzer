Eupatorium lasiophthalmum

Eupatorium lasiophthalmum, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, white to pale purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

Eupatorium lasiophthalmum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
