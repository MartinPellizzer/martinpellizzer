Eremophila veronica

Eremophila veronica, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

Eremophila veronica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
