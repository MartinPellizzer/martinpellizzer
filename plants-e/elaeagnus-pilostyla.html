Elaeagnus pilostyla

Elaeagnus pilostyla, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky areas.

Elaeagnus pilostyla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
