Excoecaria sphaerosperma

Excoecaria sphaerosperma, commonly known as the globe-spined croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that mature to a bright red color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a tendency to form dense thickets.

It is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
