Euphorbia propinqua

Euphorbia propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf pencil cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a compact, branching structure with cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are its specialized inflorescences, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water. It is a low-maintenance plant that is popular among cactus and succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.

Euphorbia propinqua is also known for its ability to store water in its stems, making it well adapted to dry environments.
