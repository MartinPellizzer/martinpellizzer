Erigeron musashensis

Erigeron musashensis, commonly known as the Japanese fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in the summer months.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
