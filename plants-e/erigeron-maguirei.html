Erigeron maguirei

Erigeron maguirei, commonly known as the Maguire's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Erigeron maguirei is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a low, spreading growth habit.
