Euplassa saxicola

Euplassa saxicola, commonly known as the rock aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and purple. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Euplassa saxicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
