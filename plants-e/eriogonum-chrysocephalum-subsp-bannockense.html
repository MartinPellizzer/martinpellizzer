Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp. bannockense

Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp.

bannockense, commonly known as the Bannock Valley buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and showy, clustered white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
