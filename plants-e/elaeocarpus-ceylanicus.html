Elaeocarpus ceylanicus

Elaeocarpus ceylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as tropical forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
