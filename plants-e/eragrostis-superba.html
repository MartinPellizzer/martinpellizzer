Eragrostis superba

Eragrostis superba, commonly known as the blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis superba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
