Eulophia latilabris

Eulophia latilabris, commonly known as the broad-lipped eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Eulophia latilabris thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
