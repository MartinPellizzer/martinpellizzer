Eriogonum inflatum var. fusiforme

Eriogonum inflatum var. fusiforme, commonly known as the inflated buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, inflated bracts that resemble inflated balloons, which are typically pink to purple in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
