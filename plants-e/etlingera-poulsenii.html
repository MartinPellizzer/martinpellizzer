Etlingera poulsenii

Etlingera poulsenii, commonly known as the Pounamu ginger, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Hawaii.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers, and is characterized by its ornamental, aromatic leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a distinct, spicy scent reminiscent of ginger.

Its growth habit is typically clumping, with rhizomatous stems that spread slowly, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
