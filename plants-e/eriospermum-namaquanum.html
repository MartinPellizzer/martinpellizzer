Eriospermum namaquanum

Eriospermum namaquanum, commonly known as the Namib Desert eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and beetles. Its leaves are typically succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently.
