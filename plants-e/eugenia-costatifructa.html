Eugenia costatifructa

Eugenia costatifructa, commonly known as the Costate-fruited Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic qualities.
