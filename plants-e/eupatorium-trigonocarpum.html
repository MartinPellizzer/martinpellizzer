Eupatorium trigonocarpum

Eupatorium trigonocarpum, commonly known as the three-angled eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
