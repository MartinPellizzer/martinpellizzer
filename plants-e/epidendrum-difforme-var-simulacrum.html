Epidendrum difforme var. simulacrum

Epidendrum difforme var. simulacrum, commonly known as the imitation epidendrum, is a fascinating orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environment.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
