Eurybia maritima var. flosculosa

Eurybia maritima var. flosculosa, commonly known as sea holly, is a coastal plant native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a distinctive, spiky flower head that resembles a cluster of small, white flowers. Its adaptability to salty air and strong winds makes it a resilient addition to coastal gardens.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to coastal ecosystems.
