Euphrasia officinalis subvar. simplex

Euphrasia officinalis subvar. simplex, commonly known as the lesser eyebright, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, with toothed margins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are used in herbal medicine for their purported eye health benefits.
