Erythrina colombiana

Erythrina colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a reddish hue. Erythrina colombiana is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration.

It is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
