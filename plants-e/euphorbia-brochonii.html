Euphorbia brochonii

Euphorbia brochonii, commonly known as the Brochon's spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its sap contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation, so caution is advised when handling it.

Euphorbia brochonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
