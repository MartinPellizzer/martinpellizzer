Echinopsilon hyssopifolius var. caspicus

Echinopsilon hyssopifolius var. caspicus, commonly known as the Caspian hyssop-leaf echinops, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, spiny fruits that resemble those of the echinops genus. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
