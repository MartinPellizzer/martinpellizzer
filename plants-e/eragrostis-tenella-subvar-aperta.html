Eragrostis tenella subvar. aperta

Eragrostis tenella subvar. aperta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. Its botanical features include fine, hairless leaves and a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
