Espeletia badilloi

Espeletia badilloi, commonly known as the Páramo rose, is a species native to the páramo ecosystems of Colombia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette growth form and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in high-altitude, cold environments. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate strong winds and intense sunlight.

Espeletia badilloi plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various invertebrates and birds.
