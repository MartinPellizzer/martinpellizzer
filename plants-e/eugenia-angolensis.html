Eugenia angolensis

Eugenia angolensis, commonly known as the Angola guava, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
