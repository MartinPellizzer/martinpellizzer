Eugenia brantiana

Eugenia brantiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or brantiana myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
