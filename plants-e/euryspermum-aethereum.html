Euryspermum aethereum

Euryspermum aethereum, commonly known as the golden thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-gray hue on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
