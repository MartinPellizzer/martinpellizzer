Erigeron subspicatus

Erigeron subspicatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
