Ervum monanthon

Ervum monanthon, commonly known as the common vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Ervum monanthon is also cultivated as a forage crop in some regions.
