Eroteum cordatum

Eroteum cordatum, commonly known as the heart-leaved eroteum, is a distinctive plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves.

The leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, indicating its preference for arid environments. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often pale green or white, contrasting with its dark green foliage. The plant is native to regions with seasonal droughts, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in botanical studies and horticultural collections.
