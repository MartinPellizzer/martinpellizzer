Elaeocarpus ferrugineus subsp. ferrugineus

Elaeocarpus ferrugineus subsp.

ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often reddish-brown underneath.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
