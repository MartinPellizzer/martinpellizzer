Eugenia griseiflora

Eugenia griseiflora, commonly known as the gray-flowered eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and as a source of ornamental foliage.
