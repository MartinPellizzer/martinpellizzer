Eleocharis palustris subsp. iranica

Eleocharis palustris subsp.

iranica, commonly known as the Iranian marsh sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in Iran. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in marshy or swampy habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is often found in regions with seasonal flooding.
