Echiochilon strigosum

Echiochilon strigosum, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear umbel-like clusters of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable plant in traditional cuisine.

Echiochilon strigosum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
