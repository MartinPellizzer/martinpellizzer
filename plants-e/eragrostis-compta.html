Eragrostis compta

Eragrostis compta, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.

Botanically, it is part of the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological resilience and ornamental potential.
