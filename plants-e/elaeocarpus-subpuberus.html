Elaeocarpus subpuberus

Elaeocarpus subpuberus, commonly known as the silky elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly Queensland and New South Wales, where it grows in rainforest and coastal habitats. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

The silky elaeocarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
