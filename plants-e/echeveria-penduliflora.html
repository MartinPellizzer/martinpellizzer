Echeveria penduliflora

Echeveria penduliflora, commonly known as the pendant-flowered echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces long, pendulous flower stalks that bear clusters of yellow flowers. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight.

Echeveria penduliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
