Euphorbia fascicaulis

Euphorbia fascicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branched stem covered in soft, woolly hairs and has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its fleshy stems.

Euphorbia fascicaulis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
