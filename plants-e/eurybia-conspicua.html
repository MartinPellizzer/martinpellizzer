Eurybia conspicua

Eurybia conspicua, commonly known as showy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps. It has alternate, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eurybia conspicua is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability.
