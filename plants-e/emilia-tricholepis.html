Emilia tricholepis

Emilia tricholepis, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-tinged flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Emilia tricholepis has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
