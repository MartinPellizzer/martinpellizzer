Eucalyptus pyriformis

Eucalyptus pyriformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spherical flower buds that are typically green or yellow-green in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus pyriformis plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
