Eriocaulon polyphyllum

Eriocaulon polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eriocaulon polyphyllum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
