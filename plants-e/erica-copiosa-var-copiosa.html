Erica copiosa var. copiosa

Erica copiosa var. copiosa, commonly known as the common heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form with small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica copiosa var. copiosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
