Euphorbia heterochroma subsp. tsavoensis

Euphorbia heterochroma subsp.

tsavoensis, commonly known as the Tsavo euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to Kenya. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

The species is notable for its unique appearance and resilience in harsh, semi-desert conditions.
