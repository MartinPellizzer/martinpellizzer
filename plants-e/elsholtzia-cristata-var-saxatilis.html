Elsholtzia cristata var. saxatilis

Elsholtzia cristata var. saxatilis, commonly known as the mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered with dense, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.
