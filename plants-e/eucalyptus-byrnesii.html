Eucalyptus byrnesii

Eucalyptus byrnesii, commonly known as the Byrnes' mallee, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Western Australia and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and clusters of small, rounded leaves. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant shrub or small tree, often found in heathlands and sandy soils.

Eucalyptus byrnesii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
