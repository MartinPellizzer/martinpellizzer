Erica constantia

Erica constantia, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica constantia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
