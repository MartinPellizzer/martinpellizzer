Euphorbia nevadensis subsp. bolosii

Euphorbia nevadensis subsp.

bolosii, commonly known as the Bolosii's milkweed, is a succulent shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular plant among xeriscaping enthusiasts.
