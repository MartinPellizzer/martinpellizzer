Epipactis tesselata

Epipactis tesselata, commonly known as the tessellated helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and a tall, erect stem that bears a single, showy flower with a distinctive pattern of markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Epipactis tesselata is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in association with other orchid species.
