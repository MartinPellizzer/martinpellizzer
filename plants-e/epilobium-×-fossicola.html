Epilobium × fossicola

Epilobium × fossicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a hybrid plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late summer. Epilobium × fossicola thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and along roadsides.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
