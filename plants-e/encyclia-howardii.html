Encyclia howardii

Encyclia howardii, commonly known as the Howard's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, typically in shades of yellow and green. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments.

Encyclia howardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
