Erica cochleariformis

Erica cochleariformis, commonly known as the spiral heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas with mild, wet winters. Its unique spiral arrangement of leaves gives it a distinctive appearance.

Erica cochleariformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
