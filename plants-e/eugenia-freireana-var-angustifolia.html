Eugenia freireana var. angustifolia

Eugenia freireana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evergreen guava, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications due to their sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
