Euphorbia tuerckheimii

Euphorbia tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's spurges, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive addition to arid landscapes.
