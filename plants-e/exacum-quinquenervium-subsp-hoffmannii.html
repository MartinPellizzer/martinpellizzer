Exacum quinquenervium subsp. hoffmannii

Exacum quinquenervium subsp.

hoffmannii, commonly known as Hoffmann's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins, characteristic of the genus Exacum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
