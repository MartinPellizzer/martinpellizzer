Erigeron bahamensis

Erigeron bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting various pollinators.

Erigeron bahamensis is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native range.
