Euphorbia heterophylla var. brasiliensis

Euphorbia heterophylla var. brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its varied leaf shapes, ranging from elliptical to spoon-like, depending on the environmental conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
