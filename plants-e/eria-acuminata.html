Eria acuminata

Eria acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Eria acuminata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in many regions for horticultural purposes.
