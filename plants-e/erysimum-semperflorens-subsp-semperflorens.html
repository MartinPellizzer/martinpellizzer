Erysimum semperflorens subsp. semperflorens

Erysimum semperflorens subsp.

semperflorens, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant has a compact growth habit with a basal rosette of fuzzy, gray-green leaves. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.
