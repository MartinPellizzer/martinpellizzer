Euphorbia comonduana

Euphorbia comonduana, commonly known as the Cordon duana, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small spines, which help minimize water loss.

Euphorbia comonduana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
