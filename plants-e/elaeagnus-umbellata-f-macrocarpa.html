Elaeagnus umbellata f. macrocarpa

Elaeagnus umbellata f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the silver leaf filbert, is a variant of the sand cherry tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, edible nuts that mature in the fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and edible landscaping. Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it a popular choice for hedges and windbreaks.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant has a long history of cultivation in China.
