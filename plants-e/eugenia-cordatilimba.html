Eugenia cordatilimba

Eugenia cordatilimba, commonly known as the broad-leafed eugenia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by edible, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
