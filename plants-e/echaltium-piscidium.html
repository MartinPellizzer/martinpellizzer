Echaltium piscidium

Echaltium piscidium, commonly known as the waterleaf, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a fish, hence its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swampy areas.

Echaltium piscidium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
