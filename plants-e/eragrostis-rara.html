Eragrostis rara

Eragrostis rara, commonly known as the rare beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments. Eragrostis rara is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is known for its drought tolerance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
