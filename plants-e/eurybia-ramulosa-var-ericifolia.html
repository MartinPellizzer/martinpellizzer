Eurybia ramulosa var. ericifolia

Eurybia ramulosa var. ericifolia, commonly known as the Ericifolia Aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
