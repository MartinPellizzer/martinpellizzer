Esenbeckia atata

Esenbeckia atata, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or esenbeckia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive coloration.

Esenbeckia atata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
