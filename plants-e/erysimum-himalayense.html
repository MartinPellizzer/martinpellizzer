Erysimum himalayense

Erysimum himalayense, commonly known as the Himalayan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Erysimum himalayense is valued in horticulture for its vibrant colors and resilience in cooler climates.
