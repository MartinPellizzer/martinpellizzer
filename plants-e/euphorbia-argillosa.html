Euphorbia argillosa

Euphorbia argillosa, commonly known as the clay euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its stems are often covered in a waxy cuticle that reduces water loss.

Euphorbia argillosa is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.
