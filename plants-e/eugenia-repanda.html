Eugenia repanda

Eugenia repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, dark purple to black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
