Echinocereus triglochidiatus subsp. pacificus

Echinocereus triglochidiatus subsp.

pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It is a small, spiny cactus that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils.

The plant features a short, cylindrical stem with dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It has short, spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
