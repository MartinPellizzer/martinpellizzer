Eragrostis fendleriana

Eragrostis fendleriana, commonly known as silverleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Eragrostis fendleriana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
