Eugenia miniata

Eugenia miniata, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Eugenia miniata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
