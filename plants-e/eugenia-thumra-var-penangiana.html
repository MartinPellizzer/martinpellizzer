Eugenia thumra var. penangiana

Eugenia thumra var. penangiana, commonly known as the Penang guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its nutritional and medicinal properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
