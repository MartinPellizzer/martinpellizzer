Echinocactus haematochroanthus

Echinocactus haematochroanthus, commonly known as the blood-flowered cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural environment.
