Eremophila densifolia subsp. densifolia

Eremophila densifolia subsp.

densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
