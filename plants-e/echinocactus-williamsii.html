Echinocactus williamsii

Echinocactus williamsii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny exterior, which provides protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant's most distinctive feature is its golden-yellow coloration, which comes from the dense covering of fine, white hairs on its surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Echinocactus williamsii is also known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
