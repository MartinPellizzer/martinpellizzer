Epidendrum vinosum

Epidendrum vinosum, commonly known as the purple wax plant, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple inflorescences and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Epidendrum vinosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
