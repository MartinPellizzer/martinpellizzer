Equisetum arvense subsp. boreale

Equisetum arvense subsp.

boreale, commonly known as northern field horsetail, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family. It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of spore-bearing branches and a distinct, hollow, jointed stem.

The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Paleozoic era.
