Encyclia brevifolia

Encyclia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, erect growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a short rhizome system.

Encyclia brevifolia is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its unique floral structure.
