Eriogonum effusum var. leptocladon

Eriogonum effusum var. leptocladon, commonly known as the slender-leaved buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and may have a slightly waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
