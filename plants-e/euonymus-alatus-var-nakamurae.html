Euonymus alatus var. nakamurae

Euonymus alatus var. nakamurae, commonly known as the Japanese winged euonymus, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, followed by orange-red berries that attract birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its use in horticulture and landscaping.
