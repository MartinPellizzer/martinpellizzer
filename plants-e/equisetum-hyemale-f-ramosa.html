Equisetum hyemale f. ramosa

Equisetum hyemale f. ramosa, commonly known as the branching horse tail, is a variant of the common horsetail.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, jointed, and branched stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. It belongs to the Equisetaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, calcareous soils. The plant reproduces via spores and is often found in wetland habitats.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it one of the oldest living plant groups.
