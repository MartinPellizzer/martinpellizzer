Epithelantha micromeris var. typica

Epithelantha micromeris var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jointfoot, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Epithelantha micromeris var. typica is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
