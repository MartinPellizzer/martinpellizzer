Emilia herbacea

Emilia herbacea, commonly known as common emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Emilia herbacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
