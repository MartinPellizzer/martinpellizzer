Evolvulus hypocrateriflorus

Evolvulus hypocrateriflorus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for gardeners.
