Erythronium americanum subsp. americanum

Erythronium americanum subsp.

americanum, commonly known as the American dog's tooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It features a bulbous root system and produces a single, upright stem with a broad, heart-shaped leaf.

The plant is notable for its showy, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
