Echinocactus denudatus var. octogonus

Echinocactus denudatus var. octogonus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that develops a spiny, globular shape as it matures. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom from areoles along the stem. Its thick, succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
