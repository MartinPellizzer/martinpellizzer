Elaeocarpus michaelii

Elaeocarpus michaelii, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
