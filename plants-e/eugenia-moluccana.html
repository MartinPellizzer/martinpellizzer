Eugenia moluccana

Eugenia moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan apple or Java apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round, red or yellow fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic wood and has traditional uses in local medicine.
