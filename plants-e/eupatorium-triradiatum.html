Eupatorium triradiatum

Eupatorium triradiatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside.

Eupatorium triradiatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
