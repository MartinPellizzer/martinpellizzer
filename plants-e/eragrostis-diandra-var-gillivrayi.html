Eragrostis diandra var. gillivrayi

Eragrostis diandra var. gillivrayi, commonly known as the Gillivray's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
