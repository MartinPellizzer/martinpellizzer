Elaeocarpus octopetalus subsp. octopetalus

Elaeocarpus octopetalus subsp.

octopetalus, commonly known as the eight-petaled elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically encased in a fleshy, reddish-purple pulp. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
