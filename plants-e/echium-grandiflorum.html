Echium grandiflorum

Echium grandiflorum, commonly known as the great mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Echium grandiflorum is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
