Eugenia maquilingensis

Eugenia maquilingensis, commonly known as the Maquiling guava, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland to montane forests. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, round berry with a smooth skin.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in local agriculture.
