Eupatorium eggersii

Eupatorium eggersii, commonly known as Eggers' boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
