Eucalyptus haemastoma var. capitata

Eucalyptus haemastoma var. capitata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, greyish-blue leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
