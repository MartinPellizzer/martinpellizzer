Echinocactus pubispinus

Echinocactus pubispinus, commonly known as the woolly hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly bristles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for succulent collections.
