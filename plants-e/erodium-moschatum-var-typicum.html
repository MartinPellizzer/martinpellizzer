Erodium moschatum var. typicum

Erodium moschatum var. typicum, commonly known as sweet everlasting or sweet scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its fragrant foliage and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
