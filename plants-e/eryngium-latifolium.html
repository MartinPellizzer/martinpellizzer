Eryngium latifolium

Eryngium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, surrounded by spiny bracts. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eryngium latifolium is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to pollinators.
