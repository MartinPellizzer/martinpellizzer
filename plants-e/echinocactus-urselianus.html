Echinocactus urselianus

Echinocactus urselianus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that is often covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
