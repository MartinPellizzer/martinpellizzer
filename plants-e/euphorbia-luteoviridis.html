Euphorbia luteoviridis

Euphorbia luteoviridis, commonly known as the yellow-green spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are typically green to yellowish in color, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are surrounded by green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
