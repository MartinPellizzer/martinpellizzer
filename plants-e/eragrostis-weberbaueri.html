Eragrostis weberbaueri

Eragrostis weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

Eragrostis weberbaueri is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
