Echinocereus fasciculatus subsp. boyce-thompsonii

Echinocereus fasciculatus subsp.

boyce-thompsonii, commonly known as the Boyce-Thompson prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to survive in harsh desert conditions.
