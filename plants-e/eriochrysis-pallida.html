Eriochrysis pallida

Eriochrysis pallida, commonly known as the pale hairgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, fine leaves and small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet, coastal environments such as salt marshes and estuaries. It is well adapted to saline conditions and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Eriochrysis pallida plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
