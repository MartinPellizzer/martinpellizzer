Eupatorium fuliginosum

Eupatorium fuliginosum, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its medicinal properties. Its roots and rhizomes are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It plays an important role in both ecological systems and herbal medicine.
