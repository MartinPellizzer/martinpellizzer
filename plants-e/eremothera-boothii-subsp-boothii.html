Eremothera boothii subsp. boothii

Eremothera boothii subsp.

boothii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a rosette growth form, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, reducing water loss through transpiration. Eremothera boothii subsp.

boothii is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.
