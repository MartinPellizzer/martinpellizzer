Eutaxia hirsuta

Eutaxia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy eutaxia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Eutaxia hirsuta is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
