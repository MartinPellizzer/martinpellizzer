Etlingera pausodipsus

Etlingera pausodipsus, commonly known as the purple torch ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its striking inflorescence, which consists of vibrant red bracts that enclose clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy, dark green surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
