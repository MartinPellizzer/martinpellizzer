Ericameria cooperi var. bajacalifornica

Ericameria cooperi var. bajacalifornica, commonly known as the Baja California greasewood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Baja California.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and compact, branching structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is drought-tolerant.

Its aromatic foliage and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it a hardy plant suited for xeriscaping.
