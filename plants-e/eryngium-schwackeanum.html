Eryngium schwackeanum

Eryngium schwackeanum, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiky, blue-green bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Eryngium schwackeanum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
