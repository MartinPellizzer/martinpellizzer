Echinaria todaroana

Echinaria todaroana, commonly known as the Todaro's echinaria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Echinaria todaroana is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in rocky, well-drained soils.
