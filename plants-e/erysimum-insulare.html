Erysimum insulare

Erysimum insulare, commonly known as the island wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Erysimum insulare thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.
