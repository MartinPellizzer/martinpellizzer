Eugenia baviensis

Eugenia baviensis, commonly known as the Bavi Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its fruits are small, berry-like, and typically turn red when mature.

Eugenia baviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
