Eupatorium dolicholepis

Eupatorium dolicholepis, commonly known as the slender boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks, and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Eupatorium dolicholepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
