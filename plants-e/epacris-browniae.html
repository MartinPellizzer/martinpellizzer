Epacris browniae

Epacris browniae, commonly known as the Brown's epacris, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, evergreen stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

Epacris browniae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
