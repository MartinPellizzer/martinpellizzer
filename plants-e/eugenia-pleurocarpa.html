Eugenia pleurocarpa

Eugenia pleurocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red, fleshy berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance.

The fruit is an important food source for various bird species and small mammals.
