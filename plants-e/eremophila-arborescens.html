Eremophila arborescens

Eremophila arborescens, commonly known as the tree eremophila, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry landscapes.

Eremophila arborescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
