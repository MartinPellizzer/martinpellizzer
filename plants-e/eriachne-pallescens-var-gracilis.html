Eriachne pallescens var. gracilis

Eriachne pallescens var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, preferring muddy or waterlogged soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in coastal regions and can tolerate salt spray.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
