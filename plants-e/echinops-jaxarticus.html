Echinops jaxarticus

Echinops jaxarticus, commonly known as the Jaxartian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a globe, which are a key feature of the genus Echinops. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Echinops jaxarticus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
