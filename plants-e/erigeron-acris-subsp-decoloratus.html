Erigeron acris subsp. decoloratus

Erigeron acris subsp.

decoloratus, commonly known as the pale blueweed, is a flowering plant in the aster family. It is characterized by its slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and are arranged in a composite head. This subspecies is native to North America and thrives in open, disturbed habitats.

It is often found in meadows, prairies, and along roadsides.
