Euonymus assamicus

Euonymus assamicus, commonly known as the Assam euonymus, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas and northeastern India.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, orbicular fruits that are distinctive and ornamental. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Euonymus assamicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
