Erysimum segusianum

Erysimum segusianum, commonly known as the Segusia wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinctive four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bright yellow or orange flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum segusianum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
