Elachyptera parvifolia

Elachyptera parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved ironwood, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Elachyptera parvifolia is known for its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
