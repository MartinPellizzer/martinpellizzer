Erythrina sandwicensis f. luteola

Erythrina sandwicensis f. luteola, commonly known as the yellow Hawaiian coral tree, is a variant of the native Hawaiian coral tree.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which contrast with the typical red or orange blooms of the species. The plant is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets. It is typically found in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii.

This form is notable for its unique coloration and is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic variation.
