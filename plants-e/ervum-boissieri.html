Ervum boissieri

Ervum boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Ervum boissieri plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological settings.
