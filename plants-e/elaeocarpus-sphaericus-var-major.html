Elaeocarpus sphaericus var. major

Elaeocarpus sphaericus var. major, commonly known as the giant globe wood or mountain globe wood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, spherical fruits that are typically green when mature and turn red or purple upon ripening. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruiting structures.
