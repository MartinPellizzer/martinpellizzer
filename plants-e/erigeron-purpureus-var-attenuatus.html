Erigeron purpureus var. attenuatus

Erigeron purpureus var. attenuatus, commonly known as the purple coneflower or swamp coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy purple to pink flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
