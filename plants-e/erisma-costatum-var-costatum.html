Erisma costatum var. costatum

Erisma costatum var. costatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf erisma, is a small perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are pale yellow and occur in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
