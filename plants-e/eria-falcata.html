Eria falcata

Eria falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces striking, pendulous flowers with a distinctive falcate (sickle-shaped) lip. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose potting media. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper light and moisture conditions.

Its unique morphology and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
