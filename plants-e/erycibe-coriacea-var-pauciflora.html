Erycibe coriacea var. pauciflora

Erycibe coriacea var. pauciflora, commonly known as the rough bark erycibe, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
