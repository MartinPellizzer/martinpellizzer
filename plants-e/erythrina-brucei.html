Erythrina brucei

Erythrina brucei, commonly known as the Bruce's flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Erythrina brucei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical areas.
