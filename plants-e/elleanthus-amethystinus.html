Elleanthus amethystinus

Elleanthus amethystinus, commonly known as the amethystine everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces numerous tiny, tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Elleanthus amethystinus is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
