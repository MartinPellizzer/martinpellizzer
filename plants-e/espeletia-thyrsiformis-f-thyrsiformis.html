Espeletia thyrsiformis f. thyrsiformis

Espeletia thyrsiformis f. thyrsiformis, commonly known as the "Páramo Lily," is a striking plant native to the páramo ecosystems of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth of broad, fleshy, silver-green leaves that help it conserve water in high-altitude, cold environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators like bees and moths. It thrives in nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the Andean highlands.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is an important species in the study of alpine plant adaptations.
