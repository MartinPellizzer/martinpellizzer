Epitrachys ciliata

Epitrachys ciliata, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Epitrachys ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
