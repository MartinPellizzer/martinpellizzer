Eritrichium axillare

Eritrichium axillare, commonly known as the rock erythronium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a dense cluster of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is pollinated primarily by bees.

Eritrichium axillare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
