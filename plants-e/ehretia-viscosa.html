Ehretia viscosa

Ehretia viscosa, commonly known as sticky ehretia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas within forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
