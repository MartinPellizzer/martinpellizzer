Eryngium longispinum

Eryngium longispinum, commonly known as the long-spined thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has grayish-green, lance-shaped leaves with coarse teeth and a distinctive, spiky appearance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Eryngium longispinum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
