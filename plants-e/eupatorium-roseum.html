Eupatorium roseum

Eupatorium roseum, commonly known as rose verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Eupatorium roseum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
