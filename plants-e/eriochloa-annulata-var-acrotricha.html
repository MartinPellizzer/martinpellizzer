Eriochloa annulata var. acrotricha

Eriochloa annulata var. acrotricha, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet cluster that matures into a whitish to grayish color.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
