Epilobium chilense var. verticillata

Epilobium chilense var. verticillata, commonly known as the Chilean willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in restoration projects.
