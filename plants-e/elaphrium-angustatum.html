Elaphrium angustatum

Elaphrium angustatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf elaphrium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in late spring.

Elaphrium angustatum has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
