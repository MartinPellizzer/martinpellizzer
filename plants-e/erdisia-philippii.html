Erdisia philippii

Erdisia philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's erdisia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in countries like Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, which is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Erdisia philippii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
