Eriogonum androsaceum var. piperi

Eriogonum androsaceum var. piperi, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
