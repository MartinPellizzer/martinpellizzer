Echeveria rhombifolia

Echeveria rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, silver-green leaves that are shaped like rhombuses, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
