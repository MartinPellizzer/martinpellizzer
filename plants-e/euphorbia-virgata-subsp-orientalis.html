Euphorbia virgata subsp. orientalis

Euphorbia virgata subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It features a branched stem system with fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers clustered in cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and may have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
