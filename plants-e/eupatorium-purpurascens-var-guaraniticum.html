Eupatorium purpurascens var. guaraniticum

Eupatorium purpurascens var. guaraniticum, commonly known as the Guarani feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple to pink flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
