Euodia confusa

Euodia confusa, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, woody bark and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers followed by small, round fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the essential oils derived from its bark.

Euodia confusa is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
