Elaeocarpus glaber var. sphaeroblastus

Elaeocarpus glaber var. sphaeroblastus, commonly known as the globular tallowwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to northern Australia, particularly Queensland and the Northern Territory. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody, spherical fruits that are an important food source for native wildlife.

The tree is often found in rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable timber.
