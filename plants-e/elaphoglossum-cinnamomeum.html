Elaphoglossum cinnamomeum

Elaphoglossum cinnamomeum, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a short, often hidden rhizome with a dense cluster of fertile fronds at the base. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines on the undersides of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum cinnamomeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
