Eria minahassae

Eria minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa orchid, is a species of orchid native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
