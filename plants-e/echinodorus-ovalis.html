Echinodorus ovalis

Echinodorus ovalis, commonly known as the broad-leaf waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Echinodorus ovalis has a robust root system that helps stabilize the substrate and improve water quality.

It is a popular choice for aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
