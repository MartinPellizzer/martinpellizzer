Euphorbia dulcis f. lanuginosa

Euphorbia dulcis f. lanuginosa, commonly known as the fuzzy euphorbia, is a succulent plant characterized by its soft, hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to southern Africa. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and leaves covered in fine, white pubescence, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typical of the genus Euphorbia.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
