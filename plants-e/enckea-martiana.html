Enckea martiana

Enckea martiana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued in the musical instrument industry. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are usually glossy green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Due to overharvesting, it is now classified as an endangered species.
