Emilia chiovendeana

Emilia chiovendeana, commonly known as the Cretan emilia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Emilia chiovendeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
