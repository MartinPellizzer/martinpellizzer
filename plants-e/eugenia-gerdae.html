Eugenia gerdae

Eugenia gerdae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cherry guava, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are white and clustered, and it produces a fragrant aroma, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The fruit's botanical features include a thin, leathery skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp with a slight tang.
