Erica helianthemifolia

Erica helianthemifolia, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is a hardy shrub that produces abundant nectar, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
