Eucalyptus calophylla var. maideniana

Eucalyptus calophylla var. maideniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, pale grey bark. This plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
