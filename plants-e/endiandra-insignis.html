Endiandra insignis

Endiandra insignis, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

Endiandra insignis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
