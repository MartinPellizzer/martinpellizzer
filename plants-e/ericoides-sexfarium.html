Ericoides sexfarium

Ericoides sexfarium, commonly known as the six-lobed heath, is a member of the Ericaceae family, characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers.

It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and mountainous regions. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the heath family.

Ericoides sexfarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
