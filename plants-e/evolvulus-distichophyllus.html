Evolvulus distichophyllus

Evolvulus distichophyllus, commonly known as the two-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.

Evolvulus distichophyllus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
