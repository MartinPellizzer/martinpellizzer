Elaeocarpus subcapitatus

Elaeocarpus subcapitatus, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it often grows in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
