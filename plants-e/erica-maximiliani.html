Erica maximiliani

Erica maximiliani, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to deep purple, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Erica maximiliani is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
