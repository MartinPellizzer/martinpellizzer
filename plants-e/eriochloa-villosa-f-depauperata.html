Eriochloa villosa f. depauperata

Eriochloa villosa f. depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-haired panicgrass, is a variant of the species Eriochloa villosa, characterized by its reduced foliage and sparse leaf hairs.

It is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States, typically found in wetland and swampy habitats. The plant exhibits a low-growing, clump-forming habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
