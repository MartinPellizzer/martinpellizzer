Erigeron uniflorus subsp. daenensis

Erigeron uniflorus subsp.

daenensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually white or pale blue, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.
