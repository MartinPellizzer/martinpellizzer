Echinocereus phoeniceus var. inermis

Echinocereus phoeniceus var. inermis, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with bright green spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in desert gardens.
