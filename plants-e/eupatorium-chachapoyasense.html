Eupatorium chachapoyasense

Eupatorium chachapoyasense, commonly known as the Chachapoyas feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
