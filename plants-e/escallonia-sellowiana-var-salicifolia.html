Escallonia sellowiana var. salicifolia

Escallonia sellowiana var. salicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf escallonia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are a striking silver-green color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries in autumn. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

Escallonia sellowiana var. salicifolia is often used in gardens for its visual interest and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
