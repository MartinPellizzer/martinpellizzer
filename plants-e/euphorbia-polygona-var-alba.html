Euphorbia polygona var. alba

Euphorbia polygona var. alba, commonly known as the white-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
