Eragrostis lobata

Eragrostis lobata, commonly known as silverleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
