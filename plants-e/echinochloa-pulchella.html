Echinochloa pulchella

Echinochloa pulchella, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Echinochloa pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
