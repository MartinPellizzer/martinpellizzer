Echeveria laxa

Echeveria laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Echeveria laxa is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
