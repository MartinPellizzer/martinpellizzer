Erythroxylum pedicellare

Erythroxylum pedicellare, commonly known as the red coca, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that contain the stimulant substance cocaine. It is often found in high-altitude areas with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is subject to strict regulations in many countries.
