Eulalia rufispica

Eulalia rufispica, commonly known as the red-bracted grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive red bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Eulalia rufispica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
