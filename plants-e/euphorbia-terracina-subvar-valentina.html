Euphorbia terracina subvar. valentina

Euphorbia terracina subvar. valentina, commonly known as the pink spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-yellow flowers. The plant has smooth, succulent stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The sap of this plant contains toxic compounds, so care should be taken when handling it.
