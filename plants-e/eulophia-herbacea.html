Eulophia herbacea

Eulophia herbacea, commonly known as the slender eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and pale green or white, arranged in a raceme.

Eulophia herbacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
