Echeveria candida

Echeveria candida, commonly known as the "White Snowball" or "Candida Echeveria," is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically white or pale blue in color, covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Echeveria candida is popular as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
