Elaeocarpus curranii

Elaeocarpus curranii, commonly known as the Kuranda satinash, is a tropical tree native to northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
