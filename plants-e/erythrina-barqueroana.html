Erythrina barqueroana

Erythrina barqueroana, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its compound leaves composed of three to five oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and savanna habitats.

Erythrina barqueroana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
