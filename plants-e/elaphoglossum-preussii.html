Elaphoglossum preussii

Elaphoglossum preussii, commonly known as the Preuss's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum preussii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
