Echinodorus ranunculoides f. graminifolius

Echinodorus ranunculoides f. graminifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a variant of the broad-leaf waterweed species.

It is a aquatic plant native to South America, typically found in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, contributing to its ornamental value in aquariums. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize sediment and improve water clarity.

This variety is prized for its ease of care and adaptability to various aquatic conditions.
