Elleanthus myrosmatis

Elleanthus myrosmatis, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Elleanthus myrosmatis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
