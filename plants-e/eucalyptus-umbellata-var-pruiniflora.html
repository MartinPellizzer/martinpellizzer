Eucalyptus umbellata var. pruiniflora

Eucalyptus umbellata var. pruiniflora, commonly known as the Pruiniflora Honey Myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of grey and white. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a whitish bloom on the underside, giving them a silvery appearance. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are clustered in umbels, which are flat-topped or rounded clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its fragrant foliage and floral display.
