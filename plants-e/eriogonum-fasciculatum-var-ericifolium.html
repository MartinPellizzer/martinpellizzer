Eriogonum fasciculatum var. ericifolium

Eriogonum fasciculatum var. ericifolium, commonly known as the redstem flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a reddish tinge, especially when mature.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is adapted to arid environments.
