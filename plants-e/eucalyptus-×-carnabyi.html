Eucalyptus × carnabyi

Eucalyptus × carnabyi, commonly known as Carnaby's eucalypt, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin sheets. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a grey-green color and a distinct midrib. This species produces small, globular white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
