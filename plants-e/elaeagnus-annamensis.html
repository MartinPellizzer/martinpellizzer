Elaeagnus annamensis

Elaeagnus annamensis, commonly known as the Annam elaeagnus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, edible drupe that is typically consumed by birds and small mammals.

Elaeagnus annamensis is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
