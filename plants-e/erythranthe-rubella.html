Erythranthe rubella

Erythranthe rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered wallflower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Native to the western United States, this plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and coastal areas.

The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
