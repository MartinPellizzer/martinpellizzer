Epimedium truncatum

Epimedium truncatum, commonly known as the barrenwort, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish or bronze in color, especially in the spring. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Epimedium truncatum is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
