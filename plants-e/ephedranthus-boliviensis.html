Ephedranthus boliviensis

Ephedranthus boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian ephedra, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae, which includes species known for their medicinal and historical uses. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It produces small greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female inflorescences.

Ephedranthus boliviensis is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its potential pharmacological properties.
