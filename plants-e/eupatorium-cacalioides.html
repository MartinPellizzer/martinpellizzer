Eupatorium cacalioides

Eupatorium cacalioides, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. It is often found in moist to wet soils and is adapted to humid, subtropical climates.

White boneset is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
