Erica hexensis

Erica hexensis, commonly known as the Hexagonal heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica hexensis typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, with drought-resistant properties that allow it to survive in dry conditions.
