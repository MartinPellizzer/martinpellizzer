Eremaea purpurea

Eremaea purpurea, commonly known as the purple eremaea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and silvery-grey foliage, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, showy purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, providing additional protection against intense sunlight and drought.

Eremaea purpurea is well adapted to low rainfall and nutrient-poor soils, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
