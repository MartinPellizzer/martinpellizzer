Elaeagnus wilsonii

Elaeagnus wilsonii, commonly known as Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and pinnately compound leaves with small, sharp leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries that are slightly sweet and tart. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Elaeagnus wilsonii is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
