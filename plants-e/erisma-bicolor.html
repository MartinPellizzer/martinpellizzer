Erisma bicolor

Erisma bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored erisma, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with distinct sepals and petals that attract pollinators like bees.
