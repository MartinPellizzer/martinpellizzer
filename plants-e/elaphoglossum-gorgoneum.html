Elaphoglossum gorgoneum

Elaphoglossum gorgoneum, commonly known as the Gorgoneum holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a slender and elegant appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in specialized structures called sori.

Elaphoglossum gorgoneum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
