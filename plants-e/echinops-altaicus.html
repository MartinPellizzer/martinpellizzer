Echinops altaicus

Echinops altaicus, commonly known as the spiked centaury, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and blue or purple, appearing in clusters at the top of the plant.

Echinops altaicus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
