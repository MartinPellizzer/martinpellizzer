Erythroxylum costaricense

Erythroxylum costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican erythroxylum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
