Erica gnidiifolia

Erica gnidiifolia, commonly known as the rock heather, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is typically found in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Erica gnidiifolia is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
