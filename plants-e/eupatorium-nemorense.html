Eupatorium nemorense

Eupatorium nemorense, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Boneset is often found in moist habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodlands.
