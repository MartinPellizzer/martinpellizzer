Eria aeridostachya

Eria aeridostachya, commonly known as the lace fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Eria aeridostachya is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and air-purifying qualities.
