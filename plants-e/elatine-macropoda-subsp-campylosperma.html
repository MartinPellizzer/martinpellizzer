Elatine macropoda subsp. campylosperma

Elatine macropoda subsp.

campylosperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterwort, is a small aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and wetlands.

The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are often submerged. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces elongated, spiral-shaped fruits.

It is characterized by its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is often found in nutrient-rich aquatic habitats.
