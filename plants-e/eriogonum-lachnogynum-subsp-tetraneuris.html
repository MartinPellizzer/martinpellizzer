Eriogonum lachnogynum subsp. tetraneuris

Eriogonum lachnogynum subsp.

tetraneuris, commonly known as the four-veined buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. Its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its four-veined leaf structure, which is a key botanical feature.
