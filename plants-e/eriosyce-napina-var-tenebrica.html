Eriosyce napina var. tenebrica

Eriosyce napina var. tenebrica, commonly known as the dark night cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green to nearly black stems, which are covered in dense, short spines. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized cactus species.
