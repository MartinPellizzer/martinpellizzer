Eurya japonica var. normalis

Eurya japonica var. normalis, commonly known as Japanese holly or Japanese waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its year-round visual interest.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
