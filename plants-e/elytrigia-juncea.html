Elytrigia juncea

Elytrigia juncea, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in dense, compact clusters.

Elytrigia juncea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
