Ehrharta villosa var. maxima

Ehrharta villosa var. maxima, commonly known as the giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
