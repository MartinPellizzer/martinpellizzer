Elaphoglossum christii

Elaphoglossum christii, commonly known as the Christ's holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that often exhibit a dark green color with a glossy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in rich, well-drained soil.

Its spore-producing structures, called soruses, are located on the undersides of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.
