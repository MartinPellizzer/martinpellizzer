Eriogonum brevicaule var. brevicaule

Eriogonum brevicaule var. brevicaule, commonly known as the shortleaf globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, yellow-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is well adapted to arid conditions and is considered a characteristic species of its ecological community.
