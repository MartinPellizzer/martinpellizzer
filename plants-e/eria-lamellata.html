Eria lamellata

Eria lamellata, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are often adorned with intricate, fringed petals.

Eria lamellata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
