Eleutherococcus spinifolius

Eleutherococcus spinifolius, commonly known as Siberian ginseng, is a perennial shrub native to the forests of eastern Russia, northern China, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with spiny margins and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Its botanical properties have made it a subject of interest in traditional medicine and modern herbal research.
