Erica agglutinans

Erica agglutinans, commonly known as the sticky heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Erica agglutinans is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Its sticky leaves and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it a notable species in its native habitat.
