Eugenia coffeoides

Eugenia coffeoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or coffee tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its ornamental value comes from its dense foliage and attractive fruiting display.

Eugenia coffeoides is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
