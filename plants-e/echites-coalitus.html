Echites coalitus

Echites coalitus, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is native to regions of South America and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
