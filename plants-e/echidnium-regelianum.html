Echidnium regelianum

Echidnium regelianum, commonly known as the Regelian horned poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery petals that often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant produces a distinctive, horn-like structure at the base of its flower, which is a unique botanical feature. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered an important species for conservation efforts.
