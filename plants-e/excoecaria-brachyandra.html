Excoecaria brachyandra

Excoecaria brachyandra, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features prominently branched stems with thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with reddish or yellowish veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
