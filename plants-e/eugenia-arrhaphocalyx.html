Eugenia arrhaphocalyx

Eugenia arrhaphocalyx, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a thick, segmented rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp that is highly valued in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
