Eugenia bryanii

Eugenia bryanii, commonly known as the Bryan's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color, with a prominent yellow or orange center. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Eugenia bryanii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
