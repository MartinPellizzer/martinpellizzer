Eragrostis sapinii

Eragrostis sapinii, commonly known as the alpine lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in arid and semi-arid regions.
