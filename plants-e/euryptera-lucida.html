Euryptera lucida

Euryptera lucida, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals with prominent green veins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

The botanical structure of Euryptera lucida includes a sympodial growth pattern and a complex root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
