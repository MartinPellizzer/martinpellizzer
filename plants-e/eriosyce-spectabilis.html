Eriosyce spectabilis

Eriosyce spectabilis, commonly known as the showy eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white to pale green hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in late winter or early spring. It typically grows in rocky soils at high elevations, where it forms compact, low-growing clumps.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
