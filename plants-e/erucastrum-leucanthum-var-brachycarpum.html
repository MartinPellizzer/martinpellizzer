Erucastrum leucanthum var. brachycarpum

Erucastrum leucanthum var. brachycarpum, commonly known as the short-carpelled whiteweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open fields or along roadsides. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The variety brachycarpum is distinguished by its shorter, more compact fruits compared to other varieties of the species.
