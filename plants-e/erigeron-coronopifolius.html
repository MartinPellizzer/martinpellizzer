Erigeron coronopifolius

Erigeron coronopifolius, commonly known as the crownleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

Erigeron coronopifolius is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
