Equisetum ramosissimum subsp. moorei

Equisetum ramosissimum subsp.

moorei, commonly known as the Moore's horse tail, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family. It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a central stem that produces spore-bearing cones.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, sandy soils in coastal areas, particularly in regions with a temperate climate. The plant is known for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its habitat.
