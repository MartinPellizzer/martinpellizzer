Eriophorum viridicarinatum var. fellowsii

Eriophorum viridicarinatum var. fellowsii, commonly known as the Fell's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in boreal and subarctic regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its botanical structure is characterized by a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
