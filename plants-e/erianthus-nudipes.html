Erianthus nudipes

Erianthus nudipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Erianthus nudipes is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability.
