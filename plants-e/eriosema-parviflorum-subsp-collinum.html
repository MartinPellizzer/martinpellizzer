Eriosema parviflorum subsp. collinum

Eriosema parviflorum subsp.

collinum, commonly known as the small-flowered eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of hairy, oval leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
