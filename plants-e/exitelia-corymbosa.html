Exitelia corymbosa

Exitelia corymbosa, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 10 meters. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in corymbs, giving it its scientific name. Its fruit is a round, segmented berry with a thick, colorful rind.

The botanical features of Exitelia corymbosa make it a distinctive and valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
