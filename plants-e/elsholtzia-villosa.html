Elsholtzia villosa

Elsholtzia villosa, commonly known as the wooly elsholtzia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Elsholtzia villosa is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.
