Epacris magnifica

Epacris magnifica, commonly known as the showy epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Epacris magnifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
