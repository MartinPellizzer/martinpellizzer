Elymus albowianus

Elymus albowianus, commonly known as the Albow's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple.

Elymus albowianus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
