Eragrostis bahiensis var. contracta

Eragrostis bahiensis var. contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
