Eryngium campestre var. virens

Eryngium campestre var. virens, commonly known as the green field eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiky, blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. Its striking blue-purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
