Elaphoglossum microlepis

Elaphoglossum microlepis, commonly known as the small-leaved holly fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a small, delicate fern characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a distinctive pattern on the undersides of its fronds.

Elaphoglossum microlepis is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.
