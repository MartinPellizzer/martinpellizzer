Eriocaulon intermedium var. glabrum

Eriocaulon intermedium var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth eriocaulon, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces tiny, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain minute flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
