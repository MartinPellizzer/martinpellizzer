Eragrostiella bifaria var. australiana

Eragrostiella bifaria var. australiana, commonly known as the Australian slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is adapted to the dry and warm climate of its native habitat, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
