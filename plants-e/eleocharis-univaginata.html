Eleocharis univaginata

Eleocharis univaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Eleocharis univaginata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to wetland conditions, making it an important component of these ecosystems.
