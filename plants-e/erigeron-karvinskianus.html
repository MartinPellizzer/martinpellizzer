Erigeron karvinskianus

Erigeron karvinskianus, commonly known as the Mexican fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
