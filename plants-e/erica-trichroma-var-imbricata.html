Erica trichroma var. imbricata

Erica trichroma var. imbricata, commonly known as the variable heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
