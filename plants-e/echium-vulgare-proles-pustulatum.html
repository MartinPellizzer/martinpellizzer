Echium vulgare proles pustulatum

Echium vulgare proles pustulatum, commonly known as the common thornweed, is a flowering plant in the borage family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various parts of Europe and Asia.
