Elymus glaucus subsp. jepsonii

Elymus glaucus subsp.

jepsonii, commonly known as Jepson's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence.

The plant produces spikelets that are loosely arranged, with lemmas that are often hairy and glabrous above. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
