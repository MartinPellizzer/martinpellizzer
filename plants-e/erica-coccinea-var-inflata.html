Erica coccinea var. inflata

Erica coccinea var. inflata, commonly known as the inflated heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces showy, red or pink flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and densely packed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
