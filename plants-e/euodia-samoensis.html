Euodia samoensis

Euodia samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan laurier or Samoan cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the islands of Samoa and nearby regions in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
