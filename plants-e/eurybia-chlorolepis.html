Eurybia chlorolepis

Eurybia chlorolepis, commonly known as the greenleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Eurybia chlorolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators in native habitats.
