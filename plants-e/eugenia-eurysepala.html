Eugenia eurysepala

Eugenia eurysepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Eugenia eurysepala is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
