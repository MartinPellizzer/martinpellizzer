Eupatorium cuatrecasasii

Eupatorium cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas' eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is known for its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
