Eragrostis tephrosanthos

Eragrostis tephrosanthos, commonly known as yellow lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish spikelets that are often clustered in dense panicles. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
