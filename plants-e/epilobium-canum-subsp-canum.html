Epilobium canum subsp. canum

Epilobium canum subsp.

canum, commonly known as sticky willow or red willow, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a sticky coating.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Epilobium canum subsp.

canum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in riparian habitats.
