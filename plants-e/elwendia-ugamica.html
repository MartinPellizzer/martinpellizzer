Elwendia ugamica

Elwendia ugamica, commonly known as the golden moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that open at night and emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its blooming period occurs during the warmer months, typically from spring to early autumn.

Elwendia ugamica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
