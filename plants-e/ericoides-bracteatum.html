Ericoides bracteatum

Ericoides bracteatum, commonly known as the bracted ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces bell-shaped, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Ericoides bracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
