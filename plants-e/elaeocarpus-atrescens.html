Elaeocarpus atrescens

Elaeocarpus atrescens, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, red or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody, spherical fruits that contain a single, hard seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
