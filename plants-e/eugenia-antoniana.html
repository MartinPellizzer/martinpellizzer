Eugenia antoniana

Eugenia antoniana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia antoniana is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
