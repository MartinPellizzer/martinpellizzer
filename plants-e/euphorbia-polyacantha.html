Euphorbia polyacantha

Euphorbia polyacantha, commonly known as the many-armed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, branched stem covered in small, spiny tubercles, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a striking addition to desert landscapes.
