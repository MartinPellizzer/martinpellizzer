Echium rosulatum var. davaei

Echium rosulatum var. davaei, commonly known as the Davaei's thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
