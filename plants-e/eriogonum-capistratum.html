Eriogonum capistratum

Eriogonum capistratum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Eriogonum capistratum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
