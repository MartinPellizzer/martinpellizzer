Eritrichium canum var. canum

Eritrichium canum var. canum, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The plant is often found in dry, sandy or rocky habitats and is considered a characteristic species of the tallgrass prairie ecosystem.
