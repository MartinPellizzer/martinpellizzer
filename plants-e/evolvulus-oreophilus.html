Evolvulus oreophilus

Evolvulus oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Evolvulus oreophilus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
