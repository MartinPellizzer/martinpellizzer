Echinopsis formosa

Echinopsis formosa, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stem and clusters of vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and extreme weather conditions. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Echinopsis formosa is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
