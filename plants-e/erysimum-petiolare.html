Erysimum petiolare

Erysimum petiolare, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow, elongated flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnately lobed leaves that are hairy and serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum petiolare is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
