Eugenia rimosa

Eugenia rimosa, commonly known as the Australian lilac, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and animals.

Eugenia rimosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
