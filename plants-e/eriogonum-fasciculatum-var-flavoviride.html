Eriogonum fasciculatum var. flavoviride

Eriogonum fasciculatum var. flavoviride, commonly known as the yellow-green buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
