Epidendrum filamentosum

Epidendrum filamentosum, commonly known as the Filamentous Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, filamentous roots and its ability to grow epiphytically on trees or rocks. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Epidendrum filamentosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
