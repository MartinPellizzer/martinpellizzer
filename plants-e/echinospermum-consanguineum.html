Echinospermum consanguineum

Echinospermum consanguineum, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces achenes, which are dry, one-seeded fruits with a hard outer covering. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in shrublands and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
