Erythroxylum ligustrinum var. grandifolium

Erythroxylum ligustrinum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved copal tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant, yellowish-green hue, while the fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
