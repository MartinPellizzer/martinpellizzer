Echinocactus williamsii var. luteus

Echinocactus williamsii var. luteus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant's most distinctive feature is its yellowish-green coloration, which comes from the presence of chlorophyll and carotenoids in its tissues. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
