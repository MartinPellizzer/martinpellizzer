Erigeron exstipulatus

Erigeron exstipulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
