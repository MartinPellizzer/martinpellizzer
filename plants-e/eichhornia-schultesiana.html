Eichhornia schultesiana

Eichhornia schultesiana, commonly known as the water hyacinth, is a free-floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has vibrant purple flowers and is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that allow it to float on the surface of water. The plant reproduces rapidly through vegetative propagation, leading to dense growth that can clog waterways. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, making it an effective indicator of water quality.

However, its aggressive growth can also lead to ecological imbalances if left unchecked.
