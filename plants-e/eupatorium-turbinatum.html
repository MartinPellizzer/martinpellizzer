Eupatorium turbinatum

Eupatorium turbinatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. Its foliage is usually gray-green and has a distinctive, fuzzy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
