Eugenia lancea

Eugenia lancea, commonly known as the lanceleaf guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested when mature. Eugenia lancea thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
