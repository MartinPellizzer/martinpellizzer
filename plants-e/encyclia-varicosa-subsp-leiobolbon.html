Encyclia varicosa subsp. leiobolbon

Encyclia varicosa subsp.

leiobolbon, commonly known as the yellow-tipped orchid, is a species native to Central and South America. It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it typically blooms during the wet season.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
