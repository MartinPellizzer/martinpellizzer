Eremostachys labiosiformis

Eremostachys labiosiformis, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
