Eupatorium gaudichaudianum f. lobatum

Eupatorium gaudichaudianum f. lobatum, commonly known as the lobed eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant features lobed leaves with a distinctive, often hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is occasionally cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and unique foliage.
