Eugenia chekan

Eugenia chekan, commonly known as the velvetleaf eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, globose berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Eugenia chekan is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
