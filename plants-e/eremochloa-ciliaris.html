Eremochloa ciliaris

Eremochloa ciliaris, commonly known as the silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats. Its adaptability to drought and poor soils makes it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Eremochloa ciliaris is often found in sandy or rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.
