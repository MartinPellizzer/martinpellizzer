Erythroxylum gaudichaudii

Erythroxylum gaudichaudii, commonly known as the red coca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, bright red flowers. Its leaves contain alkaloids, including cocaine, which have been historically used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.

The plant plays a significant role in both traditional practices and modern pharmacological research.
