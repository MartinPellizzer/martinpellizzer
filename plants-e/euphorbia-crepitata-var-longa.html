Euphorbia crepitata var. longa

Euphorbia crepitata var. longa, commonly known as the long-stemmed spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in a waxy, pale green coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive, rough texture.

Euphorbia crepitata var. longa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
