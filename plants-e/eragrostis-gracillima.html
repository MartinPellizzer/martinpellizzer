Eragrostis gracillima

Eragrostis gracillima, commonly known as slender false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
