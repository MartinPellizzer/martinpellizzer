Ericameria nauseosa var. bigelovii

Ericameria nauseosa var. bigelovii, commonly known as Bigelow's ericameria, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
