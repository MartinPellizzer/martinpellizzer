Epidendrum cuicochaense

Epidendrum cuicochaense, commonly known as the Cuicocha orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, colorful flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species.

Epidendrum cuicochaense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
