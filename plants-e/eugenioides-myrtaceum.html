Eugenioides myrtaceum

Eugenioides myrtaceum, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, warm climate to flourish.

The fruit, which is edible, is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
