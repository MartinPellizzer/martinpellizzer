Eugenia gonavensis

Eugenia gonavensis, commonly known as the Gonave hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests or disturbed habitats. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth surface and emit a mild aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a red color and is sometimes used in local traditional medicine.
