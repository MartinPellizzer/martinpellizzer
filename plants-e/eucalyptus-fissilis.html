Eucalyptus fissilis

Eucalyptus fissilis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is typically found in sandy soils in arid and semi-arid regions, often forming dense shrubs or low trees.

Eucalyptus fissilis is well adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of the local flora in its habitat.
