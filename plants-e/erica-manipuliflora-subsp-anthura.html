Erica manipuliflora subsp. anthura

Erica manipuliflora subsp.

anthura, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to deep purple, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Erica manipuliflora subsp.
