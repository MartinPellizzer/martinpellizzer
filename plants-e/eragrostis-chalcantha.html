Eragrostis chalcantha

Eragrostis chalcantha, commonly known as bluegrass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences are typical of the Poaceae family.

Eragrostis chalcantha is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
