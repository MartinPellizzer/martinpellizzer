Esenbeckia velutina

Esenbeckia velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf esenbeckia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded undergrowth. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Esenbeckia velutina is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
