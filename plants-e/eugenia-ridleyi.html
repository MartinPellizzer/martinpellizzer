Eugenia ridleyi

Eugenia ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
