Echinocactus polycephalus var. xeranthemoides

Echinocactus polycephalus var. xeranthemoides, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in dense, spiny tubercles that provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
