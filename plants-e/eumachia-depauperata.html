Eumachia depauperata

Eumachia depauperata, commonly known as the poorleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its botanical characteristics include a weak, branching stem and capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Eumachia depauperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
