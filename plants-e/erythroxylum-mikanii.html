Erythroxylum mikanii

Erythroxylum mikanii, commonly known as the Japanese belladonna tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
