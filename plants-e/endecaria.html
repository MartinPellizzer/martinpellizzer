Endecaria

Endecaria, commonly known as the wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves and produces pods that contain edible seeds. Endecaria is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
