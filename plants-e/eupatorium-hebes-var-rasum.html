Eupatorium hebes var. rasum

Eupatorium hebes var. rasum, commonly known as the New Zealand ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a creeping habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
