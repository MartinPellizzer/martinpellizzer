Euphorbia analamerae

Euphorbia analamerae, commonly known as the Analamera euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia.

Euphorbia analamerae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
