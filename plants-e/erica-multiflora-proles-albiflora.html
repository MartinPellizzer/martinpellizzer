Erica multiflora proles albiflora

Erica multiflora proles albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and is relatively easy to grow with proper care.
