Erigeron kuschei

Erigeron kuschei, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features wiry, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a silvery down. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Erigeron kuschei is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
