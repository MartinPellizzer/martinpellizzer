Eucalyptus myrtiformis

Eucalyptus myrtiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. Its bark is typically smooth and peels in thin, papery layers, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus myrtiformis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
