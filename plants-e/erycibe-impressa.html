Erycibe impressa

Erycibe impressa, commonly known as the Caribbean apple or West Indian apple, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are often oval in shape with a waxy texture. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apples.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in the regions where it is found.
