Eupatorium vagum

Eupatorium vagum, commonly known as slender eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as swamps and wetlands, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its showy white to pinkish flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
