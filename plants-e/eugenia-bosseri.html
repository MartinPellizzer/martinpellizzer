Eugenia bosseri

Eugenia bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and smooth, dark red or black berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent.

Eugenia bosseri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
