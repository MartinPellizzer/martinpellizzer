Eugenia pseudomabaeoides

Eugenia pseudomabaeoides, commonly known as the golden glory tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, orange-red berries.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
