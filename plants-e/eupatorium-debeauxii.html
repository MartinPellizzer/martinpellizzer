Eupatorium debeauxii

Eupatorium debeauxii, commonly known as Beaux's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Beaux's boneset is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
