Erica bojeri

Erica bojeri, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and summer. Erica bojeri thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
