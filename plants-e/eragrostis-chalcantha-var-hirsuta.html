Eragrostis chalcantha var. hirsuta

Eragrostis chalcantha var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and slender, tufted inflorescences that produce small, blue-green spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small wildlife species.
