Eragrostis fimbrillata

Eragrostis fimbrillata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and savannas. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
