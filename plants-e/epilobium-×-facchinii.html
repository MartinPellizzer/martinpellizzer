Epilobium × facchinii

Epilobium × facchinii, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are elongated and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Epilobium × facchinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
