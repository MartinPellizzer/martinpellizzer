Eugenia dipoda

Eugenia dipoda, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically bright red or purple when ripe. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique, segmented fruit structure.

Eugenia dipoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible, aromatic fruits it yields.
