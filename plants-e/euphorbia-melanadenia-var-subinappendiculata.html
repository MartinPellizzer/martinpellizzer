Euphorbia melanadenia var. subinappendiculata

Euphorbia melanadenia var. subinappendiculata, commonly known as the black-tipped spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often tinged with a dark coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
