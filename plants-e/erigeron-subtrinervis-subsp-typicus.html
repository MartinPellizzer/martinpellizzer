Erigeron subtrinervis subsp. typicus

Erigeron subtrinervis subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the narrow-veined blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, often in disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive three-veined structure, which is a key identifying feature.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
