Equisetum variegatum f. multirameum

Equisetum variegatum f. multirameum, commonly known as the variegated horsetail, is a variant of the common horsetail that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its slender, segmented stems covered in small, scale-like structures, which are typical of the Equisetum genus. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, a key feature of its reproductive biology. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This fern-like plant is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
