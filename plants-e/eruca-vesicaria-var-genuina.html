Eruca vesicaria var. genuina

Eruca vesicaria var. genuina, commonly known as wild mustard, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lobed, pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.

Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its persistence in various habitats.
