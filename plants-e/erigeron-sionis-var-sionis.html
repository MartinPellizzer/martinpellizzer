Erigeron sionis var. sionis

Erigeron sionis var. sionis, commonly known as the Sierra daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in summer.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
