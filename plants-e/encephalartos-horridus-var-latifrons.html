Encephalartos horridus var. latifrons

Encephalartos horridus var. latifrons, commonly known as the Broad-fronded African cycad, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky foliage and stout, unbranched trunk. The leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture and a distinct midrib. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically borne in compact clusters.

This plant is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun with minimal water requirements.
