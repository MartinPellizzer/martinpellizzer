Erysimum leptocarpum

Erysimum leptocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants.

Erysimum leptocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
