Eucalyptus uncinata subsp. crassifolia

Eucalyptus uncinata subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved matted gum, is a species native to Australia. It is characterized by its rough, fibrous bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are usually pale green or yellowish in color. This subspecies is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
