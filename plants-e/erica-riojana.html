Erica riojana

Erica riojana, commonly known as the Rioja heather, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Erica riojana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
