Eugenia fernandeziana

Eugenia fernandeziana, commonly known as the Fernandez's Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America, particularly in regions of Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
