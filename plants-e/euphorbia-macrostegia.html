Euphorbia macrostegia

Euphorbia macrostegia, commonly known as the giant spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

Euphorbia macrostegia is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
