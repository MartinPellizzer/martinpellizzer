Euonymus mammillaris

Euonymus mammillaris, commonly known as the glossy abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its attractive, waxy, and often glossy green leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, inflated seed capsules that persist into the fall. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its foliage and seasonal color changes.

Euonymus mammillaris prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
