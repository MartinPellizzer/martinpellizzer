Erica undulata

Erica undulata, commonly known as the undulating heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
