Erigeron kachinensis

Erigeron kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, along with ray flowers that are usually white or pale blue.

Erigeron kachinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
