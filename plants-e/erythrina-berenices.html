Erythrina berenices

Erythrina berenices, commonly known as the flame tree or the pink flame tree, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its pods are long, slender, and contain several seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
