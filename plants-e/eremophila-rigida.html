Eremophila rigida

Eremophila rigida, commonly known as the rigid emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance and heat tolerance. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila rigida is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
