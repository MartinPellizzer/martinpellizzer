Elodes sumatrana

Elodes sumatrana, commonly known as Sumatran waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, branching stems that are often submerged in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Elodes sumatrana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic organisms.
