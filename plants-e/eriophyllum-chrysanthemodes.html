Eriophyllum chrysanthemodes

Eriophyllum chrysanthemodes, commonly known as the golden bush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow to golden flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Eriophyllum chrysanthemodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
