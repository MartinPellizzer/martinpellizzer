Epilobium lineare f. umbrosum

Epilobium lineare f. umbrosum, commonly known as the shade-loving willow herb, is a variant of the common willow herb.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
