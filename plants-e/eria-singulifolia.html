Eria singulifolia

Eria singulifolia, commonly known as the single-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique single leaf and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Eria singulifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
