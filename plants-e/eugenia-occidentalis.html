Eugenia occidentalis

Eugenia occidentalis, commonly known as the West Indian cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Eugenia occidentalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruits it produces.
