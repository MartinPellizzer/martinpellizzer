Erica absinthoides

Erica absinthoides, commonly known as the silver heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

Erica absinthoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
