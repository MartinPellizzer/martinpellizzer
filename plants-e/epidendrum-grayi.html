Epidendrum grayi

Epidendrum grayi, commonly known as the Gray's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually dark green and somewhat fleshy, adapted to store moisture.

Epidendrum grayi prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
