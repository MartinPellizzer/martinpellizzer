Echinochloa composita

Echinochloa composita, commonly known as flatpointergrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, blade-like leaves and a dense, fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Echinochloa composita is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
