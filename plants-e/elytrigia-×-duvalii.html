Elytrigia × duvalii

Elytrigia × duvalii, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Elytrigia repens and Elytrigia bulbosa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like blades that often have a bluish-green hue. This plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.

Due to its sterility, it does not produce seeds and relies on vegetative propagation for spread.
