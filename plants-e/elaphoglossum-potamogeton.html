Elaphoglossum potamogeton

Elaphoglossum potamogeton, commonly known as the water holly fern, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic fern species native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, with its rhizomes spreading along the substrate. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a feathery appearance due to its finely divided fronds. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of many ferns.

Elaphoglossum potamogeton plays a role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
