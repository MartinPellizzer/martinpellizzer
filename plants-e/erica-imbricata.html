Erica imbricata

Erica imbricata, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica imbricata is a low-growing shrub, often forming dense mats, and is well adapted to harsh, nutrient-poor environments.

Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.
