Eriogonum verticillatum

Eriogonum verticillatum, commonly known as the California buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pale yellow to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are often arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eriogonum verticillatum is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
