Euphorbia serpillifolia subsp. hirtula

Euphorbia serpillifolia subsp.

hirtula, commonly known as the hairy leaf spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flower heads, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
