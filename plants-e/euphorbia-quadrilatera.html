Euphorbia quadrilatera

Euphorbia quadrilatera, commonly known as the four-sided spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrilateral stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and may be shed during periods of drought.

Euphorbia quadrilatera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
