Evolvulus villosissimus

Evolvulus villosissimus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can be invasive in some regions.
