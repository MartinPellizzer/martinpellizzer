Ephemerantha trilamellata

Ephemerantha trilamellata, commonly known as the three-leaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, intricately shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom briefly during the warmer months. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests or alpine meadows. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological features and its role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
