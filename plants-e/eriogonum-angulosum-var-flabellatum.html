Eriogonum angulosum var. flabellatum

Eriogonum angulosum var. flabellatum, commonly known as the flabellate buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its branched, often sprawling growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. This species thrives in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
