Euphorbia subcaerulea

Euphorbia subcaerulea, commonly known as the sky blue euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with pale blue-green foliage that resembles a rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in a unique cyathium structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
