Elaeocarpus lancipetalus subsp. lancipetalus

Elaeocarpus lancipetalus subsp.

lancipetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, typical of the genus Elaeocarpus. This subspecies is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
