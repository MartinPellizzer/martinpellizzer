Eria laxiflora

Eria laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered eria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white or pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Eria laxiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
