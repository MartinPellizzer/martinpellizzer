Eugenia surinamensis var. impunctata

Eugenia surinamensis var. impunctata, commonly known as the Surinam eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Suriname and parts of Brazil. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, dark purple berries.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens.
