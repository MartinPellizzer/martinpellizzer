Euonymus lawsonii var. salicifolius

Euonymus lawsonii var. salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the fall, adding ornamental value. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
