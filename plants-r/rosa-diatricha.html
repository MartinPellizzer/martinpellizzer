Rosa diatricha

Rosa diatricha, commonly known as the rough rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine, stiff hairs.

Rosa diatricha is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
