Rubus callosus var. cinerescens

Rubus callosus var. cinerescens, commonly known as the gray-haired raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive grayish-white pubescence on the stems and leaves.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the environment. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are pink to white in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
