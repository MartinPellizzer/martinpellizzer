Rhopalopilia madagascariensis

Rhopalopilia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Due to its restricted habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
