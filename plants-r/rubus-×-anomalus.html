Rubus × anomalus

Rubus × anomalus, commonly known as the blackberry dewberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, thorny stems that often root at the nodes. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters. The fruit, a small aggregate berry, is usually dark red to black when mature and has a tart, acidic flavor.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland and disturbed habitats.
