Ranunculus hederaceus var. coenosus

Ranunculus hederaceus var. coenosus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a creeping growth habit, with stolons that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a rough texture, and it prefers moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
