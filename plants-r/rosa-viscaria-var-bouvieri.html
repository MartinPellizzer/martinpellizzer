Rosa viscaria var. bouvieri

Rosa viscaria var. bouvieri, commonly known as the Bouvier's prickly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces yellow, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy species within the Rosa genus.
