Rosa × virgata

Rosa × virgata, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its ability to bloom continuously throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. The plant typically features fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, exhibiting moderate drought tolerance.

Rosa × virgata is also valued for its hardy nature and resistance to many common rose diseases.
