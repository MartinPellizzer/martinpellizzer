Rhodiola subopposita

Rhodiola subopposita, commonly known as the Tibetan rhodiola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with its extensive root system. It is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows, where it can withstand extreme temperatures and low oxygen levels.

Rhodiola subopposita is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for its adaptogenic and stimulant effects.
