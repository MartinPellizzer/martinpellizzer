Rubus lacertosus var. inegribasis

Rubus lacertosus var. inegribasis, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, glossy leaves that have a rough texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex inflorescence and the presence of stinging hairs on its leaves and stems.
