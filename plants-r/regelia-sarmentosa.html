Regelia sarmentosa

Regelia sarmentosa, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Regelia sarmentosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its ornamental foliage and unique appearance.
