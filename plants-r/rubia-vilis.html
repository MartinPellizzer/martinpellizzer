Rubia vilis

Rubia vilis, commonly known as lesser woodruff, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia vilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
