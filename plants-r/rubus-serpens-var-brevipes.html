Rubus serpens var. brevipes

Rubus serpens var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The species is adapted to moist, sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed habitats.
