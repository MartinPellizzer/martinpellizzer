Rosa barbulata

Rosa barbulata, commonly known as the bristly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Russia, and Kazakhstan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa barbulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
