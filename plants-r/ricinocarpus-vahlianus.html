Ricinocarpus vahlianus

Ricinocarpus vahlianus, commonly known as the Vahli's mangrove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Indian Ocean region.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing a single, hard seed. It thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it an important species in coastal and mangrove ecosystems.

Ricinocarpus vahlianus plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
