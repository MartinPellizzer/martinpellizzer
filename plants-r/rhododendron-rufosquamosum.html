Rhododendron rufosquamosum

Rhododendron rufosquamosum, commonly known as the red-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to forested landscapes.

Rhododendron rufosquamosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
