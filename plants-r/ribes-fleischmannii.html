Ribes fleischmannii

Ribes fleischmannii, commonly known as the Fleischmann's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

Ribes fleischmannii plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
