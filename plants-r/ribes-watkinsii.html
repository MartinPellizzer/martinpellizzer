Ribes watkinsii

Ribes watkinsii, commonly known as the Watkins' gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly found in North America. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ribes watkinsii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
