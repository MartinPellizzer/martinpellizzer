Rubus × subreticulatus

Rubus × subreticulatus, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color. Its stems are thorny, and it has a sprawling growth habit, making it both an ornamental and potentially invasive species.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex hybrid origin and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
