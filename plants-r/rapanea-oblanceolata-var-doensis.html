Rapanea oblanceolata var. doensis

Rapanea oblanceolata var. doensis, commonly known as the Doen's holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded gardens.
