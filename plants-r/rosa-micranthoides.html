Rosa micranthoides

Rosa micranthoides, commonly known as the dwarf evergreen rose, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, featuring dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its compact size and evergreen nature make it suitable for use in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Rosa micranthoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
