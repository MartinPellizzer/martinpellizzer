Rungia polyneura

Rungia polyneura, commonly known as the white-flowered piper, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, three-lobed berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
