Rubus rhamnifolius var. maassii

Rubus rhamnifolius var. maassii, commonly known as the Maass's blackberry, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its berries are edible, though some varieties may have a tart or astringent flavor.
