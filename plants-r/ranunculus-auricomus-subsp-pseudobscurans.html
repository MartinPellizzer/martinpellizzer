Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pseudobscurans

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pseudobscurans, commonly known as the pale buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a distinctive veined structure.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a short, stout rhizome and produces small, capsule-like fruits that disperse seeds by wind.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
