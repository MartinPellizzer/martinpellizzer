Riedlea hirsuta

Riedlea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy riedlea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Riedlea hirsuta is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
