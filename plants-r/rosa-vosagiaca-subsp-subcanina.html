Rosa vosagiaca subsp. subcanina

Rosa vosagiaca subsp.

subcanina, commonly known as the Vosagian rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers.

The leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a slightly serrated margin. This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
