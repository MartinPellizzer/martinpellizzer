Rubus × semivestitus

Rubus × semivestitus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to parts of North America and is often found in disturbed soils and along woodland edges. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and flowers with five pink to white petals.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
