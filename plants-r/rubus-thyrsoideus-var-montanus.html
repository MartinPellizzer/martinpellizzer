Rubus thyrsoideus var. montanus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
