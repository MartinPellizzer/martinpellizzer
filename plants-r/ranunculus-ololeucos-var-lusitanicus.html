Ranunculus ololeucos var. lusitanicus

Ranunculus ololeucos var. lusitanicus, commonly known as the Lusitanian crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, and its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes. It is characterized by its delicate, herbaceous stems and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family, sharing similar botanical traits.
