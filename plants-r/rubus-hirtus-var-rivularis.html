Rubus hirtus var. rivularis

Rubus hirtus var. rivularis, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a shrub native to moist, temperate regions.

It features prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other water-rich environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
