Rivina laevis f. viridiflora

Rivina laevis f. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered rivina, is a variant of the native North American plant Rivina laevis.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves and its distinctive greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage remains lush throughout the growing season, contributing to its ornamental value.

The green-flowered form is prized for its unique coloration and adaptability to various garden conditions.
