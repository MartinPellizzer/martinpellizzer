Rosa glauca var. stephanocarpa

Rosa glauca var. stephanocarpa, commonly known as the stephanocarpa rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
