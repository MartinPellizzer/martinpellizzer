Ranunculus lacustris var. terrestris

Ranunculus lacustris var. terrestris, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to wetland habitats.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are usually submerged or floating on the water surface. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy aquatic ecosystems.
