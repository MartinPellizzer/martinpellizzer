Rubus reconditus

Rubus reconditus, commonly known as the hidden raspberry, is a rare and elusive species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, dark red berries that mature in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Rubus reconditus is considered a botanically significant species with limited distribution.
