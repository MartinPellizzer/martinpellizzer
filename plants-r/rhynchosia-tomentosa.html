Rhynchosia tomentosa

Rhynchosia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces leguminous pods that contain several edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Rhynchosia tomentosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
