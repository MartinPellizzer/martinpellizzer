Rubus subaculeatus

Rubus subaculeatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets, each with a single seed. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
