Rosa sancta

Rosa sancta, commonly known as the holy rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical name reflects its historical association with religious symbolism and sacred spaces.

Rosa sancta is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and religious sites.
