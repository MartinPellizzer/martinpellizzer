Rubus × semierraticus

Rubus × semierraticus, commonly known as the wild blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in partial shade and well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, a cluster of small drupelets, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
