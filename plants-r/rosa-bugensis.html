Rosa bugensis

Rosa bugensis, commonly known as the Bunge rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in thorns.

Rosa bugensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its beauty and its use in perfumery.
