Rubus thyrsoideus var. muellerianus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. muellerianus, commonly known as the Mueller's blackberry, is a variant of the thyrsoideus species within the Rubus genus.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many brambles. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
