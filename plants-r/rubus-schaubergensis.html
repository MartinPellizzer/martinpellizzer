Rubus schaubergensis

Rubus schaubergensis, commonly known as Schauberg's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, specifically in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
