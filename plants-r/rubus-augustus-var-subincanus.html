Rubus augustus var. subincanus

Rubus augustus var. subincanus, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
