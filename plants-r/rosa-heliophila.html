Rosa heliophila

Rosa heliophila, commonly known as the sun rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun conditions. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, while its stems are covered in fine, sticky hairs.

Rosa heliophila is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
