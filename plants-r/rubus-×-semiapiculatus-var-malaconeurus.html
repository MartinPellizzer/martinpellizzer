Rubus × semiapiculatus var. malaconeurus

Rubus × semiapiculatus var. malaconeurus, commonly known as the purple-leafed blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dark purple foliage and thorny stems, which are typical features of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
