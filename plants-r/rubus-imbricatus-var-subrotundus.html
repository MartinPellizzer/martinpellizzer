Rubus imbricatus var. subrotundus

Rubus imbricatus var. subrotundus, commonly known as the rounded blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and its role in local ecosystems.
