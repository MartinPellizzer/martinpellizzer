Ranunculus pulchellus var. potaninii

Ranunculus pulchellus var. potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as meadows and forest edges. It has a short stem and produces small, greenish-white sepals beneath the showy flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
