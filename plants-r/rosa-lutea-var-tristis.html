Rosa lutea var. tristis

Rosa lutea var. tristis, commonly known as the weeping yellow rose, is a variant of the yellow rose species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange-red. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown in botanical gardens and private collections.
