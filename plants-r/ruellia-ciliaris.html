Ruellia ciliaris

Ruellia ciliaris, commonly known as the velvetleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it often grows in moist, well-drained soils. Ruellia ciliaris is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It can be propagated through seeds or stem cuttings, making it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
