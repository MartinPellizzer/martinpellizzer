Ranunculus waziristanicus

Ranunculus waziristanicus, commonly known as the Waziristan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes various species of buttercups and crowfoots. The plant is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellowish or white flowers. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
