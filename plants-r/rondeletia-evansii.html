Rondeletia evansii

Rondeletia evansii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small, white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Rondeletia evansii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
