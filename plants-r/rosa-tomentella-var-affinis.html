Rosa tomentella var. affinis

Rosa tomentella var. affinis, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence covering its stems and leaves, which provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a serrated margin.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in cooler climates.
