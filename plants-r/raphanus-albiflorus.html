Raphanus albiflorus

Raphanus albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered radish, is a biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is typically small and not as economically significant as other radish varieties.

Raphanus albiflorus is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
