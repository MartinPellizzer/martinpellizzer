Rubus scaberrimus var. fallacinus

Rubus scaberrimus var. fallacinus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark, juicy berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in local ecosystems and for wild harvesting.
