Rhododendron × ashitakayamense

Rhododendron × ashitakayamense, commonly known as the Ashitaka Yamame rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron kaempferi and Rhododendron decorum.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
