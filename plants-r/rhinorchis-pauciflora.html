Rhinorchis pauciflora

Rhinorchis pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered rhinorchis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Rhinorchis pauciflora is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its habitat.
