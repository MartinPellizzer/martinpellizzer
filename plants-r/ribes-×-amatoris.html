Ribes × amatoris

Ribes × amatoris, commonly known as the hybrid gooseberry, is a cross between Ribes nigrum (black currant) and Ribes rubrum (red currant).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, tart berries. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are white and borne in cymes.

The berries are used in culinary applications and can also be propagated through cuttings or seeds.
