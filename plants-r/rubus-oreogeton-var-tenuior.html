Rubus oreogeton var. tenuior

Rubus oreogeton var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
