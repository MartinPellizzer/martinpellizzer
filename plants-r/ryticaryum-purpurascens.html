Ryticaryum purpurascens

Ryticaryum purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-tinged mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that often display a distinctive purplish hue, especially under certain light conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by berries that are typically red or orange in color. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
