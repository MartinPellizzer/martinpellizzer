Rhynchosia cyanosperma

Rhynchosia cyanosperma, commonly known as the blue-seed cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive blue-gray pod, which is a notable feature in its botanical description.

Rhynchosia cyanosperma is often grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various culinary applications.
