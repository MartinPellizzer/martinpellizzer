Ribes sanguineum var. variegatum

Ribes sanguineum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that range in color from red to black, typically ripening in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive spring blossoms and autumn foliage coloration.
