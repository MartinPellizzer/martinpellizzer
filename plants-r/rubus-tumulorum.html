Rubus tumulorum

Rubus tumulorum, commonly known as the European bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Rubus tumulorum plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
