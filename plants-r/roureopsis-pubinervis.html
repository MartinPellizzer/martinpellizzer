Roureopsis pubinervis

Roureopsis pubinervis, commonly known as the hairy-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, with intricate veining patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
