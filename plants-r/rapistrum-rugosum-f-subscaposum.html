Rapistrum rugosum f. subscaposum

Rapistrum rugosum f. subscaposum, commonly known as the hairy dock, is a variant of the common dock species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rough, hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. It produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by kidney-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
