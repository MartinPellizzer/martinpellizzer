Rubus cinerosus

Rubus cinerosus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature into dark purple to black berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus cinerosus is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
