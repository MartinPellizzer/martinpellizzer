Ranunculus hibamontanus

Ranunculus hibamontanus, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a soft, woolly coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
