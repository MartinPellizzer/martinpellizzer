Rosa × waitziana

Rosa × waitziana, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to China. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often covered in thorns.

Rosa × waitziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
