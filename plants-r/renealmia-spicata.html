Renealmia spicata

Renealmia spicata, commonly known as the spiky reed or spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Renealmia spicata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
