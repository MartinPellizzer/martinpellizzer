Rubus styriacus var. perarmatus

Rubus styriacus var. perarmatus, commonly known as the Styrian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique morphological traits that set it apart from other blackberry varieties.
