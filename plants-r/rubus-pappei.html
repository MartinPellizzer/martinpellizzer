Rubus pappei

Rubus pappei, commonly known as the Pappe's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus pappei is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
