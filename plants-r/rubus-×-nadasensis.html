Rubus × nadasensis

Rubus × nadasensis, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

The fruit is valued for its flavor and is commonly used in culinary applications.
