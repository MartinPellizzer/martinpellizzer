Rubus triphyllus var. taquetii

Rubus triphyllus var. taquetii, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variant is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though some cultivars may have yellow or orange berries. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features include hairy foliage and a distinctive floral structure that supports pollination by insects.
