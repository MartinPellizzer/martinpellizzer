Rhododendron obtusum var. macrogemma

Rhododendron obtusum var. macrogemma, commonly known as the bigleaf rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, clustered flowers.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of China. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to specific horticultural conditions.
