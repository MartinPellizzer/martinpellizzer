Rhododendron toverenae

Rhododendron toverenae, commonly known as the Toveren's rhododendron, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, favoring acidic soils rich in organic matter. Its floral structure includes five petals and stamens, typical of the Ericaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
