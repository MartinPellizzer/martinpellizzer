Rosa coriifolia subvar. schemnitzensis

Rosa coriifolia subvar. schemnitzensis, commonly known as the Schemnitzensis shrub rose, is a botanical variant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the fruits are red, hairy rosehips.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
