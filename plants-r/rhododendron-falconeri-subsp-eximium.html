Rhododendron falconeri subsp. eximium

Rhododendron falconeri subsp.

eximium, commonly known as the Falconeri rhododendron, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep pink to purple, often with a distinctive white throat.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its foliage consists of thick, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
