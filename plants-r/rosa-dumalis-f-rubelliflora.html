Rosa dumalis f. rubelliflora

Rosa dumalis f. rubelliflora, commonly known as the red-flowered wild rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson, with a distinctive red hue that sets it apart from other rose species. Its foliage consists of serrated, dark green leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
