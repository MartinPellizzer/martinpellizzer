Rosa indica variegata

Rosa indica variegata, commonly known as the variegated tea rose, is a hybrid shrub that originates from India.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a striking mix of green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming throughout the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa indica variegata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
