Ranunculus pratensis var. adpressepilosus

Ranunculus pratensis var. adpressepilosus, commonly known as the woolly meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, greenish-brown fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, woolly hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a unique appearance.
