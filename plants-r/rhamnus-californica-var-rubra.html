Rhamnus californica var. rubra

Rhamnus californica var. rubra, commonly known as the redleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red leaves, which contrast with its dark green stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are a food source for various birds and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem.
