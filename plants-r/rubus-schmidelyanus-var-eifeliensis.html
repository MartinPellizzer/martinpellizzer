Rubus schmidelyanus var. eifeliensis

Rubus schmidelyanus var. eifeliensis, commonly known as the Eifel raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the Eifel region of Germany.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
