Renealmia occidentalis var. cylindrica

Renealmia occidentalis var. cylindrica, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often smooth and slightly compressed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
