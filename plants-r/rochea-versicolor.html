Rochea versicolor

Rochea versicolor, commonly known as the variable rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety.
