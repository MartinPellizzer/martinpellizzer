Rubus porphyrantherus

Rubus porphyrantherus, commonly known as the purple-flowered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, though they are often overshadowed by the plant's vigorous growth habit. Native to parts of East Asia, it thrives in woodland and shrubland environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
