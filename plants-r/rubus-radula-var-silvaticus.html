Rubus radula var. silvaticus

Rubus radula var. silvaticus, commonly known as the silvatic raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry species native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
