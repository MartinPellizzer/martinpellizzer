Rosa pomifera subsp. eupomifera

Rosa pomifera subsp.

eupomifera, commonly known as the giant rose or rose apple, is a species of shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, pome-like fruits that resemble apples, hence its common name.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple ovate to lanceolate leaflets.

The fruit, which is edible when ripe, contains a single large seed and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
