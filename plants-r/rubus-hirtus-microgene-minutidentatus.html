Rubus hirtus microgene minutidentatus

Rubus hirtus microgene minutidentatus, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a small shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
