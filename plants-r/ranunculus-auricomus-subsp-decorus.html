Ranunculus auricomus subsp. decorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

decorus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking yellow, saucer-shaped flowers with a distinctive golden center and deeply lobed petals.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are trifoliate, with palmate divisions, and it is often found in regions with cool, moist climates.

The subspecies decorus is distinguished by its more refined and showy flowers compared to other members of the species.
