Rafnia capensis subsp. calycina

Rafnia capensis subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the Cape raffia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by triangular, fibrous fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is valued in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
