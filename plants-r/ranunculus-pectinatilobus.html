Ranunculus pectinatilobus

Ranunculus pectinatilobus, commonly known as the tufted buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Ranunculus pectinatilobus is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
