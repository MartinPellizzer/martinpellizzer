Rhaponticum scariosum subsp. rhaponticum

Rhaponticum scariosum subsp.

rhaponticum, commonly known as the common rhapontic lettuce, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It features a rosette of broad, wavy, and often hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in gardens and as a forage crop.

This species is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly bitter flavor.
