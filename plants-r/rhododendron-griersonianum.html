Rhododendron griersonianum

Rhododendron griersonianum, commonly known as the Grierson's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
