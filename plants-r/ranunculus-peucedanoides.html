Ranunculus peucedanoides

Ranunculus peucedanoides, commonly known as the peucedanoid buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
