Ranunculus acris proles vulgatus

Ranunculus acris proles vulgatus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy, often with a reddish tinge.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape before the leaves fully develop.
