Rubus guyanensis

Rubus guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana raspberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
