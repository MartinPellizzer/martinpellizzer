Rosa congesta

Rosa congesta, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa congesta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
