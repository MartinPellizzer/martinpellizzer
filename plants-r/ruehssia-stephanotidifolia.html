Ruehssia stephanotidifolia

Ruehssia stephanotidifolia, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
