Rubus × pyramidaliformis

Rubus × pyramidaliformis, commonly known as the pyramidal blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is cultivated in gardens and for fruit production.
