Raphanus sativus var. syrengeus

Raphanus sativus var. syrengeus, commonly known as the Chinese watercress, is a leafy green vegetable native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, peppery stems and small, edible leaves that are rich in nutrients such as vitamin C, vitamin K, and antioxidants. The plant thrives in cool climates and moist soil conditions, often growing in streamside habitats. Its rapid growth cycle allows for multiple harvests throughout the growing season.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower.
