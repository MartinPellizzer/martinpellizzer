Rhodia officinarum

Rhodia officinarum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its bright yellow to orange flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhodia officinarum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
