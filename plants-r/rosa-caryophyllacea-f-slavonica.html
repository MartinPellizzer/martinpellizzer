Rosa caryophyllacea f. slavonica

Rosa caryophyllacea f. slavonica, commonly known as the Slavonic rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa caryophyllacea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and the flowers are typically pink to red, with a fragrant aroma. This form is native to the Balkan region and is adapted to temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
