Ruellia potamophila

Ruellia potamophila, commonly known as the river ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments near rivers and streams. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia potamophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
