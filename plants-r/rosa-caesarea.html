Rosa caesarea

Rosa caesarea, commonly known as the Cyprus rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, simple leaves with serrated edges, and a fragrance that is often described as sweet and delicate.

Rosa caesarea is also notable for its historical significance in traditional medicine and horticulture.
