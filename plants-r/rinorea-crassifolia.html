Rinorea crassifolia

Rinorea crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rinorea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Rinorea crassifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
