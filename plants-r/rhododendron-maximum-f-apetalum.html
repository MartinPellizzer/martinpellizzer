Rhododendron maximum f. apetalum

Rhododendron maximum f. apetalum, commonly known as the white fringed rhododendron, is a unique cultivar of the rhododendron species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers with distinctive fringed petals, which set it apart from typical rhododendrons. The plant has leathery, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its showy blooms and adaptability to moist, cool climates.
