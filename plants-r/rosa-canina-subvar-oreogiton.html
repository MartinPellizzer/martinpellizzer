Rosa canina subvar. oreogiton

Rosa canina subvar. oreogiton, commonly known as the alpine rosehip, is a subspecies native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces bright red, fuzzy fruits called rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its flowers are typically pink to white, blooming in early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
