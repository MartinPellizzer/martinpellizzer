Ravenia paruana

Ravenia paruana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and savannas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
