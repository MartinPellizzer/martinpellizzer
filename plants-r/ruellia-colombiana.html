Ruellia colombiana

Ruellia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian ruellia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ruellia colombiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
