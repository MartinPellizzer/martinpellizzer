Rumex armenus

Rumex armenus, commonly known as Armenian dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, wavy leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on leafy stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or along stream banks.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cultures adds to its botanical significance.
