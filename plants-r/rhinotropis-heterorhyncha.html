Rhinotropis heterorhyncha

Rhinotropis heterorhyncha, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhinotropis heterorhyncha is a perennial species with a short stem and narrow leaves.
