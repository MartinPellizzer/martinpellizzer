Rosa canina f. macrostyla

Rosa canina f. macrostyla, commonly known as the large-flowered dog rose, is a horticultural form of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its larger, showier flowers compared to the typical Rosa canina. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

This form is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and is often used in breeding programs to enhance flower size and color.
