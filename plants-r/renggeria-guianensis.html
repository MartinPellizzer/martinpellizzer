Renggeria guianensis

Renggeria guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan renggeria, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Renggeria guianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
