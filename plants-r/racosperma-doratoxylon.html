Racosperma doratoxylon

Racosperma doratoxylon, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its distinctive yellow floral spikes. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Racosperma doratoxylon is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also important in ecological restoration projects.
