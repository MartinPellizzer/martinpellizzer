Rubus × nakaii

Rubus × nakaii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry shrub, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
