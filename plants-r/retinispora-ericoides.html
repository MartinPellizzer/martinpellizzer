Retinispora ericoides

Retinispora ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf leatherleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.

Retinispora ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
