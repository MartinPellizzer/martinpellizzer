Rubus gyamdaensis var. gyamdaensis

Rubus gyamdaensis var. gyamdaensis, commonly known as the Gyamda blackberry, is a species native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
