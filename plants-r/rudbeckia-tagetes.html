Rudbeckia tagetes

Rudbeckia tagetes, commonly known as the blackfoot daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves and bright yellow to orange flower heads. Rudbeckia tagetes thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its drought tolerance and vibrant blooms make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to many landscapes.
