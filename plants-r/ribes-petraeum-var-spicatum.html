Ribes petraeum var. spicatum

Ribes petraeum var. spicatum, commonly known as the spiculate gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
