Rosa villarsiana

Rosa villarsiana, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil.

Rosa villarsiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
