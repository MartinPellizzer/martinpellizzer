Rosa tomentosa var. parvula

Rosa tomentosa var. parvula, commonly known as the woolly rose or small-flowered woolly rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are thorny and often covered with a woolly layer of hairs, contributing to its hardy and resilient nature.

Rosa tomentosa var. parvula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
