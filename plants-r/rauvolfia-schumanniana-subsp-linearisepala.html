Rauvolfia schumanniana subsp. linearisepala

Rauvolfia schumanniana subsp.

linearisepala, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its roots contain alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
