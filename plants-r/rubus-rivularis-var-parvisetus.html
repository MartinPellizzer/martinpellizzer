Rubus rivularis var. parvisetus

Rubus rivularis var. parvisetus, commonly known as the small-stemmed blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems that form dense mats or low shrubs. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy surface.

This variety is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
