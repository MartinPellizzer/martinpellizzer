Rubus tereticaulis subsp. roridissimus

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

roridissimus, commonly known as the coastal raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its adaptability to coastal climates.
