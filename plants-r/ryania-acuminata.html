Ryania acuminata

Ryania acuminata, commonly known as the red cedar or ryania, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to woody, spiny pods containing seeds. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in pharmacological research.

The plant is also noted for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
