Roscoea humeana f. tyria

Roscoea humeana f. tyria, commonly known as the Tyrian rose gingers, is a striking ornamental plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features showy, bright orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has rhizomatous growth and thrives in moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are evergreen and provide a lush backdrop for the blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in subtropical and tropical regions.
