Rudicularia

Rudicularia, commonly known as the "root plant," is a unique genus within the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its specialized root-like structures that function as both anchoring and photosynthetic organs. Unlike typical vascular plants, Rudicularia lacks true stems and leaves, relying instead on its root-like bodies for nutrient absorption and energy production. These structures often form dense mats on the forest floor, contributing to soil stabilization and nutrient cycling.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to low-light environments and its role in tropical ecosystems.
