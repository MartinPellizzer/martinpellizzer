Rubus serpens var. multisetus

Rubus serpens var. multisetus, commonly known as the many-stemmed blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, dark berries. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces flowers that range from white to pale pink.

Its berries are typically used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
