Rosa adenophora var. eriocephala

Rosa adenophora var. eriocephala, commonly known as the wooly rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
