Rollinia neglecta

Rollinia neglecta, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caricaceae and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a fruit that resembles a star, with a green, spiny exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp inside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Rollinia neglecta is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
