Rosa viscaria var. setocarpa

Rosa viscaria var. setocarpa, commonly known as the hairy rose or the sticky rose, is a variant of the shrub rose species.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and foliage, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
