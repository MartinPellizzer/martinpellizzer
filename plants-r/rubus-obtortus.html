Rubus obtortus

Rubus obtortus, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, red, edible fruits similar to raspberries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems that often root at the nodes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in woodland areas across North America.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and native plant gardening.
