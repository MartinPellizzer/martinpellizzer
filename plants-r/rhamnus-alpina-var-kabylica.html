Rhamnus alpina var. kabylica

Rhamnus alpina var. kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylic alpine buckthorn, is a shrub native to the alpine regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which are followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh mountain environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
