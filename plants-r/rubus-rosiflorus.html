Rubus rosiflorus

Rubus rosiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rubus rosiflorus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for birds and small mammals.
