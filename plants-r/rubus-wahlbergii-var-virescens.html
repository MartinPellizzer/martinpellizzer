Rubus wahlbergii var. virescens

Rubus wahlbergii var. virescens, commonly known as the green-leafed blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a hairy texture on both sides. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
