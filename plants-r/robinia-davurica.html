Robinia davurica

Robinia davurica, commonly known as the Chinese honey locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and are followed by long, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional craftsmanship.
