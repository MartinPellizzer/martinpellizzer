Rhaphidophora rigida

Rhaphidophora rigida, commonly known as the stiff-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its upright, stiff leaves that are often ribbed and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically enclosed within a spadix, a common feature in the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

Rhaphidophora rigida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
