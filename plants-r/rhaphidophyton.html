Rhaphidophyton

Rhaphidophyton, commonly known as the "gymnosperm-like plant," is an extinct group of seed plants that lived during the Mesozoic era.

It is characterized by its unique reproductive structures, including specialized cones and seeds, which suggest a transitional form between gymnosperms and early angiosperms. The plant's morphology includes well-developed vascular systems and leaves with distinct venation patterns. Fossil evidence indicates that Rhaphidophyton thrived in a variety of environments, including coastal and inland regions.

Its study provides important insights into the evolutionary development of modern seed plants.
