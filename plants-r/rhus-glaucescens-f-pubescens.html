Rhus glaucescens f. pubescens

Rhus glaucescens f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent smooth sumac, is a variant of the smooth sumac characterized by its hairy or pubescent foliage.

This plant is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, typically found in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive bluish-green hue when fresh. The small, yellowish-green flowers appear in panicles and are followed by red, fuzzy fruits that persist into winter.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
