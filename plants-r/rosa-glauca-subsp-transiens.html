Rosa glauca subsp. transiens

Rosa glauca subsp.

transiens, commonly known as the Transient Blue Rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender flowers, which bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorny prickles.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral coloration.
