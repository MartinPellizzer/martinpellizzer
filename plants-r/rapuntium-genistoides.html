Rapuntium genistoides

Rapuntium genistoides, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
