Rhododendron glomerulatum

Rhododendron glomerulatum, commonly known as the clustered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems.

Rhododendron glomerulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as part of natural landscapes.
