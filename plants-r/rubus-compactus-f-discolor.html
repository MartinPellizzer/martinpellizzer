Rubus compactus f. discolor

Rubus compactus f. discolor, commonly known as the white-fruited blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive white or pale-colored fruits.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically less flavorful than typical blackberries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
