Racosperma tumidum

Racosperma tumidum, commonly known as the swollen acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, swollen, woody pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Racosperma tumidum is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
