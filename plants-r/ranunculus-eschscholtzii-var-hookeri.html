Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. hookeri

Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It has palmately divided leaves and produces small, greenish sepals beneath the petals.

This variety is often found in California and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
