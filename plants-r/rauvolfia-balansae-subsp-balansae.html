Rauvolfia balansae subsp. balansae

Rauvolfia balansae subsp.

balansae, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is notable for its traditional medicinal uses and potential pharmacological applications.
