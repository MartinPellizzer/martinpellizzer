Ranunculus tridentifer

Ranunculus tridentifer, commonly known as the three-toothed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to ten petals, typically in shades of yellow or white, and a central cluster of yellow stamens. The plant has trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three parts, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus tridentifer is often found in temperate regions and is known for its early spring blooming period.
