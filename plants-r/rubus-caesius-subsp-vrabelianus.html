Rubus caesius subsp. vrabelianus

Rubus caesius subsp.

vrabelianus, commonly known as the Vrabel's blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
