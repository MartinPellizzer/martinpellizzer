Rubus pungens var. fargesii

Rubus pungens var. fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southwestern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
