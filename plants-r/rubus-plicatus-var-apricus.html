Rubus plicatus var. apricus

Rubus plicatus var. apricus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
