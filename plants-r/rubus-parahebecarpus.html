Rubus parahebecarpus

Rubus parahebecarpus, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the stems are covered in prickly hairs.

This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shrublands and forest edges.
