Rebutia spiralisepala

Rebutia spiralisepala, commonly known as the spiral-leaf cactus, is a species native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaf-like stems, which are actually modified stems rather than true leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental and ecologically interesting plant in arid environments.
