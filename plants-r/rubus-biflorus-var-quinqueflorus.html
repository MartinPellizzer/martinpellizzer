Rubus biflorus var. quinqueflorus

Rubus biflorus var. quinqueflorus, commonly known as the five-flowered blackberry, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that have three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its fruit is typically dark purple to black when mature and has a sweet-tart flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
