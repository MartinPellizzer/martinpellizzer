Rhexia cordata

Rhexia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf stellaria, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features attractive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Rhexia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
