Reseda macrocarpa

Reseda macrocarpa, commonly known as yellowrocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Reseda macrocarpa is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
