Ranunculus furvellus

Ranunculus furvellus, commonly known as the purple marsh-foam, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to purple. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Ranunculus furvellus is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
