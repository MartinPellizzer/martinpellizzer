Rebutia permutata

Rebutia permutata, commonly known as the Desert Cactus or Mexican Paddle Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Rebutia permutata is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

It is often grown as a decorative houseplant or in rock gardens due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
