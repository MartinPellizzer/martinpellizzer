Ranunculus humilis

Ranunculus humilis, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy as well. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Ranunculus humilis is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
