Rhexia arcuata

Rhexia arcuata, commonly known as the curvedleaf stellaria, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming from June to September. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Rhexia arcuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
