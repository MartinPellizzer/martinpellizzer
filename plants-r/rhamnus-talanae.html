Rhamnus talanae

Rhamnus talanae, commonly known as the Talana buckthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the buckthorn family, Rhamnaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it grows in coastal forests and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a small, smooth, black drupe that ripens in summer.

Rhamnus talanae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and birds.
