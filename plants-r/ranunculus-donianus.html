Ranunculus donianus

Ranunculus donianus, commonly known as the Donian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
