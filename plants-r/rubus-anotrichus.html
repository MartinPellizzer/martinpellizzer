Rubus anotrichus

Rubus anotrichus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that mature in late summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is often used in jams and desserts due to its sweet, tart flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
