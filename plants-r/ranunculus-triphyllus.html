Ranunculus triphyllus

Ranunculus triphyllus, commonly known as the three-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
