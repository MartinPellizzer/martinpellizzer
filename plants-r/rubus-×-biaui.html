Rubus × biaui

Rubus × biaui, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained, loamy soils. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable.
