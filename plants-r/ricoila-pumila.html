Ricoila pumila

Ricoila pumila, commonly known as the dwarf pumila, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features compact, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, appearing in clusters at the base of the plant.

Ricoila pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
