Rhynchosia karaguensis

Rhynchosia karaguensis, commonly known as the Karaguensia bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional agriculture. Rhynchosia karaguensis is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in sustainable farming practices.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
