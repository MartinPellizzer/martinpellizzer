Rubus petnicensis

Rubus petnicensis, commonly known as the Andean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
