Rubus × reuteriformis

Rubus × reuteriformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
