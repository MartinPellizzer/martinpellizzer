Ranunculus nemorosus var. multiflorus

Ranunculus nemorosus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

Ranunculus nemorosus var. multiflorus is often found in forest understories and is known for its early blooming period.
