Rosa afzeliana f. remota

Rosa afzeliana f. remota, commonly known as the remote rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the typical form. The plant produces pink to red flowers with a fragrant aroma, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of China and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
