Rubus platysepalus

Rubus platysepalus, commonly known as the flat-sepal raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. Its fruits are often used in jams and desserts, though they are less common than other raspberry varieties.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and exhibits typical characteristics of the rose family.
