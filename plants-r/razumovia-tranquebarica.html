Razumovia tranquebarica

Razumovia tranquebarica, commonly known as the Tranquebar bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the coastal regions of southern India.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals arranged in a radial symmetry. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in sandy or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
