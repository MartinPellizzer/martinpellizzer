Rhinacanthus breviflorus

Rhinacanthus breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it produces woody pods containing seeds.

Rhinacanthus breviflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
