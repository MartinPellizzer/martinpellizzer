Rhynchospora radicans var. watsonii

Rhynchospora radicans var. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's floating sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves that grow close to the water's surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as marshes and pond edges.

Due to its aquatic adaptation, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
