Ranunculus icelandicus

Ranunculus icelandicus, commonly known as the Iceland buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, rocky soils and alpine environments. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive hairy stem. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three parts, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Ranunculus icelandicus is adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.
