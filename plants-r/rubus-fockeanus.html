Rubus fockeanus

Rubus fockeanus, commonly known as the Fockean blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
