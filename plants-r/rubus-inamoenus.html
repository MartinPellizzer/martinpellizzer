Rubus inamoenus

Rubus inamoenus, commonly known as the rough blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts.

The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
