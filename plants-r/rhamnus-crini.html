Rhamnus crini

Rhamnus crini, commonly known as the Indian buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by small, round berries. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

The berries are edible but are typically used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
