Ranunculus trachycarpus var. minor

Ranunculus trachycarpus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser hairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks. It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
