Ranunculus auricomus subsp. gracilior

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

gracilior, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals and a prominent central stamen.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy and emit a mild, sometimes irritating odor. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Ranunculaceae family.
