Rumex hippiatricus

Rumex hippiatricus, commonly known as the horse bindweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense colonies, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.

Rumex hippiatricus is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.
