Rubus scherzeri

Rubus scherzeri, commonly known as the Scherzer's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves, typically found in temperate regions. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or black in color. It thrives in woodland areas and is known for its ability to spread through vegetative means.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
