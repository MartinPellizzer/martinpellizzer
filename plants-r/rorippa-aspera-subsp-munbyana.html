Rorippa aspera subsp. munbyana

Rorippa aspera subsp.

munbyana, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant has slender, ascending stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
