Rubus coarctatus var. micranthus

Rubus coarctatus var. micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, serrated margins and produces small, dark red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a thorny stem structure. Its flowers are white and clustered in umbels, and the fruit ripens in late summer.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, acidic soils.
