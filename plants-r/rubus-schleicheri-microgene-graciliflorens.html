Rubus schleicheri microgene graciliflorens

Rubus schleicheri microgene graciliflorens, commonly known as the slender-flowered blackberry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, dark purple berries.

Its botanical features include a complex inflorescence structure and a distinctive, aromatic foliage that contributes to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
