Rosa hungarica var. boehmiae

Rosa hungarica var. boehmiae, commonly known as the Hungarian rose, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It features fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces hips that are orange-red in color. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable to a range of soil conditions.
