Rosa coriifolia f. setigera

Rosa coriifolia f. setigera, commonly known as the branched shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage.

It features small, ovate leaves with a finely toothed margin, and its flowers are typically pale pink to white, appearing in clusters along the stems. The plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact form, attractive foliage, and showy blooms that appear in early summer.
