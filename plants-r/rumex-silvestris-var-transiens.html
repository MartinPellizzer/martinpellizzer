Rumex silvestris var. transiens

Rumex silvestris var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Sheep's Sorrel, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple underside, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by triangular, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
