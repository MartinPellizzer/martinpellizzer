Robinia uliginosa

Robinia uliginosa, commonly known as the black locust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with 12 to 20 oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clustered racemes during the spring. Its pods are long, flat, and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant has a deep root system and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
