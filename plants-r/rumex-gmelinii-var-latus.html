Rumex gmelinii var. latus

Rumex gmelinii var. latus, commonly known as the broadleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces basal leaves that are broad, lanceolate, and often covered with a waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
