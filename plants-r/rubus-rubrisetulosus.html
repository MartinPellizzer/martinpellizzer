Rubus rubrisetulosus

Rubus rubrisetulosus, commonly known as the red-hairy raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems covered in reddish-brown hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
