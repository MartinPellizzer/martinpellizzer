Rubus melanolasius var. concolor

Rubus melanolasius var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth blackberry.

It is a species of bramble characterized by its dark, glossy berries and thorny stems. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well.
