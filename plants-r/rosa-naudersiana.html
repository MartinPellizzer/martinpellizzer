Rosa naudersiana

Rosa naudersiana, commonly known as the Nauders rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and hips that are typically red or orange in color.

Rosa naudersiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
