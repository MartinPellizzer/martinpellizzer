Ranunculus kitinensis

Ranunculus kitinensis, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ranunculus kitinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
