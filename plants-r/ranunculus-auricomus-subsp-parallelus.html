Ranunculus auricomus subsp. parallelus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

parallelus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with prominent central sepals.

The subspecies parallelus differs from the typical form by having narrower, more elongated leaves and slightly different flower structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
