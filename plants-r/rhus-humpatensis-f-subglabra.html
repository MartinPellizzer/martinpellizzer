Rhus humpatensis f. subglabra

Rhus humpatensis f. subglabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf humpatensis, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by paired, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
