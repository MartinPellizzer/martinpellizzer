Ruta erythraea

Ruta erythraea, commonly known as the Red rue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Ruta erythraea is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its leaves and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their purported antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.
