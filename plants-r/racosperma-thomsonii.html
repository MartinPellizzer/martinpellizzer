Racosperma thomsonii

Racosperma thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices and for ecological restoration.
