Rubus ursinus var. pentaphyllus

Rubus ursinus var. pentaphyllus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional herbal practices.
