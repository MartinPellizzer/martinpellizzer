Rhamnus sibthorpiana

Rhamnus sibthorpiana, commonly known as the African buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
