Rosa spinosissima var. baicheriana

Rosa spinosissima var. baicheriana, commonly known as the Baicher's prickly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
