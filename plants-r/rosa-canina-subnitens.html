Rosa canina subnitens

Rosa canina subnitens, commonly known as the shining dog rose, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring, which are followed by orange-red hips that contain the seeds. Rosa canina subnitens is a member of the Rosaceae family and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
