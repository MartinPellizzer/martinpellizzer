Rubus pervestitus

Rubus pervestitus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberries.
