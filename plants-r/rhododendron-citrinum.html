Rhododendron citrinum

Rhododendron citrinum, commonly known as the yellow rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a glossy appearance.

Rhododendron citrinum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
