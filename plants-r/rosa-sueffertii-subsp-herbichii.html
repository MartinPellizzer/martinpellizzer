Rosa sueffertii subsp. herbichii

Rosa sueffertii subsp.

herbichii, commonly known as the herbichii rose, is a shrub native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late summer.

The plant has elliptical, serrated leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are covered in thorny prickles. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
