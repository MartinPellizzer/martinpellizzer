Rosa dumalis var. villosiuscula

Rosa dumalis var. villosiuscula, commonly known as the silky prickly rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with hairy surfaces that contribute to its common name.

This species is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
