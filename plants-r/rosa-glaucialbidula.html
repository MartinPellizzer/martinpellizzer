Rosa glaucialbidula

Rosa glaucialbidula, commonly known as the white-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale green to white foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and the stems are often covered in thorns.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
