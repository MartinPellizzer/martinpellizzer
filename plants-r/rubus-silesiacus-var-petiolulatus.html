Rubus silesiacus var. petiolulatus

Rubus silesiacus var. petiolulatus, commonly known as the Silesian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
