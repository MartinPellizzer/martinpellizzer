Ribes sanguineum var. brocklebankii

Ribes sanguineum var. brocklebankii, commonly known as the Brocklebank's currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features upright, arching branches that bear clusters of small, pink to red flowers in early spring. The plant produces edible, tart berries that ripen in late summer, making it valuable for both wildlife and human consumption. Its foliage is typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various garden conditions.
