Rubus schleicheri var. plusiacanthus

Rubus schleicheri var. plusiacanthus, commonly known as the Schleicher's blackberry variant, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Vietnam and Cambodia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often along stream banks or in mountainous areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible when ripe, though they are generally less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties.
