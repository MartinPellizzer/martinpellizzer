Rubus boudiccae

Rubus boudiccae, commonly known as the Boudica's bramble, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.

Due to its historical and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for biodiversity and conservation efforts.
