Rhaphiolepis indica var. phaeostemon

Rhaphiolepis indica var. phaeostemon, commonly known as the purpleleaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn a striking purple-red color in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red berries that persist through the winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and adaptable to a variety of soil types.
