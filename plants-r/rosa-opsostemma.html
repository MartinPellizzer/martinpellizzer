Rosa opsostemma

Rosa opsostemma, commonly known as the Mexican rose or the rose of the Andes, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often thorny.

Rosa opsostemma is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
