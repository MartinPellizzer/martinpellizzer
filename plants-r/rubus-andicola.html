Rubus andicola

Rubus andicola, commonly known as the Andean bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly the Andes. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and it produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
