Rosa × michiganensis

Rosa × michiganensis, commonly known as the Michigan rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It is native to the United States, particularly Michigan, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Rosa × michiganensis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
