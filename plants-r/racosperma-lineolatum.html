Racosperma lineolatum

Racosperma lineolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, linear leaves with a distinctive pale midrib, giving it a striking appearance. It produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes.

Racosperma lineolatum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
