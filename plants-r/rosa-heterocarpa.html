Rosa heterocarpa

Rosa heterocarpa, commonly known as the heterocarp rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces distinctive fruits called heterocarps, which are unique in structure and contain seeds. Rosa heterocarpa thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
