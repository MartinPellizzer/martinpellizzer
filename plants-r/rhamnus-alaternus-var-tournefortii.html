Rhamnus alaternus var. tournefortii

Rhamnus alaternus var. tournefortii, commonly known as the Turkish buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
