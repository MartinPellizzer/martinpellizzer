Randia fasciculata var. multiflora

Randia fasciculata var. multiflora, commonly known as the clustered randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, often thorny growth habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
