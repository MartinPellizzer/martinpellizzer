Rosa glauca var. laevigata

Rosa glauca var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth blue rose, is a variant of the blue rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender-colored flowers, which are typically single or semi-double in form. The plant has slender, thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral coloration.
