Rubus villicaulis f. eurythyrsus

Rubus villicaulis f. eurythyrsus, commonly known as the broad-throat raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. Its flowers are notable for their broad, throat-like structure, which gives the species its common name.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
