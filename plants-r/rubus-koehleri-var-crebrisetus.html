Rubus koehleri var. crebrisetus

Rubus koehleri var. crebrisetus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The leaves are typically serrated and have a hairy texture, contributing to its common name. The plant produces red or yellow berries that are edible but often tart in flavor.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
