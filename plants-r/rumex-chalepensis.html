Rumex chalepensis

Rumex chalepensis, commonly known as burnet saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often have a slightly sour taste.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
