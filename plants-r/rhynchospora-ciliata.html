Rhynchospora ciliata

Rhynchospora ciliata, commonly known as the hairy fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear small, triangular leaves with fine, hairy margins. Its flowering spikes produce numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers that are often reddish or purplish in color.

Rhynchospora ciliata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
