Rhododendron fictolacteum

Rhododendron fictolacteum, commonly known as the false milkweed rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical and leathery, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Rhododendron fictolacteum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
