Rhododendron nitidum

Rhododendron nitidum, commonly known as the glossy rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and shiny on both sides. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Rhododendron nitidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hardy shrub in suitable climates.
