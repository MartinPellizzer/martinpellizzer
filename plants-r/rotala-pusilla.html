Rotala pusilla

Rotala pusilla, commonly known as dwarf rotala, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, floating leaves that are typically less than an inch in diameter, with a bright green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, contributing to its ornamental value in aquariums. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and prefers moderate to high light conditions.

Rotala pusilla is often used in planted aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for small aquatic life.
