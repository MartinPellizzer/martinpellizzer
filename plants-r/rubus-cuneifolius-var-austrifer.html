Rubus cuneifolius var. austrifer

Rubus cuneifolius var. austrifer, commonly known as the Australian blackberry, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.
