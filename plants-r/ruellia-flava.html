Ruellia flava

Ruellia flava, commonly known as yellow ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and come in shades of yellow to orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ruellia flava is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often used in ornamental gardening.
