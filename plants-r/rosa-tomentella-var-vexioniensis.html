Rosa tomentella var. vexioniensis

Rosa tomentella var. vexioniensis, commonly known as the Vexin rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Vexin region of France.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dense, grayish-white fuzzy foliage that provides a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
