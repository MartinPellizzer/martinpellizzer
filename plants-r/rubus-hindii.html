Rubus hindii

Rubus hindii, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
