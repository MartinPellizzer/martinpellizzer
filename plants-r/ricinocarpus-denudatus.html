Ricinocarpus denudatus

Ricinocarpus denudatus, commonly known as the naked senna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal studies.
