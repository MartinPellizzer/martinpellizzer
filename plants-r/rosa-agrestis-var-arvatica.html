Rosa agrestis var. arvatica

Rosa agrestis var. arvatica, commonly known as the field rose, is a wild rose species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its hips, or fruit, are red and ornamental, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Rosa agrestis var. arvatica thrives in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.
