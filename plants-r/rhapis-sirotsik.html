Rhapis sirotsik

Rhapis sirotsik, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and the Philippines. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly folded. It produces small, yellowish-white inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils.

Rhapis sirotsik is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
