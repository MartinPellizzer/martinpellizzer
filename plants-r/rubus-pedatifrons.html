Rubus pedatifrons

Rubus pedatifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically five- to seven-lobed and have a distinctive, broad shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep black color, often with a white or yellowish bloom. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
