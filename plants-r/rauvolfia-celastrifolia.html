Rauvolfia celastrifolia

Rauvolfia celastrifolia, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and indigenous medicine.
