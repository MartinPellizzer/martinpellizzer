Rubus × polyanthoides

Rubus × polyanthoides, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
