Ranunculus auricomus subsp. curvatus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

curvatus, commonly known as the curvedleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features striking yellow flowers with sharply divided, palmately arranged petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
