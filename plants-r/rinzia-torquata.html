Rinzia torquata

Rinzia torquata, commonly known as the blue rinzia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous annual with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. It is adapted to coastal and inland environments, often thriving in well-drained conditions.

Rinzia torquata plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
