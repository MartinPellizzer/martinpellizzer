Rosa arvensis f. baldensis

Rosa arvensis f. baldensis, commonly known as the Baldens rose, is a botanical variant of the field rose.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its slender stems and small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces pink to red flowers in late spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This form is notable for its adaptability and is of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
