Racosperma cambagei

Racosperma cambagei, commonly known as the Northern Territory acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological significance in the region.
