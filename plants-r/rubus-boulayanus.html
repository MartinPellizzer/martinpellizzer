Rubus boulayanus

Rubus boulayanus, commonly known as the Boulay's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
