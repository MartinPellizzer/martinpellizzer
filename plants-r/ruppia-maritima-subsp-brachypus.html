Ruppia maritima subsp. brachypus

Ruppia maritima subsp.

brachypus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea rocket, is a halophyte that thrives in brackish and saline environments. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense mats in coastal wetlands.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind, and its seeds are dispersed by water. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

This subspecies is adapted to fluctuating salinity and water levels, making it an important component of estuarine ecosystems.
