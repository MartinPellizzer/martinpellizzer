Rosa obtusifolia var. fischeri

Rosa obtusifolia var. fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dense, thorny stems and dark green, glossy foliage that provides an attractive appearance throughout the growing season. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

The variety is noted for its resilience in harsh climates and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
