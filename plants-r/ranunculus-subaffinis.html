Ranunculus subaffinis

Ranunculus subaffinis, commonly known as the European buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Ranunculus subaffinis is also notable for its toxic properties, containing protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation.
