Rhynchosia chrysoscias

Rhynchosia chrysoscias, commonly known as the golden crown bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant bears pods that contain several seeds, which are typically small and angular in shape. Rhynchosia chrysoscias is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
