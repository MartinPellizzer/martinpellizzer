Rubus × procerifrons

Rubus × procerifrons, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that are typically larger than those of its parent species. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and they have a serrated margin.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, though it is less commonly cultivated than other Rubus species.
