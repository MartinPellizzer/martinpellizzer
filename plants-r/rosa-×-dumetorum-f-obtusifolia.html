Rosa × dumetorum f. obtusifolia

Rosa × dumetorum f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the woolly dog rose, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and thorny stems.

It produces pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a sweet, fragrant scent. The plant is native to Europe and is adapted to temperate climates, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically oval with a serrated edge and covered in fine, soft hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
