Rosa stylosa subsp. cantabrica

Rosa stylosa subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has dark green, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
