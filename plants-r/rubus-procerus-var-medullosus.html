Rubus procerus var. medullosus

Rubus procerus var. medullosus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is native to parts of North America and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
