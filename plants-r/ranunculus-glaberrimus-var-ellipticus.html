Ranunculus glaberrimus var. ellipticus

Ranunculus glaberrimus var. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is usually covered in fine hairs.

The elliptic buttercup is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
