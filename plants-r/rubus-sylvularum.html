Rubus sylvularum

Rubus sylvularum, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus sylvularum is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
