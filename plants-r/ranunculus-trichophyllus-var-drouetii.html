Ranunculus trichophyllus var. drouetii

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. drouetii, commonly known as the Drouet's hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive hairy stem and leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, cup-shaped blooms.
