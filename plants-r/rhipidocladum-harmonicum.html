Rhipidocladum harmonicum

Rhipidocladum harmonicum, commonly known as the musical cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help it conserve water in its harsh environment.

Despite its unusual name, it is not related to musical instruments but is named for the rhythmic blooming pattern of its flowers.
