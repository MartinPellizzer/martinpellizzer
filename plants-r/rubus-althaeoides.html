Rubus althaeoides

Rubus althaeoides, commonly known as the althaeoid raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the first-year stems. The plant produces clusters of small, red, edible berries that are similar in appearance to other raspberries. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

The plant thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.
