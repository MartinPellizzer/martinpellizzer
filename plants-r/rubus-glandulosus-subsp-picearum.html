Rubus glandulosus subsp. picearum

Rubus glandulosus subsp.

picearum, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
