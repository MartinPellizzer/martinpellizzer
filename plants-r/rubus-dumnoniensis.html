Rubus dumnoniensis

Rubus dumnoniensis, commonly known as the Dumnonian bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western regions of the United Kingdom, particularly in Devon and Cornwall. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible berries.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
