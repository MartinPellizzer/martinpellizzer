Ranunculus ovalifolius

Ranunculus ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are oval in shape, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates.

Ranunculus ovalifolius is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic properties.
