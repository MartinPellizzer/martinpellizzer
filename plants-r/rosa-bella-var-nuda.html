Rosa bella var. nuda

Rosa bella var. nuda, commonly known as the naked beauty rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, single-layered flowers with a soft pink hue, often appearing in clusters along the stems. The plant has thornless stems and leaves composed of 5 to 7 oval, serrated leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant blooms and compact growth habit.
