Rubia ephedroides

Rubia ephedroides, commonly known as the Tibetan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
