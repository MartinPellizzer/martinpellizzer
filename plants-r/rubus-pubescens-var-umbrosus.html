Rubus pubescens var. umbrosus

Rubus pubescens var. umbrosus, commonly known as the dark raspberry or blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry when mature.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for both its ecological role and potential culinary uses.
