Ranunculus capillaceus

Ranunculus capillaceus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are typically covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Ranunculus capillaceus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
