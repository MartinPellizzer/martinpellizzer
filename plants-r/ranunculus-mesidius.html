Ranunculus mesidius

Ranunculus mesidius, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow petals and a hairy, lobed leaf structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
