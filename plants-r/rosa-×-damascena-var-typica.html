Rosa × damascena var. typica

Rosa × damascena var. typica, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has thorny stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with oval-shaped leaflets and a serrated edge.

The flowers are widely used in the perfume and cosmetic industries due to their high essential oil content.
