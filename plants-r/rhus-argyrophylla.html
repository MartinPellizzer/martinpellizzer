Rhus argyrophylla

Rhus argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its compound leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
