Rhododendron seniavinii var. crassifolium

Rhododendron seniavinii var. crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and densely arranged. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
