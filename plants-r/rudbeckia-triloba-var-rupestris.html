Rudbeckia triloba var. rupestris

Rudbeckia triloba var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock rudbeckia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular addition to native plant gardens.
