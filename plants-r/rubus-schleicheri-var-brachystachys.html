Rubus schleicheri var. brachystachys

Rubus schleicheri var. brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
