Rapanea ciliata

Rapanea ciliata, commonly known as the silky-leafed evergreen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with soft, hairy margins on the leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
