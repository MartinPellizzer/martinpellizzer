Rosa × porrigens var. insidiosa

Rosa × porrigens var. insidiosa, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often covered in thorns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its ability to cover ground and prevent erosion.
