Rosa dijonensis

Rosa dijonensis, commonly known as the Dijon rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the region of Dijon in eastern France.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and is characterized by its large, fragrant pink flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for garden cultivation.

The Dijon rose is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
