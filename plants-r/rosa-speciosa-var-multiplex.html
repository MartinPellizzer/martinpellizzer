Rosa speciosa var. multiplex

Rosa speciosa var. multiplex, commonly known as the showy wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a fragrant aroma. The plant produces orange to red hips in the fall, which are edible and contain seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
