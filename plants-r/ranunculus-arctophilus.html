Ranunculus arctophilus

Ranunculus arctophilus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

Ranunculus arctophilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
