Ranunculus fueginos

Ranunculus fueginos, commonly known as the southern buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers can range in color from yellow to orange.

Ranunculus fueginos is often found in the Andes region, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
