Rubus micans microgene albicomus

Rubus micans microgene albicomus, commonly known as the white-berried bramble, is a shrub native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically white or pale in color, which give it its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

The leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is known for its aromatic foliage.
