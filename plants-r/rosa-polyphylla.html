Rosa polyphylla

Rosa polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets, which contribute to its common name.

Rosa polyphylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
