Rosa barcensis

Rosa barcensis, commonly known as the Barcens rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa barcensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
