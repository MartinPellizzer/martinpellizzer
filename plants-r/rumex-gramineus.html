Rumex gramineus

Rumex gramineus, commonly known as common dock, is a perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves that are typically reddish or purplish at the base. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense, terminal cluster called a head. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and near water bodies.

Rumex gramineus is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive leaf morphology and ecological adaptability.
