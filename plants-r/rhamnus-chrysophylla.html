Rhamnus chrysophylla

Rhamnus chrysophylla, commonly known as goldenleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn a striking golden-yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
