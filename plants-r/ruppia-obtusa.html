Ruppia obtusa

Ruppia obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf water plantain, is a aquatic plant that thrives in brackish and freshwater environments.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically submerged, with small white flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant is characterized by its ability to form dense underwater stands, which can help stabilize sediment and provide habitat for aquatic life. Ruppia obtusa is often found in coastal marshes, estuaries, and shallow lakes.

Its reproductive strategy involves the production of floating seeds that can travel long distances with water currents.
