Ranunculus pseudoaleae

Ranunculus pseudoaleae, commonly known as the Aleutian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Aleutian Islands of Alaska.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or orange flowers. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to the harsh coastal environment.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
