Rhynchospora splendens f. composita

Rhynchospora splendens f. composita, commonly known as the composite tussock sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay, and is often found in disturbed or aquatic habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
