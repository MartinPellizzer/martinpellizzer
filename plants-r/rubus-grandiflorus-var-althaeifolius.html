Rubus grandiflorus var. althaeifolius

Rubus grandiflorus var. althaeifolius, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that appear in early summer. The leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have a distinctive shape resembling that of the plant Althaea, hence its varietal name. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun to partial shade.
