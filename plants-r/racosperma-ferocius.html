Racosperma ferocius

Racosperma ferocius, commonly known as the bitter acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The species is known for its toxic properties, making it unpalatable to most herbivores.
