Rhododendron charitostreptum

Rhododendron charitostreptum, commonly known as the Kashmiri rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Pakistan, India, and Afghanistan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features striking clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a dense canopy that supports various wildlife.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
