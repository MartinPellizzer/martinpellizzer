Rubus nitidus proles hamulosus

Rubus nitidus proles hamulosus, commonly known as the silky raspberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or moist soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
