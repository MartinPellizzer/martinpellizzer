Ranunculus cadmicus var. parviflorus

Ranunculus cadmicus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, usually yellow or white flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain multiple seeds.

This variety is often found in the Pacific Northwest of North America and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
