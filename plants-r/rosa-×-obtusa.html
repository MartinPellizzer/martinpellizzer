Rosa × obtusa

Rosa × obtusa, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Rosa × obtusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
