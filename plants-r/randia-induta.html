Randia induta

Randia induta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian randia, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in wetland areas and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, fertile soils.

Randia induta is also known for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
