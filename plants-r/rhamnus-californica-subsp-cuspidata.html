Rhamnus californica subsp. cuspidata

Rhamnus californica subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal areas of California. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the coastline, thriving in maritime climates.

The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive reddish underside. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark, egg-shaped berries.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to coastal conditions and its role in stabilizing dunes.
