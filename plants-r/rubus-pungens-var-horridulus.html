Rubus pungens var. horridulus

Rubus pungens var. horridulus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, hairy fruits that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
