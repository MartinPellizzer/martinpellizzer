Rosa × tordensis

Rosa × tordensis, commonly known as the Tordenskiold's rose, is a hybrid species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically has thorny stems and simple, pinnately compound leaves. It is native to the Caucasus region and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

Rosa × tordensis is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
