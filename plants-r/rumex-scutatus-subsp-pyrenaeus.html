Rumex scutatus subsp. pyrenaeus

Rumex scutatus subsp.

pyrenaeus, commonly known as the Pyrenean sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The plant features serrated, triangular leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. It is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished from other Rumex species by its specific habitat and morphological traits.
