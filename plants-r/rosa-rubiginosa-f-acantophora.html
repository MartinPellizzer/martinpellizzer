Rosa rubiginosa f. acantophora

Rosa rubiginosa f. acantophora, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose characterized by its distinctive red or purplish stems.

This form exhibits a compact growth habit with dense, thornless stems that are often covered in a reddish hue. Its foliage consists of dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
