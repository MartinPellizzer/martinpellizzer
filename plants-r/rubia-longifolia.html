Rubia longifolia

Rubia longifolia, commonly known as greater cow-wheat, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rubia longifolia is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued, particularly for its use in traditional herbal remedies.
