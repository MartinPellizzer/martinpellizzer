Rosa × dumetorum var. protecta

Rosa × dumetorum var. protecta, commonly known as the rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often forming low, sprawling mats. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and somewhat prickly.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
