Rubus bushii

Rubus bushii, commonly known as the Bushii blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
