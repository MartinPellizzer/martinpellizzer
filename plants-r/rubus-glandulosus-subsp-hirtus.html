Rubus glandulosus subsp. hirtus

Rubus glandulosus subsp.

hirtus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with notable hirsute (hairy) characteristics on its stems and leaves.
