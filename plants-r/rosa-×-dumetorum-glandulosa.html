Rosa × dumetorum glandulosa

Rosa × dumetorum glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually compound, with glandular dots that secrete a sticky substance, which may deter herbivores.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
