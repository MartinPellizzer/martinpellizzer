Rosa × lasiodonta var. grandifolia

Rosa × lasiodonta var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered Rosa × lasiodonta, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It typically features semi-double to fully double blooms in shades of pink, red, or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves that are often hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent in the development of many modern garden roses.
