Ranunculus pomelianus

Ranunculus pomelianus, commonly known as the Pomelo Ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a greenish or reddish hue at the base. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a touch of elegance to garden landscapes.
