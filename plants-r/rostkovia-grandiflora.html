Rostkovia grandiflora

Rostkovia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features sturdy, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
