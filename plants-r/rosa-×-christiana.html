Rosa × christiana

Rosa × christiana, commonly known as the Christiana rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and profuse flowering, typically producing large, fragrant blooms in shades of pink and white. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and is well-suited to temperate climates. Its stems are often covered in thorns, and its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges.

This rose is valued for both ornamental purposes and its potential use in horticultural breeding programs.
