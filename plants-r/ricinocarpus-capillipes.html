Ricinocarpus capillipes

Ricinocarpus capillipes, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems. Its fibrous stems and leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Ricinocarpus capillipes plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
