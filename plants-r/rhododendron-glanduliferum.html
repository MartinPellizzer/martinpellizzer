Rhododendron glanduliferum

Rhododendron glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically dark green and leathery, with glandular dots that give the plant its scientific name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
