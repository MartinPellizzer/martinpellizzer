Ranunculus texensis

Ranunculus texensis, commonly known as the Texas buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to nine sepals that resemble petals, and it produces small, green, spherical fruits. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure.

Ranunculus texensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its cheerful appearance.
