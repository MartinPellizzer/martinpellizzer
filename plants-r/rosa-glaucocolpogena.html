Rosa glaucocolpogena

Rosa glaucocolpogena, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically borne in clusters. Rosa glaucocolpogena thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
