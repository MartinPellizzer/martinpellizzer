Rhododendron × crouxii f. plenum

Rhododendron × crouxii f. plenum, commonly known as the full-flowered rhododendron, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of leathery, dark green leaves that provide an attractive backdrop to the blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
