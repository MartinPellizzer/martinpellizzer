Rubus strugensis

Rubus strugensis, commonly known as the Strugan raspberry, is a shrub native to Eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
