Rhododendron rushforthii

Rhododendron rushforthii, commonly known as the Rushforth's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to red, depending on the specific variety.

Rhododendron rushforthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
