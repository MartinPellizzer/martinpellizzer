Rosa sepium f. dichtliana

Rosa sepium f. dichtliana, commonly known as the dense-stemmed rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant characterized by its dense growth habit and profuse flowering.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are densely arranged along its stems. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a rich, velvety texture, and they bloom in clusters during the summer months. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden settings for its visual appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
