Rosa leiothamnus

Rosa leiothamnus, commonly known as the white-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces fragrant, single or double pink to white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, woodland environments.

Rosa leiothamnus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
