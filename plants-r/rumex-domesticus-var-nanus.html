Rumex domesticus var. nanus

Rumex domesticus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its succulent, arrow-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. Its compact size makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders, while its edible leaves are often used in cooking.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.
