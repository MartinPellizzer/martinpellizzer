Rubus angustisetus var. suboblongifrons

Rubus angustisetus var. suboblongifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with sharply pointed lobes. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. The fruit is a aggregate of small drupes, usually dark purple to black when ripe.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
