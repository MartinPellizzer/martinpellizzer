Ranunculus grandifolius var. major

Ranunculus grandifolius var. major, commonly known as the large-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
