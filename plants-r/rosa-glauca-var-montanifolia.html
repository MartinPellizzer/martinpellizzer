Rosa glauca var. montanifolia

Rosa glauca var. montanifolia, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
