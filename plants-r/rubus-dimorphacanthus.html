Rubus dimorphacanthus

Rubus dimorphacanthus, commonly known as the two-spined blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a dark red or black color in the summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained habitats.

Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.
