Rosa strengnensis

Rosa strengnensis, commonly known as the Strengnens rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Rosa strengnensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in some horticultural gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
