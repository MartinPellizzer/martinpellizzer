Ranunculus petiolaris var. typicus

Ranunculus petiolaris var. typicus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight yellow petals and a greenish-yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and wetland restoration projects.
