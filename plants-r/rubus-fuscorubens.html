Rubus fuscorubens

Rubus fuscorubens, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Rubus fuscorubens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
