Rinorea copelandii

Rinorea copelandii, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Rinorea copelandii is valued for its ornamental value in horticulture due to its vibrant floral display.
