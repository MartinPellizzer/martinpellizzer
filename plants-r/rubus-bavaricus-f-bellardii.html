Rubus bavaricus f. bellardii

Rubus bavaricus f. bellardii, commonly known as the Bellard's blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a tart flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and grows as a shrub with thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisines.
