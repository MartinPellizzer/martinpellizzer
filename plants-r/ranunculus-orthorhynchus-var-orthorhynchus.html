Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. orthorhynchus

Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. orthorhynchus, commonly known as the western burr-crop, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces follicles that are covered in hooked bracts, aiding in seed dispersal by attachment to animals.

This species is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.
