Roegneria doniana

Roegneria doniana, commonly known as the Tibetan quackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Roegneria doniana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
