Rubus borreri subsp. conothyrsus

Rubus borreri subsp.

conothyrsus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberry species.
