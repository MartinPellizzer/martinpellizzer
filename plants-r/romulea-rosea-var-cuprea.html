Romulea rosea var. cuprea

Romulea rosea var. cuprea, commonly known as the copper-leafed glory flower, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features elegant, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant copper or reddish-brown color, which contrast beautifully with its delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually pale yellow to cream and have a subtle fragrance. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.
