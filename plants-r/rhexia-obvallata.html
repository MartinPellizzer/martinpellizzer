Rhexia obvallata

Rhexia obvallata, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, acidic soils within woodlands and wetlands. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly wavy margin.

Rhexia obvallata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
