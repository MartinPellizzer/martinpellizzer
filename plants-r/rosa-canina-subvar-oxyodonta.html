Rosa canina subvar. oxyodonta

Rosa canina subvar. oxyodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the typical Rosa canina. This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils. It produces red or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange or reddish hips.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
