Rubus magdeburgensis

Rubus magdeburgensis, commonly known as the Magdeburg raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in the undergrowth of forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
