Rubus propinquus

Rubus propinquus, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants. Rubus propinquus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.

Its fruits are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
