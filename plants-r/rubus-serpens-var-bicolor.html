Rubus serpens var. bicolor

Rubus serpens var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and bi-colored fruits. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. Its fruit, which ripens to a dark purple or black color, is edible and has a tart flavor.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
