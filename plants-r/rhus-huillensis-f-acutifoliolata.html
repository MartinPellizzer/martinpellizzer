Rhus huillensis f. acutifoliolata

Rhus huillensis f. acutifoliolata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into narrow, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fuzzy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
