Rosa communis var. rhynchocarpa

Rosa communis var. rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a distinctive, fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
