Rosa pimpinellifolia var. archetypa

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. archetypa, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, silvery hairs.

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. archetypa thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
