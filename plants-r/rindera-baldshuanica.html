Rindera baldshuanica

Rindera baldshuanica, commonly known as the slender rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems topped with a single, showy orchid-like flower. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Rindera baldshuanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in specialized botanical gardens.
