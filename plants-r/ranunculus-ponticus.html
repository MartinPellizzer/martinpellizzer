Ranunculus ponticus

Ranunculus ponticus, commonly known as the Pontic buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the regions of the Black Sea.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of finely divided, serrated leaves, and it produces showy yellow flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.

However, it can be invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
