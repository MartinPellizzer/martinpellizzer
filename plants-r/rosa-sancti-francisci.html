Rosa sancti-francisci

Rosa sancti-francisci, commonly known as the Franciscan rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
