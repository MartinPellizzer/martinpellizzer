Ranunculus ambiguus

Ranunculus ambiguus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow petals and finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually solitary and have a delicate, cup-shaped appearance.

Ranunculus ambiguus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
