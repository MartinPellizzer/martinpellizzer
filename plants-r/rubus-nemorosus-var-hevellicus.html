Rubus nemorosus var. hevellicus

Rubus nemorosus var. hevellicus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland and hedgerow environments.
