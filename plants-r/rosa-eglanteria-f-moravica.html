Rosa eglanteria f. moravica

Rosa eglanteria f. moravica, commonly known as the Moravian prickly rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa eglanteria species.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
