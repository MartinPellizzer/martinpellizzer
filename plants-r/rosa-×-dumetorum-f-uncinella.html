Rosa × dumetorum f. uncinella

Rosa × dumetorum f. uncinella, commonly known as the hooked rose, is a botanical hybrid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
