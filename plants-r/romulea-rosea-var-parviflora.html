Romulea rosea var. parviflora

Romulea rosea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and exotic plants.
