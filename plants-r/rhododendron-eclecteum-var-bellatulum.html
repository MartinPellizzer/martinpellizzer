Rhododendron eclecteum var. bellatulum

Rhododendron eclecteum var. bellatulum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to white.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
