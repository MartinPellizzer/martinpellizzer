Rubus silvaticus var. hungaricus

Rubus silvaticus var. hungaricus, commonly known as the Hungarian bramble, is a variant of the common bramble found primarily in Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
