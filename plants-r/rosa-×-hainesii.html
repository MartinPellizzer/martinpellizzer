Rosa × hainesii

Rosa × hainesii, commonly known as the Haines rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Rosa × hainesii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
