Rosa canina subvar. nitescens

Rosa canina subvar. nitescens, commonly known as the white rose hip, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers and distinctive, rounded, red-orange fruits known as rose hips. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in hedgerows and woodland edges. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins.

The rose hips are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
