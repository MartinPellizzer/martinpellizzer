Rumex paucifolius var. minusculus

Rumex paucifolius var. minusculus, commonly known as the small-leaved dock, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are usually greenish or reddish, with a unique structure that aids in pollination.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
