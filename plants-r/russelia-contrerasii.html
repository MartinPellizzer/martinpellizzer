Russelia contrerasii

Russelia contrerasii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Russelia contrerasii is also noted for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
