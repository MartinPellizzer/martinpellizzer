Rubus lindenbergii

Rubus lindenbergii, commonly known as Lindenberg's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable resource in local ecosystems.
