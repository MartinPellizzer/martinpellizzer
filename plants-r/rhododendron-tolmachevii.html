Rhododendron tolmachevii

Rhododendron tolmachevii, commonly known as the Tolkachev rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and leathery, elliptical leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
