Rubus dumetorum var. glabratus

Rubus dumetorum var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry.

It is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its edible berries, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter flavor.
