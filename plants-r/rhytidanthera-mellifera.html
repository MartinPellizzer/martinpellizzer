Rhytidanthera mellifera

Rhytidanthera mellifera, commonly known as the honeycomb orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, honeycomb-like structure on its flower spikes, which are composed of tightly packed, flattened segments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Rhytidanthera mellifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
