Rubus curvacanthus

Rubus curvacanthus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, translucent achenes surrounded by a fleshy, edible pericarp. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.

Rubus curvacanthus is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
