Rosa × robertsonii

Rosa × robertsonii, commonly known as the Robertson's rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its botanical features include a woody structure, simple leaves, and anthers that produce pollen for fertilization.

Rosa × robertsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
