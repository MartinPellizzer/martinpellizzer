Rubus iseranus

Rubus iseranus, commonly known as the Japanese bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
