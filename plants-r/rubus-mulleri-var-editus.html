Rubus mulleri var. editus

Rubus mulleri var. editus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a high tannin content. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is occasionally cultivated for culinary use.
