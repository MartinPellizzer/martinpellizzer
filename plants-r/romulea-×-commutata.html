Romulea × commutata

Romulea × commutata, commonly known as the hybrid marsh bulb, is a perennial geophyte native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous corms and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained soil.
