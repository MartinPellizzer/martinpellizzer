Ranunculus geraniifolius var. carinthiacus

Ranunculus geraniifolius var. carinthiacus, commonly known as the Carinthian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
