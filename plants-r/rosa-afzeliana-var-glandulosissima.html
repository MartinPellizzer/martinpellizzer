Rosa afzeliana var. glandulosissima

Rosa afzeliana var. glandulosissima, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Thailand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The leaves are pinnate, consisting of 5 to 9 leaflets, and are covered with fine, glandular hairs that give the plant its common name. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
