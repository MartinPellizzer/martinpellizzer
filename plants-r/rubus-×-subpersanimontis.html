Rubus × subpersanimontis

Rubus × subpersanimontis, commonly known as the alpine blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant's leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
