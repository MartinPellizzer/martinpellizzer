Rosa helvetica

Rosa helvetica, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa helvetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
