Ranunculus suprasericeus

Ranunculus suprasericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a contrasting greenish tinge. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
