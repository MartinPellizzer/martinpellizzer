Rumex × gamsii

Rumex × gamsii, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Polygonaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex acetobulus (bog sorrel), resulting in a plant with distinctive botanical features. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a wavy margin and a reddish or purple coloration on the veins, which is a characteristic of the genus Rumex. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an example of hybridization in the genus Rumex.
