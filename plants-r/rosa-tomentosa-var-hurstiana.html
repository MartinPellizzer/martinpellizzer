Rosa tomentosa var. hurstiana

Rosa tomentosa var. hurstiana, commonly known as the Hurst's woolly rose, is a shrub-like species native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate foliage and flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
