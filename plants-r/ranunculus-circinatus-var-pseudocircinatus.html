Ranunculus circinatus var. pseudocircinatus

Ranunculus circinatus var. pseudocircinatus, commonly known as the spiral buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, and its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
