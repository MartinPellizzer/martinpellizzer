Rosa californica var. glabra

Rosa californica var. glabra, commonly known as the California wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces fragrant, single or double flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. Its stems are often armed with straight, hooked prickles, and it produces rose hips that are red to orange in color.

This species is well adapted to dry climates and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
