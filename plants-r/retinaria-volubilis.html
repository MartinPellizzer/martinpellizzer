Retinaria volubilis

Retinaria volubilis, commonly known as the climbing starflower, is a unique plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, star-shaped flowers. The plant features small, delicate leaves that are opposite in arrangement, often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. Retinaria volubilis thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental yet low-maintenance plants.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to shaded or semi-shaded areas.
