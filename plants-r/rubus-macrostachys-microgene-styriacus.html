Rubus macrostachys microgene styriacus

Rubus macrostachys microgene styriacus, commonly known as the Styrian raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its erect or sprawling growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in clusters. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The Styrian raspberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its edible fruits in some regions.
