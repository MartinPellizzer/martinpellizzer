Ranunculus lanuginosus var. umbrosus

Ranunculus lanuginosus var. umbrosus, commonly known as the woolly buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of soft, hairy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
