Rubus × semistylosus

Rubus × semistylosus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
