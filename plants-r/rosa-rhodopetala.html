Rosa rhodopetala

Rosa rhodopetala, commonly known as the Carpathian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems with thorny shoots and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. Its botanical name reflects its red-petaled appearance, though the flowers are usually a lighter shade.

Rosa rhodopetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
