Rubus idaeus f. maximowiczii

Rubus idaeus f. maximowiczii, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry characterized by its distinctive red fruit.

It is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, native to eastern North America. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets, which are typically bright red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have serrated edges, while its stems are covered in stiff, prickly bracts.

This cultivar is valued for its flavor and is widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.
