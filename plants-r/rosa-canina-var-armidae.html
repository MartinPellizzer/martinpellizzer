Rosa canina var. armidae

Rosa canina var. armidae, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. The plant features thorny stems and pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are pinnate with serrated edges, and it produces red, hairy fruits known as hips.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
