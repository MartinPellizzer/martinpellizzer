Rubia javana

Rubia javana, commonly known as Java madder, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its roots contain a red dye called rubia, which has been historically used in textile dyeing.

Rubia javana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
