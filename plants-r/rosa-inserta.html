Rosa inserta

Rosa inserta, commonly known as the inserted rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine hairs. Its stems are thorny, and the plant is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

Rosa inserta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
