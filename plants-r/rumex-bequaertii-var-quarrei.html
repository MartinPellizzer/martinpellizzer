Rumex bequaertii var. quarrei

Rumex bequaertii var. quarrei, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada dock, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on erect, branched stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
