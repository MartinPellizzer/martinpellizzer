Ranunculus auricomus subsp. baeckii

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

baeckii, commonly known as the Baeck's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It is characterized by its subsp. baeckii distinction, which includes smaller, more compact flowers compared to the typical subspecies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
