Rubus × semisueviacus

Rubus × semisueviacus, commonly known as the purple-leafed blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are typically small to medium in size. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves that often exhibit a striking purple hue during the growing season. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
