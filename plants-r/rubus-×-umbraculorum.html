Rubus × umbraculorum

Rubus × umbraculorum, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of both Rubus and other related species, typically featuring dark green, leathery leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species that demonstrates genetic diversity within the Rubus genus.
