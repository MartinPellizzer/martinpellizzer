Rubus glaucellus var. peracutidens

Rubus glaucellus var. peracutidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
