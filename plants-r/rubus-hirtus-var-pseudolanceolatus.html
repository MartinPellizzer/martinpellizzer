Rubus hirtus var. pseudolanceolatus

Rubus hirtus var. pseudolanceolatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family.
