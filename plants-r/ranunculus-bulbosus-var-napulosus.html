Ranunculus bulbosus var. napulosus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. napulosus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant bears yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
