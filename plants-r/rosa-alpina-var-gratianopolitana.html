Rosa alpina var. gratianopolitana

Rosa alpina var. gratianopolitana, commonly known as the Alpine rose or Gratianopolitan rose, is a shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thorny.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
