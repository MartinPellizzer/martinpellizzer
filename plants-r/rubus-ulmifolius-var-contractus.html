Rubus ulmifolius var. contractus

Rubus ulmifolius var. contractus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a narrow, elliptical shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
