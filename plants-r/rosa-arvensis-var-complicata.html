Rosa arvensis var. complicata

Rosa arvensis var. complicata, commonly known as the meadow rose or creeping rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or semi-prostrate growth habit, with stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 elliptical leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Rosa arvensis var. complicata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks.
