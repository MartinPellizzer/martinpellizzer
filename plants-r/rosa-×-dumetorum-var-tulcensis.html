Rosa × dumetorum var. tulcensis

Rosa × dumetorum var. tulcensis, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
