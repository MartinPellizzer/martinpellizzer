Rhododendron porphyranthes

Rhododendron porphyranthes, commonly known as the purple-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a striking purple hue, especially in shaded environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
