Ruellia longifolia

Ruellia longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ruellia longifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
