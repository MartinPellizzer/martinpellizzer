Rinorea deflexa

Rinorea deflexa, commonly known as the hanging bell vine, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Rinorea deflexa is a climbing plant that can grow up to several meters in length, using its tendrils to support itself.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
