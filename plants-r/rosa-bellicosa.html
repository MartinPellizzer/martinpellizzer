Rosa bellicosa

Rosa bellicosa, commonly known as the war rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its botanical name reflects its historical association with warfare, as it was used in the production of red dye for military uniforms.

Rosa bellicosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
