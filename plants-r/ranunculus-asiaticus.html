Ranunculus asiaticus

Ranunculus asiaticus, commonly known as the Asian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features include a hairy stem and a basal rosette of leaves.
