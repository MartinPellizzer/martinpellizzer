Rubus cuspidiferus microgene densus

Rubus cuspidiferus microgene densus, commonly known as the Allegheny blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
