Rhodanthemum pseudocatananche

Rhodanthemum pseudocatananche, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
