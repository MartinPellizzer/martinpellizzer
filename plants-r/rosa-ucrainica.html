Rosa ucrainica

Rosa ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian rose, is a species native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Rosa ucrainica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Rosaceae family.
