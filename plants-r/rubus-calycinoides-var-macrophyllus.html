Rubus calycinoides var. macrophyllus

Rubus calycinoides var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its notably large, broad leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its stems are armed with thorns, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in local ecosystems.
