Rubus × semifimbrifolius

Rubus × semifimbrifolius, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex genetic makeup resulting from crossbreeding between different Rubus species.
