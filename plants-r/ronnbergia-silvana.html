Ronnbergia silvana

Ronnbergia silvana, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a pseudobulbous herb, with broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment it thrives in.

Ronnbergia silvana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
