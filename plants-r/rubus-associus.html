Rubus associus

Rubus associus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to western North America, particularly found in coastal regions of Oregon and California. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal habitats.
