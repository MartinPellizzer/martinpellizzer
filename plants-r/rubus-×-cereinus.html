Rubus × cereinus

Rubus × cereinus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten raw or used in culinary applications. It is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its evergreen foliage and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for wildlife.
