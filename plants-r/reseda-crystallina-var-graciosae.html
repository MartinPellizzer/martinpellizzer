Reseda crystallina var. graciosae

Reseda crystallina var. graciosae, commonly known as the slender reseda, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, airy growth habit.
