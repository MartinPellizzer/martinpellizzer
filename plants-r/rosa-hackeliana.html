Rosa hackeliana

Rosa hackeliana, commonly known as the Hackel's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include five-petaled blooms and an upright growth habit.

Rosa hackeliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
