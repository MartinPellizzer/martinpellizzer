Rosa woronowii

Rosa woronowii, commonly known as the Siberian rose or the Korean rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including parts of Russia, Korea, and China. The plant is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and large, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that contain seeds.

Rosa woronowii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
