Rhinactinidia popovii

Rhinactinidia popovii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Eastern Siberia.

It features a low-growing stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of cold-climate gardening.
