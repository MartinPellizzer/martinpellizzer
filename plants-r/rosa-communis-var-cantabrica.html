Rosa communis var. cantabrica

Rosa communis var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian rose, is a variety of the common rose native to the coastal regions of northern Spain.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of fragrant, pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing semi-double to double petals. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with thorny stems typical of the rose genus.

This variety is well adapted to maritime climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
