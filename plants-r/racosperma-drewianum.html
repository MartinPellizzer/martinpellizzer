Racosperma drewianum

Racosperma drewianum, commonly known as the Waratah, is a striking flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy red or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features compound leaves with a feathery appearance, and its flowers are often clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

The Waratah is also noted for its ornamental value and is a symbol of New South Wales.
