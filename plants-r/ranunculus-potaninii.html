Ranunculus potaninii

Ranunculus potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to seven petals, often accompanied by numerous sepals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ranunculus potaninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
