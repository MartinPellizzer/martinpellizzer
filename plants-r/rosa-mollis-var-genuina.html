Rosa mollis var. genuina

Rosa mollis var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It is a deciduous species, losing its leaves in the winter months.

Rosa mollis var. genuina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and hardy nature.
