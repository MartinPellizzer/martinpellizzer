Rosa dinota var. adaugens

Rosa dinota var. adaugens, commonly known as the Adaugens rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are compound and serrated. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. It is well adapted to high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
