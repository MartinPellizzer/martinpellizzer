Rubus × hemioxyanchus

Rubus × hemioxyanchus, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical traits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a deep purple or black color.

This hybrid is valued for its vigorous growth and flavorful, juicy berries, making it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and commercial settings.
