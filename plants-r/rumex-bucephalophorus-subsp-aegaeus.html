Rumex bucephalophorus subsp. aegaeus

Rumex bucephalophorus subsp.

aegaeus, commonly known as the Aegaeus dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family. It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are arranged in inflorescences called scorpioid cymes. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
