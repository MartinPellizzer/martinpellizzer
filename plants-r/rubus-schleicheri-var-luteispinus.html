Rubus schleicheri var. luteispinus

Rubus schleicheri var. luteispinus, commonly known as the yellow-bristled blackberry, is a shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
