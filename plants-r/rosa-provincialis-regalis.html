Rosa provincialis regalis

Rosa provincialis regalis, commonly known as the Royal Provincial Rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its botanical features include showy, single or semi-double blooms and a preference for well-drained soil.

It is native to specific regions in Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
