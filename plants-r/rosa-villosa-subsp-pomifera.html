Rosa villosa subsp. pomifera

Rosa villosa subsp.

pomifera, commonly known as the woolly rose or the apple rose, is a species of shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and fuzzy, grayish-green leaves.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically in spring. Its fruit is a large, fuzzy, apple-like berry that ripens in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
