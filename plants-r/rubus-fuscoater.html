Rubus fuscoater

Rubus fuscoater, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in woodland or hedgerow environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface.

The flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and the plant is known for its edible fruit, though it is generally less cultivated than other Rubus species.
