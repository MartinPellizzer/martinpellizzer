Rumex pulcher f. micropliodus

Rumex pulcher f. micropliodus, commonly known as the dwarf sorrel, is a compact variant of the sorrel plant characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features a low-growing habit with reddish stems and produces small, greenish flowers in spring. Its leaves are edible and have a tart flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid.

This form is often cultivated as a ornamental and culinary plant in gardens.
