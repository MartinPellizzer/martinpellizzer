Rubus allegheniensis var. neoscoticus

Rubus allegheniensis var. neoscoticus, commonly known as the Allegheny blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
