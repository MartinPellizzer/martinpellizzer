Ribes fasciculatum

Ribes fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
