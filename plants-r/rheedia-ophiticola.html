Rheedia ophiticola

Rheedia ophiticola, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Rheedia ophiticola is known for its starchy rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
