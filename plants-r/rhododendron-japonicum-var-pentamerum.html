Rhododendron japonicum var. pentamerum

Rhododendron japonicum var. pentamerum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
