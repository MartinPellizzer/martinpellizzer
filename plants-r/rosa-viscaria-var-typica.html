Rosa viscaria var. typica

Rosa viscaria var. typica, commonly known as the sticky rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are often covered in a sticky, resinous substance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is generally hardy in temperate climates.

Rosa viscaria var. typica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
