Rhynchospora tenuis var. antillana

Rhynchospora tenuis var. antillana, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swampy areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct midrib, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures.

This variety is native to the Caribbean and parts of South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments.
