Rosa collincola

Rosa collincola, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.

Rosa collincola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
