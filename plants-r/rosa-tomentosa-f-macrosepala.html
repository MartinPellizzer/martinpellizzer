Rosa tomentosa f. macrosepala

Rosa tomentosa f. macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled hairy rose, is a compact shrub native to China.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and produces large, showy pink flowers with prominent, broad sepals. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

The f. macrosepala form is distinguished by its notably larger sepals compared to the typical variety.
