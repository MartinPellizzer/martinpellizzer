Rubus castrensis

Rubus castrensis, commonly known as the Castren's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
