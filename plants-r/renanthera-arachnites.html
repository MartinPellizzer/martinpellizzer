Renanthera arachnites

Renanthera arachnites, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure with long, slender petals that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Renanthera arachnites is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
