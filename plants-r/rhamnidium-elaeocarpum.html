Rhamnidium elaeocarpum

Rhamnidium elaeocarpum, commonly known as the African sand apple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are yellowish-green and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and traditional medicine.
