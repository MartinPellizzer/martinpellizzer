Rubus canadensis var. imus

Rubus canadensis var. imus, commonly known as the Canada blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
