Roxburghia gloriosa

Roxburghia gloriosa, commonly known as the golden wax tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. Roxburghia gloriosa thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
