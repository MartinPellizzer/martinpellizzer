Rosa canina f. rossii

Rosa canina f. rossii, commonly known as the Rossii rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy hips that are rich in vitamin C. It is native to Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, typical of many rose species.
