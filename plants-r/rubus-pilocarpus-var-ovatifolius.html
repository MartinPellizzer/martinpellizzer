Rubus pilocarpus var. ovatifolius

Rubus pilocarpus var. ovatifolius, commonly known as the ovate-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive ovate-shaped leaves.

It is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
