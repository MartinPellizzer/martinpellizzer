Ranunculus incompletus

Ranunculus incompletus, commonly known as the incomplete buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It features delicate, spurred petals that range in color from yellow to orange, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers.
