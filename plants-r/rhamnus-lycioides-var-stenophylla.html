Rhamnus lycioides var. stenophylla

Rhamnus lycioides var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark purple berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
