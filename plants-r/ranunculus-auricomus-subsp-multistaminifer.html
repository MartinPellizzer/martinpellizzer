Ranunculus auricomus subsp. multistaminifer

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

multistaminifer, commonly known as the many-stamen buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features showy yellow flowers with five to nine sepals that resemble petals, and its leaves are deeply divided into lobes.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, green fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The subspecies is distinguished by its numerous stamens, which contribute to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
