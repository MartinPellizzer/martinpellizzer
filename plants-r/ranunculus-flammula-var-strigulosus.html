Ranunculus flammula var. strigulosus

Ranunculus flammula var. strigulosus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has a hairy stem and leaves that are trifoliate, with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
