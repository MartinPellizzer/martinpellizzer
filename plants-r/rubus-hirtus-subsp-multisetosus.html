Rubus hirtus subsp. multisetosus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

multisetosus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the summer. Its berries are edible but often have a tart flavor and are used in local cuisines.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and woodland areas.
