Rosa viscaria var. suecica

Rosa viscaria var. suecica, commonly known as the Swedish rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to northern Europe.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, pinnate, and covered in fine, velvety hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
