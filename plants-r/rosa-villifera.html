Rosa villifera

Rosa villifera, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, with small, glandular hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

Rosa villifera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
