Rhodocactus portulacifolius

Rhodocactus portulacifolius, commonly known as the desert rose or Mexican cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that are often reduced to spines, a feature common in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
