Raphanus sativus var. niger

Raphanus sativus var. niger, commonly known as black radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple or black exterior and a slightly spicy, pungent flavor. The plant produces a large, swollen root that serves as the primary edible part. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its root, which is rich in nutrients.

The leaves are also edible and can be used in various culinary applications.
