Ranunculus miliarakesii

Ranunculus miliarakesii, commonly known as the Greek buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

However, it is considered rare and is protected in its natural habitat due to habitat loss and over-collection.
