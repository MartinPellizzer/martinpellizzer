Ranunculus vertumnaliformis

Ranunculus vertumnaliformis, commonly known as the vertumnaliform buttercup, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or white flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
