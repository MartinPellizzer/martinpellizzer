Rubus pictorum

Rubus pictorum, commonly known as the painted raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often red or orange fruits that are typically smaller than those of other raspberry species. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
