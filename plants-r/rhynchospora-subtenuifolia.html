Rhynchospora subtenuifolia

Rhynchospora subtenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf beakspur, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in wetland or marshy environments. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Rhynchospora subtenuifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
