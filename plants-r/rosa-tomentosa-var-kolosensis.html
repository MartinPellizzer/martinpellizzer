Rosa tomentosa var. kolosensis

Rosa tomentosa var. kolosensis, commonly known as the Kolosinskii rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets covered in a fine, white down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
