Rosa kokanica

Rosa kokanica, commonly known as the Kosovo rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Rosa kokanica is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens across Europe.
