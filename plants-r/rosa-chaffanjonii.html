Rosa chaffanjonii

Rosa chaffanjonii, commonly known as the Chaffanjon rose, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa chaffanjonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
