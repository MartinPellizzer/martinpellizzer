Restrepia dayana

Restrepia dayana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant produces a single, elongated inflorescence with numerous flowers, each having a pouch-like structure that attracts pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Restrepia dayana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
