Rosa suavis var. gmelinii

Rosa suavis var. gmelinii, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a fragrant shrub native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its scent is often described as sweet and delicate, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Rosa suavis var. gmelinii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
