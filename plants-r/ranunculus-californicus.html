Ranunculus californicus

Ranunculus californicus, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along streambanks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a waxy coating.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
