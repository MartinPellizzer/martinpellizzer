Rubus danicus var. intricatus

Rubus danicus var. intricatus, commonly known as the intricate bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, though they may be tart or acidic. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the plant thrives in woodland or hedgerow environments.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
