Rubus angustifolius

Rubus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits known as blackberries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rubus angustifolius is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
