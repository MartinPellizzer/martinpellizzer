Ribes aureum var. tenuiflorum

Ribes aureum var. tenuiflorum, commonly known as golden currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellowish-green leaves that are oval and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat, attracting pollinators and providing food for birds.
