Rubus × acer

Rubus × acer, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry hybrid, is a member of the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Rubus × acer is widely cultivated for its edible fruits and is also valued in ornamental horticulture.
