Rubus pyramidalis var. arrondeauanus

Rubus pyramidalis var. arrondeauanus, commonly known as the Arrondeau raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color, with a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
