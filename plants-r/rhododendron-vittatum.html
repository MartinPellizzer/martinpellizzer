Rhododendron vittatum

Rhododendron vittatum, commonly known as the spotted rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves with distinctive white or silver spots along the veins. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
