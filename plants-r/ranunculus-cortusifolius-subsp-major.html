Ranunculus cortusifolius subsp. major

Ranunculus cortusifolius subsp.

major, commonly known as the large-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic texture.
