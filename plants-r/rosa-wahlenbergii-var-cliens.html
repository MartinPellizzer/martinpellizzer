Rosa wahlenbergii var. cliens

Rosa wahlenbergii var. cliens, commonly known as the Wahlenberg's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces red, hairy hips that persist into winter.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
