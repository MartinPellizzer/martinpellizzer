Rubus assurgens f. eliasii

Rubus assurgens f. eliasii, commonly known as the Elias' blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This subspecies is valued for its flavorful berries and is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
