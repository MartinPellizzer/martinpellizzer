Rhynchosia resinosa var. schliebenii

Rhynchosia resinosa var. schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's hairy bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed habitats and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
