Rubus fuscus proles mucronulatus

Rubus fuscus proles mucronulatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible, though often tart and less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and it blooms with white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
