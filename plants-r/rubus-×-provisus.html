Rubus × provisus

Rubus × provisus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in coastal and Mediterranean climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. It produces small, edible black berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
