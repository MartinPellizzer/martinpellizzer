Rubus afrotropicus

Rubus afrotropicus, commonly known as the African bramble, is a shrub native to various regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forest edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine.
