Retama monosperma var. hipponensis

Retama monosperma var. hipponensis, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The fruit is a pod that contains a single seed, typical of the Fabaceae family.
