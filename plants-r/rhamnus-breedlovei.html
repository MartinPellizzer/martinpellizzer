Rhamnus breedlovei

Rhamnus breedlovei, commonly known as the Texas buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southcentral United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and smooth, thornless stems. The plant produces small, black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in sandy or clay soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Rhamnus breedlovei is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.
