Rosa × bibracteata

Rosa × bibracteata, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its continuous flowering throughout the growing season, which sets it apart from many other rose varieties. The plant typically features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa × bibracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in various climates.
