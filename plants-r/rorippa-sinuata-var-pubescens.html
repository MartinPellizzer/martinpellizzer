Rorippa sinuata var. pubescens

Rorippa sinuata var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent watercress, is a flowering plant species native to moist environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, pubescent hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shallow water or damp soil, often found near streams, ditches, and wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
