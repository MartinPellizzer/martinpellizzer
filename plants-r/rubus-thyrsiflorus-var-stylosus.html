Rubus thyrsiflorus var. stylosus

Rubus thyrsiflorus var. stylosus, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple foliage and clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small, tart, and slightly acidic. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive fall coloration and fragrant blossoms.
