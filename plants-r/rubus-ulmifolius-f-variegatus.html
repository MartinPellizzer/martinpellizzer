Rubus ulmifolius f. variegatus

Rubus ulmifolius f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated bramble, is a variant of the common bramble, characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

This plant is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in woodland and hedgerow habitats. Its leaves are usually three-lobed with a silvery or white variegation that sets it apart from the standard species. The flowers are pale pink and appear in clusters, followed by red, edible berries in the autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and can be found in gardens and natural areas.
