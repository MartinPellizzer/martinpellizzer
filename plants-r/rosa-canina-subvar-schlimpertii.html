Rosa canina subvar. schlimpertii

Rosa canina subvar. schlimpertii, commonly known as the Schlimper's dog rose, is a subspecies native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets, and the plant produces red-orange hips in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
