Ranunculus jingpoensis

Ranunculus jingpoensis, commonly known as the Jingpo buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and brightly colored, often yellow or white, flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
