Rhabdadenia macrostoma

Rhabdadenia macrostoma, commonly known as the giant barrel cactus, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height, which are covered in spines for protection. The plant stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces yellow or orange flowers during the rainy season, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.

The species is adapted to extreme conditions, making it a unique example of desert flora.
