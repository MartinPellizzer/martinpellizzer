Rubus penhallowensis

Rubus penhallowensis, commonly known as the Penhallow raspberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
