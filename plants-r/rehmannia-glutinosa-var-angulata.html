Rehmannia glutinosa var. angulata

Rehmannia glutinosa var. angulata, commonly known as the twisted rehmannia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, twisted stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional Chinese medicine, valued for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of this plant make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
