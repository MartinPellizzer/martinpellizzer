Rosa obtusifolia var. sclerophylla

Rosa obtusifolia var. sclerophylla, commonly known as the Mediterranean rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, leathery leaves that are adapted to dry conditions, a characteristic reflected in its varietal name "sclerophylla.". The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically covered in thorns, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
