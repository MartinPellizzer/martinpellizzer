Rubia akane var. erecta

Rubia akane var. erecta, commonly known as the red madder plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant's most notable botanical feature is its ability to yield a deep red dye from its roots, which has been historically used in textile industries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rubia akane var. erecta is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
