Rubus omalus var. albanensis

Rubus omalus var. albanensis, commonly known as the Albanian blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus omalus species native to the Balkan region.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally significant species in its native habitat.
