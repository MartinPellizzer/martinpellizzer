Rubus schummelii

Rubus schummelii, commonly known as Schumacher's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the stems are covered in stinging hairs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
