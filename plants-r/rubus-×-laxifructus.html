Rubus × laxifructus

Rubus × laxifructus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, known for its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to parts of Asia and has been introduced to various regions around the world. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
