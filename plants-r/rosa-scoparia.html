Rosa scoparia

Rosa scoparia, commonly known as the creeping rose or field rose, is a shrub-like perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa scoparia is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas.
