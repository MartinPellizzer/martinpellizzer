Ribes uva-crispa var. hortensis

Ribes uva-crispa var. hortensis, commonly known as the European gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically serrated and hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, tart berries that turn red or orange when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in jams and wines for centuries.
