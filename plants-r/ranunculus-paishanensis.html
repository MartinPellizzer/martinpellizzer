Ranunculus paishanensis

Ranunculus paishanensis, commonly known as the Paishan buttercup, is a rare flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow petals and a cluster of sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
