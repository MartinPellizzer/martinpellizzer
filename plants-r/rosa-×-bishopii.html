Rosa × bishopii

Rosa × bishopii, commonly known as the Bishop's rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, deep pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
