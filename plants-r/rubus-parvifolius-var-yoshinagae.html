Rubus parvifolius var. yoshinagae

Rubus parvifolius var. yoshinagae, commonly known as Yoshinaga's evergreen bramble, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early summer, followed by red, edible berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Japanese gardens.
