Rhodormis rosifolia

Rhodormis rosifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, rose-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade. Its botanical features include parallel venation in the leaves and a complex flower structure with three sepals and three petals.

Rhodormis rosifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable environment.
