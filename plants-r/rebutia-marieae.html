Rebutia marieae

Rebutia marieae, commonly known as the Marieae Rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, cylindrical stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Rebutia marieae is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
