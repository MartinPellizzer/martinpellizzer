Rubus rivularis var. biserratus

Rubus rivularis var. biserratus, commonly known as the riverberry or twin-serrated blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
