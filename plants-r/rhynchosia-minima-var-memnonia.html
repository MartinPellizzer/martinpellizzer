Rhynchosia minima var. memnonia

Rhynchosia minima var. memnonia, commonly known as the red bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds that are often used as a legume in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
