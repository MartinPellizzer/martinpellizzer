Rubus vestitus f. pullus

Rubus vestitus f. pullus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry.

This plant is a flowering shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. Its leaves are typically serrated and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The flowers are white to pale pink, and the fruits, though not typically eaten by humans, are important for wildlife.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
