Rhamnus alaternus f. obovata

Rhamnus alaternus f. obovata, commonly known as the smooth buckthorn, is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves.

The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.
