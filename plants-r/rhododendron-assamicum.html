Rhododendron assamicum

Rhododendron assamicum, commonly known as the Assam rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often blooming in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Rhododendron assamicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
