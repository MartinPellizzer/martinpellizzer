Rubus obtruncatus microgene vallivagus

Rubus obtruncatus microgene vallivagus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
