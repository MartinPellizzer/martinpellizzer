Rhus glaucescens var. macrocarpa

Rhus glaucescens var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins, typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are characteristic of the genus Rhus. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age, and it has a shallow root system adapted to well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
