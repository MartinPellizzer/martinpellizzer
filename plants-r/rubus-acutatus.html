Rubus acutatus

Rubus acutatus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
