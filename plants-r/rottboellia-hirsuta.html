Rottboellia hirsuta

Rottboellia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy rottbolli, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has erect, often branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rottboellia hirsuta is often found in disturbed areas and can be invasive in some regions.
