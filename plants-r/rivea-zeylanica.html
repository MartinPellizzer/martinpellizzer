Rivea zeylanica

Rivea zeylanica, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, opening in the morning and closing at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Rivea zeylanica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
