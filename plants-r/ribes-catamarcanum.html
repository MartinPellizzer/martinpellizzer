Ribes catamarcanum

Ribes catamarcanum, commonly known as the Catamarca gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various shrubs and vines commonly found in the Americas. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant flowers in early spring.
