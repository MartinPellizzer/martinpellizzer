Rosa mollis subf. lindstorpensis

Rosa mollis subf.

lindstorpensis, commonly known as the Lindstorp's sweetbriar, is a subsp. of the sweetbriar rose native to southern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and rocky soils, preferring partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with oval-shaped leaflets that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces red-orange hips in the fall, which are rich in vitamin C and attract various wildlife.
