Rhamnus alaternus var. laurifolia

Rhamnus alaternus var. laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel privet, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens and parks.
