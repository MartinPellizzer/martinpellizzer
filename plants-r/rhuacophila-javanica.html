Rhuacophila javanica

Rhuacophila javanica, commonly known as the Javanese rock orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments on tree trunks and rocky surfaces. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
