Rubus axilliflorens

Rubus axilliflorens, commonly known as the axillary-flowered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural research.
