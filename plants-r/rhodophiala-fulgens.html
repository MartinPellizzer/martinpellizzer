Rhodophiala fulgens

Rhodophiala fulgens, commonly known as the golden flame lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are often adorned with a contrasting yellow or white throat. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

Rhodophiala fulgens is prized in horticulture for its bold, eye-catching appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
