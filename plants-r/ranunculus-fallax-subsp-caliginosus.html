Ranunculus fallax subsp. caliginosus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

caliginosus, commonly known as the clouded buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly darker, more subdued flower color compared to other ranunculus species.
