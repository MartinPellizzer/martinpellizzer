Ranunculus uncinatus var. earlei

Ranunculus uncinatus var. earlei, commonly known as the Earlei bentleaf buttercup, is a variant of the bentleaf buttercup native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a prominent central pistil. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
