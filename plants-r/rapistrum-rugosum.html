Rapistrum rugosum

Rapistrum rugosum, commonly known as the rough shepherd's purse, is an annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by triangular, seed-filled pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
