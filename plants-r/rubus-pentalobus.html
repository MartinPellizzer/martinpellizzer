Rubus pentalobus

Rubus pentalobus, commonly known as the five-lobed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with five to seven lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed areas, and is often found in the Pacific Northwest.
