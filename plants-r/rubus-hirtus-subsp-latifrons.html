Rubus hirtus subsp. latifrons

Rubus hirtus subsp.

latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers are pink to white and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
