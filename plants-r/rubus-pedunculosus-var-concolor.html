Rubus pedunculosus var. concolor

Rubus pedunculosus var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupelets that are typically eaten fresh or used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
