Ranunculus holmii

Ranunculus holmii, commonly known as the Holm's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ranunculus holmii is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
