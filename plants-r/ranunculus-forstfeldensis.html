Ranunculus forstfeldensis

Ranunculus forstfeldensis, commonly known as the Forstfeld's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have numerous sepals that resemble petals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
