Rosa canina var. hispidovillosiuscula

Rosa canina var. hispidovillosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy bristly dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its dense, bristly hairs on the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is typically found in woodland edges and disturbed soils across Europe.
