Rosa × oblonga

Rosa × oblonga, commonly known as the oval rose, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa palustris.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Rosa × oblonga is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
