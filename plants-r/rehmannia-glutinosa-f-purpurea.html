Rehmannia glutinosa f. purpurea

Rehmannia glutinosa f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-rooted rehmannia, is a variant of the Rehmannia glutinosa species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purplish roots, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white or pale purple hues.

The plant's botanical features make it valuable for both medicinal and ornamental purposes.
