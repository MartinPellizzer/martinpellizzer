Rhynchospora pseudomariscus

Rhynchospora pseudomariscus, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets. Its flowers are generally wind-pollinated, a common trait among sedges.

Rhynchospora pseudomariscus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
