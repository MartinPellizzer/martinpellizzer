Rosa mollis subf. elatiorifrons

Rosa mollis subf.

elatiorifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved rose, is a subspecies of the Rosa mollis species, characterized by its large, ovate leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. It is native to regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in temperate climates.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and fragrant blooms. Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
