Rhodoleia stenopetala

Rhodoleia stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled rhodoleia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the family Rhodoleiaceae and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, and the species is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Rhodoleia stenopetala is also of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
