Rosa arvensis var. nuda

Rosa arvensis var. nuda, commonly known as the field rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces oval, greenish-yellow fruits called rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
