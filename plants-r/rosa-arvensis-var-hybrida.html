Rosa arvensis var. hybrida

Rosa arvensis var. hybrida, commonly known as the field rose hybrid, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange-red hips. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
