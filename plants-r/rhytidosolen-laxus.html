Rhytidosolen laxus

Rhytidosolen laxus, commonly known as the soft-stemmed rhubarb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are typically covered in a soft, woolly layer of hair. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
