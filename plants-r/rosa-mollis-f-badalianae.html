Rosa mollis f. badalianae

Rosa mollis f. badalianae, commonly known as the Badaliana rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and delicate pink to white flowers. This form is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of China and Myanmar. It thrives in cool, humid climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
