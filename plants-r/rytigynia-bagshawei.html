Rytigynia bagshawei

Rytigynia bagshawei, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and has some traditional medicinal uses.
