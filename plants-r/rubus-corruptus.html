Rubus corruptus

Rubus corruptus, commonly known as the common blackberry or trailing blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature into dark purple to black berries. These berries are edible and often used in jams and wines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
