Rubus abietinus

Rubus abietinus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their tart flavor in local cuisines.
