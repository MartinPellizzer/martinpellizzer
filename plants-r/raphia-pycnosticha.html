Raphia pycnosticha

Raphia pycnosticha, commonly known as the dense-leaf palm, is a species of large, fan-shaped palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, slender trunk and large, compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The leaves are dark green and often have a waxy texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in its native habitat. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional practices in some African cultures.
