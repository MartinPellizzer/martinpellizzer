Rhododendron × geraldii

Rhododendron × geraldii, commonly known as the Geraldii rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron ferrugineum and Rhododendron caucasicum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and is often found in forested areas with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
