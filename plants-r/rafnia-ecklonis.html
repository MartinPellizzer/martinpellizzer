Rafnia ecklonis

Rafnia ecklonis, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. Rafnia ecklonis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
