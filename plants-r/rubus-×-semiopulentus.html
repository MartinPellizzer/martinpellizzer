Rubus × semiopulentus

Rubus × semiopulentus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves that provide a lush canopy. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

Its botanical features make it a hardy shrub suitable for coastal and rocky environments.
