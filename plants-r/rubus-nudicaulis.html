Rubus nudicaulis

Rubus nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stem blackberry, is a small shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
