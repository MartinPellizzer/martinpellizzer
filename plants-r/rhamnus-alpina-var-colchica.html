Rhamnus alpina var. colchica

Rhamnus alpina var. colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
