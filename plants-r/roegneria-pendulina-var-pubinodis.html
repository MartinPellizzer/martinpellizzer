Roegneria pendulina var. pubinodis

Roegneria pendulina var. pubinodis, commonly known as the hairy pendulous grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its drooping, feathery inflorescences.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the stems and nodes. Its flowers are small and spike-like, arranged in a loose panicle structure.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, flowing appearance.
