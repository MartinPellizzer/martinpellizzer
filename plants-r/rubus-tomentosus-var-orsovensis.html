Rubus tomentosus var. orsovensis

Rubus tomentosus var. orsovensis, commonly known as the Orsovenian bramble, is a shrub native to specific regions in Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, hairy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for local ecosystems and traditional herbal uses.
