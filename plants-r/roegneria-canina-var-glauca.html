Roegneria canina var. glauca

Roegneria canina var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its blue-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-rowed florets.

Roegneria canina var. glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
