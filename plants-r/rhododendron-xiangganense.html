Rhododendron xiangganense

Rhododendron xiangganense, commonly known as the Xianggan rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly in the Sichuan and Yunnan provinces.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often densely covered with fine hairs, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes many ornamental and ecologically important plants.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
