Rubus banaticus var. viridescens

Rubus banaticus var. viridescens, commonly known as the green-leaved blackberry.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Europe, characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated edge and a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.
