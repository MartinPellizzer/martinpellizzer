Ranunculus henslowii

Ranunculus henslowii, commonly known as the Henslow's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to nine petals, typically in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Ranunculus henslowii is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its vibrant blooms and ecological importance to pollinators.
