Racosperma anasillum

Racosperma anasillum, commonly known as the blue wrens bush, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a striking blue-green color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma anasillum is a member of the legume family and has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
