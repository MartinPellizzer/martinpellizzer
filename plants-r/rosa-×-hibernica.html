Rosa × hibernica

Rosa × hibernica, commonly known as the Irish rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Rosa × hibernica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
