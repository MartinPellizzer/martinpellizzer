Ranunculus dissectus subsp. huetii

Ranunculus dissectus subsp.

huetii, commonly known as the Huet's divided buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout rhizome and hairy stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its refined appearance.
