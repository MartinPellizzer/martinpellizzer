Rosa alpina f. inermis

Rosa alpina f. inermis, commonly known as the Alpine rose or the alpine pink, is a botanical variant of the alpine rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe, including the Alps and Carpathians. This plant is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa alpina f. inermis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
