Rhododendron tenuilaminare

Rhododendron tenuilaminare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its aesthetic appeal.
