Riedlea cordata

Riedlea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf riedlea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Riedlea cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
