Rubus semibracteosus

Rubus semibracteosus, commonly known as the small-bracted blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Rubus semibracteosus is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.
