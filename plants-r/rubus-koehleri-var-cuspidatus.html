Rubus koehleri var. cuspidatus

Rubus koehleri var. cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a pointed tip, giving it its specific epithet "cuspidatus.".

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for its edible fruits.
