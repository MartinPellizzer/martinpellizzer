Rhododendron bonvalotii

Rhododendron bonvalotii, commonly known as the Bonvalot's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Rhododendron bonvalotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
