Rubus ehrnsbergeri

Rubus ehrnsbergeri, commonly known as the Ehrnsberger bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that develop from its flowers. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered plant.
