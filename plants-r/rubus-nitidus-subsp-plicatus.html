Rubus nitidus subsp. plicatus

Rubus nitidus subsp.

plicatus, commonly known as the wrinkled blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible berries, making it a popular choice for both cultivation and wild habitats.
