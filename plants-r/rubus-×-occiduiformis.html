Rubus × occiduiformis

Rubus × occiduiformis, commonly known as the western blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubus genus, which includes various brambles and raspberries. This species is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
