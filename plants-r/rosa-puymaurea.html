Rosa puymaurea

Rosa puymaurea, commonly known as the Peruvian rose, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa puymaurea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
