Ranunculus songaricus

Ranunculus songaricus, commonly known as the Songarica buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has a hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
