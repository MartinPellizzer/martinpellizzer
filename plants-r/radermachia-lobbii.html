Radermachia lobbii

Radermachia lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's rock fig, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Radermachia lobbii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
