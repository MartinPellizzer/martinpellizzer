Ranunculus auricomus subsp. valdesii

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

valdesii, commonly known as the Valdes' buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petal-like sepals, which are often mistaken for true petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodlands, and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and narrower leaves compared to the typical form of R. auricomus.
