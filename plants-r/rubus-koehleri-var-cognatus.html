Rubus koehleri var. cognatus

Rubus koehleri var. cognatus, commonly known as the Hawaiian raspberry, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with new shoots emerging each year. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
