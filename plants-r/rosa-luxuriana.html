Rosa luxuriana

Rosa luxuriana, commonly known as the lush rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that are oval-shaped and serrated. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Rosa luxuriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
