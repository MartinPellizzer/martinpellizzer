Ranunculus gracilistylus

Ranunculus gracilistylus, commonly known as the slender-styled buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in alpine meadows and forest understories. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its slender, elongated style, which is a distinguishing feature in its floral morphology.
