Ranunculus fallax subsp. amaurophanes

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

amaurophanes, commonly known as the dark-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often adorned with a distinctive black or purple spot at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
