Rhododendron viscosum f. rhodanthum

Rhododendron viscosum f. rhodanthum, commonly known as the sticky alpine rhododendron, is a horticultural form of a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give its leaves a sticky texture, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in rocky, acidic soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish-purple and bloom in early spring.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
