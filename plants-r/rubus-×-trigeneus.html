Rubus × trigeneus

Rubus × trigeneus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green leaves, and clusters of small, edible black berries. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal regions. Its berries are typically harvested in late summer and are valued for their tart flavor.

Botanically, it exhibits characteristics of both Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis, making it a notable example of natural hybridization.
