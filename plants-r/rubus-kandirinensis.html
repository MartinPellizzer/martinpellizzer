Rubus kandirinensis

Rubus kandirinensis, commonly known as the Kandirin raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
