Rhododendron dilatatum subsp. decandrum

Rhododendron dilatatum subsp.

decandrum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is often found in mountainous regions, such as the Sierra Nevada and Cascade ranges.

The subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in horticulture.
