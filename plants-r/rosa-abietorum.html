Rosa abietorum

Rosa abietorum, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in alpine meadows and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a flowering period that usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Rosa abietorum is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
