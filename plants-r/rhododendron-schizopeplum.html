Rhododendron schizopeplum

Rhododendron schizopeplum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a dark green upper surface. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron schizopeplum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
