Racosperma wickhamii var. cassiterum

Racosperma wickhamii var. cassiterum, commonly known as the Wickham's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale green, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
