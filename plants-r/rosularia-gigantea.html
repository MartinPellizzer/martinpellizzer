Rosularia gigantea

Rosularia gigantea, commonly known as the giant stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in a cup-like structure. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Rosularia gigantea is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
