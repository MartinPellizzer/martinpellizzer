Rhododendron rosmarinifolium var. album

Rhododendron rosmarinifolium var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered azalea, is a variant of the azalea species characterized by its white blooms.

It is a deciduous shrub native to China, typically growing in forested areas with acidic soil. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, clustered flowers in the spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

The white coloration of its flowers is a distinguishing feature compared to the more common pink and red varieties.
