Rubus lohrei

Rubus lohrei, commonly known as the Lohr's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the蔷薇科 (Rosaceae) family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in the provinces of Sichuan and Yunnan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries in the summer.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it has attracted interest among botanists and horticulturists.
