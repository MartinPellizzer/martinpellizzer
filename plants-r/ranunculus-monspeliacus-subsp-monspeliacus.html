Ranunculus monspeliacus subsp. monspeliacus

Ranunculus monspeliacus subsp.

monspeliacus, commonly known as the Italian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals.

The plant has a short, stout stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
