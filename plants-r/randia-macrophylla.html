Randia macrophylla

Randia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Randia macrophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
