Rudbeckia bellidioides

Rudbeckia bellidioides, commonly known as the bellidium coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy yellow to orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk, with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Rudbeckia bellidioides is often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides.
