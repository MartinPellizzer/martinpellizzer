Rubus wushanensis

Rubus wushanensis, commonly known as the Wushan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible berries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus family. Its small, dark berries are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
