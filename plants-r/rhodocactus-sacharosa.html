Rhodocactus sacharosa

Rhodocactus sacharosa, commonly known as the sugar cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in a thick, waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the dry season, which are typically pollinated by nocturnal insects. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores and reducing transpiration.

Rhodocactus sacharosa is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, storing water in its stems and exhibiting a slow growth rate.
