Rosa latesecta

Rosa latesecta, commonly known as the late-sected rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring deeply lobed leaves that are more pronounced than those of most other rose species. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after addition to horticultural collections.
