Rubus minutiflorus

Rubus minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf flowered blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States, characterized by its small, delicate flowers and thorny stems. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
