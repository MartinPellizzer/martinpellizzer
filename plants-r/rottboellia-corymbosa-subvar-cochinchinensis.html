Rottboellia corymbosa subvar. cochinchinensis

Rottboellia corymbosa subvar. cochinchinensis, commonly known as red rice grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, reddish-brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways.

This species is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a weed and a potential ornamental plant.
