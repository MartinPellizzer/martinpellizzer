Rosa pineticola

Rosa pineticola, commonly known as the coastal rose, is a species native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, woody stems that are often covered in a dense layer of grayish-white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa pineticola is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
