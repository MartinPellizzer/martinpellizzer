Rottboellia foliosa

Rottboellia foliosa, commonly known as the grass of glory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Rottboellia foliosa is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.
