Rubus × chaerophyllopsis

Rubus × chaerophyllopsis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry-loganberry.

This plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (the loganberry), resulting in a hybrid with characteristics of both parents. It is a thorny, deciduous shrub that produces large, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in temperate climates.

It is valued for its flavorful fruits, which are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
