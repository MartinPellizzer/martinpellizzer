Rosa × collina var. foetida

Rosa × collina var. foetida, commonly known as the stinking rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant is notable for its strong, sometimes unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced in the flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to rocky or coastal environments.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
