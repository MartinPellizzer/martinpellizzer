Ranunculus cornutus var. cornutus

Ranunculus cornutus var. cornutus, commonly known as the horned water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, aquatic or wetland habitats, such as ponds, ditches, and stream margins. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in shallow water or along the edges of water bodies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable water conditions.
