Rumex turcomanicus

Rumex turcomanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and wet meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
