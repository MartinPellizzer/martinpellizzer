Rhaptonema densiflora

Rhaptonema densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered rhaptonema, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

Rhaptonema densiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
