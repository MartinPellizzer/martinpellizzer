Rubus viadrinus

Rubus viadrinus, commonly known as the Viadrin raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
