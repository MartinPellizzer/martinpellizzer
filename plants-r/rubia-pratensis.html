Rubia pratensis

Rubia pratensis, commonly known as field madder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems and opposite leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Field madder is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.

It is known for its historical use in dyeing textiles and as a source of medicinal compounds.
