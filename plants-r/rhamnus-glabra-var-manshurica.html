Rhamnus glabra var. manshurica

Rhamnus glabra var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in forested areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features small, oval leaves that are smooth on both sides, and it produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that ripens in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
