Rosa × gracilis

Rosa × gracilis, commonly known as the shrub rose or slender shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and is native to the eastern United States. The plant typically produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa × gracilis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
