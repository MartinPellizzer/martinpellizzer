Ranunculus acris var. nipponicus

Ranunculus acris var. nipponicus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and is often found in moist meadows and woodland areas. It is characterized by its short stature and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family, sharing similar botanical traits.
