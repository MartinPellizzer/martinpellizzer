Racosperma lineolatum subsp. multilineatum

Racosperma lineolatum subsp.

multilineatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and have a distinctive linear or wavy pattern along the leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
