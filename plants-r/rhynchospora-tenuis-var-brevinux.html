Rhynchospora tenuis var. brevinux

Rhynchospora tenuis var. brevinux, commonly known as the narrowleaf marsh sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. The plant produces spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems, with each spikelet containing several tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it plays a role in maintaining ecosystem stability.

This variety is often found in the southeastern United States and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding and high moisture levels.
