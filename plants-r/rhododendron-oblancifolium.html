Rhododendron oblancifolium

Rhododendron oblancifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
