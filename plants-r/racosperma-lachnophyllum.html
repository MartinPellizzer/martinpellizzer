Racosperma lachnophyllum

Racosperma lachnophyllum, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has finely hairy foliage, giving it a soft, silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Racosperma lachnophyllum is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
