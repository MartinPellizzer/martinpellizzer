Rivea corymbosa var. paniculata

Rivea corymbosa var. paniculata, commonly known as the clustered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically red or orange with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

This species thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.
