Rosa communis var. latibractea

Rosa communis var. latibractea, commonly known as the broad-bract rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It features large, showy flowers with broad, overlapping bracts that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.
