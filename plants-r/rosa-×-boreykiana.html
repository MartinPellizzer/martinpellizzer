Rosa × boreykiana

Rosa × boreykiana, commonly known as the Siberian shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thorny stems that form shrubs up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged in odd-pinnate patterns, with serrated leaflets.

Rosa × boreykiana is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
