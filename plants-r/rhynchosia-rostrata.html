Rhynchosia rostrata

Rhynchosia rostrata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and distinctive, elongated pods that contain edible seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Rhynchosia rostrata is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in various parts of the world.
