Rubus rabenaui

Rubus rabenaui, commonly known as the Kauai raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Hawaiian island of Kauai.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas within native forests. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and hairy.

This species is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to the island's ecosystem.
