Rumex pulcher subsp. anodontus

Rumex pulcher subsp.

anodontus, commonly known as the smooth dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and marshy areas. Its leaves are smooth and glossy, with a reddish tinge on the underside, and it produces capsules that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its astringent properties.
