Rosa uriensis var. adenophora

Rosa uriensis var. adenophora, commonly known as the腺花蔷薇, is a variant of the rose species native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique botanical appearance. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white. Its foliage is usually dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the delicate blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
