Rubus saxatilis var. americanus

Rubus saxatilis var. americanus, commonly known as the American blackberry.

It is a shrub native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

The berries are edible, though they are generally less sweet and more tart compared to other blackberry varieties.
