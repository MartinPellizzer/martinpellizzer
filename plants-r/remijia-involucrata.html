Remijia involucrata

Remijia involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Remijia involucrata is known for its rapid growth and is considered a weed in some regions.
