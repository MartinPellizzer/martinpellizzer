Rosa montana var. typica

Rosa montana var. typica, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems that support clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer. Rosa montana var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
