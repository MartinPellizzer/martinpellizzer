Romulea ramiflora var. parlatorei

Romulea ramiflora var. parlatorei, commonly known as the Parlatore's star-of-Bethlehem, is a member of the Iridaceae family.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly southern France and northern Italy. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
