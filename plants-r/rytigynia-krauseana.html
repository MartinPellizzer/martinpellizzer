Rytigynia krauseana

Rytigynia krauseana, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rytigynia krauseana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
