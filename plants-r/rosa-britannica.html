Rosa britannica

Rosa britannica, commonly known as the British rose, is a species native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Rosa britannica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in British horticulture.
