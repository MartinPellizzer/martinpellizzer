Rubus hedycarpus subsp. megacladus

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

megacladus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its large, thornless canes and dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

The subspecies is notable for its robust growth and potential use in both ornamental and culinary applications.
