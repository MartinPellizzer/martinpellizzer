Rubus gratiflorus

Rubus gratiflorus, commonly known as the sweetflower blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often edible, though they can be quite tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, adding a delicate aesthetic to its natural habitat.
