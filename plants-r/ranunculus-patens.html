Ranunculus patens

Ranunculus patens, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in temperate regions.
