Rosa trachyphylla var. typica

Rosa trachyphylla var. typica, commonly known as the roughleaf rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, rough-textured leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. It produces red-orange hips that are both ornamental and edible.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
