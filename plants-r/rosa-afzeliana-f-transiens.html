Rosa afzeliana f. transiens

Rosa afzeliana f. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Rosa or the Variable Rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that exhibit a range of colors, from pink to white. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many rose species. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
