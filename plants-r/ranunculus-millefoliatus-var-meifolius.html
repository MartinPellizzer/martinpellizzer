Ranunculus millefoliatus var. meifolius

Ranunculus millefoliatus var. meifolius, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow petals.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring partial to full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The variety meifolius is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form.
