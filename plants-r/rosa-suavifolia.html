Rosa suavifolia

Rosa suavifolia, commonly known as the sweet-scented rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its botanical features include simple, pinnate leaves and recurved petals that give it a distinctive appearance.

Rosa suavifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.
