Rubus × hirsutipes

Rubus × hirsutipes, commonly known as the bramble raspberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
