Rubus brachyadenes

Rubus brachyadenes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark red fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and the fruit is often used in local culinary traditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
