Rubus × informis

Rubus × informis, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to various regions of Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter taste.
