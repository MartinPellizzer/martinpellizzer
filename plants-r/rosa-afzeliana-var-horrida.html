Rosa afzeliana var. horrida

Rosa afzeliana var. horrida, commonly known as the horrid rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, often prickly foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its hardy nature.

Rosa afzeliana var. horrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in various climates.
