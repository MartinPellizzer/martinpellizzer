Rosa tomurensis

Rosa tomurensis, commonly known as the Tomur rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Tibet.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine soils. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa tomurensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
