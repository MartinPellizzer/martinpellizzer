Rubus furvus subsp. rosellus

Rubus furvus subsp.

rosellus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ecological contexts.
