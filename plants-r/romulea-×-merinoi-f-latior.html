Romulea × merinoi f. latior

Romulea × merinoi f. latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved romulea, is a hybrid plant within the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is native to southern Africa and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This cultivar is favored for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
