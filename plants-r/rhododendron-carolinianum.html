Rhododendron carolinianum

Rhododendron carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to reddish-purple flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical with smooth edges and turn yellow in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
