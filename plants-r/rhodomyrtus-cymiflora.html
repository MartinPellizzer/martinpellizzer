Rhodomyrtus cymiflora

Rhodomyrtus cymiflora, commonly known as the fragrant rose myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Rhodomyrtus cymiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
