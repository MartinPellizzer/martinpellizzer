Rodgersia tabularis

Rodgersia tabularis, commonly known as the broadleaf rodgersia, is a perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white, semi-double flowers in late summer. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Rodgersia tabularis is valued for its ornamental foliage and long bloom period.
