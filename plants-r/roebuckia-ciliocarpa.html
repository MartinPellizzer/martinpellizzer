Roebuckia ciliocarpa

Roebuckia ciliocarpa, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small, deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a thick, woody stem and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive ciliate margin, contributing to its botanical uniqueness.

Roebuckia ciliocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
