Ranunculus acris proles nemorivagus

Ranunculus acris proles nemorivagus, commonly known as the woodland buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental value in gardens.
