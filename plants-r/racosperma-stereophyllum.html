Racosperma stereophyllum

Racosperma stereophyllum, commonly known as the rock fig, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or pale yellow in color. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
