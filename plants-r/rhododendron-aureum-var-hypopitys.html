Rhododendron aureum var. hypopitys

Rhododendron aureum var. hypopitys, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green and densely packed on short stems. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and compact growth habit.
