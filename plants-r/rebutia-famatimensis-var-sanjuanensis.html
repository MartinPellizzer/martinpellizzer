Rebutia famatimensis var. sanjuanensis

Rebutia famatimensis var. sanjuanensis, commonly known as the San Juan rebutia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It features a compact, rounded caudex with short, cylindrical stems and sparsely arranged, fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
