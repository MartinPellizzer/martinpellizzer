Rubus teretiusculus var. tomentellus

Rubus teretiusculus var. tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a dense, woolly underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
