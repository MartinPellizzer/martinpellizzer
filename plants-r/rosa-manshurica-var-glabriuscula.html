Rosa manshurica var. glabriuscula

Rosa manshurica var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the Manchurian rose, is a shrub-like species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often covered with thorns, and the plant is adapted to temperate climates with well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature.
