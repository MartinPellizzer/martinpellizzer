Rosa subalpina

Rosa subalpina, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Rosa subalpina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
