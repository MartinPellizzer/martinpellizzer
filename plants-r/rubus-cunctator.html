Rubus cunctator

Rubus cunctator, commonly known as the delayed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is closely related to other brambles such as Rubus allegheniensis.
