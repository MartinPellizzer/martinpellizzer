Rhynchosia nyasica

Rhynchosia nyasica, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers and cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climatic conditions.

Rhynchosia nyasica is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
