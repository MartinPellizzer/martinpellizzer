Rosa reuteri f. hispidior

Rosa reuteri f. hispidior, commonly known as the hispid or hairy rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale pink, often with a slight blush of red. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
