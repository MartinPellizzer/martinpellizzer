Rosa pomifera f. gombensis

Rosa pomifera f. gombensis, commonly known as the Gombesian rose, is a unique horticultural form of the pomiferous rose.

It is characterized by its large, showy fruit, which can grow up to 10 centimeters in diameter, resembling a fuzzy orange or red berry. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and is prized for its ornamental value. It produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically in early summer.

Due to its rarity and specific cultivation requirements, it is a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
