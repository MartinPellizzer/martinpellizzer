Rubus distractus

Rubus distractus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark berries. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruits are typically small and tart, often used in local foraging practices.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible fruits and thorny stems.
