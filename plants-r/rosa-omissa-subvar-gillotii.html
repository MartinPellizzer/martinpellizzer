Rosa omissa subvar. gillotii

Rosa omissa subvar. gillotii, commonly known as the Gillot's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne singly or in clusters. It is well adapted to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
