Rumex pulcher

Rumex pulcher, commonly known as the beautiful sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, arrow-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Rumex pulcher thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, making it a valued plant in some culinary traditions.
