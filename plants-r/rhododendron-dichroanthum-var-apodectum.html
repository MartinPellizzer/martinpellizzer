Rhododendron dichroanthum var. apodectum

Rhododendron dichroanthum var. apodectum, commonly known as the Himalayan azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and India.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive two-toned appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural care.
