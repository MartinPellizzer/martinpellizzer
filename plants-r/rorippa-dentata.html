Rorippa dentata

Rorippa dentata, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, oval leaves and is typically found in wet or aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a typical feature of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in shallow water, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Rorippa dentata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
