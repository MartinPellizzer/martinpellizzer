Rudgea hospes

Rudgea hospes, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a spherical, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, often containing several seeds.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
