Rubus milliformis subsp. commixtus

Rubus milliformis subsp.

commixtus, commonly known as the mixed-form bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically serrated and arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
