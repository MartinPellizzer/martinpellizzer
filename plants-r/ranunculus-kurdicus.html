Ranunculus kurdicus

Ranunculus kurdicus, commonly known as the Kurdish buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

Ranunculus kurdicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
