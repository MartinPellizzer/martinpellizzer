Rhigiocarya nervosa

Rhigiocarya nervosa, commonly known as the veined rhigiocarya, is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive veined leaves.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves with prominent longitudinal veins, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are small and clustered, often forming dense inflorescences that bloom in early summer.

Rhigiocarya nervosa is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local insect populations.
