Rumex acetosa subsp. biformis

Rumex acetosa subsp.

biformis, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, alternate leaves that are often reddish or purple underneath.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its rhubarb-like leaves have been used historically for medicinal purposes.
