Rosa agrestis var. pseudomentita

Rosa agrestis var. pseudomentita, commonly known as the creeping wild rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. The plant features slender, arching stems that often lie close to the ground, with small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are red and hairy when mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
