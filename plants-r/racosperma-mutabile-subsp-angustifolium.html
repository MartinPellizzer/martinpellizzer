Racosperma mutabile subsp. angustifolium

Racosperma mutabile subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native flora in its habitat.
