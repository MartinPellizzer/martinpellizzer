Rubus ulmifolius microgene cruentiflorus

Rubus ulmifolius microgene cruentiflorus, commonly known as the crimson flowered bramble, is a variant of the common bramble, characterized by its distinctive red flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, known for its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called berries, which are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
