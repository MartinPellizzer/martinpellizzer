Ranunculus rufulus

Ranunculus rufulus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Ranunculus rufulus is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
