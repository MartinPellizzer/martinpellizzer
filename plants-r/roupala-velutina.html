Roupala velutina

Roupala velutina, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Roupala velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
