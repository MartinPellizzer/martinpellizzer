Rubus rudis var. platycephalus

Rubus rudis var. platycephalus, commonly known as the broad-headed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its robust, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible. This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
