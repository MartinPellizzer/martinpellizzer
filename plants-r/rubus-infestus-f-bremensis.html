Rubus infestus f. bremensis

Rubus infestus f. bremensis, commonly known as the Bremens raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and use in hedgerows.
