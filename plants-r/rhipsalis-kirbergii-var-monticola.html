Rhipsalis kirbergii var. monticola

Rhipsalis kirbergii var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain rhipsalis, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has slender, segmented stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
