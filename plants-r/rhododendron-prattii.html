Rhododendron prattii

Rhododendron prattii, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a waxy coating.

Rhododendron prattii prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
