Rotala filiformis

Rotala filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width, arranged in whorls along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, with staminate and pistillate flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Rotala filiformis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
