Rubus serpens var. calliphyllus

Rubus serpens var. calliphyllus, commonly known as the red-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tinged leaves and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in shrub form, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces edible fruits that are popular in local cuisines.

The botanical features of this variant make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
