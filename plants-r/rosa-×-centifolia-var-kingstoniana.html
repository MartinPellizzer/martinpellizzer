Rosa × centifolia var. kingstoniana

Rosa × centifolia var. kingstoniana, commonly known as the Kingstoniana rose, is a fragrant, hybrid tea rose prized for its abundant blooms.

It features semi-double to full petals that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson, depending on the cultivar. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It produces edible hips that can be used in culinary applications, and its flowers are often used in perfumery.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticultural traditions.
