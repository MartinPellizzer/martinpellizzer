Rhododendron pingianum

Rhododendron pingianum, commonly known as the Pichincha rhododendron, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of Ecuador.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in cloud forests. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing dense foliage that helps it thrive in cool, moist conditions.

Rhododendron pingianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
