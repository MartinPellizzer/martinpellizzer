Ranunculus millefolius subsp. hierosolymitanus

Ranunculus millefolius subsp.

hierosolymitanus, commonly known as the Jerusalem crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas. It has a short growing season and blooms in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
