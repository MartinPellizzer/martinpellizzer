Rubus × hirtellus

Rubus × hirtellus, commonly known as the Hirtell's blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many brambles. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
