Rubus pistillatus

Rubus pistillatus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinged berries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus pistillatus is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
