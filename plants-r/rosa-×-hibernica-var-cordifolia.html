Rosa × hibernica var. cordifolia

Rosa × hibernica var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are distinctive, with a heart-shaped outline and a deep green color, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, thriving in a range of soil types and climates.

It is commonly used in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators.
