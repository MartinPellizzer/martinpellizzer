Rapanea crassifolia

Rapanea crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by reddish-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its foliage is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate partial shade in cultivation.
