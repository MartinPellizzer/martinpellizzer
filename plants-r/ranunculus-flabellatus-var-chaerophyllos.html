Ranunculus flabellatus var. chaerophyllos

Ranunculus flabellatus var. chaerophyllos, commonly known as the chaerophyllos buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent, rounded centers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
