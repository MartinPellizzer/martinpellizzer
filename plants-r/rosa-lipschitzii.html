Rosa lipschitzii

Rosa lipschitzii, commonly known as the Lipschitz rose, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a subtle scent. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, and the stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa lipschitzii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
