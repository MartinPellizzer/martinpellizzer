Ranunculus egens

Ranunculus egens, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features a short, stout stem with trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five to eight yellow petals and numerous yellow stamens, creating a striking appearance.

Ranunculus egens is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is often found at high altitudes.
