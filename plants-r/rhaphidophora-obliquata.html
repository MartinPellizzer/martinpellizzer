Rhaphidophora obliquata

Rhaphidophora obliquata, commonly known as the zigzag monstera, is a climbing tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed, and often variegated leaves. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and climb effectively. Its leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture and can grow quite large, reaching up to 1 meter in length.

Rhaphidophora obliquata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
