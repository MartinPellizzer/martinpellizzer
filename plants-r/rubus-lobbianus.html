Rubus lobbianus

Rubus lobbianus, commonly known as the European bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. Its berries are edible but often have a tart flavor, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Rubus lobbianus plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
