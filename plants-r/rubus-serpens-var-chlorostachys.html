Rubus serpens var. chlorostachys

Rubus serpens var. chlorostachys, commonly known as the green-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive green stems and leaves, which set it apart from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its contribution to local ecosystems.
