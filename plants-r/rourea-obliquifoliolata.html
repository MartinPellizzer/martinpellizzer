Rourea obliquifoliolata

Rourea obliquifoliolata, commonly known as the zigzag rourea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant typically grows as an understory species in moist, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are often arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
