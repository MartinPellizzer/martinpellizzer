Rosa nemorivaga

Rosa nemorivaga, commonly known as the woodland rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features arching stems with narrow, dark green leaves that have serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in early summer.

Rosa nemorivaga is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
