Rubus villicaulis microgene valdeproximus

Rubus villicaulis microgene valdeproximus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry shrub, is a species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its small, thorny stems and dense clusters of small, dark berries.

It is native to temperate regions of the Americas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant exhibits a shrub-like growth habit with trifoliate leaves and produces flowers that transition into edible fruits. Its botanical features include a complex root system and a tendency to form dense thickets, making it a resilient and adaptive species.

It is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
