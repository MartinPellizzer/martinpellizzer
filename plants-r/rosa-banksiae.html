Rosa banksiae

Rosa banksiae, commonly known as the Banks' rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa banksiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
