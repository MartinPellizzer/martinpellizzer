Rafnia elliptica var. acuminata

Rafnia elliptica var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are the primary part used, valued for their starchy texture and culinary applications.

The species is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes in warm climates.
