Rheedia madruno subsp. bituberculata

Rheedia madruno subsp.

bituberculata, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rheedia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

This subspecies is notable for its unique tuberculate structures on the stem, which contribute to its scientific name.
