Rudbeckia serotina f. gigantea

Rudbeckia serotina f. gigantea, commonly known as the giant late black-eyed Susan, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features large, showy yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a dark brown disk in the center, giving it a bold, sunflower-like appearance. The plant typically grows to heights of 3 to 5 feet, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
