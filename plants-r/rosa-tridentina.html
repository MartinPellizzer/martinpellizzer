Rosa tridentina

Rosa tridentina, commonly known as the three-pronged rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Caucasus and the western Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of three to five leaflets, and its roots are deep and fibrous.

Rosa tridentina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
