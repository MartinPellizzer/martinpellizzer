Royena macrocalyx

Royena macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered royal vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five broad petals and a prominent, elongated calyx. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.
