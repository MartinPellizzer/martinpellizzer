Rubus schleicheri microgene irrufatus

Rubus schleicheri microgene irrufatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

This plant is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. The flowers are white to pale pink, clustered in umbels, and give way to small, dark purple berries.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
