Rosa jundzillii f. alsatica

Rosa jundzillii f. alsatica, commonly known as the Alsatian rose, is a botanical hybrid that exhibits unique characteristics compared to its parent species.

It is a compact, shrub-like plant with densely packed, semi-evergreen foliage that retains its leaves throughout the winter in mild climates. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

It is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and hardiness in temperate regions.
