Rosa coriifolia subvar. oblonga

Rosa coriifolia subvar. oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has ovate to oblong leaves with a slightly serrated margin and a hairy underside. It produces red-orange hips in the fall, which are edible and contain seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
