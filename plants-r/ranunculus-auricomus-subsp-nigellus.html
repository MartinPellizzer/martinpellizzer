Ranunculus auricomus subsp. nigellus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

nigellus, commonly known as the black-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive, deeply divided leaf structure.

The subspecies is characterized by its dark green, often slightly hairy leaves, which contrast with its bright yellow blooms. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
