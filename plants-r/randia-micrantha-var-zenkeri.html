Randia micrantha var. zenkeri

Randia micrantha var. zenkeri, commonly known as the African star fruit or star apple, is a tropical shrub native to Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, star-shaped berries. The fruit is typically yellow to orange when ripe and has a mildly acidic taste.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit in warm climates.
