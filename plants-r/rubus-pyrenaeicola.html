Rubus pyrenaeicola

Rubus pyrenaeicola, commonly known as the Pyrenean bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the Pyrenees mountain range, where it thrives in rocky and woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen in the summer. It plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing food for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered plant.
