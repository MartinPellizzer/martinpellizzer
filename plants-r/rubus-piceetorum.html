Rubus piceetorum

Rubus piceetorum, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces edible berries that are widely appreciated for their flavor.

Rubus piceetorum plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
