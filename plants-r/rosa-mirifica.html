Rosa mirifica

Rosa mirifica, commonly known as the miraculous rose, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle bi-color pattern. The plant features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
