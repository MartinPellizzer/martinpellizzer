Rosa mollissima f. coerulea

Rosa mollissima f. coerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered form of the soft-hairy rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and its striking blue-purplish flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant is a shrub with multiple stems and is typically found in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
