Rhexia setosa

Rhexia setosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or orange throat. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Rhexia setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
