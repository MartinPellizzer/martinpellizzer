Rosa montana var. punctillata

Rosa montana var. punctillata, commonly known as the spotted rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, arching stems that bear clusters of fragrant, pink or red flowers with a distinctive spotted or blotched appearance. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces ornamental hips that persist through winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its unique floral pattern and hardy nature contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
