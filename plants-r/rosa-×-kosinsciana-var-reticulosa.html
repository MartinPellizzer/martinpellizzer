Rosa × kosinsciana var. reticulosa

Rosa × kosinsciana var. reticulosa, commonly known as the Reticulate Rosa, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its intricate, lace-like foliage.

It originates from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × sinensis, resulting in a plant with robust growth and cold hardiness. The leaves are typically deeply lobed and have a textured, reticulate appearance, giving the plant its distinctive name. It produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late summer, and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

This variety is also noted for its disease resistance and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
