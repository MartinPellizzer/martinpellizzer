Rosa coriifolia var. glabrescens

Rosa coriifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth, glabrous surface, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
