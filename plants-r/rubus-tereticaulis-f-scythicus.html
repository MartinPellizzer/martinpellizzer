Rubus tereticaulis f. scythicus

Rubus tereticaulis f. scythicus, commonly known as the Scythian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
