Ribes inerme var. klamathense

Ribes inerme var. klamathense, commonly known as Klamath currant, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and moist woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
