Rumex iseriensis

Rumex iseriensis, commonly known as the Iseri sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their characteristic arrow-shaped leaves. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tint along the veins, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.
