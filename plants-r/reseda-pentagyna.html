Reseda pentagyna

Reseda pentagyna, commonly known as the five-angled reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, ribbed leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Reseda pentagyna is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
