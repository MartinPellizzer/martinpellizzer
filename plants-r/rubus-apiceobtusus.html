Rubus apiceobtusus

Rubus apiceobtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves with a rounded apex and serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The plant has a woody stem with thorns and is part of the Rosaceae family.
