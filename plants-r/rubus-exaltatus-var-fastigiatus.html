Rubus exaltatus var. fastigiatus

Rubus exaltatus var. fastigiatus, commonly known as the upright blackberry bush, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its upright growth habit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
