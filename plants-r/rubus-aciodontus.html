Rubus aciodontus

Rubus aciodontus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are usually silvery-white underneath, giving it its common name.

The silverleaf blackberry is adapted to moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.
