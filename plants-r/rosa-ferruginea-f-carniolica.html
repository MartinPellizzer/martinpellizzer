Rosa ferruginea f. carniolica

Rosa ferruginea f. carniolica, commonly known as the Carniolan rusted rose, is a botanical variant of the rugosa rose.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
