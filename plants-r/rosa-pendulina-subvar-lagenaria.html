Rosa pendulina subvar. lagenaria

Rosa pendulina subvar. lagenaria, commonly known as the hanging rose or the balloon rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that often trail from the branches, giving it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces large, fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are hairy and serrated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its visual interest and fragrance.
