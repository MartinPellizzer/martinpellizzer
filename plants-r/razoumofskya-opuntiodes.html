Razoumofskya opuntiodes

Razoumofskya opuntiodes, commonly known as the desert rose or opuntia-like cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features flattened, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its adaptations include thick cuticles and succulent tissues to store water, enabling survival in harsh, dry environments.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in low-maintenance gardens.
