Rosa pimpinellifolia var. flavescens

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow woodsrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with thorny growth and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and pink. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens in temperate climates.
