Rubus anchostachys

Rubus anchostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
