Ranunculus mccallae

Ranunculus mccallae, commonly known as the Stewart's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, radially symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded areas such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually yellow with five petals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important species in ecological studies of New Zealand's flora.
