Ribes rubrum var. propinquum

Ribes rubrum var. propinquum, commonly known as the red gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that mature to a deep red color by late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
