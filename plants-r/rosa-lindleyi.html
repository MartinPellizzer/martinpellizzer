Rosa lindleyi

Rosa lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy achene that contains a single seed, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

Rosa lindleyi is also valued for its historical significance in botanical research and its role in local ecosystems.
