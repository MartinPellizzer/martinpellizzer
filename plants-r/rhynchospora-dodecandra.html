Rhynchospora dodecandra

Rhynchospora dodecandra, commonly known as the twelve-angled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in tropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist environments and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of lakes.

Rhynchospora dodecandra plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
