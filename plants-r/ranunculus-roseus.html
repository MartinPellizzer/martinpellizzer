Ranunculus roseus

Ranunculus roseus, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a yellow center. The plant has compound leaves divided into lobes, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is known for its short blooming period in spring and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Ranunculus roseus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
