Ronnbergia maidifolia

Ronnbergia maidifolia, commonly known as the banana orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, banana-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Ronnbergia maidifolia produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, making it a popular ornamental plant.
