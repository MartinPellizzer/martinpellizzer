Rubus brachythyrsoides

Rubus brachythyrsoides, commonly known as the narrow-thyrsoid raspberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its fruits, which are typically reddish or black when mature, are edible and used in local cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
