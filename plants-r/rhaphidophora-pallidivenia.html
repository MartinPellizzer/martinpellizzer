Rhaphidophora pallidivenia

Rhaphidophora pallidivenia, commonly known as the pale-veined arrowroot.

It is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with pale veins. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its rhizomatous growth habit. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive, glossy appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.
