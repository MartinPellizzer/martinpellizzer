Roegneria anthosachnoides var. scabrilemmata

Roegneria anthosachnoides var. scabrilemmata, commonly known as the rough-leaved wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a rough, scabrous texture on the upper surface. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed florets. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
