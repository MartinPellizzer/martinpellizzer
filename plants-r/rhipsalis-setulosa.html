Rhipsalis setulosa

Rhipsalis setulosa, commonly known as the hairy rhipsalis, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and slender, segmented stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the winter months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Rhipsalis setulosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
