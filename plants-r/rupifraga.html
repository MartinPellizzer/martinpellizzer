Rupifraga

Rupifraga, commonly known as the rock rose, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae.

These plants are typically found in rocky or alpine environments, where they thrive in well-drained soils. Rupifraga species are characterized by their upright growth habit and showy, often pink or white flowers. They are adapted to withstand harsh conditions, including cold temperatures and strong winds.

The genus includes several species, such as Rupifraga alpina and Rupifraga saxifragoides, which are valued for their ornamental appeal in gardens.
