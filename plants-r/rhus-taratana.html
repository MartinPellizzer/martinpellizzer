Rhus taratana

Rhus taratana, commonly known as the Hawaiian holly, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas of Hawaii. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in catkins, and it produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species.

Rhus taratana is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
