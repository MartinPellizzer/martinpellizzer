Reseda lutea f. flaccida

Reseda lutea f. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid yellowrocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in temperate regions. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes. It produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its reduced growth habit compared to the typical form of Reseda lutea.
