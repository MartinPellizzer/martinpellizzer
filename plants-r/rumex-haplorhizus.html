Rumex haplorhizus

Rumex haplorhizus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five stamens and a single pistil, typical of the dock genus. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Rumex haplorhizus is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
