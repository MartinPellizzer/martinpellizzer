Ribes bracteosum var. viridiflorum

Ribes bracteosum var. viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered gooseberry, is a variant of the shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are distinctive, featuring bright yellow petals and green sepals, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and often used in jams and jellies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas along stream banks.
