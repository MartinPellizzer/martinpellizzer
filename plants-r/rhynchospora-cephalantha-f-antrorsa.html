Rhynchospora cephalantha f. antrorsa

Rhynchospora cephalantha f. antrorsa, commonly known as the reverse-facing beak sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing tiny, pale green to brownish flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral orientation, with the inflorescence facing downward, giving it its common name.
