Rubus pirifolius var. permollis

Rubus pirifolius var. permollis, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a variant of the brambles family characterized by its dark, almost black berries.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in woodlands and disturbed soils. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and serrated. Its flowers are white and clustered, giving way to edible berries that are rich in antioxidants.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
