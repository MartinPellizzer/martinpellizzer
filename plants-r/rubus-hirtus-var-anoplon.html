Rubus hirtus var. anoplon

Rubus hirtus var. anoplon, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in home gardens and commercial settings.
