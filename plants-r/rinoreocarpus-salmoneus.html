Rinoreocarpus salmoneus

Rinoreocarpus salmoneus, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in local ecosystems.
