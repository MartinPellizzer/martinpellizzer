Rosa sepium var. typica

Rosa sepium var. typica, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in traditional Chinese gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Rosa sepium var. typica is also notable for its ability to thrive in colder climates, though it may require protection in harsh winters.
