Racosperma eriopodum

Racosperma eriopodum, commonly known as the woolly wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, woody stems. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Racosperma eriopodum is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.
