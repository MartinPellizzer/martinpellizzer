Rosa andrewsii debilis

Rosa andrewsii debilis, commonly known as the weak-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, deciduous shrub characterized by its slender stems and small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces rose-like blooms in the spring, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a serrated margin.

Rosa andrewsii debilis is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
