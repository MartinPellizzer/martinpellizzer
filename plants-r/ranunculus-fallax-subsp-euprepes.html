Ranunculus fallax subsp. euprepes

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

euprepes, commonly known as the Pacific coast buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It has a short stem and is often found in coastal regions from Oregon to California.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the cool, damp climates of its native habitat.
