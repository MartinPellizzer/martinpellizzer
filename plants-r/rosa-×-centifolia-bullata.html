Rosa × centifolia bullata

Rosa × centifolia bullata, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid species of the rose family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is known for its historical use in perfumery and medicine. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorns.

The flowers are often used in the production of rose water and essential oils.
