Ranunculus auricomus subsp. viadensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

viadensis, commonly known as the Viadensian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with 5-7 sepals that are often mistaken for petals, and it typically grows in moist, calcareous soils.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often found near streams or in marshy areas.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
