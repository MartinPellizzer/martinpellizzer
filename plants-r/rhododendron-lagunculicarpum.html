Rhododendron lagunculicarpum

Rhododendron lagunculicarpum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or forested areas. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
