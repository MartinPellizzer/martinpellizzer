Rhododendron wattii

Rhododendron wattii, commonly known as the Yunnan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded, humid environments. It is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of both horticultural interest and conservation concern.
