Rubia chinensis f. chinensis

Rubia chinensis f. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese rubia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine make it a notable species in botanical studies.
