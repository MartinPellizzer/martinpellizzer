Rumex aquaticus subsp. occidentalis

Rumex aquaticus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western water sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to western North America. It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in clusters, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, waterlogged soils.
