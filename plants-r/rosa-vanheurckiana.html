Rosa vanheurckiana

Rosa vanheurckiana, commonly known as the Vanheurck's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and stipules, and it is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Rosa vanheurckiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
