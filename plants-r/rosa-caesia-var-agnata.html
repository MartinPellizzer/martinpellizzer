Rosa caesia var. agnata

Rosa caesia var. agnata, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a compact shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features simple, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers with a white center. The plant is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
