Rudbeckia foliosa

Rudbeckia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of dark brown or purple tubular flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Rudbeckia foliosa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
