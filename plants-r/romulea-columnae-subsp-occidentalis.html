Romulea columnae subsp. occidentalis

Romulea columnae subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western columnar romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
