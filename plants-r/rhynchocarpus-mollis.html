Rhynchocarpus mollis

Rhynchocarpus mollis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to grow in poor soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Rhynchocarpus mollis is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary traditions.
