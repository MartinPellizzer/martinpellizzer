Rubus nemorosoides

Rubus nemorosoides, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in countries like China, Japan, and Korea. This shrub typically grows in rocky or forested areas at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
