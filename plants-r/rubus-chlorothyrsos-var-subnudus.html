Rubus chlorothyrsos var. subnudus

Rubus chlorothyrsos var. subnudus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features arching stems with thorns and trifoliate leaves that are usually hairy on the upper surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, dark purple berries.

The species is characterized by its relatively small size and distinctive leaf morphology compared to other Rubus species.
