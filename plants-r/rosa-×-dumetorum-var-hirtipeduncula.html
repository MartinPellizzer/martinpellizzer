Rosa × dumetorum var. hirtipeduncula

Rosa × dumetorum var. hirtipeduncula, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed dog rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and the flowers are often borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
