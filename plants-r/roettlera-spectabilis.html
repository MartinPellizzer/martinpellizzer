Roettlera spectabilis

Roettlera spectabilis, commonly known as the showy roettlera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Roettlera spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
