Raphanus

Raphanus, commonly known as the radish, is a root vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, conical root that is typically white or purple in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in a raceme, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually lobed. Raphanus is cultivated for its edible root and is often grown in cool seasons.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
