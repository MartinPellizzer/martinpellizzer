Ranunculus vernus

Ranunculus vernus, commonly known as the early buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and blooms in early spring. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have a distinct, cup-like structure.

Ranunculus vernus is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
