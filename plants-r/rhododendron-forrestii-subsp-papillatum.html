Rhododendron forrestii subsp. papillatum

Rhododendron forrestii subsp.

papillatum, commonly known as the Papillate Rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of Nepal and northern India. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded mountainous areas, preferring acidic, well-drained soils. Its subspecies status reflects subtle morphological differences from the main species, including distinctive papillae on the leaf surfaces.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
