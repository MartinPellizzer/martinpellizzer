Rosa arvensis corymbosa

Rosa arvensis corymbosa, commonly known as the field rose, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems covered in thorns and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically produced in corymbs, which are flat-topped or rounded clusters.

Rosa arvensis corymbosa is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
