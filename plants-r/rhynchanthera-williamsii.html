Rhynchanthera williamsii

Rhynchanthera williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor environments, which is common among orchids.

The species is relatively rare and requires specific ecological conditions to flourish.
