Racosperma brachybotryum

Racosperma brachybotryum, commonly known as the small-fruited acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its small, spherical fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
