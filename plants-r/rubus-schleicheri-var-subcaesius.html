Rubus schleicheri var. subcaesius

Rubus schleicheri var. subcaesius, commonly known as the dark raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black fruits, which are typically smaller than those of common raspberries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets, each containing a single seed. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit color.
