Rosa mollis subf. dilaxans

Rosa mollis subf.

dilaxans, commonly known as the soft rose or the lax rose, is a flowering shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which are often glandular and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its botanical features include pinnately compound leaves and hips that are typically red or orange in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of garden settings.
