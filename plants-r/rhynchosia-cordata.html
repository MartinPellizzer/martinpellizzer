Rhynchosia cordata

Rhynchosia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf snakeweed, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy, with a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

Rhynchosia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
