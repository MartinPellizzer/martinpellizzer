Rubus balticus

Rubus balticus, commonly known as the Baltic blackberry, is a species of brambles native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
