Rubus calycacanthus var. buergerifolius

Rubus calycacanthus var. buergerifolius, commonly known as the Buerger's bramble, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with serrated edges, and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant bears red or orange berries that are typically edible but can be tart. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
