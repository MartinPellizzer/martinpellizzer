Rubus fruticosus subsp. villicaulis

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

villicaulis, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupelets, that are often dark purple to black in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in a variety of soil types.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
