Retama raetam subsp. bovei

Retama raetam subsp.

bovei, commonly known as the Bove's whitethorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its spiny branches and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, leathery fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
