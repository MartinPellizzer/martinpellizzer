Radermachera lobbii subsp. acuminata

Radermachera lobbii subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the pointed-leaf radermachera, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features glossy, ovate to elliptical leaves with a pointed tip, often with a slightly wavy margin.

The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody, elongated fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
