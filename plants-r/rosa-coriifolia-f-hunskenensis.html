Rosa coriifolia f. hunskenensis

Rosa coriifolia f. hunskenensis, commonly known as the Hunsken's shrub rose, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and a dense, thorny structure typical of the Rosa genus. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is native to specific regions in Europe and is often cultivated for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic variation and has been the subject of taxonomic study due to its distinct morphological traits.
