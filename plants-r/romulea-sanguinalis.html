Romulea sanguinalis

Romulea sanguinalis, commonly known as the red-flowered romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, typically from March to May. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Romulea sanguinalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
