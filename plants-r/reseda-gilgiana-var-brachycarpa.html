Reseda gilgiana var. brachycarpa

Reseda gilgiana var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-seeded eryngo, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow or white flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its small, capsule-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
