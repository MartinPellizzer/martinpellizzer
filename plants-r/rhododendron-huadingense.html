Rhododendron huadingense

Rhododendron huadingense, commonly known as the Huading rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan Province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and cool temperatures. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
