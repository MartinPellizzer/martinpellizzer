Ranunculus latiapex

Ranunculus latiapex, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to nine petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
