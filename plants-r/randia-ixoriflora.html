Randia ixoriflora

Randia ixoriflora, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Randia ixoriflora thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
