Rosa pendulina var. laevis

Rosa pendulina var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth trailing rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is a deciduous shrub that often grows in a trailing or climbing habit, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
