Rosa canina f. spuria

Rosa canina f. spuria, commonly known as the wild dog rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often growing in woodland edges and open areas.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
