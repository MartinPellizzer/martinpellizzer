Rosa canina var. cantabrica

Rosa canina var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian rose, is a variety of the dog rose native to the western regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, hairy berries that are edible but often tart, containing seeds used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
