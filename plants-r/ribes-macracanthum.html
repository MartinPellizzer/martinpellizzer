Ribes macracanthum

Ribes macracanthum, commonly known as the large-flowered currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red or black when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Ribes macracanthum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
