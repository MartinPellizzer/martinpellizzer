Rosa dumalis f. malmundariensis

Rosa dumalis f. malmundariensis, commonly known as the Malmundar rose, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the Rosa dumalis species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy texture, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique botanical features.
