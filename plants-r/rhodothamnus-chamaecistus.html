Rhodothamnus chamaecistus

Rhodothamnus chamaecistus, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Rhodothamnus chamaecistus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
