Ranunculus habbemensis

Ranunculus habbemensis, commonly known as the Habbemense buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic species. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
