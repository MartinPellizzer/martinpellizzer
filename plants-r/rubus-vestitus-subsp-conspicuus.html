Rubus vestitus subsp. conspicuus

Rubus vestitus subsp.

conspicuus, commonly known as the showy raspberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Rubiaceae family.
