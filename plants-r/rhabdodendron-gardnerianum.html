Rhabdodendron gardnerianum

Rhabdodendron gardnerianum, commonly known as the Gardner's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and floral structures that are often pink or reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
