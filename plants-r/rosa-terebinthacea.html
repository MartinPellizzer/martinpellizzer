Rosa terebinthacea

Rosa terebinthacea, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa terebinthacea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine highlight its importance in both horticulture and herbal practices.
