Ranunculus transochotensis

Ranunculus transochotensis, commonly known as the Transoceanic buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features compound leaves with lobed margins and produces small, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, or pink. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
