Rubus confinis

Rubus confinis, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in maritime climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a common sight in coastal ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
