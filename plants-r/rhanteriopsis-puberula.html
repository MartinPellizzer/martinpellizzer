Rhanteriopsis puberula

Rhanteriopsis puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and possesses a short, thick rhizome. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color, with a distinctive lip petal. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, contributing to its common name.

Rhanteriopsis puberula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded, humid environments.
