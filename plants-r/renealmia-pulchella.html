Renealmia pulchella

Renealmia pulchella, commonly known as the pink saddleback, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that features a bright pink spathe surrounding a yellow spadix. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near rivers or in swampy areas. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, with a distinctive waxy surface that helps it retain moisture.

Renealmia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
