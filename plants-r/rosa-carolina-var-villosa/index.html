Rosa Carolina Var. Villosa

Rosa carolina var. villosa is a unique wild rose species native to the eastern united states. it is known for its hairy stems and pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. this plant thrives in moist woodlands and along stream banks where it forms dense thickets. its fragrant blooms attract pollinators and provide food for wildlife. it is a valuable addition to native plant gardens and conservation efforts.
