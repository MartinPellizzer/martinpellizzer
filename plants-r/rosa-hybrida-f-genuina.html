Rosa hybrida f. genuina

Rosa hybrida f. genuina, commonly known as the genuine hybrid rose, is a botanical variety characterized by its complex genetic makeup resulting from crossbreeding between different Rosa species.

It typically exhibits a wide range of flower colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, with a strong, often fragrant aroma. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually large and showy, with multiple layers of petals that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
