Rhus subferruginata

Rhus subferruginata, commonly known as the red-ironwood or ironwood tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the coastal regions of Georgia. The plant is characterized by its dense, hard wood and compound leaves that are typically alternate and pinnate. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, appearing in clusters, and it produces smooth, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals.

The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.
