Rubus chlorostachys subvar. polytrichus

Rubus chlorostachys subvar. polytrichus, commonly known as the green-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, green stems and small, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, achenes, typically found in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive foliage and its role in local ecosystems.
