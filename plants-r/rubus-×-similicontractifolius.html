Rubus × similicontractifolius

Rubus × similicontractifolius, commonly known as the blackberry-boysenberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccatus (boysenberry), resulting in a plant with robust growth and thorny stems. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the plant produces large, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for cultivation in temperate climates.

This hybrid is valued for its vigorous growth and the unique flavor of its berries, which are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
