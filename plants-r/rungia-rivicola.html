Rungia rivicola

Rungia rivicola, commonly known as the water spinach or river spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, red or purple fruits. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near water bodies.

Rungia rivicola is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
