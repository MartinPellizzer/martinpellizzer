Rubus phoenicolasius var. albiflorus

Rubus phoenicolasius var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower bramble, is a variant of the Chinese evergreen bramble.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a red or black drupe, is edible and often used in jams and wines.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
