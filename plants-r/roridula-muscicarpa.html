Roridula muscicarpa

Roridula muscicarpa, commonly known as the fleshy leaf roridula, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky substance. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to arid environments and has a specialized relationship with certain insect species.

Its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
