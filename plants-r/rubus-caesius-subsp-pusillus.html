Rubus caesius subsp. pusillus

Rubus caesius subsp.

pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that ripen in late summer. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
