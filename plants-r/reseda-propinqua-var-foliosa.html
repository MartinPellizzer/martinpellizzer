Reseda propinqua var. foliosa

Reseda propinqua var. foliosa, commonly known as the hairy reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
