Rhododendron campanulatum var. wallichii

Rhododendron campanulatum var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a slight purple tinge. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments with acidic soil. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of understory plants.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
