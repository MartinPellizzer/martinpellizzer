Rhamnus alnoides

Rhamnus alnoides, commonly known as the alnus-like buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
