Rubus leptocaulon

Rubus leptocaulon, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, thorny growth. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark purple to black drupelets. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
