Rubus spinipes

Rubus spinipes, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark red to black berry.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
