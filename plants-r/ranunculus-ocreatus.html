Ranunculus ocreatus

Ranunculus ocreatus, commonly known as the golden shield or yellow shield flower, is a species of flowering plant in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae).

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow petals that surround a prominent, yellow-orange center. Native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
