Rosa webbiana subsp. wulffii

Rosa webbiana subsp.

wulffii, commonly known as the Wulff's rose, is a species native to South Africa. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its botanical features include small, oval-shaped leaves and numerous stamens in its showy blooms.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
