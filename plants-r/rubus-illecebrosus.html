Rubus illecebrosus

Rubus illecebrosus, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are popular for their sweet flavor and are used both in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
