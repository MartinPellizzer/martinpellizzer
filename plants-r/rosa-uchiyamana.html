Rosa uchiyamana

Rosa uchiyamana, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in the spring. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Rosa uchiyamana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
