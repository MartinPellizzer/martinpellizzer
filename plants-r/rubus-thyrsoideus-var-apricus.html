Rubus thyrsoideus var. apricus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. apricus, commonly known as the yellow raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, yellowish-red berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are thorny, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

The flowers are white with five petals and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
