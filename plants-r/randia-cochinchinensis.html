Randia cochinchinensis

Randia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the white randia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
