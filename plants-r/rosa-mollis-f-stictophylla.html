Rosa mollis f. stictophylla

Rosa mollis f. stictophylla, commonly known as the variegated evergreen rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa mollis species, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with striking white or cream-colored markings, giving it a unique visual appeal. The leaves are typically leathery and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, usually blooming in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is well-suited for rock gardens or as a compact shrub in gardens with well-drained soil.
