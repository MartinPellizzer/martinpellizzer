Rosa kentukensis

Rosa kentukensis, commonly known as the Kentukensis rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are elliptical in shape.

Rosa kentukensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
