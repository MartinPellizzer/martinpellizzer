Rubus franchetianus

Rubus franchetianus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
