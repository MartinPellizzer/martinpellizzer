Rhododendron × cilipinense

Rhododendron × cilipinense, commonly known as the Cilipinense rhododendron, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
