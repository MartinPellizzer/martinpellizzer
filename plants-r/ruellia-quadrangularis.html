Ruellia quadrangularis

Ruellia quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its square, angular stems, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ruellia quadrangularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
