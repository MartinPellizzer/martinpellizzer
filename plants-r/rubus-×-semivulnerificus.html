Rubus × semivulnerificus

Rubus × semivulnerificus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, that are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are not only edible but also have a high concentration of antioxidants.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is often considered an invasive species in certain regions.
