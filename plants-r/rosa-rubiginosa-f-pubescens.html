Rosa rubiginosa f. pubescens

Rosa rubiginosa f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent blusher rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, thorny stems covered in a dense layer of fine, fuzzy pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets that have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
