Rosa viscaria var. pseudolemanii

Rosa viscaria var. pseudolemanii, commonly known as the creeping red rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy leaves and produces clusters of deep red, semi-double flowers. The plant is characterized by its trailing habit, making it suitable for ground cover or hanging baskets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
