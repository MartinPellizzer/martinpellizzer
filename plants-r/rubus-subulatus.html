Rubus subulatus

Rubus subulatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
