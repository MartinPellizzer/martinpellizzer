Rubus schleicheri var. samuelis

Rubus schleicheri var. samuelis, commonly known as Samuel's bramble, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, similar to other raspberries and blackberries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
