Rubus denticulatus var. typicus

Rubus denticulatus var. typicus, commonly known as the sawtooth blackberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This species is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
