Rosa graveolens f. typica

Rosa graveolens f. typica, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a fragrant shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C and are often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental gardens.

Its historical use in perfumery and as a symbol of love and devotion adds to its cultural significance.
