Rosa alpina intercalaris

Rosa alpina intercalaris, commonly known as the alpine pink rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a compact shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring wiry stems and dense foliage. The plant produces flowers that are usually pink to red in color, with five to seven petals and a fragrant scent. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa alpina intercalaris is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
