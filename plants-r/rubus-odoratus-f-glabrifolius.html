Rubus odoratus f. glabrifolius

Rubus odoratus f. glabrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved sweetbriar, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a smooth, glossy texture. The plant produces fragrant white to pale pink flowers in spring, which are followed by red, edible berries. Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
