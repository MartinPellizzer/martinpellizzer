Ranunculus abnormis

Ranunculus abnormis, commonly known as the abnormal buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, typically yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petals. The plant has a short, hairy stem and thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure.

Ranunculus abnormis is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
