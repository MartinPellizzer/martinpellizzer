Rosa arvensis f. pseudobibracteata

Rosa arvensis f. pseudobibracteata, commonly known as the false bract dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy rosehip that matures in autumn, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, showcasing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
