Rhododendron citrinum var. discoloratum

Rhododendron citrinum var. discoloratum, commonly known as the Yellow-flowered Rhododendron, is a variant native to parts of China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with its dark green, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
