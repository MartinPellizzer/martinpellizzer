Rubus apetalus var. grossoserratus

Rubus apetalus var. grossoserratus, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins, which are often coarse and lobed. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark purple or black berries. Its stems are typically thorny, and the fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart flavor.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
