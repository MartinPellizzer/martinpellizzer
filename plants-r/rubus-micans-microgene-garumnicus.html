Rubus micans microgene garumnicus

Rubus micans microgene garumnicus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub characterized by its slender, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that emit a silvery sheen when exposed to light.

Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in culinary applications despite their tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its unique leaf morphology and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
