Rhododendron annae

Rhododendron annae, commonly known as the Annae rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron annae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
