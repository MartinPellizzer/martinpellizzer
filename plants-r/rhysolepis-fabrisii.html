Rhysolepis fabrisii

Rhysolepis fabrisii, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Rhysolepis fabrisii is an important plant for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental beauty in native plant gardens.
