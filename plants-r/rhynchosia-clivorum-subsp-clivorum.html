Rhynchosia clivorum subsp. clivorum

Rhynchosia clivorum subsp.

clivorum, commonly known as the cliff bean, is a climbing legume native to southern Africa. It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant is characterized by its twining stems and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or cliffs. Its pods are slender and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
