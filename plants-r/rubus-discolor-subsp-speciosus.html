Rubus discolor subsp. speciosus

Rubus discolor subsp.

speciosus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of white to pale pink flowers.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
