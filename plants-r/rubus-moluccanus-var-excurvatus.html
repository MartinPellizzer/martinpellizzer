Rubus moluccanus var. excurvatus

Rubus moluccanus var. excurvatus, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
