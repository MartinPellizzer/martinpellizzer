Rubus ulmifolius microgene argillaceus

Rubus ulmifolius microgene argillaceus, commonly known as the argillaceous blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
