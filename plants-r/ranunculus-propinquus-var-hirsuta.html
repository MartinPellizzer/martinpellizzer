Ranunculus propinquus var. hirsuta

Ranunculus propinquus var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous sepals. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and other water bodies.

This variety is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
