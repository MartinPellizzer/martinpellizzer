Ranunculus angustus

Ranunculus angustus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It is known for its ability to thrive in damp soils and its preference for partial to full shade.

Ranunculus angustus is often found in moist meadows and along the edges of wetlands.
