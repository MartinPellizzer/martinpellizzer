Ranunculus insignis var. glabratus

Ranunculus insignis var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, often with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

The variety 'glabratus' is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, setting it apart from other varieties of Ranunculus insignis.
