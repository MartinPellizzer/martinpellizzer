Rosa urbica ramealis

Rosa urbica ramealis, commonly known as the city rose or urban rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa urbica ramealis is often found in disturbed soils and urban environments, making it adaptable to a variety of conditions.
