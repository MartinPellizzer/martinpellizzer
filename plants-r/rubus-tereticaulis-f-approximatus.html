Rubus tereticaulis f. approximatus

Rubus tereticaulis f. approximatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
