Ranunculus hejingensis

Ranunculus hejingensis, commonly known as the Hejing buttercup, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has simple, trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
