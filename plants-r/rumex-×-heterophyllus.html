Rumex × heterophyllus

Rumex × heterophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed dock, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of morphological variations.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can vary in size and texture depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing a distinctive reddish-brown color when mature.

This species is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
