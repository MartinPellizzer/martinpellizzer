Rosa mollis f. caerulea

Rosa mollis f. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered form of the soft rose, is a hybrid cultivar characterized by its delicate, pale blue to lavender-colored flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub with arching stems and small, glossy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring regular watering during dry periods.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique floral coloration and compact growth habit.
