Rhynchosia minima

Rhynchosia minima, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trifoliate leaves and produces small, rounded pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
