Rubus bayeriformis

Rubus bayeriformis, commonly known as the Bayer's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, dark berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The berries are not only edible but also used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.
