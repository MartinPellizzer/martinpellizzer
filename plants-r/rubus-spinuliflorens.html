Rubus spinuliflorens

Rubus spinuliflorens, commonly known as the spiny-flowered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and dark purple when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
