Rubus kisoensis

Rubus kisoensis, commonly known as Kiso raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan, specifically found in the Kiso region of Nagano Prefecture. The plant is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are similar to raspberries in texture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
