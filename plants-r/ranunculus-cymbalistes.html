Ranunculus cymbalistes

Ranunculus cymbalistes, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with a hairy, glandular center, and its leaves are palmately divided with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a short, stout rhizome and produces showy, long-stemmed flowers that bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
