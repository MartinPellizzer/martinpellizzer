Romanzoffia unalaschcensis var. unalaschcensis

Romanzoffia unalaschcensis var. unalaschcensis, commonly known as the Alaska waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, often forming dense underwater vegetation. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through rhizomes.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
