Rosa stylosa var. monsoniana

Rosa stylosa var. monsoniana, commonly known as the Monson's sweetbriar, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in landscaping and horticulture.
