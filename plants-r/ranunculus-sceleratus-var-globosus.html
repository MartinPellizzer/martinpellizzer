Ranunculus sceleratus var. globosus

Ranunculus sceleratus var. globosus, commonly known as the globular crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of deeply lobed, bright green leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in moist meadows and along riverbanks.
