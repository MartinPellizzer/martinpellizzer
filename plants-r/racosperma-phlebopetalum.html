Racosperma phlebopetalum

Racosperma phlebopetalum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its environment.
