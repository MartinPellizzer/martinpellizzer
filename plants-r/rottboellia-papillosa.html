Rottboellia papillosa

Rottboellia papillosa, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.
