Rhododendron × hidaense

Rhododendron × hidaense, commonly known as the Hida rhododendron, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping and garden cultivation.
