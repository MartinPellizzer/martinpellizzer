Ranunculus pleiophyllus

Ranunculus pleiophyllus, commonly known as the many-leafed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, serrated leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Ranunculus pleiophyllus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
