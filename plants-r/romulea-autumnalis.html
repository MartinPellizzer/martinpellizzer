Romulea autumnalis

Romulea autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn glory, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Romulea autumnalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant seasonal display.
