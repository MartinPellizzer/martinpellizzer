Rubus emarginatifolius

Rubus emarginatifolius, commonly known as the margined bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
