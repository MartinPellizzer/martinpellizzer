Rhododendron metternichii var. angustifolium

Rhododendron metternichii var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved metternich rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
