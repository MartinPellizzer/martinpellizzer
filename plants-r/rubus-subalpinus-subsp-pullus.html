Rubus subalpinus subsp. pullus

Rubus subalpinus subsp.

pullus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its hairy foliage and the presence of stinging hairs on its leaves and stems.
