Ranunculus fallax subsp. euanthemus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

euanthemus, commonly known as the yellow meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth compared to other ranunculus species.
