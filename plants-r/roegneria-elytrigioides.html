Roegneria elytrigioides

Roegneria elytrigioides, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Roegneria elytrigioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
