Ranunculus setosopilosus

Ranunculus setosopilosus, commonly known as the hairy bristled buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically arranged in a cup-like structure. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
