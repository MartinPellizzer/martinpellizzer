Rubus × realmontensis

Rubus × realmontensis, commonly known as the realmontian blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated margins and a slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is valued for its sweet-tart flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
