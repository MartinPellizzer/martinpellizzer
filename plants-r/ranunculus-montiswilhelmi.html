Ranunculus montiswilhelmi

Ranunculus montiswilhelmi, commonly known as the Wilhelmine meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils among rocky outcrops and mountain meadows. Its botanical structure includes a short, stout stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
