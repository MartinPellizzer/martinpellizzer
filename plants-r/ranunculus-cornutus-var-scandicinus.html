Ranunculus cornutus var. scandicinus

Ranunculus cornutus var. scandicinus, commonly known as the Scandinavian horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, yellow flowers with two prominent, horn-like sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or alpine habitats. It has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer.

This variety is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
