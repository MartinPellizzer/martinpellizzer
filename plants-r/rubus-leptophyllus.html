Rubus leptophyllus

Rubus leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thin, leathery leaves and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces achenes that are dispersed by birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
