Rubus insericatus subsp. newbouldianus

Rubus insericatus subsp.

newbouldianus, commonly known as the Newbould's bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, elliptical leaves with a serrated margin.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit is a red, aggregate berry. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
