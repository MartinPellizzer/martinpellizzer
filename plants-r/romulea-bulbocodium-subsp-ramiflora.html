Romulea bulbocodium subsp. ramiflora

Romulea bulbocodium subsp.

ramiflora, commonly known as the ramiflorous romulea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features a short, fleshy bulb and produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant blooms with small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in rock gardens and collections of succulent flora.
