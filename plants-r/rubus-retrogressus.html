Rubus retrogressus

Rubus retrogressus, commonly known as the backward blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is notable for its unique growth habit and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
