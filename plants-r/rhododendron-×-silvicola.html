Rhododendron × silvicola

Rhododendron × silvicola, commonly known as the forest rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features dense, leathery foliage that is typically dark green with a waxy coating, aiding in moisture retention. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for erosion control and as an ornamental plant in shaded landscapes.
