Rubus pallidus var. gracillimus

Rubus pallidus var. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are edible berries that are typically small and tart.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats across the northern regions of North America.
