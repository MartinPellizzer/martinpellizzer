Rosa villosa var. leiocarpa

Rosa villosa var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white-leafed rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a soft, downy texture, which gives it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often covered in thorns, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and gardens.
