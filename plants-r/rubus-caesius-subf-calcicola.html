Rubus caesius subf. calcicola

Rubus caesius subf.

calcicola, commonly known as the limestone raspberry, is a shrub native to calcareous habitats in Europe. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant produces red or black berries that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments.
