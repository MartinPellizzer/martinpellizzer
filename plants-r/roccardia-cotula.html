Roccardia cotula

Roccardia cotula, commonly known as the small white daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces small, white ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Roccardia cotula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
