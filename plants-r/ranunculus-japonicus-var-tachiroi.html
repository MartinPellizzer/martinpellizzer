Ranunculus japonicus var. tachiroi

Ranunculus japonicus var. tachiroi, commonly known as the Tachiroi buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
