Reseda parviflora

Reseda parviflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Reseda parviflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
