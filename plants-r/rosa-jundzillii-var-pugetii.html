Rosa jundzillii var. pugetii

Rosa jundzillii var. pugetii, commonly known as the Puget Sound rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features upright, thorny stems and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its hips produce red, ornamental fruits in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riparian habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
