Rubus hirtus microgene pierratii

Rubus hirtus microgene pierratii, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
