Rosa coronopsis

Rosa coronopsis, commonly known as the crown-of-roses, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thorny.

Rosa coronopsis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for gardens with well-drained soil.
