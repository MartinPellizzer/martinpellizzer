Rosa glauca var. pilosula

Rosa glauca var. pilosula, commonly known as the woolly blue rose, is a variant of the blue rose that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cool, dry climates. The plant features small, clustered flowers with a pale blue hue, often tinged with purple, and is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
