Rosa viscaria var. rectispina

Rosa viscaria var. rectispina, commonly known as the straight-spined Rosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are a distinguishing feature of the variety. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early summer, followed by red, hairy fruits. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This species is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for various garden environments.
