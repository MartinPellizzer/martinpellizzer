Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. atrorubrum

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

atrorubrum, commonly known as the black-red azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dark red to almost black flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
