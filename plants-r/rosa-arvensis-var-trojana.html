Rosa arvensis var. trojana

Rosa arvensis var. trojana, commonly known as the Trojan meadow rose, is a variant of the field rose native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the summer. Its stems are often armed with thorns, and it has a woody, bushy growth habit.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
