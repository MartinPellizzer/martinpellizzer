Ranunculus nissaensis

Ranunculus nissaensis, commonly known as the Nissa's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa, particularly in Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
