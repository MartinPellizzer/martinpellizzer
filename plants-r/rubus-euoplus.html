Rubus euoplus

Rubus euoplus, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
