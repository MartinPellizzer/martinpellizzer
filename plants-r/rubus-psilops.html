Rubus psilops

Rubus psilops, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
