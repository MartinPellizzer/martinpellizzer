Rhododendron mekongense

Rhododendron mekongense, commonly known as the Mekong rhododendron, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam, Cambodia, and Laos.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.

Rhododendron mekongense is also notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, a characteristic common among many rhododendron species.
