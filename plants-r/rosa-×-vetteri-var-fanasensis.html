Rosa × vetteri var. fanasensis

Rosa × vetteri var. fanasensis, commonly known as the Fanash Mountain rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The foliage is dark green and glossy, adding ornamental value throughout the growing season.
