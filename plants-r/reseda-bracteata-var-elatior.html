Reseda bracteata var. elatior

Reseda bracteata var. elatior, commonly known as the greater bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its fibrous roots are used in traditional herbal medicine, and the plant is often found in grassy areas and along woodland edges.

Reseda bracteata var. elatior is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
