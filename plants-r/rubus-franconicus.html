Rubus franconicus

Rubus franconicus, commonly known as the Franconian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to central Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has serrated leaves and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

The berries are edible but can be tart, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
