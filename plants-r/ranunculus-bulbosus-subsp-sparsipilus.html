Ranunculus bulbosus subsp. sparsipilus

Ranunculus bulbosus subsp.

sparsipilus, commonly known as the sparse-hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy underside, giving it its subspecies name.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators with its vibrant color.
