Rhododendron mitriforme var. setaceum

Rhododendron mitriforme var. setaceum, commonly known as the Setaceous Mitriform Rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
