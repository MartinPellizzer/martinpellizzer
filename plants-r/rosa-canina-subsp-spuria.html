Rosa canina subsp. spuria

Rosa canina subsp.

spuria, commonly known as the dog rose variant, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red, hairy, and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
