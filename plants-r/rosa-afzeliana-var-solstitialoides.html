Rosa afzeliana var. solstitialoides

Rosa afzeliana var. solstitialoides, commonly known as the "sunset rose," is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flowering pattern, it is occasionally grown in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
