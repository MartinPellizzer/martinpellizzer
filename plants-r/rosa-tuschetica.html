Rosa tuschetica

Rosa tuschetica, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorny buds.

Rosa tuschetica is well adapted to harsh climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
