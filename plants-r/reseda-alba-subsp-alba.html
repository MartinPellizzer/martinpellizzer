Reseda alba subsp. alba

Reseda alba subsp.

alba, commonly known as white eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
