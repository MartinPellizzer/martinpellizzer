Rubus clusii

Rubus clusii, commonly known as the Clusius blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Argentina. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pink flowers in the spring.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
