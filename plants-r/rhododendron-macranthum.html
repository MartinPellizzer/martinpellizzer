Rhododendron macranthum

Rhododendron macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
