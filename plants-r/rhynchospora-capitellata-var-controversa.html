Rhynchospora capitellata var. controversa

Rhynchospora capitellata var. controversa, commonly known as the slender swamp thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its pale green to reddish stems and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
