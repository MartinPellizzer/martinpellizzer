Rebutia costata

Rebutia costata, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a compact, low-growing cactus native to South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and produces vibrant, colorful flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny areoles and is well adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Rebutia costata is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and regular light exposure to bloom successfully.
