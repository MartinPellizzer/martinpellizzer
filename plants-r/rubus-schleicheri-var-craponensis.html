Rubus schleicheri var. craponensis

Rubus schleicheri var. craponensis, commonly known as the Craponens raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorned stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

The fruit of this plant is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
