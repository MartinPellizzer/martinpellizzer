Rhododendron nigroglandulosum

Rhododendron nigroglandulosum, commonly known as the black glandular rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are dark green with prominent, glandular dots, which give it its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
