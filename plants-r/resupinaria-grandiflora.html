Resupinaria grandiflora

Resupinaria grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered resupinate, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. The plant features a slender, creeping rhizome with erect, linear leaves. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive resupinate (upturned) lip and are usually violet or purple in color.

Resupinaria grandiflora is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
