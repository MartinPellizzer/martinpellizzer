Rubus rudis var. laevifactus

Rubus rudis var. laevifactus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, less hairy stems compared to other Rubus species. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically firm and tart in flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
