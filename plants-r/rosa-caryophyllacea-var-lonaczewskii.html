Rosa caryophyllacea var. lonaczewskii

Rosa caryophyllacea var. lonaczewskii, commonly known as the Polish rose, is a perennial shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright stems with thorny branches and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is well adapted to temperate climates.
