Rubus foliosus f. deltoideus

Rubus foliosus f. deltoideus, commonly known as the deltoid leaf raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive deltoid (triangular) shaped leaves, which set it apart from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red and edible. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible berries.
