Rubus × hololeucoides

Rubus × hololeucoides, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and for local use.
