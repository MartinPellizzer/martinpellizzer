Rubus quaesitus

Rubus quaesitus, commonly known as the sought-after raspberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
