Ranunculus flammula var. intermedius

Ranunculus flammula var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
