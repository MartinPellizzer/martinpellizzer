Rhododendron caucasicum var. bicolor

Rhododendron caucasicum var. bicolor, commonly known as the Caucasian twin-colored rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking, showy flowers that typically display two distinct colors, often with a white base and a pink or red upper portion. The plant has leathery, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
