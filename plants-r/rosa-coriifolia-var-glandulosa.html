Rosa coriifolia var. glandulosa

Rosa coriifolia var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular leaf dogwood rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red-orange hips, which are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage and ornamental fruit make it a valuable addition to horticultural collections.
