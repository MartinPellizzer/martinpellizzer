Rubus sitiens

Rubus sitiens, commonly known as the Sitien blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its tart flavor and potential health benefits.
