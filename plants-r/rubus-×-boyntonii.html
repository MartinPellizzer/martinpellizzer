Rubus × boyntonii

Rubus × boyntonii, commonly known as the Boynton blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in sandy soils and humid climates, often found in coastal areas.

The species is valued for its edible berries, which have a sweet and tart flavor.
