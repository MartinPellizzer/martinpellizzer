Rosa arvensis var. pseudostylosa

Rosa arvensis var. pseudostylosa, commonly known as the creeping dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a shrub-like perennial plant characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This plant is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
