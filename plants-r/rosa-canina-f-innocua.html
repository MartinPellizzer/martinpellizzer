Rosa canina f. innocua

Rosa canina f. innocua, commonly known as the common dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has thorny stems and pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its hips, which are the fruit, are orange-red and contain seeds.

Rosa canina f. innocua is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
