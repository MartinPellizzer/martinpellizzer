Rosa communis var. macrocarpa

Rosa communis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited rose, is a cultivar characterized by its robust growth and showy, fragrant flowers.

It produces large, red or pink petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its fruit, a round, red hip, is edible and often used in culinary applications.

This variety is well-suited for garden cultivation due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
