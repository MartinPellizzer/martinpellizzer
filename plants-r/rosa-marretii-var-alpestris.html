Rosa marretii var. alpestris

Rosa marretii var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, giving them a silvery appearance.

This variety is well adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
