Ribes himalense var. trichophyllum

Ribes himalense var. trichophyllum, commonly known as the Himalayan gooseberry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in moist, shaded areas. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, with a hairy underside, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
