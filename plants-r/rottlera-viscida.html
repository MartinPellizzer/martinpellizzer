Rottlera viscida

Rottlera viscida, commonly known as the sticky rottlera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its sticky leaves and unique floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
