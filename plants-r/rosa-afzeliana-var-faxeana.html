Rosa afzeliana var. faxeana

Rosa afzeliana var. faxeana, commonly known as the Faxe rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in odd-pinnate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a source of cut flowers.
