Rhus oblanceolata

Rhus oblanceolata, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and typically have 11 to 31 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Rhus oblanceolata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
