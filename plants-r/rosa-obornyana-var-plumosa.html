Rosa obornyana var. plumosa

Rosa obornyana var. plumosa, commonly known as the plumose rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
