Rosa graveolens var. typica

Rosa graveolens var. typica, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional herbal medicine.
