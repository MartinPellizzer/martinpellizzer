Rosa sicula var. ligustica

Rosa sicula var. ligustica, commonly known as the Ligurian rose, is a variety of the Sicilian rose native to the coastal regions of southern Italy.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse blooming of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Mediterranean horticulture.
