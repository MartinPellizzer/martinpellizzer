Raphanis

Raphanis, commonly known as the wild radish, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by spherical, hairy pods containing several seeds. Raphanis is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It has a long flowering period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
