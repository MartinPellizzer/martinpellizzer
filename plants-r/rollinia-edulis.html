Rollinia edulis

Rollinia edulis, commonly known as the Brazilian paw-paw, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that produces large, yellow-green fruits with a thick, spiny rind. The fruit contains a sweet, creamy pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a tropical flavor reminiscent of custard. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

Its botanical features include simple leaves and a distinctive, showy flower that attracts pollinators.
