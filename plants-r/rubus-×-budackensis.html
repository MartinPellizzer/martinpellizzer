Rubus × budackensis

Rubus × budackensis, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in culinary applications.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
