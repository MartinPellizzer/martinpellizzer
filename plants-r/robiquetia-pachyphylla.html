Robiquetia pachyphylla

Robiquetia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved rock rose, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. Its growth habit is low and spreading, often forming dense mats on rocky soils.

It is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
