Rosa coriifolia var. erlbergensis

Rosa coriifolia var. erlbergensis, commonly known as the Erlberg's shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has a bushy, upright habit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
