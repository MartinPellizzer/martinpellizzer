Rosa pseudograveolens

Rosa pseudograveolens, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets.

Rosa pseudograveolens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and historical use in hedgerows.
