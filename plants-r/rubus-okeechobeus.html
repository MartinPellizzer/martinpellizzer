Rubus okeechobeus

Rubus okeechobeus, commonly known as the Okeechobee blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Florida.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in wetland habitats and is adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of the region.

Due to its limited range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
