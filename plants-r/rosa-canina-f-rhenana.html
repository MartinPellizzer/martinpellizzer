Rosa canina f. rhenana

Rosa canina f. rhenana, commonly known as the Hungarian rose or Rhenish rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe, including regions such as Hungary and the Czech Republic. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C.
