Rubus melanoxylon proles abnobarum

Rubus melanoxylon proles abnobarum, commonly known as the blackstem bramble, is a shrub native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, woody stems and dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
