Rogiera cordata var. longisepala

Rogiera cordata var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled heartleaf, is a rare and unique variant of the heartleaf plant.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white flowers with elongated sepals, giving it its specific variety name. It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
