Rubus nitidus var. caeresiensis

Rubus nitidus var. caeresiensis, commonly known as the Caerulescent blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Its berries are valued for their intense flavor and are often used in culinary applications.
