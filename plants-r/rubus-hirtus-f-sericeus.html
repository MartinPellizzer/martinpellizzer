Rubus hirtus f. sericeus

Rubus hirtus f. sericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive silvery-white pubescence on the leaves and stems.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin and a dense covering of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit.
