Ranunculus punctatus

Ranunculus punctatus, commonly known as the speckled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with dark brown or black spots on the petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Ranunculus punctatus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.
