Rubus melanoxylon var. sinuosidens

Rubus melanoxylon var. sinuosidens, commonly known as the sinuous blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a tart flavor. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
