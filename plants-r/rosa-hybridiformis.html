Rosa hybridiformis

Rosa hybridiformis, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species that combines traits from various rose species.

It is characterized by its continuous flowering throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for gardeners. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and pinnate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a strong fragrance and a full, layered bloom.

Rosa hybridiformis is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.
