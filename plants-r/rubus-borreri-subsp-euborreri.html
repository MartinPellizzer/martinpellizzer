Rubus borreri subsp. euborreri

Rubus borreri subsp.

euborreri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
