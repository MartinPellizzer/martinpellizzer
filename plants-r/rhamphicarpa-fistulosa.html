Rhamphicarpa fistulosa

Rhamphicarpa fistulosa, commonly known as the pipestem cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces a slender, hollow stem that gives it its common name.
