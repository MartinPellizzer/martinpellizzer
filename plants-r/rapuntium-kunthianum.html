Rapuntium kunthianum

Rapuntium kunthianum, commonly known as the Kunth's cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are edible, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
