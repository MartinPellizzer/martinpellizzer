Roscoea bhutanica

Roscoea bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutanese ginger, is a rhizomatous herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, red-bronze flower spikes that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Roscoea bhutanica thrives in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes.
