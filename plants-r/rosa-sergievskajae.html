Rosa sergievskajae

Rosa sergievskajae, commonly known as the Sergeyevskaya rose, is a species native to the Russian Far East.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color. It produces hip fruits that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Rosa genus.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in botanical gardens.
