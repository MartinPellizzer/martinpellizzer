Rosa glauca subsp. subcanina

Rosa glauca subsp.

subcanina, commonly known as the blue rose or bluebank rose, is a shrub-like species native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale blue to pink.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and hardy plant.
