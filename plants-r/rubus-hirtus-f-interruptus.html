Rubus hirtus f. interruptus

Rubus hirtus f. interruptus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry or a variant of the common blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
