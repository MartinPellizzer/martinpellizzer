Rubroshorea dispar

Rubroshorea dispar, commonly known as the redshore flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to salt spray from the ocean, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
