Rosa pendulina subvar. intercalaris

Rosa pendulina subvar. intercalaris, commonly known as the hanging rose or the climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a moderate growth rate.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
