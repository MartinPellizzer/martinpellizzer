Racosperma undosum

Racosperma undosum, commonly known as the wavy acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a distinctive wavy margin, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which attract various pollinators.

Racosperma undosum is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
