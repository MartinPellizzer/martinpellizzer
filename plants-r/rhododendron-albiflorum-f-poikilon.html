Rhododendron albiflorum f. poikilon

Rhododendron albiflorum f. poikilon, commonly known as the variegated rhododendron, is a unique cultivar characterized by its striking foliage.

This plant belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Asia. Its leaves exhibit a distinctive variegation pattern, featuring white and green mottling that adds visual interest. The flowers are typically pale pink or white, blooming in spring, and the plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
