Rosa tomentosa var. subadenophylla

Rosa tomentosa var. subadenophylla, commonly known as the woolly rose or subadenophylla rose, is a shrub-like species characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems.

It typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with soft, white or grayish hairs that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and unique texture.

This variety is also valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
