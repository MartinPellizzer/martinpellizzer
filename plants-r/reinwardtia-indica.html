Reinwardtia indica

Reinwardtia indica, commonly known as the Indian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The flowers are usually fragrant and come in various colors, making it a popular ornamental plant.
