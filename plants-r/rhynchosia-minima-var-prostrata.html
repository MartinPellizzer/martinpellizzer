Rhynchosia minima var. prostrata

Rhynchosia minima var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, flat pods. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, often spreading along the ground with slender stems. It is valued for its edible seeds and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

The variety is adapted to warm climates and well-drained soils.
