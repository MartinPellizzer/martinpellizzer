Rosa amblyotis subsp. jacutica

Rosa amblyotis subsp.

jacutica, commonly known as the Central Asian rose, is a shrub native to regions of Central Asia, including Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late summer.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional horticulture in its native regions.
