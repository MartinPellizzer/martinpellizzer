Rubus × lasiostachys

Rubus × lasiostachys, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas where it can tolerate salty air and wind.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for both its aesthetic appeal and edible fruits.
