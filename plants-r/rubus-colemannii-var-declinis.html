Rubus colemannii var. declinis

Rubus colemannii var. declinis, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils, thriving in maritime climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

The berries are often used in local cuisine and have a tart, flavorful profile.
