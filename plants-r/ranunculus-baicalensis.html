Ranunculus baicalensis

Ranunculus baicalensis, commonly known as the Baical skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Far East of Russia and parts of Mongolia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early summer, and it is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic uses.
