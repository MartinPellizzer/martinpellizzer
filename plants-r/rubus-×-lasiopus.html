Rubus × lasiopus

Rubus × lasiopus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
