Racosperma subternatum

Racosperma subternatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes.

The species is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy soils.
