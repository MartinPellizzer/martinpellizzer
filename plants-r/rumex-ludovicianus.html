Rumex ludovicianus

Rumex ludovicianus, commonly known as the eastern dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features upright, hollow stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have prominent veins. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
