Rubus brachystachys

Rubus brachystachys, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
