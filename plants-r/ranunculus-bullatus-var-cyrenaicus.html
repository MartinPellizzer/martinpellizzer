Ranunculus bullatus var. cyrenaicus

Ranunculus bullatus var. cyrenaicus, commonly known as the Cyrenaic buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive, hairy center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
