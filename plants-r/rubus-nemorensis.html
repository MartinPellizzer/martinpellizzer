Rubus nemorensis

Rubus nemorensis, commonly known as the woodland bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland edges and shrublands. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a serrated edge, while its stems are covered in thorny bracts.

Rubus nemorensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
