Rubus × defectus

Rubus × defectus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a dark purple to black berry, which is edible but has a tart, acidic flavor.
