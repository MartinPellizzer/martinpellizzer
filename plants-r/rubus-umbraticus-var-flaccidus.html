Rubus umbraticus var. flaccidus

Rubus umbraticus var. flaccidus, commonly known as the shadow raspberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark green leaves that often appear in dense clusters. The flowers are typically pale pink or white, and the fruit is a small, tart berry that ripens in late summer. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species within its native range.
