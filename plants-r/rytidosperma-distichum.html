Rytidosperma distichum

Rytidosperma distichum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spinifex, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense tussocks. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering heads are small and spike-like, producing lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Rytidosperma distichum plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local wildlife.
