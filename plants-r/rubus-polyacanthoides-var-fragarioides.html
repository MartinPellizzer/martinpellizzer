Rubus polyacanthoides var. fragarioides

Rubus polyacanthoides var. fragarioides, commonly known as the black raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black in color, with a sweet-tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically thorny. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries.
