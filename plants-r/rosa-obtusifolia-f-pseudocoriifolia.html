Rosa obtusifolia f. pseudocoriifolia

Rosa obtusifolia f. pseudocoriifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed rose, is a horticultural form of the shrub rose characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features large, leathery, ovate leaves with a rough, textured surface that sets it apart from typical rose varieties. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
