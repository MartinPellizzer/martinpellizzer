Rosa omissa var. gallicoides

Rosa omissa var. gallicoides, commonly known as the Gallicoides rose, is a variety of the Rosa omissa species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that have serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a fragrant aroma. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
