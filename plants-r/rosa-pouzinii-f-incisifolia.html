Rosa pouzinii f. incisifolia

Rosa pouzinii f. incisifolia, commonly known as the incisifoliate rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa pouzinii species.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinct appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
