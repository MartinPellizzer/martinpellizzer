Raphanus cakiloideus

Raphanus cakiloideus, commonly known as the desert radish, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant has small, yellow flowers and develops round, edible roots that are adapted to store water. Its leaves are typically hairy and succulent, helping it retain moisture in harsh environments.

Raphanus cakiloideus is often found in sandy soils and is considered a hardy species suitable for cultivation in drought-prone areas.
