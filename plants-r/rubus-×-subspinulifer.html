Rubus × subspinulifer

Rubus × subspinulifer, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (raspberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that can range in color from red to black.

This hybrid is valued for its hardy nature and the flavor of its fruit, making it a popular choice for cultivation in temperate regions.
