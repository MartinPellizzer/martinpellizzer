Rhynchosia sennaarensis

Rhynchosia sennaarensis, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Rhynchosia sennaarensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
