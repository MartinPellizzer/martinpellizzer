Roemeria vermiculata

Roemeria vermiculata, commonly known as the silverleaf roemeria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to arid and semi-arid landscapes.
