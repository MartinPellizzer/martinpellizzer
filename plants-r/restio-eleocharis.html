Restio eleocharis

Restio eleocharis, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and hollow stems.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves that are often arranged in a tight, grass-like cluster. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Restio eleocharis plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
