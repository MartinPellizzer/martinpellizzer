Rosa alpina var. pubescens

Rosa alpina var. pubescens, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

This hardy species is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
