Renantherella auyongii

Renantherella auyongii, commonly known as the Auyong's orchid, is a rare and striking tropical orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that typically display shades of red and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its humid, tropical environment. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation efforts.
