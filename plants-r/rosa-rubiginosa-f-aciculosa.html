Rosa rubiginosa f. aciculosa

Rosa rubiginosa f. aciculosa, commonly known as the prickly rose or bristly rose, is a compact, shrub-like form of the shrub rose.

It features small, narrow, needle-like leaves that give it its "aciculosa" designation, meaning "needle-like" in Latin. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
