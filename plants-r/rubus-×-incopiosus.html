Rubus × incopiosus

Rubus × incopiosus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and cooler climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
