Rosa thyrsiflora

Rosa thyrsiflora, commonly known as the thrysis rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are typically compound, with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa thyrsiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
