Raphanus sativus var. violaceus

Raphanus sativus var. violaceus, commonly known as purple sprouting broccoli, is a cultivar of the wild radish that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its dense, violet-colored flowering heads that develop from thick, fleshy stalks. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with large, palmately lobed leaves that are edible. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in compact, club-shaped inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and unique flavor, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.
