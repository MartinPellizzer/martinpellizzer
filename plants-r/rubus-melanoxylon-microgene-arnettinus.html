Rubus melanoxylon microgene arnettinus

Rubus melanoxylon microgene arnettinus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of the southern hemisphere, particularly in South America. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white or pink flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
