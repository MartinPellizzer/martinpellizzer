Rosa afzeliana var. pseudohaberiana

Rosa afzeliana var. pseudohaberiana, commonly known as the Haber's rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
