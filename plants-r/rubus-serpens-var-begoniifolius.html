Rubus serpens var. begoniifolius

Rubus serpens var. begoniifolius, commonly known as the begoniifolius raspberry, is a variant of the serpens raspberry species.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring leaves that resemble those of the begonia plant, hence its name. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that develop from its flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Its botanical interest lies in its distinctive leaf morphology and potential horticultural value.
