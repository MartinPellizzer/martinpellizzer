Rumex algeriensis var. hipporegianus

Rumex algeriensis var. hipporegianus, commonly known as Algerian sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on terminal spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their tart, sour flavor.
