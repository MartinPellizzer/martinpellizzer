Rodriguezia granadensis

Rodriguezia granadensis, commonly known as the Granada rodriguezia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, making it a dramatic addition to any landscape. The plant produces clusters of bright red, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
