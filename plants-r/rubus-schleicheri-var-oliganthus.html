Rubus schleicheri var. oliganthus

Rubus schleicheri var. oliganthus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to southern China, characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark red to black berries. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruits are typically small and edible, though they are not as widely cultivated as other Rubus species.

This variant is notable for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
