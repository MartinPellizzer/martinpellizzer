Rhododendron primulinum

Rhododendron primulinum, commonly known as the primrose azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with dense, dark green foliage that provides a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. Its flowers range in color from pale yellow to deep pink, depending on the variety and environmental conditions.

Rhododendron primulinum prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
