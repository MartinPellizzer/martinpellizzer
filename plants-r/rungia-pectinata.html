Rungia pectinata

Rungia pectinata, commonly known as the waxplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Caribbean, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small lanterns. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface.

Rungia pectinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
