Rubus hebecaulis var. dasyanthes

Rubus hebecaulis var. dasyanthes, commonly known as the hairy bramble or woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

The species is notable for its dense growth habit and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
