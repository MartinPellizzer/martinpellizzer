Rhus himalaica

Rhus himalaica, commonly known as Himalayan sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs and have a serrated edge. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for various birds and animals.

Rhus himalaica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
