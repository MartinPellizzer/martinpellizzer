Rhamnus jelskii

Rhamnus jelskii, commonly known as the Jel's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant bears small, reddish-black berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Rhamnus jelskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native environment.
