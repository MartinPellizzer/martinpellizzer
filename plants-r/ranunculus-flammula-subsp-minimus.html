Ranunculus flammula subsp. minimus

Ranunculus flammula subsp.

minimus, commonly known as the lesser buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features delicate yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common sight in damp, temperate regions.
