Rabdosia stracheyi

Rabdosia stracheyi, commonly known as the purple-flowered rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, violet to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Rabdosia stracheyi is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
