Rhodiola himalensis subsp. taohoensis

Rhodiola himalensis subsp.

taohoensis, commonly known as the Himalayan rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Its ability to withstand extreme environmental conditions makes it a unique subject of botanical study.
