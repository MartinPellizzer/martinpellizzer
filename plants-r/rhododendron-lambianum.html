Rhododendron lambianum

Rhododendron lambianum, commonly known as the Lambian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron lambianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
