Rubus leucadenes

Rubus leucadenes, commonly known as white-stemmed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
