Ribes uva-crispa lusus xanthocarpum

Ribes uva-crispa lusus xanthocarpum, commonly known as the golden currant, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow berries, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. It produces small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
