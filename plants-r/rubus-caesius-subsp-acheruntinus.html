Rubus caesius subsp. acheruntinus

Rubus caesius subsp.

acheruntinus, commonly known as the Greek raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and usually has thornless stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
