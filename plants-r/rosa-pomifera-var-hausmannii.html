Rosa pomifera var. hausmannii

Rosa pomifera var. hausmannii, commonly known as the Persian shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late spring.

Its most distinctive feature is the edible, apple-like fruit known as the "rose hip," which is rich in vitamin C. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
