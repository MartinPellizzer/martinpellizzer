Rosa × blinovskiana

Rosa × blinovskiana, commonly known as the Blinovskian rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa sempervirens.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy plant. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing an attractive appearance throughout the growing season.

Rosa × blinovskiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its resilience and aesthetic value in gardens.
