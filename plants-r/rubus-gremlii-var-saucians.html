Rubus gremlii var. saucians

Rubus gremlii var. saucians, commonly known as the Saucian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native habitat.
