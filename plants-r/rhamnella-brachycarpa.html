Rhamnella brachycarpa

Rhamnella brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas along stream banks.

Rhamnella brachycarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
