Rubus pruinosus var. suberectus

Rubus pruinosus var. suberectus, commonly known as the glossy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodlands and hedgerows.
