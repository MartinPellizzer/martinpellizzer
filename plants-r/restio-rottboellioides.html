Restio rottboellioides

Restio rottboellioides, commonly known as the Cape restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Restio rottboellioides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
