Rubus brachyadenes f. umbrosus

Rubus brachyadenes f. umbrosus, commonly known as the shadowy raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark red fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
