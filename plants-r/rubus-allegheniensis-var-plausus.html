Rubus allegheniensis var. plausus

Rubus allegheniensis var. plausus, commonly known as the Allegheny blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
