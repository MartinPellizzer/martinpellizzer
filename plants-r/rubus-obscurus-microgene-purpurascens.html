Rubus obscurus microgene purpurascens

Rubus obscurus microgene purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems.
