Rubus triphyllus var. concolor

Rubus triphyllus var. concolor, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with new canes emerging each year and flowering in the second year. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets, which are typically bright red when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually serrated and hairy.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
