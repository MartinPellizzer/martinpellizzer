Rubus debilis

Rubus debilis, commonly known as the weak bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy and have a serrated edge.

Rubus debilis is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
