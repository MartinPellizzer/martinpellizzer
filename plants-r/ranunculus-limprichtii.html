Ranunculus limprichtii

Ranunculus limprichtii, commonly known as the Limpricht's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically has palmate leaves with five to nine lobes and produces small, simple flowers with five to ten petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
