Ruellia longipedunculata

Ruellia longipedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncle ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Ruellia longipedunculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various garden conditions.
