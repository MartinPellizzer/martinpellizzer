Rhododendron kjellbergii

Rhododendron kjellbergii, commonly known as the Kjellberg rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant prefers cool, humid climates and is typically found in forested areas at high elevations. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Rhododendron kjellbergii is also noted for its ability to thrive in acidic soils, making it a popular choice for specialized garden settings.
