Rorippa indica f. longicarpa

Rorippa indica f. longicarpa, commonly known as the long-seeded watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to temperate regions.

It is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, edible leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and garden ponds. Its fleshy leaves are often used in salads, and the plant is known for its peppery flavor.

The longicarpa form is distinguished by its elongated, slender seeds compared to the typical variety.
