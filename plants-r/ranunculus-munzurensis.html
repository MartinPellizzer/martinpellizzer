Ranunculus munzurensis

Ranunculus munzurensis, commonly known as the Munzur ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant grows in rocky or alpine environments, often in areas with high elevation and cool temperatures. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
