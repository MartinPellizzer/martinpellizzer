Rhus hapemanii

Rhus hapemanii, commonly known as the Desert Buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert scrublands.

Rhus hapemanii is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
