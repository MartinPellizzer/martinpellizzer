Rosa ruprechtii

Rosa ruprechtii, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species native to China and is closely related to the common garden roses.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure, hairy stems, and a preference for well-drained soil in full sun.
