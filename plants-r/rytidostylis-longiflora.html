Rytidostylis longiflora

Rytidostylis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered rainforest ginger, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of northern Australia and parts of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves that are often adorned with prominent veins, and its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that bear showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in the understorey of dense rainforest vegetation. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

Rytidostylis longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
