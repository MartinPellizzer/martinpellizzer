Reseda tournefortii

Reseda tournefortii, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Reseda tournefortii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
