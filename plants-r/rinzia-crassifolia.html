Rinzia crassifolia

Rinzia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rinzia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests or heathlands.

Rinzia crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
