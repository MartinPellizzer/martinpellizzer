Rubus phoenicolasius var. concolor

Rubus phoenicolasius var. concolor, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
