Rubus moluccanus var. lanatus

Rubus moluccanus var. lanatus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
