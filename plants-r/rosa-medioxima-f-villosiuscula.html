Rosa medioxima f. villosiuscula

Rosa medioxima f. villosiuscula, commonly known as the silky-stemmed hybrid rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its soft, hairy stems and foliage.

This plant typically features fragrant, semi-double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a shrub-like species with thorny stems and glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Native to hybridized populations in Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
