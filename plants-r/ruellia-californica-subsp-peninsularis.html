Ruellia californica subsp. peninsularis

Ruellia californica subsp.

peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular California ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Baja California Peninsula. It is a herbaceous perennial with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy.

The plant produces clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in arid landscapes.
