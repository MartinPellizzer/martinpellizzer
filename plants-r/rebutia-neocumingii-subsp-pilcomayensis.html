Rebutia neocumingii subsp. pilcomayensis

Rebutia neocumingii subsp.

pilcomayensis, commonly known as the Pilcomayo Rebutia, is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains of Bolivia. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
