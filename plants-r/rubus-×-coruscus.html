Rubus × coruscus

Rubus × coruscus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant is native to the western regions of North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces showy white or pink flowers in the spring.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible fruits.
