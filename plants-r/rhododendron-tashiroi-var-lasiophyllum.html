Rhododendron tashiroi var. lasiophyllum

Rhododendron tashiroi var. lasiophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has dark green, leathery foliage that provides a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
