Rubus scaber subsp. ebneri

Rubus scaber subsp.

ebneri, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
