Rubus × semidiscolor subsp. pseudowahlenbergii

Rubus × semidiscolor subsp.

pseudowahlenbergii, commonly known as the redcurrant bush, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
