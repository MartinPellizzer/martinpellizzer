Rubus subcanus var. viridicaulis

Rubus subcanus var. viridicaulis, commonly known as the green-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
