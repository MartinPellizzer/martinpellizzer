Ranunculus auricomus subsp. acutipartitus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

acutipartitus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals that are often marked with dark veins.

The leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided, serrated lobes that give the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
