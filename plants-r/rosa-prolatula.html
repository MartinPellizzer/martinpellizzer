Rosa prolatula

Rosa prolatula, commonly known as the slender rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a pinkish-red hip that contains seeds.

Rosa prolatula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
