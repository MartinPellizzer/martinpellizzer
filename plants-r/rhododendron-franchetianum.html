Rhododendron franchetianum

Rhododendron franchetianum, commonly known as the Franchet's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and a distinctive, tubular corona.

Rhododendron franchetianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
