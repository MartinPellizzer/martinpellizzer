Ranunculus auricomus subsp. hannonianus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

hannonianus, commonly known as the Hannon's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals that resemble a cup or saucer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
