Rubia peregrina var. longifolia

Rubia peregrina var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are blue to purple in color, borne in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
