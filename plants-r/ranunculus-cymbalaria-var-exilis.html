Ranunculus cymbalaria var. exilis

Ranunculus cymbalaria var. exilis, commonly known as the slender water crowfoot, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often near the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are usually submerged or floating, and it produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and is sometimes used in pond ecosystems for water filtration.
