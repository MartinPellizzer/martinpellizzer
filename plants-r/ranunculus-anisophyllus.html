Ranunculus anisophyllus

Ranunculus anisophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a short, hairy stem that supports its palmately divided, bright green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a distinctive dark center. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
