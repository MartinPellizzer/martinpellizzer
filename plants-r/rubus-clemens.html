Rubus clemens

Rubus clemens, commonly known as Clemens' blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the rose family. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.
