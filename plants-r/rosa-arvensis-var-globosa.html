Rosa arvensis var. globosa

Rosa arvensis var. globosa, commonly known as the globular dogrose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, often pink or white flowers. The plant produces prickly stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its fruit, a red, hairy rosehip, contains seeds and is an important food source for wildlife.
