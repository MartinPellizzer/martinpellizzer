Rubus foliosus var. villosocarpus

Rubus foliosus var. villosocarpus, commonly known as the hairy-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its edible berries.
