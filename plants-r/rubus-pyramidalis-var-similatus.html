Rubus pyramidalis var. similatus

Rubus pyramidalis var. similatus, commonly known as the similar raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
