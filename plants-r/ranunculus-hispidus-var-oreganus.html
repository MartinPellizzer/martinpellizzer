Ranunculus hispidus var. oreganus

Ranunculus hispidus var. oreganus, commonly known as the Oregon buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often accompanied by numerous stamens, giving them a fluffy appearance.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are adaptations to its moist, cool habitat.
