Ranunculus lappaceus var. pascuinus

Ranunculus lappaceus var. pascuinus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of yellow stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, hairy layer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
