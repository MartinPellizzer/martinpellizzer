Rhaphiostylis ovatifolia

Rhaphiostylis ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved star sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strap-like, ovate leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
