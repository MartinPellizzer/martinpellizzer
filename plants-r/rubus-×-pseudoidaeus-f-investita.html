Rubus × pseudoidaeus f. investita

Rubus × pseudoidaeus f. investita, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in antioxidants and nutrients.

The foliage is usually green with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
