Rinorea angolensis

Rinorea angolensis, commonly known as the Angola rindleaf, is a flowering plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white or greenish flowers are clustered in racemes and are often accompanied by a distinctive, fleshy calyx.

Rinorea angolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
