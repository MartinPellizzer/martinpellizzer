Rhynchosia luteola var. verdickii

Rhynchosia luteola var. verdickii, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
