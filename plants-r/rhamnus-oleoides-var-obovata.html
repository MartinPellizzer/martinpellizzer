Rhamnus oleoides var. obovata

Rhamnus oleoides var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lanceolate to obovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of whitish, felt-like hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during spring, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
