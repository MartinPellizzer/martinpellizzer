Rosa caryophyllacea

Rosa caryophyllacea, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa caryophyllacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
