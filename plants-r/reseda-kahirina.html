Reseda kahirina

Reseda kahirina, commonly known as the Egyptian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Reseda kahirina is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
