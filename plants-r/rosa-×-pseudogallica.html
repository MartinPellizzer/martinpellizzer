Rosa × pseudogallica

Rosa × pseudogallica, commonly known as the French rose or Gallica rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in perfumery. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa × pseudogallica is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
