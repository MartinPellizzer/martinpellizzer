Rhamnus pusilla

Rhamnus pusilla, commonly known as dwarf buckthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet and features dense, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by black, spherical berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Rhamnus pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
