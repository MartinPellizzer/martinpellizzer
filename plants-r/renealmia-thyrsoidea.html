Renealmia thyrsoidea

Renealmia thyrsoidea, commonly known as the feather reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that resemble reed-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with a reddish or purple hue.

Renealmia thyrsoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
