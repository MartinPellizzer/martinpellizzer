Ranunculus kevoensis

Ranunculus kevoensis, commonly known as the Kevo buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous and alpine habitats. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
