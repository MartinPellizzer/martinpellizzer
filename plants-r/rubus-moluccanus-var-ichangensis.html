Rubus moluccanus var. ichangensis

Rubus moluccanus var. ichangensis, commonly known as the Ichang blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
