Rubus suberectus var. polyacanthus

Rubus suberectus var. polyacanthus, commonly known as the purple-leafed raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves that turn a striking purplish-red color in the fall. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
