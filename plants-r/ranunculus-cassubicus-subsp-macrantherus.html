Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. macrantherus

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

macrantherus, commonly known as the large-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that are often reflexed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically divided into lobes, and it grows in moist, shady habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more robust flowers compared to other members of the species.
