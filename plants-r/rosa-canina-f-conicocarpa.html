Rosa canina f. conicocarpa

Rosa canina f. conicocarpa, commonly known as the conicocarpa rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, conical-shaped fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with five petals, typical of the Rosa genus. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
