Rosa micrantha subvar. subspoliata

Rosa micrantha subvar. subspoliata, commonly known as the small-flowered shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It features slender, thorny stems and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, aggregate berry that ripens in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
