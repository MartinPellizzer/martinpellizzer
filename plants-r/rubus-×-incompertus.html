Rubus × incompertus

Rubus × incompertus, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruits, which are edible, range in size and color from dark purple to black.

This species is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
