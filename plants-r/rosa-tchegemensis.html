Rosa tchegemensis

Rosa tchegemensis, commonly known as the Tchegem rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves with serrated edges, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Rosa tchegemensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in botanical studies.
