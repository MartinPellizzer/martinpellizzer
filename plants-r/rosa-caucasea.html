Rosa caucasea

Rosa caucasea, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region, including parts of Georgia, Russia, and Turkey.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorned stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in inflorescences. Rosa caucasea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the rose family, Rosaceae.
