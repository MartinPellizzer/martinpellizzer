Rubus scaber var. botruosus

Rubus scaber var. botruosus, commonly known as the rough raspberry or brambles, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
