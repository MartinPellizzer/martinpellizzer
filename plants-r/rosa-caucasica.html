Rosa caucasica

Rosa caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region, including parts of Georgia, Azerbaijan, and Russia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically single or semi-double blooms. Rosa caucasica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its beauty and historical significance in horticulture.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
