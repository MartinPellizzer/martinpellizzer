Rosa austriaca var. dearmata

Rosa austriaca var. dearmata, commonly known as the Austrian rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa austriaca species, native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its deep pink to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorned stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
