Rubus × weissenburgensis

Rubus × weissenburgensis, commonly known as the Weissenburg blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to South America, specifically the Andean region, and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dark green, serrated leaves. Its berries are usually dark purple to black when ripe, and they are prized for their flavor and use in culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in various climates.
