Rosa coriifolia var. clavata

Rosa coriifolia var. clavata, commonly known as the clustered shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that are often hairy. This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
