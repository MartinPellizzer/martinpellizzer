Rhododendron poecilodermum

Rhododendron poecilodermum, commonly known as the variable-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that vary in color and texture, often displaying a range of green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with dense foliage that provides habitat for various wildlife.

Rhododendron poecilodermum is well adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in acidic soils.
