Ranunculus auricomus subsp. concordans

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

concordans, commonly known as the concord larkspur, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to western North America. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with yellow petals and a blue or purple center, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has palmately divided leaves and produces erect, branching stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
