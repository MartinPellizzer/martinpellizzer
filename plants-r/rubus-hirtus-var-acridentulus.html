Rubus hirtus var. acridentulus

Rubus hirtus var. acridentulus, commonly known as the rough blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and clustered, and it thrives in woodland and disturbed habitats.

The species is notable for its hirsute (hairy) appearance and is often found in moist, acidic soils.
