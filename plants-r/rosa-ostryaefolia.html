Rosa ostryaefolia

Rosa ostryaefolia, commonly known as the Ostrya-leaved rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and resemble those of the genus Ostrya, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, typically in shades of white and pink. Its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that contains seeds.

Rosa ostryaefolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
