Ranunculus acris subvar. yakushimensis

Ranunculus acris subvar. yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushimen buttercup, is a variant of the meadow buttercup native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with a distinct central stigma and showy, palmate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is known for its ornamental value. It has a short flowering period in early spring, and its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its horticultural appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
