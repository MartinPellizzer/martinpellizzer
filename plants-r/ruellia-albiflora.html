Ruellia albiflora

Ruellia albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinguishing characteristic is its clusters of white, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Ruellia albiflora is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often used in ornamental gardening.
