Rubus macrobotrys

Rubus macrobotrys, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everlasting flower, is a species of brambles native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can persist for several weeks, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces edible, red berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its flowers are rich in anthocyanins, contributing to their vibrant color and potential medicinal properties.

Rubus macrobotrys is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
