Rubus semisetosus var. wheeleri

Rubus semisetosus var. wheeleri, commonly known as Wheeler's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in local ecosystems for wildlife support.
