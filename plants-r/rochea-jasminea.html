Rochea jasminea

Rochea jasminea, commonly known as the yellow jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Rochea jasminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual and olfactory characteristics.
