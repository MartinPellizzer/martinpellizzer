Rhipsalis baccifera subsp. fortdauphinensis

Rhipsalis baccifera subsp.

fortdauphinensis, commonly known as the Madagascar mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems that often hang in cascading clusters, giving it a delicate, vine-like appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are reduced to small, hair-like structures, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
