Rhamnus awarica

Rhamnus awarica, commonly known as the Brazilian buckthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and dark, glossy berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Rhamnus awarica is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
