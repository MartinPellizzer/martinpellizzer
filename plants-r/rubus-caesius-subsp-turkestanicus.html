Rubus caesius subsp. turkestanicus

Rubus caesius subsp.

turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan raspberry, is a shrub native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial or perennial growth habit.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupes that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features include serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species.
