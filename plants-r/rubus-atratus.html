Rubus atratus

Rubus atratus, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, which are edible and widely appreciated. Rubus atratus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a valuable resource for wildlife.
