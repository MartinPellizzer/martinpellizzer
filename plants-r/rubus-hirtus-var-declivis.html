Rubus hirtus var. declivis

Rubus hirtus var. declivis, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
