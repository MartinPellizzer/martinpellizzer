Rosa × maxima

Rosa × maxima, commonly known as the giant rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant is known for its robust growth and thorny stems, making it a hardy shrub suitable for various climates. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms.

Rosa × maxima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
