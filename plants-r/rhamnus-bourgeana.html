Rhamnus bourgeana

Rhamnus bourgeana, commonly known as the Chinese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by dark purple or black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Rhamnus bourgeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
