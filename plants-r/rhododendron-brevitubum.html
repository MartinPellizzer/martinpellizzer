Rhododendron brevitubum

Rhododendron brevitubum, commonly known as the short-stemmed rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide dense coverage.

Rhododendron brevitubum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
