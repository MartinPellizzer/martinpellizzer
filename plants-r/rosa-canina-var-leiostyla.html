Rosa canina var. leiostyla

Rosa canina var. leiostyla, commonly known as the smooth hips rose, is a variant of the dog rose that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant produces pink to white flowers in the spring, followed by distinctive orange-red, smooth-petaled fruits known as hips. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is also valued for its edible hips, which are rich in vitamin C.
