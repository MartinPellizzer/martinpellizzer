Rebutia tarijensis

Rebutia tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis Paddle Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Bolivia and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that resemble paddle shapes. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, Rebutia tarijensis is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid environments.
