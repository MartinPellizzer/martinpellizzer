Rubus cuspidiferus var. vaczyi

Rubus cuspidiferus var. vaczyi, commonly known as the Florida blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its edible fruits and aesthetic qualities.
