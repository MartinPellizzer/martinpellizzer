Rubus hirtus var. braunii

Rubus hirtus var. braunii, commonly known as Braun's bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
