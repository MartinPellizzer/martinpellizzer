Racosperma bivenosum

Racosperma bivenosum, commonly known as the two-veined acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with two distinct veins. The plant produces yellow globular pods that contain seeds, and it has small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in spring.

It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
