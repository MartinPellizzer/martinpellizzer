Rhododendron taitungalpinum

Rhododendron taitungalpinum, commonly known as the Taitung rhododendron, is a species native to Taiwan.

It is a shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and acidic soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
