Rubus colemannii var. ferocissimus

Rubus colemannii var. ferocissimus, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that are typically small and tart. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces showy white or pink flowers in the spring.

The berries are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, though they are generally not cultivated commercially.
