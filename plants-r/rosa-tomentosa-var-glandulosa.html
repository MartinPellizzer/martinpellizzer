Rosa tomentosa var. glandulosa

Rosa tomentosa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular hairy rose, is a variant of the hairy rose species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green foliage and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 to 2 meters, and is known for its dense, thorny stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

The variety's unique glandular structures on its stems and leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological role.
