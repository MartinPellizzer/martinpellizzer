Rhododendron canadense f. viridifolium

Rhododendron canadense f. viridifolium, commonly known as the Canadian greenleaf rhododendron, is a variant of the Canadian rhododendron characterized by its distinctly greenish foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, typically found in moist, acidic soils. The leaves are narrow and elongated, with a leathery texture and a smooth margin. Its flowers are usually pink to red, appearing in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded woodland environments.
