Rubus malarbetii

Rubus malarbetii, commonly known as the Malarbeti raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less-studied species within the Rubus genus.
