Rutidea hispida

Rutidea hispida, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Rutidea hispida is native to South America and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
