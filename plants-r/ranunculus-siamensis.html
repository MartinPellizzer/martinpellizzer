Ranunculus siamensis

Ranunculus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, white, or pink. The plant has a short, stout stem with basal leaves that are palmately divided. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
