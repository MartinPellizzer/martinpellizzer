Rhamnus nerija

Rhamnus nerija, commonly known as the Nerija buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The bark is smooth and can become fissured with age, and the plant is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
