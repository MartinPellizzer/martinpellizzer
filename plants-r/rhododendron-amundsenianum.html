Rhododendron amundsenianum

Rhododendron amundsenianum, commonly known as the Amundsen's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils under shaded conditions. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden environments.
