Rosa dumalis var. squarrosa

Rosa dumalis var. squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa wild rose, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the summer months. Its fruit is a pinkish-red hip, which contains achenes surrounded by a fleshy, edible outer layer.

This variety is often found in rocky or woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
