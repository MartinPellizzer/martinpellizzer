Rapicactus beguinii

Rapicactus beguinii, commonly known as the beguinii cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its slow growth rate makes it suitable for small spaces, and it requires minimal care once established.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
