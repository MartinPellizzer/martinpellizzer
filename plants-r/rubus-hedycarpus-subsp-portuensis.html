Rubus hedycarpus subsp. portuensis

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

portuensis, commonly known as the Portuguese raspberry, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in woodland and scrubland habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
