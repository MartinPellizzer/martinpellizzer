Racosperma tetraneurum

Racosperma tetraneurum, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Racosperma tetraneurum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
