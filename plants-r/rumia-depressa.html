Rumia depressa

Rumia depressa, commonly known as the dwarf rumia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rubiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in small umbels.

Rumia depressa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
