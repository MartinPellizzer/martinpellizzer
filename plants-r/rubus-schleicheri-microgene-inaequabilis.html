Rubus schleicheri microgene inaequabilis

Rubus schleicheri microgene inaequabilis, commonly known as the Schleicher's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural studies.
