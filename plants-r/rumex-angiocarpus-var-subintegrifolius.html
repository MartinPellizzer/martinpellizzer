Rumex angiocarpus var. subintegrifolius

Rumex angiocarpus var. subintegrifolius, commonly known as the African sorrel, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin, while the flowers are small and clustered in loose panicles. This plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Polygonaceae family.
