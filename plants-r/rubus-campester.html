Rubus campester

Rubus campester, commonly known as field raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
