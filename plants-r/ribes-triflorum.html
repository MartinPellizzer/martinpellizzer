Ribes triflorum

Ribes triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its small, often yellow or orange berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them useful in culinary applications.
