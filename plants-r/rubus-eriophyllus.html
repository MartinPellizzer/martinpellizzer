Rubus eriophyllus

Rubus eriophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The silverleaf blackberry is a member of the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like raspberries and strawberries.
