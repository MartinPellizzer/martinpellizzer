Rubus truncifrons

Rubus truncifrons, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and river valleys.

Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them a valued resource in local ecosystems.
