Rorippa sinuata

Rorippa sinuata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed watercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
