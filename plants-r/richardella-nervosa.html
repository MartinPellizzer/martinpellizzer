Richardella nervosa

Richardella nervosa, commonly known as the slender rocknettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by soft, hairy structures. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and can thrive in environments with low nutrient availability.

Richardella nervosa is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
