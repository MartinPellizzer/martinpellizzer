Rhododendron albrechtii var. hypoleucum

Rhododendron albrechtii var. hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-veined rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically leathery and dark green, with prominent white veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. The flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually pink to white in color and are clustered in inflorescences.

This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.
