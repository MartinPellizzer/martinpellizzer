Ranunculus bulbosus proles sparsipilus

Ranunculus bulbosus proles sparsipilus, commonly known as the sparse-hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant produces a short, thickened rhizome and is often found in moist, calcareous soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is characterized by its distinctive foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
