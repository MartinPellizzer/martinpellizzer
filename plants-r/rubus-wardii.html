Rubus wardii

Rubus wardii, commonly known as the Ward's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by wildlife and birds. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
