Rumex verticillatus subsp. floridanus

Rumex verticillatus subsp.

floridanus, commonly known as Florida sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It features upright, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a reddish tinge on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their tart, sour flavor.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
