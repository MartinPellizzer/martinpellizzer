Rhachidosorus stramineus

Rhachidosorus stramineus, commonly known as the yellow hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces clusters of yellowish-green to yellow flowers during the spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rhachidosorus stramineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
