Rhododendron sessilifolium

Rhododendron sessilifolium, commonly known as the sessile-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval and have a short stalk, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
