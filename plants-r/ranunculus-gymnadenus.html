Ranunculus gymnadenus

Ranunculus gymnadenus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
