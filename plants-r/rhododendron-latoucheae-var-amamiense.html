Rhododendron latoucheae var. amamiense

Rhododendron latoucheae var. amamiense, commonly known as the Amami rhododendron, is a rare and endemic species native to the Amami Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils within shaded forest understories. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its island habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited genetic diversity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
