Rosa micrantha var. losae

Rosa micrantha var. losae, commonly known as the Los Angeles rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, pink to white flowers. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
