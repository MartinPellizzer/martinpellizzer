Rubus sulcatus subsp. libertianus

Rubus sulcatus subsp.

libertianus, commonly known as the Libertian raspberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
