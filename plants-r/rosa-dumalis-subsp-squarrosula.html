Rosa dumalis subsp. squarrosula

Rosa dumalis subsp.

squarrosula, commonly known as the squarrosula rose, is a shrub-like perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. This subspecies thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
