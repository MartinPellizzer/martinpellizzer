Rhododendron pudiense

Rhododendron pudiense, commonly known as the Pudense Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Rhododendron pudiense is valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to a variety of climatic conditions.
