Rumex graecus

Rumex graecus, commonly known as Greek sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color, especially when young. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, making them a traditional culinary herb in various cuisines.
