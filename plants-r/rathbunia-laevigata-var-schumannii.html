Rathbunia laevigata var. schumannii

Rathbunia laevigata var. schumannii, commonly known as the Schumann's smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually smooth and slightly curved. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptability, making it an important component of its native wetland ecosystems.
