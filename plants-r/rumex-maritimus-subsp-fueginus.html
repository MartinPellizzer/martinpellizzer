Rumex maritimus subsp. fueginus

Rumex maritimus subsp.

fueginus, commonly known as the Falkland Island sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southern coast of South America. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional diets by local communities.
