Ranunculus papuanus

Ranunculus papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes a variety of herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its simple, palmate leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers with five petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
