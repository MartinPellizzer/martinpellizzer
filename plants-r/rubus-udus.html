Rubus udus

Rubus udus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a fibrous root system.

Rubus udus is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and streambanks.
