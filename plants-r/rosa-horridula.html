Rosa horridula

Rosa horridula, commonly known as the horned rose, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Rosa horridula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
