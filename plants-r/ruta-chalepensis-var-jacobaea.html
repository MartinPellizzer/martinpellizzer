Ruta chalepensis var. jacobaea

Ruta chalepensis var. jacobaea, commonly known as false wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong, bitter scent, and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a challenge to local ecosystems.
