Ribes divaricatum var. klamathense

Ribes divaricatum var. klamathense, commonly known as Klamath currant, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its red, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

It is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisine and traditional practices.
