Rubus ulmifolius var. irregularidens

Rubus ulmifolius var. irregularidens, commonly known as the irregular-leaved blackberry.

This variant is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with irregularly shaped leaflets. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to Europe and prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, where it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
