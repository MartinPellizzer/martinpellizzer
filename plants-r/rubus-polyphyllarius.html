Rubus polyphyllarius

Rubus polyphyllarius, commonly known as the many-leafed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and large, compound leaves that can have up to 30 leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
