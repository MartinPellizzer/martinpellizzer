Rubus × winterianus

Rubus × winterianus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and edible fruits.
