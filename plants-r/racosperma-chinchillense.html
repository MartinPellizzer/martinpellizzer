Racosperma chinchillense

Racosperma chinchillense, commonly known as the Chinchilla tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep root systems.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species.
