Rhamnus iranica

Rhamnus iranica, commonly known as Persian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often have a laxative effect. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Rhamnus iranica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
