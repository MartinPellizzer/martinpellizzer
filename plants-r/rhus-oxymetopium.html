Rhus oxymetopium

Rhus oxymetopium, commonly known as the Brazilian sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fuzzy berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rhus oxymetopium is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
