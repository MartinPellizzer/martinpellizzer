Racosperma cretaceum

Racosperma cretaceum, commonly known as the Cretaceous acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically small and arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in winter, adding visual interest during the cooler months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance.

Its historical significance is reflected in its name, which alludes to the Cretaceous period.
