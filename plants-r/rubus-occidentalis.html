Rubus occidentalis

Rubus occidentalis, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a distinctive tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Rubus occidentalis is an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
