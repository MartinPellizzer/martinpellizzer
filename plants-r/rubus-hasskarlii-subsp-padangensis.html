Rubus hasskarlii subsp. padangensis

Rubus hasskarlii subsp.

padangensis, commonly known as the Padang raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible berries.

The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
