Ruehssia paraguaiensis

Ruehssia paraguaiensis, commonly known as Paraguay wax tree, is a small evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The tree also has historical significance in traditional medicine and local culture.
