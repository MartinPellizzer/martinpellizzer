Rosa × spinulifolia f. pilosior

Rosa × spinulifolia f. pilosior, commonly known as the hairy spinulifera rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its unique botanical features.

It exhibits a compact growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, slightly hairy foliage. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated margin. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with a high degree of variability in color and form.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
