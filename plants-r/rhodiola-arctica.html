Rhodiola arctica

Rhodiola arctica, commonly known as the Arctic rose or golden root, is a hardy perennial plant native to cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It thrives in alpine and arctic environments, often growing in rocky soils and tundra habitats. The plant features a low-growing structure with fleshy, reddish stems and small, white or pink flowers. Its leaves are typically basal, with a succulent texture that helps it conserve moisture in harsh climates.

Rhodiola arctica is also valued for its adaptogenic properties, making it a popular herb in traditional medicine.
