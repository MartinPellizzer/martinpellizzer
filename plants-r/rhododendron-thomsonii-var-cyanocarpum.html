Rhododendron thomsonii var. cyanocarpum

Rhododendron thomsonii var. cyanocarpum, commonly known as the blue-fruit rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
