Rubus × callithyrsus

Rubus × callithyrsus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various Rubus species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
