Rosa rubiginosa f. giersteri

Rosa rubiginosa f. giersteri, commonly known as the Giersteri form of the rough rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, known for its dense, thorny stems and dark green, velvety leaves. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a slightly hairy appearance, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in moderate climates.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
