Rollinia sieberi

Rollinia sieberi, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caricaceae and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.
