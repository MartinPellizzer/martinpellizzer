Rhynchospora gracilenta

Rhynchospora gracilenta, commonly known as slender spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are unisexual and arranged in dense clusters.

Rhynchospora gracilenta plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
