Rubus corylifolius subsp. acuminatus

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to East Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and dark purple to black berries.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its acuminate leaf tips and slightly smaller fruits compared to the main species.
