Robinia pseudoacacia f. pyramidalis

Robinia pseudoacacia f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal false acacia, is a compact, upright shrub or small tree with a distinctive conical shape.

It features compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in pendulous clusters during the spring. It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This cultivar is valued for its dense growth habit and ornamental appeal in gardens and urban settings.
