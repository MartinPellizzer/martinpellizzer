Rubus retinervis

Rubus retinervis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
