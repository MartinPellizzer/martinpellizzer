Rubus fuscoater var. consociatus

Rubus fuscoater var. consociatus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
