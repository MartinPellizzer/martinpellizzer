Rubus ulmifolius var. semiviridis

Rubus ulmifolius var. semiviridis, commonly known as the greenleaf blackberry, is a variant of the common brambles.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and trifoliate leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
