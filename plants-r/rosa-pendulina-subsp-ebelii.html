Rosa pendulina subsp. ebelii

Rosa pendulina subsp.

ebelii, commonly known as the Ebel's weeping rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its cascading, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, arching stems that produce fragrant, single or semi-double blooms. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a soft, silvery underside.

This species thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
