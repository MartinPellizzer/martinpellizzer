Rhamnus ludovici-salvatoris var. garcia-fontis

Rhamnus ludovici-salvatoris var. garcia-fontis, commonly known as the Garcia-Fonti's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal interest make it a valued addition to native plant gardens.
