Rosa × leucochroa var. rosea

Rosa × leucochroa var. rosea, commonly known as the white-veined rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive white veins on its pale pink to white petals.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to the Caucasus region, specifically Georgia. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in early summer. Its botanical features include narrow, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in floral arrangements and landscape design.
