Rhodiola rosea subsp. elongata

Rhodiola rosea subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the "golden root," is a perennial herb native to the cold, mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with low temperatures and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water. Rhodiola rosea subsp.

elongata is valued for its medicinal properties, including adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.
