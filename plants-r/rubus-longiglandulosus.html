Rubus longiglandulosus

Rubus longiglandulosus, commonly known as the long-glandular blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous achenes, which are typically covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
