Rosa firmula

Rosa firmula, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of down.

Rosa firmula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
