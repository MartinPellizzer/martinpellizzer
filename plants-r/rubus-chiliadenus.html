Rubus chiliadenus

Rubus chiliadenus, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
