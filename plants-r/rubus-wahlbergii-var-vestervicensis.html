Rubus wahlbergii var. vestervicensis

Rubus wahlbergii var. vestervicensis, commonly known as the Cape strawberry, is a species of bramble native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible, red berries that are similar in appearance to strawberries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The berries are used in local cuisine and have a sweet, tangy flavor.
