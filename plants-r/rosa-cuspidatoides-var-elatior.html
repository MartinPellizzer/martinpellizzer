Rosa cuspidatoides var. elatior

Rosa cuspidatoides var. elatior, commonly known as the tall prickly rose, is a variant of the prickly rose species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with serrated leaflets, and it is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
