Restio fastigiatus

Restio fastigiatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in open, sunny areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
