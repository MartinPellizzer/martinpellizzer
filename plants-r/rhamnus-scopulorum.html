Rhamnus scopulorum

Rhamnus scopulorum, commonly known as the desert buckthorn, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil and extreme temperatures.

Rhamnus scopulorum plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat in desert ecosystems.
