Ranunculus sceleratus var. typicus

Ranunculus sceleratus var. typicus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
