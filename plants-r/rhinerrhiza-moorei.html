Rhinerrhiza moorei

Rhinerrhiza moorei, commonly known as the Moore's rhinerrhiza, is a rare orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique rhizome structure, which plays a crucial role in its survival and growth in its natural habitat. The plant typically features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Rhinerrhiza moorei is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
