Rhaphiolepis pheostemon

Rhaphiolepis pheostemon, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a flowering shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhaphiolepis pheostemon is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
