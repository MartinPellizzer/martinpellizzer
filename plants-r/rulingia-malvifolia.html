Rulingia malvifolia

Rulingia malvifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or white-flowered malvifolia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Rulingia malvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
