Rosa afzeliana var. diodus

Rosa afzeliana var. diodus, commonly known as the Kordofan rose, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features woody stems with thorns and dark green, ovate leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. Its botanical structure includes a taproot system and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
