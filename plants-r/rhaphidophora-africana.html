Rhaphidophora africana

Rhaphidophora africana, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy food source, similar to taro.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
