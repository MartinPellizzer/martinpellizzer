Rhynchospora pungens

Rhynchospora pungens, commonly known as the sharpleaf marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and other moist habitats, often forming dense stands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a sharp, pointed tip, and its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It produces both male and female flowers on separate spikelets, a characteristic of monocots.

Rhynchospora pungens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
