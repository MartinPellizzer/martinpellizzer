Ranunculus repens var. loponensis

Ranunculus repens var. loponensis, commonly known as the Loponensis buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it produces a short, upright stem.

This variant is notable for its adaptation to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan.
