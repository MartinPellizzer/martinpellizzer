Ribes dombeyanum

Ribes dombeyanum, commonly known as Dombey's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, round berries that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety and ripening stage. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ribes dombeyanum is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is also valued in horticulture for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
