Racosperma trinale

Racosperma trinale, commonly known as the Tassie wattle, is a shrub native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons, attracting various pollinators. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, with multiple stems emerging from the base.

Racosperma trinale is well adapted to the moist, temperate climate of its native habitat.
