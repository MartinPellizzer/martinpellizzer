Rhytispermum apulum

Rhytispermum apulum, commonly known as the golden thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spiny leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Rhytispermum apulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
