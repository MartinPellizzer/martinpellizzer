Rubus provincialis

Rubus provincialis, commonly known as the provincial blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and moist soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in early summer.

The fruit, a small, dark purple berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.
