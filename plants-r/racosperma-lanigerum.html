Racosperma lanigerum

Racosperma lanigerum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
