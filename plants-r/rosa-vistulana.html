Rosa vistulana

Rosa vistulana, commonly known as the Vistula rose, is a species native to Europe, particularly found in the regions around the Vistula River in Poland.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange or red hips.

Rosa vistulana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardy nature.
