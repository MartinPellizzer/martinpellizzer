Racemobambos glabra

Racemobambos glabra, commonly known as the smooth bamboo, is a unique plant that exhibits characteristics of both grasses and bamboos.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant features slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, forming a tussock-like structure. Its leaves are long, narrow, and smooth, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

While it lacks the woody structure of true bamboos, it shares some growth habits and ecological roles with its relatives.
