Rhus juglandifolia var. samo

Rhus juglandifolia var. samo, commonly known as the Samoan wingnut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It features compound leaves with three to five pairs of elliptic, leathery leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, winged fruits that disperse its seeds by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
