Rubus discolor var. amoenus

Rubus discolor var. amoenus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that turn red when ripe, distinguishing it from other Rubus species. The plant typically grows as a shrub with arching canes and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its fruit production.
