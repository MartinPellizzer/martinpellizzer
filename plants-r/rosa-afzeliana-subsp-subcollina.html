Rosa afzeliana subsp. subcollina

Rosa afzeliana subsp.

subcollina, commonly known as the Himalayan rock rose, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in pink or white.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates. Its leaves are dark green and elliptical, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
