Restiaria echinata var. platyphylla

Restiaria echinata var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed spiny restiaria, is a perennial shrub native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
