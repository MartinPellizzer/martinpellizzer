Rhodomyrtus emarginata

Rhodomyrtus emarginata, commonly known as the broadleaf rhodomyrtus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
