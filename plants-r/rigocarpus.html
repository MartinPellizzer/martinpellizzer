Rigocarpus

Rigocarpus, commonly known as the "Christmas cactus," is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented stems and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in the winter months. The plant has spines or woolly hairs along its stems, which help reduce water loss in its native arid environment. Its leaves are usually modified into phylloclades, which function as stems and store water.

Rigocarpus species are popular as indoor ornamental plants due to their ease of care and striking floral displays.
