Ranunculus antygophyllus

Ranunculus antygophyllus, commonly known as the yellow meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually brightly colored flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
