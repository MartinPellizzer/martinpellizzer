Rubus pungens var. villosus

Rubus pungens var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its hairy foliage and robust growth make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
