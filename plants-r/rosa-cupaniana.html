Rosa cupaniana

Rosa cupaniana, commonly known as the Canadian rose, is a species native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring thorny stems and large, fragrant flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, hairy foliage, and a high tolerance for cold climates.

Rosa cupaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
