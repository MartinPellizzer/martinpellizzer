Rapuntium coronopifolium

Rapuntium coronopifolium, commonly known as the crown-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it has been cultivated in various parts of the world.
