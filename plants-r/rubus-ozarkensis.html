Rubus ozarkensis

Rubus ozarkensis, commonly known as the Ozark blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are pink to white and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Rubus ozarkensis is adapted to moist, acidic soils and thrives in woodland environments.
