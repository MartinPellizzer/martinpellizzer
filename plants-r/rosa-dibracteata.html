Rosa dibracteata

Rosa dibracteata, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everlasting rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Rosa dibracteata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
