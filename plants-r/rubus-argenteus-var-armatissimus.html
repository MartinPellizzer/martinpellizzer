Rubus argenteus var. armatissimus

Rubus argenteus var. armatissimus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its silvery-gray undersides on the leaves.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
