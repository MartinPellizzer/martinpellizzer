Rhododendron longiflorum var. subcordatum

Rhododendron longiflorum var. subcordatum, commonly known as the Subcordate Rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features leathery, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape with a slightly rounded base. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
