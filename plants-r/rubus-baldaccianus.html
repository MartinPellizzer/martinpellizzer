Rubus baldaccianus

Rubus baldaccianus, commonly known as the Baldaccian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, velvety berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
