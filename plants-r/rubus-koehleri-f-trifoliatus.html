Rubus koehleri f. trifoliatus

Rubus koehleri f. trifoliatus, commonly known as the trifoliate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.

Its growth habit includes thorny stems and a shrub-like structure, typical of many Rubus varieties.
