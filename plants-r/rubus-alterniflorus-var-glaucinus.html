Rubus alterniflorus var. glaucinus

Rubus alterniflorus var. glaucinus, commonly known as the glaucous blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically tart and less sweet than common blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex floral structure and adaptability to various soil types.
