Rodgersia japonica

Rodgersia japonica, commonly known as Japanese rodgersia, is a perennial plant native to Japan and Korea.

It features large, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided and often have a silvery-blue hue. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers on tall, upright stems during the summer months. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
