Ranunculus pentandrus var. glabrescens

Ranunculus pentandrus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 10 petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three lobes, and the stems are usually hairy but become smooth as the plant matures.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
