Rubus curtipes

Rubus curtipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit develops into a dark purple or black berry.

Rubus curtipes is often used in local ecosystems for its role in providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil.
