Rosa neilreichii

Rosa neilreichii, commonly known as the Neilreich's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on pedicels. Its botanical characteristics include stipules that are usually lanceolate and persistent, and its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds.

Rosa neilreichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
