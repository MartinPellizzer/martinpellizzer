Restrepia renzii

Restrepia renzii, commonly known as the Andean slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, showcasing a range of colors from white to pale pink. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-draining soil, often growing in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
