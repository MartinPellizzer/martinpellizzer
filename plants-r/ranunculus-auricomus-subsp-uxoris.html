Ranunculus auricomus subsp. uxoris

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

uxoris, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply divided into lobes, and the stems are usually hairy.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
