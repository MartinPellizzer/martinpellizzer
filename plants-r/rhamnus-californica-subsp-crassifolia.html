Rhamnus californica subsp. crassifolia

Rhamnus californica subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as coast currant, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and southern Oregon. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coast, where it thrives in mild, moist climates.

The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. Its small, dark red berries are edible and serve as a food source for various wildlife species.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
