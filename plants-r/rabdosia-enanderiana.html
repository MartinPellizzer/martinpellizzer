Rabdosia enanderiana

Rabdosia enanderiana, commonly known as the blue-flowered rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rabdosia enanderiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
