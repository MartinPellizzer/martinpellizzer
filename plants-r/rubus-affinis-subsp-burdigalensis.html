Rubus affinis subsp. burdigalensis

Rubus affinis subsp.

burdigalensis, commonly known as the Burdigalensis blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color.

The plant typically grows in shrubland and along riverbanks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in jams and desserts.
