Rosa suavis var. polyphylla

Rosa suavis var. polyphylla, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a fragrant shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
