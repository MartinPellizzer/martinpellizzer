Rubus polioclados

Rubus polioclados, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions and valleys.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated for both landscaping and culinary purposes.
