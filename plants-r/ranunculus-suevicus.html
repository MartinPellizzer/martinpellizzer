Ranunculus suevicus

Ranunculus suevicus, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 13 petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, stout stem that is often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are deeply lobed, with a distinctive, wavy edge. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows and streambanks.

Ranunculus suevicus is an important component of alpine ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
