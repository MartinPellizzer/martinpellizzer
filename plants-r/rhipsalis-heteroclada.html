Rhipsalis heteroclada

Rhipsalis heteroclada, commonly known as the silver mist cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its long, thin, and often branched stems that resemble tendrils. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to small, hair-like structures, and it lacks the typical spines found in many cacti.

Rhipsalis heteroclada is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.
