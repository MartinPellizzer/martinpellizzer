Rosa apiifolia

Rosa apiifolia, commonly known as the celery-leaved rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble those of celery, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Rosa apiifolia is typically found in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate blooms.
