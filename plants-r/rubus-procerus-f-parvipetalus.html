Rubus procerus f. parvipetalus

Rubus procerus f. parvipetalus, commonly known as the small-petaled raspberry.

This plant is a flowering shrub characterized by its slender stems and small, pale pink to white flowers. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The fruit, a aggregate berry, is typically small and edible, though less commonly cultivated than other raspberry varieties.
