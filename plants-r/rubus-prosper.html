Rubus prosper

Rubus prosper, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry shrub, is a deciduous or evergreen shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
