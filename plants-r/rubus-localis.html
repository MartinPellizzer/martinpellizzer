Rubus localis

Rubus localis, commonly known as the local raspberry, is a species of bramble native to specific regions in North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
