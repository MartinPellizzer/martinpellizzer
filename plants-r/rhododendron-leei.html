Rhododendron leei

Rhododendron leei, commonly known as the Hainan rhododendron, is a species native to the island of Hainan in China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and acidic soil. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms.

Rhododendron leei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
