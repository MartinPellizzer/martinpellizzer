Rosa × mikawamontana

Rosa × mikawamontana, commonly known as the Mikawa mountain rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant petals that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and as a cut flower.
