Rubus × horridithyrsus

Rubus × horridithyrsus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry plant, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces showy white or pink flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and is also used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.
