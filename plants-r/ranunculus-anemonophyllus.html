Ranunculus anemonophyllus

Ranunculus anemonophyllus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, with five to eight petal-like sepals. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, lobed appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus anemonophyllus is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shady woodland settings.
