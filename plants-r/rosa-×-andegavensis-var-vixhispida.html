Rosa × andegavensis var. vixhispida

Rosa × andegavensis var. vixhispida, commonly known as the Andegavensis rose with prickles, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its robust growth and dense, thorny stems.

It features clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. It is well-suited for hedging or as a decorative shrub due to its vigorous growth habit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and functional landscaping.
