Rhododendron dilatatum var. occidentalitosaense

Rhododendron dilatatum var. occidentalitosaense, commonly known as the Western Sierra azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to white, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to mountainous regions.
