Rimaria heathii

Rimaria heathii, commonly known as the Heath's rimaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Rimaria heathii is adapted to arid environments and is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
