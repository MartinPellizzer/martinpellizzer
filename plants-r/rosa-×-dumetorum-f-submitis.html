Rosa × dumetorum f. submitis

Rosa × dumetorum f. submitis, commonly known as the alpine rose or creeping rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical classification highlights its hybrid origin, combining traits from different Rosa species.
