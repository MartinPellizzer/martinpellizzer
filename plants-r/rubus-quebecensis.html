Rubus quebecensis

Rubus quebecensis, commonly known as the Quebec raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
