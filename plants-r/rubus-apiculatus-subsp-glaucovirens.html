Rubus apiculatus subsp. glaucovirens

Rubus apiculatus subsp.

glaucovirens, commonly known as the glaucous raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive bluish-white underside.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
