Rubus radula var. coloratus

Rubus radula var. coloratus, commonly known as the colored dewberry, is a variant of the dewberry species native to certain regions of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub with trifoliate leaves and small, aggregate fruits that typically ripen to a vibrant red or purple color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces persistent, showy bracts that remain after flowering.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
