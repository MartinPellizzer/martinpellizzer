Rauvolfia balansae subsp. schumanniana

Rauvolfia balansae subsp.

schumanniana, commonly known as Schumann's rosy spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stem and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
