Rubus cymosus

Rubus cymosus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Rubus cymosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
