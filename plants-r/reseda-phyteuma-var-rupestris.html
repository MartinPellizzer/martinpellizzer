Reseda phyteuma var. rupestris

Reseda phyteuma var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock reseda, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in Mediterranean regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
