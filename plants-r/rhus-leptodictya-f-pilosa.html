Rhus leptodictya f. pilosa

Rhus leptodictya f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy sumac, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and cashews. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds and small mammals.

The hairy sumac is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
