Rubus × pesianus

Rubus × pesianus, commonly known as the Persian raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, similar to other raspberries. The plant is native to the temperate regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Additionally, its berries can be used in culinary applications, though they are generally less flavorful than those of other raspberry species.
