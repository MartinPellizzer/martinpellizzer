Rosa × centifolia var. unica

Rosa × centifolia var. unica, commonly known as the single-flowered pink, is a hybrid rose species prized for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

It is characterized by its single, full, and often cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically has dark green, glossy foliage and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in perfumery and floral arrangements.
