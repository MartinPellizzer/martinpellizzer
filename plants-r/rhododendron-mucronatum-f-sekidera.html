Rhododendron mucronatum f. sekidera

Rhododendron mucronatum f. sekidera, commonly known as the Sekidera azalea, is a unique form of the species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, deep red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
