Rheum maximowiczii

Rheum maximowiczii, commonly known as the Chinese rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall flowering stalks with small, greenish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cuisines.

The plant is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
