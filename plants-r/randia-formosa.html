Randia formosa

Randia formosa, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. It produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves.

Randia formosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
