Rosa glauca var. conica

Rosa glauca var. conica, commonly known as the conical blue rose, is a unique variant of the blue rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive conical shape, which sets it apart from typical rose forms. The plant produces small, blue-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly valued in horticulture and botanical collections.
