Rhaponticum scariosum proles heleniifolium

Rhaponticum scariosum proles heleniifolium, commonly known as the Helen's leaf rhapontic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and large, heart-shaped or lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, branched clusters, typically blooming from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are notable for their thick, leathery texture and are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

This species thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
