Rosa mollis f. obliquatula

Rosa mollis f. obliquatula, commonly known as the oblique-leafed dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its elliptical, often oblique-shaped leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers with five petals, typical of many rose species. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valued addition to gardens and ecological studies.
