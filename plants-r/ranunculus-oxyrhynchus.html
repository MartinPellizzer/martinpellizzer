Ranunculus oxyrhynchus

Ranunculus oxyrhynchus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
