Rubus hebecaulis subsp. helveconicus

Rubus hebecaulis subsp.

helveconicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and trifoliate leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
