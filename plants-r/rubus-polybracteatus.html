Rubus polybracteatus

Rubus polybracteatus, commonly known as the many-bracted blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, which resembles a blackberry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

The plant is often found in woodland areas and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
