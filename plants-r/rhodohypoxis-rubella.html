Rhodohypoxis rubella

Rhodohypoxis rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered hypoxis, is a member of the Hypoxidaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant features a short, fibrous rootstock and produces clusters of bright red flowers with yellow centers. Its foliage consists of broad, grass-like leaves that are often hairy.

Rhodohypoxis rubella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
