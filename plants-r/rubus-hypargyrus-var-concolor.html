Rubus hypargyrus var. concolor

Rubus hypargyrus var. concolor, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with stems that produce flowers and fruit in their second year. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in temperate regions.

Its berries are widely cultivated for their flavor and used in various culinary applications.
