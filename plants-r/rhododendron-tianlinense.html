Rhododendron tianlinense

Rhododendron tianlinense, commonly known as the Tianlin rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers in spring. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron tianlinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
