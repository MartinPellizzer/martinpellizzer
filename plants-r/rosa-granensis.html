Rosa granensis

Rosa granensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its botanical features include fragrant petals and a central pistil surrounded by numerous stamens.

Rosa granensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
