Rosa sabinii

Rosa sabinii, commonly known as the Sabin's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, and the plant produces small, red fruits known as hips.

Rosa sabinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
