Rosa roxburghii

Rosa roxburghii, commonly known as the Chinese rose or everblooming rose, is a species of shrub native to China, India, and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a serrated edge.

Rosa roxburghii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
