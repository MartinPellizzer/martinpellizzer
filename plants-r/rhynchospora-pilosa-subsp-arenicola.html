Rhynchospora pilosa subsp. arenicola

Rhynchospora pilosa subsp.

arenicola, commonly known as the sandhair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to sandy and coastal environments. It typically grows in open, disturbed soils, often found in dunes or salt marshes.

The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered with short, stiff hairs, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

The species is adapted to saline and well-drained conditions, making it an important component of coastal vegetation.
