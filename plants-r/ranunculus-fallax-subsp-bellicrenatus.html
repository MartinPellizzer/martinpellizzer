Ranunculus fallax subsp. bellicrenatus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

bellicrenatus, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing a fuzzy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
