Rauvolfia spectabilis

Rauvolfia spectabilis, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Rauvolfia spectabilis is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in traditional medicine.
