Rhododendron primuliflorum var. lepidanthum

Rhododendron primuliflorum var. lepidanthum, commonly known as the Lepidanthum rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, waxy texture, while its flowers range in color from white to pale pink.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
