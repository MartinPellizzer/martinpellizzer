Rubus aschoffii

Rubus aschoffii, commonly known as the aschoff's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
