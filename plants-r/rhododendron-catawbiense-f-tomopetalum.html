Rhododendron catawbiense f. tomopetalum

Rhododendron catawbiense f. tomopetalum, commonly known as the Catawba roseum rhododendron, is a unique hybrid form of the Catawba rhododendron.

It is characterized by its striking pink to rose-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub with broad, leathery leaves that provide dense foliage. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and historical significance in botanical gardens.
