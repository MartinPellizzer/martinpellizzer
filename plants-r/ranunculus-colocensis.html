Ranunculus colocensis

Ranunculus colocensis, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a distinctive waxy coating. It produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Ranunculus colocensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
