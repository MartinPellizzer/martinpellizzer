Rosmarinus officinalis var. angustifolius

Rosmarinus officinalis var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf rosemary, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are narrow and dark green, with a strong, pine-like scent. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its foliage is often used in cooking and traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.
