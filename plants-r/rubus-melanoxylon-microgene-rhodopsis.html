Rubus melanoxylon microgene rhodopsis

Rubus melanoxylon microgene rhodopsis, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in antioxidants and vitamins.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
