Ruppia maritima var. brevirostris

Ruppia maritima var. brevirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beaked sea rocket, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a submerged aquatic plant that thrives in coastal salt marshes and estuaries. The species is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and compact inflorescences. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its morphology reflects its evolutionary adaptation to fluctuating salinity and tidal conditions.
