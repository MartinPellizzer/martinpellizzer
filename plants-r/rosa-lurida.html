Rosa lurida

Rosa lurida, commonly known as the desert rose or prickly pear, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Rosa lurida is also known for its edible fruits, which are often used in traditional cuisine.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important plant in desert ecosystems.
