Rourea monticola

Rourea monticola, commonly known as the mountain rourea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in moist, shaded areas. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale yellow, and are borne in terminal or axillary inflorescences.

Rourea monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
