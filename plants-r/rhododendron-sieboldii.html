Rhododendron sieboldii

Rhododendron sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a reddish or purple hue during the growing season.

Rhododendron sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
