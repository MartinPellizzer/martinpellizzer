Rhododendron uniflorum

Rhododendron uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers range in color from white to pink, and the species is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Rhododendron uniflorum is also known for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to a variety of climatic conditions.
