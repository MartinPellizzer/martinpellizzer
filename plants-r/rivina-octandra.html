Rivina octandra

Rivina octandra, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rivina octandra is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
