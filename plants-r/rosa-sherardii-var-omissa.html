Rosa sherardii var. omissa

Rosa sherardii var. omissa, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are typical of many rose species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
