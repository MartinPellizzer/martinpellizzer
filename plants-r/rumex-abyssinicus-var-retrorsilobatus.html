Rumex abyssinicus var. retrorsilobatus

Rumex abyssinicus var. retrorsilobatus, commonly known as the Abyssinian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a wavy margin and a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine due to their tart flavor and medicinal properties.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments.
