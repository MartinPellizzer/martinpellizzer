Rubus par

Rubus par, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are actually clusters of small drupes, typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

The berries are not only edible but also have a high nutritional value and are used in various culinary applications.
