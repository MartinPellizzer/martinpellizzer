Rosa × centifolia var. floretestaceo

Rosa × centifolia var. floretestaceo, commonly known as the pale pink garden rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers.

It originates from crosses between Rosa centifolia and other Rosa species, resulting in a plant with a compact growth habit and high ornamental value. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, displaying a range of pale pink to white hues. This variety is prized for its delicate appearance and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
