Rosa molliloenquistiorum

Rosa molliloenquistiorum, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Native to high-altitude regions of South America, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
