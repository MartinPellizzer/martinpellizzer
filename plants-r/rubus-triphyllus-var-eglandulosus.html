Rubus triphyllus var. eglandulosus

Rubus triphyllus var. eglandulosus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its glandular-free stems and leaves.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its foliage is trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value in gardens.
