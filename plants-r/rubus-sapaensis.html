Rubus sapaensis

Rubus sapaensis, commonly known as the Sapa blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor, making them valuable in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
