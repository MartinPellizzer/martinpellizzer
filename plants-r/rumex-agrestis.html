Rumex agrestis

Rumex agrestis, commonly known as greater dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides, riverbanks, and wetlands. It is widely distributed across Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

Rumex agrestis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
