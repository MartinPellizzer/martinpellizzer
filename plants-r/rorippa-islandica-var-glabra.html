Rorippa islandica var. glabra

Rorippa islandica var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth island watercress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and pond edges. Its leaves are alternate, broadly ovate, and often have a smooth texture, which distinguishes it from other varieties.

It is an annual or biennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
