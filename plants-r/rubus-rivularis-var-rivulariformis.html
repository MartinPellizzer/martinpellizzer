Rubus rivularis var. rivulariformis

Rubus rivularis var. rivulariformis, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features erect or decumbent stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by edible, red, raspberry-like fruits. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and hairy beneath.

This variety is adapted to moist soils and is often found along streambanks and in marshy areas.
