Rubus denticulatus var. chloroxylon

Rubus denticulatus var. chloroxylon, commonly known as the green-stem blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish stems and leaves, which set it apart from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
