Racosperma alatum var. biglandulosum

Racosperma alatum var. biglandulosum, commonly known as the large-fruited acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant features prominently branched stems with a distinctive, flattened appearance. Its leaves are alternate and have a unique, glandular structure, contributing to its classification as a variant.

This species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
