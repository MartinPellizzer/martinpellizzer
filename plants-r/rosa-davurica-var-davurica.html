Rosa davurica var. davurica

Rosa davurica var. davurica, commonly known as the Siberian rose or the shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, broad oval leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
