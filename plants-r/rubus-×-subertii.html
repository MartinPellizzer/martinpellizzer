Rubus × subertii

Rubus × subertii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary use.
