Roegneria alaskana

Roegneria alaskana, commonly known as Alaska bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and forms dense, low-growing tufts. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Roegneria alaskana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
