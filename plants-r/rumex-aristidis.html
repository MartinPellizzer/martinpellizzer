Rumex aristidis

Rumex aristidis, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at elevations above 1,000 meters. The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges and a hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, producing yellowish-green bracts.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand cold climates.
