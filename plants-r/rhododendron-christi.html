Rhododendron christi

Rhododendron christi, commonly known as the Christi rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron christi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
