Rosa canina var. vollmanniana

Rosa canina var. vollmanniana, commonly known as the Vollmann's rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in spring, followed by orange to red hips in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
