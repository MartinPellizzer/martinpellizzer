Rubus cuspidiferus var. calliacanthus

Rubus cuspidiferus var. calliacanthus, commonly known as the prickly pear raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub native to eastern North America, typically growing in moist, wooded areas. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This variety is notable for its thorny stems and is often found in naturalized habitats across the eastern United States.
