Ranunculus grandis var. grandis

Ranunculus grandis var. grandis, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring basal leaves that are palmately lobed. Its roots are tuberous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
