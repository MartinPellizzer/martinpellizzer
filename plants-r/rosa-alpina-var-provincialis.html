Rosa alpina var. provincialis

Rosa alpina var. provincialis, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in cooler climates.
