Rosa × sabinii var. sabauda

Rosa × sabinii var. sabauda, commonly known as the Sabauda rose, is a hybrid rose species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and profuse blooming habit, producing clusters of fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers. The plant typically features thorny stems and broad, dark green foliage. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires full sun for optimal growth and flowering.
