Rubus rydbergianus

Rubus rydbergianus, commonly known as the Rydberg blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible, though they are often tart and best used in jams or pies.
