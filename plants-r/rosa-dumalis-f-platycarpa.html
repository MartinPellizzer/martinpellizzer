Rosa dumalis f. platycarpa

Rosa dumalis f. platycarpa, commonly known as the flat-fruit dog玫瑰, is a botanical variant of the Rosa dumalis species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thornless stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are hairy beneath. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and borne in clusters, often appearing in late summer.

The fruit, which is the namesake of the cultivar. is notably flattened and berry-like, containing several seeds.
