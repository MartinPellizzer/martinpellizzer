Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp. eradicatus

Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp.

eradicatus, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers with five petals and a hairy stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely toothed margins, and it produces small, greenish fruits.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
