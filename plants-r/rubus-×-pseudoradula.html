Rubus × pseudoradula

Rubus × pseudoradula, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a mix of traits from both blackberries and raspberries.
