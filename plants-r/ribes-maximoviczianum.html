Ribes maximoviczianum

Ribes maximoviczianum, commonly known as the Siberian gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, orange-red berries. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are yellow and appear in early summer.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and traditional medicine.
