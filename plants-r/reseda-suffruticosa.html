Reseda suffruticosa

Reseda suffruticosa, commonly known as yellowweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Reseda suffruticosa is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
