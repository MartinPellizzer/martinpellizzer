Rosa canina var. dumetorum

Rosa canina var. dumetorum, commonly known as the shrub rose or dog rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright orange to red hips, which are the fruit and contain the seeds. Rosa canina var. dumetorum is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
