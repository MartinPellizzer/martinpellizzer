Rhamphicarpa tubulosa

Rhamphicarpa tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular root grass, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tubular roots and grass-like foliage that forms dense mats. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, adapting to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
