Rohdea nutans

Rohdea nutans, commonly known as the nodding lily, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant is characterized by its striking, drooping inflorescences composed of small, pale green to white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rohdea nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
