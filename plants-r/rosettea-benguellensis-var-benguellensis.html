Rosettea benguellensis var. benguellensis

Rosettea benguellensis var. benguellensis, commonly known as the Ben Guellens rosette, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences of yellow flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by succulent enthusiasts.
