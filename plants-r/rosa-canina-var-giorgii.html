Rosa canina var. giorgii

Rosa canina var. giorgii, commonly known as the Giorgi rose, is a variant of the dog rose, native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in herbal remedies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
