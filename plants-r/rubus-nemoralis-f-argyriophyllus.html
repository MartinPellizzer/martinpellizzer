Rubus nemoralis f. argyriophyllus

Rubus nemoralis f. argyriophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leafed blackberry.

This variant of the blackberry plant is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a striking appearance. It is a shrub that typically grows in woodland edges and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces small, edible berries similar to those of other Rubus species, though the flavor may vary.

Its ornamental value is often appreciated in gardens due to its unique foliage.
