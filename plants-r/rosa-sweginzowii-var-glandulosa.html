Rosa sweginzowii var. glandulosa

Rosa sweginzowii var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular shrub rose, is a variant of the Sweginzow rose native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The leaves are typically pinnately compound with glandular dots, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative shrub.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
