Rapanea achradifolia

Rapanea achradifolia, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features simple, opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
