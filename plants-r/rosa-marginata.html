Rosa marginata

Rosa marginata, commonly known as the margined rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its simple, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa marginata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
