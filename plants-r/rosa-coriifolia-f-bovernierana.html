Rosa coriifolia f. bovernierana

Rosa coriifolia f. bovernierana, commonly known as the Bovenier's double-flowered rose, is a cultivar of the shrub rose species Rosa coriifolia.

It is characterized by its distinctive double flowers, which are typically pink to red in color and have a highly ornamental appearance. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, pinnately compound leaves. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its showy blooms and compact growth habit.

This variety is well-suited for garden use, particularly in borders or as a specimen plant.
