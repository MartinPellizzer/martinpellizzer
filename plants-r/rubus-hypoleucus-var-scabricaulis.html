Rubus hypoleucus var. scabricaulis

Rubus hypoleucus var. scabricaulis, commonly known as the roughstalk raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they are not widely cultivated for consumption. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant conservation.
