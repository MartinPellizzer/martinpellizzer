Rubus fuscoater subsp. hartmanii

Rubus fuscoater subsp.

hartmanii, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
