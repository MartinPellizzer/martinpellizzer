Rubus glandulosus var. foliosus

Rubus glandulosus var. foliosus, commonly known as the glandular raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing multiple drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
