Rubus vestitus var. magnificus

Rubus vestitus var. magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent bramble, is a variant of the bramble family with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and large, elliptical leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, depending on the variety. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are typically large and highly flavorful, making it a popular choice for cultivation.

This variant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
