Rosa jonesii

Rosa jonesii, commonly known as Jones' rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces hip fruits that are orange-red in color.

Rosa jonesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
