Rosa viscaria var. parvifolia

Rosa viscaria var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental gardens.
