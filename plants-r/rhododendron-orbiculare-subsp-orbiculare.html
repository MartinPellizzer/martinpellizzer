Rhododendron orbiculare subsp. orbiculare

Rhododendron orbiculare subsp.

orbiculare, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with dense, leathery leaves that are oval in shape.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in early spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in horticulture.
