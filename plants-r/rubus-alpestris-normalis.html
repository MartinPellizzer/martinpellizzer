Rubus alpestris normalis

Rubus alpestris normalis, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are armed with thorns.

This plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
