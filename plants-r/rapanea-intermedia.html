Rapanea intermedia

Rapanea intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rapanea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests. The fruit is a small, drupe that matures to a dark purple or black color, containing a single seed.
