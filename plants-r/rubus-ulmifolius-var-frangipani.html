Rubus ulmifolius var. frangipani

Rubus ulmifolius var. frangipani, commonly known as the fragrant rose.

This variant is characterized by its aromatic, pale pink to white flowers that emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus. It is a shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and edible, though tart, red berries.
