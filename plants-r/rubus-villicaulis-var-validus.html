Rubus villicaulis var. validus

Rubus villicaulis var. validus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

This variant is a member of the Rubus genus, which includes brambles and blackberries, and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Its botanical features include dense, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
