Racosperma argyrophyllum

Racosperma argyrophyllum, commonly known as silverleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often silver- or grey-hairy stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Racosperma argyrophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
