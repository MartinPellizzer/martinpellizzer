Rubus pervagus subsp. oligadenes

Rubus pervagus subsp.

oligadenes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate berry. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
