Rosa akashiensis

Rosa akashiensis, commonly known as the Japanese rose or Akashi rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces oval, orange-red hips in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
