Rubus campaniensis

Rubus campaniensis, commonly known as the Campania raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
