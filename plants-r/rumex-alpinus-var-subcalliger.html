Rumex alpinus var. subcalliger

Rumex alpinus var. subcalliger, commonly known as the alpine sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous environments.
