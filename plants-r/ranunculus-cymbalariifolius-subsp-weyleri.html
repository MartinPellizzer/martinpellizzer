Ranunculus cymbalariifolius subsp. weyleri

Ranunculus cymbalariifolius subsp.

weyleri, commonly known as the Weyleri crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or high-altitude environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble a crown, giving it its common name. Its flowers are showy, with five to eight petal-like sepals that are usually bright yellow or orange in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
