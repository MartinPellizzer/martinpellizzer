Restio papyraceus

Restio papyraceus, commonly known as the paper reed, is a member of the Restionaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically in late spring or early summer. Its fibrous stems are valued for their use in traditional crafts and papermaking.

Restio papyraceus thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or riverine habitats.
