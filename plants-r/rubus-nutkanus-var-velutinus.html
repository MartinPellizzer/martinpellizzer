Rubus nutkanus var. velutinus

Rubus nutkanus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that are softly hairy, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique adaptations to its ecological niche.
