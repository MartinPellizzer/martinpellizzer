Rosa contraorbicantula

Rosa contraorbicantula, commonly known as the orbiculate rose, is a rare species characterized by its unique, rounded petals that form a nearly spherical bloom.

It is native to the temperate regions of southern Europe and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and slightly hairy.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
