Ranunculus submarginatus

Ranunculus submarginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central pistil, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has basal leaves that are trifoliate or palmately divided, with finely toothed margins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its blooming period occurs in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.
