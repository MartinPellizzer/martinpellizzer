Rubus longifolius

Rubus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
