Ranunculus spretus

Ranunculus spretus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus spretus is known for its resilience to harsh climates and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
