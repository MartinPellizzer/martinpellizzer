Rapuntium circaeoides

Rapuntium circaeoides, commonly known as the broadleaf cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its flowers are insect-pollinated, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
