Rosa canina var. arvatica

Rosa canina var. arvatica, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a variant of the dog rose that is native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and serve as a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in mountainous or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
