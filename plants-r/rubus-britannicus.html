Rubus britannicus

Rubus britannicus, commonly known as the British bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and pies. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Rubus britannicus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
