Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp. capillaceus

Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp.

capillaceus, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant features palmately divided leaves with fine, silky hairs, and it produces showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens. Its flowers are usually borne on tall, slender stems and bloom in early spring.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
