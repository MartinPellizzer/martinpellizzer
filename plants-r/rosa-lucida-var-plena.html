Rosa lucida var. plena

Rosa lucida var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered wild rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa lucida species, which is native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms that are typically pink to red in color, with numerous petals arranged in a full, layered appearance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage, often found in woodland or scrub habitats. It produces small, yellowish fruits known as hips, which are edible and used in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in horticulture.
