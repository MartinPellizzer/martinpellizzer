Rivina humilis var. glabra

Rivina humilis var. glabra, commonly known as smooth rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to reddish in color and bloom in the spring and summer. This variety is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which set it apart from other Rivina species.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
