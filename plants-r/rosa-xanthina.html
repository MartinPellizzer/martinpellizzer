Rosa xanthina

Rosa xanthina, commonly known as the yellow rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are usually solitary and have a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa xanthina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various soil conditions.
