Rosa albicans

Rosa albicans, commonly known as the white rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white petals and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. The plant produces fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Rosa albicans is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
