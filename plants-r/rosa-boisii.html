Rosa boisii

Rosa boisii, commonly known as the Boisii rose, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color. Rosa boisii is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
