Rubus uniflorifer

Rubus uniflorifer, commonly known as the single-flower bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but may vary in taste depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining ecological balance.
