Rubus × serpentiformis

Rubus × serpentiformis, commonly known as the blackberry dewberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with a sprawling or climbing form depending on environmental conditions.
