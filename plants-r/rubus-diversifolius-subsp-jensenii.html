Rubus diversifolius subsp. jensenii

Rubus diversifolius subsp.

jensenii, commonly known as Jensen's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with varying leaflet shapes, which contribute to its botanical distinctiveness.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its floral structure includes five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rubus genus.
