Rubus sprengelii subsp. hemistemon

Rubus sprengelii subsp.

hemistemon, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and thorny stems.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupes that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This subspecies is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
