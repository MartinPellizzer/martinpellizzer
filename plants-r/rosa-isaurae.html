Rosa isaurae

Rosa isaurae, commonly known as the Isaurian rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and Syria.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa isaurae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically in perfumery and herbal medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient plant adapted to arid and rocky environments.
