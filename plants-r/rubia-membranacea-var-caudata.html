Rubia membranacea var. caudata

Rubia membranacea var. caudata, commonly known as the long-haired bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 12,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
