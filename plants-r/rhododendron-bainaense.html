Rhododendron bainaense

Rhododendron bainaense, commonly known as the Baina rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its floral display, which occurs in spring, features shades of pink and red, making it a notable ornamental plant.

Rhododendron bainaense is often found in forest understories, where it plays a role in maintaining local biodiversity.
