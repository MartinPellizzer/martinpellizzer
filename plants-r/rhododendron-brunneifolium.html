Rhododendron brunneifolium

Rhododendron brunneifolium, commonly known as the brownleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to reddish-brown, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
