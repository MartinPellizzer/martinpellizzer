Rubus boraeanus

Rubus boraeanus, commonly known as the northern raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the northern regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits. It typically grows in forested areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, temperate climates.

The berries of Rubus boraeanus are edible and have a flavor similar to other Rubus species, though they are less commonly cultivated.
