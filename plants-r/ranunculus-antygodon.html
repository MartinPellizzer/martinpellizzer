Ranunculus antygodon

Ranunculus antygodon, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces a distinctive, hairy seed head.
