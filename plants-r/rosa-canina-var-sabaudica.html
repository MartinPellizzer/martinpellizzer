Rosa canina var. sabaudica

Rosa canina var. sabaudica, commonly known as the Sabaudian rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its reddish-orange hips and slightly smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the standard Rosa canina. This variety is native to the Alpine regions of Europe, particularly in Italy and France. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
