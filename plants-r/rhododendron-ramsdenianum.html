Rhododendron ramsdenianum

Rhododendron ramsdenianum, commonly known as the Ramsden's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, usually displaying shades of pink to red.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
