Rubus battakensis

Rubus battakensis, commonly known as the Batangan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
