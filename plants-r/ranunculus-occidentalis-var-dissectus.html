Ranunculus occidentalis var. dissectus

Ranunculus occidentalis var. dissectus, commonly known as the dissected buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.

The species is characterized by its finely dissected foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
