Rosa adenodonta

Rosa adenodonta, commonly known as the mountain rose or the Sierra rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the high altitudes of South America, particularly in the Andes region. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, red fruits that contain seeds.

Rosa adenodonta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
