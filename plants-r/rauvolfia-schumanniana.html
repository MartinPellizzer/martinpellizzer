Rauvolfia schumanniana

Rauvolfia schumanniana, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and medicinal contexts.
