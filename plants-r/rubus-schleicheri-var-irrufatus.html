Rubus schleicheri var. irrufatus

Rubus schleicheri var. irrufatus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a red color. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
