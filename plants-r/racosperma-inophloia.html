Racosperma inophloia

Racosperma inophloia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Racosperma inophloia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
