Ranunculus falcatus var. orthoceras

Ranunculus falcatus var. orthoceras, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a distinctive, deeply lobed structure. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by a hairy, spiny sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
