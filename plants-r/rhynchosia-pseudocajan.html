Rhynchosia pseudocajan

Rhynchosia pseudocajan, commonly known as the false pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It is native to tropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a forage crop.

Rhynchosia pseudocajan has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
