Rosa canina var. armena

Rosa canina var. armena, commonly known as Armenian rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose that is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, hip-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine hairs, typical of the Rosa genus.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal remedies.
