Rosa mosceuton

Rosa mosceuton, commonly known as the swamp rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to wetland areas in North America, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its fragrant pink to white flowers and its ability to thrive in moist, often swampy environments. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge.

Rosa mosceuton plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
