Randia parvula

Randia parvula, commonly known as the small randia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, spherical berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Randia parvula is often found in moist, tropical environments and is adapted to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
