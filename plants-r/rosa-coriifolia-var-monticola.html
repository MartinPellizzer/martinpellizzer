Rosa coriifolia var. monticola

Rosa coriifolia var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain rose or alpine rose, is a variant of the prickly rose native to mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the flowers often have a subtle, sweet scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
