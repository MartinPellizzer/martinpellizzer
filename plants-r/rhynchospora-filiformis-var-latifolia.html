Rhynchospora filiformis var. latifolia

Rhynchospora filiformis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic and wetland biodiversity.
