Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. platyphyllus

Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the flat-leaf buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
