Rhododendron chlanidotum

Rhododendron chlanidotum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron chlanidotum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
