Ranunculus pratizanus

Ranunculus pratizanus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-brown fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus pratizanus is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
