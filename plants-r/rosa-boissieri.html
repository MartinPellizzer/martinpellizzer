Rosa boissieri

Rosa boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Rosa boissieri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
