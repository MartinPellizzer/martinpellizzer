Rubus × argenteiformis

Rubus × argenteiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and used in horticulture.
