Rosa canina var. glaucoides

Rosa canina var. glaucoides, commonly known as the grey rose or glaucous dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters, and the fruits are red, hairy hips that persist into winter. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
