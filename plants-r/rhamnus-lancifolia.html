Rhamnus lancifolia

Rhamnus lancifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, dark blue berries that are typically consumed by birds. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its leaves are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is commonly found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Rhamnus lancifolia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
