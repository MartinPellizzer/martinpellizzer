Rubus anisacanthopsis

Rubus anisacanthopsis, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, typically found in woodland and shrubland habitats. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

The leaves are usually compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name.
