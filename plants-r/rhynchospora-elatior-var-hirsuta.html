Rhynchospora elatior var. hirsuta

Rhynchospora elatior var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. The plant typically grows up to 60 cm tall, with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets.

The species is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
