Rhipsalidopsis gaertneri

Rhipsalidopsis gaertneri, commonly known as the Easter cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are segmented and often covered in small, rounded spines. The plant produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the spring and early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its relatively easy care requirements and attractive appearance.
