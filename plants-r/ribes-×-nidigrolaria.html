Ribes × nidigrolaria

Ribes × nidigrolaria, commonly known as the hybrid currant, is a deciduous shrub that results from the cross between *Ribes sanguineum* (burning bush) and *Ribes nigra* (black currant).

It typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features clusters of small, red or orange flowers in early spring. The plant produces edible, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in jams and desserts. Its foliage is glossy and dark green, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

Ribes × nidigrolaria is well-suited to temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and culinary uses.
