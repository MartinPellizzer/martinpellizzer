Ruellia indecora

Ruellia indecora, commonly known as the red-veined ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.

Ruellia indecora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
