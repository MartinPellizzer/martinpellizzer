Rubus idaeus f. angustifolius

Rubus idaeus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf raspberry, is a cultivar of the red raspberry plant.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are narrower than those of the standard Rubus idaeus. This variety produces small, bright red berries that are often used in culinary applications. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and is typically grown in temperate climates.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
