Ranunculus cooleyae

Ranunculus cooleyae, commonly known as Cooley's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with prominent black centers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

Cooley's buttercup is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in cool, temperate climates.
