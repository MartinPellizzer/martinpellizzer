Rubus ballii

Rubus ballii, commonly known as the Ball's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.

Its growth habit includes shrub-like structures that can reach heights of up to six feet.
