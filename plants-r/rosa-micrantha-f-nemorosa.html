Rosa micrantha f. nemorosa

Rosa micrantha f. nemorosa, commonly known as the shrub rose or forest rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa micrantha.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments, making it well-suited for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in moist, well-drained soils.
