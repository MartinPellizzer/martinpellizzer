Rosa agrestis subvar. osmoidea

Rosa agrestis subvar. osmoidea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed dog rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, simple leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces oval, reddish-brown hips that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
