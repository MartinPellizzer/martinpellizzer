Rubus occidentalis var. incisus

Rubus occidentalis var. incisus, commonly known as the prickly pear or wild raspberry, is a species of shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This plant is often found in open woodlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.
