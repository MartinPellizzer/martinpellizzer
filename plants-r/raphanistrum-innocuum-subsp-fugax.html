Raphanistrum innocuum subsp. fugax

Raphanistrum innocuum subsp.

fugax, commonly known as the common wild radish, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and along riverbanks, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It has a taproot system and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is often considered a weed but also serves as a food source for various insects and small animals.
