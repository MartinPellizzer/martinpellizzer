Rumex laevigatus

Rumex laevigatus, commonly known as smooth dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and riverbanks. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth on both sides, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and early summer.

The plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a food source for livestock.
