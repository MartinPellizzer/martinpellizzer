Rosa canina f. hirtistylis

Rosa canina f. hirtistylis, commonly known as the hairy-styled dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive, elongated floral buds. This form exhibits unique morphological features, including the presence of dense pubescence on the pedicels. It is typically found in the wild in Europe and parts of Asia.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive flowers and historical significance in horticulture.
