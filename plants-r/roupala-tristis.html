Roupala tristis

Roupala tristis, commonly known as the silky tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
