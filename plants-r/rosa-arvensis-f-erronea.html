Rosa arvensis f. erronea

Rosa arvensis f. erronea, commonly known as the field rose's variant form, is a botanical curiosity within the Rosaceae family.

This form is considered a natural variant of Rosa arvensis, characterized by its distinct floral and foliar features. It typically exhibits smaller, more clustered flowers compared to the standard species. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its classification as a form rather than a separate species highlights the complexity of taxonomic distinctions in botany.
