Rhytiglossa scabra

Rhytiglossa scabra, commonly known as the rough-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Rhytiglossa scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
