Rhamnus legionensis

Rhamnus legionensis, commonly known as the Jordanian buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red or black berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping in regions with similar climates.
