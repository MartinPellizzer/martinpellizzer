Rapanea thwaitesii

Rapanea thwaitesii, commonly known as the Malabar satinwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody drupe containing a single seed.

Rapanea thwaitesii is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
