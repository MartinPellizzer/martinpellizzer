Rosa canina var. purpureifolia

Rosa canina var. purpureifolia, commonly known as the purpleleaf dogwood rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the western Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinged leaves and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is well adapted to temperate climates. Its foliage turns a vibrant red in the autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its visual interest throughout the growing season.
