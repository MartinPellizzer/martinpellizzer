Roupala plinervia

Roupala plinervia, commonly known as the silky tree, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Roupala plinervia is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
