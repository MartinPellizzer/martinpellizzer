Rosa megalocarpa

Rosa megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter. The plant has fragrant, pink to red petals and is known for its vigorous growth habit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Rosa megalocarpa is a deciduous shrub that can reach heights of up to 10 feet, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
