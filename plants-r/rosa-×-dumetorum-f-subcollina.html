Rosa × dumetorum f. subcollina

Rosa × dumetorum f. subcollina, commonly known as the subalpine rock rose, is a botanical hybrid of Rosa dumetorum.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often forming low shrubs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
