Rhynchodia pierrei

Rhynchodia pierrei, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Rhynchodia pierrei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
