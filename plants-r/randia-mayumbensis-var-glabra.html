Randia mayumbensis var. glabra

Randia mayumbensis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth randia, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in horticulture for its vibrant fruiting display.
