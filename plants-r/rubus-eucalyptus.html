Rubus eucalyptus

Rubus eucalyptus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically silver-gray in color. The plant produces small, black fruits that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit yield, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
