Rubus porphyracanthus f. sectilis

Rubus porphyracanthus f. sectilis, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and dark, flavorful berries.

This plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The flowers are white to pale pink, and the berries, which ripen in late summer, are prized for their tart flavor.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
