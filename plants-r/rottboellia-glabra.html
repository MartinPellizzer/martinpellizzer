Rottboellia glabra

Rottboellia glabra, commonly known as the flatgrass or slender flatgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. It is a perennial herb that can thrive in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Rottboellia glabra is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an aggressive weed in some areas.
