Rottlera Acuminata

Rottlera rice, scientifically known as Rottlera acuminata, is a rare and fascinating plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. This evergreen shrub is characterized by its dense foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Despite its modest appearance, it holds cultural and medicinal significance in local communities. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional remedies for various ailments, highlighting its importance in herbal medicine. Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Rottlera acuminata is now classified as an endangered species, making conservation efforts critical for its survival.
