Ranunculus obtruncatus

Ranunculus obtruncatus, commonly known as the blunt-tipped buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features a basal rosette of lobed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five to seven sepals. Its flowers are often covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, bluntly truncated tip.

Ranunculus obtruncatus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
