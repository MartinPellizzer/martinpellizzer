Ranunculus bonariensis subsp. trisepalus

Ranunculus bonariensis subsp.

trisepalus, commonly known as the three-sepal buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It features showy, yellow flowers with five sepals that are distinctly three-lobed, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate along the stem. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
