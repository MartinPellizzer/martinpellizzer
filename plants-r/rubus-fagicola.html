Rubus fagicola

Rubus fagicola, commonly known as the forest raspberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in acidic soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pink and appear in the spring.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
