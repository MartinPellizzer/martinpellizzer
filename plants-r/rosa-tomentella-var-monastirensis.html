Rosa tomentella var. monastirensis

Rosa tomentella var. monastirensis, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and thrives in temperate climates. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
