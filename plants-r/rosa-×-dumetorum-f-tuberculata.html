Rosa × dumetorum f. tuberculata

Rosa × dumetorum f. tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate wild rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink to white flowers. The leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
