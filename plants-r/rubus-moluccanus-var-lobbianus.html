Rubus moluccanus var. lobbianus

Rubus moluccanus var. lobbianus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
