Rhapis flabelliformis

Rhapis flabelliformis, commonly known as the fan-leaf bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and gracefully arching, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a smooth texture and can grow up to several feet in length.

Rhapis flabelliformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
