Rhamnus davurica var. japonica

Rhamnus davurica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant is notable for its smooth, greyish bark and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Additionally, it serves as a food source for various insects and birds.
