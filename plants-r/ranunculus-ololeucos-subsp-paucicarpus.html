Ranunculus ololeucos subsp. paucicarpus

Ranunculus ololeucos subsp.

paucicarpus, commonly known as the few-ovary meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and wetlands, preferring cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces a small, erect stem.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced number of carpels, which is a key feature in its botanical classification.
