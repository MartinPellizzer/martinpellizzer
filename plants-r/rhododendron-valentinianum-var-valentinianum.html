Rhododendron valentinianum var. valentinianum

Rhododendron valentinianum var. valentinianum, commonly known as the Valentine's rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are usually pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers cool, moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

The botanical features of this plant make it a striking addition to gardens and a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
