Rubus argenteus var. conformis

Rubus argenteus var. conformis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage is dense and often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
