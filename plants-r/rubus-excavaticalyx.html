Rubus excavaticalyx

Rubus excavaticalyx, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in moist, well-drained environments.
