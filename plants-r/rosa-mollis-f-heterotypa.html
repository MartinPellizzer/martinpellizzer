Rosa mollis f. heterotypa

Rosa mollis f. heterotypa, commonly known as the soft rose bush variant, is a compact shrub characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and fragrant pink flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, ovate leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its unique foliage texture and adaptability to various growing conditions.
