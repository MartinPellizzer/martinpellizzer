Rubus conglomeratus

Rubus conglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in partial shade.

The berries are edible, though they are generally less flavorful compared to other blackberry species.
