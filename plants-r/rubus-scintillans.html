Rubus scintillans

Rubus scintillans, commonly known as the sparkling raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright red, edible fruits that appear in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically thorny. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The botanical features of Rubus scintillans make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Rosaceae family.
