Ribesiodes myrtifolium

Ribesiodes myrtifolium, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall and is edible for both humans and wildlife.

Ribesiodes myrtifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
