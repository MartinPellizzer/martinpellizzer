Rottlera glauca

Rottlera glauca, commonly known as blue rottlera, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as slopes and canyons. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives them a bluish sheen.

Rottlera glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
