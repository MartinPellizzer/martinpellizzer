Rubus koehleri var. oligacanthus

Rubus koehleri var. oligacanthus, commonly known as the Koechlin's bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
