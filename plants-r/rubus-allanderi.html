Rubus allanderi

Rubus allanderi, commonly known as the allanderi blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The berries are typically edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable resource for both wildlife and humans.
