Rubus hebecarpos var. helveticus

Rubus hebecarpos var. helveticus, commonly known as the Swiss blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
