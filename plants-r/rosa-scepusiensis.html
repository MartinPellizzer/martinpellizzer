Rosa scepusiensis

Rosa scepusiensis, commonly known as the Scepusian rose, is a rare and ancient species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with serrated edges and produces small, thorny stems. Rosa scepusiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in botanical studies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
