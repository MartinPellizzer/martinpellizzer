Rhamnus cathartica var. seravschanica

Rhamnus cathartica var. seravschanica, commonly known as the Seravschan buckthorn, is a variant of the common buckthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, black berries that are rich in anthocyanins and are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in woodland edges and streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
