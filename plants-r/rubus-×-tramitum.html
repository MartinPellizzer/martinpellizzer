Rubus × tramitum

Rubus × tramitum, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is valued for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
