Rhipsalis phyllanthus

Rhipsalis phyllanthus, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing cactus with thin, flexible stems that allow it to cling to trees and other structures. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, adding a delicate beauty to its foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a unique ability to store water in its stems.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
