Raphia regalis

Raphia regalis, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a tall, woody plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in diameter. The plant has a robust trunk with a smooth, fibrous surface and can grow up to 20 meters in height. Its roots are extensive and help stabilize the soil, making it an important component of the ecosystem.

The plant is also valued for its fibrous material, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
