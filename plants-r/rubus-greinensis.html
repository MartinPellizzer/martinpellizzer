Rubus greinensis

Rubus greinensis, commonly known as the Grein's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
