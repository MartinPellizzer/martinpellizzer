Rosa alpina var. monspeliaca

Rosa alpina var. monspeliaca, commonly known as the Alpine rose or Monspeliaca rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features simple, dark green leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink to red flowers. The plant is well adapted to cold climates and grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. It is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its horticultural value lies in its hardiness and ornamental appeal in garden settings.
