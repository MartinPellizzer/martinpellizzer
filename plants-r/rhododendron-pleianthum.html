Rhododendron pleianthum

Rhododendron pleianthum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron pleianthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
