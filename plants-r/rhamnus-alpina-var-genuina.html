Rhamnus alpina var. genuina

Rhamnus alpina var. genuina, commonly known as the alpine buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its berries are typically black or dark purple and are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
