Rapuntium cuneiforme

Rapuntium cuneiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the mustard family. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.

Rapuntium cuneiforme is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
