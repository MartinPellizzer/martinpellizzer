Rosa glauca var. transiens

Rosa glauca var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Blue Rose, is a rare and delicate variation of the blue rose.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender-colored flowers, which are often more subdued in hue compared to the more vibrant blue roses. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cooler, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a striking contrast to its floral display.

Due to its rarity and unique coloration, it is highly valued in horticulture and botanical collections.
