Rosa reticulata var. saxigena

Rosa reticulata var. saxigena, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are often used in traditional medicine.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature in garden settings.
