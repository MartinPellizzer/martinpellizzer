Rapatea spruceana

Rapatea spruceana, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant has a unique growth habit, with a short, woody stem covered in a layer of old leaf bases. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

Rapatea spruceana is often found in moist, shaded environments and plays an important role in New Zealand's native ecosystems.
