Rubus ulmifolius dalmatinus

Rubus ulmifolius dalmatinus, commonly known as the Dalmatian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisines.
