Rubus dolichocarpus

Rubus dolichocarpus, commonly known as the long-fruited blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a firm, tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant is often found in disturbed areas, along streambanks, and in open woodlands.
