Rubus arrhenii var. arrhenianthus

Rubus arrhenii var. arrhenianthus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched shrub form with thorny stems. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as raspberries, which are typically red and have a tart flavor.

It is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
