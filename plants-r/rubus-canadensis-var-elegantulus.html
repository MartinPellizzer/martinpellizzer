Rubus canadensis var. elegantulus

Rubus canadensis var. elegantulus, commonly known as the Canadian blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
