Rubus serpens var. lutescens

Rubus serpens var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow trailing raspberry, is a variant of the trailing raspberry species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, aggregate fruits that are often red or yellow in color. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
