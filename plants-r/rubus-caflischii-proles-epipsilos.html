Rubus caflischii proles epipsilos

Rubus caflischii proles epipsilos, commonly known as the epipsilos blackberry, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and clustered. Its leaves are serrated and have a rough texture, while its flowers are white with five petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
