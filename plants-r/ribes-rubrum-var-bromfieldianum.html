Ribes rubrum var. bromfieldianum

Ribes rubrum var. bromfieldianum, commonly known as the Bromfield gooseberry, is a variant of the common gooseberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the United Kingdom, typically found in woodland and hedgerow habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible but generally less flavorful than other gooseberry varieties. It has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
