Rosa communis var. villosula

Rosa communis var. villosula, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and fragrant pink flowers.

This species belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. Its stems and leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
