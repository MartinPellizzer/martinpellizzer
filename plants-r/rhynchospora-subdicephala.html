Rhynchospora subdicephala

Rhynchospora subdicephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, aiding in its spread within aquatic environments.

Rhynchospora subdicephala plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
