Rubus dumetorum f. platypetalus

Rubus dumetorum f. platypetalus, commonly known as the flat-petaled blackberry, is a subspecies of the common blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the flowers are white with five petals. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it identifiable within the Rubus genus, though it differs slightly in floral structure from other subspecies.
