Rubus × brachyanchus

Rubus × brachyanchus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to various regions of North America and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The fruit, which ripens in summer, is valued for its sweet-tart flavor and is commonly used in culinary applications.
