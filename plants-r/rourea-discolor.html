Rourea discolor

Rourea discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf rourea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
