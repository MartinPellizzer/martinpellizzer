Ribes grossularia var. liberum

Ribes grossularia var. liberum, commonly known as the wild gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Grossulariaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing a high concentration of vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
