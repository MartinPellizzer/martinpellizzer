Rosa canina var. andersonii

Rosa canina var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose.

It is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The Anderson rose is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
