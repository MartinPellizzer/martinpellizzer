Ranunculus rattanii

Ranunculus rattanii, commonly known as the rattanii buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
