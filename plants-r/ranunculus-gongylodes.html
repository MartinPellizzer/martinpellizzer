Ranunculus gongylodes

Ranunculus gongylodes, commonly known as the Chinese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, double-layered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a waxy coating, and its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is relatively easy to cultivate in garden settings.
