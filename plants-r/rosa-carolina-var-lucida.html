Rosa carolina var. lucida

Rosa carolina var. lucida, commonly known as the shining dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that appear in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often armed with thorns. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
