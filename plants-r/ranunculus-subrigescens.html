Ranunculus subrigescens

Ranunculus subrigescens, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
