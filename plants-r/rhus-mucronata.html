Rhus mucronata

Rhus mucronata, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a common food source for birds. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it often grows in moist, wooded areas.

Rhus mucronata is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
