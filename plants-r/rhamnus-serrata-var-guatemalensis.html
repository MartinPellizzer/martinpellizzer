Rhamnus serrata var. guatemalensis

Rhamnus serrata var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
