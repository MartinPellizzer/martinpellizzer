Rhododendron dawuense

Rhododendron dawuense, commonly known as the Dawu rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. It is often found in forest understories at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
