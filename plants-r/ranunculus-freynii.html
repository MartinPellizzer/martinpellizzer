Ranunculus freynii

Ranunculus freynii, commonly known as the Freyn's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate, trifoliate, or pinnate leaves that are often hairy and lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Ranunculus freynii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
