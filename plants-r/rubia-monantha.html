Rubia monantha

Rubia monantha, commonly known as the single-flowered blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 12,000 species of flowering plants. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, blue or purple, and clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming in early summer.

Rubia monantha is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
