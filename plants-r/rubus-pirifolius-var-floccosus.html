Rubus pirifolius var. floccosus

Rubus pirifolius var. floccosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
