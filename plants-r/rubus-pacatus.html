Rubus pacatus

Rubus pacatus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
