Rubus argentifolius

Rubus argentifolius, commonly known as silverleaf blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and clusters of white flowers.
