Rosa lunellii

Rosa lunellii, commonly known as the moon rose, is a rare and striking species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle lavender hue. The plant produces fragrant blooms that appear in late summer, typically in clusters of five to seven petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of white down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rosa lunellii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique beauty and historical significance.
