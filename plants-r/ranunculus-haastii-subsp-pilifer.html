Ranunculus haastii subsp. pilifer

Ranunculus haastii subsp.

pilifer, commonly known as the Haast's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that are often mistaken for petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that gives it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
