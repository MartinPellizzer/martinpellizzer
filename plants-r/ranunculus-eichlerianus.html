Ranunculus eichlerianus

Ranunculus eichlerianus, commonly known as the Eichler's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a short, rhizomatous root system and thrives in well-drained, moist soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
