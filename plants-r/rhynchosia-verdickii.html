Rhynchosia verdickii

Rhynchosia verdickii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
