Rhamnus saxatilis subsp. rhodopea

Rhamnus saxatilis subsp.

rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope shrub, is a perennial shrub native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria. It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
