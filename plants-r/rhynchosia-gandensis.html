Rhynchosia gandensis

Rhynchosia gandensis, commonly known as the African bean or Gando bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Rhynchosia gandensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
