Rosa × hollandica

Rosa × hollandica, commonly known as the Dutch rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, fragrant flowers that typically range from white to deep pink. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and is known for its hardy nature, thriving in a variety of soil types. It produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The Dutch rose is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
