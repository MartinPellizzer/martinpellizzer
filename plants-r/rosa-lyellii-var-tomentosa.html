Rosa lyellii var. tomentosa

Rosa lyellii var. tomentosa, commonly known as the Lyell's fuzzy rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of grayish-white hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and blooming display.
