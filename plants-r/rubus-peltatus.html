Rubus peltatus

Rubus peltatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Rubus peltatus is an important species for both wildlife and human consumption.
