Rubus hedycarpus var. apricus

Rubus hedycarpus var. apricus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry or apricot raspberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. Its flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and has a tart, aromatic flavor.
