Ranunculus rastetteri

Ranunculus rastetteri, commonly known as the Rastetter's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing a preference for well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are palmately lobed, with a distinctive hairy texture. Its flowers are showy, with five to seven petals that range in color from white to pale yellow, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Ranunculus rastetteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cup-shaped blooms.
