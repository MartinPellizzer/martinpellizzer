Rosa × centifolia vulgaris

Rosa × centifolia vulgaris, commonly known as the centifolia rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its numerous, fragrant, pink or white petals that give it a full, fluffy appearance. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It produces small, red fruits known as hips, which are used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.

The shrub typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
