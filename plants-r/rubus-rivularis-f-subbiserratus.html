Rubus rivularis f. subbiserratus

Rubus rivularis f. subbiserratus, commonly known as the narrow-toothed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces small, dark purple or black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more pronounced, narrow teeth on the leaf margins compared to the typical form.
