Rhododendron fulvoides

Rhododendron fulvoides, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Rhododendron fulvoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
