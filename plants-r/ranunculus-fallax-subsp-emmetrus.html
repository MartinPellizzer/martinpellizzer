Ranunculus fallax subsp. emmetrus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

emmetrus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has a short growing season, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
