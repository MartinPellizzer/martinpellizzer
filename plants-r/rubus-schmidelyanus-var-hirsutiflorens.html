Rubus schmidelyanus var. hirsutiflorens

Rubus schmidelyanus var. hirsutiflorens, commonly known as the hairy-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its distinctive floral and foliar features.

It typically grows in temperate regions, often in woodland or shrubland habitats, where it thrives in partial shade. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for various wildlife species.
