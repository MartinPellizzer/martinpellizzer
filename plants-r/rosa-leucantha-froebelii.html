Rosa leucantha froebelii

Rosa leucantha froebelii, commonly known as the Froebel's white rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in Guatemala and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural habitats.
