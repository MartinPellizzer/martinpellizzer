Rorippa multicaulis

Rorippa multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed watercress, is a flowering plant native to moist or aquatic environments.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its leaves are alternate, floating or submerged, and often have a peppery taste similar to watercress.

Rorippa multicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves.
