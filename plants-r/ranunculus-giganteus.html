Ranunculus giganteus

Ranunculus giganteus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with numerous petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, eye-catching blooms.
