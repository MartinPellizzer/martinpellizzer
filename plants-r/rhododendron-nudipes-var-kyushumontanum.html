Rhododendron nudipes var. kyushumontanum

Rhododendron nudipes var. kyushumontanum, commonly known as the Kyushu mountain rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, leathery foliage with dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler, humid climates.
