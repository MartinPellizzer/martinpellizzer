Rollinia jucunda

Rollinia jucunda, commonly known as the golden star soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is typically green when mature, with a spiny exterior and a soft, creamy flesh containing black seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a mild, sweet flavor.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
