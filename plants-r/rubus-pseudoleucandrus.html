Rubus pseudoleucandrus

Rubus pseudoleucandrus, commonly known as the white-tailed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and Florida. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a small, tart berry, is typically harvested in the summer months.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
