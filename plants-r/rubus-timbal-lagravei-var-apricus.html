Rubus timbal-lagravei var. apricus

Rubus timbal-lagravei var. apricus, commonly known as the apricot raspberry, is a rare variant of the Rubus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, edible fruits that resemble apricots in color and texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical and subtropical forests. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces pale pink to white flowers during the spring season.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
