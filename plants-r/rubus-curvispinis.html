Rubus curvispinis

Rubus curvispinis, commonly known as the hooked thorned raspberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in woodland or hedgerow environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is a hairy, red aggregate berry, is edible and often used in jams and preserves.
