Rhododendron pemakoense

Rhododendron pemakoense, commonly known as the Pemako rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern Himalayas, specifically in Nepal and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments with acidic soil. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron pemakoense plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.
