Ranunculus occidentalis subsp. insularis

Ranunculus occidentalis subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western Pacific islands. It features delicate, palmate leaves with a hairy texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers have five to nine petals and are often accompanied by a cluster of small, yellow sepals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact growth compared to other ranunculus species.
