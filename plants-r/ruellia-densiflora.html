Ruellia densiflora

Ruellia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered ruellia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, blue to purple flowers that bloom from summer to fall. Ruellia densiflora is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils, as long as it receives adequate moisture.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period.
