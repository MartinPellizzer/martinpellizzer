Rosa Lacteola

Rosa lacteola is a rare and delicate species of rose known for its unique white petals and subtle fragrance. Native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, this plant thrives in cool, high-altitude environments. Its name, meaning "milky rose," refers to the pale, almost translucent appearance of its flowers. Despite its beauty, Rosa lacteola is critically endangered due to habitat loss and overharvesting. Conservation efforts are underway to protect this elusive and enchanting botanical treasure.
