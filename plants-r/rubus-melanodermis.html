Rubus melanodermis

Rubus melanodermis, commonly known as blackstem bramble.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is typically dark purple to black when mature.

The plant thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
