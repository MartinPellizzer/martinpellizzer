Ribes nebularum

Ribes nebularum, commonly known as the cloud currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated edge, while its berries are small, yellow, and edible.

Ribes nebularum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.
