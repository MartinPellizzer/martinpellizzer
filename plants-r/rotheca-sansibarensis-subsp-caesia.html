Rotheca sansibarensis subsp. caesia

Rotheca sansibarensis subsp.

caesia, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or near the shoreline.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies in local communities.
