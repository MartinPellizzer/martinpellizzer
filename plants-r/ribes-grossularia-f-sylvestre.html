Ribes grossularia f. sylvestre

Ribes grossularia f. sylvestre, commonly known as wild gooseberry, is a subspecies of the gooseberry plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and moist soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible but often tart and acidic. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and have thorn-like projections.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
