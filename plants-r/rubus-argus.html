Rubus argus

Rubus argus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to red and then to black as they mature. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus argus is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
