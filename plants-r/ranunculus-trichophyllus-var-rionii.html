Ranunculus trichophyllus var. rionii

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. rionii, commonly known as the Rion Mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive pattern of dark veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and the stems are often hairy.

This variety is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful blooms.
