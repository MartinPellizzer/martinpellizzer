Reaumuria vermiculata

Reaumuria vermiculata, commonly known as the sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its root system is extensive and adapted to stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Reaumuria vermiculata is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in preventing soil erosion.
