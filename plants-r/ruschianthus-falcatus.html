Ruschianthus falcatus

Ruschianthus falcatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hongkongia, is a striking member of the Boraginaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences, adding a decorative element to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
