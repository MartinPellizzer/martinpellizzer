Ranunculus dissectus

Ranunculus dissectus, commonly known as the dissected buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent central stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in calcareous soils.

Ranunculus dissectus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its intricate foliage and vibrant blooms.
