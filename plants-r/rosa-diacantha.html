Rosa diacantha

Rosa diacantha, commonly known as the double-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa diacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
