Rosa rhombea

Rosa rhombea, commonly known as the diamondleaf rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Rosa rhombea is a deciduous shrub that can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
