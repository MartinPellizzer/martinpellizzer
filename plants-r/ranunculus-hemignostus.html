Ranunculus hemignostus

Ranunculus hemignostus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitat.
