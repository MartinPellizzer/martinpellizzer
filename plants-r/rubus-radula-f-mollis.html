Rubus radula f. mollis

Rubus radula f. mollis, commonly known as the soft-fruited blackberry.

It is a variant of the Rubus radula species, characterized by its soft, velvety texture on the fruit surface. This plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which is small and dark purple when mature, is often used in local culinary traditions.
