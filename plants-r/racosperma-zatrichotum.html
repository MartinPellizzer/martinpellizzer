Racosperma zatrichotum

Racosperma zatrichotum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets, and it produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
