Rubus walteri

Rubus walteri, commonly known as Walter's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Rubus walteri is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential for use in native plant landscaping.
