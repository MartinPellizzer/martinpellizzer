Rhus wellmanii

Rhus wellmanii, commonly known as the wellman hickory, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and cashew. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles. Rhus wellmanii produces small, red drupes that are edible for some wildlife but generally not for humans.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can be a component of early successional habitats.
