Rhamnus picenensis

Rhamnus picenensis, commonly known as the Picentine buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Italy.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
