Rosa × curvispina

Rosa × curvispina, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, and has dark green, lobed leaves with serrated edges. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that contain seeds, and the plant is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

Rosa × curvispina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
