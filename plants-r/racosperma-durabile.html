Racosperma durabile

Racosperma durabile, commonly known as the everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, woody shrub form. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma durabile is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
