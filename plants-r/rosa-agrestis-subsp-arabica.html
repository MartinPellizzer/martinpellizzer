Rosa agrestis subsp. arabica

Rosa agrestis subsp.

arabica, commonly known as the Arabian wild rose, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in pink or white.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture. Rosa agrestis subsp.

arabica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
