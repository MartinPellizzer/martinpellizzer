Rubus dinacanthus

Rubus dinacanthus, commonly known as the Moldavian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs with edible fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland and shrubland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, a aggregate berry, is dark purple when mature and is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
