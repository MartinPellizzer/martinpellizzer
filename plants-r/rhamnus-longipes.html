Rhamnus longipes

Rhamnus longipes, commonly known as the long-fruited buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by long, narrow fruits that ripen to a dark red or black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
