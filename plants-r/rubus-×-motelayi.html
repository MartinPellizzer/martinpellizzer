Rubus × motelayi

Rubus × motelayi, commonly known as the Motelayi blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

This species is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
