Rubus warmingii

Rubus warmingii, commonly known as the Warming's raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated margins.

The berries, which are edible, are small and aggregate, containing multiple tiny seeds.
