Ranunculus diversifolius proles vaginatus

Ranunculus diversifolius proles vaginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive, narrow shape.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes found in botanical gardens.
