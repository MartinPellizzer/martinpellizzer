Rubus idaeus chrysocarpus

Rubus idaeus chrysocarpus, commonly known as the golden-fruited raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its unique golden-yellow fruits.

This species belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and it produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny achenes. The plant is known for its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental and edible qualities.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
