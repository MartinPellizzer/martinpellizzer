Riedelia maxima

Riedelia maxima, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its spiny, leaf-like structures. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Riedelia maxima is a slow-growing species that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
