Rosa canina subsp. cariotii

Rosa canina subsp.

cariotii, commonly known as the Carpathian rose, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. Its hips, which are the fruit, are typically red or orange and contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and mountainous habitats, showing resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
