Reseda papillosa

Reseda papillosa, commonly known as the silky reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Reseda papillosa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and easy cultivation.
