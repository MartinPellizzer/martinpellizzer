Rosa lactiflora f. polyacantha

Rosa lactiflora f. polyacantha, commonly known as the many-thorned milk rose, is a hybrid form of the milk rose, characterized by its profuse thorn production.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces large, fragrant flowers with a white to pale pink coloration. The plant is native to China and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its foliage is dark green and densely packed, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
