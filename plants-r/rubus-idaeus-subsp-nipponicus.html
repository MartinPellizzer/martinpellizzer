Rubus idaeus subsp. nipponicus

Rubus idaeus subsp.

nipponicus, commonly known as Japanese raspberry, is a subspecies native to East Asia, particularly Japan and Korea. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, often with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

Botanically, it is closely related to the common raspberry and shares similar growth habits and fruit structures.
