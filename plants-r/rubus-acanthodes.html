Rubus acanthodes

Rubus acanthodes, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Rubus acanthodes is often found in open woodlands and along coastal regions.
