Rosa hausimardonica

Rosa hausimardonica, commonly known as the Hausimardon rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces edible rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
