Rubus remotior

Rubus remotior, commonly known as the remote raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
