Roegneria komarovii

Roegneria komarovii, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low temperatures and poor soil nutrients.

Roegneria komarovii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in arid or disturbed areas.
