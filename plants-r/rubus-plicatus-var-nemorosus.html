Rubus plicatus var. nemorosus

Rubus plicatus var. nemorosus, commonly known as the European bramble or blackberry bush, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of white flowers that develop into edible berries. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils. Its berries, which ripen in late summer, are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.

The species is also notable for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
