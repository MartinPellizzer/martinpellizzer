Rubus dumetorum f. orthostachys

Rubus dumetorum f. orthostachys, commonly known as the erect-stemmed blackberry.

It is a subspecies of the blackberry plant characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible berries.
