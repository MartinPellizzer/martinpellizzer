Rubus chlorocladus subsp. transsilvanicus

Rubus chlorocladus subsp.

transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe, particularly Romania and the Carpathian region. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils, making it a hardy species adapted to mountainous environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the regional flora and a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
