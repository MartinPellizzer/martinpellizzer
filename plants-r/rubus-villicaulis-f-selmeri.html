Rubus villicaulis f. selmeri

Rubus villicaulis f. selmeri, commonly known as the Selmer's brambles, is a botanical variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus family. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in supporting local wildlife.
