Rockhausenia pectinata

Rockhausenia pectinata, commonly known as the featherleaf rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant's foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Rockhausenia pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
