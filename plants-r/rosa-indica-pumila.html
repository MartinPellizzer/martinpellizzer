Rosa indica pumila

Rosa indica pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Indian rose, is a compact shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and a woody stem structure typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and subtle fragrance.
