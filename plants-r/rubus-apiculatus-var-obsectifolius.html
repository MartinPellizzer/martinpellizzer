Rubus apiculatus var. obsectifolius

Rubus apiculatus var. obsectifolius, commonly known as the softleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching canes and small, trifoliate leaves that are soft to the touch. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less showy than other blackberry varieties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is relatively easy to grow in home gardens.
