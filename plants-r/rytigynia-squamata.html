Rytigynia squamata

Rytigynia squamata, commonly known as the silverleaf rytigynia, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and are covered in fine, whitish hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
