Rubus senticops

Rubus senticops, commonly known as the sticky raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its distinctive, sticky hairs on the stems and leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have serrated edges.

Rubus senticops is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
