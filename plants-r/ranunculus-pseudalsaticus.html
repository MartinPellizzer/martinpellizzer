Ranunculus pseudalsaticus

Ranunculus pseudalsaticus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green, berry-like fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus pseudalsaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
