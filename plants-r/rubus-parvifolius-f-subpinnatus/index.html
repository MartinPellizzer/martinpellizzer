Rubus Parvifolius F. Subpinnatus

Japanese raspberry, scientifically known as Rubus parvifolius f. subpinnatus, is a unique and attractive plant native to Japan. It is a compact shrub with small, glossy leaves and vibrant red berries that add a splash of color to gardens. This variety is a fascinata form of the common raspberry plant, featuring finely divided, feathery leaflets. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts and landscape designers. Growing in partial shade to full sun, it thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal maintenance.
