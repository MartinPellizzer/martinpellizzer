Rubus × semiflexuosus

Rubus × semiflexuosus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in shrub form, with compound leaves and white to pale pink flowers. Its berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.
