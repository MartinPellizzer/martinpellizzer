Rhaphidophora amplissima

Rhaphidophora amplissima, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Rhaphidophora amplissima is popular as a houseplant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
