Rhododendron stonori

Rhododendron stonori, commonly known as the Storni rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron stonori is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
