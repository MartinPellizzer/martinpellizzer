Ranunculus auricomus subsp. incrassatus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

incrassatus, commonly known as the thick-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader, more robust leaves compared to the typical form of R. auricomus. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is often found in calcareous soils and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
