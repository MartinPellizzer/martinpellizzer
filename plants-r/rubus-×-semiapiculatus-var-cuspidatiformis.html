Rubus × semiapiculatus var. cuspidatiformis

Rubus × semiapiculatus var. cuspidatiformis, commonly known as the prickly pear raspberry.

This variant is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically produces small, red or yellow berries that are clustered together. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural study.
