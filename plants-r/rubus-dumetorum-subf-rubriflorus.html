Rubus dumetorum subf. rubriflorus

Rubus dumetorum subf.

rubriflorus, commonly known as the redflower bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The flowers are usually white or pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.
