Roegneria villosa subsp. coerulea

Roegneria villosa subsp.

coerulea, commonly known as blue false brome, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Roegneria villosa subsp.

coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky and grassland habitats.
