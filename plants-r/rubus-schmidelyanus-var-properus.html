Rubus schmidelyanus var. properus

Rubus schmidelyanus var. properus, commonly known as the Schmidel's blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in Guatemala and Honduras. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a hairy underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
