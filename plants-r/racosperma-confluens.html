Racosperma confluens

Racosperma confluens, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Racosperma confluens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
