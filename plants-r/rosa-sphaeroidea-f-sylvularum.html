Rosa sphaeroidea f. sylvularum

Rosa sphaeroidea f. sylvularum, commonly known as the woodland rose, is a compact, shrubby plant native to the forests of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, bushy habit. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually borne singly or in clusters, and the plant is noted for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Rosa sphaeroidea f. sylvularum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to naturalize in woodland gardens.
