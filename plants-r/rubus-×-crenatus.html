Rubus × crenatus

Rubus × crenatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry or bramble, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers. Its berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in jams and wines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
