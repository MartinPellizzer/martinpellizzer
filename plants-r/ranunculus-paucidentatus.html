Ranunculus paucidentatus

Ranunculus paucidentatus, commonly known as the few-toothed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its attractive appearance and ecological importance.

The species is relatively rare and is often found in specialized microhabitats within its native range.
