Rosa × bruanthii

Rosa × bruanthii, commonly known as the Brant's rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically features upright growth with dark green, trifoliate leaves and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Rosa × bruanthii is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
