Rosa micrantha f. lemanii

Rosa micrantha f. lemanii, commonly known as the Leman's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, thornless stems that form a rounded, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it is well adapted to dry, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in garden settings.
