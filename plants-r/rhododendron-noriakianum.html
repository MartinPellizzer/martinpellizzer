Rhododendron noriakianum

Rhododendron noriakianum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in the spring, which are typically 5-8 cm in diameter. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
