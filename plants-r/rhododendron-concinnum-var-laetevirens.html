Rhododendron concinnum var. laetevirens

Rhododendron concinnum var. laetevirens, commonly known as the purpleleaf azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant purple to deep green, depending on the season. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
