Rosa oxyodon var. pleiantha

Rosa oxyodon var. pleiantha, commonly known as the many-flowered dogwood rose, is a variant of the Rosa oxyodon species.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing an attractive appearance throughout the growing season.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in horticultural settings.
