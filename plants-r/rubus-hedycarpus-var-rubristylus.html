Rubus hedycarpus var. rubristylus

Rubus hedycarpus var. rubristylus, commonly known as the red-styled blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its ornamental value.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
