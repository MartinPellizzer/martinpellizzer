Rubus lucens

Rubus lucens, commonly known as the shining blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Rubus lucens is valued for its edible berries and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
