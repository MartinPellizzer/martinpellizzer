Rosa afzeliana var. selandica

Rosa afzeliana var. selandica, commonly known as the Selandic rose, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
