Rosa subduplicata var. albiflora

Rosa subduplicata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered double rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa subduplicata species.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its profuse blooming of double, white petals. The plant produces small, yellow to orange-colored hips in the autumn, which are a feature of the Rosa genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
