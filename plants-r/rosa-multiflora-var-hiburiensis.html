Rosa multiflora var. hiburiensis

Rosa multiflora var. hiburiensis, commonly known as the Hiburiensis shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features multiple stems with dense, thorny growth and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its blossoms. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and adaptability.

The flowers can be used to make rose hip tea, which is rich in vitamin C.
