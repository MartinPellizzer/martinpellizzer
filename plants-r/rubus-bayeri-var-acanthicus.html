Rubus bayeri var. acanthicus

Rubus bayeri var. acanthicus, commonly known as the bayberry raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red or black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically alternate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in moist, acidic soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
