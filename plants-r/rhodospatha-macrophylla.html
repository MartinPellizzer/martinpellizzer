Rhodospatha macrophylla

Rhodospatha macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf rhodospatha, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant is notable for its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Rhodospatha macrophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
