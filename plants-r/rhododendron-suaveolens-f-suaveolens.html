Rhododendron suaveolens f. suaveolens

Rhododendron suaveolens f. suaveolens, commonly known as the Sweet-scented Rhododendron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant is native to the Himalayas and thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils. Its fragrance is a notable feature, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
