Rubus glomeratus var. gracilis

Rubus glomeratus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender dewberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible, red, hairy berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
