Rubus villicaulis f. obotriticus

Rubus villicaulis f. obotriticus, commonly known as the fuzzy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of eastern North America. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
