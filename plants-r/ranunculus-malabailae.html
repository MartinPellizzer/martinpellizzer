Ranunculus malabailae

Ranunculus malabailae, commonly known as the Malabaila buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive central reproductive structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
