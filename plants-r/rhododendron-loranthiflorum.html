Rhododendron loranthiflorum

Rhododendron loranthiflorum, commonly known as the Indian rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
