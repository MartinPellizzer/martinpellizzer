Rubus approximatus

Rubus approximatus, commonly known as the common blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupelets. The plant is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist to dry habitats. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are widely used in culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
