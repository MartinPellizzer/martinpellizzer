Rapuntium pedunculare

Rapuntium pedunculare, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of leaves that are broadly ovate with a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its flowering stems are short and often bear multiple flower heads.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
