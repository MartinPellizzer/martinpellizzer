Rhodolirium montanum

Rhodolirium montanum, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Rhodolirium montanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
