Rosa afzeliana var. bernardensis

Rosa afzeliana var. bernardensis, commonly known as the Bernard's rose, is a shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
