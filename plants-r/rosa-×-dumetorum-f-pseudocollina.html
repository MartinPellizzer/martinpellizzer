Rosa × dumetorum f. pseudocollina

Rosa × dumetorum f. pseudocollina, commonly known as the creeping pink rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
