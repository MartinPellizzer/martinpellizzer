Rosa alpina var. ravaudiana

Rosa alpina var. ravaudiana, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often have thorns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
