Rotheca caerulea

Rotheca caerulea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Rotheca caerulea is typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
