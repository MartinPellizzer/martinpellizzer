Ritchiea polypetala

Ritchiea polypetala, commonly known as the velvetleaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a dense crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with the male cones being yellow and the female cones reddish-brown, which are essential for its reproduction. Ritchiea polypetala is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments and is known for its slow growth rate.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
