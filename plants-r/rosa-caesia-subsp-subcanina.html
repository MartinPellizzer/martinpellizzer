Rosa caesia subsp. subcanina

Rosa caesia subsp.

subcanina, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant blooms that attract pollinators. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in soft, grayish hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
