Rubus × roemeri

Rubus × roemeri, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus leucodermis.

It is native to coastal regions of California and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and edible. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are covered in fine, silvery hairs.

Rubus × roemeri thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
