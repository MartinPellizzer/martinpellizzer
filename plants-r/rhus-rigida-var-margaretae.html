Rhus rigida var. margaretae

Rhus rigida var. margaretae, commonly known as the Margaretae sumac, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 leaflets, with serrated margins and a reddish hue in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
