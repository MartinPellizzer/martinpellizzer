Rubus fruticosus subsp. rhamnifolius

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

rhamnifolius, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

The plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas. It is a perennial species that produces flowers in spring and fruits in summer.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it an important part of many ecosystems and a valuable resource for wildlife.
