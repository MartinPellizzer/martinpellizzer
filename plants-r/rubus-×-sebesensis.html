Rubus × sebesensis

Rubus × sebesensis, commonly known as the Sebesen's blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that typically ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant is native to regions in Eastern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
