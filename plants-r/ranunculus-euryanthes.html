Ranunculus euryanthes

Ranunculus euryanthes, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with dark centers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and grows in moist, shady environments. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and toxic properties.

Ranunculus euryanthes is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
