Rosa canina rubra

Rosa canina rubra, commonly known as the red dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa canina rubra is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in woodland edges and open scrublands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
