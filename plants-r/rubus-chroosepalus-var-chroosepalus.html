Rubus chroosepalus var. chroosepalus

Rubus chroosepalus var. chroosepalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated margin, with a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its edible fruits and has been the subject of botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
