Rubus argenteus microgene aurensis

Rubus argenteus microgene aurensis, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, red-fruited berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
