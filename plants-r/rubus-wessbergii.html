Rubus wessbergii

Rubus wessbergii, commonly known as the Wessberg bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Scandinavia, particularly Sweden and Norway, and thrives in coastal and sandy habitats. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. Its fruits, which are small and aggregate, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Rubus wessbergii plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing coastal dunes.
