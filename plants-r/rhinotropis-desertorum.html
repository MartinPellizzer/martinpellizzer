Rhinotropis desertorum

Rhinotropis desertorum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow composite flowers, which are similar in structure to those of dandelions. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Rhinotropis desertorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for native pollinators.
