Rubus hebecaulis var. pesianus

Rubus hebecaulis var. pesianus, commonly known as the Persian raspberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some parts of the world.
