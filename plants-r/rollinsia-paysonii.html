Rollinsia paysonii

Rollinsia paysonii, commonly known as the Payson's rollinsia, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Florida.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters near the water's surface. Its roots are adapted for anchoring in shallow water, and it plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients.

Rollinsia paysonii is an important species for ecological studies due to its specialized adaptations to aquatic environments.
