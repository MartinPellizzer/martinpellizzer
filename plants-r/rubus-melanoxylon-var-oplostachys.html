Rubus melanoxylon var. oplostachys

Rubus melanoxylon var. oplostachys, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
