Rapuntium hirsutum

Rapuntium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy cabbagewort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
