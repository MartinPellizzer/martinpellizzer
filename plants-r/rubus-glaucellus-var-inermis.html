Rubus glaucellus var. inermis

Rubus glaucellus var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and edible cultivation.
