Rubus slavonicus

Rubus slavonicus, commonly known as Slavonic blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white and are followed by the fruiting bodies that develop in late summer.
