Ranunculus pseudolyratus

Ranunculus pseudolyratus, commonly known as the false larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or white petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
