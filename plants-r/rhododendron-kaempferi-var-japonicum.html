Rhododendron kaempferi var. japonicum

Rhododendron kaempferi var. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil.

Its flowers range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the specific cultivar. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
