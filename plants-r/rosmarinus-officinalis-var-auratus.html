Rosmarinus officinalis var. auratus

Rosmarinus officinalis var. auratus, commonly known as golden rosemary, is a cultivar of the rosemary plant characterized by its striking golden foliage.

This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. The leaves are narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine, golden hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Like other rosemary species, it is aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
