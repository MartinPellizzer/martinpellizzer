Rubus floribundus subsp. selerianus

Rubus floribundus subsp.

selerianus, commonly known as the Selerian blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
