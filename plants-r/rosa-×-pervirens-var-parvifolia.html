Rosa × pervirens var. parvifolia

Rosa × pervirens var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf everblooming rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces rosehips that are often used in herbal remedies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its hardiness and ability to thrive in mild climates.
