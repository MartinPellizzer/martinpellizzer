Rosa × glaucophylla

Rosa × glaucophylla, commonly known as the Glaucophylla rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and abundant production of fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers. The plant typically features broad, leathery leaves with a bluish-gray underside, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This rose is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
