Rosa viscaria var. echinocarpa

Rosa viscaria var. echinocarpa, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The leaves are typically pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
