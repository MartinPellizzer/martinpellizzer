Rosa triflora

Rosa triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters of three, hence its common name. Rosa triflora is a deciduous shrub that produces rose hips, which are the fruit of the plant.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its historical use in traditional medicine.
