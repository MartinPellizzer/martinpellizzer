Robinia umbraculifera

Robinia umbraculifera, commonly known as the sourwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by elongated, bean-like fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant has a deep root system and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
