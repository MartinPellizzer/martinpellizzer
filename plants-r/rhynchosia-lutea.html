Rhynchosia lutea

Rhynchosia lutea, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and produces pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Rhynchosia lutea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
