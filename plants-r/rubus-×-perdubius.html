Rubus × perdubius

Rubus × perdubius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a vigorous, thorny shrub that typically grows in temperate regions. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are commonly referred to as blackberries, and are valued for their flavor and culinary uses. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The plant is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
