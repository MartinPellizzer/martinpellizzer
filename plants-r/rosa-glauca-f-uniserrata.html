Rosa glauca f. uniserrata

Rosa glauca f. uniserrata, commonly known as the single-toothed blue rose, is a rare and beautiful cultivar of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender flowers, which are typically single or semi-double in form. The leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, with a distinctive blue-gray hue on the underside. This variety is prized for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
