Rosa subglobosa

Rosa subglobosa, commonly known as the globular rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its foliage consists of glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges.

Rosa subglobosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
