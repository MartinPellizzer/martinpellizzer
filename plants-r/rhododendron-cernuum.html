Rhododendron cernuum

Rhododendron cernuum, commonly known as the nodding rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
