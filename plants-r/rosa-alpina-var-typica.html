Rosa alpina var. typica

Rosa alpina var. typica, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem that becomes more branched with age.

Rosa alpina var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
