Rubus × pergratus

Rubus × pergratus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus argutus. This species is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
