Rosa × collina var. flexuosa

Rosa × collina var. flexuosa, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit, with arching stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are dark green and slightly serrated.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
