Ranunculus sericocephalus

Ranunculus sericocephalus, commonly known as the silky-headed buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, with a hairy, silky appearance on the petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ranunculus sericocephalus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
