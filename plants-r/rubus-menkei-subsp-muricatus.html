Rubus menkei subsp. muricatus

Rubus menkei subsp.

muricatus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

Its horticultural value is also recognized for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
