Rubus acutifrons var. amplifrons

Rubus acutifrons var. amplifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that mature to a dark purple or black color. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges and a hairy underside.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
