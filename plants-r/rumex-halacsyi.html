Rumex halacsyi

Rumex halacsyi, commonly known as the Hungarian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
