Rubus purpuratus subsp. venustulus

Rubus purpuratus subsp.

venustulus, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and river valleys.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
