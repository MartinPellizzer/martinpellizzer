Rosa reuteri f. marsica

Rosa reuteri f. marsica, commonly known as the Marsican rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the Apennine Mountains of central Italy.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral characteristics and habitat preferences, making it a notable example of regional botanical diversity.
