Rosa pendulina f. balsamea

Rosa pendulina f. balsamea, commonly known as the Balsamea climbing rose, is a hybrid form of the pendulous rose.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
