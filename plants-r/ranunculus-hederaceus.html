Ranunculus hederaceus

Ranunculus hederaceus, commonly known as the ivy-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, resembling the foliage of ivy, and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
