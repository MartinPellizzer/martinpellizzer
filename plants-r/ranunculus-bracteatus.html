Ranunculus bracteatus

Ranunculus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracted buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
