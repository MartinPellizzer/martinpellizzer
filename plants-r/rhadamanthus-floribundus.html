Rhadamanthus floribundus

Rhadamanthus floribundus, commonly known as the golden flame lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, long-lasting blooms.

Rhadamanthus floribundus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design to add a dramatic visual impact.
