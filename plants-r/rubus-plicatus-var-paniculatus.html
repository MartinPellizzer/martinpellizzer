Rubus plicatus var. paniculatus

Rubus plicatus var. paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
