Rutidosis arachnoidea

Rutidosis arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, white, spider-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Rutidosis arachnoidea is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
