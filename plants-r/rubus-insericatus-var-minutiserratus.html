Rubus insericatus var. minutiserratus

Rubus insericatus var. minutiserratus, commonly known as the small-toothed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential cultivation.
