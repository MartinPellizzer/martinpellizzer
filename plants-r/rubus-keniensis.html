Rubus keniensis

Rubus keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

The botanical features of Rubus keniensis make it an important plant for both ecological and economic purposes in its native region.
