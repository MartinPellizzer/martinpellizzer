Ruprechtia cruegeri

Ruprechtia cruegeri, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a striking perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is heightened by its long-lasting blooms and unique, fiery coloration.
