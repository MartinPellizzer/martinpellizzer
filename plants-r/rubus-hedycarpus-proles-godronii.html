Rubus hedycarpus proles godronii

Rubus hedycarpus proles godronii, commonly known as the Godron's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
