Ramona × palmeri

Ramona × palmeri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a hybrid plant species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area. Its flowers are yellow, resembling those of the common dandelion, and it produces a capsule-like fruit containing numerous seeds.

Ramona × palmeri is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in rocky or sandy habitats.
