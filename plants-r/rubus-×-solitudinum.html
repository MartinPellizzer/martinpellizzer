Rubus × solitudinum

Rubus × solitudinum, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, similar to other Rubus species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
