Rhaphidophora muluensis

Rhaphidophora muluensis, commonly known as the Mulu arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Rhaphidophora muluensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
