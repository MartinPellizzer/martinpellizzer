Rubus fruticosus subsp. guentheri

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

guentheri, commonly known as the Guenther's blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep purple-black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
