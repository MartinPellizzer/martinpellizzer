Rubus leggii

Rubus leggii, commonly known as the Legg's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly found in moist, shaded areas of forests. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

This shrub plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and habitat for various insects.
