Romneya

Romneya, commonly known as the "fairy lantern" or "romneya," is a genus of flowering plants native to California and southern Oregon.

It belongs to the lily family (Liliaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant features strap-like leaves and can grow up to 6 feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens. Romneya is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer, and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its resilience and beauty.
