Rhamnus erythroxyla

Rhamnus erythroxyla, commonly known as the red-fruited buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for various bird species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.
