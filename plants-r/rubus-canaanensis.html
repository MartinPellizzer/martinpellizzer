Rubus canaanensis

Rubus canaanensis, commonly known as the Canaan bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces edible berries that are similar to blackberries.

Rubus canaanensis is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
