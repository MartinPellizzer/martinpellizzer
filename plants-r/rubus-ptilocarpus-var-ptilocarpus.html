Rubus ptilocarpus var. ptilocarpus

Rubus ptilocarpus var. ptilocarpus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its thorny stems.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive fruit structure and adaptability to cool, humid climates.
