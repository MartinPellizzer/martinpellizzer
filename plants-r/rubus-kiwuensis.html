Rubus kiwuensis

Rubus kiwuensis, commonly known as the East African blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
