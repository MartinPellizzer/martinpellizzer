Rubus compositus

Rubus compositus, commonly known as the composite raspberry, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils. Its flowers are pink to white, and the fruits are usually red or yellow when mature.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
