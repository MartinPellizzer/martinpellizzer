Rosa superbissima

Rosa superbissima, commonly known as the Siberian rose or the Siberian peony, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Siberia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant, double flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for colder climates.

Rosa superbissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
