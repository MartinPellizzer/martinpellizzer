Rumex elongatus

Rumex elongatus, commonly known as the elongated dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and damp woodlands. The plant features basal leaves that are elongated and slightly lobed, with a distinctive waxy coating on the upper surface. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces oval, smooth-seeded fruits.

Rumex elongatus is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating skin conditions and digestive ailments.
