Rhododendron hirsutum lusus laciniatum

Rhododendron hirsutum lusus laciniatum, commonly known as the laceleaf rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with deeply lobed, leathery leaves that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a horticultural favorite and a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
