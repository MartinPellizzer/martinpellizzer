Rubus radula var. raduliflorus

Rubus radula var. raduliflorus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The flowers are white and appear in early spring, preceding the development of the fruit.
