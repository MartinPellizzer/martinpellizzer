Ratonia

Ratonia, commonly known as the rat's tail plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ratonia is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.
