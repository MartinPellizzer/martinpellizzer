Rosa × dumetorum f. catarractarum

Rosa × dumetorum f. catarractarum, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thorny stems.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is well adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are dark green, hairy, and oval-shaped, providing a lush appearance even when not in bloom.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
