Rosa kudrjaschevii

Rosa kudrjaschevii, commonly known as the Kudrjaschev rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by oval, woody rose hips. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
