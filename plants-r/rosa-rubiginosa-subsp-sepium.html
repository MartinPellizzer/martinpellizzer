Rosa rubiginosa subsp. sepium

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

sepium, commonly known as the rough rose or the bristly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces oval, yellowish fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
