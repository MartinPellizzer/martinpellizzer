Rosa × dumetorum subf. glandulosobiserrata

Rosa × dumetorum subf.

glandulosobiserrata, commonly known as the glandular-biserrate rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, which are often covered with glandular hairs.

The leaves are pinnate, with leaflets that have a doubly serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens, and are produced in clusters.

This plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.
