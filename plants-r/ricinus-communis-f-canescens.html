Ricinus communis f. canescens

Ricinus communis f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy castor plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its hairy foliage and stems.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, typically growing in disturbed soils and open habitats. The leaves are large, palmately lobed, and often covered with fine, white pubescence. The plant produces spiny capsules containing toxic, dark reddish-brown seeds.

It is cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine and as a source of castor oil.
