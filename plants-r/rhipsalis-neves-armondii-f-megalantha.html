Rhipsalis neves-armondii f. megalantha

Rhipsalis neves-armondii f. megalantha, commonly known as the giant rhipsalis, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to small, hair-like structures, which help it conserve moisture.

This species is well adapted to humid, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
