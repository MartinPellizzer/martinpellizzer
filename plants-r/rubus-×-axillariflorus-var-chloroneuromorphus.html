Rubus × axillariflorus var. chloroneuromorphus

Rubus × axillariflorus var. chloroneuromorphus, commonly known as the green-veined blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically larger than those of its parent species. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic fragrance.

This variety is often found in temperate regions with moist, well-drained soils.
