Ranunculus nuttallii

Ranunculus nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland areas.

Ranunculus nuttallii is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its toxic sap and delicate floral structure.
