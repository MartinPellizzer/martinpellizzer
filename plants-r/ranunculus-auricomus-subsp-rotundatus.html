Ranunculus auricomus subsp. rotundatus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

rotundatus, commonly known as the roundleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five broadly rounded petals that are often adorned with a distinctive white or greenish central mark.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three deeply lobed leaflets, and it typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
