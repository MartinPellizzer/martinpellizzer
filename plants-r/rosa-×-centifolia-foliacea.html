Rosa × centifolia foliacea

Rosa × centifolia foliacea, commonly known as the cabbage rose, is a hybrid species of rose prized for its lush, cabbage-like foliage and abundant, fragrant blooms.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant produces flowers in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and is known for its strong, sweet perfume. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in perfumery, it remains a popular choice in gardens and horticulture.
