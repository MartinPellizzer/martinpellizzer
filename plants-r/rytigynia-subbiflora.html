Rytigynia subbiflora

Rytigynia subbiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rytigynia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rytigyniaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Rytigynia subbiflora is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
