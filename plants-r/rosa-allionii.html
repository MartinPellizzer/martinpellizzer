Rosa allionii

Rosa allionii, commonly known as the Allion's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Iberian Peninsula and parts of France. The plant features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces hips, which are the fruiting bodies of the plant.

Rosa allionii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
