Ranunculus auricomus subsp. amabilis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

amabilis, commonly known as the golden shield or golden bell, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive, shield-shaped leaf structure.

Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant produces showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants with ecological value.
