Rhododendron rupicola var. muliense

Rhododendron rupicola var. muliense, commonly known as the Muli rhododendron, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white to pale lavender flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, providing year-round visual interest.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
