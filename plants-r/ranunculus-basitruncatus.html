Ranunculus basitruncatus

Ranunculus basitruncatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant features a short, stout stem with basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow, with five to eight petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, giving it a distinctive, showy appearance.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its often toxic compounds and ornamental value.
