Rhamnus tripolitana

Rhamnus tripolitana, commonly known as the Tripolitana buckthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually green on the upper surface and pale or whitish underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.

Rhamnus tripolitana plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
