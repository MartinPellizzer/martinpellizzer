Ratibida peduncularis var. picta

Ratibida peduncularis var. picta, commonly known as the painted coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its showy, spiky flower heads are composed of numerous small, tubular florets surrounded by a broad, colorful disk.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
