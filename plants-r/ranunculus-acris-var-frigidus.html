Ranunculus acris var. frigidus

Ranunculus acris var. frigidus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with 5-15 petal-like sepals, and its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions. It is often found in meadows, tundra, and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Ranunculaceae family.
