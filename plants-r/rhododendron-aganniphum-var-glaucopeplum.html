Rhododendron aganniphum var. glaucopeplum

Rhododendron aganniphum var. glaucopeplum, commonly known as the glaucous-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that exhibits a distinctive bluish-gray hue due to a thick layer of wax on the leaf surface. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes where its unique foliage and floral display can be appreciated.
