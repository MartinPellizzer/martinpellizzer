Reidia singampattiana

Reidia singampattiana, commonly known as the Singampattu red sanders, is a rare and critically endangered species native to the southern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red bark and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to overexploitation and habitat loss, it is now listed as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
