Ricinocarpus microcephalus

Ricinocarpus microcephalus, commonly known as the dwarf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of sword-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant is often found growing on sandy or coastal soils, thriving in humid and saline environments. Its foliage is used in traditional crafts and basket weaving in various island cultures.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
