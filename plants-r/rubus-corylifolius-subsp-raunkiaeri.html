Rubus corylifolius subsp. raunkiaeri

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

raunkiaeri, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, trifoliate leaves that are often deeply lobed.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
