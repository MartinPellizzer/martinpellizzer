Ranunculus flabellatus proles cinerascens

Ranunculus flabellatus proles cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray proles ranunculus, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive flabellate (fan-shaped) structure.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

The ash-gray proles ranunculus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
