Rubus purpuratus subsp. bellatulus

Rubus purpuratus subsp.

bellatulus, commonly known as the purple raspberry or bellatulus raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its erect or shrub-like growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or purple in color.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
