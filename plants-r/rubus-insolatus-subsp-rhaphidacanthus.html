Rubus insolatus subsp. rhaphidacanthus

Rubus insolatus subsp.

rhaphidacanthus, commonly known as the red raspberry. It is a shrub native to eastern Asia, characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
