Rindera umbellata

Rindera umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a broad, green spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Rindera umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
