Rubus tallahasseanus

Rubus tallahasseanus, commonly known as the Tallahassee raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Florida.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and swamps.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
