Rosa × dumetorum subvar. orthacantha

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. orthacantha, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine layer of white tomentum, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and floral display.
