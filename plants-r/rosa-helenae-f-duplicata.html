Rosa helenae f. duplicata

Rosa helenae f. duplicata, commonly known as the double-flowered Helen's rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa helenae species.

It is characterized by its double blooms, which consist of numerous small petals arranged in a dense, layered pattern. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a compact shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
