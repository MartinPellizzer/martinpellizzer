Ranunculus oxyspermus var. damascenus

Ranunculus oxyspermus var. damascenus, commonly known as the Damascus buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a distinctive, slightly hairy surface.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
