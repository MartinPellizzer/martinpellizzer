Robinia hispida var. kelseyi

Robinia hispida var. kelseyi, commonly known as the Kelseyi honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with small, stiff, hooked thorns along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the spring, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Its bark is thick and fissured, with a greyish-brown color that becomes more rugged with age.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
