Ranunculus helogeton

Ranunculus helogeton, commonly known as the desert buttercup, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus helogeton plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small organisms.
