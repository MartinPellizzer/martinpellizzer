Raphanus hispanicus

Raphanus hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish radish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has leaves that are typically lobed and hairy. Its root system is relatively small compared to other radish species, and it is often grown as a biennial.

The plant is valued for its edible leaves and roots, which have a mild, peppery flavor.
