Rubus fuscoater var. silensanus

Rubus fuscoater var. silensanus, commonly known as the silent blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, characterized by its dark, almost black berries. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are highly sought after.

The botanical interest lies in its unique genetic variation and potential for cultivation in subtropical climates.
