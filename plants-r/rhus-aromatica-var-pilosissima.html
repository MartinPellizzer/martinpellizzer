Rhus aromatica var. pilosissima

Rhus aromatica var. pilosissima, commonly known as the silky ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early summer, which are followed by red, fuzzy fruits. Its leaves are compound, with oval leaflets that turn bright red in the fall.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
