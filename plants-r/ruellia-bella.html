Ruellia bella

Ruellia bella, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually silver or gray-green in color, giving it its common name. Its flowers are tubular and come in shades of blue, purple, or white, often attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Ruellia bella is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
