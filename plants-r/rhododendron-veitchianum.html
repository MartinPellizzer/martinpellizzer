Rhododendron veitchianum

Rhododendron veitchianum, commonly known as the Veitch's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
