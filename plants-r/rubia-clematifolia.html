Rubia clematifolia

Rubia clematifolia, commonly known as the creeping raspberry or white woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by red, berry-like fruits.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
