Rosa pouzinii var. vicina

Rosa pouzinii var. vicina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The leaves are narrow and serrated, typically arranged in odd pinnate patterns. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and historical significance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
