Rosa afzeliana var. schioetzii

Rosa afzeliana var. schioetzii, commonly known as the Schioetz's rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a woody structure and a preference for well-drained, sunny soils.

It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, demonstrating adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
