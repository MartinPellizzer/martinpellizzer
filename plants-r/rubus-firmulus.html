Rubus firmulus

Rubus firmulus, commonly known as the soft-fruited blackberry shrub, is a species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Rubus firmulus is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
