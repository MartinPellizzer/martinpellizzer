Rubus colemanii var. weickeri

Rubus colemanii var. weickeri, commonly known as the Coleman's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible but often have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and pies.
