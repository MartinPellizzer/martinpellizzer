Rosa granulosa

Rosa granulosa, commonly known as the granular rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and Kazakhstan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa granulosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
