Ranunculus tripartitus var. anceps

Ranunculus tripartitus var. anceps, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that are often reflexed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three deeply lobed leaflets, which are typically basal and alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
