Ranunculus glaucus

Ranunculus glaucus, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple petals and its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to rocky slopes and meadows.

Ranunculus glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
