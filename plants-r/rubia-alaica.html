Rubia alaica

Rubia alaica, commonly known as the Caucasian balm, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 12,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

Rubia alaica thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.
