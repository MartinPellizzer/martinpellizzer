Rubus ariconiensis

Rubus ariconiensis, commonly known as the Ariconien raspberry, is a species of bramble native to specific regions in South America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries, raspberries, and dewberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
