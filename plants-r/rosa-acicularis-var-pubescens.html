Rosa acicularis var. pubescens

Rosa acicularis var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent thorned rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The leaves are alternate, simple, and covered with fine, downy hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
