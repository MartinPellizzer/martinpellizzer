Ricinocarpus acuminatus

Ricinocarpus acuminatus, commonly known as the slender senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and herbal applications.
