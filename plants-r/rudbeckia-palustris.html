Rudbeckia palustris

Rudbeckia palustris, commonly known as swamp coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of dark brown to black tubular florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rudbeckia palustris thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
