Rubus trichanthus

Rubus trichanthus, commonly known as the three-veined blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is noted for its ornamental and edible qualities.
