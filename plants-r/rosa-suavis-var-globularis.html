Rosa suavis var. globularis

Rosa suavis var. globularis, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumes due to their strong, sweet scent.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
