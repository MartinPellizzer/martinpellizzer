Rosa coriifolia subvar. cerasifera

Rosa coriifolia subvar. cerasifera, commonly known as the cherry rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits known as hip fruits, which are often used in herbal remedies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with oval-shaped leaflets that turn yellow in autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
