Racosperma kempeanum

Racosperma kempeanum, commonly known as the Kempean wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its seed pods are slender and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Racosperma kempeanum is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.
