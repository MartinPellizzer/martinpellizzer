Rubus klimmekianus

Rubus klimmekianus, commonly known as the Klimmek's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
