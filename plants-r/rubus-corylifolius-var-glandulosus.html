Rubus corylifolius var. glandulosus

Rubus corylifolius var. glandulosus, commonly known as the hairy-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
