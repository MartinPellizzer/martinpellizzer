Rosa carolina subsp. mexicoensis

Rosa carolina subsp.

mexicoensis, commonly known as the Mexican Carolina rose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Mexico and the southern United States. It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically featuring five to seven leaflets.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its adaptation to temperate and subtropical climates.
