Rosa squarrosa var. kulmensis

Rosa squarrosa var. kulmensis, commonly known as the Kulmense rose, is a variant of the square-stemmed rose native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are often covered in a distinctive whitish, woolly pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
