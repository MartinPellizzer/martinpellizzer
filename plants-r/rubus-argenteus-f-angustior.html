Rubus argenteus f. angustior

Rubus argenteus f. angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf evergreen blackberry, is a variant of the silverleaf blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and along stream banks.

This form is notable for its distinctive foliage and is valued for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
