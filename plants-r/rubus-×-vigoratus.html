Rubus × vigoratus

Rubus × vigoratus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and forest edges.

Due to its hardy nature, it is valued for both ornamental and edible purposes.
