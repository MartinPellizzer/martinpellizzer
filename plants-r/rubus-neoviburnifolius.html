Rubus neoviburnifolius

Rubus neoviburnifolius, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
