Rubus hispidus var. cupulifer

Rubus hispidus var. cupulifer, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often found in open woods, thickets, and along streambanks.
