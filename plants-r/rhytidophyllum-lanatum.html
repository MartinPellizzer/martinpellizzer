Rhytidophyllum lanatum

Rhytidophyllum lanatum, commonly known as the woolly rhytidophyllum, is a unique plant species characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It is native to the arid regions of South Africa, where it has adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions. The plant's leaves are typically thick and covered in a fine, white pubescence that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
