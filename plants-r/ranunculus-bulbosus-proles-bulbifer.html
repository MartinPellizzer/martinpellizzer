Ranunculus bulbosus proles bulbifer

Ranunculus bulbosus proles bulbifer, commonly known as the bulbous buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, rounded seed head. The plant produces bulb-like structures called bulbils, which are used for vegetative propagation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
