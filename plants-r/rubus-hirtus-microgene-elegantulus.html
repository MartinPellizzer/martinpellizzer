Rubus hirtus microgene elegantulus

Rubus hirtus microgene elegantulus, commonly known as the elegant trailing raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible red berries in the summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are hairy on the upper surface.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production.
