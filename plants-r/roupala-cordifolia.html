Roupala cordifolia

Roupala cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf roupala, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
