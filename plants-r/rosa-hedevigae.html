Rosa hedevigae

Rosa hedevigae, commonly known as the Hedvig's rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and is typically found in rocky or well-drained soils. Rosa hedevigae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
