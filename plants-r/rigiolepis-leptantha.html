Rigiolepis leptantha

Rigiolepis leptantha, commonly known as the slender-leaved rock daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Rigiolepis leptantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
