Rosa gallica var. cristata

Rosa gallica var. cristata, commonly known as the "Crested Rosa" or "Crested Gallica," is a compact, shrub-like rose native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season. The plant features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged in a compact, upright form.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its visual appeal and hardiness.
