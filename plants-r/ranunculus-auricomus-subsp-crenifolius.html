Ranunculus auricomus subsp. crenifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

crenifolius, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals that are often slightly notched.

The plant has alternate, trifoliate or pinnately compound leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
