Rhododendron nigropunctatum

Rhododendron nigropunctatum, commonly known as the black-spotted rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, often marked by small, dark spots. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a tubular shape and a prominent glandular structure at the base.

Rhododendron nigropunctatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
