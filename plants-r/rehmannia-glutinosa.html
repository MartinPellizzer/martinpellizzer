Rehmannia glutinosa

Rehmannia glutinosa, commonly known as Chinese foxglove, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, sticky, reddish-brown roots that are highly valued in traditional Chinese medicine. These roots are rich in alkaloids and are used to treat conditions such as kidney disorders and diabetes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both medicinal and horticultural contexts.
