Rubus terecaulis

Rubus terecaulis, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
