Ranunculus cuneatus var. lobatus

Ranunculus cuneatus var. lobatus, commonly known as the lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often adorned with a dark center. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
