Rhododendron traillianum var. dictyotum

Rhododendron traillianum var. dictyotum, commonly known as the Traill's rhododendron, is a rare and unique variant native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and possesses showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features leathery, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil, typically found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
