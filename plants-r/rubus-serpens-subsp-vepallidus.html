Rubus serpens subsp. vepallidus

Rubus serpens subsp.

vepallidus, commonly known as the pale-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces small, dark berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

This subspecies is notable for its hirsute stems and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for various wildlife.
