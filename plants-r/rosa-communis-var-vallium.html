Rosa communis var. vallium

Rosa communis var. vallium, commonly known as the Vallis Valley rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering.

It typically produces semi-double to fully double pink flowers with a rich, velvety texture. The plant has dark green, trifoliate leaves that are slightly serrated along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or container cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
