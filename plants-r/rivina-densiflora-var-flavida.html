Rivina densiflora var. flavida

Rivina densiflora var. flavida, commonly known as the yellow rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a glossy texture and emits a mild, sweet fragrance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
