Rubus parviflorus var. velutinus

Rubus parviflorus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by edible red berries in the summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy surface.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
