Rupicapnos africana var. minor

Rupicapnos africana var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf African flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in gardens and as a specimen plant.
