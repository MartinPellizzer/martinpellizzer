Rubus infestus var. mindensis

Rubus infestus var. mindensis, commonly known as Mindanao blackberry, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
