Rubus curvifolius

Rubus curvifolius, commonly known as the bent-leaved blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are curved at the base, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, edible blackberries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
