Rhododendron chapaense

Rhododendron chapaense, commonly known as the Chapa rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates. Its floral display typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron chapaense is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
