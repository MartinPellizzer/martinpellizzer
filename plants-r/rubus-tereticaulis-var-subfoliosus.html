Rubus tereticaulis var. subfoliosus

Rubus tereticaulis var. subfoliosus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub-like plant native to North America, characterized by its thin, spiny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique leaf and fruit morphology.
