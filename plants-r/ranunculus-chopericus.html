Ranunculus chopericus

Ranunculus chopericus, commonly known as the Choper's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
