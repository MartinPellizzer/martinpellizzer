Rosa occitanica

Rosa occitanica, commonly known as the Occitan rose, is a species of shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces red, hairy fruits called hips. Rosa occitanica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is valued for its historical and cultural significance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
