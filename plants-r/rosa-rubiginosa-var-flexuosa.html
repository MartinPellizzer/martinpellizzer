Rosa rubiginosa var. flexuosa

Rosa rubiginosa var. flexuosa, commonly known as the creeping rose or wild rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable plant, suitable for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
