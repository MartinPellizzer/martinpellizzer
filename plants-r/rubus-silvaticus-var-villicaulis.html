Rubus silvaticus var. villicaulis

Rubus silvaticus var. villicaulis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in woodland and shady areas, often forming dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
