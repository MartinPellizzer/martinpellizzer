Rhododendron populare

Rhododendron populare, commonly known as the common rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and features dense, broad leaves that are often glossy green. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the spring. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often accompanied by showy bracts.

Rhododendron populare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
