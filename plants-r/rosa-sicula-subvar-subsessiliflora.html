Rosa sicula subvar. subsessiliflora

Rosa sicula subvar. subsessiliflora, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in spring. The plant produces oval, woody fruits called hips, which contain seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
