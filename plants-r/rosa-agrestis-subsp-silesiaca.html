Rosa agrestis subsp. silesiaca

Rosa agrestis subsp.

silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian brambles, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of pale pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, hairy rosehip.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
