Rubus canadensis var. terrae-novae

Rubus canadensis var. terrae-novae, commonly known as the Newfoundland blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry native to the northeastern regions of North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and is an important component of local ecosystems.
