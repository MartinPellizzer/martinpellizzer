Rubus serpens subsp. effusus

Rubus serpens subsp.

effusus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, dark berries.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The berries are edible and often used in local cuisines or for making jams and wines.
