Romulea bulbocodium var. sublutea

Romulea bulbocodium var. sublutea, commonly known as the yellow romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically from May to July. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
