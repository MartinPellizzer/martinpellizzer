Rumex tianschanicus

Rumex tianschanicus, commonly known as the Tian Shan sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, making it a traditional food source in some regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
