Rabdosia xerophila

Rabdosia xerophila, commonly known as the desert rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. This species is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, often hairy leaves adapted to reduce water loss. The flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters, and are attractive to pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is well-suited to semi-arid environments.
