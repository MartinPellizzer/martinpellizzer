Rhododendron decorum subsp. diaprepes

Rhododendron decorum subsp.

diaprepes, commonly known as the Diaprepes rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
