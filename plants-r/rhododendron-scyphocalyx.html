Rhododendron scyphocalyx

Rhododendron scyphocalyx, commonly known as the skyflower rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive saucer-shaped corolla. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage consists of leathery, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron scyphocalyx is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aesthetic contribution to landscape design.
