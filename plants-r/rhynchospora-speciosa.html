Rhynchospora speciosa

Rhynchospora speciosa, commonly known as the showy spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the eastern United States.

It features tall, grass-like stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish at the base, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color, and they are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stems.

Rhynchospora speciosa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
