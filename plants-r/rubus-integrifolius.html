Rubus integrifolius

Rubus integrifolius, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with smooth, entire margins. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and raspberries.
