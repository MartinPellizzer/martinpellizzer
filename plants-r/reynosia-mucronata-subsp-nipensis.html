Reynosia mucronata subsp. nipensis

Reynosia mucronata subsp.

nipensis, commonly known as the Japanese sand pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its lanceolate, serrated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces small, round fruits with a rough, yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
