Rosa armidae

Rosa armidae, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa armidae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
