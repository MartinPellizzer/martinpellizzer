Rubus dumetorum f. viridis

Rubus dumetorum f. viridis, commonly known as green bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other Rubus species, particularly in its foliage and fruit coloration.
