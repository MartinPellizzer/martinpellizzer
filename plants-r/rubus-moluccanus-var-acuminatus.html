Rubus moluccanus var. acuminatus

Rubus moluccanus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
