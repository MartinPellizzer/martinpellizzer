Rubus rudis var. harmandianus

Rubus rudis var. harmandianus, commonly known as the Harmand's bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
