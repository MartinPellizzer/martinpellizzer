Rosa tomentella f. tectiglanda

Rosa tomentella f. tectiglanda, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a botanical variety characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and the Himalayas. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are covered in a thick layer of fine hairs, which provide insulation and help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
