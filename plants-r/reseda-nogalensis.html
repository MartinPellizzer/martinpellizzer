Reseda nogalensis

Reseda nogalensis, commonly known as the Mexican catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a reddish or purple tint. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.
