Rosa microphylla

Rosa microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions like Virginia and North Carolina. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant pink or white flowers. It typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade.

Rosa microphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
