Rubus dalibardoides

Rubus dalibardoides, commonly known as the Dalibard raspberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing or shrub-like growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.

The flowers are usually white or pink and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
