Roegneria abolinii f. divaricans

Roegneria abolinii f. divaricans, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
