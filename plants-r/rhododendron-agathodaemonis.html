Rhododendron agathodaemonis

Rhododendron agathodaemonis, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
