Rubus hirtus var. flaccidifrons

Rubus hirtus var. flaccidifrons, commonly known as the soft-fruited blackberry, is a species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. The plant is native to parts of North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

The variety flaccidifrons is distinguished by its softer, more floppy fruiting structures compared to other Rubus species.
