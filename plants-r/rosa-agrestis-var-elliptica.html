Rosa agrestis var. elliptica

Rosa agrestis var. elliptica, commonly known as the rough shrub rose, is a variant of the shrub rose species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which provide structural support to the plant. The leaves are typically elliptical in shape, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in various garden environments.
