Russelia retrorsa

Russelia retrorsa, commonly known as the "pink ice plant," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and profuse display of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Russelia retrorsa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and hanging baskets.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
