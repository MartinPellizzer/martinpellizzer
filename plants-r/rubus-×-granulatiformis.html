Rubus × granulatiformis

Rubus × granulatiformis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils. Its leaves are palmately compound with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a popular choice for jams and pies.
