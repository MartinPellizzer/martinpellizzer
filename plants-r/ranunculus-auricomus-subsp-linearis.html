Ranunculus auricomus subsp. linearis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent, veined sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to the typical form of *Ranunculus auricomus*.
