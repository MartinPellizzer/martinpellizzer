Ranunculus fallax subsp. subinermis

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

subinermis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens.

The leaves are typically deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a decorative garden plant and a potential weed.
