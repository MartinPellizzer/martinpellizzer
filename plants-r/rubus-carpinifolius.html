Rubus carpinifolius

Rubus carpinifolius, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. It often grows in hedgerows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor.
