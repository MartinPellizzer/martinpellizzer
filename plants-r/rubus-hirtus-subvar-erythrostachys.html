Rubus hirtus subvar. erythrostachys

Rubus hirtus subvar. erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stalked blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into bright red, hairy berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This subvariety is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in home gardens.
