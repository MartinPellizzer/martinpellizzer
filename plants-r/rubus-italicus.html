Rubus italicus

Rubus italicus, commonly known as the Italian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rubus italicus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
