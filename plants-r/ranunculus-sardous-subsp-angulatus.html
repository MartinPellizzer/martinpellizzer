Ranunculus sardous subsp. angulatus

Ranunculus sardous subsp.

angulatus, commonly known as the serrated meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers.

The plant typically features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
