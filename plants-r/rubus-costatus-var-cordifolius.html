Rubus costatus var. cordifolius

Rubus costatus var. cordifolius, commonly known as the heartleaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets, often with a heart-shaped base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
