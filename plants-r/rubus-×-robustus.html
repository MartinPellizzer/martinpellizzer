Rubus × robustus

Rubus × robustus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Rubus × robustus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
