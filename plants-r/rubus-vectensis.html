Rubus vectensis

Rubus vectensis, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which consist of small, juicy drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

Rubus vectensis is an important plant in both ecological and culinary contexts.
