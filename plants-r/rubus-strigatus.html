Rubus strigatus

Rubus strigatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular for culinary use.
