Rubus coriaceus

Rubus coriaceus, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white or pink, appearing in early summer.

Rubus coriaceus is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.
