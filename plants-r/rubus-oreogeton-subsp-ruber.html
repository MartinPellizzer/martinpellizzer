Rubus oreogeton subsp. ruber

Rubus oreogeton subsp.

ruber, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets.

The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are widely cultivated for their flavor and nutritional value.

The subspecies is notable for its robust growth habit and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
