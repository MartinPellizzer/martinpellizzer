Roettlera cinerea

Roettlera cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf roettlera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in loose, racemose inflorescences. Roettlera cinerea typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
