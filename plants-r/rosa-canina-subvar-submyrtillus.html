Rosa canina subvar. submyrtillus

Rosa canina subvar. submyrtillus, commonly known as the Myrtle rose, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical, serrated, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
