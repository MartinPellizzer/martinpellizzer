Ranunculus nipponicus var. okayamensis

Ranunculus nipponicus var. okayamensis, commonly known as the Okayama buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm, with finely divided, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
