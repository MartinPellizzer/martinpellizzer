Rubus crepinii

Rubus crepinii, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows along the ground, with arching stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
