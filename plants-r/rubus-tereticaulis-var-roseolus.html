Rubus tereticaulis var. roseolus

Rubus tereticaulis var. roseolus, commonly known as the rosehip bramble, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers are pink to white and appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
