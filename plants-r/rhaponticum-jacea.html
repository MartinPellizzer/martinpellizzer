Rhaponticum jacea

Rhaponticum jacea, commonly known as the Siberian succulent, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rhaponticum jacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
