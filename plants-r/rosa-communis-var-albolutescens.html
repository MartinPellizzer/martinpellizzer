Rosa communis var. albolutescens

Rosa communis var. albolutescens, commonly known as the white-flowered rose, is a cultivar characterized by its pale pink to white flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically features thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable to a range of soil types, making it a popular choice for both landscape design and cut flower arrangements.
