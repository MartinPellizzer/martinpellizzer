Rubus × implicatus

Rubus × implicatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained, loamy soils. The plant produces dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. Its growth habit includes both upright and trailing forms, making it adaptable to different garden environments.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its complex inflorescence and the presence of persistent sepals that remain attached to the fruit.
