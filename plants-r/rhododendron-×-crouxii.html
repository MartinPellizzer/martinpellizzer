Rhododendron × crouxii

Rhododendron × crouxii, commonly known as the Croux rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron ferrugineum and Rhododendron ponticum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and Alps.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
