Ribes sanctae-luciae

Ribes sanctae-luciae, commonly known as the Saint Lucy gooseberry, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow or orange berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in the spring.
