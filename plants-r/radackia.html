Radackia

Radackia, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, strap-like leaves and delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near water sources in forested areas. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, are often used in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.

Radackia requires careful cultivation due to its specific humidity and light needs.
