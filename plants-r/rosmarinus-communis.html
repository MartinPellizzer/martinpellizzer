Rosmarinus communis

Rosmarinus communis, commonly known as rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and blue-purple flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is known for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and flowers. Rosemary thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its botanical features contribute to its widespread use in cooking, medicine, and aromatherapy.
