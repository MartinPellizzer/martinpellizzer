Rubus nuptialis

Rubus nuptialis, commonly known as the wedding raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Rubus nuptialis is also known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
