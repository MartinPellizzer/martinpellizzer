Rubus pallidus subsp. ctenodon

Rubus pallidus subsp.

ctenodon, commonly known as the pale raspberry or ctenodon raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often described as having a tart flavor. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.
