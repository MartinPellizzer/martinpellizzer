Ranunculus apertiformis

Ranunculus apertiformis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and its basal leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It has a short growing season and is often found at high elevations.

The species is notable for its ability to survive in harsh environments, making it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
