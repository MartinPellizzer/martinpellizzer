Rubus maassii subsp. muenteri

Rubus maassii subsp.

muenteri, commonly known as the Maasai raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern Africa. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruit is a red, hairy drupe that ripens in late summer.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
