Rubus × proteus

Rubus × proteus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal fruiting.
