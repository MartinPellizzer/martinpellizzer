Rhipsalis roseana

Rhipsalis roseana, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its soft, non-woody stems that are often covered in a silvery coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically in the summer months. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Rhipsalis roseana is epiphytic, meaning it can grow on other plants without being parasitic, and it prefers indirect sunlight and well-draining soil.
