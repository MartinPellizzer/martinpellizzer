Rubus ceretanus

Rubus ceretanus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its flowers are white and clustered in inflorescences, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Rubus ceretanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
