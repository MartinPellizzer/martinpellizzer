Rapanea involucrata

Rapanea involucrata, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Rapanea involucrata is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
