Rosa × miranda

Rosa × miranda, commonly known as the Miranda rose, is a hybrid cultivar resulting from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, pink to red blooms that typically have a high number of petals. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and pruning for optimal growth.

Rosa × miranda is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in perfumery and floral arrangements.
