Ranunculus paucistamineus var. drouetii

Ranunculus paucistamineus var. drouetii, commonly known as the Drouet's buttercup, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features finely divided, palmately arranged leaves and produces small, yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.
