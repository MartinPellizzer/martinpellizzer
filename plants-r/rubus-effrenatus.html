Rubus effrenatus

Rubus effrenatus, commonly known as the wild blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus effrenatus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and pollinators.
