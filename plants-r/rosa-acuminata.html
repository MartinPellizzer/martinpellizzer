Rosa acuminata

Rosa acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its narrow, acuminate (sharply pointed) leaves and fragrant, pink to white flowers. Rosa acuminata is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its beautiful blooms and hardy nature.
