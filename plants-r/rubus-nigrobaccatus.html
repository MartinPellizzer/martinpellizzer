Rubus nigrobaccatus

Rubus nigrobaccatus, commonly known as the black-barked raspberry.

It is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its dark, almost black bark and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in woodland edges and moist soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and its leaves are compound with serrated edges.

The fruit, while not widely cultivated, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local foraging traditions.
