Restrepia antennifera subsp. hemsleyana

Restrepia antennifera subsp.

hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's lady's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a prominent yellow lip that attracts pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
