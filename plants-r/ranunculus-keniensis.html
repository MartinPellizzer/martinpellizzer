Ranunculus keniensis

Ranunculus keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, trifoliate leaves and produces flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
