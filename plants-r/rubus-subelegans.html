Rubus subelegans

Rubus subelegans, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks.

The leaves are usually trifoliate, with finely serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.
