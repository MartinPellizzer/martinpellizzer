Rubus megalococcus

Rubus megalococcus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically three times the size of common blackberries. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

The fruit is often used in jams and desserts due to its intense flavor and high sugar content.
