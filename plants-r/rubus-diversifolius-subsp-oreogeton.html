Rubus diversifolius subsp. oreogeton

Rubus diversifolius subsp.

oreogeton, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which vary in shape and size across different individuals.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
