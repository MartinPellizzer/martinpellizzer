Ruellia insignis

Ruellia insignis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Ruellia insignis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
