Rubus furvus var. latifolius

Rubus furvus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, serrated leaves and produces dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are white and clustered in rounded inflorescences, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in jams and pies.
