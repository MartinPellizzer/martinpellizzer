Ranunculus oz

Ranunculus oz, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features simple, trifoliate leaves and produces flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
