Ranunculus eximius

Ranunculus eximius, commonly known as the noble buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and sometimes pink. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Ranunculus eximius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
