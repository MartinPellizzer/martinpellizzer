Rorippa micrantha

Rorippa micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a small annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, linear leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows. Its fruits are short, cylindrical siliques that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Rorippa micrantha is often found in temperate regions and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed aquatic habitats.
