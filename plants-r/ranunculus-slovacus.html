Ranunculus slovacus

Ranunculus slovacus, commonly known as the Slovak buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Ranunculus slovacus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and toxic sap.
