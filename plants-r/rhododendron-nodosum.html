Rhododendron nodosum

Rhododendron nodosum, commonly known as the clustered rhododendron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is native to regions of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
