Rosa alpina f. adenosepala

Rosa alpina f. adenosepala, commonly known as the alpine rose or rock rose, is a botanical variant of the alpine rose species native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, adapted to thrive in cold, high-altitude environments.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in challenging climates.
