Ranunculus palmatus

Ranunculus palmatus, commonly known as the palmate buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of palmately lobed leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus palmatus is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions.
