Rumex regelii

Rumex regelii, commonly known as Regel's sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the veins. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
