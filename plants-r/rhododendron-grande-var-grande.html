Rhododendron grande var. grande

Rhododendron grande var. grande, commonly known as the giant rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
