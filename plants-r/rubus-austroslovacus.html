Rubus austroslovacus

Rubus austroslovacus, commonly known as the Southern Slovak raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
