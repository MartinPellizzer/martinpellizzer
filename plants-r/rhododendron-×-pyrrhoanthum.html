Rhododendron × pyrrhoanthum

Rhododendron × pyrrhoanthum, commonly known as the purple rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is native to the eastern United States and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
