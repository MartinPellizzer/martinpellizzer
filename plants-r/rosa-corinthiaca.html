Rosa corinthiaca

Rosa corinthiaca, commonly known as the Corinthian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their strong, sweet aroma.

Rosa corinthiaca is valued for both its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
