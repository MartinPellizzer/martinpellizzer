Rumex pseudoalpinus

Rumex pseudoalpinus, commonly known as alpine sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its rhubarb-like leaves are edible, though they contain oxalic acid and should be cooked before consumption.

Rumex pseudoalpinus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
