Rubus silvaticus var. longistylis

Rubus silvaticus var. longistylis, commonly known as the long-styled bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry species found in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with prominent stamens that extend beyond the petals. Its fruits are aggregate berries, usually dark purple to black when ripe, and are edible though often tart.

This plant thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
