Rubus guentheri var. polyacanthus

Rubus guentheri var. polyacanthus, commonly known as the Guenther's raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
