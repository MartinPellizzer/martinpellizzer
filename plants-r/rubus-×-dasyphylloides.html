Rubus × dasyphylloides

Rubus × dasyphylloides, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus armeniacus and Rubus ursinus, resulting in a shrub with dense, thorny stems. It features large, elliptical leaves with serrated edges and a deep green color. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
