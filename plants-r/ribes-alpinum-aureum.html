Ribes alpinum aureum

Ribes alpinum aureum, commonly known as the golden currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by edible, golden-orange berries in the summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a hairy underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
