Ranunculus kuepferi subsp. orientalis

Ranunculus kuepferi subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Crescent buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is characterized by its distinctive, pale yellow flowers with a prominent central blotch.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with finely serrated edges, and its flowering period occurs in early spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
