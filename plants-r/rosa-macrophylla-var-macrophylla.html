Rosa macrophylla var. macrophylla

Rosa macrophylla var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Australian sugar gum or the coastal rose, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is typically found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, often forming dense thickets.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of the local flora and a popular ornamental species in horticulture.
