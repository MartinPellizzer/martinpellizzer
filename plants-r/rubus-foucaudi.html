Rubus foucaudi

Rubus foucaudi, commonly known as the Foucaud blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southern Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
