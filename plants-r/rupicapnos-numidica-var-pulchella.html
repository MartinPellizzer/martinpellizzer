Rupicapnos numidica var. pulchella

Rupicapnos numidica var. pulchella, commonly known as the pink-flowered firecracker plant, is a striking shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, tubular pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
