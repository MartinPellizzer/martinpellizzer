Rubus pantocsekii

Rubus pantocsekii, commonly known as the Pantocsek's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Hungary.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes various shrubs and subshrubs in the family Rosaceae. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark red or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
