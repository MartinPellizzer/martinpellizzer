Rubus argenteus var. brevistamineus

Rubus argenteus var. brevistamineus, commonly known as silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its growth habit includes thorny stems and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.
