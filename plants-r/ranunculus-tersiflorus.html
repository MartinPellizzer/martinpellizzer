Ranunculus tersiflorus

Ranunculus tersiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and deeply lobed leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has a short stem and produces small, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
