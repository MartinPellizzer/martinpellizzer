Rhodiola nuristanica

Rhodiola nuristanica, commonly known as the Nuristan rose or the Himalayan rhodiola, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its roots are valued for their adaptogenic properties, making it a subject of interest in traditional medicine.

Rhodiola nuristanica is well adapted to harsh climates, showcasing resilience and unique botanical features.
