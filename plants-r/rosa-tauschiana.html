Rosa tauschiana

Rosa tauschiana, commonly known as the Tausch rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and Turkey. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets, and the plant is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.

Rosa tauschiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in hybridization programs to improve rose varieties.
