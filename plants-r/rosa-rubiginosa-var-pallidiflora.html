Rosa rubiginosa var. pallidiflora

Rosa rubiginosa var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered shrub rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The flowers are small, pinkish-white, and bloom in early summer, often in clusters. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure, aromatic foliage, and a long flowering period.
