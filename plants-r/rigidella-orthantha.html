Rigidella orthantha

Rigidella orthantha, commonly known as the rigid spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its stiff, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Rigidella orthantha typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
