Rotala lucalensis

Rotala lucalensis, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a floating aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has whorled leaves that are often spirally arranged around its stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and are borne in clusters.

Rotala lucalensis is valued in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to oxygenate water.
