Rudbeckia hirta var. rubra

Rudbeckia hirta var. rubra, commonly known as the red-globed sunflower, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It features bright red flower heads with yellow centers, surrounded by velvety, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with a compact, upright growth habit. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy textures, which provide a tactile contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
