Rosa tomentella var. stenosepala

Rosa tomentella var. stenosepala, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white fuzzy foliage, which provides a unique visual texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are simple and ovate, with a velvety surface that contributes to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
