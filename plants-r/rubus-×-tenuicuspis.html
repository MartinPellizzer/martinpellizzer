Rubus × tenuicuspis

Rubus × tenuicuspis, commonly known as the slender-lobed blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is the most notable feature, is a dark purple or black berry that is often used in culinary applications.
