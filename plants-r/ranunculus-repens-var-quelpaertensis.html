Ranunculus repens var. quelpaertensis

Ranunculus repens var. quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Korean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces showy yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers.

The species is notable for its adaptability to wetland habitats and its role in local ecosystems.
