Rorippa × hybrida

Rorippa × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit.

It typically features small, bright green, spear-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round, pod-like fruits. It thrives in moist environments such as ponds, ditches, and stream banks.

Rorippa × hybrida is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate both freshwater and slightly brackish conditions.
