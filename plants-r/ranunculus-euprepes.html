Ranunculus euprepes

Ranunculus euprepes, commonly known as the golden crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three deeply lobed leaflets and produces small, yellow to orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to early-season gardens and natural habitats.
