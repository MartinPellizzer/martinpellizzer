Ribesiodes myrtillus

Ribesiodes myrtillus, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and features compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are white and tubular, appearing in umbels, and it is often found in moist woodlands and thickets.

The berries are toxic when unripe but become edible once fully mature.
