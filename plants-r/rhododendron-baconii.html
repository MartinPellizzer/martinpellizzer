Rhododendron baconii

Rhododendron baconii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high elevations. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron baconii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
