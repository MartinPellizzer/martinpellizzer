Rubus suberectus var. sextus

Rubus suberectus var. sextus, commonly known as the six-stem raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and aggregate fruits composed of small, red drupes. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
