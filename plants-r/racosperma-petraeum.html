Racosperma petraeum

Racosperma petraeum, commonly known as the rock wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Racosperma petraeum is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to local biodiversity.
