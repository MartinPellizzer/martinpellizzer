Rhododendron tigrinum

Rhododendron tigrinum, commonly known as the tigrine rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive white or pale stripe down the center. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, and it produces small, elongated seed pods.

Rhododendron tigrinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
