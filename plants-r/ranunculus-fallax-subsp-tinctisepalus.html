Ranunculus fallax subsp. tinctisepalus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

tinctisepalus, commonly known as the purple-sepal ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep purple.

The subspecies is distinguished by its striking, often violet-colored sepals, which are prominent and hairy. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments.

This plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
