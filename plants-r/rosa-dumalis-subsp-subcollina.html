Rosa dumalis subsp. subcollina

Rosa dumalis subsp.

subcollina, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are densely covered with thorns.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
