Rosa × dumetorum subvar. contorta

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. contorta, commonly known as the alpine rose or mountain rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, and it produces red or orange hips that contain seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness in garden settings.
