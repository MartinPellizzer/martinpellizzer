Rosa alpina f. semisimplex

Rosa alpina f. semisimplex, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a subspecies of the alpine rose native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapted to cold and harsh climates. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a serrated edge. Its flowers are pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rosa alpina f. semisimplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
