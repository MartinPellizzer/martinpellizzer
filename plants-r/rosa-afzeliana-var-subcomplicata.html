Rosa afzeliana var. subcomplicata

Rosa afzeliana var. subcomplicata, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming throughout the growing season. Its botanical features include hirsute (hairy) stems and ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
