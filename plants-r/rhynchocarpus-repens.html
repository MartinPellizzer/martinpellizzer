Rhynchocarpus repens

Rhynchocarpus repens, commonly known as the creeping rhynchocarp, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

Rhynchocarpus repens is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks.
