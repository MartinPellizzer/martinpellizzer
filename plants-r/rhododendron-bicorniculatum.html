Rhododendron bicorniculatum

Rhododendron bicorniculatum, commonly known as the two-lobed rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest habitats, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture, and the flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals.

Rhododendron bicorniculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
