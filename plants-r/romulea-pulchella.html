Romulea pulchella

Romulea pulchella, commonly known as the showy romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Romulea pulchella thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
