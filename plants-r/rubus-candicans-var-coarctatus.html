Rubus candicans var. coarctatus

Rubus candicans var. coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
