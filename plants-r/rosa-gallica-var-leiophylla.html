Rosa gallica var. leiophylla

Rosa gallica var. leiophylla, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and heraldry.
