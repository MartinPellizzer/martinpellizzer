Rosa × consanguinea var. preissmannii

Rosa × consanguinea var. preissmannii, commonly known as the Preissmann's rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in leaf shape and flower color.
