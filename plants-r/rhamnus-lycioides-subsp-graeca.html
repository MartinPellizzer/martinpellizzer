Rhamnus lycioides subsp. graeca

Rhamnus lycioides subsp.

graeca, commonly known as Greek buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, dark berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its native range.
