Rumex komarovii

Rumex komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration, especially on the underside, and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
