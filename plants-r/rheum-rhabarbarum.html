Rheum rhabarbarum

Rheum rhabarbarum, commonly known as the Chinese rhubarb or simply rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and thick, reddish stalks that are used in cooking. The plant belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties. Its roots and leaves contain anthraquinone compounds, which have laxative effects.

The plant is also cultivated for its edible stalks, which are commonly used in pies and jams.
