Rosa afzeliana f. subglaucescens

Rosa afzeliana f. subglaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine, waxy coating.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
