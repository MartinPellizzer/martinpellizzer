Rubus hirsutus f. argyi

Rubus hirsutus f. argyi, commonly known as the Chinese bramble or hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on maturity. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
