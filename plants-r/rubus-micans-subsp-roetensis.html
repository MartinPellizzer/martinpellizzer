Rubus micans subsp. roetensis

Rubus micans subsp.

roetensis, commonly known as the Roetense blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or along stream banks.

The berries are not only edible but also have traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
