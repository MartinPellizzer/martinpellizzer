Rubus × emollitiformis

Rubus × emollitiformis, commonly known as the soft-leaved blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically soft and juicy. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its foliage and fruit characteristics.
