Ranunculus auricomus subsp. amoeniflorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

amoeniflorus, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive golden center and broad, shield-shaped petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has palmate leaves with three to five lobes and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
