Rosa solstitialis var. rariglanda

Rosa solstitialis var. rariglanda, commonly known as the rare-stemmed summer rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sparse foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soils. Its unique morphology makes it a sought-after specimen in horticulture and botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific growing conditions, it is considered a rare and delicate plant.
