Rubus tomentosus f. semicalvus

Rubus tomentosus f. semicalvus, commonly known as the soft-haired raspberry.

This plant is a variant of the common raspberry, characterized by its fuzzy, hairy stems and leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are covered in fine, white hairs.

It is often found in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soil.
