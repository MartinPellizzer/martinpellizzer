Rosa rubiginosa var. nudipes

Rosa rubiginosa var. nudipes, commonly known as the naked-stem dog rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red-orange hips that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets, and it is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
