Rhododendron washingtonianum

Rhododendron washingtonianum, commonly known as the Washington rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, cool climates.
