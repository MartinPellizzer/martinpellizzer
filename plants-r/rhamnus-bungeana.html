Rhamnus bungeana

Rhamnus bungeana, commonly known as the Bunge's buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
