Rubus × intectus

Rubus × intectus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible and often cultivated for their flavor and nutritional value.

Rubus × intectus thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
