Rudolphia planisiliqua

Rudolphia planisiliqua, commonly known as the flat-leaf rudolphia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring. Rudolphia planisiliqua prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is also noted for its resilience to drought and mild frost.
