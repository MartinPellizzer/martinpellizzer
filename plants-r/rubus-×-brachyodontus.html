Rubus × brachyodontus

Rubus × brachyodontus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are composed of small, drupe-like units called achenes. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces flowers that range from white to pink in color.

This species is valued for its edible fruits, which are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications.
