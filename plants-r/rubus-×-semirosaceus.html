Rubus × semirosaceus

Rubus × semirosaceus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the western United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its horticultural value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
