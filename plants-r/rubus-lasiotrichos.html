Rubus lasiotrichos

Rubus lasiotrichos, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and a dense covering of white to grayish hairs. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or sharp flavor.
