Richardia aethiopica

Richardia aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian grass, is a perennial herb native to Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Richardia aethiopica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry regions.
