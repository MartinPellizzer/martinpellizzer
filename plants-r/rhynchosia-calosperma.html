Rhynchosia calosperma

Rhynchosia calosperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Rhynchosia calosperma is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
