Rubus glandulosus var. intermedius

Rubus glandulosus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate brambles, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically growing in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters.

The fruit, a red or black raspberry-like berry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
