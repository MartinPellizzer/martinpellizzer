Ranunculus lujiangensis

Ranunculus lujiangensis, commonly known as the Lujiang buttercup, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
