Rhododendron langbianense

Rhododendron langbianense, commonly known as the Langbian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Vietnam.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters, thriving in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable subject for ecological and horticultural study in subtropical regions.
