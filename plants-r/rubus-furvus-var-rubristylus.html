Rubus furvus var. rubristylus

Rubus furvus var. rubristylus, commonly known as the red-styled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is highly valued for its flavor and culinary uses.

The red-styled variety is distinguished by its distinctive red coloration of the style in the flower, which is a key identifying feature.
