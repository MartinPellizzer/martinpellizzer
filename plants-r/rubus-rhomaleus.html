Rubus rhomaleus

Rubus rhomaleus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
