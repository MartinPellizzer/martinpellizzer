Rinorea callmanderi

Rinorea callmanderi, commonly known as the dwarf white bellflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly glossy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate flowers and evergreen foliage.
