Rubus × dejectus f. lachneus

Rubus × dejectus f. lachneus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its fruit is edible and is used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
