Rhododendron cremnastes

Rhododendron cremnastes, commonly known as the Cretan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Greek island of Crete.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink to reddish flowers in the spring. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen nature and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
