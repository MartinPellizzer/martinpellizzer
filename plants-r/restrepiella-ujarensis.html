Restrepiella ujarensis

Restrepiella ujarensis, commonly known as the Ujarensis orchid, is a rare and endemic plant species found in specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology and specialized growth habits. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane cloud forests. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and exhibit a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
