Rubus × villicauligena

Rubus × villicauligena, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to regions of eastern North America, where it thrives in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. Its growth habit includes both shrub-like and vine-like forms, depending on environmental factors.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both Rubus ursinus and Rubus allegheniensis, contributing to its adaptability and ecological significance.
