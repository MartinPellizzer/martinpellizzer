Rubus hispidus f. pleniflorus

Rubus hispidus f. pleniflorus, commonly known as the hairy bramble or full-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
