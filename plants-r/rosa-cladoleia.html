Rosa cladoleia

Rosa cladoleia, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or the pink rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features dense, thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa cladoleia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
