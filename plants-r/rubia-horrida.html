Rubia horrida

Rubia horrida, commonly known as the rough betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Rubia horrida is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and traditional medicinal uses.
