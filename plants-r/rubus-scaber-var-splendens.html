Rubus scaber var. splendens

Rubus scaber var. splendens, commonly known as the showy raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
