Rubus schmidelyanus var. oblongifoliatus

Rubus schmidelyanus var. oblongifoliatus, commonly known as the oblong-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species within its native range.
