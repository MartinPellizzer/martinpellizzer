Rosa elliptica subvar. bouvieri

Rosa elliptica subvar. bouvieri, commonly known as the Bouvier's elliptical rose, is a shrub-like species native to China.

It features upright, thorned stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are elliptical, with a slightly serrated margin, and are arranged alternately along the stems. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
