Rhaphidophora foraminifera

Rhaphidophora foraminifera, commonly known as the "spotted arrowroot," is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of white spots. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily in moist, shaded environments.

Rhaphidophora foraminifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
