Rubus floridus

Rubus floridus, commonly known as the Florida blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pinkish flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The fruit is edible, though typically tart, and is used in local foraging and traditional practices.
