Ranunculus thora

Ranunculus thora, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its early spring blooming period, often appearing before many other spring flowers.

Ranunculus thora is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
