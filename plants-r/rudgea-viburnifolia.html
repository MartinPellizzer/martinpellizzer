Rudgea viburnifolia

Rudgea viburnifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf rudgea, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that is often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions.

Rudgea viburnifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific garden conditions.
