Racemobambos multiramosa

Racemobambos multiramosa, commonly known as the multibranched bamboo, is a unique species belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form a dense clump, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences known as racemes, which are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a slight waxy coating.

This species is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in landscaping.
