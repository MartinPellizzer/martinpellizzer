Rubus mauensis

Rubus mauensis, commonly known as the Mau blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mau Forest Complex in Kenya.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical highland climate.

Rubus mauensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stability.
