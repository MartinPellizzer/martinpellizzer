Reseda luteola var. trifida

Reseda luteola var. trifida, commonly known as goldenweed or yellowweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent aroma. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and has hairy, branched stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
