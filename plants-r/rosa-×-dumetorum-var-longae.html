Rosa × dumetorum var. longae

Rosa × dumetorum var. longae, commonly known as the long-stemmed dog rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in woodland areas and along hedgerows in temperate regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
