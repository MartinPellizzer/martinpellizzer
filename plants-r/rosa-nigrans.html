Rosa nigrans

Rosa nigrans, commonly known as the black rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that contain seeds.

Rosa nigrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
