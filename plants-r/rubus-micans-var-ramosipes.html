Rubus micans var. ramosipes

Rubus micans var. ramosipes, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
