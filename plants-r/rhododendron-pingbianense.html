Rhododendron pingbianense

Rhododendron pingbianense, commonly known as the Pingbian rhododendron, is a rare and endemic species native to China, specifically found in Yunnan Province.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is typically found in mountainous regions with acidic soils. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leathery leaves, which help it adapt to its high-altitude environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
