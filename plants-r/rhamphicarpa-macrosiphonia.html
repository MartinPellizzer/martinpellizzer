Rhamphicarpa macrosiphonia

Rhamphicarpa macrosiphonia, commonly known as the giant sifaka orchid, is a rare and unique species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific lemur species for seed dispersal. Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered critically endangered.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both conservationists and botanists.
