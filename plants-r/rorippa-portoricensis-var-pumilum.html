Rorippa portoricensis var. pumilum

Rorippa portoricensis var. pumilum, commonly known as the Puerto Rican watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and rocky areas. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

This variety is distinguished by its compact size and unique adaptations to its tropical habitat.
