Rosa graveolens celtica

Rosa graveolens celtica, commonly known as the Celtic rose, is a species of shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its dark red, almost black flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

The rose is noted for its strong, spicy fragrance and historical significance in folklore and herbal medicine.
