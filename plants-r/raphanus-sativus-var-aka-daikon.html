Raphanus sativus var. aka-daikon

Raphanus sativus var. aka-daikon, commonly known as the daikon radish, is a large, cylindrical root vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its white or pale yellow flesh and crisp texture, with a mild, slightly sweet flavor. The plant produces a single, thick taproot that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Daikon is often used in Asian cuisine, both raw and cooked, and is valued for its nutritional content and versatility in cooking.
