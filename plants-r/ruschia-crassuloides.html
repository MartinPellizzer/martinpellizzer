Ruschia crassuloides

Ruschia crassuloides, commonly known as the dwarf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a powdery blue or white coating, known as farina. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.
