Rosa pendulina var. oxyodon

Rosa pendulina var. oxyodon, commonly known as the hanging rose or the weeping rose, is a botanical variety characterized by its cascading growth habit and delicate, pink to red flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant blooms throughout the growing season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to create a dramatic, flowing visual effect.
