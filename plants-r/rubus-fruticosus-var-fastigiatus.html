Rubus fruticosus var. fastigiatus

Rubus fruticosus var. fastigiatus, commonly known as the upright blackberry bush, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems that grow in a compact, upright form. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
