Rubus mississippianus

Rubus mississippianus, commonly known as the Mississippi blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
