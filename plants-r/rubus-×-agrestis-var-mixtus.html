Rubus × agrestis var. mixtus

Rubus × agrestis var. mixtus, commonly known as the field raspberry, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and waste grounds, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible but often tart.

This species is notable for its adaptability and is often found in a variety of habitats due to its vigorous growth habit.
