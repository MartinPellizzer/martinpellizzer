Ranunculus horieanus

Ranunculus horieanus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its often toxic members.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and partial shade to thrive.
