Rosa × dumetorum f. cinerosa

Rosa × dumetorum f. cinerosa, commonly known as the blackberry rose, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is distinguished by its dark, almost black, foliage and stems, which are covered in a dense layer of glandular hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to white in color, followed by fruits that resemble blackberries in appearance. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five ovate leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in the production of certain herbal remedies.
