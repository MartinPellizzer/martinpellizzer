Rosa adenocalyx adenopoda

Rosa adenocalyx adenopoda, commonly known as the腺萼玫瑰, is a species of shrub native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically compound, with oval-shaped leaflets and a serrated edge. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits known as hips, which are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
