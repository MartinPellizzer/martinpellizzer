Ruellia terminalis

Ruellia terminalis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ruellia terminalis thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
