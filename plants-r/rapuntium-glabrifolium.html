Rapuntium glabrifolium

Rapuntium glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leafed rapuntium, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, lanceolate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has a short growing season and is known for its edible leaves, which are used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
