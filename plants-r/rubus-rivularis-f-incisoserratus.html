Rubus rivularis f. incisoserratus

Rubus rivularis f. incisoserratus, commonly known as the serrated river raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins, which are finely toothed and contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, often shaded environments along riverbanks and stream sides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
