Rubus fruticosus subsp. leucostachys

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

leucostachys, commonly known as the white-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
