Rubus nutkanus var. pubescens

Rubus nutkanus var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
