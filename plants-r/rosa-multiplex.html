Rosa multiplex

Rosa multiplex, commonly known as the many-flowered rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Rosa multiplex is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
