Regelia regnellii

Regelia regnellii, commonly known as the silver torch cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in dense, silver-blue spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the stems. Regelia regnellii is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for arid gardens.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among cactus enthusiasts.
