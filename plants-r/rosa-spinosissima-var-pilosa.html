Rosa spinosissima var. pilosa

Rosa spinosissima var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy prickly rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its hairy leaves and stems give it a distinctive appearance, and it is well adapted to dry, rocky soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
