Ricinus communis subvar. gracilis

Ricinus communis subvar. gracilis, commonly known as the slender castor oil plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces distinctive spiny capsules containing toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and industry. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value is often appreciated in gardens due to its unique foliage and dramatic seed pods.
