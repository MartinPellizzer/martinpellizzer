Rhododendron augustinei var. chasmanthum

Rhododendron augustinei var. chasmanthum, commonly known as the Chasmanthum rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows at high elevations, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained acidic soils. Its flowers, which range from pink to deep red, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
