Rhodopis volubilis

Rhodopis volubilis, commonly known as the climbing beauty, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Rhodopis volubilis thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
