Ranunculus inundatus

Ranunculus inundatus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, usually yellow or white flowers. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in shallow waters and moist soils. The plant is often found in rivers, ponds, and marshes across temperate regions.

Its presence indicates healthy aquatic ecosystems due to its sensitivity to pollution.
