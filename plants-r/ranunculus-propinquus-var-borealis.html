Ranunculus propinquus var. borealis

Ranunculus propinquus var. borealis, commonly known as the northern buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture. It has a short, stout stem that often lies close to the ground.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
