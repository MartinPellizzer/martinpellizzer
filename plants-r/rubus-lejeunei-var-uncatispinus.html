Rubus lejeunei var. uncatispinus

Rubus lejeunei var. uncatispinus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and hairy on the underside. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist woodland areas.

Its fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter taste.
