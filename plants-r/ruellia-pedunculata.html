Ruellia pedunculata

Ruellia pedunculata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers throughout the growing season. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Ruellia pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
