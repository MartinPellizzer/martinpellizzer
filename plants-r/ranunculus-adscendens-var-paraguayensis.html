Ranunculus adscendens var. paraguayensis

Ranunculus adscendens var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers feature five to eight sepals that are often mistaken for petals.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
