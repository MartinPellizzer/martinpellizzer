Rubus friesii

Rubus friesii, commonly known as the Fries' bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white or pink flowers. It produces edible berries that are similar in appearance to raspberries but have a distinct flavor.

Rubus friesii is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
