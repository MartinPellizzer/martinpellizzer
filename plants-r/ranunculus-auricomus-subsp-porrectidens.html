Ranunculus auricomus subsp. porrectidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

porrectidens, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals that are often adorned with a distinctive white or greenish central mark.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with deeply lobed segments, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
