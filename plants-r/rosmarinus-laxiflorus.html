Rosmarinus laxiflorus

Rosmarinus laxiflorus, commonly known as the laxiflorous rosemary, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other rosemary species.

Rosmarinus laxiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
