Rosa maerklinii

Rosa maerklinii, commonly known as the Maerklin rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals. It produces hip fruits that contain achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

Rosa maerklinii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
