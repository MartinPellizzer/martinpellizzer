Rathbunia beneckei f. cristatus

Rathbunia beneckei f. cristatus, commonly known as the cristate Rathbunia, is a unique cultivar of the Rathbunia species, characterized by its distinctive rosette-like growth habit.

This plant is native to South Africa and belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads. The f. cristatus form exhibits a compact, bushy appearance with tightly packed, often crinkled leaves that give it a whimsical, ornamental look. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its unusual appearance, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticultural collections.
