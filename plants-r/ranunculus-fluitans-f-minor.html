Ranunculus fluitans f. minor

Ranunculus fluitans f. minor, commonly known as the lesser water buttercup, is a aquatic plant species belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves and yellow flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its roots are adapted for water, allowing it to anchor itself in muddy substrates.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
