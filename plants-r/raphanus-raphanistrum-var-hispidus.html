Raphanus raphanistrum var. hispidus

Raphanus raphanistrum var. hispidus, commonly known as hairy wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into round, hairy pods containing seeds. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found growing in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various habitats, including roadsides and agricultural fields.
