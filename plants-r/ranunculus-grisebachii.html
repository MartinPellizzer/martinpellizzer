Ranunculus grisebachii

Ranunculus grisebachii, commonly known as the grayleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive grayish-green hue, and its flowers are typically yellow with 5-7 sepals and 5-7 petals. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It is characterized by its short stature and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.

Ranunculus grisebachii is also noted for its preference for well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
