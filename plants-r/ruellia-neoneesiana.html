Ruellia neoneesiana

Ruellia neoneesiana, commonly known as the pink ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and come in shades of pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Ruellia neoneesiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
