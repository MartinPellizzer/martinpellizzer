Rubus × schleicherioides

Rubus × schleicherioides, commonly known as the Schleicher's blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
