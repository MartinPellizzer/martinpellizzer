Rosa hudsoniana var. plena

Rosa hudsoniana var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered Canadian rose, is a cultivar of the shrub rose that features densely packed, double blooms.

It is native to Canada and is characterized by its vigorous growth and thorny stems. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
