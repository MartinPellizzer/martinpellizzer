Ruellia noctiflora

Ruellia noctiflora, commonly known as night blooming ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its blooming period typically occurs in the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Ruellia noctiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.
