Rubus × ruralis

Rubus × ruralis, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. It produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

The botanical features of Rubus × ruralis make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
