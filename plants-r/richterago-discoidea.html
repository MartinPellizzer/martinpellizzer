Richterago discoidea

Richterago discoidea, commonly known as the disk-like fern, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, compact growth habit and its leaves, which form a dense, disk-like structure. The plant exhibits a complex arrangement of fronds, with some species displaying a spiral pattern. Its reproductive structures are small and clustered, typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Richterago discoidea is considered a botanically significant but understudied plant.
