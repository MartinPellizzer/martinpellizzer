Roemeria refracta subsp. occidentalis

Roemeria refracta subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western refracta bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, rocky habitats, such as dry slopes and woodland edges.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
