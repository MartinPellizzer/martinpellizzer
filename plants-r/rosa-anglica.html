Rosa anglica

Rosa anglica, commonly known as the English rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumes and as cut flowers due to their beauty and scent.

Rosa anglica is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
