Restrepia maculata subsp. ecuadoriensis

Restrepia maculata subsp.

ecuadoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian restrepia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae). It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs.

The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador and is adapted to high-altitude, cool, and humid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
