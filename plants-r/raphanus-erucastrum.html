Raphanus erucastrum

Raphanus erucastrum, commonly known as the hairy radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate, hairy leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by spherical, hairy pods containing several seeds. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
