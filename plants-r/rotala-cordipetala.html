Rotala cordipetala

Rotala cordipetala, commonly known as the heartleaf water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted for water environments. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often floating on the water surface. It thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes.

Rotala cordipetala is often used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
