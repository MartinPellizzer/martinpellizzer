Rosa glauca var. ungulata

Rosa glauca var. ungulata, commonly known as the blue rose or blue shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its unique coloration and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural research.
