Reseda phyteuma var. aragonensis

Reseda phyteuma var. aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
