Rhaphidophora motleyana

Rhaphidophora motleyana, commonly known as the Motley's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features upright, cane-like stems with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Rhaphidophora motleyana is also noted for its ability to produce edible tubers, though this is less common in cultivated varieties.
