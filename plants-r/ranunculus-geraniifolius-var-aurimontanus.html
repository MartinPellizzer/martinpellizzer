Ranunculus geraniifolius var. aurimontanus

Ranunculus geraniifolius var. aurimontanus, commonly known as the aurimontan buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple coloration. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
