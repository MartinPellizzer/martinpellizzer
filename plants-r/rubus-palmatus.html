Rubus palmatus

Rubus palmatus, commonly known as the palmate raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are palmately arranged with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Rubus palmatus is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
