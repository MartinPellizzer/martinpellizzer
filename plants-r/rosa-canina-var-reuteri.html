Rosa canina var. reuteri

Rosa canina var. reuteri, commonly known as the wild rose or dog rose, is a species of shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy berries that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along coastlines.

The botanical features of Rosa canina var. reuteri make it an important plant for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
