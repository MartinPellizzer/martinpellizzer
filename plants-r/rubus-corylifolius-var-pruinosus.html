Rubus corylifolius var. pruinosus

Rubus corylifolius var. pruinosus, commonly known as the glossy raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, red berries that are typically eaten fresh or used in jams. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
