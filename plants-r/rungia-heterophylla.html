Rungia heterophylla

Rungia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf morning glory, is a tropical climbing plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which can vary significantly between individual plants. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Rungia heterophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
