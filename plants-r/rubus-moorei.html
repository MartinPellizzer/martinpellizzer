Rubus moorei

Rubus moorei, commonly known as Moore's blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

Rubus moorei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
