Rodriguezia lanceolata

Rodriguezia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf rodriguezia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length, often with a deep green color and a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
