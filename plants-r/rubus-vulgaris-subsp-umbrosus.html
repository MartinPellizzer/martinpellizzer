Rubus vulgaris subsp. umbrosus

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

umbrosus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in woodland and hedgerow habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it is an important food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
