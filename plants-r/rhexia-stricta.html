Rhexia stricta

Rhexia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf pink, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Rhexia stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
