Rosa canina var. glabra

Rosa canina var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth hips rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia, characterized by its thornless stems and simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The flowers are pink to white, with five petals, and bloom in early summer.
