Rosa cinnamomea var. fulgens

Rosa cinnamomea var. fulgens, commonly known as the Cinnamomum rose, is a hybrid rose known for its striking, vibrant pink to red flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, glossy foliage. The flowers are borne in clusters and have a strong, spicy fragrance, reminiscent of cinnamon, which is characteristic of its parent species. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique aromatic qualities.
