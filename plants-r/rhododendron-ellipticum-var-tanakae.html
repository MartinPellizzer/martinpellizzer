Rhododendron ellipticum var. tanakae

Rhododendron ellipticum var. tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka's elliptic rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features broad, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
