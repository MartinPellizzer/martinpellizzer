Rubus eggersii

Rubus eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
