Rubus polysetosus

Rubus polysetosus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white or pale pink.

The species is native to parts of Europe and Asia, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
