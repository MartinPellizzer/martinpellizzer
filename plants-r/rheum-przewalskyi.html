Rheum przewalskyi

Rheum przewalskyi, commonly known as the Chinese rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, reddish stalks with clusters of small, greenish flowers. Its roots are rich in anthraquinones and have been used in traditional medicine for their laxative properties.

Rheum przewalskyi thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.
