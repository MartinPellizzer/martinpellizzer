Rhaphidophora ternatensis f. major

Rhaphidophora ternatensis f. major, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or large-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the lowland rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by triangular, winged fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible tubers, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and for its culinary uses.
