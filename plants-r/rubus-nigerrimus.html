Rubus nigerrimus

Rubus nigerrimus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to western North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, making it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny environments.

Rubus nigerrimus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
