Ranunculus auricomus var. pratensis

Ranunculus auricomus var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a distinctive golden center. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators in its native ecosystems.
