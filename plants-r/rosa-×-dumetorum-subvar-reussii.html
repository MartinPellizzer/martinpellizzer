Rosa × dumetorum subvar. reussii

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. reussii, commonly known as the alpine rose or Reuss's rose, is a hybrid species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This species is well adapted to cold climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
