Ruellia californica

Ruellia californica, commonly known as California ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
