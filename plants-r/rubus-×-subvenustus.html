Rubus × subvenustus

Rubus × subvenustus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary and commercial applications.
