Remirema

Remirema, commonly known as the white-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Remirema is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
