Rubus luteistylus var. pubescens

Rubus luteistylus var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is an important species in New Zealand's native flora.
