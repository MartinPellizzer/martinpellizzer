Rubia fruticosa var. angustifolia

Rubia fruticosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved woodruff, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
