Rubus pseudovestitus

Rubus pseudovestitus, commonly known as the Chinese bramble, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

The species is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
