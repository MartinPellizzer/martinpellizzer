Rytigynia macrostipulata

Rytigynia macrostipulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed rytigynia, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
