Rottboellia monostachya

Rottboellia monostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaf horse-tail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Rottboellia monostachya is an important plant in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
