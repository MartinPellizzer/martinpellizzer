Rosa coriifolia var. bernardii

Rosa coriifolia var. bernardii, commonly known as the Bernard's sweetbriar, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters. The plant has thorny stems and elliptical, serrated leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
