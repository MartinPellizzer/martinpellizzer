Rhododendron ponticum f. album

Rhododendron ponticum f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.

The species is part of the Ericaceae family, which includes other ericaceous plants like heathers and blueberries.
