Rhododendron biappendiculatum

Rhododendron biappendiculatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant floral display.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
