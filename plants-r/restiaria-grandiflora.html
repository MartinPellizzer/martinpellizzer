Restiaria grandiflora

Restiaria grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered restiaria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Restiariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in areas with high humidity and abundant moisture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinct central stamen.

Restiaria grandiflora is often found in swampy forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
