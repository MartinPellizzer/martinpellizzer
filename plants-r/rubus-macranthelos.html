Rubus macranthelos

Rubus macranthelos, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are relatively large and white, often with five petals.

It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.
