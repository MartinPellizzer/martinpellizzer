Reseda muricata var. undulata

Reseda muricata var. undulata, commonly known as the undulating mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
