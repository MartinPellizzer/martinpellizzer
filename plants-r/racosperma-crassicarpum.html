Racosperma crassicarpum

Racosperma crassicarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Aboriginal cuisine.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
