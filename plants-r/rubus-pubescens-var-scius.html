Rubus pubescens var. scius

Rubus pubescens var. scius, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and is covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
