Rubus rufescens microgene fuscermis

Rubus rufescens microgene fuscermis, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to dark purple when mature. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are pink to white, and it produces achenes that are dispersed by wind and animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some use in traditional herbal medicine.
