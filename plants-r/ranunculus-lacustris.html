Ranunculus lacustris

Ranunculus lacustris, commonly known as the lake buttercup, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as lake margins and damp meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of palmately lobed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Ranunculus lacustris is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in cool, moist environments.
