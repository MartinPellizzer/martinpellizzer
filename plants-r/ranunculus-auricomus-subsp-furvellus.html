Ranunculus auricomus subsp. furvellus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

furvellus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland areas.

The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that resemble petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
