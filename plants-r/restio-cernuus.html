Restio cernuus

Restio cernuus, commonly known as the drooping restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing small white or pale blue flowers.

Restio cernuus is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
