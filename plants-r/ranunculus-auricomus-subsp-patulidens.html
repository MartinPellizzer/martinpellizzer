Ranunculus auricomus subsp. patulidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

patulidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five petals and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

The subspecies patulidens is distinguished by its broader leaves and slightly different flower structure compared to the typical form.
