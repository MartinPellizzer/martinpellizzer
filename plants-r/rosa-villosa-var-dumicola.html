Rosa villosa var. dumicola

Rosa villosa var. dumicola, commonly known as the rough rose or prickly rose, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thorny stems covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.
