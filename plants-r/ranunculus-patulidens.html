Ranunculus patulidens

Ranunculus patulidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other buttercup species.

Its flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.
