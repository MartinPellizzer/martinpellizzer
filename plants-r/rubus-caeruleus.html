Rubus caeruleus

Rubus caeruleus, commonly known as the blueberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of small, usually white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple when mature, rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in acidic soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus caeruleus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of wild berries.
