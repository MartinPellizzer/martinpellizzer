Rosa fallax

Rosa fallax, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its flowers are pink to red, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa fallax is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cool, rocky environments.
