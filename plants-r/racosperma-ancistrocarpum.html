Racosperma ancistrocarpum

Racosperma ancistrocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stems. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family.

Racosperma ancistrocarpum is adapted to dry and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
