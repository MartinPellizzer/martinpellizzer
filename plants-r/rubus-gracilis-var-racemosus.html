Rubus gracilis var. racemosus

Rubus gracilis var. racemosus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thin, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous tiny drupes, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
