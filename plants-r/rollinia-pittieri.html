Rollinia pittieri

Rollinia pittieri, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruit that resembles a star. The plant produces bright yellow to orange fruits with a rough, spiny exterior. The fruit contains a sweet, juicy pulp with a unique, tangy flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
