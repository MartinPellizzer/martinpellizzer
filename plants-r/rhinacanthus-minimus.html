Rhinacanthus minimus

Rhinacanthus minimus, commonly known as the small horned morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its distinctive horn-shaped calyces that enclose the flowers. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
