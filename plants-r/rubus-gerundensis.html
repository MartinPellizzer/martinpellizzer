Rubus gerundensis

Rubus gerundensis, commonly known as the Gerundense blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional remedies.
