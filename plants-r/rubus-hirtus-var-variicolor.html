Rubus hirtus var. variicolor

Rubus hirtus var. variicolor, commonly known as the variable brambles, is a species of shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink or white, and they appear in clusters during the summer months. The berries, which are edible, change color from green to red as they mature.

This plant is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
