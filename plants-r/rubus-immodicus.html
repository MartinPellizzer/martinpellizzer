Rubus immodicus

Rubus immodicus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor, often used in jams and pies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodlands and along stream banks.
