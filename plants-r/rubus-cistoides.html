Rubus cistoides

Rubus cistoides, commonly known as the prickly pear raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
