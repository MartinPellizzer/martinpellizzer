Rosa stylosa var. leucochroa

Rosa stylosa var. leucochroa, commonly known as the white-stemmed wild rose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, hairy fruits called hips. Its leaves are opposite, with serrated margins, and the flowers have five petals and numerous stamens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
