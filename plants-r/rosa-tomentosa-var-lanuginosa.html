Rosa tomentosa var. lanuginosa

Rosa tomentosa var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers in spring, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are typically oval and have a soft, hairy texture, contributing to its overall thick, velvety look.

Rosa tomentosa var. lanuginosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
