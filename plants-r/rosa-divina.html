Rosa divina

Rosa divina, commonly known as the divine rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with arching stems and broad, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The Rosa divina is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
