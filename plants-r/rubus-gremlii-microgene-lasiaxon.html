Rubus gremlii microgene lasiaxon

Rubus gremlii microgene lasiaxon, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and edible berries. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces a distinctive aroma.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is relatively rare and considered a species of conservation concern.
