Rubus glandulorudis

Rubus glandulorudis, commonly known as the glandular-fruited blackberry, is a species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a distinctive glandular surface. The plant is native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is known for its vigorous growth habit.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor.
