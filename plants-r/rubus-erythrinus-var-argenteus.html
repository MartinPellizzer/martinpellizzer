Rubus erythrinus var. argenteus

Rubus erythrinus var. argenteus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a variant of the red raspberry plant characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, typically growing in woodland edges and thickets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or orange in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, whitish hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
