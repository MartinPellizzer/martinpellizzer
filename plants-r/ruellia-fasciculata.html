Ruellia fasciculata

Ruellia fasciculata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Ruellia fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
