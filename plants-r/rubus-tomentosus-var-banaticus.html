Rubus tomentosus var. banaticus

Rubus tomentosus var. banaticus, commonly known as the Banat raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Europe.

It produces small, red, aggregate fruits that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a dense covering of white, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
