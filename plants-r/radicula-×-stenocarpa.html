Radicula × stenocarpa

Radicula × stenocarpa, commonly known as the hybrid radish, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Raphanus sativus (common radish) and Raphanus raphanistrum (wild radish).

It is characterized by its rapid growth and development, often producing small, edible roots within a short growing season. The plant typically has a taproot system that is both fibrous and fleshy, which contributes to its culinary use. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for home gardeners.

Due to its genetic variability, Radicula × stenocarpa exhibits a wide range of morphological traits in different populations.
