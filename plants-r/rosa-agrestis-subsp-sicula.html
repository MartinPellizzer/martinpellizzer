Rosa agrestis subsp. sicula

Rosa agrestis subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian wild rose, is a subspecies native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily. It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically arranged alternately.

The flowers are pink to red in color, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It produces oval, red-orange fruits called hips, which are edible and contain seeds.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
