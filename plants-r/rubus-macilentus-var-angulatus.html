Rubus macilentus var. angulatus

Rubus macilentus var. angulatus, commonly known as the angular blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
