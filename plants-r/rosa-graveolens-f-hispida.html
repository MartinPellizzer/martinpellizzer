Rosa graveolens f. hispida

Rosa graveolens f. hispida, commonly known as the hispid rose, is a flowering plant species characterized by its dense, bristly pubescence.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers with five petals. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The hispid rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
