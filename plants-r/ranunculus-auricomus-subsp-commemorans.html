Ranunculus auricomus subsp. commemorans

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

commemorans, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and heart-shaped leaves, which are typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The subspecies commemorans is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant is often found in areas with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
