Racosperma flavipilum

Racosperma flavipilum, commonly known as the yellow-haired acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, with fine, hair-like structures that give it its common name.

Racosperma flavipilum is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
