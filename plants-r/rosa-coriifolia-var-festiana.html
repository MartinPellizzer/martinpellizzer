Rosa coriifolia var. festiana

Rosa coriifolia var. festiana, commonly known as the Japanese pagoda tree or Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink to white. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges. It produces small, red fruits that mature in late summer, containing hairy seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
