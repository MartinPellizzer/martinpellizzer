Rubus plicatus var. acuminatus

Rubus plicatus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the acuminated bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
