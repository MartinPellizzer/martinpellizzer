Rubus pinnatus var. defensus

Rubus pinnatus var. defensus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant produces flowers with five pink to white petals and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it identifiable within the Rubus genus, which includes numerous species of brambles and berries.
