Rathbunia grisea

Rathbunia grisea, commonly known as the grey rathbunia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, greyish foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a velvety texture that helps retain moisture.

Rathbunia grisea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
