Rubus schwenckfeldii

Rubus schwenckfeldii, commonly known as the Schwenckfeld's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black achenes surrounded by a fleshy receptacle. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.
