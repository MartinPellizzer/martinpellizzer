Ranunculus caucasicus var. buhsei

Ranunculus caucasicus var. buhsei, commonly known as the Buhsei buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
