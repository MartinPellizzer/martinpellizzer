Rubus rosifolius subsp. sumatranus

Rubus rosifolius subsp.

sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
