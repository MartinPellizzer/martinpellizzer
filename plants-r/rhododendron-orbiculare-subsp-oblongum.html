Rhododendron orbiculare subsp. oblongum

Rhododendron orbiculare subsp.

oblongum, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and leathery in texture.

The plant produces dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as part of natural forest landscapes.
