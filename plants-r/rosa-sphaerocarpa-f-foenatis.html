Rosa sphaerocarpa f. foenatis

Rosa sphaerocarpa f. foenatis, commonly known as the foenatis form of the spherical rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique floral and foliage features.

This form exhibits compact, spherical growth with dense, bright green foliage that remains evergreen in mild climates. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with a distinctive, clustered bloom pattern that adds ornamental value. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and botanical interest.
