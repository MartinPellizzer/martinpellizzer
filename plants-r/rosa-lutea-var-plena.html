Rosa lutea var. plena

Rosa lutea var. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered yellow rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa lutea species, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms that are typically a vibrant yellow, often with a slight orange or red tinge. The plant produces fragrant, semi-herbaceous stems and has glossy, dark green foliage. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and compact growth habit.
