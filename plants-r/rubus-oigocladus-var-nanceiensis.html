Rubus oigocladus var. nanceiensis

Rubus oigocladus var. nanceiensis, commonly known as the Nance's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
