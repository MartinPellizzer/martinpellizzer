Rubus × assurgens var. parvulus

Rubus × assurgens var. parvulus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its berries are edible and are sometimes used in jams and pies.
