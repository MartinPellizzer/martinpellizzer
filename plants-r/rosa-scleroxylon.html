Rosa scleroxylon

Rosa scleroxylon, commonly known as the Afghan rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Rosa scleroxylon is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
