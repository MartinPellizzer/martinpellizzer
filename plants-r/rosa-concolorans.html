Rosa concolorans

Rosa concolorans, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, smooth-stemmed growth habit and fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a single row of serrations along the edges. Rosa concolorans thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
