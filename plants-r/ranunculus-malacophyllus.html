Ranunculus malacophyllus

Ranunculus malacophyllus, commonly known as the velvetleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, deeply lobed pattern, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are often described as velvety to the touch, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
