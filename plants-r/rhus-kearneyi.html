Rhus kearneyi

Rhus kearneyi, commonly known as Keane's sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other members like poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually alternate and pinnately arranged. Rhus kearneyi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
