Rhododendron floribundum

Rhododendron floribundum, commonly known as the evergreen rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a distinctive tubular shape.

Rhododendron floribundum prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
