Rubus hostilis

Rubus hostilis, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces red or black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus hostilis is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall and is known for its hardy nature.
