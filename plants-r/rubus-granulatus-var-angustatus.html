Rubus granulatus var. angustatus

Rubus granulatus var. angustatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
