Rhytidophyllum tomentosum f. villosulum

Rhytidophyllum tomentosum f. villosulum, commonly known as the hairy leafed rhytidophyllum, is a unique variant of the base plant species Rhytidophyllum tomentosum.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with small, leathery leaves that are covered in a thick layer of fine hairs. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticultural studies.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to specific growing conditions.
