Rubus brachiatus

Rubus brachiatus, commonly known as the bracted raspberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. Its leaves are typically palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its aesthetic and edible qualities.
