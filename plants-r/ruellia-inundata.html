Ruellia inundata

Ruellia inundata, commonly known as swamp ruellia, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to saturated soils, often found in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to pale blue.

Ruellia inundata is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
