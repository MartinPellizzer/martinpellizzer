Rhododendron × bylsianum

Rhododendron × bylsianum, commonly known as the Bylsma rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron caucasicum and Rhododendron molle.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
