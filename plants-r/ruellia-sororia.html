Ruellia sororia

Ruellia sororia, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy blue to purple flowers in the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Ruellia sororia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
