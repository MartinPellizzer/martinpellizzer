Ranunculus sudermannicus

Ranunculus sudermannicus, commonly known as the Sudermann's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
