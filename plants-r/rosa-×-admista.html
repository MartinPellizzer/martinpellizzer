Rosa × admista

Rosa × admista, commonly known as the hybrid tea rose, is a popular ornamental plant resulting from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote blooming.

Rosa × admista is widely cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and fragrance.
