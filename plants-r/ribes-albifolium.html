Ribes albifolium

Ribes albifolium, commonly known as white-leaf currant, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Ribes.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its white or pale green leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ribes albifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
