Rosa × ventenatiana

Rosa × ventenatiana, commonly known as the yellow musk rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa centifolia and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and historical use in perfume and culinary applications.

Rosa × ventenatiana thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
