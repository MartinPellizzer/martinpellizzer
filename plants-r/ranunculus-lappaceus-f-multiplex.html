Ranunculus lappaceus f. multiplex

Ranunculus lappaceus f. multiplex, commonly known as the multiflorous water crowfoot, is a botanical variant of the water crowfoot genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. This plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its flowers are usually borne in multiple clusters, giving it the "multiplex" designation.

The species is notable for its ecological role in aquatic habitats and its ornamental value in gardens.
