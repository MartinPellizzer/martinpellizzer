Rubus subcoactus

Rubus subcoactus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. Its flowers are small and white, and it produces edible berries that are similar to blackberries in taste and texture.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands and shrublands.
