Rubus × hemithyrsoideus

Rubus × hemithyrsoideus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It typically grows in temperate climates and is cultivated for its flavorful, edible berries. The plant produces small, dark red to black fruits that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its botanical features include palmate leaves and inflorescences that develop into clusters of berries.

This hybrid is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
