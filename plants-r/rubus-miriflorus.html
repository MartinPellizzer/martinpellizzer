Rubus miriflorus

Rubus miriflorus, commonly known as the fairy thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and has white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.
