Ranunculus pospichalii

Ranunculus pospichalii, commonly known as the Pospichal's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically features simple, alternate leaves and produces flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
