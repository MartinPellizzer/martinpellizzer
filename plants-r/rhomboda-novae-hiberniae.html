Rhomboda novae-hiberniae

Rhomboda novae-hiberniae, commonly known as the Irish rhombod.

This plant is a flowering species native to the temperate regions of Ireland, characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying shades of white and pale green.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insect species.
