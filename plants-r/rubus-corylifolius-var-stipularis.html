Rubus corylifolius var. stipularis

Rubus corylifolius var. stipularis, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, trifoliate leaves that are often palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
