Rhododendron araiophyllum subsp. lapidosum

Rhododendron araiophyllum subsp.

lapidosum, commonly known as the stone pine rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its evergreen leaves that are leathery and dark green, often with a waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
