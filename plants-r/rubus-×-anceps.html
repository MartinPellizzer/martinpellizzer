Rubus × anceps

Rubus × anceps, commonly known as the blackberry dewberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color, containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
