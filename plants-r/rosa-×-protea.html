Rosa × protea

Rosa × protea, commonly known as the Protea rose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa × damascena.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep red. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

Rosa × protea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
