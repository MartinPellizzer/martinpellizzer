Rosa × dumetorum f. piligeroides

Rosa × dumetorum f. piligeroides, commonly known as the hairy wood rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This species is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
