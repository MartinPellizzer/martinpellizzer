Rhododendron rosendahlii

Rhododendron rosendahlii, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high elevations, often in forested areas with acidic soils. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and sturdy stems that support its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron rosendahlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
