Rosa roxburghii var. plena

Rosa roxburghii var. plena, commonly known as the full-blown rock rose, is a variant of the rock rose species native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms of pink to red flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and horticultural research.
