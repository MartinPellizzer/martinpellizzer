Rubus hirtus var. rubicundus

Rubus hirtus var. rubicundus, commonly known as the redstem blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when ripe, containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
