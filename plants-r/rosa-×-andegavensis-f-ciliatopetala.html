Rosa × andegavensis f. ciliatopetala

Rosa × andegavensis f. ciliatopetala, commonly known as the ciliate-petaled rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, softly hairy petals that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
