Rubus centiformis var. egregiusculus

Rubus centiformis var. egregiusculus, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique floral structure and dwarf growth habit compared to other Rubus species.
