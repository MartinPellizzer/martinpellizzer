Robinia × hybrida

Robinia × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid redbud, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Robinia pseudoacacia (the black locust) and Robinia americana (the pink redbud). The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
