Rhododendron hesperium

Rhododendron hesperium, commonly known as the western rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a dense, fuzzy coating.

Rhododendron hesperium is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
