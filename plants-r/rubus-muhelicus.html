Rubus muhelicus

Rubus muhelicus, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus muhelicus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
