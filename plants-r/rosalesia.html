Rosalesia

Rosalesia, commonly known as the "roses of the desert," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that often have a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The plants typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. Rosalesia species are often shrubs or small trees with thorny stems and simple, alternate leaves.

Their flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to their ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
