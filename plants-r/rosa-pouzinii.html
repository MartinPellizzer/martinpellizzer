Rosa pouzinii

Rosa pouzinii, commonly known as the Pouzin's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange-red hips. Rosa pouzinii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.
