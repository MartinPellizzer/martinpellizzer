Rosa pendulina subsp. mediterranea

Rosa pendulina subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean climbing rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region. It features long, arching stems that can climb or trail along the ground, often reaching lengths of up to 3 meters.

The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.
