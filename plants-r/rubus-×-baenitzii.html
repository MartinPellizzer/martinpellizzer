Rubus × baenitzii

Rubus × baenitzii, commonly known as the Baenitzii blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, fertile soils. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and white to pale pink flowers. Its berries are usually dark purple to black when ripe, and they are often used in jams and preserves.

The species is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various growing conditions.
