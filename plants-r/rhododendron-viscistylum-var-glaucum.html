Rhododendron viscistylum var. glaucum

Rhododendron viscistylum var. glaucum, commonly known as the Glaucous Rhododendron, is a variant of the viscistylum species within the Rhododendron genus.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that often exhibits a bluish-gray waxy coating, known as glaucous. The plant typically features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is native to specific regions in Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
