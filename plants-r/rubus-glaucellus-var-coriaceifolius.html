Rubus glaucellus var. coriaceifolius

Rubus glaucellus var. coriaceifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The leaves are thick, leathery, and have a distinctive glaucous (bluish-gray) underside. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its unique leaf texture and berry color.
