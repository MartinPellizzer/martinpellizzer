Rhaphidophora acuminata

Rhaphidophora acuminata, commonly known as the velvetleaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves can reach up to 30 inches in length, making it a striking addition to indoor spaces.

Rhaphidophora acuminata prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
