Rabdosia longituba

Rabdosia longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It often grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream sides.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
