Ribes × gordonii

Ribes × gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon currant, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Ribes nigrum* (blackcurrant) and *Ribes rubrum* (redcurrant).

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, edible berries that are typically a deep red or black in color. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include opposite, simple leaves and racemose inflorescences with five-petaled flowers.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications or for making jams and wines.
