Rubus fuscoater var. echinatus

Rubus fuscoater var. echinatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
