Ruellia sarmentosa

Ruellia sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping ruellia, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Ruellia sarmentosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground covers.
