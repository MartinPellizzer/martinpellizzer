Rhyncholepis brevicuspis

Rhyncholepis brevicuspis, commonly known as the short-spined sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, fleshy leaves and is often found growing in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhyncholepis brevicuspis is adapted to saline environments and is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
