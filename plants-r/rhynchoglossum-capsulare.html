Rhynchoglossum capsulare

Rhynchoglossum capsulare, commonly known as the bottlebrush lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its unique, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Rhynchoglossum capsulare is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
