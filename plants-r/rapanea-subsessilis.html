Rapanea subsessilis

Rapanea subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
