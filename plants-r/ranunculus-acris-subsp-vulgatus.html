Ranunculus acris subsp. vulgatus

Ranunculus acris subsp.

vulgatus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along streams, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features showy yellow flowers with five to ten petals, which are usually surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that disperse via wind.

It is often found in temperate regions and is considered a characteristic species of open, sunny habitats.
