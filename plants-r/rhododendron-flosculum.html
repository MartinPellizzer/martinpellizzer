Rhododendron flosculum

Rhododendron flosculum, commonly known as the dwarf alpine rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Rhododendron flosculum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
