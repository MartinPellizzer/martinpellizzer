Ruellia dependens

Ruellia dependens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia dependens thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.
