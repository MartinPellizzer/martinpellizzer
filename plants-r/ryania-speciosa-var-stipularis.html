Ryania speciosa var. stipularis

Ryania speciosa var. stipularis, commonly known as the red sandalwood or ryania, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves have been utilized for their medicinal properties, including antispasmodic and antihelmintic effects.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
