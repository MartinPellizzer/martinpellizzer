Rubus ulmifolius var. attenuatus

Rubus ulmifolius var. attenuatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its distinctive leaf shape and fruit structure.
