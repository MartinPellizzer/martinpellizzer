Rosa communis var. homologa

Rosa communis var. homologa, commonly known as the Homologa rose, is a rare and ancient variety of rose native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are dark green, serrated, and covered in a fine layer of downy hair. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is often sought after by botanists and rose enthusiasts alike.
