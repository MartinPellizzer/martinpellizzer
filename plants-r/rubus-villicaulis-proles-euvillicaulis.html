Rubus villicaulis proles euvillicaulis

Rubus villicaulis proles euvillicaulis, commonly known as the silky raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
