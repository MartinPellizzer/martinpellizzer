Rhododendron lamrialianum subsp. lamrialianum

Rhododendron lamrialianum subsp.

lamrialianum, commonly known as the Lamrialian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a dense canopy that supports various epiphytic organisms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
