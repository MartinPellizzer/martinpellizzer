Rosa parviflora var. pectinata

Rosa parviflora var. pectinata, commonly known as the silky rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, arching stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its soft, fuzzy petals and narrow, pinnate leaves. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
