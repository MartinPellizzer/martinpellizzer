Reseda alphonsi subsp. alphonsi

Reseda alphonsi subsp.

alphonsi, commonly known as the Alphonse mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
