Rosa × histricosa

Rosa × histricosa, commonly known as the bristly rose, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits a combination of characteristics from its parent species.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical name reflects its hybrid origin, with "×" indicating a cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa bracteata.

The bristly rose is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in various climates.
