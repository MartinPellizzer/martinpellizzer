Rhaphidophora tetrasperma

Rhaphidophora tetrasperma, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a tropical plant native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce new shoots. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and air-purifying qualities.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.
