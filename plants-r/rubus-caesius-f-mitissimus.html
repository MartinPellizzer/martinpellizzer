Rubus caesius f. mitissimus

Rubus caesius f. mitissimus, commonly known as the pale-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which distinguish it from the typical red or pink blooms of the main species. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. Its fruits are small, dark berries that are edible and have a tart flavor.

This form is often found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.
