Rhynchocorys kurdica

Rhynchocorys kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish horned orchid, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions. The plant features a distinctive floral structure with showy, violet-colored petals and a unique horn-like spur, which is characteristic of its genus. Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its potential for medicinal uses.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
