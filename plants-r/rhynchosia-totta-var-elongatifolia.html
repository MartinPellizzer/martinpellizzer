Rhynchosia totta var. elongatifolia

Rhynchosia totta var. elongatifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved snout bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
