Rubus × lineatoalpestris

Rubus × lineatoalpestris, commonly known as the alpine blackberry.

This plant is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cooler climates. The leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white or pale pink.

It is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in jams and desserts.
