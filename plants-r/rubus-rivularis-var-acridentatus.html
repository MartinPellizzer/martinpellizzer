Rubus rivularis var. acridentatus

Rubus rivularis var. acridentatus, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, thriving in partially shaded environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit, which ripens in summer, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.
