Rauvolfia dichotoma

Rauvolfia dichotoma, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in African herbal medicine.
