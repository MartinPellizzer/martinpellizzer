Rapanea comorensis

Rapanea comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro satinwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Comoros Islands.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, red drupes that are a source of food for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
