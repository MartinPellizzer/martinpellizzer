Rhododendron hyperythrum

Rhododendron hyperythrum, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting yellow or orange throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron hyperythrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
