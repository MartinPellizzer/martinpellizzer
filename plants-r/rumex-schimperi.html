Rumex schimperi

Rumex schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid.

Rumex schimperi is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
