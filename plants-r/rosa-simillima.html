Rosa simillima

Rosa simillima, commonly known as the similar rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, alternate leaves with serrated edges, and an extensive root system.

Rosa simillima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
