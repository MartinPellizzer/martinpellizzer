Rosa × innoxua

Rosa × innoxua, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a hybrid species of rose native to the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, fragrant flowers, and deciduous leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa × innoxua is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
