Rhododendron viscosum var. hispidum

Rhododendron viscosum var. hispidum, commonly known as the sticky alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give its leaves and stems a sticky texture. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
