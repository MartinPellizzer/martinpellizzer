Rubus grayanus var. yakumontanus

Rubus grayanus var. yakumontanus, commonly known as the Yakumontan blackberry.

This variant is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both its ornamental and culinary potential.
