Ranunculus nevensis

Ranunculus nevensis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow or white flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus nevensis is an important species in alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and serving as a food source for various pollinators.
