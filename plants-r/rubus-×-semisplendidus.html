Rubus × semisplendidus

Rubus × semisplendidus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red or black color, often with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners and commercial growers alike.
