Rudbeckia triloba

Rudbeckia triloba, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a dark central disk, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, rough-textured leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Rudbeckia triloba is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
