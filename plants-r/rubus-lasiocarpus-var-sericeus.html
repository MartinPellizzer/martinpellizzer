Rubus lasiocarpus var. sericeus

Rubus lasiocarpus var. sericeus, commonly known as the silky raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features prickly stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, often with a pubescent surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a red, hairy berry that ripens in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
