Rosa × revirescens

Rosa × revirescens, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. It is known for its continuous blooming habit, often flowering throughout the growing season.

Rosa × revirescens thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
