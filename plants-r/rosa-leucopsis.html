Rosa leucopsis

Rosa leucopsis, commonly known as the white-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are compound, typically with five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Rosa leucopsis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
