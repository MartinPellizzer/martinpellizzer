Ranunculus paucicarpus

Ranunculus paucicarpus, commonly known as the few-seeded buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in moist, temperate environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
