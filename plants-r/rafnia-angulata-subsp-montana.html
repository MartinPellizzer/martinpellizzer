Rafnia angulata subsp. montana

Rafnia angulata subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain rattan, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and long, flexible stems.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories. Its stems are used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

The subspecies montana is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Rafnia species.
