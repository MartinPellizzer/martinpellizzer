Rubus calycinus var. tonglooensis

Rubus calycinus var. tonglooensis, commonly known as the Tongloong blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
