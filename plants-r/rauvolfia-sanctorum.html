Rauvolfia sanctorum

Rauvolfia sanctorum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, twining stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is notable for containing alkaloids, such as reserpine, which have been used in traditional medicine and modern pharmacology.
