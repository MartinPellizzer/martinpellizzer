Rubus griseolus

Rubus griseolus, commonly known as the grayleaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to various regions of North America, particularly in the eastern and central parts of the United States. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The plant has both ornamental and ecological value, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
