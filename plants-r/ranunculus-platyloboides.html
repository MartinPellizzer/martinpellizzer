Ranunculus platyloboides

Ranunculus platyloboides, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a dark center. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically broad and lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
