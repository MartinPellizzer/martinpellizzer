Rubus thyrsiflorus microgene echinocalyx

Rubus thyrsiflorus microgene echinocalyx, commonly known as the small-fruited bramble, is a species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
