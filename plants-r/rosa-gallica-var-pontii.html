Rosa gallica var. pontii

Rosa gallica var. pontii, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching stems with thorny shoots and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
