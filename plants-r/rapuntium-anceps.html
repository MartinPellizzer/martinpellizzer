Rapuntium anceps

Rapuntium anceps, commonly known as the Italian cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a popular ingredient in salads and culinary dishes.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
