Rosa sempervirens var. latifolia

Rosa sempervirens var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf evergreen rose, is a hardy shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the winter, giving it its evergreen reputation. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and hedges.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its glossy foliage and long-lasting blooms.
