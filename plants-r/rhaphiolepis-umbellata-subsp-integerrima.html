Rhaphiolepis umbellata subsp. integerrima

Rhaphiolepis umbellata subsp.

integerrima, commonly known as the Japanese firethorn, is a shrub native to Japan and Korea. It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces bright red berries that persist through winter, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Its evergreen foliage provides year-round interest, and it is often cultivated for its hardiness and decorative appeal.
