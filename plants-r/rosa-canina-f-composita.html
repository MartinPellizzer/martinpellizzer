Rosa canina f. composita

Rosa canina f. composita, commonly known as the composite rose or the bramble rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red or orange hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It is native to Europe and thrives in a variety of soil types, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
