Rubus schomburgkii

Rubus schomburgkii, commonly known as Schomburgk's blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems characteristic of many Rubus species.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
