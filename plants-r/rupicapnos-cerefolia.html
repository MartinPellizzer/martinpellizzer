Rupicapnos cerefolia

Rupicapnos cerefolia, commonly known as the white-flowered firecracker plant, is a member of the Geraniaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Rupicapnos cerefolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
