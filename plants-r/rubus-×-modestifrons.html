Rubus × modestifrons

Rubus × modestifrons, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a hybrid shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in taste to raspberries but are typically smaller and more delicate. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in local cuisines.
