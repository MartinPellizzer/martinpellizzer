Rebutia polypetala

Rebutia polypetala, commonly known as the Polypetal Rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, cylindrical stems. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage consists of small, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, helping to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Rebutia polypetala is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
