Ruellia tubiflora var. hirsuta

Ruellia tubiflora var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
