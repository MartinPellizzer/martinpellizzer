Rhynchospora junciformis var. monocarpa

Rhynchospora junciformis var. monocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its single, terminal flower spike, which distinguishes it from other varieties of Rhynchospora.

This species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
