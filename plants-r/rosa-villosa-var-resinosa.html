Rosa villosa var. resinosa

Rosa villosa var. resinosa, commonly known as the resinous bluish rose, is a shrub-like species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage and fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces hips, which are red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
