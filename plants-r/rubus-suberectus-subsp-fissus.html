Rubus suberectus subsp. fissus

Rubus suberectus subsp.

fissus, commonly known as the fissured blackberry, is a shrub native to certain regions of North America. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
