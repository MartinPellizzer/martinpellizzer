Rhinanthus wagneri subsp. anceps

Rhinanthus wagneri subsp.

anceps, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows in grasslands and open habitats. It has bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It relies on other plants for some of its nutrients, making it an important component of nutrient cycling in grassland ecosystems.

Its presence often indicates a healthy, diverse meadow environment.
