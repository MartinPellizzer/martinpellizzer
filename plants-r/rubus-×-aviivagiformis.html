Rubus × aviivagiformis

Rubus × aviivagiformis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry.

This plant is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis. It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, with thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically pink or white, and the fruit is a aggregate of small drupelets.

It is often cultivated for its edible berries, which have a flavor profile combining the sweetness of raspberries and the tartness of blackberries.
