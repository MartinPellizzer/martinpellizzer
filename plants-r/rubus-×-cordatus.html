Rubus × cordatus

Rubus × cordatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in local cuisine in some regions.
