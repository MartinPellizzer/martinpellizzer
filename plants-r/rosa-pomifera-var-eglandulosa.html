Rosa pomifera var. eglandulosa

Rosa pomifera var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a unique variant of the rose family characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It produces large, edible fruit known as rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is typically shrub-like in growth habit. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its historical use in herbal medicine.
