Rutidosis multiflora

Rutidosis multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its decorative foliage.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
