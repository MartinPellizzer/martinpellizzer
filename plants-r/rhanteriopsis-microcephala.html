Rhanteriopsis microcephala

Rhanteriopsis microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a compact growth habit with short, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses or conservatories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes collected in the wild, raising concerns about its conservation status.
