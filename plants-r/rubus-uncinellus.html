Rubus uncinellus

Rubus uncinellus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with five petals.

Rubus uncinellus is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
