Ribes hirtellum

Ribes hirtellum, commonly known as smooth currant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features alternate, simple, and ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are usually red or yellow when mature. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, and it is often found in moist woodland habitats.

Ribes hirtellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
