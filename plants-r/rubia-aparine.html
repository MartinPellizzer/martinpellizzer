Rubia aparine

Rubia aparine, commonly known as common hedge bindweed.

It is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread aggressively. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, sandpapery texture, and the plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.
