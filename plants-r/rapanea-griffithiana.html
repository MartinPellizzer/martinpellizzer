Rapanea griffithiana

Rapanea griffithiana, commonly known as the Himalayan boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact foliage consisting of small, glossy, dark green leaves. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, dark purple berries.

Rapanea griffithiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its neat growth habit and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
