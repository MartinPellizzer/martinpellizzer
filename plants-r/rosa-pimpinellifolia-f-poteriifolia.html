Rosa pimpinellifolia f. poteriifolia

Rosa pimpinellifolia f. poteriifolia, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a hybrid form of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of pink to white flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or forested habitats. It has a compact growth habit and produces rosehips that are typically red or orange in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
