Ranunculus tuvinicus

Ranunculus tuvinicus, commonly known as the Tuvinian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ranunculus tuvinicus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
