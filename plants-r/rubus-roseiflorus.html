Rubus roseiflorus

Rubus roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered raspberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. Its fruit, similar to raspberries, is edible and has a tart flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.
