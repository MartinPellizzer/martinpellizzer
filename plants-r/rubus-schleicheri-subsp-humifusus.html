Rubus schleicheri subsp. humifusus

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

humifusus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a shrub-like plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with arching stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
