Rosa oblongifolia

Rosa oblongifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
