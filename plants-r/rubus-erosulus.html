Rubus erosulus

Rubus erosulus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically black when ripe, containing multiple small seeds. It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local use.
