Raphanistrum raphanistrum

Raphanistrum raphanistrum, commonly known as common water spinach, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the wetlands of Asia.

It features erect, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near water bodies.

Raphanistrum raphanistrum is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
