Rumex induratus

Rumex induratus, commonly known as rough dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed, and often hairy stems, which are typically reddish in color. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a wavy margin and a prominent central vein, and they are usually covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on short stalks.

The plant is often found in moist soils along roadsides, riverbanks, and in disturbed areas.
