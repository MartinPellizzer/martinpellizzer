Racosperma fimbriatum

Racosperma fimbriatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in winter, followed by elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

Racosperma fimbriatum is adapted to dry and sandy soils, making it a hardy species found in various arid and semi-arid regions.
