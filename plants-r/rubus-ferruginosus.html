Rubus ferruginosus

Rubus ferruginosus, commonly known as the rusty blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are often covered in a fine, rusty-colored bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and have a leathery texture.

The rusty blackberry is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
