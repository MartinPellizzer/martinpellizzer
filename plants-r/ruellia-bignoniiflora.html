Ruellia bignoniiflora

Ruellia bignoniiflora, commonly known as the trumpet vine or sweet flag, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces small, dark berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others.

Ruellia bignoniiflora is also valued for its adaptability and resilience in warm, humid climates.
