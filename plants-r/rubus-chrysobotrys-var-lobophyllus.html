Rubus chrysobotrys var. lobophyllus

Rubus chrysobotrys var. lobophyllus, commonly known as the goldenleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically lobed and have a distinctive yellowish or golden hue on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural areas.
