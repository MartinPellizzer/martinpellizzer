Ribes alpinum var. laciniatum

Ribes alpinum var. laciniatum, commonly known as the Alpine gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in short, glandular hairs. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with finely toothed margins and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
