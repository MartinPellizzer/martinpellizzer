Ranunculus fallacior

Ranunculus fallacior, commonly known as the false water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure, and the flowers usually have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus fallacior is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often found in riparian habitats.
