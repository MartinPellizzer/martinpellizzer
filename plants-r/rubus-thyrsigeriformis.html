Rubus thyrsigeriformis

Rubus thyrsigeriformis, commonly known as the thrysis raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
