Rubus harperi

Rubus harperi, commonly known as the Harperi raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of small, red, edible berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The berries are used in jams and wines, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
