Rosa hirtifolia var. genuina

Rosa hirtifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the rough rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
