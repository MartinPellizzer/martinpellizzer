Rubus pyramidalis subsp. amphichlorus

Rubus pyramidalis subsp.

amphichlorus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically red when mature. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The shrub thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
