Rumex ginii

Rumex ginii, commonly known as the Italian dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Rumex ginii thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
