Rosa uniflorella subsp. adenopetala

Rosa uniflorella subsp.

adenopetala, commonly known as the腺叶蔷薇, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with glandular dots on the underside, which give it its specific epithet "adenopetala.".

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, often with five petals. It is native to parts of Asia, including China and India, and thrives in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
