Rubus minutiflorus var. crassiusculus

Rubus minutiflorus var. crassiusculus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildlife habitat.
