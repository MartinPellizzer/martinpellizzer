Romulea atrandra var. luteiflora

Romulea atrandra var. luteiflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
