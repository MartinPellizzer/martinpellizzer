Rubus aculeo-idaeus

Rubus aculeo-idaeus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that develop into edible blackberries. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North America, and thrives in temperate climates. Its fruit is rich in antioxidants and is widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.

The plant's growth habit includes shrub-like forms with perennial canes that produce fruit annually.
