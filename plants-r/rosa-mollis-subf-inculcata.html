Rosa mollis subf. inculcata

Rosa mollis subf.

inculcata, commonly known as the soft rose or velvet rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include stipulate stipules and stipitate pedicels, which are common in the Rosa genus.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
