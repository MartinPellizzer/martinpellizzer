Rhododendron mollicomum var. rockii

Rhododendron mollicomum var. rockii, commonly known as the Rockii rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
