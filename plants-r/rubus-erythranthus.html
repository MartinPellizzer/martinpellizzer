Rubus erythranthus

Rubus erythranthus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pink, appearing in the spring.

The fruit, which develops from the flower, is a aggregate fruit composed of many small drupes.
