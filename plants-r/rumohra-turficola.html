Rumohra turficola

Rumohra turficola, commonly known as the turfy rumohra, is a species of grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in wetland and swampy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its habitat.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
