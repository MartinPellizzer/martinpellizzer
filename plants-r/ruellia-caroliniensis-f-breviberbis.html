Ruellia caroliniensis f. breviberbis

Ruellia caroliniensis f. breviberbis, commonly known as the short-stalked ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
