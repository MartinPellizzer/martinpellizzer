Raphanus sativus convar. hybernus

Raphanus sativus convar. hybernus, commonly known as the wild radish, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in a raceme, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
