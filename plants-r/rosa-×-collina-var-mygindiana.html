Rosa × collina var. mygindiana

Rosa × collina var. mygindiana, commonly known as the Mygindian hill rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with multiple stems forming a dense, bushy structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, ovate, and slightly serrated, providing a lush backdrop to the blooms.

Rosa × collina var. mygindiana is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for alpine gardens.
