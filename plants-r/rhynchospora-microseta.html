Rhynchospora microseta

Rhynchospora microseta, commonly known as the small-hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, undisturbed wetland ecosystems.
