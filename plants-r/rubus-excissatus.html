Rubus excissatus

Rubus excissatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically narrow and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it identifiable within the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberries.
