Ranunculus samojedorum

Ranunculus samojedorum, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has finely divided leaves and produces small, yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in its native range.
