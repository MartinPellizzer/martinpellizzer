Rhynchosia volubilis

Rhynchosia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing hyacinth or yellow climber, is a tropical vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Rhynchosia volubilis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

It is also known for its rapid growth habit, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in suitable environments.
