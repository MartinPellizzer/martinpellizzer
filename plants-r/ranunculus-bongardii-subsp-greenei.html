Ranunculus bongardii subsp. greenei

Ranunculus bongardii subsp.

greenei, commonly known as the Greene's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other herbaceous plants with distinctive floral structures.

This subspecies is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
