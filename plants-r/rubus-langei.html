Rubus langei

Rubus langei, commonly known as the Lange's blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The berries are typically harvested when fully ripe, and they are used both for culinary purposes and in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of South American flora.
