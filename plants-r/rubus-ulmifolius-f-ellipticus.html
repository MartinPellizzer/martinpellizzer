Rubus ulmifolius f. ellipticus

Rubus ulmifolius f. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The leaves are elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin, and are often darker in color compared to the typical variety. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when mature.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
