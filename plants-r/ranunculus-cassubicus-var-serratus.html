Ranunculus cassubicus var. serratus

Ranunculus cassubicus var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals that often have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided with a waxy coating, and the stems are hairy and somewhat succulent.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its showy blooms.
