Ruellia hirta

Ruellia hirta, commonly known as the hairy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1 meter in height. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and have a rough texture. Its showy blue or purple flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ruellia hirta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
