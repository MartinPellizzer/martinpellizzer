Ranunculus acris f. stevenii

Ranunculus acris f. stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's meadow buttercup, is a botanical variant of the meadow buttercup.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
