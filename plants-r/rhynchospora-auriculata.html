Rhynchospora auriculata

Rhynchospora auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers, typically greenish or brownish in color. Rhynchospora auriculata is often found in shallow water or moist soil, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic plant communities.
