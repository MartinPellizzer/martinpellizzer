Rubus × amplifoliatus var. acontophorus

Rubus × amplifoliatus var. acontophorus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to red and then to black as they mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
