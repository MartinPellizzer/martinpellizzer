Rhodiola kansuensis

Rhodiola kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of China, particularly in the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Rhodiola kansuensis is adapted to cold, arid environments and has a long growing season.

It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported adaptogenic and medicinal properties.
