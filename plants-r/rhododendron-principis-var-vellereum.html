Rhododendron principis var. vellereum

Rhododendron principis var. vellereum, commonly known as the Vellereum rhododendron, is a rare and distinctive species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in cool, moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded, forested environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its native range.
