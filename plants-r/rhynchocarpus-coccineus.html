Rhynchocarpus coccineus

Rhynchocarpus coccineus, commonly known as the red-fruited legume, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, crimson flowers that attract pollinators. The plant bears pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to regions in Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
