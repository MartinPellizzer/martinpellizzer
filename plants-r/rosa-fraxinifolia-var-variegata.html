Rosa fraxinifolia var. variegata

Rosa fraxinifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated ash-leaf rose, is a hybrid rose species with distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are broad, ovate, and feature a striking variegation pattern of white and green, resembling the leaves of the ash tree. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a compact shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and seasonal blooms.
