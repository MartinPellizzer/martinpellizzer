Ranunculus trichocarpus var. diversifolius

Ranunculus trichocarpus var. diversifolius, commonly known as the variable-fronded buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. The leaves are typically palmately divided, with variations in shape and size across different individuals. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, temperate environments.

This variant is notable for its morphological diversity, which makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
