Rubus vermontanus

Rubus vermontanus, commonly known as the Vermont blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are dark purple to black when ripe, and are edible, though often tart. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
