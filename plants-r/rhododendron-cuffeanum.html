Rhododendron cuffeanum

Rhododendron cuffeanum, commonly known as the Colombian rhododendron, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron cuffeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
