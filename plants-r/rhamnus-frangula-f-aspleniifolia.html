Rhamnus frangula f. aspleniifolia

Rhamnus frangula f. aspleniifolia, commonly known as the aspleniifoliate buckthorn, is a botanical variant of the European buckthorn.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions. It typically grows in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring moist conditions.

Its foliage provides habitat for various insects, making it ecologically significant in certain ecosystems.
