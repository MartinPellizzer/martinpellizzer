Rapistrum aegyptiacum

Rapistrum aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian rocket, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found growing in disturbed soils and can be a common weed in agricultural fields.
