Rubus infestus var. sublaevis

Rubus infestus var. sublaevis, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, with three to five lobes, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits, which have a rich, sweet flavor.
