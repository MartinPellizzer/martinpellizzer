Rubus fictus microgene teretiramus

Rubus fictus microgene teretiramus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical structure includes thorny stems and a complex floral arrangement typical of the Rubus genus.
