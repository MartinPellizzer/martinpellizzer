Rudbeckia laevigata

Rudbeckia laevigata, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flower heads with a distinctive brown central disk, which bloom from late summer to early fall. Rudbeckia laevigata thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
