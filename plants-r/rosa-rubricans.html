Rosa rubricans

Rosa rubricans, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant features upright, thorny stems that are often reddish in color, hence its common name. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a fragrant scent.

Rosa rubricans produces small, oval-shaped fruits called hips, which are edible and contain seeds.
