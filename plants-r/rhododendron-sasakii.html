Rhododendron sasakii

Rhododendron sasakii, commonly known as the Sasakii rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron sasakii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
