Rubus loropetalus

Rubus loropetalus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and prickly stems, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black in color, resembling raspberries in appearance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Rubus loropetalus thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
