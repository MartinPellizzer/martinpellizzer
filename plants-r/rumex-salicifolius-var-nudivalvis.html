Rumex salicifolius var. nudivalvis

Rumex salicifolius var. nudivalvis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are edible and have been used historically in salads and as a vegetable.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments, making it a common indicator plant in certain ecosystems.
