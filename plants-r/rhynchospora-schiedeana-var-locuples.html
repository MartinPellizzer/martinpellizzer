Rhynchospora schiedeana var. locuples

Rhynchospora schiedeana var. locuples, commonly known as the Florida marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Florida.

It typically grows in marshy or swampy areas, thriving in saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This variety is adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of its native habitat.
