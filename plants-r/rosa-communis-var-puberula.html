Rosa communis var. puberula

Rosa communis var. puberula, commonly known as the downy rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and delicate pink flowers.

This shrub typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The flowers are usually borne singly or in small clusters, adding a charming appearance to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in the production of rose essential oils.
