Rapuntium paludosum

Rapuntium paludosum, commonly known as marsh cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. This perennial herb typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along the edges of ponds and streams. It produces small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
