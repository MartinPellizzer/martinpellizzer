Rosa communis subvar. nitida

Rosa communis subvar. nitida, commonly known as the silverbark rose, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and silvery-white, peeling bark.

It produces fragrant pink to red flowers in early summer, which are followed by oval, red-orange hips. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders and hedges.

Its unique bark and showy blooms make it a popular choice in landscape design.
