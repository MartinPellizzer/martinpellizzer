Ranunculus glechomoides

Ranunculus glechomoides, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing habit with a mat-forming structure, often spreading through rhizomes. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can be covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
