Rubus hirtus var. violaceus

Rubus hirtus var. violaceus, commonly known as the purple-leaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and dark green, often purple-tinged leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and edible fruits.
