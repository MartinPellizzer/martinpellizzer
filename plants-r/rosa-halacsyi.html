Rosa halacsyi

Rosa halacsyi, commonly known as the Hungarian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soil. Rosa halacsyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the flora in its native region.
