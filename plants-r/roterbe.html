Roterbe

Roterbe, commonly known as the red beet, is a biennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are typically red in color, though they can also be yellow or white depending on the variety. The plant thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, often cultivated for its edible root and leaves. Its leaves are rich in nutrients like iron and vitamins, making them a valuable addition to the diet.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
