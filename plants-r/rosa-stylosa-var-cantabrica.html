Rosa stylosa var. cantabrica

Rosa stylosa var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian rose, is a shrub native to the western Pyrenees and the Cantabrian Mountains of northern Spain.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces red hips that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticultural traditions.
