Rubus nemoralis cornubiensis

Rubus nemoralis cornubiensis, commonly known as the Cornish blackberry, is a shrub native to the UK, particularly found in the southwest region of England.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and raspberries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus family.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
