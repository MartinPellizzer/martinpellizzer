Rosa glauca var. rigidula

Rosa glauca var. rigidula, commonly known as the rigid white rose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has sturdy, thorny stems and narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species within the rose family.
