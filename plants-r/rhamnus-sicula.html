Rhamnus sicula

Rhamnus sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Rhamnus sicula is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh environments.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of dye highlights its cultural and practical significance.
