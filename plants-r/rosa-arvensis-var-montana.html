Rosa arvensis var. montana

Rosa arvensis var. montana, commonly known as the mountain dog rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces oval, red-tinged fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Rosa arvensis var. montana is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
