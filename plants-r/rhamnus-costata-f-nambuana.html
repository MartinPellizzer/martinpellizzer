Rhamnus costata f. nambuana

Rhamnus costata f. nambuana, commonly known as the Nambu flowering buckthorn, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rhamnaceae family.
