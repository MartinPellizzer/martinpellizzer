Ruagea tomentosa

Ruagea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly ruagea, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and have a thick, waxy texture.

Ruagea tomentosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests and coastal areas.
