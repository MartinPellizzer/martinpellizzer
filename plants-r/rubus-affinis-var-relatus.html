Rubus affinis var. relatus

Rubus affinis var. relatus, commonly known as the Canadian blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This variety is valued for its fruit, which is used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
