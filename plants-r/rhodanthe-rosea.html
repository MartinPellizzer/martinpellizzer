Rhodanthe rosea

Rhodanthe rosea, commonly known as the pink burr bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing fruits that disperse its seeds effectively. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Rhodanthe rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
