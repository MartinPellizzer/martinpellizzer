Raritebe

Raritebe, commonly known as the moonflower, is a striking botanical specimen native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet, intoxicating fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers typically open only after dusk and close by morning, adapting to low-light conditions.

Raritebe thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid environment to flourish.
