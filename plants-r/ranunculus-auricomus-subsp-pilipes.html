Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pilipes

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pilipes, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent, petal-like sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the species is characterized by its distinctive, glossy foliage.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
