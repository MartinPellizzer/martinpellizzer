Rubus × dispansus

Rubus × dispansus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which develop from multiple ovaria within a single flower. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, palmately compound, and have serrated margins.

The fruit, known as a blackberry, is edible and contains numerous small, dark seeds.
