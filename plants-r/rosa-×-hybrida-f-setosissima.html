Rosa × hybrida f. setosissima

Rosa × hybrida f. setosissima, commonly known as the bristly hybrid rose, is a horticultural form of the widely cultivated Rosa × hybrida.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and foliage, which give it a rugged appearance. The plant typically produces large, showy flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote flowering.
