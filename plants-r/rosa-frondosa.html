Rosa frondosa

Rosa frondosa, commonly known as the fronded rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant pink or white flowers and its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Rosa frondosa is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to six feet.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
