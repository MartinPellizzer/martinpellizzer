Rosa xanthina var. pilosa

Rosa xanthina var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy yellow rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a distinctive addition to floral displays.
