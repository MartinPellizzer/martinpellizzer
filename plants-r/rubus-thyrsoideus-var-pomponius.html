Rubus thyrsoideus var. pomponius

Rubus thyrsoideus var. pomponius, commonly known as the Pomponius blackberry, is a variant of the thyrsoideus species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in jams and preserves due to its tart flavor and firm texture.
