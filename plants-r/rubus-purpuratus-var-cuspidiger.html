Rubus purpuratus var. cuspidiger

Rubus purpuratus var. cuspidiger, commonly known as the prickly rose or red raspberry.

It is a variant of the red raspberry plant, characterized by its thorny stems and edible, red berries. The plant is native to parts of North America and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically pink or white.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.
