Rytidosperma nudiflorum

Rytidosperma nudiflorum, commonly known as the nakedflower grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and smooth in texture. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Rytidosperma nudiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
