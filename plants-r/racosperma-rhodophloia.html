Racosperma rhodophloia

Racosperma rhodophloia, commonly known as the red-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
