Rosa celerata

Rosa celerata, commonly known as the quick-growing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa celerata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it adaptable to various garden conditions.
