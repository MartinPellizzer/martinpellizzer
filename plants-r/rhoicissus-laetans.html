Rhoicissus laetans

Rhoicissus laetans, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a silvery underside, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Rhoicissus laetans is also known for its ability to climb using tendrils, making it a versatile plant for trellises and vertical gardens.
