Rubus nitidus subsp. indutus

Rubus nitidus subsp.

indutus, commonly known as the velvet-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically edible. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters.

The species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
