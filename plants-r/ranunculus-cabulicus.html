Ranunculus cabulicus

Ranunculus cabulicus, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
