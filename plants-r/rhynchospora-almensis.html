Rhynchospora almensis

Rhynchospora almensis, commonly known as the almen's spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in temperate regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Rhynchospora almensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
