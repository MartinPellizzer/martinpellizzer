Rubus arduennensis subsp. grabowskii

Rubus arduennensis subsp.

grabowskii, commonly known as the Grabowskii blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant typically grows in woodland edges and moist soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
