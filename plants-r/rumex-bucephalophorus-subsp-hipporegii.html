Rumex bucephalophorus subsp. hipporegii

Rumex bucephalophorus subsp.

hipporegii, commonly known as the Himalayan sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

The plant features heart-shaped, reddish-pink leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing yellowish-green blooms.

The species is valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
