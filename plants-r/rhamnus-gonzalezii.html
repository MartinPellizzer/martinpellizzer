Rhamnus gonzalezii

Rhamnus gonzalezii, commonly known as the Gonzalez's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and smooth, thornless stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by reddish berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
