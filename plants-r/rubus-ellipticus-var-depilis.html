Rubus ellipticus var. depilis

Rubus ellipticus var. depilis, commonly known as the smooth-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless fruit surfaces, which distinguish it from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its flavorful fruits and adaptability to various garden environments.
