Rhododendron thayerianum

Rhododendron thayerianum, commonly known as the Thayer's rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

Rhododendron thayerianum is also notable for its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
