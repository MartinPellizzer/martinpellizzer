Rhapis multifida

Rhapis multifida, commonly known as the split-leaf bamboo, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, branched stem with arching, feathery leaves that are divided into narrow, finger-like segments. The leaves are typically bright green and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas or indoor cultivation.

This plant is valued for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid environments.
