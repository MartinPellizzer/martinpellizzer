Rubus guentheri var. adenodontus

Rubus guentheri var. adenodontus, commonly known as the Guenther's raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
