Rhododendron lophophorum

Rhododendron lophophorum, commonly known as the Yunnan rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly in the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a leathery texture and produces clusters of pink to red flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include showy, funnel-shaped blossoms that are often borne on long pedicels.

Rhododendron lophophorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
