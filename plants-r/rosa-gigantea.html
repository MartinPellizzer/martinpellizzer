Rosa gigantea

Rosa gigantea, commonly known as the giant rose, is a striking species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features robust, thorny stems and large, dark green leaves with serrated edges. Rosa gigantea thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
