Rosa parviflora plena

Rosa parviflora plena, commonly known as the double-flowered shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features dense, thornless stems and is characterized by its clusters of double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red fruits known as hips. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
