Rhododendron fuscipilum

Rhododendron fuscipilum, commonly known as the dark-haired rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring well-drained acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
