Rosa blanda var. hermannii

Rosa blanda var. hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann's sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pale pink to white, appearing in clusters. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and blooms.
