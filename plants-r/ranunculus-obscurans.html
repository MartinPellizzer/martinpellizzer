Ranunculus obscurans

Ranunculus obscurans, commonly known as the dark buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have numerous sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus obscurans is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
