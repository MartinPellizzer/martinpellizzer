Rachicallis capitata

Rachicallis capitata, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a creeping rhizomatous stem and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are aromatic, with a strong, bitter scent, and the plant is known for its medicinal and pesticidal properties.

Rachicallis capitata is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
