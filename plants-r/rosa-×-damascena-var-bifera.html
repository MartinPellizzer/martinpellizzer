Rosa × damascena var. bifera

Rosa × damascena var. bifera, commonly known as the double-flowered damask rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its fully double blooms, which consist of numerous petals arranged in layers, giving it a lush, ornamental appearance. The plant is native to the Middle East and has been widely cultivated for its fragrance and ornamental value. It typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage.

This variety is often used in perfumery and floral arrangements due to its rich, sweet scent.
