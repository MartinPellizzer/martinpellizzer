Rhododendron arborescens var. richardsonii

Rhododendron arborescens var. richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's alpine azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
