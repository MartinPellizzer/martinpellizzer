Rosa mollis f. villifera

Rosa mollis f. villifera, commonly known as the soft rose or velvet rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant with soft, fuzzy leaves and fragrant, pale pink to white flowers.

It is a form of Rosa mollis, a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and is characterized by its dense, thornless stems. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
