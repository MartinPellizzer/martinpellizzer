Rosa rubiginosa f. dimorphacantha

Rosa rubiginosa f. dimorphacantha, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown stems and dense, thorny growth habit. The leaves are typically compound, with five to seven leaflets that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
