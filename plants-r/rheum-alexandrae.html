Rheum alexandrae

Rheum alexandrae, commonly known as the Himalayan rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, reddish stalks that bear clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
