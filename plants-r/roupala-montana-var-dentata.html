Roupala montana var. dentata

Roupala montana var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated roupala, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features oval, serrated leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
