Rhynchosia longiramosa

Rhynchosia longiramosa, commonly known as the long-stemmed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Rhynchosia longiramosa is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it has potential agricultural and ecological benefits due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
