Rosa gallica var. tomentosa

Rosa gallica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the white musk rose, is a variety of the Gallica rose native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, velvety leaves. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, woolly layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This rose is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery and heraldry.
