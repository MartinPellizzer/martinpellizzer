Richella cuneiformis

Richella cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf richella, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it its scientific name "cuneiformis," which means wedge-shaped.

The flowers are usually yellow or brown and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
