Ranunculus auricomus subsp. patulidentiformis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

patulidentiformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves and more robust growth compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
