Rubus purpuratus microgene brachyandroides

Rubus purpuratus microgene brachyandroides, commonly known as the purple raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing leaves and the second year bearing fruit. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
