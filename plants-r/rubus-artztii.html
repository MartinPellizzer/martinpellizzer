Rubus artztii

Rubus artztii, commonly known as the Artzt's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
