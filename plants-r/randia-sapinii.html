Randia sapinii

Randia sapinii, commonly known as the swamp rosemary, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, swampy environments and is often found in areas with high humidity. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Randia sapinii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various insect and bird species.
