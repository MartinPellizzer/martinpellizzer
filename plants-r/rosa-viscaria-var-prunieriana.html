Rosa viscaria var. prunieriana

Rosa viscaria var. prunieriana, commonly known as the Prunier's bristly rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its hairy foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive, bristly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
