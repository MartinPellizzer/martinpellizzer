Rorippa africana subsp. africana

Rorippa africana subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in wet or marshy environments.

The plant features alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters, typical of the mustard family.

The seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are elongated, dry fruits that split open to release the seeds.
