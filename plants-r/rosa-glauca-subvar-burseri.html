Rosa glauca subvar. burseri

Rosa glauca subvar. burseri, commonly known as the Burseri rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters. The plant has grayish-green foliage that is covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its stems are thorny, and it is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
