Rosa dilutella

Rosa dilutella, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Rosa dilutella is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
