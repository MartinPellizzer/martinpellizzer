Rhodocalyx cuneifolius

Rhodocalyx cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rhodocalyx, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and display a range of colors from white to pink.

Rhodocalyx cuneifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
