Rottboellia loliacea

Rottboellia loliacea, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value comes from its distinctive foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
