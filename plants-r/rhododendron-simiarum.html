Rhododendron simiarum

Rhododendron simiarum, commonly known as the mountain rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron simiarum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
