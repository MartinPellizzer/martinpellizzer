Rubus almus

Rubus almus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

Rubus almus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
