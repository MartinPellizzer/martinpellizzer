Rhododendron peramoenum

Rhododendron peramoenum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron peramoenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
