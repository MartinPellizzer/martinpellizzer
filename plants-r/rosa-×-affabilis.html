Rosa × affabilis

Rosa × affabilis, commonly known as the English shrub rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa canina.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorned stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Rosa × affabilis is also valued for its resistance to common rose diseases.
