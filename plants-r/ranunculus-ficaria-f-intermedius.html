Ranunculus ficaria f. intermedius

Ranunculus ficaria f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate crowfoot, is a variant of the water crowfoot species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its leaves are usually submerged or floating, with the flowers emerging above the water surface.

The species is notable for its adaptability to varying water conditions and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
