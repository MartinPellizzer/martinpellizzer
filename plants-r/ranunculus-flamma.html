Ranunculus flamma

Ranunculus flamma, commonly known as the flame buttercup, is a striking flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers, which are typically cup-shaped with numerous petals. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ranunculus flamma is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ornamental value.
