Rosa lutea bicolor

Rosa lutea bicolor, commonly known as the twin-colored yellow rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive bicoloration, featuring yellow petals with a white or pink center. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant typically thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The flowers are fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
