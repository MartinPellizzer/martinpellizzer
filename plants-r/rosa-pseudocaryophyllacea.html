Rosa pseudocaryophyllacea

Rosa pseudocaryophyllacea, commonly known as the pseudocaryophyllacea rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, pinnate leaves, and an affinity for well-drained, alkaline soils.

It is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.
