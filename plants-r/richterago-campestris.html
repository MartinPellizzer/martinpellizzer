Richterago campestris

Richterago campestris, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
