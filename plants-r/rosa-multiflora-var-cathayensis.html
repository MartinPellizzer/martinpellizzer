Rosa multiflora var. cathayensis

Rosa multiflora var. cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, blooming throughout the growing season. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in hedges or as a screening plant.

The variety is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
