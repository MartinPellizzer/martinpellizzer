Ranunculus auricomus subsp. aequidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

aequidens, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often adorned with a distinctive greenish-veined center.

The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and may have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking yet delicate floral display.
