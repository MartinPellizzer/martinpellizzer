Ranunculus trivedii

Ranunculus trivedii, commonly known as the Indian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
