Rosa cinnamomea var. dahurica

Rosa cinnamomea var. dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching, thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in colder climates.
