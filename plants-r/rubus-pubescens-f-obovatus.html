Rubus pubescens f. obovatus

Rubus pubescens f. obovatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and fruit.

This plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. Its leaves are typically obovate in shape, with a soft, pubescent surface that gives it its common name. The berries produced are small, dark purple, and have a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
