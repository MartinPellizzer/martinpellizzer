Rosa acicularis var. fennica

Rosa acicularis var. fennica, commonly known as the Finnish thorned rose, is a variant of the thorned rose native to northern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
