Ridleya notabilis

Ridleya notabilis, commonly known as the Christmas Island palm, is a unique species native to Christmas Island in the Indian Ocean.

It is a small tree with a distinctive, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant is adapted to the humid tropical climate and grows in coastal areas with well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and green, and it produces bright red fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Ridleya notabilis is also notable for its role in the ecosystem and its conservation status as a vulnerable species.
