Rubus thomsonii

Rubus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
