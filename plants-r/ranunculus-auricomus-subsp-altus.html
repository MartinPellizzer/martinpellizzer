Ranunculus auricomus subsp. altus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

altus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and upright.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
