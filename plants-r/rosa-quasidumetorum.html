Rosa quasidumetorum

Rosa quasidumetorum, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Rosa quasidumetorum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
