Rosa elliptica f. longipedata

Rosa elliptica f. longipedata, commonly known as the long-stemmed elliptic rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and fragrant, pale pink flowers.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet, featuring thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its botanical features make it a desirable ornamental plant for gardens and landscapes.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
