Ranunculus linearilobus subsp. mogoltavicus

Ranunculus linearilobus subsp.

mogoltavicus, commonly known as the Mogoltav narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to specific regions in Central Asia. It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in mountainous areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
