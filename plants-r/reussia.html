Reussia

Reussia, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a genus of coniferous evergreen trees native to the western coast of North America.

These trees are characterized by their tall, straight trunks and dense, dark green foliage. Reussia species, such as Sequoia sempervirens, are known for their longevity and can live for thousands of years. They have a unique root system that helps them survive in moist, temperate climates.

The bark of Reussia trees is thick and fibrous, providing protection against fire and pests.
