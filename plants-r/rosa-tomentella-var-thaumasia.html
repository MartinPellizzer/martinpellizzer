Rosa tomentella var. thaumasia

Rosa tomentella var. thaumasia, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is a perennial shrub that produces hips, which are small, red, fleshy fruits.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
