Rubus pseudoarcticus

Rubus pseudoarcticus, commonly known as the arctic bramble, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
