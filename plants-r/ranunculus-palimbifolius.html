Ranunculus palimbifolius

Ranunculus palimbifolius, commonly known as the palimbifolius buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features trifoliate or palmately compound leaves, which are typically palmate and have a lobed appearance. Its flowers are generally yellow or white, with numerous stamens and a simple pistil.

Ranunculus palimbifolius thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
