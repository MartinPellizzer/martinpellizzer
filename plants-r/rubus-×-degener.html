Rubus × degener

Rubus × degener, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically black when ripe, though they can also be red or purple. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in various culinary applications.
