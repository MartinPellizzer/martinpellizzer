Rubus saxonicus

Rubus saxonicus, commonly known as the saxonic raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to northern Europe.

It produces small, edible red berries that are often used in jams and desserts. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
