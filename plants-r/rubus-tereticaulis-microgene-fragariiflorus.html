Rubus tereticaulis microgene fragariiflorus

Rubus tereticaulis microgene fragariiflorus, commonly known as the fragrant raspberry, is a species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its fragrant foliage and berries make it a notable species in both ornamental and ecological contexts.
