Rosa × dumetorum inaequiserrata

Rosa × dumetorum inaequiserrata, commonly known as the unequal-toothed shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are compound, with serrated edges that vary in depth, giving the plant its specific epithet. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
