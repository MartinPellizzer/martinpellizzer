Rhamnus oleoides subsp. velutina

Rhamnus oleoides subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvet buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
