Racosperma elatum

Racosperma elatum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which are typical of the genus.

Racosperma elatum is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
