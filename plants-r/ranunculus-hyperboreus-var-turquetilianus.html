Ranunculus hyperboreus var. turquetilianus

Ranunculus hyperboreus var. turquetilianus, commonly known as the Turquetilian meadow buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as tundra and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it has a short, fibrous root system.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with poor soil conditions.
