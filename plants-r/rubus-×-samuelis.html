Rubus × samuelis

Rubus × samuelis, commonly known as the Samuel's blackberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (slippery elm) and Rubus pectoralis (a type of blackberry). This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value in gardens.
