Rubus brachystemonoides

Rubus brachystemonoides, commonly known as the red-veined raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy species in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and agricultural studies.
