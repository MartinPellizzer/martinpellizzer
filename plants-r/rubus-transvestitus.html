Rubus transvestitus

Rubus transvestitus, commonly known as the Transvestite Raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is an important plant for pollinators and wildlife.
