Rottboellia heterochroa

Rottboellia heterochroa, commonly known as the variable grass or variable panic grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, two-lobed flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive weed in certain agricultural settings.
