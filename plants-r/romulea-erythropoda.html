Romulea erythropoda

Romulea erythropoda, commonly known as the red-flowered romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related genera. The plant is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Romulea erythropoda thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
