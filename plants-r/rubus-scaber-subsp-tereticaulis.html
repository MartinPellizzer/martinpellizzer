Rubus scaber subsp. tereticaulis

Rubus scaber subsp.

tereticaulis, commonly known as the slender-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered in groups of three, typical of the Rubus genus.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
