Rhynchospora ostenii

Rhynchospora ostenii, commonly known as the Florida marsh sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-parted flowers. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Rhynchospora ostenii plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
