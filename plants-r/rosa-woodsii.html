Rosa woodsii

Rosa woodsii, commonly known as the cliffrose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the western United States and parts of Canada, typically growing in rocky or alpine environments. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like form, with thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces red or orange hips in the fall.

Rosa woodsii is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
