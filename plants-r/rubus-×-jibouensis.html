Rubus × jibouensis

Rubus × jibouensis, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which resembles a raspberry, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.
