Racosperma cochlocarpum

Racosperma cochlocarpum, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which benefits surrounding vegetation. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in woodlands and shrublands.
