Ranunculus pardalianches

Ranunculus pardalianches, commonly known as the Spanish buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive black center. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wetlands. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
