Rubus × argutifolius var. tomentellus

Rubus × argutifolius var. tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white to grayish woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark red to black in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
