Ranunculus millefolius

Ranunculus millefolius, commonly known as meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive blooms and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
