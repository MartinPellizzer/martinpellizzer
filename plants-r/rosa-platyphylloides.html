Rosa platyphylloides

Rosa platyphylloides, commonly known as the flat-leaf rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems covered in thorns. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
