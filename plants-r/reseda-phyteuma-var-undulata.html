Reseda phyteuma var. undulata

Reseda phyteuma var. undulata, commonly known as the undulating ladies' mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique, low-maintenance plant.
