Rogiera versicolor

Rogiera versicolor, commonly known as the variable rogeria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that can change hue throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, adding ornamental value to gardens. Rogiera versicolor thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage and blooms make it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
