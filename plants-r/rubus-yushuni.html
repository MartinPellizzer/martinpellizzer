Rubus yushuni

Rubus yushuni, commonly known as the Yushuni blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes a wide variety of shrubs and subshrubs known for their edible fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with cool, temperate climates, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and potential as a source of novel fruits.
