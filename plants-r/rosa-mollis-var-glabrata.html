Rosa mollis var. glabrata

Rosa mollis var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth rose or the glabrous rose, is a variety of the species Rosa mollis.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and foliage, which distinguish it from other rose varieties. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
