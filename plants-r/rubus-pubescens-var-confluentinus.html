Rubus pubescens var. confluentinus

Rubus pubescens var. confluentinus, commonly known as the hairy bramble.

This variant is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color.

Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with a hairy underside, contributing to its common name.
