Rosa × carnea

Rosa × carnea, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Rosa × carnea is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in the production of rose oil.
