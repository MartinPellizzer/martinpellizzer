Rhododendron dendricola

Rhododendron dendricola, commonly known as the tree rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are clustered in trusses and bloom in shades of pink to purple, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.
