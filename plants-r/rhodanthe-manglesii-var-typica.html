Rhodanthe manglesii var. typica

Rhodanthe manglesii var. typica, commonly known as the coastal rhodanthe, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal dunes or cliff faces.

The species is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.
