Radiovittaria moritziana

Radiovittaria moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
