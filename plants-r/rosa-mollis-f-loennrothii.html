Rosa mollis f. loennrothii

Rosa mollis f. loennrothii, commonly known as the Loennroth's soft rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. This form is notable for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.

The plant is also valued for its historical significance in horticultural collections and its role in the study of rose genetics.
