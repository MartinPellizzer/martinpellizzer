Rubus rhombifolius subsp. sampaianus

Rubus rhombifolius subsp.

sampaianus, commonly known as the Sampaian raspberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the first-year stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its leaves are rhombic in shape, with serrated margins, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
