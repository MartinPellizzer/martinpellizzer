Rothia pusilla

Rothia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf rothia, is a small flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers that are often white or pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, sturdy stem.

Rothia pusilla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
