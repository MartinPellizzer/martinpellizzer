Rhytiglossa radicosa

Rhytiglossa radicosa, commonly known as the rootless orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It lacks traditional roots and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its specialized leaves. The plant is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence that bears small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
