Ranunculus phragmiteti

Ranunculus phragmiteti, commonly known as the reed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped yellow flowers and finely divided, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in marshy areas, along the edges of ponds, and in other moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in association with reed beds.

Ranunculus phragmiteti is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
