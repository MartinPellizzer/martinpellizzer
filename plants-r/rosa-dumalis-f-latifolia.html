Rosa dumalis f. latifolia

Rosa dumalis f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The leaves are broad and leathery, with a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
