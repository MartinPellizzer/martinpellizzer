Rosa viscaria var. septicoloides

Rosa viscaria var. septicoloides, commonly known as the sticky rose, is a variant of the shrub rose species characterized by its unique botanical features.

It possesses dense, glandular hairs that give its stems and leaves a sticky texture, which is a distinguishing trait. The plant typically has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a typical rose structure. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
