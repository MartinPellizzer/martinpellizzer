Rosa tomentosa subsp. semitomentosa

Rosa tomentosa subsp.

semitomentosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems. It typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green and covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
