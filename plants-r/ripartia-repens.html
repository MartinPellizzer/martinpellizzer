Ripartia repens

Ripartia repens, commonly known as the spreading ripartia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Ripartia repens is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
