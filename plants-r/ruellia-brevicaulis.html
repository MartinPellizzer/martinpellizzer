Ruellia brevicaulis

Ruellia brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Ruellia brevicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
