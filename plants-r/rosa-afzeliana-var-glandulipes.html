Rosa afzeliana var. glandulipes

Rosa afzeliana var. glandulipes, commonly known as the glandular-stem Rosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and glandular dots, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a distinctive aromatic scent.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
