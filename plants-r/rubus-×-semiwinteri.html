Rubus × semiwinteri

Rubus × semiwinteri, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late winter or early spring. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and for local foraging.
