Randia aculeata var. chiapasina

Randia aculeata var. chiapasina, commonly known as the Mexican starfruit or Chiapas starfruit, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble star-shaped berries. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers with prominent stamens. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a mild bitterness.
