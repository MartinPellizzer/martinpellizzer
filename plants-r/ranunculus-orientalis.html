Ranunculus orientalis

Ranunculus orientalis, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a distinctive silvery or fuzzy underside.

Ranunculus orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
