Rollinia emarginata var. longipetala

Rollinia emarginata var. longipetala, commonly known as the long-petaled star-apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, resembling a small, round apple, is typically yellow or orange when ripe and contains a single large seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
