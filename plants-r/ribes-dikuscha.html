Ribes dikuscha

Ribes dikuscha, commonly known as the Kamchatka gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Russian Far East and parts of eastern Siberia.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy berries that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous regions and thrives in temperate to cold climates. The berries are edible and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes by indigenous peoples.
