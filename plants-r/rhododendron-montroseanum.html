Rhododendron montroseanum

Rhododendron montroseanum, commonly known as the Montrose rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often at high altitudes where it thrives in partial shade. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
