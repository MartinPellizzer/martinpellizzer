Rubus obtruncatus subsp. rubrans

Rubus obtruncatus subsp.

rubrans, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a sweet to tart flavor. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial to full sunlight.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and use in local ecosystems as a food source for wildlife.
