Reseda suffruticulosa

Reseda suffruticulosa, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Reseda suffruticulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
