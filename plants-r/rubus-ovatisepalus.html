Rubus ovatisepalus

Rubus ovatisepalus, commonly known as the broad-sepal raspberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five ovate sepals that give it its scientific name.
