Rosa eglanteria var. cretica

Rosa eglanteria var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits are reddish berries that mature in autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.
