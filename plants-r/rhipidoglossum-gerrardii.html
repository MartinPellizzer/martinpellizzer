Rhipidoglossum gerrardii

Rhipidoglossum gerrardii, commonly known as the red-tipped plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, oval leaves with reddish tips. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Rhipidoglossum gerrardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
