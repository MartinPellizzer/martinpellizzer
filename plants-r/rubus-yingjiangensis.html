Rubus yingjiangensis

Rubus yingjiangensis, commonly known as the Yingjiang raspberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
