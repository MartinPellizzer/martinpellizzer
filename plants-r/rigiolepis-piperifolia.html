Rigiolepis piperifolia

Rigiolepis piperifolia, commonly known as the lemon-scented honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rigiolepis piperifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
