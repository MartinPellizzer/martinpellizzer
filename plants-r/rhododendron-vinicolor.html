Rhododendron vinicolor

Rhododendron vinicolor, commonly known as the purpleleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage that turns reddish in the fall, making it a visually appealing plant in gardens. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its unique foliage and floral display.
