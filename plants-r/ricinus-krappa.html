Ricinus krappa

Ricinus krappa, commonly known as the nettleleaf castor oil plant, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces spiny capsules that contain toxic, oily seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in oil production.
