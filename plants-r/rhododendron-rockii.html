Rhododendron rockii

Rhododendron rockii, commonly known as the Hengshan rhododendron, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blossoms.

Rhododendron rockii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant in horticultural collections.
