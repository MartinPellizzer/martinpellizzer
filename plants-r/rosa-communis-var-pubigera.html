Rosa communis var. pubigera

Rosa communis var. pubigera, commonly known as the pubescent rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its hairy or pubescent stems and leaves.

This plant typically features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soil. The pubescent texture of its stems and leaves distinguishes it from other rose varieties.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
