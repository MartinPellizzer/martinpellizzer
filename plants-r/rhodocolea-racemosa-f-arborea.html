Rhodocolea racemosa f. arborea

Rhodocolea racemosa f. arborea, commonly known as the tree form of the species, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
