Rubus nemorosus var. strugensis

Rubus nemorosus var. strugensis, commonly known as the Strugensian bramble, is a variant of the common bramble found in specific regions of Europe.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typically with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species for local ecosystems and traditional herbal uses.
