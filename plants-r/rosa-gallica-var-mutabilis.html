Rosa gallica var. mutabilis

Rosa gallica var. mutabilis, commonly known as the "Changing Rose," is a species of shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its variable flower color, which can shift from white to pink to red as the blooms mature. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and dark green.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
