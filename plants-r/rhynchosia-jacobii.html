Rhynchosia jacobii

Rhynchosia jacobii, commonly known as Jacob's cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Rhynchosia jacobii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
