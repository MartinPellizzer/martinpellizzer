Ranunculus glabrifolius var. gracilior

Ranunculus glabrifolius var. gracilior, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are smooth and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
