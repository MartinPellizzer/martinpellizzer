Rubus tereticaulis var. leptopus

Rubus tereticaulis var. leptopus, commonly known as the slender trailing blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that have serrated edges.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
