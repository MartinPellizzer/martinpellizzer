Ranunculus adoneus

Ranunculus adoneus, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features showy, blue to purple flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus adoneus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
