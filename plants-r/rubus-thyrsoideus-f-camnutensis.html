Rubus thyrsoideus f. camnutensis

Rubus thyrsoideus f. camnutensis, commonly known as the camnutan raspberry, is a botanical variant of the thyrsoideus species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and the presence of dense, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, similar to other raspberries. It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and potential use in horticulture.
