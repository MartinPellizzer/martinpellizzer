Rumex bucephalophorus var. hipporegii

Rumex bucephalophorus var. hipporegii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dock, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender, upright stalks. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
