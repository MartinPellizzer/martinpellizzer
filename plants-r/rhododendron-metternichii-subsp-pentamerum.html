Rhododendron metternichii subsp. pentamerum

Rhododendron metternichii subsp.

pentamerum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its mountainous habitat, including compact growth and frost resistance.
