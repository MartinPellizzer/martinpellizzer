Ranunculus platybasis

Ranunculus platybasis, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petals. The plant has trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Ranunculus platybasis is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
