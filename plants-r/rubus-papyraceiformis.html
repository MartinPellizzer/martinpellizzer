Rubus papyraceiformis

Rubus papyraceiformis, commonly known as the paper-leaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
