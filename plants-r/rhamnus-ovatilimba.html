Rhamnus ovatilimba

Rhamnus ovatilimba, commonly known as the African buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, oval berries that are edible for some animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
