Rhododendron rhodostomum

Rhododendron rhodostomum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar genera like blueberries and heathers.

Rhododendron rhodostomum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
