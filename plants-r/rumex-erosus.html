Rumex erosus

Rumex erosus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by triangular, beak-like seed pods. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Rumex erosus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
