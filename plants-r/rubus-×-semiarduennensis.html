Rubus × semiarduennensis

Rubus × semiarduennensis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry.

It is a species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus, exhibiting traits of both parent species. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically found in temperate regions. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are a mix of blackberry and raspberry flavors.

This hybrid is valued for its fruit quality and adaptability to various growing conditions.
