Rosa mollis f. denticulatior

Rosa mollis f. denticulatior, commonly known as the denticulate mollis rose, is a cultivar of the shrub rose species Rosa mollis.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, with a distinctive denticulate (toothed) margin on the leaves. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular pruning to maintain its compact form.
