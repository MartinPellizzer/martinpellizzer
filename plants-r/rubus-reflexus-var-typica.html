Rubus reflexus var. typica

Rubus reflexus var. typica, commonly known as the common bramble.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
