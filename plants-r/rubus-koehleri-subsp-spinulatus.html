Rubus koehleri subsp. spinulatus

Rubus koehleri subsp.

spinulatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
