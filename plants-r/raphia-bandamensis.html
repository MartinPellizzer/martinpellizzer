Raphia bandamensis

Raphia bandamensis, commonly known as the Bandamensis raffia, is a species of large palm native to the rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material by local communities.

The fruit produces edible seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in the forest ecosystem.
