Romulea bulbocodium var. major

Romulea bulbocodium var. major, commonly known as the giant romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a tall, slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its bulbs are adapted for storage of nutrients. Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
