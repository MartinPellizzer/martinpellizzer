Rubus hylophilus

Rubus hylophilus, commonly known as the hillberry raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which help it to climb or spread across the ground. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, containing numerous tiny drupes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest edges.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing characteristics with other raspberries and blackberries.
