Rubus lucandii

Rubus lucandii, commonly known as the golden raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its erect or scrambling growth habit, with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange berries that are typically edible and have a tangy flavor. Its flowers are white with five petals and are borne in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

Rubus lucandii is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued both for its culinary uses and ecological importance.
