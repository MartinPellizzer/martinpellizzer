Ranunculus gonnetii

Ranunculus gonnetii, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically features simple, trifoliate or pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
