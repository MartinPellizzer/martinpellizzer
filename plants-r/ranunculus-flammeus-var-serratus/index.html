Ranunculus Flammeus Var. Serratus

Ranunculus flammeus var. serratus is a striking member of the buttercup family known for its vibrant, flame-like flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of western North America, this plant thrives in rocky and alpine environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and serrated, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces showy yellow or orange blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Due to its unique beauty and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
