Rubus acutispinis

Rubus acutispinis, commonly known as the sharp-spined blackberry, is a species of brambles native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other Rubus species such as raspberry and blackberry.
