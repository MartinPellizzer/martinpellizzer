Rubus crassus var. undulaefolius

Rubus crassus var. undulaefolius, commonly known as the wavy-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, dark purple stems and large, wavy-edged leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variant is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
