Rubus odoratus var. malachophyllus

Rubus odoratus var. malachophyllus, commonly known as the sweet cherry rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, showy flowers that range from white to pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, though some varieties may have a darker hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
