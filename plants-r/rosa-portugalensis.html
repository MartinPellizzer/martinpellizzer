Rosa portugalensis

Rosa portugalensis, commonly known as the Portuguese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Rosa portugalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
