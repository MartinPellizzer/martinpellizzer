Rosa laevigata var. leiocarpa

Rosa laevigata var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese pink rose, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are typically single or semi-double, with a soft pink coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.
