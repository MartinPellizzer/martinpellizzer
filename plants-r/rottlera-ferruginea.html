Rottlera ferruginea

Rottlera ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty rottlera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Rottleraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves with a reddish or rusty-colored underside. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
