Rubus grisebachii

Rubus grisebachii, commonly known as the gray raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
