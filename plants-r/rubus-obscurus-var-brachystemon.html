Rubus obscurus var. brachystemon

Rubus obscurus var. brachystemon, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a serrated margin.

The berries, which are usually dark purple to black, are edible but may have a tart flavor.
