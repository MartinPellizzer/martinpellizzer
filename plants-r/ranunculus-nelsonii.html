Ranunculus nelsonii

Ranunculus nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it has a short, fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
