Reimaria laxa

Reimaria laxa, commonly known as the slender rein orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in humid and cool environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late summer.

Reimaria laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
