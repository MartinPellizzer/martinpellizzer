Robinia pseudoacacia var. angustifolia

Robinia pseudoacacia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf black locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are narrow and have a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces fragrant, yellow pea-like flowers in spring, followed by long, flat pods that contain dark, flat seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and hardy nature, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
