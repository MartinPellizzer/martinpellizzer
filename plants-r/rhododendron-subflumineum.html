Rhododendron subflumineum

Rhododendron subflumineum, commonly known as the floodplain rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in forest understories. Its botanical features include elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Rhododendron subflumineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
