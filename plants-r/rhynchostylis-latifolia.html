Rhynchostylis latifolia

Rhynchostylis latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Rhynchostylis latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
