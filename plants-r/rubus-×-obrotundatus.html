Rubus × obrotundatus

Rubus × obrotundatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry or Japanese blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and evergreen foliage, which remains lush throughout the year. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex hybrid origin, resulting from crosses between different Rubus species.
