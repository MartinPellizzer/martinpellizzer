Rubus × sericatiformis

Rubus × sericatiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its growth habit includes thorny stems and dense foliage, making it a hardy shrub suitable for various garden settings.
