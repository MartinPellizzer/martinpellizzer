Rubus hirtus microgene pectinatus

Rubus hirtus microgene pectinatus, commonly known as the rough raspberry or prickly pear, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red or yellow fruits. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink or white.

The fruit, while edible, has a tart flavor and is often used in jams and preserves.
