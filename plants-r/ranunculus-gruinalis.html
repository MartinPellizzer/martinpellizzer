Ranunculus gruinalis

Ranunculus gruinalis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow or orange flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant has a short, stout stem and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees.

Ranunculus gruinalis is often found in high-altitude environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
