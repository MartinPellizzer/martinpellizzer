Rheum compactum var. orientale

Rheum compactum var. orientale, commonly known as the Persian rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces tall, reddish stems that bear clusters of small, yellow flowers in the spring. Its rhubarb-like stalks are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in cooking.

The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
