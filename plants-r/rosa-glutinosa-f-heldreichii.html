Rosa glutinosa f. heldreichii

Rosa glutinosa f. heldreichii, commonly known as the Heldreich's sticky rose, is a botanical variant of the sticky rose species.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give its stems and leaves a sticky texture. The plant typically produces small, pale pink to white flowers with five petals. It is native to the mountains of the Balkans and parts of the Eastern Mediterranean.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
