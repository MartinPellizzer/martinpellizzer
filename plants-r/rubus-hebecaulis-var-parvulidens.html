Rubus hebecaulis var. parvulidens

Rubus hebecaulis var. parvulidens, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, biennial flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
