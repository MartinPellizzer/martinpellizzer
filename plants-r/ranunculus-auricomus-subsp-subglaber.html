Ranunculus auricomus subsp. subglaber

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

subglaber, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The subspecies subglaber is distinguished by its reduced leaf hairs compared to the typical form. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

This plant is an important component of high-altitude ecosystems and is adapted to cold, harsh climatic conditions.
