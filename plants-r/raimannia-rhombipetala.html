Raimannia rhombipetala

Raimannia rhombipetala, commonly known as the diamond-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, diamond-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped infloreshcence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
