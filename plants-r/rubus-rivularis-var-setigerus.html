Rubus rivularis var. setigerus

Rubus rivularis var. setigerus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles.
