Racosperma scopulorum

Racosperma scopulorum, commonly known as the rock senna, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Racosperma scopulorum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
