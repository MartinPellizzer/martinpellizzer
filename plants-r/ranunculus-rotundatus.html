Ranunculus rotundatus

Ranunculus rotundatus, commonly known as the globe ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to nine petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural habitats.
