Rosa gallica var. stenotricha

Rosa gallica var. stenotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Gallica rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, characterized by its fragrant, deep pink to crimson flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are often used in perfumery and as ornamental plants.

The variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other Gallica rose cultivars.
