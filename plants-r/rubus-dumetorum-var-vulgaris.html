Rubus dumetorum var. vulgaris

Rubus dumetorum var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its prickly stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and woodland edges, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its edible, sweet-tart berries.

The species is widely cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
