Rubus podophyllos var. vetteri

Rubus podophyllos var. vetteri, commonly known as the Vetter's blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them a valuable food source for wildlife and humans alike.
