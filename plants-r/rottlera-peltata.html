Rottlera peltata

Rottlera peltata, commonly known as the Chinese rose or wild rose, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters. Rottlera peltata produces small, red or black berries that contain the seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
