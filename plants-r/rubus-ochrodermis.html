Rubus ochrodermis

Rubus ochrodermis, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry.

It is a species of bramble within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
