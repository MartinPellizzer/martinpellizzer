Rubus paucidentatus var. guangxiensis

Rubus paucidentatus var. guangxiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi bramble, is a variant of the brambles native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
