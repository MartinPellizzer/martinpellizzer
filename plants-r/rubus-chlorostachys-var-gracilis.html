Rubus chlorostachys var. gracilis

Rubus chlorostachys var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
