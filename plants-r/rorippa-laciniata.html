Rorippa laciniata

Rorippa laciniata, commonly known as narrow-leafed watercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, which are often arranged in clusters. It thrives in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Rorippa laciniata is an annual or biennial herb that is often found in temperate regions across Europe and Asia.
