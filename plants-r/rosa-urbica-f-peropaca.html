Rosa urbica f. peropaca

Rosa urbica f. peropaca, commonly known as the urban rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more delicate flowers compared to its parent species. The plant typically features thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and urban environments, making it a resilient species.

Its flowers, which range from pink to white, bloom in early summer and attract pollinators.
