Rubus rivularis var. discolor

Rubus rivularis var. discolor, commonly known as the variable blackberry.

It is a shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on maturity. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
