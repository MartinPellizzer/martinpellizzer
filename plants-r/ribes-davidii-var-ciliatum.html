Ribes davidii var. ciliatum

Ribes davidii var. ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy goberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and fruit.
