Rubus mutabilis subsp. rubrans

Rubus mutabilis subsp.

rubrans, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to North America. It is a deciduous shrub that produces biennial canes, which flower and fruit in their second year.

The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. After flowering, it develops red, hairy fruits that are typically harvested when they turn from yellow to red.

This species is valued for its fruit, which is used in culinary applications and is also an important food source for wildlife.
