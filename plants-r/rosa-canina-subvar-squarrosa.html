Rosa canina subvar. squarrosa

Rosa canina subvar. squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose dogrose, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The fruit, a red-hued rosehip, is an important source of vitamin C. This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
