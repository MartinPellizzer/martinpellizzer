Rubus thyrsoideus microgene goniophyllus

Rubus thyrsoideus microgene goniophyllus, commonly known as the western thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, whitish hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

The flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.
