Ruellia trachyphylla

Ruellia trachyphylla, commonly known as the roughleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Ruellia trachyphylla is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
