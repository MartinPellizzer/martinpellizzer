Ranunculus hirtellus

Ranunculus hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Ranunculus hirtellus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
