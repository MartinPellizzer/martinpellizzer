Racosperma xanthinum

Racosperma xanthinum, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Indigenous practices and for ornamental purposes.
