Rosa micrantha var. pimpinellifolia

Rosa micrantha var. pimpinellifolia, commonly known as the common rock rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. The plant has woody stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
