Rhododendron calendulaceum var. croceum

Rhododendron calendulaceum var. croceum, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are often glandular-dotted, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
