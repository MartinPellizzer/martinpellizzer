Rytigynia longicaudata

Rytigynia longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed rytigynia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive elongated, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
