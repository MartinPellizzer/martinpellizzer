Rhododendron tashiroi var. tashiroi

Rhododendron tashiroi var. tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.

The species is also noted for its hardiness in temperate climates.
