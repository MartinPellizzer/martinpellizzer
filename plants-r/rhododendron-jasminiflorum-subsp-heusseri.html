Rhododendron jasminiflorum subsp. heusseri

Rhododendron jasminiflorum subsp.

heusseri, commonly known as the Heusseri rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, thriving in cool, moist mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink to white, with a distinctive jasmine-like scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
