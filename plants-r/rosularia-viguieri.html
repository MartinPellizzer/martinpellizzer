Rosularia viguieri

Rosularia viguieri, commonly known as the Viguieri's rosebay, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, typical of many succulents. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Rosularia viguieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
