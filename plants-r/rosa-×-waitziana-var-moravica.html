Rosa × waitziana var. moravica

Rosa × waitziana var. moravica, commonly known as the Moravian rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its semi-evergreen to deciduous growth habit and produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It is native to Central Europe and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
