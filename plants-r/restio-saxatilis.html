Restio saxatilis

Restio saxatilis, commonly known as the rock restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species.

Restio saxatilis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
