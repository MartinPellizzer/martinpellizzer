Renealmia macrocolea

Renealmia macrocolea, commonly known as the giant reed, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both an important ecological component and a potential invasive species in some areas.
