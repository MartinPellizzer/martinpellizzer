Rosa coriifolia f. libussae

Rosa coriifolia f. libussae, commonly known as the Libussa rose, is a rare and ornamental cultivar of the species Rosa coriifolia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It is native to Central Europe and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

This variety is particularly valued for its unique floral form and historical significance in horticultural history.
