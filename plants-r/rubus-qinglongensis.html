Rubus qinglongensis

Rubus qinglongensis, commonly known as the Qinglong blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, serrated leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

The fruit of Rubus qinglongensis is not only edible but also valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
