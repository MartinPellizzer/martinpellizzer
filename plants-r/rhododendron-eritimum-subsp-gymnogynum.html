Rhododendron eritimum subsp. gymnogynum

Rhododendron eritimum subsp.

gymnogynum, commonly known as the Kashmiri rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in mountainous forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers are often adorned with a distinctive white or pale pink throat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
