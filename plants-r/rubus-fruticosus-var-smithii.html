Rubus fruticosus var. smithii

Rubus fruticosus var. smithii, commonly known as Smith's bramble, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
