Rubus hedycarpus var. laeteroseus

Rubus hedycarpus var. laeteroseus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing a leafy stem and the second year bearing flowers and fruit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and edible fruits.
