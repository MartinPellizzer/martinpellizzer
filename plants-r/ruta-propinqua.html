Ruta propinqua

Ruta propinqua, commonly known as common rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, dark green, compound leaves and small yellowish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Rutaceae family and is known for its strong, pungent odor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Ruta propinqua has been used historically in herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
