Rhaphidophora dahlii

Rhaphidophora dahlii, commonly known as the Dull or Shiny ZZ Plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, cane-like stems and broad, dark green, waxy leaves that can be either glossy or matte depending on the variety. The plant is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are rarely seen, and it is often propagated through stem cuttings.

Rhaphidophora dahlii is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to tolerate neglect.
