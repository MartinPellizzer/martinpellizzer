Roystonea ventricosa

Roystonea ventricosa, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tall, slender palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by fruit that contains a high oil content, making it economically significant. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive, elongated stem with a prominent basal swelling.

Roystonea ventricosa is often cultivated for its oil, which is used in various industrial and culinary applications.
