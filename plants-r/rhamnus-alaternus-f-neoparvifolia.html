Rhamnus alaternus f. neoparvifolia

Rhamnus alaternus f. neoparvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in Mediterranean climates.
