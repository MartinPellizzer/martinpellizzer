Roscoea lutea

Roscoea lutea, commonly known as the yellow roscoea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking, orange-yellow inflorescences that are surrounded by broad, lance-shaped bracts. Its leaves are large, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Roscoea lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in moist, well-drained soil.
