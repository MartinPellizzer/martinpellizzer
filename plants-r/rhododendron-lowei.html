Rhododendron lowei

Rhododendron lowei, commonly known as the Low's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Rhododendron lowei is also noted for its ability to form thickets, contributing to the ecological structure of its native habitat.
