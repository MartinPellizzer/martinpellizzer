Rhynchospora culmenicola

Rhynchospora culmenicola, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. Native to wetland and marshy habitats in the southeastern United States, it thrives in saturated soils. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
