Rhododendron lineare

Rhododendron lineare, commonly known as the linear-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.

Rhododendron lineare is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
