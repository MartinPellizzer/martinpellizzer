Rubus pugnax

Rubus pugnax, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The berries are popular in local cuisines and are also used in jams and desserts.
