Rhododendron tashiroi

Rhododendron tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's rhododendron, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring, producing vibrant pink to red blossoms that attract pollinators. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Rhododendron tashiroi thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
