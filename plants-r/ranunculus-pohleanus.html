Ranunculus pohleanus

Ranunculus pohleanus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are deeply lobed.

Ranunculus pohleanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
