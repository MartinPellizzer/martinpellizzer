Rubus questieri microgene pyrenaicus

Rubus questieri microgene pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean raspberry, is a rare species of brambles native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink or white, and the fruit is a red, aggregate berry. It thrives in mountainous areas with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
