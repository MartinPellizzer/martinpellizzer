Racosperma sophorae

Racosperma sophorae, commonly known as the Australian wisteria, is a climbing legume native to eastern Australia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in long, hanging clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

It is often found in open forests and woodlands, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
