Rubus thyrsanthus var. fragrantiformis

Rubus thyrsanthus var. fragrantiformis, commonly known as the fragrant form of the Chilean blackberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are often used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.

The variety is distinguished by its more intense fragrance compared to other Rubus species.
