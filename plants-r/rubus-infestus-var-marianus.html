Rubus infestus var. marianus

Rubus infestus var. marianus, commonly known as the Marian's raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its edible berries and is often found in naturalized habitats across its range.
