Rosa montana subsp. chavinii

Rosa montana subsp.

chavinii, commonly known as the Chavin's mountain rose, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers.

The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

The subspecies is noted for its resilience to cold and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
