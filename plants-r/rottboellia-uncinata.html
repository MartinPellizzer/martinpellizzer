Rottboellia uncinata

Rottboellia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.

Rottboellia uncinata is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
