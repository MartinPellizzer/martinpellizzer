Rubus × semicuspidifer

Rubus × semicuspidifer, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis, resulting in a shrub with dense, thorny stems and evergreen foliage. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries that are edible. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.
