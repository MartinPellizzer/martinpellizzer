Rosa pisifera

Rosa pisifera, commonly known as the sweet william rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces fragrant, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa pisifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
