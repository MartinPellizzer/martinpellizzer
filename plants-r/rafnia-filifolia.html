Rafnia filifolia

Rafnia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. Rafnia filifolia typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.
