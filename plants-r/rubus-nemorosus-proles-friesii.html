Rubus nemorosus proles friesii

Rubus nemorosus proles friesii, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
