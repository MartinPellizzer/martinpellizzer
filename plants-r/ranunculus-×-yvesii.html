Ranunculus × yvesii

Ranunculus × yvesii, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically with 5 to 15 petals. The plant has a short, hairy stem and basal leaves that are palmately divided. Its flowers open in late spring to early summer and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
