Rhodohypoxis baurii var. confecta

Rhodohypoxis baurii var. confecta, commonly known as the golden star lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces tall, slender stems topped with a single, showy flower that has a white perianth with a golden-yellow center. The flower is surrounded by a cluster of bright yellow stamens, giving it its characteristic "golden star" appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
