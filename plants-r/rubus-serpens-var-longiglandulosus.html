Rubus serpens var. longiglandulosus

Rubus serpens var. longiglandulosus, commonly known as the long-hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its hairy foliage and stems are distinctive features that help in its identification.
