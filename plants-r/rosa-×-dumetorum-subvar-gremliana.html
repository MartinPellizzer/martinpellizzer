Rosa × dumetorum subvar. gremliana

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. gremliana, commonly known as the Alpine shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, demonstrating strong adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant produces hips that are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
