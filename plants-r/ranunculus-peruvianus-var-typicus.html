Ranunculus peruvianus var. typicus

Ranunculus peruvianus var. typicus, commonly known as the Peruvian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has palmately divided leaves and produces a distinctive, hairy stem.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
