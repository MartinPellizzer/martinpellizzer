Rubus × coarctatiflorus subsp. goniophyllus

Rubus × coarctatiflorus subsp.

goniophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its hybrid origin and its role in local ecosystems as both a food source and a habitat for various wildlife species.
