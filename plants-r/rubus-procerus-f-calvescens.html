Rubus procerus f. calvescens

Rubus procerus f. calvescens, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and smaller, more delicate leaves compared to the typical Rubus procerus. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry. It is native to regions of South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.
