Ranunculus plebeius

Ranunculus plebeius, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant grows from a rhizome and has palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Ranunculus plebeius is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
