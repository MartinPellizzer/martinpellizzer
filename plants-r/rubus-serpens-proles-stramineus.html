Rubus serpens proles stramineus

Rubus serpens proles stramineus, commonly known as the yellow raspberry, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is a member of the Rosaceae family, sharing characteristics with other brambles and raspberries.
