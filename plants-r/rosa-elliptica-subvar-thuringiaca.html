Rosa elliptica subvar. thuringiaca

Rosa elliptica subvar. thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian elliptic rose, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, red-orange hips in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticultural traditions.
