Ranunculus recurvatus var. nelsonii

Ranunculus recurvatus var. nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are hairy and slightly serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like flowers.
