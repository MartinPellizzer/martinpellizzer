Rubus harpaginetulus

Rubus harpaginetulus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its thorny stems and dense foliage make it a hardy, resilient shrub.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its aggregate fruits and compound leaves.
