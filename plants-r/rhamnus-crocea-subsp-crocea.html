Rhamnus crocea subsp. crocea

Rhamnus crocea subsp.

crocea, commonly known as the yellow buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by black, egg-shaped berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native habitat.
