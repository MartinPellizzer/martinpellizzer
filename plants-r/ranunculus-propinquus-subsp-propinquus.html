Ranunculus propinquus subsp. propinquus

Ranunculus propinquus subsp.

propinquus, commonly known as the marsh buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming from May to August.

The plant has alternate, trifoliate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish and hairy. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

The species is considered an indicator of wetland health due to its specific habitat requirements.
