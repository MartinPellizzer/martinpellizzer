Rubus moorei f. glabra

Rubus moorei f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless fruit surface, distinguishing it from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in plant taxonomy and horticulture.
