Rosa viscaria subsp. rubiginosa

Rosa viscaria subsp.

rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed dog rose, is a shrub-like species native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are often reddish in color, and its pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in early summer, followed by red or orange hips that persist into autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rosa genus, valued for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
