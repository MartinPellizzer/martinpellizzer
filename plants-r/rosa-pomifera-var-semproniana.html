Rosa pomifera var. semproniana

Rosa pomifera var. semproniana, commonly known as the sweet william rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Despite its beauty, it is not commonly used for fruit production compared to other Rosa species.
