Rudbeckia serotina f. pulcherrima

Rudbeckia serotina f. pulcherrima, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a variant of the late goldenrod species, characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. It features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Rudbeckia serotina f. pulcherrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators in late summer and early autumn.
