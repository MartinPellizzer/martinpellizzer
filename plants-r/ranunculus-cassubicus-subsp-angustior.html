Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. angustior

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular stem.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of R. cassubicus. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
