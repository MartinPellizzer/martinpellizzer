Rubus occidentalis var. mexicanus

Rubus occidentalis var. mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible berries and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
