Romulea cedarbergensis

Romulea cedarbergensis, commonly known as the Cedarberg bulb, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces large, rounded bulbs that store nutrients, allowing it to survive dry periods. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Romulea cedarbergensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
