Rochefortia bahamensis

Rochefortia bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian rock fig, is a flowering plant native to the Bahamas.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically found in the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or limestone habitats.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local insect and bird populations.
