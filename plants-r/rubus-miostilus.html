Rubus miostilus

Rubus miostilus, commonly known as the Miostilus blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
