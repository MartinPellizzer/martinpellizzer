Rubus melanoxylon var. faucium

Rubus melanoxylon var. faucium, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and clustered in cymes.

The fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and commonly used in jams and desserts.
