Rubus palmatus var. kisoensis

Rubus palmatus var. kisoensis, commonly known as the Kiso raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupes that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in local culinary traditions.
