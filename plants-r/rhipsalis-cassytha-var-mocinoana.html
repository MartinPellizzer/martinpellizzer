Rhipsalis cassytha var. mocinoana

Rhipsalis cassytha var. mocinoana, commonly known as the Mocino's mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its soft, leaf-like stems that are often mistaken for foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
