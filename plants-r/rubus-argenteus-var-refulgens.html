Rubus argenteus var. refulgens

Rubus argenteus var. refulgens, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the silverleaf raspberry native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves and its small, dark red berries. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include compound leaves and flowers that are typically pink to white in color.

It is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
