Rubus × permollis

Rubus × permollis, commonly known as the evergreen bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color. It is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions for ornamental purposes.

Due to its hardy nature, it can thrive in a variety of soil types and climate conditions.
