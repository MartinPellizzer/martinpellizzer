Racosperma pycnanthum

Racosperma pycnanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters.

Racosperma pycnanthum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
