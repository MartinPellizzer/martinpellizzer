Roupala spicata

Roupala spicata, commonly known as the silver tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, papery bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, spiky fruits that are consumed by various wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
