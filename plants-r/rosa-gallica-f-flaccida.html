Rosa gallica f. flaccida

Rosa gallica f. flaccida, commonly known as the French pink or the pink shrub rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa gallica species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, red-orange hips in the fall. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a serrated margin.

This variety is well-suited for garden cultivation and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
