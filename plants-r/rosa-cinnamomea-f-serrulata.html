Rosa cinnamomea f. serrulata

Rosa cinnamomea f. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated cinnamon rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa cinnamomea species.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and vibrant, deep red to purple flowers. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and produces fragrant blooms in late spring.

The foliage is glossy and dark green, adding to its visual appeal throughout the growing season.
