Rafnia rostrata subsp. rostrata

Rafnia rostrata subsp.

rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rafnia, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
