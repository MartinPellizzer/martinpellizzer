Rubus × geniculatofragrans

Rubus × geniculatofragrans, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
