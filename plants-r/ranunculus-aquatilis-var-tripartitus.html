Ranunculus aquatilis var. tripartitus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. tripartitus, commonly known as the three-part water buttercup, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often near ponds, streams, or wet meadows. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive pattern of brown or black markings. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
