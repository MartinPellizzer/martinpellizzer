Rubus hirtus var. setulifer

Rubus hirtus var. setulifer, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
