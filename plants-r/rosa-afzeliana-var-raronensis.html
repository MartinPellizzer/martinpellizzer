Rosa afzeliana var. raronensis

Rosa afzeliana var. raronensis, commonly known as the Raron rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and is typically found in rocky or well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

The species is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to a range of environmental conditions.
