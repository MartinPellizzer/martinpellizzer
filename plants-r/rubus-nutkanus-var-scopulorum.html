Rubus nutkanus var. scopulorum

Rubus nutkanus var. scopulorum, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white or pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a tart, flavorful profile.
