Rosa eglanteria var. subrubra

Rosa eglanteria var. subrubra, commonly known as the red-leaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that turn a striking red color in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in early summer. Its botanical features include stipulate leaflets and a woody, perennial structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
