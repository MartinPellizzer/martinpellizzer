Rosa vitaminifera

Rosa vitaminifera, commonly known as the vitamin-rich rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. The plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces fragrant blooms throughout the growing season.

The name "vitaminifera" reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported nutritional and therapeutic benefits.
