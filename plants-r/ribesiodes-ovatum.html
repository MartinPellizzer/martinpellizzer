Ribesiodes ovatum

Ribesiodes ovatum, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various birds and small mammals.

Ribesiodes ovatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
