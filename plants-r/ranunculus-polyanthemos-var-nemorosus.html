Ranunculus polyanthemos var. nemorosus

Ranunculus polyanthemos var. nemorosus, commonly known as the woodland buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to woodland areas in Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with numerous small petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a soft, downy layer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a common sight in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restorations.
