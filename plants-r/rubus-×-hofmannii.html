Rubus × hofmannii

Rubus × hofmannii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. This species is native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California, where it thrives in sandy soils and coastal climates. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a tart flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
