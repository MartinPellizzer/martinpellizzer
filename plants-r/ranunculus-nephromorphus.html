Ranunculus nephromorphus

Ranunculus nephromorphus, commonly known as the kidney-shaped buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a distinctive kidney-shaped outline, and its flowers are typically yellow with numerous stamens. The plant grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
