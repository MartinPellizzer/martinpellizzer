Rehmannia japonica

Rehmannia japonica, commonly known as Japanese rehmannia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rehmannia japonica is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
