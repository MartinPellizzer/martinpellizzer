Ruellia stricklandii

Ruellia stricklandii, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia stricklandii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
