Rhynchostylis gigantea f. harrisoniana

Rhynchostylis gigantea f. harrisoniana, commonly known as the giant rhynchostylis, is a striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, loose soil to support its extensive root system. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This orchid is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display and is valued in both horticultural and conservation contexts.
