Rubus russeicaulis

Rubus russeicaulis, commonly known as the Canadian blackberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
