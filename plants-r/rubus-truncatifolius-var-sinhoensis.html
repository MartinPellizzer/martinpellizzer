Rubus truncatifolius var. sinhoensis

Rubus truncatifolius var. sinhoensis, commonly known as the Sinho Chinese raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
