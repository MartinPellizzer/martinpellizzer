Rubus arduennensis var. tenuipetalus

Rubus arduennensis var. tenuipetalus, commonly known as the slender-petaled blackberry, is a rare shrub native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
