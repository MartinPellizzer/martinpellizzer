Rubus parviflorus f. villosus

Rubus parviflorus f. villosus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are typically small, tart berries that ripen in late summer.

This form is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils across temperate regions.
