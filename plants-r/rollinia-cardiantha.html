Rollinia cardiantha

Rollinia cardiantha, commonly known as the golden star soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a smooth, spiky fruit that is typically green when mature, turning yellow or orange when ripe. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and slightly acidic flavor, and is often used in local cuisines.

Rollinia cardiantha is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
