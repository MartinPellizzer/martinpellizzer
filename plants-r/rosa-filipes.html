Rosa filipes

Rosa filipes, commonly known as the creeping rose or evergreen rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rosa filipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
