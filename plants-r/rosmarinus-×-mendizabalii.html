Rosmarinus × mendizabalii

Rosmarinus × mendizabalii, commonly known as the Mendizabal rosemary, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves, which are needle-like and covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in culinary and medicinal applications.
