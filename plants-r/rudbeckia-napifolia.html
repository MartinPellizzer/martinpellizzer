Rudbeckia napifolia

Rudbeckia napifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coneflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a brown, cone-like center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Rudbeckia napifolia is a hardy perennial that is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
