Rhododendron annae subsp. annae

Rhododendron annae subsp.

annae, commonly known as the Annae rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
