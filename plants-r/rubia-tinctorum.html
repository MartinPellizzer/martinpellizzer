Rubia tinctorum

Rubia tinctorum, commonly known as common madder, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are the primary source of a red dye historically used for coloring textiles and wines.

Rubia tinctorum is also known for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating ailments such as hemorrhoids and skin conditions.
