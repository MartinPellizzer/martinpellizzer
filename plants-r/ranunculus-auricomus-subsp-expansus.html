Ranunculus auricomus subsp. expansus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

expansus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals that are often adorned with a distinctive greenish central vein.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap and hairy stems.

The subspecies expansus is distinguished by its larger, more expanded petals compared to the typical form of R. auricomus.
