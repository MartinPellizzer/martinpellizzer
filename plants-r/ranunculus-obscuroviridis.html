Ranunculus obscuroviridis

Ranunculus obscuroviridis, commonly known as the dark green buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, slightly hairy leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by five sepals that are usually green or reddish. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
