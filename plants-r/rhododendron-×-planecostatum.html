Rhododendron × planecostatum

Rhododendron × planecostatum, commonly known as the European rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron ferrugineum and Rhododendron ponticum.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in temperate climates, often found in mountainous regions of Europe. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves with a hairy underside and inflorescences that are usually clustered in racemes.

Rhododendron × planecostatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
