Rhaphiolepis bengalensis

Rhaphiolepis bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent, characterized by its dense, thorny branches and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid regions.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a hedge plant.
