Restio applanatus

Restio applanatus, commonly known as the flat-stemmed restio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often pollinated by wind.
