Ranunculus trigonus var. strigosus

Ranunculus trigonus var. strigosus, commonly known as the hairy thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thimble-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a trifoliate structure, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The foliage and flowers are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.
