Rhododendron crassifolium var. crassifolium

Rhododendron crassifolium var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are broad and thick, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in the spring, which are typical of the Rhododendron genus. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
