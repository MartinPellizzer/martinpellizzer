Rubus illecebrosus f. tokin-ibara

Rubus illecebrosus f. tokin-ibara, commonly known as the Tokin-ibara raspberry, is a botanical variant of the raspberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are popular in local cuisines.
