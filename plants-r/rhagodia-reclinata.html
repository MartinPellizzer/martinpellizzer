Rhagodia reclinata

Rhagodia reclinata, commonly known as the slender prickly pear, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Rhagodia reclinata is often found in coastal and sandy regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
