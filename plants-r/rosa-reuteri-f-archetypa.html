Rosa reuteri f. archetypa

Rosa reuteri f. archetypa, commonly known as the archetypal form of the Reuter's rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are finely toothed and arranged alternately along the stems. It is a deciduous species, losing its leaves in the autumn, and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

Rosa reuteri f. archetypa is relatively low maintenance, thriving in well-drained soil and full to partial sun.
