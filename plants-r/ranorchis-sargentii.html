Ranorchis sargentii

Ranorchis sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. The plant features a slender rhizome with erect, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often clustered in a spike, and are pollinated by insects.

Ranorchis sargentii is characterized by its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to its wetland habitat.
