Rosa abietina f. orophila

Rosa abietina f. orophila, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a botanical variant of the mountain rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which helps it thrive in high-altitude environments. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming in late summer. It is adapted to cold climates and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species found in alpine regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
