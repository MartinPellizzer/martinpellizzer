Rosa canina var. subversuta

Rosa canina var. subversuta, commonly known as the bent-thorn dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose characterized by its distinctive, curved thorns.

This plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in open woodlands and scrubland, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a fragrant scent, blooming in spring. The fruit, known as a hip, is red and contains several seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
