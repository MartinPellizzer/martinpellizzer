Rosa quasi-oehrnii

Rosa quasi-oehrnii, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces red, hairy fruits known as hips. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Rosa quasi-oehrnii thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
