Rubus echinaceus

Rubus echinaceus, commonly known as the echinaceus raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
