Rubia himalayensis

Rubia himalayensis, commonly known as Himalayan bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered with a fine layer of hair.

Rubia himalayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
