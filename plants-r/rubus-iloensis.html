Rubus iloensis

Rubus iloensis, commonly known as the Iloan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
