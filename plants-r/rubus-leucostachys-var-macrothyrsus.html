Rubus leucostachys var. macrothyrsus

Rubus leucostachys var. macrothyrsus, commonly known as the large-thryx raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by small, edible berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the stems are covered in dense, prickly bracts.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
