Rubus lasiocarpus var. furfuraceus

Rubus lasiocarpus var. furfuraceus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its hairy texture and unique growth habits make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
