Rudbeckia fulgida var. speciosa

Rudbeckia fulgida var. speciosa, commonly known as the Gay Buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, cone-shaped yellow centers surrounded by broad, showy orange to red petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers.

Rudbeckia fulgida var. speciosa is well-suited for gardens and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
