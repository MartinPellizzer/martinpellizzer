Rubus aduncispinus

Rubus aduncispinus, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
