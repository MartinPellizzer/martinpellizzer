Rubus quincyi

Rubus quincyi, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically narrow and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed areas, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
