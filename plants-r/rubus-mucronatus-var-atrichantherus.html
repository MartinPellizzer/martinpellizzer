Rubus mucronatus var. atrichantherus

Rubus mucronatus var. atrichantherus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals. This variety is distinguished by its dark, almost black, fruit that ripens in late summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
