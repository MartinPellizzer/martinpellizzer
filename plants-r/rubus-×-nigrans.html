Rubus × nigrans

Rubus × nigrans, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates and moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

Rubus × nigrans is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
