Rubus stereacanthos

Rubus stereacanthos, commonly known as the bracted blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
