Rosa × collina var. vineticola

Rosa × collina var. vineticola, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a hybrid species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, fragrant pink or white flowers, and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and rose-like flowers that bloom in spring.
