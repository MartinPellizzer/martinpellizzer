Rubus briareus subsp. herzogii

Rubus briareus subsp.

herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
