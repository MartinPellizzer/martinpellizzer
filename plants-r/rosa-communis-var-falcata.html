Rosa communis var. falcata

Rosa communis var. falcata, commonly known as the sickle rose, is a cultivar of the rose plant characterized by its distinctive, curved petals.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to various regions in Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
