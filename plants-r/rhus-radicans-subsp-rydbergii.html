Rhus radicans subsp. rydbergii

Rhus radicans subsp.

rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's winged sumac, is a shrub native to the central and western United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into narrow, wing-like segments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
