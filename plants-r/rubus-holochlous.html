Rubus holochlous

Rubus holochlous, commonly known as the golden raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
