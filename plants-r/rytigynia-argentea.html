Rytigynia argentea

Rytigynia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf rytigynia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

Rytigynia argentea produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
