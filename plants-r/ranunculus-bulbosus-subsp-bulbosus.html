Ranunculus bulbosus subsp. bulbosus

Ranunculus bulbosus subsp.

bulbosus, commonly known as the bulbous buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant is perennial and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows and wetlands. It has a short, stout rhizome that helps it survive harsh conditions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
