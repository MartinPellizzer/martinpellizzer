Rubus obscurus subsp. erraticus

Rubus obscurus subsp.

erraticus, commonly known as the erratic raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark red berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to disturbed soils and its role in supporting local wildlife.
