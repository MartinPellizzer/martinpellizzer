Rosa × andegavensis f. subsystylis

Rosa × andegavensis f. subsystylis, commonly known as the subsystylis form of the Andegavensis rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse blooming of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The subsystylis form exhibits distinct floral morphology, with stamens that are shorter than the petals, a trait that sets it apart from other forms of Rosa × andegavensis. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth and flowering.
