Rubus najas

Rubus najas, commonly known as the water raspberry, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible fruits that resemble raspberries. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Rubus najas plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
