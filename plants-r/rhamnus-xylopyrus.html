Rhamnus xylopyrus

Rhamnus xylopyrus, commonly known as the Mexican buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, dark berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
