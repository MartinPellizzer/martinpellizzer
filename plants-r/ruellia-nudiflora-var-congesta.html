Ruellia nudiflora var. congesta

Ruellia nudiflora var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
