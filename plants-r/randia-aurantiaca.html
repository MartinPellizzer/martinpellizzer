Randia aurantiaca

Randia aurantiaca, commonly known as the red randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its habitat. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Randia aurantiica is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along stream banks.
