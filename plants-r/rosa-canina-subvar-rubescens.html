Rosa canina subvar. rubescens

Rosa canina subvar. rubescens, commonly known as the red-berried dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange hips, which contrast with its pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, and it is well adapted to temperate climates. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
