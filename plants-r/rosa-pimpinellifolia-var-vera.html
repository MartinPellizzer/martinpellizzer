Rosa pimpinellifolia var. vera

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. vera, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its historical use in hedgerows and gardens dates back to ancient times.
