Rubus sulcatus var. pseudoplicatus

Rubus sulcatus var. pseudoplicatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
