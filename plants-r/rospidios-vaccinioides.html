Rospidios vaccinioides

Rospidios vaccinioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cranberry shrub, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, red, berry-like fruits that resemble cranberries. The plant has lanceolate leaves with a smooth surface and a reddish-brown underside. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Rospidios vaccinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
