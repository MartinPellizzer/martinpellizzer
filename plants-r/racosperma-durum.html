Racosperma durum

Racosperma durum, commonly known as the hardy wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
