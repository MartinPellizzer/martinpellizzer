Rosa canina var. krameri

Rosa canina var. krameri, commonly known as the Krameri rose, is a variant of the dog rose that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. The flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in spring. Its fruit, a red, hairy hip, contains seeds and is used in traditional medicine.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
