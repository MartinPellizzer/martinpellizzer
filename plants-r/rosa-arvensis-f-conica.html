Rosa arvensis f. conica

Rosa arvensis f. conica, commonly known as the conical dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive conical shape and compact growth habit, making it a unique ornamental plant. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This form is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
