Rubus × hypoleucanthemus

Rubus × hypoleucanthemus, commonly known as the white-leafed blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus idaeus.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger than those of its parent species. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable addition to both ornamental and agricultural landscapes.
