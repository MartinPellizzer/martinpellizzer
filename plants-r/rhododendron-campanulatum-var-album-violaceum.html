Rhododendron campanulatum var. album-violaceum

Rhododendron campanulatum var. album-violaceum, commonly known as the white and purple bell rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a striking bicolor pattern. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soil and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
