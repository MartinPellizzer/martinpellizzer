Roridula verticillata

Roridula verticillata, commonly known as the sticky bladderplant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often yellow or white in color. Its most distinctive feature is the specialized bladder-like structures on its leaves, which aid in water collection.

Roridula verticillata is adapted to survive in extreme conditions, making it a fascinating example of plant resilience.
