Rhododendron dentampullum

Rhododendron dentampullum, commonly known as the serrated-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with serrated margins and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring acidic soils and high humidity. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
