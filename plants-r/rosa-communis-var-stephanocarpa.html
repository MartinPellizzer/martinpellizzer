Rosa communis var. stephanocarpa

Rosa communis var. stephanocarpa, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, fragrant pink or white flowers, and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
