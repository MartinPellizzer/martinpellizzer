Rhododendron himantodes

Rhododendron himantodes, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers in the spring. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many other ornamental and ecologically important plants.

Rhododendron himantodes is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to cool, moist climates.
