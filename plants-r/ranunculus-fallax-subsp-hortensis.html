Ranunculus fallax subsp. hortensis

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

hortensis, commonly known as the garden buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features erect, branched stems that bear palmately divided, bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens.

The leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow.
