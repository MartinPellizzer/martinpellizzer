Rubus micans subsp. brevimasculus

Rubus micans subsp.

brevimasculus, commonly known as the small-fruited bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. The fruit is a small, aggregate berry that ripens to a dark red or black color.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.
