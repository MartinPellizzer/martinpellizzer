Rubus schleicheri var. disjunctus

Rubus schleicheri var. disjunctus, commonly known as the Schleicher's blackberry variant, is a shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
