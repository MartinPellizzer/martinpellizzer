Ribes rubrum var. sylvestre

Ribes rubrum var. sylvestre, commonly known as the wild redcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright red berries. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are yellow and appear in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental and edible species.

Its berries are used in jams, jellies, and wines, and the plant is also valued for its ecological benefits.
