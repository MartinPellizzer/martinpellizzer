Rechsteineria allagophylla

Rechsteineria allagophylla, commonly known as the dwarf rechsteineria, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
