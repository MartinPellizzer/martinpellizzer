Rubus hirtus var. rhodopensis

Rubus hirtus var. rhodopensis, commonly known as the Bulgarian raspberry, is a shrub native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in local traditional medicine.
