Rhus welwitschii var. angustifoliola

Rhus welwitschii var. angustifoliola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved inkberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
