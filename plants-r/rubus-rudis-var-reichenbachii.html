Rubus rudis var. reichenbachii

Rubus rudis var. reichenbachii, commonly known as the Reichenbach's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is a shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries, which are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
