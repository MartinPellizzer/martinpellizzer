Rhaponticum australe

Rhaponticum australe, commonly known as the Australian rhapontic, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a robust, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and daisy-like, arranged in a composite head, attracting various pollinators.

Rhaponticum australe is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
