Rhododendron ovatosepalum

Rhododendron ovatosepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate sepals and dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
