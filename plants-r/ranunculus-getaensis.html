Ranunculus getaensis

Ranunculus getaensis, commonly known as the Getaean buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Carpathian region.

It features bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for specific ecological conditions.
