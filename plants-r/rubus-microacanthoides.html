Rubus microacanthoides

Rubus microacanthoides, commonly known as the small-spined blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible berries. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple berry.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
