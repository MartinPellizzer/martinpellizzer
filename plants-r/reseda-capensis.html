Reseda capensis

Reseda capensis, commonly known as the Cape rue or yellow weed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the mustard family (Brassicaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

Reseda capensis is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
