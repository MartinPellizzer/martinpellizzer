Rubus silvae-norticae

Rubus silvae-norticae, commonly known as the Northern Forest Bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils, often forming dense thickets.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
