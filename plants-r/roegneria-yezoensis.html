Roegneria yezoensis

Roegneria yezoensis, commonly known as the Yezo wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Roegneria yezoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
