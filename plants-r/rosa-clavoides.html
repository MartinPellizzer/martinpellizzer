Rosa clavoides

Rosa clavoides, commonly known as the Clavie rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained, slightly acidic soils. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa clavoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
