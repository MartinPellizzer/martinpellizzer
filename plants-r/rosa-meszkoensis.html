Rosa meszkoensis

Rosa meszkoensis, commonly known as the Meszko's rose, is a species native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with 5 to 9 leaflets that are oval-shaped and serrated.

Rosa meszkoensis is characterized by its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
