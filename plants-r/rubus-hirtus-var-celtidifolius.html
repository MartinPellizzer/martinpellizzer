Rubus hirtus var. celtidifolius

Rubus hirtus var. celtidifolius, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
