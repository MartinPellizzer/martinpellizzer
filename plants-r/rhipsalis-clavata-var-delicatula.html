Rhipsalis clavata var. delicatula

Rhipsalis clavata var. delicatula, commonly known as the delicate mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in short, soft spines. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that contains seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
