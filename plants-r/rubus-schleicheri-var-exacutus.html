Rubus schleicheri var. exacutus

Rubus schleicheri var. exacutus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
