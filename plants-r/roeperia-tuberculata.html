Roeperia tuberculata

Roeperia tuberculata, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor environments.

Roeperia tuberculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
