Rubus constrictus f. robustus

Rubus constrictus f. robustus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a thorny shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten raw or used in jams and pies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
