Rosa communis var. mollardiana

Rosa communis var. mollardiana, commonly known as the Mollard's rose, is a variety of the common rose that is characterized by its semi-climbing growth habit and fragrant, pink flowers.

It originates from the Mediterranean region and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, showy flowers.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
