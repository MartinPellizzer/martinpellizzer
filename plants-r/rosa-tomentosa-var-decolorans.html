Rosa tomentosa var. decolorans

Rosa tomentosa var. decolorans, commonly known as the white-veined rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and pinnately compound leaves with white veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its botanical features include a woody structure and the ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.

This variety is often valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
