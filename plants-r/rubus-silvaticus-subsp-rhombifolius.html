Rubus silvaticus subsp. rhombifolius

Rubus silvaticus subsp.

rhombifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a food source for various wildlife species.
