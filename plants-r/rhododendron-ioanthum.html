Rhododendron ioanthum

Rhododendron ioanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms.

Rhododendron ioanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
