Rosa glauca var. placida

Rosa glauca var. placida, commonly known as the pale blue rose, is a variant of the glaucous rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its pale blue to lavender-colored flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically gray-green, covered in a fine, powdery bloom that gives the plant its common name. It is a shrub with thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.
