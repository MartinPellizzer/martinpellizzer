Rhodoscirpus

Rhodoscirpus, commonly known as the red-stemmed nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Rhodoscirpus thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It contains irritant hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.
