Rubus radula gracilis

Rubus radula gracilis, commonly known as the slender dewberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. Its berries are typically small and edible, though they are less commonly cultivated than other Rubus species.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
