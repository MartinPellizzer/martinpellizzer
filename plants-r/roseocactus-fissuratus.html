Roseocactus fissuratus

Roseocactus fissuratus, commonly known as the fissured roseocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sparse, pale green or blue-gray spines. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
