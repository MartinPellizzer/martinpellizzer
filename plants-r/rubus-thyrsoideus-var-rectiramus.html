Rubus thyrsoideus var. rectiramus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. rectiramus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces flowers that are white to pale pink, often clustered in groups of three.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
