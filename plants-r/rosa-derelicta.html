Rosa derelicta

Rosa derelicta, commonly known as the desert rose or the Mexican wild rose, is a shrub-like plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Rosa derelicta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
