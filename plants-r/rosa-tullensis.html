Rosa tullensis

Rosa tullensis, commonly known as the Tullius rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Rosa tullensis is a deciduous shrub that produces rose hips, which are orange-red, fuzzy fruits containing seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in American botany.
