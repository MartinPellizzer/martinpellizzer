Rubus hasskarlii f. pumila

Rubus hasskarlii f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and edible fruits.
