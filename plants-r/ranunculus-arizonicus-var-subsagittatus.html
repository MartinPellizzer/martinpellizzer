Ranunculus arizonicus var. subsagittatus

Ranunculus arizonicus var. subsagittatus, commonly known as the Arizona buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. The leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive arrowhead shape, giving it its subspecies name. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert environments.

It is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
