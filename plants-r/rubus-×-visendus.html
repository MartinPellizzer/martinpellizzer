Rubus × visendus

Rubus × visendus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The fruit is highly valued for its flavor and is commonly used in culinary applications.
