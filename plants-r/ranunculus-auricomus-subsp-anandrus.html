Ranunculus auricomus subsp. anandrus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

anandrus, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals and a distinctive golden center.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
