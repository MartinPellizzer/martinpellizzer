Rubus × pseudorigidulus

Rubus × pseudorigidulus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters during the spring.

The species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
