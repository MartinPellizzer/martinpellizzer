Rubus × scandens

Rubus × scandens, commonly known as the common blackberry vine, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a popular plant for cultivation.
