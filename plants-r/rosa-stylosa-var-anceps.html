Rosa stylosa var. anceps

Rosa stylosa var. anceps, commonly known as the slender-stemmed rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features slender, arching stems with small, glossy, dark green leaves that are pinnately compound. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers in late winter to early spring. Its botanical characteristics include simple, elliptical leaflets and thornless stems, making it a unique addition to native gardens.

It is well adapted to sandy or loamy soils and thrives in full sun.
