Rubus vallisparsus var. integerrimus

Rubus vallisparsus var. integerrimus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry.

It is a species of bramble characterized by its trailing growth habit and thornless stems. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and highly aromatic. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins and a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
