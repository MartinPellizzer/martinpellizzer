Rosa hetrostyla

Rosa hetrostyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
