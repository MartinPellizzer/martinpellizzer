Rosa elongatula

Rosa elongatula, commonly known as the slender rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically compound with five to seven leaflets. Rosa elongatula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
