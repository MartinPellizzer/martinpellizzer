Rubus bozae

Rubus bozae, commonly known as the Bozae bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically red or black when mature and are edible.

Rubus bozae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
