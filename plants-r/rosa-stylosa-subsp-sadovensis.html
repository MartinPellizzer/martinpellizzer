Rosa stylosa subsp. sadovensis

Rosa stylosa subsp.

sadovensis, commonly known as the Sadovenskaya rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It features thorned stems and compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets.

The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in mountainous or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
