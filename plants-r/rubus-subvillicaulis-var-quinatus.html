Rubus subvillicaulis var. quinatus

Rubus subvillicaulis var. quinatus, commonly known as the quinatus blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in woodland and riparian habitats.
