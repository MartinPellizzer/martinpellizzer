Ranunculus paishanensis var. oreodoxa

Ranunculus paishanensis var. oreodoxa, commonly known as the Shimen Ranunculus, is a rare and unique variant of the ranunculus genus native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink, with finely divided leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with rich, loamy soil. Its botanical features include a short, hairy stem and palmate leaves that are deeply lobed.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
