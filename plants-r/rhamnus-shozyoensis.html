Rhamnus shozyoensis

Rhamnus shozyoensis, commonly known as the Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
