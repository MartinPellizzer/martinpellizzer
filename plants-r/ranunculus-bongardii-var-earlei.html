Ranunculus bongardii var. earlei

Ranunculus bongardii var. earlei, commonly known as the Bongard's buttercup, is a rare and striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Pamir and Tien Shan ranges. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous stamens and a showy, hairy center. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive serrated edge.

This variant is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and limited distribution.
