Rubus strictiflorus

Rubus strictiflorus, commonly known as the stiff-flowered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the summer. Its fruit is edible but often has a tart flavor, and it is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and apples.
