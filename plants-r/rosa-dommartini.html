Rosa dommartini

Rosa dommartini, commonly known as the Dommartin rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa dommartini is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery.
