Rhigospira venulosa

Rhigospira venulosa, commonly known as the slender vein vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Rhigospira venulosa is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
