Rajania cordata var. ehrenbergii

Rajania cordata var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Ehrenberg's heartleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-bearing inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and vibrant flowers.

Botanically, it exhibits features typical of the Araceae family, including a modified stem called a peduncle and a spathe that encloses the spadix.
