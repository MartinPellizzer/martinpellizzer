Raphanus brassica-officinalis

Raphanus brassica-officinalis, commonly known as wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by rounded, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is native to Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides.

The plant has a taproot that can be harvested for its edible, radish-like flavor.
