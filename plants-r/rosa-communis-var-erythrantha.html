Rosa communis var. erythrantha

Rosa communis var. erythrantha, commonly known as the crimson rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its vibrant red flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are usually fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in perfumery.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.
