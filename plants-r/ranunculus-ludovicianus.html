Ranunculus ludovicianus

Ranunculus ludovicianus, commonly known as the Louisiana buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
