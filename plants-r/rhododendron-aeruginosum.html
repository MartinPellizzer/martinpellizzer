Rhododendron aeruginosum

Rhododendron aeruginosum, commonly known as the blueleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that often have a bluish tinge underneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
