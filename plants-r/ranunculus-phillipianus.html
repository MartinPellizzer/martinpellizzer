Ranunculus phillipianus

Ranunculus phillipianus, commonly known as the Philippine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinct central pistil. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
