Rubus lasioclados

Rubus lasioclados, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries in the summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

The plant thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas along stream banks.
