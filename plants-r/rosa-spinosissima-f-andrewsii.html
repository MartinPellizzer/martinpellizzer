Rosa spinosissima f. andrewsii

Rosa spinosissima f. andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrew's prickly rose, is a botanical variant of the prickly rose species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, slightly serrated leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five to seven petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This form is native to parts of Europe and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
