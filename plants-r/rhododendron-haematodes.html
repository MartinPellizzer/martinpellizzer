Rhododendron haematodes

Rhododendron haematodes, commonly known as the bloodroot rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its vibrant red flowers and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The botanical features of this plant make it a notable species in alpine flora studies.
