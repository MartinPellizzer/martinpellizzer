Roscoea tumjensis

Roscoea tumjensis, commonly known as the Tibetan ginger, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces striking, showy inflorescences with pale yellow to orange-red bracts. The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found at high altitudes.

Its unique botanical characteristics make it a sought-after species among horticultural enthusiasts.
