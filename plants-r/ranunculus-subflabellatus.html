Ranunculus subflabellatus

Ranunculus subflabellatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of white or pale yellow sepals. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
