Rotala debilissima

Rotala debilissima, commonly known as the dwarf rotala, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically bright green and often have a slight red or pink hue. This plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide oxygen. Rotala debilissima prefers moderate to high light conditions and can be propagated through stem cuttings.

It is also known for its ability to help control algae growth in aquatic setups.
