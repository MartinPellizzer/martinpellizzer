Ranunculus cuneaticans

Ranunculus cuneaticans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Ranunculus cuneaticans is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
