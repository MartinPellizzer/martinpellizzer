Rubus chlorothyrsos var. micradenes

Rubus chlorothyrsos var. micradenes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its small, dark berries and dense, thorny growth. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are usually narrow and serrated. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
