Rutidosis heterogama

Rutidosis heterogama, commonly known as the bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a succulent, branched stem and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Rutidosis heterogama is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap that can cause skin irritation.
