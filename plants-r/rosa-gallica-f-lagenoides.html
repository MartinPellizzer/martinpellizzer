Rosa gallica f. lagenoides

Rosa gallica f. lagenoides, commonly known as the French pink or the Queen of the roses, is a cultivated form of the Gallica rose.

It is characterized by its deep pink, semi-double flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
