Reseda biaui

Reseda biaui, commonly known as the African wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its leaves are linear and hairy, providing a texture that distinguishes it from other similar species.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
