Raphionacme chimanimaniana

Raphionacme chimanimaniana, commonly known as the Chimanimani aster, is a flowering plant native to the Chimanimani Mountains in eastern Zimbabwe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to mountainous environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
