Rhynchosia senna f. glabrata

Rhynchosia senna f. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth senna, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its specific epithet "glabrata.". The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred structure, which is a common feature in the genus Rhynchosia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
