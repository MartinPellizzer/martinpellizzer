Rubus carpinifolius var. roseus

Rubus carpinifolius var. roseus, commonly known as the rosehip bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that turn orange or yellow as they mature, containing high levels of vitamin C. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
