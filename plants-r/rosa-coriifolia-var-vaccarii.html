Rosa coriifolia var. vaccarii

Rosa coriifolia var. vaccarii, commonly known as the pink sand rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is well adapted to coastal environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in maritime climates.
