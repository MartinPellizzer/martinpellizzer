Raphia textilis

Raphia textilis, commonly known as the raffia palm, is a large tropical plant native to central and eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, fan-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, yellowish-white inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. Its trunk is fibrous and grows in a cluster, supporting the heavy foliage.

The plant is well adapted to humid, warm climates and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.
