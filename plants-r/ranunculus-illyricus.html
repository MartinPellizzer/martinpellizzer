Ranunculus illyricus

Ranunculus illyricus, commonly known as the Illyrian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woods, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
