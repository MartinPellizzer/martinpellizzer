Rubus hoffmeisterianus

Rubus hoffmeisterianus, commonly known as Hoffmeister's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has a woody structure with thorny stems.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
