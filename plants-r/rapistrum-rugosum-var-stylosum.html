Rapistrum rugosum var. stylosum

Rapistrum rugosum var. stylosum, commonly known as the woolly rocket, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
