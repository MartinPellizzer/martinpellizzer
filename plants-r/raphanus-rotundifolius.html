Raphanus rotundifolius

Raphanus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved radish, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil. Its root system is less developed compared to other radish species, and it is often cultivated for its edible leaves.

This plant is native to parts of Asia and is used in traditional cuisines for its mild, peppery flavor.
