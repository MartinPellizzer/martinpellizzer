Rubus grandibasis

Rubus grandibasis, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. The flowers are white and five-petaled, blooming in early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.
