Rubus rivularis var. incomptus

Rubus rivularis var. incomptus, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow. Its fruits are small, aggregate berries that develop from multiple ovaria.

This variety is closely related to other Rubus species and is often found in association with other brambles.
