Rosa sicula var. hispanica

Rosa sicula var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in thorny branches and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
