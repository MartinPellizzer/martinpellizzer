Rosa carolina var. inermis

Rosa carolina var. inermis, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with a serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically thornless, which distinguishes it from other rose species.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well adapted to moist, acidic soils.
