Roegneria magnipoda

Roegneria magnipoda, commonly known as the large-leaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. Roegneria magnipoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
