Racosperma plectocarpum

Racosperma plectocarpum, commonly known as the clustered raceme acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, clustered racemes, which are typical of the genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

Racosperma plectocarpum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
