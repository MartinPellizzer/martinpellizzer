Rosa coriifolia var. pseudopsis

Rosa coriifolia var. pseudopsis, commonly known as the Japanese coral tree, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a fragrant aroma. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and attractive foliage, which contribute to its popularity in horticulture.
