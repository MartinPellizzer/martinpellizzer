Rhododendron youngae

Rhododendron youngae, commonly known as the Young's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron youngae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
