Rhus rosifolia

Rhus rosifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fuzzy berries that attract wildlife. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it often has a spreading or upright growth habit.

Rhus rosifolia is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open forests.
