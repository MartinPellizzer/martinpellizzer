Rosa subhybrida

Rosa subhybrida, commonly known as the tea rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and numerous, fragrant blooms that typically appear in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features serrated, dark green leaves and thorny stems typical of many rose species. Rosa subhybrida is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their beauty and long-lasting nature.
