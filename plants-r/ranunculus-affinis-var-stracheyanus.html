Ranunculus affinis var. stracheyanus

Ranunculus affinis var. stracheyanus, commonly known as the Strachey's crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape during the cooler months.
