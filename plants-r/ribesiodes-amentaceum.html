Ribesiodes amentaceum

Ribesiodes amentaceum, commonly known as the silver-leafed willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, pale green leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow or green, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ribesiodes amentaceum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
