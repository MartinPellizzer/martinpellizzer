Rubus geniculatus microgene hebetatus

Rubus geniculatus microgene hebetatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thornless stems. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries that are typically black when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval to lance-shaped and have a hairy texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
