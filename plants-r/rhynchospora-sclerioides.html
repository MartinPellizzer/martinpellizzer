Rhynchospora sclerioides

Rhynchospora sclerioides, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its small, three-lobed flowers are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Rhynchospora sclerioides is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
