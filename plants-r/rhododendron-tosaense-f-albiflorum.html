Rhododendron tosaense f. albiflorum

Rhododendron tosaense f. albiflorum, commonly known as the Tosa Whiteflower Rhododendron, is a rare and striking variant of the Tosa Rhododendron.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that contrast sharply with its dark green foliage. The plant is native to the southern regions of Japan, specifically the Tosa area, where it grows in humid, shaded forest environments. Its botanical features include broad, leathery leaves and a compact, shrub-like growth habit.

This cultivar is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
