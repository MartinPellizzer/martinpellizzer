Ranunculus × digeneus nothosubsp.

digeneus, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers. It typically features bright yellow petals that are often adorned with a darker central blotch, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture.

Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and delicate appearance.
