Rubus caesius var. pseudocaesius

Rubus caesius var. pseudocaesius, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry species.
