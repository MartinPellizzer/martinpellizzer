Rubus kokonoricus

Rubus kokonoricus, commonly known as the Kenyan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
