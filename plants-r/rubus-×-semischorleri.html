Rubus × semischorleri

Rubus × semischorleri, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Himalayas, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that resemble small blackberries. It produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Rubus alpestris and Rubus parvifolius, making it a notable example of natural hybridization in the Rubus genus.
