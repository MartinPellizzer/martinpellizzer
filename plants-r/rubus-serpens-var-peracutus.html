Rubus serpens var. peracutus

Rubus serpens var. peracutus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant often grows in shrub form. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or astringent flavor.
