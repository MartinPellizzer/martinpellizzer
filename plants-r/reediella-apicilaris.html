Reediella apicilaris

Reediella apicilaris, commonly known as the slender reed fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and ponds. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a delicate, feathery appearance. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Reediella apicilaris plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
