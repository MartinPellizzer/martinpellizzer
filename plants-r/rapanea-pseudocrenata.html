Rapanea pseudocrenata

Rapanea pseudocrenata, commonly known as the Malabar laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
