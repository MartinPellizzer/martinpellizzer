Rhododendron nanophyton var. nanophyton

Rhododendron nanophyton var. nanophyton, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed, contributing to its dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in woodland settings.
