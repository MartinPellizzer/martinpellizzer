Rhododendron hortense

Rhododendron hortense, commonly known as the garden rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It prefers partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers appear in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the environment.
