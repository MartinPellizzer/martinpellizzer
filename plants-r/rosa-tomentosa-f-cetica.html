Rosa tomentosa f. cetica

Rosa tomentosa f. cetica, commonly known as the woolly rose or the hairy rose, is a cultivar of the species Rosa tomentosa.

It is characterized by its dense, white to greyish-white pubescence covering the stems, leaves, and flowers. The plant produces fragrant, pink to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is a deciduous shrub native to China and is often grown for its ornamental value.

The foliage is typically dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its fluffy appearance.
