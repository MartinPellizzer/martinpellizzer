Rebutia paucicostata

Rebutia paucicostata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a distinctive appearance characterized by its narrow, ribbed stems. The plant typically produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Rebutia paucicostata is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique shape and ornamental value.
