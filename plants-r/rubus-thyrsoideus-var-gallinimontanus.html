Rubus thyrsoideus var. gallinimontanus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. gallinimontanus, commonly known as the gallinomontan blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
