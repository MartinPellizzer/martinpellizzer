Rubus desmodes

Rubus desmodes, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions with moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a distinctive tart flavor.
