Rosa × collinoides

Rosa × collinoides, commonly known as the Collin's rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem structure.

Rosa × collinoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
