Rosa × involuta var. nicholsonii

Rosa × involuta var. nicholsonii, commonly known as the Nicholson's climbing rose, is a hybrid species that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is a vigorous climber with arching stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are glossy and dark green.

This variety is well-suited for trellises and pergolas, showcasing its ornamental value in garden settings.
