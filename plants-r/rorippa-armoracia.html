Rorippa armoracia

Rorippa armoracia, commonly known as horseradish, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are alternate and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, white, cross-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy roots that are the primary source of its pungent flavor.

Horseradish is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
