Ranunculus spegazzinii

Ranunculus spegazzinii, commonly known as the Spegazzini buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with five to eight petals. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
