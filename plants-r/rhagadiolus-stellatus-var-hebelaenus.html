Rhagadiolus stellatus var. hebelaenus

Rhagadiolus stellatus var. hebelaenus, commonly known as the star-shaped beauty, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-like flowers, which are typically a combination of white and yellow, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
