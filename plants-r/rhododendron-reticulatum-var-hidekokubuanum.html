Rhododendron reticulatum var. hidekokubuanum

Rhododendron reticulatum var. hidekokubuanum, commonly known as the Hidokubu rhododendron, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. This variety is prized for its compact growth habit and unique floral display, making it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
