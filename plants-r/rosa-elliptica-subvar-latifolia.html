Rosa elliptica subvar. latifolia

Rosa elliptica subvar. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are broader than they are long, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

This variety is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile addition to landscaping.
