Rebutia pseudodeminuta f. grandiflora

Rebutia pseudodeminuta f. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora form of the narrow-leaved cactus, is a compact, branching cactus native to South America.

It features small, cylindrical stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its foliage is reduced to spines, with areoles bearing both spines and floral buds.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a well-drained soil environment.
