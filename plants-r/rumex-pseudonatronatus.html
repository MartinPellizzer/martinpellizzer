Rumex pseudonatronatus

Rumex pseudonatronatus, commonly known as the false dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, adaptations that help reduce water loss in harsh climates.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
