Ranunculus felixii var. forrestii

Ranunculus felixii var. forrestii, commonly known as the Forest Ranunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, delicate flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has palmate leaves divided into three to five lobes, and its stems are hairy and often branched. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
