Rapuntium serrulatum

Rapuntium serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Rapuntium serrulatum is an annual herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and soil stabilization.
