Rhododendron brachypodum

Rhododendron brachypodum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron brachypodum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific, challenging environments.
