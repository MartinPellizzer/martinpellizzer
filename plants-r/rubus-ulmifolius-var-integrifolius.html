Rubus ulmifolius var. integrifolius

Rubus ulmifolius var. integrifolius, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible berries and is also valued in ecological restoration projects.
