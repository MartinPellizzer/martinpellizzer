Rubus × semigillotii

Rubus × semigillotii, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is known for its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are pink to white in color.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is valued for its tart flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
