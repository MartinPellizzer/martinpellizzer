Rubus tomenticaulis

Rubus tomenticaulis, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Its botanical structure includes compound leaves with serrated margins and flowers that are usually white or pale pink.
