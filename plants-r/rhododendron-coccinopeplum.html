Rhododendron coccinopeplum

Rhododendron coccinopeplum, commonly known as the scarlet-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red to purple foliage, which remains vibrant throughout the growing season. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections for its unique foliage and floral display.
