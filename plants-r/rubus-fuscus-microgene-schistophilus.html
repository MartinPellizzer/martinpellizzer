Rubus fuscus microgene schistophilus

Rubus fuscus microgene schistophilus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, achenes. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and pies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
