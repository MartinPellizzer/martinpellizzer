Rhododendron erileucum

Rhododendron erileucum, commonly known as the white-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its large, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, with a distinctive white or pale underside that gives it its common name.

Rhododendron erileucum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
