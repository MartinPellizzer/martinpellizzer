Rubus granulatus subsp. traunsteiniensis

Rubus granulatus subsp.

traunsteiniensis, commonly known as the Traunsteiner bramble, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupes, which are usually red when ripe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and flowers that bloom in clusters.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its specific ecological requirements and limited distribution.
