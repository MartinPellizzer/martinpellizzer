Rosa × hybrida

Rosa × hybrida, commonly known as the modern garden rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the crossbreeding of species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its diverse range of flower colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, as well as varying petal forms and fragrances. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa × hybrida is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
