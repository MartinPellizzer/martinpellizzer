Rubus schleicheri var. florentulus

Rubus schleicheri var. florentulus, commonly known as the Florentine blackberry, is a shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
