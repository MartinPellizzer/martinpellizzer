Ribes malvaceum var. viridifolium

Ribes malvaceum var. viridifolium, commonly known as the green-leaved gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive green foliage, which sets it apart from other varieties of Ribes. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
