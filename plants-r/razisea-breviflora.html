Razisea breviflora

Razisea breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered razisea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Razisea breviflora is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
