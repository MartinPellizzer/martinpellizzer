Rhododendron indicum var. incarnatum

Rhododendron indicum var. incarnatum, commonly known as the Crimson Flame Rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pink to deep crimson. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
