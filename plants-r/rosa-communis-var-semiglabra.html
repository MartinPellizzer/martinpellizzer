Rosa communis var. semiglabra

Rosa communis var. semiglabra, commonly known as the semi-glabrous rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its reduced hairy coverage on its stems and leaves.

It typically produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both decorative and historical horticultural purposes.
