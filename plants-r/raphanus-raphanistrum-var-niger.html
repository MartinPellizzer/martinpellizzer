Raphanus raphanistrum var. niger

Raphanus raphanistrum var. niger, commonly known as black radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its deep taproot, which can grow up to 30 cm in length, and its dark purple or black skin. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and often have a peppery or spicy aroma.

The plant is native to Europe and Asia but is now found in various parts of the world, often growing in disturbed soils.
