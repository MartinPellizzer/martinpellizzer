Rhododendron keysii

Rhododendron keysii, commonly known as the Keys' rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to purple.

Rhododendron keysii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
