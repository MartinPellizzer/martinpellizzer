Rosa coriifolia f. frutetorum

Rosa coriifolia f. frutetorum, commonly known as the fragrant shrub rose, is a cultivar of the species Rosa coriifolia, characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers and compact growth habit.

It features small, pinkish-white petals with a strong, sweet fragrance, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. The plant produces clusters of flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of leaves. Its foliage is dark green and densely arranged, providing an attractive backdrop to the blossoms.

This cultivar is well-suited for container gardening and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
