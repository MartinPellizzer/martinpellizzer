Rosa resinosa

Rosa resinosa, commonly known as the sticky rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance from its stems, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Rosa resinosa thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
