Rosa glabrivanescens

Rosa glabrivanescens, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a species native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thornless stems and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red-orange hips.

Rosa glabrivanescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
