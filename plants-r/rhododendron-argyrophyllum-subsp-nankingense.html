Rhododendron argyrophyllum subsp. nankingense

Rhododendron argyrophyllum subsp.

nankingense, commonly known as the Nanking rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically silver-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers in the spring, which are borne on elongated pedicels. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and showy blooms.
