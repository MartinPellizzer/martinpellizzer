Randia parvula var. mollis

Randia parvula var. mollis, commonly known as softleaf randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves that are soft and velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, well-drained soils.
