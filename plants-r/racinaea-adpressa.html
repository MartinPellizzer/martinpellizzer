Racinaea adpressa

Racinaea adpressa, commonly known as the pressed racinaea, is a rare and unique fern-like plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive, almost translucent appearance. It is often found in humid, forested areas where it forms dense mats on the forest floor.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Racinaea adpressa is considered an important species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
