Roeperia muricata

Roeperia muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf roeperia, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial shade to full sun. Its ornamental value lies in its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Roeperia muricata is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
