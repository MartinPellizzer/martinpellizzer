Rosa canina f. kupcokii

Rosa canina f. kupcokii, commonly known as the Kupcokii rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale pink to white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces small, oval-shaped hips that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along hedgerows.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
