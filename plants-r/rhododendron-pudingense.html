Rhododendron pudingense

Rhododendron pudingense, commonly known as the Puding rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and cool temperatures. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
