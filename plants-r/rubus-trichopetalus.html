Rubus trichopetalus

Rubus trichopetalus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
