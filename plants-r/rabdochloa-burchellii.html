Rabdochloa burchellii

Rabdochloa burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in association with other wetland vegetation, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
