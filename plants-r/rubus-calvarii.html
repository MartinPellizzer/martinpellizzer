Rubus calvarii

Rubus calvarii, commonly known as the Calvarii blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
