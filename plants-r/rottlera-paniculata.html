Rottlera paniculata

Rottlera paniculata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in paniculate inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

Rottlera paniculata is valued both for its ornamental qualities and for its use in traditional medicine.
