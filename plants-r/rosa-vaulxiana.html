Rosa vaulxiana

Rosa vaulxiana, commonly known as the Vaulxian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by oval, orange-red hips. Rosa vaulxiana is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
