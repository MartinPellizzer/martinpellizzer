Rubus × expansiformis

Rubus × expansiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a complex flowering structure typical of the Rubus genus.
