Rhododendron × pavonium

Rhododendron × pavonium, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to lavender flowers in the spring, which bloom on the previous year's growth. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Rhododendron × pavonium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
