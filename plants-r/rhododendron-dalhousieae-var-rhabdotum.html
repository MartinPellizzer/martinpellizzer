Rhododendron dalhousieae var. rhabdotum

Rhododendron dalhousieae var. rhabdotum, commonly known as the Dalhousie rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
