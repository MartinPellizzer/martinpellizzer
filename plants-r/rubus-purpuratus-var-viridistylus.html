Rubus purpuratus var. viridistylus

Rubus purpuratus var. viridistylus, commonly known as the green-styled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinct green style in the flower.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are armed with prickles.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary applications.
