Rubus koehleri subsp. bavaricus

Rubus koehleri subsp.

bavaricus, commonly known as the Bavarian blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local culinary traditions.
