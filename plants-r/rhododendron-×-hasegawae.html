Rhododendron × hasegawae

Rhododendron × hasegawae, commonly known as the Hasegawa rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron mucronatum and Rhododendron kaempferi.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative shrub. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form thickets, providing both visual appeal and habitat for small wildlife.
