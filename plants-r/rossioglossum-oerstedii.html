Rossioglossum oerstedii

Rossioglossum oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a trap to lure in pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in attracting specific insect pollinators.

Rossioglossum oerstedii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
