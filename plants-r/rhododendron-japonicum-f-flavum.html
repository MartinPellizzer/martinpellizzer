Rhododendron japonicum f. flavum

Rhododendron japonicum f. flavum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea var. flavum, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and elliptical in shape.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
