Rubus villosus var. eglandulosus

Rubus villosus var. eglandulosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, lobed leaves, and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family and is noted for its hairy foliage and fruiting habit.
