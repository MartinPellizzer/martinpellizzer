Rosa afzeliana var. conjungens

Rosa afzeliana var. conjungens, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a prominent central pistil.

It produces small, red-orange fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
