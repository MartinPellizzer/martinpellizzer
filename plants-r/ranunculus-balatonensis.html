Ranunculus balatonensis

Ranunculus balatonensis, commonly known as the Balaton buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals, giving it a distinctive cup-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
