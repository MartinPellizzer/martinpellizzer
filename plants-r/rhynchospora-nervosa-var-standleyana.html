Rhynchospora nervosa var. standleyana

Rhynchospora nervosa var. standleyana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and seasonally flooded areas. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants.
