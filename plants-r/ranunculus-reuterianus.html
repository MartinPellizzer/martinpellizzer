Ranunculus reuterianus

Ranunculus reuterianus, commonly known as the Reuter's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, herbaceous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually bright yellow with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it produces small, green berries containing seeds.

It is a perennial species that often thrives in temperate climates.
