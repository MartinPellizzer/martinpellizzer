Rosa × biserratoides

Rosa × biserratoides, commonly known as the double-barked rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thornless stems that exhibit a unique, double-bark appearance. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Rosa × biserratoides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
