Rubus siekensis

Rubus siekensis, commonly known as the Siekens bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Laos. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its fruit, a type of edible berry, is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
