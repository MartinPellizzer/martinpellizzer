Ribes aureum var. gracillimum

Ribes aureum var. gracillimum, commonly known as golden currant, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible red berries. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has slender, arching branches. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
