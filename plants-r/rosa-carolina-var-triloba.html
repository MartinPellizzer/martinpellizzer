Rosa carolina var. triloba

Rosa carolina var. triloba, commonly known as the Carolina trilobed rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, and produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The variety is distinguished by its unique leaf structure and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
