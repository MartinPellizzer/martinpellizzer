Rubus × aculifer

Rubus × aculifer, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bushes, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. It produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins.

The botanical features of Rubus × aculifer make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
