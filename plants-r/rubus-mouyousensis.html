Rubus mouyousensis

Rubus mouyousensis, commonly known as the Mouyoushan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries, raspberries, and dewberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. It typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to moist, temperate climates.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.
