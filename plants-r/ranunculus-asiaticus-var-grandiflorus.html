Ranunculus asiaticus var. grandiflorus

Ranunculus asiaticus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and delicate, palmate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It blooms in spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
