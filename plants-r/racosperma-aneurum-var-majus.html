Racosperma aneurum var. majus

Racosperma aneurum var. majus, commonly known as the large-leaf wattled pea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
