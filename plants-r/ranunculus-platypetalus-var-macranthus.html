Ranunculus platypetalus var. macranthus

Ranunculus platypetalus var. macranthus, commonly known as the large-flowered buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed and trifoliate. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. It is characterized by its upright stems and is often found in association with other woodland flora.

The botanical name reflects its distinctive floral structure and its status as a variant of the broader Ranunculus platypetalus species.
