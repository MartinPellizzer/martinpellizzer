Ranunculus trichophyllos

Ranunculus trichophyllos, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, which are typically borne on long, slender stems. The leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
