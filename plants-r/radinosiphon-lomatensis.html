Radinosiphon lomatensis

Radinosiphon lomatensis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a marine flowering plant found in coastal areas.

It thrives in intertidal zones and is characterized by its unique, spiky flower heads. The plant has a robust root system that helps it anchor in sandy or rocky substrates. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, with showy bracts that attract pollinators.

Radinosiphon lomatensis plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for small marine organisms.
