Racosperma hillianum

Racosperma hillianum, commonly known as the Hillian's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices.
