Rudbeckia perfoliata

Rudbeckia perfoliata, commonly known as the leafy coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive leaves that are often clasped around the stems, giving it its species name "perfoliata.". The plant produces showy yellow to orange flower heads with a central disk, blooming from late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Rudbeckia perfoliata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
