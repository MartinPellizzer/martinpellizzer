Ranunculus fallax subsp. kvistii

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

kvistii, commonly known as the Norwegian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern Europe. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, lobed leaf structure.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and grassland habitats. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter stature and narrower leaves compared to the typical form of *Ranunculus fallax*.
