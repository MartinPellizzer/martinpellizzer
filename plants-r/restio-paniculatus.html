Restio paniculatus

Restio paniculatus, commonly known as the silver restio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, typically during the winter months. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
