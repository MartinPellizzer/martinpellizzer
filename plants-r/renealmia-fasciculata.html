Renealmia fasciculata

Renealmia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered reed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in clusters at the base. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in humid environments.

Renealmia fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
