Ranunculus auricomus subsp. monticola

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features showy, yellow flowers with prominent, palmately lobed sepals that resemble miniature leaves.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are trifoliate or pinnately compound, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, blooming in late spring to early summer.
