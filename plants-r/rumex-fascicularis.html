Rumex fascicularis

Rumex fascicularis, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright, ribbed stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is often found in moist soils along stream banks, roadsides, and waste areas.

Rumex fascicularis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
