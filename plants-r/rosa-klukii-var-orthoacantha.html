Rosa klukii var. orthoacantha

Rosa klukii var. orthoacantha, commonly known as the Kluk's rose, is a rare and distinctive species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its rarity and unique appearance.
