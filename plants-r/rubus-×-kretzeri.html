Rubus × kretzeri

Rubus × kretzeri, commonly known as the Kretzeri blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland and disturbed habitats. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and tart. Its botanical features include palmate leaves with serrated edges and a complex flowering structure.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in gardens and for small-scale agriculture.
