Ranunculus samolifolius

Ranunculus samolifolius, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus samolifolius is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
