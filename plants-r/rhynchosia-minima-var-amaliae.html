Rhynchosia minima var. amaliae

Rhynchosia minima var. amaliae, commonly known as the dwarf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing annual legume that produces small, yellow flowers and small, edible pods. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
