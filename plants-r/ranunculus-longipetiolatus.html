Ranunculus longipetiolatus

Ranunculus longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
