Rosa ecae subsp. primula

Rosa ecae subsp.

primula, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically blooms in early summer, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine landscapes.
