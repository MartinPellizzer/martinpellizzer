Ruellia glabrata var. longipetiolatum

Ruellia glabrata var. longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and broad, lance-shaped leaves with long petioles. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
