Rubus thyrsoideus var. rotundipetalus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. rotundipetalus, commonly known as the red-veined raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its flowers are pink to white, with five rounded petals, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
