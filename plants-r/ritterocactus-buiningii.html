Ritterocactus buiningii

Ritterocactus buiningii, commonly known as the Buning's ritterocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable plant in the world of succulents.
