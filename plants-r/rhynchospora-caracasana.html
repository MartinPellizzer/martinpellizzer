Rhynchospora caracasana

Rhynchospora caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, three-lobed flowers, characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

Rhynchospora caracasana plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
