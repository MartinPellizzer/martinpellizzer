Ranunculus auricomus subsp. nannfeldtii

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

nannfeldtii, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to the typical form of *R. auricomus*. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and mountainous habitats.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
