Ranunculus montanus var. djurdjurae

Ranunculus montanus var. djurdjurae, commonly known as the Djurdjura Ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Alps.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it has a short, hairy stem.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in alpine botanical gardens.
