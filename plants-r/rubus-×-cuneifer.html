Rubus × cuneifer

Rubus × cuneifer, commonly known as the blackberry dewberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
