Ribes × futurum

Ribes × futurum, commonly known as the hybrid gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub resulting from the crossbreeding of different Ribes species.

It typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow or orange berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ribes × futurum is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
