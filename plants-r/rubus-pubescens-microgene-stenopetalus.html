Rubus pubescens microgene stenopetalus

Rubus pubescens microgene stenopetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clustered, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, usually red or black fruits known as berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
