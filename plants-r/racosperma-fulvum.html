Racosperma fulvum

Racosperma fulvum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a species of acacia native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its yellow floral spikes and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils.

Racosperma fulvum is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
