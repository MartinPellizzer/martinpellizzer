Rosa micrantha f. sanguinettii

Rosa micrantha f. sanguinettii, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose species Rosa micrantha.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of China and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
