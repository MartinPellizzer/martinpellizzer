Rosa afzeliana var. hyperpsilophylla

Rosa afzeliana var. hyperpsilophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose species native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rosa genus, valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in horticulture.

The variety is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
