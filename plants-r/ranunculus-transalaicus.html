Ranunculus transalaicus

Ranunculus transalaicus, commonly known as the Transalpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central crown. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
