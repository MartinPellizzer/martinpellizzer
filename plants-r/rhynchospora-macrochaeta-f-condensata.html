Rhynchospora macrochaeta f. condensata

Rhynchospora macrochaeta f. condensata, commonly known as the dense-hairy marsh sedge, is a botanical variant of the marsh sedge species.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered closely together, giving it its "condensata" designation.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
