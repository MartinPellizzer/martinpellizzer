Rubus hypsus

Rubus hypsus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a distinctive sweet-tart flavor. Rubus hypsus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
