Rosa × sabinii f. sabauda

Rosa × sabinii f. sabauda, commonly known as the Sabauda rose, is a fragrant, floriferous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its semi-double to fully double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems, broad ovate leaves with serrated edges, and is well-suited for cultivation in gardens due to its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental planting.
