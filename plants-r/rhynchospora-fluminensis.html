Rhynchospora fluminensis

Rhynchospora fluminensis, commonly known as the river marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are a defining feature of the sedge family.

Rhynchospora fluminensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
