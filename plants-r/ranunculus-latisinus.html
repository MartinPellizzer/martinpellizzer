Ranunculus latisinus

Ranunculus latisinus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lobed leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
