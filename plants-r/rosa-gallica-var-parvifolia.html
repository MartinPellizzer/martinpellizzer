Rosa gallica var. parvifolia

Rosa gallica var. parvifolia, commonly known as the French pink or small-leaved gallica rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thornless stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, ovate, and have a velvety texture, often with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and historical significance.
