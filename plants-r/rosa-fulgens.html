Rosa fulgens

Rosa fulgens, commonly known as the everglade rose, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the plant is well adapted to wet, swampy environments.

Rosa fulgens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping in humid climates.
