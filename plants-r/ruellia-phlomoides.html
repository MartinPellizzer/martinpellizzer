Ruellia phlomoides

Ruellia phlomoides, commonly known as the roughleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ruellia phlomoides is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both full sun and partial shade.
