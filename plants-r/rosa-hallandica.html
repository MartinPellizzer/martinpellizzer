Rosa hallandica

Rosa hallandica, commonly known as the Halland rose, is a species of shrub native to Scandinavia and northern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces red-orange hips in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Rosa hallandica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
