Racosperma constablei

Racosperma constablei, commonly known as the Constable's wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Racosperma constablei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
