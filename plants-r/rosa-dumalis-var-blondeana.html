Rosa dumalis var. blondeana

Rosa dumalis var. blondeana, commonly known as the Blenheim Beauty rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces red, hairy hips in the fall. It is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
