Rhododendron ponticum var. parviflorum

Rhododendron ponticum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered Pontic rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink to red in color, blooming in early spring. Its flowers are followed by leathery, elliptical berries that contain toxic seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities but can be invasive in certain environments.
