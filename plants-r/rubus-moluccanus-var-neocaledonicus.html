Rubus moluccanus var. neocaledonicus

Rubus moluccanus var. neocaledonicus, commonly known as the New Caledonian raspberry, is a species native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, similar to other raspberries. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive silvery-white underside.

This variant is notable for its adaptation to the unique ecological conditions of New Caledonia.
