Rosa dumalis var. sarmentoides

Rosa dumalis var. sarmentoides, commonly known as the creeping wild rose, is a shrub-like species native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or colonies. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
