Rubus thyrsoideus var. virescens

Rubus thyrsoideus var. virescens, commonly known as the green-stem blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and produces compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant bears small, white to pale pink flowers that develop into edible, dark purple berries. Its stems are typically green or reddish-green, distinguishing it from other Rubus species.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
