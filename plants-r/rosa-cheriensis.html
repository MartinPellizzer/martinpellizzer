Rosa cheriensis

Rosa cheriensis, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and produces fragrant, pink or white flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Rosa cheriensis is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It plays a significant role in traditional Chinese medicine and horticulture.
