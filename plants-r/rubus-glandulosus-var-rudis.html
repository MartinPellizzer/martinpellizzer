Rubus glandulosus var. rudis

Rubus glandulosus var. rudis, commonly known as the rough raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
