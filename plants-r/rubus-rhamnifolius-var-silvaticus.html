Rubus rhamnifolius var. silvaticus

Rubus rhamnifolius var. silvaticus, commonly known as the silvaticus blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in shaded areas under forest canopies.
