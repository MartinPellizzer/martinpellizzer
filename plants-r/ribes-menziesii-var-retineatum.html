Ribes menziesii var. retineatum

Ribes menziesii var. retineatum, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and river valleys.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
