Rosanowia conspicua

Rosanowia conspicua, commonly known as the showy lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosanowia conspicua is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
