Rosa canina var. szovitsii

Rosa canina var. szovitsii, commonly known as the Szovits dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in spring. The fruit, a red-hued hip, is edible and contains seeds.

This plant is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
