Renanthera coccinea

Renanthera coccinea, commonly known as the red orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers with a contrasting yellow lip, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, warm environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, typically arranged in a fan shape.

Renanthera coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
