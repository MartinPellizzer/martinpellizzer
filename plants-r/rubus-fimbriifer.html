Rubus fimbriifer

Rubus fimbriifer, commonly known as the fringed leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive fringed leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Rubus fimbriifer is typically found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in temperate climates.
