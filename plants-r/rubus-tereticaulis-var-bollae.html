Rubus tereticaulis var. bollae

Rubus tereticaulis var. bollae, commonly known as the California blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in coastal and inland regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in jams and pies.
