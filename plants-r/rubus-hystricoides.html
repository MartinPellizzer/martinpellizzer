Rubus hystricoides

Rubus hystricoides, commonly known as the thorny raspberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in sharp, curved thorns. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when ripe. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often serrated.

Rubus hystricoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
