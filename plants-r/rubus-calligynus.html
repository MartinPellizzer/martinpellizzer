Rubus calligynus

Rubus calligynus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible, though they are often overshadowed by more commercially valuable blackberry varieties. Botanically, it belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, sharing traits with other raspberries and blackberries.

Its growth habit and morphology make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
