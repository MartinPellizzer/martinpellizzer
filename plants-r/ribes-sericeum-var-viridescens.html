Ribes sericeum var. viridescens

Ribes sericeum var. viridescens, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, silver-haired stems and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it valuable for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
