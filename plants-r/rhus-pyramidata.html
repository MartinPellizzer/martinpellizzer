Rhus pyramidata

Rhus pyramidata, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features compound leaves with multiple leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant seasonal colors.

Rhus pyramidata is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
