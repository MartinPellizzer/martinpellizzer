Rubus dasycoccus

Rubus dasycoccus, commonly known as the dense-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
