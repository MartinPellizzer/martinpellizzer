Rosa sepium subsp. rubiginosa

Rosa sepium subsp.

rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its reddish stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red.

The plant typically grows in woodland or scrub habitats and prefers well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
