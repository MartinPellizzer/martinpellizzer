Rosa stylosa f. systyla

Rosa stylosa f. systyla, commonly known as the pink-stemmed rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa stylosa.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink-colored stems, which contrast with the typical green stems of other rose varieties. This form exhibits a unique morphological trait that is of interest in botanical studies and horticultural breeding. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically in the spring and summer months.

It is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in landscape design.
