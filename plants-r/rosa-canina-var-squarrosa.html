Rosa canina var. squarrosa

Rosa canina var. squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fuzzy, and often pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its fruit, known as a hip, is red and contains several seeds.

This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
