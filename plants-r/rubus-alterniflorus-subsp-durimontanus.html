Rubus alterniflorus subsp. durimontanus

Rubus alterniflorus subsp.

durimontanus, commonly known as the Durimontan blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible berries.
