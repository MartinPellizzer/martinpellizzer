Rosa pycnophylla

Rosa pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaved rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, overlapping foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems.

Rosa pycnophylla is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil.
