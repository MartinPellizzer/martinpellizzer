Rosa coriifolia f. oblonga

Rosa coriifolia f. oblonga, commonly known as the oblongleaf shrub rose, is a cultivar of the shrub rose species characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral display.

It features elongated, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight pubescence, contributing to its ornamental value. The flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and a prominent center, blooming in early summer. It is a compact shrub, often used in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.

This variety is well-suited for smaller spaces and can thrive in a range of soil conditions with proper care.
