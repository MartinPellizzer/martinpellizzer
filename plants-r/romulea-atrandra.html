Romulea atrandra

Romulea atrandra, commonly known as the black-veined romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black veins on its leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
