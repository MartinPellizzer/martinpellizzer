Robinia viscosa var. hartwigii

Robinia viscosa var. hartwigii, commonly known as the Hartwig's honey locust, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a grayish-green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
