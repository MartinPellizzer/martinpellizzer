Rosa canina subvar. edita

Rosa canina subvar. edita, commonly known as the European dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its fruit, known as a hip, is orange-red and contains achenes surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

It is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along riverbanks.
