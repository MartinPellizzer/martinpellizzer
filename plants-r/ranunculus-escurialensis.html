Ranunculus escurialensis

Ranunculus escurialensis, commonly known as the Spanish buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
