Rosa flagellaris

Rosa flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate rose, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and spread across surfaces. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with multiple leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated.

Rosa flagellaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
