Rosa sepium f. osmoidea

Rosa sepium f. osmoidea, commonly known as the fragrant rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa sepium species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, highly aromatic fragrance that is more pronounced than in most other rose varieties. The plant produces large, showy flowers with deep pink to crimson petals, often with a contrasting white center. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
