Rosa rubiginosa var. dubia

Rosa rubiginosa var. dubia, commonly known as the dark rose or the dusky rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to almost white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are woody, red berries that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
