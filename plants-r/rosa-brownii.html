Rosa brownii

Rosa brownii, commonly known as the Brown's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red fruits known as hip fruits.

Rosa brownii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
