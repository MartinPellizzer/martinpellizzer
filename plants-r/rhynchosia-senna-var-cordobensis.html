Rhynchosia senna var. cordobensis

Rhynchosia senna var. cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba senna, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Its seeds are encased in a slender, hairy pod, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrub areas.
