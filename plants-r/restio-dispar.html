Restio dispar

Restio dispar, commonly known as the common restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Restio dispar thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland fynbos vegetation.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
