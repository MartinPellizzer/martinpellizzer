Rubus plicatus f. rosulentus

Rubus plicatus f. rosulentus, commonly known as the rose-like raspberry, is a unique form of the common raspberry.

It is characterized by its dense, rose-like inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, edible berries.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
