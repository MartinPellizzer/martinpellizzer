Rubus ulmifolius var. arguticuspis

Rubus ulmifolius var. arguticuspis, commonly known as the bristly blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive bristly appearance, with prickly stems and leaves that have a serrated edge. It produces small, dark red fruits that are typically less palatable than those of other blackberry varieties. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
