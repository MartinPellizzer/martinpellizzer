Rebutia rauschii

Rebutia rauschii, commonly known as the Rauschii moon cactus, is a succulent native to South America.

It features a swollen, colorful tuberous rootstock that resembles a flower, with the actual flowers emerging from the top. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and can store water, typical of cacti. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Rebutia rauschii is prized for its unique appearance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
