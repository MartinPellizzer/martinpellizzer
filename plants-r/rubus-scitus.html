Rubus scitus

Rubus scitus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are dark purple to black when mature. These berries are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Rubus scitus is native to various regions of Europe and Asia and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to different soil types.
