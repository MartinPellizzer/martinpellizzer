Rhododendron brachycarpum subsp. brachycarpum

Rhododendron brachycarpum subsp.

brachycarpum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas. It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form thickets, providing both aesthetic appeal and habitat for small wildlife.
