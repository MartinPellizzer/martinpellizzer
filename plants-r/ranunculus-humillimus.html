Ranunculus humillimus

Ranunculus humillimus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, succulent stems and trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Ranunculus humillimus is often found in disturbed soils and can spread rapidly through rhizomes.
