Rorippa × sterilis

Rorippa × sterilis, commonly known as the sterile water flea, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic conditions.

Due to its hybrid nature, Rorippa × sterilis exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its parental species.
