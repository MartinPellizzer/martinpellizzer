Russowia sogdiana

Russowia sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a fine layer of hairs.

Russowia sogdiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
