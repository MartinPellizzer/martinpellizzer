Rosa spinosissima f. baicheriana

Rosa spinosissima f. baicheriana, commonly known as the Baicher's prickly rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa spinosissima.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and it produces rose hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
