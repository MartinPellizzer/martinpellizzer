Rubus × eriopsilon

Rubus × eriopsilon, commonly known as the trailing blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, contributing to its adaptability and ecological significance.
