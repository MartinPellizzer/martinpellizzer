Rhodiola crassipes var. stephani

Rhodiola crassipes var. stephani, commonly known as the alpine rose or stephan's rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and small, fleshy leaves that help it survive in cold, arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to low temperatures and intense solar radiation.

Rhodiola crassipes var. stephani is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
