Rhododendron calendulaceum var. flammeum

Rhododendron calendulaceum var. flammeum, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from bright red to orange, blooming in early spring. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
