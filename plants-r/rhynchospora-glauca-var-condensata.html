Rhynchospora glauca var. condensata

Rhynchospora glauca var. condensata, commonly known as the dense blue moor grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, blue-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, forming a compact rosette structure that helps it thrive in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which aids in water retention.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
