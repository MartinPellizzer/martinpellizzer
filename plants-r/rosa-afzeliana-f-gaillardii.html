Rosa afzeliana f. gaillardii

Rosa afzeliana f. gaillardii, commonly known as the Gaillardii rose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a hybrid form of Rosa afzeliana, characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of fragrant, semi-double petals. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with thorny stems that provide structural support.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
