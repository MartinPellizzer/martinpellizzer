Ranunculus auricomus subsp. atriviolascens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

atriviolascens, commonly known as the purple buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive purple-tinged base, often adorned with a white or pale yellow center.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided with a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
