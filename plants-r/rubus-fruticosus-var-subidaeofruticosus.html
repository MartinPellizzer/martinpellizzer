Rubus fruticosus var. subidaeofruticosus

Rubus fruticosus var. subidaeofruticosus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a thorny, perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers that develop into edible berries. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun to partial shade. Its berries, which are rich in antioxidants, are commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The shrub also plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
