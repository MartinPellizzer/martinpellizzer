Rubus maquipucunensis

Rubus maquipucunensis, commonly known as the Maquipucuni raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less-studied species within the Rubus genus.
