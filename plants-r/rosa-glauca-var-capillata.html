Rosa glauca var. capillata

Rosa glauca var. capillata, commonly known as the bluebloom rose, is a variant of the blue rose that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a compact shrub with narrow, gray-green leaves and produces striking blue-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and unique coloration.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to adapt to a range of soil conditions.
