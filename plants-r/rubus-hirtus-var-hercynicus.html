Rubus hirtus var. hercynicus

Rubus hirtus var. hercynicus, commonly known as the Hercynian bramble, is a shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
