Rapuntium hirtum

Rapuntium hirtum, commonly known as hairy rapunculus, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root is edible and has a slightly bitter taste, often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
