Rosa oenophora

Rosa oenophora, commonly known as the wine grape rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to South America, particularly the Andean region, and is characterized by its fragrant, deep red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and aromatherapy due to their intense aroma.

Rosa oenophora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
