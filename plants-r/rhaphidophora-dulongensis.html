Rhaphidophora dulongensis

Rhaphidophora dulongensis, commonly known as the Dulong's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Rhaphidophora dulongensis is also valued for its starchy roots, which can be processed into a type of arrowroot starch.
