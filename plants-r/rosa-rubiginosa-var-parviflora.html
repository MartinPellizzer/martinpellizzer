Rosa rubiginosa var. parviflora

Rosa rubiginosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf rose or parviflora rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

The variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
