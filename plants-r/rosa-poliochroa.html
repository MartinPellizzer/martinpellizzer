Rosa poliochroa

Rosa poliochroa, commonly known as the polychrome rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bicolor flowers that typically feature a white base with a deep red or pink overlay. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their unique coloration.

Rosa poliochroa prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
