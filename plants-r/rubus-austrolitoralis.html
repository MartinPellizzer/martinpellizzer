Rubus austrolitoralis

Rubus austrolitoralis, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its trailing or scrambling growth habit and produces small, dark red to black berries. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
