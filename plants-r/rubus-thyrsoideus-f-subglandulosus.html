Rubus thyrsoideus f. subglandulosus

Rubus thyrsoideus f. subglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the thyrsoideus species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

This form is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
