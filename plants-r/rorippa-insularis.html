Rorippa insularis

Rorippa insularis, commonly known as the island watercress, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually submerged or floating. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in early summer.

Rorippa insularis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, shallow waters.
