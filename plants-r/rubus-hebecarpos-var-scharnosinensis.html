Rubus hebecarpos var. scharnosinensis

Rubus hebecarpos var. scharnosinensis, commonly known as the Scharnhofer blackberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruits are small, dark berries that ripen in late summer.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to harsh alpine environments.
