Roemeria rhoeadiflora

Roemeria rhoeadiflora, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branched.

Roemeria rhoeadiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
