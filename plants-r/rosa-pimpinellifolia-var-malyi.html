Rosa pimpinellifolia var. malyi

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. malyi, commonly known as the Malyi rock rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand arid and rocky environments. It produces oval, red-orange hips that are edible and contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in harsh climates.
