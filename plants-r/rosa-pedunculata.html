Rosa pedunculata

Rosa pedunculata, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its continuous blooming habit throughout the growing season. The plant features slender, often thornless stems and produces clusters of fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates.

Rosa pedunculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
