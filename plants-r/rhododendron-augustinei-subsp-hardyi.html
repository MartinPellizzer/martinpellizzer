Rhododendron augustinei subsp. hardyi

Rhododendron augustinei subsp.

hardyi, commonly known as the hardy rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
