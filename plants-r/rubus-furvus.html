Rubus furvus

Rubus furvus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn red when mature, often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Rubus furvus is a perennial shrub that plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
