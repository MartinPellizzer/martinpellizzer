Ranunculus flammula var. angustifolius

Ranunculus flammula var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a bright splash of color to its natural habitat.
