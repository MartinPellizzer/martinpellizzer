Rosa spreta

Rosa spreta, commonly known as the white evergreen rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western Mediterranean region and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate, sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

Rosa spreta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
