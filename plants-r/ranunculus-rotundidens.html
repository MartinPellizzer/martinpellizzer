Ranunculus rotundidens

Ranunculus rotundidens, commonly known as the round-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are rounded and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
