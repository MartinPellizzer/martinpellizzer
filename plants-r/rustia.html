Rustia

Rustia, commonly known as the rock rose, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading shrubs with small, often hairy leaves. The flowers are typically clustered in racemes or panicles and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and red. Rustia species are native to regions in Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

They are often found growing in rocky or sandy soils and are adapted to dry, sunny environments.
