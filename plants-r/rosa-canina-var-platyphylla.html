Rosa canina var. platyphylla

Rosa canina var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red hips that are rich in vitamin C and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and hedgerows.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a valued plant in both horticulture and herbal practices.
