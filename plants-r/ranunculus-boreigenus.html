Ranunculus boreigenus

Ranunculus boreigenus, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold climates. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Ranunculus boreigenus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is an indicator species of harsh, cold ecosystems.
