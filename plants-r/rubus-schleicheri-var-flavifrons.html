Rubus schleicheri var. flavifrons

Rubus schleicheri var. flavifrons, commonly known as the yellow-fruited bramble, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its fruits are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
