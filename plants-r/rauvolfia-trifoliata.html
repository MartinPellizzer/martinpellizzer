Rauvolfia trifoliata

Rauvolfia trifoliata, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

It is a woody shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several elliptical seeds.

Rauvolfia trifoliata is notable for containing alkaloids, including reserpine, which has been used in traditional medicine and modern pharmacology.
