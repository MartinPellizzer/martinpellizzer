Robinia edwardsiifolia

Robinia edwardsiifolia, commonly known as the Edwards' acacia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Robinia edwardsiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
