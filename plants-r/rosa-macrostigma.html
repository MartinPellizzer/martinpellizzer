Rosa macrostigma

Rosa macrostigma, commonly known as the large-stemmed rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. Its stems are covered in thorns, which are typically short and curved.

Rosa macrostigma thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
