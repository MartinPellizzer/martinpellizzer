Ranunculus flabellatus var. pseudomonspeliacus

Ranunculus flabellatus var. pseudomonspeliacus, commonly known as the false fan-flowered buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that disperse its seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
