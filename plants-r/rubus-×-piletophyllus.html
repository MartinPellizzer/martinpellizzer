Rubus × piletophyllus

Rubus × piletophyllus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and dark purple to black fruits. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces berries that are often used in culinary applications.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
