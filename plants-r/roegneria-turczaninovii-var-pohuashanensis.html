Roegneria turczaninovii var. pohuashanensis

Roegneria turczaninovii var. pohuashanensis, commonly known as the Pohuashan grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant typically produces inflorescences that are spike-like and composed of small, two-rowed spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological studies and horticultural applications.
