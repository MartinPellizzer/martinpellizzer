Rubus guentheri f. turioniflorus

Rubus guentheri f. turioniflorus, commonly known as the Turioniflorus blackberry, is a rare and distinctive variant of the blackberry genus.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces edible, dark purple berries in the summer.

This botanical form is of interest to horticulturists and botanists due to its limited distribution and ornamental value.
