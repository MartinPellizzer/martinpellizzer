Ranunculus flagellifolius

Ranunculus flagellifolius, commonly known as the flagellifoliate buttercup, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
