Rubus obscurus var. obscurifrons

Rubus obscurus var. obscurifrons, commonly known as the dark-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and the species is notable for its aromatic foliage.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberry species.
