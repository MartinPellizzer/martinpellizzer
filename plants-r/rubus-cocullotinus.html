Rubus cocullotinus

Rubus cocullotinus, commonly known as the Andean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark berries that are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its ecological importance and economic value, it is often cultivated in local agriculture.
