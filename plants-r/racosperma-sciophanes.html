Racosperma sciophanes

Racosperma sciophanes, commonly known as the shade-loving acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into small, linear segments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
