Rubus angustisetus var. obovifrons

Rubus angustisetus var. obovifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves. The fruit is a dark purple to black berry that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
