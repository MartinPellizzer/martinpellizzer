Rubus gratiosus microgene ellipticifrons

Rubus gratiosus microgene ellipticifrons, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, elliptic-shaped leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its flowers are white with five petals and are borne in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible berries.
