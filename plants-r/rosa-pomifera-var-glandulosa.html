Rosa pomifera var. glandulosa

Rosa pomifera var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rose or the Brazilian rose, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces distinctive, fruit-like structures called rose hips, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with glandular hairs that give it a unique texture.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
