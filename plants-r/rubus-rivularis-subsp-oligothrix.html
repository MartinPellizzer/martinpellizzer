Rubus rivularis subsp. oligothrix

Rubus rivularis subsp.

oligothrix, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark red to black berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to other Rubus species.
