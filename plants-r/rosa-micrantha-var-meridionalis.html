Rosa micrantha var. meridionalis

Rosa micrantha var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern dwarf rose, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its stems are covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
