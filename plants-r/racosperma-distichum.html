Racosperma distichum

Racosperma distichum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for native pollinators.

Racosperma distichum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
