Rosa allodonta

Rosa allodonta, commonly known as the narrowleaf rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red hips containing seeds. Rosa allodonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the rose family, Rosaceae.
