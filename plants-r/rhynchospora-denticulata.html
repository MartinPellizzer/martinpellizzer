Rhynchospora denticulata

Rhynchospora denticulata, commonly known as the toothed spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant is native to various regions in North America, particularly in wetland and moist soil habitats. Rhynchospora denticulata has narrow, linear leaves with finely toothed margins, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
