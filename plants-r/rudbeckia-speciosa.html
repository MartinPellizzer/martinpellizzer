Rudbeckia speciosa

Rudbeckia speciosa, commonly known as the showy coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features bright yellow to orange ray flowers surrounding a central brown or black disk, which gives it a distinctive and showy appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Rudbeckia speciosa is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
