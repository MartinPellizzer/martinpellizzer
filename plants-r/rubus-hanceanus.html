Rubus hanceanus

Rubus hanceanus, commonly known as the Hance's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Rubus hanceanus is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
