Rosa transsilvanica var. biharensis

Rosa transsilvanica var. biharensis, commonly known as the Transylvanian rose, is a rare and endemic species native to Romania.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers with a subtle scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
