Rosa formosa

Rosa formosa, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers and is often found in dry, open habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments.

Rosa formosa is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping.
