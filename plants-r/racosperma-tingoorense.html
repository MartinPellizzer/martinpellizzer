Racosperma tingoorense

Racosperma tingoorense, commonly known as the Tingoora acacia, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its deep root system. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices for their medicinal properties.
