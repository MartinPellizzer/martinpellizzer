Rubus × kodruensis

Rubus × kodruensis, commonly known as the Kodruensian blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Rubus ulmifolius and Rubus chingii.

Its growth habit is shrub-like, with dense foliage and a preference for temperate climates.
