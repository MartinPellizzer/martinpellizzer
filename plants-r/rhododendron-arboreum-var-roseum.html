Rhododendron arboreum var. roseum

Rhododendron arboreum var. roseum, commonly known as the Rose-flowered大树杜鹃, is a variant of the evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, rose-pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
