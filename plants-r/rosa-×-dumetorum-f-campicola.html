Rosa × dumetorum f. campicola

Rosa × dumetorum f. campicola, commonly known as the creeping evergreen rose, is a hybrid species characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

It features glossy, dark green foliage that remains persistent throughout the year, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.
