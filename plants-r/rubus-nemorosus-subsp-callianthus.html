Rubus nemorosus subsp. callianthus

Rubus nemorosus subsp.

callianthus, commonly known as the sweet fern rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its arching, thornless stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink.

The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated margin and a hairy underside. The plant produces red or black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
