Rungia parviflora var. ciliata

Rungia parviflora var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy rungia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves with soft, hairy margins that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique, shade-tolerant plant.
