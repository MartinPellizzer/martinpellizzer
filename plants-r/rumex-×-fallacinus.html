Rumex × fallacinus

Rumex × fallacinus, commonly known as the broad-leaved dock, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rumex obtusifolius and Rumex acetosella.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils across temperate regions. The plant features large, broad leaves with a wavy margin and a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, erect spikes, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
