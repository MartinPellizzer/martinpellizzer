Rubus radula var. genuinus

Rubus radula var. genuinus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically clustered in dense, thorny inflorescences. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, silvery hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
