Rubus corylifolius var. serrulatus

Rubus corylifolius var. serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
