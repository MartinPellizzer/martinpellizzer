Rubus × patens var. incanus

Rubus × patens var. incanus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are typically small and tart, often used in local culinary traditions.

This species is native to parts of Europe and is adapted to a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
