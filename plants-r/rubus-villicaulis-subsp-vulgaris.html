Rubus villicaulis subsp. vulgaris

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes.

The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
