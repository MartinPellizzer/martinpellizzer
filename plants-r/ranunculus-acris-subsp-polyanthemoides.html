Ranunculus acris subsp. polyanthemoides

Ranunculus acris subsp.

polyanthemoides, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with numerous small petals arranged in a star-like pattern, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
