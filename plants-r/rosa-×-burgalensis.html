Rosa × burgalensis

Rosa × burgalensis, commonly known as the Burgalensis rose, is a hybrid species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the flowers are often used in ornamental gardening.

The Burgalensis rose is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
