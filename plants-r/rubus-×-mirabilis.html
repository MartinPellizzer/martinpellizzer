Rubus × mirabilis

Rubus × mirabilis, commonly known as the purple-leafed blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dark purple foliage and dark red to black berries. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in temperate climates. It produces edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis.
