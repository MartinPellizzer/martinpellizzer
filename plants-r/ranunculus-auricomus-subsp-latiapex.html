Ranunculus auricomus subsp. latiapex

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

latiapex, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of the Americas. It features a basal rosette of palmately lobed leaves, which are typically green and hairy.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, often with a distinctive golden hue. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaf blades compared to other members of the species.
