Ranunculus auricomus subsp. colobus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

colobus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short stem with a hairy surface and produces small, greenish-brown fruits that disperse by wind.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
