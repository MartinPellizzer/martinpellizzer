Rosa salictorum

Rosa salictorum, commonly known as the yellow shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Rosa salictorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
