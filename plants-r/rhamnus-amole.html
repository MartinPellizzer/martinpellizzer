Rhamnus amole

Rhamnus amole, commonly known as the African whitethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and smooth, often thorned stems. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Rhamnus amole is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
