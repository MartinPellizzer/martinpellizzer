Raddia sampaiana

Raddia sampaiana, commonly known as the Sampaiana cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and blooms from spring to early summer.

It is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
