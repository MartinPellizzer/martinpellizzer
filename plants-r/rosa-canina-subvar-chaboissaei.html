Rosa canina subvar. chaboissaei

Rosa canina subvar. chaboissaei, commonly known as the Barbary rose, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its hips, which are red-orange berries, are edible and contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
