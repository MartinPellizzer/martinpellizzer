Rubus ulmifolius var. weiheanus

Rubus ulmifolius var. weiheanus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are oval to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
