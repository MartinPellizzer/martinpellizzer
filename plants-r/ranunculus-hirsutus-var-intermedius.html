Ranunculus hirsutus var. intermedius

Ranunculus hirsutus var. intermedius, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its hairy stems and leaves are characteristic of the species, providing a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and early spring bloom.
