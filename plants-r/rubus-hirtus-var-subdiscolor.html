Rubus hirtus var. subdiscolor

Rubus hirtus var. subdiscolor, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a deep red. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
