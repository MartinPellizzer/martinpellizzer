Rhipsalis maricaensis

Rhipsalis maricaensis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its branching stems that are covered in small, leaf-like structures called phylloclades. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Rhipsalis maricaensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
