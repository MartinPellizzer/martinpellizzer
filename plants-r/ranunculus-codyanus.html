Ranunculus codyanus

Ranunculus codyanus, commonly known as Cody's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, which are characteristic of the genus Ranunculus.

The leaves are palmately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
