Rollinia cherimolioides

Rollinia cherimolioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherimoya, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces a fruit similar to a cherimoya, with a creamy, sweet flesh that is highly valued. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in subtropical regions.
