Rebutia glomerispina

Rebutia glomerispina, commonly known as the clustered spiny euphorbia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spine-tipped stems that form a dense, low-growing shrub. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by unique, bead-like fruits. Rebutia glomerispina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
