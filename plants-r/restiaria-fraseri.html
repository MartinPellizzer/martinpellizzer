Restiaria fraseri

Restiaria fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's restiaria, is a flowering plant native to the island of St. Helena in the South Atlantic Ocean.

It belongs to the family Restiariaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the island's unique climate. It is a rare and endemic species, with limited distribution and facing threats from habitat loss and invasive species.

Due to its restricted range and ecological significance, it is considered a priority for conservation efforts.
