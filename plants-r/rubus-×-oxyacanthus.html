Rubus × oxyacanthus

Rubus × oxyacanthus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildlife habitat.
