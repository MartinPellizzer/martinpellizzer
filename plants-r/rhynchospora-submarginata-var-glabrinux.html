Rhynchospora submarginata var. glabrinux

Rhynchospora submarginata var. glabrinux, commonly known as the glabrous margins sedge, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered inflorescences, and the species is distinguished by its glabrous (hairless) margins on the leaf blades.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
