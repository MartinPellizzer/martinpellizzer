Rosa palustris var. dasistema

Rosa palustris var. dasistema, commonly known as the swamp rose, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is typically found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a fuzzy texture.

This variety is well adapted to moist soils and plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.
