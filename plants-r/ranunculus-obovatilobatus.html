Ranunculus obovatilobatus

Ranunculus obovatilobatus, commonly known as the broad-lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Korea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, often with a waxy coating, and the flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
