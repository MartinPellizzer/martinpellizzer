Rorippa laurentii

Rorippa laurentii, commonly known as water spinach or Chinese watercress, is a semi-aquatic flowering plant native to Asia.

It thrives in wetland environments and has slender, branching stems that often grow submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant produces small, white flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, often floating on the water surface.

Rorippa laurentii is valued for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
