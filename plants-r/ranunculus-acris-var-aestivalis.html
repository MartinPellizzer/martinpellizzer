Ranunculus acris var. aestivalis

Ranunculus acris var. aestivalis, commonly known as the early meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its short bloom period.

The species is also notable for its ability to hybridize with other ranunculus species, leading to various cultivars in horticulture.
