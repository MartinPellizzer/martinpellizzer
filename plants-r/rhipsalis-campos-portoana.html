Rhipsalis campos-portoana

Rhipsalis campos-portoana, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, thin, branching stems that are often covered in a silvery, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically in winter. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well adapted to growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
