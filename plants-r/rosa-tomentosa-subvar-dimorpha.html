Rosa tomentosa subvar. dimorpha

Rosa tomentosa subvar. dimorpha, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rosa tomentosa subvar. dimorpha is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
