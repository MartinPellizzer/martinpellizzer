Rubus vestitus subsp. pyramidalis

Rubus vestitus subsp.

pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique adaptations to its alpine habitat.
