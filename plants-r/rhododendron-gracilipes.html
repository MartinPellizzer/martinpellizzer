Rhododendron gracilipes

Rhododendron gracilipes, commonly known as the slenderleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron gracilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
