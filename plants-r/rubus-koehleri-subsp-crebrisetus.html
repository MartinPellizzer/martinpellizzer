Rubus koehleri subsp. crebrisetus

Rubus koehleri subsp.

crebrisetus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it blooms in the spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
