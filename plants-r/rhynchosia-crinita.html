Rhynchosia crinita

Rhynchosia crinita, commonly known as the hairy bean or silky bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its soft, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Rhynchosia crinita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a common species in disturbed habitats and agricultural areas.
