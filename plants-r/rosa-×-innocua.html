Rosa × innocua

Rosa × innocua, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its ability to produce flowers continuously throughout the growing season, unlike many other rose varieties. The plant typically features thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a fragrant aroma.

Rosa × innocua is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in various climates.
