Rosa sikangensis f. pilosa

Rosa sikangensis f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa genus native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often sought after by horticulturists and botanists.
