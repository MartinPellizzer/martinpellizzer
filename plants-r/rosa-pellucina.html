Rosa pellucina

Rosa pellucina, commonly known as the evergreen shrub rose, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Rosa pellucina is a perennial plant that thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and wildlife habitat.
