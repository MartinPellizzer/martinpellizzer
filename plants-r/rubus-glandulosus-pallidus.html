Rubus glandulosus pallidus

Rubus glandulosus pallidus, commonly known as the pale-stemmed bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are aggregate berries, typically red or yellow, which are edible but often tart.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils, and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.
