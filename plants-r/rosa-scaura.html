Rosa scaura

Rosa scaura, commonly known as the swamp rose, is a deciduous shrub native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense colonies. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa scaura is adapted to moist soils and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
