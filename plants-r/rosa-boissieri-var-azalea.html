Rosa boissieri var. azalea

Rosa boissieri var. azalea, commonly known as the Azalea rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to produce fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and is well adapted to warm, sunny climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its hybrid origin, it may exhibit a range of morphological variations depending on the parent species.
