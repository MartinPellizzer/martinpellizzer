Rubus malacus

Rubus malacus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits, which are typically black or dark purple, are edible and often used in jams and desserts.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
