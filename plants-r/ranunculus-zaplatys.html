Ranunculus zaplatys

Ranunculus zaplatys, commonly known as the Zaplaty buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a basal rosette of leaves and produces solitary, cup-shaped blooms with numerous stamens. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive central pistil.

Ranunculus zaplatys prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
