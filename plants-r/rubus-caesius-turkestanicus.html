Rubus caesius turkestanicus

Rubus caesius turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan blackberry, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and has been used in traditional local cuisines.
