Rubus × stellulatus

Rubus × stellulatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
