Rosa virginiana var. vulgaris

Rosa virginiana var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with five to nine leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the summer, which are followed by orange-red hips. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
