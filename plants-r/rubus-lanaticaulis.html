Rubus lanaticaulis

Rubus lanaticaulis, commonly known as the coast raspberry.

It is a shrub native to the western coast of North America, particularly found in coastal regions of California and Oregon. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces small, edible berries.

It thrives in coastal environments with sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds.
