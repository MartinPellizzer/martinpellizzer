Rhynchosia rhodophylla

Rhynchosia rhodophylla, commonly known as the red-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used as a protein source in various cuisines.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important crop for sustainable agriculture.
