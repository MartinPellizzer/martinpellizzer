Ranunculus gramineus

Ranunculus gramineus, commonly known as the grass buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.

Ranunculus gramineus is often found in meadows and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soil.
