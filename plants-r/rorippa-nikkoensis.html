Rorippa nikkoensis

Rorippa nikkoensis, commonly known as Nikko watercress, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. This annual herb is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found near streams and in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and is used in traditional Japanese cuisine.
