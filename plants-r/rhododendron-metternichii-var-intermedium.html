Rhododendron metternichii var. intermedium

Rhododendron metternichii var. intermedium, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows at high elevations, often in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
