Rosa × cottetii var. zachariasiana

Rosa × cottetii var. zachariasiana, commonly known as the Zachariasian rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its semi-evergreen to evergreen foliage, which remains lush throughout much of the year. The plant produces fragrant, single or double flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is native to South Africa and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
