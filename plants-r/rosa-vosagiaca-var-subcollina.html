Rosa vosagiaca var. subcollina

Rosa vosagiaca var. subcollina, commonly known as the Subalpine Rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
