Rubus undulatus

Rubus undulatus, commonly known as the wavy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically serrated and have a wavy edge. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its fruit is used in jams and preserves, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
