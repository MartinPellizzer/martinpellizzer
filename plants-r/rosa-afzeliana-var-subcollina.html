Rosa afzeliana var. subcollina

Rosa afzeliana var. subcollina, commonly known as the subalpine rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, hairy rosehip.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
