Rhamnus decipiens

Rhamnus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant's fruit is a black, berry-like drupe that matures in the fall. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
