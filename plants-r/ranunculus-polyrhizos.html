Ranunculus polyrhizos

Ranunculus polyrhizos, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features finely divided, feather-like leaves that float on the water surface or extend underwater, aiding in photosynthesis. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in spring. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread across wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Ranunculus polyrhizos plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and stabilizing muddy substrates.
