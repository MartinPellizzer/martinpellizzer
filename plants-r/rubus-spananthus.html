Rubus spananthus

Rubus spananthus, commonly known as the Spanish blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rubus spananthus is often found in shrublands and along coastal areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
