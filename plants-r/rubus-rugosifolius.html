Rubus rugosifolius

Rubus rugosifolius, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
