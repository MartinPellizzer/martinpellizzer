Ranunculus prodigiosus

Ranunculus prodigiosus, commonly known as the red avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as tundra and wet meadows. The plant features a short, stout stem that bears a cluster of small, bright red flowers with five petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Ranunculus prodigiosus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
