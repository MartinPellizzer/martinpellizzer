Rhipidopteris peltata var. flabellata

Rhipidopteris peltata var. flabellata, commonly known as the fan-leaf fern, is a varietal form of the broad-leaf fern.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are often a dark green with a leathery texture, and they can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
