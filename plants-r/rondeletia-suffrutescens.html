Rondeletia suffrutescens

Rondeletia suffrutescens, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
