Rubus salzmannii

Rubus salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
