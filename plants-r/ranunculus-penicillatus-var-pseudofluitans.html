Ranunculus penicillatus var. pseudofluitans

Ranunculus penicillatus var. pseudofluitans, commonly known as the floating buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup species characterized by its unique aquatic adaptations.

This plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, with leaves that are often floating or submerged. Its flowers are usually yellow with 5 to 8 sepals and numerous stamens, resembling those of other ranunculus species. The leaves are usually divided into lobes and can be either floating or anchored to the substrate.

It is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along the margins of rivers.
