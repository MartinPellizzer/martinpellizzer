Rumputris fasciculata

Rumputris fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered grass, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in spikelets.

Rumputris fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
