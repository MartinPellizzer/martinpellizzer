Rhododendron bachii

Rhododendron bachii, commonly known as the Bachii rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forests or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its floral displays.

Rhododendron bachii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches.
