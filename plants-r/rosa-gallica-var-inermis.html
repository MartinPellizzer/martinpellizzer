Rosa gallica var. inermis

Rosa gallica var. inermis, commonly known as the French rose or the white gallica rose, is a species of shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is a deciduous plant that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with thornless stems.

Rosa gallica var. inermis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
