Ribes spicatum subsp. spicatum

Ribes spicatum subsp.

spicatum, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, tart berry that ripens in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
