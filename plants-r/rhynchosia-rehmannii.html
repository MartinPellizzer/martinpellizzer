Rhynchosia rehmannii

Rhynchosia rehmannii, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a white throat. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Rhynchosia rehmannii is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, sunny environments.
