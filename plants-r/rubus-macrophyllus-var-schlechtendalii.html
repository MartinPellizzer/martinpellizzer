Rubus macrophyllus var. schlechtendalii

Rubus macrophyllus var. schlechtendalii, commonly known as the large-leaf raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small, red drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
