Rhododendron formosum var. salicifolium

Rhododendron formosum var. salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features lanceolate, willow-like leaves that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
