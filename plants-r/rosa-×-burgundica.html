Rosa × burgundica

Rosa × burgundica, commonly known as the Burgundy rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a rich, velvety texture. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the blooms.

Rosa × burgundica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
