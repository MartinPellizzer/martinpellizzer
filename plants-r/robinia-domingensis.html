Robinia domingensis

Robinia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican acacia, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, appearing in clusters, and it produces long, flat pods that contain seeds.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forest ecosystems.
