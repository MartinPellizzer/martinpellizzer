Rosa umbelliflora var. minor

Rosa umbelliflora var. minor, commonly known as the lesser rose of Sharon, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, typically in late summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
