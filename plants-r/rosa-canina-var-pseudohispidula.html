Rosa canina var. pseudohispidula

Rosa canina var. pseudohispidula, commonly known as the hispid dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its prickly, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rosa genus. Its fruit, a red, hairy rose hip, is an important food source for birds and small mammals.

This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
