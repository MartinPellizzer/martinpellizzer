Ricinocarpus insulanus

Ricinocarpus insulanus, commonly known as the island senna, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated margins. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant has some ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
