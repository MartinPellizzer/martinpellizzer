Racosperma epedunculatum

Racosperma epedunculatum, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.
