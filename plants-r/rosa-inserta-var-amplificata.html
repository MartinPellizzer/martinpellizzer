Rosa inserta var. amplificata

Rosa inserta var. amplificata, commonly known as the broadleaf rock rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
