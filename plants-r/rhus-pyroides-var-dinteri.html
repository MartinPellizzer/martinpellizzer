Rhus pyroides var. dinteri

Rhus pyroides var. dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's sumac, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
