Ribes oxyacanthoides var. nevadense

Ribes oxyacanthoides var. nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly in Nevada and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of shrubs and small trees commonly found in temperate and arctic regions. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late spring, followed by edible berries that are dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
