Rosa berberifolia var. glabra

Rosa berberifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
