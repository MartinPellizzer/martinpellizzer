Rhododendron degronianum f. amagianum

Rhododendron degronianum f. amagianum, commonly known as the Amagian rhododendron, is a unique hybrid species native to the island of Amagai in Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in acidic soils with good drainage. Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and specialized collections.

Due to its limited natural habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
