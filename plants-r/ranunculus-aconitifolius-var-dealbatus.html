Ranunculus aconitifolius var. dealbatus

Ranunculus aconitifolius var. dealbatus, commonly known as the white-flowered cicutaria, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It blooms in early spring and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
