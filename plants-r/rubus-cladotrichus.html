Rubus cladotrichus

Rubus cladotrichus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, velvety leaves that have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries.
