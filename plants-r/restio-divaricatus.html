Restio divaricatus

Restio divaricatus, commonly known as the variable restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
