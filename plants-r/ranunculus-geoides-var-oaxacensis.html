Ranunculus geoides var. oaxacensis

Ranunculus geoides var. oaxacensis, commonly known as the Oaxacan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
