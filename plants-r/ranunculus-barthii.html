Ranunculus barthii

Ranunculus barthii, commonly known as Barth's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus barthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
