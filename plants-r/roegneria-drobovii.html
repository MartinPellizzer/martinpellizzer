Roegneria drobovii

Roegneria drobovii, commonly known as the Drobovii's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Roegneria drobovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
