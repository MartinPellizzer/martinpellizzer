Rhus typhina var. filicifolia

Rhus typhina var. filicifolia, commonly known as the silky dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are finely divided, giving it a fern-like appearance, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its showy, red berries that appear in the fall, providing food for wildlife. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in garden settings.
