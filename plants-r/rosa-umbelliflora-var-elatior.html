Rosa umbelliflora var. elatior

Rosa umbelliflora var. elatior, commonly known as the tall sweetbriar, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive umbel-like appearance. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces red, hairy fruits called hips. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
