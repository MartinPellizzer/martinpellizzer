Rumicastrum translucens

Rumicastrum translucens, commonly known as the translucent spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, translucent seed capsules. Its leaves are typically green and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
