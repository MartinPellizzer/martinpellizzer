Rubus avellanus

Rubus avellanus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, which resembles a blackberry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.

Rubus avellanus plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
