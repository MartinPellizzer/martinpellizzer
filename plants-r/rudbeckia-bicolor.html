Rudbeckia bicolor

Rudbeckia bicolor, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central dark brown disk, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces showy composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Rudbeckia bicolor is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
