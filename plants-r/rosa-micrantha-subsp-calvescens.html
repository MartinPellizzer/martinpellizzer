Rosa micrantha subsp. calvescens

Rosa micrantha subsp.

calvescens, commonly known as the California rose, is a shrub native to the western United States. It typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets. It is well adapted to dry climates and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

The fruit is a red, hairy rosehip that contains seeds and is an important food source for wildlife.
