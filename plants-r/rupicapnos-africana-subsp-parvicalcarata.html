Rupicapnos africana subsp. parvicalcarata

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

parvicalcarata, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
