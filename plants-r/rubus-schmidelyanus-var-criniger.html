Rubus schmidelyanus var. criniger

Rubus schmidelyanus var. criniger, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing at high altitudes with poor soil conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
