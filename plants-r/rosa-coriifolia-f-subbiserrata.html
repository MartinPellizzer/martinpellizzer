Rosa coriifolia f. subbiserrata

Rosa coriifolia f. subbiserrata, commonly known as the serrated leaf dog rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, with leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne in clusters at the base of the stems. The plant is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden borders or hedges.
