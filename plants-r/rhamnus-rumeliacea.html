Rhamnus rumeliacea

Rhamnus rumeliacea, commonly known as Turkish buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Rhamnus rumeliacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
