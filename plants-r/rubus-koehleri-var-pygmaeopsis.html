Rubus koehleri var. pygmaeopsis

Rubus koehleri var. pygmaeopsis, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
