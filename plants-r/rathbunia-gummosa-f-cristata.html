Rathbunia gummosa f. cristata

Rathbunia gummosa f. cristata, commonly known as the cristate gummosa, is a unique cultivar of the species Rathbunia gummosa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened growth habit and dense, compact foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This form is often cultivated for its unusual appearance and is prized in botanical gardens and collections.
