Rhodiola cretinii subsp. cretinii

Rhodiola cretinii subsp.

cretinii, commonly known as the Armenian rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia. It features a compact rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It is well adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in rocky or sandy soils. Rhodiola cretinii subsp.

cretinii is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
