Ranunculus flabellatus var. faurei

Ranunculus flabellatus var. faurei, commonly known as the Faure's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic compounds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
