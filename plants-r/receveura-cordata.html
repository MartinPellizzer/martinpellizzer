Receveura cordata

Receveura cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique leaf morphology and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
