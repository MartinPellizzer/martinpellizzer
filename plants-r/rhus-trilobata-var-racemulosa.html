Rhus trilobata var. racemulosa

Rhus trilobata var. racemulosa, commonly known as the racemose sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are trifoliate and often have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fuzzy berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The racemose sumac is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract wildlife.
