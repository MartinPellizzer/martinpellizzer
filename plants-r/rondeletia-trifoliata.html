Rondeletia trifoliata

Rondeletia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Rondeletia trifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
