Rhaponticum exaltatum

Rhaponticum exaltatum, commonly known as the Siberian chicory, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, bluish-green leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by small, cylindrical seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic environments.

Rhaponticum exaltatum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for its potential health benefits.
