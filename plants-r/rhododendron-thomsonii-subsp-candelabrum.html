Rhododendron thomsonii subsp. candelabrum

Rhododendron thomsonii subsp.

candelabrum, commonly known as the Candelabra Rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, multi-stemmed growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
