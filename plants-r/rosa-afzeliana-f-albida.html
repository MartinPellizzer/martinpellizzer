Rosa afzeliana f. albida

Rosa afzeliana f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its pale to white petals, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of its parent species. This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The plant produces fragrant flowers and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for container gardening.
