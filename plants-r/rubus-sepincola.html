Rubus sepincola

Rubus sepincola, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white or pale pink.

The berries are edible when ripe and are often used in culinary applications or for making jams and wines.
