Ribes bogotanum

Ribes bogotanum, commonly known as the Bogotan gooseberry, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and are often covered in fine hairs.

Ribes bogotanum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
