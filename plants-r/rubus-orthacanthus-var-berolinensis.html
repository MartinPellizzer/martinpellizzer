Rubus orthacanthus var. berolinensis

Rubus orthacanthus var. berolinensis, commonly known as the Berlin raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are red when mature, often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
