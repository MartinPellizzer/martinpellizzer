Rytigynia demeusei

Rytigynia demeusei, commonly known as the Brazilian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth vegetation.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
