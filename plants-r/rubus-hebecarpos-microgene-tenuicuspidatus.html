Rubus hebecarpos microgene tenuicuspidatus

Rubus hebecarpos microgene tenuicuspidatus, commonly known as the slender-lobed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and is often found growing along stream banks and in moist meadows.
