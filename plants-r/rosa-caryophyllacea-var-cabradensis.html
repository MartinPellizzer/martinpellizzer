Rosa caryophyllacea var. cabradensis

Rosa caryophyllacea var. cabradensis, commonly known as the Cabradense rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically oval in shape. The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a strong, spicy fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery.
