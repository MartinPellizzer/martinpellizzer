Racosperma aneurum var. microcarpum

Racosperma aneurum var. microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.
