Rubus chaerophyllus var. euchlooides

Rubus chaerophyllus var. euchlooides, commonly known as the yellow bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits are edible, though they are typically small and somewhat tart.

The species is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
