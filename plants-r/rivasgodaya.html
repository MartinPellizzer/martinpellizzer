Rivasgodaya

Rivasgodaya, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the lakebed while absorbing nutrients.

Rivasgodaya is revered in local cultures for its beauty and spiritual significance.
