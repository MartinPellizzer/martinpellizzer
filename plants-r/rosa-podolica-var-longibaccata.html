Rosa podolica var. longibaccata

Rosa podolica var. longibaccata, commonly known as the long-seed dog rose, is a variety of the dog rose species native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The leaves are pinnate, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant produces elongated, woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
