Rubus micans subsp. polycarpus

Rubus micans subsp.

polycarpus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark green leaves with a silvery underside.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, white to pale yellow achenes, which are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
