Rubus pallidus subsp. foliolatus

Rubus pallidus subsp.

foliolatus, commonly known as the pale bramble or foliolate raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
