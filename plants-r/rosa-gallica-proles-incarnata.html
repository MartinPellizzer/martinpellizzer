Rosa gallica proles incarnata

Rosa gallica proles incarnata, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of rose oil.

Its botanical name reflects its historical significance in perfumery and horticulture.
