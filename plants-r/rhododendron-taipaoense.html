Rhododendron taipaoense

Rhododendron taipaoense, commonly known as the Taipao rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to white, depending on the variety.

Rhododendron taipaoense is often found in forested areas, where it thrives in partial shade and cool, humid conditions.
