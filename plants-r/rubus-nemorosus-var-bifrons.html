Rubus nemorosus var. bifrons

Rubus nemorosus var. bifrons, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in culinary applications.

It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
