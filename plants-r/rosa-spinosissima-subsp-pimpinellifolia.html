Rosa spinosissima subsp. pimpinellifolia

Rosa spinosissima subsp.

pimpinellifolia, commonly known as the prickly rose or the mountain rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. This subspecies is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
