Rhynchospora rubra var. hirticeps

Rhynchospora rubra var. hirticeps, commonly known as the hairy-headed red sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features slender, triangular stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are grass-like with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pink to reddish in hue, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or boggy habitats.

Rhynchospora rubra var. hirticeps plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
