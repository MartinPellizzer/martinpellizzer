Rhododendron crassifolium var. pseudomurudense

Rhododendron crassifolium var. pseudomurudense, commonly known as the Murudan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded, humid environments. Its evergreen leaves are thick and dark green, providing year-round foliage.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
