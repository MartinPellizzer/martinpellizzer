Rosa elliptica subvar. gypsophila

Rosa elliptica subvar. gypsophila, commonly known as the silky rose or gypsophila rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its fluffy blooms and compact growth habit.
