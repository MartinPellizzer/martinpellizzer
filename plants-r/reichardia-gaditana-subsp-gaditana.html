Reichardia gaditana subsp. gaditana

Reichardia gaditana subsp.

gaditana, commonly known as the Mediterranean watercress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to that of other watercress species.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
