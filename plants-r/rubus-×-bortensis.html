Rubus × bortensis

Rubus × bortensis, commonly known as the Bortensian blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the crossbreeding of various Rubus species.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
