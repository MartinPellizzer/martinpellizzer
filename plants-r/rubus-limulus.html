Rubus limulus

Rubus limulus, commonly known as the Atlantic thimbleberry, is a species of brambles native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, sandy soils near the coast, often in dunes or salt marshes.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in early summer.
