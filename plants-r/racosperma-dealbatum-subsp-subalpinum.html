Racosperma dealbatum subsp. subalpinum

Racosperma dealbatum subsp.

subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine racosperma, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
