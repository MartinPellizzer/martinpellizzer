Rubus instans

Rubus instans, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Rubus instans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
