Rosa canina subsp. crataegina

Rosa canina subsp.

crataegina, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces red, fleshy fruits called pomes, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are oval and serrated, with a leathery texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
