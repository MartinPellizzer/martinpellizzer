Rhamnus quitensis

Rhamnus quitensis, commonly known as the Andean buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
