Rhynchosia clivorum subsp. gurueensis

Rhynchosia clivorum subsp.

gurueensis, commonly known as the Gurue climbing bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as forest edges and riverbanks, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
