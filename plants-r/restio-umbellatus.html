Restio umbellatus

Restio umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that resemble umbrellas, giving it its common name. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation.

Restio umbellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
