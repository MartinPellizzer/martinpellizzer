Rusbyella aurantiaca

Rusbyella aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a striking species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often surrounded by bright green bracts. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Rusbyella aurantiaca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
