Rubus × densiflorens

Rubus × densiflorens, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible and have a sweet-tart flavor, making them popular in both culinary and commercial applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
