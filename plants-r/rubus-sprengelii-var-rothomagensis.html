Rubus sprengelii var. rothomagensis

Rubus sprengelii var. rothomagensis, commonly known as the Rothomagensis raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which is a red, hairy berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.
