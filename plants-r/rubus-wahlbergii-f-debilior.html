Rubus wahlbergii f. debilior

Rubus wahlbergii f. debilior, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and edible fruits.
