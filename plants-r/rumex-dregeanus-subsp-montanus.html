Rumex dregeanus subsp. montanus

Rumex dregeanus subsp.

montanus, commonly known as the alpine sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in spring.

It is valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, slightly sour flavor.
