Ruta graveolens var. vulgaris

Ruta graveolens var. vulgaris, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, pinnately compound leaves with a strong, peppery odor due to the presence of volatile oils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into narrow, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb is well documented.
