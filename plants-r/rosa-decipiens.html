Rosa decipiens

Rosa decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant's leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers, which are typically pink or white, bloom in the spring and summer.

Rosa decipiens is known for its ability to thrive in woodland environments and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
