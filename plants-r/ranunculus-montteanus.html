Ranunculus montteanus

Ranunculus montteanus, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous sepals that resemble petals.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in its native ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.
