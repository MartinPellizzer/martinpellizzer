Ruscus terminalis

Ruscus terminalis, commonly known as butcher's broom, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves that form a compact, rounded shape. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are attractive to birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its unique, branching structure and waxy foliage.
