Ranunculus neglectus

Ranunculus neglectus, commonly known as the common buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are typically palmately lobed. The plant grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Ranunculus neglectus is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a wildflower.
