Rottboellia afraurita

Rottboellia afraurita, commonly known as African grass or false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Rottboellia afraurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
