Rubia urceolata

Rubia urceolata, commonly known as the urn-shaped woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive urn-shaped flower clusters, which are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia urceolata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
