Rosa adenosepala f. levilagensis

Rosa adenosepala f. levilagensis, commonly known as the silky rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa adenosepala.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a fine, downy texture, and the flowers bloom in clusters during the spring season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
