Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. imitans

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

imitans, commonly known as the mimic buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture, and it produces small, green berries that contain the seeds.

This species is notable for its ability to mimic the appearance of other ranunculus species, aiding in its survival through camouflage.
