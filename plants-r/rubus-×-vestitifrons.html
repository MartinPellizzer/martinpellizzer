Rubus × vestitifrons

Rubus × vestitifrons, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chlorocarpus (raspberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The species exhibits thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruit is a aggregate of drupes, resembling both blackberries and raspberries.

This hybrid is valued for its hardy nature and potential in horticulture and ecological restoration.
