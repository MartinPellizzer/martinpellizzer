Ranunculus ololeucos proles acutilobus

Ranunculus ololeucos proles acutilobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it produces a milky sap when damaged.

This species is often found in Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
