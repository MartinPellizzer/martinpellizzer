Rubus × hermannii

Rubus × hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann’s blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. Its berries are dark purple to black in color and are often used in jams and wines.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Rubus allegheniensis and Rubus hirtellus.
