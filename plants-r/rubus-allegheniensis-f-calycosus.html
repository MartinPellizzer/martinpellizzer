Rubus allegheniensis f. calycosus

Rubus allegheniensis f. calycosus, commonly known as the Allegheny blackberry, is a subspecies of the Allegheny raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in ecological studies and local horticulture.
