Rosa affinior

Rosa affinior, commonly known as the close relative of the common rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red-orange hips. Rosa affinior is characterized by its robust root system and adaptability to harsh, arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in horticultural breeding programs.
