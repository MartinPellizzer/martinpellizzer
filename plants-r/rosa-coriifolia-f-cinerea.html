Rosa coriifolia f. cinerea

Rosa coriifolia f. cinerea, commonly known as the grayleaf shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pale grayish-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in early summer, which are followed by red-orange hips. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.

This cultivar is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
