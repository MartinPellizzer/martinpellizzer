Rosa × reversa var. grandifolia

Rosa × reversa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the double-flowered rose, is a hybrid species characterized by its large, full blooms.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its showy, layered petals that give it a lush, almost peony-like appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in floral arrangements.
