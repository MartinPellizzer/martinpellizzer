Rubus lingua

Rubus lingua, commonly known as the "tongue raspberry," is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
