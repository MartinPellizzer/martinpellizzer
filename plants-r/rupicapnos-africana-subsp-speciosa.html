Rupicapnos africana subsp. speciosa

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the Natal flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in winter, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
