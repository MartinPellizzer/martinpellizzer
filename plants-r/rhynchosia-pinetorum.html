Rhynchosia pinetorum

Rhynchosia pinetorum, commonly known as the pineapple bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant bears fruit that is a pod containing several seeds, often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Rhynchosia pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
