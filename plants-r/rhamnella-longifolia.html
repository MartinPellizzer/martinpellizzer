Rhamnella longifolia

Rhamnella longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf rhamnus, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, fleshy berries.

Rhamnella longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
