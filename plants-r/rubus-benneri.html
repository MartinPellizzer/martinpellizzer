Rubus benneri

Rubus benneri, commonly known as the Benner's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and Florida. The plant is a shrub that produces small, dark berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
