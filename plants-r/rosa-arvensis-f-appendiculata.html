Rosa arvensis f. appendiculata

Rosa arvensis f. appendiculata, commonly known as the field rose or creeping rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit with thorny stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
