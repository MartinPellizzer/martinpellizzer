Rubus × brevidens

Rubus × brevidens, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its compact growth habit and small, thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries in certain regions.
