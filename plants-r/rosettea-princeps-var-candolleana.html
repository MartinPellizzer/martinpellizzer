Rosettea princeps var. candolleana

Rosettea princeps var. candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana Rosette, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

This variety is characterized by its dense, compact rosette formation of large, glossy, dark green leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, creating a visually appealing and unique appearance. It produces striking, feather-like inflorescences that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a prized specimen among collectors and enthusiasts of rare palms.
