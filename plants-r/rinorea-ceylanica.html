Rinorea ceylanica

Rinorea ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
