Ranunculus auricomus subsp. duciformis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

duciformis, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 10 petals, which are typically borne on long, hairy stems.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
