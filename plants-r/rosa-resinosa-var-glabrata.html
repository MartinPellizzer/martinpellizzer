Rosa resinosa var. glabrata

Rosa resinosa var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth prickly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers, typically in clusters, during the spring and summer months. It is native to the southern United States and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
