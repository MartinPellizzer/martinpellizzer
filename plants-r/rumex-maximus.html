Rumex maximus

Rumex maximus, commonly known as sour dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically arranged in a terminal panicle. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

The leaves and roots are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
