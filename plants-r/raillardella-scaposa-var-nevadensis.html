Raillardella scaposa var. nevadensis

Raillardella scaposa var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada raillardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
