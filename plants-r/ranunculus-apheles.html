Ranunculus apheles

Ranunculus apheles, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and sometimes pink. The plant features finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
