Racosperma latisepalum

Racosperma latisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal cycad, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
