Reseda lutea subsp. lutea

Reseda lutea subsp.

lutea, commonly known as yellowrocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. Reseda lutea subsp.

lutea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
