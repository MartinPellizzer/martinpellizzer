Robinia hispida var. boyntonii

Robinia hispida var. boyntonii, commonly known as the Boynton's false acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its bark is thick and fissured, often appearing rough and flaky.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
