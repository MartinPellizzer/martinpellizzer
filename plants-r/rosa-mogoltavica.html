Rosa mogoltavica

Rosa mogoltavica, commonly known as the Mogoltavica rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa mogoltavica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique horticultural traits.
