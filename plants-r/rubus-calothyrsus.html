Rubus calothyrsus

Rubus calothyrsus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn red when mature, often used in local foraging and culinary practices. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing similarities with other brambles like raspberries and blackberries.
