Rubus squarrosus

Rubus squarrosus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub native to North America, particularly found in the eastern and central regions of the United States. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. Its fruit, known as a raspberry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in thickets and along streambanks.
