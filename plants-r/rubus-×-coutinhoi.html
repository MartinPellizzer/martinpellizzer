Rubus × coutinhoi

Rubus × coutinhoi, commonly known as the Coutinho blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces dark purple to black berries, which are typically larger and more flavorful than those of its parent species. The plant is native to South America, specifically Brazil, and is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and culinary uses.
