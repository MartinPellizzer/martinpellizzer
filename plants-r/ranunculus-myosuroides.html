Ranunculus myosuroides

Ranunculus myosuroides, commonly known as the mouse-ear buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped or kidney-shaped, with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit and is often found in temperate regions.
