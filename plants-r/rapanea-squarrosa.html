Rapanea squarrosa

Rapanea squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa evergreen, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically growing up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, known as a pruine. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
