Rubus halsteadensis

Rubus halsteadensis, commonly known as Halstead's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
