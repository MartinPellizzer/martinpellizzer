Racosperma linarioides

Racosperma linarioides, commonly known as the slender senna, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces yellow flowers in spring. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma linarioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
