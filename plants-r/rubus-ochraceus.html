Rubus ochraceus

Rubus ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow-bracted raspberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly the western United States and Canada. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
