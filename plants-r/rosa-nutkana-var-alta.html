Rosa nutkana var. alta

Rosa nutkana var. alta, commonly known as the Alaska rose, is a shrub native to the subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late summer, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and is often used in ornamental gardening in cooler regions.
