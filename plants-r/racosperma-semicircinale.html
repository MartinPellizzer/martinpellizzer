Racosperma semicircinale

Racosperma semicircinale, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
