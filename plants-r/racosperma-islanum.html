Racosperma islanum

Racosperma islanum, commonly known as the Island Acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
