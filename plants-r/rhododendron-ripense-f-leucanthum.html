Rhododendron ripense f. leucanthum

Rhododendron ripense f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered alpine rhododendron, is a unique horticultural form of the species Rhododendron ripense.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers, which contrast with its dark green, leathery foliage. Native to the alpine regions of eastern Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.

The plant is relatively cold-hardy, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
