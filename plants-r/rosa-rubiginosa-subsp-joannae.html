Rosa rubiginosa subsp. joannae

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

joannae, commonly known as the Joanne's shrub rose, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region. It is a compact, deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that matures in the fall, containing seeds within a fleshy outer layer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in moderate climates.
