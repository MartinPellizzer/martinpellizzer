Rubus jennisonii

Rubus jennisonii, commonly known as Jennison's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces edible berries that are small and tart.

This species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its adaptation to dry, rocky habitats.
