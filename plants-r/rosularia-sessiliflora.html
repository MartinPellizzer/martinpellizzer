Rosularia sessiliflora

Rosularia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower rosebush, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Rosularia sessiliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in rocky or arid environments.
