Ribes grossularia f. domesticum

Ribes grossularia f. domesticum, commonly known as the European gooseberry, is a cultivated form of the wild gooseberry.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, tart berries in late summer. The plant belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

The fruit is typically yellow or red, and it is used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
