Rottboellia exaltata var. appendiculata

Rottboellia exaltata var. appendiculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf horse-tail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often mistaken for parts of a horse's tail, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive sheathing base.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
