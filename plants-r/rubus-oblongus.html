Rubus oblongus

Rubus oblongus, commonly known as the long-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known fruit-bearing plants.
