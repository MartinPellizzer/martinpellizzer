Rebutia haagei

Rebutia haagei, commonly known as the rock cactus or desert rose, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features a short, thick stem with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Rebutia haagei is well adapted to harsh desert environments, with a deep root system and water-storing tissues.
