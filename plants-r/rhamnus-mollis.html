Rhamnus mollis

Rhamnus mollis, commonly known as the softleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are clustered and yellowish-green, followed by small, black berries that are edible for some wildlife.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
