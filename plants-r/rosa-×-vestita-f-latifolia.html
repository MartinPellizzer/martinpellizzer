Rosa × vestita f. latifolia

Rosa × vestita f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. This plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and lush foliage.
