Rhaphidophora philippinensis

Rhaphidophora philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its upright, fibrous rhizomes that are used as a starchy food source. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces striking, spatulate-shaped flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its traditional use and potential for sustainable agriculture in the region.
