Rosa canina var. montivaga

Rosa canina var. montivaga, commonly known as the Alpine dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose that thrives in mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the typical Rosa canina. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange, containing seeds used in traditional herbal remedies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
