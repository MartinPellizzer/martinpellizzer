Rubus silesiacus var. bracteolentus

Rubus silesiacus var. bracteolentus, commonly known as the Silesian blackberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible berries.
