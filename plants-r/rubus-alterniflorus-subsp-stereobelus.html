Rubus alterniflorus subsp. stereobelus

Rubus alterniflorus subsp.

stereobelus, commonly known as the Alaska blackberry, is a shrub native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and often have a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The subspecies is notable for its hardiness and adaptability to cold climates.
