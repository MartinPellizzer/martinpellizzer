Restio trisepalus

Restio trisepalus, commonly known as the three-veined restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a perennial, herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. Restio trisepalus typically grows in coastal or sandy environments, preferring well-drained soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
