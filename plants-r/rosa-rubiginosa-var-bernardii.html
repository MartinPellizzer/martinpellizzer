Rosa rubiginosa var. bernardii

Rosa rubiginosa var. bernardii, commonly known as the Bernard's brambles, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces oval, red-brown fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
