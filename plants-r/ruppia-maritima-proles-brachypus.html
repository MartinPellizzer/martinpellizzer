Ruppia maritima proles brachypus

Ruppia maritima proles brachypus, commonly known as the dwarf seagrass, is a marine flowering plant found in coastal salt marshes and brackish waters.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are adapted to withstand tidal forces and saltwater conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, followed by the development of tiny, floating seeds. Its root system helps stabilize sediment and improve water quality in its habitat.

This species plays a vital role in supporting aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various marine organisms.
