Rhipsalis spinescens

Rhipsalis spinescens, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that produce clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is known for its ability to bloom during the holiday season, making it a beloved ornamental plant.

Rhipsalis spinescens is relatively low maintenance, making it an ideal choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
