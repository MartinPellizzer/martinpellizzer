Rorippa apetala

Rorippa apetala, commonly known as the leafy water-cress, is a small flowering plant native to moist environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often near streams and ponds. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to other watercress species.

It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
