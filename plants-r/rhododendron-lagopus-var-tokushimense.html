Rhododendron lagopus var. tokushimense

Rhododendron lagopus var. tokushimense, commonly known as the Tokushima Azalea, is a rare variant of the azalea family native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions with acidic soil. Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture, though it is relatively uncommon in cultivation.

Due to its limited distribution, conservation efforts are important to preserve this unique botanical specimen.
