Ranunculus occidentalis var. montanensis

Ranunculus occidentalis var. montanensis, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
