Rhododendron crebreflorum

Rhododendron crebreflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in humid, cool climates. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. Its botanical characteristics include a rugged, woody stem and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron crebreflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
