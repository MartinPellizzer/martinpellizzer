Rosa carolina pimpinellaefolia

Rosa carolina pimpinellaefolia, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fuzzy rose hip, which contains the seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
