Rinorea scandens

Rinorea scandens, commonly known as the climbing rinorea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a woody vine characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rinorea scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
