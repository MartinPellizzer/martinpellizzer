Rivea bona-nox

Rivea bona-nox, commonly known as the night bloomer, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and its unique habit of opening its large, fragrant white flowers at night. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Rivea bona-nox thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
