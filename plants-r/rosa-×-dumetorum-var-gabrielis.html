Rosa × dumetorum var. gabrielis

Rosa × dumetorum var. gabrielis, commonly known as the Gabriel's rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with dark green, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

The variety is named in honor of the botanist Gabriel, reflecting its historical significance in botanical classification.
