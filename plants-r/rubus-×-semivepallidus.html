Rubus × semivepallidus

Rubus × semivepallidus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, oval-shaped berries that range in color from red to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
