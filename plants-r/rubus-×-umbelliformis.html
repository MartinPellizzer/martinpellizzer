Rubus × umbelliformis

Rubus × umbelliformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to hybridize with other Rubus species, leading to a variety of cultivars with differing fruit characteristics.
