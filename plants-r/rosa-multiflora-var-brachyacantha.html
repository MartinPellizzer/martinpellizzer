Rosa multiflora var. brachyacantha

Rosa multiflora var. brachyacantha, commonly known as the Chinese rose or everblooming rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers range in color from white to pink and red. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable to various soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
