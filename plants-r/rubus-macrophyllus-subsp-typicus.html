Rubus macrophyllus subsp. typicus

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
