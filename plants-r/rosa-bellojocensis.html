Rosa bellojocensis

Rosa bellojocensis, commonly known as the Bellojocensis rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features serrated, dark green leaves and produces thorny stems typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
