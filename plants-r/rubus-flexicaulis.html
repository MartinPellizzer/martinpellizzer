Rubus flexicaulis

Rubus flexicaulis, commonly known as the flexible blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which help it climb and spread across the ground. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other members of the Rubus genus.
