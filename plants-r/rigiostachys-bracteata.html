Rigiostachys bracteata

Rigiostachys bracteata, commonly known as the bracted spirea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Rigiostachys bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
