Rubus hypoleucus microgene continens

Rubus hypoleucus microgene continens, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species native to the western regions of North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
