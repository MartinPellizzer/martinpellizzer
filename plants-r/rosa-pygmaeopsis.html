Rosa pygmaeopsis

Rosa pygmaeopsis, commonly known as the dwarf rose or miniature rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, compact stems and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white down.

Rosa pygmaeopsis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and containers.
