Ranunculus melzeri

Ranunculus melzeri, commonly known as the Melzer's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a basal rosette of leaves and produces flowers with five to nine sepals that resemble petals. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a central cluster of stamens and a single pistil.

Ranunculus melzeri thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas and along stream banks.
