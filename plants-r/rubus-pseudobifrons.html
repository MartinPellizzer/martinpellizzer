Rubus pseudobifrons

Rubus pseudobifrons, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
