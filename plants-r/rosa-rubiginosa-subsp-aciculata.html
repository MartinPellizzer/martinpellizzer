Rosa rubiginosa subsp. aciculata

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

aciculata, commonly known as the acicular rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and clusters of fragrant pink to red flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, red or orange hips that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
