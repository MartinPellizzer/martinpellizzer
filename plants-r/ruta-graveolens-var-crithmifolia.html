Ruta graveolens var. crithmifolia

Ruta graveolens var. crithmifolia, commonly known as sea fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves and seeds are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their distinctive flavor and potential health benefits.

However, it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities, requiring caution in its use.
