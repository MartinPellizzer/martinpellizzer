Rosa karjaginii

Rosa karjaginii, commonly known as the Karjagin rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Russia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in a fine layer of soft hairs.

Rosa karjaginii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
