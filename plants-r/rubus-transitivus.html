Rubus transitivus

Rubus transitivus, commonly known as the transitional blackberry, is a species of bramble native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which support its climbing structure. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, oval drupelets that mature to a deep, almost black color. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubus transitivus is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
