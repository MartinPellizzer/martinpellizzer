Rubus obvius

Rubus obvius, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins.

Rubus obvius thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
