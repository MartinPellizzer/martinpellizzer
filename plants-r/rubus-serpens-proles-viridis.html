Rubus serpens proles viridis

Rubus serpens proles viridis, commonly known as the green-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically green when ripe. Its fruits are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
