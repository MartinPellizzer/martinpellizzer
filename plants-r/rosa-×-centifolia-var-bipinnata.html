Rosa × centifolia var. bipinnata

Rosa × centifolia var. bipinnata, commonly known as the double-flowered cabbage rose, is a hybrid species of the rose family.

It is characterized by its full, layered blooms that resemble cabbage in appearance, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with arching canes and dark green, glossy foliage. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is often used in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
