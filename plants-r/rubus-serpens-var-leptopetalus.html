Rubus serpens var. leptopetalus

Rubus serpens var. leptopetalus, commonly known as the slender-petaled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a trailing shrub that produces small, edible berries, typically black when mature. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is often used in jams and pies, similar to other Rubus species.

The variant is distinguished by its slender petals and smaller fruit size compared to other blackberry varieties.
