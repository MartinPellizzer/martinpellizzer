Rhamnidium reticulatum

Rhamnidium reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate rhamnus, is a shrub native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive leaf structures and often thorny growth habits. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves with a reticulated venation pattern that gives it its scientific name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing fruits that are small berries.

Rhamnidium reticulatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
