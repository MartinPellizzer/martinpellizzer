Racosperma amycticum

Racosperma amycticum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
