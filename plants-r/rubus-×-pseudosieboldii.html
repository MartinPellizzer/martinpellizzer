Rubus × pseudosieboldii

Rubus × pseudosieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is native to East Asia, particularly Japan and China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces showy white or pink flowers in spring.

The berries are often used in culinary applications, though they can be quite tart and astringent.
