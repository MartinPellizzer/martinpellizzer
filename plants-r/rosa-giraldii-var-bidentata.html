Rosa giraldii var. bidentata

Rosa giraldii var. bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed Rosa, is a variant of the Rosa giraldii species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange to red hips. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
