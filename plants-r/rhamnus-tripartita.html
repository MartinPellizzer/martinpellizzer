Rhamnus tripartita

Rhamnus tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
