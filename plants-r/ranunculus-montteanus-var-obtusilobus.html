Ranunculus montteanus var. obtusilobus

Ranunculus montteanus var. obtusilobus, commonly known as the obtuse-lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow petals and a hairy, herbaceous stem. The leaves are deeply lobed, typically with a rounded or obtuse tip, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, early spring blooms.
