Ribes × disputabile

Ribes × disputabile, commonly known as the hybrid currant, is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Ribes nigrum (black currant) and Ribes rubrum (red currant), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The shrub typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and features compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters, while the berries are usually red or black, depending on the parent species.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
