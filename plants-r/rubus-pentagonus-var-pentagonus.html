Rubus pentagonus var. pentagonus

Rubus pentagonus var. pentagonus, commonly known as the five-angled blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five leaflets that are ovate to elliptical in shape.

This variety is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
