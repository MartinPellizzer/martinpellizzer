Rosa coriifolia var. bovernierana

Rosa coriifolia var. bovernierana, commonly known as the Bovenier's shrub rose, is a variant of the silky rose, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorned stems and serrated, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
