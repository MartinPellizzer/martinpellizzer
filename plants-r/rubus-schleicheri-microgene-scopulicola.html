Rubus schleicheri microgene scopulicola

Rubus schleicheri microgene scopulicola, commonly known as the rock raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, prickly stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, high-altitude environments. Its fruits, which are small and red, are edible and have a tart flavor.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its rugged habitat.
