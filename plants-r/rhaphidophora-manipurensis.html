Rhaphidophora manipurensis

Rhaphidophora manipurensis, commonly known as the Manipur arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the eastern Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a waxy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Its rhizomes are valued for their starchy content, making it an important food source in certain regions.
