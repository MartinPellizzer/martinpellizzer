Ranunculus fontanus

Ranunculus fontanus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. It has finely divided, palmately arranged leaves and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ranunculus fontanus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
