Rubus × reissii

Rubus × reissii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and is well adapted to coastal climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
