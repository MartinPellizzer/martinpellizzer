Rosa pouzinii subsp. jacetana

Rosa pouzinii subsp.

jacetana, commonly known as the Jacetan rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The flowers are usually pink to red in color, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
