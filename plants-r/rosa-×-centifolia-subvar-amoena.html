Rosa × centifolia subvar. amoena

Rosa × centifolia subvar. amoena, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its highly fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are primarily cultivated for their essential oils, which are used in perfumery and cosmetics.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
