Rhynchospora megaplumosa

Rhynchospora megaplumosa, commonly known as the large-fruited tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Its distinctive feature is the large, fleshy, purple-tinged berries that develop from the female flowers.

Rhynchospora megaplumosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
