Rubus × fortispinus

Rubus × fortispinus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its growth habit includes both upright and trailing forms, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its complex inflorescence and the presence of persistent calyx structures around the fruit.
