Rapuntium gramineum

Rapuntium gramineum, commonly known as the common rapunculus, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its seeds are small and oval, often dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant is sometimes considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
