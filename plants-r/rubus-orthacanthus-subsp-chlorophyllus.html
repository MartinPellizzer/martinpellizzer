Rubus orthacanthus subsp. chlorophyllus

Rubus orthacanthus subsp.

chlorophyllus, commonly known as the greenleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red or black color.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves contain high levels of chlorophyll, contributing to its distinctive green appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
