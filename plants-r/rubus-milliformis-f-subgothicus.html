Rubus milliformis f. subgothicus

Rubus milliformis f. subgothicus, commonly known as the subalpine blackberry, is a subspecies of the widespread Rubus milliformis.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark, almost black berries. This plant typically thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, where it adapts to cold and high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are usually compound, with a serrated edge, and the flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels.

The subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and its role in supporting local wildlife in mountainous regions.
