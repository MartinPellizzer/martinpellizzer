Ribes viridiminus

Ribes viridiminus, commonly known as the greenleaf currant, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Ribes.

It is native to western North America, particularly found in the mountainous regions of California, Oregon, and Washington. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically a deep green color. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red berries in the summer.

Ribes viridiminus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fruit-bearing capabilities.
