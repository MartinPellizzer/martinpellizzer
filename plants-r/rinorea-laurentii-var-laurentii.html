Rinorea laurentii var. laurentii

Rinorea laurentii var. laurentii, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy.

Rinorea laurentii var. laurentii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
