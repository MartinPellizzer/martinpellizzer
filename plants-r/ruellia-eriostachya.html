Ruellia eriostachya

Ruellia eriostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and clustered in spikes, usually displaying shades of blue or purple.

Ruellia eriostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
