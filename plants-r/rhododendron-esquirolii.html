Rhododendron esquirolii

Rhododendron esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form thickets.

Rhododendron esquirolii is also notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
