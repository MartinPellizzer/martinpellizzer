Ranunculus auricomus subsp. imparidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

imparidens, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five broad, petal-like sepals and a distinctive, hairy stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
