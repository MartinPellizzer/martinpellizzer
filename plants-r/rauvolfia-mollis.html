Rauvolfia mollis

Rauvolfia mollis, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its twining stems that climb other vegetation. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are often fragrant.

Rauvolfia mollis is cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and unique growth habit.
