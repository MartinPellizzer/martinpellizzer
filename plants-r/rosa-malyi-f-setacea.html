Rosa malyi f. setacea

Rosa malyi f. setacea, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa malyi species, characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and blooms.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cooler climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often appearing in early summer. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative shrub.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a favored choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance yet visually appealing plant.
