Rubus × seminepalensis

Rubus × seminepalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate to cold climates, often found in forested areas and along streambanks. The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, persistent hairs.

This species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
