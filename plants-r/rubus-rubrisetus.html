Rubus rubrisetus

Rubus rubrisetus, commonly known as the red-stemmed bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its distinctive red-colored stems. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the summer. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus rubrisetus is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
