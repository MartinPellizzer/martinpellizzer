Rhamnus alaternus f. argenteovariegata

Rhamnus alaternus f. argenteovariegata, commonly known as the silver-variegated buckthorn, is a decorative shrub characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are broadly ovate, with a distinctive variegation of white and green patterns, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
