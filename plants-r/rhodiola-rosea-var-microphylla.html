Rhodiola rosea var. microphylla

Rhodiola rosea var. microphylla, commonly known as the "lesser rhodiola," is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is part of the Crassulaceae family and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Its roots contain various bioactive compounds, including rosavins and salidrosides, which are believed to have adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.
