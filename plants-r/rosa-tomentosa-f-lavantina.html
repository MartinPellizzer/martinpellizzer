Rosa tomentosa f. lavantina

Rosa tomentosa f. lavantina, commonly known as the Lavantina rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant produces small, red-orange hips that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and blooms, making it a popular choice for garden use.

It is relatively low maintenance and resistant to many common garden pests and diseases.
