Rhododendron arboreum var. peramoenum

Rhododendron arboreum var. peramoenum, commonly known as the Himalayan laurel.

It is a flowering shrub native to the Himalayas, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, moist climates with partial shade. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink and bloom in late spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
