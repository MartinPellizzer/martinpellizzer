Rhododendron seranicum subsp. sparsihirtus

Rhododendron seranicum subsp.

sparsihirtus, commonly known as the sparse-hairy rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy leaves and stems, which provide a unique texture. It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
