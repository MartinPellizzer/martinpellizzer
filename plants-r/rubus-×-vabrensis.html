Rubus × vabrensis

Rubus × vabrensis, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique fruit characteristics. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its fruits are usually larger and sweeter than those of its parent species, making it a popular choice for cultivation.

The plant exhibits thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus.
