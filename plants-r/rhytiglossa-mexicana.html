Rhytiglossa mexicana

Rhytiglossa mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to Mexico, characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Rhytiglossa mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
