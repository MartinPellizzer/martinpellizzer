Rosa duplicata

Rosa duplicata, commonly known as the double-flowered rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces fragrant, double flowers with tightly packed petals that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and rockeries.

Rosa duplicata is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
