Rosa coriifolia subsp. turbatula

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

turbatula, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
