Rubus hirtus var. microthamnus

Rubus hirtus var. microthamnus, commonly known as the dwarf bramble, is a thorny shrub native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually red when ripe. It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and is often found in shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
