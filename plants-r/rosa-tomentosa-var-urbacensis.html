Rosa tomentosa var. urbacensis

Rosa tomentosa var. urbacensis, commonly known as the Urbačina rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and has thorny stems, typical of many rose species. Its leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets covered in a fine, grayish down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
