Rosa assyensis

Rosa assyensis, commonly known as the Assyrian rose, is a species of shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and produces hips that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Rosa assyensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
