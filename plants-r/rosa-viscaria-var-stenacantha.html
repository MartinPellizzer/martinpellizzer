Rosa viscaria var. stenacantha

Rosa viscaria var. stenacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are pinnately compound, with narrow, serrated leaflets. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
