Rubus selmeri var. meionacanthus

Rubus selmeri var. meionacanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
