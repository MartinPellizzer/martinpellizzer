Ranunculus rumelicus

Ranunculus rumelicus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ranunculus rumelicus is often found in temperate climates and is a common sight in early spring.
