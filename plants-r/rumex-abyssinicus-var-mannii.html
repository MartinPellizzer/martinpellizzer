Rumex abyssinicus var. mannii

Rumex abyssinicus var. mannii, commonly known as Abyssinian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with a wavy margin and a reddish underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are edible and have a sour taste, making it a traditional food source in some regions.
