Rubus × tenuipes

Rubus × tenuipes, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It is native to regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile, and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
