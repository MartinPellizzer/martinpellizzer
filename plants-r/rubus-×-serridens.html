Rubus × serridens

Rubus × serridens, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (the red raspberry). This plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically featuring three leaflets. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, leading to the development of berries that range in color from red to black.

The fruit is edible, often used in culinary applications, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and agricultural significance.
