Rubus × paratropus

Rubus × paratropus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets, which are often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various regions.
