Rumex acetosa var. auriculatus

Rumex acetosa var. auriculatus, commonly known as sourgrass or yellow dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant has upright, branching stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish at the base. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in loose spikes, and it produces elongated, ribbed fruits.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
