Rhagadiolus lampsanifolius

Rhagadiolus lampsanifolius, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and have a waxy texture, which helps in retaining moisture.

Rhagadiolus lampsanifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is prized in horticultural gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
