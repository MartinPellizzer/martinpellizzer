Robinia kelseyi

Robinia kelseyi, commonly known as the Kelseyi false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its flowers are typically pale yellow or white. It produces legume-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woods and along stream banks.
