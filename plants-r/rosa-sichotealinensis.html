Rosa sichotealinensis

Rosa sichotealinensis, commonly known as the Far Eastern rose, is a species native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorned stems and large, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange-red hips. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 elliptical leaflets, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Rosa sichotealinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
