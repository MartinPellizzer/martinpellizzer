Ranunculus bongardii var. douglasii

Ranunculus bongardii var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals and a distinctive central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply divided into lobes, and the stems are usually hairy.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
