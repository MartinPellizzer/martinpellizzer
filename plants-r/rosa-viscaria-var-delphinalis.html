Rosa viscaria var. delphinalis

Rosa viscaria var. delphinalis, commonly known as the Delphinalis rose, is a compact shrub with dense, thornless growth.

It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are oval in shape and emit a mild, sweet scent. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in partial shade to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
