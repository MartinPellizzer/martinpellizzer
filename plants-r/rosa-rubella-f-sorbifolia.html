Rosa rubella f. sorbifolia

Rosa rubella f. sorbifolia, commonly known as the sorbifolia form of the pink rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This form exhibits leaves that resemble those of the sorbus tree, featuring a serrated edge and a somewhat lobed shape. The plant typically produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a compact shrub, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The botanical interest of this form lies in its unique leaf morphology, which sets it apart from other rosa species.
