Rubus tomentosus f. fragariifolius

Rubus tomentosus f. fragariifolius, commonly known as the fragrant-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features large, broad leaves with a velvety, hairy surface that gives it a soft texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its fragrant leaves and edible berries make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
