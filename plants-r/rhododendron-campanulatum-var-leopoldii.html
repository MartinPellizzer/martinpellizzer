Rhododendron campanulatum var. leopoldii

Rhododendron campanulatum var. leopoldii, commonly known as the Leopold's bellflower rhododendron, is a unique variant of the campanulatum species.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant is native to specific regions in the western United States, particularly in California and Nevada. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
