Rubus questieri

Rubus questieri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

Rubus questieri is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in local cuisine and traditional herbal practices.
