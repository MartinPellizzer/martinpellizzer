Rhynchosia mantaroensis

Rhynchosia mantaroensis, commonly known as the Mantaro bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces edible pods that contain small, round seeds used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed or cultivated areas.

Due to its nutritional value, it is also cultivated for its protein-rich seeds.
