Rorippa floridana

Rorippa floridana, commonly known as Florida watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are typically smooth or slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in loose racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist, sandy soils.

The species is closely related to other members of the Brassicaceae family, sharing similar floral structures and chemical compositions.
