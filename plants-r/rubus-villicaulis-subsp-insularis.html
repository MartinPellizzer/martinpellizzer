Rubus villicaulis subsp. insularis

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island blackberry, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its island habitat.
