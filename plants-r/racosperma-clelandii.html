Racosperma clelandii

Racosperma clelandii, commonly known as the Cleland's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Racosperma clelandii is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility.
