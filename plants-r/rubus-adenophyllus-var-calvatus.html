Rubus adenophyllus var. calvatus

Rubus adenophyllus var. calvatus, commonly known as the smooth-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The leaves are typically smooth and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences. This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with ecological and horticultural significance in its native range.
