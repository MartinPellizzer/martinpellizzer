Rupicapnos africana subsp. oranensis

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

oranensis, commonly known as the Orange River rupicapnos, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
