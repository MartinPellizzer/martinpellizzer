Rosa stylosa f. leucochroa

Rosa stylosa f. leucochroa, commonly known as the white-stemmed wild rose, is a hybrid form of the species Rosa stylosa.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale green stems, which contrast with its typical pink or red flowers. The plant is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. It produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
