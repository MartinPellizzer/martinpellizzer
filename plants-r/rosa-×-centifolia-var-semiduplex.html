Rosa × centifolia var. semiduplex

Rosa × centifolia var. semiduplex, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are widely used in perfumery and for making rose water due to their high essential oil content.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
