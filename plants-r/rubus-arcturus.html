Rubus arcturus

Rubus arcturus, commonly known as the Alaska raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a hardy berry plant.
