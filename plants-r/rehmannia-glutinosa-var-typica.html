Rehmannia glutinosa var. typica

Rehmannia glutinosa var. typica, commonly known as Chinese foxglove, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional Chinese medicine, valued for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
