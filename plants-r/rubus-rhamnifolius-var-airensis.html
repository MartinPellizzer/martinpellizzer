Rubus rhamnifolius var. airensis

Rubus rhamnifolius var. airensis, commonly known as the Airen raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
