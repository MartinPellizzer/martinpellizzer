Rinorea scortechinii

Rinorea scortechinii, commonly known as the red-flowered rinorea, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Rinorea scortechinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
