Rubus macrostachys var. adenanthus

Rubus macrostachys var. adenanthus, commonly known as the Australian blackberry, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark purple to black berries. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its edible fruits. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its flavorful berries and ornamental value.
