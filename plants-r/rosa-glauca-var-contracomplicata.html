Rosa glauca var. contracomplicata

Rosa glauca var. contracomplicata, commonly known as the blue rose.

This variant is notable for its striking deep blue petals, a coloration achieved through the presence of delphinidin, a type of anthocyanin pigment. It is a hybrid form of Rosa glauca, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, fragrant flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique color and ornamental appeal.
