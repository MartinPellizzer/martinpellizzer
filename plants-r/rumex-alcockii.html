Rumex alcockii

Rumex alcockii, commonly known as Alcock's sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.
