Rubus schleicheri microgene elongatipes

Rubus schleicheri microgene elongatipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries.

The species is native to parts of Asia and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
