Rapanea glaucorubens

Rapanea glaucorubens, commonly known as the red-leaved holly, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-red leaves, which are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant foliage.
