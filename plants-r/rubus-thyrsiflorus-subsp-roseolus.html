Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp. roseolus

Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp.

roseolus, commonly known as the pink raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, reddish stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically pink to red in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it blooms with white to pink flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
