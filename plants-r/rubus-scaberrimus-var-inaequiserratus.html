Rubus scaberrimus var. inaequiserratus

Rubus scaberrimus var. inaequiserratus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a variant of the bramble family with distinctive botanical features.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and serrated leaves that vary in tooth size, giving it its specific epithet. The leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is native to parts of North America.
