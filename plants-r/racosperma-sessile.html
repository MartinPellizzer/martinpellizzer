Racosperma sessile

Racosperma sessile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

The seeds are often used in traditional Indigenous practices and can be processed into a nutritious food source.
