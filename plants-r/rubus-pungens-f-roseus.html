Rubus pungens f. roseus

Rubus pungens f. roseus, commonly known as the pink-stemmed blackberry.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, white flowers that give way to dark purple berries. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in native plant gardens.
