Rubus viburnifolius var. apetalus

Rubus viburnifolius var. apetalus, commonly known as the barren rose or apetalous blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, usually sterile flowers, which is why it is referred to as "barren" in some contexts.

Despite its lack of showy blooms, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.
