Rubia deserticola

Rubia deserticola, commonly known as desert madder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its stems and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional dyeing practices.

Rubia deserticola is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
