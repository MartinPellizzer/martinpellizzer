Rhododendron cladotrichum

Rhododendron cladotrichum, commonly known as the woolly rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forest understories at high elevations. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact form.

The botanical features of R. cladotrichum make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its native habitat.
