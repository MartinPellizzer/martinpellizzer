Ranunculus acutidentatus

Ranunculus acutidentatus, commonly known as the sharp-toothed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered with fine hairs, while the stems are usually hairy and glandular.

The species is noted for its early spring blooming period and is often found in regions with cooler climates.
