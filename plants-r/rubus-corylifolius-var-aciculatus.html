Rubus corylifolius var. aciculatus

Rubus corylifolius var. aciculatus, commonly known as the needle-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, acuminate leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
