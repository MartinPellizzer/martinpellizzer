Rubus neumanii

Rubus neumanii, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of white flowers that develop into edible blackberries. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species such as raspberries and dewberries. Its fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely appreciated for their flavor and nutritional value.

The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils in full sun.
