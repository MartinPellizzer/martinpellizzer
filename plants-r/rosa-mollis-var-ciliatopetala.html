Rosa mollis var. ciliatopetala

Rosa mollis var. ciliatopetala, commonly known as the soft rose with ciliated petals, is a cultivar of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers with finely ciliated petal edges. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and produces pink to red blooms in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
