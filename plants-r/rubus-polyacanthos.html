Rubus polyacanthos

Rubus polyacanthos, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The thimbleberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
