Rubus amblypetalus

Rubus amblypetalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
