Rumex conglomeratus var. nigricans

Rumex conglomeratus var. nigricans, commonly known as black-clustered sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, arrow-shaped leaves with a reddish underside, and its clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It produces oblong, reddish-brown seed capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
