Rubus idaeus var. leucocarpus

Rubus idaeus var. leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-fruited raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry plant characterized by its white or pale-colored fruits.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the berries, while less commonly consumed than the red variety, are used in some culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is valued for its unique fruit color and potential horticultural interest.
