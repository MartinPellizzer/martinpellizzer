Rudbeckia serotina f. pleniflora

Rudbeckia serotina f. pleniflora, commonly known as the Late Black-Eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features large, showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a dark brown central disk, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rudbeckia serotina f. pleniflora thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
