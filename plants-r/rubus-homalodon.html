Rubus homalodon

Rubus homalodon, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn deep red when ripe, often containing multiple tiny seeds. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, and is valued for its edible berries.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
