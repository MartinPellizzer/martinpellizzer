Rosa acicularis var. cucurbiformis

Rosa acicularis var. cucurbiformis, commonly known as the "melon-shaped rose," is a unique variant of the thorned rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved leaves that resemble the shape of a melon. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is usually covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental and hardy nature.
