Rubus × pseudohakonensis

Rubus × pseudohakonensis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus gebleri (the raspberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are pink to red, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that is usually dark purple to black when mature.

This hybrid is valued for its flavorful berries, which are used in culinary applications and jams.
