Raukaua simplex var. sinclairii

Raukaua simplex var. sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclair's raukaua, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often found in forest margins and rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
