Rubus glaucellus var. laxus

Rubus glaucellus var. laxus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rubus genus.
