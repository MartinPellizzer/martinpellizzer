Rumex pulcher var. anodontus

Rumex pulcher var. anodontus, commonly known as the beautiful sorrel, is a variant of the sorrel genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a reddish tinge and serrated edges, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Polygonaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf morphology and its role in supporting local pollinators.
