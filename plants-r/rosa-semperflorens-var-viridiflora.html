Rosa semperflorens var. viridiflora

Rosa semperflorens var. viridiflora, commonly known as the everblooming green-flowered rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the unusual flower color. This variety is prized for its continuous blooming throughout the growing season.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
