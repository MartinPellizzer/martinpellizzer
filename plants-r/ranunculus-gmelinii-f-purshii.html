Ranunculus gmelinii f. purshii

Ranunculus gmelinii f. purshii, commonly known as the Pursh's buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in alpine and subalpine regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it is often found in mountainous areas of western North America.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in ecological systems as a nectar source for pollinators.
