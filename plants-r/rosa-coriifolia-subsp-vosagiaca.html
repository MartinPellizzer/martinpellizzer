Rosa coriifolia subsp. vosagiaca

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

vosagiaca, commonly known as the Vosagian shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in early summer. Its botanical features include hairy stems and stipules that are often lobed or divided.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
