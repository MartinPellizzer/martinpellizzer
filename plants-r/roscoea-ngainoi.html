Roscoea ngainoi

Roscoea ngainoi, commonly known as the golden roscosa, is a striking member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red inflorescence that resembles a torch, with elongated, strap-shaped bracts. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and is typically found in moist, shaded forest understories. Its leaves are large, lanceolate, and often have a waxy texture.

Roscoea ngainoi is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
