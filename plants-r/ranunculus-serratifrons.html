Ranunculus serratifrons

Ranunculus serratifrons, commonly known as the serrated leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this species thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant has a short stem and produces a single flower per stem, which blooms in the spring.

Its foliage is often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance that sets it apart from other members of the buttercup family.
