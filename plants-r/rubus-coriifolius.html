Rubus coriifolius

Rubus coriifolius, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupelets that ripen in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
