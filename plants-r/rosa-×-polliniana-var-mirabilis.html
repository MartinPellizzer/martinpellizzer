Rosa × polliniana var. mirabilis

Rosa × polliniana var. mirabilis, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often forming shrubs or small trees. The plant produces fragrant, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom continuously throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in many modern rose hybrids.
