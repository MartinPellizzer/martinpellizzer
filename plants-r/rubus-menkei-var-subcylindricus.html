Rubus menkei var. subcylindricus

Rubus menkei var. subcylindricus, commonly known as the narrow-cylindrical blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often described as having a tart and slightly astringent flavor.
