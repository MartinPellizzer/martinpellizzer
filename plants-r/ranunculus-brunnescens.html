Ranunculus brunnescens

Ranunculus brunnescens, commonly known as the brown buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers with five petals, often adorned with a central tuft of stamens. It produces small, hairy fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus brunnescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
