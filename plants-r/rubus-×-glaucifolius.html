Rubus × glaucifolius

Rubus × glaucifolius, commonly known as the blueleaf raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, thriving in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature, similar to other raspberries. Its leaves are oval to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a waxy, bluish coating that gives it its common name.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
