Ripartia

Ripartia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Ripartia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
