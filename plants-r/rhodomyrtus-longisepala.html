Rhodomyrtus longisepala

Rhodomyrtus longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal rhodomyrtus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Rhodomyrtus longisepala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
