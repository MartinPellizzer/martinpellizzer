Rollinia dolabripetala

Rollinia dolabripetala, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds, making it an important food source for wildlife.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
