Rosa canina var. nyaradyana

Rosa canina var. nyaradyana, commonly known as the Hungarian rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits called hip fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
