Ranunculus lingua var. glaber

Ranunculus lingua var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-tongued buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive dark center. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically lobed or divided, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streams.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a garden ornamental and a potential weed.
