Roldana aliena

Roldana aliena, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet, fragrant aroma. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy green surface. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
