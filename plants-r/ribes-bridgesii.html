Ribes bridgesii

Ribes bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridge's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has alternate, simple, and ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

Ribes bridgesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a food source for wildlife.
