Rosa heliophila var. foliosissima

Rosa heliophila var. foliosissima, commonly known as the sun-loving rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with numerous leaflets, which are typically serrated and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
