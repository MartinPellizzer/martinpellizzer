Rosa × schottiana var. typica

Rosa × schottiana var. typica, commonly known as the Schott's rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa pendulina.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and Japan. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it has a robust root system that makes it adaptable to a variety of soil types.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
