Rubus hartmanii

Rubus hartmanii, commonly known as Hartman's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and Florida. The plant is a perennial shrub that produces clusters of small, dark red berries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.
