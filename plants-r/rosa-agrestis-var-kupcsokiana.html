Rosa agrestis var. kupcsokiana

Rosa agrestis var. kupcsokiana, commonly known as the Kupcsokiana wild rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, fragrant clusters of pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural applications.
