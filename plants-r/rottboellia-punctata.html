Rottboellia punctata

Rottboellia punctata, commonly known as the spurred grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pale-green flowers arranged in spikelets.

Rottboellia punctata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
