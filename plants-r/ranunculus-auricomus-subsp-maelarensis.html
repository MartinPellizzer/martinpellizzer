Ranunculus auricomus subsp. maelarensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

maelarensis, commonly known as the Maela buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves.

Native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Myanmar, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has rounded, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, petal-less flowers with numerous stamens.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
