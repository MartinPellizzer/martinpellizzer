Rhus virens var. virens

Rhus virens var. virens, commonly known as the greenash or smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by clusters of red, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
