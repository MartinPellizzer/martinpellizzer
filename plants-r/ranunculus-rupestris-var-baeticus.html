Ranunculus rupestris var. baeticus

Ranunculus rupestris var. baeticus, commonly known as the Iberian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb with delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This variety is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers.
