Rosa canina f. lessinensis

Rosa canina f. lessinensis, commonly known as the Lessinensis rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant is native to the Dolomites in northern Italy, where it thrives in alpine environments. It has small, oval leaves with serrated edges and produces red, hairy fruits known as hips.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
