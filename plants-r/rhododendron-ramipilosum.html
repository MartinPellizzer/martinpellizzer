Rhododendron ramipilosum

Rhododendron ramipilosum, commonly known as the silky-haired rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
