Rubus infestus var. okerensis

Rubus infestus var. okerensis, commonly known as the Okanese blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
