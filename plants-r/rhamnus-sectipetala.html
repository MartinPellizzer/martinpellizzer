Rhamnus sectipetala

Rhamnus sectipetala, commonly known as the winged buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged leaves that provide a unique visual feature. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
