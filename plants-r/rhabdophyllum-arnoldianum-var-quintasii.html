Rhabdophyllum arnoldianum var. quintasii

Rhabdophyllum arnoldianum var. quintasii, commonly known as the Quintas's rhabdophyllum, is a rare and unique bromeliad native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that form a rosette structure, which helps in capturing and retaining moisture. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
