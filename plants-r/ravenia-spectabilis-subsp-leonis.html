Ravenia spectabilis subsp. leonis

Ravenia spectabilis subsp.

leonis, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a species native to South Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking, showy inflorescences.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
