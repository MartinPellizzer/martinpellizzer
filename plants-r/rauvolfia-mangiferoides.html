Rauvolfia mangiferoides

Rauvolfia mangiferoides, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Rauvolfia mangiferoides is notable for its traditional medicinal uses, particularly in Ayurvedic medicine.
