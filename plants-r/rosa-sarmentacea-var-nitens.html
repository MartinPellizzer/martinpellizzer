Rosa sarmentacea var. nitens

Rosa sarmentacea var. nitens, commonly known as the glabrous climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thornless, sprawling growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, woody stems that can trail along the ground or climb supports, making it suitable for trellises or hedging.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
