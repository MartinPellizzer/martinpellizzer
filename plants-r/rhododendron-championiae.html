Rhododendron championiae

Rhododendron championiae, commonly known as the Champion's rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained acidic soil. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron championiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that replicate its native environment.
