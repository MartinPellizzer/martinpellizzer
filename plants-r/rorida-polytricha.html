Rorida polytricha

Rorida polytricha, commonly known as the mossy liverwort, is a non-vascular plant belonging to the family Ptilidiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, moss-like structures. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, called gametophytes, are essential for its life cycle and are often concealed beneath the surface.

Rorida polytricha plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
