Rosa labradora

Rosa labradora, commonly known as the Labrador tea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a serrated edge, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Rosa labradora is also known for its aromatic foliage, which has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
