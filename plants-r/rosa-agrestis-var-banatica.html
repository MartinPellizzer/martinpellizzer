Rosa agrestis var. banatica

Rosa agrestis var. banatica, commonly known as the Banat rose, is a wild species native to the Balkan region.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically growing in open, sunny habitats. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include simple, oval-shaped leaves and a woody structure typical of many rose species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
