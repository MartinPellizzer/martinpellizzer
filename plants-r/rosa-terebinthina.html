Rosa terebinthina

Rosa terebinthina, commonly known as the Turkish rose or Damascene rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and serrated, with a slightly hairy surface.

Rosa terebinthina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the extraction of essential oils.
