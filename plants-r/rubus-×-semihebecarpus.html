Rubus × semihebecarpus

Rubus × semihebecarpus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus fruticosus, resulting in a species with unique botanical characteristics. It typically produces small, dark purple to black berries that are highly aromatic and flavorful. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
