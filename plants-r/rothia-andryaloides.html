Rothia andryaloides

Rothia andryaloides, commonly known as the Andean snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its elegant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil.

Rothia andryaloides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
