Rubus × acroleucophorus

Rubus × acroleucophorus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically tart and edible. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Rubus ursinus and Rubus 1aniceps, resulting in a unique combination of morphology and ecology.
