Rapanea verruculosa

Rapanea verruculosa, commonly known as the rough-leafed evergreen or the African ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by dark, glossy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests.

Its wood is dense and valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities.
