Rorippa calycina var. columbiae

Rorippa calycina var. columbiae, commonly known as Columbia water-cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, rocky environments. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, producing a peppery flavor when the leaves are eaten.

This variety is valued for its culinary use and is often found in mountain streams and wet meadows.
