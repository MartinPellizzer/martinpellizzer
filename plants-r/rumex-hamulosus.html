Rumex hamulosus

Rumex hamulosus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a wavy margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Rumex hamulosus thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
