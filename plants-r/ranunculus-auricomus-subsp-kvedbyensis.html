Ranunculus auricomus subsp. kvedbyensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

kvedbyensis, commonly known as the Kvedby buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, saucer-shaped flowers with prominent central sepals that resemble petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodland edges, and streambanks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
