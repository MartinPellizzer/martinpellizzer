Rubus peruvianus

Rubus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries that range in color from red to black. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as forests and mountain slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a tart, acidic flavor similar to other raspberries.
