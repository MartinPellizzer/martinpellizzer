Rubus tasmanicus

Rubus tasmanicus, commonly known as the Tasmanian bramble, is a shrub native to Tasmania, Australia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or black berries in autumn. Its berries are edible but often have a tart flavor, and the plant is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus tasmanicus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stability.
