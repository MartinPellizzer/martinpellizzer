Ranunculus fallax subsp. validus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

validus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and adapted to cold, alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and alpine tundra.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
