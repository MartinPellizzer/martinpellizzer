Rubus intercalaris

Rubus intercalaris, commonly known as the blackberry cane or bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as berries, which are edible and have a distinctive flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rubus intercalaris is also known for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
