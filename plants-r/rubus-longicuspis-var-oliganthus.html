Rubus longicuspis var. oliganthus

Rubus longicuspis var. oliganthus, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. The fruit is a red, aggregate berry that ripens in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
