Romulea bulbocodium subsp. columnae

Romulea bulbocodium subsp.

columnae, commonly known as the columnar romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant's bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive dry periods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique floral structure and seasonal blooming habit.
