Rosa canina f. comatistyla

Rosa canina f. comatistyla, commonly known as the hairy rose or bristly dogwood rose, is a hybrid form of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruits are red, hairy berries that develop after the flowering season.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
