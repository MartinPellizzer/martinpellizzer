Rubus fruticosus var. macrostemon

Rubus fruticosus var. macrostemon, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun to partial shade.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for birds and insects.
