Ranunculus dasycarpus

Ranunculus dasycarpus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically hairy and lobed, with a distinctive yellow flower that has five to eight petals. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that are clustered in a head-like structure, giving it its specific epithet "dasycarpus," meaning "hairy fruit.". It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
