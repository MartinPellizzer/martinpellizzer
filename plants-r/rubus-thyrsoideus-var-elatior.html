Rubus thyrsoideus var. elatior

Rubus thyrsoideus var. elatior, commonly known as the tall blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its taller growth habit and larger, more robust fruit. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are white and clustered, followed by dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its edible fruit and ornamental qualities.
