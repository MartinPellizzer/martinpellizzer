Ranunculus scutatus

Ranunculus scutatus, commonly known as the shieldleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with yellow petals and a prominent central stigma, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive, ephemeral blooms.
