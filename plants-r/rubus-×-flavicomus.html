Rubus × flavicomus

Rubus × flavicomus, commonly known as the golden-leafed raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its distinctive yellowish foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
