Rubus guntheri var. coriaceus

Rubus guntheri var. coriaceus, commonly known as the roughberry blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and prickly, biennial growth habit. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorns and a complex floral structure typical of the Rubus genus.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and potential for cultivation in arid environments.
