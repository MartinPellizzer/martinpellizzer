Racosperma neurophyllum subsp. erugatum

Racosperma neurophyllum subsp.

erugatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is typically found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
