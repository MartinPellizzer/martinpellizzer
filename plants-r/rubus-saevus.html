Rubus saevus

Rubus saevus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. Rubus saevus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
