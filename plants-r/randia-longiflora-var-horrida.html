Randia longiflora var. horrida

Randia longiflora var. horrida, commonly known as the hairy randia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy foliage and stems, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with serrated edges.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.
