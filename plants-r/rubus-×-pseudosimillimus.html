Rubus × pseudosimillimus

Rubus × pseudosimillimus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a hybrid plant species native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, moist environments.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
