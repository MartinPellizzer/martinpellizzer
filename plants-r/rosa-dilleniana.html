Rosa dilleniana

Rosa dilleniana, commonly known as the Dillenius rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Rosa dilleniana is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
