Ranunculus nemorosus proles mixtus

Ranunculus nemorosus proles mixtus, commonly known as the woodland buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to ten petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, upright stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in natural habitats.
