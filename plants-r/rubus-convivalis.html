Rubus convivalis

Rubus convivalis, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus convivalis plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
