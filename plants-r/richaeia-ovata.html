Richaeia ovata

Richaeia ovata, commonly known as the oval leaf richaeia, is a rare and delicate fern native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright fronds with elongated, ovate-shaped pinnae that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds. Richaeia ovata thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
