Rosa ingressa

Rosa ingressa, commonly known as the creeping or climbing rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in woodlands and hedgerows. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

Rosa ingressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
