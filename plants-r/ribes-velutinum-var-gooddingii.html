Ribes velutinum var. gooddingii

Ribes velutinum var. gooddingii, commonly known as Goodding's gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, edible berries that are highly valued for their flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Ribes.
