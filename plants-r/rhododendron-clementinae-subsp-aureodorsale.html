Rhododendron clementinae subsp. aureodorsale

Rhododendron clementinae subsp.

aureodorsale, commonly known as the golden-back rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a dense, compact growth habit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
