Rubus granulatus var. rhenanus

Rubus granulatus var. rhenanus, commonly known as the rough-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, granular texture on the fruit surface and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
