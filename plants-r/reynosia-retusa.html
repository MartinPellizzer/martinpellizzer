Reynosia retusa

Reynosia retusa, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Reynosia retusa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
