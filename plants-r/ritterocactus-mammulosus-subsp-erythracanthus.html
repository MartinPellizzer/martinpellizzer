Ritterocactus mammulosus subsp. erythracanthus

Ritterocactus mammulosus subsp.

erythracanthus, commonly known as the red-armed cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, woolly spines, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
