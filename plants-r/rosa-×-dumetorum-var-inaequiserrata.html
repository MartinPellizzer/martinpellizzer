Rosa × dumetorum var. inaequiserrata

Rosa × dumetorum var. inaequiserrata, commonly known as the uneven-toothed dog玫瑰, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with irregularly serrated margins. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for garden borders or hedges.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both its aesthetic appeal and its ecological value.
