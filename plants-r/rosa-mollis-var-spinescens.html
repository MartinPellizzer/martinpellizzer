Rosa mollis var. spinescens

Rosa mollis var. spinescens, commonly known as the soft rose or spiny soft rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of South America.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has soft, hairy stems and leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are often followed by small, red or orange fruits called hips, which are edible and used in traditional medicine.

Rosa mollis var. spinescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
