Rosa corymbulosa

Rosa corymbulosa, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne in corymbs, which are flat-topped or rounded clusters. Rosa corymbulosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
