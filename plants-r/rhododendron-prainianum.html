Rhododendron prainianum

Rhododendron prainianum, commonly known as the Prain's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and greyish.

Rhododendron prainianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
