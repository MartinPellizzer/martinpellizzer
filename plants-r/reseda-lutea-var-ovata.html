Reseda lutea var. ovata

Reseda lutea var. ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf madder, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in loose clusters. Its root system is known for containing alkaloids, which have been historically used in dyeing textiles.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
