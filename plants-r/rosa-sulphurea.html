Rosa sulphurea

Rosa sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Russia, and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are typically produced in late summer to early autumn. Rosa sulphurea is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring thorny stems and serrated leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
