Rhododendron longiflorum var. longiflorum

Rhododendron longiflorum var. longiflorum, commonly known as the purple leaved rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to bronze foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and showy floral display.
