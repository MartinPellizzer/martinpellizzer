Rosa ferruginea subsp. glabrata

Rosa ferruginea subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the rusty rose, is a shrub-like species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in pink to red hues.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that are often covered in a fine, rusty-colored bloom. This subspecies is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in the study of Rosa taxonomy and horticulture.
