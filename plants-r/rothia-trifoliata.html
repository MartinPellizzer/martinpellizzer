Rothia trifoliata

Rothia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved rothia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring. Rothia trifoliata thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
