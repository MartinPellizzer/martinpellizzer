Rapanea ambigua

Rapanea ambigua, commonly known as the Madagascar boxwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, berry-like fruits.

Rapanea ambigua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
