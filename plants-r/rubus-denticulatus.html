Rubus denticulatus

Rubus denticulatus, commonly known as the prickly rose or sand rose, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red, hairy berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is commonly found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
