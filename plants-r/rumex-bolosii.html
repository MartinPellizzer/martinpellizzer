Rumex bolosii

Rumex bolosii, commonly known as Bolosii sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other species known for their tart, edible leaves.
