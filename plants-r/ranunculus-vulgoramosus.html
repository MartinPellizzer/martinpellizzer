Ranunculus vulgoramosus

Ranunculus vulgoramosus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or orange flowers with prominent sepals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five to seven sepals that resemble petals.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
