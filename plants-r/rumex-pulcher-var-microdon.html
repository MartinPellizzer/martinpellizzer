Rumex pulcher var. microdon

Rumex pulcher var. microdon, commonly known as the dwarf dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves are edible and have been used historically in traditional medicine and cooking.
