Raphanus chanetii

Raphanus chanetii, commonly known as the mountain radish, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its upright, branching stem and lobed leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in racemes, typical of the family.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its edible root, though it is less commonly consumed than other members of its family.
