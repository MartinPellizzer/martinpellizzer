Ranunculus zuccarinii

Ranunculus zuccarinii, commonly known as the yellow marsh cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, featuring showy yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus zuccarinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
