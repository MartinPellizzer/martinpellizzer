Ranunculus indivisus

Ranunculus indivisus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with five petals. It produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus indivisus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
