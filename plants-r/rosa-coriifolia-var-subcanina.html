Rosa coriifolia var. subcanina

Rosa coriifolia var. subcanina, commonly known as the Chinese pink rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems with thorny branches and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, pink flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with oval, serrated leaflets that turn reddish in autumn. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and compact growth habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
