Ribes orientale var. heterotrichum

Ribes orientale var. heterotrichum, commonly known as the eastern gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
