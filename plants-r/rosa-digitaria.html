Rosa digitaria

Rosa digitaria, commonly known as the swamp rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant features slender, often reddish stems that grow from rhizomes, and its leaves are alternate, with pinnate divisions and serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
