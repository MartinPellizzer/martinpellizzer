Ryania speciosa var. tomentella

Ryania speciosa var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly ryania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stem and dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
