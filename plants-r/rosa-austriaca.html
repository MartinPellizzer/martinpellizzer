Rosa austriaca

Rosa austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include five-petaled flowers and an upright growth habit.

Rosa austriaca is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
