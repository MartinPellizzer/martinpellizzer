Rulingia borealis

Rulingia borealis, commonly known as the northern rulingia, is a rare evergreen shrub native to the high altitudes of the northern regions.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a waxy texture, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Rulingia borealis is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and is valued for its resilience in alpine ecosystems.
