Rosa glauca var. slawodolica

Rosa glauca var. slawodolica, commonly known as the Polish blue rose, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender-pink flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant is native to Poland and parts of Eastern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. It has a compact shrub form with thorny stems and dark green foliage.

Due to its rarity and unique coloration, it is highly valued in horticulture and botanical collections.
