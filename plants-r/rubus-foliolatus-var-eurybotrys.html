Rubus foliolatus var. eurybotrys

Rubus foliolatus var. eurybotrys, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in local ecosystems.
