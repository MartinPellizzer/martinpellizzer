Rubus madagascarius

Rubus madagascarius, commonly known as the Madagascan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has potential for cultivation due to its unique flavor and nutritional value.
