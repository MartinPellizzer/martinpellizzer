Rubus candicans var. thyrsanthus

Rubus candicans var. thyrsanthus, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
