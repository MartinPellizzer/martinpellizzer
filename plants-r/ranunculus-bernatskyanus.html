Ranunculus bernatskyanus

Ranunculus bernatskyanus, commonly known as the Karachay ranunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves and produces flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
