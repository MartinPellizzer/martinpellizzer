Rumex hippolapathum

Rumex hippolapathum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and swollen nodes on the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a prominent midrib and a serrated margin, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
