Rhinocarpus excelsus

Rhinocarpus excelsus, commonly known as the horned cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and sandy environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like structures on its flower stems, which give it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Rhinocarpus excelsus is often found in open habitats and is adapted to withstand coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds.
