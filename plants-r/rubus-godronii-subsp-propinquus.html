Rubus godronii subsp. propinquus

Rubus godronii subsp.

propinquus, commonly known as the Godron's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and firm.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are usually covered in fine, whitish hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
