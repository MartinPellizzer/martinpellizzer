Rubus nitidus var. anglicanus

Rubus nitidus var. anglicanus, commonly known as the English bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit, a red, hairy berry, is edible but often tart and used in jams and wines.

This variety is native to Europe and has adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
