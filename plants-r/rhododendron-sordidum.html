Rhododendron sordidum

Rhododendron sordidum, commonly known as the gloomy rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forests or along stream banks. It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes many evergreen shrubs and trees.

Rhododendron sordidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
