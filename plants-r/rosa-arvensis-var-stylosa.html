Rosa arvensis var. stylosa

Rosa arvensis var. stylosa, commonly known as the creeping dog rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces pink to red flowers that are typically smaller than those of other rose species. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5-7 oval leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
