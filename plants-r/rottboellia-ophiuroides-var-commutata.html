Rottboellia ophiuroides var. commutata

Rottboellia ophiuroides var. commutata, commonly known as red rice grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some areas.

It is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
