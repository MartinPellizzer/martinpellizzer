Rubus obovatus

Rubus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to eastern North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet to tart flavor. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a hairy surface, and its flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus obovatus is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
