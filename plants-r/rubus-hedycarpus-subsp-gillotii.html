Rubus hedycarpus subsp. gillotii

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

gillotii, commonly known as the Gillot's bramble, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible red berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
