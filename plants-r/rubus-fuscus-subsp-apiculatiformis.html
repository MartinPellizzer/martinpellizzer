Rubus fuscus subsp. apiculatiformis

Rubus fuscus subsp.

apiculatiformis, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other Rubus species due to its unique leaf shape and fruit structure.
