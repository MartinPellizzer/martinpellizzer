Rapanea coxii

Rapanea coxii, commonly known as the Malayan boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, black berries.

Rapanea coxii is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
