Ribes spicatum subsp. hispidulum

Ribes spicatum subsp.

hispidulum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
