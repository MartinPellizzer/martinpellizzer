Rebutia karreri

Rebutia karreri, commonly known as the Karreri's moon cactus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features a short, thick stem covered in spines, with a distinctive crown of fleshy, segmented pads. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, blooming in the spring and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rebutia karreri is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
