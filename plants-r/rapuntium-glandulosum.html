Rapuntium glandulosum

Rapuntium glandulosum, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is considered ornamental and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
