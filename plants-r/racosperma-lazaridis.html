Racosperma lazaridis

Racosperma lazaridis, commonly known as the Lazaridis acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it access groundwater. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

Racosperma lazaridis is also valued for its hardy nature and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
