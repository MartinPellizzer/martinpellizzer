Rosa eglanteria f. subnuda

Rosa eglanteria f. subnuda, commonly known as the naked prickly rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose species Rosa eglanteria.

It is characterized by its reduced or absent thorns, making it distinct from the typical form of the species. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, with five to seven petals. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in temperate climates.

This cultivar is often valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
