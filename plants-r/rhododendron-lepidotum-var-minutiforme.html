Rhododendron lepidotum var. minutiforme

Rhododendron lepidotum var. minutiforme, commonly known as the dwarf alpine rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically small and oval in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, often blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil and partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to flourish in alpine gardens.
