Rhododendron floccigerum subsp. appropinquans

Rhododendron floccigerum subsp.

appropinquans, commonly known as the Appalachian rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in forested areas. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, with leathery leaves that provide year-round interest.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
