Rubus rudis f. umbrosus

Rubus rudis f. umbrosus, commonly known as the shady raspberry.

This is a rare and lesser-known variant of the raspberry plant, characterized by its dark green, almost black foliage. It thrives in shaded environments, making it suitable for woodland gardens. The plant produces small, tart berries similar to other raspberries but with a more intense flavor.

Its botanical features include a trailing growth habit and hairy stems, typical of the Rubus genus.
