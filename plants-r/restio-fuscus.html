Restio fuscus

Restio fuscus, commonly known as the black restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Restio fuscus is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in local horticulture for its ornamental value.
