Rosa × geminata var. fourraei

Rosa × geminata var. fourraei, commonly known as the Fourraei hybrid rose, is a botanical cultivar characterized by its compact growth habit and ornamental value.

It is a natural hybrid between Rosa × gallica and Rosa × canina, exhibiting traits from both parent species. The plant typically produces semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, with a pleasant, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote blooming.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
