Rhododendron polyraphidoideum

Rhododendron polyraphidoideum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron polyraphidoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
