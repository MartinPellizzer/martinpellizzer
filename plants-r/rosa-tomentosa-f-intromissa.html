Rosa tomentosa f. intromissa

Rosa tomentosa f. intromissa, commonly known as the woolly rose or soft-haired rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa tomentosa.

It is characterized by its dense, white to greyish pubescence covering the stems, leaves, and flowers, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically pink to pale red, with five to seven petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in gardens for its unique texture and aesthetic appeal.
