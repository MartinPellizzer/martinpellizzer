Rubus × ochrostachys

Rubus × ochrostachys, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry). This species is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The fruit is a small, aggregate berry that can vary in color from red to black, depending on the specific variety.

It is often cultivated for its tart flavor and used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
