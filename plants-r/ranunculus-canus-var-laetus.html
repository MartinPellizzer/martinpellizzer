Ranunculus canus var. laetus

Ranunculus canus var. laetus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
