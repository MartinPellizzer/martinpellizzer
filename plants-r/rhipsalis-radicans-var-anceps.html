Rhipsalis radicans var. anceps

Rhipsalis radicans var. anceps, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the Caribbean and South America.

It features long, trailing stems covered in small, spineless, fleshy segments that allow it to climb and attach to trees or other surfaces. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
