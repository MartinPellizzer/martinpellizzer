Rubus pubescens var. truncifrons

Rubus pubescens var. truncifrons, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
