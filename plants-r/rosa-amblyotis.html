Rosa amblyotis

Rosa amblyotis, commonly known as the Himalayan shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant features thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that provide a dense canopy.

Rosa amblyotis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
