Rosa × damascena var. semperflorens

Rosa × damascena var. semperflorens, commonly known as the perpetual rose or everblooming rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that are often used in perfumery and floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its everblooming nature makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
