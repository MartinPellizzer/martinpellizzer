Rhamnus × burgundiaca

Rhamnus × burgundiaca, commonly known as the European buckthorn, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by black, egg-shaped berries that are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

The bark is typically dark brown and becomes fissured with age, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
