Rhaphiolepis impressivena

Rhaphiolepis impressivena, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green color, with a slightly textured surface. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall. It is valued for its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal in gardens.

This species is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
