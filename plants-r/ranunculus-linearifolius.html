Ranunculus linearifolius

Ranunculus linearifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant has finely divided, linear leaves that give it a grass-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in pollination and ecological interactions within its native habitats.
