Ranunculus aberdaricus

Ranunculus aberdaricus, commonly known as the Aberdeenshire buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It blooms from April to June and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance.

Ranunculus aberdaricus is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
