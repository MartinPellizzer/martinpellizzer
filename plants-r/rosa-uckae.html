Rosa uckae

Rosa uckae, commonly known as the Uckean rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of soft, white hairs.

Rosa uckae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
