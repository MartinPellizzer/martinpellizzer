Rosa infrarufula

Rosa infrarufula, commonly known as the pale-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, fragrant pink or white flowers. Its leaves are compound, typically with five to seven leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stem.

Rosa infrarufula is a perennial shrub that thrives in woodland and shaded areas, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
