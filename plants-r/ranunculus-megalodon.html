Ranunculus megalodon

Ranunculus megalodon, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with numerous petals and a prominent yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Ranunculus megalodon is known for its ability to thrive in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
