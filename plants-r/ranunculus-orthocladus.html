Ranunculus orthocladus

Ranunculus orthocladus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or white flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus orthocladus is often found at high altitudes, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
