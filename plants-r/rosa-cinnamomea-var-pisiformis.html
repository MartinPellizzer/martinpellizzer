Rosa cinnamomea var. pisiformis

Rosa cinnamomea var. pisiformis, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon rose, is a flowering shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its stems are often covered in fine, reddish-brown hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
