Roeperocharis nigrescens

Roeperocharis nigrescens, commonly known as the blackened roeperocharis, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green to nearly black foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical or subtropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
