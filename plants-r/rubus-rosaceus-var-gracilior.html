Rubus rosaceus var. gracilior

Rubus rosaceus var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
