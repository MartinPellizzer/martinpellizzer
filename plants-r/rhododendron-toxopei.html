Rhododendron toxopei

Rhododendron toxopei, commonly known as the Toxopei rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
