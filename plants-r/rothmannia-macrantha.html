Rothmannia macrantha

Rothmannia macrantha, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Rothmannia macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
