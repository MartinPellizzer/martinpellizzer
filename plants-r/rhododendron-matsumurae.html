Rhododendron matsumurae

Rhododendron matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron matsumurae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
