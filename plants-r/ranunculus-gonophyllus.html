Ranunculus gonophyllus

Ranunculus gonophyllus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or white petals and deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

Ranunculus gonophyllus is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for early pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
