Rubus × scabriformis

Rubus × scabriformis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, a cluster of small drupes, is edible and often used in jams and pies.
