Ranunculus bulbosus subsp. valdepubens

Ranunculus bulbosus subsp.

valdepubens, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its botanical features include a corm-like bulb and finely divided, trifoliate leaves.

It is known for its early spring blooming period and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
