Rhododendron radians var. pubitubum

Rhododendron radians var. pubitubum, commonly known as the pubescent rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
