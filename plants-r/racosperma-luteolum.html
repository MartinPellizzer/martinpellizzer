Racosperma luteolum

Racosperma luteolum, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Racosperma luteolum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
