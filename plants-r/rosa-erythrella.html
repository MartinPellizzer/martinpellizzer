Rosa erythrella

Rosa erythrella, commonly known as the scarlet rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges. Its stems are covered in thorns, a common feature among many rose species.

Rosa erythrella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
