Rhynchospora scaberrima

Rhynchospora scaberrima, commonly known as the roughside spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and rough to the touch, contributing to its common name.
