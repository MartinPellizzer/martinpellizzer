Rubus × temnostachys

Rubus × temnostachys, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It is native to the western United States and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
