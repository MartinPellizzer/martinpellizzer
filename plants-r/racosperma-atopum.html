Racosperma atopum

Racosperma atopum, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a range of drought-resistant features. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Racosperma atopum is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in areas with low rainfall.
