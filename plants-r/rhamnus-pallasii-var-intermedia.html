Rhamnus pallasii var. intermedia

Rhamnus pallasii var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate buckthorn, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly China and the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and smooth, thornless stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by reddish or black berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
