Rubia hangii

Rubia hangii, commonly known as Hang's Rubia, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Rubia hangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
