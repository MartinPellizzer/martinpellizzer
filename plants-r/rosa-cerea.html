Rosa cerea

Rosa cerea, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically growing in woodland edges and rocky soils. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and a fragrant scent.

Rosa cerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
