Rhaphidorhynchus stylosus

Rhaphidorhynchus stylosus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this orchid thrives in humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant's roots are often covered in a dense network of mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation studies.
