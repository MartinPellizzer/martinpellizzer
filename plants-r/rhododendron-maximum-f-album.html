Rhododendron maximum f. album

Rhododendron maximum f. album, commonly known as the white laurel.

This variant of the Rhododendron maximum is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers, which contrast with its dark green, leathery leaves. It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, acidic soils. The plant can grow up to 10 feet tall and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its bloom period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens and natural habitats.
