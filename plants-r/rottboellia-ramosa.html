Rottboellia ramosa

Rottboellia ramosa, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. Rottboellia ramosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban landscapes.
