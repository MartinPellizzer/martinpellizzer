Rosa coriifolia subsp. uriensis

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

uriensis, commonly known as the Uriensis shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a landscape plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal floral display.
