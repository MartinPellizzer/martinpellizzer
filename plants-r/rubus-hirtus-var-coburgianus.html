Rubus hirtus var. coburgianus

Rubus hirtus var. coburgianus, commonly known as the Coburg raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
