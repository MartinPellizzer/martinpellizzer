Rubus peltaefolius

Rubus peltaefolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States, characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with thorny stems and compound leaves.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
