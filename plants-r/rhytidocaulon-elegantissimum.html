Rhytidocaulon elegantissimum

Rhytidocaulon elegantissimum, commonly known as the elegant staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, branching fronds that resemble the antlers of a stag. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, porous substrates to anchor its roots. Its fronds are typically leathery and can grow quite large, sometimes reaching lengths of over a meter.

Rhytidocaulon elegantissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in hanging baskets or mounted on trees in tropical gardens.
