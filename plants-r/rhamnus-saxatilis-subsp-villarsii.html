Rhamnus saxatilis subsp. villarsii

Rhamnus saxatilis subsp.

villarsii, commonly known as the alpine buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its fruits are small, reddish berries that ripen in the autumn.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
