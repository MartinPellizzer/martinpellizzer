Randia hedrophylla

Randia hedrophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
