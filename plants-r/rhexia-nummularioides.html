Rhexia nummularioides

Rhexia nummularioides, commonly known as the pennywort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, round, waxy leaves that resemble coins, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Rhexia nummularioides is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
