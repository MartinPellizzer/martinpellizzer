Rhododendron theiophyllum

Rhododendron theiophyllum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. It is often found in forested areas at high altitudes, where it forms dense thickets.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
