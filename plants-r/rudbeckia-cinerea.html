Rudbeckia cinerea

Rudbeckia cinerea, commonly known as the gray coneflower, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow-orange flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Rudbeckia cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
