Rollinia annonoides

Rollinia annonoides, commonly known as the Brazilian star-apple or yellow star-apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a yellow or orange fruit with a rough, spiky exterior, containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Rollinia annonoides is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
