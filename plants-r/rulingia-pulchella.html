Rulingia pulchella

Rulingia pulchella, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
