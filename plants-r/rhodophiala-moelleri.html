Rhodophiala moelleri

Rhodophiala moelleri, commonly known as the silver sword, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically in the spring. It thrives in cool, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
