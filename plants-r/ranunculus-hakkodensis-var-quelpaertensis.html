Ranunculus hakkodensis var. quelpaertensis

Ranunculus hakkodensis var. quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Jeju buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula, particularly found on the island of Jeju.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or white flowers. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces small, aggregated fruits called achenes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
