Rosa arkansana var. suffulta

Rosa arkansana var. suffulta, commonly known as the Arkansas rose or the prairie rose, is a shrub native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and rose-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a hardy species, often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
