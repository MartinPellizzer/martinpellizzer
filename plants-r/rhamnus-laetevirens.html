Rhamnus laetevirens

Rhamnus laetevirens, commonly known as the evergreen buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, reddish-brown berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by the characteristic berries.

Rhamnus laetevirens is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to coastal environments.
