Rollinia exalbida

Rollinia exalbida, commonly known as the white-veined star-apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a large, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

Rollinia exalbida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
