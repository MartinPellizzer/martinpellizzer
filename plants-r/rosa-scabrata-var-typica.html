Rosa scabrata var. typica

Rosa scabrata var. typica, commonly known as the roughleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with rough, serrated margins. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and autumn foliage.
