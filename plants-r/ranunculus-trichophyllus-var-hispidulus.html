Ranunculus trichophyllus var. hispidulus

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. hispidulus, commonly known as the hairy leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it its common name. The flowers are typically yellow with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This variant is distinguished by its hairy foliage and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
