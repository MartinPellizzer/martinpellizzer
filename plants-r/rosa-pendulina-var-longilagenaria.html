Rosa pendulina var. longilagenaria

Rosa pendulina var. longilagenaria, commonly known as the long-stemmed climbing rose, is a vigorous, thornless shrub that produces long, arching branches.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for trellises or fences. Its foliage is dark green and dense, providing good ground cover.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant.
