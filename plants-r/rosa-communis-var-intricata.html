Rosa communis var. intricata

Rosa communis var. intricata, commonly known as the twisted rose, is a cultivar prized for its intricate, layered blooms.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The flowers are typically a deep pink to red hue, with a complex, almost spiral-like petal arrangement. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
