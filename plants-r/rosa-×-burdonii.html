Rosa × burdonii

Rosa × burdonii, commonly known as the Burdon's rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem with thorns.

Rosa × burdonii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
