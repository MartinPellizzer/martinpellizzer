Restrepia antennifera subsp. klabochorum

Restrepia antennifera subsp.

klabochorum, commonly known as the Klaboch's orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, stocky rhizome and a single, erect stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers that have a distinctive, narrow spur. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
