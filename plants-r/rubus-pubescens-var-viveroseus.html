Rubus pubescens var. viveroseus

Rubus pubescens var. viveroseus, commonly known as the pink-stemmed raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and native plant gardening.
