Rosa vernonii

Rosa vernonii, commonly known as the Vernon's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
