Rosa pouzinii subsp. florentina

Rosa pouzinii subsp.

florentina, commonly known as the Florentine rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
