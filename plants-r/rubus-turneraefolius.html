Rubus turneraefolius

Rubus turneraefolius, commonly known as the Turnera leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves that resemble those of the Turnera plant, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
