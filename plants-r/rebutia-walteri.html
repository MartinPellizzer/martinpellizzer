Rebutia walteri

Rebutia walteri, commonly known as the Walter's Rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically found in regions of Argentina and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense, spiny stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically tubular in shape. Rebutia walteri is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative indoor or outdoor plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
