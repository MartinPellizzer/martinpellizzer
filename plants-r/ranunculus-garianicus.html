Ranunculus garianicus

Ranunculus garianicus, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus garianicus is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
