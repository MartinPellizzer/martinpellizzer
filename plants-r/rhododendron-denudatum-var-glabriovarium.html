Rhododendron denudatum var. glabriovarium

Rhododendron denudatum var. glabriovarium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved alpine rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically smooth and leathery, with a dark green color, while the flowers range from pink to white in hue. This variant is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other varieties within the species.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
