Rigiolepis sulcata

Rigiolepis sulcata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant has a rosette-shaped growth pattern and produces small, spiky seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
