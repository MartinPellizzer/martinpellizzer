Rachunia cymbiformis

Rachunia cymbiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emerging from the water surface. Rachunia cymbiformis thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
