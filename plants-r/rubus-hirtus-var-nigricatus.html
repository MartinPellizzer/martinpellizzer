Rubus hirtus var. nigricatus

Rubus hirtus var. nigricatus, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a thorny shrub native to North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and hairy stems, which are characteristic of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

The berries are edible, though some varieties may have a tart or astringent flavor.
