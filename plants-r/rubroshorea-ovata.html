Rubroshorea ovata

Rubroshorea ovata, commonly known as the redshore maple, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn, providing ornamental value. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
