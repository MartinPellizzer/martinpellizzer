Rubus idaeus subsp. kanayamensis

Rubus idaeus subsp.

kanayamensis, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a subspecies native to Japan. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberries and blackberries.
