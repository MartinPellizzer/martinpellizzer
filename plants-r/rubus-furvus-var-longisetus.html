Rubus furvus var. longisetus

Rubus furvus var. longisetus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental qualities.
