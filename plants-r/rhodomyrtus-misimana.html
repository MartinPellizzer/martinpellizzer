Rhodomyrtus misimana

Rhodomyrtus misimana, commonly known as the Queensland rose or white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive red calyx. The plant produces small, round, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface and a pleasant aromatic scent.

Rhodomyrtus misimana is often found in coastal and wetland areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
