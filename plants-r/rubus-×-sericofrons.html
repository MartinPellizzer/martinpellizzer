Rubus × sericofrons

Rubus × sericofrons, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland and thicket habitats.

Botanically, it is a shrub with thorny stems and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
