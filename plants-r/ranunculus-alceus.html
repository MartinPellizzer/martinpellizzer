Ranunculus alceus

Ranunculus alceus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are palmately divided, and it grows in rocky or alpine soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Ranunculus alceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
