Rosa mollialbida

Rosa mollialbida, commonly known as the white sweetbriar, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa mollialbida is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
