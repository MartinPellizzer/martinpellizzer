Rubus fruticosus var. discolor

Rubus fruticosus var. discolor, commonly known as the white-bracted blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This plant is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.
