Ranunculus acarpellophorus

Ranunculus acarpellophorus, commonly known as the Carpathian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
