Rhamnus japonica var. chlorocarpa

Rhamnus japonica var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-fruited buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive green berries that ripen to black. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or mountainous regions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
