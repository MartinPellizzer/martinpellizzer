Rubus × hirsutulus

Rubus × hirsutulus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are often dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Due to its vigorous growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions.
