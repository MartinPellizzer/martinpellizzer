Rupicapnos africana

Rupicapnos africana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the winter months. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and public spaces for its vibrant floral display.
