Racosperma celastrifolium

Racosperma celastrifolium, commonly known as the diamond pea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Racosperma celastrifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
