Racosperma sorophyllum

Racosperma sorophyllum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in woodland and heathland environments.

Racosperma sorophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
