Rubus pseudogravetii

Rubus pseudogravetii, commonly known as the Sierra raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes numerous species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries, raspberries, and dewberries. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The Sierra raspberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
