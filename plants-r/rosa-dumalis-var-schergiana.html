Rosa dumalis var. schergiana

Rosa dumalis var. schergiana, commonly known as the Scherg's shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to rocky soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of rose hips.
