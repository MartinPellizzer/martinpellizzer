Rosa raphinii

Rosa raphinii, commonly known as the Himalayan rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red-colored fruits that contain seeds.

Rosa raphinii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
