Rosa obtusifolia var. balcica

Rosa obtusifolia var. balcica, commonly known as the Balkan rock rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge. This variety is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
