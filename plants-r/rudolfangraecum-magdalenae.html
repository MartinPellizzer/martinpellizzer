Rudolfangraecum magdalenae

Rudolfangraecum magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalenae cycad, is a unique species native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its robust, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can live for centuries, making it a long-lived species.

Its botanical features include a thick, fibrous root system and a distinctive, palm-like appearance that sets it apart from other cycads.
