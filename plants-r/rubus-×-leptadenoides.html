Rubus × leptadenoides

Rubus × leptadenoides, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a slightly serrated margin, and the plant is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
