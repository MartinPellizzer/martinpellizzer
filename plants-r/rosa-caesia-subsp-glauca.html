Rosa caesia subsp. glauca

Rosa caesia subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the bluebells rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, often arching stems. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with elliptical leaflets that are hairy beneath.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
