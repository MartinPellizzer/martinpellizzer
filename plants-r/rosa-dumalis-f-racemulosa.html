Rosa dumalis f. racemulosa

Rosa dumalis f. racemulosa, commonly known as the Alpine rose or mountain rose, is a horticultural form of the Rosa dumalis species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, making it well-suited for alpine gardens. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and serrated leaves, typical of many rose species.
