Rosa pendulina subvar. pyrenaica

Rosa pendulina subvar. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean weeping rose, is a species native to the Pyrenees Mountains.

It is characterized by its graceful, drooping stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are edible.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
