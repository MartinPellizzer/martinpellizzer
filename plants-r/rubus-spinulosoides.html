Rubus spinulosoides

Rubus spinulosoides, commonly known as the prickly blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

Rubus spinulosoides plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
