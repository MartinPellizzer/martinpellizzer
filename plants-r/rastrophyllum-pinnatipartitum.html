Rastrophyllum pinnatipartitum

Rastrophyllum pinnatipartitum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the rainforests of Australia.

It attaches itself to the branches of host trees, such as eucalyptus and acacia, using a specialized root system called haustoria to draw water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive pinnate structure, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
