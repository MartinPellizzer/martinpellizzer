Rubus × inextricabilis

Rubus × inextricabilis, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (the red raspberry).

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible berries, which are typically a mix of blackberry and raspberry characteristics. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful fruits.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in culinary applications.
