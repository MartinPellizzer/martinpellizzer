Ranunculus auricomus subsp. laurilaensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

laurilaensis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight broad, petal-like sepals that resemble petals.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.
