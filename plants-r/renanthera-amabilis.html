Renanthera amabilis

Renanthera amabilis, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinct sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a yellow lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Renanthera amabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
