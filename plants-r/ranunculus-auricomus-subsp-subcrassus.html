Ranunculus auricomus subsp. subcrassus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

subcrassus, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features a basal rosette of broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating.

The flowers are bright yellow with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
