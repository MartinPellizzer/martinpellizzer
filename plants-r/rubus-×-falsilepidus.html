Rubus × falsilepidus

Rubus × falsilepidus, commonly known as the false blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries that are typically red or black when ripe. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist woodlands and open fields. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

Due to its adaptability and fruit production, it is often found in naturalized areas and can be a common sight in hedgerows and along forest edges.
