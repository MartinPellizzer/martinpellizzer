Rosa praeterita

Rosa praeterita, commonly known as the old garden rose, is a species native to the British Isles.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa praeterita is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

It is also valued for its historical significance in horticultural heritage.
