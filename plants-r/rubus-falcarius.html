Rubus falcarius

Rubus falcarius, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
