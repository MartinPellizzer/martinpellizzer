Rumex ramulosus

Rumex ramulosus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine, and it is also a host plant for certain butterfly species.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
