Rehmannia angulata

Rehmannia angulata, commonly known as the angular rehmannia, is a perennial herb native to China, particularly found in the provinces of Sichuan and Yunnan.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive angular stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional Chinese medicine, valued for their purported health benefits.

Rehmannia angulata is often cultivated for both medicinal and ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
