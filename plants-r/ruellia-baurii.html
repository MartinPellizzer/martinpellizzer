Ruellia baurii

Ruellia baurii, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, violet, or purple, which bloom from spring to summer. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Ruellia baurii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
