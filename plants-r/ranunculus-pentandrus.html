Ranunculus pentandrus

Ranunculus pentandrus, commonly known as the five-stamen buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, shady environments. The plant has a short, upright stem with basal leaves that are palmately lobed. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

Ranunculus pentandrus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
