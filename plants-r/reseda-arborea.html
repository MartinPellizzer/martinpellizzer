Reseda arborea

Reseda arborea, commonly known as tree reseda, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Reseda arborea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
