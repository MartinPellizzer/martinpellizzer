Rubus pilosus var. schlechtendalii

Rubus pilosus var. schlechtendalii, commonly known as the Schlechtendal's bramble, is a variant of the hairy blackberry species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
