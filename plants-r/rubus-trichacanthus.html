Rubus trichacanthus

Rubus trichacanthus, commonly known as the thorned blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of sharp, curved thorns along its stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
