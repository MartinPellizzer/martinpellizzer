Rosa hispanica f. florentina

Rosa hispanica f. florentina, commonly known as the Spanish pink or the Florentine rose, is a hybrid rose with a rich botanical history.

It is characterized by its large, semi-double to fully double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
