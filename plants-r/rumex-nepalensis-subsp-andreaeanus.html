Rumex nepalensis subsp. andreaeanus

Rumex nepalensis subsp.

andreaeanus, commonly known as the Nepal sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it produces edible, tangy rhubarb-like stalks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its unique leaf anatomy and flowering structure.
