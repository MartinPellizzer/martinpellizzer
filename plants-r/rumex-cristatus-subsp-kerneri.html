Rumex cristatus subsp. kerneri

Rumex cristatus subsp.

kerneri, commonly known as the Kent's dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on slender, erect stalks. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and marshy areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
