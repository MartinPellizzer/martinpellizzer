Rhus cinerea

Rhus cinerea, commonly known as smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with serrated leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fuzzy berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a common and recognizable species in the Anacardiaceae family.
