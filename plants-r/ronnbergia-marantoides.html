Ronnbergia marantoides

Ronnbergia marantoides, commonly known as the orange butterfly plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble butterflies. The plant features strap-like, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, red, and orange. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which are followed by bright orange berries.

Ronnbergia marantoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
