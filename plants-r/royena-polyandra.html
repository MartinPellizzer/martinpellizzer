Royena polyandra

Royena polyandra, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that matures to a reddish color.
