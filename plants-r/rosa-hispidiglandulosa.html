Rosa hispidiglandulosa

Rosa hispidiglandulosa, commonly known as the hispid glandular rose, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically borne on long pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
