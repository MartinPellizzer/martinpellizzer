Rhododendron tanastylum var. pennivenium

Rhododendron tanastylum var. pennivenium, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink and red.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
