Rubus × plusiadenius

Rubus × plusiadenius, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberries (Rubus fruticosus) and raspberries (Rubus idaeus).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

The botanical features of Rubus × plusiadenius make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
