Rosa canina f. subconcolor

Rosa canina f. subconcolor, commonly known as the smooth hips dogrose, is a variant of the common dogrose characterized by its smooth, often hairless fruits.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in woodland edges and open habitats. The plant produces pink to white flowers in spring, followed by orange to red berries in autumn. Its leaves are pinnate with serrated edges, and the stems are often armed with thorns.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
