Roegneria fedtschenkoi

Roegneria fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Siberian grass or Fedtschenko's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low temperatures and poor nutrient availability.

Roegneria fedtschenkoi is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in ecological restoration projects.
