Ruellia arcuata

Ruellia arcuata, commonly known as the spreading ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia arcuata thrives in full sun to partial shade and is commonly used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
