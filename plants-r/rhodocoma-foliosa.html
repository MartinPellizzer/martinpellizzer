Rhodocoma foliosa

Rhodocoma foliosa, commonly known as the woolly leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom during the cooler months.

Rhodocoma foliosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
