Rubus truncifolius

Rubus truncifolius, commonly known as the cut-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, truncate (notched) leaf margins and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberry species.
