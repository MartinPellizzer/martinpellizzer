Rubus × obtusatiformis

Rubus × obtusatiformis, commonly known as the blackberry-boys or brambleberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white to light pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are used in jams and pies.
