Racosperma elachanthum

Racosperma elachanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often sparsely hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature.

Racosperma elachanthum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
