Rosa gallica subvar. pumila

Rosa gallica subvar. pumila, commonly known as the creeping pink, is a low-growing shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky or dry soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

This subspecies is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
