Rubus fuscus var. regillus

Rubus fuscus var. regillus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black or dark purple color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

This species is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
