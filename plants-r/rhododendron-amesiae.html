Rhododendron amesiae

Rhododendron amesiae, commonly known as the Amesia rhododendron, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leathery leaves, which help it adapt to its high-altitude habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
