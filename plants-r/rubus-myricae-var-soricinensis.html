Rubus myricae var. soricinensis

Rubus myricae var. soricinensis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically serrated and have a dark green upper surface. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, that are usually dark purple to black in color when mature. These berries are edible, though they are often described as having a tart and slightly astringent flavor.

It is native to certain regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
