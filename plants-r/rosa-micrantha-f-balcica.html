Rosa micrantha f. balcica

Rosa micrantha f. balcica, commonly known as the Balkan rose, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in pink to white hues. The plant produces oval, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in temperate regions.
