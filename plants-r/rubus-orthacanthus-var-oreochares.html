Rubus orthacanthus var. oreochares

Rubus orthacanthus var. oreochares, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary applications. It has adapted to coastal conditions with salt-tolerant traits and a deep root system for stability.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and flowers with five petals.
