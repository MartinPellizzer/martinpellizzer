Romnalda

Romnalda, commonly known as the moonflower, is a striking plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a velvety texture, while its flowers emit a sweet, intoxicating scent.

Romnalda is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is admired for its unique nocturnal blooming pattern.
