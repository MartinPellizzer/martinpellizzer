Rosa sherardii var. umbelliflora

Rosa sherardii var. umbelliflora, commonly known as the Umbellate shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in cooler climates.
