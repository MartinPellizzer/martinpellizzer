Rhododendron arizelum

Rhododendron arizelum, commonly known as the Arizona rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and bloom in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blossoms.

Rhododendron arizelum is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun.
