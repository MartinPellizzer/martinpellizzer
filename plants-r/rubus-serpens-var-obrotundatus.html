Rubus serpens var. obrotundatus

Rubus serpens var. obrotundatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and large, broad leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
