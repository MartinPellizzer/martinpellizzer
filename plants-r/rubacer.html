Rubacer

Rubacer, commonly known as the red foxglove, is a member of the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and striking, hood-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rubacer is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
