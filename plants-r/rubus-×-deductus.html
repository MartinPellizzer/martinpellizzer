Rubus × deductus

Rubus × deductus, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The species exhibits thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a large, dark purple to black berry.

This hybrid is valued for its vigorous growth and flavorful, juicy fruits, making it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and commercial settings.
