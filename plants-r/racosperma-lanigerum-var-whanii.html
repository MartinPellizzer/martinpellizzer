Racosperma lanigerum var. whanii

Racosperma lanigerum var. whanii, commonly known as the Whanganui acacia, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often found in disturbed soils.

The variety is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and geographic distribution.
