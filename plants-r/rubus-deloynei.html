Rubus deloynei

Rubus deloynei, commonly known as the Deloyne blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
