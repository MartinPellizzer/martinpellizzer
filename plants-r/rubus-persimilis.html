Rubus persimilis

Rubus persimilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and thickets, producing small, dark red to black berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

The flowers are white and have five petals, typical of many Rubus species.
