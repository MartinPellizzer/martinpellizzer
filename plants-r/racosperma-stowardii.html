Racosperma stowardii

Racosperma stowardii, commonly known as the Stoward's acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
