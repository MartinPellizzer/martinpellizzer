Ranunculus poldinioides

Ranunculus poldinioides, commonly known as the Poldini's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish to orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
