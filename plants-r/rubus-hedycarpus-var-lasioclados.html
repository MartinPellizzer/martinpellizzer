Rubus hedycarpus var. lasioclados

Rubus hedycarpus var. lasioclados, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically covered in a fine, hairy layer. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variant is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
