Rosa lactea

Rosa lactea, commonly known as the milk rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its white petals and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa lactea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and pleasant scent.
