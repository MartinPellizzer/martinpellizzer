Rubus scitulus

Rubus scitulus, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters. Its fruits, which are edible, are aggregate berries that develop from multiple ovaria.

The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in moist, temperate regions.
