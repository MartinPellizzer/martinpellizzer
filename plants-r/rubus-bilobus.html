Rubus bilobus

Rubus bilobus, commonly known as the double-lobed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberries.
