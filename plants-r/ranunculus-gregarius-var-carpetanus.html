Ranunculus gregarius var. carpetanus

Ranunculus gregarius var. carpetanus, commonly known as the carpet buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, and its stems are usually prostrate or ascending. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its ability to form dense carpets, which can dominate certain habitats.
