Rosa × portlandia

Rosa × portlandia, commonly known as the Portland rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple canes with thorns. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

The Portland rose is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance.
