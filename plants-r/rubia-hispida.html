Rubia hispida

Rubia hispida, commonly known as hairy woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rubia hispida is often found in woodland areas and is known for its fragrant aroma.
