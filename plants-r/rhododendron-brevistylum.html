Rhododendron brevistylum

Rhododendron brevistylum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a compact shrub that typically grows to a height of less than one meter, featuring dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
