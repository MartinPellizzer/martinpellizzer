Ranunculus flabellatus var. confertus

Ranunculus flabellatus var. confertus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a distinctive white or greenish central mark. The plant has palmate or deeply lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
