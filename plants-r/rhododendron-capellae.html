Rhododendron capellae

Rhododendron capellae, commonly known as the Capella rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron capellae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as part of natural landscapes.
