Rosa arvensis var. gallicoides

Rosa arvensis var. gallicoides, commonly known as the French wild rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant pink to red flowers in the summer. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and fields. Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species, capable of thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.

The fruit, a red rosehip, is an important food source for wildlife and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
