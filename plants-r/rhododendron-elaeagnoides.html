Rhododendron elaeagnoides

Rhododendron elaeagnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that often have a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

Rhododendron elaeagnoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form thickets, which can provide habitat for various wildlife species.
