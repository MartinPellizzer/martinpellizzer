Rubus podophyllos var. parviflorens

Rubus podophyllos var. parviflorens, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark purple berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
