Rosa tomentella var. lavantina

Rosa tomentella var. lavantina, commonly known as the Lavantina shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant is characterized by its short, upright growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

The variety is noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
