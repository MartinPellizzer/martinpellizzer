Rhynchospora rudis

Rhynchospora rudis, commonly known as the slender beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. Rhynchospora rudis typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies in wetland environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
