Ranunculus auricomus subsp. rectiangulus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

rectiangulus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central stigma.

The leaves are typically divided into three parts, with a distinctive narrow shape. Native to regions in the northern hemisphere, this subspecies thrives in moist, temperate habitats.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
