Rhododendron rhaibocarpum

Rhododendron rhaibocarpum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features large, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are showy, with five to seven petals, and often display shades of pink or red.

Rhododendron rhaibocarpum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
