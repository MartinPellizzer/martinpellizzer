Ranunculus sessiliflorus var. platycarpus

Ranunculus sessiliflorus var. platycarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruited buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately divided and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The flowers are yellow with five petals and are borne on slender, hairy stems. The fruit is a rounded, many-seeded capsule that matures in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
