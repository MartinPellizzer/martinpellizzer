Rosa gallica f. magnifica

Rosa gallica f. magnifica, commonly known as the French rose or the crimson gallica rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa gallica species.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed, and vividly pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. It is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This rose is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and heraldry.
