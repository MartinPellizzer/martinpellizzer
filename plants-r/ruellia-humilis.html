Ruellia humilis

Ruellia humilis, commonly known as the small ruellia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Ruellia humilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
