Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pinguifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pinguifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features showy yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring.

The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
