Ranunculus fallax subsp. mesidius

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

mesidius, commonly known as the western variegated buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate.
