Rondeletia dichotoma

Rondeletia dichotoma, commonly known as the red rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Rondeletia dichotoma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
