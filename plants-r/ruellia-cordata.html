Ruellia cordata

Ruellia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially sunny environments.

Ruellia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and garden settings.
