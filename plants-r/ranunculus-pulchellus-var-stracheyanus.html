Ranunculus pulchellus var. stracheyanus

Ranunculus pulchellus var. stracheyanus, commonly known as the Strachey's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in cool climates and is often found in temperate regions.
