Rubus radula subsp. multifidus

Rubus radula subsp.

multifidus, commonly known as the multifidus blackberry, is a subspecies native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with multiple leaflets.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to blackberry-like aggregate. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its edible berries and is often used in local foraging and culinary practices.
