Rhododendron grande var. singalense

Rhododendron grande var. singalense, commonly known as the Singalense rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a subtle reddish tinge. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
