Ricinodendron viticoides

Ricinodendron viticoides, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
