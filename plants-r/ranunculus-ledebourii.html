Ranunculus ledebourii

Ranunculus ledebourii, commonly known as the desert buttercup, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with deeply divided petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Ranunculus ledebourii is often found in mountainous and semi-desert environments, where it thrives in full sun.
