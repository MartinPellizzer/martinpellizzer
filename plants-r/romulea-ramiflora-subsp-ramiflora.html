Romulea ramiflora subsp. ramiflora

Romulea ramiflora subsp.

ramiflora, commonly known as the golden bulb orchid, is a perennial geophyte native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its corm-like underground stem and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are borne on slender, branching stems and attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

The subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
