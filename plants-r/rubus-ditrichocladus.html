Rubus ditrichocladus

Rubus ditrichocladus, commonly known as the thick-clad raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in woodland and disturbed areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many species of raspberries and blackberries.
