Ruellia dissidens var. sarmentosa

Ruellia dissidens var. sarmentosa, commonly known as creeping ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This species is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
