Rosa viscaria var. veynensis

Rosa viscaria var. veynensis, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
