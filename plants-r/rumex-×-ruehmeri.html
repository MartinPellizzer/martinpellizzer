Rumex × ruehmeri

Rumex × ruehmeri, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a cross between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex acetobulus (broadleaf sorrel).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive sour taste due to high oxalic acid content. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Polygonaceae family, known for its ornamental and culinary uses.
