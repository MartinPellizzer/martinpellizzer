Rosa woodsii subsp. puberulenta

Rosa woodsii subsp.

puberulenta, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain rock rose, is a shrub-like species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. This subspecies thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the rose family.
