Rubus serpens proles mitigatus

Rubus serpens proles mitigatus, commonly known as the soft-stemmed blackberry.

This plant is a species of bramble characterized by its trailing growth habit and thornless stems. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in moist, temperate climates.

It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
