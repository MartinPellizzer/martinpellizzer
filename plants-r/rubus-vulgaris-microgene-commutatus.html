Rubus vulgaris microgene commutatus

Rubus vulgaris microgene commutatus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North America, and it thrives in temperate climates. It produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
