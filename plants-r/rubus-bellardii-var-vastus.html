Rubus bellardii var. vastus

Rubus bellardii var. vastus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and robust growth habit, often reaching heights of several meters. The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger than those of other Rubus species. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine, silky hairs.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
