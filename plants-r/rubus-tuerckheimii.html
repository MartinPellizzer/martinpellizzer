Rubus tuerckheimii

Rubus tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
