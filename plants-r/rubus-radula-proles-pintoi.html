Rubus radula proles pintoi

Rubus radula proles pintoi, commonly known as the Pintoi raspberry, is a unique species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are often used in local traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces pinkish-white flowers in the spring.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive ailments and skin conditions.
