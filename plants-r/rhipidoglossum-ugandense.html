Rhipidoglossum ugandense

Rhipidoglossum ugandense, commonly known as the Uganda mossy orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Uganda.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a dense cluster of small, greenish-yellow flowers that resemble moss. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or in forest undergrowth. Its botanical features include a short, rhizomatous stem and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
