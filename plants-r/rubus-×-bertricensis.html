Rubus × bertricensis

Rubus × bertricensis, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a hybrid plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes brambles and raspberries, and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in jams, desserts, and beverages.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and ecological contexts.
