Ribes subvestitum

Ribes subvestitum, commonly known as the western gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and streambanks. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a rounded, reddish-purple berry that ripens in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
