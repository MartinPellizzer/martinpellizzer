Rosa tschatyrdagi

Rosa tschatyrdagi, commonly known as the "Caucasian rose," is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant pink to white flowers in the summer. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Rosa tschatyrdagi is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
