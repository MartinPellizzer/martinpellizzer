Rosa gizellae

Rosa gizellae, commonly known as the Gizella rose, is a rare and endemic species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shrubland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its thorns are slender and numerous.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Rosa gizellae is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of conservation interest.
