Rubus polyacanthus subsp. melanadenius

Rubus polyacanthus subsp.

melanadenius, commonly known as the blackberry lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with serrated edges.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Its berries, which are edible, are dark purple to black when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
