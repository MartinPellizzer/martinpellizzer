Rubus zielinskii

Rubus zielinskii, commonly known as the Siberian blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and thrives in cooler climates. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
