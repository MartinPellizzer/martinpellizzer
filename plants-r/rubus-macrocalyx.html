Rubus macrocalyx

Rubus macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

Rubus macrocalyx thrives in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
