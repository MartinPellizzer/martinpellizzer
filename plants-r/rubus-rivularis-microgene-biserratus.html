Rubus rivularis microgene biserratus

Rubus rivularis microgene biserratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in early spring.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
