Ribes × berisioides

Ribes × berisioides, commonly known as the hybrid currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and produces clusters of small, edible berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
