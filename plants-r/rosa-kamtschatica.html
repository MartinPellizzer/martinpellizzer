Rosa kamtschatica

Rosa kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Russian Far East, particularly the Kamchatka Peninsula, and is adapted to cold climates. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa kamtschatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
