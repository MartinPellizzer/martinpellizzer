Rosa villosa var. foreziensis

Rosa villosa var. foreziensis, commonly known as the Forez rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of France and Italy.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant its species name "villosa," which means hairy in Latin. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
