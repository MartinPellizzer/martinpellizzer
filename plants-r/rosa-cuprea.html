Rosa cuprea

Rosa cuprea, commonly known as the copper rose, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in thorns. Rosa cuprea is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
