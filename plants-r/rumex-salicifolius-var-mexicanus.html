Rumex salicifolius var. mexicanus

Rumex salicifolius var. mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican dock, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features upright, ribbed stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a rough, serrated edge, often with a reddish or purple tint along the veins. This plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
