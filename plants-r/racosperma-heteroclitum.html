Racosperma heteroclitum

Racosperma heteroclitum, commonly known as the blackwood acacia, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods, which contribute to its ecological role in soil stabilization and provide food for various wildlife species.
