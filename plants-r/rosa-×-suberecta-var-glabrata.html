Rosa × suberecta var. glabrata

Rosa × suberecta var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a hybrid species characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It features thornless stems and small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and a subtle fragrance, blooming in late spring to early summer. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
