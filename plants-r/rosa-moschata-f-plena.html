Rosa moschata f. plena

Rosa moschata f. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered musk rose, is a cultivar of the musk rose species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its double blooms, which are rich in fragrance and typically pink to red in color. The plant produces small, yellow, edible fruits known as hips, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
