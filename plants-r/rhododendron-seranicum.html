Rhododendron seranicum

Rhododendron seranicum, commonly known as the Seranica rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron seranicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
