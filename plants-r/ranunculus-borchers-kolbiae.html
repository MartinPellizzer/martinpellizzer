Ranunculus borchers-kolbiae

Ranunculus borchers-kolbiae, commonly known as the Borchers' buttercup, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces yellow or orange petals that attract pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
