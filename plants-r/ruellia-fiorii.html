Ruellia fiorii

Ruellia fiorii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a mist-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Ruellia fiorii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.
