Ranunculus anatolicus

Ranunculus anatolicus, commonly known as the Anatolian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, hairy stems that bear trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often accompanied by a central cluster of yellow stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Ranunculus anatolicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant, delicate blooms.
