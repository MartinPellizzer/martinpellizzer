Rubus idaeus f. laevis

Rubus idaeus f. laevis, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and fruits.

This form is typically found in temperate regions and is cultivated for its sweet, juicy berries. The plant is a shrub with compound leaves and produces aggregate fruits composed of multiple drupes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
