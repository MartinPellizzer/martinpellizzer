Rubia discolor

Rubia discolor, commonly known as the common madder, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces red or orange-red roots that have been historically used for dyeing textiles and in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia discolor thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along roadsides.
