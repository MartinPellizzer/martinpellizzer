Rubus discolor var. plusiosetus

Rubus discolor var. plusiosetus, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
