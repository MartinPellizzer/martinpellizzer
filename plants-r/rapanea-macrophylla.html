Rapanea macrophylla

Rapanea macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved evergreen or mountain holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elongated leaves that are often leathery in texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in subtropical and tropical regions.
