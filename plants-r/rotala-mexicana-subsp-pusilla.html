Rotala mexicana subsp. pusilla

Rotala mexicana subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as dwarf rotala, is a compact aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia. It features small, rounded leaves that are typically bright green with a slight red or purple hue under strong light.

The plant thrives in freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life. It prefers moderate to high light conditions and can grow in both submerged and floating forms.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for maintaining water clarity in planted tanks.
