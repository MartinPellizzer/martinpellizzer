Rhynchospora ciliatifolia

Rhynchospora ciliatifolia, commonly known as the fringed leaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, ciliate (fringed) leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. Rhynchospora ciliatifolia is often found in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its presence can indicate the health of aquatic environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
