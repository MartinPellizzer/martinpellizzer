Raphanus sativus var. radicula

Raphanus sativus var. radicula, commonly known as the Chinese watercress, is a fast-growing aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, edible, peppery seeds.

It thrives in shallow water and moist soil, often found in stream beds and ditches. The plant is valued for its crisp, spicy flavor and is commonly used in salads and as a garnish. It contains high levels of vitamins A, C, and K, as well as minerals like calcium and iron.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for hydroponic cultivation and water garden setups.
