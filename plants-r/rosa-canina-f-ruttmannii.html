Rosa canina f. ruttmannii

Rosa canina f. ruttmannii, commonly known as the Ruttmannii rose, is a horticultural form of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse, fragrant blooms. The plant typically produces pink to red flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Its hips, like those of Rosa canina, are edible and used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
