Rosa psilophilla

Rosa psilophilla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus, where it thrives in mountainous and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rosa psilophilla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
