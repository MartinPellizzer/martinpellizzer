Ranunculus borbasianus

Ranunculus borbasianus, commonly known as the Borbasian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with deep lobes and produces small, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Ranunculus borbasianus thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
