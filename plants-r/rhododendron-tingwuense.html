Rhododendron tingwuense

Rhododendron tingwuense, commonly known as the Tingwu rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Fujian and Zhejiang provinces.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical characteristics include a preference for acidic, well-drained soils and a humid, cool climate, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
