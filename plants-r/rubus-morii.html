Rubus morii

Rubus morii, commonly known as the Morii blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
