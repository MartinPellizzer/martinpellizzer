Rhododendron lindleyi

Rhododendron lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant.

Rhododendron lindleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
