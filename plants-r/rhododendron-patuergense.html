Rhododendron patuergense

Rhododendron patuergense, commonly known as the Patuergense azalea, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a unique addition to specialized gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
