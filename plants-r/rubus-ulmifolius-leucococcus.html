Rubus ulmifolius leucococcus

Rubus ulmifolius leucococcus, commonly known as white-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other Rubus species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This plant is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
