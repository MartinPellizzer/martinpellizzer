Rubus × ohsimensis

Rubus × ohsimensis, commonly known as the Ohsima blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
