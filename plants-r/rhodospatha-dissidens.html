Rhodospatha dissidens

Rhodospatha dissidens, commonly known as the red spider orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers with intricate patterns that resemble spider webs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vivid coloration make it a popular subject among botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
