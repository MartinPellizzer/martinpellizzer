Racosperma rossei

Racosperma rossei, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Racosperma rossei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
