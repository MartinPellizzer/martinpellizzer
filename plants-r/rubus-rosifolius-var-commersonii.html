Rubus rosifolius var. commersonii

Rubus rosifolius var. commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's raspberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including shrublands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
