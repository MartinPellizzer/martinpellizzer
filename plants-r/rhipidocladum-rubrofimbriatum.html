Rhipidocladum rubrofimbriatum

Rhipidocladum rubrofimbriatum, commonly known as the red-fringed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are characterized by red or pink margins, which give it its common name.

It is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appearance.
