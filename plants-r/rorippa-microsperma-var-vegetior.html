Rorippa microsperma var. vegetior

Rorippa microsperma var. vegetior, commonly known as the greater water-cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, alternate leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release minute seeds when mature.

This variant is adapted to wetland habitats and is often found in freshwater ponds, streams, and marshes.
