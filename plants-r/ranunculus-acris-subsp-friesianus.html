Ranunculus acris subsp. friesianus

Ranunculus acris subsp.

friesianus, commonly known as the Friesian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive central tuft of stamens.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a charming, early-flowering plant.
