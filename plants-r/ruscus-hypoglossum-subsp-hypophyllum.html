Ruscus hypoglossum subsp. hypophyllum

Ruscus hypoglossum subsp.

hypophyllum, commonly known as the common ruscus, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in dry environments.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
