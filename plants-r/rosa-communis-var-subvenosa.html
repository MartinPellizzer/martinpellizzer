Rosa communis var. subvenosa

Rosa communis var. subvenosa, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its vigorous growth habit and long, flexible stems.

It typically features large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is known for its ability to climb trellises, walls, or other structures, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and its thorns are usually present along the stems.

This variety is well-suited for garden use and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
