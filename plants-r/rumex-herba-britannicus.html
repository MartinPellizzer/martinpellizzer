Rumex herba-britannicus

Rumex herba-britannicus, commonly known as British dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, wavy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in damp meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses throughout history, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
