Rubus × thyrsoideiformis

Rubus × thyrsoideiformis, commonly known as the thrysoideiform raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
