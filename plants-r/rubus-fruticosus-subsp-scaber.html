Rubus fruticosus subsp. scaber

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

scaber, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

The species is known for its edible fruits, which are widely used in culinary applications.
