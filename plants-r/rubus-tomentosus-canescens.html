Rubus tomentosus canescens

Rubus tomentosus canescens, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, dense foliage, and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black when mature. Its leaves are covered in a fine, white, woolly pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.
