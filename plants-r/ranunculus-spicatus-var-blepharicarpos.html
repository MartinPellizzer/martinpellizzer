Ranunculus spicatus var. blepharicarpos

Ranunculus spicatus var. blepharicarpos, commonly known as the eye-leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features distinctive leaves with a divided, fern-like appearance, and its flowers are usually yellow with five petals. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The variety is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, which gives it its common name.
