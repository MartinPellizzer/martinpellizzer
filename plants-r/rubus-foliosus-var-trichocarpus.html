Rubus foliosus var. trichocarpus

Rubus foliosus var. trichocarpus, commonly known as the hairy-fruited bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
