Rhynchostele maculata subsp. oestlundiana

Rhynchostele maculata subsp.

oestlundiana, commonly known as the spotted rhynchostele, is a member of the Araceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, veined leaves that often display a marbled or spotted pattern.

The plant produces inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
