Rumex bucephalophorus subsp. canariensis

Rumex bucephalophorus subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish underside.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
