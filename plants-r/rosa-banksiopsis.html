Rosa banksiopsis

Rosa banksiopsis, commonly known as the Banks' rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a pleasant fragrance.

Rosa banksiopsis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
