Rosa adscita

Rosa adscita, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit, with long, flexible stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

Rosa adscita is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.
