Rubus hillii

Rubus hillii, commonly known as the Hill's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and acidic. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries.
