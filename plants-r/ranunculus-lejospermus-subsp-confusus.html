Ranunculus lejospermus subsp. confusus

Ranunculus lejospermus subsp.

confusus, commonly known as the confused buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is a relatively rare subspecies, often found in specific ecological niches.
