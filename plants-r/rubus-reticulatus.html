Rubus reticulatus

Rubus reticulatus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically bright red when ripe. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and are widely used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
