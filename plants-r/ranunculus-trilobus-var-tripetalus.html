Ranunculus trilobus var. tripetalus

Ranunculus trilobus var. tripetalus, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a distinctive central glandular spot. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet having three lobes, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
