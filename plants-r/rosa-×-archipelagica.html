Rosa × archipelagica

Rosa × archipelagica, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a hybrid species native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Rosa × archipelagica is relatively disease-resistant and can thrive in a variety of climatic conditions.
