Racosperma gibbosum

Racosperma gibbosum, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its root system helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Racosperma gibbosum is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.
