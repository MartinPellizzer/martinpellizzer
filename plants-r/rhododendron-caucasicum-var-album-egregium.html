Rhododendron caucasicum var. album-egregium

Rhododendron caucasicum var. album-egregium, commonly known as the Caucasian white-flowered rhododendron, is a variant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with acidic, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to the blossoms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological role in its native habitat.
