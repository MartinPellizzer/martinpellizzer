Rosa × asperifolia

Rosa × asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Rosa × asperifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
