Rosa oligocephala

Rosa oligocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. Rosa oligocephala is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
