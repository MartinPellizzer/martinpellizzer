Rhododendron fauriei f. normale

Rhododendron fauriei f. normale, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to China.

It features leathery, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy texture. The plant produces dense clusters of bell-shaped, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
