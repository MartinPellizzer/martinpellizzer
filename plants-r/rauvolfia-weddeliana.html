Rauvolfia weddeliana

Rauvolfia weddeliana, commonly known as the Antarctic vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, white, tubular flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in cold, high-altitude environments. It is found in regions such as South America, Africa, and Australia.

Rauvolfia weddeliana is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of alkaloids in its roots.
