Rumex chymophyllus

Rumex chymophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in a terminal spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.
