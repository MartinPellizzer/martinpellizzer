Rauvolfia woodsoniana

Rauvolfia woodsoniana, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and have a distinctive pinkish-white color.

The roots of this plant are used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
