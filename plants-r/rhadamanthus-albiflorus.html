Rhadamanthus albiflorus

Rhadamanthus albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered rhadamanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clusters at the base.

Rhadamanthus albiflorus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
