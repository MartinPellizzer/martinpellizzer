Rosa veronikae

Rosa veronikae, commonly known as the Veronika rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a subtle fragrance. Rosa veronikae blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, showy flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
