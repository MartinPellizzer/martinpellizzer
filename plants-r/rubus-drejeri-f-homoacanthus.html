Rubus drejeri f. homoacanthus

Rubus drejeri f. homoacanthus, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, thornless stems and dense growth habit. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
