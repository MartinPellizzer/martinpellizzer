Ranunculus orbifolius

Ranunculus orbifolius, commonly known as the globe buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers with five to nine petals that are typically bright yellow or orange. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, rounded shape. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
