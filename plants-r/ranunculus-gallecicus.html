Ranunculus gallecicus

Ranunculus gallecicus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal cliffs. It is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.

Ranunculus gallecicus plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects.
