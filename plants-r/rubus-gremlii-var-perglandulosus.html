Rubus gremlii var. perglandulosus

Rubus gremlii var. perglandulosus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
