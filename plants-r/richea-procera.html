Richea procera

Richea procera, commonly known as the mountain cabbage, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant features small, waxy leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are white or pale blue and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Richea procera is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
