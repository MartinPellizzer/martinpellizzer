Rhododendron citriniflorum subsp. aureolum

Rhododendron citriniflorum subsp.

aureolum, commonly known as the golden-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a glossy surface.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
