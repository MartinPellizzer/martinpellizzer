Ranunculus delvosallei

Ranunculus delvosallei, commonly known as the Delvossalles' buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
