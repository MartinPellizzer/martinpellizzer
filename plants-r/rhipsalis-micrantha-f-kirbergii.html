Rhipsalis micrantha f. kirbergii

Rhipsalis micrantha f. kirbergii, commonly known as the Kirberg's mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, pale green stems that are covered in small, translucent, waxy tubercles. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as moths and bats. Its leaves are modified into short, rounded spines, and it lacks true leaves, relying on its stems for photosynthesis.

This rare and delicate plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in the canopy of rainforests.
