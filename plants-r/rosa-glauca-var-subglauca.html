Rosa glauca var. subglauca

Rosa glauca var. subglauca, commonly known as the pale rose or subglauca rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, red hips that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in cooler climates.
