Ranunculus oxyspermus subsp. damascenus

Ranunculus oxyspermus subsp.

damascenus, commonly known as the Damascus buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
