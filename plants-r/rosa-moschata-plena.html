Rosa moschata plena

Rosa moschata plena, commonly known as the full-blown musk rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its double, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Rosa moschata plena is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated for its perfume and historical significance in perfumery.
