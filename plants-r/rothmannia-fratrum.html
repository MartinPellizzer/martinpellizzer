Rothmannia fratrum

Rothmannia fratrum, commonly known as the African orchid or African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically have tall, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Rothmannia fratrum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as houseplants.
