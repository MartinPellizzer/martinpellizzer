Rubus pyramidalis var. parvifolius

Rubus pyramidalis var. parvifolius, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, trifoliate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
