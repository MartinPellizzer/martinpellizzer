Rhododendron roseatum

Rhododendron roseatum, commonly known as the rose-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron roseatum is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
