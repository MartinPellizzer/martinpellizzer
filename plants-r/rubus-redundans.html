Rubus redundans

Rubus redundans, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
