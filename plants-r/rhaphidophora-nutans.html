Rhaphidophora nutans

Rhaphidophora nutans, commonly known as the hanging arrow plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that droop downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its adaptability to low light makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
