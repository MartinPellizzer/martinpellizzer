Rehsonia floribunda

Rehsonia floribunda, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bell-shaped, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Rehsonia floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
