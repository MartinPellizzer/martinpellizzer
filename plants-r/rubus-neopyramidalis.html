Rubus neopyramidalis

Rubus neopyramidalis, commonly known as the Pyramidal blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
