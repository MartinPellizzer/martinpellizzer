Rhododendron aganniphum var. flavorufum

Rhododendron aganniphum var. flavorufum, commonly known as the fragrant leaved rhododendron, is a rare and unique variant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with a distinctive scent. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring acidic soils and high elevations. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a lush green backdrop to its showy blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its native range.
