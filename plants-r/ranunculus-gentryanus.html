Ranunculus gentryanus

Ranunculus gentryanus, commonly known as the Gentry's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces slender, hairy stems that bear the showy blooms. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in native plant gardens.
