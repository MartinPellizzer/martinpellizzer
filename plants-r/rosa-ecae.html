Rosa ecae

Rosa ecae, commonly known as the Ece rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and the Alps. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and dense foliage. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa ecae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
