Rhododendron mayrii

Rhododendron mayrii, commonly known as the Mayrii rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron mayrii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches.
