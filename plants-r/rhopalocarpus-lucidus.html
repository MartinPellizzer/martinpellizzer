Rhopalocarpus lucidus

Rhopalocarpus lucidus, commonly known as the shining rootstock, is a rare and little-studied plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous, tuberous roots, which are often the primary focus of its botanical interest. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks or in dense undergrowth. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface, contributing to its striking appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it remains a subject of ongoing botanical research.
