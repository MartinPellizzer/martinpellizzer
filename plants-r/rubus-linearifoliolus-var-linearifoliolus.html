Rubus linearifoliolus var. linearifoliolus

Rubus linearifoliolus var. linearifoliolus, commonly known as the slender-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thin, linear leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, dark red berries that ripen in the summer. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
