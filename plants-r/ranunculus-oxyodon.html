Ranunculus oxyodon

Ranunculus oxyodon, commonly known as the beakleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals that resemble a beak, giving it its common name. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
