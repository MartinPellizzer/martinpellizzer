Rhododendron chamahense

Rhododendron chamahense, commonly known as the Chamah rhododendron, is a species native to the Appalachian Mountains of the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils within forested areas. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing dense foliage throughout the growing season.

The Chamah rhododendron is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
