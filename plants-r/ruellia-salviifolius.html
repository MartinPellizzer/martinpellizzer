Ruellia salviifolius

Ruellia salviifolius, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ruellia salviifolius is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
