Rubus nemorosus var. ambifarius

Rubus nemorosus var. ambifarius, commonly known as the two-sided blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is often found growing in association with other brambles.
