Rubus niveus var. racemosus

Rubus niveus var. racemosus, commonly known as the white-bracted raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, giving it its scientific name. Its fruits, which are typically red or yellow, are edible and have a tart flavor.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
