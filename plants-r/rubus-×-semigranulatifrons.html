Rubus × semigranulatifrons

Rubus × semigranulatifrons, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along the edges of forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
