Rosa pellita

Rosa pellita, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa pellita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its fragrant blossoms and hardy nature.
