Rhododendron nubicola

Rhododendron nubicola, commonly known as the Ethiopian rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and inflorescences that are often clustered in racemes.

Rhododendron nubicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local ecosystems of its native habitat.
