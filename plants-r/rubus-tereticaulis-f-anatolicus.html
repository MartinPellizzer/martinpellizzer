Rubus tereticaulis f. anatolicus

Rubus tereticaulis f. anatolicus, commonly known as the Anatolian blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
