Rosa communis var. recognita

Rosa communis var. recognita, commonly known as the recognized rose, is a cultivar of the common rose, characterized by its robust growth and showy flowers.

It typically features large, fragrant blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, depending on the specific variety. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and valuable for its historical and cultural significance in horticulture.
