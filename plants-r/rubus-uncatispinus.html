Rubus uncatispinus

Rubus uncatispinus, commonly known as the thorned blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of thorns along its stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.

The flowers are white and borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
