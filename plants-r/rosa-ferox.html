Rosa ferox

Rosa ferox, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or wild rose, is a species of shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits known as rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa ferox thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
