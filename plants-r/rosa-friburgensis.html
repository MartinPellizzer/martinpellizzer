Rosa friburgensis

Rosa friburgensis, commonly known as the Fribourgh rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Switzerland, particularly the Fribourg canton, from which it gets its name. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and the plant has a woody stem covered in thorns.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
