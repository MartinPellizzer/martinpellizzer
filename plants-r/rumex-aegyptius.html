Rumex aegyptius

Rumex aegyptius, commonly known as Egyptian dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, greenish flowers in spring. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils, often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties, and it is also considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The plant's robust growth and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
