Ranunculus sardous var. angulatus

Ranunculus sardous var. angulatus, commonly known as the Sardinian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
