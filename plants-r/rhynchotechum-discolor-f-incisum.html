Rhynchotechum discolor f. incisum

Rhynchotechum discolor f. incisum, commonly known as the variegated forest fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant features fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance and a pale, variegated coloration. Its leaves are often adorned with marginal teeth, giving it the specific epithet "incisum.". The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in temperate forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its aesthetic qualities.
