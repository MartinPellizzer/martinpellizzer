Rubus idaeus var. exsuccus

Rubus idaeus var. exsuccus, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately lobed. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets, which are clustered around a central core. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications.
