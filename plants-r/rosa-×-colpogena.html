Rosa × colpogena

Rosa × colpogena, commonly known as the everblooming shrub rose, is a hybrid rose species known for its continuous flowering throughout the growing season.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and abundant clusters of fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is native to the United States and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and pruning to maintain its shape and promote blooming.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape design and cut flower arrangements.
