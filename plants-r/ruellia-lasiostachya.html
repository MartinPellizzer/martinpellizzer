Ruellia lasiostachya

Ruellia lasiostachya, commonly known as the rough-stemmed ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its stems are typically rough and branched, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Ruellia lasiostachya is often found in woodland edges and wetlands, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
