Rubus pseudostyriacus

Rubus pseudostyriacus, commonly known as the Austrian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are often used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
