Ranunculus purshii var. polymorphus

Ranunculus purshii var. polymorphus, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, typically yellow or orange flowers with five to nine petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest floors. It has palmate leaves with three to five lobes and produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
