Rhododendron tapelouense

Rhododendron tapelouense, commonly known as the Tapelouen rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forests with rich, acidic soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron tapelouense is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
