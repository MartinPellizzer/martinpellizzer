Ranunculus nemorosus subsp. polyanthemoides

Ranunculus nemorosus subsp.

polyanthemoides, commonly known as the woodland buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in moist, shaded woodlands. It prefers rich, loamy soil and partial to full shade, making it a common sight in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of wetland and woodland ecosystems.
