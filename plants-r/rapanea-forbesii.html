Rapanea forbesii

Rapanea forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as laurel and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that remain green throughout the year. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
