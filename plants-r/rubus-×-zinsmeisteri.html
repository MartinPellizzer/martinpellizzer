Rubus × zinsmeisteri

Rubus × zinsmeisteri, commonly known as the Evergreen Blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, known for its hardy and adaptable nature.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its parent species.
