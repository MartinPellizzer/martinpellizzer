Rosa glauca var. lansica

Rosa glauca var. lansica, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically dark green and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
