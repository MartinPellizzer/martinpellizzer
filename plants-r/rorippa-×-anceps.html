Rorippa × anceps

Rorippa × anceps, commonly known as the common watercress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is a semi-aquatic plant that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found near streams and ditches. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and attract various pollinators.

Rorippa × anceps is known for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
