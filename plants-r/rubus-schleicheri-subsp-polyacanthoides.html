Rubus schleicheri subsp. polyacanthoides

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

polyacanthoides, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that mature in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
