Reseda alba var. myriosperma

Reseda alba var. myriosperma, commonly known as white reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Reseda alba var. myriosperma is also valued for its historical use in dye production and as a source of essential oils.
