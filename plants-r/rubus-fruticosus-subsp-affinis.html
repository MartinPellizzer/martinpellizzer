Rubus fruticosus subsp. affinis

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and north Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These fruits are edible and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.
