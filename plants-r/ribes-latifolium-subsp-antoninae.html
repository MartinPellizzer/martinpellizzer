Ribes latifolium subsp. antoninae

Ribes latifolium subsp.

antoninae, commonly known as the coastal currant, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color, often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or riparian habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to coastal conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
