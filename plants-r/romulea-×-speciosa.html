Romulea × speciosa

Romulea × speciosa, commonly known as the hybrid glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of its parent species, Romulea capensis and Romulea macrantha. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
