Rhamnus persicifolia

Rhamnus persicifolia, commonly known as Persian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions for horticultural purposes.
