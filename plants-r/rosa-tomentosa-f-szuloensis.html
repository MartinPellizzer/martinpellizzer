Rosa tomentosa f. szuloensis

Rosa tomentosa f. szuloensis, commonly known as the Szulónszószó szülőszószó, is a cultivar of the shrub rose.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant is native to Hungary and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and hardy nature make it a popular choice for borders and hedges.
