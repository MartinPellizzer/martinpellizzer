Rhamnus castorea

Rhamnus castorea, commonly known as the Castoreum buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Rhamnus castorea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
