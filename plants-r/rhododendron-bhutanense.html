Rhododendron bhutanense

Rhododendron bhutanense, commonly known as the Bhutan rhododendron, is a species native to the Eastern Himalayas, particularly found in Bhutan, India, and Nepal.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in cool, moist climates with partial shade and well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
