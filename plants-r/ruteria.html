Ruteria

Ruteria, commonly known as the Chinese raspberry, is a genus of flowering plants in the rose family.

It consists of several species, most notably Ruteria xanthocarpa and Ruteria sinensis, which are native to China. These shrubs are characterized by their thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, yellow or orange flowers. The fruits are aggregate berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Ruteria plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and have also been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
