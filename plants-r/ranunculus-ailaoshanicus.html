Ranunculus ailaoshanicus

Ranunculus ailaoshanicus, commonly known as the Ailaoshan buttercup, is a rare and endemic species found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province, China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers can range in color from white to yellow or pink, depending on the specific population.
