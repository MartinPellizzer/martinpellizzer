Rapistrum raphanistrum

Rapistrum raphanistrum, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features a stout, branched stem that often grows up to 1 meter tall, covered with coarse, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing several seeds. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species.
