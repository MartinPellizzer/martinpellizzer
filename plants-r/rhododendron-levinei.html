Rhododendron levinei

Rhododendron levinei, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rhododendron, is a species native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coniferous forests at high elevations. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron levinei thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.
