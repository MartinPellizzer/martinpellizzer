Ranunculus lagascanus

Ranunculus lagascanus, commonly known as the Lagascan water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and ditches. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that float on the water surface or extend underwater. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens, creating a showy, delicate appearance.

Ranunculus lagascanus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
