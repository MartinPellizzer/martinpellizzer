Roulinia sinaloensis

Roulinia sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, with a glossy surface and prominent veins.

Roulinia sinaloensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
