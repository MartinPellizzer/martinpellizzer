Rubus hassleri

Rubus hassleri, commonly known as the Hassler's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
