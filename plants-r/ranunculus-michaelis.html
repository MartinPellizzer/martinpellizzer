Ranunculus michaelis

Ranunculus michaelis, commonly known as the Michael's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in early summer.
