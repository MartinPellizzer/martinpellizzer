Rhamnus tortuosa

Rhamnus tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Rhamnus tortuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
