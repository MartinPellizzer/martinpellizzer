Rosa sherardii f. resinosoides

Rosa sherardii f. resinosoides, commonly known as the resinous shrub rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa sherardii species.

It is characterized by its distinctive production of a resinous substance, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

This variety is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to challenging conditions.
