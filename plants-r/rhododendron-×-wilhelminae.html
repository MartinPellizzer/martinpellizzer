Rhododendron × wilhelminae

Rhododendron × wilhelminae, commonly known as the Wilhelmina rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

The species is native to the eastern United States, where it is found in moist, woodland habitats.
