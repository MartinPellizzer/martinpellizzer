Rubus × hallandicus

Rubus × hallandicus, commonly known as the Halland blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus fruticosus and Rubus ursinus. This species is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
