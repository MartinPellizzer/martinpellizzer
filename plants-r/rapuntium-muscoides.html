Rapuntium muscoides

Rapuntium muscoides, commonly known as the musk lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and early summer. Its roots are tuberous and can be harvested for culinary or medicinal use.

The species is noted for its distinctive musky scent, which is more pronounced in its flowers and leaves.
