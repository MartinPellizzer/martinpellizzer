Rubus erythrinellus

Rubus erythrinellus, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
