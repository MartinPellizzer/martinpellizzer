Rytidosperma bonthainicum

Rytidosperma bonthainicum, commonly known as the Bonthain's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds.

Rytidosperma bonthainicum is often found in coastal dunes and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
