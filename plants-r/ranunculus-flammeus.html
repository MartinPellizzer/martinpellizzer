Ranunculus flammeus

Ranunculus flammeus, commonly known as the flame buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange petals and its distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus flammeus is a herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
