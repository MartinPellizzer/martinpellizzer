Rubus spectatus

Rubus spectatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of botanical study.
