Rubus obrosus var. commiscibilis

Rubus obrosus var. commiscibilis, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to deep red as they mature. Its flowers are white with five petals and are typically pollinated by insects.

This variety is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in jams and desserts.
