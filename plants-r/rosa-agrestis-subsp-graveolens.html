Rosa agrestis subsp. graveolens

Rosa agrestis subsp.

graveolens, commonly known as the wild sweetbriar, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
