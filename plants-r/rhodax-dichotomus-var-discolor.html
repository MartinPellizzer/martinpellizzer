Rhodax dichotomus var. discolor

Rhodax dichotomus var. discolor, commonly known as the variable dichotomous rhodax, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal parts. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the varietal form. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.
