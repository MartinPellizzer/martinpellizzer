Rosa concinna

Rosa concinna, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and serrated, while its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rosa concinna is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal remedies.
