Rosa arvensis var. ovata

Rosa arvensis var. ovata, commonly known as the ovalleaf dog rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate in shape, with serrated edges, and the plant produces rose hips that are often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and woodland edges, and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
