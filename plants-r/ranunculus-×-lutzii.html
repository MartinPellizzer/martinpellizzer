Ranunculus × lutzii

Ranunculus × lutzii, commonly known as the Lutz hybrid buttercup, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ranunculus bulbosus and Ranunculus flammula.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with finely divided, lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its botanical characteristics include a short rhizomatous root system and a preference for cool, temperate climates.

Ranunculus × lutzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
