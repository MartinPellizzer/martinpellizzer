Rosa glauca var. pseudofalcata

Rosa glauca var. pseudofalcata, commonly known as the blue rose or bluebush rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are usually gray-green, covered in a fine layer of white waxy hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient plant in horticultural settings.
