Reseda lutea subvar. minor

Reseda lutea subvar. minor, commonly known as lesser yellowweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
