Rhododendron danbaense

Rhododendron danbaense, commonly known as the Danba rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in Sichuan Province.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron danbaense is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant role in the local biodiversity of its native habitat.
