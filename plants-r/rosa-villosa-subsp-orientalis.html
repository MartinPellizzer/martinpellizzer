Rosa villosa subsp. orientalis

Rosa villosa subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental bristly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong fragrance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
