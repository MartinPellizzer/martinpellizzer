Rosa sauvifolia

Rosa sauvifolia, commonly known as the silky rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its soft, silvery-white undersides on its leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Rosa sauvifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
