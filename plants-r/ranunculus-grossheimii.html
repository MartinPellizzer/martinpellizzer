Ranunculus grossheimii

Ranunculus grossheimii, commonly known as the Grossheim's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow petals and divided leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
