Rubus mulleri var. disjectus

Rubus mulleri var. disjectus, commonly known as the scattered blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to China, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a glossy surface. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
