Ranunculus wettsteinii

Ranunculus wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often accompanied by numerous sepals that resemble additional petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ranunculus wettsteinii is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
