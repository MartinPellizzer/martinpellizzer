Rosa praetermissa

Rosa praetermissa, commonly known as the "neglected rose," is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland and scrubland habitats. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers with a distinctive fragrance, and its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets. Rosa praetermissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.

It is also notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
