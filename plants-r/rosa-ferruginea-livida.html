Rosa ferruginea livida

Rosa ferruginea livida, commonly known as the rusty-leaved rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves that turn reddish-brown with age. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in a distinctive rust-colored bloom, which gives it its common name.

Rosa ferruginea livida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
