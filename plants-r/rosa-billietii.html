Rosa billietii

Rosa billietii, commonly known as the Madagascar rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to the tropical climate of Madagascar and its role in local ecosystems.
