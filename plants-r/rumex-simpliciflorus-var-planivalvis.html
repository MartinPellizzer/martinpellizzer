Rumex simpliciflorus var. planivalvis

Rumex simpliciflorus var. planivalvis, commonly known as the broadleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a wavy, serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. Its stems are upright and often hairy, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and roadsides.

The leaves and stems are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their astringent properties.
