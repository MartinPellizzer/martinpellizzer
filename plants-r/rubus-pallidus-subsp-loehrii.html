Rubus pallidus subsp. loehrii

Rubus pallidus subsp.

loehrii, commonly known as the pale raspberry. It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its slender stems and small, pale pink flowers.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically less flavorful compared to other raspberry species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
