Rubus spiculatus

Rubus spiculatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and strawberries.
