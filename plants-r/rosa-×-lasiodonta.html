Rosa × lasiodonta

Rosa × lasiodonta, commonly known as the everblooming roses, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × centifolia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season. The plant features deep green, serrated leaves and produces fragrant, semi-double to full blooms in shades of pink, red, and white. It is well-suited for coastal gardens due to its salt tolerance and hardiness.

The botanical name reflects its hybrid origin and its distinctive, long-lasting floral display.
