Rosa cladobotrys

Rosa cladobotrys, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
