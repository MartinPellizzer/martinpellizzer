Rubus pseudohedycarpus

Rubus pseudohedycarpus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
