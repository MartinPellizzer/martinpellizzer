Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp. spitzneri

Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp.

spitzneri, commonly known as the Spitzner's bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage, which provide habitat for various wildlife species.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. Its fruits are edible berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
