Ruellia drushelii var. macrocarpa

Ruellia drushelii var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
