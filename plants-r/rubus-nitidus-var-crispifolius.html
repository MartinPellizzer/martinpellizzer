Rubus nitidus var. crispifolius

Rubus nitidus var. crispifolius, commonly known as the crinkled-leaf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical studies.
