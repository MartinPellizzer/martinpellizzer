Rosa wichurana var. plena

Rosa wichurana var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered shrub rose, is a hybrid cultivar originating from the cross between Rosa wichurana and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms that are typically pink or white in color, with a rich, fragrant scent. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, exhibiting vigorous growth and a compact, bushy habit. It is prized in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in floral arrangements due to its abundant flowering.

The variety is relatively disease-resistant and requires minimal pruning to maintain its shape and promote continuous blooming.
