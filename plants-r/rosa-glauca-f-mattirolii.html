Rosa glauca f. mattirolii

Rosa glauca f. mattirolii, commonly known as the Mattiroli's blue rose, is a unique form of the blue rose, characterized by its deep blue to violet petals.

This cultivar is a naturally occurring variant of Rosa glauca, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The flowers are typically small to medium in size, with a compact growth habit and a strong, sweet fragrance. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a prized specimen among rose enthusiasts.
