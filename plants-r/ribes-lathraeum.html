Ribes lathraeum

Ribes lathraeum, commonly known as red gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that turn red when mature, often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Ribes lathraeum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
