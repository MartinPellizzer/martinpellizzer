Rubus × lechepoeus

Rubus × lechepoeus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (the raspberry).

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible berries, which are typically dark red to black in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful fruits.

The hybrid nature of Rubus × lechepoeus contributes to its adaptability and fruit quality.
