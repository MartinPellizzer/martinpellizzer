Ramaliella longipapposa

Ramaliella longipapposa, commonly known as the long-pappus ramaliella, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces distinctive, elongated pappus structures on its seeds, which aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
