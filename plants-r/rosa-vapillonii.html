Rosa vapillonii

Rosa vapillonii, commonly known as the Vapillon's rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers bloom in late summer.

Rosa vapillonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
