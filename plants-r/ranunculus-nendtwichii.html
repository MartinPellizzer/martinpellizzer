Ranunculus nendtwichii

Ranunculus nendtwichii, commonly known as the Nendtwich buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
