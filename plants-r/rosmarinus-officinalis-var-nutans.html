Rosmarinus officinalis var. nutans

Rosmarinus officinalis var. nutans, commonly known as the drooping rosemary, is a fragrant evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features needle-like leaves that are dark green and have a strong, pine-like aroma. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and ornamental value.
