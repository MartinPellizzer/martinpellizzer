Rubus mucronifer var. atrichantherus

Rubus mucronifer var. atrichantherus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This variety is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
