Ranunculus cochlearifolius

Ranunculus cochlearifolius, commonly known as the spiralleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
