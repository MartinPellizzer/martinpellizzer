Radermachera coriacea

Radermachera coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leafed sterculia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.
