Renealmia pyramidalis

Renealmia pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal renealmia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its upright, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Renealmia pyramidalis is often found in wetland habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
