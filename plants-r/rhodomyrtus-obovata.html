Rhodomyrtus obovata

Rhodomyrtus obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf rosemyrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a distinctive whitish or pinkish bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in spring. Rhodomyrtus obovata is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
