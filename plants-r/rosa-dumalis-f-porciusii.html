Rosa dumalis f. porciusii

Rosa dumalis f. porciusii, commonly known as the Porciusii Rosa, is a compact, shrub-like rose native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, typically in late summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces hips that are often used in traditional medicine. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil.
