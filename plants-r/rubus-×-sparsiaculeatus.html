Rubus × sparsiaculeatus

Rubus × sparsiaculeatus, commonly known as the sparsely branched blackberry.

This plant is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. The leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is a cluster of small drupes, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.
