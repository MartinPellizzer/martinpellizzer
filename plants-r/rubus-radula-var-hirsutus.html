Rubus radula var. hirsutus

Rubus radula var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy dewberry, is a variant of the dewberry species native to parts of North America.

It is a trailing shrub characterized by its hairy stems and small, often reddish stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
