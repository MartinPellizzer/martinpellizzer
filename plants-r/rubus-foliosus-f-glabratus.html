Rubus foliosus f. glabratus

Rubus foliosus f. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth-leafed blackberry.

This plant is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically smooth and glabrous. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. The species is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.
