Rosa magianica

Rosa magianica, commonly known as the Magian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are thorny.

Rosa magianica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
