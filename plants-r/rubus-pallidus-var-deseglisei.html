Rubus pallidus var. deseglisei

Rubus pallidus var. deseglisei, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically pale green in color. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
