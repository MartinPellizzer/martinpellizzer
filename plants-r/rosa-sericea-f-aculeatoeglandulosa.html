Rosa sericea f. aculeatoeglandulosa

Rosa sericea f. aculeatoeglandulosa, commonly known as the silky rose with prickly glands, is a botanical variant of the silky rose species.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-hairy foliage and thorny stems with glandular structures. The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers, typically in clusters, during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often covered in fine, silky hairs.

This subspecies is native to certain regions of Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
