Rosa elliptica f. confusa

Rosa elliptica f. confusa, commonly known as the confused elliptic rose, is a botanical variant of the elliptic rose.

It is characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. The flowers are typically pink to red, with a fragrant scent, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This form is often found in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
