Rosa arvensis var. parvifolia

Rosa arvensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the creeping red sanders, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and the plant is known for its fragrant flowers.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
