Rhynchospora wallichiana

Rhynchospora wallichiana, commonly known as the Wallich's spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and tapering, with a distinctive sheathing base.

Rhynchospora wallichiana is often found in temperate regions of Asia, including the Himalayas and parts of southern China.
