Rosa sternbergii

Rosa sternbergii, commonly known as the Sternberg rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long pedicels. Rosa sternbergii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
