Rubus dumetorum var. glandulosus

Rubus dumetorum var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with glandular dots on the underside. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The glandular hairs on its leaves and stems are a distinguishing feature of this variety.
