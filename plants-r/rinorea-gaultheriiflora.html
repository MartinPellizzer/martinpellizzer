Rinorea gaultheriiflora

Rinorea gaultheriiflora, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

Rinorea gaultheriiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
