Rhododendron delavayi var. adenostylum

Rhododendron delavayi var. adenostylum, commonly known as the Delavay's azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. It is often found in forest understories and alpine meadows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
