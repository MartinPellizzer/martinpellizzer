Rosa mollis f. improdiens

Rosa mollis f. improdiens, commonly known as the soft rose or the silky rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a hybrid form of Rosa mollis, characterized by its soft, velvety petals and a delicate, fragrant aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, and it produces clusters of small, pink to white flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological studies.
