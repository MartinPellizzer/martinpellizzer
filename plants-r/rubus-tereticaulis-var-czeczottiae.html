Rubus tereticaulis var. czeczottiae

Rubus tereticaulis var. czeczottiae, commonly known as the Czeczottia raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, shrub-like growth habit and prickly stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually serrated along the edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
