Rheum emodi

Rheum emodi, commonly known as the Bhutanese rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, Bhutan, and India.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and thick, edible stalks that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive issues and inflammation. Rheum emodi thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often growing in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil.

Its rhubarb-like appearance and unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
