Rosa viscaria var. argus

Rosa viscaria var. argus, commonly known as the Argus rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The Argus rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
