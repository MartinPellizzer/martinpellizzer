Rosa rubiginosa var. resinosa

Rosa rubiginosa var. resinosa, commonly known as the resinous rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance from its stems, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This hardy species is often used in hedges and for its ornamental value.
