Rubus idaeifolius

Rubus idaeifolius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy shrub in temperate climates.

Botanically, it is notable for its hirsute appearance and its role in local ecosystems.
