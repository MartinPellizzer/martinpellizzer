Rhysolepis fusiformis

Rhysolepis fusiformis, commonly known as the slender-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
