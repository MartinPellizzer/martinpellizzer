Rubus genevieri subsp. discerptus

Rubus genevieri subsp.

discerptus, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
