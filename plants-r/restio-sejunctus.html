Restio sejunctus

Restio sejunctus, commonly known as the slender restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
