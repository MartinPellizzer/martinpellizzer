Rhynchospora fascicularis var. trichoides

Rhynchospora fascicularis var. trichoides, commonly known as the hairy spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. The variety trichoides is distinguished by its hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance compared to other varieties of Rhynchospora. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
