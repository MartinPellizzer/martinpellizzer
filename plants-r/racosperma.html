Racosperma

Racosperma, commonly known as the genus of leguminous plants, includes species that are native to various regions of Australia.

These plants are characterized by their compound leaves and produce pods that contain edible seeds. Many species within the genus are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. They play an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil through their symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Some species are cultivated for their ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
