Rubus hebecaulis var. villosifolius

Rubus hebecaulis var. villosifolius, commonly known as the hairy-leaved blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, typically found in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often tart.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
