Rosa repens var. parvifolia

Rosa repens var. parvifolia, commonly known as the creeping rose or sand rose, is a low-growing shrub that thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically narrow and finely divided. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover in gardens. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in coastal areas and dry landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively easy to cultivate and resistant to pests and diseases.
