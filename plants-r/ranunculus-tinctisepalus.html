Ranunculus tinctisepalus

Ranunculus tinctisepalus, commonly known as the purple-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive purple or reddish sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its short lifespan, often completing its life cycle within a single growing season.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
