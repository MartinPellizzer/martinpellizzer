Rosa globularis var. adenophora

Rosa globularis var. adenophora, commonly known as the腺花月季, is a compact, shrub-like rose native to China.

It features dense, spherical growth with small, dark green leaves and produces abundant clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant is noted for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens for its persistent blooms.

The variety is distinguished by its glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, contributing to its unique botanical characteristics.
