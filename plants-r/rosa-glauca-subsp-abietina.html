Rosa glauca subsp. abietina

Rosa glauca subsp.

abietina, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, humid environments. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure. Rosa glauca subsp.

abietina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
