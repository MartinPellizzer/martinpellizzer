Rosa hudsonica

Rosa hudsonica, commonly known as the Hudson's rose, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, downy hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Rosa hudsonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
