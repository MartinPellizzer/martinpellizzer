Rosa × obovata

Rosa × obovata, commonly known as the flat-leaf rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including China and Korea, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and is adapted to temperate climates.

Rosa × obovata is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
