Rubus idaeus var. arizonicus

Rubus idaeus var. arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions.

Its flowering period occurs in early spring, followed by fruiting in late summer.
