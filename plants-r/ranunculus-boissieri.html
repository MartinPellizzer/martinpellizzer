Ranunculus boissieri

Ranunculus boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
