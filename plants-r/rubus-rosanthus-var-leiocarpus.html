Rubus rosanthus var. leiocarpus

Rubus rosanthus var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are typically smooth and firm, distinguishing it from other Rubus species. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

This variant is valued for its flavorful fruit and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
