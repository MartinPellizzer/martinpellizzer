Ribes nigrum

Ribes nigrum, commonly known as blackcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamin C. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are used in culinary applications, jams, and as a source of natural pigments.
