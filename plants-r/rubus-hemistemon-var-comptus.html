Rubus hemistemon var. comptus

Rubus hemistemon var. comptus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them a valuable resource for both wildlife and humans.
