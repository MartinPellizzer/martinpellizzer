Rubus moluccanus var. roridus

Rubus moluccanus var. roridus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are usually red when ripe and are often used in local cuisines or for ornamental purposes.
