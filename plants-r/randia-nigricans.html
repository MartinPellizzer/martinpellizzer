Randia nigricans

Randia nigricans, commonly known as black randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by dark blue to black berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.
