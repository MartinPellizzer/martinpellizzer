Rosa fraudulenta

Rosa fraudulenta, commonly known as the false rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa fraudulenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and hardy nature.
