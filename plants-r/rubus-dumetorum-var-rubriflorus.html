Rubus dumetorum var. rubriflorus

Rubus dumetorum var. rubriflorus, commonly known as the redflower bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. Its flowers are distinctive, featuring five pink to reddish petals and yellow stamens.

This species is adapted to rocky and dry environments, often growing in alpine or subalpine zones.
