Rhinanthus borbasii var. rapaicsianus

Rhinanthus borbasii var. rapaicsianus, commonly known as the Carpathian yellow-rimmed knapweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe, particularly the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive red or orange rim, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

It is often found in nutrient-poor soils and is considered a rare species in some regions.
