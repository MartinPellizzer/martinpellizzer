Romnalda strobilacea

Romnalda strobilacea, commonly known as the red-stemmed flax, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Romnalda strobilacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
