Ranunculus strongylus

Ranunculus strongylus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. The plant has palmately divided, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its attractive blooms and ecological value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
