Rubus adornatus var. purchasianus

Rubus adornatus var. purchasianus, commonly known as the purple-leafed bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple foliage, which contrasts with its green stems and small white flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural study.
