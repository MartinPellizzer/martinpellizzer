Rosa × andegavensis var. typica

Rosa × andegavensis var. typica, commonly known as the Andegavensis rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the United States, specifically the southeastern region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with multiple stems and a dense, rounded form. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that are typically borne in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop for the blooms.

Rosa × andegavensis var. typica is well-suited to warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
