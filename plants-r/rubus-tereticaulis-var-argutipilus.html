Rubus tereticaulis var. argutipilus

Rubus tereticaulis var. argutipilus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
