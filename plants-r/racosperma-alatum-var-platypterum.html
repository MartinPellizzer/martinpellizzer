Racosperma alatum var. platypterum

Racosperma alatum var. platypterum, commonly known as the broad-winged acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
