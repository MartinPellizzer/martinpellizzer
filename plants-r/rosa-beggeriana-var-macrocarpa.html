Rosa beggeriana var. macrocarpa

Rosa beggeriana var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited rock rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.
