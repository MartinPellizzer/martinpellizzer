Ranunculus pascuinus

Ranunculus pascuinus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

Ranunculus pascuinus is known for its early blooming season, often appearing in spring.
