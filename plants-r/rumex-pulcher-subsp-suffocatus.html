Rumex pulcher subsp. suffocatus

Rumex pulcher subsp.

suffocatus, commonly known as the purple sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, reddish-purple leaves with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
