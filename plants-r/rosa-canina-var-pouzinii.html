Rosa canina var. pouzinii

Rosa canina var. pouzinii, commonly known as the French rose or the dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, simple leaves, and fragrant pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
