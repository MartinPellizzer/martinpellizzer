Rubus vestii f. grosseserratus

Rubus vestii f. grosseserratus, commonly known as the large-toothed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive, large serrated leaves. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
