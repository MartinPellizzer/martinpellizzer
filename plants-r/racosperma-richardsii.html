Racosperma richardsii

Racosperma richardsii, commonly known as the Richards' acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions. Its root system helps fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is often used in rehabilitation of degraded lands.
