Rosa leveillei var. viridifolia

Rosa leveillei var. viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leafed rose, is a rare and unique variant of the Rosa leveillei species.

It is characterized by its lush, dark green foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its delicate pink to white flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically larger and more elongated compared to other rose varieties, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare flora.
