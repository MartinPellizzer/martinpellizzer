Rumex nepalensis var. remotiflorus

Rumex nepalensis var. remotiflorus, commonly known as the remote-flowered sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The species is characterized by its distinct floral structure and adaptability to high-altitude conditions.
