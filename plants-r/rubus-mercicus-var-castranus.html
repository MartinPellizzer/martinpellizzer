Rubus mercicus var. castranus

Rubus mercicus var. castranus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
