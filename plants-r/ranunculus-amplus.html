Ranunculus amplus

Ranunculus amplus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ranunculus amplus is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest edges, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
