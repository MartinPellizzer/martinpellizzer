Rumex bucephalophorus

Rumex bucephalophorus, commonly known as the broadleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a distinctive whitish or pale green waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Rumex bucephalophorus is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
