Rosa jactans

Rosa jactans, commonly known as the spreading bush rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is characterized by its arching stems and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are often borne in clusters at the ends of the branches. Rosa jactans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and adaptable member of the Rosaceae family.
