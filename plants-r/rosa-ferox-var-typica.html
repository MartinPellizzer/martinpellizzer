Rosa ferox var. typica

Rosa ferox var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its fruits are edible berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic pulp.

Rosa ferox var. typica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
