Ranunculus flammula subsp. scoticus

Ranunculus flammula subsp.

scoticus, commonly known as the Scottish buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, and its stems are slender and often hairy. It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, including the British Isles.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family, sharing similar botanical features.
