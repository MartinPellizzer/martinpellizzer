Rubus radulicaulis

Rubus radulicaulis, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and they bloom in the spring before the leaves fully develop.
