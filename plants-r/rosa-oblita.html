Rosa oblita

Rosa oblita, commonly known as the Himalayan rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are dark green and serrated, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa oblita is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
