Rubus × semiglaucellus

Rubus × semiglaucellus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
