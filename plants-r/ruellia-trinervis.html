Ruellia trinervis

Ruellia trinervis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Ruellia trinervis is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscape designs.
