Rubus meridionalis

Rubus meridionalis, commonly known as the southern blackberry, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in woodland or disturbed areas.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable food source for both wildlife and humans.
