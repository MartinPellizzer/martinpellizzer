Rubus infestus var. drejeri

Rubus infestus var. drejeri, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
