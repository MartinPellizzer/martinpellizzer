Restrepia vittata

Restrepia vittata, commonly known as the golden restia, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with several leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and often have a faint fragrance, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Restrepia vittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
