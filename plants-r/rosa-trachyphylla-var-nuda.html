Rosa trachyphylla var. nuda

Rosa trachyphylla var. nuda, commonly known as the naked-stemmed rose, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features thornless stems and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
