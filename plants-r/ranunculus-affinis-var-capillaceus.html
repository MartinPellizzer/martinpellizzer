Ranunculus affinis var. capillaceus

Ranunculus affinis var. capillaceus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5-15 sepals that resemble petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a hairy surface. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The variety capillaceus is distinguished by its slender stems and fine, hairy texture.
