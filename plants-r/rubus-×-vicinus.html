Rubus × vicinus

Rubus × vicinus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in fruit size and color.
