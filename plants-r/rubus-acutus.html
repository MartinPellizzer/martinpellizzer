Rubus acutus

Rubus acutus, commonly known as the sharpleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
