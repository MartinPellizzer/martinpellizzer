Rhamnus rugulosa var. chekiangensis

Rhamnus rugulosa var. chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chekiang buckthorn, is a shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a distinctive, glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
