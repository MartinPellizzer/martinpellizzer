Rubus koehleri proles jacquelii

Rubus koehleri proles jacquelii, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
