Ranunculus wutaishanicus

Ranunculus wutaishanicus, commonly known as the Wutaishan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces yellow or white flowers in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
