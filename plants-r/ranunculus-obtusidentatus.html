Ranunculus obtusidentatus

Ranunculus obtusidentatus, commonly known as the blunt-toothed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features showy, bright yellow flowers with five rounded petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges that give it a distinctive appearance.

The species is characterized by its short, stout stems and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.
