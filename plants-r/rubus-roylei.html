Rubus roylei

Rubus roylei, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas.

Rubus roylei is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
