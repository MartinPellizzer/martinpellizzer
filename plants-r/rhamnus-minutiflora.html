Rhamnus minutiflora

Rhamnus minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf-flowered buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Rhamnus minutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
