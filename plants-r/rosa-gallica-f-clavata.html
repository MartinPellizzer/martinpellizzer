Rosa gallica f. clavata

Rosa gallica f. clavata, commonly known as the Clavate Gallica rose, is a compact shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has a rounded, bushy form. It is well-suited for small gardens or containers due to its manageable size.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements and have a long history in horticulture and perfumery.
