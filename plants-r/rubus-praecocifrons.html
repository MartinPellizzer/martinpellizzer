Rubus praecocifrons

Rubus praecocifrons, commonly known as the early-fruited blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen early in the growing season. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus praecocifrons is an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
