Rostellularia procumbens var. hirsuta

Rostellularia procumbens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rostellularia, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
