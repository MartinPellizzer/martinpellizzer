Rubus pilulifer

Rubus pilulifer, commonly known as the pill-fruit bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or black in color. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and they bloom in the spring.

Rubus pilulifer is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
