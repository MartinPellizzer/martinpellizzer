Rosa villosa subsp. alpicola

Rosa villosa subsp.

alpicola, commonly known as the alpine evergreen rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, contributing to its common name.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to withstand harsh, cold climates.
