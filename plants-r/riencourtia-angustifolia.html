Riencourtia angustifolia

Riencourtia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Riencourtia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
