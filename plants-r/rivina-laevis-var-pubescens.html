Rivina laevis var. pubescens

Rivina laevis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance. It produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
