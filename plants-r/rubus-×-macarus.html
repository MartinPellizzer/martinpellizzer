Rubus × macarus

Rubus × macarus, commonly known as the Macarthur blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
