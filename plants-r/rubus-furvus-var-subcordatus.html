Rubus furvus var. subcordatus

Rubus furvus var. subcordatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are usually narrow and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value in gardens.
