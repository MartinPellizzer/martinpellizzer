Rubus × sabranskyanus

Rubus × sabranskyanus, commonly known as the Sabranskyan blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its edible berries, which have a rich, tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
