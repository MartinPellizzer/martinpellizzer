Rosa dumalis var. laxifolia

Rosa dumalis var. laxifolia, commonly known as the slender-stemmed wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant produces pink to white petals and has compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
