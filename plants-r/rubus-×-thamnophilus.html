Rubus × thamnophilus

Rubus × thamnophilus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, similar to other raspberry species. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine landscapes.
