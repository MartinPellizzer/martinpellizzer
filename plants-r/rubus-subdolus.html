Rubus subdolus

Rubus subdolus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to eastern North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark purple fruits. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and pies.
