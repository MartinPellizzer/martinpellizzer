Rhododendron meridionale

Rhododendron meridionale, commonly known as the Southern Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. It is often found in forested areas and along stream banks, where it forms part of the understory vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across temperate regions.
