Rubus omalus subsp. thelybatos

Rubus omalus subsp.

thelybatos, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Americas. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate of small drupes that ripen to a deep red or black color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
