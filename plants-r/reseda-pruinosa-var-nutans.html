Reseda pruinosa var. nutans

Reseda pruinosa var. nutans, commonly known as the nodding reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving the plant a distinctive, waxy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
