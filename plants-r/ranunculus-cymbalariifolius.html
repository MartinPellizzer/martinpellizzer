Ranunculus cymbalariifolius

Ranunculus cymbalariifolius, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble those of a cymbal, giving it its scientific name.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in spring and is often found in high-altitude meadows.
