Rhododendron nanothamnum

Rhododendron nanothamnum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green in color and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial shade to full sun.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
