Rosa lasiosepala

Rosa lasiosepala, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a soft, hairy texture.

Rosa lasiosepala is well adapted to high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun.
