Rubus × gothicus var. lidforssii

Rubus × gothicus var. lidforssii, commonly known as the Swedish blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
