Ranunculus lanuginosus var. parvulus

Ranunculus lanuginosus var. parvulus, commonly known as the small woolly buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lobed and covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
