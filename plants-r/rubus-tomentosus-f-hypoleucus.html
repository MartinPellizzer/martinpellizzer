Rubus tomentosus f. hypoleucus

Rubus tomentosus f. hypoleucus, commonly known as the white-haired blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its dense, white to greyish pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry. This form is native to North America and thrives in moist, wooded areas.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
