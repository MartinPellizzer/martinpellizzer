Rollinia edulis var. acuta

Rollinia edulis var. acuta, commonly known as the sweet sop or cupuacu, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a large, oblong fruit with a thick, spiny rind and a creamy, aromatic pulp. The fruit is rich in nutrients, including vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making it a valuable food source.

It is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value in warm climates.
