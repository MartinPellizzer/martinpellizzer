Rhynchospora caduciglumis

Rhynchospora caduciglumis, commonly known as the slender beak rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Rhynchospora caduciglumis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
