Rhododendron aberconwayi

Rhododendron aberconwayi, commonly known as the Aberconway rhododendron, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are critical for its survival.
