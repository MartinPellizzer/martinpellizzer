Rhododendron reticulatum f. ciliatum

Rhododendron reticulatum f. ciliatum, commonly known as the ciliate rhododendron, is a compact, broad-leaved evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it a striking visual contrast. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and shaded borders.
