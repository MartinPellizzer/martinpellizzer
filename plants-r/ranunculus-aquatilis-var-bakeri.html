Ranunculus aquatilis var. bakeri

Ranunculus aquatilis var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's water crowfoot, is a aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. The plant features delicate, floating leaves with a waxy surface and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of water quality.
