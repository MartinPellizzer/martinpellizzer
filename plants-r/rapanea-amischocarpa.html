Rapanea amischocarpa

Rapanea amischocarpa, commonly known as the Malabar tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy dark green surface with a fragrant aroma.

The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are edible.
