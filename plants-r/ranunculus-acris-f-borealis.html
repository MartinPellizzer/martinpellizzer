Ranunculus acris f. borealis

Ranunculus acris f. borealis, commonly known as the northern meadow buttercup, is a botanical variant of the meadow buttercup.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and tundra regions. It has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

This subspecies is adapted to cooler climates and is often found in northern temperate and subarctic regions.
