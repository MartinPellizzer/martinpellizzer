Raphia ruwenzorica

Raphia ruwenzorica, commonly known as the Ruwenzori palm, is a large, herbaceous plant native to the tropical rainforests of East Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of the Arecaceae family. Its fibrous leaves are used by local communities for thatching and weaving.

Raphia ruwenzorica plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
