Rubus omalus var. cuneatus

Rubus omalus var. cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, dark purple to black fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. Its flowering period occurs in spring, producing white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value in gardens.
