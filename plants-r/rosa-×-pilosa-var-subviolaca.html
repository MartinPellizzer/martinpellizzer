Rosa × pilosa var. subviolaca

Rosa × pilosa var. subviolaca, commonly known as the purple sandalwood rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers.

It produces semi-double to fully double blooms in shades of pink to deep purple, often with a bluish tinge. The plant has dark green, slightly serrated leaves and thornless stems, making it suitable for container gardening and small spaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering and pruning to maintain its shape.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant compared to other rose cultivars.
