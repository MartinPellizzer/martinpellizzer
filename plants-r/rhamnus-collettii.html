Rhamnus collettii

Rhamnus collettii, commonly known as the Collett's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental and ecological value.
