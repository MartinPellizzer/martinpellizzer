Rubus koehleri var. vogesicola

Rubus koehleri var. vogesicola, commonly known as the Vogesicola blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically clustered together. The plant has thorny stems and broad, trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its flowers are pink to white and appear in the spring before the fruiting season.
