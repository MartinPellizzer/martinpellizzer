Rosa urbica var. semiglabra

Rosa urbica var. semiglabra, commonly known as the urban rose, is a variant of the common rose that thrives in urban environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, less hairy leaves compared to other rose varieties. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a delicate fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This plant is well adapted to tolerate pollution and compacted soils, making it suitable for city gardens and street-side plantings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in urban settings.
