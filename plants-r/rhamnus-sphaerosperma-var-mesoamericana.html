Rhamnus sphaerosperma var. mesoamericana

Rhamnus sphaerosperma var. mesoamericana, commonly known as the Mesoamerican buckthorn, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and smooth, thornless stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by reddish or purplish berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and scrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
