Rheum cordatum

Rheum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red berries. Rheum cordatum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
