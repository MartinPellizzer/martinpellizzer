Rosa sherardii f. pseudomollis

Rosa sherardii f. pseudomollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved shrub rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa sherardii.

It is characterized by its finely pubescent, soft-textured foliage, which distinguishes it from the typical form of the species. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
