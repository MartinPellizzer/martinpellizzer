Ranunculus acris var. villosus

Ranunculus acris var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its hairy foliage and striking blooms, it is a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
