Rubus castellarnaui

Rubus castellarnaui, commonly known as the Castellana raspberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rubus genus in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula, particularly in Spain, where it thrives in rocky and sunny habitats. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
