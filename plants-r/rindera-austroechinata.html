Rindera austroechinata

Rindera austroechinata, commonly known as the southern echinata, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves with a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact cluster.

Rindera austroechinata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
