Rhododendron cravenii

Rhododendron cravenii, commonly known as the Craven's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude forests and moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, with a deep green color, and it has a dense, compact growth habit.

Rhododendron cravenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local ecosystems of its native habitat.
