Rubus × coarctatiflorus var. roseolus

Rubus × coarctatiflorus var. roseolus, commonly known as the pink-stemmed blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are often a distinctive pinkish hue, and its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
