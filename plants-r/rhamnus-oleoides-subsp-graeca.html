Rhamnus oleoides subsp. graeca

Rhamnus oleoides subsp.

graeca, commonly known as Greek buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

The plant bears black, egg-shaped berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
