Rosa glutinosa var. lubensis

Rosa glutinosa var. lubensis, commonly known as the Lubens rose, is a shrub-like species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late summer. This variety is known for its strong fragrance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a range of garden environments.
