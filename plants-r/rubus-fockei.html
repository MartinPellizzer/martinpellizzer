Rubus fockei

Rubus fockei, commonly known as the Focke raspberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
