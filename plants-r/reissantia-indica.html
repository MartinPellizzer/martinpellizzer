Reissantia indica

Reissantia indica, commonly known as the Indian starfruit or the golden apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into the characteristic star-shaped fruit. The fruit has a sweet, acidic flavor and is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
