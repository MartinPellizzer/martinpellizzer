Rumohra simplicior

Rumohra simplicior, commonly known as the simple grass or lesser grass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
