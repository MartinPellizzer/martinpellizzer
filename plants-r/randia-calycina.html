Randia calycina

Randia calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red root, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces woody, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Randia calycina is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some areas.
