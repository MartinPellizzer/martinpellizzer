Rapanea grandifolia

Rapanea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved rapanea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green and have a fragrant aroma. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and potential medicinal properties.
