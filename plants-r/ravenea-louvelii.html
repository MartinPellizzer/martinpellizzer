Ravenea louvelii

Ravenea louvelii, commonly known as the Louvel's Ravenea, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant features a distinctive rosette of leaf bases that form a dense, fibrous sheath around the trunk. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in panicles, and it produces edible, coconut-like fruits.

Ravenea louvelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
