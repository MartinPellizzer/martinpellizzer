Rheum rotundatum

Rheum rotundatum, commonly known as the Indian rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing high levels of anthraquinones, which have medicinal properties.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal medicine.
