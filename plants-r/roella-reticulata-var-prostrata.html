Roella reticulata var. prostrata

Roella reticulata var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate nettle.

This variant is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its spreading habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
