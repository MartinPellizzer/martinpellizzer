Roegneria intramongolica

Roegneria intramongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leaf-like blades. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Roegneria intramongolica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
