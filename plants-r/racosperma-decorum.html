Racosperma decorum

Racosperma decorum, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Racosperma decorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities in ecological restoration.
