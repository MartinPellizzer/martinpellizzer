Ranunculus obtusidentiformis

Ranunculus obtusidentiformis, commonly known as the blunt-toothed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
