Rubus odoratus var. columbianus

Rubus odoratus var. columbianus, commonly known as the Columbian raspberry, is a variant of the fragrant raspberry species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces edible berries with a sweet, tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

Its berries are often used in jams, pies, and other desserts due to their unique taste and aroma.
