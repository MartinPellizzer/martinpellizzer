Rauvolfia elliptica

Rauvolfia elliptica, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its roots are widely used in traditional medicine for their hypotensive properties.

Rauvolfia elliptica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
