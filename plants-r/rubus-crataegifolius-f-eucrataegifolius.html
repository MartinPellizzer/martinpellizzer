Rubus crataegifolius f. eucrataegifolius

Rubus crataegifolius f. eucrataegifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

Its foliage consists of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, with the central leaflet being notably narrower than the others. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its edible berries.
