Rubus × mutabiliformis

Rubus × mutabiliformis, commonly known as the evergreening bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, known for its unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It exhibits variable foliage coloration, with leaves ranging from green to reddish hues depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
