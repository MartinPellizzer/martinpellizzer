Rubus × pseudocongestus

Rubus × pseudocongestus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to regions of North America and is known for its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and tart. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is also used in ornamental gardening due to its vigorous growth and aesthetic appeal.
