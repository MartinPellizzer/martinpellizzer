Roystonea regia var. maisiana

Roystonea regia var. maisiana, commonly known as the Maisian royal palm, is a variety of the royal palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, creamy-white flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the tree. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky capsule that contains edible seeds.

This palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
