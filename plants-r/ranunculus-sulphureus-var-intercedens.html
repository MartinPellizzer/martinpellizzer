Ranunculus sulphureus var. intercedens

Ranunculus sulphureus var. intercedens, commonly known as the golden crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like appearance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and woodland edges. It is characterized by its short stature and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
