Rubus parviserratus

Rubus parviserratus, commonly known as the small-toothed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves.

The species is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other bramble species with similar botanical features.
