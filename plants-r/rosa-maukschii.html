Rosa maukschii

Rosa maukschii, commonly known as the Mauksch rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the stems are typically armed with thorns.

Rosa maukschii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
