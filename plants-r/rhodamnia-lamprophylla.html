Rhodamnia lamprophylla

Rhodamnia lamprophylla, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a glossy appearance, and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, black, oval-shaped berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and dry forest regions.

Rhodamnia lamprophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
