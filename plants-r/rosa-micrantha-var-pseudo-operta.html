Rosa micrantha var. pseudo-operta

Rosa micrantha var. pseudo-operta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in pink to white shades. The foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and the plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

It is also noted for its adaptability to cooler climates and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
