Richardia ciliata

Richardia ciliata, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Richardia ciliata plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
