Rubus × gracilidens

Rubus × gracilidens, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, a small, dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
