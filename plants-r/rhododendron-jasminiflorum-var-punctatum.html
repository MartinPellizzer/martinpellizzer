Rhododendron jasminiflorum var. punctatum

Rhododendron jasminiflorum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted rhododendron, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive foliage and blooms.

It features broad, leathery leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, often spotted underside. The flowers are typically pink or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance reminiscent of jasmine. This variety is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in shaded environments.
