Rorippa palustris subsp. fernaldiana

Rorippa palustris subsp.

fernaldiana, commonly known as the Fernald's water-cress, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to North America. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged or floating, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually alternate. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and are pollinated by insects.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, disturbed waters, making it an indicator of eutrophic conditions.
