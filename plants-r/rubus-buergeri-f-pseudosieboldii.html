Rubus buergeri f. pseudosieboldii

Rubus buergeri f. pseudosieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
