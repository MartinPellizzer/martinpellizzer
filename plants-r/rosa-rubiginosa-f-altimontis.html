Rosa rubiginosa f. altimontis

Rosa rubiginosa f. altimontis, commonly known as the alpine rose or mountain rose, is a distinctive subspecies of the shrub rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark red to purplish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in dense, glandular hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
