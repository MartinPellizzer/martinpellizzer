Rosa × suarezii

Rosa × suarezii, commonly known as the Suárez rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant features thorny stems and serrated, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It is adapted to warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil to thrive.

Rosa × suarezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
