Ranunculus altaicus subsp. sulphureus

Ranunculus altaicus subsp.

sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur yellow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which typically have five to seven petals and are surrounded by a ring of sepals.

The plant produces a distinctive, pungent odor, which is a common trait among members of the Ranunculaceae family. Its leaves are trifoliate and have a hairy texture, while the stems are usually unbranched and covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
