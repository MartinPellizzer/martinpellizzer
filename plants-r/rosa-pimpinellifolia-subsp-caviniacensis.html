Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp. caviniacensis

Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp.

caviniacensis, commonly known as the Caviniacensis rock rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and northern Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are often clustered in terminal panicles.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
