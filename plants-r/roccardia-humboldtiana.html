Roccardia humboldtiana

Roccardia humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's roccardia, is a unique member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like structure.

It is native to the coastal regions of South America, particularly Chile and Peru, where it thrives in sandy and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It is adapted to withstand strong coastal winds and salt spray, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
