Reseda fruticulosa var. suffruticosa

Reseda fruticulosa var. suffruticosa, commonly known as yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that produces yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
