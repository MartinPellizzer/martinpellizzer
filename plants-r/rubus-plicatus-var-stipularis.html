Rubus plicatus var. stipularis

Rubus plicatus var. stipularis, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
