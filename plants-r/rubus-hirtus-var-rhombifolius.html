Rubus hirtus var. rhombifolius

Rubus hirtus var. rhombifolius, commonly known as the rhombifolius blackberry, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with rhomboid-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
