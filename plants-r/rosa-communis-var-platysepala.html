Rosa communis var. platysepala

Rosa communis var. platysepala, commonly known as the flat-sepaled rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China. Its flowers are notable for their broad, flat sepals that give it a unique appearance compared to other rose species. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and pinnate leaves.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its fragrant blooms.
