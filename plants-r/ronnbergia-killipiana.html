Ronnbergia killipiana

Ronnbergia killipiana, commonly known as the Killip's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance with long, slender petals and a distinctive, narrow spur. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions with partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, Ronnbergia killipiana is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
