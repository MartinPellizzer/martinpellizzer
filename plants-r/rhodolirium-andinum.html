Rhodolirium andinum

Rhodolirium andinum, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the cold, humid conditions of the Andes. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Rhodolirium andinum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
