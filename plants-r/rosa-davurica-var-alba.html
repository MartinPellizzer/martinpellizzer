Rosa davurica var. alba

Rosa davurica var. alba, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features thorned stems and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and floral display.
