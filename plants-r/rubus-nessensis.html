Rubus nessensis

Rubus nessensis, commonly known as the Ness bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Scottish island of Ness, part of the Shetland Islands. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible berries in the fall.

Rubus nessensis is adapted to the harsh coastal climate of its native habitat.
