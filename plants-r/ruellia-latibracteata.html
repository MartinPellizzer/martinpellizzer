Ruellia latibracteata

Ruellia latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, typical of the Rubiaceae family.

Ruellia latibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
