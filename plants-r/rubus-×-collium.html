Rubus × collium

Rubus × collium, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small white or pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is widely appreciated for its sweet-tart flavor and is used in culinary and commercial applications.
