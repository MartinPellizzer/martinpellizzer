Ribes grossularia var. phoenicocarpum

Ribes grossularia var. phoenicocarpum, commonly known as the purple currant, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that transition into berries ranging from red to deep purple in color. These berries are typically harvested in late summer and are valued for their tart flavor and culinary uses.

The plant is also notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
