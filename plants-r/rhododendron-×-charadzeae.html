Rhododendron × charadzeae

Rhododendron × charadzeae, commonly known as the Caucasian rhododendron, is a hybrid plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous areas with cool, moist climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
