Ravenea musicalis

Ravenea musicalis, commonly known as the musical Ravenea, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, arching leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, giving it a dramatic, tropical appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in warm, humid conditions with bright indirect light and well-draining soil.

Ravenea musicalis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and showy blooms.
