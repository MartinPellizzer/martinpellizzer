Rosa multiflora var. poecilostyla

Rosa multiflora var. poecilostyla, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically range in color from white to pink. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, though it can become invasive in certain regions.

The variety poecilostyla is distinguished by its unique floral structure and extended blooming period.
