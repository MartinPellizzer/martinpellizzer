Rubus serpens var. inflexatus

Rubus serpens var. inflexatus, commonly known as the bent-stem blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both its ecological role and edible fruits.
