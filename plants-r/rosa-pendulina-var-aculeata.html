Rosa pendulina var. aculeata

Rosa pendulina var. aculeata, commonly known as the climbing rose with thorns, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features long, arching stems that can climb or trail, often reaching lengths of up to 10 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red hips in the fall. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5-7 oval leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
