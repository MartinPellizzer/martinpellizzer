Ripasia coerulea

Ripasia coerulea, commonly known as the blue ripasia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Ripasia coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardiness.
