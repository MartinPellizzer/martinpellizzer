Rosa tomentosa var. arrondoeana

Rosa tomentosa var. arrondoeana, commonly known as the woolly rose or Arrondel's rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has thorny stems. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets covered in a soft, white pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
