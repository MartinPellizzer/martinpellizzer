Rubus puber

Rubus puber, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of bramble in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it is known for its edible berries.
