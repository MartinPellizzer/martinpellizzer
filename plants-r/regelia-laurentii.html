Regelia laurentii

Regelia laurentii, commonly known as the lace fern, is a unique and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that resemble those of a fern. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. Regelia laurentii thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardens and shaded outdoor spaces.
