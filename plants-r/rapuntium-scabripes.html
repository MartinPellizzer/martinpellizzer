Rapuntium scabripes

Rapuntium scabripes, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and wastelands. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating inflammation and digestive issues.
