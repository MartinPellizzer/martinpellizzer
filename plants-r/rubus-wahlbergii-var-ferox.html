Rubus wahlbergii var. ferox

Rubus wahlbergii var. ferox, commonly known as the Cape rose or ferox blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a high acidity. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
