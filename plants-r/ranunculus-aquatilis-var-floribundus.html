Ranunculus aquatilis var. floribundus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. floribundus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic flowering plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and marshes. The plant features finely divided, floating leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
