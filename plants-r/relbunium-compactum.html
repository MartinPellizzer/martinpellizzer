Relbunium compactum

Relbunium compactum, commonly known as the dwarf tansy, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowering heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically thrives in moist, shady environments, such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Relbunium compactum is native to parts of Europe and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
