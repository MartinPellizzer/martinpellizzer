Rudbeckia hirta f. flavescens

Rudbeckia hirta f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a variant of the common rudbeckia that exhibits a distinct yellow flower color.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to North America, characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like composite flowers. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and a central yellow disk surrounded by yellow-orange ray florets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Rudbeckia hirta f. flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
