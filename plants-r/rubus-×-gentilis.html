Rubus × gentilis

Rubus × gentilis, commonly known as blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Rubus × gentilis is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
