Raddia pachyphylla

Raddia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved raddia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its leaves are often a deep green color and may have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Raddia pachyphylla is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
