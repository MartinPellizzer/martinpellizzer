Rubus originalis

Rubus originalis, commonly known as the blackberry plant, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor. Rubus originalis thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it plays an important role in supporting local wildlife.
