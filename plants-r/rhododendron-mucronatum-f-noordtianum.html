Rhododendron mucronatum f. noordtianum

Rhododendron mucronatum f. noordtianum, commonly known as the Noordt's rhododendron, is a unique hybrid form of the species Rhododendron mucronatum.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid climate to flourish.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
