Rubus rhomboidalis

Rubus rhomboidalis, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black in color when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.
