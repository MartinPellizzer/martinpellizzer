Rudbeckia rupestris

Rudbeckia rupestris, commonly known as the rock rudbeckia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often found in open woodlands and prairies. The plant features upright stems with bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of dark brown or black tubular florets. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are usually gray-green in color.

Rudbeckia rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
