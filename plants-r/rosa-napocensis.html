Rosa napocensis

Rosa napocensis, commonly known as the Romanian rose, is a species native to the Carpathian Mountains in Romania.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by oval, red-orange hips. Its botanical characteristics include simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a strong, sweet scent.

Rosa napocensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
