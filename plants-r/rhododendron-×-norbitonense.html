Rhododendron × norbitonense

Rhododendron × norbitonense, commonly known as the Norbiton azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in horticultural displays.

The species is notable for its adaptability to temperate climates and its ability to bloom reliably in spring.
