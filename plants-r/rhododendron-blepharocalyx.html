Rhododendron blepharocalyx

Rhododendron blepharocalyx, commonly known as the snow bell rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant is evergreen, with leathery, dark green leaves that are densely packed along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
