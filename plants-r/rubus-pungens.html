Rubus pungens

Rubus pungens, commonly known as the prickly rose or brambles, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, though they are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. It is native to various regions in Asia, Europe, and North America, and thrives in temperate climates.

Rubus pungens is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
