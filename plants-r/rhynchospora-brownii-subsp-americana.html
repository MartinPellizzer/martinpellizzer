Rhynchospora brownii subsp. americana

Rhynchospora brownii subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in saturated soils.

The plant features tall, cylindrical inflorescences composed of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a distinctive reddish or brownish hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
