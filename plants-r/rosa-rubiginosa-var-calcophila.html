Rosa rubiginosa var. calcophila

Rosa rubiginosa var. calcophila, commonly known as the limestone rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in calcareous soils, which is reflected in its scientific name. Its leaves are dark green and aromatic, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it demonstrates remarkable adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
