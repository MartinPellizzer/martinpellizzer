Rhamnus lycioides f. angustifolia

Rhamnus lycioides f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches long. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in catkins, followed by small, dark blue berries.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
