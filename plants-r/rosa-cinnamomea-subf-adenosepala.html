Rosa cinnamomea subf. adenosepala

Rosa cinnamomea subf.

adenosepala, commonly known as the cinnamon rose or Chinese cinnamon rose, is a shrub-like plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with oval to lance-shaped leaflets that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, orange-red follicles that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
