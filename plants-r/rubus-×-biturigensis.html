Rubus × biturigensis

Rubus × biturigensis, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus fruticosus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry).

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, edible berries with a distinctive tart flavor. The plant is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe and Asia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
