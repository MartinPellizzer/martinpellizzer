Retama sphaerocarpa var. mesogaea

Retama sphaerocarpa var. mesogaea, commonly known as the Mesopotamian retama, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces spherical, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland environments.

This variety is notable for its drought tolerance and historical use in traditional medicine.
