Rubus spectabilis

Rubus spectabilis, commonly known as the salmonberry, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange-red in color and have a tangy, sweet flavor. Salmonberry thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
