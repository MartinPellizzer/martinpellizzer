Rubus praecox subsp. godronii

Rubus praecox subsp.

godronii, commonly known as Godron's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
