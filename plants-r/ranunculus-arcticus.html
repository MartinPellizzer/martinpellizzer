Ranunculus arcticus

Ranunculus arcticus, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a hairy, herbaceous stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cold climates. It produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Ranunculus arcticus is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.
