Rubus gracilicaulis subsp. pachychlamydeus

Rubus gracilicaulis subsp.

pachychlamydeus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is often found growing in association with other shrubs in montane and subalpine regions.
