Rubus candicans var. patulipes

Rubus candicans var. patulipes, commonly known as the showy raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing leaves and the second year bearing fruit. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible berries, though it can be somewhat invasive in certain regions.
