Rubus corylifolius subsp. fasciculatus

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

fasciculatus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often arranged in a palmate pattern.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
