Rotala densiflora f. laxa

Rotala densiflora f. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf water lettuce, is a variant of the water lettuce species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its loose, sprawling growth habit and broad, waxy leaves that float on the water surface. The plant has a dense network of roots that help stabilize it in aquatic environments. It is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a useful aquatic plant and a potential invasive species if not managed properly.
