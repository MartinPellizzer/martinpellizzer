Ranunculus marmorarius

Ranunculus marmorarius, commonly known as the marbled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a central greenish area. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
