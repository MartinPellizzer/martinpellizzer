Rhamnus alaternus subsp. pendula

Rhamnus alaternus subsp.

pendula, commonly known as the weeping buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its pendulous branches that give it a distinctive, weeping appearance.

The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are yellowish-green and appear in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
