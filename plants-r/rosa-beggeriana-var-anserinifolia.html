Rosa beggeriana var. anserinifolia

Rosa beggeriana var. anserinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is narrow and lance-shaped, with a dense, upright growth habit.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
