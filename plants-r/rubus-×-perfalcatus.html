Rubus × perfalcatus

Rubus × perfalcatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
