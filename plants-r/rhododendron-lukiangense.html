Rhododendron lukiangense

Rhododendron lukiangense, commonly known as the Lukiang rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern China, particularly in Sichuan and Yunnan provinces.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist climates. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, and it produces striking clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and red. Rhododendron lukiangense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
