Ripidium kanashiroi

Ripidium kanashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese lily, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink blossoms. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Ripidium kanashiroi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
