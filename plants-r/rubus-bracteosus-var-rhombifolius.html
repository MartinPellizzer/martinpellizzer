Rubus bracteosus var. rhombifolius

Rubus bracteosus var. rhombifolius, commonly known as the rhombifolius blackberry, is a variant of the bracteosa blackberry species.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves, which are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
