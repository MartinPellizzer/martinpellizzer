Rosa dorica

Rosa dorica, commonly known as the Doric rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces red, hairy fruits known as rosehips.

Rosa dorica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and historical significance.
