Rosenbergia stipularis

Rosenbergia stipularis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rosenbergia, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
