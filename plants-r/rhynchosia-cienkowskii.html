Rhynchosia cienkowskii

Rhynchosia cienkowskii, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in agriculture.

However, it contains toxic compounds and requires careful handling.
