Ribes alpestre var. alpestre

Ribes alpestre var. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments, and is adapted to cold, harsh climates. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Ribes alpestre var. alpestre plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in rocky areas.
