Robinia pseudoacacia f. rozynskiana

Robinia pseudoacacia f. rozynskiana, commonly known as the Rozynski's false acacia, is a unique form of the honey locust.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive, often more compact growth habit compared to the typical Robinia pseudoacacia. It features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow, fluffy pods that contain seeds. The plant is native to Central Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
