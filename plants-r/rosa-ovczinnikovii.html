Rosa ovczinnikovii

Rosa ovczinnikovii, commonly known as the Ovczinnikov rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are compound, with serrated edges. Rosa ovczinnikovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
