Rubus flexipes

Rubus flexipes, commonly known as the bent-stem blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to white, and the fruit, a cluster of small drupes, is edible but often tart.

Rubus flexipes plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
