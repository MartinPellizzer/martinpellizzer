Rosa glauca subsp. marsica

Rosa glauca subsp.

marsica, commonly known as the Marsican bluebells, is a subspecies of the bluebelle rose native to the Apennine Mountains of central Italy. It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers and slender, thornless stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to mountainous environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
