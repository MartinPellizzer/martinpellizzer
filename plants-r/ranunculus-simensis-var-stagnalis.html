Ranunculus simensis var. stagnalis

Ranunculus simensis var. stagnalis, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and bright green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found along pond edges and marshy areas. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its aquatic adaptations, including a short, stout stem and a basal rosette of leaves.
