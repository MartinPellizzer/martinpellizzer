Ratonia tenax

Ratonia tenax, commonly known as the hardy ratonia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Ratonia tenax is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
