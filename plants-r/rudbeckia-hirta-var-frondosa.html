Rudbeckia hirta var. frondosa

Rudbeckia hirta var. frondosa, commonly known as the frondosa rudbeckia, is a variant of the common coneflower.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and showy yellow-orange flower heads. This perennial plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. It produces composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
