Rosa canina var. major

Rosa canina var. major, commonly known as the dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink or yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa canina var. major is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along coastal areas.

It has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental plants.
