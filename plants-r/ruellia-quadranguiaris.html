Ruellia quadranguiaris

Ruellia quadranguiaris, commonly known as the four-angled ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its square, or quadrangular, stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches. Ruellia quadranguiaris is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
