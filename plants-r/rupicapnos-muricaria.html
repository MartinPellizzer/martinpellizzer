Rupicapnos muricaria

Rupicapnos muricaria, commonly known as the roughleaf spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in a rough, bark-like texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

Rupicapnos muricaria is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
