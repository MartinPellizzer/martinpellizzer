Rubus rivularis var. acanthophorus

Rubus rivularis var. acanthophorus, commonly known as the rough-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in jams and pies in some regions.
