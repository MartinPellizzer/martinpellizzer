Rhipsalis salicornoides var. stricta

Rhipsalis salicornoides var. stricta, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, trailing stems that often form dense, bushy clusters. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the holiday season. Its leaves are modified into short, cylindrical stems with small, pointed leaf-like structures.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
