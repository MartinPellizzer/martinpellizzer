Ranunculus myriophyllus

Ranunculus myriophyllus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and cool climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is often found in meadows and alpine tundra.

Ranunculus myriophyllus is an important species for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
