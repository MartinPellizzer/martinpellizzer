Ranunculus madagascariensis

Ranunculus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmate, with deep lobes, and it produces small, hairy fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
