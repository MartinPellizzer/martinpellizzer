Rosa alpina var. montis-ludovici

Rosa alpina var. montis-ludovici, commonly known as the Alpine rose of Ludovico, is a subspecies native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in harsh climates.
