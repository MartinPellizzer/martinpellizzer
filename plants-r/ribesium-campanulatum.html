Ribesium campanulatum

Ribesium campanulatum, commonly known as the bellflower of the rock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Ribesium campanulatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
