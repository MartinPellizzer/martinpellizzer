Rubus tomentosus f. angustifolius

Rubus tomentosus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a narrow, lance-shaped appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
