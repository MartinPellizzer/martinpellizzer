Ranunculus auricomus subsp. compar

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

compar, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, yellow flowers that have five to ten petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.
