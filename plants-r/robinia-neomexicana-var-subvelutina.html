Robinia neomexicana var. subvelutina

Robinia neomexicana var. subvelutina, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically gray-green and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
