Robinia cornigera

Robinia cornigera, commonly known as the black locust, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in summer, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often appearing dark and rugged.

Robinia cornigera is known for its rapid growth and hardiness, making it a valuable species in reforestation and erosion control.
