Raphanus indicus

Raphanus indicus, commonly known as the Indian radish, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a taproot that is often used as a vegetable. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly spicy, peppery flavor similar to mustard.

Raphanus indicus is typically grown in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
