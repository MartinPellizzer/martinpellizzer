Rosa hispanica f. escurialensis

Rosa hispanica f. escurialensis, commonly known as the Escurial rose, is a botanical variant of the Spanish rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark red to almost black flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, semi-double to double petals. Native to Spain, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after cultivar in gardens and horticultural collections.
