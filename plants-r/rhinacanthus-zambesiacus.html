Rhinacanthus zambesiacus

Rhinacanthus zambesiacus, commonly known as the Zambesian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a distinctive flaky appearance.

Rhinacanthus zambesiacus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
