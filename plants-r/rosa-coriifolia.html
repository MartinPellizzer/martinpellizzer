Rosa coriifolia

Rosa coriifolia, commonly known as the Chinese rose or tree rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, single-layered pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Rosa coriifolia is also known for its fragrant blooms and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
