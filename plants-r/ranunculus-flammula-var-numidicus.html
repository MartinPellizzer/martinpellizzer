Ranunculus flammula var. numidicus

Ranunculus flammula var. numidicus, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow, buttercup-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in gardens.

The plant contains toxic compounds, including protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation.
