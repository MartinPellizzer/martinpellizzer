Rosa gratissima

Rosa gratissima, commonly known as the sweet rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems covered in thorns and produces compound leaves with serrated edges. Rosa gratissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its flowers are also used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.
