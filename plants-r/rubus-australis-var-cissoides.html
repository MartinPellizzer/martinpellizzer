Rubus australis var. cissoides

Rubus australis var. cissoides, commonly known as the Australian blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The flowers are white and clustered, adding ornamental value to the plant.
