Ranunculus andersonii var. tenellus

Ranunculus andersonii var. tenellus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its compact stature and is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
