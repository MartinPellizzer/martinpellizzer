Rubus cantabrigiensis

Rubus cantabrigiensis, commonly known as the Cambridge raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland and hedgerow habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
