Rumex obtusifolius var. subalpinus

Rumex obtusifolius var. subalpinus, commonly known as the Alpine dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and high altitude conditions.
