Rhynchospora polycephala

Rhynchospora polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense mat at the base.

Rhynchospora polycephala plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
