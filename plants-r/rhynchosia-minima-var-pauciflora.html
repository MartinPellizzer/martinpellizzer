Rhynchosia minima var. pauciflora

Rhynchosia minima var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
