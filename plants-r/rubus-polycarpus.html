Rubus polycarpus

Rubus polycarpus, commonly known as the many-fruited blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist woodlands and thickets. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
