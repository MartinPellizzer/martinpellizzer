Rudolphia scandens

Rudolphia scandens, commonly known as the climbing rudolphia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Rudolphia scandens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its rapid growth and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and garden displays.
