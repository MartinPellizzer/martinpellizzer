Rubus × laudabilis

Rubus × laudabilis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which is a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.
