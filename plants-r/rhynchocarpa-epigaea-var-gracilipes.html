Rhynchocarpa epigaea var. gracilipes

Rhynchocarpa epigaea var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-fruited groundsel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in heads, producing small, dry fruits known as achenes.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other groundcover plants in its habitat.
