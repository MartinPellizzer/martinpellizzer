Rubus amblycaulon

Rubus amblycaulon, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet to tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
