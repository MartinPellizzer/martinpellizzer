Ranunculus flabellatus var. acinacilobus

Ranunculus flabellatus var. acinacilobus, commonly known as the swordleaf buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features striking, deeply lobed leaves that resemble a sword, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a short flowering period in spring and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
