Rubus idaeus var. marmoratus

Rubus idaeus var. marmoratus, commonly known as the marbled raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry plant characterized by its unique variegated foliage.

Its leaves are typically a mix of green and white or pinkish hues, creating a marbled effect that distinguishes it from standard raspberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are generally less tart than typical raspberries. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its flavorful fruits.
