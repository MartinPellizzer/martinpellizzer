Robinia pseudoacacia var. decaisneana

Robinia pseudoacacia var. decaisneana, commonly known as the Decaisne false acacia, is a variant of the honey locust tree.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 12 to 20 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in upright clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
