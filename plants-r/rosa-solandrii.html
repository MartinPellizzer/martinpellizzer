Rosa solandrii

Rosa solandrii, commonly known as the Solander rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and maritime climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red-orange fruits known as hips.

Rosa solandrii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
