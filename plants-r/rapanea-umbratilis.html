Rapanea umbratilis

Rapanea umbratilis, commonly known as the Indian laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, blue-black berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
