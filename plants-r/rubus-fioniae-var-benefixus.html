Rubus fioniae var. benefixus

Rubus fioniae var. benefixus, commonly known as the Fionian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically larger and sweeter than those of its parent species. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary use and ornamental appeal in gardens.
