Rhododendron bullifolium

Rhododendron bullifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
