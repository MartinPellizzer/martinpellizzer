Rhexia grandifolia

Rhexia grandifolia, commonly known as the largeflower false mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often hairy.

Rhexia grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
