Ribes macrobotrys

Ribes macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring, often in shades of white and pink. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, though they are not as widely consumed as other gooseberry varieties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
