Ranunculus trichophyllus var. mexicanus

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent black markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streambanks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its simple, often showy flowers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be toxic if ingested.
