Rhipsalis monacantha var. espinosa

Rhipsalis monacantha var. espinosa, commonly known as the Espinosa mistletoe cactus, is a rare and unique epiphytic cactus native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its long, thin, and often pendulous stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by small, red fruits. Its leaves are reduced to small tubercles, and it lacks the typical spines found in many cacti.

This variety is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
