Ranunculus sardous var. hirsutus

Ranunculus sardous var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy sardous buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, usually yellow flowers with five petals, often adorned with a distinctive central blotch. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
