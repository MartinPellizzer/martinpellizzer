Rubus orthocladus

Rubus orthocladus, commonly known as the upright blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus orthocladus is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to local biodiversity.
