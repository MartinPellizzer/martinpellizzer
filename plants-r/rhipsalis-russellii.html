Rhipsalis russellii

Rhipsalis russellii, commonly known as the Russell's mistletoe cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that often grow along tree trunks or branches, forming dense, cascading clusters. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to small, hair-like structures, and it lacks the typical spines found in many other cactus species.

Rhipsalis russellii is popular as a houseplant due to its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
