Ranunculus schowii

Ranunculus schowii, commonly known as the Schow's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are deeply lobed, and it produces small, hairy fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
