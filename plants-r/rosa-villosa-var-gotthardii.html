Rosa villosa var. gotthardii

Rosa villosa var. gotthardii, commonly known as the Gotthard rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It features upright, thorny stems covered in a dense layer of grayish-white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that have serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
