Rubus ulmifolius

Rubus ulmifolius, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep purple-black color. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are widely used in culinary applications.

Rubus ulmifolius is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
