Rosa carolina

Rosa carolina, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red-orange hips in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include simple, ovate leaves and a woody structure that supports its growth in various soil types.

Rosa carolina is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
