Richeria grandis

Richeria grandis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant and showy appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves of Richeria grandis have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
