Rhodanthemum quezelii

Rhodanthemum quezelii, commonly known as the Quezel's rhodanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing numerous tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained, rocky soils.

Rhodanthemum quezelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
