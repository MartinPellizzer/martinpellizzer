Restrepia tabeae

Restrepia tabeae, commonly known as the Tabea restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or humus-rich soil at high altitudes. The plant produces small, clustered pseudobulbs and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown spots.

Restrepia tabeae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
