Rubus × bakabanyae

Rubus × bakabanyae, commonly known as the Bakabanyae blackberry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus fruticosus and Rubus glaucus.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The berries are valued for their intense flavor and are used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
