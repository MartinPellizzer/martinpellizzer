Rosa obtusifolia var. typica

Rosa obtusifolia var. typica, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and clusters of fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, brown fruits called hips, which are edible and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
