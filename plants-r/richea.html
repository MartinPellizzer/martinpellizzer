Richea

Richea, commonly known as the "flowering heath," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to Australia and includes species such as Richea fulva, which is known for its striking red flowers. These plants typically grow in heathlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Richea species are generally shrubs with small, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

They play an important ecological role in their native habitats by providing nectar for pollinators.
