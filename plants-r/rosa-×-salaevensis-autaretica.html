Rosa × salaevensis autaretica

Rosa × salaevensis autaretica, commonly known as the Siberian peony rose, is a hybrid species originating from the cross between Rosa salovensis and Rosa autaretica.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardy nature.
