Rubus rivularis var. accedens

Rubus rivularis var. accedens, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its trailing or ascending growth habit and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically sour and not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its hairy stems and stipules that are often fused to the leaf stalk.
