Ranunculus abortivus var. indivisus

Ranunculus abortivus var. indivisus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features simple, alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct basal rosette. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in a single bloom at the end of each stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
