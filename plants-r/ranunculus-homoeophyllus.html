Ranunculus homoeophyllus

Ranunculus homoeophyllus, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with prominent yellow stamens and a short, hairy central style. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It is known for its early spring bloom, often appearing before many other spring flowers.
