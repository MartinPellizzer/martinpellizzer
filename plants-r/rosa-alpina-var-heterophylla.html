Rosa alpina var. heterophylla

Rosa alpina var. heterophylla, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The leaves are typically alternate and have a serrated margin, varying in shape depending on the growth conditions. This variety is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
