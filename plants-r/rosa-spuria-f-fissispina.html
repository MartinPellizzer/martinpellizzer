Rosa spuria f. fissispina

Rosa spuria f. fissispina, commonly known as the fissured rose, is a botanical variant of the common wild rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fissured or split petals, which set it apart from other rose species. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in horticultural collections.
