Ranunculus alismifolius var. davisii

Ranunculus alismifolius var. davisii, commonly known as Davis' water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It features finely divided, grass-like leaves that float on the water's surface or extend underwater. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams.

Its botanical structure supports both aquatic and terrestrial ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
