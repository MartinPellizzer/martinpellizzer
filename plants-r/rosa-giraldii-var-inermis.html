Rosa giraldii var. inermis

Rosa giraldii var. inermis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed wild rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in the summer months. Its hips, which are red, fleshy fruits, are edible and contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
