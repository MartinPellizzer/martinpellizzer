Ranunculus fallax subsp. nephrodes

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

nephrodes, commonly known as the kidneyleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble a kidney shape, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Ranunculaceae family.
