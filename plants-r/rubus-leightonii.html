Rubus leightonii

Rubus leightonii, commonly known as Leighton's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and chaparral habitats. Its flowers are white to pale pink and appear in clusters, while the fruit is edible but has a tart flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
