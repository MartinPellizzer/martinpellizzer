Rubus thyrsanthoides

Rubus thyrsanthoides, commonly known as brambles or blackberry bushes, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The plant is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, particularly in moist, well-drained environments. It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Its flowers are typically white or pink and bloom in the spring.
