Richardsonia acutifolia

Richardsonia acutifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved Richardsonia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Richardsonia acutifolia thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.
