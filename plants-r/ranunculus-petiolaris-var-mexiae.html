Ranunculus petiolaris var. mexiae

Ranunculus petiolaris var. mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
