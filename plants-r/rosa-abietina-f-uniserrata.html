Rosa abietina f. uniserrata

Rosa abietina f. uniserrata, commonly known as the single-toothed alpine rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

It is a subspecies of Rosa abietina, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The leaves are serrate with a single tooth on each lobe, distinguishing it from other forms of the species. It typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
