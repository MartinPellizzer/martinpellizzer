Ruschia mutata

Ruschia mutata, commonly known as the silver leaf Ruschia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, waxy leaves that are typically blue-green or silver in color, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red, often appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is widely cultivated in regions with Mediterranean climates.
