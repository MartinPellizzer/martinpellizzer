Ranunculus fallax subsp. gregalis

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

gregalis, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright stems that bear numerous small, bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals.

The leaves are palmately divided, typically with three to five lobes, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant is known for its ability to form dense clusters, giving it its subspecific name "gregalis.".
