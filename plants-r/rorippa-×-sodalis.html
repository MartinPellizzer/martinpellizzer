Rorippa × sodalis

Rorippa × sodalis, commonly known as the yellow water-cress, is a hybrid species belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit. The plant typically has alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are often submerged or floating. It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and streams.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in plant hybridization studies.
