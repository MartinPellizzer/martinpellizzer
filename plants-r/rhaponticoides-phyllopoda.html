Rhaponticoides phyllopoda

Rhaponticoides phyllopoda, commonly known as the leafy rhapontic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, fleshy, lobed leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticultural settings.
