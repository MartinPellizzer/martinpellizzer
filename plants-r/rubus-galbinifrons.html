Rubus galbinifrons

Rubus galbinifrons, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
