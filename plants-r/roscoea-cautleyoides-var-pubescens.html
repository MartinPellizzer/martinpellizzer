Roscoea cautleyoides var. pubescens

Roscoea cautleyoides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy roscoea, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a stout, rhizomatous stem that produces large, showy inflorescences with bright orange-red bracts. The plant's foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its "hairy" designation. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in shaded garden settings.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among plant enthusiasts and horticulturists.
