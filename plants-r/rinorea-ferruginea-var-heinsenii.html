Rinorea ferruginea var. heinsenii

Rinorea ferruginea var. heinsenii, commonly known as the rusty rinorea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and shade-loving landscapes.
