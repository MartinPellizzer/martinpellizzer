Rubus rhytidophyllus

Rubus rhytidophyllus, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to red to black as they mature. The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
