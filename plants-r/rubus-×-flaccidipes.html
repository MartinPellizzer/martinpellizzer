Rubus × flaccidipes

Rubus × flaccidipes, commonly known as the trailing blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
