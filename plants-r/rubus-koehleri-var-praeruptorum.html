Rubus koehleri var. praeruptorum

Rubus koehleri var. praeruptorum, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to China.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
