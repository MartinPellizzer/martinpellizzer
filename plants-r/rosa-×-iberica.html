Rosa × iberica

Rosa × iberica, commonly known as the Iberian rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa × iberica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
