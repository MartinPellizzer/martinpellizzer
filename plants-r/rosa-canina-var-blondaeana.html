Rosa canina var. blondaeana

Rosa canina var. blondaeana, commonly known as the blond rose, is a variant of the dog rose, characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and is known for its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It produces red, hairy berries that are edible but often used in jams and herbal remedies.

The plant is hardy and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a resilient addition to gardens and natural habitats.
