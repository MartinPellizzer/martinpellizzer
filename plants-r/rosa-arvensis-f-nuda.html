Rosa arvensis f. nuda

Rosa arvensis f. nuda, commonly known as the naked-stem dog rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, often hairless stems.

It produces small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has pinnately lobed leaves with a distinctive serrated edge. It is a wild relative of the common rose and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species within the Rosaceae family.
