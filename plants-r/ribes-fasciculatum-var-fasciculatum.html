Ribes fasciculatum var. fasciculatum

Ribes fasciculatum var. fasciculatum, commonly known as the western gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring multiple stems that often grow in clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, tart berries in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
