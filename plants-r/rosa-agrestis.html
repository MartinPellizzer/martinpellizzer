Rosa agrestis

Rosa agrestis, commonly known as the field rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its stems are often covered in thorns, and it produces red, hairy fruits called hips.

Rosa agrestis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
