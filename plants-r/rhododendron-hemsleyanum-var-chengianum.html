Rhododendron hemsleyanum var. chengianum

Rhododendron hemsleyanum var. chengianum, commonly known as the Cheng's rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its vibrant spring blooms.

This variety is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
