Rumex intermedius var. incanus

Rumex intermedius var. incanus, commonly known as the hairy dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, meadows, and riverbanks. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose inflorescences, with greenish-yellow petals.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
