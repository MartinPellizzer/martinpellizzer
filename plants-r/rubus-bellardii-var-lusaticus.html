Rubus bellardii var. lusaticus

Rubus bellardii var. lusaticus, commonly known as the Lusatic blackberry, is a variant of the bellardii blackberry species native to Europe.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The botanical features of this variety make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
