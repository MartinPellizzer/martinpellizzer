Rosa adaugens

Rosa adaugens, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or Brazilian pink, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically features thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Rosa adaugens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
