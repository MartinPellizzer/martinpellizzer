Rhododendron protistum var. giganteum

Rhododendron protistum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length, providing dense foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
