Rubus haematocaulos

Rubus haematocaulos, commonly known as the bloodroot rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark red stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and wines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in woodland habitats.

The botanical features of Rubus haematocaulos make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
