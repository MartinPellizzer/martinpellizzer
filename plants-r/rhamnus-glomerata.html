Rhamnus glomerata

Rhamnus glomerata, commonly known as the clustered buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, black berries that are edible for birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface.

Rhamnus glomerata is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in partial to full shade.
