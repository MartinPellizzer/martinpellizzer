Ranunculus subglaber

Ranunculus subglaber, commonly known as the naked-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other buttercup species.

Its flowers are usually short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
