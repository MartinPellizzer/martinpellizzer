Ryticaryum oncocarpum

Ryticaryum oncocarpum, commonly known as the bladderpod, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical, inflated pods that resemble bladders, which are used for seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its small, white or yellow flowers bloom in early spring, followed by the development of its unique seed pods.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects.
