Rosettea elliotii

Rosettea elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's rosette, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, reducing water loss and enhancing its drought tolerance.

Rosettea elliotii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
