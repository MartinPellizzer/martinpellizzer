Rhododendron augustinei f. grandifolum

Rhododendron augustinei f. grandifolum, commonly known as the large-flowered Augustine rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the humid mountain forests of southern China.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its showy flowers. The plant produces large, bell-shaped blooms in shades of pink to red, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
