Rubus kumaonensis

Rubus kumaonensis, commonly known as the Kumaon raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its trailing or shrub-like growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
