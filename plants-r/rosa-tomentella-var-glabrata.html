Rosa tomentella var. glabrata

Rosa tomentella var. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy gray foliage and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thorny stems and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, woolly pubescence, which gives it its common name.
