Rubus idaeus var. purpureus

Rubus idaeus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry that exhibits distinctive purple foliage and fruit.

It is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically a deep red to purple color when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and unique coloration, making it a desirable species for both cultivation and ecological studies.
