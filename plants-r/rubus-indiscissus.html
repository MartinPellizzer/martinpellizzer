Rubus indiscissus

Rubus indiscissus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces edible berries that are dark purple to black in color, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
