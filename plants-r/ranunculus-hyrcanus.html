Ranunculus hyrcanus

Ranunculus hyrcanus, commonly known as the Hyrcanian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in regions such as the Caucasus, Iran, and parts of Russia. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its often toxic members.

The flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated primarily by insects.
