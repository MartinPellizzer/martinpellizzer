Rumex pictus

Rumex pictus, commonly known as the painted sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, reddish stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are edible and have a sour taste, often used in culinary applications.

Rumex pictus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a characteristic plant of rocky and arid environments.
