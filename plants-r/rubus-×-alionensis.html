Rubus × alionensis

Rubus × alionensis, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas like the Tien Shan and Pamir Mountains. This plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil.

The botanical features of Rubus × alionensis make it an important species for ecological studies in alpine ecosystems.
