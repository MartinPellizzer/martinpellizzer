Rindera lateriflora

Rindera lateriflora, commonly known as the lateral-flower rindera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure, with leaves that are often opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Rindera lateriflora is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
