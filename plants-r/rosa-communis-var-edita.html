Rosa communis var. edita

Rosa communis var. edita, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom in spring and summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history in traditional Chinese horticulture.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in some traditional practices.
