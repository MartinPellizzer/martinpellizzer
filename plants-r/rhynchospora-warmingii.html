Rhynchospora warmingii

Rhynchospora warmingii, commonly known as the Florida marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, three-lobed flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Rhynchospora warmingii has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in dense stands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its wetland environment.
