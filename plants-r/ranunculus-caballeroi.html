Ranunculus caballeroi

Ranunculus caballeroi, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has palmate leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in early spring.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
