Rubus fruticosus var. guentheri

Rubus fruticosus var. guentheri, commonly known as Guenther's blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
