Rhododendron reticulatum f. parvifolium

Rhododendron reticulatum f. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved reticulate rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features dense, leathery foliage with small, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
