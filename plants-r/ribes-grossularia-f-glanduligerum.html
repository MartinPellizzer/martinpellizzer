Ribes grossularia f. glanduligerum

Ribes grossularia f. glanduligerum, commonly known as the glandular gooseberry, is a variant of the common gooseberry.

It is characterized by its small, yellow to orange berries that are typically covered in glandular hairs. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It belongs to the family Grossulariaceae and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

The glandular hairs on its leaves and stems are a distinguishing feature of this form.
