Rosa tomentosa f. purpurata

Rosa tomentosa f. purpurata, commonly known as the purple-flowered shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features dense, thorny stems covered in a fine, white tomentum, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, depending on the variety. Its foliage is dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
