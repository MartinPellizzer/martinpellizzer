Rhinocidium macronyx

Rhinocidium macronyx, commonly known as the horned bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils. Its blooming period occurs in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhinocidium macronyx is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
