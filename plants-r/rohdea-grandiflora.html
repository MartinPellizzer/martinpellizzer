Rohdea grandiflora

Rohdea grandiflora, commonly known as the giant rohdea, is a perennial plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and it produces striking, pinkish-white inflorescences with small, clustered flowers. Rohdea grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
