Rhinanthus × schneiderae

Rhinanthus × schneiderae, commonly known as the hybrid yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to the United Kingdom.

It is a sterile hybrid between Rhinanthus minor (common yellow rattle) and Rhinanthus anthyllidifolius (red brome). The plant is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in grassland habitats, particularly in areas with sandy or loamy soils.

Rhinanthus × schneiderae plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
