Rubus candicans subsp. adenophorus

Rubus candicans subsp.

adenophorus, commonly known as the腺花覆盆子, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive floral structure and medicinal properties.
