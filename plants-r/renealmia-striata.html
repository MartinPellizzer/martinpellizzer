Renealmia striata

Renealmia striata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive striped pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often hidden among its foliage. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy root vegetable, similar to taro.
