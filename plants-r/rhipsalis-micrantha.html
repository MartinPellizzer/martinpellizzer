Rhipsalis micrantha

Rhipsalis micrantha, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and vibrant flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pink, often blooming during the winter months. Its succulent stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which help reduce water loss.

Rhipsalis micrantha is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
