Ranunculus dissectus subsp. glabrescens

Ranunculus dissectus subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous dissected buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are finely dissected, giving it a lace-like appearance.

The flowers are typically yellow with five petals, often adorned with a distinctive central stigma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value in gardens and its role in local ecosystems.
