Rosa × reversa var. petrogenes

Rosa × reversa var. petrogenes, commonly known as the pink double muscat rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its large, double flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth and flowering.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
