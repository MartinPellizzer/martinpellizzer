Rubus montpelierensis

Rubus montpelierensis, commonly known as the Montpelier blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of brambles and blackberries.
