Rosa tomentosa var. sinkowiensis

Rosa tomentosa var. sinkowiensis, commonly known as the Sinkowicz rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its stems are often covered in a thick layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific climatic conditions.
