Ranunculus flammula var. heterophyllus

Ranunculus flammula var. heterophyllus, commonly known as the yellow avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are typically palmately lobed and alternate leaves along the stem, which are more elongated. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
