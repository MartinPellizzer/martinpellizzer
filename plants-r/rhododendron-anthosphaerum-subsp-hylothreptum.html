Rhododendron anthosphaerum subsp. hylothreptum

Rhododendron anthosphaerum subsp.

hylothreptum, commonly known as the Sikkim rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high elevations. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a significant role in the local ecosystem.
