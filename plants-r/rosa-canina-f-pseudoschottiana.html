Rosa canina f. pseudoschottiana

Rosa canina f. pseudoschottiana, commonly known as the Schott's dog rose, is a rare and distinctive horticultural form of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and distinctive, elongated, recurved petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
