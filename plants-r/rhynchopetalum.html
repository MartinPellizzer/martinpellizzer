Rhynchopetalum

Rhynchopetalum, commonly known as the genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, includes species that are native to various regions of the world.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers that typically feature a distinctive lip or labellum. The leaves of Rhynchopetalum species are usually elongated and may be arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. They thrive in humid environments and require well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
