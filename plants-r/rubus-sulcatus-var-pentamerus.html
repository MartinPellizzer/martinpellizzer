Rubus sulcatus var. pentamerus

Rubus sulcatus var. pentamerus, commonly known as the five-merous blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five leaflets. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
