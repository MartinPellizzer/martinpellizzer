Ranunculus cymbalaria f. hebecaulis

Ranunculus cymbalaria f. hebecaulis, commonly known as the slender water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into three segments, giving it a lily-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters along the water surface. It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and marshes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for aquatic ecosystems and a popular choice for water gardeners.
