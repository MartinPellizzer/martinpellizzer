Rhamnus oleoides subsp. atlantica

Rhamnus oleoides subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic buckthorn, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring.

The plant is adapted to sandy and limestone soils, often found in dunes and rocky coastal areas. It has small, elliptical berries that are typically black when mature.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
