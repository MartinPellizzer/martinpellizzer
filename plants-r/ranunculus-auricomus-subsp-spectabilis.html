Ranunculus auricomus subsp. spectabilis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers with five to eight petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in cooler climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
