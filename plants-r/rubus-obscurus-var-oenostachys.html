Rubus obscurus var. oenostachys

Rubus obscurus var. oenostachys, commonly known as the dark bramble or blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, organic soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
