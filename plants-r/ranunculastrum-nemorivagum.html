Ranunculastrum nemorivagum

Ranunculastrum nemorivagum, commonly known as the forest ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest understories. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, yellow, or pink. Its flowers are usually borne on slender stems and bloom in the spring.

Ranunculastrum nemorivagum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate growing conditions.
