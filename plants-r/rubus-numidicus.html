Rubus numidicus

Rubus numidicus, commonly known as the Numidian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
