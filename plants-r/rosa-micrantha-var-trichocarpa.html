Rosa micrantha var. trichocarpa

Rosa micrantha var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the trichocarpous rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits called rosehips. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
