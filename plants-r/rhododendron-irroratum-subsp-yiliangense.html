Rhododendron irroratum subsp. yiliangense

Rhododendron irroratum subsp.

yiliangense, commonly known as the Yiliang rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly found in the Yiliang County region of Yunnan Province. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and landscape design in its native habitat.
