Ricinocarpus distans

Ricinocarpus distans, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces woody pods that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks in tropical regions.
