Racosperma nuperrimum

Racosperma nuperrimum, commonly known as the purple wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
