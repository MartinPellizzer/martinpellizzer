Ranunculus sardous var. littoralis

Ranunculus sardous var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal buttercup, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and rocky coastlines. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, waxy appearance.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
