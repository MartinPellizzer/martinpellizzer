Roxburghia gloriosoides var. minor

Roxburghia gloriosoides var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf glorybower, is a compact shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces striking clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

The variety 'minor' is distinguished by its smaller size compared to the typical form of Roxburghia gloriosoides.
