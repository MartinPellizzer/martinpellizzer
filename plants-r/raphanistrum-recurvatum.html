Raphanistrum recurvatum

Raphanistrum recurvatum, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red in color. It thrives in moist environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its adaptability in various wetland habitats.
