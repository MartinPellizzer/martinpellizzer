Rubus moluccanus var. pirifolius

Rubus moluccanus var. pirifolius, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically serrated and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries, though they are usually less flavorful. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.
