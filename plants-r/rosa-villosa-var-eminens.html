Rosa villosa var. eminens

Rosa villosa var. eminens, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, often thorny stems and dense clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
