Rubus hirtus f. menkei

Rubus hirtus f. menkei, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dense, bristly pubescence.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This form is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.
