Rubus insericatus microgene microbelus

Rubus insericatus microgene microbelus, commonly known as the silky raspberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. The plant is native to certain regions of Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
