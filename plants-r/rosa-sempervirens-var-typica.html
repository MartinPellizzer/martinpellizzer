Rosa sempervirens var. typica

Rosa sempervirens var. typica, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a variety of the shrub rose native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a compact, bushy shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
