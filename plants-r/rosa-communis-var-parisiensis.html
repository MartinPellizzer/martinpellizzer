Rosa communis var. parisiensis

Rosa communis var. parisiensis, commonly known as the Parisian rose, is a variety of the common rose with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its deep pink to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically fragrant and have five petals, though they may sometimes appear as double blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
