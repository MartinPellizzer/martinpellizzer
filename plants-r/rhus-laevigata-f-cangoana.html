Rhus laevigata f. cangoana

Rhus laevigata f. cangoana, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
