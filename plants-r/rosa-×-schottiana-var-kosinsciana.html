Rosa × schottiana var. kosinsciana

Rosa × schottiana var. kosinsciana, commonly known as the Kosinski rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage is dark green with a slightly serrated leaf margin. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
