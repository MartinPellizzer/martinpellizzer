Rubus mulleri var. subcinereus

Rubus mulleri var. subcinereus, commonly known as the silver-haired blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The leaves are trifoliate, with oval to elliptical leaflets that have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
