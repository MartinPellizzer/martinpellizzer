Raphia monbuttorum

Raphia monbuttorum, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a large, tropical palm native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, fan-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, facilitating wind pollination. Its trunk is robust and fibrous, often used in traditional construction and crafts.

The plant thrives in humid, lowland areas and plays a significant role in local ecosystems and economies.
