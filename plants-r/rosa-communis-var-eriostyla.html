Rosa communis var. eriostyla

Rosa communis var. eriostyla, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
