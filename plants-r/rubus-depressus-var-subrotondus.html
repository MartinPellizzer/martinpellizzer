Rubus depressus var. subrotondus

Rubus depressus var. subrotondus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a serrated margin, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink. The fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry, which is edible but often tart.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
