Rhus punjabensis var. sinica

Rhus punjabensis var. sinica, commonly known as Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which turn vibrant red in the fall, and its clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It produces red, fuzzy fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
