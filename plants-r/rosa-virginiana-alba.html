Rosa virginiana alba

Rosa virginiana alba, commonly known as the white Virgin's bower, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the summer months, which are followed by red, fuzzy fruits known as hips. Rosa virginiana alba is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
