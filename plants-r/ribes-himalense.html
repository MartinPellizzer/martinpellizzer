Ribes himalense

Ribes himalense, commonly known as Himalayan gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Ribes himalense is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in high-altitude environments.
