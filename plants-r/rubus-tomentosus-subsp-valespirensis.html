Rubus tomentosus subsp. valespirensis

Rubus tomentosus subsp.

valespirensis, commonly known as the Valespirensis blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features woody stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a pale underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves, followed by dark purple to black berries.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to moist, woodland habitats and its role in local ecosystems.
