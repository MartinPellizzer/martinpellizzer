Ranunculus spicatus subsp. maroccanus

Ranunculus spicatus subsp.

maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streams. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy surface that helps retain moisture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
