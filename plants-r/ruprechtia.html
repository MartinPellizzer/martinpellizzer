Ruprechtia

Ruprechtia, commonly known as the golden bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Ruprechtia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

They are also valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
