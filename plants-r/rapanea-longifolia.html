Rapanea longifolia

Rapanea longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved rapanea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elongated and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue berries.

Rapanea longifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
