Rhamnus chimalapensis

Rhamnus chimalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican buckthorn, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in dry to moist forests and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
