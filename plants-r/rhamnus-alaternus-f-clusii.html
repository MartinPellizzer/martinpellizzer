Rhamnus alaternus f. clusii

Rhamnus alaternus f. clusii, commonly known as the Clusius form of the common buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by black, egg-shaped berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental purposes.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
