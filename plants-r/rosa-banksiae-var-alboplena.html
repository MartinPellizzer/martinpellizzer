Rosa banksiae var. alboplena

Rosa banksiae var. alboplena, commonly known as the white-flowered banksia rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its large, double white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value lies in its showy, long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
