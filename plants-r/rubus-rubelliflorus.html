Rubus rubelliflorus

Rubus rubelliflorus, commonly known as the redflower blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
