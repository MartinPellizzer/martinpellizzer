Rubus parcepilosus

Rubus parcepilosus, commonly known as the soft-hairy raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
