Rubus lorenzii

Rubus lorenzii, commonly known as the Lorentz blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

The botanical features of Rubus lorenzii include its thorny stems, compound leaves, and its ability to form dense thickets.
