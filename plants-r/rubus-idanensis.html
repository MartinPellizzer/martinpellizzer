Rubus idanensis

Rubus idanensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
