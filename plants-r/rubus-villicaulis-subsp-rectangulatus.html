Rubus villicaulis subsp. rectangulatus

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

rectangulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive narrow shape.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, achenes, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
