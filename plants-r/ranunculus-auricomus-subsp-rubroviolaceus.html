Ranunculus auricomus subsp. rubroviolaceus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

rubroviolaceus, commonly known as the red-violet buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of red, violet, and yellow.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more intense and varied flower colors compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
