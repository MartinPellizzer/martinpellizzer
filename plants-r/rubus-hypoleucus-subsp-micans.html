Rubus hypoleucus subsp. micans

Rubus hypoleucus subsp.

micans, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-tinged leaves and small, dark purple berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the eastern United States. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the development of its edible fruits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and habitat preferences.
