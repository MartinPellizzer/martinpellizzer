Rosa leveillei

Rosa leveillei, commonly known as the Leveille's rose, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white in color, with a fragrant aroma. Rosa leveillei thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
