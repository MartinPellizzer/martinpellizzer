Rosa eglanteria f. anacantha

Rosa eglanteria f. anacantha, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a horticultural form of the rose species Rosa eglanteria.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
