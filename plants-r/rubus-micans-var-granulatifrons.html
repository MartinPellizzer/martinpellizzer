Rubus micans var. granulatifrons

Rubus micans var. granulatifrons, commonly known as the granulate bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a red, raspberry-like aggregate fruit that ripens in late summer.

This variety is native to parts of eastern North America and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed sites.
