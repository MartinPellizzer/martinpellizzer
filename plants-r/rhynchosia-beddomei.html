Rhynchosia beddomei

Rhynchosia beddomei, commonly known as the beddome's horsegram, is a species of legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Rhynchosia beddomei thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environments.
