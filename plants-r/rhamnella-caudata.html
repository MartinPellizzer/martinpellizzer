Rhamnella caudata

Rhamnella caudata, commonly known as the trailing arum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with long, trailing stems that can root at the nodes. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Rhamnella caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and interesting floral structure.
