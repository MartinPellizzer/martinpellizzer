Ranunculus siculus

Ranunculus siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ranunculus siculus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
