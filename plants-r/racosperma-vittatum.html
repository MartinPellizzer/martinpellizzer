Racosperma vittatum

Racosperma vittatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, bipinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
