Rosa subglobosa var. agrisensis

Rosa subglobosa var. agrisensis, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a compact shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense, rounded foliage and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. It is well adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in alpine settings.
