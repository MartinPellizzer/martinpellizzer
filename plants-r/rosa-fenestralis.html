Rosa fenestralis

Rosa fenestralis, commonly known as the window rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and shallow. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Rosa fenestralis is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
