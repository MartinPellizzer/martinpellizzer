Ranunculus dolichodon

Ranunculus dolichodon, commonly known as the long-beaked buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a basal rosette of leaves and produces clusters of yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. Its flowers have a distinctive long, narrow beak-like structure, giving it its common name.

Ranunculus dolichodon prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
