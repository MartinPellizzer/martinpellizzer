Rubus anadenes

Rubus anadenes, commonly known as the California blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
