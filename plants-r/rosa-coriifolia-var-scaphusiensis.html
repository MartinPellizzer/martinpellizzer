Rosa coriifolia var. scaphusiensis

Rosa coriifolia var. scaphusiensis, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, thornless stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
