Rubus × pustulatiformis

Rubus × pustulatiformis, commonly known as the flat-topped blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad, flat-topped inflorescences and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are usually armed with prickles.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
