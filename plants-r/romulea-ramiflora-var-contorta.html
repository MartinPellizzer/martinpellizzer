Romulea ramiflora var. contorta

Romulea ramiflora var. contorta, commonly known as the twisted ramiflorous romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, twisted, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. Its unique morphology and vibrant floral display make it a notable species in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
