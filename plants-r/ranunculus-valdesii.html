Ranunculus valdesii

Ranunculus valdesii, commonly known as the Valdes' buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or white flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to nine lobes, and its flowers have numerous stamens and a prominent central style. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
