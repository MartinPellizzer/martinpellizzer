Rubus coptophyllus f. palmatus

Rubus coptophyllus f. palmatus, commonly known as the palmate bramble, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven lobes resembling a hand. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
