Rubus × multifidifrons

Rubus × multifidifrons, commonly known as the multifidifrons blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations.
