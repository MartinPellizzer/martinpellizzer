Rhododendron sublanceolatum

Rhododendron sublanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-lanceleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Asia.

It features striking clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
