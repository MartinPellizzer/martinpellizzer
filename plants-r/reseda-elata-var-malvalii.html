Reseda elata var. malvalii

Reseda elata var. malvalii, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow upright and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a woolly texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Reseda elata var. malvalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
