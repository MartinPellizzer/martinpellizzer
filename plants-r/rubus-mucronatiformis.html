Rubus mucronatiformis

Rubus mucronatiformis, commonly known as the narrow-tipped bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that develop from its flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
