Rapanea coriifolia

Rapanea coriifolia, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that remain glossy throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
