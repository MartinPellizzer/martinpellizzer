Racosperma oldfieldii

Racosperma oldfieldii, commonly known as Oldfield's wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its typical pod fruit and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
