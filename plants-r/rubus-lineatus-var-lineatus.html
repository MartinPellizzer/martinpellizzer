Rubus lineatus var. lineatus

Rubus lineatus var. lineatus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
