Ruellia leucantha subsp. postinsularis

Ruellia leucantha subsp.

postinsularis, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in areas with high humidity.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
