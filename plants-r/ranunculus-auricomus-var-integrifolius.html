Ranunculus auricomus var. integrifolius

Ranunculus auricomus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a distinctive golden center. The leaves are typically palmately divided, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
