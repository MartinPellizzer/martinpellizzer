Rosa × ravellae

Rosa × ravellae, commonly known as the Ravalle rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the United States, specifically California.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical classification as a hybrid reflects its parentage from Rosa woodsii and Rosa californica.

The Ravalle rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
