Rubus × oblongithyrsus

Rubus × oblongithyrsus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are often used in culinary applications, though they can be quite tart.

Due to its vigorous growth and adaptability, it is often found in temperate regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
