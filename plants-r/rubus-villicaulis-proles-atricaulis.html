Rubus villicaulis proles atricaulis

Rubus villicaulis proles atricaulis, commonly known as the blackstem bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple small drupes.

This species is typically found in temperate regions, growing in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
