Racosperma ligulatum var. minus

Racosperma ligulatum var. minus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers arranged in dense, globular heads. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological significance includes providing nectar for native pollinators and serving as a habitat for various insects and small animals.
