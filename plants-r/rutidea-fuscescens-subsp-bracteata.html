Rutidea fuscescens subsp. bracteata

Rutidea fuscescens subsp.

bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive bracts and smaller, more compact growth form compared to other Rutidea species.
