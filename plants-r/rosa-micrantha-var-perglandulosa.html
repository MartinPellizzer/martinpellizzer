Rosa micrantha var. perglandulosa

Rosa micrantha var. perglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
