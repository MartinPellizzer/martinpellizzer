Ranunculus oreophilus subsp. balcanicus

Ranunculus oreophilus subsp.

balcanicus, commonly known as the Balkan water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat preference and is often found in shallow water or damp soil.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flower structure compared to the nominate form.
