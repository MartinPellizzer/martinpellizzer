Rubus prestonensis

Rubus prestonensis, commonly known as the Preston bramble, is a shrub native to the United Kingdom.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland and scrub habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
