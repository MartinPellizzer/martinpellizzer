Ranunculus tebulossicus

Ranunculus tebulossicus, commonly known as the Turkish buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountains of Turkey and the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and produces palmately divided, hairy leaves that are often lobed. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
