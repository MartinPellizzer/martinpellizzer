Rubus fruticosus var. affinis

Rubus fruticosus var. affinis, commonly known as bramble or blackberry bush, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, usually white or pink flowers that develop into edible berries. It thrives in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This species is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along coastal areas.
