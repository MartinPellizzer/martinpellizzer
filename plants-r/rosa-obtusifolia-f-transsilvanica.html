Rosa obtusifolia f. transsilvanica

Rosa obtusifolia f. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian rose, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
