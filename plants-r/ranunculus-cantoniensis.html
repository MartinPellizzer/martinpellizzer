Ranunculus cantoniensis

Ranunculus cantoniensis, commonly known as the Chinese buttercup, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
