Rubus guentheri

Rubus guentheri, commonly known as Guenther's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the rose family. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.
