Romulea oliveri

Romulea oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, early spring blooms. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred shape. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Romulea oliveri thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
