Rytidosperma lepidopodum

Rytidosperma lepidopodum, commonly known as the silky porcupine grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Rytidosperma lepidopodum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
