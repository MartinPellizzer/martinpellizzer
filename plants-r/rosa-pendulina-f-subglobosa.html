Rosa pendulina f. subglobosa

Rosa pendulina f. subglobosa, commonly known as the globe rose, is a compact, shrub-like species with rounded, semi-evergreen foliage.

Its flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small spaces or container gardening. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, and it is relatively low maintenance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
