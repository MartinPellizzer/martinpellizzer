Rubus radula microgene pustulatus

Rubus radula microgene pustulatus, commonly known as the spotted blackberry, is a rare and unique species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its small, dark berries that often have a distinctive spotted appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands or stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its limited distribution and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features.
