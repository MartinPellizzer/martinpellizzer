Rosa trachyphylla subsp. glabra

Rosa trachyphylla subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features thornless stems and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a smooth surface.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its botanical classification places it within the Rosaceae family, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
