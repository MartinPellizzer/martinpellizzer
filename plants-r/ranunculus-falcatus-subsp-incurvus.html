Ranunculus falcatus subsp. incurvus

Ranunculus falcatus subsp.

incurvus, commonly known as the bentleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often curved, giving it its common name.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
