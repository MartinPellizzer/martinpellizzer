Rhododendron insigne var. insigne

Rhododendron insigne var. insigne, commonly known as the grand rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting red or purple throat. The plant has leathery, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and densely arranged along the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
