Rubus fruticosus subsp. suberectus

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

suberectus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a perennial deciduous shrub native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that mature into dark purple to black berries.

The plant typically grows in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a flowering period in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
