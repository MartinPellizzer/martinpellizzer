Rosa onoei f. rosea

Rosa onoei f. rosea, commonly known as the pink form of the Japanese rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa onoei.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which are typically smaller than those of the typical form. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves consisting of five to seven leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate floral display and compact growth habit.
