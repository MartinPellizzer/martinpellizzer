Rudgea parquioides subsp. caprifolium

Rudgea parquioides subsp.

caprifolium, commonly known as the purple-leafed rudgea, is a species native to the high Andes of South America. It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and striking purple foliage.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
