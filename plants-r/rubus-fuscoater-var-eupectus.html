Rubus fuscoater var. eupectus

Rubus fuscoater var. eupectus, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
