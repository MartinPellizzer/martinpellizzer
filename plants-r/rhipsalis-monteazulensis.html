Rhipsalis monteazulensis

Rhipsalis monteazulensis, commonly known as the Monte Azul mistletoe, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its hanging, branched stems that bear small, white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests. Its foliage is usually green with a slight waxy coating, and it produces berries that are edible for certain wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
