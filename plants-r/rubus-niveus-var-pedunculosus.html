Rubus niveus var. pedunculosus

Rubus niveus var. pedunculosus, commonly known as the white-tipped blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its white-tipped bracts and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical structure includes compound leaves with serrated edges and an aggregate fruit formed from multiple drupelets.
