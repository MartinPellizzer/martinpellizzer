Rhododendron bainbridgeanum

Rhododendron bainbridgeanum, commonly known as Bainbridge's rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include sturdy stems, evergreen foliage, and a preference for acidic soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
