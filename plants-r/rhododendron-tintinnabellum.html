Rhododendron tintinnabellum

Rhododendron tintinnabellum, commonly known as the bell-shaped rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
