Rhus toxicodendra

Rhus toxicodendra, commonly known as staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like stems that are covered in a dense layer of stinging hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fuzzy berries in the fall. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges and a distinctive red coloration in the autumn.

Rhus toxicodendra is often found in moist, open woodlands and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
