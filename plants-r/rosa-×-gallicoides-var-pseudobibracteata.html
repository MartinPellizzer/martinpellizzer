Rosa × gallicoides var. pseudobibracteata

Rosa × gallicoides var. pseudobibracteata, commonly known as the French rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

The variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
