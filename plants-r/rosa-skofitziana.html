Rosa skofitziana

Rosa skofitziana, commonly known as the Skofitziana rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and is typically deciduous, shedding its foliage in the winter. Rosa skofitziana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
