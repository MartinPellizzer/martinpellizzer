Ranunculus flammula var. vulgaris

Ranunculus flammula var. vulgaris, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodland floors and along stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its small stature and common occurrence in various regions.
