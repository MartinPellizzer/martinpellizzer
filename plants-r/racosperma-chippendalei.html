Racosperma chippendalei

Racosperma chippendalei, commonly known as the Chippendale's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves composed of several narrow, linear leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which are typically visited by a variety of insects. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Racosperma chippendalei is well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to harsh conditions.
