Rubus × collinus var. setosoglandulosus

Rubus × collinus var. setosoglandulosus, commonly known as the rough-hairy blackberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
