Rungia tonkinensis

Rungia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers, which are usually blue or violet, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
