Ranunculus auricomus var. nothus

Ranunculus auricomus var. nothus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, often with a distinctive greenish center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
