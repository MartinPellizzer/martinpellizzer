Racosperma paradoxum

Racosperma paradoxum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Racosperma paradoxum is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
