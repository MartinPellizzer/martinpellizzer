Rosa virginiana var. arkansana

Rosa virginiana var. arkansana, commonly known as the Arkansas rose, is a subspecies of the common wild rose native to the central United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its hips, which are the fruit, are orange-red and contain seeds used in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
