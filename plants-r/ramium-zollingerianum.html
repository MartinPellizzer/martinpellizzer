Ramium zollingerianum

Ramium zollingerianum, commonly known as the Zollinger's grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
