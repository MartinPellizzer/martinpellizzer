Ranunculus arvensis

Ranunculus arvensis, commonly known as the field buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils, such as fields and along roadsides. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus arvensis is known for its toxic properties, containing protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation.
