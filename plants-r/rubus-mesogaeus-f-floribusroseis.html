Rubus mesogaeus f. floribusroseis

Rubus mesogaeus f. floribusroseis, commonly known as the rose-flowered raspberry.

This variant of the raspberry plant is characterized by its striking pink to rose-colored flowers, which appear in clusters during the spring. The foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus. It produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and fruit yield.
