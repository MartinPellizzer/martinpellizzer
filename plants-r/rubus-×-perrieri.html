Rubus × perrieri

Rubus × perrieri, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus fruticosus (brambles) and Rubus ideaus (red raspberries). The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a distinctive raspberry-like flavor. Its thorny stems and dense foliage make it a hardy, vigorous plant often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The botanical interest of Rubus × perrieri lies in its genetic diversity and adaptability to various soil conditions.
