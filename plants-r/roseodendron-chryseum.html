Roseodendron chryseum

Roseodendron chryseum, commonly known as the golden roseodendron, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a vivid contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
