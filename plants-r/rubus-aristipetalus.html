Rubus aristipetalus

Rubus aristipetalus, commonly known as the aristipetalus raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
