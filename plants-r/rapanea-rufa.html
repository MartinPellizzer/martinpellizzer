Rapanea rufa

Rapanea rufa, commonly known as the red-leafed holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn a striking red or orange-red in the autumn. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, dark berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Rapanea rufa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
