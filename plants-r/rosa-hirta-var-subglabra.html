Rosa hirta var. subglabra

Rosa hirta var. subglabra, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems covered with short, stiff hairs. The leaves are typically pinnate, with small, ovate leaflets that are also hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in spring.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
