Rubus × pseudoidaeus f. latifolius

Rubus × pseudoidaeus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a hybrid plant species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
