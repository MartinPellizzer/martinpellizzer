Ranunculus dissimilifolius

Ranunculus dissimilifolius, commonly known as the variable leaf buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped yellow flowers and variable leaf morphology, which can range from trifoliate to palmate. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are often deeply lobed, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus dissimilifolius is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
