Rosa × subcristata subsp. contraexcellens

Rosa × subcristata subsp.

contraexcellens, commonly known as the Chinese everblooming rose, is a hybrid rose species native to China. It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and profuse flowering habit, producing clusters of fragrant, pink flowers throughout the growing season.

The plant is well adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure. Its botanical features include simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for both ornamental and hedge applications.

This subspecies is valued for its continuous bloom and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
