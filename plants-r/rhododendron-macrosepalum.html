Rhododendron macrosepalum

Rhododendron macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and arranged in a dense, compact form. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron macrosepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.
