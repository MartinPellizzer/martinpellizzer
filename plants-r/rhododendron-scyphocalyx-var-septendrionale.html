Rhododendron scyphocalyx var. septendrionale

Rhododendron scyphocalyx var. septendrionale, commonly known as the purpleleaf rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that turn a striking purple-red color in the fall. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and seasonal color changes.
