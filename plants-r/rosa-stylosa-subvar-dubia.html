Rosa stylosa subvar. dubia

Rosa stylosa subvar. dubia, commonly known as the slender-stemmed wild rose, is a shrub native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, often arching stems and small, fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves with serrated edges, and its hips are typically small and orange-red, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in woodland or shaded areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
