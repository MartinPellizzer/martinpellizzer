Ranunculus geoides var. standleyi

Ranunculus geoides var. standleyi, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
