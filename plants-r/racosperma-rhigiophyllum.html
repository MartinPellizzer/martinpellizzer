Racosperma rhigiophyllum

Racosperma rhigiophyllum, commonly known as the river senna, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
