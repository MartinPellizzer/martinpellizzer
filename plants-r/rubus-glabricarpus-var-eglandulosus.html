Rubus glabricarpus var. eglandulosus

Rubus glabricarpus var. eglandulosus, commonly known as the smooth-fruited blackberry variant, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thornless stems and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
