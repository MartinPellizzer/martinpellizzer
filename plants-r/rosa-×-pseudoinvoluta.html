Rosa × pseudoinvoluta

Rosa × pseudoinvoluta, commonly known as the hybrid tea rose, is a species that results from the cross between Rosa centifolia and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have multiple layers of petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote blooming.

Rosa × pseudoinvoluta is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
