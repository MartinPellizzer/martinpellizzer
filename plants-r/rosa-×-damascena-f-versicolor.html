Rosa × damascena f. versicolor

Rosa × damascena f. versicolor, commonly known as the variegated damask rose, is a fragrant shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa, characterized by its diverse flower colors, including white, pink, and red, with some varieties exhibiting bicolor or variegated petals. The plant produces small, oval leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and culinary applications due to their rich, sweet scent.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
