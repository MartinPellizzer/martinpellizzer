Rhododendron × agastum var. pennivenium

Rhododendron × agastum var. pennivenium, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows in cool, humid climates and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
