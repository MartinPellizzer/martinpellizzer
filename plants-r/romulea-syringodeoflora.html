Romulea syringodeoflora

Romulea syringodeoflora, commonly known as the golden bulb orchid, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble those of the orchid genus, though it is not a true orchid. Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Romulea syringodeoflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
