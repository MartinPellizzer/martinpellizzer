Ranunculus pensylvanicus var. sieboldii

Ranunculus pensylvanicus var. sieboldii, commonly known as Siebold's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves divided into three to five lobes, which are usually hairy and serrated. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in native plant communities.
