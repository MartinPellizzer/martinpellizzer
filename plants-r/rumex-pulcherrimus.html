Rumex pulcherrimus

Rumex pulcherrimus, commonly known as the beautiful sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, reddish-veined leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor similar to rhubarb.
