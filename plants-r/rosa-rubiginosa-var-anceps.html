Rosa rubiginosa var. anceps

Rosa rubiginosa var. anceps, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces oval, reddish-brown fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
