Rosa communis var. grandifolia

Rosa communis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered rose, is a cultivar renowned for its showy, fragrant blooms.

It is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The flowers, which can be pink, red, or white, are typically large and borne in clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape and encourage blooming.

This variety is valued for its beauty and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
