Ranunculus valtae

Ranunculus valtae, commonly known as the Valtae buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers feature five to eight petals, usually in shades of yellow or orange.

Ranunculus valtae is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
