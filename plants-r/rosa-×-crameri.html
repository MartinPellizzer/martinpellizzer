Rosa × crameri

Rosa × crameri, commonly known as the Cramer's rose, is a hybrid species of rose that originated from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a shrub or small tree. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with thorny stems that provide structural support.

Rosa × crameri is adaptable to various soil types and is relatively disease-resistant, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
