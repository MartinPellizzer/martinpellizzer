Rosa jaroschenkoi

Rosa jaroschenkoi, commonly known as the Jaroschenskaya rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Rosa jaroschenkoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
