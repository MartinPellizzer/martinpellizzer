Rotala thymoides var. angustifolia

Rotala thymoides var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf waterwheel plant, is a aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or floating. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize sediment and improve water clarity.

It is a popular choice for pond landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
