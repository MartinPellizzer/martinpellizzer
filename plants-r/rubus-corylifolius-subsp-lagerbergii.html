Rubus corylifolius subsp. lagerbergii

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

lagerbergii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black fruits, which are typically small and aggregate in clusters.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its edible fruits in some regions.
