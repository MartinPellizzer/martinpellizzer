Rumex montanus proles amplexicaulis

Rumex montanus proles amplexicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, greenish flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and damp woodlands. Its leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties, and the plant is also valued as a forage crop for livestock.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to a variety of wetland environments.
