Ruta acutifolia var. perforata

Ruta acutifolia var. perforata, commonly known as the perforate rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, compound leaves with a distinctive perforated or fenestrated appearance, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is often cultivated in gardens.
