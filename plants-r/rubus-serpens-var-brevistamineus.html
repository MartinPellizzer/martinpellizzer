Rubus serpens var. brevistamineus

Rubus serpens var. brevistamineus, commonly known as the short-stamen blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it has a hairy stem covered in fine, stiff bristles.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
