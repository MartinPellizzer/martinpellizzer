Rubus × flaccididens

Rubus × flaccididens, commonly known as the flat-stemmed blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are usually compound, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries that are edible.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and as a source of edible fruit in certain regions.
