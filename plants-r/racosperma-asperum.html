Racosperma asperum

Racosperma asperum, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are typical of the genus Racosperma. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.

The seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for extended periods.
