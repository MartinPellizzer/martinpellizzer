Rosa glabrata var. typica

Rosa glabrata var. typica, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces rose hips that are typically red or orange. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
