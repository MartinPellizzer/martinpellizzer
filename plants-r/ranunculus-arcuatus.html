Ranunculus arcuatus

Ranunculus arcuatus, commonly known as the bentleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and often have a waxy coating, while the stems are hairy and upright.

Ranunculus arcuatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
