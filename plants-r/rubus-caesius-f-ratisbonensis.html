Rubus caesius f. ratisbonensis

Rubus caesius f. ratisbonensis, commonly known as the Ratisbon raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dense clusters of small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in jams and desserts.
