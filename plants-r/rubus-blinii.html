Rubus blinii

Rubus blinii, commonly known as the Blinii bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its berries, which are typically red when mature, are edible but often have a tart flavor.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shrublands and forest edges.
