Rubus villicaulis subsp. melanoxylon

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

melanoxylon, commonly known as black-stemmed bramble, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America. It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, juicy berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, often found in forest understories.
