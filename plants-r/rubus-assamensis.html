Rubus assamensis

Rubus assamensis, commonly known as the Assam blackberry, is a shrub native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
