Rosa afzeliana var. subbiserrata

Rosa afzeliana var. subbiserrata, commonly known as the serrated Rosa afzeliana, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins, typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, often with a slight red hue at the base. Its stems are woody and covered in thorns, which are characteristic of many rose species.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
