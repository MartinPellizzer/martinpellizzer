Ranunculus sceleratus subsp. multifidus

Ranunculus sceleratus subsp.

multifidus, commonly known as the multifidus buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streams and in wet meadows. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many species with similar floral structures.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in temperate regions with high humidity.
