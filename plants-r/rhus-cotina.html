Rhus cotina

Rhus cotina, commonly known as the sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into 11 to 31 leaflets, and its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in the fall. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance for poor soil conditions. Rhus cotina is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
