Ravenea xerophila

Ravenea xerophila, commonly known as the Desert Ravenea, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces striking yellow and red flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. Its slow growth rate and ability to store water in its trunk make it well-suited for drought-prone environments.

Ravenea xerophila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
