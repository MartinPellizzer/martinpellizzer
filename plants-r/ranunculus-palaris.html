Ranunculus palaris

Ranunculus palaris, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as streams, ponds, and wet meadows. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves that float on the water's surface or emerge from the water. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and numerous stamens, creating a showy appearance.

Ranunculus palaris plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
