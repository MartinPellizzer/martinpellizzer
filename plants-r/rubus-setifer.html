Rubus setifer

Rubus setifer, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters.

Rubus setifer is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
