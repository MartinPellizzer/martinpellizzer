Ricinocarpus macrodontus

Ricinocarpus macrodontus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are typically white or pink, with a distinctive cup-shaped structure that houses its reproductive organs. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow water while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

Ricinocarpus macrodontus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water quality through its natural filtration processes.
