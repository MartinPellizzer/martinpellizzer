Rubus × divexus

Rubus × divexus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
