Rosa lindbergiana

Rosa lindbergiana, commonly known as the Lindberg's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Rosa lindbergiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
