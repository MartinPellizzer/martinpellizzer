Ranunculus auricomus subsp. concinnifrons

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

concinnifrons, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, typically surrounded by a ring of white or pale yellow sepals.

The plant has a basal rosette of dark green, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental member of the Ranunculaceae family.
