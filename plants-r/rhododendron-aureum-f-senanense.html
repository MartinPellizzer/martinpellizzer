Rhododendron aureum f. senanense

Rhododendron aureum f. senanense, commonly known as the Senanese golden rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
