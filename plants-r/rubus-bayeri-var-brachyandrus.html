Rubus bayeri var. brachyandrus

Rubus bayeri var. brachyandrus, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus native to specific regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
