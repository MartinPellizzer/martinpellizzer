Rubus chloephilus

Rubus chloephilus, commonly known as the green-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and large, lobed leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
