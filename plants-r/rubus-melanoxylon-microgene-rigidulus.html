Rubus melanoxylon microgene rigidulus

Rubus melanoxylon microgene rigidulus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the southern hemisphere. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The rigidulus subspecies is distinguished by its dense, upright growth habit and robust stems.
