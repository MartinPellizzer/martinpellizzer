Rhynchospora polyphylla f. umbrosa

Rhynchospora polyphylla f. umbrosa, commonly known as the dark-leaved tussock sedge, is a variant of the tussock sedge species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest understoreys. It has a robust rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to form dense tussocks.

The foliage and floral structure of Rhynchospora polyphylla f. umbrosa make it a distinctive component of its native wetland ecosystems.
