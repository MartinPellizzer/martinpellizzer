Rosa ramosissima

Rosa ramosissima, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in mountainous regions.

Rosa ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
