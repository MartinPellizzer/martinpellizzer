Rubus obliquus

Rubus obliquus, commonly known as the bent-stem blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus obliquus is an important food source for various wildlife species, including birds and small mammals.
