Ranunculus squamosus

Ranunculus squamosus, commonly known as the scaly buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or white flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with lobed segments and a hairy stem that often bears small, scale-like structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland margins or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
