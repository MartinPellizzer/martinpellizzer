Rhododendron kochii

Rhododendron kochii, commonly known as the Koch's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a complex root system adapted to rocky soils.

Rhododendron kochii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountainous ecosystems.
