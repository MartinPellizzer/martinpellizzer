Ramium hirtum

Ramium hirtum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks in temperate regions.

Ramium hirtum is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
