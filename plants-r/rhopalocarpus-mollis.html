Rhopalocarpus mollis

Rhopalocarpus mollis, commonly known as the slender rock fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a soft, feathery appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Rhopalocarpus mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
