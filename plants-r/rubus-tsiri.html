Rubus tsiri

Rubus tsiri, commonly known as the Tsiri blackberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
