Rubus micans var. albifrons

Rubus micans var. albifrons, commonly known as the white-eyed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early summer. The fruit, which is typically a small, dark purple berry, is edible and has a tart flavor. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in local cuisine.
