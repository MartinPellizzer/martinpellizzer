Rubus crassus

Rubus crassus, commonly known as the thick-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and leathery leaves, which are often dark green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus crassus is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along the edges of forests.
