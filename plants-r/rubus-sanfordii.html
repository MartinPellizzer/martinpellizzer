Rubus sanfordii

Rubus sanfordii, commonly known as Sanford's blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in rocky soils and arid environments, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
