Rhodanthe polygalifolia

Rhodanthe polygalifolia, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Rhodanthe polygalifolia is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes.
