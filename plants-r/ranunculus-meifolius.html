Ranunculus meifolius

Ranunculus meifolius, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
