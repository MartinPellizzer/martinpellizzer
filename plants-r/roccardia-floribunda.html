Roccardia floribunda

Roccardia floribunda, commonly known as the flowering rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Roccardia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
