Rosa woodsii subsp. arizonica

Rosa woodsii subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose-like blooms in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
