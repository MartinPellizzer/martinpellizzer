Rubus macraei

Rubus macraei, commonly known as the Macrae's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
