Redfieldia hitchcockii var. micrantha

Redfieldia hitchcockii var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved redfieldia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
