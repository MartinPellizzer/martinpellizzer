Rosa diamantiaca

Rosa diamantiaca, commonly known as the Diamond Rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces oval, red or orange fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
