Rubus rosifolius normalis

Rubus rosifolius normalis, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a species of bramble in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

It is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges across its native range.
