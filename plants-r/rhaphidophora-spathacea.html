Rhaphidophora spathacea

Rhaphidophora spathacea, commonly known as the saddle leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with a short rhizome, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.
