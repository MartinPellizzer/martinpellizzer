Rosa cinnamomea var. intermedia

Rosa cinnamomea var. intermedia, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a hybrid species that exhibits a blend of characteristics from its parent species, Rosa cinnamomea and Rosa multiflora.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring arching, thornless stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange to reddish hips. Its leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptic leaflets that are often slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
