Rhamnus bithynica

Rhamnus bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Rhamnus bithynica is also noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
