Rosa sericea var. tetrapetala

Rosa sericea var. tetrapetala, commonly known as the white sand rose, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy foliage and showy white flowers with four petals, which is a distinguishing feature of the tetrapetala variety. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its botanical structure includes thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to xeriscaping in dry climates.
