Rosa transturkestanica

Rosa transturkestanica, commonly known as the Trans-Turkestan rose, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, pink to red flowers during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
