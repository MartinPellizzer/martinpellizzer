Rapistrum rugosum var. orientale

Rapistrum rugosum var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent aroma. The plant typically grows in saline or sandy soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant is also valued for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional cuisines.
