Rottlera alba

Rottlera alba, commonly known as white rottlera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and emits a strong citrus-like aroma. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Rottlera alba is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal uses.
