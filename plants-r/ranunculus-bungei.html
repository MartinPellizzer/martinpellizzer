Ranunculus bungei

Ranunculus bungei, commonly known as the Bunge's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.

Ranunculus bungei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with good drainage.
