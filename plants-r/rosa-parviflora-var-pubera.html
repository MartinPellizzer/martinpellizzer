Rosa parviflora var. pubera

Rosa parviflora var. pubera, commonly known as the pubescent shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubera," meaning "hairy" in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
