Rosa fendleri

Rosa fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Rosa fendleri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its unique nocturnal blooming habit.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.
