Rudbeckia mohrii

Rudbeckia mohrii, commonly known as Mohri's coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear large, showy yellow-orange flower heads with dark brown central disks. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
