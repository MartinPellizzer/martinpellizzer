Ruellia nudiflora var. yucatana

Ruellia nudiflora var. yucatana, commonly known as the Yucatan ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

This variety is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and long flowering period.
