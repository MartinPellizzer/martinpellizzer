Rosa pseudocomplicata

Rosa pseudocomplicata, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called hips, which are typically red when mature. Rosa pseudocomplicata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
