Rhododendron longipes

Rhododendron longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations between 1,000 and 3,000 meters. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron longipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
