Rubus poliophyllus var. ximengensis

Rubus poliophyllus var. ximengensis, commonly known as the Ximeng blackberry, is a variant of the bramble plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a distinctive, slightly tart flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and as a minor fruit crop.
