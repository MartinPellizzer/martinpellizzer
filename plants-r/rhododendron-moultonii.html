Rhododendron moultonii

Rhododendron moultonii, commonly known as the Moulton's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
