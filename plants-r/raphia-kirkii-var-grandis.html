Raphia kirkii var. grandis

Raphia kirkii var. grandis, commonly known as the giant African palm, is a large, fan-shaped palm native to the rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, robust leaves that can span several meters in diameter, making it one of the largest palms in the world. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, and its fruit is a fibrous, spiny capsule that contains a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.

The plant's trunk is thick and segmented, supporting its immense size and providing structural strength.
