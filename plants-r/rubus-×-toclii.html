Rubus × toclii

Rubus × toclii, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color, containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This hybrid is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
