Rochea dichotoma

Rochea dichotoma, commonly known as the twin-branch rochea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a distinctive dichotomous pattern, giving it its scientific name.

The flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.
