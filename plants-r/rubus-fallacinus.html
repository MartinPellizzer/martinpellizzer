Rubus fallacinus

Rubus fallacinus, commonly known as the purple-leafed bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically purple-tinged on the underside, and its clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are small, tart, and often used in jams and pies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Rubus fallacinus is a member of the Rosaceae family and is known for its thorny stems and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
