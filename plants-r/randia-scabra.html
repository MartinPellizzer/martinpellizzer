Randia scabra

Randia scabra, commonly known as the rough randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a rough, hairy texture on both surfaces. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are yellow when mature.

Randia scabra is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
