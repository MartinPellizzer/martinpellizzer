Rhus obscura

Rhus obscura, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically composed of 11 to 31 pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a common food source for birds. Rhus obscura thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
