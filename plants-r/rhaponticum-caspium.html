Rhaponticum caspium

Rhaponticum caspium, commonly known as the Caspian rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by spherical, red berries. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It is often cultivated for its edible roots and ornamental value in gardens.
