Ranunculus fuscus

Ranunculus fuscus, commonly known as the brown buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to nine sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus fuscus is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
