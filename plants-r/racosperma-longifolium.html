Racosperma longifolium

Racosperma longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes.

Racosperma longifolium is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy soils across arid and semi-arid regions.
