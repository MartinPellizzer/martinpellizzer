Ranunculus montanus subsp. hornschuchii

Ranunculus montanus subsp.

hornschuchii, commonly known as the Hornschuch's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that helps retain moisture in harsh environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and wildflower meadows.
