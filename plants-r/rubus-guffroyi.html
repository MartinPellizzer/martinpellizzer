Rubus guffroyi

Rubus guffroyi, commonly known as the Guffroy's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that typically ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, moist environments.

The botanical features of Rubus guffroyi make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
