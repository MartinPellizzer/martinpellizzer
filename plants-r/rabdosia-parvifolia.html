Rabdosia parvifolia

Rabdosia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale blue or purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Rabdosia parvifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
