Rosa caucasica var. collina

Rosa caucasica var. collina, commonly known as the Caucasian mountain rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa caucasica var. collina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
