Restrepia antennifera subsp. leontoglossa

Restrepia antennifera subsp.

leontoglossa, commonly known as the leopard orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to the Andes of South America. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its distinctive, hairy pseudobulbs and long, slender leaves.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern resembling a leopard's spots. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
