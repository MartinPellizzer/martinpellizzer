Ranunculus tripartitus

Ranunculus tripartitus, commonly known as the three-part buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive three-lobed structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, which is a key identifying feature.

Ranunculus tripartitus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful blooms.
