Ranunculus hirtellus var. hirtellus

Ranunculus hirtellus var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, nodding yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often surrounded by a ring of white sepals. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its hairy characteristics, with "hirtellus" referring to the hairy texture of its stems and leaves.
