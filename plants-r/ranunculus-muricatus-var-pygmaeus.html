Ranunculus muricatus var. pygmaeus

Ranunculus muricatus var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and meadows. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture. It is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many regions.
