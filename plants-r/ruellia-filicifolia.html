Ruellia filicifolia

Ruellia filicifolia, commonly known as the fernleaf ruellia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its attractive, fern-like leaves that are often deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Ruellia filicifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
