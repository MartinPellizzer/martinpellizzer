Radermachera quadripinnata subsp. lobbii

Radermachera quadripinnata subsp.

lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's four-feather maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmate leaves that give it a maple-like appearance.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
