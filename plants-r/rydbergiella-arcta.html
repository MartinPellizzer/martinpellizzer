Rydbergiella arcta

Rydbergiella arcta, commonly known as the Arctic lousewort, is a small flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy, grayish-green leaves. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Rydbergiella arcta plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators in its harsh environment.
