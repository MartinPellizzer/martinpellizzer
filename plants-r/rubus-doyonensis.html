Rubus doyonensis

Rubus doyonensis, commonly known as the Doyon's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested for their tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
