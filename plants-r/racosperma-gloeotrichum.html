Racosperma gloeotrichum

Racosperma gloeotrichum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Racosperma gloeotrichum is often used in revegetation projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
