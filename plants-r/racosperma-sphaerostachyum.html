Racosperma sphaerostachyum

Racosperma sphaerostachyum, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. Its yellow flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it its scientific name.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
