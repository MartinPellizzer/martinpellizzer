Rubus nemoralis var. glabratus

Rubus nemoralis var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and foliage, distinguishing it from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
