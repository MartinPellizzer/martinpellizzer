Rhaphis pauciflora

Rhaphis pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered rhaphis, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its slender, arching stems. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are typically followed by small, round fruits. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Rhaphis pauciflora is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its compact size and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
