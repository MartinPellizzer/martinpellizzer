Rhamnus triflora

Rhamnus triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, reddish berries that ripen in the fall. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
