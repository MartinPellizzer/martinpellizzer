Ranunculus auricomus subsp. papilionaceus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

papilionaceus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow, butterfly-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has palmately divided leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, streambanks, and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
