Rubus × nudicaulis

Rubus × nudicaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It is native to western North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
