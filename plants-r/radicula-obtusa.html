Radicula obtusa

Radicula obtusa, commonly known as the blunt radicula, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a short, fibrous root system and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Radicula obtusa is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils, making it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
