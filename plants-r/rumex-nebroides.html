Rumex nebroides

Rumex nebroides, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright, slender stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Rumex nebroides thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in wetlands and along stream banks.
