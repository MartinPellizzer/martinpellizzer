Raimannia longiflora

Raimannia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered raimannia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, elongated flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Raimannia longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
