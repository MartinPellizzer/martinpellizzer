Rubus micans var. amygdalothyrsus

Rubus micans var. amygdalothyrsus, commonly known as the sweet-scented raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, reddish stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, with a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible berries.
