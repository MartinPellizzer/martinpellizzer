Rhododendron faithiae

Rhododendron faithiae, commonly known as the Faith's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with intricate patterns.

Rhododendron faithiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique beauty.
