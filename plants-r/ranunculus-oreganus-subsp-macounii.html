Ranunculus oreganus subsp. macounii

Ranunculus oreganus subsp.

macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streambanks.

The plant features showy yellow flowers with five to seven petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed and hairy. It has a short, stout stem that supports the flowers and is often surrounded by a basal rosette of leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
