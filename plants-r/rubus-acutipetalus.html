Rubus acutipetalus

Rubus acutipetalus, commonly known as the sharp-petioled blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The botanical features of Rubus acutipetalus make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
