Rubus bollianus

Rubus bollianus, commonly known as the Bollianus blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
