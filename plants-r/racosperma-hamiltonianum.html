Racosperma hamiltonianum

Racosperma hamiltonianum, commonly known as the Hamilton's acacia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
