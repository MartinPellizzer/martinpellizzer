Restio gaudichaudianus

Restio gaudichaudianus, commonly known as the golden restio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained conditions to flourish.
