Rhodiola tangutica

Rhodiola tangutica, commonly known as the Tibetan rhodiola, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low oxygen levels. Its roots contain various bioactive compounds, including rosavins and salidrosides, which are responsible for its adaptogenic properties.

Rhodiola tangutica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported ability to enhance physical endurance and reduce stress.
