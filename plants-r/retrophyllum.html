Retrophyllum

Retrophyllum, commonly known as the backward leaf plant, is a rare and unique species characterized by its unusual leaf arrangement.

Its leaves grow in a spiral pattern that appears to curve backward, creating a distinctive visual effect. This plant is native to specific regions with high humidity and moderate temperatures. It thrives in shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, Retrophyllum is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
