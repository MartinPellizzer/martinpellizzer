Rhododendron dauricum subsp. mucronulatum

Rhododendron dauricum subsp.

mucronulatum, commonly known as the Daurian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white to grayish hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
