Rhododendron × russelianum f. album

Rhododendron × russelianum f. album, commonly known as the White Rhododendron, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its pale blossoms.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
