Rumex vesceritensis var. papillosus

Rumex vesceritensis var. papillosus, commonly known as the hairy dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces elongated, ribbed fruits.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
