Rhododendron loranthiflorum subsp. loranthiflorum

Rhododendron loranthiflorum subsp.

loranthiflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from red to pink.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
