Rosa semperflorens

Rosa semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse, continuous blooming throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a high degree of variability in color. It is widely cultivated as a garden plant due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in various climates.

The leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.
