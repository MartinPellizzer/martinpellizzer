Rosa pomifera f. murithii

Rosa pomifera f. murithii, commonly known as the Murithii rose, is a unique horticultural form of the rose family.

It is characterized by its large, edible fruits, which resemble fuzzy, white berries and are often referred to as "rose hips.". The plant is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its potential use in culinary and medicinal applications.
