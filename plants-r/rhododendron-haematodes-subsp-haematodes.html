Rhododendron haematodes subsp. haematodes

Rhododendron haematodes subsp.

haematodes, commonly known as the bloodroot rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, thriving in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers are usually pink or reddish with a contrasting white throat.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
