Rosa subafzeliana

Rosa subafzeliana, commonly known as the Afghan rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne on long pedicels. Rosa subafzeliana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
