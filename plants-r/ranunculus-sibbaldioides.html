Ranunculus sibbaldioides

Ranunculus sibbaldioides, commonly known as the Sibbald's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
