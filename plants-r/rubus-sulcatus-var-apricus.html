Rubus sulcatus var. apricus

Rubus sulcatus var. apricus, commonly known as the apricot raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, resembling apricots in appearance. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are often used in jams and preserves due to their tart flavor.
