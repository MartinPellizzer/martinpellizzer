Rosa platysepala

Rosa platysepala, commonly known as the flat-sepal rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, often fragrant flowers that typically have five to seven petals. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called hips, which are edible and contain seeds. Rosa platysepala thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
