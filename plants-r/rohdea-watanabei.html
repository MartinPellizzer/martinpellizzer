Rohdea watanabei

Rohdea watanabei, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of white or pale green flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, shade-tolerant plant.
