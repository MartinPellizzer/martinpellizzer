Ranunculus fallax subsp. lucorum

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

lucorum, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, though it prefers cool, temperate climates.
