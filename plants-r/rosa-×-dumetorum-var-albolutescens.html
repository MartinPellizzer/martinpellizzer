Rosa × dumetorum var. albolutescens

Rosa × dumetorum var. albolutescens, commonly known as the white-flowered shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often forming low shrubs with thorny stems. The plant produces small, white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
