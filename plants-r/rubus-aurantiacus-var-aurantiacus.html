Rubus aurantiacus var. aurantiacus

Rubus aurantiacus var. aurantiacus, commonly known as the scarlet raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of bright red, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and has a shallow, fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used both in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
