Rubus hassleri var. paraguariensis

Rubus hassleri var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan raspberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and northern Argentina.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
