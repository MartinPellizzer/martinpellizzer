Rosa mollis subf. apylota

Rosa mollis subf.

apylota, commonly known as the soft rose or woolly rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in pink to white hues.

The plant produces rose hips that are often used in traditional medicine and herbal remedies. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in apothecary practices.
