Rubus setosolignosus

Rubus setosolignosus, commonly known as the bristly thorned blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
