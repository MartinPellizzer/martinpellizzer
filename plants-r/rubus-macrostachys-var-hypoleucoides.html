Rubus macrostachys var. hypoleucoides

Rubus macrostachys var. hypoleucoides, commonly known as the white-flowered bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and the berries are small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark red or black color. This plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects, due to its nectar-rich flowers and edible fruits.
