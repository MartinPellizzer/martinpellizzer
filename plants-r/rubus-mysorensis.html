Rubus mysorensis

Rubus mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest areas. The plant produces small, red, aggregate fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are thorny.

Rubus mysorensis plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
