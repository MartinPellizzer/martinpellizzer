Rosa × dumetorum f. pseudoglabra

Rosa × dumetorum f. pseudoglabra, commonly known as the rough-stemmed dog rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves.

Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

It produces small, red, hairy fruits known as hip fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
