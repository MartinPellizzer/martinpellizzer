Rubus tomentosus var. dissimilis

Rubus tomentosus var. dissimilis, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

This variant is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark, velvety foliage. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy underside, and the plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
