Ranunculus demissus var. major

Ranunculus demissus var. major, commonly known as the greater alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and the stems are often hairy.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
