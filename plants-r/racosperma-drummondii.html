Racosperma drummondii

Racosperma drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
