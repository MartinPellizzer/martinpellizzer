Racosperma tratmanianum

Racosperma tratmanianum, commonly known as the Waratah, is a striking shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It is often found in coastal and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

The Waratah is a symbol of New South Wales and is celebrated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
