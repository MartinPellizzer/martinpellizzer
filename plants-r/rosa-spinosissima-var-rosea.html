Rosa spinosissima var. rosea

Rosa spinosissima var. rosea, commonly known as the pink spiny rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical use in herbal remedies.
