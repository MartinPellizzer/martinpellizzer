Rosa beggeriana var. lioui

Rosa beggeriana var. lioui, commonly known as the Liou's rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dark green, leathery leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
