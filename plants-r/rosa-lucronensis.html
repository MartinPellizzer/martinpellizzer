Rosa lucronensis

Rosa lucronensis, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems covered in thorns and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Rosa lucronensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
