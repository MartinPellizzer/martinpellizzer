Ranunculus kittilensis

Ranunculus kittilensis, commonly known as the Kittil's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals.

Ranunculus kittilensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
