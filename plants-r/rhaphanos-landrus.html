Rhaphanos landrus

Rhaphanos landrus, commonly known as the African turnip, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. The plant features alternate, pinnately compound leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the family.

The edible roots and leaves are often used in traditional African cuisine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
