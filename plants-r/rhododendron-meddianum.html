Rhododendron meddianum

Rhododendron meddianum, commonly known as the Meddian rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features dense, broad leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a flowering period that coincides with the warmer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
