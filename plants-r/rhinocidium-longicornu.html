Rhinocidium longicornu

Rhinocidium longicornu, commonly known as the longhorned rhinocidium, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated floral structures that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant features vibrant, often bi-colored petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically broad and waxy, adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments.

Rhinocidium longicornu is also noted for its slow growth and rarity in cultivation, making it a prized specimen among botanists and horticulturists.
