Ranunculus auricomus subsp. multistamineus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

multistamineus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 11 sepals that are often adorned with red or brown markings.

The plant has a short, hairy stem and basal leaves that are deeply lobed or divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its multiple stamens, which contribute to its unique floral structure.
