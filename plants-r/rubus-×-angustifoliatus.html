Rubus × angustifoliatus

Rubus × angustifoliatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary and commercial applications.
