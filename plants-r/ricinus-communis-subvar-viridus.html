Ricinus communis subvar. viridus

Ricinus communis subvar. viridus, commonly known as the green castor plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its distinctively green foliage.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces unique, spiny seed capsules that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and industry. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and often have a velvety texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
