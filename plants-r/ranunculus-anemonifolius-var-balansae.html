Ranunculus anemonifolius var. balansae

Ranunculus anemonifolius var. balansae, commonly known as the Balansae buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow petals that resemble those of the common buttercup, though it has a more refined and delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble those of an anemone, giving it its species name.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
