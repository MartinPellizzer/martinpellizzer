Rosa facsarii

Rosa facsarii, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a species native to Chile and is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
