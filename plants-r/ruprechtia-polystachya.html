Ruprechtia polystachya

Ruprechtia polystachya, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

Ruprechtia polystachya is also noted for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and floral display.
