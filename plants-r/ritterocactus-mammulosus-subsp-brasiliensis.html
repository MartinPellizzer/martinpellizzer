Ritterocactus mammulosus subsp. brasiliensis

Ritterocactus mammulosus subsp.

brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brasiliensis Ritterocactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
