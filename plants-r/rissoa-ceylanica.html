Rissoa ceylanica

Rissoa ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, dark brown seeds used in traditional medicine and as a protein source. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Due to its nutritional value, it has been studied for potential agricultural and medicinal applications.
