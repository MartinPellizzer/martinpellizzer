Rubus × obscuratus

Rubus × obscuratus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to parts of North America and is known for its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits, though it can be invasive in some regions.
