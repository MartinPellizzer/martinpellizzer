Robinia tomentosa

Robinia tomentosa, commonly known as the honey locust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a dense, silvery-gray pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its root system is capable of fixing nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.

The tree is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
