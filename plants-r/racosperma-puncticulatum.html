Racosperma puncticulatum

Racosperma puncticulatum, commonly known as the punctuated acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often glandular and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by wind.

Racosperma puncticulatum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
