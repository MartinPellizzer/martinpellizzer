Rosa × axmannii var. coriacea

Rosa × axmannii var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, waxy cuticle. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
