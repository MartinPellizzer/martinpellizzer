Rosa canina f. sassinensis

Rosa canina f. sassinensis, commonly known as the Sardinian dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces pink to red flowers in early summer, followed by orange to reddish hips that are rich in vitamin C. Its botanical features include a woody structure, pinnate leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
