Restio multiflorus var. tuberculatus

Restio multiflorus var. tuberculatus, commonly known as the knobbed restio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, where it contributes to the biodiversity of the region.

The species is notable for its distinctive tuberculate (knobbed) nodes on its stems.
