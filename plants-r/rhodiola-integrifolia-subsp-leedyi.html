Rhodiola integrifolia subsp. leedyi

Rhodiola integrifolia subsp.

leedyi, commonly known as the Sierra lily or mountain rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of western North America. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers in late summer. It is adapted to cold climates and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
