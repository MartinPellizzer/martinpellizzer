Rubus microstachys

Rubus microstachys, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow in color, similar to other raspberries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
