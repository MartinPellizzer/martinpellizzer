Rubus ericetorum

Rubus ericetorum, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Rubus ericetorum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food for birds and insects.
