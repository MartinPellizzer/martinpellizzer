Rosa obtusifolia var. hispidior

Rosa obtusifolia var. hispidior, commonly known as the hispid rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
