Ranunculus nephelogenes

Ranunculus nephelogenes, commonly known as the cloudland buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus nephelogenes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow or white blooms.
