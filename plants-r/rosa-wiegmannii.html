Rosa wiegmannii

Rosa wiegmannii, commonly known as the Wiegmann's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries such as Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of fragrant pink to red flowers. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Rosa wiegmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
