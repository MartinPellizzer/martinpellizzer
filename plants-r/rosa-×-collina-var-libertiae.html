Rosa × collina var. libertiae

Rosa × collina var. libertiae, commonly known as the Liberty rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a shrub or ground cover. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its botanical features include thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its showy blooms.
