Rhododendron fuchsiiflorum

Rhododendron fuchsiiflorum, commonly known as the purple-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from deep purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its floral structure consists of five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ericaceae family.

Rhododendron fuchsiiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
