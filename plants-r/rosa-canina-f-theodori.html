Rosa canina f. theodori

Rosa canina f. theodori, commonly known as the Theodori form of the dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose, Rosa canina.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, including larger, more showy flowers with a deeper pink hue compared to the typical form. The plant exhibits robust growth habits, often forming dense shrubs with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
