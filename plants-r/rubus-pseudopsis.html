Rubus pseudopsis

Rubus pseudopsis, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a tart, wild flavor.
