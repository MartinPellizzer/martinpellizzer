Rosa communis var. subcuneata

Rosa communis var. subcuneata, commonly known as the narrow-angled rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its distinctive leaf shape.

It typically features a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and compound leaves that have a narrow, acute base. The flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. It is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
