Rubus × congestus

Rubus × congestus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the raspberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry). The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart and complex flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, often cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
