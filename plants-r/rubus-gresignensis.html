Rubus gresignensis

Rubus gresignensis, commonly known as the Gresigna bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Italy, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen in the summer. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered plant.
