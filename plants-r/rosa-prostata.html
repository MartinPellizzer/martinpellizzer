Rosa prostata

Rosa prostata, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a low-growing shrub native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its hardy nature, Rosa prostata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
