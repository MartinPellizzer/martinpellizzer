Ribes hirticaule

Ribes hirticaule, commonly known as hairy currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a hairy underside.

Ribes hirticaule is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
