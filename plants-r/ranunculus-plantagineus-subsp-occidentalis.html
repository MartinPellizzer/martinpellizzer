Ranunculus plantagineus subsp. occidentalis

Ranunculus plantagineus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, lobed leaf structure.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy clusters of flowers that attract pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
