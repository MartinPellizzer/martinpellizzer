Rosa communis var. pseudomontivaga

Rosa communis var. pseudomontivaga, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand cold climates, contributing to its popularity among gardeners in temperate regions.
