Rubus schleicheri subsp. inaequabilis

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

inaequabilis, commonly known as the unequal-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth.

The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins and a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
