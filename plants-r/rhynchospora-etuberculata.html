Rhynchospora etuberculata

Rhynchospora etuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. Rhynchospora etuberculata thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
