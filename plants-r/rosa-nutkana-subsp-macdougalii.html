Rosa nutkana subsp. macdougalii

Rosa nutkana subsp.

macdougalii, commonly known as the MacDougal's rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring arching stems covered in thorny projections.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange to red hips. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in native habitats.
