Rosa evoluta

Rosa evoluta, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces thorny stems.

Rosa evoluta is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and botanical collections.
