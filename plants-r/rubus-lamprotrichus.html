Rubus lamprotrichus

Rubus lamprotrichus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of silvery hairs on its leaves and stems. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus lamprotrichus is often found in disturbed areas and along stream banks.
