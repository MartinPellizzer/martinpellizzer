Rumex acetoselloides

Rumex acetoselloides, commonly known as sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Rumex acetoselloides is known for its tart, sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid in its leaves.
