Rubus dissimilis

Rubus dissimilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
