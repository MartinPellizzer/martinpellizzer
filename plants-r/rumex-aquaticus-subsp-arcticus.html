Rumex aquaticus subsp. arcticus

Rumex aquaticus subsp.

arcticus, commonly known as Arctic sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions. It typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found near water bodies such as lakes and streams.

The plant has alternate, broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in terminal spikes, with greenish-yellow bracts.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, flowering and setting seed quickly during the summer months.
