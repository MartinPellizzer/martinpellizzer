Rubus subtibetanus var. glandulosus

Rubus subtibetanus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau and neighboring regions. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The variety is distinguished by its glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a unique appearance and some degree of resistance to certain pests.
