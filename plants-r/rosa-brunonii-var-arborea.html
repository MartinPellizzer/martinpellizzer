Rosa brunonii var. arborea

Rosa brunonii var. arborea, commonly known as the tree rose, is a unique variant of the Rosa brunonii species.

It is characterized by its woody stems that form a tree-like structure, distinguishing it from the more typical shrub-form roses. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the blossoms.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
