Restio parviflorus

Restio parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy environments, often in the understory of fynbos vegetation. It produces tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its ability to colonize open areas.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
