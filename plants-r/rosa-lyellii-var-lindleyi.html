Rosa lyellii var. lindleyi

Rosa lyellii var. lindleyi, commonly known as the Lyell's rose, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
