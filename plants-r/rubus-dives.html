Rubus dives

Rubus dives, commonly known as the purple-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark, nearly black stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to edible, dark purple berries. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive purple hue on the underside.

Rubus dives is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
