Rytidosperma javanicum

Rytidosperma javanicum, commonly known as the Java grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Rytidosperma javanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
