Rubus koehleri subsp. asperidens

Rubus koehleri subsp.

asperidens, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
