Rhododendron phaeochristum

Rhododendron phaeochristum, commonly known as the purple-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a striking purple hue, especially during the growing season. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates with partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
