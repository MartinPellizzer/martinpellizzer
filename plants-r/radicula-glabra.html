Radicula glabra

Radicula glabra, commonly known as the smooth radicula, is a small herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically smooth and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Radicula glabra thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
