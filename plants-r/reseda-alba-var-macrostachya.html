Reseda alba var. macrostachya

Reseda alba var. macrostachya, commonly known as white cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of stems, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
