Rubus odoratus f. paraheteradenius

Rubus odoratus f. paraheteradenius, commonly known as the fragrant raspberry, is a botanical variant of the common raspberry.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a unique combination of stamens and pistils. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its aromatic qualities and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
