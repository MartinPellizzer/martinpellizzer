Rosa × belgica

Rosa × belgica, commonly known as the Belgian rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink or white flowers with multiple layers of petals. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Rosa × belgica is also valued for its use in perfumery due to the aromatic qualities of its blooms.
