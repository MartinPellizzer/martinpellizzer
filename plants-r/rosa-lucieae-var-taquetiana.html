Rosa lucieae var. taquetiana

Rosa lucieae var. taquetiana, commonly known as the Taquet's rose, is a rare and unique variant of the Rosa lucieae species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists.
