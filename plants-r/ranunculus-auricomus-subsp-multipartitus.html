Ranunculus auricomus subsp. multipartitus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

multipartitus, commonly known as the golden crown imperial, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure, which is formed by the reflexed sepals.

The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and showy blooms.
