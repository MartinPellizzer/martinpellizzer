Ranunculus nicklesi

Ranunculus nicklesi, commonly known as the Nickles' buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically arranged in a cup-like structure. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are deeply lobed, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
