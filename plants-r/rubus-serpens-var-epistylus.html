Rubus serpens var. epistylus

Rubus serpens var. epistylus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

This variant is a trailing shrub native to South America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features include hairy foliage and a distinctive floral structure that aids in pollination.
