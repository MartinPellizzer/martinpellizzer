Reichertia gracilis

Reichertia gracilis, commonly known as the slender reichertia, is a small shrub native to the south-western regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Reichertia gracilis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its succulent-like leaves and deep root system.

It is also noted for its role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
