Rosa globularis var. imponens

Rosa globularis var. imponens, commonly known as the globular rose, is a unique cultivar characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive double petal structure. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange, containing seeds used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking a low-maintenance yet visually appealing plant.
