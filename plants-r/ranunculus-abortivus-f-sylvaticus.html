Ranunculus abortivus f. sylvaticus

Ranunculus abortivus f. sylvaticus, commonly known as the woodland buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and yellow flowers with five to seven petals. This variety typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in naturalized areas across temperate regions.
