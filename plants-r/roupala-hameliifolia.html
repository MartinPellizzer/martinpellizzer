Roupala hameliifolia

Roupala hameliifolia, commonly known as the silky roupala, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves with a silky, hairy texture on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
