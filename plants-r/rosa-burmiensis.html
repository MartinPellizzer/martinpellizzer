Rosa burmiensis

Rosa burmiensis, commonly known as the Burmese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and an upright growth habit.

Rosa burmiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
