Rubus × similifrons

Rubus × similifrons, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe, though they may also be black or purple. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible and have a flavor similar to blackberries, with a slightly tart profile.
