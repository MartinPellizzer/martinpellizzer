Roseodendron millsii

Roseodendron millsii, commonly known as the Mill's roseodendron, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle iridescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in the understory of dense forests. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of epiphytic organisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
