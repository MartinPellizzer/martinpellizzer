Ranunculus peucedanifolius

Ranunculus peucedanifolius, commonly known as the peucedanifolius buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
