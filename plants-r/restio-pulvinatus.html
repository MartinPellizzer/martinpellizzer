Restio pulvinatus

Restio pulvinatus, commonly known as the purple restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in dune ecosystems.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
