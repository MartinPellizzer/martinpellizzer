Rhododendron glaphyrum var. dealbatum

Rhododendron glaphyrum var. dealbatum, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
