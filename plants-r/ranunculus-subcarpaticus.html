Ranunculus subcarpaticus

Ranunculus subcarpaticus, commonly known as the Karakorum buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus subcarpaticus is often found in areas with high altitude and limited vegetation, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
