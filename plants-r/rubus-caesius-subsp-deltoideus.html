Rubus caesius subsp. deltoideus

Rubus caesius subsp.

deltoideus, commonly known as the deltoid raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
