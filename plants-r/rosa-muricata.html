Rosa muricata

Rosa muricata, commonly known as the spiny rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cooler, temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa muricata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
