Rosa rubella f. elata

Rosa rubella f. elata, commonly known as the Elated Rosa, is a botanical variety characterized by its distinctive floral structure and coloration.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to specific regions in the northern hemisphere. The plant features showy, semi-double flowers that are typically a blend of red and white, with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its adaptability to various soil types.
