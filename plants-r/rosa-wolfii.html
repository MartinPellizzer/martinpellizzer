Rosa wolfii

Rosa wolfii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. Rosa wolfii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
