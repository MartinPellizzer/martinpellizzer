Rubus villicaulis var. obtusicaulis

Rubus villicaulis var. obtusicaulis, commonly known as the blunt-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with rounded lobes. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy foliage and its role in local ecosystems.
