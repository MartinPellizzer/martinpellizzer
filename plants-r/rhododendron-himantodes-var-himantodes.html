Rhododendron himantodes var. himantodes

Rhododendron himantodes var. himantodes, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dense thickets and is often found in temperate to subalpine habitats. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink and red.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
