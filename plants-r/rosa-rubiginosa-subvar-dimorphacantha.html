Rosa rubiginosa subvar. dimorphacantha

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. dimorphacantha, commonly known as the redstem rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. Its hips, which are orange-red berries, contain seeds and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
