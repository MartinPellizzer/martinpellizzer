Rosa canina f. biserrata

Rosa canina f. biserrata, commonly known as the serrated dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its hips, which are the fruit, are typically orange-red and contain numerous seeds.

This form is often found in woodland edges and scrub habitats across Europe.
