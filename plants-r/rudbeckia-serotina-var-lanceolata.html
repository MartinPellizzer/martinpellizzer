Rudbeckia serotina var. lanceolata

Rudbeckia serotina var. lanceolata, commonly known as the late yellow coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces large, showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a brown, woolly center, blooming from late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and seeds for birds.
