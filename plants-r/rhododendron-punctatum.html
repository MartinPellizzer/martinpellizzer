Rhododendron punctatum

Rhododendron punctatum, commonly known as the spotted rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture, while the flowers often bear distinctive spots or markings. This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
