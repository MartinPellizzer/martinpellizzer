Rosa coriifolia var. glandulifera

Rosa coriifolia var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in glandular dots. Its fruit is a red, fuzzy rose hip that matures in autumn.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
