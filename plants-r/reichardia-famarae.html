Reichardia famarae

Reichardia famarae, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, forming a composite head that resembles a dandelion.

Reichardia famarae is often found in regions with warm, temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
