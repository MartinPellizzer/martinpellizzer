Rosa × sublaevis

Rosa × sublaevis, commonly known as the flat-stemmed rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairless stems and broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically in clusters of five petals. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

Rosa × sublaevis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
