Rubus tereticaulis microgene argutipilus

Rubus tereticaulis microgene argutipilus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and culinary uses.
