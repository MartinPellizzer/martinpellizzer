Ranunculus helenae

Ranunculus helenae, commonly known as the Helen's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves and produces small, cup-shaped blooms in shades of yellow, white, or pink. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
