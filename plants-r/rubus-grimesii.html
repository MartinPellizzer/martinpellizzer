Rubus grimesii

Rubus grimesii, commonly known as Grimes' blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable resource for both wildlife and humans.
