Reynoutria japonica var. uzenensis

Reynoutria japonica var. uzenensis, commonly known as Japanese knotweed variant Uzenensis, is a variant of the invasive plant species Reynoutria japonica.

It is characterized by its robust growth and rhizomatous root system, which allows it to spread rapidly and aggressively. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It produces small, pale green flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This variant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is known for its ecological impact due to its competitive nature.
