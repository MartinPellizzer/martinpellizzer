Roegneria abolinii var. pluriflora

Roegneria abolinii var. pluriflora, commonly known as the Chinese forest grass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in natural landscapes.
