Rhododendron × filidactylis

Rhododendron × filidactylis, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a species resulting from the cross between Rhododendron ferrugineum and Rhododendron caucasicum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its botanical features include leathery leaves with a glossy surface and inflorescences that can range in color from pink to red.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
