Rubus aculeatipes

Rubus aculeatipes, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale yellow flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are often used in jams and wines.
