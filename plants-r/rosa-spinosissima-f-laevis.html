Rosa spinosissima f. laevis

Rosa spinosissima f. laevis, commonly known as the smooth spiny rose, is a botanical variant of the spiny rose species.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and foliage, distinguishing it from the typical prickly form of its parent species. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five to seven petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its attractive appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of temperate climates.
