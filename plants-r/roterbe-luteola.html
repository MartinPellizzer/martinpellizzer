Roterbe luteola

Roterbe luteola, commonly known as the yellow poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
