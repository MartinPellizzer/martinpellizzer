Rubus pauxillus

Rubus pauxillus, commonly known as the small-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
