Rosa canina f. suboblonga

Rosa canina f. suboblonga, commonly known as the suboblong form of the dog rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its elongated, suboblong-shaped leaves.

This form exhibits slightly modified leaf morphology compared to the typical Rosa canina, with broader and more elongated leaflets. The flowers are similar to those of the standard dog rose, featuring pink to red petals and a fragrant aroma. It is typically found in hedgerows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and resilience in various climates.
