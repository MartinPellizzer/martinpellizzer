Rhynchotheca spinosa var. integrifolia

Rhynchotheca spinosa var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf rhynchotheca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a robust, branching stem covered with spiny structures, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have entire margins, giving it its species name "integrifolia.". The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season.

It is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can tolerate periods of drought.
