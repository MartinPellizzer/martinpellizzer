Rudbeckia grandiflora

Rudbeckia grandiflora, commonly known as the orange coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a dark brown central disk, which gives it a distinctive coneflower appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces its vibrant blooms from late summer into early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Rudbeckia grandiflora is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
