Rosa coriifolia var. subpallens

Rosa coriifolia var. subpallens, commonly known as the pale-flowered shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a velvety texture. It produces red hips that contain seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
