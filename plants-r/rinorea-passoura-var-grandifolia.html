Rinorea passoura var. grandifolia

Rinorea passoura var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered rinorea, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
