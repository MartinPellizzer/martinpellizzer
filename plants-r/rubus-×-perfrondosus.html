Rubus × perfrondosus

Rubus × perfrondosus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often used in culinary applications. It is native to the western United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex hybrid origin and adaptability to various soil types.
