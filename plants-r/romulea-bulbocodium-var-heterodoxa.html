Romulea bulbocodium var. heterodoxa

Romulea bulbocodium var. heterodoxa, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive dry periods.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
