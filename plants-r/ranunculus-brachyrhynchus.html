Ranunculus brachyrhynchus

Ranunculus brachyrhynchus, commonly known as the short-styled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, rounded fruits containing several seeds.
