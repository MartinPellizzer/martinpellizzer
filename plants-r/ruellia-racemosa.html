Ruellia racemosa

Ruellia racemosa, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Ruellia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
