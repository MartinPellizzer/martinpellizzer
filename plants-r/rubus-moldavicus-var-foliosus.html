Rubus moldavicus var. foliosus

Rubus moldavicus var. foliosus, commonly known as the Moldavian blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Balkans, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
