Rapanea walkeriana

Rapanea walkeriana, commonly known as the Malabar laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, red outer layer.

Rapanea walkeriana is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
