Ranunculus balbisii subsp. weyleri

Ranunculus balbisii subsp.

weyleri, commonly known as the Weyleri crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually less than 2 inches in diameter and feature five to seven petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form of R. balbisii.
