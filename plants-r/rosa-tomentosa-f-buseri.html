Rosa tomentosa f. buseri

Rosa tomentosa f. buseri, commonly known as the Buser's hairy rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa tomentosa species, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticultural collections.
