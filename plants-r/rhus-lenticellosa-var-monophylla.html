Rhus lenticellosa var. monophylla

Rhus lenticellosa var. monophylla, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically smooth and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a distinctive feature. Its bark is smooth and gray, with visible lenticels that aid in gas exchange.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woods or along streambanks.
