Rhipsalidopsis gaertneri var. tiburtii

Rhipsalidopsis gaertneri var. tiburtii, commonly known as the Tiburtian Christmas cactus, is a hybrid cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in winter. The plant typically produces bright red, pink, or white flowers, often in clusters. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

This variety is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
