Rhamnella franguloides

Rhamnella franguloides, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its fruits are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in providing habitat and food in forest edge and woodland environments.
