Rollandia scabra

Rollandia scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Rollandia scabra is typically herbaceous, with a sprawling growth habit that allows it to colonize disturbed or open areas.
