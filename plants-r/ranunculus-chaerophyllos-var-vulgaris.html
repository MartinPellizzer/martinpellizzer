Ranunculus chaerophyllos var. vulgaris

Ranunculus chaerophyllos var. vulgaris, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
