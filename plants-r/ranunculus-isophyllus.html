Ranunculus isophyllus

Ranunculus isophyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in spring. Its leaves and flowers contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

Ranunculus isophyllus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
