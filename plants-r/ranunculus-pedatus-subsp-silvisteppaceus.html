Ranunculus pedatus subsp. silvisteppaceus

Ranunculus pedatus subsp.

silvisteppaceus, commonly known as the forest-steppe buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as forest edges and steppe areas.

The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins. It produces yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens.

This subspecies is adapted to semi-arid conditions and is characterized by its compact growth habit and medicinal potential.
