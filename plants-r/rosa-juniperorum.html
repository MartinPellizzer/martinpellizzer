Rosa juniperorum

Rosa juniperorum, commonly known as the Juniper rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage that helps it survive in harsh climates.

Rosa juniperorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
