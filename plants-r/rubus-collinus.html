Rubus collinus

Rubus collinus, commonly known as the sand raspberry, is a shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
