Ranunculus raddeanus

Ranunculus raddeanus, commonly known as the Radde's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
