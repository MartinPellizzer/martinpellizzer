Rubus kolbei var. glandulosus

Rubus kolbei var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
