Rosa woodsii var. hispida

Rosa woodsii var. hispida, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a variant of the woods rose native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces pink to red petals and has elliptical, serrated leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in rocky or woodland habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

The variety hispida is distinguished by its more pronounced thorns and hairy foliage compared to the typical form of Rosa woodsii.
