Ranunculus floribundus

Ranunculus floribundus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, green, berry-like fruits.

Ranunculus floribundus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
