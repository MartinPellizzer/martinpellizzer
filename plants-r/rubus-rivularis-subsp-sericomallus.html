Rubus rivularis subsp. sericomallus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

sericomallus, commonly known as the silky-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems that give it a distinctive silken appearance.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy underside.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
