Romulea bulbocodium subsp. apollinis

Romulea bulbocodium subsp.

apollinis, commonly known as the Apollo's romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that serve as storage organs for nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
