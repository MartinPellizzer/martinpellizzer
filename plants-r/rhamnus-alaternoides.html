Rhamnus alaternoides

Rhamnus alaternoides, commonly known as the alaternum or European buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Rhamnus alaternoides is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
