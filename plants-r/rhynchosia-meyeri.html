Rhynchosia meyeri

Rhynchosia meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces small, yellow flowers and bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and hairy stems, which help in water retention. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Rhynchosia meyeri is also valued as a forage crop for livestock due to its nutritional content.
