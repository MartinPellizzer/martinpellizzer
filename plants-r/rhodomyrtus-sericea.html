Rhodomyrtus sericea

Rhodomyrtus sericea, commonly known as the silver leaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Rhodomyrtus sericea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
