Ranunculus aquatilis var. codyanus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. codyanus, commonly known as the Cody's water buttercup, is a variant of the water buttercup native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet meadows and along stream banks. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive central tuft of stamens. Its leaves are usually deeply divided and have a distinctive waxy coating.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and aquatic organisms.
