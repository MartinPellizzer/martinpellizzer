Rubus infestus subsp. altipratensis

Rubus infestus subsp.

altipratensis, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
