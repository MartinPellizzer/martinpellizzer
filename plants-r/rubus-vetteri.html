Rubus vetteri

Rubus vetteri, commonly known as the Vetter's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, specifically in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
