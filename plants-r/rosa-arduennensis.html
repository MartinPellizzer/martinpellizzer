Rosa arduennensis

Rosa arduennensis, commonly known as the Arduennes rose, is a rare and ancient species native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with multiple stems that often form a bushy structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems.

Rosa arduennensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical studies.
