Rosa rubiginosa subsp. micranthiformis

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

micranthiformis, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, pinnately compound leaves.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that mature in autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
