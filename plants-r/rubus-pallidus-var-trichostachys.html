Rubus pallidus var. trichostachys

Rubus pallidus var. trichostachys, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a red, edible raspberry-like berry. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This plant plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
