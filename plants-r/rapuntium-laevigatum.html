Rapuntium laevigatum

Rapuntium laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, oval-shaped leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, green pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Rapuntium laevigatum is also known for its peppery flavor, making it a sometimes-used culinary herb.
