Rytigynia longituba

Rytigynia longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed rytigynia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to its adaptability in various soil types.

Rytigynia longituba is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
