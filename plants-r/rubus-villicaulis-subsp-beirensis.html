Rubus villicaulis subsp. beirensis

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

beirensis, commonly known as the Beirensis blackberry, is a shrub native to China. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant typically grows in forested areas and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit, a dark purple berry, is edible and valued for its flavor.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and potential in horticulture.
