Ranunculus auricomus subsp. eumorphus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

eumorphus, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals that are often adorned with a distinctive golden crown at the base.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking yet relatively low-maintenance ornamental plant.
