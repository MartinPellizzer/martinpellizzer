Rubus × semieglandulosus

Rubus × semieglandulosus, commonly known as the smooth-leafed blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually smooth or only slightly hairy, distinguishing it from other Rubus species.

It is valued for its edible berries and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
