Ranunculus noricus

Ranunculus noricus, commonly known as the Austrian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Alps of Europe.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a dark center, and it has a short, hairy stem. The plant has palmate leaves divided into three to five lobes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Ranunculus noricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
