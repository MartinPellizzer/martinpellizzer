Robertsonia crenata

Robertsonia crenata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in cloud forests. The plant features small, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters. Robertsonia crenata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
