Rubus ulmifolius f. obtusatus

Rubus ulmifolius f. obtusatus, commonly known as the blunt-leaf blackberry, is a subspecies of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and rounded leaves, which are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in culinary applications.
