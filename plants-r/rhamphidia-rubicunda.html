Rhamphidia rubicunda

Rhamphidia rubicunda, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and green veined leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its botanical features include a short, stocky rhizome and showy, lip-shaped petals that attract pollinators.

Rhamphidia rubicunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
