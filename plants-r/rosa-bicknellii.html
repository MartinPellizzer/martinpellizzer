Rosa bicknellii

Rosa bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the Appalachian Mountains in the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with arching stems and fragrant pink to red flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins, while its fruits are aggregate fruits called rose hips.

Bicknell's rose is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
