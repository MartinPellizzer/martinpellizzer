Rapuntium roseum

Rapuntium roseum, commonly known as the pink cabbagewort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants. The plant features rosettes of fleshy, lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
