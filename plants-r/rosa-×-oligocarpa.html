Rosa × oligocarpa

Rosa × oligocarpa, commonly known as the Pacific coast rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and river valleys. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, and the hips that follow the flowers are usually red or orange.

Rosa × oligocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and horticulture.
