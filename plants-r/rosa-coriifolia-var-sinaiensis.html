Rosa coriifolia var. sinaiensis

Rosa coriifolia var. sinaiensis, commonly known as the Sinai rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Sinai Peninsula.

It features slender, thornless stems and is characterized by its clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and compact growth habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.
