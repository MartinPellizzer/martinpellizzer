Rhododendron indicum obtusum-album

Rhododendron indicum obtusum-album, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white to pink. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained acidic soils. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves with a leathery texture and floral bracts that often persist after the flowers have faded.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats.
