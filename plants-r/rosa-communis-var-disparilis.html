Rosa communis var. disparilis

Rosa communis var. disparilis, commonly known as the Variable Rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its diverse floral morphology.

This plant exhibits a range of petal colors, from pale pink to deep crimson, depending on environmental conditions. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms. The plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Due to its genetic variability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and botanical collections.
