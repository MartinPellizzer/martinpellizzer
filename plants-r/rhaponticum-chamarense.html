Rhaponticum chamarense

Rhaponticum chamarense, commonly known as the Armenian rhubarb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features large, broad leaves that are often lobed, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

Rhaponticum chamarense thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
