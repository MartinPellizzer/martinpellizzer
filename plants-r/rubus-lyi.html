Rubus lyi

Rubus lyi, commonly known as the Andean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Due to its unique appearance and nutritional value, it is of interest to both botanists and local communities.
