Rosa tomentosa f. decolorans

Rosa tomentosa f. decolorans, commonly known as the pale-stemmed dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose characterized by its distinctive pale or whitish stems.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and the Himalayas. The plant features simple, pinnately compound leaves with a dense, woolly underside that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals, and are produced in clusters.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
