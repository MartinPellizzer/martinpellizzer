Rosa pilifera

Rosa pilifera, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are simple and serrated, with a hairy texture that gives it its common name.

Rosa pilifera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and historical significance in horticulture.
