Rubia krascheninnikovii

Rubia krascheninnikovii, commonly known as the Krascheninnikov's madder, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species known for their medicinal and dye-producing properties. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Rubia krascheninnikovii is adapted to survive in harsh environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
