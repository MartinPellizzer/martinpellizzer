Ribes komarovii var. breviracemum

Ribes komarovii var. breviracemum, commonly known as the short-stalked gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of shrubs and small trees commonly found in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits, which are edible, develop into red, berry-like structures that mature in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
