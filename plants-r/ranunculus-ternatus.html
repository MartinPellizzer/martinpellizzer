Ranunculus ternatus

Ranunculus ternatus, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has a short, stout stem that bears the flower at the top. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers contain toxic compounds, making it a plant to handle with care.
