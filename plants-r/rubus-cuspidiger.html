Rubus cuspidiger

Rubus cuspidiger, commonly known as the prickly pear raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red or yellow fruits. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are sometimes used in jams and preserves.
