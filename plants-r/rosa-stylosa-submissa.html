Rosa stylosa submissa

Rosa stylosa submissa, commonly known as the dwarf pink, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in forested areas. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine hairs.

Rosa stylosa submissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
