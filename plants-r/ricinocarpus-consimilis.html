Ricinocarpus consimilis

Ricinocarpus consimilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.
