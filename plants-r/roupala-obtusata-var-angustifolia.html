Roupala obtusata var. angustifolia

Roupala obtusata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed roupala, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features elliptical to lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers in spring, followed by spherical, red-brown fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
