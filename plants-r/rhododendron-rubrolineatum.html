Rhododendron rubrolineatum

Rhododendron rubrolineatum, commonly known as the red-line rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes where it adds vibrant color and texture.
