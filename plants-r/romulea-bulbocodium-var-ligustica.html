Romulea bulbocodium var. ligustica

Romulea bulbocodium var. ligustica, commonly known as the Ligustic Rock-Jelly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces striking, yellow-orange inflorescences resembling clusters of jellyfish. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
