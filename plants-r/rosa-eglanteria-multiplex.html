Rosa eglanteria multiplex

Rosa eglanteria multiplex, commonly known as the bristly rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits are red, hairy rose hips that contain seeds.

It is often found in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils.
