Rubus × linkianus

Rubus × linkianus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These fruits are edible and often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

Rubus × linkianus thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
