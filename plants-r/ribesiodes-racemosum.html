Ribesiodes racemosum

Ribesiodes racemosum, commonly known as the clustered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, red berries that mature in the fall and are edible for birds and some mammals.

Ribesiodes racemosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
