Ranunculus broteri

Ranunculus broteri, commonly known as the Congo buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and Western Africa.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and occasionally pink, with prominent yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and swampy areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its preference for specific environmental conditions.
