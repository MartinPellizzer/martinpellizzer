Randia chloroleuca

Randia chloroleuca, commonly known as the whiteleaf randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a distinctive white or pale underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
