Racinaea tripinnata

Racinaea tripinnata, commonly known as the three-parted fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are divided into three parts, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, rich soil to flourish. Its leaves are typically green with a slightly waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are not particularly showy.

Racinaea tripinnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
