Rumex ephedroides

Rumex ephedroides, commonly known as the smooth dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along roadsides, and in other open habitats. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. Its root system is fibrous, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.

Rumex ephedroides is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine.
