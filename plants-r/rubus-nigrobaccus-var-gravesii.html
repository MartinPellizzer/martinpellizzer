Rubus nigrobaccus var. gravesii

Rubus nigrobaccus var. gravesii, commonly known as the blackberry rose, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant produces flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in local ecosystems for wildlife habitat.
