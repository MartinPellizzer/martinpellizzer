Ruellia spectabilis

Ruellia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ruellia spectabilis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
