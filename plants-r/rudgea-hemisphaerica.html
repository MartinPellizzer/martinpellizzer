Rudgea hemisphaerica

Rudgea hemisphaerica, commonly known as the rosewood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Due to overharvesting, Rudgea hemisphaerica is considered an endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
