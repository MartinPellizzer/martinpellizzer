Rubus × nudatus

Rubus × nudatus, commonly known as the naked-stemmed blackberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and large, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces white or pale yellow flowers in the spring.

The fruit is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications due to its sweet and tart flavor.
