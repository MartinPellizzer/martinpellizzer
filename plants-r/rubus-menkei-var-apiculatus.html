Rubus menkei var. apiculatus

Rubus menkei var. apiculatus, commonly known as the Hairy Blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically covered in a fine, hairy layer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive floral structure and the presence of bracts beneath its inflorescence.
