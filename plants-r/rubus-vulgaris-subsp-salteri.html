Rubus vulgaris subsp. salteri

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

salteri, commonly known as brambles or blackberry bushes, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries.

The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

The subspecies is closely related to other Rubus species and is known for its hardy nature and ecological importance.
