Rhipsalis crispata

Rhipsalis crispata, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, leaf-like stems that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
