Rubus coronarius

Rubus coronarius, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, achenes. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Rubus coronarius is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with a sprawling growth habit.
