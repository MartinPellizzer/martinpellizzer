Rubus hedycarpus var. pubescens

Rubus hedycarpus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, similar to other Rubus species. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The hairy raspberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
