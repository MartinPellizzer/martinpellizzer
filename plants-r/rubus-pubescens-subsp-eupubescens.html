Rubus pubescens subsp. eupubescens

Rubus pubescens subsp.

eupubescens, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia and the Americas. It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark red to black berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more densely hairy foliage and fruit compared to other Rubus species.
