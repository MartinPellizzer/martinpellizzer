Ranunculus confusus var. submersus

Ranunculus confusus var. submersus, commonly known as the submerged crowfoot, is a rare aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. The plant has finely divided, floating leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and bloom in early spring.
