Rumex uruguayensis

Rumex uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Rumex uruguayensis is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and grasslands.
