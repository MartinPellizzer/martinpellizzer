Rhynchospora bakhuisensis

Rhynchospora bakhuisensis, commonly known as the Bakhuis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges. It thrives in marshy or aquatic environments, often forming dense mats that contribute to wetland vegetation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
