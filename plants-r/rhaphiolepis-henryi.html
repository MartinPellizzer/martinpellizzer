Rhaphiolepis henryi

Rhaphiolepis henryi, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, glossy dark green leaves and produces bright orange-red berries that are visually striking. The plant is notable for its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

However, its berries are toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in garden settings.
