Racosperma subflexuosum subsp. capillatum

Racosperma subflexuosum subsp.

capillatum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, drooping leaves and yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

The subspecies capillatum is distinguished by its finely hairy stems and smaller, more delicate leaves compared to other subspecies.
