Racosperma laricinum var. crassifolium

Racosperma laricinum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various native wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across suitable habitats.
