Ranunculus × goldei

Ranunculus × goldei, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a darker central blotch. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
