Rubus schlechtendalii subsp. subcentreuropus

Rubus schlechtendalii subsp.

subcentreuropus, commonly known as the European blackberry bush, is a shrub native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rubus genus, with specific adaptations to its native habitat.
