Ricinocarpos glaucus var. genuinus

Ricinocarpos glaucus var. genuinus, commonly known as the blue-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its glaucous, bluish-green leaves that are typically narrow and linear in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely used for commercial purposes.
