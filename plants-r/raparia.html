Raparia

Raparia, commonly known as the water plantain, is a genus of aquatic and semi-aquatic flowering plants.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Raparia species are characterized by their rhizomatous growth habit and their ability to thrive in moist conditions.

These plants play an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
