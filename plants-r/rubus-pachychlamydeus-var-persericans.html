Rubus pachychlamydeus var. persericans

Rubus pachychlamydeus var. persericans, commonly known as the Persian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or pink in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
