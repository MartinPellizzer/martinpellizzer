Rosa caledoniae

Rosa caledoniae, commonly known as the Caledonian rose, is a rare and endemic species native to the Scottish Highlands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and apical inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
