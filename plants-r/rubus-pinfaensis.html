Rubus pinfaensis

Rubus pinfaensis, commonly known as the Pinfang blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has thorny stems with compound leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local horticulture.
