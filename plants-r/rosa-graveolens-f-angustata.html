Rosa graveolens f. angustata

Rosa graveolens f. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweet briar, is a horticultural form of the sweet briar rose.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and evergreen foliage in mild climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders or as a climbing plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate blooms and aromatic fragrance.
