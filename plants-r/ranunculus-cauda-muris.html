Ranunculus cauda-muris

Ranunculus cauda-muris, commonly known as the mouse-tail buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its name refers to the elongated, tail-like shape of its seed pods.

Ranunculus cauda-muris is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
