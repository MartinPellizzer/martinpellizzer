Rubus hirtus var. reussii

Rubus hirtus var. reussii, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is often used in jams and desserts.
