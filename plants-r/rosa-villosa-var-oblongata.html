Rosa villosa var. oblongata

Rosa villosa var. oblongata, commonly known as the hairy rose or the woolly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are covered in a dense layer of white to grayish pubescence. The leaves are typically pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval to oblong in shape. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
