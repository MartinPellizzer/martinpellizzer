Rubus hebridensis

Rubus hebridensis, commonly known as the Hebridean bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the British Isles, particularly the Hebrides and other Scottish islands. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically growing in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are pinkish-white, and it produces edible blackberries, though the fruit is often small and tart.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various insect and bird species.
