Rosa afzeliana var. biserrata

Rosa afzeliana var. biserrata, commonly known as the serrated rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The leaves are compound, with serrated edges that give the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
