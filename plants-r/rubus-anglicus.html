Rubus anglicus

Rubus anglicus, commonly known as the English blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

Rubus anglicus is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
