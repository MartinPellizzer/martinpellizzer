Rubus moluccanus var. obsoletus

Rubus moluccanus var. obsoletus, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a shrub native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
