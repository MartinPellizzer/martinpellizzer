Rubus clusii var. perglandulosus

Rubus clusii var. perglandulosus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and dense, glandular hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its flowers are white or pink and are clustered in groups of three to five.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
