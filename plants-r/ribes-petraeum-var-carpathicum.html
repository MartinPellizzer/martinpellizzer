Ribes petraeum var. carpathicum

Ribes petraeum var. carpathicum, commonly known as the Carpathian gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

It is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential culinary uses.
