Rubus rusticanus subsp. striatus

Rubus rusticanus subsp.

striatus, commonly known as the striated blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive leaf venation and slightly different fruit structure compared to other Rubus species.
