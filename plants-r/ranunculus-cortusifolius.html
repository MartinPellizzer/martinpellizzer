Ranunculus cortusifolius

Ranunculus cortusifolius, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread and form dense mats.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
