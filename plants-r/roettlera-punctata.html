Roettlera punctata

Roettlera punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features erect, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Roettlera punctata is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
