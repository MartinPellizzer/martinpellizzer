Relhania relhanioides

Relhania relhanioides, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring a dense, rounded crown. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds.

The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
