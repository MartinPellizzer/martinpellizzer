Rosa ferruginea var. glaucescens

Rosa ferruginea var. glaucescens, commonly known as the rusty rose or glaucous rose, is a variant of the species Rosa ferruginea.

It is characterized by its distinctive rust-colored stems and leaves that often have a bluish-gray waxy coating. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a strong, sweet fragrance. This plant is native to parts of Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
