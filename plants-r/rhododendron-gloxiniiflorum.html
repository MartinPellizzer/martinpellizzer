Rhododendron gloxiniiflorum

Rhododendron gloxiniiflorum, commonly known as the Gloxinia-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its flowers are particularly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
