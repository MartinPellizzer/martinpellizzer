Racosperma falcatum

Racosperma falcatum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has slender, wiry stems and is often found in dry, open habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are dispersed by wind.
