Racosperma auripilum

Racosperma auripilum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Racosperma auripilum is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
