Rosa canina var. glaucina

Rosa canina var. glaucina, commonly known as the glaucous dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale greenish-yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and scrublands, preferring well-drained soil. It produces red-orange hips that are edible and used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
