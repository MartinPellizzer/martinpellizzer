Rosa lucieae

Rosa lucieae, commonly known as the Lucie's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its clustered, semi-evergreen foliage and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Rosa lucieae typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
