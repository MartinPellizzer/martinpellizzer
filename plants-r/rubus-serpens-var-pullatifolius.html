Rubus serpens var. pullatifolius

Rubus serpens var. pullatifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in horticulture.
