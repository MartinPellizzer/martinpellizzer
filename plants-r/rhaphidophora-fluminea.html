Rhaphidophora fluminea

Rhaphidophora fluminea, commonly known as the river bamboo or water bamboo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, often growing in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain areas.

Rhaphidophora fluminea is well adapted to humid conditions and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
