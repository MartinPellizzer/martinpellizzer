Rubus incarnatus var. leventii

Rubus incarnatus var. leventii, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces bright red, aggregate fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the stems are covered in prickly bracts.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in local ecosystems for its ecological benefits.
