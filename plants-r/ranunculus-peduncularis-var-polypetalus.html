Ranunculus peduncularis var. polypetalus

Ranunculus peduncularis var. polypetalus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, arranged in a showy, cup-like structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.
