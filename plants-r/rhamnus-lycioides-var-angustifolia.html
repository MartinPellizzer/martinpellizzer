Rhamnus lycioides var. angustifolia

Rhamnus lycioides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It is characterized by its woody stems and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is part of the Rutaceae family, which includes other plants with similar aromatic properties.
