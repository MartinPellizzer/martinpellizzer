Rubus fasciatus

Rubus fasciatus, commonly known as the striped blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic fruit.

Rubus fasciatus is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
