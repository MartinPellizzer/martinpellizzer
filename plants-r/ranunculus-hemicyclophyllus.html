Ranunculus hemicyclophyllus

Ranunculus hemicyclophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features dissected, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
