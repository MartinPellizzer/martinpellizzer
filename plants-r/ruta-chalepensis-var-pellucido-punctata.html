Ruta chalepensis var. pellucido-punctata

Ruta chalepensis var. pellucido-punctata, commonly known as the white-spotted rue, is a variant of the common rue plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves that are often adorned with white or pale green spots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by small, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
