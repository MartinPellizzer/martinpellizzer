Rhododendron calophytum var. openshawianum

Rhododendron calophytum var. openshawianum, commonly known as the Openshaw's rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
