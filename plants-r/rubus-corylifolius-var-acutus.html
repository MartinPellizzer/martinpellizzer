Rubus corylifolius var. acutus

Rubus corylifolius var. acutus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves with acuminate tips. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black achenes surrounded by a fleshy receptacle. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been cultivated for its edible berries in some regions.
