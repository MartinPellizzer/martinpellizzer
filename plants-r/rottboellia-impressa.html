Rottboellia impressa

Rottboellia impressa, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a waxy coating and a unique, star-shaped pattern on the leaf surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Rottboellia impressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its striking foliage.
