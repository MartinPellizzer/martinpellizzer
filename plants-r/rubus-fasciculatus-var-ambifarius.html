Rubus fasciculatus var. ambifarius

Rubus fasciculatus var. ambifarius, commonly known as the twin-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of two leaflets on some of its compound leaves, giving it its specific epithet "ambifarius.". The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory flora in its native habitat.
