Reseda malatyana

Reseda malatyana, commonly known as Turkish bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Reseda malatyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
