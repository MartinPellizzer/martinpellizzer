Ranunculus acris f. plenus

Ranunculus acris f. plenus, commonly known as the full-flowered meadow buttercup, is a horticultural form of the meadow buttercup.

It is characterized by its dense, double flowers that are typically bright yellow with many petals. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It blooms in early spring and is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers.
