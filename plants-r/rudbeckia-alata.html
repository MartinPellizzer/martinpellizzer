Rudbeckia alata

Rudbeckia alata, commonly known as the winged sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow ray flowers surrounded by a series of brown, papery disk flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Rudbeckia alata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
