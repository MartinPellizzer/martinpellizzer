Rubus ulmifolius foliis-variegatis

Rubus ulmifolius foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that exhibit variegation, often with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
