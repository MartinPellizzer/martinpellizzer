Rubus hanryi

Rubus hanryi, commonly known as the Hanryi blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many shrubs with thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
