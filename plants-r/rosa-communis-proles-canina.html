Rosa communis proles canina

Rosa communis proles canina, commonly known as the canine rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its botanical features include compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and an edible, aromatic fruit known as a rose hip.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
