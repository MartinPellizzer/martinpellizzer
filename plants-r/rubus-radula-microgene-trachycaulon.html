Rubus radula microgene trachycaulon

Rubus radula microgene trachycaulon, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, dark purple berries that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
