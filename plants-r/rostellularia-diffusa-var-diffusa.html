Rostellularia diffusa var. diffusa

Rostellularia diffusa var. diffusa, commonly known as the creeping rostellularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a prostrate or trailing growth habit with slender, wiry stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually white or pale green, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
