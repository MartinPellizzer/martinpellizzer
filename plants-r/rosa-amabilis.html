Rosa amabilis

Rosa amabilis, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant flowers that range from white to deep pink. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa amabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
