Roeperia speciosa

Roeperia speciosa, commonly known as the showy roeperia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a typical feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Roeperia speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
