Rubus crispifrons

Rubus crispifrons, commonly known as the crinkled bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that have a distinctive crinkled appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
