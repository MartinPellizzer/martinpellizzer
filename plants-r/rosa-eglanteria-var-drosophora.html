Rosa eglanteria var. drosophora

Rosa eglanteria var. drosophora, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, arching stems covered in sharp, curved prickles. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with serrated edges.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
