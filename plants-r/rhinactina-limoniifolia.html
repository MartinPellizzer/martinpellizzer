Rhinactina limoniifolia

Rhinactina limoniifolia, commonly known as the lemon-scented fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that emit a mild citrus aroma when crushed. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its fronds are typically light green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Rhinactina limoniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
