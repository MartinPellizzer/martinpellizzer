Ranunculus marginatus

Ranunculus marginatus, commonly known as the marginal buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive white margin, and its leaves are typically divided into lobes. The plant grows in moist, often calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
