Rheum strictum

Rheum strictum, commonly known as the Indian rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by small, green berries. Rheum strictum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its roots and leaves contain compounds that have been used to treat digestive issues and skin conditions.
