Rigiolepis macrophylla

Rigiolepis macrophylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces vibrant orange-red flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
