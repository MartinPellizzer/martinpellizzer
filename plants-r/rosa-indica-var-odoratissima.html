Rosa indica var. odoratissima

Rosa indica var. odoratissima, commonly known as the Kashmiri rose, is a variant of the Indian rose that is prized for its intense fragrance and ornamental value.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India, Pakistan, and Afghanistan. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a deep red center. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is widely cultivated for both its beauty and its use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
