Rapuntium pyramidale

Rapuntium pyramidale, commonly known as the pyramidal rapunculus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a biennial plant that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, featuring a basal rosette of hairy, oval leaves. The plant produces upright stems with clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are small and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize rocky and alpine habitats.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
