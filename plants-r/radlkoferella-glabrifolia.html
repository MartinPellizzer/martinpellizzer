Radlkoferella glabrifolia

Radlkoferella glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved radlkoferella, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, glabrous leaves that are typically covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
