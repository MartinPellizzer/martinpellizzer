Rhododendron quinquefolium

Rhododendron quinquefolium, commonly known as the Allegheny rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to pink and bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, five-leafed foliage that is dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron quinquefolium is often found in moist woodlands and is an important species for wildlife habitat.
