Rubus pekinensis

Rubus pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
