Racosperma heteroneurum

Racosperma heteroneurum, commonly known as the narrow-veined acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Racosperma heteroneurum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is well-suited to arid environments.
