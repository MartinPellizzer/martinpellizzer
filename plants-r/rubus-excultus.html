Rubus excultus

Rubus excultus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
