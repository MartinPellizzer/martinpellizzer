Ranunculus auricomus subsp. defectiflorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

defectiflorus, commonly known as the yellow pond-lily, is a aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, floating leaves and yellow flowers with five petals.

The subspecies defectiflorus is distinguished by its reduced floral structure, often with fewer stamens and a simpler pistil. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
