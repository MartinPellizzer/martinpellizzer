Rhaphiolepis indica subsp. umbellata

Rhaphiolepis indica subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the Chinese holly or Japanese holly, is a flowering shrub native to East Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This subspecies is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.
