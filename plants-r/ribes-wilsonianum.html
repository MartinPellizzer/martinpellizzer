Ribes wilsonianum

Ribes wilsonianum, commonly known as the Wilson's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly found in North America. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn red in the fall.

The berries it produces are edible, though they are typically small and tart.
