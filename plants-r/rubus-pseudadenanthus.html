Rubus pseudadenanthus

Rubus pseudadenanthus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
