Rhododendron lateriflorum

Rhododendron lateriflorum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive yellowish or reddish throat.

Rhododendron lateriflorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained, acidic soil.
