Randia confusa

Randia confusa, commonly known as the swamp rosemary, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and coastal areas of the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a branched inflorescence, making it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.

Randia confusa is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
