Rosa pimpinellifolia var. subdiminuta

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. subdiminuta, commonly known as the dwarf rock rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and often grows in coastal or mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
