Ribes grossularium

Ribes grossularium, commonly known as the gooseberry plant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Grossulariaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes thorny stems and it often thrives in well-drained soils.

Ribes grossularium is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
