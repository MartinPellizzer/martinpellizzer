Rubus × bavaricola

Rubus × bavaricola, commonly known as the Bohemian blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus penduliflorus.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological and genetic variability.
