Rhynchospora indianolensis

Rhynchospora indianolensis, commonly known as the Indian marsh sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. It produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, three-lobed berries.

Rhynchospora indianolensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
