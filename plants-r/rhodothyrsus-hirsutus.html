Rhodothyrsus hirsutus

Rhodothyrsus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy rhodothyrsus, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
