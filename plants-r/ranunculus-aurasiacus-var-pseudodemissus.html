Ranunculus aurasiacus var. pseudodemissus

Ranunculus aurasiacus var. pseudodemissus, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy stem, adapted to its high-altitude environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
