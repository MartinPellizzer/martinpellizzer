Rinorea dawei

Rinorea dawei, commonly known as the white-flowered rinorea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Rinorea dawei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
