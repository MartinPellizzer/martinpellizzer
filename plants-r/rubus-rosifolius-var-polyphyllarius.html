Rubus rosifolius var. polyphyllarius

Rubus rosifolius var. polyphyllarius, commonly known as the many-leafed blackberry, is a variant of the Rosifolius blackberry species.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can have up to 15 leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and its edible fruits, which have a rich, tart flavor.
