Rosa cinnamomea var. davurica

Rosa cinnamomea var. davurica, commonly known as the Cinnamomea rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountains of central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its hips, which are reddish-orange, are edible and contain numerous small seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in cooler climates.
