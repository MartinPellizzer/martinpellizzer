Rubus euprepes

Rubus euprepes, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Rubus euprepes is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
