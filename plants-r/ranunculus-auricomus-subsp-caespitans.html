Ranunculus auricomus subsp. caespitans

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

caespitans, commonly known as the tufted buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features bright yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant grows in dense clusters, forming low-growing mats, and has finely divided, serrated leaves. It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
