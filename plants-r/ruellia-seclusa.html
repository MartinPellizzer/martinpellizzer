Ruellia seclusa

Ruellia seclusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ruellia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Ruellia seclusa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
