Racosperma dangarense

Racosperma dangarense, commonly known as the Dangareng wattle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep roots and waxy leaf surfaces.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
