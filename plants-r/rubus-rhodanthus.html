Rubus rhodanthus

Rubus rhodanthus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. These fruits are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making them both nutritious and flavorful.

Rubus rhodanthus is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value in gardens.
