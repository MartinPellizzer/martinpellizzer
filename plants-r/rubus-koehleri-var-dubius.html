Rubus koehleri var. dubius

Rubus koehleri var. dubius, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.
