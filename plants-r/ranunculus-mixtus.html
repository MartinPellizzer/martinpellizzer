Ranunculus mixtus

Ranunculus mixtus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a central cluster of stamens, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are palmately lobed, and it produces a single, showy flower per stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Ranunculus mixtus is an herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
