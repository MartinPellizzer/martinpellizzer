Rubus moluccanus var. obtusangulus

Rubus moluccanus var. obtusangulus, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
