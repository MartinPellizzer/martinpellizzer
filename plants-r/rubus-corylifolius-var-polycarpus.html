Rubus corylifolius var. polycarpus

Rubus corylifolius var. polycarpus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are usually serrated and have a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature, resembling raspberries in appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its fruit and medicinal properties.
