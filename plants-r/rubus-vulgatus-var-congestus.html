Rubus vulgatus var. congestus

Rubus vulgatus var. congestus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a thorny shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist and well-drained habitats.
