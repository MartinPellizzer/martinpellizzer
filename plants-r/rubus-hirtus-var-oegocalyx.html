Rubus hirtus var. oegocalyx

Rubus hirtus var. oegocalyx, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
