Rubus nemorosus var. glabratus

Rubus nemorosus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in jams and pies.
