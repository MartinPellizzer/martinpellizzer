Rosa ochroleuca var. glaberrima

Rosa ochroleuca var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth white rose, is a variant of the white rose species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers and smooth, hairless stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is usually dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
