Rhododendron × sochadzeae

Rhododendron × sochadzeae, commonly known as the Sochadze rhododendron, is a hybrid plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
