Rubus milliformis f. glaber

Rubus milliformis f. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, hairless fruit surface.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to various regions in North America. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

This form is valued for its flavor and is commonly cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
