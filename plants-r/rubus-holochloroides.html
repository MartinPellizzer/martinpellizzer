Rubus holochloroides

Rubus holochloroides, commonly known as the yellow-tailed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
