Rubus costatus var. thyrsoideus

Rubus costatus var. thyrsoideus, commonly known as the thyrsoideus raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
