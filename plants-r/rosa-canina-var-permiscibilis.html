Rosa canina var. permiscibilis

Rosa canina var. permiscibilis, commonly known as the wild dog玫瑰 or the rose dog, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals, and produce hips that are rich in vitamin C. This plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often growing in woodlands and along hedgerows.

It has historical uses in herbal medicine and as a source of natural dye.
