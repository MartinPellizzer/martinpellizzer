Renealmia guianensis

Renealmia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan reed, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
