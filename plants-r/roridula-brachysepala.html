Roridula brachysepala

Roridula brachysepala, commonly known as the sticky leaf plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves that trap insects, contributing to its nutrient acquisition. The plant belongs to the family Roridulaceae, which is closely related to the cactus family. Its flowers are small and typically green or yellow, with a distinctive structure.

Roridula brachysepala is notable for its ability to thrive in arid environments due to its specialized adaptations.
