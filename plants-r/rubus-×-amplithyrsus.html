Rubus × amplithyrsus

Rubus × amplithyrsus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a hybrid plant species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine, silvery hairs.

The fruit is highly valued for its intense flavor and is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
