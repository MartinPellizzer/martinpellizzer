Rubus oberdorferi

Rubus oberdorferi, commonly known as the Oberdorfer's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, specifically occurring in countries like South Africa, Namibia, and Botswana. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
