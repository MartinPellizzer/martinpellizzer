Ranunculus latisinuatus

Ranunculus latisinuatus, commonly known as the broad-creased buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in soft, hairy textures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus latisinuatus is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.
