Regelia tristis

Regelia tristis, commonly known as the weeping regelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that resemble cascading waterfalls. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a lush, tropical appearance. It produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer.

Regelia tristis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique visual appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
