Rungia maculata

Rungia maculata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
