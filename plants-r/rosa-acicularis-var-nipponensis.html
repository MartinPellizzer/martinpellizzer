Rosa acicularis var. nipponensis

Rosa acicularis var. nipponensis, commonly known as the Japanese thorned rose, is a variant of the thorned rose native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its sharp, recurved thorns and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange, containing seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with elliptical leaflets and a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in Japanese horticulture.
