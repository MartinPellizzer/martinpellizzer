Rosa glauca f. killermannii

Rosa glauca f. killermannii, commonly known as the Killermann's blue rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa glauca.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender-colored flowers, which are typically smaller than those of the typical form. The plant is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The foliage is dark green and glossy, adding to its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
