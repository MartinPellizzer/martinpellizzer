Rubus dolichophyllus

Rubus dolichophyllus, commonly known as the long-leaf blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically black when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the cultivar. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
