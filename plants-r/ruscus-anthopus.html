Ruscus anthopus

Ruscus anthopus, commonly known as butcher's broom, is a evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, clustered stems that are typically red or purple in color, covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
