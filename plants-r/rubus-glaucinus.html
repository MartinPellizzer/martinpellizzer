Rubus glaucinus

Rubus glaucinus, commonly known as the glaucous blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
