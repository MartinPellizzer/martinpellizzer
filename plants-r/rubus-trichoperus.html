Rubus trichoperus

Rubus trichoperus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rubus genus in the family Rosaceae. This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The plant is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
