Rubus × languidus

Rubus × languidus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.
