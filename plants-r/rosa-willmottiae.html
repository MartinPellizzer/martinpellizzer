Rosa willmottiae

Rosa willmottiae, commonly known as the Willmott's rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its flowers typically bloom in late summer. Rosa willmottiae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many temperate landscapes.
