Rubus piletostachys var. discolores

Rubus piletostachys var. discolores, commonly known as the variegated bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a striking variegation of green and white or yellow. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
