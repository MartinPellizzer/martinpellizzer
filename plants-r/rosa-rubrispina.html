Rosa rubrispina

Rosa rubrispina, commonly known as the red-spined rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, which are a distinguishing feature, and its clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa rubrispina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
