Rubus costaricanus var. concolor

Rubus costaricanus var. concolor, commonly known as the Costa Rican blackberry, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental and potential culinary value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
