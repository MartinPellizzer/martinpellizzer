Rhadamanthus secundus

Rhadamanthus secundus, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom during the late spring and early summer months. Rhadamanthus secundus thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
