Rosa provincialis variegata

Rosa provincialis variegata, commonly known as the variegated provincial rose, is a decorative shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are dark green with white or cream variegation, adding visual interest to the plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
