Rubus chloocladus subsp. emollitus

Rubus chloocladus subsp.

emollitus, commonly known as the soft-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are soft and velvety in texture.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
