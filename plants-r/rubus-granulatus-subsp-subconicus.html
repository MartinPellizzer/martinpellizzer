Rubus granulatus subsp. subconicus

Rubus granulatus subsp.

subconicus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in dry environments. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, blooming in early spring before the fruit develops.
