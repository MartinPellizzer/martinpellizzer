Rhapis kwamwonzick

Rhapis kwamwonzick, commonly known as the Kwamwonzick palm, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil. Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.

The flowers are small and yellowish, appearing in clusters, and the plant produces fruit that is edible but not commonly cultivated for food.
