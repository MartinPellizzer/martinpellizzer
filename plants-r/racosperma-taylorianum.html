Racosperma taylorianum

Racosperma taylorianum, commonly known as Taylor's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in dense spikes.

Racosperma taylorianum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
