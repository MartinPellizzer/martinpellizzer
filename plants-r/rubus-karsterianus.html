Rubus karsterianus

Rubus karsterianus, commonly known as the Karst raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
