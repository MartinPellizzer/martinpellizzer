Rubus argentatus var. amiantinus

Rubus argentatus var. amiantinus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and somewhat tart. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

The botanical features of this plant make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Rubus genus.
