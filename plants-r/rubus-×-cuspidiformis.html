Rubus × cuspidiformis

Rubus × cuspidiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
