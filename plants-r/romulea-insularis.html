Romulea insularis

Romulea insularis, commonly known as the Island Romulea, is a small perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related genera. The plant produces delicate, grass-like leaves and features showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Romulea insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with suitable conditions.
