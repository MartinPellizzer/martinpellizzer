Racosperma griseum

Racosperma griseum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, linear leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods.

Racosperma griseum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
