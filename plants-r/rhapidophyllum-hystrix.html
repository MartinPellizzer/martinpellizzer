Rhapidophyllum hystrix

Rhapidophyllum hystrix, commonly known as the spiny rush or the cannonball plant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its stiff, spiny leaves and dense, cylindrical flower heads. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in wetland environments such as swamps and marshes. The plant's unique morphology includes a short, stout stem and a cluster of spikelets at the top.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
