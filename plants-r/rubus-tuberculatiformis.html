Rubus tuberculatiformis

Rubus tuberculatiformis, commonly known as the tuberculate raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to certain regions of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces bright red or yellow berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

This plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
