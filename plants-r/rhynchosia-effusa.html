Rhynchosia effusa

Rhynchosia effusa, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. Rhynchosia effusa is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary preparations.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil improvement due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
