Ruellia arborescens

Ruellia arborescens, commonly known as the showy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots make it suitable for erosion control in tropical and subtropical environments.

Ruellia arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
