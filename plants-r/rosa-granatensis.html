Rosa granatensis

Rosa granatensis, commonly known as the granadilla rose, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Rosa granatensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.
