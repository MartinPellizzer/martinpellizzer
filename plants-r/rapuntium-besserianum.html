Rapuntium besserianum

Rapuntium besserianum, commonly known as the Besserianum cress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically lobed and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The seeds are enclosed in a siliqua, a type of dry, dehiscent fruit characteristic of the Brassicaceae family.
