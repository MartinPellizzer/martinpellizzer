Ranunculus inconspectiformis

Ranunculus inconspectiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, grassy habitats. The plant features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, giving it a star-like appearance. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and the stems are hairy.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
