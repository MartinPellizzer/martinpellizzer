Rhododendron flavidum

Rhododendron flavidum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron flavidum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
