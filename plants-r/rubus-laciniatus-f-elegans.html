Rubus laciniatus f. elegans

Rubus laciniatus f. elegans, commonly known as the elegant blackberry, is a cultivar of the blackberry plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and sweeter than those of the common blackberry. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular pruning for optimal fruit production.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in jams and desserts.
