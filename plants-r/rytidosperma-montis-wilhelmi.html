Rytidosperma montis-wilhelmi

Rytidosperma montis-wilhelmi, commonly known as the Wilhelmine's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically spike-like, with small, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it thrives in well-drained sandy soils.

Rytidosperma montis-wilhelmi is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
