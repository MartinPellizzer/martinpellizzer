Rubus pinnatisepalus

Rubus pinnatisepalus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries, typically ripening to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and thickets.

The fruit is edible, though the plant is generally considered invasive in some regions.
