Rafnia capensis subsp. elsieae

Rafnia capensis subsp.

elsieae, commonly known as the Elsie's rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
