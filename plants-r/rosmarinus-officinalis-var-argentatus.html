Rosmarinus officinalis var. argentatus

Rosmarinus officinalis var. argentatus, commonly known as silver rosemary, is a cultivar characterized by its silvery-gray foliage.

This variant of rosemary features dense, compact growth with leaves that are covered in fine, white to silver hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is a hardy evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces small blue or purple flowers, which are typical of the rosemary genus.

Its aromatic leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine, similar to other rosemary varieties.
