Rubus × adrienii

Rubus × adrienii, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical traits. The plant produces large, dark purple to black fruits that are typically larger and sweeter than its parent species. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in suitable climates.
