Rubus vulgatus var. subcuneatus

Rubus vulgatus var. subcuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-angled blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
