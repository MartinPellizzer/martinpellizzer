Ribes rubrum var. pubescens

Ribes rubrum var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent red currant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.

The variety is distinguished by its pubescent (hairy) foliage, which gives it a unique appearance compared to other red currant varieties.
