Rosa gallica subsp. gallicosempervirens

Rosa gallica subsp.

gallicosempervirens, commonly known as the perpetual pink rose, is a subspecies native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse blooming of semi-double to double pink flowers.

The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white buds that open to soft pink petals, often with a subtle red flush. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and shrubbery.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
