Rubus vikensis

Rubus vikensis, commonly known as the Vik's blackberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Nordic countries.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
