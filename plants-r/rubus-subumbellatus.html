Rubus subumbellatus

Rubus subumbellatus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, dark berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pinkish flowers in late spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas and along stream banks.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them valuable both for culinary use and wildlife habitat.
