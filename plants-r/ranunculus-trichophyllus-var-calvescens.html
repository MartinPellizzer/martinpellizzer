Ranunculus trichophyllus var. calvescens

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. calvescens, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in alpine flora studies.
