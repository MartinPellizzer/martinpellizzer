Rubus occidentalis var. pallidus

Rubus occidentalis var. pallidus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
