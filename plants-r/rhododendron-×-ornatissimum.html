Rhododendron × ornatissimum

Rhododendron × ornatissimum, commonly known as the ornate rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron ferrugineum and Rhododendron caucasicum.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring acidic, well-drained soils and cool, moist climates. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Ericaceae family.
