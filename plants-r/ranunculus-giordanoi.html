Ranunculus giordanoi

Ranunculus giordanoi, commonly known as the Giordano's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow petals that resemble those of the common buttercup. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
