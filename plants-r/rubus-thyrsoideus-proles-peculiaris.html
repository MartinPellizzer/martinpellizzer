Rubus thyrsoideus proles peculiaris

Rubus thyrsoideus proles peculiaris, commonly known as the peculiar thorned bramble, is a rare and unique species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are white with five petals and are often accompanied by yellow stamens, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
