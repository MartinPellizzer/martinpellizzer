Rubus arduennensis var. ramiflorus

Rubus arduennensis var. ramiflorus, commonly known as the ramiflorous blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The flowers are distinctive, with multiple small florets arranged in a clustered inflorescence. The fruits, which are edible, develop from the flowering structures and are often used in local culinary traditions.

This plant plays a significant role in its ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
