Rhamnus arnottiana

Rhamnus arnottiana, commonly known as the Arnott's buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically glossy green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits that are edible for some wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in woodland and heathland environments.

Rhamnus arnottiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local fauna.
