Rhododendron indicum f. balsaminiflorum

Rhododendron indicum f. balsaminiflorum, commonly known as the balsam-flowered rhododendron, is a hybrid form native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble those of the balsam plant, with a unique combination of colors and shapes. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that provide a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a subject of botanical study.
