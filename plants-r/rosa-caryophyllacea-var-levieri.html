Rosa caryophyllacea var. levieri

Rosa caryophyllacea var. levieri, commonly known as the Levieri's rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces rose hips that are ornamental and edible. It is well adapted to rocky and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
