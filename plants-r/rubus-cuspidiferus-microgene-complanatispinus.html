Rubus cuspidiferus microgene complanatispinus

Rubus cuspidiferus microgene complanatispinus, commonly known as the flat-spined blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of flat, spiny stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
