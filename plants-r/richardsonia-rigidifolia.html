Richardsonia rigidifolia

Richardsonia rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed richardsonia, is a rare and unique plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, rigid, and often branched stems that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to withstand the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized growth requirements, Richardsonia rigidifolia is considered a sensitive indicator of high-altitude ecosystems.
