Rhododendron × emasculum

Rhododendron × emasculum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Rhododendron molle and Rhododendron agardhii.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural use in temperate climates.
