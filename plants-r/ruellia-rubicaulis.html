Ruellia rubicaulis

Ruellia rubicaulis, commonly known as the redstem ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
