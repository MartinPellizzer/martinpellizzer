Rhus villosa var. cuneifoliolata

Rhus villosa var. cuneifoliolata, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a characteristic feature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
