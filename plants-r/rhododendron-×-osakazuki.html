Rhododendron × osakazuki

Rhododendron × osakazuki, commonly known as the Osaka Azalea, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron indicum and Rhododendron schlippenbachii.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is native to Japan, specifically the Kansai region. The plant typically produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron × osakazuki is well-suited for container gardening and ornamental use in shaded garden settings.
