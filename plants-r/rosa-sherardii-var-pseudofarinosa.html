Rosa sherardii var. pseudofarinosa

Rosa sherardii var. pseudofarinosa, commonly known as the Chinese rose or everblooming rose, is a shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a finely toothed margin, while the flowers are typically borne in groups of three to five. This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
