Rubus wahlbergii var. obscurus

Rubus wahlbergii var. obscurus, commonly known as the dark-fruited bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often in mountainous or coastal regions. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
