Rhododendron javanicum subsp. cladotrichum

Rhododendron javanicum subsp.

cladotrichum, commonly known as the Java rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Indonesia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, while its flowers are often borne in inflorescences at the ends of the branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
