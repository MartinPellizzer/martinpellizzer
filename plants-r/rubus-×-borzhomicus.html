Rubus × borzhomicus

Rubus × borzhomicus, commonly known as the Siberian blackberry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus chingii and Rubus caesius.

It is native to the cold regions of Siberia and is well adapted to harsh climatic conditions. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a long, cold winter to break dormancy and produce fruit.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental value and its potential in food and medicinal applications.
