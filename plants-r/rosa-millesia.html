Rosa millesia

Rosa millesia, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has woody stems with thorns and compound leaves that are trifoliate or pinnate. Its flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

Rosa millesia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
