Rubus centronotus

Rubus centronotus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The fruit is edible and often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
