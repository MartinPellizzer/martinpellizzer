Rubus heteracanthus

Rubus heteracanthus, commonly known as the roughbracted blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California and Oregon, where it grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a hairy surface. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
