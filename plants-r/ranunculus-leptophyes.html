Ranunculus leptophyes

Ranunculus leptophyes, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
