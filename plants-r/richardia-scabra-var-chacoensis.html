Richardia scabra var. chacoensis

Richardia scabra var. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chacoan sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the dry regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in shrublands and grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
