Rosa × dumetorum var. puberula

Rosa × dumetorum var. puberula, commonly known as the woolly dog rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give its stems and leaves a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces pale pink to white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and fragrance.
