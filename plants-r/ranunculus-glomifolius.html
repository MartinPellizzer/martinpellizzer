Ranunculus glomifolius

Ranunculus glomifolius, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of palmately lobed, bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy stems that bear alternate, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
