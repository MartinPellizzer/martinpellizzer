Rhaphidorhynchus fimbriatus

Rhaphidorhynchus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed horned orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers with intricate fringed petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
