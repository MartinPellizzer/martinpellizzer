Rumex bucephalophorus var. subaegaeus

Rumex bucephalophorus var. subaegaeus, commonly known as the narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and roadside areas. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a dense terminal cluster, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are usually covered with a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich environments and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
