Rosa × dumetorum f. pseudoincerta

Rosa × dumetorum f. pseudoincerta, commonly known as the creeping shrub rose, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Rosa rugosa and Rosa serotina.

It is a low-growing, thorny shrub with dense foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins, and the plant produces small, red-orange hips in the autumn. It is often found in coastal areas and moist soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This form is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
