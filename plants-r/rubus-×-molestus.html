Rubus × molestus

Rubus × molestus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it produces fragrant white flowers in the spring.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its sweet, juicy flavor and is used in culinary and commercial applications.
