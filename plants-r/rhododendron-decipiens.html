Rhododendron decipiens

Rhododendron decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble those of other rhododendron species, which can lead to confusion in identification. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
