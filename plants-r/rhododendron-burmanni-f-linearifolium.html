Rhododendron burmanni f. linearifolium

Rhododendron burmanni f. linearifolium, commonly known as the linear-leaved rhododendron, is a unique variant of the species Rhododendron burmanni.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that give it a distinct appearance compared to typical rhododendron foliage. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Myanmar and Thailand. It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, thriving in cool, humid climates.

Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in spring and add ornamental value to its natural habitat.
