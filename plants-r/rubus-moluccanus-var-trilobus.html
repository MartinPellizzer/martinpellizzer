Rubus moluccanus var. trilobus

Rubus moluccanus var. trilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
