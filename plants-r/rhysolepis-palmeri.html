Rhysolepis palmeri

Rhysolepis palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's rhyolepis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow to orange in color, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
