Rubus milliformis var. tenuifolius

Rubus milliformis var. tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems, serrated leaf margins, and flowers that are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.
