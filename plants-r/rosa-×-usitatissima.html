Rosa × usitatissima

Rosa × usitatissima, commonly known as the garden rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the crossbreeding of Rosa rugosa and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its diverse range of flower colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, and often features a fragrant aroma. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

Rosa × usitatissima is widely cultivated for its beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
