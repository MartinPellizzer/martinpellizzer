Rumex falcarius

Rumex falcarius, commonly known as the swordleaf sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color and have a distinctive sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Rumex falcarius is sometimes cultivated as a leaf vegetable but is also considered a weed in agricultural settings.
