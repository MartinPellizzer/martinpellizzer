Rosa myriacantha var. magna

Rosa myriacantha var. magna, commonly known as the giant thorned rose, is a variant of the thorned rose species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant features thick, thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
