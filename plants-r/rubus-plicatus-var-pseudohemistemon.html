Rubus plicatus var. pseudohemistemon

Rubus plicatus var. pseudohemistemon, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
