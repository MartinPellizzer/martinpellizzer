Rubus arcticus

Rubus arcticus, commonly known as the arctic raspberry, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, rocky soils and tundra environments. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs.

Rubus arcticus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for various wildlife species.
