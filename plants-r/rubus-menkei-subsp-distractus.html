Rubus menkei subsp. distractus

Rubus menkei subsp.

distractus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
