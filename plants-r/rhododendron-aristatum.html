Rhododendron aristatum

Rhododendron aristatum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rhododendron, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-elevation forests and alpine meadows. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, dark green leaves. Its flowers are showy, appearing in clusters of pink to red blossoms in the spring.

It is well adapted to cold climates and requires well-drained, acidic soils for optimal growth.
