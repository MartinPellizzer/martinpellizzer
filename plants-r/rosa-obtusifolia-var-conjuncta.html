Rosa obtusifolia var. conjuncta

Rosa obtusifolia var. conjuncta, commonly known as the Turkish rose or the Armenian rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include small, oval leaves with a slightly toothed margin and a compact growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
