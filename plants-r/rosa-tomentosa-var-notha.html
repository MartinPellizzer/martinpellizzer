Rosa tomentosa var. notha

Rosa tomentosa var. notha, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage that covers its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
