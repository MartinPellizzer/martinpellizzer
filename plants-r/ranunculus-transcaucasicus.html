Ranunculus transcaucasicus

Ranunculus transcaucasicus, commonly known as the Transcaucasian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens with proper moisture and light conditions.
