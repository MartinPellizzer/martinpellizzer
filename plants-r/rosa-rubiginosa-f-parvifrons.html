Rosa rubiginosa f. parvifrons

Rosa rubiginosa f. parvifrons, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are covered in short, hooked prickles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
