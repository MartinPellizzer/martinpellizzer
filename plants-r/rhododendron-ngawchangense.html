Rhododendron ngawchangense

Rhododendron ngawchangense, commonly known as the Ngawchang rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include hairy stems and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
