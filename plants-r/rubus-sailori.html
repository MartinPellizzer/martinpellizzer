Rubus sailori

Rubus sailori, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits resembling raspberries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in early summer.
