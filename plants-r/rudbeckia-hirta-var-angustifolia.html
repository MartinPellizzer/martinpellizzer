Rudbeckia hirta var. angustifolia

Rudbeckia hirta var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf coneflower, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces yellow-orange flower heads composed of a central disc surrounded by ray flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
