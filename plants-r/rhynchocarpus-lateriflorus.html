Rhynchocarpus lateriflorus

Rhynchocarpus lateriflorus, commonly known as the side-flowered legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, lateral flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. Its root system is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Rhynchocarpus lateriflorus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
