Ruellia tuberosa var. humilis

Ruellia tuberosa var. humilis, commonly known as the small tuberous ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
