Rosa pendulina f. laevis

Rosa pendulina f. laevis, commonly known as the smooth pendulous rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa pendulina species, characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with long, arching stems that cascade gracefully. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, distinguishing it from other rose varieties. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and pleasant scent, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
