Rumex elbrusensis var. kurdicus

Rumex elbrusensis var. kurdicus, commonly known as the Kurdish sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf structure and flowering habits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are succulent and have a tangy, sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
