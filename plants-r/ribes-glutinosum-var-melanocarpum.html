Ribes glutinosum var. melanocarpum

Ribes glutinosum var. melanocarpum, commonly known as black currant, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant has alternate, simple leaves with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
