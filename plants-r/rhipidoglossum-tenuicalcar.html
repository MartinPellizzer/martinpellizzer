Rhipidoglossum tenuicalcar

Rhipidoglossum tenuicalcar, commonly known as the slender-spurred orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, each with a distinctive, elongated spur that aids in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
