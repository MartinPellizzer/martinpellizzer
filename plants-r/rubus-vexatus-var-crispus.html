Rubus vexatus var. crispus

Rubus vexatus var. crispus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its prickly stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
