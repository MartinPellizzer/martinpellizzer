Rubus × trigenes

Rubus × trigenes, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Rubus × trigenes is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
