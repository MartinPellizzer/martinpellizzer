Ranunculus pseudoacris

Ranunculus pseudoacris, commonly known as the showy ranunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five to nine petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and its stems are usually hairy.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
