Ranunculus chodzhamastonicus

Ranunculus chodzhamastonicus, commonly known as the Chodzhar stonicus buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, colorful blooms with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. It has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its flowers are often bright yellow or orange.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
