Rhododendron platyphyllum

Rhododendron platyphyllum, commonly known as the flatleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its leaves are broad and flat, which gives it its common name, and it is often found in forest understories.

Rhododendron platyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
