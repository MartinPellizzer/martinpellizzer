Rubus sagittarius

Rubus sagittarius, commonly known as the arrowleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble arrows, hence its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Rubus sagittarius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
