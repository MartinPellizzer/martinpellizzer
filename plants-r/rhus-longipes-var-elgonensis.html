Rhus longipes var. elgonensis

Rhus longipes var. elgonensis, commonly known as the Elgon gum tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in panicles. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
