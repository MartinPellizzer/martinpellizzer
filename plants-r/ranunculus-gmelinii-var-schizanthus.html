Ranunculus gmelinii var. schizanthus

Ranunculus gmelinii var. schizanthus, commonly known as the Schizanthus ranunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, typically in shades of yellow and white. The plant has a short, upright stem and basal leaves that are trifoliate or palmately divided. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
