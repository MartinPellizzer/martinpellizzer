Rubus pergratiosus

Rubus pergratiosus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically serrated and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are dark purple to black when mature. These berries are edible and have a tart, sweet flavor, making them popular for culinary use.

Rubus pergratiosus thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
