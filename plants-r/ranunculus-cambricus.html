Ranunculus cambricus

Ranunculus cambricus, commonly known as the Welsh buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to western Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed and pinnately divided. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the buttercup and celandine.

Ranunculus cambricus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
