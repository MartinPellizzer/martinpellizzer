Riedlea tomentella

Riedlea tomentella, commonly known as the woolly riedlea, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Riedlea tomentella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
