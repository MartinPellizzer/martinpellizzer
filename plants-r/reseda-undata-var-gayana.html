Reseda undata var. gayana

Reseda undata var. gayana, commonly known as the Gayana ladies' mantle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that form a compact, mat-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
