Rubus uber

Rubus uber, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and broad, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of dark purple to black berries, which are edible but have a strong, tart flavor. Rubus uber thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant member of the Rubus genus.
