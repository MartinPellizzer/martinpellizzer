Racosperma verriculum

Racosperma verriculum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattled legume, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

Racosperma verriculum is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to local biodiversity.
