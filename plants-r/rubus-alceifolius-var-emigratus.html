Rubus alceifolius var. emigratus

Rubus alceifolius var. emigratus, commonly known as the Emigrating Blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
