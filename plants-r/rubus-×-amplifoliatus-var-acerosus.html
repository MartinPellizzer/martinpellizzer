Rubus × amplifoliatus var. acerosus

Rubus × amplifoliatus var. acerosus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
