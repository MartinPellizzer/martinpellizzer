Rubus pulvereus

Rubus pulvereus, commonly known as the powdered raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, pale green stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, followed by red, hairy fruits that are edible but often tart. Rubus pulvereus thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
