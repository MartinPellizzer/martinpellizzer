Rosa pseudovestita

Rosa pseudovestita, commonly known as the pink sand rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of Central Asia, particularly parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and the plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy environments.

Rosa pseudovestita is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
