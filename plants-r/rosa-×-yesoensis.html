Rosa × yesoensis

Rosa × yesoensis, commonly known as the Yeso rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is a shrub-like plant native to Japan, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
