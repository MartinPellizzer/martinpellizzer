Rhododendron cephalanthum subsp. platyphyllum

Rhododendron cephalanthum subsp.

platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are typically broad and leathery, with a deep green color and a waxy surface. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable component of its native ecosystem.
