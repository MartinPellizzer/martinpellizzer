Ranunculus hertfordensis

Ranunculus hertfordensis, commonly known as Hertford's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the United Kingdom.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
