Rubus funiculiformis

Rubus funiculiformis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, edible berries. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. The fruit is a aggregate of small drupes, which ripen to a dark purple or black color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local ecosystems.
