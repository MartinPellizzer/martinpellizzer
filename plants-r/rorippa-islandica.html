Rorippa islandica

Rorippa islandica, commonly known as the island watercress, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has smooth, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom from June to August.

Rorippa islandica is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich habitats and is considered an indicator species for moist environments.
