Rosa livida

Rosa livida, commonly known as the purpleleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its pink to white flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
