Rubus scaber var. schackii

Rubus scaber var. schackii, commonly known as Schack's bramble, is a variant of the common bramble, characterized by its prickly stems and dense growth habit.

It is native to Europe and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
