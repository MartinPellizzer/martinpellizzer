Rosa tomentella var. modenensis

Rosa tomentella var. modenensis, commonly known as the Modena rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in pink to white hues. The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to dry, rocky soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

The variety is noted for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance choice for gardens.
