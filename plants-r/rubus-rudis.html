Rubus rudis

Rubus rudis, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dunes or along the shoreline.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
