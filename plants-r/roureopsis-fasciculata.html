Roureopsis fasciculata

Roureopsis fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, often with a faintly colored lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
