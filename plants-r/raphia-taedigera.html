Raphia taedigera

Raphia taedigera, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a large, tropical palm native to the rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by edible, fibrous fruits that are an important food source for local communities. Its robust trunk and strong fibers make it valuable for construction and handicrafts.

Raphia taedigera plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
