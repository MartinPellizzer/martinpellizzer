Rubus idaeus erythrocarpa

Rubus idaeus erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a perennial shrub that often grows in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its fruit is valued for its flavor and is used in culinary applications, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
