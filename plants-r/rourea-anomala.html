Rourea anomala

Rourea anomala, commonly known as the velvetleaf rourea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
