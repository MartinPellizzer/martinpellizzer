Ranunculus malessanus

Ranunculus malessanus, commonly known as the Malessa ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has simple, trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
