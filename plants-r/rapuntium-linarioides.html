Rapuntium linarioides

Rapuntium linarioides, commonly known as the slender rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant its scientific name "linarioides," which refers to its resemblance to the genus Linaria.
