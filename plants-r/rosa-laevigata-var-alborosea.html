Rosa laevigata var. alborosea

Rosa laevigata var. alborosea, commonly known as the white-rose Chinese rose, is a variant of the Chinese rose that features predominantly white petals.

It is native to China and is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and a woody stem structure typical of many rose species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
