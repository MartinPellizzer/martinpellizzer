Rhodanthemum arundanum

Rhodanthemum arundanum, commonly known as the meadow rhodanthemum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Rhodanthemum arundanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
