Rubus swinhoei

Rubus swinhoei, commonly known as the Swinhoe's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
