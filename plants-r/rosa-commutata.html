Rosa commutata

Rosa commutata, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in regions of Georgia, Florida, and Alabama. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, pleasant fragrance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
