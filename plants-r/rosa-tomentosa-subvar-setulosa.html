Rosa tomentosa subvar. setulosa

Rosa tomentosa subvar. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent stems and foliage, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a source of cut flowers.
