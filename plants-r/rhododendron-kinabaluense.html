Rhododendron kinabaluense

Rhododendron kinabaluense, commonly known as the Kinabalu rhododendron, is a species native to the high altitudes of Mount Kinabalu in Borneo.

It is a shrub that typically grows in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

This species is notable for its adaptation to the unique ecological conditions of the mountainous region.
