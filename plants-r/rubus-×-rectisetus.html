Rubus × rectisetus

Rubus × rectisetus, commonly known as the brambleberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often edible, though they can vary in taste and texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations.
