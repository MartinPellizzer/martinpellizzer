Rosa arvensis f. scardica

Rosa arvensis f. scardica, commonly known as the Scardica form of field rose, is a botanical variant of the field rose, characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces pink to red flowers with a pleasant fragrance, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rosa genus.

This form is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique traits and adaptability.
