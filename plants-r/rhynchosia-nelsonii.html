Rhynchosia nelsonii

Rhynchosia nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds.

Rhynchosia nelsonii is often cultivated for its edible beans and is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
