Ranunculus auricomus subsp. cinereoviridis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

cinereoviridis, commonly known as the silver-veined buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive white or pale green center.

The leaves are deeply lobed and often have a silvery or pale green underside, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
