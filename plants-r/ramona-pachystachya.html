Ramona pachystachya

Ramona pachystachya, commonly known as the giant feather grass, is a striking ornamental grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, arching stems that bear long, silky, silver-green plumes, which create a dramatic visual impact in gardens. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and low-maintenance landscapes. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round interest, while its feathery flowers add movement and texture.

Ramona pachystachya is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
