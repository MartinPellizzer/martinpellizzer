Rhamnus philippinensis

Rhamnus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine buckthorn, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
