Ruellia ischnopoda

Ruellia ischnopoda, commonly known as the velvetleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ruellia ischnopoda thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats like roadsides and gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in warm climates.
