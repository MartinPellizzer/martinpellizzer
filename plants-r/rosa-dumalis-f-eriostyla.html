Rosa dumalis f. eriostyla

Rosa dumalis f. eriostyla, commonly known as the soft-styled rock rose, is a horticultural form of the rock rose species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.
