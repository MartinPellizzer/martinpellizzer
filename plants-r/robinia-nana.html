Robinia nana

Robinia nana, commonly known as the dwarf false acacia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 12 to 20 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets, and produces small, yellow, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring. The plant is characterized by its woody, thornless stems and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and as a hedge.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ecological and aesthetic purposes.
