Ripartia istriaca

Ripartia istriaca, commonly known as the Istrian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Adriatic.

It typically features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its foliage is used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Ripartia istriaca is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing coastal soils.
