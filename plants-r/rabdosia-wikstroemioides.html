Rabdosia wikstroemioides

Rabdosia wikstroemioides, commonly known as the Chinese bluebells, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Rabdosia wikstroemioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
