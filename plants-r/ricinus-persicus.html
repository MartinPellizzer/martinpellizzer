Ricinus persicus

Ricinus persicus, commonly known as Persian castor, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia, including Persia, from which it gets its name. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and distinctive, spiny seed capsules that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The plant's unique appearance and historical significance have made it a subject of both botanical study and cultural interest.
