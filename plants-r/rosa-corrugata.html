Rosa corrugata

Rosa corrugata, commonly known as the zigzag rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a serrated edge, and its flowers typically bloom in spring. Rosa corrugata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
