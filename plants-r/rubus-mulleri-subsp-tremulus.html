Rubus mulleri subsp. tremulus

Rubus mulleri subsp.

tremulus, commonly known as the trembling blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
