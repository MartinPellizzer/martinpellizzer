Rhododendron orthocladum

Rhododendron orthocladum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in humid and cool climates. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with broad, leathery leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in the spring.

Rhododendron orthocladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
