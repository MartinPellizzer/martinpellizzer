Ranunculus geraniifolius proles accessivus

Ranunculus geraniifolius proles accessivus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and is often found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and sometimes toxic sap.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling due to its potentially irritant properties.
