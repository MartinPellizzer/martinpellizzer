Rosa decurtata var. ervallensis

Rosa decurtata var. ervallensis, commonly known as the Ervall's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its hardiness and adaptability.

The species is also noted for its potential in horticultural and ecological applications.
