Rubus × ambiguus

Rubus × ambiguus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberry (Rubus fruticosus) and raspberry (Rubus idaeus).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark red to black in color, containing numerous small drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

The botanical features of Rubus × ambiguus make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
