Rhododendron calostrotum

Rhododendron calostrotum, commonly known as the Mexican flame azalea, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow or orange center. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron calostrotum thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
