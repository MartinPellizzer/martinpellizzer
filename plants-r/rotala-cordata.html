Rotala cordata

Rotala cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to cordate leaves that are often dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in slow-moving to still freshwater environments and is often used in pond landscaping.

Rotala cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
