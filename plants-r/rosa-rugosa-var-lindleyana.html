Rosa rugosa var. lindleyana

Rosa rugosa var. lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's rugosa rose, is a shrub-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth, thorny stems, and clusters of fragrant pink or red flowers. The plant produces large, edible hips that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in coastal areas and is highly tolerant of salt spray and harsh weather conditions.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for landscaping in challenging environments.
