Ranunculus conspicuus

Ranunculus conspicuus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with 5 to 15 petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ranunculus conspicuus is often found in meadows, stream banks, and forest edges, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
