Recchia connaroides

Recchia connaroides, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
