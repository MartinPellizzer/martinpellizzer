Rhododendron rhodanthum

Rhododendron rhodanthum, commonly known as the Chilean rhododendron, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include woody stems and opposite, leathery leaves.

Rhododendron rhodanthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
