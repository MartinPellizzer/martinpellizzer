Ranunculus hirtellus var. parciflorus

Ranunculus hirtellus var. parciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a ring of hairy sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, with lobed segments that are covered in fine, hairy textures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
