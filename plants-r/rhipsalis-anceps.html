Rhipsalis anceps

Rhipsalis anceps, commonly known as the silver hair cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its long, trailing stems covered in white, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in winter, adding ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
