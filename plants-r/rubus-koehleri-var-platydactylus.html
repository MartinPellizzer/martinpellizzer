Rubus koehleri var. platydactylus

Rubus koehleri var. platydactylus, commonly known as the broad-fingered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its trailing or climbing habit. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.
