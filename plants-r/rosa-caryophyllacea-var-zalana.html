Rosa caryophyllacea var. zalana

Rosa caryophyllacea var. zalana, commonly known as the Zalana rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
