Rubus genevensis

Rubus genevensis, commonly known as the Geneva raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
