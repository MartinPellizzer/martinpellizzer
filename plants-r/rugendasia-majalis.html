Rugendasia majalis

Rugendasia majalis, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers typically bloom in late summer. Rugendasia majalis thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
