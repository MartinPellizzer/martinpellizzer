Rubus lasiothyrsus microgene prolongatus

Rubus lasiothyrsus microgene prolongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed bramble, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
