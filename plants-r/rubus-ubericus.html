Rubus ubericus

Rubus ubericus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, dark purple berries that are highly prized for their tart flavor. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, often found in forested areas or along rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is used in jams, wines, and culinary dishes.
