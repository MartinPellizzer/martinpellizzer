Rhodomyrtus parviflora

Rhodomyrtus parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a distinctive red or pinkish coloration on the underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or red, and they appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Rhodomyrtus parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
