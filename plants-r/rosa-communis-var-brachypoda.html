Rosa communis var. brachypoda

Rosa communis var. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed rose, is a variant of the common rose species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter, more upright stems compared to other rose varieties. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of serrated, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the blossoms.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
