Rubus × lasiothyrsoides

Rubus × lasiothyrsoides, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant that combines traits of blackberries and raspberries.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries, which are usually dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The botanical characteristics of Rubus × lasiothyrsoides make it a popular choice for both commercial and home gardening.
