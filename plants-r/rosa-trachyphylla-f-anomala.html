Rosa trachyphylla f. anomala

Rosa trachyphylla f. anomala, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a unique horticultural form of the species Rosa trachyphylla.

It is characterized by its rugged, bristly stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is well adapted to harsh, arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The foliage is typically covered in fine, velvety hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
