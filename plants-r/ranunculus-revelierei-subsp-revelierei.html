Ranunculus revelierei subsp. revelierei

Ranunculus revelierei subsp.

revelierei, commonly known as the Revelier's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its environment.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
