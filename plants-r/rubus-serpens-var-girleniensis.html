Rubus serpens var. girleniensis

Rubus serpens var. girleniensis, commonly known as the Georgian blackberry, is a variant of the serpentine blackberry native to the Caucasus region.

It is a shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are small and white, typically appearing in clusters. The fruit is a dark purple to black berry, which is edible and often used in local cuisine.

This plant thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.
