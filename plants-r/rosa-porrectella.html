Rosa porrectella

Rosa porrectella, commonly known as the upright rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically growing in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant features thorny stems and produces showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.

Rosa porrectella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
