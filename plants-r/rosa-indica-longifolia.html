Rosa indica longifolia

Rosa indica longifolia, commonly known as the Indian rose or longifolia rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayas and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is well adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soil.
