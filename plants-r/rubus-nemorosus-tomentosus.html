Rubus nemorosus tomentosus

Rubus nemorosus tomentosus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through vegetative means.
