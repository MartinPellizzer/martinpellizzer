Ranunculus acris subsp. nathorstii

Ranunculus acris subsp.

nathorstii, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
