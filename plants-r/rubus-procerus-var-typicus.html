Rubus procerus var. typicus

Rubus procerus var. typicus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or chaparral habitats.

The botanical features of this species make it an important component of local ecosystems.
