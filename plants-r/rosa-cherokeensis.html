Rosa cherokeensis

Rosa cherokeensis, commonly known as the Cherokee rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets and produces red, hairy fruits called hips. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its historical significance to the Cherokee people adds cultural value to its botanical importance.
