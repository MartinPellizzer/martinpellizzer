Rhinactinidia eremophila subsp. grubovii

Rhinactinidia eremophila subsp.

grubovii, commonly known as the Grubov's desert bell, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
