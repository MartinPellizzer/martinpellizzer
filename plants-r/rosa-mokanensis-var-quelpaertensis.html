Rosa mokanensis var. quelpaertensis

Rosa mokanensis var. quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Jeju rose, is a shrub native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are often used in traditional Korean gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
