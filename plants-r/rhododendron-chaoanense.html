Rhododendron chaoanense

Rhododendron chaoanense, commonly known as the Chaoan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
