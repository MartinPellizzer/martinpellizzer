Rumex angiocarpus var. australis

Rumex angiocarpus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian dock, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has alternate, lobed leaves that are often red-veined and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, terminal spikes, typically blooming in spring.

The plant produces distinctive, three-lobed, reddish-brown fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal.
