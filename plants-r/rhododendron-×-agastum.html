Rhododendron × agastum

Rhododendron × agastum, commonly known as the Indian rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Rhododendron × agastum is also known for its ability to form thickets, which can play a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
