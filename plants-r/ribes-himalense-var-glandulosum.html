Ribes himalense var. glandulosum

Ribes himalense var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, aggregate berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
