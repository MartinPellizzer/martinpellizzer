Rupicapnos ochracea var. battandieri

Rupicapnos ochracea var. battandieri, commonly known as the golden currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaves and a compact growth habit that adds visual interest to the landscape.
