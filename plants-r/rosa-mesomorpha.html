Rosa mesomorpha

Rosa mesomorpha, commonly known as the Mexican rose, is a species of shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils. It is a deciduous species, shedding its leaves in the winter season.

Rosa mesomorpha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
