Rhus bucku-amela

Rhus bucku-amela, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

Rhus bucku-amela is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant seasonal display.
