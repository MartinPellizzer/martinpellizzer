Rumicastrum wilsonii

Rumicastrum wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's burrobrush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, three-lobed fruits that contain seeds. Its adaptations, such as hairy foliage and deep root systems, enable it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Rumicastrum wilsonii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
