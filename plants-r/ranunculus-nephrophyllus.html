Ranunculus nephrophyllus

Ranunculus nephrophyllus, commonly known as the water crown, is a species of flowering plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water's surface. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white with a distinctive greenish center.

Ranunculus nephrophyllus thrives in shallow water and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
