Rhododendron coenenii

Rhododendron coenenii, commonly known as the Coenen's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Rhododendron coenenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
