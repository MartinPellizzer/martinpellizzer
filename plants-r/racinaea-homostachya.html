Racinaea homostachya

Racinaea homostachya, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in waterlogged soils. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, often with a fragrant scent. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, typically floating on the water surface or emerging from the mud.

Racinaea homostachya plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by providing food and shelter for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
