Rosa × collina var. laevigata

Rosa × collina var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed hybrid rose, is a species characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, pale pink to white flowers.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rosa rugosa and Rosa bracteata, exhibiting traits of both parent species. The plant typically has smooth, reddish-brown stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
