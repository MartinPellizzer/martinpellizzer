Racosperma carneorum

Racosperma carneorum, commonly known as the pink-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has a woody stem with a rough texture.

Racosperma carneorum is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
