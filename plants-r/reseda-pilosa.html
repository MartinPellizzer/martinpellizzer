Reseda pilosa

Reseda pilosa, commonly known as hispid rattleweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that rattle when shaken, giving it its common name. Reseda pilosa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
