Rhodanthe pyrethrum

Rhodanthe pyrethrum, commonly known as the golden rattle.

It is a flowering plant native to South Australia, characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its showy, daisy-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Rhodanthe pyrethrum is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
