Rubus procerus var. medius

Rubus procerus var. medius, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles.
