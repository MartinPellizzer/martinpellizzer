Rumex transitorius var. monotylos

Rumex transitorius var. monotylos, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically arranged in a loose panicle. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along streambanks.

Narrowleaf dock is a perennial species that is widely used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential health benefits.
