Racosperma warramaba

Racosperma warramaba, commonly known as the Warramaba acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Racosperma warramaba plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
