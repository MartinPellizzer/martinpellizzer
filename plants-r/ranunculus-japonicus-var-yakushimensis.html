Ranunculus japonicus var. yakushimensis

Ranunculus japonicus var. yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushimen buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it produces a cluster of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
