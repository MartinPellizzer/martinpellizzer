Rosa glauca var. recurvata

Rosa glauca var. recurvata, commonly known as the blue rose.

It is a variant of the blue rose, characterized by its unique bluish-pink petals. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Caucasus. The flowers are typically small and borne in clusters, with a delicate fragrance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
