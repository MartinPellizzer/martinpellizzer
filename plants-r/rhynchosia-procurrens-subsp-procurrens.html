Rhynchosia procurrens subsp. procurrens

Rhynchosia procurrens subsp.

procurrens, commonly known as the creeping cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that trail along the ground.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It has compound leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and is often used as a forage crop.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings.
