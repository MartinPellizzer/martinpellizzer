Rubus × marmarcus

Rubus × marmarcus, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for both gardens and small-scale cultivation.
