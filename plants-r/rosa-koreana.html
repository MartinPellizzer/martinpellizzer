Rosa koreana

Rosa koreana, commonly known as the Korean rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Rosa koreana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
