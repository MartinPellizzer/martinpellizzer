Rosa lucieae var. nakaiana

Rosa lucieae var. nakaiana, commonly known as the Nakaiana rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanically significant and sometimes endangered species.
