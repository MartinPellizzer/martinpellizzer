Rorippa portoricensis subsp. pumila

Rorippa portoricensis subsp.

pumila, commonly known as the Puerto Rican watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the island of Puerto Rico. It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and limestone cliffs.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its native environment.
