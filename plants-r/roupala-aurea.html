Roupala aurea

Roupala aurea, commonly known as the golden roupala, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow or golden green, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring, followed by spherical, red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and seasonal color changes.

Roupala aurea prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
