Roupala pseudocordata

Roupala pseudocordata, commonly known as the false holly or holly tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and small, red berries that persist through winter. The leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture and a distinctive midrib. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
