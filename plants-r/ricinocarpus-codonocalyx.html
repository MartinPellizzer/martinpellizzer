Ricinocarpus codonocalyx

Ricinocarpus codonocalyx, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The plant is notable for its potential medicinal uses and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
