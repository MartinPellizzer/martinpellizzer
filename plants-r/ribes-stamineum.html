Ribes stamineum

Ribes stamineum, commonly known as the redcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow and appear in clusters, blooming in early summer.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible berries, which are used in jams, jellies, and other culinary applications.
