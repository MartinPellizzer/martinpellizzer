Rhus grandidieri

Rhus grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a distinctive plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its thick, fissured bark and dense foliage. The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, followed by spherical, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a velvety texture.

Rhus grandidieri thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and coastal areas.
