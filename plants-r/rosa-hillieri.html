Rosa hillieri

Rosa hillieri, commonly known as the Hillier rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their strong, sweet scent.

Rosa hillieri is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in botanical gardens.
