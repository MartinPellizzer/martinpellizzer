Ranunculus monopetalus

Ranunculus monopetalus, commonly known as the single-petaled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with a single, prominent petal and numerous sepals that resemble petals.

The leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its delicate, herbaceous structure.
