Rubus erythrogynus

Rubus erythrogynus, commonly known as the red-stamen raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, red, edible berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces yellowish-white flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are used in culinary applications and are also valued for their ornamental qualities.
