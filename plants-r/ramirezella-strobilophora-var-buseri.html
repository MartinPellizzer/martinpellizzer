Ramirezella strobilophora var. buseri

Ramirezella strobilophora var. buseri, commonly known as the Buser's ramireza, is a rare and specialized fern native to specific regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricately veined fronds that exhibit a unique morphology adapted to its microhabitat. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in association with other epiphytic species. Its reproductive structures, including strobili, are small and clustered, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to habitat destruction and its restricted range, this variant is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
