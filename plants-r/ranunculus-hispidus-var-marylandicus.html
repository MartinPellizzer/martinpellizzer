Ranunculus hispidus var. marylandicus

Ranunculus hispidus var. marylandicus, commonly known as the Maryland buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
