Rosa calvilla

Rosa calvilla, commonly known as the Calvilla rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, which can germinate in well-drained soil.

Rosa calvilla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
