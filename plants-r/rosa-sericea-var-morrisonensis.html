Rosa sericea var. morrisonensis

Rosa sericea var. morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's silvery rose, is a variant of the silvery rose species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves and pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
