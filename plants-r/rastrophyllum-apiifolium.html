Rastrophyllum apiifolium

Rastrophyllum apiifolium, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are palmately lobed, resembling the leaves of the carrot plant, hence its scientific name.

Rastrophyllum apiifolium plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
