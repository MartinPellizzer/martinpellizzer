Rosa × bifera alba

Rosa × bifera alba, commonly known as the white everblooming rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its continuous flowering habit.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers throughout the growing season. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
