Rubus mercieri var. frondosoumbrosus

Rubus mercieri var. frondosoumbrosus, commonly known as the broadleaf bramble, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and thorny stems, typical of many bramble species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of shrubland ecosystems.
