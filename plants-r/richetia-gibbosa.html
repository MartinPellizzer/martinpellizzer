Richetia gibbosa

Richetia gibbosa, commonly known as the cushion plant, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in alpine and arctic environments.

It has dense, compact foliage that helps it withstand harsh weather conditions. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Richetia gibbosa is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
