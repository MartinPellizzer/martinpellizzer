Rhododendron christi var. loniceroides

Rhododendron christi var. loniceroides, commonly known as the Christ's rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during the spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has a rounded, bushy form. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
