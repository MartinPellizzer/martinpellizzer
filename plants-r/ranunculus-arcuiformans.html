Ranunculus arcuiformans

Ranunculus arcuiformans, commonly known as the bentleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves that are often curved or twisted, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that resemble petals. It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows.

Ranunculus arcuiformans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
