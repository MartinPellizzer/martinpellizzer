Ridsdalea uvarioides

Ridsdalea uvarioides, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or shaded forest environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. Its floral structure is characterized by a labellum that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
