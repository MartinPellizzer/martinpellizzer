Rosa mollis f. deficientipes

Rosa mollis f. deficientipes, commonly known as the soft rose with reduced petals, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its delicate, semi-evergreen foliage and smaller, less showy flowers compared to typical roses. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers partial to full sun exposure. Its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a unique texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
