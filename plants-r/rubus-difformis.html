Rubus difformis

Rubus difformis, commonly known as the bent-thorn blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland and thicket habitats, often growing in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
