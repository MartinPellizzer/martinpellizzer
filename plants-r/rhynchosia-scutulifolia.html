Rhynchosia scutulifolia

Rhynchosia scutulifolia, commonly known as the shieldleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds.

Rhynchosia scutulifolia is often grown for its nutritious seeds, which are used in local cuisines, and it has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
