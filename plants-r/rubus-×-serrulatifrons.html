Rubus × serrulatifrons

Rubus × serrulatifrons, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
