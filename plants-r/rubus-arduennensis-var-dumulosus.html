Rubus arduennensis var. dumulosus

Rubus arduennensis var. dumulosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and an aggregate fruit structure composed of multiple drupelets.

This variant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in local ecosystems.
