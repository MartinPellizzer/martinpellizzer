Rubus subriglandulosus

Rubus subriglandulosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
