Ranunculus arvidii

Ranunculus arvidii, commonly known as the Arvid's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus arvidii is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
