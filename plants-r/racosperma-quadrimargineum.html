Racosperma quadrimargineum

Racosperma quadrimargineum, commonly known as the four-margin acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with four margins, giving it its scientific name. It produces yellow flowers in dense spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus.

Racosperma quadrimargineum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry woodlands and shrublands.
