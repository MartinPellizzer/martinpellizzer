Rubus drenthicus

Rubus drenthicus, commonly known as the Drenthicus blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

The fruit, a dark purple-black berry, is edible but often has a tart flavor.
