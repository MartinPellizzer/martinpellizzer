Rubus caesius var. rubrohispidus

Rubus caesius var. rubrohispidus, commonly known as the red-hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, red-tomentose (hairy) foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and a perennial growth habit.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
