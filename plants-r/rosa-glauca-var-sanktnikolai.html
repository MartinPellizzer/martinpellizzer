Rosa glauca var. sanktnikolai

Rosa glauca var. sanktnikolai, commonly known as the Sankt Nikolai rose, is a shrub-like plant native to Central Europe.

It features thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and a distinctive, slightly fragrant aroma. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and resilience in temperate climates.
