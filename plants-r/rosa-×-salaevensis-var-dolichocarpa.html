Rosa × salaevensis var. dolichocarpa

Rosa × salaevensis var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-stemmed Siberian rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to cold climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Rosa genus.
