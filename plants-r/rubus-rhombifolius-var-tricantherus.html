Rubus rhombifolius var. tricantherus

Rubus rhombifolius var. tricantherus, commonly known as the three-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is characterized by its thorny stems and is part of the Rosaceae family.
