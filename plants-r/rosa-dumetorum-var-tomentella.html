Rosa dumetorum var. tomentella

Rosa dumetorum var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage that covers its stems and leaves, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
