Rumex × confusus

Rumex × confusus, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex acetobulus (broad-leaved sorrel), resulting in a plant with intermediate characteristics. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, with a reddish or purple coloration on the veins, and they have a tangy, sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
