Rubia margaritifera

Rubia margaritifera, commonly known as the pearlflower or white-flowered bugloss, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia margaritifera is often found in meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
