Rubus asclepiadeus

Rubus asclepiadeus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are dark purple berries that ripen in the summer and are edible.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
