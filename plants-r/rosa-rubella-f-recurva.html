Rosa rubella f. recurva

Rosa rubella f. recurva, commonly known as the recurved pink rose, is a unique cultivar characterized by its distinctive floral form.

It features semi-double to fully double pink flowers with a recurved petal structure, giving it an elegant and ornamental appearance. The plant is a compact shrub with thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often grown in gardens for its visual appeal.
