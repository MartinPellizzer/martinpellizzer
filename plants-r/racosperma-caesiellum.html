Racosperma caesiellum

Racosperma caesiellum, commonly known as the blue raceme wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in racemes. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma caesiellum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
