Rubus corchorifolius var. oliveri

Rubus corchorifolius var. oliveri, commonly known as Oliver's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

This variety is valued for its robust growth and high fruit yield.
