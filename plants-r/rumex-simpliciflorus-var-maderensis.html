Rumex simpliciflorus var. maderensis

Rumex simpliciflorus var. maderensis, commonly known as Mader's dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and slightly lobed, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
