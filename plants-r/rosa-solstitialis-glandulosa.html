Rosa solstitialis glandulosa

Rosa solstitialis glandulosa, commonly known as the summer solstice rose, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glandular hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, sticky secretions. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its thorns are short and numerous.

This species is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks.
