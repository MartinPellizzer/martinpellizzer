Rhododendron macrophyllum f. album

Rhododendron macrophyllum f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a cultivar characterized by its large, showy white flowers.

It is a hybrid of Rhododendron macrophyllum, which is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub with thick, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking white display to gardens and natural landscapes.
