Ryparosa calotricha

Ryparosa calotricha, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ryparosa calotricha is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
