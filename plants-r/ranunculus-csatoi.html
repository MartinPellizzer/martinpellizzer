Ranunculus csatoi

Ranunculus csatoi, commonly known as the Csatoi buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins, and its flowers typically have five to seven sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
