Rhus cismontana var. flavescens

Rhus cismontana var. flavescens, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 11 to 31 leaflets that turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by orange to red berries that attract birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it an important species in ecological succession.
