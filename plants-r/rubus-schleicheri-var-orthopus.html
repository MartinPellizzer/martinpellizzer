Rubus schleicheri var. orthopus

Rubus schleicheri var. orthopus, commonly known as the mountain blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
