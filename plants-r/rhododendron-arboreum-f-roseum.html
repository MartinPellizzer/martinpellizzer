Rhododendron arboreum f. roseum

Rhododendron arboreum f. roseum, commonly known as the Rose-flowered大树杜鹃, is a botanical variant of the species Rhododendron arboreum.

It is characterized by its striking pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in dense forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that provide dense shade, making it a valuable component of mountainous ecosystems.
