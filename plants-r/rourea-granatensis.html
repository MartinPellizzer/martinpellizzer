Rourea granatensis

Rourea granatensis, commonly known as the granadilla or red granadilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and evergreen foliage, with leaves that are typically elliptical and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Rourea granatensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
