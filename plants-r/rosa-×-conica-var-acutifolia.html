Rosa × conica var. acutifolia

Rosa × conica var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with acute tips. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It is typically cultivated as a shrub or small tree, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
