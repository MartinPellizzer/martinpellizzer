Ranunculus grandis var. ozensis

Ranunculus grandis var. ozensis, commonly known as the Ozensis buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically with five to nine petals. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
