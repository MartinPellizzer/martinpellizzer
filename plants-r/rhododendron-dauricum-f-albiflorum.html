Rhododendron dauricum f. albiflorum

Rhododendron dauricum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the Daurian rhododendron var. albiflorum, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the northeastern regions of Asia.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, with a faint pinkish hue at the base. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
