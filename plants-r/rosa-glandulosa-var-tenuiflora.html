Rosa glandulosa var. tenuiflora

Rosa glandulosa var. tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved shrub rose, is a perennial species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and small, dark green leaves with glandular dots. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a fragrant scent, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
