Ranunculus skottsbergii

Ranunculus skottsbergii, commonly known as the Skottsberg's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
