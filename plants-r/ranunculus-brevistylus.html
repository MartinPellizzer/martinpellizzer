Ranunculus brevistylus

Ranunculus brevistylus, commonly known as the short-styled buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its delicate appearance.

Ranunculus brevistylus is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in the spring and early summer.
