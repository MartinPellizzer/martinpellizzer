Rhexia helvola

Rhexia helvola, commonly known as white fringed gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white margin. Its flowers have five petals and are often adorned with yellow-orange veins, enhancing their visual appeal.

Rhexia helvola is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
