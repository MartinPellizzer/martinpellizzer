Rhododendron hallaisanense

Rhododendron hallaisanense, commonly known as the Hallaisan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
