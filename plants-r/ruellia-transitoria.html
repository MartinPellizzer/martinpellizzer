Ruellia transitoria

Ruellia transitoria, commonly known as the transitory ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers. Its flowering period occurs primarily in the spring and summer months.

Ruellia transitoria is often found in open woods, wetlands, and along stream banks.
