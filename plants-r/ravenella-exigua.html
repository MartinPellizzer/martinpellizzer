Ravenella exigua

Ravenella exigua, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which resemble those of the hibiscus genus. The plant has a woody stem and produces showy, five-petaled blooms that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a glossy appearance.

Ravenella exigua thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
