Ranunculus fallax subsp. connectens

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

connectens, commonly known as the western meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America. It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features showy yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it produces small, greenish-brown achenes as fruits. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
