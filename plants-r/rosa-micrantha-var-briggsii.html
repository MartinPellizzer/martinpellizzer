Rosa micrantha var. briggsii

Rosa micrantha var. briggsii, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle scent. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
