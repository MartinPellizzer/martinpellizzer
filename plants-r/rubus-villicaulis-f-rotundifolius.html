Rubus villicaulis f. rotundifolius

Rubus villicaulis f. rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctly rounded leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage is dense and often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and pollinators.
