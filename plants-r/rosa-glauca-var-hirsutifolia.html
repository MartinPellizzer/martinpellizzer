Rosa glauca var. hirsutifolia

Rosa glauca var. hirsutifolia, commonly known as the hairy blue rose, is a variety of the blue rose that exhibits distinctive hirsute characteristics.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a unique texture and appearance. The flowers are typically pale blue or white, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a hardy shrub that requires full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
