Rhetinantha encyclioides

Rhetinantha encyclioides, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, floating on the surface of still water. The plant's flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and a distinctive red center, blooming in the early morning and closing by midday. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

This species plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
