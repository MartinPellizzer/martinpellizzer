Ribes spicatum var. pubescens

Ribes spicatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent currant, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible red berries in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
