Rapuntium grande

Rapuntium grande, commonly known as the giant cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found near water bodies or in wet meadows. Its edible leaves have a peppery taste and are used in some traditional cuisines.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to spread through self-seeding make it both a useful and sometimes invasive species.
